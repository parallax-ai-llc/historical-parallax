## Summary

Marc Andreessen is an American entrepreneur and software engineer who co-authored Mosaic, the first widely used web browser, and co-founded Netscape. Mosaic popularized the World Wide Web for mainstream users, and Netscape's IPO in 1995 launched the dot-com era. He later co-founded the venture capital firm Andreessen Horowitz, one of the most influential technology investors in Silicon Valley.
