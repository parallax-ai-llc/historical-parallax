## Summary

Sierra Leonean Civil Defence Forces leader convicted of war crimes for the CDF militia's atrocities including murder, cruel treatment, and looting during the civil war.

## Civil War Context

Moinina Fofana is primarily discussed in relation to the Sierra Leone Civil War (1991–2002), a conflict involving multiple armed factions and marked by widespread abuses against civilians.

## Civil Defence Forces and the Kamajors

Fofana has been associated with the Civil Defence Forces (CDF), a coalition that included the Kamajors militia. In reporting and tribunal records, the CDF is often framed as a pro-government fighting force, though it was also accused of committing serious crimes during the conflict.

## Accountability and International Justice

Post-war Sierra Leone saw the establishment of the Special Court for Sierra Leone (SCSL), which prosecuted individuals considered to bear the greatest responsibility for atrocities. The CDF cases are frequently cited in discussions about the legal treatment of pro-government militias and the limits of "legitimate" self-defence claims in civil wars.
