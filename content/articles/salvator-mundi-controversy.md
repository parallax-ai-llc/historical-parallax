## Summary

The most expensive painting ever sold ($450 million), attributed to Leonardo da Vinci, remains controversial. Experts dispute its authorship; it disappeared into Saudi Crown Prince MBS's collection.

## What the Controversy Is

The “Salvator Mundi” controversy concerns the attribution, restoration history, and ownership of a painting presented as a work by Leonardo da Vinci and sold at a record price in 2017. The debate centers on whether the work should be considered fully autograph Leonardo, a workshop production with later overpainting, or a different classification—questions that matter for both scholarship and market value.

## Why It Became High-Profile

The combination of a headline auction result, heavy marketing, and unresolved scholarly disagreement made the painting a flashpoint in public discussions about authentication and the art market. Commentators often point to the role of restoration, incomplete provenance narratives, and expert dissent as reasons the attribution remains contested in the public sphere.

## Public Visibility and Ownership Questions

After the sale, planned public exhibitions were reported and then changed, and the painting’s precise location and display status became unclear in mainstream reporting. The limited public access further fueled speculation and contributed to the “mystique” surrounding the work.
