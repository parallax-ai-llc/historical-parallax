## Summary

Hackers compromised Mixin Network's cloud service provider and stole approximately $200 million in cryptocurrency. The attack targeted the platform's hot wallet infrastructure.

## Additional Context

### What Happened (High-Level)

- Reported as a compromise involving Mixin Network's cloud service provider.
- Attackers were reported to have accessed infrastructure connected to the platform's hot wallets, enabling unauthorized transfers.

### Hot Wallet Context

- Hot wallets are internet-connected wallets used for operational liquidity; they carry higher compromise risk than cold storage.
- Platforms typically try to mitigate this risk via segregation of keys, access controls, and withdrawal limits.

### Immediate Response

- The platform reportedly paused withdrawals and deposits during incident response.

### Notes

- Public reporting commonly cited losses of roughly $200 million, though exact totals and attribution details can vary by source.
