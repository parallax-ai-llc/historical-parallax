## Summary

Thomas Newcomen invented the atmospheric steam engine in 1712, the first practical engine to harness steam power for useful work. His engine was primarily used to pump water from coal mines and was the predecessor to all modern steam engines. It was the most important technological development of the early Industrial Revolution.
