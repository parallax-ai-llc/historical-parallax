## Summary

Monica Seles won nine Grand Slam titles before being stabbed on court by a fan of rival Steffi Graf in 1993. She was just 19 and ranked world number one.

## Early Career

Seles turned professional as a teenager and quickly rose to the top of women's tennis with a powerful two-handed game on both sides. By the early 1990s she had become one of the sport's most successful players, winning multiple majors in rapid succession.

## Playing Style

Her baseline aggression, early ball-striking, and ability to generate pace from both wings made her difficult to defend against. She was also known for taking the ball early and redirecting it down the line, which pressured opponents into shorter reaction times.

## 1993 On-Court Attack

The 1993 stabbing in Hamburg was carried out by an individual later described as an obsessed fan of Steffi Graf. The incident removed Seles from the tour for an extended period and became a defining moment in tennis history, prompting renewed focus on player security at events.

## Return and Later Career

Seles returned to competition in 1995 and won another Grand Slam title, but she did not regain the same sustained dominance she had shown before 1993. She continued to compete at a high level for several more years and remained a prominent figure in the sport.

## Legacy

Seles is remembered both for her extraordinary early achievements and for the broader impact of the 1993 attack on how professional tennis approaches safety for athletes. Her career is often discussed in "what if" terms given her trajectory before the interruption.
