## Background

- Kenyan national described in open sources as a senior commander within al-Shabaab.
- Often referenced under the name "Mohamed Kuno" in English-language reporting; some sources also describe him by additional aliases.

## Role in al-Shabaab

- Reported to have held an operational leadership role, including coordinating attacks and logistics in the Kenya–Somalia theater.
- Became a notable figure in regional counterterrorism coverage due to links made by authorities and media to major incidents in Kenya.

## Garissa University College attack (2015)

- Widely described in open sources as a key planner behind the Garissa University College attack in Garissa, Kenya.
- The attack targeted students and staff and became one of the deadliest militant attacks in Kenya’s recent history.
- Attribution details vary by source; the association with Kuno is typically presented as an allegation by security officials rather than a court-adjudicated finding.

## Death

- Reported killed in March 2016 during a security operation in Somalia.
- Subsequent reporting generally treated his death as a significant blow to al-Shabaab’s external-operations capability focused on Kenya.
