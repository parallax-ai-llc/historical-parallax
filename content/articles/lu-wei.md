## Background

Lu Wei is a Chinese former government and Communist Party official who rose through propaganda and information-control roles and became closely associated with the modernization and tightening of China's internet governance framework.

## Role in cyber and media governance

Lu served in senior positions connected to state media oversight and propaganda work before becoming a leading figure in national-level internet regulation. As head of the Cyberspace Administration of China (CAC), he was often described internationally as an "internet czar" because the CAC is central to policy-making and enforcement related to online content controls, platform regulation, and censorship.

During his tenure, China promoted the concept of "cyber sovereignty"—the idea that states have broad authority to regulate and control domestic internet space according to national priorities. Lu was a prominent public advocate of this framing in international forums.

## Corruption case

In 2017, Lu was removed from his positions and placed under investigation as part of China's anti-corruption campaign. Chinese authorities later accused him of abusing power and accepting bribes over an extended period while holding senior posts.

In 2019, a Chinese court sentenced him to a lengthy prison term for bribery, with reported figures including tens of millions of yuan. The case was widely interpreted as both a corruption prosecution and a major political fall for a once-prominent official.

## Significance

Lu's career is commonly referenced in analyses of how China built institutions for internet control and how leadership changes can rapidly reshape the personnel at the top of sensitive regulatory agencies.
