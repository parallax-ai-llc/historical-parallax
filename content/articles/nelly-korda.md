## Summary

Nelly Korda is an American professional golfer competing on the LPGA Tour, widely regarded as one of the best players in women's golf today. Born on July 28, 1998, in Bradenton, Florida, she comes from a family of elite athletes — her father Petr Korda is a former world No. 1 tennis player and Australian Open champion, and her sister Jessica Korda is also a professional golfer. Nelly turned professional in 2016 and rose swiftly through the ranks to become the world No. 1-ranked women's golfer.

Her Olympic gold medal at the 2020 Tokyo Games and her dominant 2024 LPGA season cemented her status as the face of women's golf. Korda combines exceptional ball-striking with composure under pressure, drawing comparisons to the sport's all-time greats. Her success has brought renewed mainstream attention to the LPGA Tour.

## Early Life & Background

Nelly Korda was born in Bradenton, Florida, into a household steeped in professional sports. Her father, Petr Korda, won the 1998 Australian Open and reached world No. 1 in tennis; her mother Regina Rajchrtová was also a professional tennis player. Growing up alongside her sister Jessica, who preceded her on the LPGA Tour, Nelly was immersed in competitive sport from an early age.

She began playing golf seriously as a teenager and honed her game in Florida's competitive junior golf circuit. She represented the United States in amateur international competition before turning professional at age 17 in 2016, forgoing college golf to pursue a Tour career directly.

## Rise to Prominence

Korda earned her LPGA Tour card for 2017 and steadily climbed the rankings over the following years. Her first LPGA Tour victory came at the 2018 Lorena Ochoa Invitational. She continued to add wins and in June 2021 captured her first major championship at the KPMG Women's PGA Championship, shooting a final-round 67 to win by three strokes. Weeks later, she claimed the Olympic gold medal at the Tokyo Games, holing a crucial bunker shot to seal victory — a performance that introduced her to a global audience.

By July 2021 she ascended to world No. 1 in the Rolex Women's World Golf Rankings. After a 2022 health scare involving a blood clot in her arm that required surgery and a recovery period, she returned to the Tour and reclaimed her elite form.

## Key Achievements

- **Olympic Gold Medal** — 2020 Tokyo Olympics (golf)
- **KPMG Women's PGA Championship** — 2021 (first major title)
- **World No. 1** — reached the top ranking in July 2021
- **2024 LPGA Season** — won six tournaments including the Chevron Championship (her second major), posting one of the most dominant single-season performances in recent LPGA history
- **Multiple LPGA Tour victories** — 15+ career wins through early 2026
- Named **LPGA Player of the Year** in 2021 and 2024

## Controversies & Criticism

Korda has largely avoided significant controversy throughout her career. Some commentary has touched on the broader question of depth in women's golf during her dominant 2024 stretch, with observers debating whether her winning margin reflected the Tour's competitive landscape. She has not been involved in any rules controversies or off-course incidents of note.

## Legacy & Influence

Korda represents a new generation of LPGA stars capable of transcending the sport's traditional fan base. Her Olympic gold, combined with her 2024 dominance, has elevated her profile considerably in mainstream sports media. Coming from a multi-generational athletic family, she serves as an ambassador for the idea that elite athleticism can span generations and disciplines. Her success alongside her sister Jessica has also made the Korda name synonymous with women's professional golf in the United States.
