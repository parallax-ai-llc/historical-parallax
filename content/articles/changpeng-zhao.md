## Background

- Changpeng Zhao (often known as "CZ") is a Chinese-Canadian technology entrepreneur best known for founding the cryptocurrency exchange Binance.
- Before Binance, he worked in software and trading-related roles, including infrastructure supporting financial markets.

## Binance and Scale

- Binance became one of the world’s largest cryptocurrency exchanges by volume, operating across many jurisdictions and serving a global user base.
- The company’s growth brought increased regulatory attention regarding licensing, customer due diligence, and anti-money-laundering (AML) controls.

## U.S. Criminal Case and Compliance Issues

- U.S. authorities alleged that Binance failed to implement and maintain an effective AML program as required, and that the platform processed transactions that raised compliance concerns.
- In 2023, Zhao pleaded guilty in the United States in connection with AML-related violations and stepped down as Binance’s CEO as part of a broader settlement.
- Public reporting on the settlement describes Binance agreeing to pay multi‑billion‑dollar penalties and to accept ongoing compliance oversight.

## Significance

- The case is widely cited as a landmark enforcement action in the cryptocurrency sector, illustrating the expectation that large exchanges operate with bank‑like compliance programs.
- It also highlighted the tension between fast global scaling in crypto and the slower, jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction requirements of financial regulation.
