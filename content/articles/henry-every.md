## Summary

## Additional Context

- Every (also spelled **Avery**) is one of the most famous pirates of the late 17th century largely because a single successful raid made him a legend.
- Contemporary and later retellings often exaggerate numbers and treasure values; estimates for the raid’s “modern value” vary widely across sources.
- His post-raid fate is uncertain in the historical record, which helped create a mythology of the pirate who escaped justice and vanished.

## Why He Matters

- **Piracy and global trade:** his story intersects with the growth of long-distance maritime commerce and the vulnerability of shipping routes.
- **Imperial repercussions:** large pirate raids could trigger diplomatic and military responses because they threatened state revenue and commercial interests.
- **Mythmaking:** “disappearing” figures tend to accumulate folklore, turning partial records into enduring legends.
