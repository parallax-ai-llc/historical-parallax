## Background

Matthew Lawrence Perna was an American man from Pennsylvania who became nationally known due to his involvement in the January 6, 2021 U.S. Capitol attack and his subsequent death by suicide while his case was pending.

## January 6 and Legal Case

- Perna traveled from Sharpsville, Pennsylvania to Washington, D.C. for the “Stop the Steal” rally and then moved with the crowd to the U.S. Capitol.
- In court documents summarized by major news outlets, he described being pushed into the Capitol through an open door and spending a short period of time inside.
- He recorded and posted videos about the event online; reporting notes he promoted claims assigning blame to “Antifa” and criticizing then–Vice President Mike Pence.
- He was charged in federal court (including witness tampering and disorderly conduct-related counts) and later pleaded guilty (December 2021, per Wikipedia’s cited reporting).
- His case experienced delays, and prosecutors discussed the possibility of a sentencing enhancement related to domestic terrorism, according to coverage cited on Wikipedia.

## Death

Perna died by suicide on February 25, 2022, before he was sentenced. Reporting cited on Wikipedia notes that his obituary described his death as related to stress and despair over the legal process and delays.
