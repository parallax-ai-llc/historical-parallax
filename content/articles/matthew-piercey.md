## Summary

American Ponzi scheme operator who defrauded investors of $35 million and led authorities on a dramatic boat chase across Lake Shasta. He attempted to flee on an underwater scooter.

## Scheme Overview

Open-source reporting describes Piercey as operating a fraudulent investment operation associated with **Family Wealth Legacy**. Such schemes typically claim to generate returns through sophisticated trading or proprietary strategies, but instead use incoming funds from newer investors to pay earlier investors, while the operator misappropriates money for personal use.

Public case summaries characterize the conduct as a **Ponzi scheme** and place the investor losses in the tens of millions of U.S. dollars.

## Law Enforcement Pursuit and Arrest

Piercey’s case received media attention because of a law-enforcement pursuit on **Lake Shasta** in Northern California. Reports describe him fleeing by boat and later attempting to evade capture using an underwater device (often described as an underwater scooter).

Regardless of the dramatic details, the central legal issues concerned the alleged fraud, investor deception, and financial losses.

## Trial and Sentencing

- **Conviction**: Piercey was convicted in federal court on fraud-related charges (commonly reported as wire fraud).
- **Sentence**: He was sentenced to a lengthy prison term, reflecting both the scale of losses and the number of victims.

## Broader Context

The case is often cited in discussions about:

- How affinity networks and trust can be exploited in investment fraud
- The importance of independent custody, auditing, and regulatory verification for investment products
- The difficulty of recovering funds after fraudulent transfers and dissipation
