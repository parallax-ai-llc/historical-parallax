## Additional Context

### Scientific Contributions (High Level)

Ada Yonath is an Israeli crystallographer known for pioneering work in X-ray crystallography of the ribosome. Her research helped clarify how ribosomes translate genetic information into proteins, a foundational process in biology.

### Nobel Prize

In 2009 she shared the Nobel Prize in Chemistry (with Venkatraman Ramakrishnan and Thomas A. Steitz) for studies of the structure and function of the ribosome.

### Impact

Ribosome structural biology has been important for basic science and for applied research, including efforts to understand how certain antibiotics interact with bacterial ribosomes.
