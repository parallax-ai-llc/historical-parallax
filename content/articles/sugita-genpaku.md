## Summary

Sugita Genpaku was a Japanese physician who co-translated Kaitai Shinsho (New Book of Anatomy) in 1774, the first translation of a Western medical text into Japanese. This landmark translation, based on a Dutch anatomy book, transformed Japanese understanding of human anatomy and marked the beginning of serious Western medical study in Japan.
