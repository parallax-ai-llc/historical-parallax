## Summary

Capitol rioter who assaulted officers and stole a Senator's notebook on January 6.

Sandlin is described in reporting as an internet marketer who traveled to Washington, D.C. with other participants and was later prosecuted in federal court for conduct during the breach.

## Background

- Sources describe Sandlin as having been born in Mexico (reported as 1986 or 1987) and later adopted in the United States.[^1]
- He has been described as living in the Las Vegas area, and also having lived in Millington, Tennessee around 2020–2021.[^1]

## January 6, 2021 — Reported conduct

According to court reporting summarized by Wikipedia:

- He entered the U.S. Capitol building during the attack on January 6, 2021.[^1]
- Allegations/claims in reporting include attempting to remove an officer's helmet, making threats toward police, taking items from inside the building, and recording video from inside the Capitol.[^1]

## Legal proceedings

- Arrest: He was arrested later in January 2021 in Las Vegas and detained pending trial.[^1]
- Guilty plea: In September 2022, he pleaded guilty to conspiracy to obstruct an official proceeding and to assaulting/resisting/impeding officers.[^1]
- Sentencing: In December 2022, a federal judge sentenced him to 63 months in prison; reporting also notes a fine that was later increased after discussions of fundraising activity.[^1]
- Release and later clemency: Wikipedia reports he was released in December 2024 and was later pardoned in January 2025 as part of a broad set of January 6-related pardons.[^1]
