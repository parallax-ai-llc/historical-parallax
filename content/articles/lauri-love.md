## Summary

British hacktivist accused of hacking US government agencies including the FBI, Federal Reserve, and NASA. His extradition case became a landmark for how the UK handles cybercrime suspects with mental health conditions.

## Allegations (High-Level)

U.S. authorities alleged that Love participated in intrusions into government-related networks and systems, often framed in public reporting as politically motivated or “hacktivist” activity.
Charges were pursued in the United States, while Love remained in the United Kingdom.

## Extradition Proceedings

The case centered on whether Love should be extradited to face trial in the U.S. versus being prosecuted in the UK.
UK courts considered factors including the forum for trial, proportionality, and the impact of incarceration on Love’s health.

## Mental Health Considerations

Public reporting and court findings highlighted Love’s mental health conditions.
The 2018 decision blocking extradition was widely discussed as a significant precedent in how UK courts weigh medical and human-rights concerns in extradition cases.

## Broader Significance

The case is frequently referenced in debates on cross-border cybercrime enforcement, the jurisdictional reach of U.S. prosecutions, and safeguards for defendants facing severe sentencing exposure abroad.
