## Summary

Venezuelan serial killer and cannibal known as the People-Eater of San Cristóbal who murdered and consumed at least 10 people. He targeted men in the Tórbes River area and claimed to prefer the taste of lean men.

## Crimes and Arrest (High Level)

Open-source accounts describe Vargas as a serial murderer whose crimes were associated with the Trbes River area near San Cristbal. Investigations reported that human remains were discovered at a makeshift camp, leading to his identification and arrest.

## Legal and Psychiatric Proceedings

After his detention, he was reported to have been declared mentally incompetent to stand trial (or otherwise assessed as having severe mental illness). In many legal systems, such findings affect whether a person is tried in a conventional criminal court or placed into a secure psychiatric setting.

## Public Perception

The case became widely known in Venezuela due to the graphic nature of the allegations and extensive media coverage. Later discussion often focused on broader issues such as public safety, homelessness, and gaps in mental-health infrastructure.
