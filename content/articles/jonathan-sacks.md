## Summary

Jonathan Sacks was the Chief Rabbi of the United Hebrew Congregations of the Commonwealth from 1991 to 2013. He was one of the most articulate and influential religious voices in the English-speaking world. His books, including The Dignity of Difference and Not in God's Name, addressed interfaith understanding, morality, and the challenges of secularism. He was knighted and made a life peer in the House of Lords.
