## Summary

François Jacob was a French biologist who shared the 1965 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for the discovery of genetic regulation of enzyme synthesis. He co-discovered messenger RNA and the operon model of gene regulation with Jacques Monod. His work revealed how genes are switched on and off, a fundamental mechanism in biology. He was also a decorated WWII veteran.
