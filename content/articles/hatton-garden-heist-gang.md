## Summary

Group of elderly British career criminals who carried out the Hatton Garden safe deposit burglary in London in 2015, stealing valuables worth £14 million. The oldest participant was 76 years old.

## Career Timeline

## Group profile

Media coverage emphasized the group’s age and long criminal histories, which contributed to the “retirement-age” framing of the story.
Accounts typically describe a division of labor: planning, physical entry, specialist drilling/cutting, and handling of stolen goods.

## Links to the Hatton Garden burglary

The gang is primarily known for the 2015 Hatton Garden safe deposit burglary, a case that became symbolic of:

- How traditional “physical” crime can still generate very large losses.
- The importance of timing (holiday weekends) and reconnaissance.
- The role of forensic investigation and surveillance in post-event identification.

## Sentencing and aftermath

Following convictions, sentencing decisions and appeals attracted additional attention, including discussion of culpability and mitigation given age and health.
Some stolen property was reportedly recovered, but the headline value cited in public reporting remained extremely high.
