## Why He Matters (High-Level)

- Widely recognized as a key scientific and institutional figure in India's **Green Revolution** era.
- Helped adapt and advance **high-yielding cereal varieties** (especially wheat and rice) to Indian agro-ecological conditions.
- Advocated linking agricultural productivity to **nutrition, rural livelihoods, and ecological sustainability**.

## Major Contributions

### Crop Improvement and Food Security

- Worked in plant genetics and breeding-related research that supported the spread of high-yielding wheat and rice in India.
- Contributed to efforts that increased grain production and reduced famine risk in the mid-to-late 20th century.

### Institutions and Policy Influence

- Served in prominent leadership roles in Indian agricultural science and policy bodies (including major national research institutions).
- Supported science-led approaches to food systems, combining research, extension, and public policy.

### Sustainability and "Evergreen Revolution"

- Popularized the idea of an **"Evergreen Revolution"**: sustaining productivity gains without ecological harm.
- Emphasized soil health, water stewardship, biodiversity protection, and farmer-centered development.
