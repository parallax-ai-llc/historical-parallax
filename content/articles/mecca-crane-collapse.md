## Summary

Collapse of a construction crane at the Grand Mosque in Mecca on September 11, 2015 that killed 107 people and injured 238. The accident occurred during preparations for the Hajj pilgrimage.

## Context: construction and safety at pilgrimage sites

- The Grand Mosque in Mecca has undergone multiple expansion and renovation projects, which increases the presence of heavy construction equipment near large crowds.
- Hajj preparations include infrastructure and crowd-management planning to accommodate large numbers of pilgrims.
- Large construction accidents are typically analyzed through contributing factors such as weather, equipment positioning, site controls, and emergency response.

## Reported impacts (high level)

- Casualty figures from the incident are widely reported in international media and official statements.
- The disaster raised questions about construction-site risk controls around sacred sites and the adequacy of weather-related shutdown procedures.
- Saudi authorities publicly addressed compensation and accountability measures after the incident.

## How investigations are generally handled

- Post-incident investigations commonly review compliance with safety standards, crane operation protocols, and supervision responsibilities.
- Authorities may impose project suspensions, contractor sanctions, or legal proceedings depending on findings.
- Public reporting can differ between preliminary statements and final determinations.
