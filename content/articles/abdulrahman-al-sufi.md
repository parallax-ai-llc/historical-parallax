## Summary

Abd al-Rahman al-Sufi was a Persian astronomer who published the 'Book of Fixed Stars' in 964, one of the most important works in observational astronomy. He was the first to describe the Andromeda Galaxy and the Large Magellanic Cloud. His observations corrected and updated Ptolemy's star catalog with more accurate positions and magnitudes. The lunar crater Azophi is named in his honor.
