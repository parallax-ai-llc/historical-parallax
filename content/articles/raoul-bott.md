## Contributions

Bott is best known for Bott periodicity, a result that became central to parts of algebraic topology and K-theory. His name is also attached to several other concepts and theorems across geometry and topology (for example, Morse–Bott theory and a number of fixed-point and localization formulas developed with other mathematicians).

## Education and Academic Career

According to biographical summaries, Bott emigrated from Europe to North America before World War II, studied electrical engineering at McGill University, and completed a PhD in mathematics at Carnegie Mellon University. He held faculty positions including a long tenure at Harvard University.

## Awards and Honors

Major recognitions associated with Bott include the National Medal of Science (1987) and the Wolf Prize in Mathematics (2000), among other prizes.
