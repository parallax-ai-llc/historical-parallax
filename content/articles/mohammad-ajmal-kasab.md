## Background

- Kasab was born in Faridkot village in Punjab, Pakistan.
- Open sources describe a modest upbringing and limited formal education.
- He was reported (by Indian investigators and later coverage summarized in Wikipedia) to have been recruited and trained by the Pakistan-based militant organization Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT).

## Role in the 2008 Mumbai attacks

- Kasab was part of a 10-member assault team that entered Mumbai by sea in late November 2008.
- The group carried out coordinated shootings and attacks at multiple locations across the city.
- Kasab’s actions during the assault were widely reported to have involved attacks around Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus and the Cama Hospital area.
- He was one of the few perpetrators taken into custody alive, which made him a central figure in subsequent investigations and trials.

## Arrest, trial, and execution

- Kasab was arrested on 26 November 2008 by Mumbai Police after an exchange of fire.
- He was tried in India on charges including murder and terrorism-related offenses.
- In 2010, an Indian court convicted him and imposed the death penalty; subsequent appeals were heard in higher courts.
- He was executed by hanging on 21 November 2012 at Yerwada Jail in Pune, India.

## Significance (high level)

- Because he was captured alive, Kasab became an important evidentiary source for investigators assembling a public account of recruitment, training, and operational planning behind the attacks.
- The case was closely watched in India and internationally, and it remained a prominent example of a large-scale, multi-site urban terrorist attack.
