## Summary

Uzbek terrorist who drove a hijacked truck into pedestrians on Drottninggatan in Stockholm on April 7, 2017, killing 5 people. He had pledged allegiance to ISIS. He was an asylum seeker facing deportation. Sentenced to life.

## Background

Rakhmat Akilov is an Uzbek national associated with the 2017 Stockholm truck attack. Reporting around the case describes him as having lived in Sweden and sought asylum, with Swedish authorities later determining that he should be deported. In accounts presented at trial, Akilov expressed support for the Islamic State (ISIS) and framed the attack as ideologically motivated.

## The 2017 Stockholm Truck Attack

On 7 April 2017, a truck was driven into pedestrians along Drottninggatan, a busy shopping street in central Stockholm. The attack killed five people and injured others, prompting a major police response and an intensive manhunt. Swedish authorities treated the incident as terrorism from the outset.

## Investigation and Trial

Akilov was arrested shortly after the attack. Prosecutors argued that the act was planned and that Akilov intended to maximize casualties in a public area. The case became one of Sweden's most prominent terrorism trials of the decade, focusing on motive, planning, and the role of extremist propaganda.

## Sentence and Legal Outcome

In 2018, he was convicted and sentenced to life imprisonment. Swedish courts also addressed immigration consequences, with reporting noting a deportation order to take effect after the prison term. Life imprisonment in Sweden is subject to later review and potential conversion to a fixed term under Swedish law.

## Significance

The attack is often cited in European security discussions as an example of a low-complexity, high-impact vehicle-ramming tactic used in multiple countries during the mid-2010s. It also renewed Swedish debate about asylum processing, deportation enforcement, and counterterrorism monitoring.
