## Summary

Jamaican gang leader of the Shower Posse and political strongman of Tivoli Gardens. The 2010 operation to arrest him was the most violent law enforcement action in Jamaican history.

## Background

Michael "Dudus" Coke was associated with Tivoli Gardens in Kingston, Jamaica, and became widely known as a leader of the Shower Posse, an organized crime group linked in reporting to trafficking and violence.

## Tivoli Gardens and the "Don" System

Coke was often described as a community "don" who combined coercive control with the provision of services and informal governance within his neighborhood. This phenomenon has been discussed in connection with Jamaica's history of garrison communities and patronage politics.

## Extradition Dispute and 2010 Operation

In 2010, Jamaican authorities moved to execute an extradition request from the United States. The security operation to locate and arrest Coke led to intense violence and civilian deaths and triggered national debate about policing tactics, state legitimacy, and the influence of criminal organizations.

## U.S. Federal Case

After capture, Coke was extradited to the United States and later pleaded guilty in U.S. federal court. He received a lengthy prison sentence, and the case is frequently cited in discussions of transnational organized crime and U.S.-Caribbean law-enforcement cooperation.

## Key Points

- Coke's influence was tied to both criminal leadership and local political structures.
- The 2010 operation became a watershed moment for Jamaican internal security.
- The extradition case had political and diplomatic consequences beyond the criminal proceedings.
