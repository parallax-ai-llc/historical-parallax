## Additional Context

### Background and rise

Tymoshenko built her early career in business during the turbulent post-Soviet transition, later becoming a prominent figure in Ukrainian politics during periods of major constitutional and geopolitical contestation.

### Prime minister and national politics

She served as Prime Minister of Ukraine in two separate periods (mid-2000s and 2007–2010), including during and after the Orange Revolution era. Her governments were associated with pro-European political forces and recurring coalition instability in parliament.

### 2009 gas agreement and later prosecution

A key episode in her career was a 2009 natural-gas agreement with Russia during a supply crisis. In 2011 she was convicted and imprisoned in a case widely characterized by international institutions and foreign governments as selective justice and politically motivated prosecution under President Viktor Yanukovych.

### Release and later activity

She was released from imprisonment in 2014 amid the political upheaval of the Euromaidan period and the change of government. She remained an influential political actor, including running in subsequent presidential elections and maintaining a party organization with parliamentary presence.

## Notable themes

- **Rule of law and selective justice:** her prosecution became a frequently cited example in debates over judicial independence in Ukraine.
- **Energy policy and state capture risks:** the gas sector and associated contracts were central to both her political narrative and criticism of her rivals.
- **Geopolitical alignment:** her career intersected with Ukraine’s oscillation between closer ties to the EU and influence from Russia.
