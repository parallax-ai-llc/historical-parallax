## Summary

Former Philippine police officer who hijacked a tourist bus in Manila, killing 8 Hong Kong tourists.

## Background

- Rolando Mendoza was a former officer of the Philippine National Police (PNP).[^1]
- Reporting commonly describes him as having grievances related to disciplinary proceedings and employment status, which he referenced during the crisis.[^1]

## 2010 Manila Hostage Crisis (Tourist Bus Hijacking)

- **Date**: 2010-08-23 (Manila, Philippines).[^1]
- **Target**: A tourist bus carrying visitors from Hong Kong.[^1]
- **Demands**: Mendoza sought attention to his complaints and asked for outcomes favorable to him regarding his case with authorities.[^1]
- **Outcome**: The incident ended with multiple casualties, including **8 Hong Kong tourists killed**, and injuries among survivors.[^1]

## Aftermath and Impact

- The event triggered extensive media coverage and public scrutiny of crisis management and negotiation tactics.[^1]
- It became a significant diplomatic and political issue between the Philippines and Hong Kong, including calls for accountability and reforms.[^1]
- The Manila hostage crisis is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - inter-agency coordination during high-risk incidents
  - negotiation strategy vs. tactical assault decision-making
  - protection of civilians during prolonged standoffs

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- Mendoza was not an active-duty officer at the time of the hijacking; sources describe him as a former police officer involved in disputes with the PNP.[^1]
- The incident is widely referred to as the **Manila hostage crisis** in international reporting.[^1]
