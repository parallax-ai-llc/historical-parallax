## Background

Tesha Miller is a Jamaican woman who has been described by law enforcement and media reporting as an alleged leader within the **Clansman** gang, a criminal organization associated with Spanish Town in St. Catherine Parish, Jamaica.[^1]

## Clansman gang (context)

High-level context frequently noted in reporting about the Clansman gang includes:

- Its association with organized violence and territorial control in parts of St. Catherine.
- Repeated, long-running investigations by Jamaican authorities into alleged gang leadership and participation.
- The use of prosecutions to target not only direct perpetrators but also alleged organizers and facilitators (e.g., accessories).

(These points summarize common framing in public sources; specific allegations and findings vary by case.)[^1]

## Legal proceedings and conviction

- In **2022**, Miller was **convicted** of being an **accessory before the fact to murder**.[^1]
- “Accessory before the fact” typically refers to someone who is found to have aided, encouraged, or arranged an offense *prior* to its commission, even if they were not the person who carried out the killing.

## Public significance

Miller’s case is often cited as an example of Jamaican courts pursuing alleged gang leadership through conspiracy and accessory theories of liability, rather than limiting prosecutions to those who physically committed an offense.[^1]
