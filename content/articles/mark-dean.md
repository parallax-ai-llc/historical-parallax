## Summary

Mark Dean is an African American computer engineer who holds three of IBM's original nine patents for the personal computer. He co-created the ISA bus (Industry Standard Architecture) that allowed peripherals to be connected to PCs. He later led the team that created the first gigahertz processor. He was inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame.
