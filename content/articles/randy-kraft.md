## Summary
Randolph Kraft, the 'Scorecard Killer,' is an American serial killer convicted of 16 murders and suspected of committing up to 67. He killed young men in Southern California between 1971 and 1983. Police found a coded 'scorecard' in his car listing 61 potential victims.

## Early Life
Born March 19, 1945, in Long Beach, California. He attended Claremont Men's College and was politically active. He served briefly in the Air Force. He worked as a computer consultant and was outwardly successful and intelligent. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1971 and 1983, Kraft abducted, drugged, tortured, and murdered young men across Southern California. Many victims were Marines or hitchhikers. He often mutilated victims' bodies. A coded list found in his car, dubbed the 'scorecard,' appeared to track 61 potential victims. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Kraft was arrested on May 14, 1983, when a highway patrol officer found a dead marine in his car. Investigation revealed evidence linking him to multiple murders. He was convicted of 16 murders in 1989 and sentenced to death. He remains on death row. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case advanced forensic techniques for linking serial crimes [^4]
- Led to improved tracking of military personnel missing from bases
- Demonstrated the value of evidence cataloging in serial investigations

## Negative Perspectives
- Convicted of 16 murders, suspected in up to 67 [^5]
- Targeted vulnerable young men
- Drugged and tortured victims
- Maintained a coded record of his crimes
- May be one of America's most prolific serial killers
