## Summary

German neo-Nazi terrorist and NSU member who along with Böhnhardt carried out a decade-long series of murders targeting immigrants. He killed himself and Böhnhardt when they were about to be caught.

## Background

- Mundlos was active in far-right circles in Thuringia and became part of the NSU core.
- Together with Uwe Böhnhardt and Beate Zschäpe, he went underground in the late 1990s.
- The trio used clandestine logistics and supporters to avoid detection for years.

## NSU period (overview)

- The National Socialist Underground (NSU) was later linked to murders, bombings, and bank robberies.
- Investigations initially failed to identify the perpetrators; the crimes were not connected publicly for a long period.
- The murders predominantly targeted immigrants, shaping the later public controversy.

## 2011 end of the NSU underground phase

- After a failed bank robbery in 2011, police located a camper van in Eisenach.
- Mundlos and Böhnhardt died inside; the case’s exposure followed quickly afterward.
- Evidence associated with the NSU prompted major criminal proceedings and inquiries.

## Significance

- The NSU case became a landmark example in Germany of sustained far-right terrorist violence.
- It triggered broad debate about investigative bias, institutional oversight, and extremist threat assessment.
