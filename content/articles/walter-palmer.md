## Summary

American dentist who illegally killed Cecil the lion in Zimbabwe's Hwange National Park in 2015. The killing of the famous lion sparked worldwide outrage and reignited debates about trophy hunting. He was not charged.

## Background

Walter James Palmer is a Minnesota-based dentist who became internationally known after a high-profile trophy hunting incident in 2015. Prior to that event, he was largely known through his dental practice and periodic participation in big-game hunting.

## 2015 Cecil the lion incident

Cecil was a well-known lion in Zimbabwe's Hwange National Park area and was associated with a long-running research project. In July 2015, Palmer took part in a hunt that resulted in Cecil being killed outside the park's boundaries after being wounded.

Key elements commonly reported about the incident include:

- The hunt involved professional guides and tracking over an extended period.
- The killing raised questions about permits, baiting practices, and the location where the lion was ultimately taken.
- Zimbabwean authorities reviewed the circumstances; Palmer later returned to the United States.

## Public and institutional reaction

The incident triggered intense global attention and backlash. Public reaction included protests, widespread criticism on social media, and significant media coverage. In Minnesota, Palmer's dental practice was temporarily affected by demonstrations and heightened scrutiny.

## Broader impact

Cecil's death became a reference point in debates about trophy hunting and wildlife conservation, including questions about:

- Whether trophy hunting revenue supports conservation efforts and local communities.
- How wildlife permits are issued and enforced near protected areas.
- Ethical considerations around hunting animals that are habituated to humans or monitored by researchers.
