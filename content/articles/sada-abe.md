## Context and Background

Sada Abe was a Japanese woman whose 1936 case became nationally notorious and has remained a reference point in Japanese popular culture and discussions of sexuality, violence, and media sensationalism.

Accounts commonly summarize her as having worked in urban service and entertainment settings in interwar Japan, before becoming involved in a relationship that ended in homicide. The episode drew intense tabloid attention at the time and was repeatedly revisited in later books, films, and academic commentary.
