## Summary

Royal Dutch Shell's operations in Nigeria's Niger Delta have caused devastating environmental damage over 60 years. Oil spills have contaminated water, destroyed farmland, and caused health crises. Shell has been sued in multiple countries.

## Operating structure and region

- Shell's on-the-ground presence in the Niger Delta has historically been associated with joint ventures and local subsidiaries (often summarized under **Shell Petroleum Development Company of Nigeria** in public reporting).
- The Niger Delta is a complex environment of wetlands, fisheries, and farming communities, making spill impacts particularly severe when they reach waterways.

## Causes of spills and disputed attribution

- Reporting on Niger Delta pollution frequently distinguishes between spills caused by **equipment failure/operational issues** and those linked to **sabotage, pipeline tapping, or theft**.
- The relative contribution of these causes has been contested in public debate and litigation, and courts have evaluated evidence case by case.

## Litigation and accountability (high-level)

- Shell has faced lawsuits in multiple jurisdictions, including claims brought in the UK and the Netherlands concerning pollution and corporate responsibility.
- Some cases have focused on whether parent-company oversight created duties toward communities affected by subsidiary operations.
- Settlements and judgments have, in different instances, required compensation, remediation commitments, or changes in monitoring and maintenance practices.

## Ogoni, activism, and international scrutiny

- The Ogoni region and the activism associated with **Ken Saro-Wiwa** became globally known as symbols of environmental justice struggles in the Delta.
- The broader controversy has been tied to questions of state security, corporate-community relations, and the governance of oil revenue.

## Remediation and long-term impacts

- Environmental assessments have described extensive contamination in parts of the Delta, with cleanup efforts framed as technically challenging and slow.
- Long-term impacts cited in summaries include loss of livelihoods, health concerns, and reduced trust between communities, companies, and the state.
