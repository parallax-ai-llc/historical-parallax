## Summary

Jennie Kim, known mononymously as Jennie, is a South Korean singer, rapper, and fashion icon best known as a member of the globally acclaimed K-pop group BLACKPINK, formed by YG Entertainment in 2016. As one of the four members of BLACKPINK — alongside Jisoo, Rosé, and Lisa — she helped the group become the most-followed music act on YouTube and one of the highest-charting K-pop groups in Western markets.

Beyond her role in BLACKPINK, Jennie has built a formidable solo career and a prominent presence in global fashion. Her debut solo single "SOLO" (2018) made her the first BLACKPINK member to release an individual track, and she has maintained an influential position at the intersection of music, luxury fashion, and pop culture through the early 2020s.

Her impact extends beyond music — she is widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in the Korean Wave (Hallyu), contributing to K-pop's mainstream acceptance in North America and Europe while reshaping perceptions of Asian artists in global entertainment.

## Early Life & Background

Jennie was born on January 16, 1996, in Heukseok-dong, Dongjak District, Seoul, South Korea. At age 10, she moved to New Zealand, where she attended Takapuna Grammar School in Auckland, developing fluency in English. She returned to South Korea in her early teens and trained at YG Entertainment for approximately six years before her debut, developing her skills in singing, rapping, and performance.

## Rise to Prominence

BLACKPINK debuted on August 8, 2016, with the double single album *Square One*, featuring "Whistle" and "Boombayah." The group achieved immediate commercial success in South Korea, and international recognition followed rapidly. Jennie gained particular attention for her dual role as both lead rapper and vocalist within the group — an unusual combination in K-pop.

In November 2018, she released "SOLO," which debuted at number one on South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart and remained there for multiple consecutive weeks, marking her as a commercially viable solo act. She became the first solo Korean female artist to perform at Coachella when BLACKPINK headlined the festival in April 2019.

Her acting debut came in 2023 in HBO's *The Idol*, the controversial series created by The Weeknd and Sam Levinson, in which she played a recurring role.

## Key Achievements

- Co-wrote and co-produced tracks on BLACKPINK's discography, contributing to creative development within the group.
- "SOLO" spent over 30 weeks on the Gaon Digital Chart following its 2018 release.
- BLACKPINK's 2022 album *Born Pink* debuted at number one in South Korea and charted in the top five in the US and UK.
- Appointed global brand ambassador for Chanel in 2017, becoming one of the youngest artists to hold such a role with a major French luxury house.
- Her solo debut album *Ruby* was released in March 2025 to significant commercial attention, expanding her profile as a solo act.

## Controversies & Criticism

Jennie has faced criticism regarding YG Entertainment's management of BLACKPINK's schedules, with fans citing limited releases and long gaps between group activities. In 2022, she was subject to privacy violations when unauthorized photos were leaked online. Her appearance in *The Idol* drew criticism due to the show's controversial content and production, though reviews of her personal performance were largely neutral.

## Legacy & Influence

Jennie has consistently ranked among the most influential figures in fashion and music globally, appearing on Forbes Korea's Power Celebrity list multiple times. Her aesthetic — combining streetwear with high fashion — has been widely imitated and helped normalize Korean artists as global luxury brand ambassadors. She is credited with expanding BLACKPINK's reach into Western audiences and demonstrating the viability of K-pop artists as solo crossover acts in English-language markets.
