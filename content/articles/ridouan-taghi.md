## Summary
Ridouan Taghi is a Dutch-Moroccan crime boss considered the most wanted criminal in the Netherlands. He led a cocaine trafficking network and was convicted of ordering multiple murders, including the assassination of investigative journalist Peter R. de Vries and lawyer Derk Wiersum.

## Early Life
Born February 14, 1977, in the Netherlands to Moroccan immigrant parents. He grew up in the Staatsliedenbuurt neighborhood of Amsterdam. He entered the criminal underworld at a young age and built an international drug trafficking network. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Taghi led a cocaine trafficking network bringing tons of cocaine from South America to Europe. His organization was connected to multiple assassinations, including the murders of Reduan B. (his former associate's brother), lawyer Derk Wiersum (September 2019), and journalist Peter R. de Vries (July 2021). The killings of professionals shocked the Netherlands. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Taghi was arrested in Dubai on December 16, 2019, and extradited to the Netherlands. His trial (the Marengo process) was the largest criminal trial in Dutch history. He was convicted in February 2024 and sentenced to life imprisonment for ordering multiple murders and leading a criminal organization. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case exposed the extent of narco-infiltration in the Netherlands [^4]
- Led to unprecedented reforms in Dutch criminal justice and witness protection
- The trial was a landmark in Dutch legal history

## Negative Perspectives
- Ordered assassinations of a journalist and a lawyer [^5]
- Led a massive cocaine trafficking network
- His murders attacked the foundations of Dutch rule of law
- The killings of De Vries and Wiersum traumatized Dutch society
- His organization demonstrated the global reach of the cocaine trade
