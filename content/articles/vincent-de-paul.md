## Summary

Vincent de Paul was a French Catholic priest who dedicated his life to serving the poor and is the patron saint of charitable organizations. He founded the Congregation of the Mission (Vincentians/Lazarists) and co-founded the Daughters of Charity with Louise de Marillac. He organized missions to evangelize the rural poor and established seminaries for the formation of priests. His legacy of charitable work continues through the Society of St. Vincent de Paul.
