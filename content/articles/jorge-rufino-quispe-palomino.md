## Summary

Peruvian guerrilla leader who heads the remnants of the Shining Path in the VRAEM region and is Peru's most wanted terrorist. He combines Maoist ideology with cocaine trafficking.

## Shining Path and VRAEM (High-Level Context)

- Shining Path (Sendero Luminoso) began as a Maoist insurgency in Peru and became known for extremist violence during Peru's internal conflict.
- In later years, surviving factions have been associated with the VRAEM area (Valley of the Apurímac, Ene and Mantaro Rivers), a remote region tied to coca cultivation and trafficking routes.
- Armed groups in such environments have often funded operations through taxation, protection, or direct participation in drug economies.

## Designations and Counterterrorism Measures

- The U.S. Specially Designated Global Terrorist (SDGT) designation is an economic-sanctions mechanism used to target individuals or entities involved in terrorism.
- Designations can restrict financial transactions and aim to reduce access to resources, facilitators, and international mobility.

## Operational Characteristics (General)

- Insurgent-criminal hybrid groups may blend political rhetoric with pragmatic control of territory, logistics, and local enforcement.
- Such groups often rely on small-unit tactics, knowledge of terrain, and intimidation of communities and local authorities.
