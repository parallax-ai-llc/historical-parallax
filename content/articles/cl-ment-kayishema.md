## Summary

Rwandan prefect of Kibuye convicted of genocide for organizing the massacre of thousands of Tutsi refugees at Bisesero hills. He was captured in South Africa after 22 years as a fugitive.

## Role During the 1994 Genocide

Kayishema served as a prefect (a senior regional administrator) during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi. As a state official, a prefect could influence policing, local administration, and the mobilization of resources—factors that later became central to how international tribunals assessed responsibility.

## Crimes and Findings

Cases connected to Kibuye prefecture included mass killings and attacks on sites where civilians sought refuge. The Bisesero area is often referenced in accounts of resistance and survival during the genocide, and prosecutions focused on how officials and armed groups coordinated attacks and prevented escape.

## Flight and Arrest

After the genocide, a number of suspects evaded capture for years by moving across borders and using false identities. His arrest in South Africa highlighted the long time horizons of genocide investigations and the reliance on international cooperation to locate and extradite suspects.

## Legacy and Context

The Kayishema case sits within wider efforts to document the genocide, prosecute organizers and local authorities, and establish legal precedent on command and administrative responsibility for mass atrocities.
