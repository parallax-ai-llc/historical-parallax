## Summary

Cédric Villani is a French mathematician who won the Fields Medal in 2010 for his proofs of nonlinear Landau damping and convergence to equilibrium for the Boltzmann equation. Known for his distinctive style including large spider brooches, he later became a member of the French National Assembly. His popular book "Birth of a Theorem" made mathematics accessible to a wide audience.
