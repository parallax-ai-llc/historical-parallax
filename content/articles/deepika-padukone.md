## Summary

Deepika Padukone is one of India's most celebrated actresses and a global cultural ambassador for Indian cinema. Since her Bollywood debut in 2007, she has built a career defined by commercial dominance, critical acclaim, and a willingness to tackle complex, unconventional roles. She consistently ranks among the highest-paid actresses in India and has appeared on numerous international lists recognizing her influence in entertainment and culture.

Beyond acting, Padukone has become a prominent voice in mental health advocacy, openly discussing her battle with clinical depression and founding The Live Love Laugh Foundation in 2015. Her willingness to speak publicly about mental illness—at significant personal and professional risk—has had a measurable impact on how the topic is discussed in South Asian communities. She is widely regarded as a crossover figure who has expanded the visibility of Indian cinema on the global stage.

## Early Life & Background

Deepika Padukone was born on January 5, 1986, in Copenhagen, Denmark, where her father, legendary Indian badminton player Prakash Padukone, was competing at the time. The family returned to Bangalore, India, shortly after, where she was raised. She attended Sophia High School in Bangalore and later enrolled at Mount Carmel College, though she left before completing her degree to pursue modeling full-time.

Her early exposure to elite sport through her father's career instilled discipline that she has frequently cited as foundational to her work ethic. She began modeling professionally in her teens, appearing in print campaigns and music videos before transitioning to film.

## Rise to Prominence

Padukone made her Hindi film debut in Farah Khan's *Om Shanti Om* (2007) opposite Shah Rukh Khan. The film was a massive commercial success and earned her the Filmfare Award for Best Female Debut. Her early career included both hits and setbacks, but her performances in *Cocktail* (2012) and *Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani* (2013) established her as a leading actress capable of carrying high-profile productions.

Her international profile rose significantly with *xXx: Return of Xander Cage* (2017), in which she starred alongside Vin Diesel, and with *Padmaavat* (2018), a period epic that became one of the highest-grossing Bollywood films of the decade despite significant political controversy surrounding its release.

## Key Achievements

- Won three Filmfare Awards for Best Actress: *Ram-Leela* (2014), *Bajirao Mastani* (2016), and *Padmaavat* (2019)
- *Padmaavat* grossed over ₹5.85 billion (approximately $85 million) worldwide
- Named in *Time* magazine's 100 Most Influential People list in 2018
- Served as a jury member at the 2022 Cannes Film Festival, one of the first Indian actresses to do so
- Founded The Live Love Laugh Foundation (2015), focused on mental health awareness in India
- Produced and starred in *Chhapaak* (2020), based on the true story of acid attack survivor Laxmi Agarwal

## Controversies & Criticism

*Padmaavat* generated sustained controversy before its release, with fringe groups threatening violence over its depiction of the Rajput queen Padmavati. Padukone publicly defended the film and its director, Sanjay Leela Bhansali, drawing both praise and criticism. In 2020, she attended a solidarity vigil at Jawaharlal Nehru University following student protests, a visit that generated significant political backlash from nationalist groups who called for a boycott of her films. She did not retract her position.

## Legacy & Influence

Padukone has materially shifted the conversation around mental health in India. Her 2015 public disclosure of her depression diagnosis—and the founding of her foundation—normalized help-seeking behavior in a cultural context where stigma remained severe. In film, her career arc from commercial entertainer to producer of socially engaged projects reflects a broader evolution in what leading actresses pursue in Indian cinema. She married actor Ranveer Singh in November 2018; the couple had their first child in September 2024.
