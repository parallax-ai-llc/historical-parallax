## Summary

Teresa of Ávila was a Spanish Carmelite nun, mystic, and reformer who founded the Discalced Carmelites. Her mystical writings, including The Interior Castle and The Way of Perfection, are classics of Christian spirituality. She was the first woman to be named a Doctor of the Church in 1970. Despite facing opposition from the Inquisition, she established 17 convents across Spain.
