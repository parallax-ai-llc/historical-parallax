## Summary

Charles Kuen Kao was a Chinese-born British-American physicist and engineer who is known as the 'father of fiber optics.' He shared the 2009 Nobel Prize in Physics for groundbreaking achievements concerning the transmission of light in fibers for optical communication. His 1966 paper proposed using ultra-pure glass fibers for long-distance communication, which became the backbone of modern telecommunications.
