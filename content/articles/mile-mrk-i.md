## Summary

Serbian military officer convicted for the Vukovar massacre of Croatian POWs.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1991 | Commanded forces at Vukovar massacre |

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- **Affiliation**: Yugoslav People's Army (JNA); senior officer during the breakup of Yugoslavia.
- **Notability**: Convicted by the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) for crimes linked to the **Vukovar** events in 1991.
- **Core finding (ICTY)**: Responsibility related to the treatment and killing of detainees removed from Vukovar hospital and taken to **Ovčara** (near Vukovar).

## Context

In late 1991, during fighting around Vukovar in Croatia, JNA and associated Serb forces captured Croatian defenders and civilians. The post-capture handling of detainees became one of the best-known atrocity cases of the conflict and a major ICTY prosecution.

## ICTY Proceedings (Overview)

- **Tribunal**: International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY), seated in The Hague.
- **Charges**: War crimes / crimes against humanity counts were pursued in relation to Vukovar/Ovčara (exact charge headings vary by indictment version).
- **Outcome**: Conviction for involvement tied to the massacre; sentence imposed and later reflected in ICTY records.

## Why This Case Matters

- Helped establish a detailed judicial record of **command responsibility**, detainee transfers, and the chain of custody around Vukovar.
- Frequently cited in summaries of ICTY jurisprudence concerning the Croatian War of Independence.
