## Background

- Romanian politician associated primarily with the Social Democratic Party (PSD).
- Known for wielding significant influence over government formation and parliamentary majorities during the late 2010s.

## Major Roles (selected)

- **Leader of the Social Democratic Party (PSD):** served as party president in the 2010s.
- **President of the Chamber of Deputies:** held the office during the 2016–2019 period.
- **Deputy Prime Minister of Romania:** served in the early-to-mid 2010s.
- **Ministerial posts:** held cabinet-level responsibilities related to public administration and internal affairs.

## Political Context

- Became a central figure in Romanian domestic politics after PSD electoral victories, while not serving as prime minister.
- Widely described as coordinating policy priorities and coalition management from parliament and party leadership.

## Legal Cases (high level)

- In Romanian public discourse, Dragnea is frequently discussed in relation to corruption and governance controversies.
- **Election-related conviction:** received a conviction connected to electoral irregularities (reported in relation to a national referendum period).
- **2019 imprisonment:** sentenced to prison for a case involving the instigation of abuse of office, connected in reporting to public-sector employment practices.
- **Post-sentencing:** subsequent court proceedings and incarceration outcomes were followed closely in Romanian media and politics.

## Why He Matters

- Illustrates tensions in Romanian politics between anti-corruption institutions, party power, and electoral mandates.
- Became an emblematic case in debates about rule-of-law reforms, political patronage, and accountability.
