## Summary

Norman Borlaug is the father of the Green Revolution, developing high-yielding, disease-resistant wheat varieties that saved over a billion people from starvation. He received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970 for his contributions to world food supply. His semi-dwarf wheat varieties transformed agriculture in Mexico, India, Pakistan, and across the developing world.
