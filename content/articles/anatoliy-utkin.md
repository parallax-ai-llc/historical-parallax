## Summary

Russian serial killer who murdered at least 9 women in the Saratov region. He was a former butcher who dismembered his victims after killing them.

## Background

Anatoliy Utkin was identified as a serial killer in Russia’s Saratov region. Public summaries of the case describe him as having worked as a butcher, a detail often mentioned in reporting about the investigation.

## Crimes and Pattern (High-Level)

- **Victims:** Women, with at least nine killings attributed to him.
- **Time frame:** The murders spanned multiple years.
- **Modus operandi:** Accounts emphasize post-mortem dismemberment; public descriptions typically avoid further detail.

## Investigation and Arrest

Serial investigations in the post-Soviet period often relied on a mix of forensic evidence, witness statements, and link analysis across cases. Utkin was arrested after remains were discovered and connected to him.

## Trial and Sentence

He was convicted and sentenced to life imprisonment, reflecting the severity and number of murders attributed to him.

## Significance

The case is sometimes discussed in the context of regional crime reporting and the challenges of investigating repeat offenders over extended periods.
