## Summary

Sebastian Vettel won four consecutive F1 World Championships with Red Bull from 2010 to 2013, becoming the youngest champion at 23.

## Competitive Profile

- Vettel is associated with a driving style that emphasized precise qualifying laps and consistent race management during his peak championship years.
- His Red Bull era (2010–2013) is frequently summarized as one of the most dominant multi-season runs in modern Formula One.

## Teams and Phases (High-Level)

- Early Formula One breakthrough included notable performances that elevated him from junior series promise to a full-time F1 seat.
- Career is commonly described in major overviews as spanning championship success with Red Bull and later high-profile stints with other top teams.

## Records and Milestones

- Youngest World Champion at the time of his first title (age 23).
- Four consecutive World Championships (2010–2013), a distinction shared by only a small set of drivers in F1 history.

## Post-Racing Reputation

- After retirement, Vettel’s public image has often been connected to athlete citizenship themes, including advocacy and interest in issues beyond racing.
- In retrospective coverage, he is frequently placed among the defining drivers of the 2010s era.
- His championship run is often used as a reference point when comparing team-era dominance and competitive balance in Formula One.
