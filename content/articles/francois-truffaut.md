## Summary

Francois Truffaut was a French filmmaker who was a founding figure of the French New Wave. His debut The 400 Blows is one of the defining films of the movement. The five Antoine Doinel films, Jules and Jim, and Day for Night demonstrate his humanistic vision and passionate love of cinema.
