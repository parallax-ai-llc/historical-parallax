## Summary

Park Hyung-sik (born November 16, 1991) is a South Korean actor and singer who first gained recognition as a member of the K-pop boy group ZE:A before establishing himself as one of South Korea's most versatile leading men in television drama. Known for his warm screen presence and ability to move between romantic comedies and intense melodramas, he has built a devoted international fanbase across Asia and beyond.

His dual career in music and acting mirrors the trajectory of many K-pop idols, yet Park stands out for the depth he has brought to dramatic roles. His performances in high-profile series have earned him multiple acting awards and cemented his reputation as a bankable leading actor rather than merely a crossover celebrity. He completed mandatory military service in the Republic of Korea Marine Corps between 2018 and 2020, returning to a career that has only grown stronger since.

## Early Life & Background

Park Hyung-sik was born in Gwangmyeong, Gyeonggi Province, South Korea. He attended Hanlim Multi Art School, a performing arts high school in Seoul that has produced numerous K-pop artists and actors. His early interest in performance led him to audition for talent agencies while still a teenager, and he was recruited by Star Empire Entertainment, which would debut him as part of a group act.

## Rise to Prominence

In 2010, Park debuted as a member of ZE:A (Children of Empire), a ten-member boy group under Star Empire Entertainment. The group achieved moderate chart success in South Korea and cultivated a following in Japan and Southeast Asia throughout the early 2010s. Park's visuals and charisma drew attention, and the agency began cultivating his acting career alongside his idol work.

His acting breakthrough came with supporting and leading roles in Korean dramas from 2013 onward. The 2015 romantic comedy *High Society* and the period drama *Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth* (2016–2017), in which he starred alongside Park Seo-joon and BTS's V, raised his profile considerably. His performance as the male lead in the 2017 melodrama *Strong Woman Do Bong-soon* opposite Park Bo-young became a defining moment — the show was a major hit on JTBC and across streaming platforms in Asia, turning Park into a household name regionally.

## Key Achievements

- Lead role in *Strong Woman Do Bong-soon* (2017), one of JTBC's highest-rated dramas at the time of broadcast.
- Lead role in *Suits* (2018), a Korean adaptation of the American legal drama, earning praise for his portrayal of a genius con man.
- Return drama *Happiness* (2021), a post-pandemic thriller on tvN, was critically well-received and demonstrated his range in darker material.
- Multiple Baeksang Arts Awards nominations and wins across his acting career.
- Active solo music releases alongside acting, maintaining a presence in the Korean pop music industry.

## Controversies & Criticism

Park Hyung-sik's career has been largely free of major public controversies. During ZE:A's early years, the group's then-leader Kwanghee publicly raised concerns about their management company's treatment of members, though Park himself was not a central figure in those disputes. His career has otherwise proceeded without significant scandal.

## Legacy & Influence

Park Hyung-sik represents a generation of K-pop idols who successfully transitioned into respected dramatic actors, demonstrating that idol training can serve as a foundation for sustained screen careers. His work in *Strong Woman Do Bong-soon* in particular contributed to the global spread of Korean romantic comedy content, reaching audiences through early streaming expansion into Asia. He remains an influential figure in the Hallyu wave, with a fanbase that has followed him from group idol to solo artist to leading actor across more than fifteen years of public life.
