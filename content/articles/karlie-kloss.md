## Summary

Karlie Kloss is an American supermodel and entrepreneur who rose from local ballet and modeling classes in St. Louis to become one of the most recognizable faces in global fashion. Named one of the "30 Models of the Decade" by Models.com, she has graced the covers of Vogue, Harper's Bazaar, and numerous international editions, while walking for virtually every major luxury house from Chanel to Versace.

Beyond the runway, Kloss has distinguished herself as a tech-education advocate and businesswoman. She founded Kode With Klossy in 2015, a nonprofit offering free coding camps for young women, reflecting her belief that fashion and technology are not mutually exclusive worlds. Her dual identity as model and entrepreneur has made her an influential figure for a generation navigating careers at the intersection of creativity and innovation.

## Early Life & Background

Karlie Elizabeth Kloss was born on August 3, 1992, in Chicago, Illinois, and raised primarily in Webster Groves, a suburb of St. Louis, Missouri. She began studying ballet at age five, training seriously through her early teens. At 13, she was scouted at a local charity fashion show in St. Louis, which led to representation by Savvy Model Management before she signed with Elite Model Management in Chicago.

She completed high school in St. Louis while balancing a growing modeling career, later enrolling at New York University's Gallatin School of Individualized Study, where she pursued coursework in technology and business.

## Rise to Prominence

Kloss moved to New York City around 2007 and quickly attracted industry attention. She made her New York Fashion Week debut in 2008 at just 15, walking for Calvin Klein — a pivotal breakthrough. By 2009 she was walking exclusively for Victoria's Secret, becoming a Victoria's Secret Angel in 2013, a role she held until 2015.

Her editorial profile expanded rapidly. She appeared on the cover of American Vogue in October 2012 and became a regular fixture for top photographers including Mario Testino and Patrick Demarchelier. In 2013, models.com ranked her among the world's top ten models. She also signed major campaigns with L'Oréal Paris, Swarovski, and Adidas.

## Key Achievements

- **Victoria's Secret Angel** (2013–2015), one of the brand's most prominent ambassadors of the era.
- **Vogue covers** across more than 40 international editions throughout her career.
- **Kode With Klossy** (founded 2015): the nonprofit has reached over 1,500 young women across the United States with free two-week coding and entrepreneurship camps.
- **Project Runway host**: She joined the rebooted Lifetime series in 2019 as host and executive producer, succeeding Heidi Klum.
- Co-founded **Difficult Terrain**, a media and production venture, expanding her work behind the camera.

## Controversies & Criticism

Kloss faced criticism in 2012 when a Vogue editorial featured her styled in Native American headdress and accessories; the magazine subsequently apologized for the imagery, acknowledging its insensitivity. She has also navigated scrutiny over her connection to the Kushner family following her 2018 marriage to Joshua Kushner, particularly regarding her brother-in-law Jared Kushner's political role in the Trump administration. Kloss publicly stated she voted for Joe Biden in 2020 and has expressed support for LGBTQ+ rights and reproductive rights, though some observers felt her public statements on family-adjacent political matters were delayed or insufficient.

## Legacy & Influence

Kloss represents a transitional archetype in modeling — the supermodel who leveraged platform into purpose. Kode With Klossy has directly shaped conversations about gender equity in tech, and her Project Runway tenure brought mainstream visibility to fashion's next generation. She is frequently cited as an example of how models can exercise agency as founders and advocates, influencing how younger talent approaches brand-building in the social media era.
