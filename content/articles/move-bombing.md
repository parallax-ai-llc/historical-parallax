## Summary

Philadelphia police dropped a bomb on the MOVE organization's house, killing 11 people including 5 children and destroying 61 homes. The only aerial bombing of US civilians by police.

## Background

- **MOVE** was a Black liberation and back-to-nature group founded in Philadelphia.
- In 1985, MOVE members were living in a rowhouse at **6221 Osage Avenue** in the Cobbs Creek neighborhood.
- Tensions with city officials and neighbors had escalated through prior confrontations and legal disputes.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- On May 13, 1985, Philadelphia police attempted to execute arrest warrants and remove MOVE members from the house.
- Police used extensive force (including gunfire and tear gas) during the standoff.
- A police helicopter dropped an explosive device onto the fortified roof/bunker area of the rowhouse.
- The resulting fire spread and ultimately destroyed a large part of the surrounding block.

## Casualties and Destruction

- **11 people died**, including **5 children**.
- **Two people survived** (commonly reported as Ramona Africa and a child).
- **61 homes** were destroyed as the fire spread through the rowhouses.

## Aftermath and Public Response

- The incident triggered intense national scrutiny of Philadelphia’s policing and political leadership.
- A city-commissioned investigation (often referred to as the **MOVE Commission**) criticized key decisions surrounding the use of explosives and fire management.
- The event remains a widely cited example in discussions of police militarization, urban governance, and state violence.
