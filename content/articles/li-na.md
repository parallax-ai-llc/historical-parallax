## Summary

Li Na won two Grand Slam titles and was the first Asian player to win a Grand Slam singles title. Her 2011 French Open victory inspired a boom in Chinese tennis.

## Background

Li Na is a Chinese professional tennis player (retired) widely recognized for breaking through on the women’s tour at a time when Chinese tennis was still developing its international presence.

## Playing career

- **Historic wins:** Her Grand Slam singles titles are frequently cited as milestones for Asian representation in elite tennis.
- **Style and coaching:** Public profiles often note her aggressive baseline game and the international coaching teams she worked with during her rise.
- **Impact in China:** Li’s success contributed to increased visibility for tennis in China, including greater participation and commercial interest.

## Recognition

Li Na’s induction into the International Tennis Hall of Fame reflects her significance beyond ranking points—particularly her role in changing perceptions of what Asian players could achieve in global tennis.
