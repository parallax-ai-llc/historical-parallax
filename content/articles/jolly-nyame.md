## Summary

Former Governor of Taraba State convicted of embezzling $1.64 billion in state funds. Sentenced to 14 years but pardoned alongside Joshua Dariye.

## Additional Context

## Governorship of Taraba State

Nyame served as Governor of Taraba State in Nigeria’s Fourth Republic, first winning office in 1999 and serving multiple terms into the mid-2000s. His tenure is often discussed in Nigerian political reporting in connection with questions about public finance, procurement, and accountability at the state level.

## Prosecution, Conviction, and Pardon

Anti-corruption investigations and prosecutions involving Nyame extended over many years. He was ultimately convicted in 2022 on charges related to the diversion/misappropriation of state funds (often reported as approximately ₦1.64 billion), receiving a prison sentence. Later in 2022, he was granted a presidential pardon in a decision that drew public attention and debate.

## Public Reception

Nyame’s conviction and subsequent pardon have been cited in public debate about anti-corruption enforcement, elite accountability, and the use of executive clemency in Nigeria. Commentary has ranged from views that legal outcomes signaled progress in prosecutions to criticism that pardons undermined deterrence.
