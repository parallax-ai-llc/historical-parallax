## Background

Alfred Yekatom is a Central African militia figure who rose to prominence during the Central African Republic (CAR) civil conflict. He became associated with the Anti-Balaka movement, a loose coalition of predominantly Christian and animist militias that mobilized after the 2013–2014 crisis.

## Role in the Central African Republic conflict

Anti-Balaka factions were implicated in serious abuses, including attacks on Muslim civilians and forced displacement. Yekatom was identified in reporting and subsequent international proceedings as a commander with influence over fighters and operations in and around Bangui and western CAR.

## Arrest and transfer to the International Criminal Court

In 2018, Yekatom was arrested in Bangui after an incident in the National Assembly in which he fired a weapon. He was subsequently transferred to the International Criminal Court (ICC) in The Hague. ICC proceedings associated with him have focused on alleged crimes committed during the 2013–2014 period, including war crimes and crimes against humanity.
