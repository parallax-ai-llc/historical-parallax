## Summary

Penny Heyns won Olympic gold in both the 100m and 200m breaststroke at the 1996 Atlanta Olympics.

## Highlights and Achievements

- **Double Olympic champion (Atlanta 1996):** gold medals in the **100m breaststroke** and **200m breaststroke**.
- **Prominent figure in South African swimming:** one of the country’s best-known swimmers of the post-apartheid era.

## Olympic Games

### 1996 Atlanta

- **100m breaststroke:** Gold
- **200m breaststroke:** Gold

## World Records and Notable Performances

Heyns is closely associated with the global rise of women’s breaststroke in the mid-to-late 1990s, a period when record times in the event improved quickly. During her competitive peak, she held world-record status in breaststroke events and was known for being effective across both sprint (100m) and middle-distance (200m) breaststroke.

## Style and Strengths

- **Breaststroke specialization:** strong technique and race pacing in both the 100m and 200m distances.
- **Championship execution:** remembered for delivering peak performances at major meets, particularly at the Olympic Games.

## After Competitive Swimming

After retiring from elite competition, Heyns has been involved in swimming-related work and broader sports leadership and advocacy, including roles connected to athlete development and women’s sport.

## Legacy

- Often cited as a benchmark athlete for **South African swimming excellence**.
- Her 1996 Olympic double remains a defining achievement in South Africa’s Olympic history and in women’s breaststroke.
