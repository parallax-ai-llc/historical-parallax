## Summary

John Glenn became the first American to orbit Earth on February 20, 1962, aboard Friendship 7. He later served as a U.S. Senator from Ohio for 25 years. In 1998, at age 77, he returned to space aboard the Space Shuttle Discovery, becoming the oldest person to fly in space at that time.
