## Scientific Work

Bhabha made early contributions to theoretical physics, including research connected to cosmic rays and particle interactions. In physics literature, “Bhabha scattering” refers to electron–positron scattering, a process important in quantum electrodynamics and experimental calibration.

## Institution Builder

Beyond his research, Bhabha is widely remembered as a science administrator and institution founder. He played a central role in establishing the Tata Institute of Fundamental Research (TIFR) as a major center for Indian physics and mathematics, and he helped shape national policy for scientific education and laboratories.

## India’s Nuclear Program

Bhabha advocated for a civilian nuclear energy capability and led key organizations involved in planning and coordination. He served as the first chairman of India’s Atomic Energy Commission and was influential in the development of India’s nuclear infrastructure, including reactor and fuel-cycle planning.

## Legacy

In India, Bhabha is often described as a pivotal figure in post-independence scientific modernization—linking international physics training with domestic capacity building. His legacy is visible in enduring research institutions and in debates about the strategic, economic, and ethical dimensions of nuclear technology.
