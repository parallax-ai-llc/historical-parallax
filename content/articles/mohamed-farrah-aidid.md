## Summary

Somali warlord whose forces attacked UN peacekeepers and fought US forces in the Battle of Mogadishu in 1993 depicted in Black Hawk Down. His militia shot down two US Black Hawk helicopters.

## Historical Context

Aidid emerged as a central figure during the collapse of Somalia's state institutions after the fall of Siad Barre in 1991. The period was marked by civil war, fragmentation among clan-based factions, and a major humanitarian crisis.

## Role in the 1992–1995 International Interventions

During the early 1990s, the United Nations and the United States led missions in Somalia intended to secure relief operations and stabilize conditions (including UNOSOM and the US-led UNITAF). Aidid's forces became involved in escalating confrontations with UN peacekeepers and, later, US forces.

## Legacy and Depictions

Aidid is widely referenced in accounts of the 1993 Battle of Mogadishu and is depicted in journalism and popular media about the episode (including the book and film commonly known as *Black Hawk Down*). Interpretations of his role vary, but he is frequently treated as a symbol of the warlord-era politics of early-1990s Somalia.
