## Summary

Israel Adesanya, known as "The Last Stylebender," is a Nigerian-born New Zealand mixed martial artist and former UFC Middleweight Champion. Combining a background in kickboxing and taekwondo with an instinctive flair for showmanship, he became one of the most technically gifted strikers in MMA history. His fluid movement, precise counter-striking, and ring generalship drew comparisons to Anderson Silva and elevated him to the sport's elite.

Adesanya held the UFC Middleweight Championship across two separate reigns, first capturing the belt in October 2019 and defending it successfully five times before losing it to Sean Strickland in September 2023. He recaptured the title in January 2024 by defeating Dricus du Plessis, cementing his status as one of the division's all-time greats. Beyond the octagon, his anime-inspired entrances, outspoken personality, and dedication to his craft made him a crossover cultural figure.

## Early Life & Background

Israel Mobolaji Temitayo Adesanya was born on July 22, 1989, in Lagos, Nigeria. He immigrated to New Zealand with his family at age 10, eventually settling in Rotorua and later Auckland. As a teenager he trained in taekwondo, achieving a black belt, and took up kickboxing in his late teens after being inspired by watching Anderson Silva fight.

He relocated to Auckland's City Kickboxing gym, where he trained under Eugene Bareman. Before transitioning to MMA, Adesanya compiled an extensive kickboxing résumé of roughly 80 fights, competing in organizations such as Glory Kickboxing and K-1, and establishing himself as one of the premier strikers in the world.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Adesanya made his professional MMA debut in 2012 and built a perfect 11-0 record on the regional circuit before signing with the UFC in 2017. He debuted in February 2018 with a unanimous decision over Rob Wilkinson and quickly attracted attention with a Fight of the Night performance against Brad Tavares in July 2018.

His profile skyrocketed in 2019. A split-decision win over Kelvin Gastelum at UFC 236 in April 2019 earned him the interim middleweight title in a widely acclaimed five-round war. He unified the title in October 2019 by defeating champion Robert Whittaker via second-round TKO at UFC 243 in Melbourne before 57,127 fans — the largest crowd in UFC history at that time.

## Key Achievements

- UFC Middleweight Championship: two separate reigns (2019–2023, 2024)
- Five successful title defenses in his first reign, defeating Yoel Romero, Paulo Costa, Marvin Vettori (twice), and Robert Whittaker
- Recaptured the title with a fourth-round submission of Dricus du Plessis at UFC 305 in August 2024
- UFC 243 co-headliner drew the largest arena crowd in UFC history at the time
- Kickboxing record of approximately 80 bouts across Glory, K-1, and other promotions

## Controversies & Criticism

In February 2021, Adesanya challenged Jan Blachowicz for the light heavyweight title at UFC 259 and was outgrappled in a unanimous decision loss — the first professional MMA defeat of his career. Critics had warned the jump to 205 lbs exposed grappling vulnerabilities he had long avoided at middleweight.

He drew significant backlash in 2021 after making an insensitive social media comment referencing the 2019 Christchurch mosque shootings. Adesanya apologized publicly, acknowledging the hurt caused to the affected communities. Additionally, his first-round loss to Sean Strickland in September 2023 was seen as a flat performance, fueling debate about whether his defensive style had become overly passive.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Adesanya is widely regarded as one of the greatest strikers in MMA history and the finest middleweight of his generation. His success helped raise the profile of African and New Zealand fighters on the global stage and inspired a generation of kickboxers to transition to MMA. His theatrical entrances — often elaborate anime or video game tributes — and vocal promotion style influenced how fighters build personal brands in the sport. City Kickboxing in Auckland, where he trained, became recognized as a world-class gym producing multiple UFC champions, in part due to his success.
