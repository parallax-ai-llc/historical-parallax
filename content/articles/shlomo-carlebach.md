## Summary

Shlomo Carlebach was an Austrian-American rabbi, singer, and songwriter who was one of the most influential figures in modern Jewish music. Known as 'the Singing Rabbi,' he composed thousands of melodies that transformed Jewish liturgical and popular music worldwide. He founded the House of Love and Prayer in San Francisco during the 1960s counterculture. His musical legacy permeates synagogue services across all Jewish denominations.
