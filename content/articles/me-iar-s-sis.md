## Summary

Slovak intelligence service under Mečiar accused of kidnapping, car bombings, and political murders.

## Background

- **SIS** is Slovakia's intelligence service; the label “Mečiar's SIS” is commonly used for the period when **Vladimír Mečiar** and his government exerted strong political influence over state institutions.
- The period is frequently discussed in the context of post-communist state-building in the 1990s and the consolidation of executive power.

## Allegations and Controversies (high level)

Sources commonly describe multiple alleged abuses associated with the service during this era, including:

- **Political surveillance and intimidation** of opponents.
- **Abductions and covert operations** targeting political figures.
- **Violence connected to high-profile cases**, including car-bomb attacks and deaths linked to witnesses.

## Key Case: Abduction of Michal Kováč Jr.

- The 1995 abduction of **President Michal Kováč’s son** (Michal Kováč Jr.) became one of the most prominent political scandals of the decade.
- The case was repeatedly cited by critics as evidence of state capture and the use of security institutions for political ends.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- The controversies surrounding “Mečiar's SIS” became a reference point in Slovak debates about **rule of law**, democratic consolidation, and intelligence-service oversight.
- The dispute over **amnesties** and whether they should block prosecution remained a long-running political and legal issue.
