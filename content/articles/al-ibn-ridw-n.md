## Summary

Alī ibn Ridwān was an Egyptian physician and astrologer who became one of the most important medical writers of medieval Cairo. He wrote extensive commentaries on Galen's works and made astronomical observations including a detailed description of the supernova of 1006 (SN 1006). His autobiography provides rare insight into the life of an Islamic scholar rising from poverty to prominence.
