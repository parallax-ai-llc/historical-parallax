## Key Facts (High-Level)

- Coffman is associated with events surrounding the January 6, 2021 U.S. Capitol attack.
- He was arrested after law enforcement discovered a cache of weapons and incendiary devices connected to him.
- His case is frequently cited as an example of the presence of firearms and improvised incendiaries in the broader January 6 milieu.

## January 6 Context and Arrest

- According to publicly reported court filings and contemporaneous news coverage summarized by Wikipedia, Coffman traveled to Washington, D.C. around January 6.
- He was detained in the vicinity of the U.S. Capitol complex after police located his vehicle.
- A subsequent search of the vehicle led to the discovery of firearms, ammunition, and materials described as Molotov cocktails.

## Charges and Proceedings (Overview)

- Federal prosecutors brought charges related to possession of unregistered firearms and possession of destructive devices.
- The destructive-device allegations centered on the incendiary devices reportedly found in the vehicle.
- The case proceeded in U.S. District Court as part of the broader federal January 6 investigative and prosecution effort.

## Sentencing

- Coffman was sentenced to a term of imprisonment reported as 46 months.
- Sentencing in cases like this typically considers the nature of the weapons, the circumstances of possession, and any demonstrated intent to use them; public summaries emphasize the weapons cache as aggravating context.

## Why This Case Matters

- The incident is often referenced to illustrate that some participants or affiliates brought weapons and incendiary devices to Washington, D.C. during the January 6 period.
- It is also used in discussions distinguishing between defendants who entered the Capitol and those charged based on weapons possession and related conduct in the surrounding area.
