## Summary

Severo Ochoa was a Spanish-American biochemist who shared the 1959 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his discovery of the mechanisms in the biological synthesis of RNA. He discovered polynucleotide phosphorylase, an enzyme that can synthesize RNA in vitro. This discovery was crucial for understanding the genetic code. He fled Spain during the Civil War and spent most of his career at New York University.
