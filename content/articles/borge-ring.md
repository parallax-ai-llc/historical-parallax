## Summary

Borromeo Enrique Henríquez, known as the leader of a major MS-13 clique, directed transnational drug operations and violent crimes from El Salvador and the United States. He was responsible for ordering murders of rival gang members and cooperating witnesses.

## Background: MS-13 and Transnational Gang Prosecutions

Mara Salvatrucha (MS-13) is a transnational street gang with roots in Central America and the United States.
In major cases, prosecutors often frame allegations around enterprise-style structures, using conspiracy and racketeering statutes to connect leadership roles to acts committed by members.
Investigations can involve multiple agencies and cross-border cooperation, especially when defendants move between jurisdictions or direct activity from abroad.

## Indictment and Extradition (High Level)

U.S. federal indictments in organized-crime cases typically rely on witness testimony, seized communications, and documentary evidence that ties individuals to an alleged criminal enterprise.
When a suspect is located outside the United States, extradition depends on bilateral treaties, domestic court proceedings, and executive decisions in the requested state.
Extradition requests in gang cases often emphasize the scope of alleged crimes and the risk of witness intimidation.

## Witness Intimidation and Violence Allegations

Cases involving cooperating witnesses frequently include allegations of threats or retaliation, which can become separate charges.
Because intimidation can undermine investigations, law enforcement agencies may seek protective measures and sealed filings during sensitive stages.
Court records and trial testimony are typically the key public sources for these allegations.
