## Summary

Hiraga Gennai was an Edo-period Japanese polymath who studied Western science and created an erekiteru (static electricity generator), experimented with asbestos, and promoted botanical studies and mining technology. He organized Japan's first exhibition of natural products and was an early advocate for applying Western science to Japanese industry.
