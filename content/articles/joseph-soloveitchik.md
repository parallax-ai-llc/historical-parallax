## Summary

Joseph Soloveitchik was an American Orthodox rabbi and philosopher who was the leading authority in Modern Orthodox Judaism for much of the 20th century. His essay The Lonely Man of Faith explored the dialectical nature of religious experience. He was the head of the Rabbinical Council of America's Beth Din and ordained over 2,000 rabbis at Yeshiva University. His students include most of the leaders of American Modern Orthodoxy.
