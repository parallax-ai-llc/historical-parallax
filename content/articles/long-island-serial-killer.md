## Summary

Rex Heuermann was arrested in 2023 as the suspected Gilgo Beach serial killer who murdered at least 10-16 women over two decades. The case went unsolved for years.

## Case overview

- The term **"Long Island Serial Killer" (LISK)** is used for a series of killings and the discovery of remains on Long Island, New York, including the area near **Gilgo Beach**.
- The case gained major attention after human remains were discovered during searches in 2010 and subsequent years.

## Victim identification and investigative challenges

- Investigators faced challenges including partial remains, varying discovery dates, and debates about whether all cases were linked to a single offender.
- Public reporting and investigative work over the years included analysis of cell-site data, communications records, and forensic evidence.

## Arrest and legal process

- In 2023, authorities arrested **Rex Heuermann** and charged him in connection with specific murders tied to the Gilgo Beach investigation.
- The case remained active and legally unresolved at the time of many summaries; in criminal proceedings, allegations must be proven in court.

## Public and institutional impact

- The long duration of the investigation led to sustained media scrutiny and public concern.
- The case is often cited in discussions about missing-person investigations, coordination across agencies, and the role of new forensic and digital methods.
