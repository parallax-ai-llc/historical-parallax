## Summary

Marcus Licinius Crassus was considered the richest man in Roman history, amassing a fortune through real estate speculation, silver mining, and slave trading. He formed the First Triumvirate with Julius Caesar and Pompey, wielding enormous political power through his wealth. He financed Roman armies and political campaigns. He died leading a disastrous military campaign against the Parthian Empire at the Battle of Carrhae.
