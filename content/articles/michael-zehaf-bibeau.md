## Summary

Canadian who shot and killed a soldier at the National War Memorial then attacked Parliament.

## Background

- Michael Zehaf-Bibeau was a Canadian-born man who had lived in multiple provinces and was reported to have had periods of instability, including struggles with substance use and housing insecurity.
- Contemporary reporting and later summaries describe him as having expressed extremist views and an interest in travelling abroad to join militant groups; Canadian authorities had been aware of him prior to the attack.
- Public accounts often note that his mother worked in an immigration-related role and later publicly discussed aspects of his life; biographical details vary by source.

## 2014 Ottawa attack

- Date: 2014-10-22.
- Location(s): National War Memorial (Ottawa) and the Parliament Hill precinct.
- Sequence (high level):
  - He shot Corporal Nathan Cirillo, a Canadian soldier standing ceremonial guard at the National War Memorial.
  - He then entered the Centre Block area of Parliament (via the precinct) and exchanged gunfire with security.
  - He was shot and killed during the confrontation.
- The event is commonly referred to as the 2014 Ottawa shooting and is frequently discussed together with other attacks in Canada in October 2014.

## Immediate aftermath and response

- The attack led to a major security incident in Canada’s capital, including lockdowns and emergency response operations around Parliament Hill.
- Canadian officials and investigators treated the event as terrorism-related; public statements emphasized the role of ideological motivation, while also acknowledging personal factors discussed in background reporting.
- The incident contributed to debates in Canada about counterterrorism policy, intelligence coordination, and physical security measures around federal institutions.
