Pan Jianwei is a Chinese physicist and world leader in quantum information science who launched the first quantum satellite.

## Field: Quantum Information Science

Pan Jianwei’s work sits at the intersection of quantum optics, quantum communication, and information theory. The central idea is to use quantum phenomena—such as entanglement and single-photon states—to enable tasks that are difficult or impossible with classical signals.

## Notable Contributions (high-level)

- **Quantum communication experiments:** Demonstrations of quantum key distribution (QKD) and long-distance entanglement distribution.
- **Satellite-based quantum links:** Experiments associated with the “Micius” satellite helped test quantum communication over distances far beyond typical terrestrial fiber links.
- **National research infrastructure:** His career is often discussed as part of China’s broader push to lead in foundational quantum technologies.

## Why the Quantum Satellite Was Significant

A satellite platform can reduce loss compared with very long fiber links because photons travel through space with lower attenuation than through glass. This makes space-based experiments a plausible route to global-scale quantum communication networks.

## Legacy and Ongoing Relevance

Pan is frequently cited in overviews of the “quantum race” because his projects connect fundamental physics with potential applications in secure communication and future quantum networking.
