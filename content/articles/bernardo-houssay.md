## Summary

Bernardo Houssay was an Argentine physiologist who shared the 1947 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his discovery of the role played by the pituitary gland in the metabolism of sugar. He was the first Latin American to receive a Nobel Prize in science. He demonstrated how the pituitary gland regulates blood sugar through its hormones, advancing understanding of diabetes.
