## Summary

Charles Francis Richter was an American seismologist who developed the Richter magnitude scale for measuring earthquake intensity. Created in 1935 with Beno Gutenberg at Caltech, the scale became the standard worldwide measurement for earthquake magnitude. His work helped establish seismology as a quantitative science and improved earthquake hazard assessment.
