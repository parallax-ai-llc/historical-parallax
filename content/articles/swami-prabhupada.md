## Summary

A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada was an Indian spiritual teacher who founded the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON) in New York City in 1966. At age 69, he traveled to America with little money and built a worldwide movement of Krishna devotion. He translated and commented on over 80 volumes of Vedic literature. ISKCON grew into one of the most visible Hindu movements in the West.
