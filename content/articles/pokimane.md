## Summary

Imane Anys, known online as Pokimane, is a Canadian-Moroccan content creator and one of the most prominent female streamers in the history of Twitch. Born in Morocco and raised in Canada, she built a massive following through League of Legends and variety gaming content, becoming a cultural touchstone for a generation of online audiences. At her peak she held the title of the most-followed female streamer on Twitch, amassing over 9 million followers on the platform.

Beyond streaming, Pokimane has established herself as a businesswoman and media personality. She was a founding member of OfflineTV, a collaborative content house that shaped modern streaming culture, and has expanded her brand into merchandise, media partnerships, and her own snack company, Myna Snacks. Her influence extends well beyond gaming, touching on representation, creator economics, and the normalization of women in esports and livestreaming.

## Early Life & Background

Imane Anys was born on May 14, 1996, in Morocco and immigrated with her family to Canada, settling in the Montreal area. She grew up in a Moroccan household and has spoken about navigating cultural expectations while developing her interest in gaming and technology. She attended McMaster University in Hamilton, Ontario, where she enrolled in chemical engineering before ultimately leaving to pursue content creation full-time around 2017.

## Rise to Prominence

Pokimane created her Twitch channel in 2013 and her YouTube channel the same year, initially focusing on League of Legends gameplay and guides. Her approachable personality and consistent output helped her grow steadily through 2015–2016. In 2017 she joined OfflineTV, a Los Angeles-based content creator collective that included other prominent streamers such as Disguised Toast and Lily "LilyPichu" Ki. The move to Los Angeles accelerated her visibility and she diversified into variety content, IRL streams, and collaborative videos. By 2018–2019 she was regularly among Twitch's most-watched streamers, and her presence in the broader pop-culture conversation grew through podcast appearances, brand deals, and mainstream media coverage.

## Key Achievements

- Reached over 9 million Twitch followers, making her the most-followed female streamer on the platform for several years.
- Co-founded OfflineTV, one of the most influential collaborative creator groups in streaming history.
- Launched Myna Snacks in 2022, a consumer snack brand targeting the gaming demographic.
- Named to Forbes 30 Under 30 in the Games category in 2021.
- Publicly advocated for fair creator compensation, which contributed to broader industry conversations about Twitch's revenue-share model.
- In early 2024, she moved her primary streaming presence to Kick, citing creative and financial reasons, marking a notable signal about the shifting streaming landscape.

## Controversies & Criticism

Pokimane has faced criticism on several fronts. She was involved in a prolonged public dispute with content creator Jidion in January 2022, during which he organized a hate raid against her stream; Twitch banned Jidion as a result. She has also been criticized by some in the creator community for perceived favoritism in platform moderation. In 2023, controversy arose over her decision to place a DMCA-sensitive copyright strike on a smaller creator, drawing backlash about double standards in how large creators use intellectual property rules. She has generally addressed controversies directly through streams or social media statements.

## Legacy & Influence

Pokimane's legacy is tied closely to the mainstreaming of female creators in gaming. She demonstrated that women could build top-tier audiences in a space historically dominated by men, and her success opened doors for subsequent generations of female streamers. Her entrepreneurial moves, particularly Myna Snacks and investment in creator infrastructure, signal a broader model for how streamers can build durable businesses beyond the platform. Her career arc — from bedroom streamer to Forbes-listed entrepreneur — reflects the maturation of the creator economy as a legitimate industry.
