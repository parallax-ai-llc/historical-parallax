## Summary

Algerian jihadist and smuggler known as the Uncatchable and Mr. Marlboro who masterminded the 2013 In Amenas gas plant hostage crisis that killed 39 foreign workers. He led al-Mourabitoun militant group.

## Background

- Belmokhtar emerged from the militant milieu shaped by the Algerian Civil War, later becoming associated with jihadist organizations operating across the Sahara and Sahel.
- He was also widely described as a smuggler, reflecting how militant groups in the region financed operations through illicit trade routes.

## Organizations and Affiliations

- Commonly linked to AQIM-aligned networks and later to al-Mourabitoun.
- Public reporting often emphasizes the fluidity of faction names, mergers, and splinters among Sahelian militant groups.

## Major Attack: In Amenas (2013)

- The In Amenas gas facility hostage crisis in Algeria resulted in the deaths of dozens of people, including foreign workers.
- The incident became a key reference point for the security risks facing energy infrastructure and expatriate personnel in the region.

## Status and Reporting

- His reported status has been disputed at various times, reflecting the difficulty of confirming outcomes in remote conflict environments.
