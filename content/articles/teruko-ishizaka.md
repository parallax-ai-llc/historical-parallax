## Summary

Teruko Ishizaka was a Japanese immunologist who, together with her husband Kimishige Ishizaka, discovered immunoglobulin E (IgE), the antibody responsible for allergic reactions, in 1966. This discovery was a landmark in immunology, explaining the mechanism of allergies, asthma, and anaphylaxis, and enabling the development of allergy treatments.
