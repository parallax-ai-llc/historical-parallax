## Summary

Taliban attack on the Army Public School in Peshawar on December 16, 2014 that killed 149 people including 132 schoolchildren. It was Pakistans deadliest terrorist attack.

## Background

- **Target:** The Army Public School in Peshawar served children connected to Pakistan’s military community and operated within a broader context of militant violence affecting the country.
- **Perpetrators:** Reporting widely attributes the attack to militants associated with the Pakistani Taliban (Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan, TTP).

## What Happened (High Level)

- **December 16, 2014:** Attackers entered the school and carried out a prolonged assault, killing students and staff.
- **Security response:** Pakistani security forces engaged the attackers; accounts describe a siege-like event ending with all attackers killed.

## Aftermath

- **National mourning:** The massacre triggered widespread public grief and became a defining moment in Pakistan’s contemporary history.
- **Policy and security debates:** The event intensified debate about counterterrorism strategy, militancy, and the balance between security measures and civil liberties.
- **Victims and remembrance:** Annual commemorations and memorial efforts have focused on honoring the children and staff who died.

## Notes

- Casualty figures and detailed timelines vary slightly across sources; major summaries consistently emphasize the scale of child victims and the attack’s national impact.

## Key Data Points (Often Cited)

- **Location:** Peshawar, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, Pakistan
- **Date:** 2014-12-16
- **Deaths:** 149 (including 132 children, as commonly reported)
- **Impact:** A major inflection point in Pakistan’s public debate about militancy and school security.
