## Summary

Oswaldo Cruz transformed public health in Brazil by leading campaigns to eradicate yellow fever, bubonic plague, and smallpox in Rio de Janeiro. He founded the Instituto Oswaldo Cruz (Fiocruz), which became one of the world's leading biomedical research institutions. His mandatory vaccination campaign was controversial but ultimately saved countless lives.
