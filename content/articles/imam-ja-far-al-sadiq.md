## Summary

Ja'far al-Sadiq was the sixth Imam in Twelver Shia Islam and a pivotal figure in Islamic intellectual history. He is credited with founding the Ja'fari school of jurisprudence, the primary legal school of Shia Islam. He taught many prominent scholars, including Abu Hanifa and Malik ibn Anas. He was also known for his contributions to early Islamic science, including chemistry and medicine.
