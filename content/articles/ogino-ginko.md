## Summary

Ogino Ginko was the first licensed female physician in modern Japan. After contracting gonorrhea from her husband, she resolved to become a doctor so women could be treated by female physicians. Overcoming tremendous institutional barriers, she obtained her medical license in 1885 and opened her own practice.
