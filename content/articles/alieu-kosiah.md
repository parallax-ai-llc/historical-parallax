Former ULIMO commander during the First Liberian Civil War convicted by a Swiss court of war crimes. First person tried under Switzerland's universal jurisdiction for Liberia crimes.

## Background

Alieu Kosiah is associated with the United Liberation Movement of Liberia for Democracy (ULIMO), a faction that emerged during the First Liberian Civil War. ULIMO later split into rival wings, and Kosiah has been described in reporting and court materials as a commander operating in Liberia's Lofa County during the conflict.

The allegations against him relate to violence committed during the 1990s civil war period, including attacks on civilians and other abuses attributed to forces under his command.

## Legal Proceedings (Switzerland)

Switzerland arrested and prosecuted Kosiah under principles of universal jurisdiction, which allow national courts to try certain grave international crimes regardless of where they were committed.

Key high-level points from the case as described in public summaries:

- The proceedings were brought before the Swiss Federal Criminal Court.
- The case is widely noted as a first for Switzerland regarding crimes committed during the Liberian civil war.
- The conviction covered multiple counts categorized as war crimes under Swiss law.

## Significance

The case is frequently cited in discussions of:

- Universal jurisdiction as a mechanism to address alleged war crimes when local accountability is limited.
- Long-delayed prosecutions connected to the Liberian civil wars.
- The role of European courts in investigating and trying international crimes connected to conflicts in Africa.
