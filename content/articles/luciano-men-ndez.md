## Summary

Argentine general convicted of crimes against humanity for his role in the Dirty War.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Historical Context

Menéndez is most commonly discussed in connection with Argentina’s "Dirty War" (roughly 1974–1983), a period marked by state terrorism, clandestine detention, torture, and enforced disappearances carried out by security forces and allied groups during and around the last military dictatorship.[^2]

## Background

- Born in Argentina in 1927 and pursued a career as an Army officer.
- Became a senior commander during the 1970s, a period of intense political violence and repression.

## Role During the Dirty War

As a commander of the Argentine Army’s III Corps (based in Córdoba), Menéndez was linked by courts to repression conducted through military units and police structures in the region. Proceedings concerning his command addressed patterns of abduction, torture, and killings attributed to the apparatus of state security in that era.[^1]

## Trials and Convictions

- **2008**: Convicted of crimes against humanity, a landmark conviction involving senior military leadership after Argentina’s amnesty-era obstacles were lifted and trials resumed.[^1]
- In subsequent proceedings, courts continued to sentence him for additional crimes connected to dictatorship-era repression, including acts such as kidnapping, torture, and homicide (as characterized in the judgments and reporting).[^1]
- He received multiple convictions over time and was held in custody through the later years of his life.[^1]

## Later Life and Death

- Menéndez remained a symbol of the dictatorship-era security apparatus due to the prominence of the cases involving him.
- He died in 2018 while in custody.[^1]
