## Summary

Ivorian youth leader known as the 'Street General' who mobilized mobs to support Laurent Gbagbo. He was charged by the ICC with crimes against humanity but acquitted.

## Political Role

- Blé Goudé rose to prominence through pro‑Gbagbo youth activism in Côte d’Ivoire, where street mobilization and mass rallies played a significant role in political contention.
- He became associated with nationalist and anti‑foreign rhetoric during periods of acute polarization, especially around contested elections.

## 2010–2011 Post‑Election Crisis

- Following the disputed 2010 election, Côte d’Ivoire experienced violence and a breakdown in political order as rival camps claimed legitimacy.
- International reporting and court filings described organized street actions and attacks on civilians during this period, which later became the basis for international charges.

## ICC Proceedings

- The International Criminal Court (ICC) charged Blé Goudé with crimes against humanity in connection with the post‑election violence.
- He was tried alongside Laurent Gbagbo; both were acquitted at the ICC, and the acquittals were later confirmed on appeal.

## Significance

- The case is often referenced in debates about international criminal justice, evidentiary standards, and the difficulty of attributing responsibility for mass violence in politically chaotic settings.
- In Ivorian politics, his profile reflects the long afterlife of the 2010–2011 crisis and the continuing contest over historical narratives.
