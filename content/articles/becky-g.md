## Summary

Rebbeca Marie Gomez, known professionally as Becky G, is an American singer, rapper, and actress born on March 2, 1997, in Inglewood, California. Rising from humble beginnings to become one of the most recognizable Latin pop crossover artists of her generation, she has built a career that bridges English-language pop and Spanish-language reggaeton and Latin trap. Her bilingual artistry and authentic representation of Mexican-American identity have made her a cultural touchstone for Latinx youth in the United States.

Becky G first gained mainstream attention in 2013 with her viral freestyle raps uploaded to YouTube, which led to a record deal with Kemosabe Records and Epic Records. Since then, she has charted hits across both the Billboard Hot 100 and Latin charts, collaborated with global superstars, and expanded her presence into acting and advocacy. Her resilience and entrepreneurial savvy have cemented her status as more than a pop act — she is a brand, an activist, and a role model.

## Early Life & Background

Rebbeca Marie Gomez was born on March 2, 1997, in Inglewood, California, to parents of Mexican descent. She grew up in Moreno Valley in a financially struggling household, where her family of six — including her parents and three younger siblings — lived in her grandparents' converted garage for several years. Financial hardship accelerated her drive to work; she began booking local commercials and small acting roles as a child to help support her family.

Her musical influences were deeply shaped by her Mexican heritage as well as American hip-hop and pop. She taught herself to rap by studying artists she admired and began posting videos online at age 14.

---

## Rise to Prominence

In 2011–2012, Becky G uploaded freestyle rap videos to YouTube, one of which — a rap over Dr. Dre's "Still D.R.E." beat — went viral and caught the attention of producer Dr. Luke. This led to a signing with Kemosabe/Epic Records in 2013. Her debut single "Becky from the Block" established her persona, and "Shower" (2014) became her first major commercial hit, reaching number 17 on the Billboard Hot 100.

Her pivot toward Latin music proved transformative. "Mayores" (2017), a collaboration with Bad Bunny, surpassed one billion YouTube views and introduced her to a massive Spanish-language audience. "Sin Pijama" (2018) with Natti Natasha was another billion-view milestone. Her debut Spanish-language album *Mala Santa* (2019) debuted at number two on the Billboard Latin Albums chart and solidified her place in the Latin music industry.

## Key Achievements

- "Shower" peaked at number 17 on the Billboard Hot 100 (2014)
- "Mayores" ft. Bad Bunny exceeded 1.5 billion YouTube views
- *Mala Santa* (2019) debuted at number two on Billboard Latin Albums
- Won Premio Lo Nuestro's Female Artist of the Year in 2023
- Performed "Can't Stop the Feeling" at the 2018 FIFA World Cup closing ceremony
- Voiced the character Penny in *Power Rangers* (2017) and appeared in multiple film and TV productions

## Controversies & Criticism

In 2023, Becky G publicly addressed her split from longtime partner, soccer player Sebastian Lletget, after reports emerged of his infidelity. The public breakup drew significant media coverage. She has also faced criticism from some quarters of the Latin music community for code-switching and the degree to which her earlier English-language pop work authentically reflected her heritage, though she has consistently spoken openly about navigating dual cultural identities.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Becky G occupies a unique position as a Mexican-American artist who successfully operates in both the English-language pop mainstream and the Spanish-language Latin market — a crossover few artists have achieved with sustained commercial and critical success. Her visibility as a Latina in pop culture, her philanthropic work supporting immigrant and Latinx communities, and her candid public persona have made her an influential figure beyond music. She has inspired a generation of bilingual artists who see her trajectory as proof that embracing dual identities is a strength, not a compromise.
