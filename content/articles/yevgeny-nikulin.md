## Background

Yevgeny (Evgeniy) Nikulin is a Russian national who became associated with high-profile credential theft and data-breach incidents involving major U.S. internet companies.

## Alleged Offenses and Targets

Public reporting and U.S. court filings connected Nikulin to intrusions affecting:

- LinkedIn (large-scale theft of user credentials)
- Dropbox (attempted compromise of accounts and infrastructure)
- Formspring (unauthorized access and data exposure)

These cases are often discussed in the broader context of credential stuffing, password reuse, and the secondary markets that trade in stolen login data.

## Arrest and Extradition Proceedings

Nikulin was arrested in the Czech Republic in 2016 and became the subject of competing extradition requests. The case drew attention because it intersected with diplomatic and legal disputes over where a defendant should be tried when multiple jurisdictions claim harm.

He was ultimately extradited to the United States, where federal prosecutors pursued charges related to computer intrusion and related offenses.

## U.S. Trial and Conviction

In 2020, Nikulin was convicted in U.S. federal court on charges linked to hacking and attempted intrusions into major platforms. Sentencing and case details are commonly cited as examples of cross-border cybercrime enforcement and the role of international cooperation in arrests and evidence collection.

## Impact and Significance

Large credential theft incidents have downstream effects that extend beyond the initially breached service:

- Users face increased account-takeover risk when passwords are reused across sites.
- Organizations incur costs for incident response, user notifications, and security upgrades.
- Breach datasets can be repackaged and resold, enabling follow-on attacks.
