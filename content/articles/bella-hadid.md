## Summary

Isabella Khair Hadid, known professionally as Bella Hadid, is an American supermodel who rose to become one of the most in-demand figures in the fashion industry during the 2010s. Born on October 9, 1996, she has graced the covers of Vogue, Harper's Bazaar, and Elle across dozens of international editions, and walked for virtually every major luxury fashion house, including Chanel, Versace, Givenchy, and Dior.

Beyond modeling, Hadid has used her platform to advocate for Palestinian rights — reflecting her heritage through her father, Palestinian-American real estate developer Mohamed Hadid — and has spoken openly about her struggles with Lyme disease, mental health, and body image. Her willingness to discuss personal hardship has resonated with millions of followers, making her a cultural figure who transcends the runway.

## Early Life & Background

Bella Hadid was born in Washington, D.C., and raised primarily in Santa Barbara, California, alongside her siblings Gigi and Anwar. Her mother is Dutch-born former model Yolanda Hadid (née van den Heijden), known from the reality series *The Real Housewives of Beverly Hills*. The family later relocated to New York.

As a teenager, Hadid was a competitive equestrian with aspirations of competing in the 2016 Rio Olympics. Those plans were derailed when she was diagnosed with Lyme disease in 2012 — a condition she shared publicly years later, describing chronic symptoms that affected her daily life for much of her early career.

She briefly attended the New School's Parsons School of Design in New York, studying photography, before committing fully to modeling.

## Rise to Prominence

Hadid signed with IMG Models and made her professional debut in 2014. Her breakthrough accelerated rapidly: she appeared on the cover of *Teen Vogue* in 2015 and was named Model of the Year by *Model.com* in 2016. That same year, she appeared in the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue and walked in her first Victoria's Secret Fashion Show, returning in 2017.

A defining moment came when she walked for the Versace Spring 2017 show, recreating her mother Yolanda's iconic 1990s Versace look — a moment that underscored her lineage in fashion history. She became a brand ambassador for Dior Beauty in 2016, a partnership that elevated her to the luxury beauty market.

## Key Achievements

- Named Model of the Year by *Model.com* in 2016
- Covered over 250 magazine editions globally by the early 2020s
- Walked in more than 100 runway shows in a single season during peak years
- Named one of *Time* magazine's most influential people on the internet
- Launched Kin Euphorics, a non-alcoholic functional beverage brand, in 2019 as a co-founder

## Controversies & Criticism

Hadid has faced scrutiny over allegations of cosmetic surgery, which she denied for years before acknowledging rhinoplasty in a 2022 interview, stating she regretted it. Her vocal support of Palestinian rights — including posts criticizing Israeli military operations — drew both widespread support and accusations of antisemitism from some critics, a charge she and her representatives rejected. She was also among celebrities photographed attending the ill-fated Fyre Festival in 2017, drawing criticism for promoting the event.

## Legacy & Influence

Hadid helped define the aesthetic of 2010s high fashion alongside her sister Gigi, representing a shift toward models with strong personal brands and social media presences. Her openness about Lyme disease raised awareness of the condition globally. As fashion moved toward greater diversity in body type and background, her mixed heritage and outspoken identity positioned her as a figure who bridged runway exclusivity and cultural accessibility.
