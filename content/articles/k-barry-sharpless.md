## Summary

K. Barry Sharpless is an American chemist who has won the Nobel Prize in Chemistry twice: in 2001 for his work on chirally catalyzed oxidation reactions (Sharpless epoxidation and asymmetric dihydroxylation) and in 2022 for the development of click chemistry. He is only the fifth person to win two Nobel Prizes. He coined the concept of 'click chemistry' in 2001.
