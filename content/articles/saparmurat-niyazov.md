## Summary

Turkmen dictator known as 'Turkmenbashi' (Father of all Turkmen) who created a bizarre personality cult. He renamed months after himself and his family, banned ballet and gold teeth, and required his book Ruhnama to be studied by all.

## Additional Details

- **Rise to power**: emerged from the late-Soviet Communist Party apparatus in Turkmenistan and consolidated authority as the USSR dissolved and the country became independent.[^1]
- **Political system**: maintained a highly centralized state with limited political pluralism, widely described by observers as authoritarian.[^1]
- **Personality cult**: promoted the title *Turkmenbashi* and associated symbolism across public life (statues, renamed institutions, and mandatory civic rituals).[^1]
- **Ruhnama**: his book was treated as an ideological text, reportedly incorporated into education and civil-service requirements, and used as a marker of loyalty.[^1]
- **Domestic policy**: issued decrees affecting cultural and social practices; the best-documented examples are the renaming of months/days and restrictions on some entertainment and personal-expression norms.[^1]
- **Economy and energy**: presided over a gas-rich economy where export infrastructure and state control over revenues were central to governance and foreign relations.[^1]
- **Succession**: after his death in 2006, Gurbanguly Berdimuhamedow became his successor, and some high-profile policies of the Niyazov era were later revised or softened.[^1]
