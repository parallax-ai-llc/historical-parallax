## Background

Idiagbon was a career officer in the Nigerian Army and rose through a series of command and staff postings during a period of repeated military intervention in Nigerian politics.

## Role in the Buhari government (1983–1985)

Under Major General Muhammadu Buhari, Idiagbon served as Chief of Staff, Supreme Headquarters, a position widely described as central to day-to-day government direction in the regime.

## War Against Indiscipline

The "War Against Indiscipline" (WAI) was a nationwide campaign associated with the Buhari–Idiagbon era that emphasized public order, punctuality, queuing, and anti-corruption messaging.

## Governance and controversies

The regime was also linked to strict decrees, detention of political opponents, and restrictions on the press; these policies have been debated in Nigeria as trade-offs between order and civil liberties.

## Later life and legacy

After the 1985 coup that brought Ibrahim Babangida to power, Idiagbon’s political influence ended.
He is often remembered in Nigerian public memory through the lens of WAI and the broader arguments about military governance, discipline, and human rights.
