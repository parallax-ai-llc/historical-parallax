## Context: OneCoin

- **OneCoin** was marketed globally as a cryptocurrency but has widely been described by authorities as a large fraud.
- Proceeds from OneCoin-related activity moved through traditional banking rails, often via layered corporate structures and cross-border transfers.

## Alleged Role

High-level descriptions in public reporting describe Scott as helping move large sums tied to OneCoin through the financial system.

- Associated with **moving and/or concealing** funds connected to OneCoin.
- The scale described in court reporting is **hundreds of millions of dollars**.

## Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- Scott was **convicted in U.S. federal court** in connection with laundering OneCoin proceeds.
- The case is commonly discussed in the context of U.S. enforcement actions against OneCoin organizers and facilitators.

## Why This Case Matters

- Illustrates how large fraud schemes can rely on **professional service providers** and international banking infrastructure.
- Serves as an example of U.S. focus on **money-laundering enforcement** connected to crypto-adjacent frauds.
- Highlights that laundering cases may involve both:
  - **movement of funds** (transactions), and
  - **concealment** (structures designed to obscure origin/ownership).
