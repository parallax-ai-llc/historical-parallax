## Summary

Indian female serial killer from Kerala who murdered 6 family members with cyanide between 1972 and 2006 for property and insurance.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1972 | First murder |
| 2006 | Arrested |
| 2009 | Convicted |

## Known For

- A series of **intra-family murders** in Kerala, India.
- Use of **cyanide poisoning** as a method.
- Motives described in reporting as involving **property** and **insurance/financial gain**.

## Pattern of Crimes (High-Level)

- The killings occurred across multiple years, spanning from the 1970s through the 2000s.
- Victims were primarily family members, making the crimes difficult to detect quickly.
- Poisoning cases can present as natural or accidental deaths, which often delays investigation unless patterns emerge.

## Arrest, Trial, and Sentencing

- Authorities arrested her in the mid-2000s after renewed scrutiny of prior deaths.
- Courts convicted her in connection with multiple murders.
- Sentencing and case details have been widely cited in Indian crime reporting as a rare example of a female serial killer case.

## Broader Context

- The case is often discussed in relation to:
  - the challenges of proving **poisoning** without early toxicology,
  - the importance of consistent death investigation procedures,
  - and the role of family dynamics and inheritance disputes in domestic crimes.
