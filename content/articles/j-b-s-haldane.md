## Summary

J. B. S. Haldane was a British-Indian geneticist and evolutionary biologist who was one of the founders of population genetics alongside Ronald Fisher and Sewall Wright. He mathematically demonstrated how natural selection could drive evolution and estimated human mutation rates. He also contributed to enzyme kinetics and was a noted science popularizer. He became an Indian citizen in 1961.
