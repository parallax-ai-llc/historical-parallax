## Overview
On April 4, 1968, Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. was shot and killed while standing on the balcony of the Lorraine Motel in Memphis, Tennessee.

## The Event
King was in Memphis to support striking sanitation workers. James Earl Ray, a fugitive, fired the fatal shot from a nearby boarding house. Kings death caused widespread grief and anger, leading to riots in over 100 cities.

## Legacy
King is remembered as a martyr for the Civil Rights Movement. His assassination galvanized the movement to push for the Civil Rights Act of 1968, which prohibited housing discrimination.
