## Political Role

Win Myint is a Burmese politician who served as **President of Myanmar** during the period of civilian-led government associated with the National League for Democracy (NLD), alongside State Counsellor **Aung San Suu Kyi**. His presidency is frequently discussed in relation to Myanmar’s partial political opening and the enduring power of the military.

## 2021 Coup and Detention

- In February 2021, the Myanmar military seized power, removing elected leaders from office.
- Win Myint was detained and subsequently faced a series of prosecutions that international observers and human-rights organizations widely criticized as politically motivated.

## International Response

The coup prompted broad condemnation and sanctions from multiple governments. The treatment of deposed civilian leaders, including Win Myint, became a focal point in discussions about rule of law, political prisoners, and democratic backsliding.

## Legacy (Ongoing)

Win Myint’s public profile is closely tied to the 2021 rupture in Myanmar’s political trajectory. Assessments of his tenure and significance continue to evolve as the country’s crisis persists.
