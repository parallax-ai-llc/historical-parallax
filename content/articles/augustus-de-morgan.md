## Summary

Augustus De Morgan was a British mathematician and logician who formulated De Morgan's laws, which describe the relationship between set operations (union, intersection, and complement). He was a co-founder of symbolic logic and made important contributions to the development of mathematical induction. He was also the first president of the London Mathematical Society.
