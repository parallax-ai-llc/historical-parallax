## Summary

Sandra Bland was found dead in a Texas jail cell three days after a violent traffic stop arrest. Her death was ruled suicide but sparked widespread suspicion and protests.

## Background

- **Sandra Bland** was a Black woman whose death in custody became a nationally visible case in the United States.
- The incident occurred amid broader public debate about **policing, traffic stops, and the treatment of detainees**, especially Black Americans.

## Traffic Stop and Arrest

- Bland was stopped by a Texas state trooper during a traffic stop that escalated into a physical confrontation.
- She was arrested and booked into the **Waller County** jail system.
- Public attention intensified after video and reporting raised questions about the escalation, arrest decisions, and jail procedures.

## Death in Custody and Investigations

- Bland was found dead in her jail cell three days after the arrest.
- Authorities ruled the death a **suicide**, while many observers and supporters expressed skepticism and demanded additional scrutiny.
- Multiple investigations and reviews examined the arrest, jail supervision, and the circumstances of her death.

## Legal and Political Aftermath

- The case led to civil litigation and public settlements.
- In Texas, it contributed to legislative reforms commonly grouped under the **Sandra Bland Act**, aimed at improving jail screening, mental health interventions, and related practices.
