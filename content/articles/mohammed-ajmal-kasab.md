## Summary

Only surviving gunman from the 2008 Mumbai attacks, hanged four years after the attack.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2012 | Executed for Mumbai attacks |

## Background

- Born in Faridkot, Punjab, Pakistan, and grew up in a poor family.
- Reported in court and investigative reporting to have left home as a teenager and later been recruited and trained by Lashkar-e-Taiba (LeT), a Pakistan-based militant organization.

## 2008 Mumbai attacks (overview)

- In November 2008, a coordinated series of shootings and bombings took place in Mumbai, India.
- Kasab was identified by Indian authorities as one of the attackers who arrived by sea with other gunmen.
- He was captured alive by Mumbai police after being wounded during the attacks, making him the only attacker taken into custody.

## Investigation and prosecution

- Indian investigators said Kasab provided statements about training and planning; authorities also cited forensic and documentary evidence during prosecution.
- He was tried in India under multiple criminal statutes related to murder, waging war, and terrorism.
- The trial court convicted him; the Bombay High Court largely upheld the conviction and sentence.
- India’s Supreme Court upheld the death sentence in 2012.

## Execution

- Kasab was executed by hanging at Yerwada Central Jail in Pune, India, in November 2012.
- The execution followed the exhaustion of legal appeals and a rejected mercy petition.

## Legacy and significance

- His capture and trial became a focal point in India’s public and political response to the attacks.
- The case is frequently cited in discussions of counterterrorism, cross-border militancy, and criminal justice procedures in terrorism trials.
