## Summary
Ahmad Suradji was an Indonesian serial killer who murdered 42 women in North Sumatra between 1986 and 1997. A self-proclaimed sorcerer, he lured victims with promises of magical healing, burying them facing his home to gain supernatural power. He was executed by firing squad in 2008.

## Early Life
Born in 1951 in Medan, North Sumatra. He worked as a cattle breeder and dukun (traditional healer). He claimed his father's ghost instructed him to kill 70 women and drink their saliva. He was married to three wives, one of whom became an accomplice. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1986 and 1997, Suradji lured at least 42 women with ritual ceremonies promising beauty and fortune. During rituals, he had women stand in pre-dug holes, then strangled them with cable. He drank their saliva and buried bodies facing his house. His youngest victim was 11. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
In May 1997, a body was found near his home. Police discovered 42 bodies buried around his property. He confessed and was sentenced to death in 1998. His wife Tumini received life imprisonment. He was executed by firing squad on July 10, 2008. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- Led to scrutiny of traditional healing practices in Indonesia [^4]
- Improved missing persons procedures in North Sumatra
- Raised awareness about exploitation of superstition

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered 42 women over 11 years [^5]
- Exploited cultural trust in healers
- Targeted women seeking help
- Youngest victim was 11
- Crimes undetected for a decade
