Founder of Hamas in 1987. He directed suicide bombings against Israeli civilians during the Second Intifada.

## Additional Context

## Early Life and Role in Hamas

Ahmed Yassin was a Palestinian cleric who became a central figure in the founding of Hamas in 1987, as the First Intifada began. He was widely regarded as a spiritual and organizational leader within the movement.

## Political and Security Impact

- During the 1990s and early 2000s, Hamas developed both political and armed wings, and the group carried out attacks that shaped Israeli and Palestinian security policies.
- Yassin’s public statements and influence were frequently cited by supporters as evidence of religious legitimacy and by opponents as justification for targeting Hamas leadership.

## Death and Aftermath

Yassin was killed in 2004 in an Israeli helicopter strike in Gaza. The killing drew international reactions and was followed by heightened tensions and retaliatory dynamics in the Israel–Palestinian conflict.
