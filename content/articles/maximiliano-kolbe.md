## Summary

Maximilian Kolbe was a Polish Franciscan friar who volunteered to die in place of a stranger at the Auschwitz concentration camp. Before the war, he founded an influential Catholic publishing operation and a friary that became the largest in the world. He used modern media including radio to spread the Catholic faith. He was canonized as a martyr of charity by Pope John Paul II in 1982.
