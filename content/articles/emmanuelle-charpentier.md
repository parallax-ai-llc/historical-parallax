## Summary

Emmanuelle Charpentier is a French microbiologist and biochemist who shared the 2020 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with Jennifer Doudna for developing the CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing method. She discovered the tracrRNA molecule essential for the CRISPR system while studying Streptococcus pyogenes. Her collaboration with Doudna transformed CRISPR from a bacterial defense mechanism into a revolutionary gene editing tool.
