## Summary

Sierra Leonean military officer convicted by the Special Court for Sierra Leone for atrocities committed by AFRC forces. He directed forces that committed mass rapes, amputations, and killings.

## Sierra Leone Civil War Context

Alex Tamba Brima is associated with the Armed Forces Revolutionary Council (AFRC), a military faction that played a central role in Sierra Leone’s civil war and the 1997 coup. The conflict involved shifting alliances among armed groups and was marked by widespread attacks on civilians.

## Special Court for Sierra Leone (SCSL)

Brima was tried by the Special Court for Sierra Leone, a hybrid international tribunal established to prosecute those bearing the greatest responsibility for serious violations of international humanitarian law during the conflict.

High-level themes in the case include:

- **command responsibility and leadership roles** within armed formations
- patterns of violence against civilians, including sexual violence
- accountability for crimes committed during campaigns to control territory and intimidate populations

## Significance

The AFRC-related prosecutions are often cited as landmark examples of post-conflict justice in West Africa. They are used in discussions of:

- how hybrid courts can combine international standards with domestic relevance
- the evidentiary challenges of documenting mass atrocities
- sentencing and deterrence debates for war crimes and crimes against humanity
