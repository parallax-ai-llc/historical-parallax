## Summary

See Maguindanao Massacre. Political mass murder that killed 58 people.

## Background and Context

- **Location:** Maguindanao province, in the Mindanao region of the Philippines.
- **Political setting:** The killings occurred in the context of intense local political rivalry and election-related competition.
- **Alternate name:** The event is widely referred to as the **Maguindanao massacre** (and sometimes the Maguindanao/Ampatuan massacre depending on source).

## What Happened (High Level)

- The attack occurred on **23 November 2009**.
- A convoy was ambushed while traveling to file election-related paperwork.
- **58 people were killed**, including **journalists** and members/supporters associated with the convoy.
- The incident is frequently cited as one of the deadliest single attacks on journalists.

## Alleged Perpetrators and Accountability

- Investigations and prosecutions focused on individuals linked to the **Ampatuan political clan**.
- The case became a landmark test of the Philippine justice system’s capacity to prosecute politically connected violence.
- In **2019**, a court issued **convictions** for multiple accused; appeals and related proceedings have continued in subsequent years.

## Impact and Legacy

- **Press freedom:** The massacre is regularly referenced in discussions of risks faced by journalists covering politics and corruption.
- **Election violence:** It remains a prominent example in debates about private armed groups, local political dynasties, and election security.
- **Public memory:** Anniversaries of the massacre are marked by advocacy groups and media organizations calling for accountability and protection for the press.
