## Summary

Xiao Zhan (肖战), born October 5, 1991, in Chongqing, China, is one of the most prominent entertainers in contemporary Chinese pop culture. He first gained widespread recognition as a member of the boy band X NINE before launching a successful solo acting and music career. His role as Wei Wuxian in the 2019 fantasy drama *The Untamed* catapulted him to international fame, making him one of the most followed celebrities in China.

Known for his dual talent as both an actor and a singer, Xiao Zhan has demonstrated remarkable range across dramatic, romantic, and action-oriented roles. His fanbase, known as "Xiao Zhan Scatter Flowers" (肖战散花), is among the most dedicated and active in Chinese entertainment. Despite significant controversy in 2020, he has maintained and rebuilt his standing as a top-tier celebrity in the Chinese market.

## Early Life & Background

Xiao Zhan was born and raised in Chongqing, a major municipality in southwestern China. He developed an interest in art and design from a young age, eventually earning a degree in industrial design from the Chongqing College of Arts and Sciences. Before pursuing entertainment full-time, he worked as a graphic designer, a background that continues to inform his aesthetic sensibilities. His transition into the entertainment industry came through talent competitions, reflecting a common pathway for Chinese entertainers of his generation.

## Rise to Prominence

In 2015, Xiao Zhan joined the Chinese boy band X NINE after competing on the talent show *Super Idol*. The group achieved moderate success, but it was his individual acting career that defined his trajectory. He appeared in several historical and fantasy dramas throughout 2017 and 2018, building a following before landing the role that would change his career entirely.

In 2019, *The Untamed* (陈情令), a 50-episode xianxia fantasy drama based on the novel *Mo Dao Zu Shi*, premiered and became a cultural phenomenon. Xiao Zhan's portrayal of the roguish, charismatic Wei Wuxian earned him widespread critical and public acclaim. The drama generated billions of views online and was sold to international streaming platforms, introducing him to audiences across Asia, Europe, and the Americas.

## Key Achievements

- *The Untamed* (2019) surpassed 8 billion views on Chinese streaming platform Youku, setting records at the time of release.
- His debut solo album *Spotlight* (2020) topped Chinese music charts and demonstrated his viability as a recording artist.
- He starred in the historical drama *Wolf Warrior* spinoff-adjacent project and the acclaimed *Oath of Love* (2022), the latter becoming one of the top-rated Chinese dramas of that year.
- Consistently ranked among China's top celebrity brand endorsers, representing major international brands including Cartier and Lancôme.
- Won multiple awards including the iQIYI Scream Night Best Actor and various fan-voted popularity accolades.

## Controversies & Criticism

In February 2020, a segment of Xiao Zhan's fanbase reported a fanfiction platform (AO3) to Chinese authorities for hosting content they deemed inappropriate. The incident resulted in Archive of Our Own being blocked in China and triggered significant backlash from the broader Chinese internet community. Critics directed considerable hostility toward Xiao Zhan personally, despite no evidence of his direct involvement. The controversy, dubbed the "227 Incident," damaged his public image substantially and led several brands to distance themselves temporarily. Xiao Zhan issued a public statement calling for civility but stopped short of directly condemning his fans' actions, which drew further criticism from some observers.

## Legacy & Influence

Xiao Zhan represents the maturation of the Chinese idol system into a globally recognized entertainment model. His success helped legitimize danmei (boys' love) adaptations as mainstream commercial properties, opening the door for similar productions. His career trajectory — from boy band member to lead actor to solo musician — has become something of a template within Chinese entertainment. By the mid-2020s, he had largely recovered his commercial standing and remained a defining figure of his generation in Chinese pop culture.
