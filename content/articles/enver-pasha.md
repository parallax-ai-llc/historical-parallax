## Summary

Ottoman military leader and one of the 'Three Pashas' who orchestrated the Armenian Genocide during WWI, killing 1.5 million Armenians. He also caused the deaths of tens of thousands of Ottoman soldiers through reckless campaigns. Killed in battle.

## Additional Context

- Enver Pasha was a leading figure in the Committee of Union and Progress (CUP), the movement that dominated late Ottoman politics after the 1908 Young Turk Revolution.
- As minister of war, he helped shape Ottoman strategy and internal security policy during World War I.

## Policies and Responsibility

- Under the wartime CUP regime, mass deportations and killings of Armenians were implemented across the empire; Enver is widely regarded by historians as one of the central decision-makers.
- The period also saw repression of other minority communities and severe wartime measures justified as security policy.

## Military Leadership

- Enver is associated with ambitious and often disastrous campaigns, including the Caucasus operations early in WWI that produced catastrophic Ottoman losses.
- The Ottoman war effort faced structural constraintslogistics, disease, and industrial weaknessthat magnified the impact of poor planning.

## Aftermath and Legacy

After the Ottoman defeat, Enver fled abroad and later pursued anti-Bolshevik and pan-Turkic projects in Central Asia, where he was killed in 1922. His historical memory remains deeply contested, particularly due to his role in mass atrocities.

In Turkey and across the former Ottoman lands, debates about his reputation often track broader disputes over nationalism and historical accountability.
