## Summary

Dag Hammarskjöld was the second Secretary-General of the United Nations and a profound Christian mystic. His posthumously published spiritual diary Markings revealed a deep inner life of faith and contemplation. He was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize posthumously after dying in a plane crash in Africa. His integration of public service with private devotion made him a unique figure in modern Christianity.
