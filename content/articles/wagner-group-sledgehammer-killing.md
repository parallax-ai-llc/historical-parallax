## Summary

Wagner mercenary Yevgeny Nuzhin was filmed being executed with a sledgehammer after attempting to defect to Ukraine. Prigozhin called it 'a dog's death for a dog.'

## Context

The incident is discussed in the broader context of Russia’s invasion of Ukraine and the role of private military companies, particularly the Wagner Group, which operated alongside Russian interests in multiple theaters. Public summaries typically frame the episode as both a wartime propaganda artifact and an example of coercion and internal discipline within mercenary structures.[^1]

## Key Figures (as commonly described)

- **Yevgeny Nuzhin**: Identified in coverage as the individual shown in the video and described as a Wagner-linked fighter who attempted to defect.[^1]
- **Yevgeny Prigozhin**: Then associated with Wagner leadership; public statements attributed to him were reported as endorsing the killing with a harsh phrase that circulated widely.[^1]
