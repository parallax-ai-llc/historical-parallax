## Summary

Charles Best was a Canadian-American medical scientist who co-discovered insulin with Frederick Banting in 1921. As a 22-year-old medical student, he assisted Banting in the crucial experiments that isolated insulin from pancreatic extracts. He was controversially excluded from the Nobel Prize awarded to Banting and John Macleod.
