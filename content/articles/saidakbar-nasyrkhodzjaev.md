## Summary

Uzbek serial killer who murdered at least 15 people in Tashkent between 1995 and 2002. He targeted women and elderly people in their homes. One of Central Asia's most prolific known serial killers.

## Additional Context

### Case Overview

- Sources describe a multi-year series of homicides in **Tashkent** attributed to Nasyrkhodzjaev.
- The crimes are typically summarized as **home-invasion murders** with victims selected for perceived vulnerability.

### Victim Profile (Reported)

- Reports emphasize that victims included **women and elderly people**, often attacked in or near their residences.
- The pattern is commonly framed as opportunistic targeting rather than attacks on public figures.

### Investigation and Arrest

- Public reporting places the arrest in **2002**, following investigative work by local authorities.
- As with many serial investigations, the case is often discussed in the context of building evidence across multiple incidents rather than a single crime scene.

### Trial and Sentence

- Wikipedia and other summaries note a conviction and a **death sentence** issued in **2004**.
- Details of legal proceedings (charges, evidence, and appeals) vary by source and are typically reported only at a high level in English-language coverage.

### Historical Significance

- In English-language summaries, he is sometimes described as one of the more widely cited serial-killer cases from **post-Soviet Central Asia**.
- The case is frequently referenced when discussing the challenges of record-keeping and media coverage for violent crime in the region during the 1990s.
