## Overview

Pratibha Devisingh Patil (born 1934) is an Indian politician who served as the President of India from 2007 to 2012. She is widely noted as the first woman to hold the office of President in India.

## Early Life and Education

- Born in Jalgaon district (Maharashtra), then part of British India.
- Studied in Maharashtra and trained in law, later practicing as an advocate.

## Political Career (High-Level)

- Built an early career in Maharashtra state politics, including legislative roles and ministerial responsibilities.
- Served in senior positions within state government, including Deputy Chief Minister of Maharashtra.
- Later moved into national politics as a Member of Parliament (Lok Sabha).

## Presidency of India (2007–2012)

As President, Patil’s role was primarily constitutional and ceremonial, including:

- Appointing the Prime Minister and other constitutional offices on the advice of the elected government.
- Granting assent to legislation passed by Parliament (with the power to return a bill for reconsideration in specific cases).
- Serving as the Supreme Commander of the Indian Armed Forces in a constitutional capacity.
- Representing India in state visits and receiving foreign dignitaries.

## Public Discussion and Controversies (Context)

- Public commentary has included scrutiny of official expenditures and use of government facilities, a recurring theme for many high-profile offices.
- Allegations of corruption have circulated in public discourse, but it is important to note that this entry’s earlier labeling was inaccurate: Patil was not convicted of corruption.

## Legacy

- Often cited as a symbolic milestone for women’s political representation in India due to her election as the first female President.
- Her career is also used as a reference point for the pathways from state-level politics to national constitutional office.
