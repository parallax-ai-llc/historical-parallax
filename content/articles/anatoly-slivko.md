## Summary

Soviet serial killer and youth leader who murdered 7 boys in Nevinnomyssk between 1964 and 1985. He hanged and filmed his victims.

## Background

Anatoly Slivko lived in Nevinnomyssk in the Russian SFSR (Soviet Union) and held a position of trust as a youth leader. This social role is frequently highlighted in summaries of the case because it increased his access to children.

## Crimes (High-Level)

- **Victims:** Seven boys.
- **Time frame:** 1964–1985.
- **Documenting acts:** Reports note that he filmed aspects of the crimes, which later became central evidence.

## Discovery and Arrest

Slivko’s arrest is commonly described as stemming from the discovery of incriminating film materials. This evidence helped investigators connect him to unsolved disappearances and killings.

## Trial and Execution

He was tried in the Soviet legal system, convicted, and ultimately executed. The case remains one of the more widely cited examples of serial predation concealed behind a respected public role.

## Historical Context

Public discussion of serial crime in the Soviet era was often limited, and many cases became broadly known only later through reporting and archival publication.
