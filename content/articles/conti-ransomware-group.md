## Summary

Russian ransomware gang that extorted hundreds of millions of dollars from hospitals, schools, and companies worldwide. They attacked Irelands health system and Costa Ricas government.

## Additional Context

- **Operating model:** Commonly described as a ransomware operation with elements of **ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS)**, including affiliates who gained access to victims and shared proceeds.
- **Double extortion:** Like many modern groups, Conti was associated with encrypting systems while also threatening to **leak stolen data** to increase pressure.
- **Targeting:** Public reporting highlighted attacks affecting public services (healthcare and government) and private-sector organizations.

## Tactics (high-level)

- Initial access via phishing, credential theft, or exploitation of exposed remote services.
- Lateral movement and privilege escalation inside enterprise networks.
- Deployment of ransomware at scale to disrupt operations, followed by ransom negotiation.

## Leaks and Decline

- **Internal leaks (2022):** Leaked chats and operational materials provided rare insight into staffing, workflows, and infrastructure.
- **Rebranding:** After disruptions and exposure, operators were reported to move into successor brands and related criminal ecosystems.

## Policy and Response

- Law-enforcement advisories and sanctions targeted people and infrastructure associated with major ransomware syndicates.
- Incident-response guidance emphasized backups, segmentation, least-privilege controls, and rapid containment.
