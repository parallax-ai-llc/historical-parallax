## Background

- Michael Spavor is a Canadian businessman known publicly for work related to cultural and tourism exchanges in Northeast Asia, including links to North Korea through his organization Paektu Cultural Exchange.[^1]
- He became a prominent example of "hostage diplomacy" or "detained diplomacy" in commentary about CanadaChina relations during the late 2010s and early 2020s.[^2]

## Detention and Release (high-level)

- **Detained:** December 2018 by Chinese authorities, shortly after Canada detained Meng Wanzhou in Vancouver on a U.S. extradition request.[^3]
- **Parallel case:** Spavor and fellow Canadian Michael Kovrig were widely referred to as the "Two Michaels" in international media.[^3]
- **Sentence (reported):** Spavor was reported to have been sentenced to prison during the detention period; detailed court information was limited in public reporting.[^1]
- **Released:** September 2021; Spavor returned to Canada the same day Meng Wanzhou departed Canada.[^3]

## Why the case mattered

- **Diplomatic signaling:** The timing and pairing of detentions were broadly interpreted by governments and analysts as retaliatory pressure tied to Meng Wanzhou's case.[^3]
- **Public attention:** The case was frequently cited in discussions about travel risk, consular access, and the use of detention as leverage in state-to-state disputes.[^2]
