## Summary

Daniel Hale Williams was an African-American surgeon who performed one of the first successful open-heart surgeries in 1893. He founded Provident Hospital in Chicago, the first non-segregated hospital in the United States, which also had the first nursing school for African-Americans. He was a pioneer in cardiac surgery.
