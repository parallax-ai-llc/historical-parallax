## Summary

Son Yeon-jae was the first Korean rhythmic gymnast to reach an Olympic final.

## Background

- **Sport**: Rhythmic gymnastics is a discipline that combines gymnastics elements with dance and apparatus work (such as ribbon, hoop, ball, clubs, and rope).
- **National significance**: In countries where rhythmic gymnastics has had fewer Olympic finalists, reaching an Olympic final can be treated as a milestone for the sport’s visibility and youth participation.

## Notability

Son Yeon-jae is widely cited for helping raise the profile of rhythmic gymnastics in South Korea, including by demonstrating that Korean athletes could be competitive at major international events.

## Competitive Highlights (high level)

- **Olympics**: Reaching an Olympic final is typically the result of placing high enough in qualification to advance to the final round.
- **Asian Games**: Winning gold at the Asian Games is considered one of the most prominent achievements for athletes competing within the Olympic movement in Asia.

## Legacy

Athletes who serve as “firsts” (for example, the first from a country to reach an Olympic final) often influence:

- the depth of the next generation (more entrants into the sport),
- investment in coaching and training structures, and
- media attention that can sustain sponsorship and federation support.

This kind of impact is often measured over time by whether a country continues to produce finalists and medal contenders after the breakthrough athlete.
