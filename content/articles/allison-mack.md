## Background

Mack was best known as an actor prior to her involvement with NXIVM, appearing in television and film and gaining wider recognition through the series *Smallville*.

## NXIVM and DOS (context)

NXIVM presented itself publicly as a personal-development organization. Within the group, a secret subgroup called DOS ("Dominus Obsequious Sororium") operated with a master–slave hierarchy.

Prosecutors alleged Mack helped recruit women into DOS, where members were coerced into providing collateral and subjected to branding and other abusive practices under the direction of NXIVM leader Keith Raniere.

## Legal Proceedings

Mack was charged in the United States in connection with racketeering and sex-trafficking-related conduct tied to NXIVM.

She pleaded guilty to racketeering conspiracy and racketeering in 2019 and later received a prison sentence. Public reporting notes she was released from custody in 2022.

## Aftermath and Public Impact

The case became a widely covered example of how coercive-control dynamics can operate inside high-demand groups, particularly when combined with secrecy, collateral, and asymmetric authority.

Mack’s involvement is frequently cited in discussions of celebrity influence within organizations and how public trust can be leveraged in recruitment.

- Recruitment and retention often relied on social ties, private commitments, and escalating demands.
- The legal case centered on organized coercion and exploitation rather than a single isolated incident.
- The NXIVM prosecutions resulted in multiple convictions and substantial media attention.
