## Summary

Cambodian anti-trafficking activist who fabricated her story of being sold into slavery. Foundation collapsed after exposure.

## Background

- Somaly Mam became internationally known through advocacy focused on sex trafficking and exploitation in Southeast Asia.
- Her public profile grew through speaking engagements, media coverage, and partnerships with donors and celebrities.

## Organizations and Public Work

- She founded and led initiatives commonly described as rescue and rehabilitation programs for trafficking survivors.
- The Somaly Mam Foundation operated for years as a prominent anti-trafficking charity, raising funds internationally.

## Controversy and Verification Issues

- Investigative reporting and subsequent reviews concluded that key elements of Mam’s personal narrative were not accurate.
- Discrepancies extended to accounts of specific incidents and to stories attributed to individuals presented as victims.
- After the credibility issues became public, the foundation announced leadership changes and later closed.

## Broader Impact

- The episode is frequently cited in discussions about due diligence, verification, and “hero narrative” risks in international aid.
- It also influenced how anti-trafficking organizations communicate survivor stories, emphasizing consent, accuracy, and safeguarding.

## Legacy

- Somaly Mam remains a case study in how advocacy work can be undermined by false claims, even when addressing real harms.
- The controversy prompted renewed attention to rigorous monitoring and evaluation in the NGO sector.
