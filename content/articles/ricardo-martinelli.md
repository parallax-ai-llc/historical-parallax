## Summary

Former president of Panama convicted of money laundering and sentenced to over 10 years in prison. He was also accused of illegal wiretapping of over 150 people including political opponents.

## Early Career and Rise

- Martinelli built a business career before entering high-level politics, later positioning himself as a pro-business reformer.
- He was associated with the Democratic Change (Cambio Democrático) party and became a prominent figure in Panama’s center-right politics.

## Presidency (2009–2014)

- His administration emphasized infrastructure spending and public-works projects, alongside efforts to attract foreign investment.
- Supporters credited the period with economic growth and modernization initiatives; critics raised concerns about governance standards and the politicization of state institutions.

## Legal Controversies

- One major case involved allegations of illegal surveillance (“wiretapping”) of political opponents, journalists, and civil society figures.
- Another high-profile matter related to corruption and money laundering allegations; open sources report a conviction and a lengthy prison sentence.

## Post-Presidency Political Impact

- Martinelli remained influential within Panamanian politics after leaving office, including through alliances and endorsements that shaped subsequent elections.
- His legal status and asylum-related developments have been closely followed domestically and internationally, reflecting the intersection of politics, courts, and diplomatic protection.
