## Summary

Kwon Ji-yong, known professionally as G-Dragon, is a South Korean rapper, singer, songwriter, and fashion icon widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in K-pop history. As the leader of BIGBANG, one of the best-selling boy groups of all time, he helped define the sound and aesthetic of contemporary Korean pop music while building a global fanbase unprecedented for Asian artists in the early 2010s.

Beyond music, G-Dragon has become a cultural force in fashion and art, collaborating with luxury houses including Chanel and Nike, and earning recognition from outlets such as Time magazine and Forbes. His ability to blend hip-hop, electronic, and experimental genres with high-concept visual artistry set a template that countless K-pop acts would follow.

## Early Life & Background

Born on August 18, 1988, in Seoul, South Korea, Kwon Ji-yong showed an early aptitude for performance. At age six he was scouted by SM Entertainment, and by age nine he had signed with YG Entertainment, where he trained intensively for years before debuting. He attended Apgujeong High School and later studied at Kyung Hee Cyber University. His prolonged trainee period gave him deep grounding in songwriting and production, skills that would distinguish him from peers.

## Rise to Prominence

G-Dragon debuted as a member of BIGBANG in 2006 under YG Entertainment. The group quickly rose to dominance in South Korea and across Asia. His first solo mini-album, *Heartbreaker* (2009), sold over 100,000 copies in its first week and broke records on the Gaon Chart, establishing him as a credible solo act apart from the group. The 2012 single "Crayon" and his collaboration album with label-mate T.O.P, *GD&TOP* (2010), further cemented his status. BIGBANG's 2015 "MADE" series produced multiple chart-topping singles simultaneously, an industry first in Korea.

## Key Achievements

- *Heartbreaker* (2009) debuted at number one on the Gaon Album Chart.
- His solo album *One of a Kind* (2012) won Album of the Year at the Mnet Asian Music Awards.
- *COUP D'ÉTAT* (2013) became the first K-pop album to chart on the Billboard 200 by a solo Korean artist at that time.
- BIGBANG's "Fantastic Baby" (2012) surpassed 100 million YouTube views, among the earliest K-pop videos to do so.
- He held a solo world tour, "Act III: M.O.T.T.E" in 2017, selling out arenas across Asia, North America, and Australia.
- Named to Time magazine's 100 Most Influential People list in 2015.

## Controversies & Criticism

G-Dragon has faced several controversies. In 2011, he received a suspended sentence after testing positive for marijuana, which he attributed to secondhand smoke at a party abroad — a claim that drew both sympathy and skepticism. In 2023, he was investigated again on drug-related allegations; prosecutors ultimately cleared him of charges in early 2024 after tests returned negative results. He has also drawn criticism for cultural appropriation in certain visual concepts, sparking debate about creative boundaries within K-pop.

## Legacy & Influence

G-Dragon's influence extends well beyond chart performance. He is credited with introducing high-fashion sensibility and auteur-level creative control into mainstream K-pop, paving the way for artists to function as full creative directors of their work. His streetwear brand PEACEMINUSONE gained international recognition, and his Nike Air Force 1 collaboration sold out within minutes globally. Artists across genres cite him as a primary influence, and his career arc — from child trainee to global icon — remains a benchmark in the industry.
