## Summary

Australian murderer who killed her partner John Price in 2000, skinned him, and cooked parts of him into a meal for his children. She is the first Australian woman sentenced to life without parole.

## Background

Knight was born in New South Wales and worked for periods in manual and industrial jobs, including work in abattoirs. Accounts of her life frequently emphasize a long pattern of violent incidents and escalating threats toward intimate partners prior to the 2000 killing.

## Investigation and Trial (high level)

Following the death of John Price, the case quickly became a major national news story in Australia due to its severity and the allegations presented in court. Knight pleaded guilty to murder, and the court proceeded to sentencing on the basis of the admitted facts and psychiatric assessments.

## Sentence and Legal Significance

In 2001, she was sentenced to life imprisonment without the possibility of parole. The sentence is often cited in discussions of Australian criminal justice because it is rare and because the judge explicitly recommended that parole never be granted.

## Public and Media Impact

The case has been referenced in later reporting and true-crime media as an example of extreme domestic violence and the challenges of identifying and responding to persistent patterns of coercion and prior assaults. It is also sometimes discussed in the context of victim risk assessment and the way repeated violent behavior can precede a fatal incident.
