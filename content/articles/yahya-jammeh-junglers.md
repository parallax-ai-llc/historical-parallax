## Summary

Death squad operated by Gambian President Yahya Jammeh that carried out extrajudicial killings, torture, and disappearances. Members were drawn from the National Intelligence Agency and State Guard.

## Background

- **Political context:** The Junglers are described in reporting as operating under the rule of President **Yahya Jammeh**, during a period marked by accusations of political repression.
- **Composition:** Accounts describe membership drawn from state security units, including intelligence and presidential guard structures.

## Alleged Activities (high level)

- **Methods:** Reports and testimony describe **extrajudicial killings**, enforced disappearances, and torture.
- **Targets:** Allegations often focus on perceived political opponents, dissidents, and others viewed as threats by the regime.

## Post-2016 Documentation

- **Truth commission record:** The **Truth, Reconciliation and Reparations Commission (TRRC)** in The Gambia documented patterns of abuses and collected testimony.
- **Public testimony:** Some former members were reported to have given public accounts describing operations and chains of command.

## Impact

- **Human rights legacy:** The Junglers are widely cited as emblematic of state-linked violence during the Jammeh era.
- **Accountability:** Investigations and legal processes have been discussed in the context of transitional justice and potential prosecutions.

## Notes

- The strongest claims about specific incidents and perpetrators come from sworn testimony, commission records, and court proceedings.
- Terminology varies by source; some use "Junglers" as a broad label for multiple operational units.
