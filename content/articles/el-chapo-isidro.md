## Summary

Mexican drug lord named Isidro Meza Flores who controls the Mazatlán faction of the Beltrán Leyva Organization. He is a major methamphetamine and fentanyl trafficker in Sinaloa with a multi-million dollar US bounty.

## Background and Identity

The nickname “El Chapo Isidro” is associated with Isidro Meza Flores, a figure described in open reporting as operating in and around the Pacific coast region of Sinaloa, Mexico. Sources frequently link him to the broader ecosystem of splinter groups that emerged after leadership disruptions within major trafficking organizations.

## Reported Role in Organized Crime

Public sources generally describe Meza Flores as:

- a regional organizer connected to the **Beltrán Leyva Organization**’s fragmentation,
- involved in logistics for synthetic drugs, and
- competing with rival factions for control of corridors and port access on Mexico’s northwest coast.

Because much of the available information comes from law-enforcement reporting and journalism, details can vary by source and over time.

## Law-Enforcement Attention

United States and Mexican authorities have publicly identified Meza Flores as a wanted figure. Notable elements mentioned in public listings include:

- a **Rewards for Justice / State Department**-publicized monetary reward offer,
- allegations related to drug trafficking and associated organized-crime activity, and
- periodic updates tied to broader counter-narcotics operations in the region.

## Broader Context

The Sinaloa region’s trafficking landscape has often been characterized by shifting alliances, where local enforcement pressure, rival competition, and leadership disputes can rapidly change which groups control specific territories. In that environment, individuals with regional influence may be described as “faction leaders” even when organizational structures are fluid.
