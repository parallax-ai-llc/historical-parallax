## Summary

Israeli president who was convicted of two counts of rape and other sexual offenses against employees. He was the first Israeli president convicted of a crime and the first to serve prison time. Served 5 of 7 years.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2000 | Became president of Israel |
| 2010 | Convicted of rape |
| 2011 | Sentenced to 7 years |
| 2016 | Released after serving 5 years |

## Political Background

- **Long-running public career**: Before the presidency, Katsav held senior political roles and was a prominent figure within Israeli party politics.
- **Ceremonial office with symbolic weight**: The Israeli presidency is largely non-executive, but the office carries significant moral and representative authority—making the later criminal case particularly consequential.

## Criminal Case

- **Allegations**: Multiple accusations were made by employees and associates, including claims of rape and other sexual offenses.
- **Investigation and indictment**: The case proceeded through police investigation and prosecutorial decisions that drew extensive media attention inside and outside Israel.
- **Trial outcome**: Katsav was convicted on major counts, including rape, and sentenced to a multi-year prison term.

## Imprisonment and Release

- **Custody**: He served a substantial portion of his sentence in prison.
- **Release**: He was later released after serving years of the term, a point often summarized as “served 5 of 7 years.”

## Public Significance

The Katsav case is frequently referenced as:

- a landmark moment demonstrating that even the highest officeholders can face criminal accountability,
- an inflection point in Israeli public discussion about workplace power dynamics and sexual misconduct,
- and a reminder of how reputational collapse can occur rapidly once credible allegations enter the legal system.
