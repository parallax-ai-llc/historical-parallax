## Summary

Niklaus Wirth was a Swiss computer scientist who designed several programming languages including Pascal, Modula, and Oberon. Pascal became one of the most popular teaching languages in computer science education. He formulated Wirth's law, observing that software gets slower faster than hardware gets faster. He received the Turing Award in 1984 for developing a sequence of innovative computer languages.
