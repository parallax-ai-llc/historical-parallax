## Summary

Frances Allen was the first woman to receive the Turing Award (2006), recognized for her pioneering contributions to the theory and practice of compiler optimization. Her work on program optimization and parallelization made high-performance computing possible. She spent her career at IBM, where she became the first female IBM Fellow.
