## Summary

Ibn Khaldun was a Tunisian-Arab historian, sociologist, and philosopher who is considered the founder of sociology, historiography, and modern economics. His 'Muqaddimah' introduced a scientific approach to the study of history, society, and civilization. He proposed theories of social cohesion, economic cycles, and the rise and fall of civilizations. His work anticipated many modern social science concepts by centuries.
