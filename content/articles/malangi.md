## Summary

Feared dacoit of the Bundelkhand region of Uttar Pradesh who carried out robberies and kidnappings. He was killed in a police encounter after years on the run.

## Regional context

The Bundelkhand region, spanning parts of Uttar Pradesh and neighboring areas, has periodically been associated in Indian media and policing narratives with dacoity (banditry) and rural criminal networks. Social factors sometimes cited include difficult terrain, poverty, and limited state capacity in certain districts.

## Criminal activity (high level)

Accounts of Malangi typically describe a pattern of armed robbery and kidnapping associated with dacoit gangs. Because such cases are often reported through police and press sources, details can vary and should be treated as allegations unless confirmed through court records.

## Policing and "encounters"

Malangi's death is described as occurring in a police "encounter", a term used in India for armed confrontations between police and suspects. Encounters have been controversial in some cases, with debates about due process, accountability, and whether specific incidents were genuine firefights or extrajudicial killings.

## Public image and legacy

Figures labeled as dacoits sometimes acquire mythic or folk reputations in local storytelling, while also being viewed as symbols of insecurity and coercion. Malangi is mainly remembered in this broader tradition of bandit narratives in north-central India.
