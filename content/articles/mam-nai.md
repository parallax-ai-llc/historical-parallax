Chief interrogator at Tuol Sleng torture prison who oversaw extraction of forced confessions.

## Expanded Notes

### Names and Identifiers

- Mam Nai is also reported as **Mam Nay**.
- His nom de guerre is commonly given as **Comrade Chan**; sources also mention **Ta Chan**.

### Background

- Born in **1934** in **Kampong Thom Province** when Cambodia was part of **French Indochina**.
- Trained as an educator and worked as a **natural sciences teacher** (mid-1950s) before moving into administrative roles.
- Became principal of a college in Kampong Thom in the late 1950s; during this period he knew **Kang Kek Iew ("Duch")**, later the head of S-21.

### Khmer Rouge Security Apparatus (High-level)

- Mam Nai is described as a lieutenant of **Santebal**, the Khmer Rouge internal security branch.
- He is widely described as leading (or being among the key personnel of) the **interrogation unit** at **Tuol Sleng (S-21)**, where prisoners were interrogated under torture and later executed.
- Prior to S-21, he is reported to have assisted Duch at the earlier **M-13** prison camp, where interrogation practices were developed and refined.

### Allegations and Documentary Trail

- Secondary accounts describe his **signature** appearing on numerous documents associated with interrogation and torture practices.
- Multiple sources portray him as directly involved in interrogation work; he has denied being a leader of the torture system when testifying.

### Post-1979 and Later Activity

- After the fall of Phnom Penh in **January 1979**, he is reported to have fled with other Khmer Rouge cadres.
- Some accounts state that he continued working as an interrogator for Khmer Rouge factions into the **1990s**.
- He later lived as a farmer; despite being publicly identified by researchers and journalists, he was not charged in the main Khmer Rouge tribunal proceedings as of the referenced reporting.

### Tribunal Testimony (ECCC)

- He gave testimony at the **Extraordinary Chambers in the Courts of Cambodia (ECCC)** in **July 2009**.
- Reporting on this testimony emphasizes repeated denials and claims of limited memory regarding specific acts and chain-of-command responsibility.
