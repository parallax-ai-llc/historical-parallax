## Summary

Martin Bucer was a German Protestant reformer who played a key role in the Reformation in Strasbourg. He attempted to mediate between Luther and Zwingli on the Eucharist and influenced John Calvin's theology. He later moved to England where he influenced the development of Anglicanism. His emphasis on church discipline and pastoral care shaped Reformed ecclesiology.
