## Summary

Lee Dong-wook (born November 6, 1981) is a South Korean actor and model who has established himself as one of the most versatile and enduring presences in Korean entertainment. Rising from supporting roles in the early 2000s, he became a household name through a string of critically acclaimed dramas spanning romance, fantasy, and thriller genres. His commanding screen presence, distinctive visual style, and emotional range have earned him a loyal fanbase across Asia and beyond.

What distinguishes Lee Dong-wook is his ability to inhabit supernatural and morally complex characters with equal conviction. His roles as the Grim Reaper in *Goblin* (2016–2017) and the nine-tailed fox in *Tale of the Nine-Tailed* (2020) demonstrated a gift for blending otherworldly menace with tender vulnerability. Beyond acting, his work as a model and brand ambassador has made him a significant figure in Korean fashion and advertising, cementing his status as a cultural icon of the Hallyu wave.

## Early Life & Background

Lee Dong-wook was born in Seoul, South Korea, on November 6, 1981. He developed an early interest in performance and pursued studies related to film and theater, attending Sungkyunkwan University, where he majored in Film. His path into entertainment began in his late teens when he signed with a talent agency, eventually making his acting debut in 2001.

## Rise to Prominence

Lee made his television debut in the KBS2 drama *School 2* (2001) and built his early career through supporting roles. His first significant lead role came with *My Girl* (2005), a romantic comedy that earned strong ratings and introduced him to a broader audience. He continued to grow his profile with *Scent of a Woman* (2011), a critically praised melodrama in which he played opposite Kim Sun-ah, and which showcased his capacity for nuanced emotional performance.

His career reached a new peak with the fantasy epic *Goblin* (tvN, 2016–2017), written by renowned screenwriter Kim Eun-sook. Playing Wang Yeo, the Grim Reaper, alongside Gong Yoo, Lee delivered a performance that was both comedic and deeply melancholic, earning widespread critical acclaim and a devoted international following. The drama became one of the highest-rated cable dramas in Korean television history.

## Key Achievements

- Lead role in *Goblin* (2016–2017), one of the highest-rated Korean cable dramas ever produced
- Lead in *Tale of the Nine-Tailed* (2020) and its sequel *Tale of the Nine-Tailed 1938* (2023), both successful fantasy dramas on tvN
- Host of the popular variety program *Life Bar* (2016–2018)
- Multiple Brand of the Year and popularity awards at major Korean broadcasting awards ceremonies
- Long-running status as a top-tier brand ambassador for luxury and lifestyle brands in South Korea

## Controversies & Criticism

Lee Dong-wook's career has been largely free of major controversies. In 2019, his brief public relationship with fellow actor Yoo In-na attracted substantial media attention, though both parties handled the coverage with discretion and the relationship ended without significant controversy. Some critics have noted that his selection of projects has at times leaned heavily on fantasy and supernatural genres, suggesting a narrowing of range, though his 2023 thriller *Bad and Crazy* (2021) and continued work challenge that characterization.

## Legacy & Influence

Lee Dong-wook represents a generation of Korean actors who helped elevate the global profile of Korean drama through the Hallyu wave. His portrayal of supernatural beings — executed with restraint and emotional intelligence — has influenced how such characters are written and cast in subsequent Korean fantasy productions. With a career spanning more than two decades and continued leading-man status well into his forties, he stands as a model of longevity and sustained relevance in an industry that often cycles quickly through talent.
