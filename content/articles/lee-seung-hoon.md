## Background

Lee Seung-hoon is a South Korean long-distance speed skater whose career spans multiple Olympic cycles. Long-distance events in speed skating reward aerobic capacity, pacing discipline, and the ability to maintain efficient technique across many laps, with small time losses compounding quickly.

## Olympic events and formats (high level)

Speed skating at the Olympic level includes both time-trial style races (where athletes skate in pairs against the clock) and, in some editions, the **mass start**, a pack-racing format that resembles track cycling in its tactical dynamics.

In broad terms:

- **10,000m** is a traditional long-distance time trial in which pacing strategy and lap consistency are central.
- **Mass start** involves a group start, where position, drafting, and timing of attacks can be as important as raw speed.
