## Summary

Ottoman military commander and one of the Three Pashas responsible for the Armenian Genocide. Also oversaw the brutal repression of Arab nationalists in Syria and Lebanon.

## Background

Djemal Pasha was a leading figure of the Committee of Union and Progress (CUP), the political movement that dominated the late Ottoman state during World War I. Together with Talaat Pasha and Enver Pasha, he formed part of the ruling triumvirate often referred to as the "Three Pashas."

## Political and Military Roles

He held senior posts during the war years, including ministerial responsibilities and command roles. In the Ottoman provinces of Greater Syria, administrators and commanders exercised broad power over security policy, censorship, and the treatment of political opponents.

## World War I Context

The Ottoman Empire entered World War I on the side of the Central Powers. The war intensified internal repression and contributed to mass displacement, famine, and coercive measures across multiple regions.

## Armenian Genocide and Repression in the Levant

Historical accounts and modern scholarship widely identify the CUP leadership as responsible for the Armenian Genocide. In addition to policies directed at Armenians, Djemal Pasha is also associated with harsh measures against Arab activists and nationalists in Syria and Lebanon, including executions and crackdowns intended to deter dissent.

## Assassination and Legacy

After the war, several former Ottoman leaders became targets of retaliatory assassinations. Djemal Pasha was killed in Tbilisi in 1922; this is commonly linked to Operation Nemesis, a campaign of Armenian revolutionary vengeance against figures held responsible for genocide.

His reputation remains heavily shaped by wartime atrocities and imperial collapse. In historical writing, he is typically discussed as an example of how centralized wartime rule enabled mass violence, as well as how postwar accountability often took extra-judicial forms when formal prosecution failed.
