## Summary

Giorgio Perlasca was an Italian businessman and humanitarian who, during the final months of World War II, saved approximately 5,200 Hungarian Jews from deportation to Nazi death camps. Operating in Budapest in late 1944 and early 1945, he impersonated a Spanish consul-general and issued thousands of false Spanish protective passes, sheltered Jews in safe houses under Spanish protection, and negotiated directly with Nazi and Arrow Cross officials to prevent deportations. Despite having no diplomatic authority, his audacious deception saved more lives than many official government programs. Decades after the war, he was recognized as Righteous Among the Nations by Yad Vashem in Israel.
