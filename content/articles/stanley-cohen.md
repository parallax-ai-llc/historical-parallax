## Summary

Stanley Cohen is an American geneticist who co-developed recombinant DNA technology with Herbert Boyer. Their 1973 experiment showed how to combine DNA from different organisms, creating the foundation for genetic engineering and biotechnology. This breakthrough made possible the production of genetically engineered medicines, crops, and industrial enzymes.
