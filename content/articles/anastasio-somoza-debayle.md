## Summary

Last of the Somoza dynasty dictators of Nicaragua. His regime's corruption and brutality led to the Sandinista Revolution. He embezzled millions and ordered bombings of his own cities. Assassinated in exile in Paraguay.

## Background: the Somoza dynasty

Anastasio Somoza Debayle is commonly framed as the final ruler of the Somoza family dynasty, which dominated Nicaraguan politics through control of the National Guard and close ties between economic power and state authority.

## Governing style and the National Guard

Somoza's rule is often characterized by:

- Heavy reliance on the National Guard for internal security.
- Concentration of economic assets among family-aligned networks.
- Political repression of opposition movements.

## 1972 Managua earthquake and legitimacy crisis

The 1972 earthquake and the international relief effort that followed are frequently discussed as a turning point. Allegations of corruption and diversion of aid contributed to a widening legitimacy crisis and increased support for revolutionary opposition.

## Sandinista Revolution and exile

By the late 1970s, armed conflict intensified and the Sandinista movement gained momentum. Somoza's fall in 1979 is often presented as a watershed event in Central American Cold War history, followed by a period of revolutionary government and broader regional instability.

## Assassination and legacy

Somoza's assassination in exile is widely reported and is often cited as emblematic of the region's violent political struggles. Debates about his legacy typically focus on authoritarianism, corruption, and the long-term institutional damage attributed to dynastic rule.
