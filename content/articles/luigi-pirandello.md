## Summary

Luigi Pirandello was an Italian playwright and novelist who received the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1934. His play Six Characters in Search of an Author revolutionized modern theater by questioning the nature of reality, identity, and theatrical illusion. His work anticipated Absurdist theater and influenced Beckett, Brecht, and countless others.
