## Summary

Ramzi Ahmed Yousef (born 1968) is a convicted militant best known for organizing the 1993 bombing of the World Trade Center in New York City. He was later linked by investigators to other planned attacks in the mid-1990s, and was captured in 1995.

He was tried in U.S. federal court and convicted on terrorism-related charges, receiving a life sentence. The name “Ramzi Yousef” has also appeared in reporting alongside other major figures in transnational jihadist networks of the era, including family ties to Khalid Sheikh Mohammed.

## Background and associations

- **Identity and aliases:** Yousef used false travel documents and multiple identities; early reporting and later court proceedings discuss contested or incomplete biographical details.
- **Network links:** He has been described as a nephew of **Khalid Sheikh Mohammed**, a relationship cited in multiple journalistic and government summaries.[^2]

## Notability

- **1993 World Trade Center bombing:** Yousef’s role as an organizer made him one of the most prominent defendants in early 1990s U.S. terrorism prosecutions.[^1]
- **Mid-1990s plots:** Investigators associated him with additional planned attacks that were disrupted before execution; public descriptions typically emphasize scope and intent rather than operational detail.[^1][^3]

## Legal outcome

- **Jurisdiction:** Prosecuted in U.S. federal court.
- **Sentence:** Publicly reported as **life imprisonment** following conviction on terrorism-related charges.[^1][^3]
