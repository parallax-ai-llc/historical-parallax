## Summary

Mildred Dresselhaus, known as the 'Queen of Carbon Science,' made pioneering contributions to the study of carbon nanostructures including graphene, carbon nanotubes, and fullerenes. Her research on the electronic properties of carbon materials laid the groundwork for nanotechnology. She was the first woman to receive MIT's Institute Professor title.
