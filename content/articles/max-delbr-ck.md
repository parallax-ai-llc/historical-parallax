## Summary

Max Delbrück was a German-American biophysicist who shared the 1969 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for discoveries concerning the replication mechanism and genetic structure of viruses. He pioneered the use of bacteriophages for understanding genetics at the molecular level. His phage group at Caltech was instrumental in launching the molecular biology revolution.
