## Summary

The Banū Shākir were three brothers (Muhammad, Ahmad, and Hasan) who were 9th-century Arab mathematicians and engineers at the Baghdad House of Wisdom. They wrote the Book of the Measurement of Plane and Spherical Figures, advancing geometry. Their engineering works included mechanical devices, canals, and they sponsored the translation of Greek scientific texts.
