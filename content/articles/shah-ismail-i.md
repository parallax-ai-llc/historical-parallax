## Summary

Shah Ismail I was the founder of the Safavid dynasty and the ruler who established Twelver Shia Islam as the state religion of Iran. He unified Iran under his rule and made Shia identity central to Persian national consciousness. He was also a noted poet writing under the pen name Khatai. His decision to impose Shiism transformed the religious landscape of the Middle East and defined Iran's identity to the present day.
