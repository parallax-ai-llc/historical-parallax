## Summary

Massive Brazilian corruption investigation that uncovered billions in bribes paid by construction company Odebrecht to politicians across Latin America. It led to the conviction of former presidents.

## Origins and Scope

- Operation Car Wash (Lava Jato) began in 2014 as a money-laundering investigation and expanded into a wide-ranging anti-corruption probe.
- The investigation became closely associated with contracts and kickback schemes involving Petrobras and major construction firms.
- The ripple effects extended beyond Brazil, with plea agreements and evidence touching political and business elites across Latin America.

## Mechanisms (High-Level)

- Prosecutors alleged that companies coordinated bribe payments and inflated contract values in exchange for favorable treatment.
- Investigators used tools such as plea bargains and cooperation agreements to build cases against higher-level figures.

## Political Impact and Controversy

- The investigations reshaped Brazilian politics, contributing to major institutional crises and high-profile prosecutions.
- Critics and subsequent court rulings raised concerns about due process, judicial conduct, and the politicization of anti-corruption enforcement.
- Some convictions and procedural outcomes changed over time through appeals and higher-court decisions, illustrating how the legal record evolved beyond initial headlines.
