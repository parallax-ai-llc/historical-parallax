## Disambiguation Note

"Ramanujan" most often refers to **Srinivasa Ramanujan**, the Indian mathematician (1887–1920). Some sources use the surname alone, but the standard full name helps avoid ambiguity. A dedicated biography is usually indexed under "Srinivasa Ramanujan".

## Overview

Srinivasa Ramanujan was a self-taught mathematician whose notebooks contained thousands of formulas and results, many written without proof in a style that reflected discovery rather than exposition. His work has had lasting impact in number theory and related fields, and a significant portion of his output was validated and extended by later mathematicians.

## Cambridge Period

After initiating correspondence with G. H. Hardy in 1913, Ramanujan traveled to Cambridge and worked at Trinity College. Hardy and other mathematicians helped translate Ramanujan's intuition into publishable proofs and frameworks while preserving the novelty of his ideas. During this period Ramanujan produced influential work on partitions and modular-type functions.

## Notebooks and Later Influence

Ramanujan's notebooks were compiled and studied for decades after his death. The later discovery of his "lost notebook" further expanded the known corpus of his results and inspired new research, particularly around mock theta functions and q-series.
