## Summary

Chemical weapons attack using sarin gas on suburbs of Damascus on August 21, 2013 that killed over 1,400 people. The Assad regime was held responsible despite denials.

## What Happened

- The attack struck multiple locations in the Ghouta suburbs of Damascus on 21 August 2013, producing mass casualties consistent with exposure to a nerve agent.
- International attention was heightened because the use of chemical weapons was widely treated as a major escalation in the Syrian civil war.

## International Response

- United Nations inspectors investigated in the aftermath, and the incident became central to diplomatic negotiations over Syria's chemical weapons program.
- In late 2013, Syria acceded to the Chemical Weapons Convention and an internationally supervised process began to declare and remove or destroy declared chemical weapons.

## Ongoing Dispute

- Attribution and casualty estimates have been contested in public debate, but the incident remains one of the most cited chemical weapons attacks of the 21st century.

## Key Points

- Chemical weapons incidents generate complex evidentiary questions because medical, environmental, and intelligence sources must be integrated under time pressure.
- The Ghouta attack is often used as a reference point in discussions about deterrence and enforcement of international norms.
