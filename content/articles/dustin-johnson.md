## Summary

Dustin Johnson is an American professional golfer widely regarded as one of the most physically dominant players in the history of the sport. Born on June 22, 1984, in Columbia, South Carolina, Johnson has combined prodigious length off the tee with a refined all-around game to reach the pinnacle of professional golf, including a stint as the world's number one ranked player for a combined total of over 130 weeks.

Johnson turned professional in 2007 and quickly established himself as a force on the PGA Tour, winning his first title in 2008. His career trajectory culminated in two major championships and a historic performance at the 2020 Masters, cementing his place among the elite of his generation. His 2021 defection to LIV Golf reshaped discussions about the sport's future and his own legacy.

## Early Life & Background

Dustin Johnson grew up in Columbia, South Carolina, where he was a multi-sport athlete excelling in baseball and basketball in addition to golf. He attended Coastal Carolina University on a golf scholarship, where he became one of the most decorated players in program history, earning All-American honors. His combination of a 6-foot-4 frame and exceptional athleticism set him apart physically from most of his peers, foreshadowing the power game he would bring to the professional ranks.

## Rise to Prominence

Johnson turned professional in 2007 and earned his PGA Tour card almost immediately. He claimed his first Tour victory at the 2008 Turning Stone Resort Championship. His ascent was rapid: by 2010 he was a fixture among the world's top players, known for his booming drives regularly exceeding 310 yards. He rose to world number one for the first time in February 2017, a position he would reclaim multiple times over the following years.

## Key Achievements

Johnson's résumé includes two major championships. He won the 2016 U.S. Open at Oakmont Country Club by three strokes, his first major title after several agonizing near-misses. His most dominant performance came at the 2020 Masters Tournament, where he set the all-time major championship scoring record at 20-under par, winning by five strokes. He has won more than 24 PGA Tour titles and reached world number one on multiple occasions. He was a key member of multiple U.S. Ryder Cup and Presidents Cup teams, compiling one of the strongest records among American players in those competitions.

## Controversies & Criticism

Johnson's career has not been without turbulence. He received three suspensions from the PGA Tour between 2009 and 2014 related to violations of the Tour's substance abuse policy, though details were never officially confirmed. He also suffered a series of unfortunate rulings at major championships, most notably at the 2010 PGA Championship, where he was penalized a stroke for grounding his club in a bunker he was unaware of, costing him a chance at a playoff. In 2021, Johnson resigned from the PGA Tour to join the Saudi-backed LIV Golf League, drawing significant criticism from traditionalists and fellow players who viewed the move as prioritizing financial gain over competitive integrity.

## Legacy & Influence

Dustin Johnson redefined expectations for power golf at the highest level, helping usher in an era where elite driving distance became a prerequisite for major contention. His Masters record and U.S. Open title ensure his place in the sport's history regardless of the LIV controversy. He remains one of the most naturally gifted players of his generation.
