## Summary

Lee De Forest was an American inventor who held over 300 patents and is credited with inventing the Audion (triode vacuum tube) in 1906. This device was the key component that made electronic amplification possible and laid the groundwork for radio, television, radar, and early computers. He is often called the 'father of radio' and the 'grandfather of television.'
