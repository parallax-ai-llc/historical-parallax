## Summary

Kim Ok-gyun was a Korean political reformer who advocated for modernization based on Western science and technology. He led the Gapsin Coup of 1884 attempting to modernize Korea along Japanese Meiji-style lines, including scientific education reform. Though the coup failed and he was later assassinated, his ideas influenced Korean modernization.
