## Summary

Shaku Soen was a Japanese Rinzai Zen master who was the first Zen teacher to come to the United States. He represented Zen Buddhism at the 1893 World Parliament of Religions in Chicago. He was the teacher of D. T. Suzuki and instrumental in bringing Zen to the West. He was also engaged with modern social issues and participated as a chaplain in the Russo-Japanese War.
