## Summary

Carlos Chagas was a Brazilian physician and infectious disease researcher who discovered Chagas disease (American trypanosomiasis), its causative agent (Trypanosoma cruzi), and its insect vector. This is a unique case in medical history where a single researcher discovered the disease, the pathogen, and the vector. Chagas disease affects millions of people in Latin America and remains a major public health challenge.
