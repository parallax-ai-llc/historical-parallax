## Summary

Series of 52 murders committed by Andrei Chikatilo in the Rostov Oblast of the Soviet Union between 1978 and 1990. He mutilated and cannibalized victims near railway stations.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1978 | First murder |
| 1990 | Chikatilo arrested |
| 1994 | Executed |

## Geography and Modus Operandi (High Level)

- Many attacks occurred in and around Rostov-on-Don and nearby areas in the Russian SFSR.
- Victims were often approached in public areas and later attacked in secluded locations.
- The crimes produced sustained fear around transport hubs and rural wooded areas.

## Investigation

- The investigation unfolded over many years and involved large-scale policing operations.
- Investigators used surveillance and stakeouts, including attention to patterns near railway lines.
- Public sources about the case often discuss the difficulties of serial-crime investigation in the late Soviet period.

## Prosecution and Aftermath

- Chikatilo was tried and convicted in the early 1990s and later executed.
- The case is frequently cited as influential in the development of profiling and modern investigative methods in the region.
- It also remains a recurring example in discussions of investigative error, including the risk of wrongful convictions in high-pressure cases.

## Cultural Impact

- The murders have been the subject of books, documentaries, and dramatizations.
- The case shaped public understanding of serial homicide in the former Soviet Union.
