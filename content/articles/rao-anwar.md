## Background

- Rao Anwar is described in public reporting as a senior police officer who served in Karachi, including roles associated with the Malir district.
- His notoriety is primarily linked to allegations that "encounter" killings (purported shootouts with suspects) were staged.

## Notable Allegations (High-Level)

- Allegations include participation in, or command responsibility for, extrajudicial killings carried out under the pretext of police encounters.
- Media and human-rights reporting around his case often cites patterns such as: suspects killed without due process, disputed circumstances of shootouts, and post-incident narratives challenged by families and civil society.

## Legal and Institutional Proceedings

- In 2018, he was charged in relation to extrajudicial killings; the case became a high-profile test of police accountability in Pakistan.
- Proceedings involved investigations, court oversight, and public scrutiny; outcomes and statuses can change over time and should be verified against up-to-date sources.

- The Rao Anwar case is frequently referenced in discussions about:
