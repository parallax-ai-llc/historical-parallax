## Summary

Black Liberation Army member and stepfather of Tupac Shakur, convicted of armored car robbery and murder.

## Background

- **Full name**: Jeral Wayne Williams (known publicly as Mutulu Shakur).[^1]
- **Family**: Stepfather of rapper Tupac Shakur (2Pac).[^1]
- **Political milieu**: Associated in public reporting with Black liberation and revolutionary movements in the United States during the 1970s–1980s.[^1]

## Legal Case (High Level)

- Shakur was **convicted in U.S. federal court** for his role in an armed robbery case connected to a 1981 Brink's armored truck incident in New York.[^1]
- The case is commonly referenced in relation to fatalities that occurred during the robbery and subsequent events, and it became a prominent example of federal prosecution of underground political networks of that era.[^1]

## Incarceration and Health

- Shakur served a lengthy federal prison sentence following his conviction.[^1]
- Public reporting later discussed **serious health issues** near the end of his life, including cancer, and legal filings related to compassionate release requests.[^1]

## Notes on Public Legacy

- Shakur has been described as a polarizing figure: viewed by some as part of a broader liberation struggle and by others primarily through the lens of violent crime prosecutions.[^1]
- He remains widely known in popular culture largely due to the Tupac Shakur connection, which kept attention on his biography beyond activist and legal history.[^1]
