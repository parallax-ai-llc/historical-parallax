## Summary

Patrick Blackett was a British experimental physicist who won the 1948 Nobel Prize in Physics for his development of the Wilson cloud chamber method and his discoveries in nuclear physics and cosmic radiation. He confirmed the existence of the positron and made important contributions to operations research during World War II. He was also a prominent public intellectual who advised on defense and science policy.
