## Summary

Crypto lender that collapsed after Three Arrows Capital defaulted on a $650 million loan.

## What Happened (High-Level)

Voyager Digital was a retail-facing crypto brokerage and lending platform. Its collapse is commonly described as a liquidity-and-counterparty failure: a concentrated exposure to a single large borrower (3AC) combined with broader market stress made Voyager unable to meet withdrawal demand.

Key elements frequently cited in reporting and court filings include:

- **Counterparty concentration:** a major loan exposure to 3AC.
- **Market drawdown:** falling crypto prices reduced collateral values and investor confidence.
- **Run dynamics:** once withdrawals increased, Voyager lacked liquid assets to satisfy them.
- **Bankruptcy process:** customer asset recovery became dependent on auction outcomes, recoveries, and the wind-down plan.

## Aftermath and Customer Asset Recovery

Post-filing, Voyager pursued a restructuring and sale process intended to maximize recoveries for customers and other creditors. Two headline outcomes shaped the trajectory:

1. **A proposed sale to FTX US** (selected as a winning bid) that later failed due to FTX’s collapse.
2. **A proposed sale to Binance.US** that was ultimately terminated, after which Voyager proceeded with a wind-down and distributions.

Recovery for customers generally depended on the mix of remaining crypto holdings, cash, and litigation or settlement recoveries, distributed through the bankruptcy plan over time.
