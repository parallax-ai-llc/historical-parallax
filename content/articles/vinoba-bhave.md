## Summary

Vinoba Bhave was an Indian advocate of non-violence and the spiritual successor of Mahatma Gandhi. He led the Bhoodan (land-gift) movement, walking across India asking landlords to donate land to the landless poor. He translated the Bhagavad Gita into Marathi. He was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize and received the Bharat Ratna. He is considered one of the most important figures in the Gandhian tradition.
