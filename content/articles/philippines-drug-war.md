## Summary

President Duterte's drug war killed over 12,000-30,000 people through police operations and vigilante killings. ICC opened an investigation for crimes against humanity.

## Policy Framing and Rhetoric

- The campaign was presented as an emergency response to illegal drugs and associated crime.
- Public rhetoric emphasized “law and order,” while critics argued it normalized violence and undermined due process.

## Operational Features (High-Level)

- Police-led operations against suspected drug users and dealers were central.
- Commonly reported features included:
  - “watch lists” and community-level targeting,
  - raids and arrests based on intelligence tips,
  - pressure on suspects to “surrender,” and
  - deaths during encounters that authorities often described as armed resistance.

## Controversy and Human Rights Allegations

- Human rights organizations alleged widespread extrajudicial killings and patterns consistent with a campaign of violence rather than lawful policing.
- Concerns included:
  - lack of warrants or weak probable cause,
  - intimidation of witnesses and families,
  - limited transparency on case files, and
  - the disproportionate impact on poor neighborhoods.

## Political and Legal Aftermath

- The drug war became a defining issue of the Duterte presidency and a major subject of domestic political conflict.
- International scrutiny focused on whether national investigations were sufficient to preclude external jurisdiction.
- Debate persisted over the balance between public safety, civil liberties, and the rule of law.
