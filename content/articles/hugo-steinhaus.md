## Summary

Hugo Steinhaus was a Polish mathematician known for his contributions to functional analysis, mathematical logic, and applied mathematics. He co-founded the Lwów School of Mathematics and discovered Stefan Banach. His book "Mathematical Snapshots" popularized mathematics for the general public. He also developed the ham sandwich theorem and the Steinhaus theorem.
