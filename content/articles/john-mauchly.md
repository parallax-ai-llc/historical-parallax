## Summary

John Mauchly was an American physicist and computer pioneer who co-invented ENIAC with J. Presper Eckert. He conceived the idea of an electronic computer for weather prediction and collaborated with Eckert to build ENIAC during World War II. He also co-designed UNIVAC I. His vision of general-purpose electronic computing was transformative for the 20th century.
