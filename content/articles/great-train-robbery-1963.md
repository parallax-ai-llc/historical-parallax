## Summary
The Great Train Robbery was the theft of £2.6 million (equivalent to ~£55 million today) from a Royal Mail train on August 8, 1963, at Bridego Railway Bridge in Buckinghamshire. It was the largest robbery in British history at the time. Most of the gang were caught and sentenced to 30 years.
