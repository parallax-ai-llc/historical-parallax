## Summary

Mexican journalist and co-editor of the weekly newspaper Zeta in Tijuana who was assassinated for his investigations into drug cartel corruption. He was shot dead in front of his children.

## Additional Context

### Work at Zeta

- Zeta is a Tijuana-based weekly known for investigative reporting on organized crime, corruption, and public security in Baja California.
- As an editor and journalist, Ortiz Franco contributed to a reporting tradition in which the newsroom continued publishing despite repeated threats and attacks.

### Risks for journalists

- His assassination is frequently referenced in broader discussions of violence against journalists in Mexico, particularly those covering drug-trafficking organizations and state corruption.
- Such cases often involve complex attribution questions because they can intersect with political, economic, and criminal interests.

### Aftermath and legacy (high level)

- Attacks on prominent reporters can have a chilling effect on local media, while also drawing international attention to press-freedom conditions.
- Zeta and similar outlets are commonly cited as examples of high-risk journalism that nonetheless shapes public understanding of cartel influence and government responses.

### Broader context

- Northern border cities such as Tijuana have been strategic points for trafficking routes, which increases both cartel violence and the public demand for investigative coverage.
- High-profile killings of journalists are often used as indicators in international assessments of Mexico’s security environment and rule-of-law challenges.
