## Summary

Russian troll farm based in St. Petersburg that conducted social media interference in the 2016 US presidential election and other campaigns worldwide. Funded by Yevgeny Prigozhin.

## Overview and Modus Operandi

- The Internet Research Agency (IRA) is widely described as an organization involved in coordinated online influence operations.
- Reported tactics include creating false personas, operating networks of social media accounts, amplifying divisive narratives, and staging or promoting real-world events through online outreach.

## Notable Campaigns (High Level)

- The IRA is most prominently associated with interference efforts targeting U.S. political discourse around the 2016 election cycle.
- Public reporting and official statements have also linked similar methods to campaigns aimed at audiences in Europe and other regions.

## Investigations and Legal Actions

- The IRA has been referenced in U.S. government investigations into foreign interference, including criminal indictments describing coordinated social media activity.
- Attribution in influence operations is often probabilistic and evidence-based; public claims typically rely on a mix of technical indicators, intelligence assessments, and platform enforcement data.

## Broader Significance

- The IRA is frequently cited in policy debates about platform governance, information integrity, and the difficulty of distinguishing organic political speech from coordinated manipulation.
- The case has influenced how social media companies label state-linked operations and how researchers study “inauthentic behavior” at scale.
