## Summary

Assassination of Mahatma Gandhi by Hindu nationalist Nathuram Godse on January 30, 1948 at a prayer meeting in New Delhi. Gandhi was shot three times at point-blank range.

## Historical context

The assassination occurred shortly after the 1947 Partition of British India and the creation of India and Pakistan, a period marked by mass displacement and communal violence. Gandhi was publicly associated with efforts to reduce sectarian conflict and promote interreligious coexistence.

## The assassination

On 30 January 1948, Gandhi was attacked during an evening prayer gathering in New Delhi. Contemporary accounts describe the killing as a close-range shooting that immediately became a major national and international event.

## Perpetrators and motives (high level)

Nathuram Godse, identified as the assassin, was linked in reporting and historical discussion to Hindu nationalist political currents of the time. Interpretations of motive commonly cite opposition to Gandhi's positions on communal reconciliation and the political settlement after Partition.

## Investigation and trial

Indian authorities pursued an investigation that led to the prosecution of multiple individuals for conspiracy and participation. The ensuing trial and appeals are often cited in discussions of early post-independence legal institutions and political violence.

## Aftermath and legacy

Gandhi's death intensified debates about extremism, national identity, and the boundaries of political disagreement in the new republic. Memorialization of Gandhi and periodic public discussion of the assassination remain part of India's civic and political life.
