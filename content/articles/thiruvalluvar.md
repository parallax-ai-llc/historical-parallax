## Summary

Thiruvalluvar was a Tamil poet and philosopher who authored the Thirukkural, one of the most celebrated works of Tamil literature and ethics. The Thirukkural consists of 1,330 couplets organized into three sections: virtue, wealth, and love. It is a secular ethical text that transcends religious boundaries and has been translated into over 40 languages. It is sometimes called the 'Universal Veda' for its comprehensive moral teachings.

## The Thirukkural (Work Overview)

- The text is written in **kural venba** meter: compact two-line couplets designed for memorability.
- It is organized into three broad books:
  - **Aram (Virtue):** ethics, character, self-discipline, and social responsibility.
  - **Porul (Wealth/Polity):** governance, leadership, economics, and public life.
  - **Inbam (Love):** human relationships, affection, and emotional life.
- The couplet form and non-sectarian framing helped the work travel across regions, languages, and religious communities.

## Themes and Ideas (High-Level)

- **Personal ethics:** truthfulness, restraint, compassion, and avoidance of harm.
- **Social virtue:** hospitality, friendship, gratitude, and justice.
- **Statecraft:** qualities of rulers, counsel, diplomacy, and the administration of law.
- **Universalism:** moral reasoning is presented without requiring adherence to a single theological system.

## Biography and Historicity

- Very little is known with certainty about Thiruvalluvar’s life.
- Traditional accounts vary, and proposed dates span many centuries.
- Modern scholarship typically treats the biography as partly legendary and focuses on the text’s language, themes, and reception history.

## Reception and Influence

- The Thirukkural is a foundational text in Tamil cultural life and education, frequently quoted for moral instruction.
- It has influenced writers, reformers, and public discourse in South India and the Tamil diaspora.
- Extensive translation efforts—into Indian and global languages—reflect its perceived applicability beyond its original context.

## Commemoration

- Thiruvalluvar is widely commemorated in Tamil Nadu through statues, festivals, and civic naming (institutions, roads, and public programs).
- The author’s name and the Thirukkural’s couplets are commonly used in speeches and popular media as ethical aphorisms.
