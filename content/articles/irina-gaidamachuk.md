## Summary

Russian serial killer who murdered 17 elderly women in Sverdlovsk Oblast between 2002 and 2010. She bludgeoned victims with a hammer to steal money for alcohol.

## Background

- The murders occurred in Sverdlovsk Oblast in Russia over an extended period, making patterns difficult to detect early.
- The victims were typically elderly women, often targeted in their homes.

## Method and Pattern

- Reports describe blunt-force attacks, including the use of a hammer.
- The motive was described as robbery, with small sums taken to finance alcohol use.
- The repeated targeting of a vulnerable demographic contributed to public fear in affected communities.

## Investigation and Arrest

- Because the crimes spanned years and involved similar victim profiles, investigators worked to connect cases and identify consistent forensic markers.
- She was arrested in 2010 after law enforcement linked her to multiple incidents.

## Trial and Sentence

- In 2012 she received a 20-year prison sentence.
- Coverage of the case often highlights the unusually high number of victims attributed to a single offender in the region during that period.

## Notes

- Case summaries often emphasize the prolonged timeframe (2002–2010), which allowed the offender to evade detection across multiple incidents.
- The victims’ age profile is frequently cited as a factor in both the vulnerability of targets and the community impact.
