## Summary

Nyanaponika Thera was a German-born Theravada Buddhist monk who became one of the most important Western-born Buddhist scholars of the 20th century. His book The Heart of Buddhist Meditation was one of the first comprehensive presentations of satipatthana (mindfulness) practice for Western audiences. He co-founded the Buddhist Publication Society in Kandy, Sri Lanka. His work helped lay the foundation for the modern mindfulness movement.
