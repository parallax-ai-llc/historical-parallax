## Summary

Kary Mullis was an American biochemist who won the 1993 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his invention of the polymerase chain reaction (PCR) technique. PCR enables rapid amplification of specific DNA segments and has become one of the most important tools in molecular biology, forensics, medical diagnostics, and genetic research. The technique was essential for DNA fingerprinting, genetic testing, and COVID-19 testing.
