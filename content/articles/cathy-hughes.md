## Summary

Cathy Hughes founded Radio One, the largest African American-owned radio company in the United States, and TV One, a cable television network targeting African Americans. She was the first African American woman to head a publicly traded corporation when Radio One went public in 1999. Starting with a single struggling radio station in Washington, D.C., she built a media empire that gave voice to the African American community.
