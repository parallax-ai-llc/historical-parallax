## Summary

Forced disappearance of 43 students from the Ayotzinapa Rural Teachers College in Iguala, Guerrero on September 26, 2014 by police and cartel members. The case became Mexicos worst human rights crisis.

## Overview

- The Ayotzinapa case refers to the 2014 disappearance of 43 students from the Rural Teachers' College of Ayotzinapa in Guerrero, Mexico.
- The event triggered nationwide and international protests, focusing on impunity, corruption, and violence.

## Key Issues

- Investigations involved allegations of collusion between local officials, police, and organized crime.
- The case became a reference point for debates about the credibility of official narratives and the need for independent inquiries.

## Social and Political Impact

- The disappearances intensified scrutiny of security policy and human-rights conditions in Mexico.
- Families of the students and civil-society groups remained central actors, pressing for truth, accountability, and identification of remains.

## Legacy

- Ayotzinapa is frequently invoked as a symbol of forced disappearances and the challenges of criminal justice reform.
- Ongoing investigations and political commitments have been closely watched both domestically and abroad.
