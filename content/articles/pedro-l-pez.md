## Summary

Colombian serial killer known as the 'Monster of the Andes' who confessed to murdering over 300 girls across Colombia, Ecuador, and Peru. Released from an Ecuadorian psychiatric hospital in 1998, his whereabouts are unknown.

## Background and Modus Operandi

- López is reported to have targeted young girls, often approaching them in public places and luring them away.
- Many accounts describe a pattern of traveling across multiple countries in the Andean region, which complicated investigations and record-keeping.
- Public claims about victim totals vary widely; media summaries often cite his confessions, while confirmed cases are lower.

## Arrest, Conviction, and Release

- He was arrested in Ecuador after authorities connected him to multiple killings and burial sites.
- Court outcomes and institutional handling are frequently summarized as involving psychiatric evaluation and treatment rather than long-term incarceration.
- Reports state he was released in 1998; subsequent sightings and re-arrests have been claimed in some outlets, but his long-term whereabouts remain unclear.

## Legacy and Notes on Sources

- The case is often cited in discussions of cross-border policing and the reliability of confession-based victim counts.
- Because documentation spans multiple jurisdictions and time periods, secondary sources may disagree on dates, confirmed victims, and legal details.
