## Summary

Arieh Warshel is an Israeli-American biochemist who shared the 2013 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for developing multiscale models using QM/MM methods.

## Key Contributions

- **QM/MM methodology**: Warshel is widely associated with advancing hybrid quantum mechanics/molecular mechanics (QM/MM) approaches that allow chemically accurate modeling of reactions within large biomolecules.
- **Computational enzymology**: His work helped make it practical to study how enzymes catalyze reactions by combining physics-based models with realistic protein environments.
- **Modeling in chemistry and biology**: The broader impact of his research is the normalization of computer simulation as a core tool alongside experiment for understanding molecular function.

## Context

The 2013 Nobel Prize in Chemistry recognized methods for building multiscale models for complex chemical systems, enabling researchers to connect electronic structure calculations with larger-scale molecular behavior.

## Legacy and Impact

- Influenced how molecular simulations are used in drug discovery, enzymatic mechanism studies, and materials-related chemistry.
- Helped establish computational chemistry as an essential discipline for explaining and predicting molecular phenomena.

## Related Topics

- **Multiscale modeling**: linking fine-grained electronic models to coarse-grained molecular motion.
- **Enzyme reaction mechanisms**: explaining rate enhancement via electrostatics, dynamics, and transition-state stabilization.
- **Simulation validation**: using experimental observables (rates, structures, thermodynamics) to test and calibrate computational models.
