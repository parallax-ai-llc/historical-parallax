## Summary

Henry Purcell was the greatest English composer of the Baroque era. His opera Dido and Aeneas and his semi-operas including The Fairy-Queen combined Italian and French influences with a distinctly English sensibility. His music for the stage, church, and court represents the pinnacle of English Baroque.
