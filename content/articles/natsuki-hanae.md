## Summary

Natsuki Hanae (花江夏樹) is a Japanese voice actor born on July 28, 1993, in Tokyo, Japan. Represented by INT, he rose to national prominence in 2014 with his breakthrough role as Kaneki Ken in *Tokyo Ghoul*, a performance that showcased his remarkable range from timid vulnerability to anguished intensity. He has since become one of the most in-demand voice actors of his generation.

Hanae is particularly celebrated for his ability to convey emotional depth and psychological complexity, lending authenticity to protagonists who undergo dramatic transformations. His warm, expressive vocal quality has made him a staple in major anime productions throughout the 2010s and 2020s. He won the Best Actor in a Leading Role award at the 9th Seiyu Awards in 2015, cementing his status as a leading figure in the industry.

Beyond performance, Hanae is known for his approachable public persona and active engagement with fans through variety content and events, making him one of the most recognized voices in contemporary anime culture.

## Early Life & Background

Hanae was born and raised in Tokyo. He developed an interest in voice acting during his school years, inspired by anime he watched growing up. He enrolled at the Tokyo School of Anime (東京アニメ・声優専門学校) to pursue formal training in voice performance. His academic focus on craft and technique laid the groundwork for the emotional precision that would define his later career.

## Rise to Prominence

Hanae made his professional debut around 2012, taking on minor roles in anime productions. His career trajectory shifted dramatically in 2014 when he was cast as Kaneki Ken in *Tokyo Ghoul*. The role required him to portray a character's psychological unraveling across two tonal extremes — a challenge that earned him widespread critical recognition. That same year, he also voiced Kousei Arima in *Your Lie in April* (*Shigatsu wa Kimi no Uso*), a deeply emotional role that further demonstrated his versatility. Landing two culturally significant leads in a single year was a rare achievement and firmly established his name within the industry.

## Key Achievements

- **Seiyu Award (2015):** Won Best Actor in a Leading Role at the 9th Seiyu Awards, one of the most prestigious honors in Japanese voice acting.
- **Kaneki Ken** (*Tokyo Ghoul*, 2014–2018): His defining performance across multiple seasons and the *Tokyo Ghoul:re* continuation.
- **Kousei Arima** (*Your Lie in April*, 2014): A role praised for its sensitive portrayal of grief and artistic passion.
- **Tanjiro Kamado** (*Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba*, 2019–present): His most globally recognized role, voicing the lead protagonist in one of the best-selling manga and highest-grossing anime franchises in history.
- He has appeared in numerous other productions including *Cardcaptor Sakura: Clear Card*, *We Never Learn*, and *Fruits Basket* (2019 remake).

## Controversies & Criticism

Hanae has maintained a largely controversy-free public profile. Some fan discourse has emerged around casting choices in high-profile sequels, though such discussions are common across the industry and have not reflected negatively on Hanae personally. No significant professional or personal controversies are on record.

## Legacy & Influence

Hanae's career represents a defining arc in 2010s anime voice acting — a performer who came of age alongside a new generation of globally distributed anime and helped shape the emotional register audiences came to expect from leading male protagonists. His work on *Demon Slayer* in particular has reached audiences worldwide, making him one of the few Japanese voice actors with genuine international name recognition. Younger voice actors frequently cite his emotional naturalism as an influence, and his continued output ensures his legacy remains active rather than retrospective.
