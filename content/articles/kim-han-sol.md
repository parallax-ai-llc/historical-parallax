## Summary

Kim Han-sol is the elder son of the late Kim Jong-nam, the eldest son of former North Korean leader Kim Jong-il, making him a grandson of Kim Jong-il, a nephew of current leader Kim Jong-un, and a member of the extended Kim family with potential claim to leadership succession. Born in Pyongyang in 1995 but largely raised abroad, he became one of the most publicly visible members of the Kim family's younger generation through interviews, social media presence, and his education at United World College in Mostar and Sciences Po in Paris. Following the February 2017 assassination of his father at Kuala Lumpur International Airport by North Korean agents, Kim Han-sol went into hiding with his mother and sister, reportedly under the protection of a shadowy dissident organization known as Cheollima Civil Defense (later rebranded as Free Joseon). As one of the few Kim family members to live publicly outside North Korea and to indirectly criticize the regime, he represents both a potential alternative to Kim Jong-un's leadership and a continuing object of North Korean state interest.

## Early Life and Background

Kim Han-sol was born on June 16, 1995, in Pyongyang, North Korea, the first son of Kim Jong-nam and his second wife Lee Hye-kyung[^1]. His father Kim Jong-nam was the eldest son of Kim Jong-il and was at the time of Han-sol's birth still considered a leading candidate to eventually succeed his father as leader of North Korea[^2]. His mother Lee Hye-kyung, reportedly a former ethnic Korean actress from Japan, was one of Kim Jong-nam's several romantic partners during a period of personal and political complexity[^3].

His early years were spent in the unusual circumstances of the Kim family's internal politics, with his father Kim Jong-nam spending substantial time outside North Korea, particularly in Macau, China, and other locations[^4]. Kim Jong-nam's infamous 2001 incident, when he was caught attempting to enter Japan on a fake Dominican passport to visit Tokyo Disneyland, reportedly with his wife and his then-six-year-old son Kim Han-sol, contributed to his fall from favor as heir apparent[^5]. This incident marked the beginning of Kim Jong-nam's effective exile from the North Korean succession race and had significant consequences for Kim Han-sol's subsequent life.

Kim Han-sol grew up primarily in Macau, where the family maintained residence under Chinese protection[^6]. This childhood in a cosmopolitan city provided him with experiences radically different from those of any Kim family member who had stayed in North Korea, including exposure to foreign cultures, open information sources, international friendships, and travel[^7]. He reportedly attended international schools in Macau and received an education that combined Chinese, English, and possibly Portuguese language instruction, giving him linguistic capabilities unusual for any member of the Kim family.

His father Kim Jong-nam's relationship with Chinese authorities, including reported connections to Chinese intelligence services, provided the family with protection and stability during Kim Han-sol's childhood[^8]. At the same time, his father's increasingly public criticism of the North Korean regime after Kim Jong-un's succession in 2011 created tension that ultimately culminated in the 2017 assassination[^9]. Kim Han-sol grew up witnessing his father's gradual transformation from favored heir to alienated exile, an experience that reportedly affected his own perspectives on North Korean politics.

Kim Han-sol's international education continued when he was accepted to United World College in Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina, a two-year international secondary school program, which he attended from 2011 to 2013[^10]. His admission to this prestigious international institution, which brings together students from around the world, represented an extraordinary step for any member of the Kim family and exposed him to classmates from dozens of countries including the United States, Israel, and various African and Asian states[^11]. This period in Bosnia, in a country with its own complex post-communist history, proved formative for his personal and political development.

## Rise to Public Visibility

Kim Han-sol's public emergence occurred during his time at United World College in Mostar, when he gave a remarkable October 2012 interview to former Finnish diplomat Elisabeth Rehn that was broadcast on Finnish television[^12]. In this interview, Kim Han-sol spoke about his dream of "contributing to peace" in his country, discussed his interest in promoting reunification of the Korean peninsula, and expressed sympathy for ordinary North Koreans' suffering[^13]. While he was careful not to directly criticize his uncle Kim Jong-un or the North Korean regime, his tone was sympathetic to the people rather than the leadership and implicitly distinguished his perspective from that of the ruling family.

The interview went viral internationally, making Kim Han-sol the most publicly visible member of the younger Kim family generation and establishing him as a figure of international fascination[^14]. His articulate English, international perspective, discussion of his Korean identity, and evident intelligence contrasted sharply with the typical presentation of North Korean leadership and suggested possibilities for a different kind of Kim family member[^15]. Foreign commentators speculated extensively about whether he might eventually play a political role, serve as a symbol for alternative Korean leadership, or continue to live as a private citizen.

During his time at United World College, Kim Han-sol maintained a relatively active Facebook profile with photos of his life in Bosnia, his studies, and his friendships with classmates from various countries[^16]. This social media presence, unprecedented for a member of the North Korean ruling family, provided glimpses into a young person navigating ordinary adolescent experiences while bearing an extraordinary family legacy[^17]. His Facebook activities, while never directly political, demonstrated an engagement with the broader world that was fundamentally incompatible with North Korean political norms.

After completing his International Baccalaureate at United World College, Kim Han-sol was accepted to Sciences Po, one of France's most prestigious universities specializing in political science and international affairs[^18]. He began studies at the Sciences Po campus in Le Havre, Normandy, in 2013, continuing his unusual educational trajectory of international academic institutions[^19]. His studies at Sciences Po, combining French-language academic work with international political analysis, provided further preparation for potential future roles in international affairs or dissident politics, though his actual plans remained private.

His time at Sciences Po reportedly included academic success, continued international friendships, and increasing political consciousness about the situation in his country of origin[^20]. He maintained a relatively low profile during these years compared to his initial viral moment, focusing on his studies and apparently avoiding the political spotlight that his early interview had briefly placed him in[^21]. French intelligence services reportedly provided some protection during his studies in France, recognizing the potential security concerns given his family background.

## Major Turning Points

The February 13, 2017 assassination of Kim Han-sol's father Kim Jong-nam at Kuala Lumpur International Airport fundamentally transformed his life and public position[^22]. Kim Jong-nam was killed by VX nerve agent applied to his face by two women—a Vietnamese and an Indonesian—who were apparently deceived into believing they were participating in a prank video, though evidence showed North Korean agents coordinated the operation[^23]. The assassination, conducted in one of the most public possible locations in broad daylight, sent a clear message that the North Korean regime considered Kim Jong-nam and potentially his family to be legitimate targets for elimination.

Within weeks of his father's assassination, Kim Han-sol appeared in a video released on YouTube by an organization calling itself Cheollima Civil Defense (later rebranded as Free Joseon)[^24]. In the brief video, Kim Han-sol confirmed his identity, expressed his hope that his family would be safe, and thanked those who had helped them[^25]. The video, which went viral globally, represented the first confirmation that Kim Han-sol, his mother, and his sister had survived and were in hiding, apparently under the protection of this mysterious organization.

Cheollima Civil Defense, later Free Joseon, has remained a shadowy organization that claims to be a provisional government in exile opposing the Kim Jong-un regime[^26]. Its 2019 raid on the North Korean embassy in Madrid, during which documents and computers were stolen, made it an object of international attention and concern[^27]. The FBI has investigated members of the organization, and its connections to intelligence services of various countries remain speculative[^28]. Kim Han-sol's relationship with this organization, whether as protected subject or active participant, has not been publicly clarified.

Following his public appearance in the Cheollima video, Kim Han-sol has effectively disappeared from public view[^29]. His location has been kept secret by whatever protective arrangements exist, and he has not given interviews, maintained social media, or made public statements in the years since 2017[^30]. His apparent move into witness-protection-style arrangements, possibly with support from the CIA or other intelligence services, reflects the serious threat he continues to face from North Korean operatives[^31]. Whether he will ever reemerge publicly, and under what circumstances, remains entirely uncertain.

Reports emerging periodically from various intelligence and journalistic sources have placed Kim Han-sol at different times in the United States, Taiwan, the Netherlands, and other locations[^32]. These reports, which are typically denied or refused comment by relevant authorities, reflect continuing international interest in his welfare and potential political significance[^33]. The practical challenges of protecting a publicly identified target of North Korean intelligence agencies have required elaborate security arrangements whose details remain classified.

The political significance of Kim Han-sol has grown rather than diminished in the years since his father's assassination, despite his absence from public view[^34]. As the eldest son of the eldest son of Kim Jong-il, he carries bloodline credentials that some analysts believe could potentially position him as a successor or alternative leader in the event of regime change or internal crisis[^35]. The fact that the North Korean regime reportedly continues to consider him a potential threat—with ongoing reports of assassination plots—supports the assessment that his symbolic significance remains substantial.

## Major Achievements

Kim Han-sol's most significant achievement has been his successful navigation of the extraordinarily difficult circumstances created by his family position, including surviving the assassination of his father and maintaining his education and personal development despite constant security threats[^36]. His completion of the International Baccalaureate at United World College and his studies at Sciences Po represent real academic accomplishments that required sustained effort under unusual pressures[^37]. These educational achievements distinguish him from other Kim family members and demonstrate genuine personal capability beyond mere bloodline status.

His October 2012 interview with Elisabeth Rehn, in which he spoke articulately about peace, reunification, and his hopes for the Korean people, represented a significant intervention in international perception of the Kim family[^38]. The interview showed that the Kim family could produce members capable of humane, thoughtful public engagement, which challenged dominant narratives about the fundamental incompatibility of Kim bloodlines with Korean reconciliation[^39]. Whatever his subsequent trajectory, this interview provided an alternative image of Kim family possibilities that has influenced how international observers think about North Korean futures.

His maintenance of personal relationships, academic commitments, and ordinary adolescent activities during his time at United World College and Sciences Po demonstrated a human normalcy that contrasts starkly with the regime his uncle leads[^40]. The photos he shared on Facebook, his friendships with classmates from various countries, and his evident enjoyment of ordinary experiences (ski trips, sports, parties, academic projects) showed a young person determined to have a normal life despite the absurdity of his family position[^41]. This capacity for normalcy represents a personal achievement that has symbolic significance beyond his specific circumstances.

His survival of what appears to have been serious North Korean assassination plots represents ongoing achievement in the face of persistent threat[^42]. The 2017 assassination of his father demonstrated that North Korean intelligence services are capable of global operations against identified targets, and Kim Han-sol's continued survival—with his mother and sister—requires both his own caution and significant protective arrangements from sympathetic parties[^43]. His persistence as a living alternative Kim family member, despite these threats, represents a form of survival achievement.

The video he recorded with Cheollima Civil Defense in 2017 provided confirmation of his survival and signaled continued resistance to North Korean pressure[^44]. While brief and carefully worded, the video served to establish his ongoing existence in the international public consciousness and prevented North Korean propaganda from simply erasing him[^45]. The decision to make this public statement, despite its risks, represented a choice to maintain his public existence as a continuing possibility.

Kim Han-sol's symbolic significance as a Kim family member who lives outside North Korea, has received an international education, and implicitly represents an alternative to Kim Jong-un's leadership, has influenced international policy discussions about post-Kim scenarios[^46]. While he has made no apparent moves toward claiming political leadership, his mere existence provides a potential alternative that shapes how analysts and policymakers consider possible futures for North Korea[^47]. This influence, though passive, represents substantive political significance.

## Controversies and Criticisms

Kim Han-sol's life has been relatively free of personal controversy, largely because he has maintained a private existence for the past several years. However, his position raises important questions about bloodline politics, asylum practices, and the politics of regime change that involve complicated moral terrain[^48]. His continued reliance on bloodline credentials for his international profile, even while living outside North Korea, illustrates the persistence of dynastic thinking in Korean politics.

Critics have noted that Kim Han-sol's symbolic significance, and the international attention he receives, stems entirely from his family background rather than any personal accomplishments or political contributions[^49]. While his individual academic achievements are real, the international fascination with him reflects the same dynastic logic that sustains the North Korean regime—treating political legitimacy as transmissible through bloodline rather than arising from democratic selection or meritocratic achievement[^50]. Focusing on him as a potential alternative leader may inadvertently validate the dynastic principle that democratic Korea should ultimately reject.

His association with Cheollima Civil Defense/Free Joseon has raised questions about the organization's legitimacy, funding sources, and operational conduct[^51]. The 2019 raid on the North Korean embassy in Madrid, which some characterized as a theft operation conducted by activists, others as a hostile action against North Korean diplomatic premises, created legal and political complications[^52]. Kim Han-sol's association with an organization that conducts such operations, while he has apparently not been involved directly, raises questions about the methods used by his protectors.

The circumstances of his father Kim Jong-nam's life, which shaped Kim Han-sol's childhood, involved extensive privilege obtained from the North Korean regime through corrupt means[^53]. The family's Macau residence, international travel, and lifestyle were funded by illicit activities of the North Korean regime, including counterfeiting, drug trafficking, and other criminal enterprises[^54]. While Kim Han-sol himself was a child who had no choice in these matters, the ultimate source of his childhood privilege was the same regime he is now seen as opposing.

His mother Lee Hye-kyung's background, including her prior marriage history and relationships before her connection to Kim Jong-nam, has been subject to various reports of differing accuracy[^55]. The complexities of his father's multiple romantic relationships—Kim Jong-nam had several children with different mothers—create potential family complications that have affected Kim Han-sol's position in various ways[^56]. These private matters have occasionally surfaced in media coverage, reflecting the continued fascination with Kim family personal affairs.

The question of his potential political role, and whether he or his supporters intend for him eventually to play a leadership position, remains contentious[^57]. If Kim Han-sol or those around him harbor genuine political ambitions, his apparent passivity since 2017 would represent either strategic patience or practical inability to act[^58]. If, on the other hand, he genuinely seeks only private life, then the international attention he receives and the organizational structures built around him may be imposing expectations he has not chosen.

The ethics of intelligence agency protection of political figures, while not uncommon in the history of Cold War dissidents and defectors, raises questions about the ongoing operations and their potential effects[^59]. If American, South Korean, or other intelligence services are actively protecting Kim Han-sol while considering potential future uses of his symbolic status, this represents a form of patient cultivation of a potential asset that reflects Cold War-era practices[^60]. The ethics of such protection, and its potential effects on eventual Korean politics, deserve scrutiny.

The security situation creating his isolation, while not his fault, has imposed severe personal costs on him and his family[^61]. Living without public identity, unable to pursue normal academic or professional opportunities, cut off from ordinary social connections—these represent serious personal costs imposed by the choices of others (including his own father, Kim Jong-un, and various international actors)[^62]. Whether any future public role will compensate for these costs depends on entirely unpredictable future developments.

## Personal Life

Kim Han-sol's personal life has been hidden from public view since his brief 2017 video appearance, making detailed information about his current circumstances impossible to verify[^63]. He lives with his mother Lee Hye-kyung and his younger sister, whose name has been kept private, under protective arrangements whose details are not publicly disclosed[^64]. The family's location has been reported variously as the United States, Taiwan, the Netherlands, and other sites, though none of these reports have been officially confirmed.

His reported relationship with his younger sister—whose safety he explicitly mentioned in his 2017 video—appears to have been close, reflecting their shared experience of extraordinary family circumstances[^65]. Whether his sister has pursued education or other activities is unknown, as is whether she too is considered a potential political figure by various observers[^66]. The protection extended to the family includes her welfare, suggesting continued concern about threats to her personal safety.

His mother Lee Hye-kyung has lived under the extreme stress of her husband's assassination and the subsequent ongoing security situation[^67]. Her specific role in coordinating the family's protection, making decisions about their location and activities, and providing psychological support during this period has not been publicly documented but would have been essential for their survival and stability[^68]. Her personal resilience in these circumstances represents significant accomplishment.

Kim Han-sol's relationship with his half-siblings from his father's other romantic relationships is unclear[^69]. Kim Jong-nam reportedly had multiple children with different partners, and whether Kim Han-sol has maintained contact with these half-siblings or their mothers has not been publicly documented[^70]. The complicated family dynamics resulting from Kim Jong-nam's personal life have added to the challenges faced by all his descendants.

His educational interests, as expressed in his earlier public presence, included international relations, peace and conflict studies, and the potential for Korean reconciliation[^71]. Whether he has continued academic work through remote or alternative means during his period of hiding is unknown[^72]. The intellectual interests he displayed before 2017 suggested genuine engagement with questions about his country of origin rather than merely personal survival concerns.

His personality, as glimpsed through his earlier public appearances, showed a thoughtful, articulate young man with a sophisticated worldview and apparent sincerity about his values[^73]. His interviews and social media presence before 2017 displayed intelligence, sensitivity, and commitment to humane values in ways that seemed genuine rather than performative[^74]. Whether these characteristics have been preserved or altered by the traumatic experiences of the past several years cannot be known.

The psychological impact of his father's assassination, followed by years of hiding, must have been severe[^75]. Research on the psychological effects of prolonged security protection on public figures suggests substantial challenges including isolation, identity questions, depression, and anxiety[^76]. How Kim Han-sol has coped with these pressures, and whether he has had access to appropriate psychological support, remains unknown but would have been essential for his continued functioning.

His current health status, whether he has maintained educational or professional pursuits, and how he spends his days are all unknown[^77]. The deliberate opacity of his current life, essential for his security, means that basic questions about his daily experience cannot be answered[^78]. Whether he experiences himself as living a meaningful life or merely existing under constraint is a private question whose answer depends on psychological factors inaccessible to external observers.

## Legacy

Kim Han-sol's legacy, even at his young age, is substantial and continues to develop through his symbolic significance even during his period of hiding[^79]. His mere existence as a member of the Kim family who lives outside North Korea, received an international education, and appears to hold different values from the current regime has fundamentally affected international perceptions of potential North Korean futures. Whether he eventually returns to public life in any capacity or remains permanently hidden, his significance is already established.

His precedent of a Kim family member pursuing international education, public presence, and implicit distancing from the regime's ideology has established possibilities that did not previously exist in contemporary Korean politics[^80]. Future members of the Kim family, should any emerge into potential political roles, will inevitably be compared to his example and will either continue or reject the patterns he established[^81]. This precedential significance affects the range of possible Kim family trajectories beyond his own specific fate.

The symbolic existence of Kim Han-sol has influenced international policy discussions about regime change scenarios in North Korea[^82]. Analysts considering various futures—including collapse, reform, or negotiated transition—have factored in the possibility of Kim Han-sol or his descendants potentially playing roles in post-Kim Jong-un governance[^83]. While these discussions are speculative, they affect how international actors think about long-term strategy toward North Korea.

For Korean reunification questions, Kim Han-sol's international exposure, multilingual capabilities, and apparent openness to engagement with non-Korean perspectives represent potential assets for any future reconciliation process[^84]. If his development has continued during his years of hiding, he may possess unique qualifications as a potential bridge figure in eventual North-South dialogue[^85]. Whether he will ever be positioned to play such a role depends on political developments impossible to predict.

His family tragedy—the assassination of his father, the destruction of his public life, the years of hiding—has become one of the most dramatic narratives of 21st-century Korean politics[^86]. The story has been discussed in journalism, academic work, and potentially fiction as an exemplar of the human costs of authoritarian politics[^87]. Future historical treatments of the Kim Jong-un era will inevitably include his story as an important element of how the current period is remembered.

For human rights advocacy regarding North Korea, Kim Han-sol represents both a specific concern and a broader symbol of the regime's willingness to target even members of the extended ruling family[^88]. His father's assassination demonstrated that proximity to the ruling bloodline does not guarantee protection if one falls out of favor, and Kim Han-sol's continued security concerns illustrate the ongoing threat[^89]. His case has been cited in various human rights reports and advocacy efforts as illustrating the regime's pervasive violence.

The long-term implications of his hidden existence for his personal development and potential future contributions remain unpredictable[^90]. He could eventually emerge to play a public role in Korean politics, could continue in private life indefinitely, could die of natural or unnatural causes, or could find other paths his family and protectors have not anticipated[^91]. His multiple possible futures create an unusual form of legacy-in-potential that both observers and interested parties continue to watch.

The ethical questions raised by his situation—about bloodline politics, intelligence agency cultivation of potential assets, the ethics of regime change planning, and the moral status of Kim family members—will continue to be discussed in academic, policy, and human rights contexts[^92]. His case does not lend itself to simple conclusions and will likely be cited in various arguments about related questions for years to come. This conceptual legacy, though diffuse, represents a substantive contribution to how we think about these matters.

## Historical Assessment

### Positive Views

Supporters and sympathetic observers of Kim Han-sol, including many South Korean liberals, international human rights advocates, and those who have personally known him, emphasize his apparent personal qualities, his educational achievements, and his potential to represent a more humane future for Korean politics[^93]. From this perspective, he represents the possibility that the Kim family's political tragedy could yet produce something redemptive—a descendant who rejects the family's worst legacies while retaining the cultural and historical understanding to potentially contribute to Korean reconciliation.

Proponents argue that Kim Han-sol's 2012 interview revealed a young person with genuine commitment to peace, reunification, and the welfare of ordinary Koreans on both sides of the DMZ[^94]. His articulate expressions of these values, at an age when most people are focused on more immediate concerns, suggested a seriousness of purpose that could potentially be channeled into constructive activities[^95]. His education at international institutions, including United World College and Sciences Po, has prepared him for substantive engagement with complex political questions.

Those who knew him personally during his educational years in Bosnia and France have reportedly described him as kind, thoughtful, and committed to humane values[^96]. His classmates and teachers described an intelligent, curious student who engaged genuinely with his education and developed authentic relationships across cultural boundaries[^97]. These personal qualities, developed during his formative years, suggest real capacity for constructive contributions to Korean and international affairs.

Some analysts have praised the strategic wisdom of his decision to remain in hiding rather than attempting immediate political activity[^98]. By preserving his safety and avoiding premature political commitments, he has maintained his potential for future roles while not compromising the welfare of his mother and sister[^99]. This patient approach, contrasting with the attention-seeking behavior of some political figures, may represent wise long-term strategy.

The symbolic existence of Kim Han-sol as a possible alternative Kim family member has been viewed positively by those who hope for peaceful Korean change[^100]. Rather than requiring regime collapse or violent transition, his existence suggests the possibility of eventual evolution that could include bloodline continuity with different political values[^101]. While this "dynastic reform" possibility has obvious limitations, it offers imaginative alternatives to purely violent transition scenarios.

Korean conservative opponents of the Kim regime have sometimes considered Kim Han-sol as a potential asset for eventual regime change efforts[^102]. His combination of bloodline legitimacy (from the North Korean perspective), international education, and evident moderation could potentially be deployed in various scenarios to facilitate transition[^103]. Whether such potential use would materialize, and whether it would be welcome by Kim Han-sol himself, remains uncertain, but the potential has been recognized.

---

### Negative Views

Critics of focus on Kim Han-sol, including both North Korean regime supporters and democratic Koreans who reject dynastic politics entirely, argue that attention to him reflects the same illegitimate bloodline thinking that sustains the North Korean regime[^104]. From this perspective, treating any Kim family member as politically significant—whether the current leader or a potential alternative—validates the fundamental principle that legitimate political leadership can be transmitted through bloodline, which democratic politics must reject.

Critics emphasize that Kim Han-sol's personal qualities, while reportedly admirable, have not been tested by actual political responsibility or public accountability[^105]. Whatever values he expressed at age 17 in a single interview, the actual practice of political leadership involves compromises, difficult decisions, and challenges that cannot be predicted from youthful statements[^106]. Investing in him as a potential leader based on his youthful presentation repeats the historical error of placing hopes in politically untested figures based on their bloodline.

His association with Cheollima Civil Defense/Free Joseon has raised concerns about the organization's methods and motivations[^107]. The February 2019 raid on the North Korean embassy in Madrid, during which documents and computers were removed, represented a violation of diplomatic premises that, regardless of the target, raises concerns about the rule of law[^108]. Kim Han-sol's apparent alignment with an organization engaged in such operations complicates any claim to represent peaceful alternatives to the Kim Jong-un regime.

The resources devoted to his protection and potential cultivation, whatever their specific sources, represent investments in bloodline politics that could alternatively support genuine democratic development[^109]. If intelligence services or other actors are expending significant resources on protecting him as a potential asset, those resources could arguably be better used supporting democratic activists, defectors, or civil society organizations inside and outside North Korea[^110]. The opportunity cost of focusing on a single Kim family member may have costs that are not adequately considered.

Some critics have questioned whether Kim Han-sol's background has genuinely prepared him for the complexities of Korean politics[^111]. His upbringing in Macau, his education in Bosnia and France, and his apparent separation from actual North Korean society raise questions about how well he understands the country he might someday be expected to help lead or reform[^112]. The gap between his international cosmopolitanism and the realities of North Korean society may be substantial.

From a democratic perspective, any political role that Kim Han-sol might eventually play would require clear distancing from his bloodline claim and establishment of democratic credentials through accountable political activity[^113]. Simply being the grandson of Kim Jong-il does not qualify him for any legitimate political role, and any assumption that his family connection creates political entitlements contradicts democratic principles[^114]. If he wishes to contribute to Korean politics, he should establish himself through accountable political engagement rather than through bloodline symbolism.

The psychological costs imposed on Kim Han-sol by the choices of others—his father's risky defection and criticism of the regime, his uncle's decision to have his father assassinated, the involvement of intelligence services in his protection and potential cultivation—raise ethical questions about how he has been used by various parties[^115]. A young man's life has been substantially determined by political games he did not choose, and whether any potential future political role would compensate for these costs deserves careful consideration[^116]. The ethics of using individuals as potential political assets, even when their protection is justified by genuine security threats, involves complicated tradeoffs.
