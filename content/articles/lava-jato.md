## What It Was

Lava Jato ("Operation Car Wash") was a Brazilian federal investigation and prosecution effort centered on corruption, bid-rigging, and money laundering tied to state-controlled oil company Petrobras, major contractors, and political financing networks. It became one of the largest and most consequential anti-corruption cases in Brazil’s modern history.

## Methods and Legal Tools (High Level)

- **Plea bargains (delação premiada):** cooperation agreements used to map networks and recover funds.
- **Financial tracing:** following shell companies, kickback pipelines, and offshore routes.
- **Coordinated enforcement:** federal police investigations, prosecutors’ charges, and judicial oversight.

## Impact and Controversies

- **Institutional impact:** influenced public debate on corruption, compliance, and prosecutorial power.
- **Economic and political effects:** contributed to major corporate disruptions and reshaped electoral narratives.
- **Due-process debate:** later rulings and reporting raised concerns about impartiality, procedure, and the boundary between justice and politics.
