## Summary

One of Mexicos oldest drug trafficking organizations based in Tamaulipas. It controlled drug routes along the Gulf coast before being weakened by the Los Zetas split.

## Historical Development

- The Gulf Cartel is often described as one of Mexico’s longest-running trafficking organizations, with roots in cross-border smuggling that later expanded into drug trafficking.
- Its base of operations has been associated with Tamaulipas and strategic routes into Texas.

## Relationship with Los Zetas

- A widely cited turning point was the creation of Los Zetas as an armed wing, initially composed of deserters from elite military units.
- The later split between the Gulf Cartel and Los Zetas contributed to intensified violence and fragmentation among criminal groups in northeastern Mexico.

## Operations and Tactics

- Like other major cartels, the organization has been linked to control of plazas (territories and corridors), bribery networks, and violent enforcement.
- Shifts in leadership and internal disputes have repeatedly reshaped alliances and rivalries.

## Current Significance

- The Gulf Cartel’s influence has been portrayed as reduced compared to its peak, but it remains relevant in analyses of Mexico’s organized-crime landscape.
- The cartel’s history is frequently used to illustrate how militarization of enforcement and splinter dynamics can escalate conflict.
