## Summary

American figure skater whose ex-husband orchestrated an attack on rival Nancy Kerrigan before the 1994 Olympics. Harding pled guilty to hindering the investigation and was banned from skating.

## Background

- Harding was raised in Portland, Oregon, and trained as a competitive figure skater from a young age.
- She became known for powerful jumping ability and for pushing technical difficulty in women’s skating in the early 1990s.

## The 1994 Kerrigan attack

- In January 1994, an assailant struck Nancy Kerrigan’s leg during practice ahead of the U.S. Championships.
- Investigators linked the attack to individuals connected to Harding’s then-husband, Jeff Gillooly, and an associate, Shawn Eckardt.
- Harding denied planning the assault but later admitted she did not fully cooperate with investigators.

## Legal outcome and sanctions

- Harding pleaded guilty to hindering prosecution (often summarized as hindering the investigation).
- The U.S. Figure Skating Association imposed a lifetime ban, and her national title was revoked.

## Later public life

- After competitive skating, Harding appeared in boxing and other entertainment ventures.
- The case remained a major sports scandal and has been revisited in documentaries and dramatizations.
