## Summary

Yang Mi is one of China's most commercially successful actresses and a dominant force in the Chinese entertainment industry. Born on September 12, 1986, in Beijing, she began her career as a child actress and evolved into a superstar whose influence spans television, film, and business. Her ability to anchor both historical fantasy dramas and contemporary romances has made her one of the most bankable stars in the Chinese-speaking world.

Beyond acting, Yang Mi co-founded Jaywalk Studio (嘉行传媒) in 2015, establishing herself as a producer and talent manager. Her dual role as artist and entrepreneur set a template widely followed by Chinese celebrities. With tens of millions of social media followers and a string of hit productions to her name, she remains a defining figure in 21st-century Chinese popular culture.

## Early Life & Background

Yang Mi was born in Beijing and showed an early aptitude for performance. She made her screen debut at age four in the 1990 historical drama *Tang Ming Huang*, becoming one of China's youngest working actors. She studied at the Beijing Film Academy, one of China's most prestigious performing arts institutions, graduating in 2009. Her academic training gave her a formal foundation that complemented her years of on-set experience.

## Rise to Prominence

Yang Mi first attracted wide attention with a supporting role in the 2009 period drama *The Dream of Red Mansions*. Her breakout came in 2011 when she starred in the time-travel fantasy *Palace* (*Gong), which became a cultural phenomenon across China and sparked a broader wave of time-travel dramas on Chinese television. The show's success catapulted her from a recognizable face to a household name virtually overnight.

She consolidated her status with the 2017 fantasy romance *Eternal Love* (*Ten Miles of Peach Blossoms*), based on the novel *Three Lives Three Worlds*. The series drew over 50 billion online views, a record at the time for a Chinese streaming drama, and cemented her as the leading actress of the xianxia (immortal fantasy) genre. Her 2018 sci-fi thriller *Reset* (*Bao Bao*) further demonstrated her range, earning her the Best Actress award at the 2018 Seoul International Drama Awards.

## Key Achievements

- Starred in *Eternal Love* (2017), which surpassed 50 billion views on streaming platforms, setting a Chinese drama viewership record at the time.
- Won Best Actress at the 2018 Seoul International Drama Awards for *Reset*.
- Co-founded Jaywalk Studio in 2015, which grew into one of China's leading talent agencies representing numerous A-list celebrities.
- Consistently ranked among China's highest-paid actresses, appearing on Forbes China Celebrity 100 lists multiple times throughout the 2010s and early 2020s.
- Starred in the film *Reset* (2017), China's first feature film to use large-scale time-loop narrative mechanics.

## Controversies & Criticism

Yang Mi's high public profile has attracted scrutiny. Her 2014 marriage to actor Hawick Lau and their 2018 divorce were subjects of intense tabloid coverage. Critics have occasionally noted that her acting style in some productions prioritizes commercial appeal over dramatic depth, though her range across genres has largely countered this perception. Her management company has faced criticism regarding contract disputes with former clients, reflecting broader tensions in China's fast-growing talent management industry.

## Legacy & Influence

Yang Mi's career arc — from child actress to producer to entrepreneur — mirrors the professionalization of the Chinese entertainment industry itself. She helped establish the xianxia drama as a globally recognized Chinese export, with *Eternal Love* introducing the genre to international streaming audiences. Her Jaywalk Studio model influenced how Chinese stars approach career management and intellectual property ownership. Younger generations of Chinese actresses frequently cite her as a career blueprint, underscoring her lasting cultural imprint.
