## Summary

South African serial killer known as the Wemmer Pan Killer who murdered at least 27 people in Johannesburg between 1996 and 1997. He attacked victims at random using hammers, knives, and firearms.

## Names used in reporting

The same offender is described in sources under multiple nicknames (for example **“Wemmer Pan Killer”** and **“Nasrec Strangler”**) tied to areas of Johannesburg where some victims were found.[^1] Articles and court summaries also vary in spelling (Cedrick/Cedric) and in how totals are reported.

## Crimes and investigation

High-level descriptions emphasize a short, intense period of violence in 1996–1997 with apparently random victim selection.[^1] The investigation involved connecting separate cases, collecting witness statements, and ultimately identifying Maake as a suspect, leading to his 1997 arrest.[^1]

## Trial and sentencing

Maake was convicted in 2000 and received a sentence reported as **1,340 years**, reflecting consecutive terms across many counts rather than a single finite term.[^1]

## Context

The case is frequently cited in South African crime history due to the number of victims and the public fear created by attacks that appeared opportunistic and difficult to predict.
