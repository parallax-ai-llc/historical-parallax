## Summary

Russian serial killer active in the Rostov region who murdered 8 women between 1998 and 2007. He strangled and mutilated his victims, initially evading detection due to similarities to the Chikatilo case.

## Additional Context

### Modus operandi and victimology

Open-source accounts describe Khorosenko attacking women in the Rostov region, killing by strangulation and in some cases mutilating the bodies. The pattern contributed to public fear and comparisons to earlier serial-murder cases in the region.

### Why the case was difficult

Investigators initially faced challenges due to limited forensic links between incidents and the possibility of multiple perpetrators. Media comparisons to Andrei Chikatilo also shaped public expectations and reporting.

### Identification and conviction

He was arrested after forensic work and investigative leads connected him to the crimes. Courts later sentenced him to life imprisonment.

### Aftermath

As with many serial-murder cases, the documentation accessible in English tends to emphasize the crime series, arrest, and sentence rather than detailed investigative records.
