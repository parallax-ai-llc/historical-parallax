## Summary

Ian Donald pioneered the use of diagnostic ultrasound in obstetrics and gynecology, publishing the first clinical results in The Lancet in 1958. He adapted industrial sonar technology for medical imaging, enabling non-invasive visualization of the fetus. His work created the field of prenatal ultrasound imaging that is now standard in obstetric care worldwide.
