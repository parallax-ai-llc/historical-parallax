## Background

The conflict escalated after the Houthi movement took control of the capital, Sana’a, amid political transition and state fragmentation.

It developed into a multi-sided war involving domestic factions and major external intervention.

## Key Parties (High-Level)

Commonly cited actors include:

- The Houthis (Ansar Allah).
- The internationally recognized government associated with President Abdrabbuh Mansur Hadi (and later leadership structures).
- A Saudi-led coalition that intervened militarily beginning in 2015.
- Various local militias and political groups, including southern separatist forces in parts of the country.

## Humanitarian Consequences

The war has had severe humanitarian effects, frequently described by the UN as among the world’s worst crises.

Major impacts have included:

- Large-scale civilian casualties and displacement.
- Acute food insecurity and malnutrition.
- Damage to health systems and outbreaks of preventable disease.
- Infrastructure collapse affecting water, sanitation, and electricity.

## Diplomacy, Ceasefires, and Negotiations

The conflict has seen repeated rounds of UN-led diplomacy, temporary ceasefires, and partial agreements.

Even when front lines stabilize, economic fragmentation and governance disputes can continue to drive suffering.

## Why the Conflict Matters

Beyond Yemen, the war is often discussed in terms of:

- Regional power competition and security dynamics.
- Risks to maritime trade routes near the Bab-el-Mandeb strait.
- The difficulty of ending civil wars with multiple armed actors and external backers.
