## Summary

Longtime Uzbek intelligence chief who oversaw systematic torture and surveillance of the population for 23 years.

## Background

- Born in 1944 in Sherabad (Surxondaryo Region), in what was then the Uzbek SSR.
- Trained in Iranian philology at Tashkent State University (per biographical sources).
- Entered Soviet security service during military service and worked in multiple KGB roles.

## Role in Uzbekistan’s Security Apparatus

The National Security Service (SNB) was one of the most powerful institutions in Uzbekistan’s state system during the Karimov era. As its long-serving chief, Inoyatov:

- Oversaw internal intelligence and counterintelligence functions attributed to the SNB.
- Became associated in reporting with elite factional politics (often described as “clans” in Uzbekistan).
- Was widely characterized in secondary reporting as an influential behind-the-scenes figure within the Uzbek political system.

## Post-2018

After leaving the SNB leadership in 2018, biographical reporting describes a transition into an advisory role to the president, followed by departure from that role in 2021.

## Notes on Human Rights Allegations

This article’s summary reflects widely reported allegations about the SNB’s use of coercive interrogation and pervasive surveillance. Publicly available sources often describe the SNB as implicated in serious human rights abuses during the period Inoyatov led the service; specific claims and responsibility are contested and vary by source.
