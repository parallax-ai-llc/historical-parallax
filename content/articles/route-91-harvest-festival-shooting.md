## Summary

Stephen Paddock fired over 1,000 rounds from the 32nd floor of the Mandalay Bay hotel into a country music festival crowd, killing 60 and injuring 413. His motive was never determined.

## Context and Setting

- **Location:** The attack targeted the Route 91 Harvest music festival area on the Las Vegas Strip, adjacent to Mandalay Bay Resort and Casino.
- **Date:** Night of October 1, 2017 (with victims pronounced into October 2 due to the timing and severity of injuries).
- **Crowd:** A large outdoor concert audience was present when gunfire began.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The shooter fired from a high floor of Mandalay Bay into the festival grounds below.
- The sustained burst pattern created confusion about the source and number of shooters during the first minutes.
- Law enforcement and emergency medical services responded rapidly to triage and evacuate attendees.

## Casualties and Immediate Impact

- **Fatalities:** 60 people were killed.
- **Injuries:** Hundreds were wounded by gunfire, and many more experienced related injuries during the panic and evacuation.
- The incident is widely described as the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history by number of fatalities.

## Investigation (Publicly Reported)

- Authorities identified the shooter as **Stephen Paddock**; his motive was not publicly established.
- Reporting emphasized the extensive planning involved, including bringing firearms and related equipment into the hotel.
- The event triggered broad review of hotel security practices and large-event emergency response planning.

## Aftermath and Responses

- Community and national responses included memorials, vigils, and fundraising efforts for victims and families.
- Civil litigation and insurance-related disputes followed, focusing on venue, hotel, and event security responsibilities.
- Policy discussions included attention to rapid-fire enabling devices (e.g., bump stocks) and other regulatory questions.

## Legacy

- The shooting remains a reference point in U.S. discussions on mass-casualty preparedness and public-space security.
- It is frequently cited in training and planning for coordinated emergency response at large public events.
