## Summary

Magdalena Abakanowicz was a Polish sculptor known for her monumental fiber and bronze sculptures of headless human figures. Her large-scale installations of anonymous, crowd-like figures address themes of individuality and collective identity under totalitarianism. She pioneered the use of textile as a sculptural medium.
