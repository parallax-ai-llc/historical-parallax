## Additional Context

### Early life and background

- Vidkun Abraham Lauritz Jonssøn Quisling was a Norwegian army officer before entering national politics.
- His military career and later public service helped him build contacts within Norway’s state and security institutions.

### Political position and ideology

- Quisling became associated with far-right, authoritarian, and explicitly fascist politics in the interwar period.
- He founded the Norwegian fascist party *Nasjonal Samling* (National Unity), which remained a minor party before the war.

### World War II collaboration

- After Germany invaded Norway in April 1940, Quisling attempted to seize power and align Norway with Nazi Germany.
- His regime relied on German occupation authorities and Norwegian collaborators rather than broad public support.
- The collaborationist government pursued policies consistent with Nazi objectives, including political repression.

### Rule, collapse, and prosecution

- Quisling’s authority was tied to the occupation; as Germany’s position deteriorated, the collaborationist project collapsed.
- After the war, he was tried for treason and other crimes under Norwegian legal proceedings.
- He was convicted and executed in 1945.

### Legacy

- The term “quisling” entered multiple languages as a common noun meaning a traitor or collaborator with an occupying enemy.
- In Norwegian historical memory, his name is closely associated with wartime betrayal and the broader issue of collaboration.
