## Summary

Venkatraman Ramakrishnan is an Indian-born British-American structural biologist who shared the 2009 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for studies of the structure and function of the ribosome. His work used X-ray crystallography to reveal the atomic structure of the ribosome, providing insights into how proteins are manufactured in cells. He served as president of the Royal Society from 2015 to 2020.
