## Summary

Félix Savón won three Olympic boxing golds (1992-2000) and six World titles.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Background

- Savón was a product of Cuba’s state-supported amateur boxing system, which historically emphasized Olympic and world championship competition rather than professional careers.
- He competed primarily as an amateur heavyweight and became one of the most decorated boxers in Olympic history.

## Major Achievements

- Three Olympic gold medals in the heavyweight division (1992, 1996, 2000).
- Multiple world championship titles, reinforcing Cuba’s long-standing dominance in amateur boxing.
- Known for consistency over a long competitive span, staying elite across three Olympic cycles.

## Style and Reputation

- He was widely recognized for disciplined fundamentals, ring control, and the ability to impose pace against top international competition.
- In boxing discussions, he is often mentioned alongside other all-time great amateurs because of his medal count and longevity.

## After Competition

- Cuban champions commonly transition into coaching or sports administration roles within the national system.
- His accomplishments remain a reference point in debates about amateur vs. professional legacies in boxing.
- He is regularly cited in Olympic retrospectives as an example of sustained excellence without a professional résumé.
