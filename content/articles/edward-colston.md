## Summary
Edward Colston was an English merchant, slave trader, and philanthropist. As a member of the Royal African Company, he was involved in the transportation of approximately 84,000 enslaved Africans. His statue in Bristol was toppled by protesters in 2020 during the George Floyd protests.
