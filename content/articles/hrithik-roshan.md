## Summary

Hrithik Roshan (born January 10, 1974) is one of Bollywood's most celebrated actors and widely regarded as one of the finest dancers in Indian cinema. With a career spanning over two decades, he has starred in some of the highest-grossing Hindi films of all time and earned a reputation for physically demanding roles that showcase his athleticism and versatility.

His debut in Kaho Naa... Pyaar Hai (2000) shattered box office records and made him an overnight sensation, setting the stage for a career defined by blockbusters, critical acclaim, and a devoted global fanbase. Beyond acting, Roshan's dancing ability has drawn comparisons to international icons and placed him in a category few Indian actors occupy.

Roshan's influence extends beyond film — he is a fitness icon, a fashion trendsetter, and a philanthropist whose HRX brand has reshaped India's activewear market. Consistently ranked among the most handsome and influential celebrities in the world by various publications, he represents a global face of Indian cinema.

## Early Life & Background

Hrithik Roshan was born in Mumbai, Maharashtra, into a prominent film family. His father, Rakesh Roshan, is a successful director and producer, and his grandfather, Roshan, was a noted music composer. Despite this privileged background, Hrithik faced a significant personal challenge from childhood — a stutter, which he worked to overcome through years of therapy and determination.

He attended Sydenham College of Commerce and Economics in Mumbai. He appeared as a child actor in several of his father's films during the 1980s, including Aasha (1980) and Bhagwan Dada (1986), and worked as an assistant director on his father's productions before launching his acting career.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Roshan's debut film, Kaho Naa... Pyaar Hai (2000), directed by his father, became a massive commercial and critical success, winning him two Filmfare Awards — Best Actor and Best Male Debut — in the same year, a rare achievement. His dancing, screen presence, and looks generated unprecedented public frenzy.

He followed up with Fiza (2000) and Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham... (2001), then cemented his action-hero status with Krrish (2006) and its sequels. His role in Jodhaa Akbar (2008) demonstrated dramatic depth, while Zindagi Na Milegi Dobara (2011) and Bang Bang! (2014) reinforced his bankability. Kaabil (2017) and Super 30 (2019) — where he played mathematician Anand Kumar — earned strong critical notices.

## Key Achievements

- Six Filmfare Awards, including Best Actor wins for Kaho Naa... Pyaar Hai, Dhoom 2, Jodhaa Akbar, and Bang Bang!
- War (2019) grossed over ₹3.17 billion, becoming one of the highest-grossing Bollywood films at the time of its release
- Ranked among People magazine's "Sexiest Asian Men" lists multiple times
- Co-founded HRX, a fitness and lifestyle brand launched in 2013 that became one of India's leading activewear labels
- Super 30 (2019) was India's official submission consideration period entry and grossed over ₹2 billion worldwide

## Controversies & Criticism

Roshan was involved in a highly publicized and acrimonious legal dispute with actress Kangana Ranaut beginning around 2016, which included allegations of harassment and defamation exchanged by both parties. The case attracted extensive media coverage and divided public opinion. Legal proceedings continued for several years without a definitive public resolution.

His filmography has also been critiqued at points for an over-reliance on spectacle over substance, though later career choices such as Super 30 countered that narrative.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Hrithik Roshan has left an indelible mark on Bollywood's evolution into a more action-oriented, visually ambitious industry. His dedication to physical transformation for roles — including drastic weight changes and learning new skills — raised the bar for actor preparation in Indian cinema. His dancing continues to influence a generation of performers, and his commercial brand success demonstrated a viable path for Indian celebrities into entrepreneurship.
