## Additional Context

- “Wazawaka” is presented as an online alias in the broader ransomware ecosystem, where individuals and groups frequently rebrand or move between malware families.
- Modern ransomware operations are often organized as **RaaS (ransomware-as-a-service)**, separating roles such as developers, affiliates, initial-access brokers, and money launderers.

## Typical Tactics (High Level)

- Initial access via stolen credentials, phishing, or exploitation of exposed services.
- Lateral movement inside networks to reach high-value servers and backups.
- “Double extortion” pressure by combining encryption with threats to leak stolen data.

## Law Enforcement Context

- Public indictments and reward programs are used to name suspected actors, restrict travel, and deter third parties from supporting infrastructure.
- When suspects reside in jurisdictions unlikely to extradite, enforcement may focus on disrupting servers, seizing cryptocurrency, and sanctioning facilitators.

## Why This Case Is Notable

- Attacks attributed to ransomware crews have targeted public services and critical infrastructure operators, raising national security and continuity-of-government concerns.
