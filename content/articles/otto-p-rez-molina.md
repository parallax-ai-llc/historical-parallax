## Summary

Former president of Guatemala who was forced to resign and arrested for his role in a massive customs fraud ring called La Línea. He was a former military intelligence officer accused of wartime atrocities.

## Military Background and Civil War Context

Pérez Molina rose through Guatemala’s military during and after the country’s long internal armed conflict. His career has been politically contentious because human-rights investigations into wartime abuses overlapped with his later public roles.

## Presidency (2012–2015)

As president, Pérez Molina led a conservative administration facing persistent challenges around corruption, security, and public trust. His term became dominated by a major customs-fraud investigation that escalated into a national political crisis.

## La Línea Corruption Case

“La Línea” referred to an alleged scheme in which importers paid bribes to reduce customs duties, with proceeds distributed through a network of officials. The case drew international attention in part because of:

- Large, sustained street protests
- The role of investigators and prosecutors working with anti-impunity bodies
- The speed with which the scandal destabilized the government

## Resignation and Legal Proceedings

After Congress stripped his immunity, Pérez Molina resigned and was arrested. The case remains a prominent example in Latin American politics of a sitting president being forced from office through corruption investigations.

## Broader Significance

The episode is frequently referenced in debates about:

- Institutional anti-corruption capacity
- Prosecutorial independence
- The durability of democratic accountability under elite pressure
