## Summary

Aung San Suu Kyi (born 1945) is a Burmese politician who became a global icon of democracy and human rights for her nonviolent resistance to Myanmar's military junta. Placed under house arrest for 15 of 21 years (1989-2010), she was awarded the Nobel Peace Prize (1991) for her "non-violent struggle for democracy and human rights." She led her National League for Democracy to landslide election victories and served as State Counsellor (2016-2021), Myanmar's de facto leader. However, her legacy became deeply controversial due to her defense of the military's brutal campaign against the Rohingya Muslim minority (2016-2017), widely condemned as ethnic cleansing or genocide. She is again under house arrest following the 2021 military coup.

---

## Early Life

Born June 19, 1945, Rangoon (Yangon), Burma (Myanmar). Daughter of Aung San, founding father of modern Burma, assassinated when she was 2 years old.

**Education:**
- Lady Shri Ram College, Delhi
- Oxford University (Philosophy, Politics, Economics)
- Worked at UN, New York

**Marriage:**
Married Michael Aris, British academic (1972). Two sons. They knew her return to Burma might separate them.

---

## Return to Burma (1988)

Returned in 1988 to care for dying mother. Became involved in pro-democracy protests (8888 Uprising) against military dictatorship.

**National League for Democracy (NLD):**
Founded party (1988), became General Secretary. Called for democracy, human rights, nonviolent resistance.

---

## House Arrest (1989-2010)

**First House Arrest (1989-1995):**
Military junta placed her under house arrest days before 1990 elections. NLD won landslide (80% of seats), but military refused to transfer power.

**Nobel Peace Prize (1991):**
Awarded while under house arrest. Son Alexander accepted on her behalf.

**Subsequent Detentions:**
- 2000-2002
- 2003-2010

Total: ~15 years out of 21 under house arrest. Refused exile, staying to continue struggle.

**Personal Sacrifice:**
Husband Michael Aris died of cancer (1999); junta refused him a visa. She couldn't visit him. Missed sons growing up.

---

## Release and Political Rise (2010-2021)

**Released (2010):**
Junta began limited reforms. NLD boycotted 2010 elections (restrictive rules) but participated in 2012 by-elections. Suu Kyi elected to parliament.

**2015 Elections:**
NLD won landslide. Constitution barred Suu Kyi from presidency (her children are foreign nationals), so she became "State Counsellor" (created position)â€”de facto leader.

**In Power:**
- Limited reforms
- Difficult relationship with still-powerful military
- Press freedom, political prisoners issues
- Economy slowly opened

## Rohingya Crisis (2016-2017)

**Background:**
Rohingya: Muslim minority in Myanmar's Rakhine state, long persecuted, denied citizenship.

**2017 Military Campaign:**
Following Rohingya militant attacks, military launched brutal crackdown:
- Villages burned
- Thousands killed
- Sexual violence widespread
- 700,000+ fled to Bangladesh
- UN called it "textbook ethnic cleansing"

**Suu Kyi's Response:**
- Defended military
- Denied genocide
- Appeared at International Court of Justice (2019) to defend Myanmar
- Never acknowledged atrocities

**International Condemnation:**
- Stripped of numerous honors
- Nobel committee statement of disappointment
- Human rights organizations condemned her silence
- Former admirers felt betrayed

**Explanation Attempts:**
- Constrained by military (retained power over security)
- Political calculation (Rohingya unpopular in Myanmar)
- Nationalism
- Decline in moral authority

---

## 2021 Military Coup

**February 1, 2021:**
Military seized power, arrested Suu Kyi and NLD leaders. Public protests met with deadly force. Hundreds killed.

**Suu Kyi:**
- Under detention
- Criminal charges (fabricated, politically motivated)
- Sentenced to years in prison (multiple trials)
- Health concerns

---

## Controversy and Legacy

**Before Rohingya:**
- Global icon of democracy
- Compared to Mandela, Gandhi
- Moral authority on nonviolent resistance

**After Rohingya:**
- Deeply tarnished reputation
- Many former supporters condemn her
- Some argue she was constrained, others call her complicit

**Complexity:**
Illustrates how democracy icons can fail moral tests when in power.
