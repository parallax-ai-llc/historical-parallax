## Summary

Sean Hannity is one of the most influential conservative media figures in the United States, best known as the host of *Hannity* on Fox News and a nationally syndicated radio program. Since joining Fox News at its launch in 1996, he has become a defining voice of right-wing political commentary, consistently drawing tens of millions of viewers and listeners each week. His unwavering support for Republican politicians — most notably Donald Trump — has made him both a cultural touchstone for conservative audiences and a lightning rod for criticism from media critics and political opponents.

Hannity's career spans more than three decades in talk radio and television, during which he helped shape the modern conservative media ecosystem. His ability to connect with working-class audiences through a populist, combative style has kept him at or near the top of cable news ratings for much of his tenure at Fox News, frequently outpacing competitors on CNN and MSNBC.

---

## Early Life & Background

Sean Patrick Hannity was born on December 30, 1961, in New York City and raised in Franklin Square, Long Island. The son of a probation officer and a court stenographer, he grew up in a working-class Irish Catholic household. He attended Sacred Heart Seminary in Hempstead but did not complete a degree. He later enrolled at New York University and Adelphi University but left without graduating — a biographical detail that critics have occasionally highlighted given his national platform.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Hannity began his radio career in the late 1980s at UC Santa Barbara's college station, KCSB-FM, where his show was cancelled after he made remarks critics deemed anti-gay. He went on to host shows in Huntsville, Alabama, and Atlanta before landing at WABC in New York City in 1996. That same year, Fox News launched and hired Hannity as co-host of *Hannity & Colmes*, a debate-format program that ran until 2009. After Alan Colmes departed, the show became simply *Hannity*, cementing his solo prime-time presence. His radio show, syndicated by Premiere Networks, reaches an estimated 15 million listeners weekly.

## Key Achievements

- Hosted *Hannity & Colmes* (1996–2009) and *Hannity* (2009–present) on Fox News
- His radio program is consistently one of the top three most-listened-to talk radio shows in the United States
- Author of three *New York Times* bestselling books: *Let Freedom Ring* (2002), *Deliver Us from Evil* (2004), and *Conservative Victory* (2010)
- Fox News's highest-rated prime-time host for multiple consecutive years during the mid-2010s and early 2020s

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Hannity has faced sustained criticism for blurring the line between journalism and political advocacy. His close personal relationship with Donald Trump — including reported off-air phone calls and stage appearances at Trump campaign rallies — drew scrutiny from press freedom organizations. In 2018, it emerged that he shared attorney Michael Cohen with Trump, raising conflict-of-interest questions he had not disclosed to viewers. He has promoted disputed claims about topics including the 2020 election, COVID-19 treatments, and the murder of DNC staffer Seth Rich, the last of which resulted in a Fox News retraction. Media watchdogs have repeatedly cited his program as a vehicle for misinformation rather than news reporting.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Hannity represents a generation of media figures who transformed political commentary into mass entertainment. His career helped establish the template for ideologically driven prime-time television and demonstrated that partisan advocacy could sustain enormous commercial audiences. Whether viewed as a champion of conservative values or a propagandist for a political movement, his influence on American media and politics over the past 30 years is difficult to overstate.
