## Summary

Yuan Tseh Lee is a Taiwanese chemist who shared the 1986 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for developing crossed molecular beam techniques for studying chemical reaction dynamics. His method enabled scientists to observe individual molecular collisions and understand reaction mechanisms at the atomic level. He later served as president of Taiwan's Academia Sinica.
