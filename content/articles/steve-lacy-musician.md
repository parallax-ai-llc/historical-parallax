## Summary

Steve Lacy is an American singer, songwriter, guitarist, and record producer who first gained recognition as the guitarist of the Grammy-nominated R&B band The Internet. Known for his genre-blending approach that fuses R&B, funk, rock, and indie pop, Lacy emerged as one of the most distinctive voices of his generation.

His 2022 single "Bad Habit" reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100, cementing his crossover appeal and establishing him as a solo force beyond his work with The Internet. Lacy's creative process — notably producing early material entirely on his iPhone using GarageBand — became emblematic of a new era of DIY music production.

## Early Life & Background

Steve Thomas Lacy-Moya was born on May 23, 1998, in Compton, California. Raised in a modest household, he developed an interest in music at a young age, teaching himself guitar during his teenage years. He attended The Performing Arts Center in Los Angeles, where he honed his musical skills and connected with like-minded artists.

Lacy's early musical influences ranged from Jimi Hendrix and Prince to Pharrell Williams and N.E.R.D., a blend that would come to define his eclectic sonic palette. While still in high school, he began experimenting with production on his iPhone, a limitation that paradoxically shaped his minimalist, textured sound.

## Rise to Prominence

In 2015, at just 17 years old, Lacy joined The Internet — an Odd Future offshoot led by Syd — as their guitarist. He contributed to the band's critically acclaimed third album *Ego Death* (2015), which earned a Grammy nomination for Best Urban Contemporary Album. His guitar work and production on the record drew immediate attention.

Lacy released his debut solo project, the EP *Steve Lacy's Demo* in 2017, which he produced entirely on his phone. The project showcased his ability to craft polished, soulful tracks with unconventional tools. In 2019, he released his debut studio album *Apollo XXI*, which received widespread critical praise and won the BET Award for Best New Artist.

## Key Achievements

Lacy's second studio album, *Gemini Rights* (2022), marked his commercial breakthrough. The album debuted at number seven on the Billboard 200, and its lead single "Bad Habit" spent one week at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 in September 2022, becoming a viral sensation on TikTok and streaming platforms. The song earned him a Grammy nomination for Song of the Year and Record of the Year at the 65th Grammy Awards in 2023.

Beyond his solo career, Lacy has produced and written for artists including Kendrick Lamar (contributing to *DAMN.*), Vampire Weekend, Solange, J. Cole, and Mac DeMarco. His production credit on Kendrick Lamar's "PRIDE." brought him recognition as a producer while he was still a teenager.

## Controversies & Criticism

In December 2022, Lacy drew criticism after a video surfaced showing him throwing a fan's disposable camera into the crowd during a concert in New Orleans after the fan tossed it onstage. The incident sparked debate about boundaries between performers and audiences and led Lacy to address the situation publicly, asking fans not to throw objects at him during performances.

Some critics have also noted that his live performances occasionally fell short of studio polish during his earlier touring years, though his stagecraft improved significantly as he gained experience on larger stages.

## Legacy & Influence

Steve Lacy represents a generational shift in how music is created and consumed. His iPhone production origin story inspired countless young musicians to begin creating with whatever tools were available to them, democratizing the idea of what a professional studio setup looks like.

His fluid approach to genre and identity — openly bisexual and unconfined by traditional R&B boundaries — has resonated with younger audiences seeking authenticity in their artists. At 27, Lacy has already built a body of work that bridges underground credibility and mainstream success, positioning him as one of the defining artist-producers of the 2020s.
