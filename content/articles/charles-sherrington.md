## Summary

Charles Sherrington was a British neurophysiologist who shared the 1932 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his discoveries regarding the functions of neurons. He coined the term 'synapse' and established the integrative action of the nervous system. His work laid the foundation for modern neuroscience and our understanding of how the nervous system coordinates body functions.
