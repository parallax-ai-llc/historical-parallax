## Summary

Abdul Qadir Gilani was a Persian Sufi preacher, jurist, and mystic who founded the Qadiriyya Sufi order, one of the largest and most widespread in the world. Known as the 'Sultan of Saints,' he attracted enormous audiences to his sermons in Baghdad. His emphasis on adherence to sharia alongside mystical practice made his order widely accepted. His teachings spread across the Islamic world from West Africa to Southeast Asia.
