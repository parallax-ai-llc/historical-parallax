## Summary

Nwankwo Kanu won Olympic gold with Nigeria in 1996 and the Champions League with Ajax. He played for Arsenal, winning the FA Cup.

## Club Career (High-Level)

Kanu rose to prominence in the 1990s and is strongly associated with Ajax, where he was part of a European title-winning side. He later played in Italy for Inter Milan and in England for Arsenal, where he became known for his technical skill and ability to influence games as a creative forward.

At Arsenal, he was part of teams that won major domestic honors, including FA Cup success. He later continued his Premier League career with Portsmouth and also played for West Bromwich Albion, extending his influence across multiple clubs.

## International Career

With Nigeria, Kanu’s most celebrated moment is the 1996 Olympic gold medal, achieved by a Nigerian team that became iconic in the country’s football history. He also represented Nigeria at senior international level over many years and served as a captain, contributing to the national team’s identity across tournament cycles.

## Playing Style and Reputation

Kanu is often described as a tall, elegant forward with close control and an unorthodox dribbling style. Rather than relying purely on pace, he was known for:

- Ball retention under pressure
- Improvisational skill in tight spaces
- Creating chances for teammates through movement and touch

## Health and Off-Field Work

Public biographies frequently mention that Kanu dealt with a serious heart condition early in his career and underwent surgery, after which he returned to top-level football. He is also associated with charitable work, including initiatives focused on children’s health, which became a significant part of his public profile after retirement.

## Legacy

Kanu remains one of Nigeria’s most recognizable footballers internationally. His club trophies, Olympic gold medal, and long career in elite European leagues contribute to his reputation as a landmark figure for Nigerian players competing abroad.
