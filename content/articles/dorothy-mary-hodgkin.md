## Additional Context

### Name and Duplicate Entry Note

- **Dorothy Mary Hodgkin** refers to **Dorothy Mary Crowfoot Hodgkin**, most commonly cited as **Dorothy Hodgkin**.
- This entry appears to be a **duplicate/name-variant**; it can be used to catch searches that include her middle name.

### What She Is Known For

- Hodgkin was a leading figure in **X-ray crystallography**, applying diffraction methods to solve structures of biologically important molecules.
- Key achievements often summarized in biographies include:
  - Determining the structure of **penicillin**, helping confirm the molecular basis of an essential antibiotic.
  - Solving the complex structure of **vitamin B12**, a landmark in the field.
  - Long-term work culminating in the structure of **insulin**, relevant to diabetes research.

### Honors and Influence

- She was awarded the **Nobel Prize in Chemistry (1964)**.
- Her work helped establish crystallography as a foundational technique for modern structural science, bridging chemistry and biology.

### Academic Role

- Hodgkin spent much of her career at the **University of Oxford**, where she trained and influenced multiple generations of chemists.
- Her work is frequently cited as part of the historical foundation for later advances in **structural biology** and drug discovery.
