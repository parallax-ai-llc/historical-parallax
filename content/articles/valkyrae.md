## Summary

Rachell Hofstetter, known online as Valkyrae, is an American livestreamer, content creator, and co-owner of 100 Thieves. Rising from a part-time GameStop employee to one of the most-watched streamers in the world, she became a defining figure in the livestreaming industry during the early 2020s. In 2020, she was named YouTube Gaming's most-watched female streamer, a milestone that cemented her status as a trailblazer for women in gaming content.

Valkyrae's influence extends beyond gaming. Her authentic personality, mental health advocacy, and business acumen helped reshape how audiences and brands perceive female creators in esports and gaming culture. As a co-owner of 100 Thieves — a major esports and lifestyle organization — she became one of the few content creators to hold an equity stake in a prominent esports brand, signaling a broader shift in how top-tier creators negotiate their value in the industry.

## Early Life & Background

Rachell Hofstetter was born on January 8, 1999, in Arlington, Washington, to a Filipino mother and a German-American father. She grew up in a working-class household and attended school in the Pacific Northwest. Before her streaming career took off, she worked at GameStop while building a small following on Twitch, streaming games like PlayerUnknown's Battlegrounds (PUBG) and Fortnite in her spare time.

Her multicultural background and relatable upbringing resonated strongly with her audience, and she has spoken openly about financial struggles during her early career — including a period when she nearly quit streaming due to slow growth.

## Rise to Prominence

Valkyrae began streaming on Twitch around 2014–2015, gaining gradual traction. Her career accelerated significantly in 2020 when she signed an exclusivity deal with YouTube Gaming, making her one of the platform's flagship streamers. That same year, she was named a co-owner of 100 Thieves alongside other prominent creators including Pokimane and other roster members.

Among Us became a cultural phenomenon in 2020, and Valkyrae's streams with friends including Sykkuno, Disguised Toast, and Corpse Husband drew millions of viewers. YouTube Gaming named her the most-watched female streamer on the platform for 2020, a title she retained for multiple consecutive years.

## Key Achievements

- Named YouTube Gaming's most-watched female streamer in 2020 and 2021
- Became co-owner of 100 Thieves in 2020, one of the first female creator-owners in major esports
- Accumulated over 4 million subscribers on YouTube and millions of followers across social platforms
- Won the Streamer Award for "Streamer of the Year" at the inaugural Streamer Awards in 2022
- Launched collaborations with major brands including Microsoft, Puma, and HyperX

## Controversies & Criticism

In October 2021, Valkyrae co-launched RFLCT, a skincare line marketed around reducing the effects of "blue light" from screens. The product faced immediate backlash from dermatologists and consumers who argued that the blue light skincare premise lacked scientific backing. Valkyrae withdrew from the partnership within days and issued a public apology, acknowledging she should have vetted the scientific claims more rigorously. The episode drew broader discussion about creator responsibility when endorsing health-adjacent products.

## Legacy & Influence

Valkyrae is widely credited with expanding the visibility of women in gaming content, particularly on YouTube Gaming. Her co-ownership role at 100 Thieves challenged conventional boundaries between creator and executive, influencing how major organizations subsequently pursued creator partnerships. Her candid discussions about anxiety, burnout, and mental health helped normalize these conversations within streaming communities. By the mid-2020s, she remained one of the most recognizable names in gaming entertainment globally.
