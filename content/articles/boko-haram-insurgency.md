## Summary

Islamist insurgency in northeastern Nigeria since 2009 that has killed over 35,000 people and displaced 2.3 million. Known for mass kidnappings, suicide bombings, and attacks on schools.

## Background and Actors

- The insurgency is associated with **Boko Haram**, an Islamist militant movement that emerged in northern Nigeria and escalated into large-scale violence from 2009 onward.
- Over time, internal splits and leadership disputes produced factions; one widely reported offshoot is the **Islamic State West Africa Province (ISWAP)**, which became prominent in the Lake Chad region.
- The conflict has involved Nigerian federal and state security forces, local militias, and regional partners.

## Geography

- Violence has been concentrated in **northeastern Nigeria**, but spillover effects have affected neighboring countries around Lake Chad, including parts of Niger, Chad, and Cameroon.
- The terrain, porous borders, and limited state presence in some rural areas have complicated sustained security operations.

## Tactics and Targeting (high level)

- Reporting frequently notes attacks on civilians, raids on villages, kidnappings (including school abductions), suicide bombings, and assaults on military bases.
- Attacks on education and public institutions have had long-term social costs, including disrupted schooling and mass displacement.

## Humanitarian Impact

- The conflict has generated a protracted humanitarian emergency, with large numbers of internally displaced people and refugees.
- Estimates of deaths and displacement vary by source and by reporting period; widely cited figures can differ as methodologies change and access to affected areas is limited.

## Government and Regional Response

- Nigeria has pursued military campaigns alongside stabilization and deradicalization efforts, with varying results across different phases of the conflict.
- Regional coordination has included multinational operations and joint task forces aimed at addressing cross-border movement of fighters and weapons.

## Current Status (high level)

- The insurgency is often described as reduced from its peak territorial control but still capable of deadly attacks and localized control in some areas.
- Analysts commonly emphasize that long-term resolution requires both security measures and governance reforms, including community protection and economic recovery.
