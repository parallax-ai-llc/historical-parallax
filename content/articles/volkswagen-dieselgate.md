## Summary

VW installed defeat devices in 11 million diesel vehicles to cheat emissions tests. NOx emissions were up to 40 times legal limits; the fraud caused an estimated 1,200 premature deaths in Europe.

## What happened (high level)

- Volkswagen Group diesel vehicles were programmed to detect when they were being tested for emissions (laboratory conditions) and to temporarily reduce nitrogen oxides (NOx) during the test.
- In normal driving, the same vehicles emitted far higher NOx than the certified values, creating a large real-world gap versus regulatory limits.

## Key organizations and people

- **Volkswagen Group**: Manufacturer responsible for the diesel engine software and compliance representations.
- **Regulators**: U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and California Air Resources Board (CARB) played central roles in the 2015 disclosure.
- **Martin Winterkorn**: CEO at the time the scandal broke; later faced criminal charges in Germany related to the affair (proceedings have spanned years).

## Legal and financial consequences (selected)

- Volkswagen agreed to large settlements and penalties in the United States, including vehicle buybacks/repairs and environmental mitigation payments.
- The company also reached resolutions in multiple countries, including civil settlements, regulatory fines, and criminal proceedings against individuals in some jurisdictions.

## Why it mattered

- **Public health and environment**: Elevated NOx contributes to smog and fine particulate formation, with downstream health impacts.
- **Trust and governance**: The case became a reference point for corporate compliance failures, internal controls, and the limits of laboratory-only testing.
- **Industry impact**: Accelerated scrutiny of diesel technology, testing protocols (including more real-world testing), and electrification strategies.

## Aftermath

- Volkswagen pursued corporate restructuring and a strategic shift toward battery-electric vehicles while continuing to manage ongoing litigation and reputational impact.
- The scandal influenced broader regulatory reforms and public debate about emissions testing, transparency, and enforcement.
