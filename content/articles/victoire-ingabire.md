## Background

Victoire Ingabire Umuhoza (born 3 October 1968) is a Rwandan politician associated with opposition politics and advocacy for expanded democratic space. She lived in the Netherlands for many years and became active in diaspora opposition organizing before returning to Rwanda.

She has been described as chairwoman of the Unified Democratic Forces (UDF) during the period from 2006 to 2019.

## Return to Rwanda and political activity

Ingabire returned to Rwanda in January 2010 after an extended period in exile. She sought to participate in national politics and was associated with efforts to build a unified opposition platform.

Her stated political themes have included:

- rule of law and constitutional governance
- political pluralism and non-violent competition
- reconciliation framed around civic rights rather than ethnic affiliation
- empowerment of women in public life

## Arrest, charges, and imprisonment

According to widely cited accounts, Ingabire was arrested in 2010 and later convicted (2012) on charges that included genocide ideology, as well as offenses framed in terms of national security such as terrorism-related allegations.

She served part of a longer sentence in Kigali Central Prison, with reporting commonly describing roughly eight years served of a fifteen-year term.

## International attention

Her case has drawn international attention from political and human-rights audiences. She has been nominated for recognition connected to freedom of thought (including a 2012 nomination for the European Parliament's Sakharov Prize).

She has also received international awards from civil-society organizations, alongside reports that travel to accept awards has at times been restricted.

## Political positioning after imprisonment

Ingabire has continued to be associated with calls for greater political space in Rwanda. She has been linked with the party Development And Liberty For All, described as focusing on political freedoms and development.
