## Summary

Explosion at the AZF chemical plant in Toulouse, France on September 21, 2001 that killed 31 people and injured 2,500. 300 tonnes of ammonium nitrate detonated.

## Overview

- The AZF disaster was a major industrial explosion in 2001 at a chemical plant in Toulouse, France.
- The blast caused fatalities and widespread injuries and damaged large parts of the city, including homes, schools, and infrastructure.

## Causes and Investigations

- The incident led to lengthy investigations and court proceedings to determine the cause and responsibility.
- It is often discussed in the context of industrial safety, hazardous materials storage, and regulatory oversight.

## Response and Aftermath

- Emergency services undertook a large-scale response, and the city faced a significant recovery and reconstruction effort.
- The event contributed to renewed attention to risk management around industrial sites near dense urban areas.

## Significance

- AZF is frequently cited in European discussions of major-accident hazards and the societal costs of industrial disasters.
- It also shaped public debate in France about chemical industry practices and safety standards.
