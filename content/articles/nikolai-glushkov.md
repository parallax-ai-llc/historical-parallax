## Background

- Nikolai Aleksandrovich Glushkov (Николай Александрович Глушков) was a Russian businessman who became known internationally through his association with Boris Berezovsky.
- He worked in the orbit of Aeroflot and related business structures during the post-Soviet period, a time marked by rapid privatization and high-profile corporate and political disputes.

## Connection to Boris Berezovsky

- Glushkov is commonly described as an associate of Boris Berezovsky, a prominent Russian businessman and political figure who later lived in the United Kingdom.
- In public reporting, their names often appear together in discussions of Aeroflot-related controversies and subsequent legal and political fallout.

## Death (London, 2018)

- Glushkov was found dead at his home in New Malden, southwest London, on 12 March 2018.
- British authorities treated the death as suspicious; it was later described as murder by strangulation in widely cited summaries.
- The case attracted attention because it occurred in the UK and involved an individual linked to earlier, high-profile Russian political and business conflicts.

## Why He Is Notable

- His death became part of a broader public conversation about the safety of Russian exiles and associates living abroad, particularly in the UK.
- The event is frequently referenced in timelines of notable deaths connected (directly or indirectly) to Russian elite disputes, without implying any specific perpetrator absent official attribution.
