## Summary

Alexander Calder was an American sculptor who invented the mobile, revolutionizing modern sculpture by introducing movement. His hanging mobiles and standing stabiles combine engineering ingenuity with playful artistic expression. He also created Calder's Circus, a performative miniature wire circus.
