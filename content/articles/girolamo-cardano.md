## Summary

Girolamo Cardano was an Italian polymath who published the first systematic treatment of probability and the solutions to cubic and quartic equations. His 'Ars Magna' (1545) was the most important algebra book of the Renaissance, containing the general solutions discovered by Tartaglia and Ferrari. He also invented the Cardan shaft (universal joint) used in vehicles today.
