## Summary

Stefanie Maria "Steffi" Graf is a German former professional tennis player widely regarded as one of the sport’s greatest champions. She is best known for her dominance from the mid-1980s through the 1990s, her powerful forehand, and exceptional footwork.

Graf won **22 Grand Slam singles titles** (women’s record in the Open Era) and is the only player to complete the **“Golden Slam”**—winning all four Grand Slam singles titles plus Olympic gold in the same calendar year (1988).

## Playing Style and Strengths

- **Forehand (“Fräulein Forehand”)**: a signature weapon, struck with heavy pace and aggressive angles.
- **Footwork and athleticism**: efficient movement that supported both defense and sudden offense.
- **All-court adaptability**: elite results on clay, grass, and hard courts, with particular success on faster surfaces.
- **Mental resilience**: known for sustaining pressure in major finals and high-stakes matches.

## Major Achievements (High-Level)

- **Grand Slam singles titles**: 22
- **Olympic gold**: 1988 (Seoul) singles
- **Calendar-year Golden Slam**: 1988 (unique achievement in singles)
- **World No. 1**: held the top ranking for a record total of **377 weeks** (WTA)

## Rivalries and Era

Graf’s prime overlapped with a historically strong field that included long-running rivalries with other top players of the era. Her matchups in major finals and late-round Slam encounters helped define women’s tennis in the late 1980s and 1990s.

## Retirement and Legacy

Graf retired in 1999 after returning to win the French Open, closing her career with one of the most decorated records in tennis history. She is frequently cited in debates about the greatest players ever, in part due to her peak dominance, longevity at No. 1, and rare 1988 season.
