## Summary

Georgian Chechen fighter assassinated in Berlin by a Russian intelligence agent.

## Background

- Khangoshvili was an ethnic Chechen (Kist) born in the Pankisi Gorge region of Georgia.
- He fought as a volunteer for the Chechen Republic of Ichkeria during the Second Chechen War and later served as a Georgian military officer during the 2008 Russo-Georgian War.[^1]

## Security and Exile

- According to reporting summarized by Wikipedia, Russian authorities viewed him as a wanted militant, while Georgian sources described him as a person of intelligence interest who could identify hostile networks.[^1]
- After multiple reported attempts on his life in Georgia, he and his family sought refuge in Germany.[^1]

## Assassination in Berlin (2019)

- On 23 August 2019, he was shot and killed in Kleiner Tiergarten, Berlin, by an assailant who approached on a bicycle and used a suppressed handgun.[^1]
- The suspect was detained shortly after the shooting, and subsequent investigations identified him as Vadim Krasikov, described in public reporting as an operative linked to Russian security services.[^1]

## Aftermath and Significance

- The killing became a major diplomatic and security case in Germany because it was treated as a likely state-directed assassination on German soil.[^1]
- The case is often cited in discussions of targeted killings, counter-intelligence disputes, and the risks faced by exiled figures from conflict zones.
