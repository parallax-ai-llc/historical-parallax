## Summary

Jin Jong-oh won four Olympic gold medals in shooting, including consecutive 50m pistol golds at 2008 and 2012.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2004 | Won Olympic silver (Athens) in men's 50 m pistol |
| 2008 | Won Olympic gold (Beijing) in men's 50 m pistol; also won gold in 10 m air pistol |
| 2012 | Won Olympic gold (London) in men's 50 m pistol for a second consecutive time |
| 2016 | Competed again at the Olympics as one of the sport's most decorated shooters |

## Disciplines

Jin is associated mainly with pistol events, especially 50 m pistol and 10 m air pistol. These events reward extreme consistency in stance, trigger control, and mental focus under time pressure.

## Olympic Significance

Winning multiple gold medals across different Games placed Jin among the most successful pistol shooters in Olympic history. Consecutive titles in the same event are particularly difficult because of small performance margins and the high variance inherent in precision sports.

## Training and Technique (High Level)

Elite pistol shooting depends on repeatable mechanics: stable posture, breathing rhythm, sight alignment, and a smooth trigger press. Athletes also train psychological routines to manage heart rate and maintain concentration through long qualification rounds and finals.

## Impact in South Korea

Jin's results contributed to South Korea's strong reputation in Olympic shooting. Medal-winning shooters often become influential in national sports programs, helping raise participation and providing a model for professionalized training pathways.

## Beyond the Olympics

Top shooters typically build careers that include World Cup circuits and world championship events in addition to the Games. Jin's profile is often discussed as an example of sustained excellence across many competition cycles rather than a single peak performance.

In sport history summaries, he is frequently listed among South Korea's most accomplished Olympic medalists.
