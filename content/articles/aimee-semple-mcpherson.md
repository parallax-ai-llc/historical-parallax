## Summary

Aimee Semple McPherson was a Canadian-American evangelist and the founder of the International Church of the Foursquare Gospel. She was one of the most famous evangelists of the 1920s and 1930s, pioneering the use of radio for religious broadcasting. She built the 5,300-seat Angelus Temple in Los Angeles. Her dramatic style and alleged kidnapping made her a major public figure.
