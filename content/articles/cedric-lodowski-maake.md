## Summary

South African serial killer known as the Nasrec Strangler who killed at least 27 people in Johannesburg between 1996 and 1997. His victims included both men and women attacked at random.

## Crimes and modus operandi

Media and court reporting described a pattern of opportunistic attacks in the Johannesburg area during 1996–1997.[^1] The nickname **“Nasrec Strangler”** refers to the Nasrec area near Johannesburg, where a number of incidents were linked to the same offender.[^1] Accounts commonly summarize:

- victims of different ages and genders,
- attacks that appeared random and opportunistic,
- methods involving strangulation and blunt force in several cases.[^1]

## Investigation and arrest

Police connected multiple cases through investigative work and witness information, eventually identifying Maake as a suspect.[^1] He was arrested in 1997 and charged in relation to numerous killings and additional violent offenses.

## Trial, conviction, and sentencing

Maake was tried in South Africa and convicted in 2000.[^1] His sentence is often reported as the **equivalent of 1,340 years**, reflecting consecutive terms across multiple counts rather than a single fixed “years in prison” figure.[^1]

## Significance

The case is frequently cited in South African crime history because of the high number of victims attributed to a single offender within a relatively short period and the public anxiety created by apparently random attacks.

## Notes on victim counts

Different summaries may cite different totals depending on how victims and counts are defined (e.g., confirmed murders vs. charges and convictions). High-level accounts generally emphasize the scale of violence attributed to Maake during the 1996–1997 period.[^1]
