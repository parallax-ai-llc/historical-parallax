## Context

- The Rabaa al-Adawiya sit-in formed after the July 2013 removal of President Mohamed Morsi and became one of the largest pro-Morsi protest encampments in Cairo.
- On 14 August 2013, Egyptian authorities moved to disperse the sit-ins at Rabaa al-Adawiya (Nasr City) and al-Nahda, resulting in mass casualties.

## Perpetrators (as a collective actor)

This page uses "perpetrators" as a **collective label** for the state security entities involved in the operation, rather than naming a single individual.

Commonly cited participants include:

- Ministry of Interior forces (including police and Central Security Forces)
- Supporting state security units and, in some accounts, elements coordinating with the armed forces

## Findings and documentation

- Human Rights Watch described the Rabaa dispersal as one of the world's largest killings of demonstrators in a single day in recent history and called for criminal accountability for those responsible for planning and ordering the operation.[^2]
- International and regional coverage frequently notes the operation's scale, the use of live ammunition, and the limited avenues for independent investigation inside Egypt at the time.

## Aftermath and accountability

- Egyptian authorities framed the dispersal as a security operation; critics and human-rights organizations characterized it as a massacre and pushed for independent investigations.
- Debates about responsibility typically distinguish between **direct perpetrators** (units on the ground), **operational commanders**, and **political decision-makers** who authorized or oversaw the dispersal.
