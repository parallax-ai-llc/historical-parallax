## Summary

Harold Varmus is an American Nobel Prize-winning scientist who shared the 1989 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine with J. Michael Bishop for their discovery that normal cells contain genes (proto-oncogenes) that can cause cancer when mutated. This discovery revolutionized cancer biology. He served as director of the National Institutes of Health and the National Cancer Institute.
