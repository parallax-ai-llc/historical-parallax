## Summary

Philip Neri was an Italian priest and mystic who founded the Congregation of the Oratory and is known as the 'Apostle of Rome.' He was famous for his humor, joy, and unconventional approach to spirituality. He used music, poetry, and informal discussions to attract people to the faith. He is the patron saint of joy and humor. His Oratory movement became an important force in Catholic renewal.
