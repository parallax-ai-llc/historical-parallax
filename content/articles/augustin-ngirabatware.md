## Summary

Rwandan minister of planning during the genocide who distributed weapons to militia and directed killings in his home prefecture. He was convicted of genocide and incitement by the ICTR.

## Expanded overview

Augustin Ngirabatware is a Rwandan political figure associated with the former government during the period leading up to and during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi in Rwanda.

## Government position and context

- Held a ministerial role in the interim government era referenced in many historical sources.
- The period involved organized propaganda, mobilization, and violence carried out by state-aligned actors and militias.

## ICTR conviction and legal record

Ngirabatware was tried at the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR) and was convicted on charges related to incitement and participation in genocide, according to publicly available tribunal judgments. Appeals and sentencing details are typically summarized from ICTR records.

## Broader significance

The case is frequently discussed in relation to international law on incitement, particularly the role of speeches, meetings, and directives in enabling mass violence.
