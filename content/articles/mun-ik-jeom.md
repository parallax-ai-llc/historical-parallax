## Summary

Mun Ik-jeom was a late Goryeo Dynasty scholar who secretly brought cotton seeds from China to Korea in 1363, revolutionizing Korean textile production. He hid the seeds in his writing brush tube. His technological transfer enabled Korea to produce cotton clothing for the first time, transforming Korean material culture and economy.
