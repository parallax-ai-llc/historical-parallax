## Summary

Aaron Klug was a Lithuanian-born British chemist and biophysicist who won the 1982 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his development of crystallographic electron microscopy and his structural elucidation of biologically important nucleic acid-protein complexes. He determined the structure of the tobacco mosaic virus and transfer RNA. He also served as president of the Royal Society.
