## Summary

Russian arms dealer known as the Merchant of Death who supplied weapons to conflicts in Africa, Asia, and South America through a fleet of cargo planes. He was the inspiration for the film Lord of War.

## Background (High Level)

Bout was linked by investigators and journalists to networks that moved weapons and other cargo using aircraft registered through a web of companies and jurisdictions.
His operations were often described as taking advantage of the post–Cold War environment, including access to surplus arms and aircraft and the demand created by civil wars.

## Arrest, Trial, and Sentence

In 2008, Bout was arrested in Thailand following a U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) sting operation.
He was extradited to the United States, prosecuted, and convicted on charges including conspiracy to kill U.S. nationals and officers and conspiracy to provide material support to a terrorist organization.
A U.S. federal court sentenced him to 25 years in prison.

## Release and Public Attention

In 2022, Bout returned to Russia as part of a prisoner exchange.
His notoriety also grew through popular culture references and comparisons to fictionalized arms dealers, though the details of such portrayals differ from court records and reporting.
