## Summary

Jackie Joyner-Kersee holds the heptathlon world record of 7,291 points set in 1988. She won six Olympic medals and was named Greatest Female Athlete of the 20th Century.

## Athletic Profile

Joyner-Kersee is best known for excellence in two demanding disciplines: the heptathlon (a seven-event combined competition) and the long jump. Her heptathlon record from 1988 remains one of the sport’s landmark performances and is often used as a reference point for the modern era.

## Major Championships

Across multiple Olympic cycles, she combined consistency with peak performances, earning medals in both combined events and long jump. Her career is frequently highlighted for longevity at the top level and for competing through injuries while remaining among the best in the world.

## Work Beyond Competition

After her peak competitive years, Joyner-Kersee became associated with youth and community initiatives in the United States, particularly in her home region. She is also referenced in discussions about women’s sports and the professionalization and visibility of track and field.
