## Summary

César Milstein was an Argentine-British biochemist who shared the 1984 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for developing the technique to produce monoclonal antibodies. This revolutionary technique, developed with Georges Köhler, has become essential in medical diagnostics, treatment of cancers and autoimmune diseases, and biological research. He chose not to patent the technique, making it freely available.
