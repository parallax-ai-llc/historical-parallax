## Background

Lalit Kumar Modi is an Indian businessman and former cricket administrator best known for his role in launching the Indian Premier League (IPL). The IPL’s franchise model, media-rights strategy, and emphasis on entertainment presentation became influential in later discussions about commercialization in cricket.

## Role in Indian cricket administration

Modi held senior positions within Indian cricket administration, including involvement with the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) and the Indian Cricket League/association structures referenced in mainstream summaries. His administrative work placed him at the intersection of governance, sponsorship, and broadcasting—areas that later became central to allegations of financial and procedural misconduct.

## Creation of the Indian Premier League

The IPL began in 2008 as a professional Twenty20 (T20) cricket tournament featuring city-based franchises. Its early success was driven by:

- A fast-paced match format suited to television scheduling.
- Aggressive sale of broadcast and sponsorship rights.
- Player auctions that created a high-profile transfer market.
- A league calendar designed to attract international stars.

## Controversies and allegations

Modi’s tenure became controversial amid accusations of financial irregularities and conflicts of interest. Public reporting and official inquiries have referenced issues such as franchise bidding processes, disclosure of ownership interests, and the handling of contracts and rights agreements. The BCCI ultimately imposed sanctions, including a lifetime ban as described in many summaries.

Because different proceedings have involved separate allegations and jurisdictions, summaries often distinguish between:

- Internal cricket governance actions (disciplinary findings and bans).
- Law-enforcement or regulatory investigations (which may proceed on different timelines).

## Exile and legal status

After disputes with cricket authorities intensified, Modi left India and has been reported to reside in the United Kingdom for extended periods. News coverage has periodically referenced ongoing legal and administrative matters, including requests for cooperation or extradition-related discussions, though the specifics have varied over time.

## Impact and legacy

Regardless of the controversies, Modi’s role in the IPL’s launch is frequently cited in sports-business analysis as a turning point for cricket’s commercial model. The IPL is often used as a case study in how leagues can:

- Monetize media rights at scale.
- Blend sport with entertainment programming.
- Create global demand through short-form formats and star talent.
