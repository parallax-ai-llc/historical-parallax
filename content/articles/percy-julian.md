## Summary

Percy Julian was an African-American research chemist who pioneered the chemical synthesis of medicinal drugs from plants. He was the first to synthesize physostigmine for glaucoma treatment and developed methods for mass-producing cortisone, steroids, and birth control pills from soybeans. Despite facing severe racial discrimination, he became one of the most influential chemists of the 20th century.
