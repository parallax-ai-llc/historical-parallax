## Summary

Arthur Compton was an American physicist who won the 1927 Nobel Prize in Physics for his discovery of the Compton effect, demonstrating the particle nature of electromagnetic radiation. His experiment provided key evidence for the quantum theory of light. He also played a major role in the Manhattan Project, overseeing plutonium production. Compton later served as chancellor of Washington University in St. Louis.
