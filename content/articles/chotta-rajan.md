## Summary
Rajendra Sadashiv Nikalje, known as Chhota Rajan, was an Indian crime boss who split from Dawood Ibrahim's D-Company. He was arrested in Bali in 2015 and extradited to India.

## Background
- **Birth name:** Rajendra Sadashiv Nikalje
- **Common alias:** Chhota Rajan (often spelled "Chotta" in English transliterations)
- **Known for:** Prominence in the Mumbai underworld and later separation from Dawood Ibrahim's D-Company network.

## Notability and Associations (high level)
- Media and law-enforcement reporting commonly describe Rajan as a key figure in organized crime linked to **extortion, contract killings, and gang rivalries**.
- He is frequently discussed in connection with the broader ecosystem of **Mumbai organized crime** that drew national attention in the late 20th century.

## Split from D-Company
- Accounts generally place Rajan's break with Dawood Ibrahim's syndicate after the early-1990s period, when underworld alliances shifted and internal rifts intensified.
- After the split, Rajan is widely reported to have operated outside India for periods of time, with an emphasis on **avoiding capture** and maintaining a transnational footprint.

## Arrest and Extradition
- **2015:** Rajan was arrested in **Bali, Indonesia** and subsequently **extradited to India**.
- Following extradition, he was placed into Indian custody to face multiple pending criminal cases.

## Legal Proceedings (overview)
- Indian courts have pursued Rajan across a range of cases; reporting describes **multiple prosecutions** and long-running proceedings.
- Public coverage includes convictions in some matters, including a high-profile case involving the killing of journalist **J. Dey (2011)**.
