## Notability

Svartenbrandt is widely cited in Swedish media and popular accounts as one of the country's most notorious career criminals, associated particularly with bank robberies and repeated escapes from custody.

## Background

- Born in Sweden in 1945.
- Public attention around his life centers on his criminal activity and the repeated cycle of arrest, imprisonment, escape, and re-arrest.

## Criminal Activity (High-Level)

- Known primarily for bank robberies.
- Described in secondary sources as a prolific offender over multiple decades.
- Often discussed as an example of recidivism and the challenges of long-term incarceration and rehabilitation.

## Escapes and Incarceration

- Nicknames such as "the king of escape" reflect a reputation for multiple prison breaks.
- Accounts typically emphasize that several of his headline-making episodes involved escaping (or attempting to escape) from secure detention and then being captured again.

## Legacy and Cultural Impact

- His story is frequently referenced in Swedish true-crime discussions.
- The figure of a repeat-escaping bank robber has been used in commentary about corrections policy, prison security, and media sensationalism.
