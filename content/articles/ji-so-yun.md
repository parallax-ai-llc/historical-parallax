## Summary

Ji So-yun is South Korea's greatest female footballer, starring for Chelsea Women in the WSL with over 60 goals.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2010s | Played club football abroad and became a leading figure for the South Korea women's national team |

| 2015 | Named PFA Women's Players' Player of the Year (England) |
| 2018 | Won WSL title with Chelsea |
| 2020s | Continued as a key midfielder through Chelsea's domestic title runs |
| 2023 | Left Chelsea after a long tenure and significant contribution |

## Club Career (Chelsea)

At Chelsea, Ji was primarily used as an attacking midfielder with a role linking midfield buildup to final-third chance creation. Over multiple seasons she became one of the team's most recognizable players, noted for consistency and technical quality.

## International Career (South Korea)

Ji has been an important player for South Korea across major tournaments and qualification campaigns. Her career is often discussed in the context of the growth of women's football in South Korea and the increased visibility of Korean players in European leagues.

## Playing Style (High Level)

She is typically described as a creative central player with strong first touch, passing range, and the ability to arrive late in the box to score. Her influence is often measured not only by goals but also by assists and tempo-setting in possession.

## Legacy and Impact

Because of her long spell at a top English club and sustained international presence, she is frequently cited as a reference point for the modern era of South Korean women's football.

## Selected Recognitions

- PFA Women's Players' Player of the Year (2015)
- Multiple domestic titles with Chelsea during the WSL era
