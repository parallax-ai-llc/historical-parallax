## Summary

Luis Federico Leloir was an Argentine biochemist who won the 1970 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his discovery of sugar nucleotides and their role in the biosynthesis of carbohydrates. His work elucidated how organisms build complex sugars from simple sugars. He was the first Argentine Nobel laureate in Chemistry and conducted his groundbreaking research at the Fundación Instituto Campomar with modest funding.
