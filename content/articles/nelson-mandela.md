## Summary

Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela (1918-2013) was a South African anti-apartheid revolutionary, political leader, and philanthropist who served as President of South Africa from 1994 to 1999. The country's first Black head of state and first elected in a fully representative democratic election, Mandela focused on dismantling apartheid's legacy through racial reconciliation. After 27 years in prison for his anti-apartheid activities, Mandela became a global symbol of resistance to racial oppression. He received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1993 and is revered in South Africa as the "Father of the Nation." Mandela's transition from militant activist to reconciler, his dignity during imprisonment, and his forgiveness toward oppressors made him one of the 20th century's most admired figures.

## Early Life

Born July 18, 1918, Mvezo, South Africa. Given name "Rolihlahla" (troublemaker). English teacher gave him "Nelson."

Orphaned at 9 (father died), raised by regent of Thembu people. Educated at mission schools, Fort Hare University (expelled for student protest).

**Johannesburg:**
- Worked as law clerk
- Completed law degree by correspondence
- Opened first Black law firm in South Africa with Oliver Tambo

## Anti-Apartheid Activism

**Apartheid:**
- System of racial segregation and white supremacy (1948-1994)
- Blacks disenfranchised, forcibly relocated, systematically oppressed

**African National Congress (ANC):**
- Joined 1944
- Co-founded ANC Youth League
- Organized nonviolent resistance (strikes, boycotts)

**Sharpeville Massacre (1960):**
- Police killed 69 peaceful protesters
- ANC banned
- Mandela went underground, called "Black Pimpernel"

**Umkhonto we Sizwe ("Spear of the Nation"):**
- Founded armed wing of ANC (1961)
- Turned to sabotage after nonviolence failed
- Targeted infrastructure, not people

---

## Imprisonment (1962-1990)

**Arrest (1962):**
Captured after 17 months underground.

**Rivonia Trial (1963-1964):**
- Charged with conspiracy to overthrow state
- "I Have Cherished the Ideal" speech from dock:
> "I have cherished the ideal of a democratic and free society in which all persons live together in harmony and with equal opportunities. It is an ideal which I hope to live for and to achieve. But if needs be, it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die."
- Sentenced to life imprisonment

**Robben Island (1964-1982):**
- Hard labor in lime quarry
- 6x6 foot cell
- One visitor per year, one letter every 6 months
- Refused multiple offers of release in exchange for renouncing violence

**Pollsmoor Prison (1982-1988):**
- Transferred  to mainland prison
- International "Free Mandela" campaign intensified

**Victor Verster Prison (1988-1990):**
- House arrest conditions
- Secret negotiations with government began

## Release and Presidency

**Release (February 11, 1990):**
- President F.W. de Klerk released Mandela
- 71 years old after 27 years imprisoned
- Worldwide celebration

**Negotiations:**
- Led ANC in negotiations to end apartheid
- 1993: Nobel Peace Prize (shared with de Klerk)

**1994 Election:**
- First multi-racial democratic elections
- ANC won landslide
- **May 10, 1994**: Mandela inaugurated as President

**Presidency (1994-1999):**
- **Racial Reconciliation**: Promoted forgiveness over revenge
- **Truth and Reconciliation Commission**: Investigated apartheid crimes
- **Economic Policy**: Mixed results, inequality persisted
- **Constitution**: Progressive, guaranteed rights
- **HIV/AIDS**: Later criticized for slow response

**Rugby World Cup (1995):**
- Wore Springboks jersey (once symbol of apartheid)
- United nation through sport
- Depicted in *Invictus* film

## Retirement and Later Life

Stepped down 1999 (served one term by choice). Remained active in HIV/AIDS advocacy, children's rights, conflicts mediation.

- Six children

**Health Decline:**
- Recurring lung problems (TB during imprisonment)
- Multiple hospitalizations

**Death:**
December 5, 2013, age 95, Johannesburg. Respiratory infection.

**State Funeral:**
Attended by world leaders, 10-day national mourning, memorial at FNB Stadium.

## Legacy

**South Africa:**
- Father of the nation
- Symbol of forgiveness, reconciliation
- Prevented racial civil war

**Global Icon:**
- Symbol of anti-oppression struggle
- Moral authority
- "Madiba" (clan name) affectionately used

**Controversies:**
- Some criticize compromises during negotiations (economic inequality persists)
- Winnie Mandela's violent actions
- ANC corruption post-Mandela

**Honors:**
- 250+ honors including Nobel Peace Prize (1993), Presidential Medal of Freedom, Bharat Ratna (India)
- Nelson Mandela Day (July 18, his birthday)
