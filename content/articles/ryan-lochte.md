## Summary

Ryan Lochte won 12 Olympic medals including six gold, the second-most decorated American male swimmer after Phelps.

## Notability and competitive profile

Lochte was known for his versatility across multiple strokes and distances, with particular success in the individual medley events. His international career overlapped heavily with Michael Phelps and other members of the dominant U.S. men’s teams of the 2000s and early 2010s.

At the Olympics he collected medals in both individual and relay events, contributing to U.S. relay lineups while also winning individual titles. Across World Championships and other major meets, he accumulated additional medals that placed him among the most decorated swimmers of his era.

## Major highlights (high-level)

- Olympic medals: 12 total (6 gold), spread across multiple Games.
- Signature events: individual medley (200 m / 400 m) and relay participation for Team USA.
- Era: prominent figure in U.S. swimming during a period of unusually deep talent and intense internal competition.

## Public attention and legacy

Lochte’s career illustrates how elite swimming careers are shaped by both individual event specialization and relay selection within a strong national program. His medal count and longevity made him a recurring presence at major competitions for more than a decade.

He also drew substantial media attention beyond competition results, which affected public perception of his career in addition to his athletic accomplishments.
