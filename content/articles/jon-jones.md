## Summary

Jon Jones became the youngest UFC champion at 23 and is widely considered the greatest MMA fighter ever. He dominated the light heavyweight division before moving to heavyweight.

## Additional Context

- Jones is best known for a long, title-level run in the UFC's **light heavyweight** division and later competing at **heavyweight**.
- Commentary on his style often highlights a mix of wrestling, long-range striking, and clinch work, with particular emphasis on reach and tactical control.
- His career has also been shaped by **controversies and suspensions**, including issues related to anti-doping policy and legal incidents that affected scheduling and public perception.

## Legacy / Debate

- Many observers separate two narratives: (1) competitive results and skill, and (2) conduct and rule compliance.
- Discussions of "greatest of all time" in MMA frequently cite his achievements while also noting the complicating factor of disciplinary history.
- His move to heavyweight is often framed as an attempt to test his legacy against larger opponents and add a second divisional title.
