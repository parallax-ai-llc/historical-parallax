## Summary

Former Yunnan party secretary who accepted 246 million yuan in bribes over two decades. Received suspended death sentence.

## Positions held (high-level)

- Served as **Party Secretary of Yunnan** (the province's top CCP post) from **2001 to 2011**.[^1]
- Previously held senior posts in **Qinghai** including **Governor** (1997–1999) and **Party Secretary** (1999–2001).[^1]
- Earlier career included senior roles in **Inner Mongolia** and party-state management positions leading up to provincial leadership appointments.[^1]

## Investigation and discipline

- In **2014**, he was publicly announced as being investigated for **serious violations of discipline and law**.[^1]
- Following the investigation, he was **dismissed** from positions and later **expelled** from the Communist Party, according to open-source summaries.[^1]

## Conviction and sentence

- Open-source reporting describes convictions including **bribery** and **amassing wealth of unclear origin**.[^1]
- In **2016**, he received a **death sentence with a two-year reprieve** (commonly translated as a suspended death sentence), a penalty form used in China that can be commuted subject to conditions and subsequent rulings.[^1]

## Context

Bai Enpei is frequently cited in summaries of China's post-2012 anti-corruption campaign as a senior provincial-level official whose case involved unusually large alleged sums and a severe headline sentence.[^1]
