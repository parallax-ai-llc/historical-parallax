## Summary

Rwandan military officer considered the mastermind of the 1994 Rwandan genocide that killed over 800,000 Tutsi and moderate Hutu. Convicted by the ICTR of genocide and crimes against humanity. Died in prison in Mali.

## Military and Political Role

Bagosora was a senior officer in Rwanda’s armed forces and held positions connected to defense administration in the period leading up to the 1994 genocide. He is frequently discussed in historical accounts of the crisis as an influential figure within hardline circles of the then-government.

## 1994 Genocide and Command Responsibility

International court findings emphasized command responsibility and the use of state and military structures in enabling mass violence. In many summaries, Bagosora is described as having played a leading role during the immediate aftermath of the April 1994 assassination of President Juvénal Habyarimana, when the political and security situation rapidly deteriorated.

## ICTR Prosecution and Judgments

The International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR) prosecuted Bagosora for genocide, crimes against humanity, and war crimes. He was convicted, and his initial sentence was later modified on appeal (often summarized as a reduction from life imprisonment to a fixed term).

ICTR cases like Bagosora’s are frequently referenced in discussions of international criminal law, especially on standards of proof, individual accountability in mass-atrocity contexts, and how tribunals interpret leadership roles.

## Imprisonment and Death

Bagosora served his sentence in Mali under arrangements for enforcement of ICTR sentences. He died in custody in 2021.

## Historical Significance

Bagosora’s case remains one of the most cited ICTR proceedings because it is used to illustrate how international tribunals built evidentiary narratives about planning, coordination, and authority during the Rwandan genocide.
