## Background

Wu Dajing is a Chinese short track speed skater best known for sprint distances, particularly the 500 meters.
Short track is characterized by tight racing, frequent contact, and tactical positioning, where starts and passing strategy can decide outcomes.

## Olympic Highlights

At the 2018 Winter Olympics in PyeongChang, Wu won gold in the men’s 500m, a result widely reported as a landmark for China in the event.
In later Olympic competition, he also contributed to team and relay performances, reflecting the sport’s mix of individual and collective formats.

## Competitive Profile

- Often described as a powerful starter, Wu’s racing is associated with aggressive acceleration and maintaining speed through corners.
- His results have made him a recognizable figure in China’s winter-sports programs and a reference point for later skaters.
- In short-track narratives, he is frequently mentioned alongside the broader rise of Chinese performance in speed skating disciplines.

## Legacy
