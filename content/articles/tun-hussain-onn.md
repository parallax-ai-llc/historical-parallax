## Overview

Tun Hussein bin Dato' Onn (commonly known as Tun Hussein Onn) was a Malaysian politician who served as the **3rd Prime Minister of Malaysia** (1976–1981). He was widely associated with efforts to promote national cohesion in a multiethnic society and is often remembered with the honorific reputation of a "Father of Unity" in Malaysia.

## Early Life and Education

He was born in Johor and was the son of Onn Jaafar, a prominent Malay nationalist leader. Hussein Onn received formal schooling in Johor and later pursued military training, which shaped his early professional life before he became a full-time political figure.

## Military and Early Career

During the World War II era, he trained as an officer and served in the armed forces (commonly described in biographies as service connected to British-led military structures in the region). After his military period, he also worked in administrative and public-service roles before returning to politics.

## Political Career (UMNO and Government)

Hussein Onn’s political path is closely tied to Malaysia’s dominant post-independence coalition politics:

- He participated in early nationalist political movements associated with his father’s generation.
- He later returned to the United Malays National Organisation (UMNO) and re-entered parliamentary politics.
- By the mid-1970s, he held senior federal cabinet responsibilities and became **Deputy Prime Minister**.

## Prime Ministership (1976–1981)

Hussein Onn became Prime Minister following the death of Tun Abdul Razak. In office, he emphasized:

- **National unity and social stability** in the aftermath of major political and ethnic tensions of the preceding decade.
- Continued implementation of major national development and restructuring policies associated with the Barisan Nasional era.
- Strengthening of government institutions and administrative coherence, with a cautious, consensus-oriented political style.

He resigned in 1981, with health commonly cited as a contributing factor, and was succeeded by Mahathir Mohamad.

## Later Work and Legacy

After leaving office, Hussein Onn remained a respected elder statesman. He is associated with the establishment of Malaysia’s strategic-policy and international-studies capacity through the founding of **ISIS Malaysia** (Institute of Strategic and International Studies).

His public legacy is often framed around moderation, institutional governance, and efforts to maintain intercommunal trust.
