## Summary

Decentralized hacktivist collective that has targeted governments, corporations, and organizations worldwide. Known for Guy Fawkes masks and operations against Scientology, ISIS, and Russia.

## Overview

- "Anonymous" is a label used by a loose, decentralized network rather than a single organization with formal membership or leadership.[^1]
- Participation is typically organized through online forums and ad-hoc coordination, which makes attribution and continuity difficult.[^1]

## Notable operations (high-level)

- **Project Chanology (2008):** Protests and cyber actions against the Church of Scientology, which helped bring Anonymous into mainstream awareness.[^1]
- **Support and opposition campaigns:** Over time, the name has been used in operations targeting a wide variety of entities, including governments, corporations, and extremist groups.[^1]
- **Russia–Ukraine war context (2022):** Individuals using the Anonymous banner claimed cyber operations and data disclosures in opposition to the Russian government and related organizations.[^1]

## Tactics and identity

- Commonly associated tactics include website defacements, distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks, doxxing, and data leaks, though activity varies by faction and era.[^1]
- The Guy Fawkes mask (popularized by the graphic novel and film *V for Vendetta*) became a recognizable symbol in public demonstrations linked to Anonymous.[^1]

## Impact and controversy

- Public perception ranges from seeing Anonymous as digital civil disobedience to viewing it as criminal hacking; legal outcomes depend on specific acts and jurisdictions.
- Because the label is open to adoption, unrelated groups and individuals may claim responsibility for actions, complicating verification and reporting.[^1]
