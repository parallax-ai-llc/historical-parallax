## Summary

Haile Gebrselassie won two Olympic 10,000m golds and set 27 world records. He broke the marathon world record twice.

## Additional Context

## Early Life and Rise

Gebrselassie grew up in Ethiopia and became part of the country’s emergence as a global powerhouse in distance running. His early international success helped establish a modern era of Ethiopian track dominance, contributing to a deep pipeline of elite runners.

## Track Career Highlights

He is best known for the 10,000 meters, where Olympic and world championship performances combined with world records made him one of the defining athletes of the event. His career also included major results on the track circuit in the 5,000 meters and indoor distances, reflecting versatility across long-distance formats.

## Transition to Road Racing and the Marathon

After years at the top of track racing, Gebrselassie shifted focus to road events. His marathon performances—including multiple world record improvements—were widely discussed as evidence that elite track speed could translate into record-setting road endurance when paired with specialized training and pacing strategy.

## Rivalries and Era

The period in which he competed featured strong head-to-head matchups with other leading distance runners. These rivalries were central to the sport’s visibility and to the rapid progression of world-class times during the late 1990s and 2000s.

## Impact Beyond Competition

Gebrselassie is often cited as a cultural and sporting icon in Ethiopia. Beyond athletics, he has been involved in business and public life, and he is frequently referenced in discussions of how superstar runners shaped the global perception of Ethiopian distance running.
