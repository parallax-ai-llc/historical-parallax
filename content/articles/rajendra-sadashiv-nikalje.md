## Summary

Indian gangster known as Chhota Rajan who ran a criminal empire from Southeast Asia and was involved in dozens of contract killings. He was a former D-Company associate who split from Dawood Ibrahim.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1988 | Split from D-Company |
| 2000 | Survived assassination attempt in Bangkok |
| 2015 | Arrested in Bali and deported to India |

## Background and Aliases

- Commonly known by the alias **Chhota Rajan**.
- Originated from Mumbai’s underworld milieu and rose through networks tied to contract killing and extortion.
- Became widely discussed in Indian media in the context of factional splits within organized-crime groups.

## Split with D-Company (High-Level)

- Nikalje was described as a former associate of **D-Company**, the network associated with Dawood Ibrahim.
- After the split, he was portrayed as part of a rival faction that claimed to oppose Ibrahim’s influence.
- This rivalry became a recurring frame for reports about attacks, reprisals, and underworld alignments.

## Law-Enforcement Focus

- Authorities in India linked him to a wide range of serious offenses typically associated with organized-crime activity, including:
  - alleged contract killings,
  - conspiracy and organized-crime charges,
  - extortion and intimidation.
- His 2015 arrest outside India and subsequent return to Indian custody was a major inflection point for ongoing cases.

## Why This Matters

- His story is frequently used as a case study for:
  - transnational mobility of organized-crime figures in South and Southeast Asia,
  - the role of factional conflict in criminal networks,
  - long-running investigations that span multiple jurisdictions.
