## Summary

Systematic torture and abuse of Iraqi prisoners by US military personnel at Abu Ghraib prison in 2003-2004. Photographs showing abuse shocked the world and damaged Americas reputation.

## Additional Context

## Setting

Abu Ghraib was a prison complex near Baghdad that became a major U.S.-run detention site after the 2003 invasion of Iraq. The abuses documented there occurred during a period of intense counterinsurgency operations, high detainee throughput, and contested interrogation practices.

## Exposure and Investigations

The public release of photographs in 2004 prompted global outrage. Multiple investigations followed, including U.S. military inquiries and high-profile reports that examined command climate, training, detention conditions, and the relationship between military police units and intelligence interrogations.

## Accountability and Legal Outcomes

Several enlisted U.S. personnel were court-martialed and convicted for specific acts of abuse. The scandal also triggered broader debates about responsibility up the chain of command and about the legal and ethical boundaries of interrogation policy.

## Strategic and Political Impact

Abu Ghraib became a defining symbol of the Iraq War for many observers and was cited as harming U.S. credibility and fueling anti-American sentiment. It remains a reference point in discussions about wartime detention, human rights, and oversight mechanisms.
