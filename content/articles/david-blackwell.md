## Summary

David Blackwell was the first African American elected to the National Academy of Sciences and one of the most influential statisticians of the 20th century. He made fundamental contributions to game theory, probability theory, Bayesian statistics, and information theory. The Rao-Blackwell theorem and Blackwell's theorem are named after him.
