## Summary

Heidi Klum is a German-American supermodel, television host, producer, and businesswoman who rose to global fame in the 1990s as one of the most recognizable faces in the fashion industry. Born in Bergisch Gladbach, Germany, she became the first German model to appear on the cover of the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue and earned a coveted spot among Victoria's Secret's elite "Angels." Her career trajectory shifted from runway and print modeling into media and entertainment, where she built a second identity as a television personality.

Beyond modeling, Klum is best known as the creator, host, and executive producer of Project Runway, the long-running fashion competition series that aired from 2004 to 2017 on Bravo and later on Lifetime. Her crossover success made her one of the few supermodels to successfully reinvent herself as a mainstream entertainment figure. She has remained a prominent cultural presence through television, business ventures, and a highly engaged social media following.

## Early Life & Background

Heidi Klum was born on June 1, 1973, in Bergisch Gladbach, a city near Cologne, West Germany. Her father, Günther Klum, worked as a cosmetics company executive, and her mother, Erna, was a hairdresser. She grew up in a middle-class household and showed an early interest in fashion and design. At age 18, she entered a modeling contest called "Model 92" — a nationwide search in Germany — and won out of approximately 25,000 entrants. The prize was a modeling contract with a major agency, which launched her career almost immediately.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Klum relocated to New York City in the early 1990s and quickly secured representation with Elite Model Management. By the mid-1990s, she had appeared on the covers of major international editions of Vogue, Elle, and Marie Claire. In 1998, she made history as the first German model to appear on the cover of the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue. That same year, she was signed by Victoria's Secret, becoming one of the brand's "Angels" — a role she held until 2010, walking in the annual fashion show for over a decade.

Her television career began in earnest in 2004 when she co-created and began hosting Project Runway alongside mentor Tim Gunn. The series became a critical and commercial hit, winning multiple Emmy Awards and helping to popularize the reality competition format in the fashion world. Klum herself won the Emmy for Outstanding Host for a Reality or Reality-Competition Program in 2013.

## Key Achievements

- First German model on the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue cover (1998)
- Victoria's Secret Angel from 1999 to 2010
- Co-created and hosted Project Runway (2004–2017), which won multiple Emmy Awards
- Emmy Award winner for Outstanding Host for a Reality or Reality-Competition Program (2013)
- Host and judge of Germany's Next Topmodel since 2006, a role she has maintained for over 20 seasons
- Launched her own fragrance line, jewelry collections, and clothing lines with retailers including New Balance and Lidl

## Controversies & Criticism

Klum has faced criticism on several occasions related to her hosting role on Germany's Next Topmodel, with former contestants and commentators alleging that the show's format promotes unhealthy body image standards and psychologically pressures young participants. In 2011, she drew media attention when she filed for divorce from singer Seal after seven years of marriage; the separation played out publicly and generated tabloid coverage. Her 2019 marriage to musician Tom Kaulitz, 17 years her junior, also attracted commentary, though neither event had lasting impact on her professional standing.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Heidi Klum's career represents a significant template for supermodel-to-media-mogul transitions. Her success with Project Runway demonstrated that fashion figures could command serious television audiences and production roles. She helped bring fashion industry culture into mainstream American households and mentored a generation of designers through her show. As a German-born figure who achieved peak visibility in American media, she also holds a symbolic role in transatlantic cultural exchange within the fashion world. Her continued presence on German television through Germany's Next Topmodel underscores her sustained relevance across multiple markets.
