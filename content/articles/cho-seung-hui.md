## Summary
Already covered as Seung-Hui Cho.

Cho Seung-hui (Korean: 조승희), also known as Seung-Hui Cho, was a South Korean-born U.S. resident who carried out the Virginia Tech shooting on April 16, 2007 in Blacksburg, Virginia, killing 32 people and injuring 17 others before dying by suicide. It remains among the deadliest mass shootings by a single perpetrator on a U.S. college campus.

This entry summarizes high-level, widely reported facts about the incident, his background as a Virginia Tech student, and the major institutional and policy responses that followed.

## Background
- **Residency**: South Korean-born; later lived in the United States.
- **Education**: Attended Virginia Tech as a student.
- **Prior concerns**: Multiple accounts describe troubling behavior and interventions prior to the attack, including contact with campus authorities and mental-health services (details vary across reports).

## Virginia Tech shooting (2007)
- **Date / location**: April 16, 2007; Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University (Virginia Tech), Blacksburg, Virginia.
- **Casualties**: 32 people killed; 17 injured; the perpetrator died by suicide.
- **Structure of the incident**: Widely reported as occurring in two phases on campus, with the later, deadlier phase occurring inside an academic building.
- **Media materials**: The perpetrator mailed a package containing writings and recordings to a news organization during the incident timeline (reported by major media outlets).

## Aftermath and impact
- **Institutional response**: Virginia Tech and Virginia authorities conducted reviews of emergency response, communications, and campus safety procedures.
- **Threat assessment**: The case became a frequently cited reference point for campus threat assessment teams and early-intervention programs.
- **Policy debate**: The incident intensified U.S. debates over gun laws, background checks, and the intersection of mental-health systems and firearm access.
- **Memorialization**: The university held major memorial events and established permanent remembrance efforts for the victims.

## Key facts (high-level)
- The event is commonly listed among the deadliest mass shootings in modern U.S. history.
- Cho is typically described in reporting as having a history of social isolation and disturbing communications prior to the attack.
- Because many details remain contested or interpreted differently across sources, summaries often focus on the confirmed timeline and casualty counts.
