## Summary

Jean Baptiste Perrin was a French physicist who won the 1926 Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on Brownian motion, which confirmed the existence of atoms and molecules. His experiments provided definitive proof of atomic theory, settling a debate that had lasted centuries.
