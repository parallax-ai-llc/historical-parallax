## Summary

Former Albanian president and PM placed under house arrest for corruption.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

## Key Roles

- Leader, Democratic Party of Albania (DP) (1991–2013; later returned as a central figure in party disputes)
- President of Albania (1992–1997)
- Prime Minister of Albania (2005–2013)

## Background (High-Level)

- Trained as a physician (cardiology) and entered politics during Albania’s transition away from one-party rule.
- Rose quickly within the opposition movement in the early 1990s, becoming a defining figure in post-communist Albanian politics.

## Controversies and Legal/Sanctions Context

- Frequently associated in reporting with allegations of corruption and abuse of power; he has publicly denied wrongdoing.
- International actions have included public designations/sanctions announcements (notably by the United States) citing corruption allegations.
- Albanian legal proceedings and investigations have been reported in connection with corruption-related cases; outcomes can change over time.

## Why He Matters

- Central actor in Albania’s democratic transition period and a long-running partisan figure.
- His leadership periods coincide with major political and institutional developments, including elections, reforms, and EU/NATO-related foreign-policy alignment debates.
