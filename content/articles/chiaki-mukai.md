## Summary

Chiaki Mukai was the first Japanese woman in space, flying aboard the Space Shuttle Columbia in 1994. A cardiovascular surgeon, she conducted medical experiments in space and flew again in 1998, becoming the first Japanese citizen to fly in space twice. Her research focused on the effects of microgravity on the human body.
