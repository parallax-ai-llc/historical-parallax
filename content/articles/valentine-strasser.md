## Summary

World's youngest head of state when he seized power in Sierra Leone at age 25. His brief rule was marked by human rights abuses before being overthrown by his deputy.

## Additional Context

## Rise to power

Strasser emerged from the Sierra Leone Army amid political and economic instability and the growing pressure of the civil war that began in 1991. In 1992 he and other junior officers led a coup that replaced the existing government and established a military regime.[^1]

## Governance and the civil war

His government attempted to prosecute the war against the Revolutionary United Front (RUF) while also facing criticism for abuses and failures of administration. The period is often described in terms of weak state capacity, factionalism inside the armed forces, and the broader regional dynamics of the conflict.[^1]

## Transition attempts and removal

In the mid-1990s, under domestic and international pressure, the regime moved toward elections. Strasser was removed in an internal coup led by his deputy, Julius Maada Bio, who then oversaw a transition that resulted in civilian elections in 1996.[^1]

## After power

Strasser’s later life has been reported as relatively private compared with the notoriety of his early rule. Public discussion of his legacy typically focuses on the extraordinary fact of his age in office and the violent context of Sierra Leone’s civil war.[^1]
