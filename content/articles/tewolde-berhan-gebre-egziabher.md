## Summary

Tewolde Berhan Gebre Egziabher is an Ethiopian ecologist who played a crucial role in negotiating the Cartagena Protocol on Biosafety, protecting developing countries' rights regarding genetically modified organisms. He won the Right Livelihood Award in 2000 for his advocacy of biodiversity and food security. He has been a leading African voice in international environmental negotiations.
