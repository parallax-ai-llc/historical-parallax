Paolo Maldini spent 25 years at AC Milan, winning five CLs. He is the greatest defender in football history.

## Playing Style and Role

Maldini played primarily as a left-back and center-back and was known for positioning, tackling timing, and reading of the game rather than relying on fouls. He spent his entire senior club career with **AC Milan**, serving as captain for many seasons.

## Major Honors (High Level)

Across domestic and European competition, Maldini's Milan teams won multiple **Serie A** titles and **European Cups/UEFA Champions League** titles. His longevity at the top level (spanning the late 1980s through the 2000s) is a defining feature of his career.

## Italy National Team

He earned a large number of appearances for **Italy** and captained the national side. He played in multiple major tournaments, including FIFA World Cups and UEFA European Championships, and was part of the team that finished runner-up at the **1994 World Cup**.

## After Playing

Following retirement, he remained associated with Milan in leadership roles, including work in club management and sporting direction. His career is often referenced in discussions of elite defensive play and one-club player legacies.

## Recognition

Across his career he was regularly included in domestic and international "team of the year" selections and is frequently cited among the most influential defenders of the modern era. His family connection to professional football (including his father Cesare Maldini) is also part of his public narrative.
