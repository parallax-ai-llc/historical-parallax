## Summary

South Korean businessman and cult leader whose family owned the ferry Sewol that sank killing 304 people. He died as a fugitive while being hunted by thousands of police.

## Background

Yoo Byung-eun was a South Korean businessman connected to religious and corporate entities that, according to public summaries, played roles in his wealth and influence.

He is widely associated with the group often referred to as the Evangelical Baptist Church of Korea (sometimes called “Guwonpa”).

## Connection to the Sewol Disaster

The sinking of the MV Sewol in April 2014 became a national tragedy in South Korea, with 304 deaths.

In the public aftermath, scrutiny focused on ownership, operating practices, and regulatory oversight, with Yoo and related companies becoming central targets of investigation.

## Investigation and Manhunt

Wikipedia describes a large-scale manhunt as authorities sought Yoo following the disaster.

The pursuit became notable not only for the number of personnel involved but also for the intensity of public attention and media coverage.

## Death and Aftermath

Yoo was later found dead in 2014; the circumstances of his death were widely discussed at the time.

His death did not end broader investigations into corporate structures, accountability for safety failures, and the regulatory environment surrounding passenger ferry operations.

## Why the Case Matters

The Yoo Byung-eun story is often cited in discussions about:

- Corporate governance and opaque ownership structures.
- Safety culture and regulatory enforcement in transportation.
- How crises can expose longstanding institutional weaknesses.
