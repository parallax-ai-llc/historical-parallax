## Summary

Al-Zarqali (Arzachel) was an Andalusian astronomer and instrument maker who developed an improved astrolabe (the Saphaea) that could be used at any latitude. He compiled the Toledan Tables, the most accurate astronomical tables of the medieval world. He also discovered the motion of the solar apogee and calculated the year's length with great precision.
