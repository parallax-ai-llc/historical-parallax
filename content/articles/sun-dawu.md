## Background

Sun Dawu (孙大午; born 1954) is a Chinese entrepreneur associated with the Dawu Group, a large private enterprise that began in agriculture and later expanded into areas reported to include animal feed, real estate, education, and healthcare.

Reporting has described Sun as an advocate for farmers’ rights and rural land reform, and as someone who hosted and supported human-rights lawyers.

## Public Speech and Political Sensitivity

Coverage has highlighted that Sun sometimes expressed views critical of Chinese government policies, including by sharing commentary and liberal essays through company-linked channels. Accounts also describe prior administrative pressure on the company’s online publishing, including orders to remove content and temporary shutdowns.

## Detention, Trial, and Sentence (High Level)

Sun was detained in late 2020 alongside relatives and associates, with reporting linking the case to a land dispute. He was later charged and convicted on multiple counts; public summaries often single out “picking quarrels and provoking trouble” (sometimes translated as “provoking trouble”) among the charges. He received an 18-year prison sentence in 2021.

## Why the Case Drew Attention

Observers frequently framed the case as a signal about the risks faced by outspoken private business figures in China, especially when business disputes intersect with local authorities and politically sensitive advocacy.

## Dawu Group (Context)

Public profiles of the Dawu Group often emphasize its origins in small-scale poultry and livestock operations, later scaling into a diversified “company town”-style footprint in Hebei that reportedly included employee and community services. This corporate context is frequently used to explain why Sun’s detention drew attention beyond a typical private-business dispute.
