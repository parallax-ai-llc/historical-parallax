## Summary

Kukai (posthumously known as Kobo Daishi) was a Japanese Buddhist monk who founded the Shingon (True Word) school of esoteric Buddhism. He studied tantric Buddhism in China and brought it to Japan. He was also a poet, calligrapher, engineer, and one of Japan's greatest cultural figures. He is credited with inventing the kana syllabary and establishing the pilgrimage circuit of 88 temples on Shikoku.
