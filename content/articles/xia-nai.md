## Summary

Xia Nai was a Chinese archaeologist who directed the excavation of the Ming Dynasty Dingling tomb in 1956-1958, one of the most significant archaeological excavations in Chinese history. He served as director of the Institute of Archaeology at the Chinese Academy of Sciences and helped establish modern archaeological methodology in China.
