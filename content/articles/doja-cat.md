## Summary

Doja Cat (born Amala Ratna Zandile Dlamini on October 21, 1995, in Los Angeles, California) is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter known for her genre-blending style, viral internet presence, and visually inventive artistry. She first attracted widespread attention with the novelty hit "Mooo!" in 2018 before establishing herself as a mainstream pop and hip-hop force with the albums *Hot Pink* (2019) and *Planet Her* (2021). A Grammy Award winner, she has placed multiple singles in the top ten of the Billboard Hot 100, including the number-one hit "Say So." Her 2023 album *Scarlet* marked a deliberate pivot toward harder rap, signaling a new artistic chapter.

## Early Life & Background

Amala Ratna Zandile Dlamini was born in the Tarzana neighborhood of Los Angeles to a South African father, actor Dumisani Dlamini, and a Jewish American mother, painter Deborah Elizabeth Sawyer. Though her father is a well-known actor in South Africa, he was largely absent from her upbringing. She was raised primarily by her mother in Los Angeles, where she began making and uploading music to SoundCloud as a teenager. She dropped out of high school to pursue music full-time, teaching herself production, singing, and rap through online resources.

## Rise to Fame

Doja Cat signed with Kemosabe Records and RCA Records in 2014 after her early SoundCloud tracks drew industry attention. Her debut EP *Purrr!* (2014) showcased a playful, genre-fluid approach. However, it was the 2018 release of "Mooo!" — a comedic song accompanied by a low-budget music video in which she rapped about being a cow — that made her a viral sensation. The track's absurdist humor spread rapidly across social media and established her reputation as an artist unafraid of internet culture.

Her 2019 album *Hot Pink* yielded the smash single "Say So," which reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 after a remix featuring Nicki Minaj was propelled by a TikTok dance trend. The follow-up *Planet Her* (2021) debuted at number two on the Billboard 200 and produced hits including "Kiss Me More" featuring SZA, "Need to Know," and "Woman." The album's Afrofuturist visual aesthetic and blend of pop, rap, R&B, and dance music earned widespread critical praise.

## Key Achievements

- "Say So" reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 (2020)
- Won **Grammy Award for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance** for "Kiss Me More" with SZA (2022 ceremony)
- *Planet Her* (2021) certified 2× platinum; debuted at number two on the Billboard 200
- Named to *Time* magazine's list of the 100 most influential people (2023)
- Multiple MTV Video Music Award wins, including Best New Artist (2021)
- Over 90 million monthly listeners on Spotify at her peak

## Controversies

Doja Cat's career has been punctuated by several public controversies. In 2020, a video surfaced of her participating in online chat rooms allegedly frequented by white supremacists, along with an older song titled "Dindu Nuffin" that critics characterized as mocking victims of police violence. She issued an apology, stating she had been naive but denying holding racist beliefs. In 2023, she clashed publicly with fans on social media after some criticized her relationship with musician Jxdn, leading her to change her Twitter display name and declare she did not care about her audience's opinions — a confrontation that caused some fans to unfollow her in large numbers. She has framed these episodes as expressions of her refusal to perform parasocial intimacy.

## The *Scarlet* Pivot

Her 2023 album *Scarlet* represented a sharp artistic turn. Largely abandoning the pop hooks and playful aesthetics of her earlier work, Doja Cat leaned into aggressive, bass-heavy rap production, with confrontational lyrics addressing her critics and the music industry. The album debuted at number two on the Billboard 200 and was received as a statement of artistic independence, though some pop-oriented fans were divided by the shift.

## Cultural Influence

Doja Cat's impact extends beyond music into digital culture. Her ability to generate viral moments — from "Mooo!" to TikTok-driven chart runs — has made her a case study in how internet-native artists can build and sustain mainstream careers. Her visual creativity, willingness to experiment across genres, and unapologetic online persona have influenced a generation of artists navigating the intersection of social media and the music industry.
