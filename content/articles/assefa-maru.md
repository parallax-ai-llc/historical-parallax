## Summary

Ethiopian military officer involved in the Derg regime's Red Terror campaign that killed thousands. The Ethiopian Federal High Court convicted numerous Derg officials of genocide in one of Africa's longest running war crimes trials.

## Historical Context

Assefa Maru is primarily discussed in relation to Ethiopia's Derg military government (197487) and the period commonly referred to as the **Red Terror** (late 1970s). During this era, large-scale political violence and repression targeted perceived opponents of the regime.

## Charges and Trial (High Level)

Ethiopia pursued prosecutions of Derg-era officials in a long-running set of cases often described as among the most extensive war-crimes trials conducted in Africa. The proceedings involved:

- allegations of crimes committed during the Red Terror period
- large numbers of defendants and lengthy evidentiary records
- verdicts that included **genocide** convictions in Ethiopian courts

## Why the Case Is Notable

The Derg trials are frequently referenced for their scope and for demonstrating a domestic (in-country) approach to accountability for past state violence. They also illustrate challenges common to transitional justice efforts, such as:

- evidentiary and documentation constraints decades after the events
- the complexity of attributing responsibility within security and party structures
- balancing retributive justice with broader reconciliation goals
