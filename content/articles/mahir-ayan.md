## Summary

Turkish Marxist militant who kidnapped NATO technicians and was killed in the subsequent military operation.

## Background

- Mahir Çayan (1946–1972) is commonly described as a Turkish Marxist militant and a key figure in the early-1970s armed left in Turkey.[^1]
- He is most often associated with the **People's Liberation Party-Front of Turkey (THKP-C)**, an organization described in secondary sources as an urban-guerrilla group.[^1]

## Political Activity (High-Level)

- In the late 1960s and early 1970s, segments of the Turkish left moved from legal/party and student organizing toward clandestine armed struggle; Çayan is frequently cited as one of the prominent proponents of this shift.[^1]
- Accounts emphasize a blend of ideological writing, organizational work, and participation in armed actions attributed to his group.[^1]

## 1972 Kızıldere Incident

- The best-known episode linked to Çayan is the **Kızıldere** incident (March 1972), involving the kidnapping of **three NATO technicians** (often reported as two British and one Canadian) and a subsequent raid/operation by Turkish security forces.[^1]
- Çayan and several militants were killed during the operation; sources also report that the hostages died in the same event.[^1]

## Legacy and Reception

- Within parts of the Turkish far left, Çayan has been treated as a revolutionary figure and martyr; in other framings he is characterized primarily through the lens of terrorism and political violence.[^1]
- His name and the organizations associated with him are frequently discussed in histories of Turkey's "long 1970s" political polarization and insurgency/counterinsurgency dynamics.[^1]
