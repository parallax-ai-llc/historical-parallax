## Summary

DEA agent who infiltrated the Guadalajara Cartel and was kidnapped, tortured, and murdered in 1985. His death prompted a major US response against Mexican drug trafficking.

## Service and assignment

- Enrique "Kiki" Camarena was a U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) special agent.
- He was assigned to work narcotics investigations in Mexico during a period of escalating cross-border drug trafficking.

## Kidnapping and aftermath

- Camarena was abducted in Guadalajara in February 1985 and later found dead.
- His killing became one of the most consequential attacks on a U.S. law-enforcement officer in the context of drug enforcement.

## Operation Leyenda

- The DEA launched "Operation Leyenda" ("Operation Legend") as a large-scale investigation in response to the murder.
- The case led to major arrests and long-running prosecutions connected to the Guadalajara Cartel.

## Broader impact

- Camarena's death intensified U.S.–Mexico cooperation and tensions around drug enforcement and sovereignty.
- The case is frequently cited in discussions of cartel power, corruption risks, and the dangers faced by investigators.

## Legacy

- "Red Ribbon Week" school drug-prevention campaigns in the United States are commonly associated with Camarena's memory.
