## Summary

Shan State drug lord known as Naw Kham who controlled drug trafficking along the Mekong River and piracy. He was executed in China for the massacre of 13 Chinese sailors.

## Context and Background

Sai Naw Kham (often rendered as Naw Kham) was a figure associated with organized crime and armed activity in the Golden Triangle region, an area spanning parts of Myanmar, Laos, and Thailand. Reporting commonly describes his network as combining drug trafficking with river piracy and intimidation along commercial routes on the Mekong River.

## Mekong River Incident and Regional Response

The 2011 killings of Chinese sailors on the Mekong became a major regional incident because it directly affected cross-border trade and China’s perceived security interests along the river. In subsequent years, authorities in multiple countries coordinated more visibly on river patrols and investigations, and the case was used to justify stronger law-enforcement cooperation in the region.

## Trial, Sentencing, and Significance

Naw Kham was tried in China and sentenced to death, with his execution reported as a high-profile conclusion to the case. Public coverage emphasized the message of deterrence and the reach of Chinese law enforcement when Chinese nationals were victims abroad.
