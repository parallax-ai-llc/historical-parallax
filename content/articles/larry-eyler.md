## Summary
Larry Eyler, the 'Interstate Killer,' was an American serial killer convicted of the 1984 murder of 15-year-old Danny Bridges and suspected of killing at least 21 young men across Indiana, Illinois, Kentucky, and Wisconsin between 1982 and 1984.

## Early Life
Born December 21, 1952, in Crawfordsville, Indiana. He had a troubled childhood and struggled with his sexuality. He worked as a house painter. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1982 and 1984, Eyler is suspected of killing at least 21 young men, leaving their bodies along highways in multiple states. He targeted young men, often runaways or hitchhikers. He was arrested in 1983 but released due to a search irregularity. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Eyler was finally arrested in August 1984 after the dismembered body of Danny Bridges was found in a dumpster outside his Chicago apartment. He was convicted and sentenced to death. He died of AIDS-related complications in prison on March 6, 1994. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case highlighted problems with inter-state serial crime coordination [^4]
- His premature release after the illegal search raised questions about legal procedures
- Led to improved multi-state investigation protocols

## Negative Perspectives
- Killed at least 21 young men [^5]
- Targeted vulnerable young men
- An illegal search led to his release, allowing further murders
- Operated across four states
- Mutilated victims' bodies
