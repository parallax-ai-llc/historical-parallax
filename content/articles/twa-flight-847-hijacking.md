## Summary

Hijacking of TWA Flight 847 by Hezbollah on June 14, 1985. The plane was diverted to Beirut where hijackers murdered US Navy diver Robert Stethem and held 39 passengers for 17 days.

## Additional Context

TWA Flight 847 was a 1985 hijacking of a Trans World Airlines passenger flight, part of a period when airline hijackings and hostage-taking were prominent international security crises.

### Key features of the incident

- The aircraft was diverted multiple times, and hostages were held over an extended period.
- The event received intense international media coverage and became a focus for U.S. counterterrorism policy debates.

### Historical significance

- The hijacking is often discussed in relation to the geopolitics of the Middle East in the 1980s and the rise of non-state armed groups using hostage tactics.
- It is also cited in the history of aviation security and crisis negotiation.
