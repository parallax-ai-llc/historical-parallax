## Summary

Akira Yoshino is a Japanese chemist who shared the 2019 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for the development of lithium-ion batteries. He created the first commercially viable lithium-ion battery in 1985, using Goodenough's cathode material with a carbon anode. His design became the standard for lithium-ion batteries, which now power smartphones, laptops, and electric vehicles worldwide.
