## Summary

Jacobus Henricus van 't Hoff was a Dutch physical chemist who won the first Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1901. He made foundational contributions to chemical kinetics, chemical equilibrium, and stereochemistry. He proposed the tetrahedral geometry of carbon atoms, laying the groundwork for modern stereochemistry. Van 't Hoff's work established physical chemistry as a distinct discipline.
