## Summary

Canadian convicted of murdering four members of a Muslim family in a deliberate truck attack.

## Background (high level)

- The June 2021 attack occurred in London, Ontario, Canada, and is widely described in reporting and court records as being motivated by anti-Muslim hatred.[^1]
- Canadian prosecutors pursued a terrorism allegation in connection with the killings, which has been described as a significant precedent in Canadian criminal law.[^1]

## Key events (supplement)

- **June 2021**: A pickup truck struck members of a Muslim family, killing four people and injuring one child.[^1]
- **2023–2024**: The case proceeded through a jury trial and public reporting focused on motive, planning, and the legal framing of the offense.[^1]
- **February 2024**: A jury found Veltman guilty of four counts of first-degree murder and one count of attempted murder; the verdict included a terrorism finding.[^1]
- **2024 (sentencing)**: Sentenced to life imprisonment with no parole eligibility for 25 years for the murder convictions, consistent with Canadian law for first-degree murder.[^1]

## Public impact / context

- The attack is frequently cited in discussions about Islamophobia in Canada and community safety.
- The case is also referenced in policy and legal commentary because of how terrorism provisions were applied to a mass-violence case without an international organization link.
