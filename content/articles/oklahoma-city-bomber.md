## Context

- **Event**: The Oklahoma City bombing was a domestic terrorist attack targeting a U.S. federal building in downtown Oklahoma City.
- **Casualties**: 168 people were killed, including children; hundreds were injured.
- **Significance**: It was the deadliest act of domestic terrorism in U.S. history prior to the September 11, 2001 attacks.

## What the label usually means

In popular and journalistic usage, **"Oklahoma City bomber"** is a *descriptor* rather than a unique personal name. It is typically used to refer to:

- **Timothy McVeigh** (primary perpetrator)
- **Terry Nichols** (co-conspirator who was also convicted)

Because this page is titled as a role/descriptor, the short summary above points readers to the better-scoped biography page for **Timothy McVeigh**.

## High-level overview (non-technical)

- The attack involved a **truck bomb** detonated outside the Alfred P. Murrah Federal Building.
- The blast caused major structural damage and triggered a large-scale emergency response.
- The investigation led to arrests and prosecutions in federal and state courts.

## Aftermath and impact

- **Investigations and prosecutions**: The case produced multiple criminal trials and appeals.
- **Security changes**: The attack influenced U.S. federal building security practices and public discussions about domestic extremism.
- **Public memory**: The Oklahoma City National Memorial & Museum was established to commemorate victims and document the event.
