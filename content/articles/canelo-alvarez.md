## Summary

Saúl "Canelo" Álvarez is a Mexican professional boxer widely regarded as the best pound-for-pound fighter in the world throughout the 2010s and 2020s. Born in Guadalajara, Jalisco, he turned professional at age 15 and built one of the most decorated careers in boxing history, capturing world championships in four weight divisions — super welterweight, middleweight, super middleweight, and light heavyweight.

His dominance in the super middleweight division, where he unified all four major world titles (WBA, WBC, IBF, WBO) in 2021, cemented his legacy as a generational talent. Canelo's combination of technical precision, power, and an enormous commercial profile made him the sport's biggest draw for over a decade, routinely headlining the highest-grossing boxing events in history.

## Early Life & Background

Saúl Álvarez was born on July 18, 1990, in Guadalajara, Jalisco, Mexico, the youngest of eight children in a family deeply immersed in boxing. His natural red hair earned him the nickname "Canelo" (Spanish for cinnamon). Growing up in Juanacatlán, a small town in Jalisco, he was introduced to the sport by his older brothers and began training seriously as a child. He dropped out of school at an early age to focus entirely on boxing, turning professional at just 15 years old in 2004.

## Rise to Prominence

Canelo compiled an impressive record as a teenager, capturing the WBC light middleweight title in 2011 at age 20. His international profile exploded on September 14, 2013, when he faced Floyd Mayweather Jr. in one of the most anticipated boxing matches in years. Though he lost via majority decision, the fight — which generated over 2.2 million pay-per-view buys — established him as a global superstar.

He rebounded with dominant performances, winning the WBC middleweight title by stopping Miguel Cotto in November 2015. A trilogy with Gennady Golovkin from 2017 to 2022 defined an era: the first bout ended in a controversial draw (2017), and Canelo won a disputed majority decision in the rematch (2018) before securing a clear decision victory in the third fight (2022).

## Key Achievements

- Unified super middleweight champion (WBA, WBC, IBF, WBO) — achieved November 2021 by defeating Caleb Plant via TKO
- World titles across four weight classes: super welterweight, middleweight, super middleweight, light heavyweight
- Defeated light heavyweight champion Sergey Kovalev by KO in November 2019 to claim his fourth divisional title
- Ring Magazine Fighter of the Year multiple times
- Signed a landmark $365 million, 11-fight deal with DAZN in 2018 — one of the largest sports contracts in history
- Professional record of 61 wins (39 by KO), 2 losses, and 2 draws through 2025

## Controversies & Criticism

Canelo served a six-month suspension in 2018 after testing positive for clenbuterol, a banned substance, though he attributed the result to contaminated Mexican beef — a claim VADA partially accepted. The two scorecards from his first fight with Golovkin drew widespread condemnation from media and fans who felt Golovkin had clearly won, fueling accusations of favoritism in judging. His lucrative DAZN deal later collapsed into a public legal dispute in 2020 over cancelled fights during the COVID-19 pandemic.

## Legacy & Influence

Canelo Álvarez elevated Mexican boxing to its greatest commercial heights since the era of Julio César Chávez. His success helped normalize massive pay-per-view pricing and streaming-based boxing distribution globally. As a four-division champion who consistently fought elite opponents in their primes, he is considered one of the greatest Mexican fighters of all time and a strong contender for all-time pound-for-pound rankings alongside Sugar Ray Robinson, Muhammad Ali, and Floyd Mayweather Jr.
