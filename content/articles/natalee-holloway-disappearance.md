## Summary

American teenager disappeared in Aruba after being seen leaving a bar with Joran van der Sloot. Her body was never found; van der Sloot later murdered another woman in Peru.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2005 | Natalee Holloway disappeared |

## Background

- Natalee Holloway was an 18-year-old from Alabama traveling to Aruba on a school graduation trip in May 2005.
- She was last seen in the early hours of May 30, 2005, after leaving a nightlife venue with several local young men.

## Key People and Entities

- **Natalee Holloway** — missing person.
- **Joran van der Sloot** — repeatedly identified as a key suspect; later convicted in an unrelated homicide in Peru.
- **Aruban authorities** — led the initial investigation, including searches and detentions.
- **U.S. and Dutch involvement** — diplomatic and law-enforcement interest increased due to the case’s international profile.

## Investigation and Searches (High Level)

- Extensive searches were conducted on the island and surrounding waters; no confirmed remains were recovered.
- Multiple detentions and releases occurred as investigators pursued leads; the case remained unresolved.
- Public tips, media reports, and intermittent claims of new evidence periodically renewed attention.

## Related Legal Developments

- Van der Sloot faced legal scrutiny connected to Holloway’s disappearance but was not convicted for it.
- In 2010, he murdered **Stephany Flores Ramírez** in Peru and was later convicted there.
- Separate U.S.-related proceedings connected to alleged extortion and information claims further kept the Holloway case in the news.

## Public Impact

- The disappearance became a major international media story, influencing public perceptions of tourist safety and high-profile missing-person investigations.
- The case is often cited in discussions about cross-border jurisdiction, evidence challenges, and the role of sustained media coverage.

## Open Questions

- Holloway’s exact fate and the location of any remains have not been confirmed publicly.
- Competing narratives and unverified claims have circulated for years; primary-source documentation remains central to credible summaries.
