## Background

Giovanni Motisi is associated with the Sicilian Mafia (Cosa Nostra) in Palermo. In reporting about Cosa Nostra leadership, he is often described as part of the post-Toto Riina era of bosses, operating under conditions of sustained law-enforcement pressure and fragmented internal power.

## Fugitive status

Motisi has been listed among Italy’s most-wanted fugitives and is widely described as having avoided capture for decades.

Common features of long-term fugitive cases in Italy include:

- reliance on trusted intermediaries for logistics and communications
- use of rural safe houses and short-distance movement to avoid detection
- periodic “search blitzes” and targeted raids by Italian police and Carabinieri

## Why the case is notable

- **Longevity**: remaining at large for many years makes the case a recurring benchmark in discussions of mafia fugitives.
- **Organizational resilience**: long-running manhunts highlight how criminal organizations can support evasion through networks of associates.
- **Public attention**: most-wanted lists and occasional media updates keep such cases in the public record even when there are few new details.
