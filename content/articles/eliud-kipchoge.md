## Summary

Eliud Kipchoge is the greatest marathoner ever, first to break 2 hours (1:59:40 in 2019). He won Olympic marathon gold in 2016 and 2021.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2016 | Won Olympic marathon gold |
| 2018 | Set marathon world record |
| 2019 | Broke 2-hour barrier with 1:59:40 |
| 2021 | Won second Olympic gold |

## Additional Context

- **Early career**: Kipchoge first became widely known as a track athlete, including winning the 5,000m at the 2003 World Championships as a teenager.
- **Move to the marathon**: He transitioned to road racing and the marathon later in his career, becoming dominant over the classic 42.195 km distance.

## Sub-2 Hour Milestone (Context)

- The 2019 1:59:40 performance was achieved in the INEOS 1:59 Challenge under special conditions (paced formation, controlled course, and support). It is widely recognized as historic, but it is not counted as an official world record under standard record-ratification rules.

## Training and Reputation

- Kipchoge is frequently associated with disciplined, high-volume training camps in Kenya and a reputation for consistency, calm racing strategy, and even pacing.

## Key Takeaways

- His legacy combines championship results (including Olympic gold) with record-setting performances and a symbolic “limits” narrative in endurance sport.

## Major Milestones (Selected)

- **2018 Berlin Marathon**: Set an official marathon world record (at the time).
- **Olympic success**: Won Olympic marathon gold in two consecutive Games (2016, 2021).
- **Course strategy**: Known for controlled pacing and negative-split style in several major races.

## Notes

- Records and “firsts” in marathon running are sensitive to course conditions and governing-body ratification rules; Kipchoge’s achievements are often discussed in both competitive and technological/experimental contexts.
