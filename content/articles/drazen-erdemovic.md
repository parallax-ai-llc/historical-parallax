## Summary

Bosnian Croat-Serb soldier who participated in the Srebrenica massacre and was the first person to plead guilty at the ICTY. He testified that he was ordered to shoot Bosniak prisoners. His testimony was crucial to establishing the massacre's facts.

## Background

Erdemović served in units associated with the Army of Republika Srpska during the Bosnian War. The events he later described involved the execution of Bosniak men and boys captured after the fall of Srebrenica in July 1995.

## ICTY Proceedings

Key features of his ICTY case and cooperation include:

- His guilty plea was among the earliest at the Tribunal and helped establish procedural precedent.
- He provided detailed testimony about the mechanics of mass executions, including chain-of-command issues and the role of coercion.
- His cooperation was cited as a factor in sentencing, alongside the gravity of the crimes.

## Historical Importance

- His testimony has been widely referenced in subsequent judicial findings about Srebrenica.
- The case is often discussed in debates over individual culpability, duress, and participation in atrocity crimes.
