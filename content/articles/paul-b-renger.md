## Overview

Paul Raymond Bérenger (born 26 March 1945) is a Mauritian politician and long-time leader of the Mauritian Militant Movement (MMM). He served as Prime Minister of Mauritius from 2003 to 2005, and has also held senior cabinet roles including Deputy Prime Minister, Minister of Finance, and foreign affairs portfolios across multiple administrations.[^1]

Bérenger is often noted for being the first Franco-Mauritian to serve as Prime Minister, reflecting Mauritius’s multi-ethnic political landscape and coalition-driven parliamentary system.[^1]

## Key Roles (selected)

- Prime Minister of Mauritius (30 Sep 2003 – 5 Jul 2005)[^1]
- Deputy Prime Minister (2000–2003; 1995–1997; and again from Nov 2024)[^1]
- Minister of Finance (1982–1983; 2000–2003)[^1]
- Minister of External/Foreign Affairs (1991–1993; 1995–1997)[^1]
- Leader of the Opposition (multiple periods, including 2005–2006 and 2014–2016)[^1]

## Education and Background

- Born in Curepipe (then British Mauritius).[^1]
- Studied at Bangor University (Wales).[^1]

## Political Party: MMM

The Mauritian Militant Movement (MMM) is one of Mauritius’s major political parties and has played a recurring role in coalition governments and opposition leadership. Bérenger has been closely associated with the party’s national strategy, including coalition negotiations that periodically shift governing majorities in the National Assembly.[^1]

## Notes on the 2003–2005 Premiership

Bérenger became Prime Minister in September 2003 following a coalition agreement and a mid-term rotation arrangement. His premiership ended in July 2005 after elections brought a change of government.[^1]
