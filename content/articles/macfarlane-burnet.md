## Summary

Macfarlane Burnet proposed the clonal selection theory of immunity, which explained how the immune system produces specific antibodies and develops tolerance to self. He shared the 1960 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine with Peter Medawar. His theoretical framework became the central paradigm of modern immunology.
