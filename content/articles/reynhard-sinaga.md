## Summary

Indonesian national convicted in the UK as Britain's most prolific rapist, drugging and sexually assaulting at least 48 men in Manchester between 2015 and 2017. Sentenced to life with a minimum of 30 years.

## Background

- Sinaga is an Indonesian national whose crimes were prosecuted in the United Kingdom.
- The case drew extensive attention because many victims were unknown to him and were targeted after nights out.

## Pattern of Offending (as described in reporting and court summaries)

- Prosecutors described a repeated pattern in which victims were brought to an apartment and incapacitated.
- Investigators relied on extensive digital material to establish the scope of offending and to identify victims.

## Investigation and Trial (high level)

- Police statements and media reporting characterized the inquiry as one of the largest rape investigations in UK history.
- Sinaga was convicted on numerous counts and received a life sentence with a long minimum term before any possibility of parole.

## Public Impact

- The case is frequently cited in discussions about:
  - underreporting of sexual assault against men,
  - nightlife safety and bystander intervention,
  - and investigative methods for identifying victims.
