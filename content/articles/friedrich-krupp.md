## Summary

Friedrich Krupp founded the Krupp steel works in Essen in 1811, laying the foundation for one of the most powerful industrial dynasties in German history. He spent years trying to produce high-quality cast steel to match British standards. Although he died in debt, his son Alfred transformed the company into Europe's largest industrial enterprise. The Krupp name became synonymous with German industrial might.
