## Summary

David Wineland is an American physicist who shared the 2012 Nobel Prize in Physics for ground-breaking experimental methods for measuring and manipulating individual quantum systems. He pioneered the use of trapped ions for quantum computing and developed the most accurate atomic clocks. His work laid the foundation for practical quantum computing.
