## Summary

Cameroonian president since 1982, one of the longest-ruling non-royal leaders in the world. His regime has been accused of election fraud, corruption, and human rights abuses including crackdowns in Anglophone regions.

## Governance and Political System

Biya's tenure is closely tied to the evolution of Cameroon's political system from a dominant-party state toward a formally multiparty system in which the incumbent party retained significant advantages. Observers have frequently pointed to the role of state resources, security services, and electoral administration in sustaining long-term incumbency.

## Anglophone Crisis

A major domestic challenge during Biya's later decades in office has been the conflict in Cameroon's English-speaking regions. What began as protests over legal and educational issues escalated into a broader political and security crisis, with accusations of abuses and displacement affecting civilians.

## Economic and International Relations

Cameroon has relied on a mixed economy with important roles for agriculture, oil, and public-sector employment. Biya's government has engaged with international partners and lenders on development and infrastructure projects, while criticism has persisted about corruption, transparency, and the distribution of economic gains.

## Criticism and Human-Rights Concerns

Human-rights organizations and political opponents have alleged restrictions on civil liberties, repression of dissent, and violence by security forces in conflict-affected areas. The government has disputed many allegations and emphasized security and territorial integrity.
