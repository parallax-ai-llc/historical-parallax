## Summary

Brazilian former president who was briefly arrested on corruption charges in 2019. He was accused of leading a criminal organization that embezzled hundreds of millions from state contracts. Charges were later suspended.

## Early Life and Political Rise

Temer was born in Tietê, São Paulo state, and built a career as a lawyer and academic before entering electoral politics. He became a long-serving figure within the Brazilian Democratic Movement (MDB), a centrist party that has often played a pivotal role in coalition governments.

## Vice Presidency and Path to the Presidency

As vice president under Dilma Rousseff, Temer occupied a constitutionally significant position during a period of deep political polarization and economic difficulty. Rousseff's 2016 impeachment transferred the presidency to Temer, making him head of state and government for the remainder of the term.

## Presidency (2016–2018) — High-Level Themes

- Pursued fiscal and labor-related reforms presented by his government as measures to stabilize public finances.
- Faced widespread public protests and low approval ratings amid continuing political turbulence.
- Governed through coalition-building in Congress, reflecting Brazil's fragmented party system.

## Corruption Allegations and Legal Proceedings

After leaving office, Temer faced investigations and legal actions tied to alleged corruption schemes involving state-linked contracts and intermediaries. In 2019 he was arrested and later released; subsequent procedural decisions affected the continuation of certain cases, illustrating how jurisdictional and evidentiary questions can shape outcomes in Brazil's judicial system.

## Legacy and Context

Temer's tenure is commonly discussed in relation to Brazil's post-impeachment transition, the broader anti-corruption wave that affected many political figures, and the long-term debate about economic reform and democratic legitimacy during crisis periods.
