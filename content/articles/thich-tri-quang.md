## Summary

Thich Tri Quang was a Vietnamese Buddhist monk who was one of the most politically influential religious figures in South Vietnam during the 1960s. He led the Buddhist crisis of 1963 that contributed to the overthrow of President Ngo Dinh Diem. He organized massive demonstrations against the South Vietnamese government and was a key figure in the Buddhist peace movement. He was characterized as the 'most dangerous opponent' of the Saigon government by some American officials.
