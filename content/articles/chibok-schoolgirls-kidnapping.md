## Summary

Kidnapping of 276 female students from Government Girls Secondary School in Chibok by Boko Haram on April 14, 2014. The incident sparked global outrage and the BringBackOurGirls campaign.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2014 | 276 girls kidnapped |
| 2017 | 82 released in negotiated deal |
| 2021 | Over 100 still missing |

## Background

- The abduction took place in Borno State, a region heavily affected by the Boko Haram insurgency.
- The students were at the school for examinations when militants attacked and transported many of them out of the town.

## International Response

- The hashtag and slogan **#BringBackOurGirls** became a major global advocacy campaign.
- Governments and international organizations offered intelligence, training, and other support to Nigeria in response to the crisis.

## Outcomes and Releases (High Level)

- Some of the girls escaped shortly after the attack, while others remained in captivity for years.
- Several groups of students were later released, including releases associated with negotiations and prisoner exchanges.
- The number of girls still missing has decreased over time, but reports have continued for years of those unaccounted for.

## Significance

- The kidnapping became a defining example of mass abductions in modern insurgency and terrorism.
- It spurred sustained attention to girls’ education, school safety, and the protection of civilians in conflict zones.

## Legacy

- The case remains a reference point in discussions of accountability, missing persons in conflict, and long-term support for survivors.
