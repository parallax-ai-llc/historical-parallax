## Summary

Park Ji-sung was the first Asian to win the Champions League (2008 with Manchester United). He starred in Korea's 2002 World Cup run.

## Club Career (High Level)

After establishing himself in South Korea and Japan, Park moved to Europe and became well known at **PSV Eindhoven** before joining **Manchester United** in 2005. At United, he was used across midfield roles and was valued for tactical discipline in major matches.

## Playing Style

Park was widely described as a high-work-rate, two-way midfielder: intense pressing, intelligent positioning, and willingness to play different roles to fit the team's system. This "utility" profile made him a frequent choice for games requiring defensive balance against elite opponents.

## International Career

He represented **South Korea** across multiple major tournaments, including the **2002 FIFA World Cup** (co-hosted by South Korea and Japan), where the team reached the semi-finals. He later captained the national team and remained a prominent figure for a generation of Korean footballers.

## Later Years and Legacy

Park also had spells at clubs such as **Queens Park Rangers** and returned briefly to PSV on loan before retiring. His success in the Premier League and Champions League is often referenced as a milestone for Asian players in top European leagues.

## After Retirement

After leaving top-level play, Park remained involved in football through ambassadorial and advisory roles, including association work connected to Manchester United and Korean football. He is frequently used as a reference point for professional pathways from the K League into elite European clubs.
