## Summary

Head of Niger's presidential guard who led the 2023 coup overthrowing President Bazoum. The coup triggered sanctions from ECOWAS and threats of military intervention.

## Background

- Tiani is a Nigerien military officer best known for his role in the Presidential Guard (also called the Republican Guard).
- Prior to 2023 he was a prominent security figure within the presidency, where the Guard was central to protecting the head of state and key institutions.
- Public biographical details are relatively limited compared with more overtly political leaders.

## 2023 coup d'état

- In late July 2023, elements of Niger's security forces detained President Mohamed Bazoum and announced the overthrow of his government.
- Tiani emerged as the public face of the junta and was presented as the leader of the coup authorities.
- The takeover ended a period in which Niger had been treated by several partners as a key security ally in the Sahel.

## Regional and international response

- ECOWAS condemned the coup, imposed sanctions, and issued ultimatums calling for Bazoum's reinstatement, including discussion of possible military action.
- Neighboring military-led governments in the region expressed support for Niger's junta, complicating regional diplomacy.
- The coup became part of a broader pattern of military takeovers in the Sahel during the 2020s.

## Governance and impact

- After the coup, the junta announced transitional arrangements and made changes to senior security and government posts.
- The political crisis contributed to economic disruption, diplomatic tension, and uncertainty around international security cooperation.
- Niger's internal politics and external partnerships continued to shift as the transition process and regional negotiations evolved.

## Additional context

### Position in Niger’s security apparatus

- The presidential guard has historically been among the most influential units in Niger because of its proximity to executive power.
- Leadership of the guard can translate into leverage over senior appointments, internal security decision-making, and crisis response.
- In many Sahel states, elite guard formations have been central actors in coups because they can quickly control leadership compounds and communications.

### Framing and messaging after the takeover

- Junta leaders typically justify seizures of power with claims of restoring security, ending corruption, or correcting governance failures.
- In Niger’s 2023 crisis, messaging emphasized sovereignty and rejection of external pressure, themes that resonated with some domestic audiences.
- Transitional authorities often seek legitimacy through public statements, symbolic appointments, and outreach to regional partners.

### Strategic stakes

- Niger’s location and security situation make it a focal point for counterinsurgency and regional stabilization efforts in the Sahel.
- Political instability can affect military coordination, aid flows, and civilian protection in areas facing militant violence.
- The crisis also intersected with debates about foreign basing, sanctions effectiveness, and the credibility of regional organizations.
