## Summary

Jean-Pierre Serre is a French mathematician who has made fundamental contributions to algebraic topology, algebraic geometry, and number theory. He was the youngest person to receive the Fields Medal (1954) and was the first recipient of the Abel Prize (2003). He is considered one of the greatest mathematicians alive.
