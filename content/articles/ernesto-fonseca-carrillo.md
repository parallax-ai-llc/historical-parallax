## Summary

Mexican drug lord known as 'Don Neto' who co-founded the Guadalajara Cartel. He was involved in the kidnapping and murder of DEA agent Camarena. Released from prison in 2016 due to age and health.

## Additional Context

- Fonseca Carrillo ("Don Neto") was one of the early power brokers in Mexico's modern drug trafficking networks, operating during a period of consolidation and expansion of transnational routes.
- The Guadalajara Cartel is often described as a precursor to later cartel structures, helping shape patterns of organization, corruption, and violence.

## Guadalajara Cartel (High Level)

- The 1985 kidnapping and murder of DEA agent Enrique "Kiki" Camarena became a major turning point in USMexico counternarcotics relations.

## Legacy

Fonseca Carrillo's prosecution and later house arrest are frequently cited in discussions about impunity, institutional corruption, and the long-running evolution of organized crime in Mexico.

The era he represents is often used to explain how fragmented successor organizations emerged after major arrests.
