## Summary

Indian national arrested in Pakistan and sentenced to death by a military court for alleged espionage. The International Court of Justice ordered Pakistan to review the sentence.

## Background

Kulbhushan Jadhav’s case sits at the intersection of India–Pakistan relations, military-justice systems, and diplomatic protection.

## Core dispute (high-level)

- Pakistan has alleged espionage and related activity.
- India has maintained he was denied adequate consular access and due process.
- The case became a prominent bilateral flashpoint, amplified by security tensions.

## International Court of Justice (ICJ)

The ICJ decision is commonly summarized as requiring Pakistan to provide effective review and reconsideration of the conviction and sentence, and to address consular-access obligations under international law.

## Why this matters

- Illustrates how individual criminal cases can become major diplomatic disputes.
- Shows the practical role of international adjudication when states disagree on process and rights.
