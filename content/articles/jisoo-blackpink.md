## Summary

Kim Ji-soo, known professionally as Jisoo, is a South Korean singer, actress, and model born on January 3, 1995, in Gunpo, South Korea. She is best known as a vocalist and visual of BLACKPINK, the globally recognized K-pop girl group formed by YG Entertainment in 2016. BLACKPINK became the first Korean girl group to perform at Coachella (2019) and to top the Billboard 200, with Jisoo serving as a central figure in the group's identity.

Beyond her work in BLACKPINK, Jisoo has established herself as a solo artist and actress. Her 2023 solo debut single album *ME* debuted at number one on the Gaon Album Chart and entered global charts, demonstrating her reach beyond the group. She is also recognized as a global brand ambassador for Dior, one of the most prestigious luxury fashion partnerships in the K-pop industry.

Jisoo's influence spans music, fashion, and entertainment, making her one of the most prominent Korean celebrities of her generation. Her transition into acting and solo music has underscored her versatility as an entertainer and her capacity to build a sustained career independent of the group.

## Early Life & Background

Jisoo was born Kim Ji-soo on January 3, 1995, in Gunpo, Gyeonggi Province, South Korea. She grew up in a supportive family environment and attended Hyehwa Girls' High School in Seoul. From a young age, she displayed interest in performance and entertainment, which led her to audition for YG Entertainment. She was accepted as a trainee in 2011 and spent approximately five years training before debuting with BLACKPINK in August 2016.

## Rise to Prominence

Jisoo debuted with BLACKPINK on August 8, 2016, alongside Jennie, Rosé, and Lisa, with the single albums *Square One* and *Square Two*. The group rapidly gained international attention, and Jisoo's role as the group's main Korean vocalist and visual brought her significant recognition. BLACKPINK's 2019 Coachella performances marked a historic moment as the first Korean act to headline the festival, amplifying all four members' global profiles.

In 2019, Jisoo was appointed as a global ambassador for Dior Beauty and later for Dior Fashion, cementing her status as a fashion icon. She made her acting debut in the 2021 JTBC drama *Snowdrop*, playing the female lead opposite Jung Hae-in, which further expanded her public profile.

## Key Achievements

- Co-credited on BLACKPINK's *The Album* (2020), which debuted at number two on the Billboard 200.
- Solo debut with *ME* (March 2023); the title track "Flower" peaked at number 35 on the Billboard Hot 100.
- Global brand ambassador for Dior since 2019, one of K-pop's most high-profile luxury fashion partnerships.
- Participated in BLACKPINK's 2022–2023 *Born Pink* world tour, one of the highest-grossing tours by a Korean act.

## Controversies & Criticism

Jisoo's 2021 drama *Snowdrop* attracted significant controversy prior to and during its broadcast. Critics and viewers alleged the show distorted historical events surrounding the 1987 pro-democracy movement in South Korea, with petitions calling for its cancellation gathering hundreds of thousands of signatures. JTBC and the production team defended the drama's fictional framing, and the show completed its run. The controversy drew attention to sensitivities around historical representation in Korean media.

## Legacy & Influence

Jisoo has helped shape the global visibility of K-pop girl groups, particularly through her fashion influence and solo career trajectory. Her Dior partnership is widely cited as elevating the standard for luxury brand collaborations in K-pop. As BLACKPINK members pursue increasingly independent ventures, Jisoo's successful solo debut and acting work position her as a multi-disciplinary entertainer with sustained relevance beyond the group's collective output.
