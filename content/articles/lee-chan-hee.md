## Summary

Lee Chan-hee was a Korean marine biologist who pioneered seaweed aquaculture research in South Korea. His work on laver (gim) and kelp cultivation techniques helped establish Korea as one of the world's leading seaweed producers. He developed improved cultivation methods that increased yields and quality of edible seaweed.
