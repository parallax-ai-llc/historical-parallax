## Summary

American hacker known as 'Pompompurin' who created and operated BreachForums, a major cybercrime marketplace for stolen data. He was arrested at age 20.

## Online Alias and Activity

Fitzpatrick has been publicly linked to the alias “Pompompurin,” associated with operating an online forum used for the trading and publication of compromised data. Such forums typically function as marketplaces and social hubs for cybercrime communities.

## BreachForums Context

- The platform was described as a successor-style venue after prior takedowns of similar forums.
- Users reportedly posted data leaks, sold access, and coordinated illegal services.
- Public reporting framed it as a high-visibility site that attracted both criminals and researchers monitoring the ecosystem.

## Law Enforcement Action

Authorities arrested Fitzpatrick and pursued federal charges. Court proceedings and sentencing (as reported) are frequently referenced in discussions of attribution, platform liability, and how cybercrime marketplaces are disrupted.

## Why It Matters

The case is used as an example of how individual operators can play outsized roles in enabling a broader criminal market, and how takedowns may create churn rather than permanently ending the underlying activity.


## Key Takeaways

- Forums centralize stolen-data distribution and reputation-building for criminals.
- Enforcement actions can disrupt platforms while the broader market adapts.
