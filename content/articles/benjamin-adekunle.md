## Summary

Nigerian military commander known as the 'Black Scorpion' who led brutal operations during the Biafran War. He was accused of ordering the killing of anything that moved in rebel territory.

## Early life and military formation

According to public biographical accounts, Adekunle was born in Kaduna and joined the Nigerian Army in the late 1950s.

He trained at the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst in the United Kingdom and was commissioned as an officer in 1960.

He also served in international assignments, including UN-related operations during the Congo Crisis.

## Role in the Nigerian Civil War

Adekunle became a prominent commander during the Nigerian Civil War (1967–1970), including leadership roles associated with amphibious and riverine operations in the Niger Delta region.

He is often described as a hard-charging field commander, and the nickname “Black Scorpion” is frequently attached to his wartime reputation.

## Controversy and allegations

Accounts of the Biafran War commonly note severe humanitarian consequences, including civilian suffering connected to fighting, blockades, and reprisals.

Adekunle’s name appears in historical discussions of wartime conduct, including allegations of brutality by forces under his command.

As with many civil-war-era figures, interpretations differ depending on the source and political vantage point.

## Later career and retirement

After the war, Adekunle continued in military service for several years before retiring in the 1970s.

His post-service public profile is often framed through the lens of the civil war and the controversies attached to it.

## Legacy

Adekunle remains a frequently cited example in Nigerian civil-war historiography of how tactical success, political aims, and human-rights concerns can collide during internal conflicts.
