## Summary

Jihadist groups including JNIM and ISGS have killed thousands across Mali, Burkina Faso, and Niger. Military coups in all three countries have failed to resolve the crisis.

## Background and Context

- The crisis emerged from instability in northern Mali in 2012, following the Tuareg rebellion and the collapse of state control in parts of the country.
- Armed Islamist groups expanded their influence through insurgency tactics, local alliances, and control of rural corridors.
- Over time, violence spread beyond Mali into Burkina Faso and Niger, with the tri-border Liptako-Gourma region becoming a persistent hotspot.

## Key Armed Actors (High Level)

- **JNIM (Jama’at Nusrat al-Islam wal-Muslimin)**: A coalition of al-Qaeda-aligned factions operating across the central Sahel.
- **ISGS / Islamic State in the Greater Sahara** (often referenced as an Islamic State branch in the region): A rival jihadist formation active especially in the tri-border area.
- **State forces and auxiliaries**: National militaries, local security units, and various community self-defense groups.

## Major Phases

- **2012–2013: Initial escalation in Mali**
  - Northern Mali saw rapid territorial losses and international attention.
- **2013–2014: International military intervention and reconfiguration**
  - French-led operations pushed back some territorial control but did not eliminate insurgent networks.
- **2015–2019: Regional spillover and intensification**
  - Attacks increased in central Mali and expanded into Burkina Faso and Niger.
- **2020s: Coups and shifting partnerships**
  - Mali (2020–2021), Burkina Faso (2022), and Niger (2023) experienced military takeovers amid persistent insecurity.

## Humanitarian and Governance Impact

- Large-scale displacement affected communities across the Sahel, with many fleeing rural areas due to attacks and intimidation.
- Civilian harm included massacres, targeted killings, and disruption of basic services.
- Insecurity constrained education, health delivery, and market access in many provinces.

## International and Regional Responses

- **MINUSMA** (UN mission in Mali, deployed 2013 and later withdrawn): provided stabilization support amid complex security conditions.
- **French operations** (including Operation Serval and Operation Barkhane): focused on counterterrorism and support to local forces.
- **G5 Sahel** initiatives: attempted coordination among regional states, with uneven capacity and political setbacks.

## Why the Crisis Proved Persistent (Commonly Cited Factors)

- Vast terrain and limited state presence outside major cities.
- Local grievances, intercommunal tensions, and competition over land and resources.
- Adaptive insurgent tactics (mobility, fragmentation, and exploitation of governance gaps).
