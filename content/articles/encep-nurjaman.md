## Summary

Birth name of Hambali, the Indonesian terrorist who was the chief operational planner for Jemaah Islamiyah's attacks across Southeast Asia. He remained in Guantánamo Bay for over 20 years.

## Alias and organizational ties

- Encep Nurjaman is better known by the alias "Hambali".
- He is associated with Jemaah Islamiyah (JI), a militant Islamist network active in parts of Southeast Asia.
- Reporting and legal filings have described him as a coordinator who moved between countries and facilitated funding, training, and operational planning.

## Major alleged activities (high level)

- He has been linked to planning and coordination for large-scale bombings in the region, including attacks in Indonesia.
- The extent of his involvement and responsibility has been a central issue in later legal proceedings.

## Detention and legal process

- After his arrest in Thailand in 2003, he was held by the United States and later transferred to the detention facility at Guantánamo Bay.
- His case has been handled through the U.S. military commission system, reflecting the post-9/11 legal framework for certain terrorism prosecutions.

## Significance

- Hambali is frequently referenced in analyses of transnational militant networks in Southeast Asia and their connections to global jihadist movements.
- The length of his detention and the pace of military commission proceedings have also been noted in broader debates about counterterrorism policy and due process.
