## Vulcanization (What It Solved)

Natural rubber is sticky in heat and brittle in cold. Goodyear's key breakthrough—**vulcanization**—uses sulfur and heat to create a more stable material that retains elasticity across a wider range of temperatures.

## Impact on Industry

- Enabled durable rubber goods used in **belts, hoses, seals, and waterproofing**.
- Became a foundational process for later **automotive** and industrial applications where reliable elastomers were essential.
- Helped shift rubber from a novelty material into a scalable, engineered product category.

## Career and Challenges

Goodyear is often remembered not only for the discovery itself but also for the long period of experimentation and financial hardship that accompanied it. His story is frequently cited as a case study in how invention and commercialization can diverge.

## Legacy

- The Goodyear name is strongly associated with rubber and tires; the later **Goodyear Tire & Rubber Company** was named in his honor rather than being a company he founded.
- Vulcanization remains a central concept in polymer and materials science, including modern variations for different rubber formulations.
