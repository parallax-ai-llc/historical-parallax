## Summary

Rwandan Army chief of staff during the 1994 genocide who directed military forces in killing Tutsi civilians. He was convicted by the ICTR of genocide and crimes against humanity. Sentenced to 30 years.

## Background

- Bizimungu was a senior officer in the Rwandan Armed Forces during the period surrounding the 1994 genocide.
- He is associated with the leadership structure of the former government forces after the outbreak of mass violence.

## International Criminal Accountability

- He was tried by the ICTR for crimes connected to the genocide, including allegations involving military authority and operations.
- The prosecution of senior military figures is often discussed as part of an effort to establish accountability beyond direct perpetrators.

## Significance

- His case is frequently cited in discussions of how chain-of-command structures can contribute to large-scale atrocities.
- The ICTR's work in cases like this shaped later expectations for documenting, prosecuting, and judging genocide-related crimes.

## Aftermath and Historical Discussion

- The outcomes of ICTR proceedings remain part of the historical record and are used in research on transitional justice.
- Public understanding of individual responsibility continues to be debated alongside the wider political and military context of 1994.
