## Background

- Saeed Mortazavi is an Iranian jurist and former prosecutor and judge who became prominent in Iran's judiciary in the late 1990s and 2000s.[^1]
- International and Iranian civil-society reporting has linked his prosecutorial work to major press closures and to abuses against detainees, particularly around the 2009 post-election protests.[^2][^3]

## Notable Roles and Actions (high level)

- **Press and publications**: Frequently associated with crackdowns on reformist newspapers and journalists, which contributed to his "Butcher of the Press" nickname in media coverage.[^1][^2]
- **Tehran prosecutor's office**: Served as a prosecutor in Tehran during a period that included high-profile political cases and mass detentions following contested elections.[^1][^3]
- **Kahrizak detention center**: Named in investigations and reporting related to the detention facility where several detainees died in custody in 2009.[^1][^3]

## Why He Is Covered

- Mortazavi is often used as a case study in discussions about **accountability in security and judicial institutions**, particularly when civilian deaths in custody are investigated years after the fact.[^1][^3]
- His career is also cited in analyses of **state pressure on the press** in Iran, including the use of prosecutions and closures against reformist media outlets.[^2][^3]
