## Summary

Masaccio was a revolutionary Early Renaissance painter whose brief career transformed Western art. His frescoes in the Brancacci Chapel demonstrated unprecedented mastery of linear perspective, chiaroscuro, and human anatomy. Despite dying at just 26, his innovations directly influenced Michelangelo and Raphael.
