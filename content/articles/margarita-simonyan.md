## Summary

Editor-in-chief of RT sanctioned for leading Russian state propaganda operations worldwide.

## Background

- Born in Krasnodar (Russian SFSR, Soviet Union) on 6 April 1980.[^1]
- Studied journalism at Kuban State University.[^1]
- Participated in the FLEX (Future Leaders Exchange) program as an exchange student in the United States (1995).[^1]

## Journalism (early career)

- Began working in journalism in the late 1990s and reported from Russia’s southern regions.[^1]
- Covered the Second Chechen War as a correspondent in the early 2000s.[^1]
- Reported on the 2004 Beslan school hostage crisis as a regional correspondent for the Rossiya TV channel.[^1]

## Leadership roles

- Appointed editor-in-chief of Russia Today (later rebranded as RT) in 2005 at age 25.[^1]
- Has been identified as editor-in-chief of RT and, additionally, the media group Rossiya Segodnya.[^1]
- RT launched its international broadcasting on 10 December 2005.[^1]

## Sanctions and designations (selected)

- The European Union sanctioned Simonyan in relation to her role in Russian state propaganda (2022–2023).[^1]
- The United Kingdom and Ukraine have also imposed sanctions connected to the Russian invasion of Ukraine.[^1]
- The United States sanctioned Simonyan in 2024 (U.S. allegations included election-interference activity).[^1]
