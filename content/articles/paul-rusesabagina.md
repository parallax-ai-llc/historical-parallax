## Summary

Hotel Rwanda hero convicted of terrorism charges widely seen as politically motivated.

## Background

- Rusesabagina is a Rwandan figure who became internationally known for his actions during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi, when he was the manager of Hôtel des Mille Collines in Kigali.[^1]
- His story informed the film *Hotel Rwanda* (2004), which portrayed him as sheltering and assisting people who sought refuge at the hotel.[^1]

## Public Profile and Advocacy

- After leaving Rwanda, he became a prominent critic of the Rwandan government and spoke publicly on human-rights issues and political repression.[^1]
- He received international recognition and awards in the 2000s, including honors in the United States.[^1]

## Arrest, Trial, and Detention

- In 2020, Rusesabagina was arrested and transferred to Rwanda, an episode that drew significant international attention and dispute over the circumstances of his travel and arrest.[^1]
- In 2021, a Rwandan court convicted him on terrorism-related charges connected to an opposition group; multiple governments and human-rights organizations criticized the proceedings and raised due-process concerns.[^1]
- In 2023, his sentence was commuted and he was released, after which he left Rwanda.[^1]

## Key Points

- Rusesabagina’s reputation is closely tied to competing narratives: international acclaim for his actions during the 1994 genocide, and later sharp political conflict with the Rwandan state.[^1]
- Coverage of his prosecution often focuses on (1) the alleged links to armed opposition activity and (2) whether the legal process met international fair-trial standards.[^1]
