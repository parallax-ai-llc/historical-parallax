## Expanded Overview

Xiomara Castro (Iris Xiomara Castro Sarmiento) is a Honduran politician who became the country’s first woman president after winning the 2021 general election and taking office in January 2022.[^1] She rose to national prominence during and after the 2009 political crisis in Honduras, in which her husband, President Manuel Zelaya, was removed from office by the military and sent into exile. In the years that followed, Castro became a leading figure associated with the political movement that opposed the coup and later consolidated into the LIBRE (Libertad y Refundación) party.

As president, Castro has governed in a polarized environment shaped by longstanding security challenges, migration pressures, public distrust of institutions, and concerns about corruption and impunity. Commentators often frame her administration in the context of Honduras’s broader struggle to strengthen the rule of law and reduce the influence of organized crime in politics. At the same time, assessments of her presidency vary widely across Honduras’s political spectrum.

## Background and Public Role

- **Public profile:** First lady of Honduras (as spouse of President Manuel Zelaya) prior to the 2009 crisis; later a recurring presidential candidate and party leader.[^1]
- **Political identity:** Commonly described as part of the Honduran left; her campaigns emphasized institutional “refoundation,” social policy, and anti-corruption themes.[^1]
