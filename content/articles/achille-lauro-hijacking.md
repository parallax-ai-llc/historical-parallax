## Summary

Hijacking of the Italian cruise ship Achille Lauro by the Palestine Liberation Front on October 7, 1985. The hijackers murdered wheelchair-bound American Leon Klinghoffer and threw him overboard.

## Additional Context

### What Happened

The *Achille Lauro* hijacking refers to the 1985 seizure of an Italian cruise ship by members of the Palestine Liberation Front (PLF). The incident became internationally known after the killing of passenger Leon Klinghoffer, an American citizen who used a wheelchair.

### Diplomatic and Legal Aftermath

The crisis triggered complex negotiations involving multiple governments, and it became a touchstone in discussions about counterterrorism, jurisdiction, and state responsibility. Several of the alleged perpetrators were later arrested and prosecuted in different jurisdictions over time.

### Why It Matters

The event is frequently cited in modern terrorism studies because it combined mass-hostage dynamics with global media attention and high-stakes diplomatic bargaining.
