## Summary

Brandon Sanderson is an American fantasy and science fiction author best known for creating the Cosmere, an interconnected universe spanning dozens of novels and short stories. Born in 1975 in Lincoln, Nebraska, he has become one of the most prolific and commercially successful authors in modern speculative fiction, with over 20 million books sold worldwide. His work is celebrated for its rigorously constructed magic systems, complex world-building, and carefully plotted narratives.

Sanderson rose to international prominence when he was chosen to complete Robert Jordan's legendary Wheel of Time series following Jordan's death in 2007. His own Stormlight Archive series, launched in 2010, has become a cornerstone of contemporary epic fantasy. Beyond his publishing output, Sanderson has cultivated an unusually direct relationship with his fanbase through transparency about his creative process, prolific online engagement, and a record-breaking Kickstarter campaign in 2022.

## Early Life & Background

Brandon Winn Sanderson was born on December 19, 1975, in Lincoln, Nebraska. A self-described reluctant reader as a child, he discovered a love of fantasy fiction at age 13 when a teacher handed him Barbara Hambly's *Dragonsbane*. That encounter redirected his academic and creative trajectory entirely. He enrolled at Brigham Young University (BYU) in Provo, Utah, initially studying biochemistry before switching to English. He served a two-year mission to Seoul, South Korea, for The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, an experience that broadened his worldview and fed directly into his interest in diverse cultural mythologies. He completed his undergraduate degree at BYU in 2000 and earned a Master of Fine Arts in creative writing from the same institution in 2004.

## Rise to Prominence

During his graduate studies, Sanderson wrote and shelved twelve novels before selling his thirteenth, *Elantris*, to Tor Books in 2003 (published 2005). His breakout came with *Mistborn: The Final Empire* in 2006, a novel distinguished by its internally consistent, rule-based magic system — a hallmark that would define his career. In December 2007, Harriet McDougal, widow of Robert Jordan, selected Sanderson to complete the Wheel of Time series after reviewing his work. The resulting three volumes — *The Gathering Storm* (2009), *Towers of Midnight* (2010), and *A Memory of Light* (2013) — were all New York Times bestsellers and introduced Sanderson to a global readership of millions.

## Key Achievements

- **The Stormlight Archive**: Four volumes released between 2010 and 2023, each exceeding 1,000 pages, with multiple entries debuting at #1 on the New York Times bestseller list.
- **Cosmere universe**: A shared fictional cosmos encompassing series including *Mistborn*, *Elantris*, *Warbreaker*, *The Stormlight Archive*, and *Elantris*.
- **2022 Kickstarter**: A campaign for four secret novels raised over $41 million from more than 185,000 backers, making it the most-funded publishing project in Kickstarter history at the time.
- **BYU lecturer**: Has taught creative writing at Brigham Young University since 2004, influencing a generation of genre writers.

## Controversies & Criticism

Sanderson's prose style has drawn criticism for being functional rather than literary, with some reviewers noting that his dialogue and character interiority can feel workmanlike compared to his structural ambition. His commitment to a conservative LDS lifestyle — he does not drink or use profanity, themes he largely excludes from his fiction — has occasionally generated debate about representation and the limits of his fictional worlds. Some readers have also questioned the pacing of very long novels, arguing that his books reward dedicated fans but present a steep barrier to casual readers.

## Legacy & Influence

Sanderson has materially reshaped reader expectations for world-building depth and authorial reliability in epic fantasy. His concept of "Sanderson's Laws of Magic" — a set of publicly articulated principles for constructing believable magic systems — has become widely cited in writing communities. His transparent communication with fans, including regular video updates on his YouTube channel and frequent Reddit engagement, has set a new standard for author-audience relationships in genre fiction. His Kickstarter success demonstrated the viability of direct-to-reader publishing at scale and influenced how publishers think about established author platforms.
