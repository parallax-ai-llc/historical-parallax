## Summary

Marie-Anne Paulze Lavoisier was a French chemist who made important contributions to chemistry through her laboratory work, translations, and illustrations. She translated key scientific texts from English, illustrated her husband Antoine Lavoisier's experiments with detailed drawings, and hosted scientific salons. She is considered one of the earliest women to contribute significantly to chemistry.
