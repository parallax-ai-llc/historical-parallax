## Overview
On October 6, 1981, President Anwar Sadat was assassinated during a victory parade in Cairo celebrating Operation Badr.

## The Event
As air force planes flew overhead, a group of soldiers led by Khalid Islambouli disembarked from a truck and opened fire on the reviewing stand, throwing grenades. Sadat was killed, along with several dignitaries.

## Context
Sadat had become controversial in the Arab world for signing the Camp David Accords and making peace with Israel, which angered Islamists and Arab nationalists. His deputy, Hosni Mubarak, succeeded him.

## Perpetrators and Motive (High-Level)
- The attackers were linked to **Egyptian Islamist militant networks** and opposed Sadat’s policies, including the peace treaty with Israel and domestic crackdowns.
- The assassination is often treated as a turning point in modern Egyptian political violence.

## Immediate Aftermath
- Egypt entered a heightened security state.
- A major trial followed; several conspirators were sentenced and executed or imprisoned.
- **Hosni Mubarak** assumed the presidency and ruled for decades.

## Key People
- **Anwar Sadat**: President of Egypt (1970–1981).
- **Khalid Islambouli**: Lead attacker during the parade.
- **Hosni Mubarak**: Vice President, successor.
