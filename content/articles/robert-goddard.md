## Summary

Robert Goddard was an American physicist, professor, and inventor who is credited with creating and building the world's first liquid-fueled rocket. He launched his first rocket in 1926 in Auburn, Massachusetts. Despite being ridiculed during his lifetime, his work established the foundation for modern rocketry and space exploration. NASA's Goddard Space Flight Center is named in his honor.
