## Summary

Born Kenichi Shinoda, led the Yamaguchi-gumi as its sixth kumicho. Released from prison in 2011 and resumed leadership of Japan's most powerful criminal organization.

## Background

- Tsukasa Shinobu is the yakuza name of Kenichi Shinoda.
- He is associated with the Yamaguchi-gumi, historically one of Japan’s largest and most influential organized-crime syndicates.
- Public reporting often describes the group as structured around a hierarchical leadership model with affiliated sub-groups.

## Leadership of the Yamaguchi-gumi

- Shinobu became the sixth kumichō (boss) of the Yamaguchi-gumi in 2005.
- His appointment occurred while he was incarcerated, and later he resumed public leadership after his release.
- Under his tenure, the organization remained a central focus of Japanese law-enforcement efforts aimed at curbing organized crime.

## Legal issues and state response

- Japanese authorities have pursued a mix of criminal enforcement and administrative measures against yakuza groups, including anti-boryokudan ordinances.
- Restrictions in some jurisdictions have targeted business relationships, recruitment, and public activity connected to designated syndicates.
- Coverage of Shinobu’s leadership is often framed in the context of these policy and enforcement pressures.

## Broader significance

- Leadership figures in major yakuza groups are frequently discussed in relation to shifts in Japan’s underworld economy and the state’s evolving approach to organized crime.
- The Yamaguchi-gumi has also been associated with internal disputes and factional tensions over time, which can affect violence levels and policing priorities.
