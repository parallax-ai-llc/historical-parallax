## Background

- **Origin:** Milan Ševo (often rendered without diacritics as *Sevo*) was a Serbian-born figure who became active in Sweden, particularly in the Malmö area.[^1]
- **Public profile:** He was widely described in Swedish media and secondary reporting as a leading criminal actor in southern Sweden during the 2000s.[^1]

## Activities in Malmö (high-level)

The public record most often associates Ševo with organized crime in Malmö, including:

- **Network leadership:** described as leading or coordinating criminal associates rather than acting solely as a lone offender.[^1]
- **Underworld conflict context:** his name is frequently mentioned in the broader context of rivalries and violence connected to organized crime groups in the region.[^1]

## Death

- **Killed in 2010:** Ševo was shot and killed in 2010 in what was widely reported as a gang-related killing in Malmö.[^1]
- **Aftermath:** His death is commonly cited as part of the escalation of organized-crime violence in the city during that period.[^1]

## Why he appears in crime-history summaries

- **Symbol of a period:** He is often referenced as emblematic of Malmö’s 2000s organized-crime landscape.[^1]
- **Media and public attention:** Coverage tended to portray him as a prominent “boss” figure, contributing to his lasting notoriety.[^1]

## Notes on names and spelling

- Sources vary between **Milan Sevo** and **Milan Ševo** depending on whether diacritics are used.[^1]
