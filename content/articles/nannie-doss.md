## Summary

American serial killer known as the 'Giggling Granny' who poisoned 11 family members including four husbands between 1927 and 1954. She killed for insurance money and reportedly smiled during her confession.

## Modus Operandi and Investigation

- Doss is often described as a familial poisoner: many of her known or alleged victims were close relatives and spouses.
- Poisoning cases are historically difficult to detect without suspicion and toxicological testing, especially when symptoms resemble common illness.
- In mid-20th-century investigations, evidence frequently relied on exhumations, chemical tests for arsenic, and corroborating statements.

## Cultural Footnote

- The nickname "Giggling Granny" reflects how media coverage emphasized her demeanor and persona, which contrasted with the nature of the crimes.
- Serial murder cases like this are often used in criminology discussions about motive (e.g., financial gain), opportunity structures within the home, and the limits of contemporaneous forensic practice.
