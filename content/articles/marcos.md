## Overview

- **Ferdinand Marcos** (1917–1989) was a Filipino politician who served as **President of the Philippines** (1965–1986).
- His rule is widely characterized as **authoritarian**, especially after the declaration of **martial law**.
- Marcos was removed from power during the **People Power Revolution** (1986) and went into exile.

## Why He Matters

- Marcos is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - **Democratic backsliding** and executive power
  - **Martial law** and civil liberties
  - **Corruption and kleptocracy** allegations
  - **Political dynasties** and historical memory

## Governance and Policies (High-Level)

- **Centralization of power:** Governance increasingly relied on executive decrees and security forces during and after martial law.
- **Economic direction:** The period included major infrastructure projects alongside heightened debt and allegations of cronyism.
- **Security and dissent:** Accounts commonly describe arrests, censorship, and human-rights abuses during martial-law years.

## Legacy and Debate

- Marcos’s era remains a contested reference point in Philippine politics.
- Public debate often centers on:
  - claimed modernization and infrastructure achievements
  - documented repression and allegations of large-scale corruption
  - ongoing efforts to shape public memory through education, media, and political messaging
