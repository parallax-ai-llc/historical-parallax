## Summary

Muhammad al-Idrisi was a Muslim geographer, cartographer, and scholar who created one of the most advanced medieval world maps. Working at the court of Roger II of Sicily, he produced the Tabula Rogeriana, a comprehensive geographical work with detailed maps. His work represented the pinnacle of medieval Islamic geography and was used by European navigators for centuries. He combined Islamic, Greek, and Norman geographical knowledge.
