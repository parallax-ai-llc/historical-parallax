## Summary

Tim Kretschmer killed 15 people at his former school and during a subsequent car chase in Winnenden, Germany. He used his father's unsecured pistol.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2009 | 15 killed at school and during flight |

## Background and Context

- The attack occurred in Winnenden, Baden-Württemberg, and extended into the nearby area of Wendlingen am Neckar.
- It drew national attention in Germany and renewed debate about firearm storage, youth violence, and school security.

## Perpetrator

- The perpetrator was Tim Kretschmer, a former student of the school.
- Reporting commonly notes that he used a handgun belonging to his father that had not been secured in a locked safe at the time.

## Sequence of Events (High-Level)

- The shooting began at the perpetrator's former school.
- After leaving the school, the incident continued during his flight from the scene, including further violence and a police pursuit.
- The episode ended later the same day.

## Immediate Response

- Local and regional police responded, pursued the perpetrator, and secured affected sites.
- Emergency services treated the injured and coordinated victim support.

## Aftermath and Public Impact

- The event prompted public mourning and long-term support efforts for survivors, families, and the local community.
- It contributed to ongoing discussions in Germany about:
  - safe firearm storage and enforcement,
  - prevention and early intervention for at-risk youth,
  - crisis preparedness in schools.
