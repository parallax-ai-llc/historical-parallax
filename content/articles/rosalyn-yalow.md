## Summary

Rosalyn Yalow was an American medical physicist who shared the 1977 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for the development of radioimmunoassay (RIA), a revolutionary technique for measuring minute quantities of biological substances. RIA transformed endocrinology, blood banking, and many areas of clinical medicine.
