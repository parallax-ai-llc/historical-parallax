## Summary

Dilraba Dilmurat (迪丽热巴), born June 3, 1992, in Ürümqi, Xinjiang, China, is one of the most prominent actresses and models in Chinese entertainment. Of Uyghur ethnicity, she has become a crossover star whose appeal spans mainland China and broader East Asian markets. Her combination of distinctive features, comedic timing, and dramatic range has made her one of the most sought-after faces in Chinese television and advertising.

Since rising to national prominence in the mid-2010s, Dilraba has consistently ranked among China's highest-earning and most-followed celebrities. She commands tens of millions of followers across social media platforms and serves as a brand ambassador for numerous international luxury and lifestyle companies. Her success as a Uyghur woman in an industry dominated by Han Chinese artists has made her a culturally significant figure.

## Early Life & Background

Dilraba Dilmurat was born on June 3, 1992, in Ürümqi, the capital of the Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region. She grew up in a Uyghur household and attended local schools before pursuing higher education in the arts. She was accepted into the Shanghai Theatre Academy, one of China's most prestigious performing arts institutions, where she majored in acting and graduated in 2013. Her time at the academy provided her with formal dramatic training and early exposure to the competitive entertainment industry.

## Rise to Prominence

Dilraba made her acting debut shortly after graduation, appearing in supporting roles in television dramas. Her breakthrough came with the 2015 historical fantasy drama *Bride's Legends* and further exposure in *King's Woman* (2017), which attracted large viewership. Her starring role in the 2017 romantic fantasy drama *Pretty Li Hui Zhen* demonstrated her range in contemporary settings. The 2018 drama *Eternal Love of Dream* (also known as *Three Lives Three Worlds: The Pillow Book*) paired her with top-tier co-stars and cemented her status as a leading actress in costume dramas. Her role in the 2021 spy thriller *Love Designer* and action romance *You Are My Glory* (2021) opposite Yang Yang attracted record-breaking online viewership, reportedly exceeding 3 billion plays.

## Key Achievements

- Named one of Forbes China's most influential celebrities multiple times between 2018 and 2025.
- Brand ambassador for Adidas, Lancôme, Estée Lauder, Chow Tai Fook, and other major international brands.
- Won the Best Actress award at the 2017 iQIYI Scream Night for her performance in *Pretty Li Hui Zhen*.
- *You Are My Glory* (2021) became one of the most-watched Chinese dramas of the year, accumulating over 3 billion streams on Youku.
- Recognized as one of China's "Four Dan Actresses" of her generation alongside Yang Mi, Zhao Liying, and Tang Yan.

## Controversies & Criticism

Dilraba has largely maintained a clean public image, though she has not been entirely free of scrutiny. As a Uyghur celebrity with massive mainstream visibility, her public silence on issues concerning Xinjiang and Uyghur rights has drawn criticism from international human rights observers and members of the Uyghur diaspora, who argue that her platform comes with a responsibility to speak out. Within China, she has faced occasional online criticism regarding acting technique, with some viewers contending her performances rely heavily on her appearance rather than technical depth.

## Legacy & Influence

Dilraba Dilmurat holds a distinctive place in Chinese cultural history as one of the few ethnic minority artists to achieve top-tier mainstream stardom in the People's Republic. Her success has helped raise visibility for Xinjiang and Uyghur cultural identity within Chinese popular culture, even as that visibility operates within tightly constrained political boundaries. She has inspired a generation of young Uyghur women pursuing careers in entertainment and modeling, and her cross-cultural appeal has contributed to the internationalization of Chinese celebrity culture across Southeast Asia, South Korea, and beyond.
