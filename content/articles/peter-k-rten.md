## Summary

German serial killer known as the 'Vampire of Düsseldorf' who committed a series of murders and sexual assaults in Düsseldorf in 1929. He drank blood from some victims. Executed by guillotine.

## Background

- Kürten’s early life is commonly described as marked by poverty, violence, and early criminal behavior.
- He accumulated a long history of theft and violent offenses before the 1929 Düsseldorf attacks that made him internationally infamous.

## Crimes and Investigation

- The Düsseldorf case involved multiple murders and assaults with varying methods, which fueled public panic and led to one of the most intensive investigations in Weimar-era Germany.
- Contemporary reporting and later summaries emphasize the role of forensic work and systematic questioning as police tried to connect scattered incidents.

## Arrest and Trial

- His wife’s cooperation is frequently cited as a key factor that led to identification and arrest.
- At trial, medical and psychological assessments were central to public discussion, including debates about responsibility and mental illness.

## Legacy

- The case influenced later criminological writing and is often referenced in the history of criminal profiling and modern serial-murder investigations.
- Many details in popular retellings are sensationalized; reliable summaries distinguish between court findings, confessions, and later folklore.
