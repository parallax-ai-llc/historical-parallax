## Summary

Bosnian Croat paramilitary leader and crime boss convicted of war crimes in the Convicts' Battalion.

## Additional Context

### Background

- Known by the nickname **"Tuta"**.
- Associated with Bosnian Croat armed formations during the Bosnian War.
- Often described in sources as combining paramilitary leadership with organized-crime activity.

### Role and Activities (high level)

- Led or influenced units operating in areas of Bosnia and Herzegovina during the 1990s conflict.
- The unit linked to him is commonly referred to in English sources as the **Convicts' Battalion**.
- Reporting and court records characterize the group as involved in abuses against detainees and civilians.

### Legal Proceedings

- Prosecuted by the **International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY)**.
- The ICTY case is frequently cited as an example of accountability efforts for wartime abuses in the region.
- Conviction is widely reported in 2003; sentencing and appellate developments are documented in ICTY materials.

### Why He Appears in Historical-Parallax

- Illustrates the overlap between irregular armed groups and criminal networks in the Yugoslav wars.
- Serves as a reference point for ICTY-era jurisprudence on command responsibility and participation in abuses.
