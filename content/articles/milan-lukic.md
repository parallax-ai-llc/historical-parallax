## Summary

Bosnian Serb war criminal who led the Visegrad massacres, locking Bosniak civilians in houses and setting them on fire, killing over 120.

## Višegrad Atrocities (Context)

During the Bosnian War, the town of Višegrad became the site of systematic violence against Bosniak civilians. The events associated with Lukic are commonly discussed as part of a broader campaign of persecution, killings, and forced displacement in eastern Bosnia.

## Crimes and Conviction (High Level)

International reporting and tribunal findings describe multiple incidents in which groups of civilians were targeted, including notorious arson attacks where victims were confined inside buildings that were then set on fire. These incidents became emblematic of the brutality of the conflict and were later central to war-crimes prosecutions.

## ICTY Proceedings

At the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY), Lukic was tried and convicted for crimes committed in and around Višegrad. The tribunal’s work in such cases aimed to establish individual criminal responsibility, document patterns of violence, and provide a record beyond national political narratives.

## Arrest and Transfer

Lukic was arrested in Argentina in 2005 and transferred for prosecution. His arrest illustrates how international cooperation and extradition can play a decisive role in apprehending suspects years after the crimes.

## Sentence and Historical Significance

In 2009, the ICTY sentenced Lukic to life imprisonment. The case is frequently cited in discussions of:

- the prosecution of mass-atrocity crimes;
- evidentiary reconstruction of events long after the fact;
- accountability for violence against civilians in ethnic conflicts.
