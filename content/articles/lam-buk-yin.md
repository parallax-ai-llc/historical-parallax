## Summary

Hong Kong serial killer who murdered four people between 1982 and 1983. He dismembered victims and stored body parts in jars at his home. Convicted and imprisoned.

## Case Overview

Lam Buk Yin’s crimes became one of Hong Kong’s more widely reported homicide cases of the early 1980s.
He was ultimately apprehended following police investigation and was prosecuted under Hong Kong’s then-existing criminal justice framework.

## Trial and Sentencing (High-Level)

Hong Kong courts convicted Lam of multiple counts of murder.
He was initially sentenced to death; the sentence was later commuted to life imprisonment, reflecting broader shifts in how capital punishment was applied and reviewed in the territory during that period.

## Public and Historical Context

The case is often discussed in the context of policing capacity, forensic methods, and public fear surrounding serial crime in a densely populated city.
Over time, it has been referenced in true-crime media and retrospective reporting, typically as an example of Hong Kong’s handling of major violent-crime investigations.
