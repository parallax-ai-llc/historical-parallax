## Summary

Belgian-Moroccan ISIS bomb-maker who constructed the explosives used in both the Paris attacks and Brussels bombings. He died as a suicide bomber at Brussels Airport.

## Role in ISIS-Linked Attack Networks

- Laachraoui is widely described by investigators as a specialist in constructing improvised explosive devices (IEDs) for coordinated attacks.
- The Paris attacks (November 2015) and Brussels bombings (March 2016) were treated by authorities as connected operations involving overlapping personnel and logistics.

## Identification and Investigation

- Media reporting and official statements have emphasized forensic evidence (including DNA) used to connect suspects to safe houses, materials, and bomb-making activity.
- The cases highlighted the challenges of monitoring networks that move across European borders and use rented properties and stolen or falsified documents.

## Impact

- The attacks led to major security responses in Belgium and France, including heightened alert levels, raids, and policy debates about surveillance and counter-radicalization.
- They also intensified European discussions about foreign fighter travel, online recruitment, and the management of extremist returnees.

## Operational Lessons Often Discussed

- Coordinated attacks have highlighted the importance of sharing intelligence quickly across agencies and jurisdictions.
- Investigations frequently focus on supply chains for precursor chemicals, rental vehicles, and communications discipline.

## Public Discourse

- The events intensified debates about balancing civil liberties with counterterrorism powers and about preventing radicalization.
