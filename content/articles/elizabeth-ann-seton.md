## Summary

Elizabeth Ann Seton was the first native-born American to be canonized by the Catholic Church. She founded the Sisters of Charity, the first American religious community for women, and established the first Catholic school in the United States. A convert from Episcopalianism, she dedicated her life to Catholic education and care for the poor. She was canonized in 1975.
