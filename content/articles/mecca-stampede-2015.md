## Summary

Deadly stampede during the Hajj pilgrimage in Mina near Mecca on September 24, 2015 that killed over 2,400 people according to independent counts. It was the deadliest Hajj disaster ever.

## Context: crowd disasters at mass gatherings

- Stampedes and crush incidents can occur when crowd density becomes too high, movement is constrained, or opposing flows intersect.
- The Hajj is one of the world’s largest annual mass gatherings, and safety planning involves routing, scheduling, barriers, and real-time management.
- The Mina area includes ritual sites that draw large synchronized flows of pilgrims.

## Reported casualty figures and controversy

- Publicly reported death tolls varied across sources, and independent compilations sometimes differed from official counts.
- The incident became a major political and diplomatic issue for some countries whose citizens were among the victims.
- Debates after the disaster focused on crowd-control decisions, route management, and transparency.

## Typical prevention and response measures

- Crowd science emphasizes monitoring density, reducing counterflows, and using controlled entry/exit points.
- Emergency response planning includes medical surge capacity, rapid triage, and communication systems to locate missing people.
- Post-incident reviews often lead to procedural changes, infrastructure adjustments, and updated safety protocols.
