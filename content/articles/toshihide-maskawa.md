## Summary

Toshihide Maskawa was a Japanese theoretical physicist who shared the 2008 Nobel Prize in Physics with Makoto Kobayashi for their work on CP violation and the CKM matrix. Their 1973 paper predicted the existence of a third generation of quarks before experimental confirmation. Maskawa was known for his modesty and reportedly could not speak English, giving his Nobel lecture in Japanese.
