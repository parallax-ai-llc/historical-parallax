## Summary

Nigo, born Tomoaki Nagao, is a Japanese fashion designer, DJ, record producer, and entrepreneur who became one of the most influential figures in streetwear culture. As the founder of A Bathing Ape (BAPE), he helped bridge the gap between Japanese street fashion and global pop culture, creating one of the most recognizable and coveted brands in the world during the late 1990s and 2000s.

His distinctive approach — blending American hip-hop aesthetics with Japanese craftsmanship and playful graphic design — redefined what streetwear could be. Beyond BAPE, Nigo has collaborated with major luxury houses and global brands, culminating in his appointment as artistic director of Kenzo in 2021. His career represents the evolution of streetwear from underground subculture to mainstream fashion force.

## Early Life & Background

Tomoaki Nagao was born on December 23, 1970, in Maebashi, Gunma Prefecture, Japan. He developed an early interest in fashion, music, and American pop culture, particularly hip-hop. He enrolled at the Bunka Fashion College in Tokyo, one of Japan's most prestigious fashion institutions, where he met Jun Takahashi, who would go on to found Undercover. During his time at Bunka, Nigo also connected with Hiroshi Fujiwara, a pioneering figure in Tokyo's streetwear scene who became a mentor and collaborator. The nickname "Nigo" — meaning "number two" — was given to him because of his resemblance to Fujiwara, making him a "second Fujiwara."

## Rise to Prominence

In 1993, Nigo founded A Bathing Ape (BAPE) in the Harajuku district of Tokyo, opening a small store called "Nowhere" alongside Jun Takahashi. The brand's name was inspired by the Japanese saying "a bathing ape in lukewarm water," a commentary on the complacency of overconsumption. Nigo employed a strategy of extreme scarcity, producing limited quantities of each design to generate demand. BAPE's signature camo patterns, shark hoodies, and the ape head logo became iconic symbols of streetwear culture.

By the early 2000s, BAPE had gained international recognition, fueled by endorsements from hip-hop artists like Pharrell Williams and Kanye West. Nigo opened flagship stores in New York, London, and Hong Kong, expanding the brand into a global phenomenon.

## Key Achievements

Nigo's collaboration with Pharrell Williams produced the Billionaire Boys Club (BBC) and Ice Cream footwear lines, launched in 2003. He collaborated with major brands including Nike, Adidas, and Pepsi. In 2011, Hong Kong fashion conglomerate I.T Group acquired a 90% stake in BAPE for approximately $2.8 million, though Nigo remained involved creatively for a period. He launched the brand Human Made, which reflects his passion for vintage Americana and craftsmanship. In September 2021, LVMH appointed Nigo as artistic director of Kenzo, succeeding Felipe Oliveira Baptista. His debut Kenzo collection in January 2022 was widely praised for its vibrant energy and respectful nods to founder Kenzo Takada's legacy.

## Controversies & Criticism

Nigo faced financial difficulties in the late 2000s, reportedly owing significant tax debts to Japanese authorities, which contributed to his decision to sell BAPE. Some critics argued that BAPE's heavy reliance on limited releases and hype-driven marketing prioritized exclusivity over design innovation. The sale to I.T Group and later to TJM (a subsidiary of I.T) was seen by some longtime fans as a dilution of the brand's original ethos.

## Legacy & Influence

Nigo is widely regarded as one of the architects of modern streetwear. His model of scarcity-driven releases, brand collaborations, and celebrity partnerships became the template that brands like Supreme, Off-White, and Fear of God would later follow. His ability to move seamlessly between streetwear and high fashion — exemplified by his role at Kenzo — demonstrated that the two worlds were not mutually exclusive. Nigo's influence extends beyond clothing into music, art, and design, making him one of the most multifaceted creative forces in contemporary fashion.
