## Summary

Indonesian bomb maker for Jemaah Islamiyah who was responsible for the 2000 Rizal Day bombings in Manila that killed 22 people. He escaped from a Philippine prison and was later shot dead by Philippine police.

## Background

Fathur Rahman al-Ghozi was associated with the Southeast Asian militant network Jemaah Islamiyah (JI). Reporting on his case often highlights his technical role as a bomb maker and trainer, rather than as a public leader or ideologue.

## Regional Context

The late 1990s and early 2000s saw a series of coordinated attacks and disrupted plots in Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, and the Philippines, with investigators linking some operations to overlapping networks. Al-Ghozi's activities became part of broader counterterrorism cooperation among regional governments.

## Arrest, Escape, and Death

After his arrest in the Philippines, al-Ghozi was held in custody but later escaped from detention. He was subsequently killed in a police operation in 2003, bringing his personal involvement to an end while investigations into connected cells and facilitators continued.

## Significance

The case is often cited in discussions of:

- **Technical expertise in militant groups** (training, explosives procurement, and bomb construction)
- **Cross-border mobility** within Southeast Asia
- **Law-enforcement challenges** around detention security and fugitive recovery
