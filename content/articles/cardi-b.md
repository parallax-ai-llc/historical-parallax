## Summary

Cardi B, born Belcalis Marlenis Almánzar, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter who rose from social media fame to become one of the most commercially successful female rappers in history. Her debut single "Bodak Yellow" reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 in September 2017, making her the second female rapper to achieve a solo number-one hit after Lauryn Hill in 1998.

Her debut studio album *Invasion of Privacy* (2018) was certified triple platinum and won the Grammy Award for Best Rap Album, making her the first solo female artist to win in that category. Known for her unfiltered personality, sharp lyricism, and charismatic public presence, Cardi B has transcended music to become a significant cultural figure in fashion, politics, and entertainment.

## Early Life & Background

Belcalis Marlenis Almánzar was born on October 11, 1992, in Washington Heights, Manhattan, New York City. Her father is Dominican and her mother is Trinidadian. She was raised in the Highbridge neighborhood of the South Bronx. She attended Renaissance High School for Musical Theater & Technology before enrolling at Borough of Manhattan Community College, which she later left.

Cardi B worked as a stripper during her late teens and early twenties, a period she has spoken about openly as a means of escaping poverty and an abusive relationship. She credited the job with giving her financial independence and confidence.

## Rise to Prominence

Cardi B first gained public attention through her candid and humorous videos on Vine and Instagram between 2013 and 2015, amassing millions of followers. In 2015, she joined the cast of VH1's reality television series *Love & Hip Hop: New York*, appearing on seasons six and seven. She departed the show in 2017 to focus on her music career.

She signed with Atlantic Records in February 2017 and released "Bodak Yellow" in June of that year. The single climbed to number one on the Hot 100 by September, where it remained for three consecutive weeks. She followed this with several collaborative hits, including "Finesse (Remix)" with Bruno Mars and "I Like It" featuring Bad Bunny and J Balvin, which also reached number one.

## Key Achievements

Her debut album *Invasion of Privacy*, released on April 6, 2018, debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 and produced five top-ten Hot 100 singles. At the 61st Grammy Awards in 2019, it won Best Rap Album. Cardi B became the first woman to have five top-ten hits simultaneously on the Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart. Her 2020 single "WAP" featuring Megan Thee Stallion debuted at number one and became one of the most-streamed songs of the year, with over 93 million first-week streams in the United States alone.

As of 2025, she has sold over 100 million records globally, earned multiple BET Awards, American Music Awards, and MTV Video Music Awards, and accumulated billions of streams across platforms.

## Controversies & Criticism

Cardi B has faced several controversies, including a 2018 viral video in which she admitted to drugging and robbing men during her time as a stripper, which drew significant backlash. In 2018, she was involved in an altercation at a New York strip club, leading to indictment on multiple charges including assault; she rejected a plea deal and the case proceeded through the legal system. Her explicit lyrics and public persona have drawn both praise for their authenticity and criticism for their content. She has also engaged in high-profile public feuds with other artists, most notably Nicki Minaj.

## Legacy & Influence

Cardi B's success helped reshape the commercial landscape for female rappers, contributing to a wave of mainstream breakthroughs in the late 2010s and 2020s. Her blend of social media savvy, unfiltered personality, and musical talent established a new blueprint for artist development in the streaming era. She has also used her platform for political engagement, endorsing candidates, advocating for healthcare reform, and speaking on immigration issues tied to her own heritage.
