## Summary

British woman known as the White Widow who was the wife of one of the 7/7 London bombers and later became a wanted terrorist linked to al-Shabaab. She is one of the world's most wanted women.

## Public profile and nickname

Lewthwaite has often been described in media as the "White Widow," a label that reflects a mix of confirmed biographical details (including her marriage to a perpetrator of the 7 July 2005 London bombings) and a larger body of allegations that have varied in strength over time.

## Allegations and investigations

- **Militant links:** She has been reported as having ties to militant Islamist networks in East Africa, including alleged association with al-Shabaab.
- **Wanted status:** Authorities in the region have issued warrants and public appeals for information, but public evidence has been uneven and frequently contested.
- **Attribution caution:** Some high-profile attack attributions circulated in press reporting have been described as unconfirmed or disputed.

## Why the case is notable

Her story is often referenced in broader discussions about:

- radicalization pathways and recruitment networks
- the reliability of early media reporting during major security incidents
- cross-border manhunts and the difficulty of definitive identification
