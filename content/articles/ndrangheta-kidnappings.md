Series of high-profile kidnappings by the Ndrangheta in the 1970s-1990s that financed the organizations rise. Victims included J. Paul Getty III whose ear was cut off.

## Background: The 'Ndrangheta

The 'Ndrangheta is a mafia-type criminal organization originating in Calabria, southern Italy. It is structured around family-based clans and has been associated with activities including extortion, drug trafficking, and infiltration of legitimate business.

## Kidnapping as a Revenue Strategy

In the late 20th century, kidnapping-for-ransom became a prominent criminal enterprise in parts of Italy. In public reporting, the period is linked to networks sometimes referred to as "anonima sequestri" (kidnapping rings), with victims targeted for their wealth or perceived ransom value.

Ransom kidnappings served multiple purposes:

- generating large cash payments that were difficult to trace
- financing other criminal ventures and expansion
- exerting fear and control beyond the local region

## Notable Case: J. Paul Getty III

The 1973 kidnapping of J. Paul Getty III became internationally famous, particularly because proof-of-life demands included a severed ear. The case highlighted both the brutality of ransom tactics and the complex negotiations that often unfolded between families, intermediaries, and law enforcement.

## Decline of the Kidnapping Era

By the 1990s, large-scale kidnapping in Italy declined substantially. Factors commonly cited include improved policing and intelligence, stronger legal tools, financial monitoring, and changes in criminal incentives as other revenue streams (notably drug trafficking) became more profitable or less risky relative to prolonged hostage management.
