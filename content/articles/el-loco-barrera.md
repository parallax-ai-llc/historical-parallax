## Summary

Daniel Barrera Barrera, Colombian drug lord who was one of the last of the great independent drug traffickers. Known as the Last Great Capo, he managed coca production and trafficking from the eastern plains.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2000 | Became major independent drug trafficker |
| 2010 | Became Colombia's most wanted drug lord |
| 2012 | Arrested in Venezuela |
| 2016 | Extradited to US, sentenced to 35 years |

## Role in Colombian Drug Trafficking (High Level)

- **Independent operator:** Barrera was described as an influential trafficker who worked across networks rather than as a classic cartel boss with a single unified organization.
- **Eastern plains base:** Reporting commonly connects his operations to Colombia's Llanos region, with influence over production zones and trafficking corridors.

## Law Enforcement Pressure

- **Most-wanted status:** Colombian and international authorities pursued him for years as several earlier cartel structures fragmented and successor groups emerged.
- **Arrest and extradition:** He was captured in Venezuela, transferred to Colombia, and later extradited to the United States to face drug-trafficking charges.

## Significance

- **End-of-era narrative:** His capture was portrayed as symbolizing the decline of the "old model" of large, identifiable kingpins, replaced by more decentralized trafficking networks.
- **Transnational dimension:** The case illustrates how coca cultivation, armed group control, and cross-border logistics combine in the modern drug economy.

## Key Takeaways

- **Networked crime:** Modern drug trafficking often depends on flexible alliances and service providers rather than a single hierarchical cartel.
- **Territorial control:** Control of routes and production areas can be as decisive as direct ownership of shipments.
- **Cross-border enforcement:** His capture shows how regional cooperation and extradition shape outcomes in transnational crime cases.
