## Summary

Deadliest school shooting in a decade; 19 children and 2 teachers killed in Uvalde, Texas. The 77-minute police response failure became as controversial as the shooting itself.

## Key Facts

- **Date**: May 24, 2022
- **Location**: Robb Elementary School, Uvalde, Texas, United States
- **Fatalities**: 21 people (19 children, 2 teachers)
- **Injuries**: Multiple people were wounded (exact counts vary by report).
- **Broader significance**: Widely described as one of the deadliest U.S. school shootings in modern history.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- A gunman entered the school and attacked students and staff.
- The incident triggered a large multi-agency response involving local police, school district police, and state/federal partners.
- Public attention intensified not only because of the death toll, but also because of questions about **command, coordination, and timing** during the response.

## Response and Controversy

- The law-enforcement response lasted **over an hour** from first engagement to the final breach of the classroom.
- Families and observers criticized the perceived delay in confronting the shooter.
- Subsequent releases of timelines, audio, and video (including body-camera footage) fueled broader scrutiny of training, leadership, and inter-agency communication.

## Investigations and Reporting

- Multiple reviews and investigative efforts examined decision-making on scene, including:
  - how an “active shooter” situation was interpreted during key minutes,
  - whether incident command was clearly established,
  - the adequacy of equipment, training, and radio interoperability,
  - and how information flowed between responders and the public.
- Findings and recommendations from these reviews were widely cited in discussions of school safety and policing practices.

## Aftermath

- The shooting prompted sustained community grief, national mourning, and renewed political debate about firearms, school security, and policing.
- The event became a reference point in policy discussions about:
  - emergency response doctrine (e.g., immediate engagement vs. perimeter containment),
  - coordination among school police, municipal police, and state agencies,
  - and transparency expectations following critical incidents.
