## Summary

Tijuana Cartel enforcer known as 'El Muletas' responsible for hundreds of murders during the cartel wars. He operated a network of assassins across Baja California.

## Context: Baja California and cartel conflict

Baja California has been a strategic corridor for drug trafficking due to its border crossings and access to U.S. markets. During periods of fragmentation and rivalry among major Mexican criminal organizations, cities such as Tijuana experienced spikes in violence linked to territorial disputes and retaliation cycles.

## Role and alleged activities

Raydel López Uriarte is commonly described as an enforcer associated with the Tijuana Cartel (often referenced in connection with the Arellano Félix Organization). Accounts portray him as a coordinator of armed cells tasked with protecting routes, targeting rivals, and enforcing internal discipline.

Reported casualty counts attributed to individuals in cartel conflicts can be difficult to verify, since they may come from law enforcement claims, media reporting, or intelligence assessments that are not always publicly substantiated.

## Arrest and prosecution

He was arrested by Mexican authorities and became part of broader efforts to dismantle cartel leadership and operational networks. High-profile arrests in this period were often paired with changes in local security strategy and interagency coordination.

## Public attention and legacy

Cases involving named enforcers became emblematic of a violence-driven phase of Mexico’s drug war. In public debate, they are cited in discussions about how cartel “enforcement” roles function, why border regions are contested, and how arrests can reshape local power balances without necessarily ending violence.
