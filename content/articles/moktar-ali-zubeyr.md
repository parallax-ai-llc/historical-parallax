## Summary

Somali militant leader also known as Ahmed Abdi Godane who led al-Shabaab and ordered the 2013 Westgate mall attack in Nairobi that killed 67 people. He pledged al-Shabaab's allegiance to al-Qaeda.

## Organizational Role

Moktar Ali Zubeyr, also known as Ahmed Abdi Godane, has been described as a leader of al-Shabaab, the Islamist militant group based in Somalia. In many open-source profiles, he is associated with the organization's strategic direction during periods of intensified insurgency.

## Regional Context

Al-Shabaab's conflict with Somalia's federal authorities and with regional and international forces (including the African Union mission) is a major theme in accounts of modern Somali security. Reporting often situates al-Shabaab within broader dynamics of state fragility, local governance, and transnational jihadist movements.

## Death and Aftermath

Open reporting indicates that Godane was killed in a US airstrike in 2014. Analyses of subsequent years often discuss leadership succession and how the group adapted operationally and politically.
