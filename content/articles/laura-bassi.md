## Summary

Laura Bassi was an Italian physicist who became the first woman to earn a professorship in physics at a European university (University of Bologna, 1732). She was a leading Newtonian physicist in Italy and conducted experiments on electricity. She published 28 papers on physics and held a chair in experimental physics.
