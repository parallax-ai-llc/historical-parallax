## Summary

Dutch animal rights activist who assassinated politician Pim Fortuyn on May 6, 2002—the first political assassination in the Netherlands in over 300 years. He said he killed Fortuyn to protect vulnerable groups. Served 12 years.

## Additional Context

### Political context

Pim Fortuyn was a prominent Dutch politician known for outspoken views on immigration and Islam and for rapidly gaining electoral momentum in 2002. His killing occurred shortly before the Dutch general election and had a major impact on national politics and public debate.

### Motive and statements

Van der Graaf was associated with animal-rights and environmental activism. In court proceedings and later accounts, he framed the assassination as intended to protect vulnerable groups from what he saw as Fortuyn's political agenda.

### Trial and sentence

Dutch courts convicted him of murder and sentenced him to 18 years. He was released in 2014 after serving roughly two-thirds of the sentence, subject to conditions.

### Legacy

The assassination is frequently described as a watershed moment in modern Dutch political history, influencing security practices around politicians and altering the tone of political discourse.
