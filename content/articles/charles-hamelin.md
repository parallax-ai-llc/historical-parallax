## Background

- Charles Hamelin is a Canadian short track speed skater who competed internationally across multiple Olympic cycles.
- Short track is characterized by pack racing on a smaller oval than long-track speed skating, with frequent contact, tactics, and penalties playing a major role in outcomes.

## Competitive Profile

- Hamelin is known for strong starts, positioning, and tactical racing, skills that are especially valuable in short track where crashes can eliminate top contenders.
- In addition to Olympic success, elite short-track skaters typically compete in World Cup circuits and World Championships, where points, rankings, and relay teams are established.

## Team and Relay Impact

- Canada has historically been a major short-track nation; relay events reward coordination, clean exchanges, and team depth.
- Hamelin’s medal record includes relay results that reflect broader national program strength alongside individual performance.

## Significance

- His career illustrates the longevity possible in short track, where experience and racecraft can offset the sport’s high volatility.
