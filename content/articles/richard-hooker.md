## Summary

Richard Hooker was an English theologian whose work Of the Laws of Ecclesiastical Polity is considered the foundational text of Anglican theology. He articulated a via media between Catholicism and Puritanism, grounding church authority in Scripture, tradition, and reason. His political philosophy influenced John Locke and the development of liberalism. He is regarded as the founding father of Anglican theological identity.
