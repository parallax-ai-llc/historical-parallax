## Summary

French terrorist of Algerian descent who was one of three gunmen who attacked the Bataclan theater during the November 2015 Paris attacks, killing 90 concert-goers. He had been under judicial supervision for terrorism before traveling to Syria.

## Additional Context

### Background and radicalization (reported)

- Amimour was from the Paris suburbs and was described in reporting as having undergone a process of radicalization in the early 2010s.
- French judicial supervision measures at the time reportedly included periodic reporting requirements, which were intended to restrict travel.
- His departure to Syria in 2013 became part of a broader pattern of French nationals traveling to join jihadist groups during the Syrian civil war.

### Role in the November 2015 Paris attacks

- The Bataclan assault was one of several coordinated attack sites on 13 November 2015.
- The Bataclan attackers used firearms and explosive vests; many victims were killed inside the concert venue.
- The assault ended when French police stormed the venue; multiple attackers died at the scene.

### Aftermath

- Investigations into the November 2015 attacks focused on transnational facilitation networks, including travel routes and logistical support.
- Trials and judicial proceedings in France later convicted numerous individuals for roles ranging from direct participation to aiding the attackers.
