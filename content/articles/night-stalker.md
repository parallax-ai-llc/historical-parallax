## Notes on the Name and Attribution

- **Common usage**: In U.S. media and popular culture, **"Night Stalker" most often refers to Richard Ramirez**, the offender linked to the 1984–1985 crime spree in Southern California.
- **Ambiguity**: The nickname has also been used in other contexts (including law-enforcement/media labels for different cases). When writing or researching, it is best to **specify the person and jurisdiction** to avoid conflating unrelated investigations.
- **This entry**: This article exists mainly as a **redirect-style reference** because the consolidated biography is under **Richard Ramirez**.

## High-Level Overview (Non-Graphic)

- **Time period**: The crimes associated with the "Night Stalker" label were widely reported in the mid-1980s.
- **Geography**: Reporting and court proceedings primarily involved **California**.
- **Modus (general)**: The case is characterized in summaries as **nighttime home invasions**; detailed victim-level accounts are intentionally omitted here.
- **Legal outcome**: Ramirez was convicted and received a death sentence, later remaining in custody until his death.
