## Summary

Rwandan businessman who financed the 1994 genocide and helped establish Radio Télévision Libre des Mille Collines, which broadcast anti-Tutsi propaganda. Evaded capture for 26 years before arrest in Paris.

## Additional Context

Kabuga is widely described as a key financial figure linked to the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi in Rwanda. Allegations against him emphasized the enabling role of money, procurement, and media influence in mass violence, rather than direct battlefield command.

### Alleged Forms of Support

- **Financing and procurement**: Prosecutors alleged he helped fund or facilitate the acquisition of resources for extremist militias.
- **Media**: His association with RTLM is often highlighted because broadcasts are widely documented as inciting violence and reinforcing anti-Tutsi ideology.

### International Justice Process

- The **ICTR indictment (1997)** placed him among high-profile fugitives sought for genocide-related crimes.
- After the ICTR closed, related cases were handled by successor mechanisms under the United Nations framework.
- His **2020 arrest in France** was treated as a major development because he had evaded capture for decades using false identities and support networks.

### Broader Significance

Kabuga’s prosecution is commonly discussed as part of a broader effort to demonstrate that genocide accountability extends to alleged financiers and propagandists, not only direct perpetrators.

### Proceedings Note

Accounts of the case note that complex procedural issues and questions of fitness to stand trial can affect the pace of international-crimes proceedings.
