## Summary

Nick Holonyak Jr. invented the first practical visible-spectrum light-emitting diode (LED) in 1962 while working at General Electric. His red LED was the precursor to all modern LEDs used in displays, lighting, and indicators worldwide. He is considered the father of the LED and predicted LEDs would eventually replace incandescent bulbs.
