## Summary

Atisha was an Indian Buddhist scholar and monk who played a crucial role in the revival of Buddhism in Tibet. He was invited to Tibet in 1042 and spent the rest of his life teaching there. His work Bodhipathapradipa (Lamp for the Path to Enlightenment) systematized the stages of the Buddhist path (lamrim) and became foundational for Tibetan Buddhism. He helped establish the Kadampa school.
