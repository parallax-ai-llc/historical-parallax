## Summary

Augustin-Louis Cauchy was a French mathematician who was a pioneer in mathematical analysis and group theory. He gave the first rigorous definition of continuity and proved the fundamental theorem of calculus. His contributions to complex analysis, including the Cauchy integral theorem and Cauchy-Riemann equations, were foundational. He was one of the most prolific mathematicians in history, publishing over 800 papers.
