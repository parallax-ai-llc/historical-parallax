## Summary
Colin Ireland was a British serial killer who murdered five gay men in London in 1993, earning the label 'Gay Slayer' in the press. He specifically targeted the gay community and killed five men to meet the FBI's definition of a serial killer. He was convicted and given five life sentences.

## Early Life
Born March 16, 1954, in Dartford, Kent. He had a troubled childhood and spent time in various institutions. He had a history of petty crime. He had studied the FBI's definition of serial killers and reportedly planned to become one. [^1]

## Criminal Career
In 1993, Ireland murdered five gay men over a period of several months, targeting men he met at the Coleherne pub in Earl's Court, London. He went to their homes, bound and tortured them, then killed them. He positioned bodies and placed items deliberately to send messages. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Ireland was identified through CCTV footage at a London railway station with one of his victims. He turned himself in on July 20, 1993, initially to provide an alibi, but was confronted with the CCTV evidence. He confessed and was convicted of five murders, receiving five life sentences with a whole life tariff. He died in prison on February 21, 2012. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case was one of the first in the UK to use CCTV evidence to identify a serial killer [^4]
- Led to increased CCTV coverage in London public spaces
- His targeting of the gay community prompted improved police-LGBTQ relations

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered five gay men in targeted hate crimes [^5]
- Deliberately studied serial killers and planned to become one
- Targeted a vulnerable community
- Used deception to gain victims' trust
- His crimes reflected homophobic hatred
