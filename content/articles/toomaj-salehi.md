## Summary

Toomaj Salehi is an Iranian rapper and outspoken critic of the Islamic Republic whose music and public statements drew international attention during and after the Mahsa Amini protest movement. He became widely described as a political prisoner after repeated arrests and prosecutions linked to his artistic work and activism.

## Background

Salehi is from Iran’s Chaharmahal and Bakhtiari Province and is associated with socially and politically critical rap. His work has been discussed both as contemporary Iranian protest music and as part of a broader pattern of state pressure on dissident artists.

## Activism, Arrests, and Legal Proceedings

After the 2022–2023 wave of protests, Salehi was arrested and faced serious charges that Iranian authorities connected to his support for demonstrations and his public messaging. Reporting around his detention has included allegations of ill-treatment and concerns over due process, which were cited by human-rights organizations and international observers.

In 2024, Iranian judicial authorities reported a death sentence in his case. Subsequent reporting also indicated further judicial review and procedural developments. Because legal status can change through appeals and retrials, summaries typically emphasize the sequence of court actions rather than treating any single verdict as final.
