## Summary

Wally Herbert led the first confirmed surface crossing of the Arctic Ocean in 1968-69, traveling from Alaska to Svalbard via the North Pole.

## Additional Context

### Early life and polar experience

Herbert served with the British armed forces and developed experience in the Arctic, including work with dog teams and travel over sea ice. This field expertise positioned him for leadership roles in long-duration polar expeditions.

### Trans-Arctic Expedition (1968–1969)

The crossing described in the summary is often referred to as the **Trans-Arctic Expedition**. The route involved traveling over drifting pack ice, coping with pressure ridges, open leads, and extreme cold while moving from Alaska toward Svalbard via the North Pole.

### Later work

After the expedition, Herbert continued as a polar explorer and author, contributing to public understanding of Arctic exploration history and the practical challenges of travel in polar environments.

### Legacy

Herbert is remembered for combining traditional travel methods (including dog sledging) with modern expedition logistics. His achievement is frequently cited when discussing verified surface crossings of the Arctic Ocean.
