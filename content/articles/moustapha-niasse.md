## Summary

Senegalese politician and former Prime Minister who has served in multiple administrations across decades of Senegalese politics, navigating corruption scandals.

## Background

- Born in Keur Madiabel, Senegal (then French West Africa).
- Studied at Cheikh Anta Diop University and the University of Paris.

## Government and Diplomatic Roles (high-level)

- Director of the Presidential Cabinet (1970–1979).
- Minister of Town Planning, Housing and Environment (1979).
- Minister of Foreign Affairs (1979–1984), later holding the rank of Minister of State for Foreign Affairs (from 1981).
- Briefly served as Prime Minister in April 1983 during a period when the office was abolished and reinstated in later years.
- Minister of Foreign Affairs again (1993–1998).
- Representative of the UN Secretary-General in the Great Lakes region (late 1990s).

## Party and Electoral Politics

- Founded the Alliance of the Forces of Progress (AFP) after splitting with the Socialist Party leadership in the late 1990s.
- Ran in the 2000 presidential election, placing third in the first round, then backed Abdoulaye Wade in the runoff.
- Appointed Prime Minister under President Wade (April 2000–March 2001), resigning amid reported tensions in the relationship.
- Elected to the National Assembly in 2001; later resigned his seat in 2006 in protest of extending the assembly’s term.
- Served as President of the National Assembly (2012–2022).

## Notable Mediation and International Work

- Appointed by the UN as an envoy connected to the Democratic Republic of the Congo peace and transition process in the early 2000s.
- Associated with negotiations leading to the Global and Inclusive Agreement on Transition (Pretoria Agreement), a milestone in efforts to end the Second Congo War.

## Controversies and Public Disputes (summary)

- Faced public accusations and counter-accusations in Senegalese political conflicts, including corruption allegations raised in the post-2000 era.
- Was publicly accused by political opponents of wrongdoing related to diplomatic passports; he denied wrongdoing.
