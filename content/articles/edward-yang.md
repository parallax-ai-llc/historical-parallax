## Summary

Edward Yang was a Taiwanese filmmaker whose masterpiece Yi Yi is regularly cited as one of the greatest films ever made. His films explore the complexities of modern Taiwanese life with compassion and formal rigor. Along with Hou Hsiao-hsien, he was a central figure of the Taiwanese New Cinema movement.
