## Summary

One of the last notorious dacoits of the Chambal Valley in Madhya Pradesh who carried out robberies and kidnappings for ransom. He was killed in a police encounter.

## Context: Dacoity in the Chambal Valley

The Chambal Valley region (spanning parts of Madhya Pradesh, Rajasthan, and Uttar Pradesh) has a long history of banditry, locally referred to as **dacoity**. Over decades, armed gangs operated in ravines and rural areas, engaging in robbery, extortion, and kidnapping, and periodically drawing intense police and paramilitary attention.

## Criminal Activity (High Level)

Rambabu Gadariya is described in Indian reporting as one of the last prominent dacoits associated with the Chambal region. Accounts typically attribute to him a pattern of **robberies** and **kidnappings for ransom**, with law enforcement pursuing his gang over an extended period.

## Police Encounter and Death

He was killed in 2002 in what is commonly described as a police "encounter" (an armed confrontation between police and suspects). Such encounters have been a recurring feature of anti-dacoit operations in the Chambal area, particularly during periods when gangs were heavily armed and operating in difficult terrain.

## Legacy

Gadariya's death is often framed as part of the winding down of large-scale dacoit activity in the Chambal Valley. While smaller criminal groups persisted, the era of widely known "Chambal dacoits" declined due to sustained policing, changing local conditions, and shifting criminal incentives.
