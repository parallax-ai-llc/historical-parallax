## Summary

Spanish serial killer known as the 'Vampire of Barcelona' who kidnapped, prostituted, and murdered children in early 20th century Barcelona. She allegedly sold potions made from children's remains to wealthy clients. Killed in prison.

## Historical context

- Martí became a notorious figure in early 20th-century Barcelona, a period marked by sharp social inequality and sensationalist crime reporting.
- Many accounts surrounding her case blend documented facts with rumor, contributing to an enduring and sometimes mythologized public image.

## Allegations and arrest

- She was accused of kidnapping children and exploiting them, with some stories alleging murders and the sale of "remedies" made from human remains.
- Her arrest in 1912 followed the discovery of a child being held captive, which drew immediate public attention.

## Death and legacy

- Martí died in prison in 1913 before a full legal resolution of all accusations.
- The "Vampire of Barcelona" label remains part of local lore and has inspired later books, journalism, and cultural portrayals.
