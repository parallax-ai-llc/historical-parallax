## Summary

Matthew Flinders was the first person to circumnavigate Australia and is credited with naming the continent. His cat Trim accompanied him on his voyages.

## Early Career

Flinders joined the **Royal Navy** as a teenager and developed skills in navigation and surveying during Britain’s maritime wars and imperial expansion. He is often paired in historical accounts with **George Bass**, with whom he undertook voyages that improved European knowledge of Australia’s coastline.

## Exploration and Surveying

- **Tasmania (Van Diemen’s Land)**: In 1798–1799, Flinders’ voyage helped demonstrate that Tasmania was separated from the mainland by what became known as **Bass Strait**, a major discovery for shipping routes.
- **Circumnavigation of Australia**: Beginning in 1801, he led an expedition that produced extensive coastal charts. His surveys contributed to safer navigation and to European colonial planning.

## Naming “Australia”

Flinders advocated for the use of the name **“Australia”** for the continent in place of terms such as “New Holland.” While naming conventions evolved over time, he is widely credited with helping popularize the term through his writings.

## Imprisonment and Later Life

After being shipwrecked and seeking repairs, Flinders was detained on **Mauritius** (then under French control) for several years during wartime, a period that significantly delayed publication and recognition of his work.

He eventually returned to Britain and prepared his major account, **_A Voyage to Terra Australis_ (1814)**, including maps and coastal descriptions drawn from his surveys.

## Trim the Cat

Flinders’ cat **Trim** became a minor historical celebrity. Flinders wrote affectionately about Trim, and later retellings use the story to humanize long and dangerous naval expeditions.

## Legacy

Flinders is remembered as one of the most important early chartmakers of Australia. Places, institutions, and landmarks in Australia bear his name, reflecting his role in mapping the coastline and shaping European geographic understanding of the continent.
