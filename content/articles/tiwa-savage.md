## Summary

Tiwa Savage (born February 5, 1980, in Isale Eko, Lagos, Nigeria) is one of Africa's most celebrated recording artists, widely regarded as the "Queen of Afrobeats." With a career spanning over two decades, she has been instrumental in bringing Nigerian music to global audiences, earning recognition from major Western media outlets and sharing stages with some of the world's biggest artists. Her fusion of Afrobeats, R&B, and pop has redefined the sound of contemporary African music.

Beyond her musical achievements, Tiwa Savage has become a cultural ambassador for Nigeria and the broader African continent. Her fearless artistry, combined with her willingness to confront personal struggles publicly, has made her an icon of resilience and authenticity. She has consistently broken barriers for African women in the music industry, both commercially and critically.

## Early Life & Background

Born Tiwatope Savage in Isale Eko, Lagos, Tiwa moved to London at the age of 11 with her family. She studied music at the Berkeley College of Music in Boston before returning to the UK, where she honed her craft as a backing vocalist and songwriter. She wrote and performed for artists including Mary J. Blige, George Michael, and Emeli Sandé during her early career in the UK music industry.

## Rise to Prominence

Tiwa Savage returned to Nigeria in 2009 and signed with Mavin Records, founded by producer Don Jazzy, in 2012. Her debut album *Once Upon a Time* (2013) was a critical and commercial success, earning her multiple awards including Best New Act at the 2013 MTV Africa Music Awards. The album's singles, particularly "Kele Kele Love" and "Without My Heart," established her as a dominant force in Afrobeats.

In 2019, she signed a distribution deal with Universal Music Group's Def Jam Africa, marking a significant milestone for African artists on the global stage. Her EP *Celia* (2020) debuted at number one on the Apple Music Africa chart and featured collaborations with Sam Smith and Brandy, demonstrating her crossover appeal.

## Key Achievements

- Won Best African Act at the 2018 MTV Europe Music Awards
- First African artist to appear on the cover of *Vibe* magazine (2019)
- Named on Time magazine's list of influential people
- Signed a global deal with Sony Music Entertainment in 2021
- Released the acclaimed EP *Water & Garri* (2021), which garnered widespread critical praise
- Performed at the 2022 Global Citizen Festival in Accra, Ghana
- Received an honorary doctorate from the University of Lagos in 2023

## Controversies & Criticism

In 2021, Tiwa Savage disclosed that she was being blackmailed over an intimate video, which she addressed publicly rather than pay the extortionist. Her decision to speak out was widely praised as courageous. She has also faced criticism over public disputes with former husband Teebillz (Tunji Balogun), whom she married in 2013 and separated from in 2016, with both parties making accusations in public forums. The breakup attracted significant media attention and scrutiny of her personal life.

## Legacy & Influence

Tiwa Savage's career has fundamentally shifted the global perception of African music and African women in entertainment. She has mentored younger African artists and used her platform to advocate for gender equality in the music industry. Her success paved the way for a generation of female Afrobeats artists including Ayra Starr and Tems. She remains one of the highest-earning African female musicians and continues to influence Afrobeats' ongoing global expansion.
