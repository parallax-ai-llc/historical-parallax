## Summary

The Marc Dutroux affair in 1996 exposed a pedophile ring and massive failures in Belgian police and justice system. 300,000 people marched in the White March demanding reforms.

## Case Background

The term commonly refers to the **Marc Dutroux** case, which became a national crisis in Belgium after revelations about child abduction, sexual abuse, and murders, as well as apparent failures and miscoordination among police and judicial authorities.

## Institutional Failures Highlighted

Public anger focused on issues such as:

- **Communication breakdowns** between investigative bodies.
- **Missed opportunities** to locate victims.
- **Perceived negligence** and lack of accountability.

## Public Response

The **White March** in 1996 drew hundreds of thousands of participants and became a symbol of public demand for reform, victim protection, and more effective handling of crimes against children.

## Longer-Term Effects

The scandal influenced reforms and public debate around policing, judicial processes, and child-protection systems, and it remains a defining event in modern Belgian social and political history.
