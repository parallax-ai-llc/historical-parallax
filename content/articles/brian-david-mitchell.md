## Summary
Brian David Mitchell kidnapped 14-year-old Elizabeth Smart from her Salt Lake City home in 2002 and held her for nine months. He was convicted of kidnapping and sexual assault, sentenced to life.

## Background (High Level)
- Brian David Mitchell was a former street preacher and itinerant figure associated with religiously framed delusions and coercive control narratives reported in court proceedings.
- Public reporting describes a pattern of instability, including prior arrests and behavior that drew law-enforcement attention before the 2002 kidnapping.

## 2002 Kidnapping of Elizabeth Smart
- **Date/Location:** Elizabeth Smart was abducted from her family home in Salt Lake City, Utah (June 2002).
- **Captivity:** She was held for roughly nine months, moved between campsites/locations, and subjected to repeated sexual assault.
- **Control Methods:** Reporting and testimony describe threats, isolation, and psychological coercion as core elements of the captivity.

## Investigation and Recovery
- **Initial search:** The case prompted a large-scale local and national search effort, including extensive media coverage.
- **Recovery:** Smart was located alive in March 2003 in Sandy, Utah, after police and the public identified Mitchell and his associate Wanda Barzee.

## Legal Proceedings and Sentence
- Mitchell was tried in federal court and convicted on counts that included kidnapping and aggravated sexual abuse.
- He received a **life sentence** in federal prison; Wanda Barzee also received a federal sentence for her role.

## Public Impact
- The case influenced public awareness around child abduction, trauma-informed victim advocacy, and the complexity of recovery after prolonged captivity.
- Elizabeth Smart later became a prominent advocate, speaking publicly about survivor support and policy.
