## Historical Background (High-Level)

### Second Boer War concentration camps (1899–1902)

During the Second Boer War, the British military established a system of civilian concentration camps, primarily for Boer women and children (and also separate camps for Black South Africans).

These camps became internationally notorious due to:

- Overcrowding and inadequate shelter
- Poor sanitation and limited medical care
- High mortality from disease and malnutrition

Public pressure grew as information about conditions reached audiences in Britain, Ireland, and elsewhere through investigations, reports, and press coverage.

### Irish anti-imperial sentiment

In the same period, Irish nationalist politics often framed British imperial warfare as part of a broader pattern of coercion and colonial governance.

Irish public debate about the Boer War included:

- Moral condemnation of civilian suffering
- Political critique of empire and militarism
- Solidarity narratives that linked Irish and Boer experiences under British rule
