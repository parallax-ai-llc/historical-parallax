## Summary

Robert Gabriel Mugabe (February 21, 1924 â€“ September 6, 2019) led Zimbabwe for 37 years, first as Prime Minister (1980-1987) then as President (1987-2017). Initially celebrated as a hero of African liberation who peacefully transitioned white-ruled Rhodesia to black-majority rule, Mugabe transformed into an authoritarian dictator. His rule was marked by violent suppression of opposition, disastrous land seizures that destroyed agriculture, hyperinflation reaching absurd levels, political violence, and refusal to relinquish power despite economic collapse. A military coup finally forced him from office in 2017, ending one of Africa's longest-running dictatorships.

## Early Life and Education

### Origins

Born February 21, 1924, in Kutama, Southern Rhodesia:

**Background**:
- Shona ethnic group
- Catholic mission station
- Father abandoned family
- Mother raised him
- Educated by Jesuits

### Exceptional Student

**Education**:
- Jesuit mission schools
- Fort Hare University, South Africa (BA)
- University of London (correspondence degrees)
- Eventually held seven degrees
- Teacher for many years

**Characteristics**:
- Brilliant student
- Voracious reader
- Intellectual
- Bookish rather than athletic

## Anti-Colonial Struggle

### Rhodesian Context

**White Minority Rule**:
- British colony
- 5% white, 95% black
- Whites controlled everything
- Ian Smith's Unilateral Declaration of Independence (1965)
- International sanctions

### Joining Liberation Struggle

**1960s**:
- Joined nationalist movement
- ZANU (Zimbabwe African National Union) leader
- Imprisoned by Rhodesian government (1964-1974)
- Ten years in prison
- Continued studies while imprisoned
- Wife Sally died while he was in prison

### Guerrilla War (1972-1979)

**ZANLA**:
- ZANU's military wing
- Based in Mozambique
- Guerrilla warfare against Rhodesia
- Mugabe overall leader
- Brutal conflict

**Lancaster House Agreement** (1979):
- Negotiations in London
- Transition to majority rule
- Elections planned
- Land reform delayed

## Independence and Early Rule (1980-1987)

### 1980 Elections

**ZANU Victory**:
- Won 63% of vote
- Mugabe became Prime Minister
- Initially peaceful transition
- Whites reassured

**Reconciliation**:
- Famous speech calling for unity
- "Let us forgive and forget"
- Ian Smith remained in parliament
- Whites kept farms
- International praise

### Gukurahundi (1983-1987)

**Matabeleland Massacres**:
- Targeted Ndebele ethnic group (Joshua Nkomo's base)
- North Korean-trained Fifth Brigade
- Villages massacred
- 20,000-30,000 killed
- Buried in mass graves

**Purpose**:
- Eliminate opposition
- Crush ZAPU (Nkomo's party)
- Assert Shona dominance
- Create one-party state

**Cover-Up**:
- Called "brigands" suppression
- International community ignored
- Only acknowledged decades later

## President for Life (1987-2017)

### Constitutional Changes

**1987**:
- Abolished Prime Minister position
- Created Executive President
- Merged ZANU and ZAPU
- One-party state (ZANU-PF)

**1990s**:
- Economic problems emerged
- Corruption rampant
- War veterans demanding benefits

---

## Land Reform Disaster (2000-2008)

### Fast-Track Land Reform

**Background**:
- White farmers (4,000) owned 70% of arable land
- Waited for willing seller-willing buyer
- Process slow
- Pressure from war veterans

**2000**:
- Encouraged "war veterans" to seize white-owned farms
- Often not actual war veterans
- Violence against farmers
- Several killed
- Thousands fled

**Consequences**:
- Commercial agriculture collapsed
- Farm workers (black) displaced
- Food shortages
- Breadbasket of Africa became food importer
- Economy devastated

### Democratic Movement Crushed

**MDC** (Movement for Democratic Change):
- Morgan Tsvangirai led opposition
- Won support among workers, urban poor
- Challenged ZANU-PF monopoly

**Response**:
- Violent suppression
- Opposition beaten, tortured
- Tsvangirai beaten publicly
- Elections rigged
- 2008 election violence particularly brutal

---

## Economic Collapse

### Hyperinflation

**World Record**:
- Reached 79.6 billion percent monthly (2008)
- Prices doubled every 24 hours
- $100 trillion Zimbabwe dollar notes printed
- Worthless paper

**Causes**:
- Land reform destroyed agriculture
- Printing money
- Corruption
- Mismanagement
- Sanctions (blamed by Mugabe)

**Daily Life**:
- Wheelbarrows of cash to buy bread
- Abandoned currency for USD, South African rand
- Savings wiped out
- Middle class destroyed

### Cholera and Healthcare

**2008 Cholera Outbreak**:
- 100,000 cases
- 4,000 deaths
- Infrastructure collapsed
- No clean water
- Mugabe blamed British

**Brain Drain**:
- Millions fled (3-4 million to South Africa alone)
- Doctors, teachers, skilled workers left
- Remittances kept families alive

## The 2017 Coup

### "Operation Restore Legacy"

**November 15, 2017**:
- Military took control
- House arrest for Mugabe
- "Not a coup" (officially)
- Claimed to target criminals around Mugabe

**Negotiations**:
- Mugabe refused to resign initially
- Given television appearance (confused, rambling)
- Expected resignation, didn't happen

**November 21, 2017**:
- Parliament began impeachment
- Mugabe finally resigned
- Letter read in parliament
- Cheering in streets

**Emmerson Mnangagwa**:
- Became president
- Former ally, intelligence chief
- "The Crocodile"
- Complicit in Gukurahundi
