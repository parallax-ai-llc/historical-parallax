## Summary

Paul Berg was an American biochemist who shared the 1980 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his fundamental studies of the biochemistry of nucleic acids, particularly recombinant DNA. He created the first recombinant DNA molecule in 1972, combining DNA from different species. He also organized the Asilomar Conference in 1975 to address the safety of genetic engineering.
