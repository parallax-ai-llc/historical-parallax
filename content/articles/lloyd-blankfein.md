## Summary

Lloyd Blankfein served as CEO and chairman of Goldman Sachs from 2006 to 2018, navigating the firm through the 2008 financial crisis. Under his leadership, Goldman converted to a bank holding company and received a $10 billion government bailout. He successfully transformed Goldman from a partnership culture to a more diversified financial institution. His controversial statement that Goldman was 'doing God's work' drew widespread criticism.
