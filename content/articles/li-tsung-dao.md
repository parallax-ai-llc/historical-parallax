## Summary

Li Tsung-Dao (Tsung-Dao Lee) was a Chinese-American physicist who shared the 1957 Nobel Prize in Physics with Yang Zhenning for proposing that parity is not conserved in weak nuclear interactions. At age 30, he was one of the youngest Nobel laureates. He also made important contributions to statistical mechanics and field theory.
