## Summary

American student Amanda Knox was convicted and later acquitted of the murder of British roommate Meredith Kercher in Italy. The case became one of the most controversial trials of the 21st century.

## Background

- The case arose from the 2007 killing of Meredith Kercher, a British exchange student in Perugia, Italy.
- Amanda Knox (an American student) and Raffaele Sollecito (an Italian student) were among those investigated and prosecuted.

## Investigation and Evidence (High-Level)

- Public controversy centered on contested forensic evidence, interrogation practices, and interpretation of circumstantial indicators.
- Over time, some evidence and reasoning used in early proceedings were challenged in later appeals, contributing to reversals.

## Trial and Appeals (High-Level)

- The legal process involved multiple trials and appellate decisions within the Italian system over several years.
- The 2015 outcome is widely described as a definitive acquittal for Knox and Sollecito in the Italian courts.

## Media and Public Impact

- Became an international media phenomenon, illustrating how intense coverage can shape public narratives around guilt, innocence, and legal process.
- Frequently cited in discussions of cross-border criminal justice, language barriers, and the risks of sensationalism.

## Related Outcomes

- Rudy Guede was also convicted in connection with the murder in separate proceedings.
