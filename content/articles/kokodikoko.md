## Summary

Anti-Balaka militia leader whose real name is Yvon Koudou who commanded forces responsible for ethnic violence against Muslims in the Central African Republic.

## Background and context

- The Anti-Balaka movement emerged during the Central African Republic conflict as predominantly Christian and animist militias.
- Many Anti-Balaka groups presented themselves as self-defense forces, but were widely implicated in retaliatory attacks and mass abuses.
- The conflict was marked by cycles of violence between the Seleka coalition and Anti-Balaka factions, with civilians frequently targeted.

## Role and allegations (high level)

- Kokodikoko has been described in reporting as a commander associated with Anti-Balaka operations.
- Accounts tie his forces to violent campaigns that included intimidation, displacement, and attacks on Muslim communities.
- Responsibility in militia settings is often discussed in terms of command authority, participation, and failure to prevent abuses.

## Impact

- Violence associated with armed groups contributed to large-scale displacement and the breakdown of local security.
- Attacks on religious and ethnic minorities intensified polarization and undermined reconciliation efforts.
- International peacekeeping and transitional justice initiatives faced challenges due to fragmented militias and shifting alliances.
