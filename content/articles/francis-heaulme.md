## Summary

French serial killer known as 'The Criminal Backpacker' suspected of murdering up to 11 people while traveling across France between 1984 and 1992. A drifter, he left no forensic evidence and confessed to nothing. Convicted of two murders.

## Additional Context

### Background

- Heaulme was a drifter who moved frequently and performed short-term jobs, making it difficult for investigators to link crimes across jurisdictions.
- French press coverage often emphasized his transient lifestyle, which contributed to the nickname sometimes translated as “the backpacker criminal.”

### Pattern of crimes (high level)

- The suspected series involved attacks on strangers in multiple regions of France during the 1980s and early 1990s.
- Investigators have described the crimes as opportunistic, with limited evidence left behind and few consistent forensic traces.

### Investigation and prosecutions

- Several cases associated with Heaulme were revisited years later as investigative methods improved and cold cases were re-examined.
- His criminal proceedings illustrate how serial-crime investigations can be complicated when offenses span different police districts and time periods.

### Why the case is notable

- The Heaulme case is frequently cited in discussions of inter-agency coordination and the challenges of building a coherent case narrative without decisive forensic evidence.
- Later convictions (including the Montigny-lès-Metz case) are often treated as a focal point for evaluating earlier unsolved attacks attributed to him.
