## Summary

Liu Xiang became the first Asian man to win Olympic sprint gold (110m hurdles, Athens 2004). He set the WR of 12.88s. His 2008 Beijing withdrawal was a national heartbreak.

## Athletic Profile

Liu Xiang specialized in the 110 meters hurdles, an event combining sprint speed, technique, and rhythmic hurdle clearance under high biomechanical stress.

His international success was notable in the context of Chinese track and field, where sprint-hurdle medals at the highest level were historically rare.

## Major Achievements (High-Level)

The Athens 2004 Olympic gold positioned Liu as a global star and a national symbol of competitiveness in a marquee athletics discipline.

His 12.88 second performance is frequently cited as a benchmark moment for the event and for Asian representation in elite sprinting.

## Injuries and Public Narrative

Liu's later career became closely associated with injury management and high-pressure expectations, particularly around home-soil competition.

The 2008 Beijing Olympics withdrawal drew intense public attention because it happened at the start line in a highly anticipated national moment.

## Legacy

Liu Xiang is often referenced in discussions of sports systems and the psychological weight placed on individual athletes as national representatives.

In Chinese sports history summaries, he appears both as an emblem of breakthrough success and as a case study in how injuries can reshape public perception of elite careers.
