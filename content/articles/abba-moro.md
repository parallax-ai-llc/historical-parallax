## Summary

Nigerian minister of interior under whose watch the 2014 immigration recruitment stampede killed at least 16 job applicants across the country. He was charged with fraud for the botched recruitment exercise.

## Additional Context

### Background and political profile

Abba Moro is a Nigerian politician best known for serving as Minister of Interior during the administration of President Goodluck Jonathan. He has also been active in party politics and electoral contests at the national level.

### 2014 immigration recruitment exercise

In 2014, the Nigerian Immigration Service ran a large-scale recruitment exercise that drew crowds of applicants across multiple states. Reports of deadly crowd crushes during the exercise became a major national controversy, raising questions about planning, contracting, and public safety oversight at the ministry and agency level.

### Allegations and court process

Following investigations into the recruitment exercise, Moro was charged in relation to alleged fraud and corruption connected to the handling of recruitment fees and contracts. The case became part of a broader pattern of high-profile anti-corruption prosecutions in Nigeria, with proceedings stretching over multiple years.

### Why this matters

The episode is often cited in discussions about:

- Public-sector procurement and outsourcing risks
- Safety planning for mass recruitment and testing events
- Accountability for contractors and supervising ministries
