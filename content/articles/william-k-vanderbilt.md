## Summary

William Kissam Vanderbilt was an American member of the Vanderbilt family who served as a director of several railroads. He commissioned the Marble House and Petit Chateau mansions and was prominent in New York and Newport society. He was a grandson of Commodore Vanderbilt and helped manage the family's railroad interests during the Gilded Age.
