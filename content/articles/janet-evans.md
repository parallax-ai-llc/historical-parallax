## Summary

Janet Evans won four Olympic gold medals in distance freestyle and held the 800m freestyle world record for 19 years.

## Breakthrough and Olympic Success

Evans became a standout in American distance freestyle while still a teenager, known for a high-tempo stroke and exceptional endurance. At the 1988 Seoul Olympics she won multiple gold medals and quickly became one of the most recognizable swimmers of her generation.

## World Records

Across the late 1980s and early 1990s Evans set world records in long-distance freestyle events, including the 800 m freestyle record that endured for many years. Her record-setting times helped define the performance standard for women's distance swimming during the period.

## Competition Style

Commentators often noted Evans's comparatively smaller stature relative to some rivals and her ability to maintain speed through sustained cadence and efficient pacing. Her success is frequently used as an example of how aerobic capacity and race strategy can dominate in distance events.

## Later Career and Comeback Attempts

After stepping back from elite competition, Evans remained involved in swimming and later attempted a competitive comeback around the time of the 2012 Olympic cycle. While she did not return to Olympic medal contention, the effort drew attention as a rare high-profile return in a sport that typically peaks at younger ages.

## Influence and Legacy

Evans is remembered as one of the great American distance swimmers, with Olympic titles and records that remained influential long after her prime. Her career is often referenced in discussions of training evolution, endurance development, and the history of women's swimming.
