## Summary

Chang Ki-young was a Korean technocrat who served as Deputy Prime Minister and played a crucial role in planning South Korea's science-driven economic development strategy. He helped establish the Korea Institute of Science and Technology (KIST) and created the policy framework that linked scientific research to industrial development.
