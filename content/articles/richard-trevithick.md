## Summary

Richard Trevithick built the first high-pressure steam engine and the first full-scale working railway steam locomotive. His 1804 Penydarren locomotive demonstrated that steam traction on rails was feasible. His pioneering work laid the groundwork for the entire railway age, though he received little recognition during his lifetime.
