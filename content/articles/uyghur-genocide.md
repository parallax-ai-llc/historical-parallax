## Summary

Chinese governments mass detention and persecution of Uyghur Muslims in Xinjiang since 2017. Over one million detained in camps facing forced labor, sterilization, and cultural erasure.

## Background

- The term refers to allegations and findings concerning severe human-rights abuses against Uyghurs and other Turkic Muslims in the Xinjiang region of China, particularly since 2017.
- Chinese authorities have described their policies as counter-terrorism and "vocational education" initiatives; critics describe them as mass repression and forced assimilation.

## Reported and documented abuses (high-level)

- Mass detention in a network of facilities commonly described by researchers and governments as internment camps.
- Coercive labor programs and restrictions on movement, religious practice, and cultural expression.
- Accounts and reports alleging forced sterilization, family separation, and pervasive surveillance.

## International response

- Several governments and legislatures have characterized the situation as genocide or crimes against humanity, while others have used different legal and political language.
- International organizations, journalists, and researchers have used satellite imagery, leaked documents, and witness testimony to document the system.
- Diplomatic disputes and sanctions have followed, alongside calls for independent access and investigation.

## Significance

- The issue has become a major focal point in contemporary debates over human rights, state surveillance, and the use of forced labor in global supply chains.
- It has also affected China’s relations with multiple countries, including in trade and security contexts.
