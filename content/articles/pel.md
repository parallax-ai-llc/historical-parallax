## Summary

Pelé won three World Cups (1958, 1962, 1970) and scored over 1,000 career goals. He is widely regarded as the greatest footballer in history.

## Background

Pelé (Edson Arantes do Nascimento) was a Brazilian footballer widely regarded as one of the greatest players in the sport’s history. He became a global icon through his performances for Brazil’s national team and for Santos FC.

## Career Highlights

Open sources commonly note his major role in Brazil’s World Cup successes and his extraordinary goal-scoring record, while also acknowledging that historical statistics can vary by counting conventions across competitions.

## Legacy

- Pelé’s influence extended beyond the pitch, shaping the worldwide image of Brazilian football.
- He became an ambassador-like public figure for the sport in the late 20th century, contributing to football’s global popularity.
