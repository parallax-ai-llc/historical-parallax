## Background

Pelé (Edson Arantes do Nascimento) was a Brazilian footballer widely regarded as one of the greatest players in the sport’s history. He became a global icon through his performances for Brazil’s national team and for Santos FC.

## Career Highlights

Open sources commonly note his major role in Brazil’s World Cup successes and his extraordinary goal-scoring record, while also acknowledging that historical statistics can vary by counting conventions across competitions.

## Legacy

- Pelé’s influence extended beyond the pitch, shaping the worldwide image of Brazilian football.
- He became an ambassador-like public figure for the sport in the late 20th century, contributing to football’s global popularity.

## Additional Context

### Club career

Pelé spent most of his prime at Santos FC, where he won major domestic and continental titles and became a central figure in the club’s international tours. He later played for the New York Cosmos, contributing to increased attention to football (soccer) in the United States.

### Playing style

Contemporary and retrospective accounts describe him as a complete forward: a prolific finisher with two-footed shooting, strong aerial ability, creativity in tight spaces, and an ability to combine playmaking with goal scoring.

### Cultural impact

Pelé’s fame made him one of the first truly global sports superstars. His name became closely associated with the international commercialization of football and with Brazil’s national sporting identity.
