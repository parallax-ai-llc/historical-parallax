## Context and significance

Colt is most closely associated with the commercialization of the percussion-cap revolver in the United States.
His designs helped make repeating handguns more practical to produce, maintain, and market at scale.

## Manufacturing and business

Colt’s Patent Fire-Arms Manufacturing Company in Hartford, Connecticut, became a prominent 19th-century arms firm.
The company is often discussed in histories of American industrialization because it adopted standardized parts and organized production methods.
Those methods supported interchangeability and repair, and they reduced reliance on single artisans for complete weapons.

## Military and civilian adoption

Colt revolvers were sold to civilian buyers and were also procured in military contexts, especially as demand grew for reliable sidearms.
In general terms, the revolver’s appeal was its ability to fire multiple shots without reloading after each discharge.

## Legacy

The “Colt” brand remained influential after his death, and later Colt products continued to be widely recognized.
Colt’s work is frequently cited in discussions of how patents, marketing, and manufacturing systems shaped the U.S. arms industry.
Colt is also remembered as an early example of aggressive product promotion and brand-building in American manufacturing.
