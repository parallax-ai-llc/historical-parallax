## Career overview

Roberto Baggio is widely regarded as one of Italy's most talented attacking players of the modern era. He played primarily as a **second striker / attacking midfielder**, known for technique, creativity, and set-piece ability.[^1]

## Clubs and domestic career

Baggio's club career spanned multiple top Italian teams, and is often summarized through:

- High-profile transfers between major Serie A clubs
- Consistent goal and assist production from advanced playmaking positions
- Significant injury challenges, particularly earlier in his career

Specific statistics and club-by-club records are detailed in standard references.[^1]

## International career

For the Italy national team, Baggio is closely associated with the 1994 FIFA World Cup, where he played a decisive role in earlier rounds. The missed penalty in the final shootout became a lasting image of that tournament.[^1]

## Awards and recognition

- **Ballon d'Or (1993)** and FIFA World Player of the Year recognition are central to his legacy.[^1]
- He is frequently included in lists of all-time great Italian footballers.

## Legacy

Baggio's legacy is often discussed as a contrast between:

- Peak brilliance and signature moments of skill
- The randomness and psychological weight of penalty shootouts at the highest level
