## Summary

Brazilian serial killer known as 'Pedrinho Matador' who confessed to over 70 murders. Many of his victims were other criminals. He served 34 years in prison before release and was later shot dead.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1968 | Committed first murder at age 14 |
| 1973 | Killed 10 people in revenge for father's imprisonment |
| 2003 | Released after serving maximum 34-year sentence |
| 2023 | Shot and killed in Minas Gerais |

## Background

- Rodrigues Filho became notorious in Brazil for describing many of his killings as targeting criminals, contributing to a “vigilante” narrative in some coverage.
- Accounts of his life often emphasize early exposure to violence and conflict within his family, which he later cited as motivation.

## Crimes and Incarceration

- He reportedly continued killing while imprisoned, including conflicts with other inmates, which reinforced his reputation inside the prison system.
- Confessed totals and attributed victim counts differ by source; public numbers often exceed what can be independently confirmed.

## Release and Public Profile

- He was released after serving a long sentence under Brazil’s then-maximum term rules, and later returned to custody before being released again.
- After prison, he gained an online following through interviews and social media content, turning into a widely discussed (and controversial) public figure.

## Death

- He was killed by gunfire in 2023. Reporting generally treats the killing as an execution-style attack; detailed responsibility varies by source.

## Notes on Sources

- Many details in popular summaries come from interviews and confessions; careful biographies separate documented convictions from self-reported claims.
