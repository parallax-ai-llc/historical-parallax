## Summary

George E. Smith co-invented the charge-coupled device (CCD) with Willard Boyle at Bell Labs in 1969. The CCD image sensor enabled digital photography and imaging in astronomy, medicine, and consumer electronics. He shared the 2009 Nobel Prize in Physics for this invention that replaced film photography.
