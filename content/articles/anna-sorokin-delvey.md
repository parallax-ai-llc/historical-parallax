## Summary

Russian-born German woman who posed as a wealthy German heiress named Anna Delvey and defrauded New York banks, hotels, and friends of hundreds of thousands of dollars. Her story became a Netflix series.

## Background

Sorokin was born in Russia and later lived in Germany before moving in social and professional circles in New York City.

## The "Anna Delvey" persona

Using the name **Anna Delvey**, she presented herself as a wealthy heiress and pursued plans for a private arts and social club. Investigators and journalists later described how financial documents and verbal claims were used to support the image of substantial wealth.

## Criminal case

- Prosecutors alleged a pattern of fraud affecting hotels, banks, and acquaintances.
- The case culminated in a 2019 conviction and a prison sentence within the New York state system.

## Public and cultural impact

Sorokin’s story became a widely discussed example of confidence schemes in elite social settings and inspired dramatized retellings, including the series **Inventing Anna**.

## Notes

Coverage after her conviction also discussed immigration and residency issues alongside the criminal proceedings; reporting differs by outlet and time period.
