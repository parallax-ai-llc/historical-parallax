## Background

- Rodrigo Granda is a Colombian guerrilla figure associated with the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (FARC).
- In media and reporting, he has often been described as the organization’s "foreign minister" because of his role in external contacts and political representation.

## Role in the FARC

- Worked on international outreach, messaging, and liaison activity rather than being known primarily for battlefield command.
- His public profile is tied to FARC’s efforts to seek political legitimacy and international interlocutors during periods of conflict.

## 2004 Capture and Diplomatic Fallout

- In 2004, Granda was detained in Venezuela and subsequently transferred to Colombian authorities.
- The incident drew significant attention because it was widely reported as involving cross-border operation and raised questions of sovereignty and due process.
- The event contributed to diplomatic tension between Colombia and Venezuela at the time.
