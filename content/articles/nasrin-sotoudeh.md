## Summary

Iranian human rights lawyer sentenced to 38 years and 148 lashes for defending women's rights.

## Background

- Born in Tehran (1963) and educated in law, including graduate study in international law.[^1]
- Widely described as a human rights lawyer and activist, particularly known for taking politically sensitive cases in Iran.[^1]

## Focus of Legal Work

Sotoudeh is especially associated with legal defense work involving:

- Activists and opposition figures detained after the 2009 Iranian presidential election protests.[^1]
- Defendants facing the death penalty for acts committed as minors.[^1]
- Women prosecuted for appearing in public without a hijab (mandatory hijab laws).[^1]

## Arrests, Sentences, and Detention (High-Level)

- **2010 arrest:** detained on charges including “spreading propaganda” and alleged national security offenses; held in Evin Prison and later sentenced, with aspects later reduced on appeal.[^1]
- **2018 arrest:** arrested again; reports of her 2019 sentencing varied by source, but widely reported outcomes included a long prison term and corporal punishment (lashes), with later reductions also reported.[^1]

## Recognition and Public Profile

- Recipient of international human rights awards, including PEN America’s Freedom to Write Award (2011) and the European Parliament’s Sakharov Prize (2012).[^1]
- Subject of the documentary *Nasrin* (2020), filmed clandestinely in Iran, portraying her advocacy and legal work.[^1]
- Named to *TIME*’s list of the 100 most influential people (2021).[^1]

## Why She Matters

- Frequently cited as a prominent example of the risks faced by lawyers who defend dissidents and marginalized groups under restrictive legal and political conditions.[^1]
- Her cases are often discussed in the broader context of Iran’s human rights record, due process concerns, and women’s rights activism.[^1]
