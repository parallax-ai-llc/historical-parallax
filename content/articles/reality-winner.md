## Summary

American former NSA translator who leaked a classified report about Russian interference in the 2016 US election to The Intercept. She was identified through printer steganography. Sentenced to 63 months—longest for unauthorized disclosure.

## Context and Background

Reality Winner is known publicly for a high-profile **unauthorized disclosure** of a U.S. intelligence document in 2017.

- She served in the **U.S. Air Force** and later worked as a **contractor** supporting the National Security Agency (NSA), including language/translation work.
- Media and court records commonly describe her as an intelligence professional/contractor rather than a spy in the traditional sense; the case is frequently discussed in the context of whistleblowing and leak prosecutions.

## The 2017 Disclosure

- The leaked material involved an NSA assessment related to **Russian interference** efforts targeting U.S. election infrastructure.
- The document was provided to the press (reported as **The Intercept**) and became part of broader public reporting on 2016 election interference.

## Identification via Printer Tracking Dots

A widely noted element of the case is that investigators reportedly used **printer steganography** (also called “microdot” or tracking-dot patterns) from a printed page to help trace the source.

- Some color laser printers embed machine-identifying patterns in printouts.
- The case is often cited as an example of operational security failures when handling printed documents.

## Legal Proceedings and Sentence

- Winner was arrested in 2017 and later pleaded guilty.
- She received a **63-month** federal sentence, widely reported as one of the longest sentences for a leak to the media under the Espionage Act.
- She was released in 2022 after serving her sentence (with reported additional restrictions during supervised release).

## Public Impact

The case is referenced in discussions about:

- press freedom and source protection,
- classification and transparency,
- and the legal framework used to prosecute unauthorized disclosures.
