## Summary

Pei Wenzhong was a Chinese paleontologist who discovered the first complete skull of Homo erectus pekinensis (Peking Man) at Zhoukoudian in 1929. This discovery was one of the most important finds in paleoanthropology, providing crucial evidence of human evolution. He became the father of Chinese paleontology and led archaeological research for decades.
