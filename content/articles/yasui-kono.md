## Summary

Yasui Kono was a pioneering Japanese female botanist and cytogeneticist who was the first Japanese woman to earn a Doctor of Science degree (1927). She studied plant chromosomes and genetics at a time when women were largely excluded from Japanese academia. She became the first female professor at a Japanese university.
