## Summary

Colombian drug lord known as Otoniel who led the Clan del Golfo, Colombia's largest drug trafficking organization and paramilitary successor group. He was Colombia's most wanted criminal before his capture.

## Background and Aliases

Úsuga, commonly known by the alias “Otoniel,” is described in many summaries as a prominent figure in Colombia’s post-paramilitary criminal landscape. Reporting typically situates his rise within the fragmentation of armed groups and the emergence of successor organizations involved in drug trafficking and territorial control.

## Clan del Golfo (Gulf Clan)

The Clan del Golfo (also known in some sources as the Autodefensas Gaitanistas de Colombia, or AGC) has been characterized as one of Colombia’s largest criminal organizations, combining:

- Drug trafficking and export logistics
- Control of smuggling corridors and local extortion systems
- Armed enforcement capacity in contested regions

## Capture and Extradition

Colombian authorities captured Úsuga in 2021 in an operation that was widely covered in national and international media. Subsequent extradition to the United States and prosecution were described as significant steps in Colombia’s efforts against large-scale trafficking networks.

## Broader Significance

The case is often referenced in discussions about how organized crime persists after formal demobilizations, and how criminal groups adapt by blending political violence, local coercion, and transnational narcotics markets.
