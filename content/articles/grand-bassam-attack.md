## Summary

Al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb attacked the beach resort town of Grand-Bassam in Ivory Coast killing 19 people. Gunmen opened fire on beachgoers and hotel guests.

## Context

Grand-Bassam is a coastal town near Abidjan that is known for tourism and historic sites.
The 2016 attack was significant because Ivory Coast had not previously experienced an event of comparable scale in a major leisure destination.
Regional militant groups had carried out similar attacks in neighboring countries, increasing concern about cross-border threats.

## Attack

On 13 March 2016, multiple gunmen attacked areas frequented by tourists, including beachfront locations and hotels.
Victims included local residents and foreign nationals.
Attackers moved through public spaces firing at civilians, creating a fast-moving incident that was difficult to contain.

## Response

Ivorian security forces engaged the attackers, and the incident ended after the assailants were killed.
Authorities subsequently increased security measures and conducted investigations aimed at identifying logistical support networks.

## Aftermath and Significance

The attack was widely attributed to Al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb (AQIM) in public reporting.
It contributed to renewed emphasis on counterterrorism cooperation in West Africa, including intelligence sharing and protection of tourist areas.
The event also became part of broader debates about how countries balance open public spaces and economic activity with security risks.
