## Summary
On October 4, 1997, David Ghantt, a vault supervisor at Loomis Fargo, stole $17.3 million from the Charlotte, NC branch — the second largest cash robbery in US history at the time. He was caught after accomplices spent lavishly. The story was made into the film 'Masterminds.'

## How the Robbery Worked
The theft relied on insider access: a trusted employee with knowledge of the vault and procedures was able to move large amounts of cash without immediately triggering suspicion. The sheer volume of currency created logistical problems for the group, including transport, concealment, and managing attention during spending.

## Investigation and Arrests
Investigators focused on associates connected to the inside employee and used financial and surveillance leads to identify participants. Reports of conspicuous purchases and inconsistent stories helped authorities build a case that eventually resulted in multiple arrests.

## Legal Outcomes
Participants received prison sentences and were ordered to forfeit assets tied to the robbery. The case became a widely cited example of how lifestyle changes after a major theft can accelerate detection.
