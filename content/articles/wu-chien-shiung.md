## Notes on Naming

This page title reflects an alternate romanization for the physicist commonly known in English as Chien-Shiung Wu.
In biographical sources, she may appear under different spellings depending on transliteration conventions.

## Scientific Work

Wu was an experimental physicist known for influential work in nuclear physics and beta decay.
She is especially associated with experiments that helped demonstrate parity violation in weak interactions.
Her work is frequently cited in accounts of 20th-century physics where experimental verification changed fundamental assumptions.

## Career and Recognition

- Wu worked in American research universities and laboratories during a period when women and immigrants faced significant barriers in academia.
- She contributed to wartime and postwar research programs, and later became a prominent scientific educator and mentor.
- In historical narratives of modern physics, she is often highlighted as a major experimentalist whose contributions were not always matched by institutional recognition.

## Legacy

Wu’s career is used as an example in discussions of both scientific excellence and gender equity in the sciences.
Her name is frequently invoked when analyzing how credit is assigned between theoretical proposals and experimental confirmation.
In popular science writing, she is sometimes called the “First Lady of Physics,” reflecting her stature as an experimentalist.
