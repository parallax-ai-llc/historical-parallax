## Summary

Ibn Sina (Avicenna) was a Persian polymath who is regarded as one of the most significant physicians, astronomers, and philosophers of the Islamic Golden Age. His 'Canon of Medicine' was a standard medical text in Europe and the Islamic world for centuries. He wrote over 400 works on a wide range of subjects, with approximately 240 surviving. Ibn Sina's philosophical system influenced both Islamic and European medieval thought.
