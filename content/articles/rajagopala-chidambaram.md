## Summary

Rajagopala Chidambaram is an Indian nuclear physicist who served as chairman of the Atomic Energy Commission and principal scientific adviser to the Indian government. He played key roles in India's nuclear tests, including Pokhran-I (1974) and Pokhran-II (1998). He also made significant contributions to crystallography and high-pressure physics.
