## Summary
On May 7, 2021, the Colonial Pipeline, which supplies 45% of fuel to the US East Coast, was shut down by a DarkSide ransomware attack. The company paid a $4.4 million ransom. The attack caused fuel shortages and panic buying. The FBI recovered most of the ransom.

## Technical and Operational Details
Investigators attributed the incident to the DarkSide ransomware-as-a-service group. Although reporting emphasized the IT network intrusion, Colonial Pipeline halted pipeline operations in part as a precaution because of billing and operational uncertainty. The episode illustrated how disruption to business systems can cascade into critical infrastructure downtime.

## Ransom Payment and Recovery Efforts
Colonial Pipeline paid a multimillion-dollar ransom in cryptocurrency to obtain a decryption tool and to reduce the outage duration. U.S. authorities later announced they had seized a large portion of the paid ransom by gaining access to a cryptocurrency wallet associated with the attackers.

## Government Response
The attack prompted emergency declarations and federal coordination to reduce regional impacts, alongside renewed emphasis on cybersecurity for critical infrastructure operators. It also contributed to policy discussions on mandatory incident reporting, baseline security practices, and the balance between public safety and operational continuity.
