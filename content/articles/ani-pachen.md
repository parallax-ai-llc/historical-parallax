## Summary

Ani Pachen was a Tibetan Buddhist nun who was one of the most remarkable Tibetan freedom fighters of the 20th century. She led an armed resistance against the Chinese invasion of Tibet in 1958 and spent 21 years in Chinese prisons and labor camps. After her release, she escaped to India and became an advocate for Tibetan independence. Her autobiography Sorrow Mountain documented her extraordinary life of courage and spiritual strength.
