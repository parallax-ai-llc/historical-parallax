## Summary

Fazlur Rahman was a Pakistani-American Islamic scholar who was one of the most important modernist thinkers in 20th-century Islam. He developed a comprehensive methodology for interpreting the Quran in its historical context while extracting universal moral principles. He served as director of Pakistan's Islamic Research Institute before being forced into exile. His work at the University of Chicago trained a generation of Islamic studies scholars.
