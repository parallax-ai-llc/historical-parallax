## Summary

Rowan Williams served as the 104th Archbishop of Canterbury from 2002 to 2012, leading the worldwide Anglican Communion. He is one of the most respected Christian theologians of the contemporary era, writing on topics from patristics to modern philosophy. He navigated the Anglican Communion through divisive debates on homosexuality and women bishops. He was subsequently appointed Master of Magdalene College, Cambridge.
