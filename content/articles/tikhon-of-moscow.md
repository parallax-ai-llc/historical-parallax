## Summary

Tikhon of Moscow was the first Patriarch of Moscow in the restored Russian patriarchate, serving during the Bolshevik Revolution and its aftermath. He was elected Patriarch in 1917 just days before the October Revolution. He opposed the Soviet government's persecution of the Church and its seizure of church property. He was placed under house arrest and died under mysterious circumstances.
