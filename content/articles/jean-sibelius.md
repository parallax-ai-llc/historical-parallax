## Summary

Jean Sibelius was Finland's greatest composer and one of the most important symphonists of the 20th century. His seven symphonies, the Violin Concerto, and the tone poem Finlandia are central to the orchestral repertoire. His music became a powerful symbol of Finnish national identity during the struggle for independence.
