## Summary

Rwandan academic and co-founder of RTLM radio that broadcast hate propaganda and direct incitement to kill during the 1994 genocide. The ICTR's 'Media Case' was a landmark in international criminal law.

## Role in Rwandan Media

Nahimana was associated with Radio Télévision Libre des Mille Collines (RTLM), a private broadcaster that became notorious for disseminating extremist propaganda during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi. Analyses of RTLM emphasize both its reach and its role in amplifying anti-Tutsi ideology in a rapidly escalating political and security crisis.

## The ICTR "Media Case"

The International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR) prosecuted several media figures in what is widely known as the "Media Case." The judgments became influential in international criminal law by clarifying how speech acts, broadcasting, and editorial control can amount to crimes such as direct and public incitement to commit genocide when tied to intent and context.

## Significance and Debate

The case is often discussed for its implications for:

- **Accountability for mass-atrocity propaganda**
- **The boundary between protected speech and criminal incitement**
- **Media ethics and professional responsibility** during conflict

## Legacy

Nahimana's conviction and sentence reductions on appeal remain part of broader scholarship on Rwanda, transitional justice, and the legal standards for incitement-related liability.
