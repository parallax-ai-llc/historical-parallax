## Summary

Ravidas (Raidas) was a North Indian mystic, poet-saint, and social reformer who was born into an 'untouchable' leather-working caste. He was a disciple of Ramananda and composed devotional songs challenging caste discrimination and promoting spiritual equality. His poetry is included in the Guru Granth Sahib and is central to the Ravidassia community. He envisioned 'Begumpura,' an ideal city without sorrow or discrimination.
