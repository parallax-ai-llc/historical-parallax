## Origins and Development

Comando Vermelho (CV) is commonly described as emerging from Brazil’s prison system, where political prisoners and common criminals interacted under the military dictatorship. The group later expanded into drug trafficking and armed control of territory in parts of Rio de Janeiro.

## Organization and Activity (High Level)

- Prison-based coordination and influence over street-level affiliates.
- Revenue primarily associated with narcotics trafficking and illicit markets.
- Armed disputes with rival factions and intermittent confrontations with police.

## Relationship to the State and Society

CV’s history is intertwined with prison overcrowding, inequality, and the governance vacuum in some urban areas. Crackdowns and “pacification” strategies have at times reshaped local control but have not eliminated factional dynamics.

## Regional Significance

The organization is frequently cited in analyses of Brazilian public security because it illustrates how prison politics, local militias/factions, and drug economies can reinforce one another over decades.


## Key Takeaways

- Prison dynamics can strongly shape organized crime on the outside.
- Territorial control and illicit revenue streams reinforce faction longevity.
