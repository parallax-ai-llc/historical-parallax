## Summary

Addison Rae Easterling, known professionally as Addison Rae, is an American social media personality, actress, and singer who rose to prominence on TikTok in 2019. By 2020, she had become one of the platform's most-followed creators, amassing over 80 million followers and earning recognition as TikTok's highest-paid creator by Forbes. Her crossover into mainstream entertainment — spanning film, music, and fashion — marks her as one of the most commercially successful figures to emerge from the social media generation.

Born on October 6, 2000, in Lafayette, Louisiana, Rae parlayed a passion for dance into a digital career that quickly transcended the platform. She became emblematic of a new wave of influencers who leveraged short-form video content into multi-industry careers, signing with major talent agencies and landing brand deals worth millions of dollars before her 21st birthday.

Her trajectory reflects the broader shift in celebrity culture toward social-media-native stars, and her business moves — including a beauty brand, acting roles, and music releases — established a template widely observed by other creators seeking mainstream crossover appeal.

## Early Life & Background

Addison Rae Easterling was born on October 6, 2000, in Lafayette, Louisiana, to Sheri Nicole and Monty Lopez. She grew up in a competitive dance environment, training from a young age and competing in dance circuits across the South. She attended Calvary Baptist Academy before enrolling at Louisiana State University in 2019 to study broadcast journalism — a program she left within months as her TikTok following exploded.

## Rise to Prominence

Rae began posting dance videos on TikTok in mid-2019 and gained millions of followers within weeks. By early 2020 she had surpassed 50 million followers, making her one of the app's fastest-growing accounts. In August 2020, Forbes estimated her annual earnings at $5 million, crowning her TikTok's highest-paid creator. She was a founding member of the Hype House, a collaborative content creator collective in Los Angeles that generated substantial media coverage throughout 2020.

Her friendship with Kourtney Kardashian brought additional mainstream visibility, and in 2021 she signed with talent agency WME. That same year, Netflix released her lead film role in *He's All That*, a gender-swapped remake of the 1999 teen comedy — making her one of the first TikTok-native stars to headline a major studio production.

## Key Achievements

- Reached 88+ million TikTok followers, among the highest totals on the platform
- Named Forbes' highest-paid TikTok creator in 2020 ($5 million in earnings)
- Starred in Netflix's *He's All That* (2021), which debuted at No. 1 on the platform
- Released debut single "Obsessed" in March 2021, which charted on the Billboard Hot 100
- Launched beauty brand Item Beauty in 2020, in partnership with Madeby Collective

## Controversies & Criticism

Rae faced criticism in 2021 when she appeared on *The Tonight Show* performing dances without crediting the Black creators who originated them — a controversy that sparked broader conversation about cultural appropriation and credit within TikTok's dance community. She also received scrutiny over her family's public presence, particularly regarding her father's behavior on social media. Critics have questioned the depth of her acting and musical abilities, characterizing her mainstream ventures as products of follower count rather than demonstrated craft.

## Legacy & Influence

Rae is frequently cited as a defining example of the TikTok-to-Hollywood pipeline, demonstrating that short-form video fame can translate into conventional entertainment careers. Her business model — brand deals, a beauty line, music, and film — became a reference point for subsequent creators pursuing similar paths. She helped legitimize TikTok as a serious launchpad for mainstream careers at a time when the platform was still regarded skeptically by traditional entertainment industries.
