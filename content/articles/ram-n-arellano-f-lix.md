## Summary

Mexican drug lord and enforcer of the Tijuana Cartel known for extreme violence. He was responsible for numerous murders and was on the FBI's Ten Most Wanted list. Killed in a shootout with police in Mazatlán.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 1997 | Placed on FBI Ten Most Wanted list |

## Role in the Tijuana Cartel (High-Level)

- Arellano Félix was widely described as a leading enforcer within the **Tijuana Cartel** (also associated with the Arellano Félix Organization).
- Reporting often emphasized his reputation for:
  - coercion and intimidation,
  - targeted killings,
  - internal discipline within trafficking operations.

## Cross-Border Significance

- The organization’s activity was frequently framed around:
  - trafficking corridors between northwestern Mexico and the United States,
  - competition with rival trafficking groups,
  - violent disputes over routes and local control.
- U.S. and Mexican law enforcement treated the group as a major transnational organized-crime actor during the 1990s.

## Manhunt and Law-Enforcement Attention

- His placement on the **FBI Ten Most Wanted** list signaled a high-priority pursuit and elevated public awareness.
- Such listings are typically used to:
  - distribute identifying information,
  - encourage tips,
  - coordinate multi-agency efforts across jurisdictions.

## Death and Aftermath

- His death in 2002 removed a prominent operational figure from the cartel’s leadership structure.
- Accounts of the period after his death often discuss:
  - shifts in cartel hierarchy,
  - changes in rivalry patterns,
  - continued violence linked to trafficking competition.
