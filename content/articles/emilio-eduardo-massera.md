## Summary

Argentine Navy admiral who ran the ESMA detention center during the Dirty War where thousands were tortured and killed. Convicted of murder, torture, and theft of babies.

## Additional Context

### Role in Argentinas military government

- Massera was one of the three members of the first military junta that seized power in March 1976, representing the Argentine Navy.[^1]
- During the period commonly known as the Dirty War, security forces carried out widespread illegal detention, torture, forced disappearance, and extrajudicial killing of perceived political opponents.[^2]

### ESMA and clandestine detention

- The Navy Mechanics School (ESMA) in Buenos Aires functioned as a major clandestine detention and torture center under the dictatorship; many detainees were later killed or disappeared.[^3]
- Massera is widely associated with the Navys repressive apparatus and with ESMAs operation during the junta period.[^1][^3]

### Legal proceedings and later years

- In the 1985 Trial of the Juntas, Massera was convicted and sentenced to life imprisonment for crimes committed under the dictatorship.[^4]
- He was later pardoned during President Carlos Menems administration; those pardons were subsequently challenged and overturned as Argentina resumed prosecutions for dictatorship-era crimes.[^1][^4]
