## Summary

ISIS-allied militants seized Marawi City in a five-month siege that killed over 1,000 fighters and 168 soldiers. The battle destroyed much of the historic city center.

## Additional Context

### Alternate names and framing

In many sources the event is referred to as the **Siege of Marawi** or the **Battle of Marawi**. It is widely discussed both as a counterterrorism operation and as a major episode in the long-running Moro conflict in Mindanao.

### Key armed actors

Reporting commonly describes the militants as being aligned with the Islamic State’s regional networks, with prominent factions including the **Maute group** and elements of **Abu Sayyaf**. The fighting began after Philippine security forces moved to capture a senior militant leader believed to be in the city.

### Military and political significance

The battle is frequently characterized as one of the most intense urban engagements in modern Philippine history. It also became a reference point in domestic security policy debates, including the scope of emergency powers and the role of the armed forces in internal security operations.

### Humanitarian and reconstruction impact

Beyond combatant casualties, the siege caused substantial destruction in central Marawi and drove large-scale civilian displacement. Post-siege attention has focused on clearing unexploded ordnance, rebuilding housing and commercial areas, and addressing the long-term social and economic effects on displaced communities.

### Information environment

Accounts of casualty totals and group affiliations can differ by source (government statements, independent reporting, and later historical summaries). For careful study, it is useful to compare multiple datasets and official after-action reporting.
