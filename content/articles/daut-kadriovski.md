## Summary

Macedonian drug lord who ran one of the largest heroin trafficking networks in Europe. He supplied heroin from Turkey through the Balkans to Western Europe. Arrested in Turkey in 2003.

## Context

Kadriovski is typically discussed in the context of Balkan and Turkish trafficking routes that moved heroin toward Western European markets.

Such networks often relied on cross-border logistics, corruption, compartmentalized roles, and the ability to adapt routes in response to policing pressure.

## Trafficking Route (High-Level)

Public descriptions of the “Balkan route” generally refer to a corridor connecting production regions and transit hubs through southeastern Europe toward central and western European consumer markets.

These supply chains can include multiple independent groups rather than a single vertically integrated organization.
