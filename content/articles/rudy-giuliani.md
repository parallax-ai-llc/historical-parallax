## Background

- Born May 28, 1944, in New York City, U.S.[^1]
- Studied at Manhattan College (B.A.) and New York University School of Law (J.D.).[^1]

## Public Service and Political Career

- Served as an Assistant United States Attorney and later as a senior federal official in the U.S. Department of Justice (including Associate Attorney General).[^1]
- U.S. Attorney for the Southern District of New York (1983–1989), associated with high-profile organized-crime and public-corruption prosecutions.[^1]
- Entered New York City politics and was elected Mayor of New York City, serving two terms (1994–2001).[^1]

## Mayor of New York City (1994–2001)

- Emphasized crime reduction and "quality-of-life" enforcement; his administration is often discussed alongside policing data systems such as CompStat and broader NYPD strategy debates of the 1990s.[^1]
- National profile increased after the September 11, 2001 attacks while he was still mayor, due to the city’s emergency response and public communications during the crisis.[^1]

## Post-Mayoral Activities

- Founded and led private-sector ventures (including consulting and security-focused businesses) after leaving office.[^1]
- Ran for the 2008 Republican presidential nomination, ending his campaign during the primary season.[^1]

## Role in 2020 Election Disputes

- Served as a personal attorney to Donald Trump during and after the 2020 U.S. presidential election.[^1]
- Became a prominent public advocate for claims of election irregularities; many of these claims were rejected in court proceedings and official reviews.[^1]

## Legal and Disciplinary Actions

- Faced professional discipline in relation to post-2020 election legal advocacy, including suspension and later disbarment in New York state (per reporting summarized in Wikipedia).[^1]
- Was found liable in a defamation case brought by Georgia election workers Ruby Freeman and Shaye Moss, with a jury awarding substantial damages in 2023 (as summarized by Wikipedia).[^1]
- Was charged or indicted in connection with election-related investigations in at least one jurisdiction (notably the Georgia 2020 election interference case), with case status evolving over time.[^1]
