## Summary

Italian-American mobster who was boss of the Bufalino crime family in northeastern Pennsylvania. He was one of the most powerful and secretive mob bosses in history, portrayed by Joe Pesci in 'The Irishman.' Connected to Hoffa disappearance.

## Crime Family and Influence

Russell Bufalino is generally described as the head of the Bufalino crime family, an organized-crime group associated with northeastern Pennsylvania. Compared to more publicly visible mafia leaders, he is often characterized in secondary accounts as low-profile and cautious, with influence exercised through relationships and intermediaries.

## Links to Major Mafia Episodes

Bufalino’s name appears in narratives about broader mid-20th-century American organized crime, including inter-family coordination and meetings. He is also frequently mentioned in discussions of the 1975 disappearance of labor leader Jimmy Hoffa, though specific claims about involvement are disputed and depend on sources.

## Legal Troubles

He faced federal prosecution and, in later years, was convicted on charges such as extortion-related offenses. Like many organized-crime figures, his public biography is shaped by a combination of court records, investigative reporting, and later reconstructions.

## Cultural Portrayal

Bufalino’s depiction in the film "The Irishman" contributed to renewed popular interest, but dramatized portrayals can compress timelines and simplify networks. Historical treatment usually distinguishes between what is established in court and what remains allegation or inference.

## Notes on Sources

For organized-crime history, biographies often blend confirmed legal records with claims from informants or journalists. When assessing specific allegations (especially around the Hoffa disappearance), careful sourcing and corroboration are important.
