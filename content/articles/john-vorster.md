## Summary

Apartheid South African Prime Minister whose government killed Steve Biko and dozens of detainees. As Justice Minister, he created BOSS intelligence agency and detained thousands.

## Additional Context

## Early Political Trajectory

Vorster rose within South Africa’s National Party and held key security-related portfolios before becoming Prime Minister. As Minister of Justice (early 1960s), he was associated with tightening internal security laws and the state’s approach to political opposition during the apartheid era.

## Prime Ministership (1966–1978)

As Prime Minister, Vorster presided over an apartheid government facing growing internal resistance and international condemnation. His administration expanded security and intelligence capabilities, and the period is frequently discussed in relation to detentions, political repression, and abuses within the security apparatus.

The death in custody of anti-apartheid activist Steve Biko (1977) occurred during Vorster’s tenure. The case became an international flashpoint, intensifying criticism of the apartheid state.

## Later Office and Resignation

Vorster resigned as Prime Minister in 1978 amid the Information scandal (often called "Muldergate"). He later served as State President for a short period before leaving frontline politics.
