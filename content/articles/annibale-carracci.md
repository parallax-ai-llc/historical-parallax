## Summary

Annibale Carracci reformed Italian painting by rejecting Mannerist artificiality in favor of nature and classical ideals. His ceiling frescoes in the Farnese Gallery in Rome rival the Sistine Chapel in ambition. He also pioneered the ideal landscape and the genre of caricature.
