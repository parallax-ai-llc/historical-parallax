## Summary

Akasaki Isamu was a Japanese engineer and physicist who invented the efficient blue light-emitting diode (LED), enabling white LED lighting. He shared the 2014 Nobel Prize in Physics with Hiroshi Amano and Shuji Nakamura. His decades-long persistence in gallium nitride research transformed global energy-efficient lighting.
