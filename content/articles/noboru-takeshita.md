Japanese PM forced to resign over the Recruit insider trading scandal.

## Prime Ministership (1987–1989)

- Took office in November 1987 after succeeding Yasuhiro Nakasone as leader of the Liberal Democratic Party (LDP).[^1]
- Served as Prime Minister during the transition from Emperor Shōwa (Hirohito) to Emperor Akihito, and is often noted as the last Prime Minister to have served under Emperor Shōwa.[^1]
- His administration is commonly associated with late-1980s Japanese political economy and the lead-up to the 1989 introduction of a national consumption tax (implemented under the LDP government of that period).[^2]

## Recruit Scandal and resignation

- Takeshita was implicated in the Recruit scandal, a major political corruption controversy involving shares in Recruit Cosmos before public listing and alleged efforts to cultivate political influence.[^1]
- Political fallout from the scandal led to his resignation in 1989, after which he continued to exert influence inside the LDP through faction leadership.[^1]

## Factional politics and influence

- Widely described as a key factional operator inside the LDP, inheriting and leading a large faction linked to Kakuei Tanaka and later the Takeshita faction.[^1]
- Referred to in some accounts as the “last shadow shogun” for behind-the-scenes influence even after leaving the premiership.[^1]
