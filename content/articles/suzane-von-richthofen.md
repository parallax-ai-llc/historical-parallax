## Background

Suzane von Richthofen became one of Brazil’s most widely covered true-crime cases due to the social profile of the family, the alleged premeditation, and the role of multiple participants. Reporting commonly emphasizes the conflict between her relationship choices and her parents’ opposition, alongside financial motives tied to inheritance.

## The Crime (High-Level)

According to widely cited summaries (including Wikipedia), on the night of **31 October 2002** she participated in a plan in which her boyfriend **Daniel Cravinhos** and his brother **Christian** entered the Richthofen residence after the home security system was disabled. The parents were killed in their bedroom, and the scene was later staged to resemble a burglary.

## Investigation and Trial

Early investigation reportedly found the burglary narrative inconsistent with household security details and forensic evidence, leading to rapid suspicion of acquaintances rather than unknown intruders. The case proceeded through pre-trial detention and legal motions, and ultimately to a jury trial in São Paulo.

In **2006**, she was convicted and sentenced to **39 years and 6 months** in prison in connection with the murders (as stated by Wikipedia and common secondary summaries).

## Later Developments

Public attention continued for years due to prison-status updates, media portrayals, and periodic legal decisions. Wikipedia and other reporting describe a later release under **parole** (noted there as occurring in 2023).
