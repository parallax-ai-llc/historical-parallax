## Summary

Jean-Luc Godard was a French-Swiss filmmaker who was the most radical and influential figure of the French New Wave. His debut Breathless revolutionized cinema with its jump cuts, hand-held camera, and narrative experimentation. His prolific career, from Contempt and Pierrot le Fou to his late video essays, constantly challenged cinematic conventions.
