## Summary

Yasunari Kawabata was the first Japanese author to receive the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1968. His novels Snow Country, The Sound of the Mountain, and Thousand Cranes are celebrated for their lyrical prose and evocation of Japanese aesthetic traditions. His writing captures beauty and sadness with extraordinary delicacy.
