## Summary

Hans Küng was a Swiss Catholic theologian and one of the most prolific and controversial theologians of the 20th century. He challenged papal infallibility and other Catholic doctrines, leading to the Vatican revoking his license to teach as a Catholic theologian in 1979. He founded the Global Ethic Foundation promoting interfaith dialogue. His books on Christianity, Islam, and Judaism reached a wide audience.
