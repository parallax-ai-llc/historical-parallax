## Summary

Iranian karate champion executed for allegedly killing a paramilitary member during 2022 protests.

## Background

- Mohammad-Mehdi Karami was known in Iranian sports as a karate athlete.
- International coverage of his case primarily connected it to the Iranian state response to the 2022–2023 protest movement.

## Case (high-level)

- Karami was accused in connection with the death of a member of the Basij, a paramilitary force affiliated with Iran’s Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC).
- Human-rights advocates and many media reports characterized the proceedings as part of a broader wave of protest-related prosecutions.
- Public summaries emphasize allegations of due-process problems; specifics vary by source and reporting.

## Broader Context

- The 2022 protests in Iran (sparked by the death of Mahsa Amini) led to a nationwide crackdown that included mass arrests and multiple death sentences.
- Karami’s execution is frequently cited in lists of protest-linked executions from that period.

## Legacy and Reaction

- Karami is referenced as a symbol of the protest movement’s human cost, particularly among athletes and young Iranians.
- His case is often discussed alongside other protest-linked defendants and executions.
