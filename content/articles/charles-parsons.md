## Additional Context

### Why the steam turbine mattered

- Parsons’ steam turbine offered a smoother, higher-speed alternative to reciprocating steam engines, with advantages in efficiency, vibration, and scalability.
- Turbines became central to large-scale electricity generation, where high rotational speeds can directly drive generators.

### Turbinia demonstration (1897)

- Parsons built *Turbinia*, a turbine-powered vessel used to publicly demonstrate the performance of marine turbines.
- Its high speed and maneuverability helped convince navies and shipbuilders that turbine propulsion was practical for warships and fast commercial vessels.

### Industrial legacy

- Parsons’ companies and engineering work contributed to the wider adoption of turbines in power stations and marine propulsion.
- Steam turbines later became standard components in thermal power plants, including coal, oil, gas, and nuclear facilities, where steam cycles remain fundamental.

### Broader influence

- The turbine is often cited as one of the key technologies enabling 20th-century electrification and modern naval engineering.
