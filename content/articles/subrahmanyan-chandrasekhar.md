## Summary

Subrahmanyan Chandrasekhar was an Indian-American astrophysicist who shared the 1983 Nobel Prize in Physics for his theoretical studies of physical processes important to the structure and evolution of stars. He calculated the Chandrasekhar limit, the maximum mass of a stable white dwarf star. His work laid the foundation for understanding black holes and neutron stars. NASA's Chandra X-ray Observatory is named in his honor.
