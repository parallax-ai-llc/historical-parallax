## Summary

Nikkyo Niwano was a Japanese Buddhist leader who co-founded Rissho Kosei-kai, one of the largest lay Buddhist organizations in Japan with over 6 million members. He was a pioneer of interreligious dialogue, co-founding the World Conference of Religions for Peace (now Religions for Peace). He received the Templeton Prize in 1979 for his contributions to interfaith understanding. His organization emphasizes the Lotus Sutra and small-group counseling.
