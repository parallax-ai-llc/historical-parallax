## Summary

Chinese lawyer and wife of Bo Xilai who murdered British businessman Neil Heywood by poisoning him with cyanide in a Chongqing hotel. Her arrest triggered the biggest Chinese political scandal in decades.

## Background

- Gu Kailai trained and worked as a lawyer in China and became well known in part through her marriage to Bo Xilai, a senior Communist Party figure.
- The case became internationally prominent because it intersected with elite Chinese politics, business ties, and internal party discipline.

## Neil Heywood Case (Overview)

- Neil Heywood, a British businessman with connections to the Bo/Gu family circle, died in Chongqing in 2011; authorities later said he was murdered.
- Chinese prosecutors alleged that Gu Kailai poisoned him, and the case was presented as a targeted killing rather than a broader pattern of violence.

## Political Scandal and Investigations

- The episode escalated into a wider political scandal after Wang Lijun, a police official associated with Bo Xilai, sought refuge at a U.S. consulate.
- The scandal contributed to investigations that ultimately led to Bo Xilai’s removal from power and later prosecution.

## Sentencing and Later Status

- Gu Kailai received a death sentence with a two-year reprieve (commonly referred to as a suspended death sentence), a form that is often commuted to life imprisonment.
- The case is frequently cited as an example of how criminal proceedings and party discipline can overlap during high-profile political crises.
