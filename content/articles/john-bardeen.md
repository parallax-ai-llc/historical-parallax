## Summary

John Bardeen was an American physicist and electrical engineer who is the only person to have won the Nobel Prize in Physics twice. He shared the 1956 prize for the invention of the transistor and the 1972 prize for the BCS theory of superconductivity. His invention of the transistor revolutionized electronics and computing. The BCS theory provided the first microscopic explanation of superconductivity.
