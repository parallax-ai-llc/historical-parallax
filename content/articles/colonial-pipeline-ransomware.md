## Summary

DarkSide ransomware group attacked Colonial Pipeline forcing a six-day shutdown of the largest fuel pipeline in the eastern United States. Gas shortages and panic buying ensued across multiple states.

## What Happened

The incident involved ransomware deployed against Colonial Pipeline’s corporate network. In response, the company shut down pipeline operations as a precaution, which reduced fuel supply to parts of the U.S. East Coast.

## Operational and Economic Effects

- Regional gasoline shortages and queueing in some states.
- Price spikes and panic buying driven by uncertainty and disrupted distribution.
- Emergency declarations and temporary regulatory waivers to ease transport constraints.

## Response and Aftermath

- Colonial Pipeline paid a ransom (reported publicly at the time) and began recovery.
- U.S. authorities traced and recovered a portion of the ransom in subsequent actions.
- The event accelerated public and private focus on cyber risk in critical infrastructure.

## Why It Matters

The Colonial Pipeline attack became a widely cited case study for how disruptions to IT systems can cascade into real-world impacts, even when operational technology is not directly compromised.


## Key Takeaways

- Critical infrastructure operators may halt operations to contain IT incidents.
