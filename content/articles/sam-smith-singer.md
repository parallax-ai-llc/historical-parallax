## Summary

Sam Smith is a British singer and songwriter who rose to international fame in 2014 with their debut album *In the Lonely Hour*, which produced the Grammy Award-winning single "Stay with Me." Known for a powerful, soulful voice spanning multiple octaves, Smith has become one of the best-selling music artists of their generation, with over 45 million records sold worldwide. Their music draws heavily on themes of love, heartbreak, and identity, connecting with audiences across pop, soul, and R&B genres.

Beyond commercial success, Smith has been a prominent figure in LGBTQ+ visibility. In 2019, they came out as non-binary and adopted they/them pronouns, using their platform to advocate for gender identity awareness. Smith's willingness to be publicly open about their personal life and emotional struggles has earned them a devoted global fanbase and a reputation as one of contemporary pop's most authentic voices.

Their career has spanned multiple chart-topping albums, Oscar and Grammy wins, and high-profile collaborations, cementing their place as a defining artist of the 2010s and 2020s.

## Early Life & Background

Sam Smith was born on May 19, 1992, in London, England, and grew up in the village of Great Chishill in Cambridgeshire. Showing an early aptitude for music, Smith began performing in school productions and local venues as a child. They studied musical theatre and were influenced by artists such as Whitney Houston, Chaka Khan, and Mary J. Blige. Smith moved to London as a teenager to pursue a music career, working various jobs while writing and performing original material.

## Rise to Prominence

Smith first gained widespread attention in 2012 when they featured on Disclosure's single "Latch," which reached the UK top ten. Further exposure came with a feature on Naughty Boy's "La La La" in 2013, which reached number one in the UK. Their debut solo album, *In the Lonely Hour*, was released in May 2014 and became a massive commercial success, reaching number one in the UK and number two on the US Billboard 200. The album's lead single "Stay with Me" became a global hit and earned Smith four Grammy Awards in 2015, including Record of the Year and Song of the Year.

## Key Achievements

- **Grammy Awards (2015):** Won four Grammys, including Record of the Year, Song of the Year, Best New Artist, and Best Pop Vocal Album.
- **Academy Award (2016):** Won Best Original Song for "Writing's on the Wall," the James Bond theme for *Spectre*.
- **"Stay with Me"** peaked at number two on the US Billboard Hot 100 and number one in the UK.
- **Second album** *The Thrill of It All* (2017) debuted at number one in both the UK and the US.
- **"Unholy"** (2022), a collaboration with Kim Petras, spent nine weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 and won Best Pop Duo/Group Performance at the 2024 Grammy Awards.
- Kim Petras became the first openly transgender woman to win a Grammy in that category as a result of the collaboration.

## Controversies & Criticism

Smith's 2023 Grammy performance of "Unholy," featuring theatrical devil-themed imagery, drew backlash from religious groups and conservative commentators who deemed it sacrilegious. Smith addressed the criticism by affirming their right to artistic expression. Additionally, early in their career, Smith faced a plagiarism dispute with Tom Petty over "Stay with Me," which shared melodic similarities with Petty's "I Won't Back Down." The matter was settled out of court, with Petty and Jeff Lynne added as co-writers and awarded royalties.

## Legacy & Influence

Sam Smith's impact extends beyond chart statistics. Their openness about being non-binary has contributed to broader mainstream conversations around gender identity and representation in the music industry. Artistically, their emotive vocal style has influenced a wave of introspective pop singers in the 2010s and 2020s. Smith's career trajectory — from featuring on dance records to winning Oscars and Grammys — demonstrates a rare versatility and enduring commercial relevance across more than a decade in music.
