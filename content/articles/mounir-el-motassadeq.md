## Summary

Moroccan convicted in Germany for aiding the 9/11 Hamburg cell that planned the attacks.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2007 | Convicted of aiding 9/11 plotters |

## Background and Education

- Mounir el-Motassadeq moved from Morocco to Germany in 1993 and later to Hamburg in 1995.
- He studied electrical engineering in Hamburg and became part of the social circle later associated with the so-called “Hamburg cell.”

## Connection to the 9/11 Hamburg Cell

- German and U.S. authorities described el-Motassadeq as a friend and associate of key Hamburg-based 9/11 plot participants, including Mohamed Atta, Marwan al-Shehhi, and Ziad Jarrah.
- Reporting on the Hamburg group emphasized shared social and religious networks (e.g., a prayer group) rather than a formal “cell” membership record.

## Travel and Alleged Support Activities

- Public reporting notes travel from Germany to Turkey and onward to Afghanistan in 2000, followed by a return to Germany.
- In court findings discussed in public sources, he was alleged to have provided forms of logistical support that helped plot participants maintain the appearance of being students (e.g., handling payments such as tuition and rent).

## Legal Proceedings (Germany)

High-level timeline (not exhaustive):

- **2001–2002:** Investigations in Germany focused on Hamburg-based associates connected to the 9/11 hijackers.
- **February 2003:** Convicted in Germany of thousands of counts of accessory to murder in connection with 9/11-related deaths; the conviction was later set aside on appeal.
- **August 2005:** Re-tried and convicted on a terrorism-organization membership theory; that conviction was also rejected on appeal.
- **2006:** German courts addressed evidentiary and procedural issues, including disputes tied to access to detainee testimony (e.g., Ramzi bin al-Shibh).
- **January 2007:** Sentenced in Hamburg to **15 years** for **246 counts of accessory to murder**, alongside **membership in a terrorist organization**.

## Sentence, Release, and Deportation

- After serving his sentence, he was deported from Germany to Morocco (reported as occurring in 2018).

## Notes on Evidence and Public Descriptions

- Public accounts of the trials commonly distinguish between (a) knowledge of specific operational details of the 9/11 plot and (b) assistance that, in a court’s view, materially supported participants.
- Because elements of the case were litigated across multiple trials and appeals, summaries often focus on the final 2007 sentence as the outcome most frequently cited in later references.
