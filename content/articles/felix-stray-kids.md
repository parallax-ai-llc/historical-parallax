## Summary

Lee Felix, known professionally as Felix, is an Australian-born South Korean singer, rapper, and dancer who is a member of the K-pop group Stray Kids, formed by JYP Entertainment in 2017. Born on September 15, 2000, in Sydney, Australia, Felix is recognized for his distinctively deep baritone voice, charismatic stage presence, and bilingual fluency in English and Korean.

As a member of Stray Kids, Felix has helped the group become one of the most commercially successful K-pop acts of the fourth generation, contributing to album sales exceeding 20 million copies worldwide. His unique vocal tone — unusually deep for K-pop — has become one of the group's most instantly recognizable sonic signatures, setting him apart in an industry that often favors higher-register vocals.

Felix's warmth, positivity, and cross-cultural appeal have made him one of K-pop's most beloved international members. He has become a prominent figure in fashion and social media, amassing over 25 million Instagram followers by early 2025, and serves as a bridge between Stray Kids' Korean identity and their vast global audience.

## Early Life & Background

Lee Felix was born Lee Yongbok on September 15, 2000, in Sydney, New South Wales, Australia, to South Korean parents. He grew up in the Sydney suburb of Seven Hills, attending Patrician Brothers' College Blacktown. Felix spoke primarily English during his upbringing and developed an interest in dance during his school years, participating in local dance competitions. In 2016, he auditioned for JYP Entertainment during their global auditions held in Australia and was accepted as a trainee, relocating to Seoul, South Korea, at age 16. His limited Korean proficiency at the time presented a significant challenge during his trainee period.

## Rise to Prominence

Felix appeared on the 2017 Mnet reality show *Stray Kids*, which documented the formation of the group. Midway through the program, he was eliminated by JYP founder Park Jin-young due to concerns about his Korean language skills and overall readiness. However, he was brought back by group leader Bang Chan and the other members, who advocated strongly for his inclusion. Felix secured his place in the final lineup, and Stray Kids officially debuted on March 25, 2018, with the EP *I Am NOT*. His deep voice quickly became a signature element of the group's music, featured prominently in tracks like "Thunderous" (2021) and "MANIAC" (2022). By 2022, Stray Kids had risen to the top tier of K-pop, with *MAXIDENT* selling over 3.74 million copies domestically, and their 2023 album *5-STAR* debuting at number one on the Billboard 200.

## Key Achievements

As part of Stray Kids, Felix has contributed to multiple world tours, including the "MANIAC" world tour (2022–2023) and the "DOMINÂTE" world tour (2024–2025), performing at stadiums and arenas across Asia, North America, Europe, and Australia. The group won consecutive Daesang (Grand Prize) awards at the MAMA Awards in 2023 and 2024. Felix gained individual recognition as a fashion icon, becoming a global ambassador for Louis Vuitton in 2023 — one of the highest-profile luxury brand partnerships for a K-pop idol. He walked in Louis Vuitton runway shows in Paris and appeared in major fashion publications. His solo track "Keep Your Head Up" and his contributions to Stray Kids' SKZ-RECORD and SKZ-PLAYER series showcased his individual artistry. Felix also became known for his baking hobby, frequently sharing content that endeared him to fans worldwide.

## Controversies & Criticism

Felix has largely avoided major personal controversies throughout his career. Some early criticism focused on his Korean language proficiency during the group's formation, though he improved significantly over time and is now fluent. As with Stray Kids broadly, the group faced scrutiny over their departure from the traditional K-pop sound with their noise music style, though this approach ultimately became central to their identity and commercial success. Felix has occasionally faced the broader challenges common to foreign-born K-pop idols, including navigating cultural expectations and being distant from family in Australia.

## Legacy & Influence

Felix has become one of the most prominent Australian-born figures in K-pop history, paving the way for greater international representation in the genre. His unusually deep voice challenged conventional expectations of K-pop vocal aesthetics and demonstrated that distinctive, unconventional tones could become major assets. As a Louis Vuitton ambassador and fashion week regular, he has helped expand K-pop's influence in the luxury fashion world. His openness about the challenges of adapting to life in South Korea as a foreign trainee has resonated with fans and aspiring idols from non-Korean backgrounds. Felix's combination of performance talent, visual appeal, and genuine warmth has made him a defining figure of fourth-generation K-pop and one of Stray Kids' most internationally recognized members.
