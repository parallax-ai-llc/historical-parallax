## Summary

Mexican drug lord known as El H who led the Beltran-Leyva Cartel after his brothers death. Managed financial operations.

## Role in the Beltrán-Leyva organization

Public reporting portrays Héctor Beltrán Leyva as a senior figure within the Beltrán-Leyva Organization, sometimes described as handling finances and coordination.
After internal fractures and leadership losses in the late 2000s, he was widely regarded as one of the key remaining leaders.

## Broader context

The cartel’s history is closely tied to shifts in alliances and rivalries among major Mexican trafficking groups.
These shifts often followed arrests, extraditions, or killings of senior figures, producing splinter groups and localized violence.

## Arrest and detention

He was captured by Mexican authorities in 2014.
His later years were spent in custody, and he died in prison in 2018.

## Significance

Beltrán Leyva is frequently referenced in analyses of:

- The role of financial management and logistics alongside “street-level” violence.
- State responses combining military and law-enforcement strategies.
