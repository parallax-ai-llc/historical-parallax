## Summary

Jean-Claude Killy swept all three alpine skiing golds at the 1968 Grenoble Olympics.

## Additional Context

### Background and rise

- Killy emerged from the French alpine ski system in the 1960s, a period when televised winter sports helped create global stars.
- His peak competitive years coincided with the early era of the FIS Alpine Ski World Cup, established in 1967.

### 1968 Grenoble Olympics

- The 1968 Winter Olympics were held in Grenoble, France, making Killy a central figure for the host nation.
- He won gold in downhill, giant slalom, and slalom, a rare sweep in alpine skiing that helped define his legacy.

### World Cup and major titles

- He is associated with the first overall World Cup title (1967), reflecting dominance across multiple disciplines.
- In addition to Olympic success, his reputation was built through World Championship and World Cup performances typical of elite all-around skiers.

### Public profile and later roles

- After retiring from top-level competition, he remained prominent in sport administration and Olympic-related roles.
- He became known as a figure connecting elite competition, event organization, and the broader commercialization of winter sport.

### Legacy

- Killy is often cited as one of the athletes who helped popularize alpine skiing internationally.
- His 1968 performance is frequently referenced in Olympic histories as a benchmark for single-Games dominance in a technical, risk-heavy sport.
