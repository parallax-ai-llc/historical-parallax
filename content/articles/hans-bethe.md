## Summary

Hans Bethe was a German-American nuclear physicist who won the 1967 Nobel Prize in Physics for his work on the theory of stellar nucleosynthesis. He discovered the carbon-nitrogen-oxygen cycle by which stars produce energy. He played a crucial role in the Manhattan Project as head of the theoretical division at Los Alamos. Bethe was also a prominent advocate for arms control and peaceful use of nuclear energy.
