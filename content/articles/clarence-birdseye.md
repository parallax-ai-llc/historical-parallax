## Summary

Clarence Birdseye invented the quick-freezing process that revolutionized the food industry and founded the frozen food industry. Inspired by Inuit fish-freezing techniques in Labrador, he developed flash-freezing technology that preserved food quality. He sold his patents and brand to Postum Company (later General Foods) for $22 million in 1929. His invention made seasonal foods available year-round and transformed how Americans eat.
