## Summary

Vyasa (Krishna Dvaipayana) is a central figure in Hindu tradition, credited with compiling the Vedas, authoring the Mahabharata (the longest epic poem in the world), and composing the Puranas. He is also the traditional author of the Brahma Sutras, the foundational text of Vedanta philosophy. He is considered an immortal sage (chiranjeevi) in Hindu tradition and one of the seven chiranjeevis. He is venerated as a divine avatar and the symbol of Hindu scholarship.
