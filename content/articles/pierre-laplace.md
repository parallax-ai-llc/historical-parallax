## Expanded Overview

Pierre-Simon Laplace was a French mathematician and astronomer central to the development of **celestial mechanics** and mathematical physics in the late 18th and early 19th centuries.

## Major Contributions (High-Level)

- **Celestial mechanics:** refined the Newtonian description of planetary motion and stability problems.
- **Potential theory:** associated with what is now called *Laplace's equation* and the Laplacian operator, foundational in physics and engineering.
- **Probability and statistics:** helped formalize analytical approaches to probability; widely associated with early Bayesian ideas (including the “principle of insufficient reason”).
- **Cosmology:** proposed versions of what later became known as the nebular hypothesis for the formation of the Solar System.

## Key Works and Influence

- Laplace's multi-volume *Mécanique céleste* synthesized prior work and recast celestial mechanics in a powerful analytical framework.
- His writing influenced not only astronomy but also a broader ideal of scientific explanation: complex phenomena described through mathematics and deterministic laws.

## Public Life

- Laplace lived through the French Revolution, the Napoleonic era, and the Bourbon Restoration.
- He held academic and administrative roles in French scientific institutions, reflecting the close coupling of science and state in that period.
