## Summary

Thomas Sydenham, known as the English Hippocrates, pioneered clinical observation and the classification of diseases based on symptoms. He distinguished between acute and chronic diseases and described numerous conditions including Sydenham's chorea. His emphasis on bedside observation over theoretical speculation influenced the development of modern clinical medicine.
