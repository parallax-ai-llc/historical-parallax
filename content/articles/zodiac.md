## Summary

Duplicate consolidated.

The "Zodiac" is the name used by an unidentified person who claimed responsibility for a series of attacks in Northern California in the late 1960s. The case became widely known through communications sent to newspapers, including ciphers and letters, and it remains officially unsolved.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1968 | Attacks in the San Francisco Bay Area begin (as later attributed/claimed in case files) |
| 1969 | Multiple attacks and the start of widely publicized letters to newspapers under the "Zodiac" name |
| 1969 | A cipher later known as the "408" is published in newspapers and solved shortly after publication |
| 1969–1974 | Additional letters and claims attributed to the Zodiac are reported by investigators and media |
| 2020 | A 340-character cipher ("Z340") is publicly reported as solved by independent codebreakers |

## Communications and Ciphers

- The case is closely associated with mailed letters in which the author adopted the moniker "Zodiac" and sought publication.
- Several messages included cryptograms (ciphers). Some were solved quickly after publication, while others remained unsolved for decades.
- The authenticity of individual letters has been debated over time; law enforcement has treated some as more credible than others based on evidence and patterns.

## Investigation (High Level)

- Investigations involved multiple agencies due to incidents occurring across different jurisdictions in Northern California.
- The public nature of the communications created intense media attention and prompted large numbers of tips.
- Numerous suspects have been proposed in public discussion and in investigative work; none has been definitively identified as the Zodiac.

## Status and Legacy

- The Zodiac case is frequently cited in discussions of unsolved crimes in the United States.
- It has influenced true-crime media and popular culture, including books, documentaries, and films.
- Beyond the criminal investigation, the case is also noted for its intersection with cryptography, public puzzles, and the way mass media amplified the offender's messaging.
