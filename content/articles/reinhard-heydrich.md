## Summary

High-ranking German Nazi official who chaired the Wannsee Conference that planned the Final Solution. Known as 'The Butcher of Prague' as Protector of Bohemia and Moravia. Assassinated by Czech resistance in Prague.

## Roles in the Nazi Security Apparatus

Reinhard Heydrich was a senior Nazi leader and one of the principal architects of the regime’s security and persecution systems.

- He led the **Sicherheitsdienst (SD)**, the SS intelligence service.
- He became head of the **Reich Main Security Office (RSHA)**, which coordinated agencies including the Gestapo and criminal police under SS control.
- He played a major role in organizing and directing policies of repression and mass violence in territories under German control.

## Holocaust Administration and Wannsee

- Heydrich chaired the **Wannsee Conference** (January 1942), a meeting that coordinated aspects of the bureaucratic implementation of the mass murder of Europe’s Jews.
- The conference did not “start” the Holocaust, but it is widely cited as a key moment in formalizing inter-agency coordination for deportation and killing policies.

## Protectorate of Bohemia and Moravia

As acting **Reich Protector** in the Czech lands, Heydrich intensified repression.

- He pursued crackdowns on resistance networks.
- His administration combined terror with efforts to increase industrial output for the German war economy.

## Assassination (Operation Anthropoid)

- In 1942, Heydrich was attacked in Prague by Czechoslovak resistance operatives trained and supported from abroad.
- He died of complications from the attack.
- The German response included severe reprisals, which are frequently discussed in accounts of the occupation.

## Historical Significance

Heydrich’s career is often highlighted by historians as an example of how administrative power and security institutions enabled large-scale state violence.
