## Summary

Uzbek-born Russian organized crime figure known as 'Taiwanchik.' He was indicted for fixing the 2002 Winter Olympics figure skating competition and runs illegal gambling operations. On FBI wanted list.

## Additional Context

- Tokhtakhounov is widely reported as a figure connected to post-Soviet organized crime networks and illicit gambling businesses.
- He is known by the nickname “Taiwanchik,” and appears in media reporting about Russian-language criminal groups operating across borders.

## 2002 Olympic Figure Skating Allegations

- U.S. prosecutors alleged that he helped orchestrate bribery or influence related to judging at the 2002 Winter Olympics figure skating events.
- The controversy became a high-profile example of how reputational and competitive damage can arise when elite sports governance is perceived as vulnerable to corruption.

## Later Indictments and Evasion

- Subsequent U.S. indictments described an illegal sports-betting and gambling enterprise with ties to luxury real estate and international clientele.
- Tokhtakhounov remained outside U.S. custody for years, illustrating the practical limits of law enforcement reach when suspects reside in jurisdictions that do not extradite.

## Why He Matters

His story is often used as a case study in transnational organized crime: how money, entertainment, sports, and political connections can intersect with illicit markets.
