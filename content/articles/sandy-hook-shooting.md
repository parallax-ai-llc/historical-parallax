## Summary

Mass shooting at Sandy Hook Elementary on December 14, 2012 killing 26 including 20 children aged 6-7.

## Key Facts

- **Date:** December 14, 2012
- **Location:** Sandy Hook Elementary School, Newtown, Connecticut, United States
- **Fatalities:** 28 total deaths including the perpetrator (26 victims at the school)
- **Victims at the school:** 20 children and 6 adult staff members
- **Injuries:** Multiple injuries were reported; the incident is widely described as one of the deadliest U.S. school shootings.

## Background

Sandy Hook Elementary School is an elementary school in Newtown, a town in Fairfield County, Connecticut. The shooting occurred in the context of long-running U.S. public debates over firearm access, school safety, and mental health services.

## What Happened

On the morning of December 14, 2012, an armed attacker entered the school and shot students and staff. Prior to arriving at the school, the attacker killed their mother at a residence in Newtown. Law enforcement responded rapidly after emergency calls; the attacker died at the scene.

## Immediate Response

- Local and state police secured the campus and evacuated surviving students.
- Families were directed to reunification sites as authorities verified identities and locations.
- The incident prompted extensive national media coverage and a significant outpouring of public grief.

## Aftermath and Impact

The Sandy Hook shooting had sustained political and cultural impact in the United States. It intensified discussion of:

- **Gun policy:** background checks, assault-weapon restrictions, and magazine-capacity limits
- **School security:** access controls, emergency procedures, and threat assessment programs
- **Mental health:** availability of treatment, crisis intervention, and early-warning systems

The event is also closely associated with community-led memorial and support efforts for victims’ families and survivors.

## Related Developments

In the years following the shooting, the tragedy became a focal point for activism around gun violence prevention and school safety. It also generated misinformation and conspiracy theories, which in turn led to prominent civil litigation concerning defamation and harassment.
