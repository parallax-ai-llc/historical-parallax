## Summary

Russias most powerful organized crime group based in Moscow. With over 9,000 members, it controls drug trafficking, money laundering, and extortion operations worldwide.

## Background

- The name refers to the Solntsevo District of Moscow, where the group is commonly described as having emerged.
- In many accounts, the organization is treated as part of the broader post-Soviet wave of criminal networks that expanded during the late 1980s and 1990s.

## Organization and Reach

- The group is often characterized as a loose coalition of crews and leaders rather than a single centralized hierarchy.
- Reported influence has included business fronts, financial schemes, and cross-border partnerships with other criminal groups.
- Media and law-enforcement reporting frequently describes activities and associates across Europe and other regions.

## Commonly Reported Criminal Activities

- Narcotics trafficking and logistics support for drug distribution.
- Extortion, intimidation, and “protection” rackets directed at businesses.
- Money laundering through shell companies, cash-intensive businesses, and international transfers.

## Law Enforcement Attention

- The group has been the subject of investigations, prosecutions, and sanctions discussions in multiple jurisdictions.
- Cases associated with Russian organized crime frequently involve financial crime, immigration fraud, or racketeering charges in addition to violent offenses.

## Historical Significance

- The Solntsevskaya Bratva is routinely cited in overviews of modern Russian organized crime as a prominent example of scale and internationalization.
- Its notoriety has influenced popular portrayals of post-Soviet criminal networks in journalism and media.
