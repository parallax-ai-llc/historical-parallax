## Background

Tara Fares was an Iraqi model and social media personality known for a public-facing lifestyle brand and a large online following.

In profiles of her public life, she is commonly described as part of a generation of influencers and entertainers who became prominent through Instagram and other platforms rather than through traditional media.

## Public Profile

Her public persona drew attention both for fashion and beauty content and for being outspoken and visible as a young woman in a socially conservative environment.

Media coverage after her death often situated her in a broader discussion about gender norms, celebrity culture, and the risks faced by prominent women in Iraq.

## Death (High-Level)

- **Date:** 27 September 2018
- **Place:** Baghdad, Iraq
- **Method:** Shot by gunmen (commonly reported as a drive-by style attack)

Public reporting and reference summaries describe her being attacked while in a vehicle in Baghdad, after which she died of her injuries.

## Investigation and Attribution

Open-source summaries note that the killing was widely condemned and that Iraqi authorities announced investigative steps.

## Wider Context

Fares’ death was frequently grouped with a wave of violence and intimidation targeting high-profile women in Iraq, including activists and public figures.

Commentary around the case has referenced a mix of potential motives—ranging from criminal violence to politically or socially motivated intimidation—without a single universally accepted narrative in brief accounts.

## Significance

The case is often used to illustrate how online visibility can translate into real-world vulnerability, especially in environments where public prominence intersects with polarized politics and contested social norms.

For historical documentation, the event is typically recorded as part of a broader pattern of attacks on civil society and on women with public platforms in Iraq in the late 2010s.
