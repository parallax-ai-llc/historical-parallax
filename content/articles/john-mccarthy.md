## Summary

John McCarthy was an American computer scientist who coined the term 'artificial intelligence' and was a pioneer of AI research. He invented the Lisp programming language, one of the earliest high-level languages still in use. He also developed time-sharing computing and made fundamental contributions to the theory of computation. McCarthy received the Turing Award in 1971.
