## Summary

Takehito Koyasu (子安武人) is one of Japan's most prolific and recognizable voice actors, born on May 5, 1967, in Yokohama, Kanagawa Prefecture. With a career spanning over three decades, he has lent his distinctive deep, often menacing voice to hundreds of anime, video game, and audio drama productions. His range — from charismatic villains to eccentric antiheroes — has made him a cornerstone figure in the seiyuu (voice acting) industry.

Koyasu is particularly celebrated for his ability to inhabit complex, morally ambiguous characters, earning him a devoted fan following in Japan and internationally. He founded his own talent agency, Intention, giving him an unusual degree of creative and professional independence in an industry where such autonomy is rare.

---

## Early Life & Background

Born in Yokohama on May 5, 1967, Koyasu developed an interest in performance during his youth. He pursued voice acting professionally after completing his education, training in the discipline at a time when the anime industry was beginning its major expansion in the late 1980s. His early years were spent building foundational skills that would later define his trademark vocal style — a smooth, resonant baritone capable of conveying both refinement and menace.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Koyasu broke through to wider recognition in the early 1990s. A pivotal role came in 1995 with *Neon Genesis Evangelion*, where he voiced Ryoji Kaji, a suave and enigmatic operative whose scenes became fan favorites. That same period saw him take on Hotohori in *Fushigi Yuugi* (1995), demonstrating his ability to carry romantic leads alongside darker characters.

His profile rose further with the role of Zechs Merquise / Milliardo Peacecraft in *Mobile Suit Gundam Wing* (1995), a morally complex antagonist whose popularity cemented Koyasu's reputation as the go-to voice for sophisticated, brooding characters. Throughout the late 1990s and 2000s, he accumulated roles at a remarkable pace across virtually every major anime genre.

---

## Key Achievements

Koyasu's most iconic role is widely considered to be Dio Brando in *JoJo's Bizarre Adventure*, a part he first voiced in the 1993 OVA and reprised in the 2012 TV anime series. His portrayal of Dio — imperious, theatrical, and terrifying — became definitive, with his delivery of lines like "WRYYYY" entering anime cultural lexicon.

Other landmark roles include Excalibur in *Soul Eater* (2008), Shiro Ogami in *Black Blood Brothers* (2006), Bobobo in *Bobobo-bo Bo-bobo* (2003), and Tsukishima in *Bleach*. In video games, he voiced Hakumen in the *BlazBlue* series. He founded his agency Intention in 1998, through which he has managed his career independently. He has accumulated well over 300 credited roles across anime and games.

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Koyasu's career has been largely free of major public controversies. Some industry observers have noted that his strong association with villainous roles occasionally limits casting diversity, though this has not appeared to hinder his prolific output. His decision to operate independently through Intention was unconventional but has not generated significant criticism.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Koyasu's influence on the seiyuu profession is substantial. He helped define a template for the "charismatic antagonist" archetype in anime voice acting, inspiring a generation of voice actors who followed. His decades-long consistency and the iconic status of roles like Dio Brando ensure his place in anime history. International audiences who encountered his work through *JoJo's Bizarre Adventure* have helped extend his recognition well beyond Japan.
