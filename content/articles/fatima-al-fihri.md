## Summary

Fatima al-Fihri was a Tunisian-born woman who founded the University of al-Qarawiyyin in Fez, Morocco in 859 AD, recognized by UNESCO and the Guinness World Records as the oldest continuously operating educational institution in the world. She used her inheritance to build the mosque-university, which became a center of learning in mathematics, sciences, and Islamic studies.
