## Summary

US military sprayed 20 million gallons of Agent Orange over Vietnam, contaminating the land and causing cancer, birth defects, and disease in millions of Vietnamese and US veterans.

## Composition and Use

Agent Orange was the code name for a herbicide mixture used by the U.S. military during the Vietnam War as part of a broader defoliation and crop-destruction campaign. The mixture is most commonly associated with contamination by **dioxin (TCDD)**, a highly toxic byproduct linked in scientific and policy discussions to long-term health and environmental harms.

## Health Impacts (High-Level)

Public discussion of Agent Orange centers on long-term effects on multiple groups, including Vietnamese civilians, Vietnamese military personnel, and U.S. and allied veterans.

Commonly cited impact categories include:

- **Cancers and chronic disease risks** described in epidemiological and veteran-health literature.
- **Reproductive and developmental harms**, including birth defects and intergenerational health concerns.
- **Community-wide exposure pathways**, such as soil, sediment, and food-chain contamination in some affected areas.

## Environmental and Remediation Issues

Beyond immediate defoliation, concern has focused on persistence of dioxin in certain "hotspot" locations and the difficulty of long-term cleanup. Remediation efforts have included targeted soil and sediment treatment at heavily contaminated sites, along with public-health and disability-support initiatives, though the scope and adequacy of these programs remain subjects of debate.

## Legal, Political, and Ethical Aftermath

Agent Orange has been central to:

- **Veterans' benefit and recognition systems**, where exposure status and eligible conditions have been contested and periodically updated.
- **Litigation and accountability debates** involving governments, military policy, and chemical manufacturers.
- **War ethics and international-law arguments**, particularly around the distinction between tactical military objectives and foreseeable civilian harm.

## Key Terms

- **Defoliant:** chemical used to remove leaves and vegetation cover.
- **Dioxin (TCDD):** toxic compound associated with some Agent Orange contamination.
- **Exposure assessment:** the process of estimating who was exposed, when, and by what pathways.
