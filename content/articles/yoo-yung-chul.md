South Korean serial killer who murdered 20 wealthy elderly people and prostitutes in Seoul.

## Background

Yoo Yung-chul is a South Korean serial killer whose crimes drew extensive national attention in the early 2000s. Public reporting has described a history of childhood hardship and prior criminal behavior before the murders for which he became known.

## Crimes and Modus Operandi

His victims were reported to include:

- Wealthy, older individuals targeted in their homes
- Sex workers who were lured under false pretenses

Accounts generally describe the crimes as involving home invasions or meetings arranged through intermediaries, followed by killings carried out with blunt objects. The pattern of victim selection led to significant fear and heightened caution among at-risk groups in Seoul at the time.

## Investigation and Arrest

Police investigations linked multiple incidents through similarities in method and circumstances. Yoo was arrested during the investigation period and later provided statements that connected him to a larger number of killings than had initially been confirmed.

## Trial and Sentence

In 2005, a South Korean court sentenced Yoo to death. South Korea has maintained a moratorium on executions for many years, so the sentence has been widely discussed in the context of capital punishment policy and high-profile violent crime.

## Public Impact

The case was frequently cited in South Korean media coverage about:

- Safety concerns for seniors living alone
- Risks associated with informal or underground sex-work markets
- The role of profiling and linkage analysis in major crime investigations
