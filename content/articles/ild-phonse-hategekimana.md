## Summary

Rwandan military officer who commanded the Ngoma military camp during the genocide and organized massacres in Butare Prefecture. He ordered soldiers and militia to systematically kill Tutsi civilians.

## Role During the 1994 Genocide (High Level)

Hategekimana is described in reporting as a senior officer associated with the Ngoma camp in the former Butare prefecture during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi. Allegations in international proceedings have focused on command responsibility and on orders or encouragement given to soldiers and associated militia to participate in attacks on civilians.

## Arrest and Transfer

He was arrested years later in the Democratic Republic of the Congo. Cases of this kind often involve cooperation between regional authorities and international justice bodies to locate suspects, secure detention, and facilitate transfer for trial.

## Legal Proceedings

The case has been handled within the system that followed the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR), commonly referred to as the UN residual mechanism. Proceedings have addressed charges connected to genocide-era killings in southern Rwanda, with the court examining evidence about his position, actions, and influence over forces operating from Ngoma camp.

## Significance

His prosecution is part of the broader effort to pursue accountability for the 1994 genocide, including cases brought decades after the crimes. The matter is also often referenced as an example of long-running international criminal investigations and the logistical difficulty of trying suspects who moved across borders after conflict.
