## Summary

Nickname for Yoon Hyung-ho, a South Korean serial killer from Buyeo who murdered 5 women between 1990 and 1995 in the ancient Baekje capital region. He buried victims in rural farmland.

## Background

The nickname “Baekje Sorcerer Killer” is a media label used in South Korea for crimes attributed to **Yoon Hyung-ho**. Such labels are often linked to geography (the former Baekje capital region) and to elements of public rumor or sensational framing that circulate during high-profile investigations.

## Case Overview (High Level)

Public summaries of the case typically highlight:

- a pattern of attacks against women over multiple years
- concealment of victims’ remains in rural areas
- the eventual identification and arrest of the perpetrator and subsequent sentencing

## Investigation and Public Reaction

Serial crime cases in rural settings often pose challenges for policing due to dispersed locations and limited immediate evidence. Media coverage and public fear can also influence investigative pressure, resource allocation, and later discussions of criminal-justice procedures.

## Aftermath

The case is commonly referenced in broader conversations in South Korea about:

- offender profiling and investigative methods
- how sensational nicknames shape public perception of crimes
- victim protection and community safety in non-urban areas
