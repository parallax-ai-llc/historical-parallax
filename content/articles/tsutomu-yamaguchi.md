## Background

Tsutomu Yamaguchi was a Japanese engineer associated with Mitsubishi Heavy Industries. He is best known for being present in both Hiroshima and Nagasaki during the atomic bombings in August 1945 and surviving each attack.

Accounts generally describe him as being in Hiroshima on a business trip when the first bomb was dropped on 6 August 1945. After sustaining injuries, he returned to his home area near Nagasaki, where he experienced the second bombing on 9 August 1945.

## Recognition and Public Attention

The term *nijuu hibakusha* is used in Japan for people exposed to both bombings. Yamaguchi became one of the most widely cited examples due to later media coverage and official recognition.

In 2009, Japanese authorities recognized him as a double atomic-bomb survivor, a designation that contributed to renewed public discussion about the long-term health and social impacts faced by survivors.

## Later Life and Advocacy (high level)

In later years, Yamaguchi participated in interviews and public testimony about his experiences. He is frequently referenced in discussions of nuclear disarmament and the ethical and humanitarian consequences of nuclear weapons.

## Historical Context

The Hiroshima and Nagasaki bombings occurred in the final days of World War II and were followed by Japan's surrender later in August 1945. Survivor narratives have played an important role in how the events are remembered, documented, and debated.
