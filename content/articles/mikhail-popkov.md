## Summary
Mikhail Popkov, 'The Werewolf,' is a former Russian police officer convicted of 78 murders committed between 1992 and 2010 in the Irkutsk region of Siberia. He used his police uniform and vehicle to gain victims' trust, making him one of the most prolific serial killers in Russian history.

## Early Life
Born March 7, 1964, in Angarsk, Irkutsk Oblast, Russia. He served as a police officer in Angarsk, giving him access to police vehicles, uniforms, and investigative knowledge that helped him avoid detection. He was married and his wife was unaware of his crimes. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1992 and 2010, Popkov murdered 78 women in the Irkutsk region. He would offer women rides home in his police car, drive them to remote locations, rape and murder them using hammers, axes, screwdrivers, and knives. He used his police expertise to avoid leaving evidence. Some murders occurred while he was on duty. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Popkov was identified through DNA analysis in 2012. He was first convicted of 22 murders in 2015 and sentenced to life. In December 2018, he was convicted of an additional 56 murders, bringing his total to 78. He told investigators there may be more victims. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case exposed the danger of serial killers operating within law enforcement [^4]
- Led to reforms in Russian police oversight and accountability
- Demonstrated the importance of DNA databases in solving serial crimes
