## Summary

Theophan the Recluse was a Russian Orthodox bishop, theologian, and prolific writer who spent the last 22 years of his life in solitary confinement at the Vysha Hermitage. He translated the Philokalia into Russian, making the great texts of Eastern Christian mysticism accessible to Russian readers. His letters of spiritual direction and his commentaries on Paul's epistles are widely read in the Orthodox world.
