## Summary

Mao Yisheng was a pioneering Chinese structural engineer who designed and built the Qiantang River Bridge in 1937, the first modern bridge designed and built by Chinese engineers. He was forced to destroy his own bridge to slow the Japanese advance during WWII, then rebuilt it after the war. He is considered the father of Chinese bridge engineering.
