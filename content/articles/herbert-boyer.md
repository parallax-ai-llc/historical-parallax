## Summary

Herbert Boyer is an American biochemist who co-founded Genentech, the first genetic engineering company, and co-invented recombinant DNA technology. With Stanley Cohen, he developed methods to cut and splice DNA, enabling the production of human proteins in bacteria. This led to the production of synthetic human insulin, the first genetically engineered drug.
