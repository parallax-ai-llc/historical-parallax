## Summary
Adolfo de Jesus Constanzo was a Cuban-born cult leader and criminal associated with a group sometimes referred to in reporting as the “Matamoros cult.” In the late 1980s he drew followers in northern Mexico, blending Palo Mayombe–influenced rituals with organized crime activity. He was linked to multiple murders, including the 1989 killing of U.S. college student Mark Kilroy, before dying during a police raid.

## Early Life
Adolfo de Jesus Constanzo was born on 1962-11-01 in Cuba / Mexico. Details of early life shaped the path that would lead to a criminal career. [^1]

## Criminal Career
As a serial killer, Adolfo de Jesus Constanzo became known for criminal activities that drew significant attention from law enforcement and the public. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
A major manhunt followed the disappearance of Mark Kilroy in March 1989. Authorities investigating the case discovered a ranch outside Matamoros connected to Constanzo's group, where evidence of ritual activity and human remains were reported. Constanzo fled but was later located in an apartment in Mexico City; during the ensuing police raid on 1989-05-06, he was shot and killed.

## Methods and Influence
Constanzo cultivated an image as a spiritual practitioner who could provide protection and “luck” to followers involved in smuggling and other crimes. The case became widely publicized as an example of how coercive group dynamics, belief, and criminal enterprise can reinforce each other.

## Aftermath
Following the raid, several associates were arrested and prosecuted, and the case received extensive media coverage in both Mexico and the United States. Public attention focused on the intersection of trafficking networks, corruption risks, and sensationalized portrayals of ritual violence.
