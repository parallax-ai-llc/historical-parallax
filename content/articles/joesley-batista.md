## Summary

Head of JBS, the world's largest meatpacking company, who secretly recorded Brazil's president discussing bribes. The recording caused a political crisis and stock market crash.

## JBS and the Batista Brothers

Joesley Batista is associated with the rapid growth of JBS from a Brazilian company into one of the world's largest meat and food-processing groups. The company's international expansion, acquisitions, and financing decisions made it a major corporate and political actor in Brazil.

## Political Scandal and the 2017 Crisis

In 2017, reports about a secretly recorded conversation involving President Michel Temer triggered a major political shock in Brazil. The episode intensified debate about corruption, leniency agreements, and the credibility of evidence gathered through informants and recordings.

## Legal Proceedings and Allegations

Batista became linked to investigations into corruption and market misconduct, including accusations of insider trading connected to market movements around the release of the recording. The case is often cited as an example of how legal revelations can rapidly affect currency, equities, and investor confidence.

## Broader Context: Anti-Corruption Investigations

The scandal unfolded during a period of sweeping Brazilian investigations into political and corporate corruption (often associated with Operation Car Wash and related cases). These investigations reshaped party politics and created ongoing disputes over prosecutorial power, plea bargains, and judicial oversight.

## Economic and Governance Significance

Because JBS is a large employer and exporter, controversy around its leadership has been discussed in relation to corporate governance, regulation of strategic industries, and the vulnerability of markets to sudden political news.
