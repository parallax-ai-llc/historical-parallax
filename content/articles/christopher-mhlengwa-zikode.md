## Summary

South African serial killer known as the Donnybrook Serial Killer who murdered 18 people in KwaZulu-Natal between 1993 and 1997. He attacked victims with an axe and was one of South Africa's most prolific killers.

## Additional Context

### Modus operandi (reported)

Some descriptions summarize Zikode as an attacker who used an axe. Other accounts (including Wikipedia) describe a pattern of forcing entry into rural homes, shooting male family members, and abducting and repeatedly raping women in nearby fields or plantations. These differences likely reflect secondary retellings versus detailed case reporting.

### Arrest, trial, and sentencing

- He was arrested on **29 September 1995**.
- In court he was convicted on multiple counts including **murder**, **rape**, **attempted murder**, and **indecent assault**.
- Sentencing is often summarized as multiple life terms; one commonly cited breakdown is a total sentence equivalent to **140 years** in prison.

### Media and legacy

The case has been discussed in South African true-crime and profiling contexts, including coverage connected to criminal profiler **Micki Pistorius** and adaptations such as *Catch Me a Killer*.
