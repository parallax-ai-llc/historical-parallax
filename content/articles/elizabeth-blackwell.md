## Summary

Elizabeth Blackwell was a British-born physician who became the first woman to receive a medical degree in the United States in 1849. She founded the New York Infirmary for Indigent Women and Children and established a medical school for women. Her pioneering career opened the medical profession to women in both America and Britain. She also played an important role in the US Sanitary Commission during the Civil War.
