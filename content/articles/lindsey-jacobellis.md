## Sport and Event Context

- **Snowboard cross** is a head-to-head race format in which riders navigate jumps, banked turns, and variable terrain on the same course.
- Results can hinge on tactics, starts, and avoidance of contact or mistakes in tight racing.

## Olympic Arc

- The 2006 Turin incident became one of the most famous Olympic near-misses, frequently replayed as an example of risk and decision-making under pressure.
- Jacobellis remained a top competitor for many years, returning to multiple Olympic cycles before finally winning gold in 2022.

## Career Profile (High Level)

- Known for longevity at the elite level and consistency across World Cup seasons.
- Often cited as one of the defining athletes of women’s snowboard cross in the sport’s modern era.

## Legacy

- Her 2022 victory is commonly framed as a late-career culmination and a narrative of persistence after a highly publicized early disappointment.

## Key Takeaways

- Jacobellis’ career is often told as a long arc from a famous 2006 error to a 2022 breakthrough.
- Snowboard cross rewards both speed and risk management, which shaped her Olympic story.
- Her gold medal became a widely shared example of persistence across multiple Olympic cycles.
