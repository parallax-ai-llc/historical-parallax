## Overview

The Shining Path (Spanish: *Sendero Luminoso*) is a Peruvian Maoist insurgent organization that launched an armed rebellion in 1980 and became one of the principal perpetrators of political violence during Peru's internal conflict.

It was founded and led for many years by philosopher and party leader Abimael Guzmán (also known as "Chairman Gonzalo").

## Ideology and Organization

The movement described itself as MarxistLeninistMaoist and pursued the overthrow of the Peruvian state through a "people's war" strategy influenced by Maoist revolutionary doctrine.

It built clandestine political structures alongside armed units, using party discipline and ideological indoctrination to maintain internal control.

## Insurgency and Violence

Shining Path carried out bombings, assassinations, and attacks on infrastructure, as well as intimidation of civilians and coercive control in areas where it operated.

Peruvian truth and reconciliation efforts have attributed a large share of conflict-era deaths to Shining Path violence, alongside significant abuses committed by state forces and allied groups.

## Decline

The group's influence declined sharply after the 1992 capture of Abimael Guzmán by Peruvian security forces, which disrupted its command structure and undermined its political momentum.

Although remnants persisted, the organization fractured, and later activity was often associated with small armed bands operating in remote regions.

## Current Status (High-Level)

Peru and multiple international actors have designated Shining Path as a terrorist organization.

In the 2000s and 2010s, reports frequently described remaining factions as operating primarily in limited geographic areas, sometimes linked to drug-trafficking routes, rather than as a nationwide insurgency.
