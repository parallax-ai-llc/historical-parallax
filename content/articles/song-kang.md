## Summary

Song Kang (born April 23, 1994) is a South Korean actor who rose to prominence through a string of leading roles in popular Korean dramas during the early 2020s. Known for his striking visuals and emotionally nuanced performances, he has established himself as one of the most bankable young actors in the Korean entertainment industry. His work spans romance, fantasy, and thriller genres, earning him a dedicated international fanbase through streaming platforms.

His appeal extends well beyond South Korea, with dramas such as *Love Alarm* and *Sweet Home* reaching global audiences on Netflix. Song Kang represents a generation of Korean actors who have benefited from the explosive worldwide growth of Korean content, leveraging streaming distribution to build fanbases across Asia, Europe, and the Americas.

---

## Early Life & Background

Song Kang was born on April 23, 1994, in Daejeon, South Korea. He grew up in a relatively ordinary household and developed an interest in performance during his school years. He studied at Dong-ah Institute of Media and Arts, where he majored in acting — a program that has produced several notable figures in Korean entertainment. His formal training gave him a strong technical foundation that would serve his later television work.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Song Kang made his acting debut in 2017 with a minor role in the web drama *The Liar and His Lover*. His early career involved small television and web series appearances that gradually built his profile. The turning point came in 2019 when he was cast in the Netflix original series *Love Alarm*, playing Hwang Sun-oh, a popular and emotionally complex high schooler. The show's global release introduced him to international audiences almost immediately.

He followed that with the 2020 Netflix horror-thriller *Sweet Home*, in which he played Cha Hyun-su, a reclusive teenager navigating a monster apocalypse. The series was a massive hit, ranking among Netflix's most-watched non-English titles in late 2020. In 2021, he starred in *Navillera*, a critically praised drama about an elderly man pursuing ballet, and *Nevertheless*, a romance drama that generated significant buzz. His 2022 drama *Forecasting Love and Weather* further cemented his status as a leading man.

## Key Achievements

- Lead role in *Sweet Home* (2020), one of Netflix's top-performing Korean originals, which spawned seasons 2 and 3.
- Multiple appearances on Forbes Korea's Power Celebrity list.
- Named global brand ambassador for several international fashion and cosmetics brands.
- Won the Excellence Award (Actor) at the 2021 KBS Drama Awards for *Navillera*.
- *Love Alarm* (2019–2021) ranked consistently in Netflix's top charts across Southeast Asia.

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Song Kang has maintained a largely controversy-free public image. Some critics have pointed to a degree of typecasting in brooding romantic lead roles, questioning the range demonstrated across his early filmography. Additionally, the later seasons of *Sweet Home* received a more mixed critical reception than the first, though this was attributed primarily to writing and direction rather than his performance.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Song Kang is emblematic of the Hallyu Wave's second generation — actors who built global careers primarily through streaming rather than traditional broadcast television. His success helped demonstrate that Korean dramas could sustain genre diversity (horror, romance, melodrama) at the international level. As Korean content continues to grow globally, he remains a significant figure in shaping what a mainstream Korean leading man looks like to worldwide audiences.
