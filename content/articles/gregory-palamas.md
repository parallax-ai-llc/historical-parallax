## Summary

Gregory Palamas was a Byzantine monk and Archbishop of Thessalonica who is one of the most important theologians in Eastern Orthodox history. He defended the Hesychast monks' practice of contemplative prayer and developed the distinction between God's essence and energies. His theology was endorsed by several councils and became normative for Orthodox theology. He is celebrated in the Orthodox Church on the second Sunday of Lent.
