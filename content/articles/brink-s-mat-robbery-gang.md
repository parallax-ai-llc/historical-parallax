## Summary

Criminal gang that stole £26 million in gold bullion, diamonds, and cash from the Brink's-Mat warehouse at Heathrow Airport in 1983. The robbery's aftermath led to multiple murders as criminals fought over the proceeds.

## The Brink's-Mat Robbery (High Level)

The Brink's-Mat robbery is remembered as one of the most significant crimes in modern British history because of the value of the haul and the long shadow of its proceeds.
The theft involved gold bullion and other valuables stored in a secure warehouse facility associated with Heathrow Airport.
The immediate crime was followed by a complex effort to distribute and conceal the value of the stolen assets.

## Money Laundering and Disposal of Stolen Gold

Unlike cash, large quantities of bullion are difficult to monetize without leaving traces.
A common method in such cases is to melt and re-cast gold, mixing it with legitimate supply so that it becomes harder to trace.
The laundering process can involve intermediaries in the precious-metals trade and a chain of transactions that obscures the original source.

## Violence and Criminal Competition

High-value robberies can generate internal conflict as participants dispute shares and attempt to control downstream laundering.
Reporting on the Brink's-Mat case frequently emphasizes that violence followed the initial robbery, including killings linked to disputes over the proceeds.
This dynamic is often cited as an illustration of how “dirty money” can destabilize criminal networks and draw in additional actors.

## Investigation, Trials, and Legacy

Major robbery investigations typically combine forensic work at the scene, informant intelligence, and financial inquiries into sudden wealth.
Convictions in the mid-1980s established responsibility for key parts of the scheme, but the broader story continued as authorities pursued the movement of the gold and related profits.
The Brink's-Mat case has remained a reference point in discussions of British organized crime, proceeds-of-crime enforcement, and the challenges of tracing commodities once they enter legitimate markets.
