## Summary

Hanni Pham (born October 6, 2004), known mononymously as Hanni, is an Australian singer, dancer, and model of Vietnamese descent who rose to international fame as a member of the South Korean girl group NewJeans. Debuting in 2022 under ADOR, a sub-label of HYBE, she quickly became one of the most recognized faces of the fourth generation of K-pop, celebrated for her multilingual fluency, charismatic stage presence, and blend of cultures.

As a member of NewJeans, Hanni contributed to a wave of critically acclaimed releases that redefined mainstream K-pop aesthetics in the early 2020s. The group's emphasis on Y2K nostalgia, understated styling, and experimental production distinguished them from contemporaries, with Hanni's distinct vocal and dancing abilities earning her a dedicated global fanbase.

Beyond music, Hanni has made significant inroads in fashion and modeling, becoming a brand ambassador for several luxury labels and representing a new generation of multicultural K-pop artists who challenge conventional industry norms.

## Early Life & Background

Hanni Pham was born on October 6, 2004, in Melbourne, Australia, to Vietnamese parents. She grew up in Melbourne, where she developed an early passion for music and performance. She trained in classical piano and was drawn to contemporary dance from a young age. Her multicultural upbringing gave her fluency in English and Vietnamese, and she later acquired proficiency in Korean after being scouted and relocating to South Korea to train under ADOR.

Her Australian upbringing lent a distinctive quality to her artistry, and she has spoken in interviews about the cultural duality she navigates as an international artist in the Korean entertainment industry.

## Rise to Prominence

Hanni debuted with NewJeans on July 22, 2022, with the self-titled EP *NewJeans*. The release, which included the singles "Attention," "Hype Boy," and "Cookie," was a commercial and critical success, with "Attention" debuting at number one on South Korea's Gaon Digital Chart. NewJeans became one of the fastest K-pop groups to achieve widespread recognition, and Hanni was frequently cited by critics for her performance versatility.

In 2023, NewJeans released their second EP *OMG* and mini-album *Get Up*, the latter featuring the international hit "Super Shy," which charted in multiple countries and reached the Billboard Global 200. Hanni's visibility grew substantially through this period, bolstered by international press coverage and brand partnerships.

## Key Achievements

- Co-debuted with NewJeans in 2022; their debut EP sold over 400,000 copies within weeks of release.
- "Hype Boy" and "Attention" simultaneously charted on the Billboard Hot 100 in 2022.
- Appointed global ambassador for Chanel in 2023, one of the youngest K-pop artists to hold such a role.
- NewJeans won multiple awards at the 2023 Melon Music Awards and Mnet Asian Music Awards, including Best Group.

## Controversies & Criticism

In 2024, NewJeans became embroiled in a high-profile dispute with HYBE following the dismissal of ADOR CEO Min Hee-jin, who had been the creative force behind the group's identity. The members, including Hanni, publicly expressed support for Min and made formal statements criticizing HYBE's management decisions. The situation drew international media attention and sparked debate about artist rights and corporate control in the K-pop industry. In late 2024, NewJeans announced their departure from ADOR/HYBE, a move that generated significant legal and public controversy.

## Legacy & Influence

Hanni and NewJeans are widely credited with shifting K-pop's aesthetic direction in the early 2020s, popularizing a softer, more nostalgic visual language at a time when the genre was dominated by high-concept, maximalist production. As an Australian-Vietnamese artist succeeding at the apex of the Korean entertainment industry, Hanni has become a symbol of K-pop's growing internationalism and cultural diversity.
