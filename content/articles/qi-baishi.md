## Summary

Qi Baishi was one of the most celebrated Chinese painters of the modern era, known for his lively brushwork paintings of shrimp, crabs, flowers, and insects. Self-taught until middle age, he combined folk art spontaneity with literati tradition. Named a People's Artist by the Chinese government and awarded the International Peace Prize, his work bridges traditional Chinese painting and modernity.
