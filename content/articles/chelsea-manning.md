## Summary

American former Army intelligence analyst who leaked hundreds of thousands of classified documents to WikiLeaks in 2010, including the 'Collateral Murder' video. Sentenced to 35 years, commuted by Obama. Later jailed for contempt.

## Background and Role

Chelsea Manning served as a U.S. Army intelligence analyst during the Iraq War era. In that role, she had access to military and diplomatic reporting systems that contained a large volume of classified material.

## The 2010 Disclosures

The materials provided to WikiLeaks included battlefield reports, diplomatic cables, and video footage that became widely circulated. The disclosures triggered a global debate about transparency, wartime conduct, and the handling of sensitive national-security information.

## Prosecution and Sentence

Manning was arrested in 2010 and prosecuted under U.S. military law. She was convicted in 2013 and received a lengthy prison sentence, later commuted in 2017 by President Barack Obama.

## Civil Contempt and Grand Jury Proceedings

After release, Manning was jailed again in 2019 for refusing to comply with grand-jury testimony demands. That episode highlighted the legal tension between compelled testimony and an individual’s refusal to cooperate with certain investigative processes.

## Public Debate and Legacy

Manning’s case is frequently cited in discussions of whistleblowing versus unauthorized disclosure, the scope of secrecy in modern states, and the human costs of prolonged legal and political conflict. She has also been part of wider conversations about the treatment of incarcerated individuals and the visibility of transgender people in public life.

## Related Topics
