## Overview

Wei Hsueh-kang is a Chinese-born fugitive wanted by authorities including the United States and Thailand for alleged drug trafficking linked to the Golden Triangle region.

He has been described as both a senior figure connected to the United Wa State Army (UWSA) and as a businessman with extensive investments along the Myanmar border.

## Early life and migration

Biographical accounts place his origins in Yunnan, China, with later movement into Burma (present-day Myanmar) after the Chinese Civil War period, in the context of Kuomintang-linked forces operating in border regions.

## Armed-group and political links

Wei has been associated with the United Wa State Party (UWSP) and with the UWSA, the armed wing tied to the self-administered Wa area in Myanmar.

Reporting describes him as a regional commander within the UWSA structure and as an influential financier through business holdings.

## Wanted status and reward

Wei has been listed on U.S. “most wanted” style programs for narcotics trafficking, with a reward of up to US$2 million for information leading to his capture.

Thai authorities pursued him in the late 1980s; accounts note that he obtained Thai citizenship in the mid-1980s, later lost it, and avoided custody after legal proceedings.

## Business activities

Later profiles describe Wei as a major business figure in Myanmar’s border economy, including investment in diversified enterprises (often described as spanning construction, trade, and related sectors).

These business activities are frequently discussed alongside allegations that revenues were historically connected to narcotics trafficking.

## Aliases

Sources commonly note multiple pseudonyms used over time, reflecting efforts to evade arrest and operate across borders.
