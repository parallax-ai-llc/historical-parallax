## Summary

Nigerian Yoruba activist who led agitation for Oduduwa Republic and attacked Fulani herder settlements. He was arrested in Benin Republic while fleeing Nigeria.

## Context

Igboho became prominent amid heightened insecurity in parts of southwestern Nigeria, where disputes involving armed banditry, community vigilante actions, and farmer–herder tensions were widely debated in national politics.

## Political Activity

Reporting around Igboho often frames him as a Yoruba self-determination figure associated with calls for an "Oduduwa Republic." His public profile combined grassroots mobilization with polarizing rhetoric, drawing both supporters who emphasized community protection and critics who warned about ethnic escalation.
