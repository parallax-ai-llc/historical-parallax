## Summary

Whitney Wolfe Herd founded Bumble, the dating app where women make the first move, and became the youngest woman to take a company public in the U.S. She was previously a co-founder of Tinder before leaving amid a sexual harassment lawsuit. Bumble went public in 2021 with a valuation over $8 billion. She was named to Time's 100 Most Influential People and Forbes 30 Under 30.
