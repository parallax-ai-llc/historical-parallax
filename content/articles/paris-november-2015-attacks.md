## Overview

The attacks on **13 November 2015** were a coordinated series of shootings and suicide bombings in Paris and the suburb of Saint-Denis. Key sites included the **Bataclan** concert hall, areas of outdoor cafés and restaurants, and the vicinity of the **Stade de France** during an international football match.

## Perpetrators and Attribution

French authorities attributed the operation to militants connected to the Islamic State (ISIS/ISIL). The events became a major case study in transnational extremist networks, logistics, and the use of multiple teams targeting civilian venues.

## Immediate Response

France declared a state of emergency and intensified domestic security operations and international counterterrorism coordination. The attacks also prompted broad public vigils and acts of solidarity, alongside debates over surveillance, policing, and civil liberties.

## Investigation and Trial

A long investigation led to a major criminal trial in France, culminating in convictions and sentences for a number of defendants connected to planning, support, and facilitation. The judicial process became one of the largest terrorism trials in modern French history.

## Memorialization

The attacks are commemorated annually in France, with particular attention to the victims at the Bataclan and other sites. They remain a reference point in European counterterrorism policy discussions and in public debates about resilience, security, and social cohesion.
