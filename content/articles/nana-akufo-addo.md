## Background

Nana Addo Dankwa Akufo-Addo is a Ghanaian politician and lawyer associated with the New Patriotic Party (NPP). He has been a prominent figure in Ghanaian public life across multiple decades, including roles in government, opposition politics, and party leadership.

## Early Career

- Trained and worked in law, building a professional profile prior to national executive office.
- Entered Ghanaian politics during the period of multi-party democratic consolidation, serving in elected and appointed positions.

## Political Roles (Selected)

- **Member of Parliament:** Served as an MP, contributing to parliamentary debates and party strategy.
- **Foreign Affairs:** Held ministerial responsibility for foreign affairs (as recorded in widely cited biographical accounts), shaping Ghana’s diplomatic engagement.
- **Attorney-General / Justice:** Served in senior legal-government roles, linked to governance and legal policy.

## Presidency (2017–)

Key themes often associated with his administration include:

- **Education policy:** Expansion of senior high school access is frequently cited as a flagship initiative.
- **Economic management:** The government has faced recurring debates over fiscal discipline, debt, inflation, and currency stability.
- **Public administration:** Appointments, procurement, and public spending have been major areas of scrutiny by media and civil society.

## Controversies and Public Criticism

The administration has been the subject of allegations and public controversy, including:

- **Agyapa Royalties transaction:** Criticized in public discourse as a contentious attempt to monetize mineral royalty interests; supporters and critics have framed it differently in terms of transparency and value.
- **COVID-19 spending and procurement:** Like many governments, Ghana faced intense oversight on emergency spending, contracting, and accountability.

## International Relations

As president, Akufo-Addo has represented Ghana in regional and international forums, including engagement with ECOWAS and the African Union, and bilateral relationships with major partners.
