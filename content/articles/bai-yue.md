## Summary

Former vice governor of Yunnan Province who was convicted of massive bribery and corruption. He leveraged his position to extract bribes from mining and real estate businesses.

## Additional Context

### Role and portfolio

As a vice governor in Yunnan, Bai Yue held a senior provincial executive post in a region with substantial mining activity, cross-border trade corridors, and large-scale infrastructure and real estate development—sectors that often generate high-stakes permitting and procurement decisions.

### Nature of the allegations

Public summaries of the case describe the misconduct primarily in terms of bribery linked to business interests seeking preferential treatment. Such cases commonly involve:

- approvals and regulatory clearances (especially for resource extraction and land use)
- contract awards and project financing decisions
- leveraging influence over subordinate departments and state-owned entities

### Investigation and prosecution

The case unfolded during China’s broader anti-corruption drive, in which the Central Commission for Discipline Inspection (CCDI) and procuratorate authorities investigated senior officials across provinces and ministries.

### Significance

Bai Yue is frequently cited in coverage of Yunnan’s anti-corruption enforcement as an example of how senior provincial officials could become entangled with local business networks, particularly where resource rents and construction-led growth create strong incentives for bribery.
