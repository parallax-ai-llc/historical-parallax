## Overview
In the spring of 1989, students occupied Tiananmen Square, calling for democratic reforms. The protests attracted broad support but were declared "counter-revolutionary" by the government.

## Crackdown
On June 4, military troops and tanks entered the square, firing on demonstrators. The exact death toll remains unknown but is estimated to be in the hundreds or thousands. The event remains highly censored in China.
