## Summary

Emil Nolde was a German-Danish Expressionist known for intensely colored religious paintings and vivid watercolors. Despite being a Nazi supporter, he was paradoxically condemned as 'degenerate.' He secretly created hundreds of small watercolors when forbidden to paint.
