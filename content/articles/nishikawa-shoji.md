## Summary

Nishikawa Shoji was a Japanese physicist who pioneered X-ray crystallography in Japan and independently observed X-ray diffraction from fibrous materials, contributing to structural biology. He made early observations of crystal structures using X-ray diffraction, advancing Japanese materials science.
