## Background

- Moninder Singh Pandher is an Indian businessman associated with the "Nithari" serial killings case, which drew nationwide attention due to the number of child victims and the allegations of systemic failure in early investigations.[^1]
- Reporting around the case commonly centers on a residential property in the village of Nithari (Noida, Uttar Pradesh) connected to Pandher, and on his domestic help, Surinder Koli, who was also prosecuted in multiple related cases.[^1]

## Nithari (Noida) killings — high-level overview

- The case concerns the disappearance and murder of multiple children (and some adults) from the area around Nithari, with extensive forensic investigation after human remains were discovered in 2006.[^1]
- The investigation involved multiple agencies and a large number of individual criminal cases/chargesheets; outcomes differed across specific trials and counts.[^1]
- Public discussion of the case has frequently highlighted issues such as delayed reporting, inadequate early police response, and the social vulnerability of affected families.[^1]

## Legal proceedings (summary-level)

- Pandher and Koli were arrested during the 2006 investigation and faced prosecution across multiple cases linked to the Nithari killings.[^1]
- Pandher was convicted alongside Koli in at least one case; the broader set of proceedings spans multiple trials and appeals, and the results can vary by case and by court.[^1]
- Because the Nithari prosecutions involve many separate matters, a single "convicted/acquitted" label can be incomplete without specifying the particular charge and judgment being referenced.[^1]

## Public impact and significance

- The Nithari killings became one of India’s most widely reported criminal cases of the 2000s, prompting intense media scrutiny and public outrage.[^1]
- The case is often cited in conversations about child safety, policing standards, forensic capacity, and the treatment of missing-person reports—especially where victims come from lower-income communities.[^1]
