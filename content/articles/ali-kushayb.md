## Summary

Sudanese Janjaweed militia leader charged by the ICC with 31 counts of war crimes and crimes against humanity in Darfur. He led attacks on villages that killed thousands of civilians.

## Context: Darfur conflict

Ali Kushayb is most often discussed in connection with the War in Darfur, a conflict involving Sudanese government forces, allied militias, and multiple rebel movements. The conflict drew international attention for large-scale civilian harm, displacement, and allegations of systematic violence.

## Allegations and ICC proceedings

The ICC case against Kushayb has been cited as an example of efforts to pursue individual accountability for militia command responsibility. Public reporting and court documents frequently describe allegations including:

- Participation in or ordering attacks on villages.
- Responsibility for killings, sexual violence, and forced displacement.
- Coordination with state-linked security structures and militia mobilization.

## Subsequent developments

In later reporting on the case, Kushayb's custody transfer to the ICC has been treated as a major milestone, particularly because suspects in the Darfur situation often remained beyond the Court's reach for many years.

## Significance

Kushayb's case is frequently referenced in discussions of:

- International criminal law and the difficulties of arrest and surrender.
- Command responsibility in irregular forces.
- The interaction between domestic political change in Sudan and international justice mechanisms.
