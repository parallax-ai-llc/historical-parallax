## Additional Context

### Nuclear shell model (high level)

Goeppert Mayer is best known for helping establish the nuclear shell model, which treats protons and neutrons in the nucleus as occupying discrete energy levels ("shells"). This framework helped explain why certain numbers of nucleons (so-called *magic numbers*) correspond to unusually stable nuclei.

### Other contributions

Her name is also associated with important theoretical ideas in:

- **Two-photon absorption**, a process in which an atom or molecule absorbs two photons simultaneously.
- **Double beta decay**, a rare nuclear process that remains relevant to modern neutrino physics.

### Recognition and legacy

In 1963 she shared the Nobel Prize in Physics with J. Hans D. Jensen and Eugene Wigner. Her career is frequently discussed in the context of twentieth-century physics, including the challenges that women scientists faced in obtaining paid academic positions despite major research contributions.
