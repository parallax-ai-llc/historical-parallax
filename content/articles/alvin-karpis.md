## Summary
Alvin Karpis was the last 'Public Enemy Number One' and a member of the Barker-Karpis gang. He served 26 years at Alcatraz — the longest sentence there. Personally arrested by J. Edgar Hoover.

## Early Life
- Born in Canada; later operated primarily in the United States.
- Became involved in criminal activity in early adulthood.

## Criminal Career (High-Level)
- Associated with the **Barker–Karpis gang**, known for bank robberies and high-profile kidnappings during the 1930s.
- Gained national notoriety during the Depression-era "Public Enemy" period.

## Arrest
- Captured in 1936.
- Notably, J. Edgar Hoover claimed personal involvement in the arrest, which became part of Hoover’s public narrative.

## Imprisonment
- Served time at **Alcatraz Federal Penitentiary**, with a record long tenure.
