## Summary

Full name of Hambali, the Jemaah Islamiyah operations chief who was Southeast Asia's most wanted terrorist. He orchestrated attacks across the region from his base in Thailand.

## Identity and Alias

Nurjaman Riduan Isamuddin is widely known by the alias “Hambali.” In public reporting and government statements, he has been described as a key operational figure within Jemaah Islamiyah (JI), a Southeast Asian militant Islamist network, and as an important connector between regional extremists and transnational jihadist circles.

## Role in Regional Militancy (High-Level)

Accounts typically characterize Hambali as an organizer and facilitator involved in planning and coordination for major attacks in the region. This included involvement associated with high-casualty bombings in Indonesia and the Philippines around the early 2000s. Descriptions emphasize leadership and logistical coordination rather than front-line participation.

## Arrest and Detention

Hambali was arrested in Thailand in 2003 in a joint counterterrorism operation involving Thai authorities and U.S. personnel. After capture, he was held in U.S. custody and later transferred to the detention facility at Guantánamo Bay.

## Legal and Political Context

His detention has been referenced in broader debates about counterterrorism policy, intelligence cooperation, and the legal processes available for terrorism suspects held by the United States. Public sources discuss issues such as the use of military commissions, the complexity of cross-border evidence, and the diplomatic implications of detaining a high-profile suspect from Southeast Asia.

## Significance

Hambali’s case is often cited as emblematic of:

- The internationalization of counterterrorism operations after 2001
- The role of regional networks in enabling mass-casualty attacks
- The long-running legal challenges surrounding high-value detainees
