## Summary

Gregory the Great served as Pope from 590 to 604 and was one of the most influential popes in history. He reformed the liturgy, promoted Gregorian chant, and sent missionaries to England. His Pastoral Care became the standard guide for bishops for centuries. He also wrote important theological works including Moralia in Job and Dialogues.
