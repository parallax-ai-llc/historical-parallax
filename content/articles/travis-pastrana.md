## Summary

Travis Pastrana is a motorsports legend who won multiple X Games gold medals in motocross and rallying. He performed the first double backflip on a motorcycle at the 2006 X Games.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1999 | Won first X Games gold at age 15 |
| 2006 | First double backflip on a motorcycle at X Games |
| 2020 | Recreated Evel Knievel's motorcycle jumps |

## Disciplines and Style

- **Motocross and freestyle:** Pastrana is closely associated with pushing the technical and risk limits of freestyle motocross, combining competitive results with spectacle.
- **Rally and action sports crossover:** He also competed in rally and other motorsport formats, helping popularize cross-discipline “action sports” careers.

## Signature Moments (High Level)

- **Innovation in tricks:** The 2006 double backflip became a landmark moment in freestyle motocross, widely referenced as a turning point in what was considered feasible.
- **Stunt performance:** He became known for high-profile stunts and public demonstrations that blended athleticism, showmanship, and calculated risk.

## Media and Business Presence

- **Nitro Circus:** Pastrana has been associated with action-sports entertainment projects that showcase extreme stunts across vehicles and disciplines.
- **Brand influence:** Through competition, media, and touring shows, he contributed to mainstream visibility for freestyle motocross and adjacent sports.

## Legacy

- **Sport progression:** He is frequently cited as a driver of progression in freestyle motocross trick difficulty and event expectations.
- **Cultural impact:** His career is often discussed as an example of how athletes can shape a sport through both competitive achievement and media storytelling.
