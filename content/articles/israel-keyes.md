## Summary
Israel Keys was an American serial killer, rapist, and bank robber who committed crimes across the United States between 2001 and 2012. He pre-planned murders by burying 'kill kits' years in advance. He is confirmed to have murdered at least three people. He died by suicide in his cell in 2012.

## Early Life
Born January 7, 1978, in Richmond, Utah. He was raised in a fundamentalist family, eventually leaving home. He served in the U.S. Army. He worked as a general contractor in Anchorage, Alaska. He was intelligent and methodical. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Keyes traveled across the country to commit crimes far from his home in Alaska. He buried 'kill kits' — containing weapons, cash, and other supplies — in various locations years before returning to use them. He targeted strangers to avoid connections. He is confirmed to have murdered at least three people but FBI suspects many more. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Keyes was arrested in March 2012 in Texas after kidnapping and murdering barista Samantha Koenig in Anchorage, Alaska, and using her debit card. He confessed to multiple murders during interrogations but died by suicide in his Anchorage jail cell on December 2, 2012, before revealing all details. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case provided FBI with valuable data on planning-oriented serial killers [^4]
- Advanced understanding of how serial offenders minimize detection
- Highlighted the challenges of identifying killers who target strangers across jurisdictions

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered at least three people across multiple states [^5]
- Pre-buried 'kill kits' showing extreme premeditation
- Targeted strangers to avoid connections
- Died before revealing the full scope of his crimes
- His suicide denied families of potential victims closure
