## Summary

Tirunesh Dibaba won three Olympic golds and was the first woman to achieve the 5,000m/10,000m double at one Olympics (2008 Beijing).

## Background

Dibaba is part of Ethiopia’s prominent tradition in distance running, which has produced multiple Olympic and world champions. She rose quickly as a teenager, competing successfully in both track events and cross country, and became known for her ability to sustain a fast pace over long distances.

## Major Championships

Her career highlights include titles at the Olympic Games and the World Championships, particularly in the 5,000 meters and 10,000 meters. The 2008 Olympic “double” is frequently cited as a rare achievement, reflecting both endurance and tactical versatility across two demanding events.

## Racing Style

Analysts often describe Dibaba as an athlete with a strong finishing kick combined with high aerobic capacity. This combination allowed her to win in different race shapes—either by controlling pace from the front or by responding to surges late in the race.

## Legacy

Dibaba is widely regarded as one of the greatest women’s long-distance runners of her era. Along with other Ethiopian and East African champions, she helped expand global interest in women’s distance events and set performance benchmarks that influenced later generations.
