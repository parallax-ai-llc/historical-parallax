## Summary

Klaus Schwab is the founder and executive chairman of the World Economic Forum (WEF), the most influential platform where global business and political leaders converge. He founded the WEF in 1971, and its annual meeting in Davos, Switzerland became the world's premier gathering of business elites, heads of state, and thought leaders. He has authored several books including 'The Fourth Industrial Revolution' and shaped global discourse on stakeholder capitalism.
