## Background

Yuna Kim is a South Korean figure skater whose career is closely associated with the international rise of South Korean figure skating.

## Competitive Highlights (high level)

- **2010 Winter Olympics (Vancouver):** Won the **women’s singles gold medal** in figure skating.
- **2014 Winter Olympics (Sochi):** Won the **women’s singles silver medal**.
- **World Championships:** Won major titles at the senior level and was a consistent medal contender across the peak of her competitive years.

## Style and Reputation

Kim is widely cited for:

- Strong technical consistency under pressure
- Clean jump execution and efficient skating skills
- Programs built around musical interpretation and performance quality

## Discipline: Figure Skating (quick explainer)

In Olympic singles figure skating, athletes typically compete in two segments:

- **Short program:** A required set of elements performed in a shorter routine
- **Free skating:** A longer program with more elements, where cumulative score determines final placement

Scores combine technical element values and program component scores (skating skills, transitions, performance, composition, and interpretation), with deductions for errors.

## Legacy (high level)

- Kim’s Olympic success is frequently referenced as a catalyst for increased domestic interest, media attention, and investment in figure skating in South Korea.
- She is commonly listed among the most prominent athletes in modern South Korean sports history.
