## Summary

Choe Si-hyeong was the second leader of Donghak who organized the movement after the execution of founder Choe Je-u. He developed the theological and organizational framework of the religion, including the concept of 'treating people as God' (sain yeocheon). He led the Donghak Peasant Revolution of 1894, one of the largest peasant uprisings in Korean history. He was captured and executed by the Korean government.
