## Summary

Myanmar general who led Bureau of Special Operations during the Rohingya genocide. UN fact-finding mission named him as responsible for atrocities requiring criminal prosecution.

## Military Role (High Level)

Aung Kyaw Zaw is a senior officer in Myanmar’s armed forces (the Tatmadaw). He has been described as holding command responsibilities linked to the Bureau of Special Operations (BSO), a structure used to coordinate large-scale military activity across regional commands.[^1]

In reporting on the 2017 violence in Rakhine State, the UN’s Independent International Fact-Finding Mission on Myanmar (FFM) identified several senior commanders and recommended that they be investigated and prosecuted for serious international crimes. Aung Kyaw Zaw was among those named in the FFM’s 2018 report in connection with operations affecting the Rohingya population.[^2]

## Context: 2017 Rakhine State Operations

The 2017 crisis in Rakhine State involved mass displacement of Rohingya civilians, widespread destruction of villages, and extensive allegations of killings and sexual violence. International bodies and human rights organizations documented patterns they argued were consistent with crimes against humanity and other grave violations.[^2]

## Accountability and International Scrutiny

The UN FFM’s core recommendation was accountability: credible, independent criminal investigations and prosecutions of senior military leadership and those responsible for planning, ordering, or enabling abuses.[^2]

Because much of the detailed evidence is contained in UN documentation and subsequent reporting, biographical details for some commanders are limited in public sources. Summaries of roles and responsibilities should therefore be read as high-level attributions rather than complete operational histories.

## Notes on Terminology

Some datasets and summaries label individuals as "war criminals" based on alleged responsibility and the gravity of reported acts. A formal criminal conviction requires due process in a competent court; the UN FFM report is a basis for investigation and prosecution recommendations rather than a judgment of guilt.[^2]
