## Summary

Bosnian Croat political leader convicted by the ICTY of crimes against humanity for his role in the ethnic cleansing of Bosniaks in Herzegovina. He was president of the self-proclaimed Herzeg-Bosnia entity.

## Political Role

Prlić was a Bosnian Croat politician associated with the wartime self-proclaimed Croatian Republic of Herzeg-Bosnia during the breakup of Yugoslavia. In that context he held senior governmental roles, which later became central to questions of responsibility for policies and actions on the ground.

## ICTY Case

The International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) prosecuted Prlić and other leaders in a long-running case concerning crimes committed during the Bosnian War. The court addressed allegations including persecution, forcible displacement, and other crimes against humanity.

In 2017, the ICTY convicted him and sentenced him to 25 years’ imprisonment. The verdict is frequently referenced in discussions of command responsibility, joint criminal enterprise theories used in international law, and the legal record of the conflict.

## Legacy and Historical Debate

The case remains politically sensitive in the region and is part of the broader post-war effort to document atrocities, establish legal accountability, and support historical reckoning.
