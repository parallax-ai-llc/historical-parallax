## Summary

German SS officer and commandant of Auschwitz concentration camp where over 1.1 million people were murdered. He oversaw the expansion of the camp and implementation of gas chambers. Hanged at Auschwitz.

## Role in the Holocaust (High Level)

Rudolf Höss was a senior SS figure associated with the operation and expansion of Auschwitz, which became a central site of mass murder during the Holocaust. Historical accounts describe him as an administrator who helped implement systems for imprisonment, forced labor, and industrial-scale killing.

## Postwar Capture and Trial

After the Second World War, Höss was captured by British forces, transferred to Polish authorities, and tried for war crimes. He was convicted and executed at Auschwitz in 1947.

## Memoirs and Historical Record

Höss is often discussed in relation to postwar statements and memoir-like writings attributed to him while in custody. These materials are frequently cited by historians, but, like other perpetrator accounts, are typically read critically and cross-checked against documentary evidence and survivor testimony.

## Legacy and Documentation

Auschwitz has extensive archival documentation, scholarship, and memorial work. In that context, Höss is generally treated as one of the principal perpetrators connected to the camp’s administration, and his name appears in historical treatments of how Nazi institutions organized and carried out genocide.

## Notes on Terminology

This article uses "Auschwitz" for the larger complex and "Birkenau" for Auschwitz II, which is commonly described as the primary extermination site within the complex.
