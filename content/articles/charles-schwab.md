## Summary

Charles Schwab founded Charles Schwab Corporation, revolutionizing the brokerage industry by introducing discount stock trading for individual investors. He pioneered the concept of low-cost brokerage services after deregulation of broker commissions in 1975. His company grew into one of the largest financial services firms in the United States. The acquisition of TD Ameritrade in 2020 made Schwab the largest brokerage in the U.S.
