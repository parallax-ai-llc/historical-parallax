## Summary

Martin de Porres was a Peruvian Dominican lay brother who was the son of a Spanish nobleman and a freed enslaved woman. He devoted his life to caring for the sick and poor, regardless of race or social status. He is credited with many miraculous healings and was known for his humility and love of animals. He was the first Black saint from the Americas, canonized in 1962. He is the patron saint of mixed-race people, public health, and social justice.
