## Summary

Riduan Isamuddin, Indonesian terrorist known as Hambali who served as the key operational link between al-Qaeda and Jemaah Islamiyah. He was the mastermind behind the 2002 Bali bombings.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2002 | Planned Bali bombings killing 202 |

| 2006 | Transferred to Guantánamo Bay |

## Additional Context

- **Identity note:** "Hambali" is a nom de guerre; he is commonly identified as **Riduan Isamuddin** (also reported as Encep Nurjaman in some sources).
- **Regional role:** He has been described as a central connector between Southeast Asian militant networks (notably Jemaah Islamiyah) and al-Qaeda-linked figures.

## Detention and Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- **Capture:** He was apprehended in Thailand in 2003 in an operation involving US and Thai authorities.
- **Secret detention:** Public reporting and official disclosures have described periods of CIA custody prior to transfer to Guantánamo.
- **Military commission:** Reporting describes charges being brought in US military-commission proceedings years after initial detention, reflecting the long-running legal complexity of Guantánamo cases.
