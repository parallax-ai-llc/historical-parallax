## Summary

Dalilah Muhammad was the first American woman to win Olympic 400m hurdles gold (2016). She set the WR of 52.16s.

## Additional Context

## Background

- Muhammad competed for the United States in the 400 metres hurdles, an event requiring sprint speed, rhythm between barriers, and strong finishing endurance.
- She became a defining athlete of the late-2010s era in women’s 400m hurdles, when global standards accelerated rapidly.

## 400m Hurdles Overview

- The event features **10 hurdles** around one lap of the track; athletes aim to keep a consistent stride pattern between barriers.
- Small rhythm disruptions compound over the lap, so championship races often reward technical control and pacing.

## Major Highlights

- **Olympic champion (2016):** Won gold in the women’s 400m hurdles at Rio, noted as the first American woman to take Olympic gold in the event.
- **World record (2019):** Set a world record of **52.16 seconds**, marking a historically fast time for the discipline.
- **Championship consistency:** Recognized for repeatedly performing at peak level in global finals.

## Significance

- Her results helped redefine expectations for what was possible in the event and contributed to a broader surge in women’s hurdles performance.

- World records in the 400m hurdles are often viewed as the product of both raw speed and optimized hurdle rhythm over a full lap.
