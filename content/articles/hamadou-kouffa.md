## Summary

Malian jihadist preacher who founded Katiba Macina targeting central Mali's Fulani communities. He exploited ethnic tensions to recruit fighters for JNIM.

## Additional Context

### Context: Central Mali Conflict

Hamadou Kouffa is typically profiled within the escalation of violence in central Mali after 2012, where insurgent organizations, local militias, and state security forces competed amid weak administration and contested local authority. Many analyses emphasize that conflict dynamics in the region involve both ideological militancy and localized disputes.

### Organization and Affiliations

Kouffa is widely described as the founder or leading figure of **Katiba Macina**, a militant faction operating in central Mali, and as aligned with **JNIM** (Jama'at Nasr al-Islam wal Muslimin). Reporting often highlights:

- Use of religious preaching as a recruitment and legitimization tool
- Mobilization through grievances tied to security, land, and community protection
- Integration into wider regional networks that coordinate messaging and operations

### Impact and Controversy

Accounts of his influence frequently note that the conflict environment became increasingly polarized, with cycles of retaliation and displacement. Descriptions of Kouffa’s role tend to focus on how leadership figures can amplify existing tensions rather than create them from scratch.

### Status and Reporting Uncertainty

Because militant leadership in active conflict zones is difficult to verify, open sources have at times contained conflicting claims about his fate and operational status. Summaries often note reported “kills” followed by later indications of survival.

### Significance

Kouffa’s profile is often cited in discussions of how Sahelian insurgencies adapt to local social structures and how counterinsurgency outcomes depend on governance, community security, and credible dispute resolution—alongside military pressure.
