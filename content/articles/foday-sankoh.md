## Summary

Sierra Leonean rebel leader who founded the Revolutionary United Front (RUF) and waged an 11-year civil war infamous for mass amputations. His forces abducted children as soldiers and used diamonds to fund the war. Died awaiting trial.

## Background

- **Early career**: Sankoh served in Sierra Leone’s military before emerging as a rebel leader.
- **Regional influences**: The RUF insurgency is often discussed in the context of West African conflicts in the 1990s, including cross-border support networks and resource-driven warfare.

## The RUF and the Sierra Leone Civil War

- **Insurgency tactics**: The RUF became notorious for attacks on civilians, forced labor, and the widespread use of child soldiers.
- **"Blood diamonds"**: Control of diamond-producing areas and illicit diamond sales were central to financing and sustaining the rebellion.
- **International response**: The conflict involved UN peacekeeping efforts and foreign interventions, and it became a major reference point for debates on humanitarian intervention.

## Peace Process and Breakdown

- **Lomé Peace Accord (1999)**: The agreement attempted to end the war by integrating rebel leadership into government structures, but implementation proved unstable.
- **Renewed violence**: Fighting and hostage-taking resumed, contributing to Sankoh’s arrest and the collapse of the power-sharing arrangement.

## Legal Proceedings and Death

- **Special Court for Sierra Leone**: Sankoh was indicted for war crimes and crimes against humanity; he died in custody before a full trial could be completed.
- **Historical significance**: His case is frequently cited in discussions of accountability for wartime atrocities and the development of hybrid international tribunals.
