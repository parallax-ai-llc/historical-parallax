## Summary

Collis P. Huntington was one of the 'Big Four' railroad barons who built the Central Pacific Railroad. He later controlled the Southern Pacific Railroad and the Chesapeake and Ohio Railway. A shrewd lobbyist in Washington, he wielded enormous political influence. He also developed Newport News, Virginia, and its shipbuilding industry.
