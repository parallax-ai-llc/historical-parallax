## Summary

Chinese cryptocurrency Ponzi scheme that defrauded 2.6 million investors of $5.7 billion, one of the largest crypto frauds in history. Organizers were arrested across Asia.

## Background

PlusToken was promoted primarily to Chinese- and Korean-speaking users as a high-yield crypto “wallet” and investment program. Marketing emphasized referrals and promised returns that were inconsistent with normal market risk, a pattern typical of Ponzi-style schemes.

## How the Scheme Worked (High Level)

- **Deposit intake**: Users were encouraged to deposit assets (commonly including BTC, ETH, and other major cryptocurrencies) into PlusToken-controlled addresses.
- **Return promises**: The program advertised steady, high returns, often framed as coming from arbitrage, mining, or “quantitative trading,” without transparent verifiable records.
- **Referral incentives**: Multi-level referral rewards helped drive rapid user growth and created strong incentives for members to recruit others.
- **Custody risk**: Because the program controlled the private keys, participants were exposed to total loss once withdrawals were restricted.

## Scale and Market Impact

Investigators and later reporting described PlusToken as one of the largest alleged crypto frauds by dollar value. Large transfers from wallets associated with PlusToken were widely tracked by on-chain analysts, and the liquidation of seized or moved holdings was cited as a potential source of market selling pressure during parts of 2019–2020.

## Law Enforcement and Aftermath

Organizers were arrested across multiple jurisdictions in Asia as authorities investigated fraud and related financial crimes. Subsequent prosecutions focused on the operators and the handling of customer deposits.

## Why PlusToken Is Often Cited

- Demonstrated how **high-yield promises + referral structures** can rapidly scale in crypto.
- Highlighted the systemic risk of **centralized custody** in unregulated “investment” wallet products.
- Became a widely referenced case study for **on-chain tracing** of fraud-linked funds.
