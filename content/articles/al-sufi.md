## Summary

Abd al-Rahman al-Sufi was a Persian astronomer who wrote the Book of Fixed Stars in 964, one of the most important medieval astronomical texts. He made the first recorded observation of the Andromeda Galaxy (calling it a 'nebulous smear') and the Large Magellanic Cloud. He corrected and updated Ptolemy's star catalog with improved magnitudes and positions.
