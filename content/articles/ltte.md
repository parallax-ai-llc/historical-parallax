## Summary

Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam who waged a 26-year insurgency in Sri Lanka. They pioneered suicide bombing and were responsible for assassinating two heads of state.

## Background

- The LTTE (Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam) emerged within the broader context of ethnic and political conflict in Sri Lanka, where demands for **Tamil autonomy/separatism** escalated into prolonged civil war.
- The group is strongly associated with its leader **Velupillai Prabhakaran**, and with a centralized organizational model relative to many insurgent movements.

## Political and Military Characteristics (High-Level)

- The LTTE combined guerrilla warfare with conventional capabilities at different phases, and it developed naval and limited air elements in some accounts.
- In areas under its influence, it has been described as building parallel administrative functions (policing, taxation, and courts), though descriptions and assessments vary by source.

## Tactics and Controversies

- The group is widely associated with the operationalization of **suicide attacks** in South Asia and with high-profile political assassinations.
- The LTTE has also been accused of abuses including forced recruitment, political repression in controlled areas, and attacks on civilians.

## International Designations

- Multiple governments designated the LTTE as a terrorist organization, which affected diaspora fundraising, logistics, and diplomatic engagement.

## End of the Conflict and Legacy

- The Sri Lankan government declared military victory over the LTTE in **2009**, ending the civil war's main conventional phase.
- Post-war issues have included accountability debates, treatment of displaced people, reconciliation politics, and the long-term effects on Sri Lanka's governance and civil liberties.
