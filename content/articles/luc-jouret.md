## Summary

Congolese-Belgian cult leader who co-founded the Order of the Solar Temple. His cult was responsible for mass murder-suicides in Switzerland and Quebec in 1994-1997 that killed 74 people. He died in one of the ritual mass deaths.

## Background

- Luc Jouret is described in public sources as a **Belgian physician** who later practiced **homeopathy** and other forms of alternative medicine.
- He studied medicine at the **Université libre de Bruxelles** and is commonly reported to have received a medical doctorate in the mid‑1970s.
- Jouret became known as a public speaker who blended health-related themes with **New Age** and esoteric ideas.

## Role in the Order of the Solar Temple

- Jouret co-founded the **Order of the Solar Temple (OTS)** with **Joseph Di Mambro** in 1984.
- Many accounts distinguish between internal leadership and external presentation: Di Mambro is often portrayed as the core organizer, while Jouret served as the movement's **public-facing recruiter and lecturer**.

## Relationship to the 1994–1997 Events

- The deaths associated with OTS in the mid‑1990s are typically characterized in investigations and mainstream histories as a complex sequence involving both **homicide and suicide**, with contested questions about coercion.
- Jouret is reported to have died in October 1994 during the first wave of OTS-linked deaths.
