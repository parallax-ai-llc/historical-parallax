## Summary

FBI investigation exposed a college admissions bribery scheme. Parents including actresses Lori Loughlin and Felicity Huffman paid up to $6.5 million for fraudulent SAT scores and athletic admissions.

## Background

- The investigation centered on a nationwide college admissions bribery conspiracy that involved standardized test cheating and falsified athletic recruiting profiles.
- The scheme was publicly announced by federal prosecutors in March 2019 and was prosecuted primarily by the U.S. Attorney’s Office for the District of Massachusetts.

## How the Scheme Worked (High-Level)

- **Entrance exam manipulation:** Bribes were paid to facilitate cheating on college entrance exams (including the SAT and ACT), often by arranging test proctors or administrators who could correct answers or otherwise enable fraud.
- **Athletic recruitment fraud:** Some applicants were presented as athletic recruits for sports they did not play; fabricated credentials and staged photos were used to support the false profiles.
- **Intermediary coordination:** A central organizer helped connect families with corrupt test administrators and coaches, routing payments and coordinating the fraudulent submissions.

## Notable People (Examples)

- **William “Rick” Singer:** Identified by prosecutors as a key organizer who ran a private college counseling operation and cooperated with investigators.
- **Lori Loughlin and Mossimo Giannulli:** Accused of paying bribes connected to their daughters’ admissions.
- **Felicity Huffman:** Pleaded guilty in connection with admissions fraud related to standardized testing.

## Charges and Legal Outcomes (Overview)

- Defendants faced federal charges that included conspiracy, fraud-related offenses, and (in some cases) money laundering.
- The case resulted in a large number of guilty pleas alongside contested cases.
- Sentences varied by individual conduct and charges, ranging from probation/community service to periods of incarceration.

## Public Impact

- The scandal intensified scrutiny of legacy admissions, athletic recruiting pipelines, and the role of private counseling in selective college admissions.
- Universities reviewed admissions and compliance processes, including authentication of athletic recruiting claims and controls around standardized test reporting.
