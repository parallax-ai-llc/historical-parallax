## Summary

Guru Gobind Singh was the tenth and final human Sikh Guru who established the Khalsa (the community of initiated Sikhs) in 1699. He introduced the Five Ks (Kesh, Kangha, Kara, Kachera, Kirpan) that define Sikh identity. He declared the Guru Granth Sahib as the eternal Guru after his death. He was a accomplished poet and warrior who fought against Mughal oppression. His four sons all died in the struggle against the Mughals.
