## Summary

The Treaty of Ganghwa (Kanghwa) of 1876 was the first unequal treaty imposed on Korea by Japan, opening three Korean ports to Japanese trade. Modeled on Perry's treaties with Japan, it ended Korea's isolation and began Japanese encroachment.
