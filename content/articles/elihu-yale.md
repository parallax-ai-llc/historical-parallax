## Summary

Elihu Yale was a Welsh merchant and colonial administrator who served as governor of Fort St. George in Madras for the East India Company. He amassed a large fortune through trade and his administrative position in India. His donation to the Collegiate School of Connecticut led it to be renamed Yale College (later Yale University) in his honor. His wealth came primarily from the diamond trade and colonial commerce.
