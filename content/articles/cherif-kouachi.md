## Summary
Chérif Kouachi, with his brother Saïd, attacked the Charlie Hebdo office in Paris, killing 12 including prominent cartoonists. Both brothers were killed in a police standoff.

## Background
- Chérif Kouachi was a French citizen of Algerian descent.
- He and his brother Saïd Kouachi became associated with militant Islamist networks in France in the 2000s.
- French authorities linked Chérif Kouachi to recruitment and facilitation efforts for travel to fight in Iraq during that period.

## Radicalization and Legal Proceedings
- Kouachi was connected to what French investigators described as a jihadist recruitment pipeline centered around Paris-area networks.
- He was arrested and later convicted in France for involvement in activities related to facilitating travel for combat in Iraq.
- Following imprisonment and surveillance, he remained of interest to French security services.

## 2015 Charlie Hebdo Attack (High-Level Overview)
- Date: 2015-01-07.
- Target: the offices of the satirical newspaper *Charlie Hebdo* in Paris.
- Outcome: 12 people were killed and others were injured; the attack prompted a major national and international response.
- The attack was widely treated as part of a broader wave of terrorism-related incidents affecting France and Europe.

## Manhunt and Death
- A nationwide manhunt followed the Paris attack.
- On 2015-01-09, Chérif and Saïd Kouachi were located and engaged in a police operation in Dammartin-en-Goële.
- Both brothers were killed during the final confrontation.

## Claimed Affiliation and Disputed Details
- Media and investigative reporting discussed purported links between the Kouachi brothers and al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP).
- The precise nature and extent of operational direction versus ideological alignment has been debated across reporting and official statements.

## Impact and Aftermath
- The attack intensified public debate in France on security policy, counterterrorism powers, integration, and free expression.
- It also became a catalyst for large public demonstrations and global solidarity events.
