## Summary

Mansur al-Hallaj was a Persian Sufi mystic who was executed for his ecstatic declaration 'Ana al-Haqq' (I am the Truth/God). His execution became the most famous martyrdom in Sufi history. He taught that the mystic could achieve complete union with God. His death sparked centuries of debate about the limits of mystical expression in Islam. His poetry and sayings continue to inspire Sufi spirituality.
