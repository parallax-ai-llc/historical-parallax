## Summary

Rwandan military officer acquitted by the ICTR of genocide charges. He was accused of planning military operations against Tutsi civilians during the genocide.

## ICTR Context (High-level)

- The International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR) was created to prosecute persons responsible for genocide and other serious violations of international humanitarian law committed in 1994.
- ICTR trials typically examined whether a defendant had the required intent and whether the prosecution proved participation (directly or through planning, ordering, or aiding) beyond a reasonable doubt.
- An acquittal means the charged crimes were not proven to the tribunal’s standard; it does not necessarily resolve every factual dispute discussed in public narratives.

## Why This Case Is Often Cited
