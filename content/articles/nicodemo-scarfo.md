## Summary

American mobster who was boss of the Philadelphia crime family from 1981 to 1989. He was one of the most violent mob bosses in history, ordering the murders of anyone who questioned him. Serving life plus 55 years.

## Rise to Power

Scarfo built influence in the Philadelphia underworld before taking control of the crime family in the early 1980s. Contemporary accounts emphasize a leadership style centered on strict loyalty tests and a willingness to use lethal violence to enforce discipline.

## Leadership of the Philadelphia Crime Family

During Scarfo’s tenure, the organization was involved in traditional mafia rackets such as illegal gambling, loansharking, extortion, and control of certain labor and business sectors. His reign is frequently described as especially turbulent, with internal conflict and a high number of suspected mob-related killings.

## Violence and Internal Conflict

Histories of Scarfo often highlight the extent of violence directed both at rivals and at perceived traitors within his own circles. This approach could consolidate short-term control but also intensified law-enforcement attention and created instability.

## Prosecution and Imprisonment

Scarfo was prosecuted in major federal and state cases and received severe sentences, including life imprisonment. His incarceration marked the end of his direct control of the family, though his era continued to shape perceptions of Philadelphia organized crime for decades.

## Death and Legacy

He died while in federal custody. Scarfo’s legacy is commonly framed as an example of late-20th-century mafia leadership colliding with expanded investigative tools (wiretaps, informants, and RICO-style prosecutions) and the long-term consequences of extreme internal violence.
