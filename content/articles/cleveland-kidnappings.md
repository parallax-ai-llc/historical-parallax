## Summary

Three women - Amanda Berry, Gina DeJesus, and Michelle Knight - were held captive by Ariel Castro in Cleveland from 2002-2013. They escaped when Berry broke through a door.

## Additional Context

### What happened (high-level)

Between **2002 and 2004**, Ariel Castro abducted three young women in Cleveland, Ohio—**Michelle Knight**, **Amanda Berry**, and **Gina DeJesus**—and held them captive at his home on **Seymour Avenue** for roughly a decade.

### Escape and rescue

On **May 6, 2013**, Amanda Berry escaped with her young daughter (born while she was held captive) and alerted neighbors and police. Officers then rescued Michelle Knight and Gina DeJesus, and Castro was arrested the same day.

### Charges, plea, and sentence

Court proceedings resulted in charges including kidnapping and rape. Castro ultimately entered a guilty plea to a very large number of counts (often summarized as **hundreds** of criminal counts) as part of a plea agreement and received a sentence described as **life imprisonment plus 1,000 years** without parole.

### Aftermath

The case prompted extensive public discussion about missing-person investigations, community awareness, and victim support services. The Seymour Avenue house became a focal point for public attention and was later removed as part of the community response.

### Note on naming

The event is commonly referred to as the "Cleveland kidnappings" in media, while Wikipedia titles it "Ariel Castro kidnappings."
