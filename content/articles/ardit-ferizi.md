## Summary

Kosovar hacker who stole personal data of 1,300 US military and government personnel and provided it to ISIS to target for attacks. He was the first person convicted in the US for both hacking and terrorism charges.

## Case Overview (High Level)

The case is frequently cited as an example of how conventional cybercrime (unauthorized access and data theft) can intersect with terrorism-related investigations when stolen information is shared with designated terrorist organizations.

## What Was Stolen

Public summaries describe the theft and disclosure of personally identifying information (PII) for U.S. military and government personnel. Such data can enable doxxing, harassment, targeting, or follow-on compromise attempts against individuals and their families.

## Arrest and Prosecution

Ferizi was arrested in Malaysia and later faced prosecution in the United States. Reporting and court documents emphasize that the charges combined computer intrusion offenses with terrorism-related counts tied to providing material support.

## Significance

- Demonstrated that “downstream use” of stolen data can materially change the legal framing of a hacking case.
- Highlighted operational risk from aggregation of PII even when the underlying data sources appear mundane.
- Contributed to public awareness of the threat posed by targeted exposure of service members’ personal details.
