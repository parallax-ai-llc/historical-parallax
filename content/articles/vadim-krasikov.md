## Summary

Russian agent and contract killer convicted in Germany for the 2019 assassination of Zelimkhan Khangoshvili in Berlin’s Tiergarten. German prosecutors and the trial court assessed the killing as a state-directed operation linked to Russian security services.

## Background

- Krasikov has been widely described in reporting and court proceedings as connected to the Russian security apparatus, including the FSB.
- Open-source accounts frequently describe him as operating under aliases and using travel/identity documents that were central to the German investigation.
- Public sources differ on some biographical details (including exact birth date and place), but consistently identify him as a Russian national tied to the Berlin murder case.

## The Berlin (Tiergarten) Assassination (2019)

- Target: Zelimkhan Khangoshvili, a Georgian citizen of Chechen origin who had fought in conflicts in the North Caucasus and later lived in Europe.
- Location: Kleiner Tiergarten / Tiergarten park area in Berlin.
- Method (as established by investigators and reported in court coverage): a close-range shooting in daylight followed by an attempted escape.
- The incident became a major bilateral dispute between Germany and Russia and was treated by German authorities as more than an ordinary homicide case.

## Investigation and Trial (Germany)

German authorities pursued the case as a politically sensitive homicide with potential state sponsorship. Key themes in public reporting include:

- Identity and documentation: the use of an assumed identity (“Vadim Sokolov”) and travel records was highlighted as evidence of an organized operation.
- Motive assessment: German prosecutors argued the killing was ordered by Russian state entities; Russia has denied responsibility.
- Security and diplomatic impact: the case contributed to diplomatic expulsions and a deterioration of relations.
