## Summary

Akihiko Hoshide was a JAXA astronaut who commanded the ISS and performed multiple spacewalks. He flew three missions to space.

## Education and early career

Hoshide trained as an engineer and worked in roles associated with Japan’s aerospace sector before being selected as an astronaut. JAXA astronauts typically follow a path that combines technical education, operational experience, and years of training in mission systems, robotics, and international crew procedures.

## Spaceflight overview

Across his three space missions, Hoshide participated in Space Shuttle and Soyuz/ISS expeditions. His flights are notable for spanning different eras of human spaceflight: the Shuttle program’s later years, long-duration stays aboard the ISS, and the post-Shuttle period in which Soyuz vehicles provided routine crew transport.

## EVA and station operations

Spacewalks (extravehicular activities, EVAs) are a major part of ISS maintenance and upgrade work, including installation of new hardware and repairs to external systems. Hoshide is credited with multiple EVAs and with operational responsibilities in areas such as robotics (the station’s robotic arm), internal station systems, and coordination with ground control teams.

## ISS command

During his 2021 mission, Hoshide served as commander of the International Space Station, a leadership role that includes responsibility for crew safety, daily operations, and integration across international partner agencies.

## Significance

Hoshide’s career is often cited in the context of Japan’s long-term participation in the ISS program and the development of a sustained human spaceflight capability through collaboration with NASA and other partners.
