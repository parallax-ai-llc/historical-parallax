## Summary

Former US Army Special Forces officer who spied for Russian intelligence for years.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2020 | Convicted of espionage for Russia |

## Background

- **Full name:** Peter Rafael Dzibinski Debbins (also reported with the middle name **Rafael**).
- **Origin:** Born and raised in Minnesota.
- **Education:** University of Minnesota (ROTC). Later earned a master's degree in Strategic and International Studies (Institute of World Politics).

## Military Service

- Served on active duty in the **U.S. Army** (1998–2005) and became a **Special Forces (Green Beret) officer**.
- Reported overseas assignments included **Korea, Germany, and Azerbaijan**.
- Continued in the **inactive reserve** after leaving active service (reported through 2010).

## Espionage Case (High-Level)

- Russian intelligence officers reportedly first approached him in the **mid-1990s**; he later met contacts in Russia and was assigned a codename reported as **"Ikar Lesnikov"**.
- U.S. authorities alleged he provided or agreed to provide **U.S. national defense information** to Russian intelligence over a period of years.
- Public reporting describes the case as involving both **classified information** and efforts to identify or suggest potential U.S. persons for recruitment.

## Arrest, Plea, and Sentence

- **Arrest:** August 2020 (reported at his home in Gainesville, Virginia).
- **Guilty plea:** November 2020, to one count of **conspiracy to commit espionage** (federal court in Alexandria, Virginia).
- **Sentence:** 188 months (15 years, 8 months) in federal prison (reported May 2021).
- **Incarceration:** Reported as held at **ADX Florence** (federal supermax) in Colorado.

## Why This Case Matters

- Illustrates the risk posed when personnel with access to sensitive military programs are **cultivated over long periods**.
- Often cited as an example of counterintelligence cases involving **Special Operations** backgrounds and foreign travel/contacts.
