## Summary

Moroccan-born terrorist who was one of three attackers in the 2017 London Bridge attack. They drove a van into pedestrians then stabbed people in Borough Market, killing 8 people. Shot dead by police within 8 minutes.

## Attack Overview

- The 2017 London Bridge and Borough Market attack involved a vehicle-ramming phase followed by knife attacks in crowded public areas.
- The incident was treated by UK authorities as a terrorism event and prompted an immediate armed response.
- Redouane was identified as one of three assailants; all were shot by police at the scene.

## Broader Context

- The attack occurred during a period of heightened concern in the UK about both ISIS-inspired violence and locally radicalized networks.
- Public reporting emphasized the challenge of monitoring large numbers of potential extremists, especially when perpetrators had limited prior profiles.

## Response and Aftermath

- Emergency services response, public safety messaging, and rapid neutralization became focal points in official reviews.
- The event contributed to ongoing debates about counterterrorism resources, intelligence sharing, and preventive interventions.
- Victim commemoration and community solidarity efforts were widely covered in the months following the attack.
