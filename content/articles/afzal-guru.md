## Background

Afzal Guru was from Jammu and Kashmir and became associated with militancy in the region before being arrested in connection with the December 2001 attack on the Parliament of India in New Delhi.

## Legal Process and Aftermath

- The case moved through India’s trial and appellate courts and became a long-running political and legal controversy.
- His execution in February 2013 was carried out at Tihar Jail, and news of the execution triggered protests and shutdowns in parts of Kashmir.
- Debates around the case often focus on standards of evidence, due process, and the broader conflict dynamics in Kashmir.

## Why He Remains Notable

Guru’s name is frequently cited in discussions of Indian counterterrorism policy, Kashmir politics, and the public impact of high-profile terrorism trials.
