## Summary

King of Belgium who privately owned the Congo Free State where an estimated 10 million Congolese people died through forced labor, mutilation, and murder during rubber extraction. His atrocities sparked one of the first international human rights campaigns.

## Background

Leopold II was the second King of the Belgians and is most controversially associated with the Congo Free State, a territory he controlled as a personal possession before it became a Belgian colony.

## Congo Free State and international response

- **Colonial governance:** Administration of the Congo Free State relied on coercive extraction of resources (notably rubber and ivory) and the use of armed force to compel labor.
- **Humanitarian campaign:** Missionaries, journalists, and activists helped bring abuses to international attention; the Congo Reform Association is often referenced as an early example of transnational human-rights advocacy.
- **Transfer to Belgium:** International and domestic pressure contributed to the annexation of the Congo by the Belgian state in 1908.

## Historical legacy

Leopold II’s memory remains contested in Belgium and internationally, with ongoing debates about commemoration, public monuments, and responsibility for colonial-era atrocities.
