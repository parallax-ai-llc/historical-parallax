## Summary

Former head of China's State Food and Drug Administration executed for accepting bribes to approve untested medicines. His corruption led to fake medicines that killed dozens of people.

## Background and Role at the SFDA

Zheng Xiaoyu was a senior Chinese official associated with the national regulatory system for pharmaceuticals and medical products. The State Food and Drug Administration (SFDA) was responsible for approving drugs and overseeing safety standards, a role that became more prominent as China’s domestic pharmaceutical market expanded and as Chinese manufacturers increased exports.

## Corruption Case and Trial

Investigations alleged that Zheng accepted bribes and favors in exchange for regulatory approvals. The case was framed by Chinese authorities as an example of high-level corruption affecting public health, and it drew broad attention because the alleged misconduct involved medicines that were not properly tested or reviewed.

Zheng was prosecuted, convicted, and sentenced to death. In China, the death penalty has periodically been applied to corruption cases that the state characterizes as exceptionally serious, particularly when linked to major social harm.

## Public-Health Impact and Aftermath

The scandal contributed to wider concern about counterfeit and substandard medicines and about weaknesses in enforcement capacity. It also became part of a broader narrative supporting regulatory tightening and institutional reforms. In subsequent years, Chinese regulators pursued reforms aimed at improving drug review processes, increasing oversight of manufacturers, and strengthening accountability within the approval system.
