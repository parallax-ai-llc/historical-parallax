## Summary

Knights Templar cartel leader known as 'El Tío' who was captured after the cartel's collapse under pressure from autodefensa vigilante groups.

## Background

Dionicio Loya Plancarte is commonly described as a senior member of the Knights Templar cartel (Caballeros Templarios), a criminal organization that operated primarily in the Mexican state of Michoacán and emerged from earlier cartel fragmentation.

The group became associated in reporting with:

- Control of regional trafficking routes and local economies.
- Extortion and coercive taxation of businesses and communities.
- Public messaging and a quasi-ideological narrative used to justify or normalize criminal rule.

## Michoacán Security Crisis and Autodefensas

During the early 2010s, parts of Michoacán experienced a severe security breakdown. Community self-defense groups (autodefensas) formed in some areas in response to cartel violence and extortion. Their rise, along with intensified government operations and internal cartel splits, contributed to the weakening of Knights Templar influence.

## Arrest and Legal Proceedings

Loya Plancarte's arrest by Mexican federal authorities in 2014 was reported as part of broader efforts to dismantle remaining leadership of the organization. High-profile arrests in this period often involved coordinated actions across federal security institutions and were followed by prosecutions under organized-crime statutes.

## Significance

The case is frequently referenced in discussions about how non-state armed actors can emerge during governance crises, and how the dismantling of cartel structures can involve complex interactions among federal forces, local communities, and rival criminal groups.
