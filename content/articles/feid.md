## Summary

Salomón Villada Hoyos, known professionally as Feid (pronounced "Fay-d"), is a Colombian singer, songwriter, and reggaeton artist born on August 19, 1992, in Medellín, Colombia. Rising from behind-the-scenes songwriter to one of Latin music's most prominent solo performers, Feid has become a defining voice of the modern urban Latino sound, blending reggaeton, R&B, and pop into a style he calls "merreggaetón."

His ascent over the past decade mirrors the broader globalization of Latin urban music, and his ability to craft infectious, melodic hooks has made him a sought-after collaborator across the genre. With hundreds of millions of streams across platforms, multiple chart-topping albums, and high-profile romances that keep him in the public eye, Feid stands as one of the most commercially successful and culturally resonant artists to emerge from Colombia's urban music scene.

## Early Life & Background

Born and raised in Medellín, Feid grew up immersed in music from an early age. He studied at Berklee College of Music in Boston, Massachusetts, where he honed his songwriting and production skills. This formal training distinguished him from many of his reggaeton peers and gave him a compositional depth that would later define his catalog. He returned to Colombia with both technical ability and a refined ear for melody, positioning himself initially as a behind-the-scenes talent rather than a frontman.

## Rise to Prominence

Feid's early career centered on songwriting for major Latin acts. He co-wrote hits for artists including J Balvin and Maluma, earning industry credibility before stepping into the spotlight himself. He signed with Interscope Records and released his debut studio album *Gangster Rude Boy Lovers* in 2019, which established his aesthetic: smooth vocal delivery, sensual themes, and polished production. His 2021 album *INTER SHIBUYA - LA MAFIA* and the 2022 follow-up *FELIZ CUMPLEAÑOS FERXXO TE PIRATEAMOS EL ÁLBUM* deepened his fanbase, with the latter reaching No. 1 on Billboard's Top Latin Albums chart. Collaborations with Bad Bunny, Karol G, J Balvin, and Myke Towers expanded his reach considerably throughout 2022 and 2023.

## Key Achievements

- His 2023 album *MORBO* debuted at No. 1 on Billboard's Top Latin Albums chart and spawned multiple Latin Airplay hits.
- "Classy 101" featuring J Balvin peaked in the top 10 of the Hot Latin Songs chart in 2023.
- Received multiple Billboard Latin Music Award nominations, including Hot Latin Song of the Year.
- Accumulated over 30 billion combined streams on Spotify by 2024, making him one of the platform's most-streamed Latin artists.
- His high-profile relationship with Karol G generated significant media coverage and collaborative momentum throughout 2023–2024.

## Controversies & Criticism

Feid has largely avoided major public controversies. Some critics have noted that his lyrical content, which frequently focuses on romance, sensuality, and luxury lifestyle, lacks social depth compared to peers who engage with political or social themes. His music has occasionally been grouped into broader critiques of reggaeton's repetitive commercial formulas, though supporters argue his melodic sophistication sets him apart from the genre's more formulaic output.

## Legacy & Influence

Feid represents a generation of Latin artists who blurred the line between songwriter and performer, bringing pop craftsmanship into reggaeton's rhythmic framework. His success helped cement Medellín's reputation as a global hub for urban Latin music, alongside peers like J Balvin and Maluma. His aesthetic sensibility — fashion-forward, melodically adventurous, and digitally native — has influenced a younger wave of Colombian and Latin urban artists emerging in the mid-2020s.
