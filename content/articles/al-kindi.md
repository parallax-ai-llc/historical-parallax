## Summary

Al-Kindi is known as the father of Islamic philosophy. He was the first to develop a systematic philosophy in the Arabic tradition and made contributions to mathematics (introducing Indian numerals to the Islamic world), optics, music theory, and cryptanalysis. His manuscript On Deciphering Cryptographic Messages founded the science of cryptanalysis.
