## Summary

Henri Nouwen was a Dutch Catholic priest and one of the most widely read spiritual writers of the 20th century. He taught at Notre Dame, Yale, and Harvard before joining the L'Arche Daybreak community in Toronto, living with people with intellectual disabilities. His books, including The Wounded Healer and The Return of the Prodigal Son, sold millions of copies worldwide. He wrote openly about his struggles with loneliness and the search for belonging.
