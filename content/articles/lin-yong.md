## Context

Allegations of forced organ harvesting in China have been raised by researchers, journalists, and human-rights advocates for many years. Because much of the underlying activity (e.g., detention, medical testing, transplant logistics, and hospital reporting) is not transparent to outside observers, many claims rely on a mix of witness testimony, statistical analysis, and investigative reporting.

Key terms that often appear in this context:

- **Falun Gong**: a spiritual practice whose adherents have faced persecution in China since 1999.
- **Transplant system**: hospitals, transplant registries, and organ allocation mechanisms.
- **Detention and screening**: accounts of detainees undergoing blood tests and medical exams consistent with organ-compatibility screening.

## China Tribunal (2019) — broader finding

In 2019, an independent people's tribunal in London (commonly referred to as the **China Tribunal**) concluded that:

- **Forced organ harvesting has occurred in China** on a significant scale.
- The practice was found to have been **committed for years**, and the tribunal stated it was **continuing** at the time of its judgment.
- The tribunal identified **Falun Gong practitioners** as a major source of organs in its findings.

The tribunal's finding is frequently referenced as a high-level, public, third-party assessment of the broader allegation set, even when individual-level accountability is not clearly documented in open sources.
