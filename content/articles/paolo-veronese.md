## Summary

Paolo Veronese was a major Venetian Renaissance painter known for large-scale feast scenes and brilliant use of color. His Feast in the House of Levi led to an Inquisition trial over its secular figures. Along with Titian and Tintoretto, he formed the great triumvirate of Venetian painting.
