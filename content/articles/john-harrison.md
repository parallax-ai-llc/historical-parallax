## Summary

John Harrison was a British clockmaker who invented the marine chronometer, solving the longitude problem that had plagued maritime navigation for centuries. His H4 sea watch was the first clock accurate enough to determine longitude at sea, transforming ocean navigation and saving countless lives. Despite opposition from the scientific establishment, his solution proved superior to astronomical methods.
