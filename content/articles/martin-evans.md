## Summary

Martin Evans is a British geneticist who shared the 2007 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his discoveries of principles for introducing specific gene modifications in mice using embryonic stem cells. He was the first to identify and culture embryonic stem cells from mice, making gene targeting possible. His work has been fundamental to understanding gene function and disease.
