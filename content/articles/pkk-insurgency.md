## Summary

Armed conflict between the Kurdistan Workers Party and the Turkish state since 1984. The PKK seeks Kurdish rights and autonomy in a conflict that has killed over 40,000 people.

## Background and Context

- The conflict is rooted in longstanding disputes over Kurdish identity, political rights, and governance within Turkey.
- The PKK (Partiya Karkerên Kurdistanê) emerged in the late 1970s and developed a militant campaign that shifted in intensity across decades.

## Major Phases (High-Level)

- **1980s–1990s:** Initial expansion of insurgent operations and large-scale security responses.
- **1999–mid-2000s:** Periodic ceasefires and organizational changes following the capture of PKK leader Abdullah Öcalan.
- **2010s:** Renewed escalation amid regional instability and breakdowns in peace efforts.

## Humanitarian and Political Impact

- The fighting has driven displacement, affected civilian life in Turkey’s southeast, and shaped domestic politics and security policy.
- The conflict also intersects with broader Kurdish movements and regional dynamics in Iraq and Syria.
