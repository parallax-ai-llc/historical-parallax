## Summary

Turkmen president who was installed by his father in 2022, continuing a dynastic autocracy. He won a sham election with 73% of the vote. Turkmenistan remains one of the world's most repressive and closed countries.

## Background and rise

Serdar Berdimuhamedow is the son of Gurbanguly Berdimuhamedow, who led Turkmenistan from 2006 until the 2022 transition. Prior to becoming president, Serdar held a sequence of positions across Turkmen state institutions that observers often interpret as building administrative credentials and public visibility ahead of succession.

## 2022 transition and election context

In 2022, Serdar became president following an election that many international observers and press accounts described as tightly controlled, with limited political competition and restricted media. The transition is widely characterized as a dynastic handover within an authoritarian political system.

## Governance themes (high level)

Public reporting about Turkmenistan during Serdar’s tenure has emphasized continuity in:

- **State control of politics and media** and limited space for opposition.
- **Personality-centered symbolism** in state messaging, though styles can vary by period.
- **Economic constraints** tied to energy exports, foreign currency availability, and state planning.

## Foreign policy and international posture

Turkmenistan maintains a policy described as “permanent neutrality,” while engaging pragmatically with neighbors and major powers on energy, transit, and security issues. Much of the country’s external visibility comes through natural-gas diplomacy, infrastructure projects, and carefully managed international appearances.

## Notes

Assessments of elections, rights conditions, and governance frequently rely on NGO and press reporting because independent domestic reporting is limited.
