## Summary

Sarita Devi was a multiple-time Asian and World Championship boxing medalist and one of India's pioneering female boxers.

## Background

Sarita Devi is associated with the development of women’s boxing in India during the era when the sport was still gaining structured support, training pathways, and consistent international exposure.

## Notable Moments

- She is widely noted for her participation at major multi-sport events and continental championships, where Indian women’s boxing began to emerge as a competitive program.
- At the 2014 Asian Games, she became the center of a high-profile dispute regarding officiating and medal allocation, which drew international attention to judging controversies in amateur boxing.

## Style and Weight Classes

As an amateur boxer, her record is most often discussed in terms of weight-class competition and tournament performance rather than professional fight statistics.

## Legacy

In Indian sports coverage, Sarita Devi is frequently cited alongside other early medal-winning women boxers as part of the cohort that helped normalize women’s participation in boxing and expanded the sport’s visibility for younger athletes.

## Why She Matters

Her career is often used as a reference point for how Indian boxing institutions, selection policies, and international tournament structures affected athletes in the pre-professionalized era of the women’s game.

This framing appears in both sports reporting and retrospective discussions of Indian women’s boxing.
