## Summary

Osamu Shimomura discovered green fluorescent protein (GFP) in the jellyfish Aequorea victoria in 1962, a discovery that would revolutionize biology. GFP became the most widely used tool for visualizing proteins and cellular processes in living organisms. He shared the 2008 Nobel Prize in Chemistry. He spent decades studying bioluminescence in marine organisms.
