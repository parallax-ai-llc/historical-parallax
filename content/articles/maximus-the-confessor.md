## Summary

Maximus the Confessor was a Byzantine monk and theologian who played a crucial role in defending orthodox Christology against Monothelitism. He articulated the doctrine that Christ had two wills, divine and human. He was tortured and mutilated for his theological convictions. His mystical and philosophical writings deeply influenced Eastern Orthodox theology.
