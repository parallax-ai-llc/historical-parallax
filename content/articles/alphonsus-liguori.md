## Summary

Alphonsus Liguori was an Italian Catholic bishop, theologian, and Doctor of the Church who founded the Congregation of the Most Holy Redeemer (Redemptorists). He was one of the most widely read Catholic authors, writing over 100 works on theology, morals, and spirituality. His moral theology, which took a moderate position between rigorism and laxism, became the standard for Catholic confession practice. He was a noted musician and composer of hymns.
