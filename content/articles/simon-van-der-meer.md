## Summary

Simon van der Meer was a Dutch particle accelerator physicist who shared the 1984 Nobel Prize in Physics for contributions to the discovery of the W and Z bosons. He invented stochastic cooling, a technique that made it possible to produce intense antiproton beams at CERN, enabling the discovery of the W and Z bosons.
