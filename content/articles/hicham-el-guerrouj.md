## Summary

Hicham El Guerrouj holds world records in the 1,500m (3:26.00) and mile (3:43.13). He won 1,500m and 5,000m at 2004 Athens.

## Background

El Guerrouj emerged from Morocco’s strong middle-distance tradition and became a defining figure of the late-1990s and early-2000s global circuit. His racing style emphasized sustained speed and a powerful finishing kick.

## Competitive Highlights

- Multiple-time world champion at 1,500m and a consistent medal contender across major championships.
- Achieved the rare Olympic double of 1,500m and 5,000m in 2004, reinforcing his versatility beyond a single event.

## Records and Training Legacy

His 1,500m and mile marks are often cited as benchmarks for aerobic capacity and pace control in middle-distance running. Coaches frequently reference his split patterns and tactical positioning as case studies in how to manage championship racing versus record-attempt pacing.

## Impact

El Guerrouj’s accomplishments helped raise the international profile of Moroccan athletics and inspired later generations of runners in North Africa and beyond. He remains a frequent point of comparison in discussions about all-time great 1,500m specialists.
