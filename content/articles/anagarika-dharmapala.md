## Summary

Anagarika Dharmapala was a Sri Lankan Buddhist revivalist who played a major role in the revival of Buddhism in South Asia and its spread to the West. He founded the Maha Bodhi Society to restore Buddhist holy sites in India, particularly Bodh Gaya. He represented Theravada Buddhism at the 1893 World Parliament of Religions in Chicago. He was one of the first modern Buddhist missionaries to bring Buddhism to the West.
