## Summary

Genocide of the Herero and Nama peoples by German colonial forces in present-day Namibia from 1904-1908. An estimated 65,000 Herero and 10,000 Nama were killed, the first genocide of the 20th century.

## Additional Context

- The events unfolded in **German South West Africa** (present-day Namibia) amid colonial land seizures, labor coercion, and armed resistance.
- Historical discussions often note the use of expulsion into harsh environments, confinement, forced labor, and camp systems as mechanisms of mass death.
- The genocide is frequently referenced in scholarship and public history as an early 20th-century case that foreshadowed later patterns of colonial and state violence.

## Why It Matters

- **Colonial history:** a key case in understanding how colonial rule could escalate into systematic mass killing.
- **Memory and reparations:** contemporary politics includes recognition, apologies, and debates over compensation and historical responsibility.
- **Documented policy:** surviving orders, reports, and records are central to how historians reconstruct intent and implementation.
