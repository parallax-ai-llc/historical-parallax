## Summary

Peruvian president who was removed from office and arrested after attempting to dissolve Congress in December 2022. He was a rural teacher with no political experience who won as a populist. Charged with corruption and rebellion.

## Additional Context

### Early life and rise

Castillo worked as a rural schoolteacher and union activist before entering national politics. He became a prominent figure during teacher strikes in Peru, and later ran for president as an outsider candidate with support concentrated in rural regions.

### Presidency (2021–2022)

As president, Castillo faced persistent political instability, including repeated cabinet changes and conflict with Peru’s Congress. Media coverage and official investigations also focused on allegations of corruption involving his associates and administration.

### December 2022 constitutional crisis

In December 2022, Castillo announced measures widely described as an attempted “self-coup,” including an effort to dissolve Congress and rule by decree. Congress responded by impeaching and removing him, after which he was detained and placed under investigation.

### Legal proceedings and public debate

Public reporting describes ongoing legal cases and pretrial detention related to charges such as rebellion and corruption. The events are frequently cited in analyses of Peru’s chronic executive–legislative conflict and the country’s broader pattern of presidential removals and prosecutions.
