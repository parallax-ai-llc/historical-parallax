## Summary

Kim Whan-ki was Korea's most important abstract painter whose work explored the intersection of Korean traditional aesthetics and modern abstraction. While not a scientist, his systematic study of color, light, and spatial perception demonstrated a scientific approach to art that influenced Korean visual culture and design.
