## Summary

Whitfield Diffie is an American cryptographer who co-invented public-key cryptography with Martin Hellman, revolutionizing information security. Their 1976 paper 'New Directions in Cryptography' introduced the concept of public-key exchange, which underpins modern internet security including HTTPS, digital signatures, and secure communications. He received the Turing Award in 2015.
