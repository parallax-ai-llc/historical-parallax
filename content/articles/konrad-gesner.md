## Summary

Konrad Gesner was a Swiss naturalist and physician who is considered the father of modern zoology and bibliography. His five-volume 'Historiae Animalium' was the most comprehensive zoological work since antiquity. He also compiled the 'Bibliotheca Universalis,' the first modern bibliography, listing all known books in Latin, Greek, and Hebrew.
