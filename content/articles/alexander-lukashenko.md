## Summary

Belarusian president since 1994, often called 'Europe's last dictator.' He has rigged elections, violently suppressed protests, and imprisoned thousands of political opponents. He aided Russia's 2022 invasion of Ukraine.

## Political System and Governance

Lukashenko’s long tenure is commonly characterized by:

- concentration of executive authority around the presidency
- weak checks and balances in parliament and courts
- heavy reliance on state security services and police powers
- state influence over major media outlets and the economy

International monitoring bodies and human-rights organizations have repeatedly criticized Belarus under Lukashenko for restrictions on civil liberties and political competition.

## Elections and Protest Cycles

Belarus has experienced multiple waves of protest during Lukashenko’s rule. The **2020 presidential election** and the subsequent mass demonstrations became a defining event, with widespread reporting of:

- disputed vote totals and claims of fraud
- large-scale detentions and allegations of abuse in custody
- the forced exile or imprisonment of prominent opposition figures

## Foreign Policy Orientation

Belarus under Lukashenko has balanced relationships between Russia and the West, but is widely viewed as economically and security-dependent on Russia.

Key themes include:

- participation in Russia-led regional structures and the **Union State** project
- periodic tensions with Moscow over energy pricing and sovereignty concerns
- deteriorating relations with the EU and the United States amid sanctions

## Role in the Russia–Ukraine War

In 2022, Belarus permitted Russian forces to use Belarusian territory as part of the invasion of Ukraine. This deepened Belarus’s international isolation and increased scrutiny of Minsk’s security alignment with Moscow.

## International Sanctions and Isolation

Sanctions by Western governments have targeted Belarusian officials, state-linked entities, and sectors of the economy. The stated rationales typically cite election-related repression, human-rights abuses, and actions affecting international aviation and regional security.

## Significance

Lukashenko’s rule is frequently discussed as a modern example of durable authoritarian governance in Europe, and as a case where domestic political survival is closely tied to external patronage, security apparatus loyalty, and control of information.
