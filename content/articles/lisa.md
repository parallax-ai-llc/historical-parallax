## Summary

LiSA (born Orikasa Ri on June 24, 1987) is a Japanese singer-songwriter from Seki, Gifu Prefecture, whose powerful vocal style and energetic performances have made her one of the most prominent figures in the anime music industry. Rising to fame through her association with the *Angel Beats!* franchise in 2010, she built a dedicated fanbase through relentless touring and emotionally charged rock anthems that blur the line between J-pop and J-rock.

Her 2020 single "Gurenge," the opening theme for the globally successful anime *Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba*, became a cultural phenomenon, catapulting her from anime music specialist to mainstream superstar both in Japan and internationally. The song's success marked a turning point not only for LiSA but for anime tie-in music as a commercial force worldwide.

LiSA is widely credited with demonstrating that anime theme songs can achieve mainstream chart dominance without compromising artistic integrity. Her career has helped legitimize anime music as a serious genre, influencing a generation of artists who followed her template of pairing raw vocal power with high-production rock instrumentation.

## Early Life & Background

LiSA was born on June 24, 1987, in Seki, Gifu Prefecture, Japan. She developed an interest in music at a young age and relocated to Osaka to pursue a career in the entertainment industry. Prior to her breakthrough, she performed as a support vocalist and struggled to find her footing in Japan's competitive music scene. Her early years were marked by persistent effort and a series of small live performances that sharpened her stage presence.

## Rise to Prominence

LiSA's career accelerated in 2010 when she voiced Yui, a character in the anime *Angel Beats!*, and contributed vocals to the in-universe band Girls Dead Monster. The exposure led to her solo debut under Aniplex in 2011 with the EP *Letters to U*. She released her first full studio album, *Letters to U*, later that year, establishing her signature blend of pop melodies and rock energy.

Throughout the 2010s, she released a consistent string of albums and anime tie-in singles, including themes for *Sword Art Online* ("crossing field," 2012) and *Fate/Zero*. Each release expanded her audience, and her live performances—known for their intensity and crowd interaction—built a fiercely loyal following. She signed with Sacra Music (Sony Music) in 2015, giving her wider distribution and production resources.

## Key Achievements

- **"Gurenge" (2020):** The *Demon Slayer* opening theme peaked at No. 1 on the Billboard Japan Hot 100 and became one of the best-selling singles in Japan in decades. It was certified Diamond by the Recording Industry Association of Japan (RIAJ).
- **"Homura" (2020):** The theme for *Demon Slayer: Mugen Train* won the 63rd Japan Record Award (2021), one of the most prestigious music honors in Japan.
- **NHK Kōhaku Uta Gassen:** LiSA performed at the iconic New Year's Eve broadcast in 2020 and 2021, a milestone typically reserved for Japan's most commercially successful artists.
- Her 2020 album *LEO-NiNE* debuted at No. 1 on the Oricon chart.

## Controversies & Criticism

In 2021, LiSA's husband, anime director Takeuchi Tomohisa, was reported to have had an extramarital affair, which generated significant media attention in Japan. LiSA addressed the situation publicly, expressing her intention to continue her musical career. The episode drew scrutiny of celebrity private lives but did not substantially derail her professional momentum. Some critics have also noted that her work is heavily concentrated in the anime tie-in sector, occasionally framing her mainstream crossover as dependent on franchise success rather than standalone artistry—a characterization her broader discography largely refutes.

## Legacy & Influence

LiSA's career arc has redefined expectations for anime-adjacent musicians in Japan. "Gurenge" demonstrated that an anime opening theme could rival any mainstream pop release in commercial reach, paving the way for increased investment in anime soundtrack talent globally. She is frequently cited by younger J-rock and anime music artists as a direct influence. Her success contributed to the broader international explosion of interest in Japanese music during the early 2020s, coinciding with the global expansion of anime streaming platforms.
