## Summary

László Papp was the first boxer to win three consecutive Olympic golds (1948-1956).

## Career Timeline

| 1948 | Won gold at London |

## Additional Context

### Olympic achievement

- Winning three consecutive Olympic boxing gold medals is exceptionally rare due to changes in weight classes, competition cycles, and the difficulty of sustaining peak form.
- Papp's medals (1948, 1952, 1956) made him a landmark figure in amateur boxing history.

### Boxing in the Cold War sports system

- Papp competed in an era when Eastern Bloc countries invested heavily in Olympic sport as a demonstration of national prestige.
- Amateur boxing results were closely followed, and elite athletes often trained within state-supported structures.

### Professional career and legacy

- Papp also pursued professional boxing and is remembered as one of Hungary's most famous fighters.
- His legacy is frequently discussed in Hungary as part of the country's mid-20th-century sporting golden age.

### Why he remains notable

- A durable example of sustained excellence across multiple Olympic cycles.
- Often referenced when comparing amateur boxing eras and the relationship between amateur and professional pathways.
