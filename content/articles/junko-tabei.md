## Summary

Junko Tabei was the first woman to reach the summit of Mount Everest in 1975 and the first to complete the Seven Summits. She inspired generations of female climbers.

## Additional Context

### Early Life and Entry into Climbing

Tabei (née Ishibashi) was born in **Miharu, Fukushima** and began climbing as a child. Biographical accounts often emphasize that she was not initially viewed as an obvious candidate for elite mountaineering, but she continued in the sport because she valued its non-competitive character and the experience of natural landscapes.

### Building Women’s Climbing Networks

In 1969 she founded the **Joshi-Tohan Club** (Women’s Mountaineering Club), a women-only group formed in response to barriers within a male-dominated climbing culture. The club became a platform for women-led expeditions and is frequently cited as part of her broader legacy beyond summit achievements.

### Everest and the Seven Summits

Her 1975 ascent of **Mount Everest** marked a widely recognized milestone for women in high-altitude mountaineering. In 1992 she became the first woman documented to complete the **Seven Summits**, climbing the highest peak on each continent.

### Writing, Education, and Environmental Work

In later years she worked as an **author and teacher** and became known for organizing **environmental clean-up efforts** connected to climbing routes, including attention to waste left on Everest. She also led annual climbs of **Mount Fuji** associated with youth groups, including programs connected to recovery and resilience after major disasters in Japan.

### Recognition

Her name has been commemorated in scientific and geographic naming (for example, an asteroid and later a named feature on Pluto), reflecting the international reach of her story.
