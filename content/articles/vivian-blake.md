## Background

Vivian Blake was a Jamaican criminal figure associated with the Shower Posse, a gang originating in Kingston, Jamaica.
He moved to New York City in the 1970s and became involved in drug distribution, later expanding operations beyond a single neighborhood.

## Criminal activities and organization

The Shower Posse has been described in law-enforcement and media accounts as a transnational criminal organization involved in:

- cocaine and marijuana trafficking
- weapons smuggling
- intimidation and targeted violence tied to the protection of drug markets

Blake is commonly identified as a founder or key organizer of the group’s American operations.

## Prosecution and legal outcomes

Public reporting describes Blake as facing serious U.S. charges connected to narcotics trafficking and racketeering.
He was extradited to the United States in the late 1990s, later pleading guilty to racketeering-related offenses, and received a lengthy prison sentence.

After serving part of that sentence, he was released and returned to Jamaica.

## Death

Blake died in Jamaica in 2010.
Contemporary reports cited health complications and hospitalization shortly before his death.

## Legacy and significance

Blake’s case is often referenced in discussions of:

- the growth of Caribbean-linked drug networks in the United States during the 1970s–1990s
- how gang structures adapt across borders through migration and distribution networks
- the use of racketeering statutes to prosecute large criminal enterprises
