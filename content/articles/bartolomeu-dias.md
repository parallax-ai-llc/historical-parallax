## Summary

Bartolomeu Dias was the first European to sail around the southern tip of Africa in 1488, opening the sea route to the Indian Ocean.

## Historical Context

In the late 15th century, Portugal invested heavily in maritime exploration to reach Asian markets by sea. Finding a route around Africa was strategically important for trade in spices and other goods, and it also carried geopolitical significance in competition with other European powers.

## Voyage and the Cape

Dias’s expedition (1487–1488) reached the southern tip of Africa and proved that the Atlantic and Indian Oceans were connected. The cape was later named the **Cape of Good Hope**, reflecting the prospect of a sea route to India.

## Why the Voyage Mattered

- **Navigation milestone:** Rounding the cape required advanced seamanship, including coping with strong winds and currents in southern waters.
- **Foundation for later expeditions:** Dias’s route informed subsequent Portuguese voyages, including Vasco da Gama’s journey to India.
- **Expansion of European maritime knowledge:** The voyage improved European understanding of African coastlines and oceanic routes.

## Later Life

Dias later sailed with the 1500 Portuguese fleet led by Pedro Álvares Cabral. He died during that voyage in a storm near the Cape.
