## Summary

Paraguayan drug lord who controlled vast marijuana plantations and cocaine trafficking routes. He operated his criminal empire from prison.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

| 2005 | Built drug empire controlling marijuana and cocaine traffic |

## Criminal Operations (High-Level)

Accounts of Pavão typically focus on Paraguay's role as a major marijuana producer and as a corridor for cocaine trafficking in South America:

- **Marijuana production**: described as controlling large-scale cultivation and distribution networks.
- **Transit routes**: linked to moving illicit drugs across porous border regions, where geography and weak enforcement can enable smuggling.

## Prison Power and Corruption Risk

A recurring element in reporting is the idea that parts of the operation continued while he was incarcerated:

- use of visitors, intermediaries, and communications to manage associates,
- alleged bribery and intimidation within the prison system,
- the broader problem of "prison-based" criminal governance seen in parts of Latin America.

## Law Enforcement and Prosecution

- His case has been cited in discussions of Paraguay's efforts to dismantle trafficking organizations and reduce corruption.
- Convictions and transfers between facilities are often described as attempts to disrupt command-and-control capacity.

## Significance

Pavão's story is frequently used to illustrate how organized crime can survive through networks, patronage, and institutional weakness rather than a single physical headquarters.
