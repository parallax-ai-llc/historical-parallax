## Summary

Elizabeth of Hungary was a Hungarian princess who became a Franciscan tertiary and devoted her life to serving the poor and sick. Widowed at 20, she founded a hospital and personally cared for the sick. She died at only 24, having given away her wealth and lived in voluntary poverty. She was canonized just four years after her death. She is the patron saint of bakers, beggars, and charitable organizations.
