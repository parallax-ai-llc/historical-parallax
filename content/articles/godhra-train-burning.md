## Background

The Godhra train burning occurred on 27 February 2002, when a coach of the Sabarmati Express carrying Hindu pilgrims was set on fire near Godhra in Gujarat, India. The incident became a flashpoint in already tense communal relations.

## Immediate aftermath

The event was followed by widespread violence in Gujarat.

Key themes in summaries of the aftermath include:

- rapid escalation from a localized incident to statewide riots
- large-scale loss of life and displacement
- intense political controversy and long-running legal proceedings

## Investigations and trials

Multiple investigations and court cases followed over many years. Public discussion has included disputes over the precise sequence of events and questions about planning versus spontaneous violence.

## Why it matters historically

- **Trigger event**: it is often presented as the immediate trigger for the 2002 Gujarat riots.
- **Institutional trust**: the incident and the response became central to debates about policing, governance, and accountability.
- **Communal violence studies**: it is widely cited in research and reporting on Hindu–Muslim communal conflict in contemporary India.
