## Summary

Yiliang "Peter" Peng, known professionally as Doublelift, is one of the most decorated and iconic League of Legends players in North American history. Competing as a bot lane AD carry, he won a record nine LCS (League Championship Series) titles across three different organizations, making him the most successful domestic player in the region's history.

Beyond his competitive achievements, Doublelift became a defining personality of North American League of Legends. His brash confidence, mechanical skill, and willingness to speak his mind made him one of the scene's most compelling figures. His career arc — from a teenager kicked out of his home to the winningest player in LCS history — is one of esports' most remarkable stories.

## Early Life & Background

Doublelift was born on July 19, 1993, in Mission Viejo, California, to Chinese immigrant parents. He grew up in a strict household where gaming was discouraged. After committing to pursuing a professional gaming career, he was kicked out of his family home at age 18. He was taken in by Travis Gafford, an esports journalist, who provided him a place to stay during his early competitive days. This difficult beginning became a foundational part of his public narrative and motivated his drive to succeed.

## Rise to Prominence

Doublelift first gained attention in the competitive scene playing for Counter Logic Gaming (CLG), which he joined in 2011. He quickly established himself as one of North America's best mechanical players, known for his aggressive laning and teamfight positioning. Despite individual talent, team success eluded him for years, and he became associated with the "forever second place" narrative.

His breakthrough came in 2015 when CLG won the NA LCS Summer Split — the organization's first title in years. Shortly after, Doublelift was controversially released from CLG and joined Team SoloMid (TSM), where he won four consecutive LCS titles between 2016 and 2017. He took a brief competitive break in Spring 2017 before returning to continue his dominant run.

In 2020, he joined Team Liquid and later returned to TSM, adding more titles to his collection. He retired from professional play in November 2020, finishing with nine LCS championships.

## Key Achievements

- **Nine NA LCS/LCS titles** — the most by any player in the league's history, earned across CLG, TSM, and Team Liquid
- **Multiple Worlds appearances** — represented North America at the League of Legends World Championship in 2011, 2015, 2016, 2017, 2018, 2019, and 2020
- **First Blood King** — became statistically renowned for his laning dominance and early-game aggression
- **2015 NA LCS Summer MVP candidate** — recognized as one of the top-performing players during CLG's breakthrough split
- **All-Pro selections** — named to multiple All-Pro teams throughout his career

## Controversies & Criticism

Doublelift's career was not without controversy. His outspoken personality led to frequent trash talk, which occasionally drew backlash from fans and fellow players. His departure from CLG in 2015 was marked by public disputes about team dynamics and communication issues.

In April 2018, Doublelift's family suffered an unimaginable tragedy when his brother fatally stabbed their mother and seriously injured their father. The incident drew widespread attention and sympathy from the esports community. Doublelift returned to competition shortly afterward, citing the support of teammates and fans.

He also faced criticism for his international performances, as North American teams consistently struggled at Worlds despite domestic dominance. Critics pointed to repeated group stage exits as a stain on an otherwise stellar career.

## Legacy & Influence

Doublelift is widely regarded as the greatest North American League of Legends player of all time. His nine titles set a standard that may never be surpassed, and his longevity across multiple rosters demonstrated rare adaptability. After retirement, he transitioned to streaming and content creation, remaining a prominent voice in the League of Legends community. His story — from being homeless as a teenager to becoming the most winning player in his region — continues to inspire aspiring esports professionals.
