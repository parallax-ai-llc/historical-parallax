## Summary

British serial killer who murdered three men and stabbed two others in a 10-day killing spree in 2013. She is one of only three women in England given a whole life order. She told police she killed for 'fun.'

## Overview of the 2013 Attacks

Dennehy's known victims were adult men killed over a short period in March 2013 in the Peterborough area. The case drew attention because of the speed of the attacks, the involvement of associates, and the subsequent efforts to locate additional victims.

## Investigation and Arrest

Police operations focused on missing-person reports, forensic work at disposal sites, and tracing movements and communications of Dennehy and those connected to her. She was arrested after a sequence of events that included further violence and a multi-force response.

## Trial and Sentencing

In 2014, Dennehy was convicted of multiple murders and attempted murders and received a whole life order, meaning she would not be eligible for release. Her case is frequently cited in discussions of the rarity of whole life orders imposed on women in England and Wales.

## Legal and Public Context

The Dennehy case has been used in commentary about sentencing policy, the management of violent offenders, and the ways in which short, intense crime sprees differ from longer-term patterns of serial offending.
