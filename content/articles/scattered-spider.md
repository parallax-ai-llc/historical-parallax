## Summary

Hacking group of young English-speaking cybercriminals responsible for high-profile attacks on MGM Resorts and Caesars Entertainment in 2023 using social engineering techniques.

## Overview

“Scattered Spider” is a label used by security researchers and journalists for a loose set of mostly young, English-speaking threat actors associated with social-engineering heavy intrusions into large organizations.

## Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs)

Coverage of the group commonly highlights:

- Impersonation of employees or contractors to persuade IT help desks to reset passwords or multi-factor authentication.
- Credential theft and account takeovers aimed at identity and access management systems.
- Rapid lateral movement once privileged access is obtained, sometimes followed by data theft and extortion.

Some reporting links Scattered Spider–attributed intrusions to the broader ransomware ecosystem (for example, working with or enabling ransomware deployment), though attribution and group membership boundaries can be fluid.

## Why the 2023 Incidents Stood Out

The MGM Resorts and Caesars incidents drew attention because they affected consumer-facing operations and demonstrated how human-centered attack paths can bypass otherwise strong technical controls.

## Defensive Lessons

Organizations frequently cite the case when emphasizing strict identity verification for help-desk workflows, phishing-resistant MFA, privileged-access monitoring, and rapid containment playbooks for identity compromise.
