## Summary

Pacific Gas & Electric was convicted of 84 counts of involuntary manslaughter for the Camp Fire. The utility's neglected equipment has caused multiple deadly wildfires in California.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The article refers to PG&E's criminal convictions related to the 2018 Camp Fire in Butte County, California.
- Prosecutors alleged that PG&E's failures around inspection, maintenance, and operation of electrical equipment contributed to ignition conditions.
- The 2020 criminal case focused on involuntary manslaughter counts tied to fatalities from the Camp Fire.

## Camp Fire Context

- The Camp Fire began in November 2018 and became one of the most destructive wildfires in California history.
- The fire destroyed large portions of the town of Paradise and surrounding areas.
- Investigations and litigation around utility equipment and wildfire risk became a major theme in California energy regulation following the event.

## Criminal Case: Key Points

- **Charges**: Involuntary manslaughter (84 counts) and a related unlawful-fire charge are commonly cited in summaries of the case.
- **Plea/Outcome (2020)**: PG&E pleaded guilty to the involuntary manslaughter counts in connection with the Camp Fire fatalities.
- **Scope**: This criminal matter is distinct from civil lawsuits and bankruptcy proceedings, though they occurred in the same general period.

## Consequences and Aftermath

- Criminal convictions can include court supervision, fines, probation-like conditions, and mandated compliance measures.
- In parallel, PG&E faced extensive civil claims from wildfire victims and broader scrutiny from California regulators.
- The case contributed to ongoing policy debates about investor-owned utilities, grid hardening, and wildfire mitigation obligations.

## Related Topics

- **Wildfire mitigation**: vegetation management, equipment inspection cycles, and targeted power shutoffs during extreme fire weather.
- **Grid safety and aging infrastructure**: modernization programs and risk-based maintenance strategies.
- **Corporate accountability**: how prosecutors apply criminal law to corporate entities after public safety disasters.

## Notes on Terminology

- **Involuntary manslaughter** (general sense): a homicide offense based on negligence or unlawful conduct short of intent to kill.
- **Corporate criminal liability**: corporations can be convicted of crimes in many U.S. jurisdictions, typically through actions/omissions attributed to the organization.
