## Summary

Park Se-ri won the LPGA Championship and US Women's Open in her rookie year (1998), inspiring an entire generation of Korean female golfers. She won 25 LPGA titles.

## Why She Matters

Park Se-ri is widely credited with accelerating the globalization of women’s professional golf and with inspiring a surge of elite South Korean golfers on the LPGA Tour. Her early success became a cultural reference point in Korea for what was possible on the world stage.

## Career Significance (High-Level)

- **Immediate major wins**: Winning major championships early in an LPGA career is rare and instantly changes an athlete’s status and expectations.
- **Iconic moments**: The widely remembered hazard shot from the 1998 U.S. Women’s Open became part of golf highlight culture and reinforced her reputation for composure.
- **Sustained excellence**: Multiple seasons of wins and high finishes indicate not only peak ability but also long-term adaptability.

## Broader Impact

- Her success is often discussed as a catalyst for increased junior participation, sponsorship, and institutional support for golf in South Korea.
- On the LPGA, her presence helped broaden the tour’s audience and contributed to a more international competitive field.
- She continues to be referenced as a benchmark when evaluating later Korean golfers’ careers and influence.
