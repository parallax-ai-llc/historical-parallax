## Summary

South African serial killer known as the 'West End Serial Killer' who murdered at least 16 women in Johannesburg between 2008 and 2009. He raped and strangled victims. Sentenced to 16 life terms plus 256 years.

## Context and Pattern

Reports about Mogale describe a pattern of targeting women in and around Johannesburg during 2008–2009. The killings were linked by similarities in sexual assault and strangulation, and the case received significant attention in South Africa due to the number of victims.

## Investigation and Prosecution

Police connected multiple assaults and murders through witness statements and investigative work in the West End area. After his arrest in 2009, prosecutors pursued multiple counts to reflect the scale of the crimes.

In 2013, a South African court convicted him on a large set of charges connected to the attacks. The sentence—multiple life terms plus additional years—was designed to ensure he would remain incarcerated.

## Public Impact

The case is often cited in discussions about violence against women and the challenges of policing serial offenses in large urban areas. It also illustrates how multi-count prosecutions can be used to account for numerous victims within a single high-profile case.
