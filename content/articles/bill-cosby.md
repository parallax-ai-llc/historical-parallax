## Summary

American comedian convicted of drugging and sexually assaulting Andrea Constand. Over 60 women accused him of sexual assault spanning decades. His conviction was later overturned on a technicality.

## Background

- Cosby rose to national prominence as a stand-up comedian and television performer in the 1960s and 1970s.
- He became one of the most influential figures in American television through roles as an actor, producer, and spokesperson.

## Criminal Case and Appeals (High-Level)

- The criminal case that led to conviction centered on allegations by Andrea Constand.
- In 2018, he was convicted in Pennsylvania state court and sentenced to a term of imprisonment.
- In 2021, the Supreme Court of Pennsylvania vacated the conviction and ordered his release, citing due-process concerns tied to reliance on prior prosecutorial representations.

## Public Allegations and Civil Litigation

- Numerous women publicly accused Cosby of sexual assault or misconduct across multiple decades.
- Several civil claims were brought, and the allegations reshaped public understanding of his career and cultural standing.

## Legacy and Cultural Impact

- Cosby’s case is frequently discussed in relation to celebrity accountability, prosecutorial discretion, and the interaction between criminal and civil processes.
- It also contributed to wider conversations about believing accusers and how institutions respond to allegations of sexual violence.
