## Summary

Félix Lengyel, known online as xQc (formerly xQcOW), is a Canadian streamer and content creator born on November 12, 1995, in Laval, Quebec. Rising from competitive Overwatch play to become one of the most-watched streamers on Twitch, he has built a reputation for high-energy, unfiltered entertainment across a wide range of content including gaming, react streams, and gambling. His raw, unscripted personality has earned him a devoted global audience.

At his peak, xQc routinely ranked among the top streamers on Twitch by viewership, regularly drawing tens of thousands of concurrent viewers and accumulating billions of stream minutes watched. In July 2023, he signed a reported $100 million non-exclusive deal with Kick, a competing streaming platform, while maintaining a presence on Twitch — one of the largest streamer contracts publicly disclosed at the time.

His influence extends beyond entertainment metrics. xQc has shaped streaming culture through his improvisational style, his participation in high-profile online events, and his willingness to engage directly and candidly with his audience and peers, often blurring the line between streamer and cultural commentator.

## Early Life & Background

Félix Lengyel was born on November 12, 1995, in Laval, Quebec, Canada. He grew up in a French-speaking household and developed an early interest in video games. He began playing Overwatch competitively after the game's 2016 launch, quickly climbing the ranks to reach the top tiers of competitive play as a tank player, specializing in the hero Winston.

## Rise to Prominence

xQc's competitive Overwatch career launched his public profile. He played for the Dallas Fuel in the inaugural Overwatch League (OWL) season in 2018, but was released following multiple suspensions for unsportsmanlike behavior, including the use of homophobic slurs and stream sniping. Despite the turbulent end to his esports career, his Twitch stream continued to grow rapidly.

Transitioning fully to full-time streaming, xQc became known for marathon sessions — sometimes streaming 12 or more hours daily — and a chaotic, reactive style. He participated in high-profile collaborative events including multiple Twitch Rivals tournaments and various "GTA RP" (NoPixel) streams, the latter of which drew enormous audiences in 2021. His involvement in the Ludwig "Subathon" era and other platform-wide moments helped cement his status as a central figure in streaming culture.

## Key Achievements

- Reached rank #1 on Twitch by viewership on multiple occasions
- Signed a reported $100 million deal with Kick in July 2023
- Accumulated over 11 million Twitch followers and billions of minutes watched
- Competed in the Overwatch League's inaugural 2018 season with the Dallas Fuel
- Participated in high-profile events including various Rajjchelor, Rust, and NoPixel GTA RP streams that generated record viewership

## Controversies & Criticism

xQc's career has been marked by repeated controversies. His OWL suspensions for homophobic language and stream sniping led to his dismissal from the Dallas Fuel. On stream, he has engaged in public disputes with fellow streamers, made statements criticized as insensitive, and faced scrutiny over his promotion of gambling content — particularly gambling sponsorships he later distanced himself from. Critics have also raised concerns about the influence of his gambling streams on younger audiences.

## Legacy & Influence

xQc represents a generation of streamers who built massive audiences through personality-driven, unscripted content rather than polished production. His trajectory — from banned esports player to one of streaming's biggest names — reflects both the volatility and opportunity of the creator economy. His $100 million Kick deal signaled a broader shift in platform competition for top streaming talent, influencing how creators negotiate exclusivity and multi-platform deals across the industry.
