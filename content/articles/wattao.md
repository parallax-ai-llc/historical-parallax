## Summary

Ivorian warlord and former rebel commander whose real name is Issiaka Ouattara. He became a senior military officer after the war and was accused of involvement in illicit activities.

## Background

Issiaka Ouattara (nicknamed “Wattao”) was an Ivorian military figure associated with the Forces Nouvelles, the rebel movement that emerged during the First Ivorian Civil War.

He was born in 1967 and later became known as a commander during the country’s period of armed conflict and political crisis.

## Role in the Ivorian conflicts

During the civil-war era, Wattao was regarded as one of the prominent commanders on the rebel side, operating within the forces aligned with the Forces Nouvelles.

In post-conflict reporting, he was often grouped with other former rebel “comzones” (commanders) who transitioned into formal security roles after changes in power.

## Post-war integration and controversies

After the conflict period, Wattao moved into the regular security apparatus and was described in reporting as a senior officer (including references to the rank of lieutenant colonel).

Public allegations and media coverage linked his name to illicit cross-border commerce and resource trafficking; these allegations were part of broader concerns about the wartime economy persisting into peacetime.

## Death

Ouattara died in January 2020 in New York City.
