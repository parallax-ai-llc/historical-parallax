## Summary

Megyn Kelly is an American journalist and television host who rose to national prominence as an anchor at Fox News before a high-profile move to NBC News. Known for her sharp interviewing style and commanding on-air presence, she became one of the most-watched cable news personalities of the 2010s. Her willingness to challenge powerful figures — including then-candidate Donald Trump during the 2015 Republican primary debate — cemented her reputation as a formidable journalist willing to ask difficult questions regardless of political affiliation.

Kelly's career trajectory took dramatic turns shaped by her own controversies, most notably remarks about blackface in 2018 that led to the cancellation of her NBC morning show. Since her departure from traditional broadcast television, she has rebuilt her public platform through podcasting and independent media, demonstrating adaptability in a rapidly changing media landscape.

## Early Life & Background

Megyn Kelly was born on November 18, 1970, in Champaign, Illinois, and raised primarily in Delmar, New York. Her father, Edward Kelly, was a professor at the State University of New York at Albany; he died of a heart attack when Megyn was fifteen, an event she has cited as formative. She earned a Bachelor of Arts in political science from Syracuse University in 1992, then a Juris Doctor from Albany Law School in 1995. She practiced law as a corporate litigation associate at Bickel & Brewer in Chicago for nine years before transitioning to journalism.

## Rise to Prominence

Kelly joined Washington, D.C. ABC affiliate WJLA-TV in 2003 with no prior broadcast experience. Fox News hired her in 2004 as a Washington correspondent. She rose steadily through the network, hosting the daytime program *America Live* beginning in 2010 before landing the prime-time slot *The Kelly File* in 2013, which quickly became one of Fox's highest-rated programs, regularly drawing over three million viewers.

Her national profile surged after she moderated the first Republican primary debate in August 2015, during which she pressed Donald Trump on his comments about women. Trump's subsequent public attacks on Kelly made her a central figure in conversations about press freedom and journalistic independence. In January 2017, she left Fox News for NBC, signing a reported $69 million, three-year contract.

## Key Achievements

- Anchored *The Kelly File* (2013–2017), consistently among the top-rated cable news programs
- Co-moderated three Republican primary debates in 2015–2016
- Named one of *Time* magazine's 100 Most Influential People in 2014 and 2016
- Published memoir *Settle for More* (2016), which debuted at number one on the *New York Times* bestseller list
- Hosted NBC's *Sunday Night with Megyn Kelly* (2017) and *Megyn Kelly Today* (2018)

## Controversies & Criticism

In October 2018, Kelly made on-air comments during a panel discussion suggesting she did not understand why wearing blackface in Halloween costumes was offensive, provided the intent was a costume tribute. NBC canceled *Megyn Kelly Today* within days, and she departed the network with the remainder of her contract paid out — reportedly around $30 million. Critics argued her comments reflected racial insensitivity; Kelly subsequently apologized but maintained the cancellation was an overreaction. Earlier controversies included accusations of racial bias in comments she made on air about Santa Claus being white in 2013.

## Legacy & Influence

Kelly's career illustrates both the heights achievable by women in cable news and the speed with which public standing can collapse. After leaving NBC, she launched *The Megyn Kelly Show* podcast, which by 2023 had grown into one of the top-ranked news podcasts in the United States, reaching audiences outside traditional broadcast structures. Her trajectory helped normalize female prime-time news anchors at major networks and demonstrated that hard-edged political interviewing was not exclusively a male domain. Her independent media success post-NBC reflects broader industry shifts toward creator-driven platforms.
