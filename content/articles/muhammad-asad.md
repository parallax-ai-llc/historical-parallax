## Summary

Muhammad Asad (born Leopold Weiss) was an Austrian Jewish journalist who converted to Islam and became one of the most influential Muslim intellectuals of the 20th century. His autobiography The Road to Mecca is a classic of Islamic literature. His translation and commentary of the Quran, The Message of the Quran, is widely regarded as one of the finest English-language Quran translations. He contributed to the founding of Pakistan and served as its ambassador to the UN.
