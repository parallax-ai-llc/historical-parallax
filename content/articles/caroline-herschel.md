## Summary

Caroline Herschel was a German-British astronomer who was the first woman to receive recognition in astronomy. She discovered eight comets and several deep-sky objects, and she was the first woman to receive a salary as a scientist. She assisted her brother William Herschel in his astronomical observations and later received the Gold Medal of the Royal Astronomical Society. Caroline was a pioneering figure for women in science.
