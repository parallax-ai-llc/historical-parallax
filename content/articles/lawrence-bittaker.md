## Summary

American serial killer who, with Roy Norris, kidnapped, tortured, raped, and murdered five teenage girls in Southern California in 1979. Known as the 'Toolbox Killers' for tools used in torture. Audio recordings used in FBI training.

## Crimes and Investigation (High-Level)

Bittaker and Norris were linked to the 1979 murders through a combination of investigative work and physical evidence.
The case involved multiple jurisdictions in Southern California and drew intense public attention.

## Trial and Sentencing

Bittaker was convicted in 1981 and sentenced to death.
His co-defendant Roy Norris received a separate sentence and later died in prison.
Bittaker remained on California’s death row for decades as appeals and post-conviction proceedings continued.

## Use in Training and Public Discussion

The case is sometimes referenced in law-enforcement training contexts and in discussions of evidentiary materials used to convey the reality of violent crime.
It also appears in debates about media ethics and the balance between documentation, sensationalism, and the dignity of victims.

## Later Life and Death

Bittaker died in prison in 2019 while still under a death sentence.
