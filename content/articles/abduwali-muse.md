## Summary

Somali pirate who led the hijacking of the Maersk Alabama in 2009, the first successful pirate seizure of a US-flagged vessel in 200 years. His story was depicted in the film Captain Phillips.

## Overview

Abduwali Muse is a Somali national known for his role in the 2009 hijacking of the U.S.-flagged cargo ship *Maersk Alabama* off the coast of Somalia. The incident drew international attention to Somali piracy in the Gulf of Aden and the broader security risks to global shipping routes.

## The *Maersk Alabama* hijacking (high-level)

- **Event:** Pirates boarded the vessel and took the ship’s captain hostage, leading to a high-profile standoff.
- **International response:** The incident involved U.S. naval forces and became a widely reported example of anti-piracy operations.
- **Legal outcome:** Muse was captured, prosecuted in the United States, and convicted in a U.S. federal court, making the case notable in maritime piracy jurisprudence.

## Broader context

The case is commonly referenced in discussions of how legal systems handle piracy, the economics that fueled piracy off Somalia in the 2000s, and the multinational security measures adopted to protect shipping lanes.
