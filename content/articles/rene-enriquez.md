## Background

- Rene Enriquez (born 1962) is associated with the **Mexican Mafia (La Eme)**, a U.S. prison gang with influence inside correctional systems and, through allied street gangs, outside prison as well.
- Public reporting generally describes him as a **high-ranking member** who later cooperated with law enforcement.

## Role in the Mexican Mafia

- As presented in mainstream summaries, Enriquez is often cited as an example of how prison-gang governance relies on:
  - internal discipline and enforcement,
  - written/implicit rules,
  - taxation of affiliated street gangs,
  - communication networks that move information and orders between facilities.
- His case is frequently used to illustrate how **leadership status** inside prison gangs can carry significant leverage and personal risk.

## Cooperation and Informant Period

- Enriquez later became a **government informant** and provided information about the Mexican Mafia’s structure and operations.
- Cooperation in this context typically involves:
  - debriefings about organizational roles and relationships,
  - identifying participants in racketeering-related activity,
  - supporting broader investigations and prosecutions.
- Accounts of his cooperation emphasize the **trade-offs** involved (personal safety concerns, credibility challenges, and the legal/ethical complexities of informant use).
