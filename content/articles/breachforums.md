## Summary

Cybercrime forum for buying and selling stolen data created by Conor Brian Fitzpatrick (Pompompurin) who was arrested by the FBI. The forum hosted sales of databases containing billions of records.

## Additional context

## What BreachForums was

- An online marketplace-style forum oriented around cybercrime, especially:
  - advertising stolen databases,
  - negotiating access to compromised systems,
  - trading “leads” and tooling used in intrusions.

## Typical goods and services advertised on similar forums

- Credential dumps and account “combos”
- Customer databases (often from hacked companies)
- Access to corporate VPN/RDP accounts
- Fraud tutorials and monetization methods

## Law-enforcement significance

- Forums like this are routinely used by investigators to:
  - identify sellers and repeat actors,
  - correlate usernames across platforms,
  - trace payment flows and infrastructure.

## Operational risks and disruptions

- Such communities can be disrupted via arrests, domain seizures, hosting takedowns, or administrator compromise.
- Even when a forum is “offline,” datasets previously offered for sale often persist and recirculate.

## Why it matters

- Breach-marketplaces reduce transaction costs for criminals and can accelerate secondary harms from data breaches (account takeovers, fraud, extortion).
- They also create “reputation economies” where vendors compete on trust signals, escrow, and public feedback.
