## Summary

Neymar won the CL with Barcelona in 2015 and is Brazil's all-time top scorer. He moved to PSG for a world-record €222M.

## Background

Neymar da Silva Santos Júnior emerged from Santos FC as one of the most highly publicized Brazilian talents of his generation. His profile combined elite on-ball skill with heavy commercial attention at a young age.

## Playing Style (High Level)

- **Role flexibility:** Frequently used as a wide forward or second striker rather than a classic No. 9.
- **Chance creation:** Known for drawing fouls, creating separation in tight spaces, and producing assists as well as goals.
- **Risk-reward:** A high-volume dribbler whose game depends on attempting difficult actions repeatedly.

## Notable Milestones

Beyond the 2015 UEFA Champions League title, his career is also associated with:

- A record-breaking transfer fee that helped reshape expectations for elite player valuations.
- A long run as a central figure for Brazil’s national team across multiple World Cups and Copa América tournaments.
- Extended injury interruptions that affected availability at key moments in domestic league and European competition seasons.

## Legacy & Debate

Neymar’s career is often discussed in terms of the gap between peak talent and consistent availability, and as a symbol of the modern era where sporting performance, branding, and transfer-market economics are tightly linked.
