## Summary

Egyptian terrorist who led the 19 hijackers in the September 11 attacks and piloted American Airlines Flight 11 into the North Tower of the World Trade Center. He was the operational leader of the Hamburg cell.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Background

- Atta was born in Egypt and studied architecture and urban planning.
- He later lived in Germany and became part of the **Hamburg cell**, a group of extremists tied to al-Qaeda.

## Path to the 9/11 plot

- Members of the Hamburg cell traveled to meet al-Qaeda leadership and were selected for roles in a large-scale operation.
- Investigations later described Atta as an operational leader among the hijackers.

## Operational preparation

- Traveled to the United States and undertook flight training.
- Coordinated logistics such as travel, housing, and timing for parts of the hijacker team.

## September 11, 2001

- Atta piloted **American Airlines Flight 11** into the North Tower of the World Trade Center.
- The attacks killed thousands and produced major shifts in U.S. and global security policy.

## Historical significance

- Atta is a central figure in histories of transnational jihadist networks and the operational methods of al-Qaeda.
- The Hamburg cell’s radicalization and planning became a key focus of counterterrorism studies after 2001.
