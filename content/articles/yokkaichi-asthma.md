## Summary

Industrial air pollution in Yokkaichi caused severe respiratory illness in thousands of residents. Petrochemical companies were found liable in a landmark 1972 ruling.

## Background

Yokkaichi, in Mie Prefecture, experienced rapid industrialization after World War II, including the development of petrochemical complexes.

The term “Yokkaichi asthma” became associated with severe air-pollution-related respiratory illness affecting local residents.

## Pollution Source and Health Effects

Summaries in public sources describe sulfur oxide (notably sulfur dioxide) emissions from industrial facilities as a central driver.

Residents experienced symptoms consistent with chronic respiratory disease, and the public-health impact was large enough to become a nationally recognized pollution case.

## Legal and Policy Significance

A landmark court ruling in 1972 found companies liable, setting an important precedent for pollution litigation and compensation.

The episode is commonly cited in the history of Japan’s environmental regulation as one of the “Big Four” pollution diseases/incidents that helped motivate stronger controls.

## Aftermath

Key themes in the aftermath include:

- Compensation and medical support for certified victims.
- Industrial emissions control and monitoring.
- Broader public awareness of the health costs of rapid industrial growth.
