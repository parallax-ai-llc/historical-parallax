## Identity and Naming

“Rhazes” is the Latinized name commonly used in older Western sources for **Muhammad ibn Zakariya al-Razi** (also spelled **al-Rāzī**), a Persian physician and polymath active during the Islamic Golden Age.

## Why This Entry Exists

In many modern references, Rhazes is treated as the same historical figure as **al-Razi**. When a knowledge base contains both entries, this page typically functions as an alias or pointer to the primary article.

## High-level Contributions (attributed to al-Razi)

- practiced and wrote extensively on clinical medicine and medical ethics
- produced large compilations of medical knowledge (often associated with later Latin/European transmission)
- described and differentiated infectious diseases in ways that influenced subsequent medical writing
- contributed to alchemy/chemistry traditions and the classification of substances and procedures

## Notes on Sources

Because the name “Rhazes” can be used as a synonym for al-Razi, readers should verify whether a given claim is based on:

- a primary manuscript tradition attributed to al-Razi
- later commentaries and translations
- modern historiography of medieval medicine
