## Summary

Milkha Singh, the 'Flying Sikh,' narrowly missed a medal at the 1960 Rome Olympics 400m. He held the Indian 400m record for 38 years and won Asian Games gold.

## Additional Context

- Milkha Singh was one of India’s most prominent track athletes and is often known by the nickname “The Flying Sikh.”
- He became a national sporting icon in the decades after Indian independence.

## Sporting Significance

- His career is commonly discussed as an early example of post-colonial India’s emergence on the international athletics stage.
- Public memory of Singh often highlights the discipline of elite training and the role of sport in national identity.

## Cultural Impact

- His life story has been retold in popular media and is frequently used as a narrative of resilience and achievement.
- He is also associated with efforts to promote sport and fitness, especially for younger athletes.
