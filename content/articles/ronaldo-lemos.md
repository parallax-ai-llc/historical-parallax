## Biography

Ronaldo Lemos is a Brazilian lawyer and academic focused on technology and society, with work spanning intellectual property, privacy, digital rights, and the governance of emerging technologies.[^1]

He has been described as an influential voice in Brazil’s internet policy debates, including work connected to the Marco Civil da Internet, a landmark Brazilian internet law.[^1]

## Areas of Work (high level)

- **Digital rights and internet governance:** Legal frameworks for online civil rights and accountability.[^1]
- **Intellectual property and open knowledge:** Work connected to Creative Commons initiatives in Brazil.[^1]
- **Privacy and data policy:** Public-facing commentary and research on the societal impact of data collection and regulation.[^1]
- **Artificial intelligence and emerging tech policy:** Engagement with AI-related regulatory discussions and governance structures.[^1]
- **Innovation and public policy:** Research and institutional work on how technology shapes public institutions.[^1]
