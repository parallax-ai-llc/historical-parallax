## Summary

Lee Dae-ho was one of the greatest KBO sluggers who also played in NPB for Softbank Hawks and briefly for the Seattle Mariners.

## Playing Style

Lee Dae-ho is primarily known as a power-hitting first baseman and designated hitter. Profiles of his game commonly emphasize:

- Strong contact to the pull side combined with home-run power.
- Plate discipline and the ability to drive mistakes in the strike zone.
- A body type and approach more typical of a middle-of-the-order slugger than a speed or defense-first player.

## Career Across Leagues

His career is often summarized as a rare modern example of a Korean star succeeding in multiple top leagues:

- **KBO (Korea):** Developed into an elite hitter and a franchise centerpiece.
- **NPB (Japan):** Played for Orix Buffaloes and Fukuoka SoftBank Hawks, contributing as a middle-order bat.
- **MLB (United States):** Joined the Seattle Mariners for the 2016 season, serving mainly as a platoon/bench power option and becoming a notable fan favorite.

## International Competition

Lee represented South Korea in major international tournaments, and is frequently remembered for key moments at the World Baseball Classic and other events where Korea performed strongly against global competition.

## Legacy

In Korean baseball discussions, Lee is often placed among the defining sluggers of his era. His cross-league résumé is cited as evidence that KBO hitters can adapt to differing styles of pitching, ballparks, and travel schedules at the highest levels.
