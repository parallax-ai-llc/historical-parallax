## Summary

Bosnian Serb political leader convicted of genocide, war crimes, and crimes against humanity during the Bosnian War. He orchestrated the Srebrenica massacre and the Siege of Sarajevo. Sentenced to life imprisonment by the ICTY.

## Political Role

Karadžić was a central political figure for Bosnian Serbs during the breakup of Yugoslavia and the Bosnian War. As president of Republika Srpska, he was associated with policies and campaigns that aimed to reshape territory through violence, expulsions, and terror against civilian populations.

## Siege of Sarajevo

The siege of Sarajevo (1992–1996) involved prolonged shelling and sniper fire that killed and injured thousands of civilians and created severe humanitarian conditions. International prosecutions treated the siege as a sustained campaign of attacks on civilians rather than isolated battlefield incidents.

## Srebrenica and Mass Atrocities

International courts found that the 1995 Srebrenica killings constituted genocide, with organized detention, transportation, and execution of Bosniak men and boys. Karadžić’s responsibility was assessed in terms of political leadership, decision-making, and coordination with military and security structures.

## ICTY Trial and Sentence

The ICTY tried Karadžić on multiple counts, including genocide, crimes against humanity, and war crimes. The tribunal’s judgments and the final life sentence were widely cited as part of the post-conflict accountability architecture for the former Yugoslavia.

The case is frequently referenced in discussions of political leadership liability in international criminal law.

## Arrest and Fugitive Period

After years as a fugitive, Karadžić was arrested in Serbia in 2008. Reports about his concealment and use of an alternative public persona became a notable element of media coverage surrounding the case.
