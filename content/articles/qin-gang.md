## Summary

Chinese foreign minister who mysteriously disappeared from public life and was removed from office.

## Key Positions (selected)

- **Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MFA), PRC** — Career diplomat in the Chinese foreign service.
- **Ambassador of China to the United States** (2021–2023) — China’s chief envoy in Washington, D.C.
- **State Councilor** (2023) — Named to the State Council after becoming foreign minister; later removed.
- **Minister of Foreign Affairs** (late 2022–2023) — Held the top diplomatic post before an unexplained removal.

## Expanded Timeline (publicly reported)

- **2017–2018:** Served as **Spokesperson** of China’s Ministry of Foreign Affairs (widely reported; see Wikipedia).
- **2021:** Appointed **Ambassador to the United States**.
- **Dec 2022:** Appointed **Foreign Minister** of the People’s Republic of China.
- **Mar 2023:** Formally appointed a **State Councilor**.
- **Jun 2023:** **Stopped appearing in public** and was reported absent from a series of diplomatic events.
- **Jul 2023:** **Removed as foreign minister** and replaced by **Wang Yi**; Chinese authorities did not provide a detailed public explanation.
- **Oct 2023:** **Removed as State Councilor** (reported in official state media summaries and widely covered by international press).

## Disappearance and Removal (high-level)

Public reporting generally agrees on the sequence (absence → replacement → later removal from State Council), but:

- No comprehensive official account has been released that explains the underlying cause.
- Commentary in international media has included multiple hypotheses; these remain **unverified** unless confirmed by primary official statements.

## Why This Case Drew Attention

- The foreign minister role is one of China’s most visible cabinet positions, making prolonged public absence unusual.
- The replacement by a predecessor (Wang Yi) signaled an abrupt leadership change in China’s top diplomatic office.
- The gap between disappearance and formal removals produced sustained international coverage.
