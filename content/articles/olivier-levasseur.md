## Summary

Known as 'La Buse' (The Buzzard), French pirate who captured the richest prize in pirate history from a Portuguese ship. Allegedly threw a cryptogram to the crowd at his hanging.

## Additional Context

### Indian Ocean Piracy (Early 18th Century)

Levasseur is often placed within the broader history of piracy in the Indian Ocean, where European privateers and pirates targeted shipping routes connecting Europe, Africa, India, and island ports. This region’s trade involved high-value cargoes, including precious metals, gems, and goods from colonial networks.

### The Nossa Senhora do Cabo Capture

The seizure of a Portuguese vessel identified in many accounts as Nossa Senhora do Cabo is repeatedly cited as Levasseur’s most famous action. Historical summaries treat the episode as part of a period when pirate groups operated in loose coalitions and shifted between bases along island chains and coastal settlements.

### Legend of the Cryptogram

The story that Levasseur threw a coded message (a cryptogram) to spectators at his execution is widely circulated as a legend associated with “lost pirate treasure.” The cryptogram narrative is often used to illustrate how folklore can grow around real historical figures, blending archival record with later speculation.

### Colonial and Legal Setting

Executions for piracy in this era served as deterrence and were part of an expanding legal and naval effort by European powers to secure sea lanes. Trials and hangings were also public events that communicated state authority in colonial port societies.
