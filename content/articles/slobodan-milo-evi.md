## Summary

Serbian and Yugoslav president who instigated the Yugoslav Wars of the 1990s, leading to ethnic cleansing and hundreds of thousands of deaths. He was the first sitting head of state charged with war crimes. Died during trial.

## Rise to Power

Milošević rose through the League of Communists during the late Yugoslav socialist period and became a dominant political figure as the federation entered crisis. His ascent was closely tied to nationalist mobilization and power struggles within Serbia and the broader Yugoslav system.

A commonly cited turning point was his consolidation of authority in Serbia and influence over autonomous regions, which altered the balance of power inside Yugoslavia and intensified conflict among republic leaderships.

## Yugoslav Wars (1990s) — High-Level Context

The wars of the 1990s involved the breakup of Yugoslavia, competing national projects, and multiple armed conflicts (including in Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, and later Kosovo). While many actors contributed to the conflicts, Milošević is frequently identified in international narratives as a central political driver of Serbian war aims and a key figure in the region’s escalation.

## Kosovo and NATO Intervention

In the late 1990s, violence in Kosovo and the state response became a major international crisis. The conflict culminated in NATO’s 1999 bombing campaign against the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, a watershed event that remains heavily debated in terms of legality, humanitarian justification, and long-term regional consequences.

## ICTY Indictment and Trial

Milošević was indicted by the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY). The case was historically significant because it treated a former (and recently serving) head of state as individually accountable under international criminal law.

He was transferred to The Hague in 2001 and conducted much of his own defense. The trial was lengthy and complex, involving multiple conflict theaters and extensive evidentiary records. Milošević died in detention in 2006 before a verdict was reached, leaving the case without a final judicial determination of guilt or innocence.

## Political Legacy

Milošević’s legacy remains polarizing:

- critics emphasize authoritarian governance, nationalist politics, and responsibility for mass atrocities and regional devastation,
- supporters or nationalist narratives sometimes frame him as defending Serbian interests against external pressure.

In post-Yugoslav politics, his era is often used as a reference point in debates about democratization, nationalism, international intervention, and transitional justice.
