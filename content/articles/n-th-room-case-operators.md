## Summary

Cho Ju-bin operated the 'Nth Room' on Telegram, blackmailing women and minors into creating sexual exploitation material distributed to 260,000 paying subscribers. It was one of South Korea's worst digital sex crime cases.

## Case Context

- The Nth Room case is frequently cited as a watershed moment in South Korea’s response to technology-facilitated sexual violence.
- The crimes exploited anonymity, private channels, and payment mechanisms to scale coercion and distribution.
- Victims were reportedly pressured through blackmail and escalating demands, a pattern consistent with grooming and coercive control in online exploitation.

## Public Response and Impact

- The case triggered sustained public outrage, including petitions and calls for stronger sentencing and better victim protection.
- It also intensified scrutiny of platform responsibility, encryption, moderation limits, and the role of intermediaries in trafficking illegal content.

## Legal and Policy Themes (High-Level)

- Strengthening penalties for production, possession, and distribution of sexual exploitation material.
- Improving investigative capacity for digital forensics and cross-platform evidence collection.
- Expanding support services for victims, including takedown assistance and psychological care.
