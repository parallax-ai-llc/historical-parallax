## Summary

Nelson Rockefeller served as the 41st Vice President of the United States and four-term Governor of New York. The grandson of John D. Rockefeller, he was a central figure in the liberal wing of the Republican Party. He oversaw massive infrastructure projects including the New York State Thruway and the construction of the World Trade Center. He was also a major art collector and patron of the arts.
