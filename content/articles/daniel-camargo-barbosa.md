## Summary
Daniel Camargo Barbosa was a Colombian serial killer and rapist who murdered at least 72 girls in Colombia and Ecuador in the 1970s and 1980s. After escaping a Colombian prison, he continued killing in Ecuador. He was murdered in prison by the relative of one of his victims in 1994.

## Early Life
Born January 22, 1930, in Anolaima, Colombia. He had a troubled childhood with an abusive stepmother. He later claimed his first girlfriend's betrayal triggered his hatred of women. He worked various jobs while committing crimes. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Camargo raped and murdered young girls across Colombia starting in the 1970s. He was arrested and convicted of murder in Colombia but escaped from prison on a boat. He fled to Ecuador, where he continued his killing spree, murdering at least 71 more victims, primarily young schoolgirls. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Arrested in Ecuador in 1989, he confessed to 72 murders and led police to victims' remains. He was sentenced to 16 years, Ecuador's maximum. On November 13, 1994, he was murdered in prison by Luis Masache Narv√°ez, a cousin of one of his victims. [^3]
