## Summary

Yemeni-American imam and al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula leader who inspired numerous terrorist attacks through online propaganda. He influenced the Fort Hood shooter and the underwear bomber. Killed by US drone strike.

## Role in AQAP and Propaganda

Al-Awlaki became prominent for English-language sermons and online messaging that reached audiences beyond Yemen. Accounts commonly describe his influence as partly ideological and inspirational, with propaganda encouraging violence and providing narratives that some attackers cited in later investigations.

## Notable Controversies and Legal Issues

His case became especially controversial because he was a U.S. citizen targeted for killing by the United States without a criminal trial. The U.S. government argued he posed an operational threat as part of al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP), while critics raised constitutional and due process concerns.

## Death and Aftermath

He was killed in Yemen in a 2011 U.S. drone strike. In the years that followed, the dissemination of extremist content online and the role of charismatic propagandists like al-Awlaki became a recurring focus in counterterrorism, social media moderation, and radicalization research.

## Historical Significance

Al-Awlaki is often referenced in discussions of "homegrown" radicalization and the shift toward digital recruitment and influence campaigns, where direct command-and-control may be less visible than motivational messaging.
