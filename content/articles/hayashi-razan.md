## Summary

Hayashi Razan was a Japanese Neo-Confucian scholar who served as advisor to the first four Tokugawa shoguns. He established Zhu Xi's Neo-Confucianism as the official ideology of the Tokugawa shogunate. He founded the Shoheizaka academy (later Yushima Seido), which became the center of Confucian learning in Edo. He helped design the social hierarchy and ideological framework of the Tokugawa period.
