## Summary

Oscar Robertson, 'The Big O,' averaged a triple-double for an entire season in 1961-62, a feat not repeated until Russell Westbrook in 2017. He won an NBA championship with Milwaukee.

## Early Life and College

Robertson grew up in Indianapolis and became a standout basketball player before starring at the University of Cincinnati. His college career helped elevate Cincinnati into national contention and set the stage for his pro profile.

## NBA Career Highlights

- Played most prominently for the Cincinnati Royals and later the Milwaukee Bucks.
- Known for elite all-around production: scoring, rebounding, playmaking, and durability.
- Won the NBA Most Valuable Player award early in his career and made multiple All-NBA teams.

## The Triple-Double Season (1961–62)

Averaging a triple-double for an entire season became a shorthand for “complete” guard play. The achievement is frequently used as a benchmark when comparing eras and styles, because it reflects a player contributing across every major box-score category.

## The Robertson v. NBA Lawsuit

Beyond statistics, Robertson’s legacy includes his leadership role in a landmark antitrust suit brought by the NBA players’ association. The settlement (often referred to as the “Oscar Robertson Rule”) helped reshape:

- Free agency and player movement
- Salary and contract negotiation dynamics
- The balance of power between teams and players

## Legacy

Robertson is commonly listed among the greatest guards in basketball history, valued for his combination of size, court vision, and consistent production across a long career.
