## Summary

Daley Thompson won consecutive Olympic decathlon golds in 1980 and 1984, dominating every major competition for nearly a decade.

## Additional Context

## Background

- Thompson is associated with a golden era of British athletics and is best known for dominance in the decathlon, a two-day, ten-event combined discipline.
- In the Olympic cycle of the early 1980s, he became the standard-setter for consistent championship performances.

## Decathlon Overview

- The decathlon includes sprints, jumps, throws, and a 1500m, with points awarded by scoring tables rather than head-to-head finishes.
- Champions typically combine elite speed (100m/400m), explosive power (long jump/shot put), and endurance (1500m) with minimal weaknesses across events.

## Competitive Peak

- **1980 & 1984 Olympics:** Consecutive Olympic gold medals in the decathlon.
- **World records:** Set world records during his career, reinforcing his status as the leading all-round athlete of his era.
- **Major meets:** Widely noted for winning across the biggest championships for nearly a decade.

## Legacy

- Thompson is often referenced in UK sport as a benchmark for multi-event excellence and competitive toughness.
- His rivalry narratives and consistent delivery at major events helped popularize the decathlon in mainstream British sports coverage.
