## Summary

Oh Jin-hyek won individual archery gold at the 2012 London Olympics.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2012 | Won individual gold at London Olympics |
| 2012 | Won men's team bronze at London Olympics (with Im Dong-hyun, Kim Bub-min) |
| 2013 | Won individual gold at the Archery World Cup Final (Paris) |
| 2013 | Reached world no. 1 ranking (Apr 2013–Jun 2014) |
| 2020 | Won Olympic men's team gold at Tokyo Olympics |

## Key Facts

- Korean name: 오진혁 (Oh Jin-hyek)
- Bowstyle: men's recurve
- Known for: becoming the first Korean male archer to win Olympic individual gold (London 2012)

## Career Highlights (High-Level)

- **International debut (national team):** first competed for South Korea's national team in 1999.
- **Olympic breakthrough:** won **men's individual gold** at the **2012 Summer Olympics** in London.
- **Olympic team medals:** earned a **team bronze** at London 2012, and later a **team gold** at the **2020 Tokyo Olympics**.
- **Ranking peak:** held the **world no. 1** position from **April 2013 to June 2014**.

## Major Results (Selected)

### Olympic Games

- **London 2012**
  - Individual: **Gold**
  - Team: **Bronze**
- **Tokyo 2020**
  - Team: **Gold**
  - Individual: reached the Round of 16

### World Championships / World Cup

- World Championships (individual): **Silver** (2011), **Silver** (2013)
- World Cup Final (individual): **Gold** (2013)

## Affiliation and Style

- Competed for the **Hyundai Steel** corporate archery team.
- Described as having a **distinctive, dynamic** shooting technique.
