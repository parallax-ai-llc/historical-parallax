## The Richter Scale (High-Level)

- The original Richter scale is a **logarithmic magnitude scale**: each whole-number increase corresponds to a large increase in measured wave amplitude.
- It was designed for specific types of seismic recordings and regional conditions, making it a practical tool for comparing earthquake sizes in a consistent way.

## Scientific Context

Richter's work helped standardize how earthquakes are quantified, which supports:

- Comparing events across time and regions
- Building earthquake catalogs for research
- Communicating earthquake size to the public and decision-makers

## Later Developments

As instrumentation and global seismology improved, other magnitude measures (notably **moment magnitude**) became widely used for large earthquakes because they better reflect the physical energy release across a broader range of event sizes.

## Legacy

Even when other scales are used in technical work, "Richter scale" remains a common shorthand in popular media for the concept of earthquake magnitude.
