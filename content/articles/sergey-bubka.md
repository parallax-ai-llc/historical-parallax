## Summary

Sergey Bubka set the pole vault world record 35 times, first clearing 6m in 1985. He won six World Championships and Olympic gold in 1988.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

## Athletic Profile

- **Event:** Pole vault.
- **Era:** Dominant through the 1980s and early 1990s.
- Known for combining speed on the runway with exceptional takeoff strength and technical efficiency.

## Records and Achievements (High-Level)

- Set the pole vault world record numerous times.
- First athlete credited with clearing **6.00 meters**.
- Won multiple World Championship titles and an Olympic gold medal.

## Legacy

- Bubka's record-breaking strategy and consistency made him a benchmark for later generations of vaulters.
- His performances helped popularize the event and push equipment and technique development.

## Post-Competition

- After his competitive peak, he became involved in sports administration and Olympic movement roles.

## Notable Milestone

- Bubka's repeated incremental record improvements are often cited as an example of optimizing both performance and competition incentives.
