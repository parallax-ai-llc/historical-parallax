## Summary

Lime Asset Management suspended redemptions on $1.7 billion worth of funds after investing in illiquid assets and covering up losses. Multiple executives convicted of fraud.

## What Happened (High Level)

- The scandal centered on funds marketed to investors that became difficult to redeem after money was placed into **illiquid or hard-to-value assets**.
- When redemption pressure rose, the firm suspended withdrawals, triggering a broader loss of confidence.

## Structural Issues Often Cited

- **Liquidity mismatch**: open-ended funds offering frequent redemptions while holding assets that could not be sold quickly.
- **Valuation and disclosure**: allegations that losses and portfolio conditions were not transparently reflected to investors.
- **Distribution incentives**: scrutiny of how products were sold and the adequacy of suitability checks for investors.

## Investigations and Accountability

- The case led to criminal investigations and convictions for multiple individuals connected to the operation and concealment of fund losses.
- It also became a touchstone for debates about regulator oversight of private credit, alternative investments, and structured products.

## Broader Impact

- The Lime episode is frequently referenced in South Korea as a major event shaping investor-protection reforms and discussions about fund governance.

## Key Takeaways

- The scandal is often summarized as a liquidity and disclosure crisis in complex fund products.
- It reinforced scrutiny of how alternative investments are sold to retail and institutional clients.
- Subsequent reforms and enforcement actions are commonly discussed as part of its legacy.
