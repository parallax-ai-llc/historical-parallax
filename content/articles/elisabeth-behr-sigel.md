## Summary

Elisabeth Behr-Sigel was a French Orthodox theologian who was one of the most important Orthodox women theologians of the 20th century. Born into an Alsatian Protestant family, she converted to Orthodoxy and spent her career promoting dialogue between Eastern and Western Christianity. She advocated for the role of women in the Orthodox Church. Her biography of Lev Gillet and her work on women's ordination were groundbreaking.
