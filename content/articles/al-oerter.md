## Summary

Al Oerter won four consecutive Olympic discus golds from 1956 to 1968, the only athlete to win the same field event four straight times.

## Background

Al Oerter was an American track-and-field athlete best known for the discus throw. He is widely remembered for longevity at the elite level and for a record-setting Olympic run that spanned four Games across three different decades.

## Olympic Record and Competitive Profile

Oerter’s four straight Olympic gold medals (1956, 1960, 1964, 1968) are notable both for their rarity and for the changing context of the sport over that period (training methods, global depth of competition, and track-and-field professionalism).

High-level features commonly noted about his Olympic performances include:

- **Consistency under pressure:** winning finals repeatedly despite the small margins typical in throwing events
- **Peak performance timing:** producing top throws when it mattered most at the Games
- **Durability:** remaining competitive across long cycles and different Olympic venues

## Legacy

In Olympic history, Oerter is frequently cited as a benchmark for sustained excellence in a single discipline. His career is used as an example in discussions of:

- what multi-Olympiad success looks like in a technical power event
- athlete development and maintaining top form over 12+ years
- the historical evolution of track-and-field competition in the mid-20th century
