## Position and Tenure

Riad Salameh served for decades as governor of the **Banque du Liban** (Lebanon’s central bank). His tenure is closely associated with Lebanon’s long-running financial model, including maintenance of a currency peg and heavy reliance on inflows of foreign currency.

## Crisis Context

Lebanon’s financial crisis that became acute from 2019 onward involved:

- banking sector insolvency and deposit restrictions
- sharp currency depreciation and inflation
- sovereign default dynamics and prolonged political deadlock

Analyses of the period often debate how much responsibility lies with fiscal policy, banking practices, and central-bank measures.

## Allegations and Investigations

Salameh has faced allegations (reported in multiple jurisdictions) related to:

- embezzlement or misappropriation of funds
- money laundering via complex financial and real-estate structures
- abuse of office and illicit enrichment

Investigations and legal actions have been reported in Lebanon and in parts of Europe, reflecting the cross-border nature of banking flows and asset holdings.

## End of Term and Aftermath

His departure from office occurred amid intense domestic scrutiny and international attention to Lebanon’s governance failures. The central bank’s leadership and policy direction remain central issues in any plan for financial restructuring.
