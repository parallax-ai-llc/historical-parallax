## Summary

Ryan Crouser won three consecutive Olympic shot put golds (2016, 2021, 2024) and holds the WR of 23.56m.

## Athletic Profile

- Crouser is known for elite consistency at the highest level in men’s shot put, combining high maximum distance with reliable series performance in championship finals.
- His 23.56 m throw set the world record, surpassing the longstanding 23 m barrier that had previously been considered exceptional.

## Collegiate and Development

- He competed in U.S. collegiate athletics and developed within a strong American throwing tradition that emphasizes technical refinement and strength training.
- Many top shot putters progress through NCAA competition before transitioning to full-time professional training and international meets.

## Major Championships

- In addition to Olympic success, Crouser has been a leading figure in global championships, often contending for or winning medals at World Athletics events.
- His era is frequently compared with other modern elite throwers as the discipline’s performance level rose during the 2010s and 2020s.

## Technique Notes (High-Level)

- Shot put technique generally falls into “glide” and “rotational” styles; Crouser is associated with the modern rotational approach.
- Small gains in footwork, timing, and release angle can translate into meaningful distance increases at world-class levels.

## Shot Put Basics

- In international men’s competition, the shot weighs 7.26 kg (16 lb), making peak performance heavily dependent on strength and efficient technique.
