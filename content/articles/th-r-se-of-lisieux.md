## Summary

Thérèse of Lisieux, known as the 'Little Flower,' was a French Carmelite nun whose autobiography Story of a Soul became one of the most popular spiritual books of the 20th century. She developed the 'Little Way' of spiritual childhood — doing small acts with great love. Despite dying of tuberculosis at age 24, she became one of the most popular saints. She was named a Doctor of the Church in 1997.
