## Summary

Corporate fraud at Tyco International where CEO Dennis Kozlowski and CFO Mark Swartz looted $600 million from the company through unauthorized bonuses and stock fraud.

## Additional Context

The Tyco International scandal became emblematic of early-2000s corporate governance failures, involving allegations of executive misconduct and misuse of corporate funds.

### Governance and controls

- The case is frequently discussed alongside other major corporate scandals of the era as a driver for stronger internal controls and board oversight.
- It underscores the importance of transparent compensation policies, expense controls, and independent audit functions.

### Broader impact

- Public attention to corporate fraud and accounting issues in this period contributed to regulatory and market responses aimed at improving reporting and accountability.
