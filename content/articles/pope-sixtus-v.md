## Summary

Pope Sixtus V was Pope from 1585 to 1590 and one of the most energetic and reforming popes of the Counter-Reformation. He reorganized the Roman Curia, established the system of congregations that governed the Church until Vatican II, and commissioned the dome of St. Peter's Basilica. He cleaned up Rome of bandits and undertook major urban planning projects. He also established the modern form of the College of Cardinals.
