## Summary

Manfred Eigen was a German biophysical chemist who shared the 1967 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for studies of extremely fast chemical reactions. He developed relaxation techniques to study chemical reactions occurring in nanoseconds to microseconds. He also made significant contributions to the theory of self-organization in biological systems and viral quasispecies theory.
