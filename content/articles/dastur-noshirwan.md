## Summary

Dastur Noshirwan was a Zoroastrian high priest during the late Sasanian period who contributed to the compilation and preservation of the Avesta and Pahlavi religious literature. During the reign of Khosrow I, he helped systematize Zoroastrian theology and ritual. The late Sasanian period saw the final organization of Zoroastrian texts before the Arab conquest of Persia. His work helped preserve Zoroastrian tradition for future generations.
