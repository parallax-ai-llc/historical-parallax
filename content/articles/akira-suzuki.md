## Summary

Akira Suzuki is a Japanese chemist who shared the 2010 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with Richard Heck and Ei-ichi Negishi for palladium-catalyzed cross couplings in organic synthesis. The Suzuki reaction, which he developed, became one of the most widely used chemical reactions for forming carbon-carbon bonds. It is extensively used in pharmaceutical synthesis and materials science.
