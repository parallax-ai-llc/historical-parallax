## Summary
Dennis Nilsen, the 'Muswell Hill Murderer,' killed at least 12 young men in London between 1978 and 1983. He lured vulnerable homeless and gay men to his home, strangled them, and kept their bodies for extended periods. He was caught when human flesh blocked his drains.

## Early Life
Born November 23, 1945, in Fraserburgh, Scotland. Deeply affected by his grandfather's death at age six. He served in the Army, then worked as a civil servant. He was openly gay during a time of significant stigma. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1978 and 1983, Nilsen murdered at least 12 men at two London addresses. He targeted homeless or unemployed young men from pubs. He kept bodies for weeks, bathing and dressing them. He disposed of remains through burning or flushing. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
On February 8, 1983, a plumber found human remains blocking drains at 23 Cranley Gardens. Nilsen confessed immediately. Convicted of six murders and two attempted murders on November 4, 1983. Died in prison May 12, 2018. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
