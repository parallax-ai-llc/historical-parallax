## Summary

21 people were killed in a crowd crush at the entrance tunnel to the Love Parade festival in Duisburg. Organizers and city officials were blamed for inadequate safety planning.

## Background

Love Parade began as a music and dance demonstration in Berlin and later evolved into a large-scale electronic music festival. By 2010, the event was hosted in Duisburg (North Rhine-Westphalia) as part of a regional program of cultural events, and it attracted very large crowds with free entry.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- Attendees approached the festival area primarily through a confined access route (a tunnel leading to a ramp).
- Crowd density increased near the entrance and bottlenecks formed as inflow and outflow intersected.
- In the most congested area, people fell and others were compressed in a crowd crush.
- **Fatalities:** 21 people died.
- **Injuries:** reports commonly cite **500+ injured**.

## Contributing Factors (Commonly Cited)

These factors are frequently discussed in official reviews and public reporting, including the Wikipedia summary:

- **Site design constraints:** limited entry/exit capacity relative to peak demand.
- **Bottlenecks and counter-flows:** simultaneous movement of people in opposing directions.
- **Crowd management complexity:** difficulty maintaining safe flow once densities became critical.
- **Operational decisions under pressure:** attempts to control flow using cordons and routing changes as the situation developed.

## Aftermath

- The disaster prompted extensive public debate in Germany about permitting, risk assessment, and the responsibilities of organizers and local authorities.
- The event was widely treated as a turning point for mass-gathering safety practices in the region.

## Safety Lessons (General)

- Match **capacity and access routes** to realistic peak arrival rates, not average attendance.
- Avoid designs that force crowds through **single points of failure** (narrow tunnels/ramps) without overflow options.
- Separate **inbound and outbound** pedestrian flows where possible.
- Establish clear **stop/hold** procedures and real-time monitoring for density escalation.
