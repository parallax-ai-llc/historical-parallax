## Background

Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo is a military officer-turned-politician who has ruled Equatorial Guinea for decades. He is widely described as one of the world’s longest-serving leaders.

## Governance and Political System

Obiang’s rule is commonly characterized by:

- Strong executive control over state institutions
- Limited space for political opposition and independent media
- A security apparatus that plays a central role in regime stability

Supporters point to infrastructure projects and increased state capacity during the oil era, while critics emphasize persistent authoritarianism and restrictions on civil liberties.

## Oil, State Revenue, and Inequality

From the mid-1990s onward, petroleum production made Equatorial Guinea one of sub-Saharan Africa’s higher per-capita income states on paper. Reporting by international organizations and journalists has frequently highlighted a gap between state revenues and broad-based improvements in living standards, with concerns about corruption and weak transparency.

## International Scrutiny and Legal Cases

Obiang’s family and inner circle have faced international scrutiny related to alleged corruption and asset holdings abroad. A notable focus has been on Teodorín, who has been the subject of investigations and legal actions in several jurisdictions.
