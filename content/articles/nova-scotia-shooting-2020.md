## Summary

Gabriel Wortman killed 22 people across Nova Scotia over 13 hours while disguised as a police officer in a replica RCMP car. Canada's worst mass shooting.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The attacks began on April 18, 2020 and continued into April 19, 2020.
- The perpetrator used a police-style uniform and drove a replica RCMP vehicle to mislead victims and bystanders.
- The events unfolded across multiple locations in Nova Scotia over roughly 13 hours, resulting in 22 deaths.

## Investigation and Public Inquiry

- Canadian authorities conducted criminal investigations and released periodic public updates.
- A joint federal–provincial Mass Casualty Commission (public inquiry) was established to examine the causes, the response, and prevention lessons.
- The Commission’s work included reviewing emergency alerts, inter-agency communication, and how information was shared with the public during the incident.

## Impact and Aftermath

- The incident is widely described as Canada’s deadliest mass shooting.
- It prompted sustained public discussion about firearms access, domestic violence risk factors, and emergency communications.
- Memorials and commemorative events were held for victims and affected communities.

## Key Takeaways (Factual, Non-Speculative)

- Disguise and impersonation can materially complicate emergency response and public safety messaging.
- Multi-site incidents require rapid, consistent public warning systems and clear coordination among agencies.
- Post-incident inquiries commonly focus on actionable system improvements rather than individual blame.
