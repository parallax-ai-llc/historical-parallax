## Summary

Japanese serial killer known as the 'Twitter Killer' who murdered 9 people he contacted through social media in 2017. He targeted suicidal people, inviting them to his apartment in Zama where he killed and dismembered them. Sentenced to death.

## Background

Shiraishi was a Japanese man who lived in Kanagawa Prefecture. Media reporting around the case highlighted how he used online platforms to locate people expressing distress, then moved conversations into private channels.

## Crimes and Modus Operandi

The case became widely known because he initiated contact through Twitter (now X), searching for posts that mentioned self-harm or suicide. Investigators said he presented himself as someone willing to help, and then arranged in-person meetings. The victims were primarily young women, with at least one young man also killed.

## Investigation and Arrest

The killings were uncovered in 2017 after police searched his residence in Zama during a missing-person investigation. The discovery prompted extensive national media coverage and a rapid shift from a missing-person case to a homicide investigation.

## Trial and Sentencing

He was charged with multiple counts of murder and related offenses. In 2020, a court sentenced him to death; the judgment and subsequent appeals process were closely followed because capital punishment is rare but still used in Japan.

## Public Impact

The case intensified debate in Japan about social-media safety, platform responsibility, and the risks faced by vulnerable users online. It also led to renewed attention to mental-health outreach and how public posts about self-harm can attract predatory behavior.

## Platform Response (General)

In the aftermath, platform moderation practices and reporting pathways for crisis-related content became a recurring topic in commentary, alongside calls for clearer routes to support services when users post about severe distress.
