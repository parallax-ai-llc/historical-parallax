## Summary

Former governor of Borno State accused of sponsoring Boko Haram's rise through political patronage. He used the group for political purposes before they turned violent.

## Overview

- Nigerian politician best known for serving as Governor of Borno State (2003–2011) during a period of rising insecurity in northeastern Nigeria.
- Also served in Nigeria’s Senate and held national-level party roles, including a short period as an acting national chairman within the People’s Democratic Party (PDP).
- Has changed party affiliations over time, including ties to the All Nigeria Peoples Party (ANPP), All Progressives Congress (APC), and PDP.

## Political career

- Served as Senator for Borno Central (with non-consecutive terms noted in many public biographies).
- Elected Governor of Borno State in 2003 and re-elected for a second term, making him one of the early governors in Borno to serve two consecutive elected terms.
- Became involved in national party leadership disputes in the mid-2010s, reflecting broader factional struggles within major Nigerian parties.

## Party affiliations and roles

- ANPP: Associated with the party during key phases of his gubernatorial career.
- APC: Identified as a founding member in some accounts; later affiliations shifted.
- PDP: Served briefly as acting national chairman in 2016 before being replaced by a caretaker arrangement.
