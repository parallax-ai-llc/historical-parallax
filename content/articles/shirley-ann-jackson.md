## Summary

Shirley Ann Jackson was the first African American woman to earn a PhD from MIT (1973) and her research in theoretical physics led to breakthroughs enabling the portable fax, touch tone telephone, solar cells, and fiber optic cables. She became the first Black woman to head the U.S. Nuclear Regulatory Commission and later president of Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute.
