## Summary

Master Hsuan Hua was a Chinese Chan Buddhist monk who was one of the most important figures in bringing Chinese Buddhism to the Western world. He founded the City of Ten Thousand Buddhas in Ukiah, California, one of the largest Buddhist communities in the Western hemisphere. He translated major Chinese Buddhist sutras into English and established Dharma Realm Buddhist University. He was known for his strict discipline and comprehensive approach to Buddhist practice.
