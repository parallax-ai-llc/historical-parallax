## Summary

Minneapolis police officer who murdered George Floyd by kneeling on his neck for 9 minutes while Floyd pleaded 'I can't breathe.' Convicted of murder; also pled guilty to federal charges.

## Background

Chauvin worked as a police officer with the Minneapolis Police Department before becoming internationally known in connection with the death of George Floyd in 2020. The case became a major focus of scrutiny on police use of force, accountability, and racial justice in the United States.

## George Floyd Incident (2020)

The incident occurred during an arrest in Minneapolis and was recorded by bystanders. The video circulated widely and triggered large-scale protests in the U.S. and abroad. Public discussion centered on the duration and nature of restraint, duty of care after a subject becomes unresponsive, and departmental policies.

## Criminal Trials and Convictions

Chauvin was tried in Minnesota state court and convicted on charges related to Floyd’s death. Separate federal proceedings addressed civil-rights violations tied to use of force.

## Sentencing (High Level)

Following the state conviction, he received a prison sentence under Minnesota law. In the federal case, he entered a guilty plea and received an additional sentence, with decisions about how sentences are served depending on court orders and correctional policy.

## Broader Impact

The Floyd case and the prosecution of Chauvin influenced national debate and policy discussions about policing, including training, oversight mechanisms, and legal standards for use of force. It also became a reference point in discussions about the role of video evidence and public transparency in criminal-justice outcomes.
