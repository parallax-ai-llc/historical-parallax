## Summary

Abraham Darby I developed the process of smelting iron with coke instead of charcoal in 1709, a breakthrough that enabled mass production of cheap iron and fueled the Industrial Revolution. His innovation at Coalbrookdale, Shropshire, is considered one of the most important developments in the history of metallurgy and manufacturing.
