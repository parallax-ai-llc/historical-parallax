## Summary

War crimes committed during the Yemeni Civil War since 2014 by all parties. Saudi-led coalition airstrikes killed thousands of civilians while Houthi forces used child soldiers and indiscriminate shelling.

## Context

The phrase "Yemen war crimes" is used in reporting and advocacy to describe alleged violations of international humanitarian law during the conflict commonly dated from 2014 onward. The war involves multiple armed actors, including the internationally recognized Yemeni government, the Houthi movement (Ansar Allah), the Saudi-led coalition and allied forces, as well as local militias and other armed groups.

## Categories of Allegations

Public documentation by the United Nations and human-rights organizations has described allegations that include:

- Airstrikes hitting civilian areas and protected objects, including markets, weddings, funerals, medical facilities, and infrastructure.
- Indiscriminate shelling and artillery fire in populated areas.
- Use or recruitment of child soldiers by armed groups.
- Arbitrary detention, enforced disappearance, torture, and ill-treatment.
- Blockades or restrictions that exacerbate shortages of food, fuel, and medical supplies, raising concerns about collective harm to civilians.

## Investigations and Reporting

Several international bodies and non-governmental organizations have issued reports assessing incidents under the laws of armed conflict, including the principles of distinction, proportionality, and precautions in attack. These reports often emphasize evidentiary limits (access constraints, security risks, incomplete strike data) while still identifying patterns of harm and repeated failure to protect civilians.

## Accountability and Legal Mechanisms

Efforts to pursue accountability have included calls for independent investigations, national prosecutions, and international mechanisms. The effectiveness of these efforts has been influenced by diplomatic constraints, the complexity of attribution in a fragmented conflict environment, and the practical challenges of collecting admissible evidence during active hostilities.

## Humanitarian Consequences

Beyond individual incidents, the conflict has produced widespread humanitarian impact, including displacement, collapse of health services, and increased mortality linked to malnutrition and disease. Many summaries treat civilian harm as a cumulative result of direct violence plus indirect effects from damaged infrastructure and restricted access to essential goods.
