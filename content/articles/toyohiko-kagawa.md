## Summary

Toyohiko Kagawa was a Japanese Christian social reformer, labor organizer, and pacifist who was one of the most famous Christians in Asia. He lived among the poor in the Shinkawa slums of Kobe and organized labor unions and cooperatives. He was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize multiple times. His autobiographical novel Across the Death Line became a bestseller in Japan.
