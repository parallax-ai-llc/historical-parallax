## Summary

Peruvian president who was forced to resign in 2018 over corruption allegations related to Odebrecht bribes. He was placed under house arrest and is facing trial. Every living Peruvian president since 1985 has faced corruption charges.

## Background

- Kuczynski is an economist and former technocrat who worked in international finance and held senior roles in Peru’s economic ministries.
- He served in multiple administrations prior to the presidency, including periods as prime minister and as minister responsible for economic policy.

## Presidency (2016–2018)

- His administration emphasized market-oriented policies and sought to maintain investor confidence while facing a fragmented legislature.
- Political stability was strained by repeated congressional confrontations, including attempts to remove him from office.

## Odebrecht and Corruption Allegations

- Allegations centered on links between consulting payments and Odebrecht-related projects during earlier periods of public service.
- The case became part of a broader regional anti-corruption wave, with Peru seeing investigations involving many high-profile officials.

## Resignation and Aftermath

- He resigned in 2018 amid escalating political pressure and allegations connected to influence-peddling and vote-buying.
- Reporting also connects his tenure to controversy around a humanitarian pardon granted to former president Alberto Fujimori, which intensified political backlash.
