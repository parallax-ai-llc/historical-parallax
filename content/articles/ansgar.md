## Summary

Ansgar (Oscar) was a Frankish archbishop and missionary known as the 'Apostle of the North' for his efforts to bring Christianity to Scandinavia. He undertook missions to Denmark and Sweden and established the first Christian churches in both countries. He became the first Archbishop of Hamburg-Bremen. Although his missions had limited immediate success, he laid the groundwork for the eventual Christianization of Scandinavia.
