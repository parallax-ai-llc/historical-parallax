## Summary

Esther Lederberg discovered the lambda bacteriophage and developed replica plating, a technique fundamental to microbiology and genetics. Her method of transferring bacterial colonies enabled the study of antibiotic resistance. Despite her crucial contributions, she received little public recognition during her lifetime while her husband Joshua Lederberg won the Nobel Prize.
