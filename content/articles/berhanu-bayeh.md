## Background

Berhanu Bayeh is described in public sources as an Ethiopian military officer and political figure who rose within the Derg (the military government that ruled Ethiopia after 1974).

He held senior responsibilities including legal-affairs work within the Derg’s internal structures and later served as Minister of Foreign Affairs (1986–1989).

## Role during the Derg era (high level)

Senior Derg officials were involved in governing during periods of civil conflict, counterinsurgency, and severe political repression.

The “Red Terror” is commonly referenced as a campaign of arrests and killings targeting political opponents, and it became a central focus of later accountability efforts.

## Conviction and accountability

In the post-Derg era, Ethiopian courts prosecuted many former officials for atrocities connected to the Red Terror.

Bayeh is described as among those convicted of genocide, with sentencing reported as life imprisonment.

## Embassy refuge

A notable feature of Bayeh’s later story is that he reportedly lived for decades as a resident within the Italian Embassy compound in Addis Ababa following the Derg’s fall, leaving the embassy much later.

This episode is often cited in discussions of post-regime-change insecurity, the legal limbo of political figures, and the practical constraints of arrest and extradition in domestic transitions.

## Historical significance

Bayeh’s case is typically used to illustrate how accountability processes after mass violence can stretch across decades, intertwining legal judgments, diplomatic realities, and the politics of memory.
