## Scientific Contributions (High Level)

Yang Chen-Ning (C. N. Yang) is best known for foundational work in theoretical physics, especially in particle physics and quantum field theory.

### Parity Violation

In the mid-1950s, Yang and Tsung-Dao Lee challenged the assumption that parity symmetry must hold in weak interactions. Their theoretical analysis helped motivate experimental tests, and the subsequent confirmation of parity violation became a landmark in modern physics.

### Yang–Mills Theory and Gauge Symmetry

The 1954 Yang–Mills framework introduced what are now called non-abelian gauge fields. Gauge theories built on these ideas later became central to the Standard Model’s description of fundamental forces.

## Influence and Recognition

Yang’s work is frequently cited not only for specific results but for shaping the mathematical and conceptual toolkit used across high-energy physics. He is often discussed as part of a generation of theorists who helped define postwar particle physics.

## Broader Context

Biographical summaries commonly note Yang’s academic career in the United States and later connections to Chinese institutions, reflecting broader patterns of international scientific exchange.
