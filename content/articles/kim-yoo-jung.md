## Summary

Kim Yoo-jung (born September 22, 1999) is a South Korean actress and singer who began her career as a child actress and successfully transitioned into leading adult roles. She is widely regarded as one of the most talented actresses of her generation, having accumulated over two decades of performance experience before reaching her mid-twenties. Her ability to carry major productions across genres — from historical dramas to romantic comedies — has made her one of the most sought-after performers in the Korean entertainment industry.

Having debuted around 2003 in commercials and minor television roles, Kim Yoo-jung became a household name through high-profile historical dramas in the early 2010s. Her transition to adult stardom was smooth compared to many child performers, culminating in leading roles in primetime dramas that drew strong ratings and international viewership. She represents a generation of Korean actresses who benefited from and contributed to the global spread of Korean Wave (Hallyu) content.

## Early Life & Background

Kim Yoo-jung was born in Seoul, South Korea, on September 22, 1999. She entered the entertainment industry at an exceptionally young age, appearing in television commercials as a toddler. Her natural on-screen presence led to early acting roles in television dramas, where she portrayed younger versions of lead characters. She attended Dongguk University, majoring in theater and film, balancing academics with an active entertainment career.

## Rise to Prominence

Kim rose to significant national recognition playing the young version of the female lead in the historical drama *Moon Embracing the Sun* (MBC, 2012), which became one of the highest-rated Korean dramas of that era. Her emotionally expressive performance drew widespread praise and established her as a standout child actress. She continued building her profile through *Angry Mom* (MBC, 2015), where she starred alongside Kim Hee-sun.

Her transition to adult leading roles was confirmed with *Moonlight Drawn by Clouds* (KBS2, 2016), a Joseon-era romantic drama in which she starred opposite Park Bo-gum. The series averaged ratings above 20% nationally and was a major commercial and critical success, earning Kim her first leading actress recognition at major award ceremonies. The drama aired simultaneously on Netflix, significantly expanding her international profile.

## Key Achievements

- Leading role in *Moonlight Drawn by Clouds* (2016), one of the highest-rated KBS dramas in years
- Won the Excellence Award (Actress) at the 2016 KBS Drama Awards
- Lead role in *Clean with Passion for Now* (JTBC, 2018–2019), a popular romantic comedy
- Starred in *Lovers of the Red Sky* (MBC, 2021), a fantasy historical drama
- Released music singles, including OST contributions to her dramas
- Named brand ambassador for multiple major South Korean companies across beauty, fashion, and lifestyle sectors

## Controversies & Criticism

Kim Yoo-jung has maintained a relatively clean public image throughout her career. Some commentary has focused on the intense pressures placed on child performers in the Korean entertainment industry more broadly, with observers noting the demanding schedules young actors like Kim navigated. No major personal controversies have been widely reported.

## Legacy & Influence

Kim Yoo-jung stands as a rare example of a child actor who made a seamless and critically respected transition to adult stardom. Her success helped demonstrate that long-form career continuity is achievable in the Korean entertainment industry. She remains a prominent figure in Korean Wave exports, with her dramas consistently performing well on international streaming platforms.
