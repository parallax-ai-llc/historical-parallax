## Summary

US Army soldier who plotted a second attack on Fort Hood in 2011. He was arrested at a motel near the base with bomb-making materials and weapons. He was an AWOL soldier who opposed deployment to Afghanistan.

## Background

Naser Jason Abdo was a member of the United States Army whose case drew attention because it involved an alleged planned attack near Fort Hood, a major military installation in Texas. Public reporting connected the plot to the heightened security environment following the 2009 Fort Hood shooting.

## Events Leading to Arrest (2011)

- **AWOL status:** Abdo was reported to have left his unit without authorization.
- **Motel arrest near Fort Hood:** Authorities arrested him at a motel after receiving information that led them to search his room.
- **Materials and weapons:** Investigators reported finding components consistent with improvised explosive devices along with firearms and ammunition.

## Charges and Prosecution

Abdo faced federal charges that included weapons-related counts and charges tied to an alleged plan to target soldiers. The case proceeded in federal court rather than through the military justice system.

## Trial and Sentence

In 2012, Abdo was convicted on multiple counts, including attempted murder-related charges, and received a life sentence. Court proceedings focused on evidence recovered during the arrest and the government’s account of intended targets and methods.
