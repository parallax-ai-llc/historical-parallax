## Summary

British terrorist known as the 'Shoe Bomber' who attempted to detonate explosives hidden in his shoes on a transatlantic flight in December 2001. Alert passengers subdued him. Serving three life terms plus 110 years.

## Early Life and Radicalization (overview)

Reid was born in the UK and later became associated with Islamist extremist networks. Public reporting and court records describe a path of radicalization that included travel and contact with militants linked to al-Qaeda.

## American Airlines Flight 63 (Dec 2001)

- Reid boarded **American Airlines Flight 63** from Paris to Miami.
- He attempted to ignite an explosive device concealed in his footwear.
- Passengers and crew restrained him; the aircraft diverted and he was taken into custody.

The incident is frequently cited as an example of an aviation attack attempt disrupted by immediate onboard intervention.

## Trial and Sentence

Reid pleaded guilty in U.S. federal court and received multiple life sentences. He is held within the U.S. federal prison system.

## Security and Policy Impact

The attempted attack contributed to long-term changes in aviation security practice, including heightened screening of footwear and a broader shift toward treating small concealed devices as a key threat scenario in passenger screening.
