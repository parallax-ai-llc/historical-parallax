## Summary

British serial killer who murdered four young men between 2014 and 2015 by poisoning them with lethal doses of GHB. He met victims through dating apps and dumped their bodies near his East London flat. Sentenced to life.

## Additional context

### Modus operandi

- Port contacted men through dating and social networking apps and invited them to his address in Barking, East London.
- He used gamma-hydroxybutyrate (GHB), sometimes described as a “date rape” drug, to incapacitate and ultimately kill his victims.
- Investigators and later reporting described a pattern in which Port staged scenes and wrote misleading notes to suggest overdoses or suicide, complicating early interpretations of the deaths.

### Investigation and prosecution

- The four deaths occurred over roughly 15 months (2014–2015). Early police responses treated several of the cases as non-suspicious, which later became a major point of public scrutiny.
- Port was arrested after links between the deaths were established and forensic evidence connected him to the victims.
- In 2016 he was convicted in the United Kingdom of four murders and related offenses, and received a whole life order.

### Public impact

- The case prompted broader discussion in the UK about policing of crimes affecting LGBTQ+ communities and how potential patterns are recognized across multiple incidents.
- Media coverage and subsequent reviews highlighted issues around risk assessment, information-sharing, and the handling of vulnerable-person investigations.
