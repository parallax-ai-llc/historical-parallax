## Canonical Subject: Sam Walton (Walmart)

Sam Moore Walton (191862) was an American entrepreneur best known for founding Walmart and, later, Sam's Club.
He is often cited for building a low-cost, high-volume retail model focused on operational efficiency, rural and small-town expansion, and disciplined logistics.

### Verified Highlights

- **Early career:** Worked in retail after college; later operated a Ben Franklin variety store franchise.
- **First Walmart:** Opened the first Walmart Discount City store in **Rogers, Arkansas (1962)**.
- **Growth model:** Expanded through standardized store operations, tight cost control, and reinvestment in distribution.
- **Public company:** Walmart Stores, Inc. went public in **1970**.
- **Legacy:** Walmart became one of the world's largest retailers; Walton remained closely associated with a culture of frugality and store-level execution.
