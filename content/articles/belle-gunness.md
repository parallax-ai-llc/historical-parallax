## Summary
Belle Gunness was a Norwegian-American serial killer who murdered over 40 people at her Indiana farm between the 1880s and 1908. She lured suitors through matrimonial ads, killed them for their money, and may have faked her own death in a fire. Her true fate remains a mystery.

## Early Life
Born November 11, 1859, in Selbu, Norway. Emigrated to the US in 1881. Her first husband died in 1900 on the one day two insurance policies overlapped. She collected $8,500 and moved to La Porte, Indiana. [^1]

## Criminal Career
At her La Porte farm, Gunness placed ads in newspapers luring men with marriage promises. Suitors were told to bring cash and tell no one. They disappeared. She also murdered her second husband and possibly her children for insurance. Over 40 victims were found on the property. [^2]

## Positive Perspectives
- Led to improved regulation of personal ads [^4]
- Advanced forensic identification techniques
- Drew attention to vulnerability of lonely rural men

## Negative Perspectives
- Believed to have murdered over 40 people [^5]
- Killed her own children for insurance
- Lured lonely men with false promises of love
- May have escaped justice entirely
