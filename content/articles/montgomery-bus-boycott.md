## Summary

The Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955-56), sparked by Rosa Parks' refusal to give up her seat, was a pivotal civil rights protest. For 381 days, Black residents boycotted city buses, ultimately winning a Supreme Court ruling desegregating buses.
