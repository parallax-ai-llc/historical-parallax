## Summary

Inbee Park won seven LPGA major championships and Olympic gold at the 2016 Rio Games. She dominated women's golf in the 2010s.

## Early Life and Development

Park In-bee is a South Korean professional golfer who emerged as an international contender in her late teens. She is often noted for building a career that combined elite putting with consistent ball-striking, allowing her to contend across different course setups and major-championship conditions.

## Major Championships and Peak Years

Her 2008 U.S. Women's Open victory established her early as a major champion. The 2013 season is frequently highlighted as a peak, with multiple major wins in the same year and sustained time at or near the top of the women's world rankings.

Park is also commonly described as completing a "career Grand Slam" (winning all of the recognized major championships across her career), a milestone achieved by only a small group of players in LPGA history.

## Olympic Gold (2016)

At the 2016 Rio de Janeiro Olympics, golf returned to the Olympic program, and Park won the women's gold medal. The result carried particular visibility in South Korea and is often referenced as a defining achievement alongside her major titles.

## Recognition and Legacy

She has been recognized within the LPGA for sustained excellence, and her résumé is frequently cited in discussions of the strongest eras in modern women's golf. Commentators often point to her calm performance under pressure and her efficiency on the greens as key differentiators.
