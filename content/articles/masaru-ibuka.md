## Summary

Masaru Ibuka co-founded Sony Corporation with Akio Morita in 1946, originally named Tokyo Telecommunications Engineering Corporation. He was the technical visionary behind Sony's innovation in consumer electronics. Under his engineering leadership, Sony developed the transistor radio, Trinitron color TV, and the Walkman concept. He served as honorary chairman until his death and was revered as one of Japan's greatest entrepreneurs.
