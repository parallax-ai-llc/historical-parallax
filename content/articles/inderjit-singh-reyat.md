## Background

Reyat is associated in Canadian court records and media coverage with Sikh separatist extremism during the 1980s, a period in which political tensions related to Khalistan advocacy and violence had significant effects within parts of the Sikh diaspora.

## Air India Bombing Context (1985)

The bombing of Air India Flight 182 in 1985 remains one of the deadliest aviation terrorist attacks, killing 329 people. The case had lasting impact on Canadian public life, including scrutiny of intelligence-sharing, aviation security, and the handling of politically motivated violence affecting diaspora communities.

Reyat is frequently described as the only person to have been convicted in connection with the Flight 182 attack itself, though the broader investigation involved multiple suspects and long-running prosecutions.

## Prosecution and Convictions

He was convicted in Canada on charges connected to the events and later faced additional legal consequences related to testimony. The perjury conviction is often summarized as reflecting the difficulty prosecutors faced in securing reliable witness cooperation in the larger Air India cases.

## Legacy and Public Discussion

The Air India proceedings are commonly referenced in Canadian inquiries and commentary about systemic failures, the challenge of prosecuting complex conspiracies, and the long-term effects on victims' families.
