## Summary

Former Goldman Sachs partner who pled guilty to laundering money from 1MDB. Helped facilitate $4.5 billion in theft from Malaysia's sovereign wealth fund for personal enrichment.

## Background

- German-born investment banker who rose to senior roles at Goldman Sachs, including work tied to Southeast Asia.
- Became publicly associated with the 1MDB scandal after U.S. and other authorities investigated a series of bond offerings and related transactions.

## Role in the 1MDB Corruption Scheme (High Level)

- Prosecutors described a network of bankers, intermediaries, and officials who helped raise funds for 1MDB and divert portions through offshore entities.
- Leissner has been reported as participating in arrangements that facilitated proceeds moving through the financial system, including laundering-related conduct.
- The overall scandal is widely described as involving billions of dollars misappropriated from a Malaysian sovereign wealth fund.

## Legal Proceedings and Outcomes

- Entered a guilty plea in U.S. federal court on charges including money laundering conspiracy related to the 1MDB matter.
- Became one of several individuals prosecuted in connection with 1MDB, alongside corporate and political figures.
- Sentencing and restitution/forfeiture outcomes have been covered in public court filings and major reporting; specific amounts can vary by source and stage of proceedings.

## Why It Matters

- The case is frequently cited as a benchmark example of transnational kleptocracy intersecting with major financial institutions.
- It helped drive expanded compliance attention on high-risk sovereign/sovereign-linked clients, intermediaries, and complex offshore structures.

## Related People / Entities (Commonly Mentioned)

- **1MDB** (1Malaysia Development Berhad) — Malaysian state fund at the center of the scandal.
- **Goldman Sachs** — employer involved in 1MDB bond deals that later triggered major penalties and settlements.
- **Jho Low (Low Taek Jho)** — financier widely reported as a central figure in 1MDB-related diversion of funds.
