## Summary
Albert DeSalvo confessed to being the 'Boston Strangler,' who murdered 13 women in Boston between 1962 and 1964. Never tried for the murders, he was convicted of sexual assaults. DNA confirmed his link to the final victim in 2013. He was murdered in prison in 1973.

## Early Life
Born September 3, 1931, in Chelsea, Massachusetts. Grew up in an extremely abusive household. Served in the U.S. Army in Germany. Married in 1954. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1962 and 1964, 13 women aged 19 to 85 were strangled in Boston. DeSalvo confessed in 1965 while incarcerated, providing details matching crime scenes. Some researchers question whether he committed all murders. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Arrested in 1964 for sexual assaults. He confessed to the Strangler murders but was convicted only of the 'Green Man' assaults. Stabbed to death in Walpole State Prison, November 25, 1973. DNA confirmed his link to victim Mary Sullivan in 2013. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- 2013 DNA helped resolve decades of doubt [^4]
- Advanced DNA technology for cold cases
- Led to reforms in Boston police serial crime investigation

## Negative Perspectives
- Confessed to strangling 13 women [^5]
- Targeted women living alone
- Committed numerous sexual assaults
- Terrorized Boston for over two years
