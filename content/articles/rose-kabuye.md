## Background

- Rose Kabuye is a Rwandan former military officer and public official who became a prominent figure in Rwanda’s post-1994 government.
- She was raised and educated in Uganda and is reported to have graduated from Makerere University (political science and social administration).
- She joined the Rwandan Patriotic Front (RPF) and participated in the period of armed conflict that preceded the 1994 genocide.

## Public Roles (High Level)

- **Mayor of Kigali (1994–1998)**: Oversaw early post-war reconstruction and basic service restoration in Rwanda’s capital, alongside humanitarian and infrastructure priorities.
- **Member of Parliament (from 1998)**: Associated with defense/security oversight and women’s political mobilisation initiatives.
- **Chief of State Protocol (from 2003, multi-year tenure)**: Served in a senior protocol role under President Paul Kagame, coordinating state visits and official travel logistics.

## 2008–2009 Arrest and Legal Context

- In November 2008, she was arrested in Frankfurt, Germany, after a French judicial request.
- The arrest was linked to allegations concerning the 1994 shooting down of the aircraft carrying President Juvénal Habyarimana—an event widely considered a catalyst for the genocide.
- She was later released, and reporting indicates the charges connected to that arrest were lifted in March 2009.

## Why She Appears in Historical Records

- Her case is often cited in discussions about:
  - **International jurisdiction and arrest warrants** involving Rwandan officials.
  - **Accountability disputes** related to the 1994 plane attack and competing narratives around responsibility.
  - **Women in military and political leadership** in Rwanda’s post-conflict era.
