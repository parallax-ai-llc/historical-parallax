## What Theranos Claimed

Theranos stated that it had developed a platform capable of performing a large menu of clinical laboratory tests using very small volumes of blood, marketed as faster, cheaper, and more convenient than standard venipuncture-based testing.

## What Investigations Found (High-Level)

Reporting and subsequent investigations concluded that many claims about the core technology were misleading, that key testing was often performed using conventional analyzers rather than the advertised proprietary devices, and that internal validation and quality-control practices did not support the public narrative.

## Impact and Aftermath

The Theranos case is frequently cited in discussions about governance, diligence, and regulatory oversight in health technology. It also became a cautionary example of how secrecy, charismatic leadership, and incentive misalignment can coexist with patient-safety risk in clinical settings.
