## Summary

First President of The Gambia who ruled for 24 years before being overthrown by Yahya Jammeh. His government became increasingly corrupt in its later years.

## Political Background

Jawara rose to national leadership during the transition from colonial rule to independence, first serving as prime minister and then as head of state after The Gambia became a republic. His tenure is often discussed in the context of building a small-state parliamentary system and maintaining relative stability in a region that experienced repeated coups.

## Governance and Controversies

Long periods in office can accumulate both institutional experience and entrenched patronage. Accounts of Jawara’s later years in power include criticism about governance quality and allegations of corruption, while supporters have emphasized constitutional continuity and the avoidance of large-scale political violence during much of his rule.

## 1994 Coup and Exile

The 1994 military coup led by Yahya Jammeh ended Jawara’s presidency and reshaped Gambian politics for decades. Jawara went into exile and later remained a prominent reference point in debates about democratic legitimacy and civil–military relations in the country.

## Legacy

Jawara’s legacy is mixed and often framed around two themes: the early post-independence project of state-building, and the vulnerabilities of long-running governments in small political systems. His removal also marked a turning point that influenced subsequent discussions about term limits, accountability, and democratic resilience in The Gambia.
