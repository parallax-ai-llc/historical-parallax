## Summary

Eugene O'Neill was an American playwright who is considered the father of American drama. He won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1936 and four Pulitzer Prizes. His masterwork Long Day's Journey into Night is one of the finest American plays. His works, including The Iceman Cometh and Mourning Becomes Electra, brought tragic depth to American theater.
