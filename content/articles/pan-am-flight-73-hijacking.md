## Summary

Hijacking of Pan Am Flight 73 at Karachi airport by Abu Nidal Organization on September 5, 1986. When lights went out, hijackers opened fire killing 20 people and injuring 150.

## Background

Pan Am Flight 73 was a Boeing 747 operating an international route with a stop at Karachi, Pakistan. The hijacking was carried out by gunmen associated with the Abu Nidal Organization, part of a broader wave of airline and airport terrorism in the 1970s and 1980s.

## Course of the Hijacking (high-level)

- Hijackers seized the aircraft while it was on the ground at Karachi’s airport.
- Passengers and crew were held for hours during a standoff.
- The crisis escalated into mass shooting when conditions changed inside the aircraft (often summarized as the cabin going dark).

## Victims and Response

The incident caused significant loss of life and injury, with survivors describing chaotic conditions and attempts to evacuate. It remains notable for the scale of casualties in a single airline hijacking event.

## Aftermath

The hijackers were later captured and convicted in Pakistan. Over time, the case was referenced in wider counterterrorism narratives and in changes to airline security practices.

## Why It Matters

Flight 73 is frequently cited in discussions of:

- Airport perimeter security and on-ground aircraft vulnerability
- Hostage crisis management and escalation dynamics
- The internationalization of counterterrorism and prosecution
