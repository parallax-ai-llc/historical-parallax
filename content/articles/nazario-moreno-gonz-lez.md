## Summary

Mexican drug lord known as 'El Más Loco' (The Craziest One) who co-founded La Familia Michoacana and later the Knights Templar Cartel. He created a pseudo-religious cult around his leadership. Killed by Mexican military.

## Ideology, Image, and Control

Moreno González is often singled out among cartel leaders for emphasizing a quasi-ideological narrative: he blended criminal authority with moral language, internal codes, and religious symbolism. This kind of framing can help justify discipline inside an organization and project legitimacy to communities under coercion.

## Cartel Fragmentation and Rebranding

- The transition from La Familia Michoacana to the Knights Templar is frequently cited as an example of how groups rebrand after state pressure or leadership disputes.
- Rebranding can involve changes in names, symbols, and public messaging while maintaining core trafficking and extortion operations.
- The perception of Moreno’s death and later survival also illustrates how uncertainty and propaganda can shape narratives during active conflict.

## Regional Impact

In many accounts, the cartels associated with Moreno González are linked to a mix of narcotics trafficking, local extortion, and territorial control in Michoacán. These dynamics contributed to cycles of violence and to the emergence of community self-defense forces and intensified state operations in the region.
