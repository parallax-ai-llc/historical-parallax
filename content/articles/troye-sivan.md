## Summary

Troye Sivan Mellet (born June 5, 1995) is an Australian singer, songwriter, and actor who rose to fame through YouTube before transitioning into a successful mainstream music career. Known for his introspective pop sound and candid exploration of queer identity, Sivan has established himself as one of the most prominent LGBTQ+ voices in contemporary pop music.

His debut studio album *Blue Neighbourhood* (2015) reached the top 10 in multiple countries, and subsequent releases cemented his reputation as a thoughtful artist unafraid to center queer experiences in mainstream pop. Beyond music, Sivan has acted in films including the *X-Men* franchise and the critically praised *Boy Erased* (2018).

---

## Early Life & Background

Troye Sivan Mellet was born in Johannesburg, South Africa, to a Jewish family. His family emigrated to Perth, Western Australia, when he was two years old. He began performing in musical theatre as a child and appeared in several stage productions, including *Oliver!* and *Grease*. He attended Charles Newman College in Perth.

From around 2007, Sivan built a significant following on YouTube, where he posted covers, vlogs, and comedic videos. By his early teens he had amassed millions of subscribers, giving him an unusually direct line to a global fanbase before his professional music career began.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Sivan's acting career offered an early mainstream foothold when he portrayed a young James Howlett (Wolverine) in *X-Men Origins: Wolverine* (2009) and reprised a young Professor X in *X-Men: Days of Future Past* (2014). In 2013 he publicly came out as gay in a YouTube video that garnered widespread attention and praise.

He signed with EMI Australia and released the EP *TRXYE* in August 2014, which debuted at number 5 on the US Billboard 200 — an exceptional chart position for an independent EP. The follow-up EP *Wild* (2015) further built anticipation for his full debut album.

---

## Key Achievements

- *Blue Neighbourhood* (2015) debuted at number 1 in Australia and reached number 7 in the United States.
- The single "Youth" became his breakthrough international hit, earning platinum certifications in multiple countries.
- His second studio album *Bloom* (2018) debuted at number 4 in Australia and number 7 in the US, with the title track becoming a cultural touchstone for queer audiences.
- *Something to Give Each Other* (2023) was released to strong critical acclaim, with the single "Rush" reaching number 1 on Billboard's Dance/Electronic Songs chart and earning a Grammy nomination for Best Pop Solo Performance.
- He received a Golden Globe nomination for Best Original Song for "Revelation" from *Boy Erased* (2018).

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Sivan has faced relatively little significant controversy throughout his career. Some critics have noted that his music, while polished, can lean toward thematic sameness across albums. His casting choices and public persona have occasionally drawn scrutiny, though these have not resulted in sustained negative attention.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Sivan's decision to come out publicly in 2013, at a time when few young mainstream entertainers did so openly, had a measurable cultural impact, particularly among LGBTQ+ youth. His music consistently centers queer desire and identity without euphemism, a deliberate creative choice that has influenced a subsequent generation of pop artists. He is widely credited with helping normalize queer narratives in mainstream pop.
