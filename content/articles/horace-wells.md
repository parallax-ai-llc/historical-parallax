## Summary

Horace Wells pioneered the use of nitrous oxide (laughing gas) as a dental anesthetic in 1844, becoming one of the founders of anesthesiology. He first demonstrated pain-free tooth extraction using the gas. After a failed public demonstration and priority disputes, he suffered depression and died by suicide at age 33.
