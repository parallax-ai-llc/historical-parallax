## Summary

Bartolomé de las Casas was a Spanish Dominican friar and bishop who became the most prominent advocate for the rights of indigenous peoples in the Americas. His work A Short Account of the Destruction of the Indies documented Spanish atrocities against Native Americans. He debated Juan Ginés de Sepúlveda at Valladolid on the humanity and rights of indigenous peoples. He is regarded as one of the earliest advocates for universal human rights.
