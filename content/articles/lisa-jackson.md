Lisa P. Jackson is an American environmental policy leader who served as Administrator of the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and later became a senior executive in the technology sector, focusing on environmental initiatives and clean energy.

## Overview

- Known for public-sector leadership on air and water quality, climate policy, and environmental justice.
- Led large regulatory and scientific organizations that coordinate across federal, state, and local governments.
- Transitioned from government service to corporate sustainability leadership roles.

## Notable Roles

### U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (Administrator)

- Managed federal environmental regulatory programs affecting air, water, waste, and chemical safety.
- Worked with states on implementation of national standards (e.g., permitting, compliance, enforcement).
- Communicated the public-health rationale for pollution controls, linking environmental standards to health outcomes.

### Apple (Environment & Sustainability Leadership)

- Helped represent and coordinate company positions on renewable energy and environmental initiatives.
- Associated with corporate strategies to reduce operational emissions and expand clean-energy use in facilities and supply chains.

## Policy Themes (High-Level)

- **Air quality and industrial pollution:** standards and compliance frameworks intended to reduce harmful emissions.
- **Climate and greenhouse gases:** regulatory approaches to limit emissions under existing environmental statutes.
- **Water protection:** safeguarding drinking-water sources and waterways through permitting and pollution controls.
- **Environmental justice:** attention to disproportionate burdens faced by communities near major pollution sources.

## Why She Matters

Lisa Jackson is often cited as an example of a senior public administrator who translated technical and regulatory tools into nationwide environmental and public-health outcomes, and later brought similar policy fluency into corporate sustainability leadership.
