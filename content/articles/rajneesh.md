## Background and Early Life

Rajneesh (born Chandra Mohan Jain) was an Indian spiritual teacher who rose to prominence in the 1960s and 1970s through lectures that mixed meditation practices with critiques of conventional religion, politics, and social norms. His movement developed a distinctive organizational identity, including a community of sannyasins (initiates) and a strong emphasis on personal transformation through a blend of therapy, group work, and meditation.

## Movement and Organization

The Rajneesh movement expanded internationally, attracting followers in India, Europe, and North America. In the early 1980s the group established a large communal settlement in Wasco County, Oregon, commonly known as Rajneeshpuram. The community built infrastructure rapidly and became a focal point of local political conflict, legal disputes, and media attention.

## Oregon Conflict and 1984 Salmonella Attack

Tensions between Rajneeshpuram and surrounding communities escalated over land use, governance, and voting. In 1984, members of the group carried out a deliberate contamination of salad bars in The Dalles, Oregon, using Salmonella bacteria in an attempt to influence a local election by incapacitating voters. The incident sickened hundreds of people and is widely cited as the largest bioterrorism attack in United States history by number of victims.

## Legal Case and Deportation

In 1985, Rajneesh was arrested by US authorities and later entered a plea agreement on immigration-related charges. He was deported from the United States and returned to India, where he continued teaching under the name Osho. His later years were marked by ongoing controversy over leadership, finances, and the conduct of close associates.

## Legacy and Historical Significance

Rajneesh remains notable both for the global reach of his teachings and for the criminal acts committed by members of his organization during the Oregon period. Modern discussions of the movement often distinguish between Rajneesh's lectures and practices and the actions of followers who pursued political control and engaged in violence or coercion.
