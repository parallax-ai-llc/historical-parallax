## Summary

Russian cybercriminal who led the Evil Corp hacking group responsible for stealing over $100 million from banks worldwide using Dridex malware. The US offered a $5 million bounty for his arrest.

## Evil Corp and Dridex

- **Evil Corp**: Commonly described as a financially motivated cybercrime group that targeted organizations globally.
- **Dridex malware**: Dridex has been characterized as banking-focused malware used to steal credentials and facilitate fraudulent transactions.
- **Operational model**: High-level descriptions of the group include phishing-based initial access, credential theft, and the use of compromised accounts to move or monetize funds.

## US Charges and Sanctions

- **Indictment**: US authorities publicly named Yakubets in connection with alleged large-scale theft and related cybercrime offenses.
- **Rewards program**: The bounty has been framed as part of broader efforts to identify and disrupt major cybercriminal operators.
- **Financial restrictions**: US reporting has also described sanctions targeting individuals and entities associated with Evil Corp, aiming to limit access to the international financial system.

## Wider Context

- The case is often cited in discussions about the challenges of cross-border cybercrime enforcement, especially when suspects reside in jurisdictions where extradition is unlikely.
- The Evil Corp ecosystem is frequently discussed alongside the broader trend of criminal groups blending traditional banking theft with other forms of digital extortion.
