## Summary

Founder of Boko Haram killed extrajudicially by Nigerian police after the 2009 uprising.

## Background

- Mohamed Yusuf (1970–2009) was a Nigerian Islamist preacher associated with the early leadership of the group commonly known as **Boko Haram**.
- He became a prominent figure in northeastern Nigeria, especially around **Maiduguri** in Borno State.
- Public reporting describes him as influential in shaping the movement’s early religious messaging and recruitment.

## Role in Boko Haram

- Yusuf is widely reported as the group’s **founder/early leader**, with his teachings used to justify rejection of parts of the secular Nigerian state.
- The group’s early phase included a community of followers and a religious center that later became linked to militancy.
- After his death, leadership is commonly described as shifting to **Abubakar Shekau**, after which violence escalated substantially.

## 2009 Uprising and Death

- In **July 2009**, clashes between Boko Haram members and Nigerian security forces escalated into a major confrontation.
- Yusuf was **captured** by Nigerian authorities after the fighting.
- He died shortly after in police custody; accounts in reporting and later commentary frequently characterize the circumstances as **extrajudicial**.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- Yusuf’s death is often cited as a key turning point that contributed to the group’s transformation into a more violent insurgency.
- Boko Haram went on to conduct attacks across Nigeria and the Lake Chad region, becoming one of the most lethal militant organizations in Africa during the 2010s.
- Analysts and historians frequently discuss the 2009 crackdown and leadership transition as factors in the group’s subsequent trajectory.
