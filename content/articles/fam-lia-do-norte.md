## Additional Context

## Organizational Context

- Família do Norte (FDN) is typically described as a prison- and regionally rooted criminal organization in the Brazilian Amazon, with influence connected to Manaus and surrounding routes.
- The Amazon region’s riverine geography creates distinctive logistics for both licit commerce and illicit trafficking.

## Alliances and Conflict

- Brazilian criminal politics often involves temporary alliances between major groups; these can shift rapidly when profit-sharing and territorial control break down.
- The FDN’s conflicts, including episodes linked to nationwide prison violence, are discussed as part of a broader struggle over control of trafficking corridors and retail markets.

## Prison System Link

- In Brazil, prison-based governance structures can amplify gang power by enabling recruitment, coordination, and enforcement behind bars.
- Major prison riots and massacres have been analyzed as both retaliatory violence and as signaling in inter-gang conflicts.

## Why It Matters

- The FDN case is frequently used to illustrate how local gangs can become nationally relevant by controlling chokepoints in international supply routes.
