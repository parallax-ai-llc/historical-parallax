## Summary

Anne L'Huillier is a French-Swedish physicist who shared the 2023 Nobel Prize in Physics for experimental methods that generate attosecond pulses of light. She discovered the foundational high-harmonic generation process in 1987, which became the basis for creating attosecond pulses. She became only the fifth woman to receive the Nobel Prize in Physics.
