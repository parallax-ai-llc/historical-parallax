## Additional Context

### Early life and development

Rojas is a Venezuelan track and field athlete who emerged internationally as a teenager, competing in both the long jump and triple jump. Her rapid progression in the triple jump placed her among the event’s all-time leaders while still in her early 20s.

### Training and coaching

A major part of her elite development has been training under renowned coach Iván Pedroso, a former Olympic long jump champion known for producing top jumpers. This period is often cited as crucial to her technical consistency and record-level performance.

### Major championships

She won Olympic gold in the women’s triple jump at the Tokyo 2020 Olympics (held in 2021), and has also won multiple World Championship and World Indoor Championship titles. Across these events, she is recognized for combining championship execution with record-setting distances.

### Records and historical significance

- **Outdoor world record:** 15.67 m, set at the Tokyo Olympics (2021).
- **Indoor world record:** 15.74 m, set at the World Athletics Indoor Championships (2022).

Her marks made her the first woman to surpass 15.50 m, and positioned her as one of the most dominant triple jumpers in modern athletics.

## Style and strengths

- Powerful approach speed combined with efficient phase balance (hop–step–jump).
- Ability to peak for global finals, not only for one-off record attempts.
- Competitiveness in both long jump and triple jump early in her career.
