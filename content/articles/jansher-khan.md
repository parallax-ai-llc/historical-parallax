## Summary

Jansher Khan won eight World Open squash titles and six British Open titles. His rivalry with Jahangir Khan defined squash in the late 1980s and 1990s.

## Overview
Jansher Khan is a Pakistani former professional squash player who is widely regarded as one of the sport’s greatest champions. He is most closely associated with a long period of dominance in the late 1980s and 1990s.

## Career highlights
Khan won multiple major titles, including repeated victories at the World Open and the British Open, which historically were considered the premier events in men’s squash. His style was noted for speed, endurance, and tactical control of rallies.

## Era and rivals
His peak years overlapped with a strong generation of Pakistani squash, following earlier stars such as Jahangir Khan. Matches between top Pakistani players and international challengers helped define a highly competitive period for the sport.

## Recognition
Jansher Khan’s record across major tournaments and long tenure near the top of world rankings made him a reference point in debates about the greatest squash players. He has been honored in Pakistan and internationally for his contributions to squash.
