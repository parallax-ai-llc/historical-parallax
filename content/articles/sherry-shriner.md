## Summary

American internet cult leader who mixed UFO conspiracy theories with biblical prophecy and built a following through YouTube and internet radio. Her follower Steven Mineo was murdered by his girlfriend over the cult.

## Beliefs and online presence

Shriner is commonly described as an internet-based conspiracy and apocalyptic figure who blended religious language with UFO and “alien” themes. Her content circulated through online platforms such as streaming video, talk-radio style broadcasts, and blogs, which allowed her to build a geographically dispersed audience.

## Community dynamics

Open-source reporting portrays her following as a high-control online community, with:

- Strong in-group identity and shared jargon.
- Claims of hidden enemies or infiltrators.
- Escalating accusations directed at perceived dissenters.

These dynamics can intensify when followers treat online narratives as direct guidance for real-world decisions.

## Connection to violence

The case is often cited in discussions of how conspiracy movements can contribute to harm, even when leaders are not physically co-located with followers. Reporting around the Mineo homicide focused on interpersonal conflict within the group and the role of belief reinforcement.

## Death and aftermath

Shriner died in 2018. Coverage of her death and her group’s later trajectory generally focuses on how the community fragmented or migrated to other spaces, and on the lasting effects on former members.

## Broader significance

Shriner’s story is frequently referenced as an example of:

- Online radicalization within small, insular subcultures.
- The blurred boundary between “fringe media” and community organizing.
- The risks of harassment and paranoia-driven targeting in conspiracy ecosystems.
