## Summary

Abebe Bikila was the first black African Olympic gold medalist, winning the 1960 Rome marathon barefoot. He won again in 1964 Tokyo.

## Background and Context

Bikila was born in the Shewa region of Ethiopia and served in the Imperial Guard before being selected for elite-distance training. His breakthrough came in the lead-up to the 1960 Rome Olympics, when Ethiopia began investing more systematically in international sport.

## Racing Style and Training

He was known for even pacing and late-race strength, traits that are critical in championship marathons where tactical surges can decide medals. Contemporary accounts emphasize disciplined preparation, strong aerobic conditioning, and the ability to perform in heat and at sea level.

## Historical Significance

- First sub-Saharan African to win Olympic gold.
- First athlete to win consecutive Olympic marathon titles (1960 and 1964).
- Became a symbolic figure for post-colonial African presence in global sport.

## Later Life and Legacy

After a serious car accident in 1969, Bikila lived with paralysis and competed in some adapted sports events. In Ethiopia, he remains a national sports icon; internationally, his Rome victory is frequently cited in discussions of how distance running globalized beyond its early European and North American centers.
