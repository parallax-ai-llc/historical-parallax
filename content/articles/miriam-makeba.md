## Summary

Miriam Makeba, known as Mama Africa, was a South African singer and civil rights activist who brought African music to the world stage. Her hit Pata Pata and her testimony against apartheid at the United Nations made her an international symbol of the anti-apartheid movement. She spent 31 years in exile from South Africa.
