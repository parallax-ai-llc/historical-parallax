## Summary

US aid program to combat drug trafficking and insurgency in Colombia launched in 2000. Over $10 billion was spent on military aid, coca eradication, and economic development.

## Program Components (High-Level)

- **Security assistance:** Training, equipment, and support for Colombian security forces aimed at weakening insurgent groups and drug trafficking networks.
- **Counternarcotics:** Coca crop eradication efforts (including aerial spraying in some periods) alongside interdiction.
- **Institutional and economic support:** Funding for governance, judicial capacity, human rights programs, and development initiatives.

## Controversies and Critiques

- Human rights concerns were raised regarding abuses by armed actors, accountability, and the effects of militarization.
- Eradication policies were criticized for environmental and public-health impacts and for shifting cultivation rather than eliminating it.

## Outcomes and Legacy

- Security conditions and state capacity improved in many areas, while coca cultivation and trafficking adapted over time.
- Plan Colombia is often cited as a major U.S.–Colombia security partnership and a precedent for later regional security cooperation.
