## Summary

Khmer Rouge district commander convicted of genocide by the ECCC for overseeing the execution and burial of thousands at Kraing Ta Chan security center.

## Additional Context

### Khmer Rouge period (overview)

The Khmer Rouge ruled Cambodia from 1975 to 1979, a period associated with mass violence, forced labor, starvation, and widespread detention and execution. Local command structures and security centers played a major role in implementing repression at the district and regional levels.

### Kraing Ta Chan security center

Kraing Ta Chan is described in historical accounts as a security center and execution site where large numbers of detainees were held and killed. The site is part of the broader network of Khmer Rouge detention facilities that have been investigated by historians and prosecutors.

### ECCC proceedings

The Extraordinary Chambers in the Courts of Cambodia (ECCC) was established to prosecute senior Khmer Rouge leaders and those most responsible for crimes committed during the regime. Ta An’s conviction is typically discussed in relation to the ECCC’s efforts to document local-level command responsibility and the mechanics of detention and execution.

### Legacy and documentation

Trials related to Khmer Rouge-era crimes have contributed to the public record through witness testimony, archival material, and site documentation. These processes are often framed as part of Cambodia’s long-term efforts toward accountability and historical memory.
