## Summary

Nigerian militia leader known as 'Ghana' who led a violent gang in Benue State terrorizing communities through killings and cattle rustling. He was killed by security forces.

## Context

- Benue State has experienced recurring violence linked to criminal banditry and broader communal tensions, including disputes involving cattle rustling and attacks on rural communities.

## Activities and Reputation

- Akwaza, known by the nickname "Ghana," was reported to have led an armed group implicated in killings, robbery, and intimidation.
- The group was portrayed as exploiting weak law enforcement presence in some areas and the availability of small arms.

## Security Response

- Nigerian security forces periodically launched operations against armed groups in the region, combining military and police units.
- His death in 2020 was reported as part of a joint operation, and it was framed by authorities as a significant disruption to local militia activity.
