## Summary
Masten Milimo Wanjala was a Kenyan serial killer who confessed to the murders of at least 10 children between 2019 and 2021. He lured children with promises of sweets or football matches in Nairobi and western Kenya. He was lynched by a mob after escaping from police custody in October 2021.

## Early Life
Born around 1999 in Bungoma County, Kenya. Details of his early life are limited. He was a young man living in Nairobi's slum areas. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 2019 and 2021, Wanjala kidnapped and murdered at least 10 children aged 11-13 in Nairobi and western Kenya. He lured them with promises of sweets or football matches. He drugged them before killing some by sucking blood from their veins. He drank victims' blood, leading to comparisons with vampirism. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Wanjala was arrested in July 2021 and confessed to the murders, leading police to burial sites. He escaped from Jogoo Road Police Station in Nairobi on October 13, 2021. He was tracked to his home village in Bungoma County where an angry mob lynched him on October 20, 2021. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case exposed serious security lapses in Kenyan police custody [^4]
- Prompted reforms in how Kenyan police investigate cases of missing children
- Raised awareness about child safety in Nairobi's informal settlements

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered at least 10 children [^5]
- Lured victims with promises of sweets and games
- Drank victims' blood
- Escaped police custody, highlighting security failures
- Targeted children in poor communities
