## Summary

Pakistani-American terrorist who, with wife Tashfeen Malik, carried out the San Bernardino attack on December 2, 2015, killing 14 people at a holiday party. They were ISIS-inspired. Both killed in shootout with police.

## Additional Context

### Incident overview

The 2 December 2015 attack in San Bernardino, California, is commonly described as both a mass shooting and a terrorism-related incident in subsequent reporting and official summaries. It produced significant loss of life and injuries at a workplace-related gathering.

### Immediate response

After the shooting, Farook and Malik left the scene and were later located by law enforcement. The confrontation that followed ended with both attackers being killed. The short time window between the initial incident and the final shootout is often highlighted in discussions of emergency response and coordinated policing.

### Broader significance

In the years that followed, the event remained a reference point in U.S. debates about radicalization, violence prevention, and community resilience after mass-casualty incidents. It also became a case study for evaluating interagency coordination, public communication during crises, and the long-tail effects on survivors and local institutions.
