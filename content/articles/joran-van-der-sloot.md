## Summary

Dutch national suspected in the disappearance of Natalee Holloway in Aruba who later murdered Stephany Flores in Peru. Extradited to the US for wire fraud charges related to Holloway case.

## Background

- Van der Sloot is a Dutch national whose name became internationally known after the 2005 disappearance of American teenager Natalee Holloway in Aruba.
- The Holloway case generated extensive media coverage in the United States and the Netherlands and remained a recurring subject of investigative reporting.

## Natalee Holloway case (Aruba)

- Holloway disappeared in May 2005 while on a high-school trip to Aruba. Van der Sloot was among the last known people seen with her and was detained and questioned during the early investigation.
- Over the years, law-enforcement scrutiny and civil litigation in the United States focused in part on statements and alleged financial solicitations connected to information about the case.

## Peru murder (Stephany Flores)

- In 2010, Flores was killed in Lima, Peru. Van der Sloot was arrested in Chile and returned to Peru.
- He was convicted in Peru for the murder and sentenced to a lengthy prison term.

## U.S. federal case and extradition

- U.S. authorities pursued charges related to alleged wire fraud and extortion stemming from promises to provide information about Holloway in exchange for money.
- Van der Sloot was later extradited to the United States to face those federal charges while still under Peruvian custody arrangements.

## Public impact

- The Holloway investigation influenced public discussion about missing-person cases, media attention, and cross-border criminal investigations.
- The later Peru conviction reinforced the case’s enduring notoriety and the international dimension of related proceedings.
