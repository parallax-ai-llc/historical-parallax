## Summary

US military invasion of Panama on December 20, 1989 codenamed Operation Just Cause to remove dictator Manuel Noriega. Between 500 and 4,000 Panamanians were killed.

## Additional Context

The U.S. invasion of Panama (1989), commonly known as **Operation Just Cause**, was a large-scale military intervention that led to the removal of Panamanian leader Manuel Noriega from power.

### Stated objectives and outcomes

- U.S. officials cited objectives including protecting U.S. citizens, defending democracy, combating drug trafficking, and safeguarding the Panama Canal treaties.
- The operation resulted in significant fighting in urban areas and had substantial humanitarian and political consequences.

### Ongoing debate

- The invasion remains debated in terms of legality, proportionality, civilian impact, and its precedent for post–Cold War interventions.
