## Summary

Tenzin Gyatso, the 14th Dalai Lama, is the spiritual leader of Tibetan Buddhism who has become one of the world's most recognized and respected religious figures. He has led the Tibetan exile community since fleeing Chinese-occupied Tibet in 1959. He received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1989 for his non-violent struggle for Tibetan freedom. He has written extensively on Buddhist philosophy, science, compassion, and secular ethics.
