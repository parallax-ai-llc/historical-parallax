## Summary

Marianne Vos is the greatest female cyclist in history, winning Olympic gold, World Championships in road, cyclocross, and track.

## Multidiscipline dominance

Vos is notable for elite-level success across multiple cycling disciplines:

- **Road cycling** (one-day races, stage races, and national-team championships)
- **Cyclo-cross** (winter discipline with repeated world-title contention)
- **Track cycling** (including Olympic-level events)

This breadth is uncommon at the highest level, where specialization is typical.

## Major accomplishments (high level)

- Multiple **UCI World Championships** titles over the course of her career, including on the road and in cyclo-cross.
- Olympic champion status, with medals earned across different Olympic cycling formats.
- Victories in major women's road races and consistent leadership roles for top professional teams.

## Style and reputation

Vos is often described as a rider with a rare combination of:

- sprint finishing ability
- tactical race sense
- endurance for long, selective events

These traits allowed her to win from reduced-group sprints as well as from more selective finales.

## Legacy

In discussions of women's cycling history, Vos is frequently cited as one of the most successful and influential riders of her era, shaping expectations for all-round excellence across seasons and disciplines.
