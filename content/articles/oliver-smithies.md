## Summary

Oliver Smithies was a British-American geneticist who shared the 2007 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his discoveries of principles for introducing specific gene modifications in mice using embryonic stem cells. His gene targeting technique (gene knockout) became essential for understanding gene function and modeling human diseases. He also developed gel electrophoresis for separating proteins.
