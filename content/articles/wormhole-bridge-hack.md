## Summary

Hackers exploited a vulnerability in the Wormhole cross-chain bridge to steal $320 million in wrapped Ethereum. Jump Crypto covered the losses.

## Context

Wormhole is a cross-chain bridge designed to move assets and messages between different blockchains.
Bridges often rely on smart contracts plus off-chain validation to confirm that an asset was locked on one chain before minting a representation on another.
Because they sit at the intersection of multiple ecosystems, bridges have historically been high-value targets for attackers.

## What Happened

The Wormhole incident is commonly described as an exploit that resulted in the unauthorized minting of wrapped ETH on Solana.
Once minted, the attacker could convert or move value, effectively extracting funds from the bridge’s reserves.
The reported loss figure of roughly $320 million made it one of the larger DeFi exploits of its period.

## Response

- The bridge operator and ecosystem partners worked to contain the incident and restore confidence.
- Jump Crypto, which was associated with Wormhole, replenished funds to cover the shortfall reported in public summaries.
- Post-incident, the event accelerated calls for stronger bridge security reviews, monitoring, and safer upgrade practices.

## Why It Matters

The hack reinforced a broader lesson in crypto security: interoperability layers can concentrate risk.
It is frequently cited in discussions about audit quality, defense-in-depth, and the difficulty of securing complex cross-chain designs.
