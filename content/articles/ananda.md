## Summary

Ananda was the primary attendant and cousin of the Buddha, famous for his extraordinary memory. He is credited with reciting the Buddha's discourses (suttas) from memory at the First Buddhist Council after the Buddha's death, thus preserving the Sutta Pitaka. He advocated for the admission of women into the monastic order. He is one of the most beloved figures in Buddhist history.
