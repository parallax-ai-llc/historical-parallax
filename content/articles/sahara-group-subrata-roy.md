## Summary

Indian businessman jailed for contempt for failing to return $3.5 billion raised from investors.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1978 | Began building the Sahara India business group (commonly reported as starting with a small finance business). |
| 2000s | Sahara expanded into diverse businesses (real estate, media, hospitality, and sports sponsorship), becoming a high-profile conglomerate in India. |
| 2011 | India’s securities regulator (SEBI) scrutinized Sahara’s fundraising via OFCDs marketed to large numbers of investors. |
| 2012 | Supreme Court of India ordered Sahara to refund investors and deposit money with SEBI, launching a long-running compliance and verification process. |
| 2014 | Taken into custody after failing to meet court-ordered refund/deposit terms; detained in Delhi’s Tihar Jail in connection with contempt proceedings. |
| 2010s | Received limited interim releases/parole on specific humanitarian grounds while refund litigation and compliance disputes continued. |
| 2023 | Died in India after a prolonged legal controversy surrounding Sahara’s investor repayments. |

## Legal and Regulatory Issues (High Level)

- **Core dispute:** Indian courts and SEBI pursued Sahara for returning money raised from large numbers of investors through securities described as **optionally fully convertible debentures (OFCDs)**.
- **Refund mechanics:** Court orders focused on **depositing funds with SEBI** and verifying investor claims for repayment—an administrative process that can take years at scale.
- **Contempt and custody:** Roy’s detention is widely described as tied to **non-compliance with Supreme Court directions** on refunds and deposits.

## Why This Case Matters

- **Retail investor protection:** The dispute became a high-profile test of India’s ability to police mass fundraising and enforce repayment.
- **Regulatory perimeter:** It highlighted friction between corporate fundraising practices and securities-law definitions when offerings reach broad public distribution.
- **Reputation and governance:** Sahara’s brand and operations were repeatedly affected by the ongoing legal uncertainty and court supervision.

## Key Entities

- **Sahara India Pariwar / Sahara Group:** A conglomerate associated with finance, real estate, media, hospitality, and sponsorship activities.
- **SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India):** Primary regulator involved in the OFCD investor refund process.
- **Supreme Court of India:** Issued major refund-related orders and supervised compliance.
