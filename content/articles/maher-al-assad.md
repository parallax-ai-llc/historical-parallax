## Background

Maher al-Assad is a Syrian military figure and the younger brother of Syrian president Bashar al-Assad. He has been closely associated with elite military formations and with the Syrian state's security posture during periods of unrest.

## Military role

He is widely reported to have held a senior command position in the Syrian Arab Army, including leadership linked to the 4th Armoured Division. Coverage of the Syrian conflict often portrays this division as a well-equipped unit with significant influence in internal security operations.

## Syrian uprising and civil war context

In 2011, protests and state responses escalated into a broader conflict. Reporting frequently connects Maher al-Assad to major episodes of repression and the use of force against opposition activity, including early operations in southern Syria.

## Sanctions and international response

Multiple governments and international bodies have imposed sanctions on Syrian officials and entities during the civil war. Maher al-Assad has been listed in sanctions regimes that cite his senior military role and connection to state actions during the conflict.

## Allegations and accountability discussions

Human rights organizations and media reporting have alleged that Syrian security forces and aligned units committed serious violations during the war. References to Maher al-Assad in this context generally concern command responsibility and the conduct of forces under his influence; specific legal findings vary by jurisdiction and should be distinguished from allegations.
