## Summary

M.F. Husain was India's most internationally recognized modern painter, known for bold, colorful paintings of horses, Mother Teresa, and Indian mythology. A founding member of the Progressive Artists' Group, he was often called the Picasso of India. Controversy over his nude depictions of Hindu deities forced him into exile.
