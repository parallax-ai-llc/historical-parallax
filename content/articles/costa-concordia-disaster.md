## Summary

Sinking of the cruise ship Costa Concordia off the Italian island of Giglio on January 13, 2012 after Captain Francesco Schettino hit a reef. 32 people died and Schettino abandoned ship.

## Additional Context

- **Location:** The ship grounded near **Isola del Giglio** (Tuscany, Italy) during a close-to-shore maneuver that became central to later investigations.
- **Evacuation:** The incident involved a complex, nighttime evacuation; survivors described confusion and delays as the vessel listed.
- **Casualties:** Public reporting most often cites **32 deaths**.

## Investigation and Legal Outcome

- **Accountability:** Italian prosecutors pursued charges related to manslaughter and maritime safety violations.
- **Sentence:** Captain Francesco Schettino was sentenced to a lengthy prison term (commonly reported as **16 years**).
- **Industry impact:** The disaster prompted renewed emphasis on emergency drills, bridge procedures, and passenger safety briefings.

## Salvage and Environmental Response

- **Salvage operation:** The removal of the wreck was a major engineering project involving stabilization, refloating, and towing.
- **Environmental concerns:** Authorities monitored fuel and debris risks due to the sensitive coastal setting.

## Broader Themes

- **Bridge resource management:** The case is frequently cited in maritime safety discussions about decision-making, communication, and adherence to navigation protocols.
- **Crisis communication:** The timing and clarity of distress calls and orders were scrutinized in official reviews.
