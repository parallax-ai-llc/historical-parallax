## Summary

Series of killings in and around Beni territory in North Kivu, eastern DRC, beginning in 2014 and often attributed to armed groups including the Allied Democratic Forces (ADF). The violence has involved civilian massacres, displacement, and contested narratives about perpetrators and security failures.

## Conflict Context

Beni territory sits within a wider landscape of protracted conflict in eastern DRC, where multiple armed groups, local militias, and regional dynamics intersect.

Factors commonly discussed in analysis include:

- competition over land, resources, and political influence
- weak state security capacity and fragmented command structures
- cross-border militant networks and shifting alliances

## Humanitarian Impact

The massacres have contributed to repeated waves of displacement and long-term insecurity for communities.

High-level consequences often include:

- disruption of farming and local markets
- pressure on humanitarian services and shelter capacity
- heightened communal tensions and cycles of retaliation fears

## Attribution and Accountability

While many reports have linked major incidents to the ADF, attribution has at times been contested, with public debate over the roles of different armed actors and possible failures by security forces.

Calls from civil society and international observers have emphasized:

- credible investigations and transparent reporting
- protection of civilians and early-warning mechanisms
- support for survivors and community-level recovery
