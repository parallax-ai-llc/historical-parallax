## Additional Context

### Name and Duplicate Entry Note

- **Dorothy Crowfoot Hodgkin** is the same person widely known as **Dorothy Mary Crowfoot Hodgkin** (often shortened to **Dorothy Hodgkin**).
- This article exists as a **name-variant entry**; content may overlap with other Dorothy Hodgkin pages in the collection.

### Scientific Contributions

- Hodgkin was a pioneer of **X-ray crystallography**, using diffraction data to determine the three-dimensional structures of complex molecules.
- Her most celebrated structure determinations include:
  - **Penicillin** (clarifying its β-lactam structure during WWII-era research)
  - **Vitamin B12** (a major achievement in structural chemistry)
  - **Insulin** (a long-running project culminating in a full structure solution)

### Recognition

- She received the **1964 Nobel Prize in Chemistry** for her work on the structures of important biochemical substances.
- Her career is frequently highlighted as an example of how advances in experimental methods can reshape both **chemistry** and **molecular biology**.

### Public Life

- Beyond research, Hodgkin was involved in scientific education and international scientific cooperation, and is remembered for combining high-impact scholarship with public engagement.
