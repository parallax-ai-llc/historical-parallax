## Summary

Somali dictator who ruled for 21 years and ordered the bombardment of Hargeisa killing up to 50,000 civilians. His overthrow plunged Somalia into decades of civil war and state collapse.

## Additional Context

### Core facts already established above
- Took power in Somalia in a 1969 military coup and ruled for 21 years.
- Ordered the bombardment of Hargeisa, associated with mass civilian deaths.
- Overthrown in 1991; the overthrow is linked to decades of civil war and state collapse.

### Why the fall of a long dictatorship can be destabilizing
- Long rule can centralize decision-making while weakening succession and dispute-resolution mechanisms.
- When a regime collapses quickly, armed actors may compete to fill the vacuum.
- Civilian harm during late-regime violence can harden grievances and fragment society.
