## Summary

American politician who was convicted of sexting a minor. His laptop, seized during the investigation, contained emails that FBI Director Comey used to reopen the Clinton email investigation 11 days before the 2016 election.

## Political Career (Context)

Weiner served in the U.S. House of Representatives as a Democrat from New York. In Congress, he was associated with domestic-policy debates typical of the period, and he became a recognizable media figure in part because of his communication style and television appearances.

## Scandals and Legal Case (High Level)

- **2011 resignation:** Weiner resigned from Congress following public revelations involving sexually explicit messages.
- **2013 mayoral campaign:** He attempted a political comeback by running for Mayor of New York City; additional explicit-message reporting emerged during the campaign.
- **2016–2017 federal prosecution:** The investigation into his communications led to federal charges; he pleaded guilty to a felony count related to transferring obscene material to a minor and was sentenced to prison.

## Broader Public Impact

The seizure of Weiner’s laptop became a notable element of U.S. political history because investigators found emails relevant to a separate, high-profile inquiry. Public reporting at the time connected these developments to the closing weeks of the 2016 U.S. presidential election.
