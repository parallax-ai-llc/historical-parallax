## Summary

ISIS-linked gunmen attacked a bus of Coptic Christians in Minya killing 29 including children.

## Background and Context

- The victims were Coptic Christians, a religious minority in Egypt.
- The attack occurred during a period of heightened militant violence in Egypt, particularly involving ISIS-linked cells.
- The targeted bus was traveling in Minya Governorate, a region south of Cairo that has been affected by sectarian tension and insurgent attacks.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- Date: 2017-05-26.
- Target: A bus (and associated vehicles, as reported) carrying Coptic Christian pilgrims.
- Location: Near the Monastery of Saint Samuel the Confessor, on a desert road in Minya Governorate.
- Method: Armed assailants stopped the vehicles and opened fire.
- Reported casualties: Widely reported as around 28–29 people killed (including children) and multiple injured.

## Immediate Aftermath

- Egyptian authorities treated the incident as a terrorist attack and launched security operations in response.
- The attack drew domestic and international condemnation, including statements of solidarity with Egypt’s Coptic community.

## Attribution and Claims

- The perpetrators were described in reporting as ISIS-linked gunmen.
- As with many such incidents, attribution in early reporting can rely on claims of responsibility and subsequent security investigations.

## Why It Matters

- The Minya Bus Attack is frequently cited as an example of targeted violence against religious minorities in modern Egypt.
- It contributed to broader debates about security policy, protection of vulnerable communities, and counterterrorism strategy.
