## Summary

Pierre Deligne is a Belgian mathematician who received the Fields Medal in 1978 for proving the last of André Weil's conjectures, the Riemann hypothesis for algebraic varieties over finite fields. He also received the Abel Prize in 2013. His work in algebraic geometry has been profoundly influential.
