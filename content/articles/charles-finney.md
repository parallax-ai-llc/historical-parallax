## Summary

Charles Grandison Finney was an American evangelist and theologian who was the leading figure of the Second Great Awakening. He pioneered 'new measures' in revivalism, including the anxious bench and protracted meetings. He served as president of Oberlin College, one of the first colleges to admit women and African Americans. His Lectures on Revivals of Religion remains an influential work on evangelism.
