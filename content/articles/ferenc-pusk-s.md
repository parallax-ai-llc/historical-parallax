## Summary

Ferenc Puskás scored 84 goals in 85 internationals and led Hungary's 'Mighty Magyars.' The FIFA goal award bears his name.

## Additional Context

### Playing style and reputation

Puskás was known for powerful left-footed shooting, elite positioning, and high-volume goal scoring. He became a symbol of Hungarian football during the 1950s and later enjoyed major success with Real Madrid.

### Club and international highlights

Commonly noted milestones include:

- Key figure for Budapest Honvéd and the Hungarian national team in the "Mighty Magyars" era.
- Participation in Hungary's famous win over England at Wembley in 1953.
- Later career in Spain with Real Madrid, including European Cup success.

### Legacy

His name is attached to major commemorations of football achievement, including:

- The FIFA Puskás Award, given for an outstanding goal.
- Ongoing cultural status in Hungary, where he is remembered as a national sporting icon.
