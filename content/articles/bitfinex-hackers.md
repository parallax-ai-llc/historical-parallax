## Summary

Ilya Lichtenstein and Heather Morgan stole 120,000 Bitcoin from the Bitfinex exchange in 2016 and attempted to launder the $4.5 billion through elaborate schemes. Morgan was also an aspiring rapper known as Razzlekhan.

## The 2016 Bitfinex Hack (Context)

- Bitfinex suffered a major security breach in August 2016 resulting in the theft of roughly 120,000 BTC.
- The incident became one of the largest exchange thefts of its era and remained a prominent open investigation for years.

## Laundering Methods (Reported)

- U.S. prosecutors described a multi-step laundering effort involving thousands of transactions, the use of mixing/obfuscation techniques, and movement across multiple services and accounts.
- Some funds were allegedly converted into other assets and spent via cards and accounts tied to false or layered identities.

## Investigation and Seizure

- In 2022, U.S. authorities announced the seizure of a large portion of the stolen bitcoin, which they described as the largest financial seizure in Department of Justice history at the time.
- The case drew attention to blockchain forensics and the long time horizons that can apply to tracing large cryptocurrency thefts.

## Legal Outcomes

- The defendants faced federal charges including conspiracy to launder money and conspiracy to defraud the United States.
- Public reporting describes guilty pleas related to money laundering, with sentencing to be determined by the federal court process.
