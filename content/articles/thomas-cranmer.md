## Summary

Thomas Cranmer was the Archbishop of Canterbury who shaped the English Reformation and created the Book of Common Prayer. He supported Henry VIII's break with Rome and annulled the king's marriage to Catherine of Aragon. His liturgical prose in the Book of Common Prayer profoundly influenced the English language. He was burned at the stake under Queen Mary I.
