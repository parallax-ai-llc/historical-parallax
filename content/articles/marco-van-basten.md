## Summary

Marco van Basten won three Ballon d'Or awards. His 1988 Euro final volley is one of football's greatest goals.

## Additional Context

### Club career (high level)

Van Basten emerged at **Ajax**, where his goal-scoring rate quickly made him one of Europe’s standout strikers. He later moved to **AC Milan**, becoming a central figure in a dominant Milan side that won major domestic and European trophies.

### Style of play

He is often described as a complete center-forward: strong movement in the box, elite finishing with either foot, aerial ability, and a notable capacity for technically difficult strikes (volleys and first-time shots). His 1988 European Championship final goal is a widely cited example of this skill set.

### International record

For the Netherlands, van Basten’s peak years culminated at **UEFA Euro 1988**, where he finished as top scorer and the team won the tournament.

### Injuries and early retirement

A recurring ankle problem sharply limited his availability and ultimately ended his playing career unusually early for a player of his stature. This has made his achievements a frequent “what could have been” reference in football history.

### Post-playing roles

After retiring, he remained in football as a coach and technical figure, including head-coaching stints and advisory work. Assessments of his later career often separate his iconic playing legacy from the different demands of management.

He is consistently placed among the greatest strikers of the modern era.
