## Summary

Mary Barra became the first female CEO of a major global automaker when she was named CEO of General Motors in 2014. She navigated GM through the ignition switch recall crisis, invested heavily in electric vehicles (Ultium platform), and acquired Cruise for autonomous driving. Under her leadership, GM committed to an all-electric future and launched the GMC Hummer EV and Chevrolet Equinox EV. She transformed GM's culture from bureaucratic to entrepreneurial.
