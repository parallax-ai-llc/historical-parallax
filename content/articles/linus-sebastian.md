## Summary

Linus Sebastian is a Canadian YouTuber, tech reviewer, and entrepreneur best known as the founder and CEO of Linus Media Group (LMG), the company behind the massively popular YouTube channel Linus Tech Tips. With over 15 million subscribers on his flagship channel alone, Sebastian has become one of the most recognizable voices in consumer technology media, helping millions of viewers navigate hardware purchases, PC builds, and the broader tech industry.

His influence extends well beyond simple product reviews. Sebastian helped define the modern format of tech YouTube — fast-paced, personality-driven, and deeply technical — and built a media company that operates multiple channels, employs dozens of staff, and generates content at a scale rarely seen in independent online media. Through his work, he has shaped how both enthusiasts and everyday consumers think about technology.

## Early Life & Background

Linus Gabriel Sebastian was born on August 20, 1986, in Victoria, British Columbia, Canada. He grew up in the Greater Vancouver area and developed an early passion for computers and consumer electronics. He attended Trinity Western University but did not complete his degree, instead pursuing opportunities in the tech industry directly.

His entry into professional tech media came through NCIX (Newegg Canada's competitor), a Canadian computer hardware retailer, where he worked in their media department creating product videos and reviews. This role gave him hands-on experience with hardware and on-camera presentation, laying the groundwork for his future career.

## Rise to Prominence

Sebastian launched the Linus Tech Tips YouTube channel in 2008 while still working at NCIX. The channel grew steadily through detailed hardware reviews, PC build guides, and commentary on the tech industry. In 2013, he left NCIX to found Linus Media Group full-time alongside his wife Yvonne Ho, who serves as COO.

LMG expanded aggressively throughout the 2010s, launching additional channels including Techquickie, ShortCircuit, and the WAN Show podcast. By 2020, the network collectively surpassed 30 million subscribers across all channels. The company also launched Floatplane, a subscription-based video platform designed as a creator-friendly alternative to YouTube, offering early access to content for paying subscribers.

## Key Achievements

- Founded Linus Media Group, growing it into one of the largest independent tech media companies in the world
- Linus Tech Tips surpassed 15 million subscribers, with total LMG network views exceeding billions
- Launched Floatplane Media as a standalone creator monetization platform, attracting multiple prominent YouTubers
- Hosted and co-organized LTX (Linus Tech Expo), an annual fan and industry event in Vancouver
- Consistently ranked among the most influential figures in tech media by publications including Forbes and PCMag

## Controversies & Criticism

In August 2023, Sebastian and LMG faced significant public backlash following a video by Gamers Nexus that documented alleged quality control failures, factual inaccuracies in LMG videos, and the mishandling of a prototype GPU sent by a small manufacturer. The controversy — widely referred to online as "Linus Tech Tips drama" — prompted Sebastian to temporarily step back from content creation and commission an internal review. LMG subsequently published a detailed response outlining process improvements and editorial changes. The episode raised broader questions about accuracy standards in large-scale tech media operations.

## Legacy & Influence

Sebastian's most durable contribution may be demonstrating that independent tech media can operate at the scale of traditional broadcasters while remaining creator-led. His career helped establish YouTube as a viable long-term platform for technical journalism and pushed manufacturers to engage more transparently with online reviewers. Floatplane represents his ongoing bet on decentralizing video distribution beyond a single platform. As of 2026, LMG continues to be a benchmark for production quality and business viability in the creator economy.
