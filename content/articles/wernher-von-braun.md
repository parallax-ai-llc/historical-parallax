## Summary

Wernher von Braun was a German-American aerospace engineer who was one of the most important figures in the development of rocket technology and space exploration. He led the development of the V-2 rocket for Nazi Germany during World War II, which used forced labor from concentration camps. After the war, he moved to the United States and became a central figure in NASA, designing the Saturn V rocket that carried astronauts to the Moon.
