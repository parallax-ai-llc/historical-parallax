## Summary

Vandana Mataji (Sister Vandana Joshi) was an Indian Catholic nun who was a pioneer of Hindu-Christian inculturation. She lived as a Christian sannyasini (renunciant) and integrated Hindu spiritual practices into her Christian life. She founded Christa Prema Seva Ashram and later led Jeevan Dhara Ashram in the Himalayas. Her books, including Gurus, Ashrams and Christians, explored the meeting of Hindu and Christian spirituality.
