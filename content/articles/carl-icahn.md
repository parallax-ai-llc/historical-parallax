## Summary

Carl Icahn is one of Wall Street's most feared activist investors, known for taking large stakes in companies and pushing for changes to increase shareholder value. He gained fame with his hostile takeover of TWA in 1985. His investment style, known as the 'Icahn Lift,' involves buying undervalued companies and pressuring management for change. He has targeted major corporations including Apple, eBay, and Herbalife.
