## Context: Marshall Islands and Compact funds

- The Republic of the Marshall Islands (RMI) is a Pacific island nation that has a Compact of Free Association (COFA) relationship with the United States.
- In general usage, “compact funds” refers to U.S.-provided financial assistance associated with COFA arrangements and related grant programs.
- These funds are typically intended to support public services and government operations, and are commonly subject to auditing and oversight requirements.

## What “embezzlement of compact funds” usually implies

- “Embezzlement” is a form of financial wrongdoing involving the misappropriation of money that an individual was entrusted to manage or disburse.
- In public-sector cases, this can involve diversions of grant money, improper reimbursements, or unauthorized personal use of public resources.
- Convictions in such cases generally indicate that a court found the elements of the offense proven under the applicable criminal statute(s).
