## Summary

North Korean agents who bombed the Martyrs' Mausoleum in Rangoon, killing 21 including South Korean officials.

## Context

- **Target and setting:** The 1983 Rangoon bombing was aimed at a visiting South Korean delegation during an official trip to Burma (now Myanmar).
- **Location:** The attack is widely described as occurring at the **Martyrs' Mausoleum** in Rangoon (Yangon), a major national memorial site.
- **Attribution:** Contemporary reporting and later summaries commonly attribute the operation to **North Korean agents**.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The operation involved planting and detonating an explosive device at the memorial site around the time of a planned visit.
- The blast killed and injured members of the South Korean party and local individuals present at the scene.
- The incident is frequently cited as one of the most consequential overseas attacks linked to North Korea during the Cold War era.

## Aftermath and Impact

- **Diplomatic fallout:** The bombing contributed to significant diplomatic consequences for North Korea in the region and intensified security concerns around high-level visits.
- **Security implications:** The attack is commonly referenced in discussions of protective security, advance-site screening, and the vulnerability of public memorial venues.
- **Historical significance:** In Korean modern history narratives, the event is often discussed alongside other major North Korea–South Korea confrontations.

## Notes

- This entry uses the name **"Rangoon Bombing Team"** as a collective label for the perpetrators described in public sources.
- Public accounts vary on the level of detail available for individual operatives; many summaries focus on the operation and its consequences rather than biographies.
