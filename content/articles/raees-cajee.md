## Summary

Co-founder of AfriCrypt who vanished with his brother and billions in Bitcoin.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2021 | Vanished with $3.6 billion |

## Key Context

- **AfriCrypt** was a South Africa–based cryptocurrency investment platform associated with brothers **Raees Cajee** and **Ameer Cajee**.
- The case is frequently cited as a major early example of **crypto fraud / alleged exit scam risk** in lightly regulated retail crypto investing.
- Reporting commonly summarizes alleged losses at **approximately 69,000 BTC** (often valued around **US$3.6B** at the time), though exact totals are disputed and depend on valuation date and claimant set.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **April 2021 (reported):** Clients were informed the platform had suffered a **“hack”** and were asked not to pursue legal action during an alleged recovery period.
- **Soon after:** The Cajee brothers were reported to be **unreachable**, and investigators alleged funds were moved through **mixers/tumblers** to reduce traceability.
- **Aftermath:** Law firms and investigators in South Africa reportedly began efforts to trace assets and assess claims; recovery prospects were widely described as uncertain.
