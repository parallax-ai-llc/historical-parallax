## Summary

Shah Waliullah Dehlawi was an Indian Islamic scholar, reformer, and Sufi who is considered one of the most important Muslim thinkers of the 18th century. He translated the Quran into Persian, making it accessible to non-Arabic speakers in South Asia for the first time. He sought to reconcile the different schools of Islamic jurisprudence and to revive Islamic thought. His intellectual legacy shaped modern Islamic reform movements in South Asia.
