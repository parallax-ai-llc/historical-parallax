## Background

- Born in Port-au-Prince, Haiti (1961).[^1]
- Became widely known as a musician and performer under the stage name **"Sweet Micky"** before entering politics.[^1]

## Political Career

- Entered electoral politics after a long entertainment career and built a public profile around populist messaging.[^1]
- Served as **President of Haiti (2011–2016)**.[^1]
- His administration operated amid major reconstruction and governance challenges following the 2010 earthquake.[^1]

## Presidency (High-Level Themes)

The public record commonly associates Martelly’s presidency with:

- Post-disaster reconstruction and aid coordination
- Persistent political polarization and institutional instability
- Ongoing concerns about corruption and accountability (as described by journalists and civil-society groups)

## Sanctions & Allegations

- Public reporting and official actions have linked Martelly to allegations involving corruption and relationships with armed groups.
- Canada has announced sanctions against multiple Haitian political and economic figures, including Martelly, citing alleged links to criminal activity and gang violence (as summarized by Wikipedia and widely reported at the time).[^1]
