## Summary

Ibn Rushd (Averroes) was the most influential Islamic philosopher in European intellectual history and a pioneering physician. His medical encyclopedia Kitab al-Kulliyat (Colliget) was used across Europe, and his commentaries on Aristotle profoundly influenced Western philosophy. He championed rational inquiry and the compatibility of faith and reason.
