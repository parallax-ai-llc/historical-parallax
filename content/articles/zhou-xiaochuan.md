## Summary

Zhou Xiaochuan served as Governor of the People's Bank of China from 2002 to 2018, the longest tenure in the institution's history. He managed China's currency policy, oversaw the internationalization of the renminbi, and navigated China through the 2008 global financial crisis. His push for RMB inclusion in the IMF's Special Drawing Rights basket was a landmark achievement for Chinese finance.
