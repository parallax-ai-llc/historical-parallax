## Summary

Guerrero drug lord known as 'El Vago' who controlled heroin and opium production in Mexico's Sierra Madre. He was one of the most wanted traffickers in southern Mexico.

## Additional Context

## Regional Context (Guerrero)

- Guerrero has been a focal point for opium poppy cultivation and heroin trafficking, partly due to mountainous terrain and limited state presence in some areas.
- Trafficking dynamics in the region have often involved shifting alliances among local armed groups and larger national networks.

## Alleged Role and Activities (High-level)

- Cruz Sánchez (often associated with the nickname “El Vago” in reporting) has been described as linked to the control of production areas and local trafficking corridors.
- Such roles typically involve managing growers, protecting laboratories, moving product to handoff points, and handling cash flows.

## Law Enforcement Pressure

- Mexican federal and state authorities have periodically released “most wanted” lists that reflect both intelligence assessments and political priorities.
- The emphasis on Guerrero’s heroin economy increased during periods of heightened violence and fragmentation among criminal organizations.
