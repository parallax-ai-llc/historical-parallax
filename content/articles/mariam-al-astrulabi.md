## Summary

Mariam al-Astrulabi was a 10th-century Syrian woman astronomer and astrolabe maker, one of the few known female scientists of the medieval Islamic world. She apprenticed under her father, a noted astrolabe craftsman, and became skilled in designing and constructing astrolabes used for astronomical calculation and navigation. The main-belt asteroid 7060 is named after her.
