## Summary

Tim Berners-Lee launched the World Wide Web on August 6, 1991. His HTML, URL, and HTTP inventions transformed the internet into a global information platform used by billions.

## Additional Context

## What “Created” Usually Refers To

The creation of the World Wide Web is generally associated with Tim Berners‑Lee’s late‑1980s and early‑1990s work at CERN, where he proposed and built a system for linking and retrieving documents over the internet using hypertext.

## Core Building Blocks

- **URL/URI:** a standard way to identify resources.
- **HTTP:** a protocol for requesting and delivering resources.
- **HTML:** a markup language for documents that can include hyperlinks.

## Why It Was Different From “the Internet”

The internet is the underlying network of networks and protocols (like TCP/IP). The Web is an application layer built on top of the internet that made information browsing and linking accessible through standard formats.

## Early Adoption

The web’s growth accelerated when web software became broadly available and when standards were published openly, enabling independent implementations and rapid expansion.
