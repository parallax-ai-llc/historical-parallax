## Summary

Martha Graham was an American dancer and choreographer who is considered the mother of modern dance. Her revolutionary technique, based on contraction and release, created a new vocabulary of movement. She choreographed 181 works including Appalachian Spring and continued performing into her 70s, making her the longest-performing dancer in history.
