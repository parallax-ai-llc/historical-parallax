## Summary

Hamilton Smith is an American microbiologist who shared the 1978 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for the discovery of restriction enzymes and their application to problems of molecular genetics. His discovery of the first type II restriction enzyme enabled precise cutting of DNA at specific sequences, making genetic engineering possible.
