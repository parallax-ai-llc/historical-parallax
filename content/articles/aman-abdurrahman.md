## Summary

Indonesian ISIS ideologue who inspired multiple terrorist attacks in Indonesia from prison including the 2016 Jakarta attacks. He was sentenced to death.

## Background

- Known as an Indonesian extremist cleric/ideologue associated in public reporting with pro-IS (Islamic State) networks.
- Frequently described as operating and influencing supporters while incarcerated, via messaging and intermediaries rather than direct operational command.

## Ideology and Influence (High-Level)

- Promoted takfiri-jihadist interpretations that framed Indonesian state authorities and security forces as legitimate targets.
- Served as a symbolic figure for segments of Indonesia's militant ecosystem, where propaganda, recruitment, and doctrinal justification can matter as much as logistics.
- His perceived role illustrates a recurring counterterrorism challenge: detention may constrain mobility but not necessarily ideological reach.

## Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- Indonesian courts convicted him on terrorism-related charges; he received a death sentence (reported in 2018).
- Public summaries of the case often emphasize incitement/instigation and ideological facilitation as the central allegations, alongside links to specific plots and attacks.

## Broader Significance

- Demonstrates how prison-based radicalization and external communications can sustain extremist influence.
- Used by analysts as a case study for the relationship between ideology, charismatic authority, and downstream violence carried out by small cells.
