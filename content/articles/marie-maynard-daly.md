## Summary

Marie Maynard Daly was an American biochemist who was the first African-American woman to earn a PhD in chemistry in the United States. She made important contributions to understanding the relationship between cholesterol and cardiovascular disease. Her research on the effects of sugar, cholesterol, and other nutrients on the cardiovascular system was pioneering. She also worked to increase minority enrollment in medical school programs.
