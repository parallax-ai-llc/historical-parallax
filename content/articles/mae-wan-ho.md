## Summary

Mae-Wan Ho was a pioneering biophysicist known for her research on the liquid crystalline nature of organisms and her critique of genetic engineering. She developed techniques for visualizing living organisms using interference colors and was an influential voice in the debate over genetically modified organisms and their environmental impact.
