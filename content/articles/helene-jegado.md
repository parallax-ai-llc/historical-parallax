## Summary

French serial killer who poisoned 36 people with arsenic between 1833 and 1851 while working as a domestic servant in Brittany. She moved between households, poisoning residents at each. Guillotined in Rennes.

## Additional Context

- Accounts of Jegado’s crimes are closely tied to 19th-century domestic service, where cooks and household staff often had direct access to food preparation.
- **Arsenic** was historically common in household and commercial products, which could make suspicious poisonings harder to detect at the time.
- The number of victims attributed to her is discussed in historical sources; court proceedings focused on a subset of cases sufficient for conviction.

## Themes

- **Mobility and anonymity:** moving between employers can reduce scrutiny and delay pattern recognition.
- **Forensic limits:** medical and investigative constraints in the mid-1800s shaped how poisoning cases were proven.
- **Public fear and sensational coverage:** notorious poisoning trials often became major news events in 19th-century Europe.
