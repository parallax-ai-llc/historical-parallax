## Scam Methods (High Level)

- **Impersonation scripts**: Call center fraud schemes often rely on standardized scripts that impersonate government agencies (e.g., tax authorities) or well-known technology brands.
- **Urgency and fear**: Victims are pushed into quick decisions using threats (arrest, fines) or claims of immediate account compromise.
- **Payment channels**: Common payment mechanisms include wire transfers, prepaid cards, or other hard-to-reverse transactions.

## Operational Structure

- **Division of labor**: These operations typically separate roles (lead “closers,” initial callers, supervisors, money movers).
- **Lead generation**: Targets may be reached via robocalls, pop-up ads, spoofed numbers, or purchased lead lists.
- **Cross-border laundering**: Proceeds are often layered through intermediaries and accounts to reduce traceability.

## Law Enforcement Response

- **International coordination**: Investigations frequently involve cooperation between Indian authorities and U.S./U.K. agencies, especially when victims are overseas.
- **Evidence types**: Casework can include call recordings, seized computers, payment records, and victim statements.

## Impact

- **Victim harm**: Beyond financial loss, victims often experience stress and ongoing identity-security concerns.
- **Public awareness**: High-profile prosecutions are used to deter similar operations and to educate the public about impersonation fraud.
