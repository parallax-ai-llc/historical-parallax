## Summary

Giovanni Cassini was an Italian-French astronomer who discovered four moons of Saturn and the gap in Saturn's rings now known as the Cassini Division. He made accurate measurements of the distances of planets from the Sun and was the first director of the Paris Observatory. NASA's Cassini spacecraft, which explored Saturn from 2004 to 2017, was named in his honor.
