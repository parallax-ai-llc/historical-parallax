## Summary

Chinese spree killer who murdered 15 people and robbed military armories in Beijing between 1996 and 1998. He was a former convict who sought revenge on society. Executed by shooting.

## Background

Bai Baoshan is known from Chinese criminal reporting as a perpetrator of multiple murders and robberies in the late 1990s. His crimes have been described as both violent and highly instrumental, aimed at acquiring weapons and money.

## Pattern of Crimes (High Level)

Public summaries typically emphasize:

- repeated attacks over a multi-year period
- the use of firearms and theft of weapons
- multiple victims across separate incidents

Because “spree” and “serial” categories can be used differently across jurisdictions, many accounts focus on the concrete timeline and confirmed victim counts rather than a single label.

## Investigation and Trial

The case is often referenced as an example of intensive manhunt-style policing and the difficulties of tracking mobile offenders. After arrest, Bai was tried, convicted, and executed under China’s criminal-justice system.

## Public Impact

High-casualty violent crime cases in major cities such as Beijing tend to receive significant attention and can influence public discussion about policing, gun control enforcement, and the communication of risk during active investigations.
