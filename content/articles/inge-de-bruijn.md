## Summary

Dutch sprint freestyle swimmer who became one of the defining athletes of her era, winning multiple Olympic and world titles and helping set a new standard for women’s sprint events in the late 1990s and early 2000s.

## Specialization and racing style

De Bruijn is most closely associated with sprint freestyle (50m and 100m) and was also competitive in butterfly events. Sprint swimming at the highest level typically depends on start reaction, underwater phases, stroke rate, and the ability to maintain speed with minimal deceleration. Her peak period coincided with rapid evolution in training methods and the professionalization of national programs.

## Major achievements

Across her prime years, her results are commonly summarized by:

- Olympic medals, including golds in sprint freestyle.
- World championship titles and podium finishes.
- Periods holding world records or world-leading times in multiple events.

Her prominence also reflected the Netherlands’ broader strength in swimming during that era, including relay depth and a strong coaching pipeline.

## Impact on Dutch sport

In the Netherlands, de Bruijn became a high-visibility figure for individual Olympic success in swimming. Her achievements are often referenced in discussions about how smaller nations can compete in elite aquatic sports through focused investment in coaching, sports science, and talent identification.

## Post-peak period and public profile

After her peak competitive period, she remained a recognizable sports personality. Like many top swimmers, her legacy is shaped not only by medals but also by the way her performances influenced expectations for speed in women’s sprint events.
