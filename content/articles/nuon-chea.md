## Summary

Cambodian politician known as 'Brother Number Two' in the Khmer Rouge hierarchy. He was convicted of genocide, crimes against humanity, and war crimes by the ECCC. He was the highest-ranking Khmer Rouge official tried.

## Role in the Khmer Rouge

Nuon Chea was widely described as a principal ideologue and senior decision-maker within the Khmer Rouge leadership. The title “Brother Number Two” reflected his position immediately below Pol Pot in the movement’s internal hierarchy.

## Trials at the ECCC

The Extraordinary Chambers in the Courts of Cambodia (ECCC) was created with international support to prosecute senior Khmer Rouge leaders. Proceedings against Nuon Chea focused on leadership responsibility for policies and actions carried out during Democratic Kampuchea, with judgments addressing crimes against humanity and, in later rulings, genocide-related charges.

## Historical Significance

Because many Khmer Rouge leaders died before they could be tried, the prosecution of Nuon Chea became a central case for documenting the regime’s crimes and establishing an official judicial record. The trial record is frequently cited in scholarship and public education about the Khmer Rouge period.

## Notes on Terminology

- **Khmer Rouge:** the communist movement that ruled Cambodia as Democratic Kampuchea.
- **ECCC:** a hybrid Cambodian-international tribunal.
- **“Brother Number Two”:** an informal designation indicating senior rank, not a formal state office.

## Public Memory

The convictions associated with Nuon Chea are frequently discussed as part of Cambodia’s effort to acknowledge mass violence and to create an evidentiary record for future generations, even as debates continue over the limits and timing of transitional justice.
