## Summary

Francis Xavier was a Spanish Jesuit missionary and co-founder of the Society of Jesus, considered the greatest Christian missionary since the Apostle Paul. He evangelized extensively in India, Southeast Asia, and Japan, baptizing tens of thousands. He attempted to enter China but died on Shangchuan Island off the Chinese coast. He was canonized in 1622 and is the patron saint of missions.
