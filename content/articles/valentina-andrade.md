## Summary

Brazilian UFO cult leader accused of ordering the castration and murder of young boys in ritualistic killings in Altamira, Pará. The Altamira case involved the mutilation of at least 19 boys.

## Background

- Andrade became known in Brazil as a leader of a spiritual/UFO-oriented group.
- Her name is associated in public reporting with the "Altamira" case in Pará, Brazil.

## Altamira case (overview)

- The case involved the abduction and mutilation of boys in Altamira, including castration in several instances.
- Investigations and prosecutions focused on whether the crimes were connected to ritualistic motives and a broader group.
- The case was widely covered and remains one of Brazil’s most notorious criminal investigations involving children.

## Legal proceedings

- Andrade was accused of ordering or orchestrating crimes tied to the Altamira killings.
- Court outcomes reported in sources include acquittals in later proceedings due to insufficient evidence.
- The case also generated controversy over investigative methods, evidentiary standards, and public pressure.

## Significance

- The Altamira case is often cited in discussions of moral panic, cult allegations, and miscarriages-of-justice risks.
- It also illustrates the challenges of prosecuting complex serial crimes with fragmented evidence and multiple suspects.
