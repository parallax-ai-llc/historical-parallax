## Summary

Japanese woman who murdered her lover Kichizo Ishida by strangulation during erotic asphyxiation in 1936 and then severed his genitalia. She carried them with her for days. The case became a cultural sensation in Japan. Served 5 years.

## Additional Context

## Background

Abe Sada worked in a variety of service and entertainment roles in early 20th-century Japan, including periods associated with geisha and sex work, before becoming known nationally due to the 1936 killing.

## Public and Cultural Impact

The case became widely reported and discussed in Japan, in part because it intersected with themes of sexuality, modern urban life, and sensational crime reporting. Over the decades, it has been referenced in Japanese popular culture and has inspired books, films, and stage works, often as a lens on censorship, desire, and social norms.

## Legal Aftermath and Later Life

Abe was convicted and served a prison sentence. After her release, public interest continued, but details of her later life and death have been treated as uncertain or not reliably documented in mainstream sources.
