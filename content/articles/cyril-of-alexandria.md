## Summary

Cyril of Alexandria was the Patriarch of Alexandria and a central figure in Christological controversies. He led the opposition to Nestorius at the Council of Ephesus in 431, defining the doctrine of the hypostatic union. His theological writings shaped the definition of Christ's nature adopted at Chalcedon. He is venerated as a Doctor of the Church.
