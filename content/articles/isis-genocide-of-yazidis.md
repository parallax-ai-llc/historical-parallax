## Summary

Genocide committed by ISIS against the Yazidi people in Sinjar, Iraq beginning August 2014. Over 5,000 were killed, 7,000 women and girls enslaved, and 400,000 displaced.

## Background

- The Yazidis are a religious minority with historic communities in northern Iraq, particularly around the Sinjar region.
- ISIS ideology framed Yazidis as legitimate targets, enabling mass violence and systematic abuses.

## Major Components of the Atrocities

- Mass killings during the August 2014 assault on Sinjar and surrounding areas.
- Large-scale abduction of women and girls, many of whom were subjected to sexual slavery and forced marriage.
- Forced displacement of civilians, including people trapped on Mount Sinjar during the initial assault.

## Humanitarian and Political Response

- The events triggered international humanitarian attention, including emergency aid and evacuation efforts.
- The crisis became a focus for documentation by journalists, NGOs, and international bodies seeking to preserve evidence.

## Recognition and Accountability

- Multiple international institutions and governments have described the campaign against Yazidis as genocide.
- Court cases in several jurisdictions have pursued prosecutions connected to ISIS crimes, including cases involving crimes against Yazidis.
- Long-term issues include the return of displaced families, reconstruction, and the search for missing persons.
