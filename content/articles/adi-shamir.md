## Additional Context

### Cryptography Contributions

Adi Shamir is an Israeli cryptographer and computer scientist. He is best known as a co‑inventor of the RSA public‑key cryptosystem (with Ron Rivest and Leonard Adleman), one of the most influential developments in modern cryptography.

### Other Notable Work

Shamir also introduced ideas such as Shamir’s Secret Sharing, a method for splitting a secret into parts so that only a chosen subset of parts can reconstruct it.

### Awards

He has received major honors in computing and cryptography, including the Turing Award (shared with his RSA co‑inventors).
