## Sport and discipline

Risako Kawai competes in **women's freestyle wrestling**, an Olympic discipline organized by weight classes. Across her career she has competed at different weights as international categories and Olympic cycles changed.[^1]

## Major achievements (overview)

High-level accomplishments associated with Kawai include:

- **Two Olympic gold medals** (Rio 2016 and Tokyo 2020/2021)[^1]
- Multiple medals at major international events (commonly including World Championships and Asian competitions)[^1]

## Competition profile

In elite wrestling, medals are typically influenced by:

- Match-to-match tactical adjustments (pace control, shot selection, mat awareness)
- Conditioning and injury management through tournament brackets

Kawai is often described as part of a strong Japanese women's wrestling system that has produced sustained Olympic success.[^1]

Kawai's back-to-back Olympic titles placed her among the most prominent Japanese wrestlers of her generation. Her results are frequently cited in discussions of:
