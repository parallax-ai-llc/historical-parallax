## Summary

Megan Rapinoe won two Women's World Cups and the 2019 Ballon d'Or. She was a leading advocate for pay equity.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2015 | Won Women's World Cup |
| 2019 | Won WC, Golden Boot, and Ballon d'Or |
| 2023 | Retired |

## Context

Rapinoe’s career is often described through two overlapping lenses: elite performance for club and country, and high-profile advocacy in debates around gender equity and LGBTQ+ rights.

## Playing profile (high-level)

- Known for left-footed delivery, crossing, and set-piece threat.
- Often deployed as a winger in systems that value wide play and service into the box.

## National team impact

- A central figure in the U.S. women’s national team during an era of sustained international success.
- Major tournaments during her career helped expand global attention and commercial growth in women’s football.

## Advocacy and public role

- Frequently associated with campaigns for pay equity in women’s football.
- Became a prominent athlete-activist figure in U.S. sports media.

## Legacy

- Remembered both for on-field achievements and for accelerating public conversation about compensation, visibility, and labor rights in women’s sports.
