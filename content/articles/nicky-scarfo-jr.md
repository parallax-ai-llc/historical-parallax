## Summary

American mobster and son of Philadelphia boss Nicodemo Scarfo. He was a key figure in the Lucchese crime family's illegal gambling operations. He survived being shot 7 times in a restaurant. Convicted of racketeering.

## Family and Underworld Context

Scarfo Jr. is the son of Nicodemo “Little Nicky” Scarfo, who led the Philadelphia crime family during the 1980s. His name is often discussed in accounts of how mafia influence and family ties can shape recruitment, expectations, and conflicts inside organized crime groups.

## Affiliations

While linked by family to Philadelphia’s mob history, Scarfo Jr. became associated with the Lucchese crime family, particularly through gambling and related racketeering activity.

## 1989 Shooting

He survived a high-profile attempted murder in 1989, when he was shot multiple times in a restaurant. Survival after such attacks is rare and the incident became a notable part of his public reputation, illustrating the volatility of internal and inter-group disputes.

## Racketeering Case and Conviction

Federal prosecutors later charged Scarfo Jr. in connection with organized crime operations, including illegal gambling and extortion-related conduct. He was convicted on racketeering counts and received a lengthy prison sentence.

## Significance

Scarfo Jr.’s career is frequently used as an example in modern U.S. organized-crime reporting: traditional mafia structures adapting to new enforcement pressure, reliance on gambling as a revenue source, and the role of violent conflict alongside courtroom outcomes.
