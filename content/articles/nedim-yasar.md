## Summary

Danish former gang leader shot dead moments after the launch of a book about leaving gang life.

## Background

- Born in Turkey to Kurdish parents and immigrated to Denmark as a child.
- Grew up in the Grantoften area of Ballerup, in Copenhagen’s western suburbs.

## Gang Involvement and Exit

- Became involved in the gang environment as a teenager.
- Founded and led Los Guerreros, described as a support club of the Bandidos.
- Served prison sentences for crimes including extortion and violence.
- Later entered an exit program for former gang members and publicly spoke about leaving criminal life.

## Public Role

- Worked as a radio host on the Danish talk station Radio24syv.
- Known for sharing first-hand experiences from gang life and for mentoring or speaking to at-risk youth.

## Death and Aftermath

- Shot in Copenhagen on 19 November 2018 and died the next day from his wounds.
- Killed shortly after attending a reception connected to a newly released book about his life and exit from gang life.
- Danish police arrested multiple suspects; court proceedings in subsequent years resulted in convictions and later an acquittal for one defendant on appeal.

## Works and Related Media

- A book about his life was released in 2018 (Danish title: *Rødder: En Gangsters Udvej*), written by his co-host Marie Louise Toksvig.
- His story is frequently referenced in Danish discussions about gang exit programs and rehabilitation.
