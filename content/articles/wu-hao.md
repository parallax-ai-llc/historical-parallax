## Summary

Wu Hao is a Chinese-American structural biologist at Boston Children's Hospital and Harvard Medical School who has determined the molecular structures of key immune system proteins. Her work on innate immunity signaling pathways has advanced understanding of inflammatory diseases and infection response.

## Field: Structural Biology and Immunology

Structural biologists aim to explain biological function by determining the **3D shapes** of macromolecules (proteins, complexes, and assemblies). In immunology, this can clarify how receptors recognize signals, how signaling complexes assemble, and how mutations shift immune responses.

Common approaches in the field include:

- X-ray crystallography
- cryo-electron microscopy (cryo-EM)
- complementary biochemical and biophysical assays

## Why Molecular Structures Matter

Knowing a structure can help researchers:

- map interaction interfaces between immune proteins
- propose mechanisms for activation or inhibition
- interpret disease-associated variants
- identify targets for therapeutic modulation

## Institutional Context

Work at major research hospitals and medical schools often connects structural discoveries to disease relevance, including inflammation and infection response, by linking molecular mechanisms to cell and organism-level phenotypes.
