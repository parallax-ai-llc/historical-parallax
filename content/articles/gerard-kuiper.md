## Summary

Gerard Kuiper was a Dutch-American astronomer who is considered the father of modern planetary science. He discovered the moons Miranda (Uranus) and Nereid (Neptune), and predicted the existence of the Kuiper Belt of icy bodies beyond Neptune. He pioneered infrared astronomical techniques and founded the Lunar and Planetary Laboratory at the University of Arizona. The Kuiper Belt is named in his honor.
