## Summary

Egyptian military general who overthrew elected President Morsi and has presided over the worst crackdown on human rights in Egyptian history. Rabaa massacre killed over 800 protesters.

## Positions and Power Base

- **Military career**: Rose through Egypt's armed forces, including military intelligence roles, and became **Minister of Defense** during the post-2011 transition.
- **Security apparatus**: His governance is commonly described as relying on the military, internal security services, and a tightly controlled media environment.

## Domestic Governance

### Political environment

- **Opposition suppression**: Authorities have pursued wide-ranging arrests and prosecutions of political opponents, including members of the Muslim Brotherhood and secular activists.
- **Press and civil society**: Egypt has faced sustained criticism from international rights groups for restrictions on media, NGOs, and public protest.

### Counterinsurgency and security

- **Sinai conflict**: The state has waged a long-running campaign against insurgent groups in North Sinai, framed as counterterrorism.

## Economy and Megaprojects

- **Macroeconomic reforms**: His administration pursued subsidy reductions and currency/IMF-linked reforms, often described as stabilizing in some metrics while increasing cost-of-living pressures.
- **State-led development**: Flagship projects have included large-scale infrastructure and construction initiatives associated with the military and state entities.

## Foreign Relations (High-Level)

- **Regional diplomacy**: Maintained close ties with Gulf partners and positioned Egypt as a regional security actor.
- **Great-power relations**: Balanced relationships with the United States, Russia, and European states, including over security cooperation and arms purchases.
