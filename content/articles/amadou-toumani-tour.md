## Summary

Former President of Mali overthrown in a 2012 coup while the country was losing territory to Islamist rebels. Found guilty in absentia of high treason.

## Background

Amadou Toumani Touré (often referred to as “ATT”) was a Malian soldier and political leader. He first came to international attention in 1991 when he led a military coup that removed President Moussa Traoré after mass protests. He then headed a transitional military-led government and oversaw elections that transferred power to a civilian administration.

## Political Role and Presidency

Touré returned to politics as a civilian and won the presidency in 2002, presenting himself as a consensus candidate. He was re-elected in 2007.

During his presidency, Mali faced persistent security and governance challenges, including armed rebellions and the growth of militant Islamist groups in the broader Sahel region. His administration also navigated coalition politics and relied on broad-based political agreements that supporters framed as stability-oriented, while critics argued they weakened accountability.

## 2012 Coup and Aftermath

In March 2012, Touré was overthrown by a military coup amid public and military frustration with the handling of the conflict in northern Mali, where the state was rapidly losing control of territory. The coup contributed to a severe political crisis and a security vacuum that accelerated instability.

After his removal, Touré lived outside Mali for a period. Legal and political processes later focused on alleged failures and decisions during his presidency; reporting has described proceedings that included accusations such as high treason, including judgments rendered while he was not present.

## Legacy

Touré’s public legacy is often discussed in terms of two contrasting narratives: his role in facilitating Mali’s early-1990s democratic transition, and the later deterioration of security and state capacity that culminated in the 2012 coup. Assessments vary across Mali’s political spectrum and among international observers.
