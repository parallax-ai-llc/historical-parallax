## Background

Winston Peters is a prominent New Zealand politician known for long-running involvement in national politics and for playing a recurring “kingmaker” role in coalition negotiations under New Zealand’s Mixed-Member Proportional (MMP) system.

## Political Career (high-level)

- **Member of Parliament:** Elected to Parliament in the late 1970s and served in multiple periods across several decades, representing electorates and later returning via party lists.
- **Party leadership:** Founded and led **New Zealand First**, a party associated with populist messaging and coalition bargaining power.
- **Senior cabinet roles:** Held major portfolios in coalition governments, including **Deputy Prime Minister** and **Minister of Foreign Affairs**.
