## Summary

Campaign of political repression by Stalin from 1936-1938. An estimated 750,000 people were executed and over a million sent to the Gulag. Show trials eliminated most Old Bolsheviks.

## Mechanisms of Repression (High-level)

- Repression operated through secret police investigations, coerced confessions, party disciplinary processes, and rapid judicial or quasi-judicial procedures.
- Public show trials served both as instruments of intimidation and as propaganda to frame opponents as traitors or conspirators.
- The purge extended beyond top political elites to the military, bureaucracy, and ordinary citizens, often through denunciations and quota-based arrest practices described in historical accounts.

## Consequences

- The period reshaped Soviet leadership by removing real and perceived rivals and consolidating Stalin’s personal power.
- Large-scale arrests and executions inflicted long-term social trauma, disrupted institutions, and contributed to pervasive fear and self-censorship.
- The repression also intersected with forced-labor systems, with many victims sent to the Gulag network.
