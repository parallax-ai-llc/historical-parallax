## Summary

Argentine naval officer known as the 'Angel of Death' who infiltrated the Mothers of the Plaza de Mayo during the Dirty War. He was responsible for the kidnapping of two French nuns and Swedish-Argentine teenager Dagmar Hagelin. Sentenced to life.

## Additional Context

### Dirty War and ESMA

Astiz served in Argentine naval intelligence during the last military dictatorship (1976–1983). He is widely associated with the ESMA detention center in Buenos Aires, which functioned as a major site of illegal detention, torture, and forced disappearances.

### Infiltration and kidnappings

He gained notoriety for infiltrating human-rights circles and for identifying targets for abduction. Among the best-known cases linked to him are the disappearance of French nuns Alice Domon and Léonie Duquet and the shooting of Dagmar Hagelin, who later died.

### Legal proceedings

For years, prosecutions were delayed by amnesty measures and jurisdictional disputes, including international cases in which victims’ families sought accountability. After those barriers were lifted, Argentine courts tried major dictatorship-era cases as crimes against humanity.

### Sentence and historical significance

Astiz received a life sentence in Argentina. His case is frequently referenced in discussions of transitional justice, state terror, and the role of intelligence services in targeting civil society during the dictatorship.
