## Summary

Harvey Cushing is considered the father of modern neurosurgery. He developed many of the basic surgical techniques for operating on the brain and described Cushing's syndrome and Cushing's disease. His meticulous approach reduced surgical mortality for brain tumors from nearly 90% to less than 10%.
