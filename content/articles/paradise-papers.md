## Summary

13.4 million documents leaked from offshore law firm Appleby revealed tax avoidance by corporations and wealthy individuals. Exposed offshore holdings of Queen Elizabeth II and many politicians.

## Background

- The leak was published in November 2017 by the International Consortium of Investigative Journalists (ICIJ) alongside media partners worldwide.
- The documents originated largely from Appleby (a Bermuda-based offshore law firm) and also included materials from corporate services providers such as Estera and Asiaciti Trust.
- The reporting focused on how offshore jurisdictions and corporate structures can be used for tax planning, asset protection, and—in some cases—tax evasion or money laundering.

## What the documents showed (high-level)

- Offshore companies and trusts linked to corporations, high-net-worth individuals, and political figures.
- Cross-border ownership chains that can obscure beneficial ownership (the natural person ultimately controlling an asset).
- The use of tax havens and low-tax jurisdictions to hold assets, finance deals, and route investments.

## Notable themes reported by partner outlets

- Politically exposed persons (PEPs) and public officials connected to offshore entities.
- Corporate tax planning structures involving subsidiaries in multiple jurisdictions.
- Luxury assets (e.g., yachts, real estate) held through offshore vehicles.

## Public impact and responses

- The leak intensified global discussion about transparency, beneficial ownership registries, and the regulation of offshore financial services.
- Some named individuals and firms disputed the reporting or emphasized that offshore structures can be legal when properly disclosed and taxed.
- Regulators and tax authorities in multiple countries reported reviewing or investigating information connected to the leak.

## Relationship to other major offshore leaks

- **Panama Papers (2016):** Earlier ICIJ-led investigation based on documents from Mossack Fonseca.
- **Pandora Papers (2021):** Later ICIJ investigation covering offshore holdings across many service providers.
