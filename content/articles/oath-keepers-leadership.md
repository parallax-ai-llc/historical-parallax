## Background

The Oath Keepers are a U.S.-based far-right militia movement that has recruited current and former military, law-enforcement, and first-responder personnel around the idea of refusing orders they view as unconstitutional. Public reporting and court filings associated with January 6, 2021 describe a leadership structure that coordinated regional members, fundraising, and mobilization for events.

## Notable figures (commonly cited)

- **Stewart Rhodes** — founder and long-time public leader; convicted in federal court for seditious conspiracy related to January 6.
- **Kelly Meggs** — Florida-based leader described in court records as a key organizer; convicted of seditious conspiracy.
- **Jessica Watkins** — Ohio leader associated with an Oath Keepers group; convicted on charges including seditious conspiracy.
- **Kenneth Harrelson** — Florida member associated with leadership communications in the case; convicted on seditious conspiracy.

*(Names above reflect figures frequently referenced in mainstream reporting and federal prosecutions connected to January 6; exact titles and roles can vary across sources.)*

## January 6, 2021 and legal outcomes

Federal prosecutors alleged that Oath Keepers leaders and members planned and coordinated to obstruct the certification of the 2020 U.S. presidential election results, including arranging travel, equipment, and communications. Multiple defendants were tried in U.S. federal court, with several receiving convictions for **seditious conspiracy** and related offenses.
