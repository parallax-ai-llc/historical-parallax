## Summary

Korean Air executive who caused the 'nut rage' incident by forcing a plane to return to the gate over macadamia nut service. Convicted of violating aviation safety law.

## Corporate background

- Cho Hyun-ah is associated with the Hanjin Group / Korean Air family leadership structure (a South Korean chaebol).
- She held senior roles at Korean Air, and the incident became a widely discussed example of “gapjil” (abuse of power by executives toward subordinates).

## The “nut rage” incident

- The event occurred aboard a Korean Air flight departing New York in late 2014.
- Public reporting describes a dispute over how nuts were served in first class; the aircraft was ordered to return to the gate, and a cabin crew member was removed from duty.
- The incident quickly became international news because it involved operational disruption and allegations of intimidation and misuse of authority.

## Legal proceedings and aftermath

- South Korean prosecutors brought charges that included violations related to aviation safety and obstruction of duty.
- Courts convicted Cho Hyun-ah; public summaries emphasize that the case was treated as more than a customer-service dispute because it involved interfering with flight operations and crew responsibilities.
- The controversy also triggered broader discussions in South Korea about corporate governance, accountability of controlling families, and workplace culture.

## Impact

- The incident became shorthand in Korea for elite impunity and workplace bullying, and it is frequently referenced in discussions of service-sector labor conditions and executive accountability.
- Korean Air and the wider Hanjin Group faced reputational damage and heightened scrutiny of leadership behavior.
