## Summary

Leader of the Unité pour la Paix en Centrafrique armed group and member of the Coalition des Patriotes pour le Changement. He controls territory in eastern CAR and is sanctioned by the UN.

## Background and Identity

- Ali Darassa Mahamat is widely described as a Nigerian-born commander who became a key leader inside armed movements operating in the Central African Republic (CAR).[^1]
- Reporting commonly identifies him as ethnic Fula (Fulani), and notes that the UPC has drawn significant support from Fulani communities in parts of CAR.[^1]

## Armed-Group Role and Areas of Influence

- Darassa is associated with the Union for Peace in the Central African Republic (UPC), an ex-Séléka faction that emerged after the Séléka coalition fractured.[^1]
- Coverage frequently places UPC influence in and around Bambari and parts of central/eastern CAR, with armed checkpoints and control of movement cited as recurring features of the conflict environment.[^1]

## Conflict Dynamics (High-Level)

- The CAR conflict has involved shifting alliances among ex-Séléka factions and Anti-balaka groups; fighting has periodically taken on an ethnic dimension, including violence affecting Fulani civilians.[^1]
- International actors, including the UN peacekeeping mission MINUSCA, have been central to attempts to stabilize areas where UPC and rival forces operated.[^1]

## Sanctions and Legal Proceedings

- Public reporting notes U.S. Treasury sanctions announced in December 2021, describing the designation as related to serious human-rights abuses attributed to UPC leadership.[^1]
- Some reporting also notes court proceedings in CAR in which Darassa was sentenced in absentia for roles linked to the CPC coalition.[^1]

## Later Developments

- Sources report that in July 2025 Darassa surrendered in Bangui and publicly announced the end of UPC’s rebellion in a ceremony with CAR authorities.[^1]
