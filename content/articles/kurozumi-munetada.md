## Summary

Kurozumi Munetada was a Japanese Shinto priest who founded Kurozumikyo, one of the earliest Japanese new religious movements. In 1814, he experienced a mystical union with the sun goddess Amaterasu during a period of illness. He taught that becoming one with the divine through sincerity and gratitude could heal sickness and bring happiness. Kurozumikyo influenced later new religions and has approximately 300,000 adherents today.
