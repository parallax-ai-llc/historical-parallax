## Summary

Abdul-Baha was the eldest son of Baha'u'llah and the appointed leader of the Baha'i Faith from 1892 to 1921. He spent over 40 years as a prisoner and exile alongside his father. After his release in 1908, he traveled extensively in Europe and North America, spreading the Baha'i Faith. His talks and writings, including Some Answered Questions, elaborated on Baha'i social teachings including gender equality, racial unity, and world peace.
