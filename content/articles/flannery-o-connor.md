## Summary

Flannery O'Connor was an American Catholic author whose fiction explored the nature of grace, sin, and redemption in the American South. Her works, including Wise Blood and A Good Man Is Hard to Find, are considered masterpieces of 20th-century American literature. Her devout Catholic faith profoundly shaped her grotesque and violent literary style. She wrote despite battling lupus, which claimed her life at 39.
