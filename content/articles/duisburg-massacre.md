## Summary

Murder of six Italians outside a restaurant in Duisburg, Germany on August 15, 2007. The killings were part of an Ndrangheta blood feud from San Luca, Calabria.

## Additional Context

### What Happened

- The Duisburg massacre refers to a **multiple homicide** on **15 August 2007** in Duisburg, North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany.
- Six Italian men were shot outside the Italian restaurant **Da Bruno**, drawing immediate international attention.

### Link to 'Ndrangheta Feud

- Investigators connected the killings to the **'Ndrangheta**, a mafia-type organization originating in Calabria.
- The victims were associated with a long-running feud rooted in **San Luca**, a town in the province of Reggio Calabria.
- The attack illustrated how Italian organized-crime conflicts could be carried out **outside Italy**, reflecting the transnational reach of 'Ndrangheta networks.

### Aftermath

- The murders prompted intensified law-enforcement focus in Germany on:
  - The presence and economic activities of Italian organized crime.
  - Cross-border cooperation with Italian authorities.
  - The use of Germany as an operational and logistical environment for mafia-linked groups.

### Significance

- The case is frequently referenced as a turning point in public awareness of **organized crime in Europe**, particularly regarding how clan structures can maintain influence across borders while remaining difficult to detect.
