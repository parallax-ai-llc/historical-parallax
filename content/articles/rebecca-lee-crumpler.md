## Summary

Rebecca Lee Crumpler was the first African American woman to earn a medical degree in the United States, graduating from the New England Female Medical College in 1864. She dedicated her career to caring for freed slaves and impoverished patients in the post-Civil War South. Her 1883 book was one of the first medical publications by an African American.
