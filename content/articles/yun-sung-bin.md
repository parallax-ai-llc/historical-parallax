## Summary

Yun Sung-bin won the skeleton gold at the 2018 PyeongChang Olympics, Asia's first Olympic medal in the sport.

## Background

- Yun Sung-bin is a South Korean skeleton racer known for elite sprint power and explosive starts, a key determinant of skeleton performance.
- He emerged as a leading figure in Asian skeleton during the build-up to the 2018 Winter Olympics held in PyeongChang, South Korea.

## Competitive Highlights (high level)

- **2018 Winter Olympics (PyeongChang):** Won the **men’s skeleton gold medal**, becoming the first athlete from Asia to win an Olympic medal in skeleton.
- **Role in the sport:** His Olympic victory helped raise the profile of skeleton and sliding sports within South Korea.

## Why the 2018 Result Mattered

Skeleton races are often decided by small margins across multiple runs. A gold medal at the Olympics typically reflects:

- Consistent driving lines (minimizing steering corrections)
- Strong start times (sprint + push technique)
- The ability to reproduce performance over successive heats under pressure

## Discipline: Skeleton (quick explainer)

Skeleton is a winter sliding sport where athletes:

- Sprint while pushing the sled at the start, then dive onto it head-first
- Navigate an ice track at high speed using subtle body and steering inputs
- Compete on timed runs where cumulative time determines placement
