## Summary

Robert Woodruff led The Coca-Cola Company for over 60 years, transforming it into the world's most recognized brand. As president from 1923 to 1955, he expanded Coca-Cola internationally and made it a symbol of American culture. He declared that Coca-Cola should be within 'arm's reach of desire' everywhere. He was also one of Atlanta's most generous philanthropists, donating over $400 million anonymously.
