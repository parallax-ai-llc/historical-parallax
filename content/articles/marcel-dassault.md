## Summary

Marcel Dassault founded Dassault Aviation, one of the world's leading military and civil aircraft manufacturers. Born Marcel Bloch, he survived the Buchenwald concentration camp during WWII and changed his name after the war. He designed the Mystère and Mirage fighter jets that became France's premier military aircraft. He also built Dassault into a diversified group including publishing and electronics.
