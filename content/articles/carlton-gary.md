## Summary
Carlton Gary, the 'Stocking Strangler,' murdered at least three elderly women in Columbus, Georgia in 1977-78. He was executed by lethal injection in 2018.

## Background
- Carlton Gary was a U.S. convicted murderer and rapist associated with a series of attacks in Columbus, Georgia.
- The nickname **"Stocking Strangler"** references the use of hosiery/stockings as ligatures in the killings attributed to him.

## Crimes (1977–1978)
- Victims were primarily **elderly women** in Columbus.
- The cases generally involved **sexual assault**, burglary/forced entry, and **strangulation**.
- Authorities attributed multiple homicides to the same offender pattern during this period; Gary was ultimately convicted for three murders.

## Investigation and Arrest
- The crimes generated substantial local fear and pressure on police to identify a suspect.
- Investigators relied on a mixture of witness leads, forensic comparisons available at the time, and later review of case materials.

## Trial and Convictions
- Gary was convicted for the murders of **three women** connected to the 1977–1978 Columbus series.
- The case proceeded through the standard capital-case phases: guilt determination followed by sentencing.

## Appeals and Post-Conviction Litigation
- The convictions and death sentence were challenged through direct appeals and post-conviction proceedings.
- Controversies discussed in public reporting included disputes over evidentiary handling and questions raised about the strength of certain forensic links.

## Execution
- Gary was executed by **lethal injection** in Georgia in **2018**.
- His execution was widely reported because it closed one of Columbus's most infamous criminal cases.
