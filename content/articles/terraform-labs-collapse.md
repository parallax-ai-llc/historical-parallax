## Summary

Collapse of the TerraUSD algorithmic stablecoin and Luna token wiped out $40 billion in value in days. The crash triggered a cascade of failures across the crypto industry.

## Background

- Terraform Labs developed the Terra blockchain ecosystem, including the TerraUSD (UST) stablecoin and the LUNA token.
- UST was designed as an algorithmic stablecoin, relying on market incentives and a mint/burn mechanism rather than traditional cash-equivalent reserves.

## What Happened in May 2022

- A loss of confidence in UST triggered a "de-pegging" event where UST traded below its intended $1 value.
- The mechanism intended to restore the peg led to rapid creation of LUNA, contributing to a reflexive spiral as prices fell.
- Market participants described the episode as a bank-run-like dynamic in a largely unregulated environment.

## Industry Contagion

- Losses from Terra/Luna were widely discussed as a catalyst for subsequent distress across crypto lenders, hedge funds, and exchanges.
- The collapse intensified attention on stablecoin design, risk disclosures, and counterparty exposure in crypto markets.

## Legal and Regulatory Aftermath

- Authorities in multiple jurisdictions pursued investigations related to securities/financial rules and consumer harm allegations.
- Do Kwon became the focal point of enforcement actions and extradition-related proceedings following his arrest.

## Lessons Cited by Commentators

- Algorithmic stablecoins can fail abruptly under stress when confidence and liquidity disappear.
- Transparency of reserves (when applicable), governance, and risk management became central talking points in later policy debates.
