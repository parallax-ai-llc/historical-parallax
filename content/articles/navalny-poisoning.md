## Summary

Russian opposition leader Alexei Navalny was poisoned with Novichok nerve agent by FSB agents. He survived, was imprisoned upon return to Russia, and died in Arctic penal colony in 2024.

## Incident Overview

In August 2020, Russian opposition figure Alexei Navalny fell severely ill during travel within Russia and was later treated in Germany. Multiple governments and investigative reports publicly attributed the poisoning to a Novichok-type nerve agent, an agent class associated with Soviet-era chemical-weapons development.

The incident quickly became an international political flashpoint, involving questions of state responsibility, the use of prohibited chemical agents, and the safety of political opposition figures.

## Investigations and Attribution

Public discourse around the poisoning has centered on:

- medical and toxicological findings reported by treating institutions
- investigative journalism and open-source analysis attempting to reconstruct travel and surveillance patterns
- official statements and denials by Russian authorities

Because attribution disputes are common in high-stakes incidents, much of the debate has hinged on competing evidentiary standards, transparency, and access to primary materials.

## Political and Geopolitical Effects

The poisoning contributed to:

- heightened tensions between Russia and multiple Western governments
- renewed discussion of targeted sanctions and accountability mechanisms
- broader concern about chemical-weapons norms and enforcement

## Aftermath for Navalny

Navalny’s return to Russia, subsequent imprisonment, and reported death in custody in 2024 are often discussed as part of a single continuum of repression risk faced by prominent dissidents.
