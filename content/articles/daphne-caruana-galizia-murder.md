## Summary

Assassination of Maltese investigative journalist Daphne Caruana Galizia by car bomb on October 16, 2017. She had been investigating corruption involving Maltese politicians and organized crime.

## Context

The assassination of Daphne Caruana Galizia became a defining event in modern Maltese political history, triggering international scrutiny of governance, corruption, and the rule of law.

Her reporting was widely characterized as investigative and confrontational, and she was involved in sustained public controversies prior to her death.

## Investigation and Public Response

The case prompted domestic and international calls for accountability, including sustained attention from European institutions and press-freedom organizations.

Public protests and political fallout centered on questions about:

- who ordered and financed the killing,
- whether state institutions were captured or compromised,
- and how investigative journalism could be protected in small states with concentrated power networks.

## Legal Proceedings

As with many high-profile political assassinations, proceedings unfolded over multiple years and included a mixture of arrests, plea deals, and trials.

The case also generated debate about evidentiary transparency, the pace of justice, and institutional reforms intended to reduce impunity.

## Impact

The murder is frequently cited as a landmark example of risks faced by investigative journalists in Europe and is used in broader discussions about the intersection of organized crime, business interests, and politics.
