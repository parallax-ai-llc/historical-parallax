## Summary

Pope Gregory XIII was Pope from 1572 to 1585 and is best known for introducing the Gregorian calendar in 1582, which corrected the drift of the Julian calendar. He implemented many of the reforms of the Council of Trent and founded seminaries throughout Europe. He also established the Pontifical Gregorian University. His calendar reform, though initially opposed by Protestant and Orthodox countries, was eventually adopted worldwide.
