## Summary

Algerian jihadist who led al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb (AQIM) for 16 years, directing terrorist attacks across North and West Africa. He was killed by French special forces in northern Mali.

## Overview

Abdelmalek Droukdel (1970–2020) was an Algerian militant Islamist leader best known for leading al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb (AQIM). He rose through Algeria’s armed Islamist movement landscape after the civil conflict of the 1990s and later became a prominent figure in transnational jihadist networks in North and West Africa.

## Role in AQIM

- **Leadership:** Droukdel led AQIM for many years, overseeing a shift from primarily Algeria-focused insurgency toward broader regional activity.
- **Regional operations:** Under his leadership, AQIM and affiliated groups were linked to attacks, kidnappings for ransom, and efforts to establish influence across the Sahel.
- **Counterterrorism pressure:** AQIM faced sustained military pressure from regional governments and international partners, contributing to leadership attrition over time.

## Death and aftermath

Droukdel was reported killed in 2020 during a counterterrorism operation in Mali, an event widely discussed as a significant blow to AQIM’s central leadership even as affiliated networks continued operating.
