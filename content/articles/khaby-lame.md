## Summary

Khaby Lame is an Italian content creator of Senegalese origin who became the most-followed person on TikTok in June 2022, surpassing Charli D'Amelio. His signature format — silently reacting to absurdly overcomplicated life-hack videos with a deadpan expression and open-handed gesture — transcended language barriers and turned him into a global phenomenon.

Lame's rise is one of the most remarkable stories in social media history. Laid off from a factory job during the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020, he began posting videos from his home in Chivasso, Italy. Within two years, he amassed over 150 million TikTok followers. His content requires no translation, giving him a uniquely universal audience that spans virtually every country on the platform.

Beyond TikTok, Lame has parlayed his fame into brand partnerships with companies including Hugo Boss, Xbox, Binance, and Juventus FC. He has appeared at the Cannes Film Festival, been featured on the cover of Forbes Italia, and become one of the highest-earning content creators in the world.

## Early Life & Background

Khabane Lame was born on March 9, 2000, in Dakar, Senegal. His family moved to Italy when he was one year old, settling in a public housing complex in Chivasso, a small town near Turin in the Piedmont region. He grew up in Italy, attending local schools, but for years was unable to obtain Italian citizenship due to the country's restrictive nationality laws, which do not grant automatic citizenship to children born to or raised by non-citizen parents.

Before his social media career, Lame worked at a factory near Turin. When the COVID-19 pandemic hit in early 2020, he was laid off, an event that would inadvertently redirect his life.

## Rise to Prominence

Lame joined TikTok in March 2020, initially posting dance and comedy videos. He found his breakthrough format later that year: short, wordless reaction clips in which he watches someone perform an unnecessarily complicated task — peeling a banana with a knife, opening a door with an elaborate gadget — then demonstrates the obvious simple solution while gesturing with his trademark flat expression and outstretched hands.

The format's simplicity was its genius. With no spoken language, the videos were instantly accessible worldwide. Growth accelerated through 2021, and by June 22, 2022, Lame overtook Charli D'Amelio to become TikTok's most-followed creator, a title he has held since.

## Key Achievements

- **Most-followed person on TikTok** since June 2022, with over 162 million followers as of early 2025
- Signed with talent management firm Riggio Entertainment and later with major agencies for global representation
- Brand ambassador for Hugo Boss, partnering on their 2022 campaign
- Received Italian citizenship on August 17, 2022, after years of bureaucratic delays — an event that reignited national debate on Italy's citizenship laws
- Named to the Forbes 30 Under 30 Europe list in 2023
- Appeared at the 2022 Cannes Film Festival and multiple international events as a presenter and guest

## Controversies & Criticism

Lame's long wait for Italian citizenship drew significant public attention and became a flashpoint in the broader Italian debate over *ius soli* (birthright citizenship) versus *ius sanguinis* (citizenship by descent). Some critics argued his case received attention only because of his fame, while thousands of long-term residents faced similar or worse bureaucratic obstacles in obscurity.

Some commentators have also questioned the sustainability and originality of his content format, noting that reaction-based content relies on other creators' videos. Others have raised broader concerns about the economics of TikTok fame and whether creators like Lame benefit proportionally from the value they generate for the platform.

## Legacy & Influence

Khaby Lame demonstrated that social media stardom does not require language, expensive production, or geographic privilege. His rise from an unemployed factory worker in a small Italian town to the most-followed person on one of the world's largest platforms reshaped assumptions about what viral content looks like. His success also brought international attention to Italy's citizenship policies and the experiences of second-generation immigrants in Europe.
