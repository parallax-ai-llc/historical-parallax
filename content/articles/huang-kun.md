## Summary

Huang Kun was a Chinese physicist who co-authored 'Dynamical Theory of Crystal Lattices' with Max Born in 1954, one of the most important texts in solid-state physics. The Huang-Born theory describes lattice vibrations and their interactions with electromagnetic radiation. He later became president of Peking University and received China's State Preeminent Science Award.
