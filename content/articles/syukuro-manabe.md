## Summary

Syukuro Manabe is a Japanese-American meteorologist and climatologist who shared the 2021 Nobel Prize in Physics for the physical modelling of Earth's climate. He developed the first computer models demonstrating the relationship between CO2 concentration and global temperature, laying the foundation for climate science. His pioneering work in the 1960s predicted global warming decades before it became widely recognized.
