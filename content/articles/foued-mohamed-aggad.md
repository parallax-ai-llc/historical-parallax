## Summary

French terrorist who was one of three attackers at the Bataclan theater during the November 2015 Paris attacks. He had traveled to Syria in 2013 with a group from Strasbourg.

## Background

- Public reporting describes Mohamed-Aggad as a French national from the Strasbourg area.
- He was reported to have traveled to Syria in 2013, a period during which multiple European nationals joined jihadist groups in the region.

## Link to the November 2015 Paris Attacks

- The Bataclan attack was part of a coordinated series of attacks across Paris on 13 November 2015.
- Attackers used firearms and explosive suicide vests; Mohamed-Aggad is widely reported as one of the assailants inside the concert venue.

## Investigative and Security Context

- The attacks accelerated policy debates in France and across Europe regarding intelligence sharing, surveillance authorities, border controls, and counter-radicalization programs.
- Investigations have typically examined travel networks, recruitment pathways, and operational planning connecting individuals in France and Belgium to actors in Syria/Iraq.

## Aftermath

- The Bataclan attack produced significant civilian casualties and had a lasting impact on French public life, security posture, and emergency-response planning.
- Commemorations and trials related to the attacks became major national events, reflecting ongoing efforts to document responsibility and learn operational lessons.
