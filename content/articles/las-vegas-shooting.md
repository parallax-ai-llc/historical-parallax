## Summary

Mass shooting at music festival on October 1, 2017 killing 60. Stephen Paddock fired from hotel room. Deadliest modern US mass shooting.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

| 2018 | Final FBI public statements report no clear motive or ideology established. |

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- **Location:** Route 91 Harvest music festival area (Las Vegas Strip), with shots fired from the Mandalay Bay hotel.
- **Victims:** 60 people killed; hundreds injured (counts vary by source and by definition of injury).
- **Perpetrator:** Stephen Paddock.
- **Type of incident:** Mass shooting.
- **Notability:** Widely described as the deadliest mass shooting in modern U.S. history (by fatality count at the time).

## Background & Setting

- The Route 91 Harvest festival was a large outdoor country-music event drawing thousands of attendees.
- The Mandalay Bay is a major resort/hotel overlooking the festival grounds.
- The incident prompted immediate multi-agency emergency response in a dense tourist corridor.

## Investigation (What is Publicly Known)

- Law enforcement investigated planning, procurement, and the shooter’s movements prior to the attack.
- The FBI reviewed possible motivations including ideology, personal grievances, and mental health factors.
- Public-facing reports have stated that a definitive motive was not established.

## Impact & Aftermath

- The shooting led to broad public debate on U.S. gun policy, venue security, and emergency preparedness.
- Survivors and families pursued civil litigation; multiple settlements were reported in subsequent years.
- Large venues increased focus on risk screening, crowd egress planning, and rapid medical response.

## Policy & Security Discussions

- The event influenced discussion around **"bump stocks"** and related firearm accessories; U.S. federal regulation changes followed in 2018.
- Hotels and event operators reassessed procedures such as monitoring, room access controls, and threat reporting.
