## Summary

Syrian business tycoon and cousin of Bashar al-Assad who controlled up to 60% of Syria's economy. He fell out with Assad and publicly pleaded against the seizure of his business empire.

## Business Empire

Rami Makhlouf is widely described as one of Syria's most prominent businessmen during the rule of the Assad family. He held interests across multiple sectors, and was particularly associated with telecommunications and finance. A frequent example cited in reporting is **Syriatel**, a major mobile phone operator, which became emblematic of how politically connected elites could dominate key industries.

## Political Connections and Sanctions

Makhlouf's influence was closely tied to his family relationship with President Bashar al-Assad. As the Syrian civil war unfolded, Makhlouf and other Syrian elites faced international scrutiny and sanctions, with governments alleging that his business network helped finance or sustain the regime.

## Dispute with the Assad State

In 2020, Makhlouf released public video messages and statements that were interpreted as an unusually open rupture with the Syrian leadership. The dispute centered on state demands for large payments and the effective seizure or restructuring of assets linked to his companies. The episode was notable because public dissent from such a high-profile insider was rare in the Syrian system.

## Charitable and Social Organizations

Makhlouf has also been linked to charitable and social initiatives, which supporters framed as philanthropy and critics sometimes characterized as another channel of patronage. In authoritarian or patronage-heavy environments, such organizations can serve both welfare and political functions.

## Significance

Makhlouf's rise and subsequent marginalization are often used to illustrate the dynamics of oligarchy and state power in Syria: business success depended heavily on political proximity, and assets could be redistributed when elite coalitions shifted.
