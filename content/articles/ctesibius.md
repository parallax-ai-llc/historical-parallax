## Summary

Ctesibius (also spelled Ktesibios) was a Greek inventor and mathematician who lived in Alexandria, Egypt during the 3rd century BCE. Often regarded as the father of pneumatics, he made groundbreaking contributions to the understanding of compressed air and its applications. He is best known for inventing an improved water clock (clepsydra), a water organ (hydraulis), and a force pump. His work laid the foundations for the study of pneumatics and hydraulics, influencing later engineers such as Philo of Byzantium and Hero of Alexandria.
