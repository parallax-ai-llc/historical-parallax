## Summary

Yi Hwang (pen name Toegye) was a Korean Neo-Confucian philosopher who is considered the greatest Korean philosopher. He developed a systematic interpretation of Zhu Xi's philosophy, emphasizing the primacy of li (principle) over qi (material force). His Ten Diagrams on Sage Learning became a foundational text of Korean Confucianism. His philosophical school, the Yeongnam school, competed with Yi I's Giho school for centuries.
