## Summary

Crypto hedge fund that collapsed owing $3.5 billion. Founders Su Zhu and Kyle Davies fled but Zhu was arrested in Singapore airport while attempting to flee.

## Business Model (Overview)

Three Arrows Capital (often abbreviated **3AC**) operated as a crypto-focused hedge fund. Like many hedge funds, it aimed to generate returns by taking positions across markets; in crypto, this frequently involves a mix of spot holdings, derivatives, and lending/borrowing arrangements.

## Rise and Market Context

3AC became widely discussed during the 2020–2021 period when crypto markets expanded rapidly and leverage became common across centralized lenders, exchanges, and over-the-counter (OTC) desks. In that environment, large funds could obtain substantial credit lines and increase exposure.

## Collapse and Liquidation

In 2022, crypto prices fell sharply and multiple leveraged strategies unwound. Public reporting described 3AC as unable to meet certain margin calls and repayment obligations. Insolvency proceedings followed, with liquidators appointed to:

- identify and secure remaining assets,
- reconstruct trading and custody records,
- pursue recovery actions on behalf of creditors.

## Key People

## Why It Mattered

3AC’s failure was frequently cited as a catalyst for broader “contagion” during the 2022 crypto credit crisis:

- creditor losses and disputed claims across multiple platforms,
- reduced confidence in unsecured lending practices,
- pressure on other firms to strengthen collateral, custody segregation, and risk controls.

## Clarification on Claims

Descriptions of wrongdoing vary across sources. Definitive conclusions depend on the results of specific court proceedings, regulatory actions, and liquidator reports, which may develop over time.
