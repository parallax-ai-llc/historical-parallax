## Summary

Palestinian militant group that carried out the 1972 Munich Olympics massacre and other international terrorist attacks. It was linked to Yasser Arafats Fatah movement.

| Year | Event |

| 1970 | Founded |

## Context and Origins

- The name "Black September" is commonly associated with the September 1970 conflict in Jordan, in which Palestinian armed groups were expelled and the PLO’s position in Jordan was severely weakened.
- The organization is generally described as operating in the early 1970s as a clandestine militant network connected to factions within the Palestine Liberation Organization (PLO), often linked in reporting to Fatah.
- Accounts differ on whether it was a distinct standing organization or a label used for particular operations and cells.

## Ideology and Methods

- Black September is typically categorized as a militant / terrorist organization due to its use of violence against civilian and diplomatic targets.
- Operations attributed to the group emphasized international visibility, including attacks outside the Middle East.
- Like other clandestine groups of the era, it relied on compartmentalized cells and covert logistics to reduce traceability.

## Notable Operations (high level)

- **Munich Olympics (1972):** The group is widely held responsible for the hostage-taking and killings involving the Israeli Olympic team.
- The group has also been associated in historical accounts with additional assassinations and attempted attacks targeting Israeli, Western, and Arab figures abroad.

## Aftermath and Historical Debate

- Following Munich and other incidents, international counterterrorism cooperation increased, including tighter security for global sporting events and diplomatic facilities.
- Researchers and journalists continue to debate the command relationships between Black September, Fatah, and the broader PLO umbrella, partly because of the covert nature of the operations.
- By the mid-1970s, Black September is commonly described as having been wound down or absorbed, as strategic priorities and political constraints shifted.

## Legacy

- The Munich attack remains one of the most cited cases in discussions of modern terrorism, crisis response, and security planning for mass events.
- Black September is frequently referenced in analyses of how militant groups used international media attention to advance political messaging.
