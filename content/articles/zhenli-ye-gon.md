## Summary

Chinese-Mexican businessman arrested for drug trafficking after $207 million in cash was found at his Mexico City mansion in 2007—one of the largest drug cash seizures ever. He claimed the money was forced on him by a politician.

## Business Activities and Alleged Trafficking Links

Ye Gon was known publicly as a businessman involved in pharmaceutical-related commerce in Mexico. In reporting around the 2007 investigation, authorities and journalists connected his activities to supply chains for chemical precursors used in the production of methamphetamine, a link that became central to the prosecution narrative.

## 2007 Cash Seizure

The seizure of approximately $207 million in cash at a Mexico City property became the defining public fact of the case. The amount was widely described as one of the largest cash seizures associated with alleged drug trafficking. Ye Gon’s later public statements claimed that the money had been left with him under coercion; this assertion was disputed and treated skeptically by investigators.

## Legal Proceedings in the United States

Ye Gon was arrested in the United States in 2007 and faced a lengthy legal process involving detention, charges, and disputes about whether he would be extradited to Mexico or tried in the United States. Ultimately, U.S. proceedings led to a conviction related to drug trafficking.

## Broader Significance

The case has been cited in discussions of:

- The role of transnational business networks in moving drug proceeds.
- The importance of controlling chemical precursors, not only finished narcotics.
- Political controversy and claims of corruption surrounding major organized-crime investigations.

## Notes

Biographical details and the framing of Ye Gon’s public claims differ across sources, and some accounts emphasize the political context in Mexico during the period of his arrest and prosecution.
