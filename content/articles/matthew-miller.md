## Background

- Matthew Todd Miller is an American citizen who traveled to North Korea in 2014.
- He became known internationally after an incident at arrival in which he tore up his visa and requested asylum, triggering immediate detention by North Korean authorities.

## Detention in North Korea (2014)

- North Korean state media and officials accused him of hostile acts and described him as having entered the country under the guise of tourism.
- He was held for several months and later faced a legal process under North Korean authorities.
- Public reporting commonly characterizes his case as part of a broader pattern in which foreign nationals have been detained during periods of diplomatic tension.

## Release and Return

- In late 2014, Miller was released from detention and returned to the United States.
- His release was widely reported alongside other detainee cases and was treated as a humanitarian/diplomatic event rather than an ordinary judicial outcome.

## Broader Context

- North Korea has periodically detained foreign visitors, including U.S. citizens, in cases that often become high-profile international issues.
- Governments and travel advisories frequently warn that consular access and due process protections can be limited or inconsistent in North Korea.
