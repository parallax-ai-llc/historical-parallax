## Background

- Mijailo Mijailović (Serbian: Мијаило Мијаиловић) is a Swedish national born in Stockholm in 1978 to Serbian parents.
- In accounts of his childhood, he spent part of his early schooling years living with relatives in Serbia before returning to Sweden during the Yugoslav Wars.

## Prior incidents (as reported)

- Prior to the 2003 assassination, he had earlier convictions and police encounters related to violent behavior and weapons possession.
- These earlier incidents are often cited in Swedish media coverage as part of the public discussion about risk assessment and psychiatric care.

## Assassination of Anna Lindh (2003)

- On 10 September 2003, he attacked Swedish Minister for Foreign Affairs Anna Lindh at the NK department store in central Stockholm.
- Lindh sustained multiple stab wounds and was taken to hospital; she died the following day after surgical treatment.

## Investigation, trial, and sentencing

- He was arrested after the attack; surveillance images from the department store were widely reported as important to identification.
- On 6 January 2004, he confessed to stabbing Lindh and stated it was not politically motivated.
- His trial took place in January 2004; the court ordered psychiatric evaluation as part of the sentencing process.

## Aftermath and public impact

- The killing of a sitting foreign minister prompted national mourning in Sweden and renewed debate on political security.
- The case is also frequently referenced in discussions about mental health evaluations, criminal responsibility, and media coverage during major national crises.
