## Scientific Contributions

Ashkin carried out much of his research at Bell Labs, where he worked on laser physics and the interaction of light with matter. His key insight was that focused laser beams can exert forces on small particles, allowing them to be trapped and manipulated without physical contact.

## Optical Tweezers and Applications

Optical tweezers made it possible to hold and move microscopic objects—such as dielectric beads, cells, and subcellular components—in a controlled way. The technique became widely used in biophysics and molecular biology, including single-molecule experiments that probe forces at the scale relevant to DNA, proteins, and cellular machinery.

## Recognition and Legacy

Ashkin shared the 2018 Nobel Prize in Physics for the optical tweezers invention, becoming one of the oldest Nobel laureates. His work is also connected to broader developments in precision measurement and experimental methods that rely on non-contact manipulation.
