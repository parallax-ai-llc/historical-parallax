## Background

Adnan Abu Walid al-Sahrawi was a Sahrawi militant figure associated with jihadist networks operating across the Sahel region (notably Mali, Niger, and Burkina Faso).

## Militant Affiliations

- He was linked to several armed Islamist groups in the region, including factions connected to al-Qaeda-aligned and later Islamic State-aligned organizations.
- He became publicly associated with the Islamic State in the Greater Sahara (ISGS), an Islamic State affiliate active in the tri-border area of Mali, Niger, and Burkina Faso.

## Tongo Tongo Ambush (2017)

- In October 2017, a joint US–Nigerien patrol was attacked near the village of Tongo Tongo in Niger.
- The ambush killed four US soldiers and several Nigerien troops and drew wide attention to the expanding insurgency in the Sahel.
- Al-Sahrawi was widely reported as the ISGS leader connected to the operation.

## Role in the Sahel Insurgency

Under his leadership, ISGS was associated with attacks on military and civilian targets in the Sahel, contributing to regional instability and prompting expanded counterterrorism operations by local governments and international partners.

## Death

- French authorities reported that he was killed in August 2021 during an operation in the Sahel; the news was publicly announced by France’s president.
- His death was presented as a significant blow to Islamic State-aligned militants in the region.
