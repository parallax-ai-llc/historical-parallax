## Summary

Tanni Grey-Thompson won 16 Paralympic medals including 11 gold across five Games. She is the most decorated British Paralympic athlete and later became a member of the House of Lords.

## Athletic Career (High Level)

Grey-Thompson’s career is closely associated with wheelchair racing on the track, where success depends on a combination of aerobic endurance, start speed, racing tactics, and highly optimized equipment. Competing across five Paralympic Games indicates long-term elite performance and the ability to adapt across changing competitive fields and classification systems.

## Major Achievements

- 16 Paralympic medals (11 gold), making her one of the most decorated Paralympians in the United Kingdom
- Multiple world-level titles and major race wins across a long competitive period
- A prominent public role in raising the visibility of Paralympic sport in the United Kingdom

## Public Service and Advocacy

After retiring from full-time competition, Grey-Thompson became active in public life and policy discussions. As Baroness Grey-Thompson, she has been associated with work relating to disability rights, sport governance, and broader accessibility issues, reflecting a common transition for high-profile athletes into advocacy and institutional roles.

## Broader Significance

Grey-Thompson is often referenced in narratives about the professionalization and mainstream recognition of Paralympic sport. Her career illustrates how elite para-athletes can influence public attitudes toward disability and participation in sport, and how sporting success can translate into a platform for civic engagement.
