## Summary

Guinean special forces colonel who overthrew 83-year-old President Condé in 2021 after Condé changed the constitution to seek a third term.

## Background

- Doumbouya is a Guinean military officer associated with the country’s special forces.
- He became internationally prominent after the September 2021 coup d’état that removed President Alpha Condé.

## 2021 Coup d’état (high-level)

- In September 2021, Guinean soldiers announced that they had dissolved the government and suspended the constitution.
- The takeover followed a period of political tension, including controversy surrounding Condé’s constitutional changes and third-term bid.
- Doumbouya was presented as the leader of the coup and the figurehead of the post-coup military authorities.

## Post-coup role and governance (overview)

- After the coup, Doumbouya led the military-led transition authorities and became the central decision-maker during the early transition period.
- The transition period involved announcing a new political roadmap and engaging with domestic stakeholders and regional/international partners.

## Regional and international context

- Military takeovers in West Africa during the early 2020s increased scrutiny from regional bodies.
- Organizations such as ECOWAS have historically used sanctions and diplomatic pressure in response to unconstitutional changes of government.
