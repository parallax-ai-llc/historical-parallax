## Overview

"Thowheed Jamat" is a name used for a Sri Lankan Islamist extremist grouping discussed in reporting around the 2019 Sri Lanka Easter bombings. Coverage commonly links the group to militant preacher Zahran Hashim and to the network of suicide bombers who carried out coordinated attacks on churches and hotels.

## 2019 Easter bombings (Sri Lanka)

In April 2019, a series of suicide bombings targeted Christian worshippers and foreign tourists in Sri Lanka. Public accounts attribute operational responsibility to a local extremist network associated with Zahran Hashim, and report that the attackers pledged allegiance to the Islamic State (IS/ISIS). Investigations and media reporting have treated Thowheed Jamat/National Thowheeth Jama'ath (NTJ) as a key label for the local component involved.

## Aftermath

After the attacks, Sri Lankan authorities conducted raids and arrests, and public discussion focused on radicalization networks, financing, and the role of small domestic extremist groups. The incident also prompted debate over intelligence-sharing and security coordination before the bombings.
