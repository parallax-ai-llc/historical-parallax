## Summary

Kurdish leader who founded the PKK and led its armed struggle against Turkey. Captured in Kenya in 1999 and sentenced to death, later commuted to life. He has been in solitary confinement on İmralı Island for over 25 years.

## Overview

Abdullah Öcalan (born 1948) is a Kurdish political figure and the founding leader of the Kurdistan Workers' Party (PKK). He has been a central figure in the Kurdish–Turkish conflict for decades and has been imprisoned in Turkey since 1999.

## Political influence

- **PKK leadership:** Öcalan’s writings and strategic direction have shaped the PKK’s ideology and tactics over time.
- **Peace and negotiations:** Various periods of ceasefire and negotiation have involved debates about Öcalan’s role and communication from prison, alongside broader regional developments.
- **Regional impact:** The conflict has had significant consequences in Turkey and has interacted with wider Kurdish political movements in neighboring countries.

## Current status

Öcalan remains a consequential and polarizing figure, viewed by different audiences through sharply divergent lenses ranging from militant leadership to political symbolism.
