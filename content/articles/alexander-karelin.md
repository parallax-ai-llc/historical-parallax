## Summary

Alexander Karelin went undefeated for 13 years in Greco-Roman wrestling, winning three Olympic golds. His loss to Gardner in 2000 was a monumental upset.

## Athletic Profile

Karelin competed in **Greco-Roman wrestling**, a style that forbids holds below the waist and emphasizes upper-body control, throws, and clinch work. He became known for an unusually powerful combination of strength, conditioning, and technical discipline for the heavyweight division.

Commonly discussed aspects of his competitive profile include:

- dominance in **par terre** positions (top control and defense)
- ability to score with high-amplitude throws despite heavyweight size
- a long competitive peak marked by consistent tournament performance

## Major Achievements (Overview)

At the highest level, Karelin’s résumé is typically summarized by:

- **three Olympic gold medals** (1988, 1992, 1996)
- an Olympic **silver medal** (2000)
- multiple **World Championship** and **European Championship** titles

These accomplishments made him a reference point in debates about the greatest wrestlers in Greco-Roman history.

## The 2000 Olympic Upset

Karelin’s 2000 Olympic final loss to **Rulon Gardner** is often cited as one of the most notable upsets in Olympic combat-sports history. The match is remembered not only because Karelin was heavily favored, but also because it ended a long period in which he rarely conceded points in elite competition.

## Post-Competition Public Life

After retiring from active competition, Karelin became involved in public and political life in Russia. In many profiles, his transition is framed as an example of how high-profile athletes in post-Soviet states sometimes moved into national institutions and party politics.

## Legacy

Karelin’s career is frequently used as a case study in:

- how long-term **training systems** and specialization shape elite performance
- the relationship between rule changes and tactics in Greco-Roman wrestling
- the cultural role of Olympic champions as national symbols
