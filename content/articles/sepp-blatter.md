## Summary

FIFA president for 17 years who resigned amid a massive corruption scandal. Banned from football for financial misconduct involving a $2 million payment to Michel Platini.

## FIFA tenure (high level)

Blatter rose through FIFA’s administration before becoming president in 1998. His presidency coincided with major commercial growth for FIFA and the expansion of World Cup participation and development funding programs, alongside persistent criticism about governance, transparency, and patronage politics within world football.

## 2015 crisis and resignation

In 2015, multiple FIFA officials and associated figures were indicted by U.S. authorities in a sweeping case involving allegations of racketeering, wire fraud, and bribery related to marketing and media rights. Although Blatter was not charged in the U.S. indictment, the episode triggered intense scrutiny of FIFA leadership and contributed to his decision to resign.

## Ethics proceedings and bans

FIFA’s ethics bodies sanctioned Blatter in connection with governance and financial-oversight issues, including a widely reported payment to Michel Platini. The details and legal characterization of the payment have been disputed in public statements and proceedings. Blatter’s eligibility to participate in football administration has been affected by these rulings.

## Legacy and debate

Assessments of Blatter’s legacy tend to split between:

- **Institutional growth:** commercialization and global reach of FIFA competitions.
- **Governance failures:** allegations and findings across multiple cases involving corruption or misconduct within FIFA during the era.
