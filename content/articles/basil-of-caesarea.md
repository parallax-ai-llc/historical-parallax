## Summary

Basil of Caesarea, also known as Basil the Great, was a bishop and one of the Cappadocian Fathers. He made crucial contributions to the doctrine of the Trinity and the development of Eastern monasticism. He composed the Liturgy of Saint Basil, still used in Eastern Orthodox churches. He also established hospitals and charitable institutions, pioneering Christian social work.
