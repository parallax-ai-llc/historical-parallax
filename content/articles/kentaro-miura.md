## Summary

Kentaro Miura (1966–2021) was a Japanese manga artist best known as the creator of *Berserk*, a dark fantasy epic widely regarded as one of the greatest manga series ever produced. His meticulous draftsmanship, intricate world-building, and unflinching exploration of trauma, fate, and human perseverance earned him a devoted global audience and the deep respect of peers across the comics and animation industries.

Miura's influence extended far beyond manga into video games, anime, and literary fiction. His visual style — defined by extraordinarily dense cross-hatching, nightmarish creature design, and painterly composition — set a benchmark for artistic ambition in the medium that few have matched. Though *Berserk* remained unfinished at the time of his death in May 2021, its cultural footprint continues to grow.

## Early Life & Background

Kentaro Miura was born on July 11, 1966, in Chiba City, Chiba Prefecture, Japan. He began drawing manga at the age of ten and self-published his first work, *Miuranger*, at age fourteen as a classroom photocopied comic. He enrolled at Nihon University's College of Art, where he honed his craft and developed the dark aesthetic sensibility that would define his career. While still a student, he worked as an assistant to manga artist Jiro Kuwata.

## Rise to Prominence

Miura debuted professionally in 1985 with *Futanabi*, published in the Hakusensha magazine *Monthly Animal House*. In 1988, he submitted a prototype chapter of *Berserk* — then titled *Berserk: The Prototype* — which won him a prize from Hakusensha and secured serialization. *Berserk* began its formal serialization in *Young Animal* magazine in 1989. The series distinguished itself from the outset through its mature themes, graphic violence, and a level of detail in each panel that far exceeded industry norms. By the mid-1990s, *Berserk* had become a phenomenon in Japan and was gaining international recognition.

## Key Achievements

- *Berserk* ran for over 40 volumes (tankōbon) from 1989 until Miura's death in 2021, with serialization continuing posthumously under the supervision of his studio, Gaga Studio, and longtime friend Kouji Mori.
- The series sold over 50 million copies worldwide as of 2021, making it one of the best-selling manga of all time.
- Miura received the Tezuka Osamu Cultural Prize Award for New Creator in 1997.
- *Berserk* inspired multiple anime adaptations (1997, 2012–2013, 2016–2017) and had a direct and acknowledged influence on the FromSoftware video game series *Dark Souls*, *Bloodborne*, and *Elden Ring*.

## Controversies & Criticism

Miura was criticized throughout his career for lengthy hiatuses in *Berserk*'s serialization, sometimes stretching over a year. Readers and commentators debated whether the breaks reflected perfectionism, health concerns, or other priorities — including Miura's publicly admitted enthusiasm for idol group culture and video games. The series' explicit depictions of sexual violence, particularly in the "Eclipse" arc, generated ongoing debate about the line between unflinching narrative and gratuitous content.

## Legacy & Influence

Miura died on May 6, 2021, from an acute aortic dissection, at age 54. His death prompted an outpouring of tributes from fans and creators worldwide. *Berserk* resumed publication in June 2022, with Kouji Mori writing and Gaga Studio illustrating based on Miura's detailed notes and discussions. His influence on dark fantasy as a genre — in manga, games, and animation — is considered foundational. Miura demonstrated that manga could aspire to the ambition of literary and visual fine art, a legacy that continues to shape the medium.
