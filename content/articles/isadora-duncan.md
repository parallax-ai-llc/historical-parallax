## Summary

Isadora Duncan was an American dancer who pioneered modern dance by rejecting the rigid structure of classical ballet in favor of free, natural movement. She danced barefoot in flowing tunics, drawing inspiration from Greek art and natural rhythms. Her dramatic life and revolutionary approach to dance made her an icon of artistic freedom.
