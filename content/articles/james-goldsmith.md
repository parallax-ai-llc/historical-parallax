## Summary

James Goldsmith was a British-French billionaire financier and corporate raider who led some of the most dramatic hostile takeovers of the 1980s. He built and dismantled conglomerates, made fortunes in food companies, and was one of the most feared corporate raiders in Anglo-American business. He later became a politician, founding the Referendum Party to oppose European integration. His flamboyant lifestyle and bold business moves defined the era of leveraged buyouts.
