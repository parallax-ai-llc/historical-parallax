## Summary

Nandini Harinath is a rocket scientist at ISRO who served as deputy operations director for the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan). She has worked on 14 missions including satellite launches and the Mars mission. She is an advocate for women in STEM and has been recognized as one of India's pioneering women in space science.

## Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan) Context

- India’s Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), popularly called Mangalyaan, placed an orbiter around Mars in 2014 and is often discussed as a milestone for cost-effective planetary exploration.
- Mission operations typically involve continuous monitoring of spacecraft health, orbit determination, maneuver planning, and coordination between ground stations and flight dynamics teams.

## Role and Professional Significance

- Serving in an operations leadership role on an interplanetary mission implies responsibility for procedures, anomaly response, and maintaining safe mission margins.
- Engineers in such roles often work at the intersection of systems engineering (end-to-end constraints) and real-time decision-making during critical phases.

## Broader Impact

- Public visibility of women engineers and scientists in high-profile missions can influence STEM participation by providing concrete role models.
- The MOM program is frequently referenced in discussions of India’s expanding space capabilities, including deep-space navigation, communications, and mission management.
