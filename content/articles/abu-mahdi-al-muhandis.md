## Summary

Iraqi militia commander who was deputy head of the Popular Mobilization Forces. He was designated a terrorist by the US for his role in the 1983 Kuwait bombings. Killed alongside Qasem Soleimani in the US drone strike.

## Additional Context

## Militia and Political Influence

Al-Muhandis was a prominent figure within Iraq’s network of armed groups that gained formalized status after 2014 under the umbrella of the Popular Mobilization Forces (PMF). He was also closely associated with Kata'ib Hezbollah, a powerful Iran-aligned militia within Iraq’s security and political landscape.

## Role in the Fight Against ISIS

During the campaign against the Islamic State (ISIS), the PMF became a major auxiliary force alongside Iraqi security forces. Al-Muhandis’ leadership role made him influential in operational coordination and in the subsequent debates about integrating militias into state structures.

## International Designations and Controversy

The United States and other actors linked him to past militant activity and treated him as a sanctioned or designated figure. Supporters framed him as a key anti-ISIS commander, while critics emphasized his militia ties and regional alignment.

## 2020 U.S. Strike and Aftermath

He was killed in a U.S. strike near Baghdad International Airport alongside Iranian commander Qasem Soleimani. The event escalated U.S.–Iran tensions and became a major inflection point in Iraqi domestic politics, including discussions about foreign troops and sovereignty.
