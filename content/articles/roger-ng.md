## Background

- Roger Ng (full name commonly reported as **Roger Ng Chong Hwa**) is a Malaysian former Goldman Sachs banker.
- He became widely known through the international investigations into **1Malaysia Development Berhad (1MDB)**, a Malaysian state fund at the center of large-scale fraud and bribery allegations.

## Connection to the 1MDB scheme (high level)

- U.S. prosecutors alleged that bankers and intermediaries helped raise billions of dollars for 1MDB bond offerings and that portions of the proceeds were **misappropriated**.
- Ng was accused of participating in a conspiracy involving **bribery and money laundering**, including efforts to secure business and conceal the flow of funds.
- Public reporting and court proceedings frequently connect his case to other figures in the broader 1MDB scandal, including **Tim Leissner** (a former Goldman Sachs banker who pleaded guilty) and financier **Jho Low** (often described as a key architect of the scheme).

## Legal proceedings

- **Extradition to the United States:** Ng was extradited from Malaysia to face U.S. charges (widely reported as occurring in 2019).
- **Trial and conviction (U.S.):** In 2022, a federal jury in the Eastern District of New York convicted Ng for his role in the 1MDB-related conspiracy.
- **Sentencing:** He was later sentenced in U.S. federal court; the sentence is commonly reported as **10 years** (see references).

## Significance

- The Ng case is often cited as part of the U.S. Department of Justice’s broader "kleptocracy"-related enforcement actions tied to 1MDB.
- It also reinforced regulatory and reputational fallout for financial institutions involved in major sovereign or quasi-sovereign fund transactions.
