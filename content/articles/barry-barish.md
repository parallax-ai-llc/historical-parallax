## Summary

Barry Barish is an American experimental physicist who shared the 2017 Nobel Prize in Physics for decisive contributions to the LIGO detector and the observation of gravitational waves. As director of LIGO from 1997 to 2006, he transformed it from a small prototype into a full-scale observatory. His leadership and organizational skills were crucial to making LIGO a success.
