## Summary

Bettino Craxi was an Italian socialist politician who served as prime minister in the 1980s and later became a central figure in the *Tangentopoli* ("Bribesville") corruption investigations of the early 1990s. He was convicted in multiple corruption-related cases and spent his final years in Tunisia.

## Political Career (High Level)

Craxi rose through the Italian Socialist Party (PSI) and became one of the most prominent leaders of Italy’s post-war party system. His time in office is often summarized as part of the “First Republic” era, characterized by coalition governments, strong party structures, and extensive patronage networks.

## Tangentopoli and Mani Pulite

The early-1990s investigations commonly referred to as *Mani Pulite* ("Clean Hands") uncovered widespread illegal financing and bribery across major parties and public contracts. Craxi’s prosecutions became emblematic of the wider crisis because they connected a top national leader to practices that investigators argued were systemic rather than isolated.

## Exile and Legal Status

After convictions and sentencing, Craxi lived in Hammamet, Tunisia. The question of whether he was a political scapegoat or a principal architect of illicit party financing became a long-running debate in Italian public life, but the court outcomes are a fixed part of the historical record.

## Legacy

- **End of an era:** Craxi’s fall is frequently treated as a marker of the collapse of Italy’s traditional party system in the 1990s.
- **Reform debates:** Discussions of anti-corruption policy in Italy often cite Tangentopoli as a turning point, with Craxi as a defining case.
- **Contested memory:** Supporters and critics continue to argue about how to weigh his policy record against the scandal and convictions.

In academic and journalistic accounts, Craxi is often used to illustrate how party financing mechanisms can become normalized, and how legal enforcement can rapidly destabilize a political equilibrium.

His death in exile further strengthened his symbolic role in Italy’s memory of the 1990s upheaval.
