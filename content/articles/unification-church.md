## Additional Context

The Unification Church, formally the **Unification Movement**, is a new religious movement founded in South Korea by Sun Myung Moon in the 1950s and later expanded internationally.

### Beliefs and organization (high level)

- It developed a distinctive theology and a strong organizational structure with affiliated groups and institutions.
- The movement became widely known for large group wedding ceremonies, often referred to in media as “mass weddings.”

### Public controversy

- The group has been a subject of controversy and scrutiny in multiple countries, including debates about recruitment practices, politics, and finances.
- In academic and media contexts, it is frequently discussed as a case study in modern religious movements and transnational networks.
