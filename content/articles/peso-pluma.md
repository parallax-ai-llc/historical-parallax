## Summary

Peso Pluma, born Hassan Emilio Kabande Laija on June 15, 1999, in Zapopan, Jalisco, Mexico, is a singer-songwriter who became one of the most commercially successful Latin artists of the 2020s. His hybrid style — blending regional Mexican music, particularly corridos tumbados, with urban and trap influences — helped redefine the genre for a younger global audience.

His 2023 breakout placed him at the intersection of mainstream pop and narcocorrido culture, making him the first regional Mexican artist to reach the top five of the Billboard Hot 100. By 2024, he had accumulated billions of streams, performed at major festivals worldwide, and reshaped the commercial trajectory of Latin music.

Peso Pluma's rise marked a generational shift in how regional Mexican music is produced, packaged, and consumed globally, drawing comparisons to the crossover moments of artists like Bad Bunny in reggaeton a decade earlier.

## Early Life & Background

Hassan Kabande Laija was born on June 15, 1999, in Zapopan, a municipality of the Guadalajara metropolitan area in Jalisco, Mexico. He grew up immersed in both Mexican regional music and American pop and hip-hop, a dual influence that would later define his sound. He spent a portion of his youth in Caborca, Sonora, before relocating to Guadalajara. His stage name, Peso Pluma — Spanish for "featherweight" — is a reference to his lean build.

He began writing music as a teenager and taught himself guitar, drawing from norteño traditions while absorbing contemporary urban production styles. He relocated to the United States briefly, spending time in the Houston, Texas area, where he was further exposed to rap and trap music.

## Rise to Prominence

Peso Pluma signed with Double P Records and began releasing music in 2020, gaining regional traction before breaking nationally. His 2022 collaboration with Natanael Cano on the song "El Belicon" helped establish him within the corridos tumbados scene. His 2023 album *Génesis* was the definitive commercial breakthrough. The album's single "Ella Baila Sola," a collaboration with Eslabon Armado, reached number four on the Billboard Hot 100 in June 2023 — a historic milestone for regional Mexican music.

That same year, his collaboration with Bad Bunny, "MONACO," further cemented his mainstream crossover. He performed at Coachella in April 2024, one of the highest-profile stage appearances an artist in his genre had achieved.

## Key Achievements

- "Ella Baila Sola" peaked at No. 4 on the Billboard Hot 100 (2023), the highest charting regional Mexican song at the time.
- *Génesis* (2023) debuted at No. 3 on the Billboard 200, another record for the genre.
- Named Spotify's most-streamed artist in Mexico for 2023.
- Won Billboard Latin Music Award for Latin Rhythm Airplay Artist of the Year (2023).
- Performed at Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival (April 2024).
- Collaborated with artists across genres including Becky G, Marshmello, and Bad Bunny.

## Controversies & Criticism

In September 2023, Peso Pluma received a narco threat — reportedly from the Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG) — warning him not to perform in Tijuana. He canceled the concert and addressed the threat publicly, drawing international media attention to the real dangers artists in the corridos tumbados space can face due to lyrical associations with cartel culture.

Critics have also raised ongoing questions about the glorification of drug trafficking and cartel life inherent to the narcocorrido tradition. Peso Pluma has acknowledged the genre's roots while positioning his work as artistic expression rather than endorsement.

## Legacy & Influence

Peso Pluma is widely credited with accelerating the global mainstreaming of corridos tumbados and regional Mexican music broadly. His success opened doors for a wave of artists in the genre and shifted major label attention toward Mexican regional music in a sustained way. His aesthetic — mixing cowboy and street fashion, Spanish-language lyricism, and trap-influenced production — has been widely replicated by emerging artists.

He represents a broader cultural moment in which Mexican identity and regional traditions have achieved unprecedented visibility on global pop charts, influencing how the music industry markets and signs Latin talent.
