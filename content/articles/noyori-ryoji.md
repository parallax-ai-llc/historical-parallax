## Summary

Noyori Ryoji is a Japanese chemist who won the 2001 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work on chirally catalyzed hydrogenation reactions. He developed BINAP-based asymmetric catalysis, enabling the efficient production of single-enantiomer drugs and chemicals. His methods revolutionized pharmaceutical manufacturing.
