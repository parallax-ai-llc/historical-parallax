## Summary

Indonesian serial killer known as the Singing Sorcerer who murdered 12 girls in Purwokerto between 1986 and 1993 using their body parts in rituals.

## Overview of the case

Siswanto was convicted for the murder of multiple girls in Indonesia, with crimes reported in the Purwokerto area across the late 1980s and early 1990s. Media accounts sometimes attach sensational labels (such as "Singing Sorcerer") to emphasize the brutality of the acts and alleged ritual elements.

## Modus and allegations of ritual use

Reports in the case have described:

- Targeting of minors who were vulnerable and often alone.
- Sexual violence and homicide.
- Claims that body parts were used in ritual contexts.

In many serial crime narratives, alleged "ritual" dimensions are contested or exaggerated in popular retellings; court records and credible reporting are typically the most reliable basis for determining what was proven.

## Arrest, trial, and sentencing

Siswanto was arrested in the mid-1990s and later sentenced to death. The case became widely known in Indonesia, both because of the number of victims and because it highlighted gaps in child protection and investigative resources in the regions affected.

## Legacy

The case is often referenced in discussions about serial violence in Indonesia and the role of media framing in shaping public understanding of high-profile crimes.
