## Summary

Grigori Yefimovich Rasputin was a Russian mystic, self-proclaimed holy man, and influential figure at the court of Tsar Nicholas II. Born to a peasant family in Siberia, he rose to extraordinary prominence in St. Petersburg society through his purported healing abilities and spiritual charisma. He became especially close to the imperial family after reportedly alleviating the suffering of Tsarevich Alexei, the tsar's hemophiliac son. His growing influence over Empress Alexandra and, by extension, over state affairs made him deeply controversial and a lightning rod for criticism of the Romanov dynasty. He was assassinated in December 1916 by a group of nobles who believed his presence was damaging the Russian imperial house.
