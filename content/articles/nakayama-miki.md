## Summary

Nakayama Miki was the founder of Tenrikyo, one of the oldest and largest new religious movements in Japan. In 1838, she claimed to have become possessed by 'God the Parent' (Tenri-O-no-Mikoto). She taught that joyful living and charitable acts would create a world of happiness. She composed the Ofudesaki and Mikagura-uta, the foundational scriptures of Tenrikyo. Today Tenrikyo has millions of followers worldwide.
