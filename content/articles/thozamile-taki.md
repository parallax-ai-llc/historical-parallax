## Summary

South African serial killer known as the 'Frankfort Killer' who murdered at least 9 women in the Free State between 1996 and 1998. He raped and strangled his victims. Sentenced to life imprisonment.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Crimes (High-Level)

- The murders occurred in and around **Frankfort** in South Africa’s Free State province.
- Victims were primarily **women**, and reports describe a pattern involving sexual assault and strangulation.
- The number of confirmed victims is generally reported as at least nine, with some accounts discussing additional suspected cases.

## Investigation and Arrest

- Serial cases in smaller communities can be difficult to solve due to limited resources and the need to connect incidents that may initially appear isolated.
- Investigators ultimately identified and arrested Taki in 1998, ending the active period of killings.

## Trial and Sentence

- In 1999, a South African court sentenced Taki to **life imprisonment**.
- The sentence reflected both the severity of the crimes and the repeated nature of the violence.

## Public Impact

- The case contributed to heightened public fear in the region during the late 1990s.
- It is often referenced in discussions of violent crime in post-apartheid South Africa and the challenges of policing and victim protection.

## Notes on Reporting

- Public sources vary in how they count victims and describe specific incidents.
