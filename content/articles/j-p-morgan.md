## Summary

John Pierpont Morgan was the most powerful banker in American history, organizing the formation of major corporations including U.S. Steel, General Electric, and AT&T. He personally bailed out the U.S. government during the Panic of 1893 and organized private bankers to stop the Panic of 1907. His banking house, J.P. Morgan & Co., dominated corporate finance and industrial consolidation during the Gilded Age.
