## Summary

Françoise Barré-Sinoussi co-discovered HIV (Human Immunodeficiency Virus) as the cause of AIDS in 1983. Her work at the Pasteur Institute was crucial to identifying the retrovirus from a lymph node biopsy. She received the 2008 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine and has spent her career working on HIV/AIDS research and advocacy.
