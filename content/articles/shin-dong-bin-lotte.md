## Summary

Lotte Group chairman convicted of bribery for providing $7 million to former President Park Geun-hye in exchange for duty-free shop licenses. He was initially acquitted but convicted on appeal.

## Background

Shin Dong-bin (also known as Akio Shigemitsu) is a South Korean-Japanese business executive associated with **Lotte Group**, a major conglomerate with significant operations in South Korea and Japan. He became the group’s chairman during a period of corporate governance disputes within the founding family.

## Role at Lotte Group

At a high level, Lotte Group spans retail, food, chemicals, hospitality, and entertainment businesses. As chairman, Shin Dong-bin was linked to strategic decisions and restructuring initiatives, including governance reforms and efforts to clarify control between Lotte’s Korean and Japanese entities.

## Legal and Political Context

The bribery case referenced in many summaries is typically discussed in the broader context of South Korea’s **Park Geun-hye** corruption scandal. Reporting and court records commonly describe corporate donations and alleged quid-pro-quo arrangements involving state influence over licensing and regulatory decisions.

## Notes on Public Office

Although the legal controversy involves political figures and state licensing, Shin Dong-bin is primarily a **corporate executive**, not a career politician. Biographical treatments usually focus on his business leadership and the legal proceedings connected to corporate-government relations.

## Aftermath and Impact

The case is often cited in discussions of chaebol governance, compliance, and the recurring tension between large conglomerates and political power in South Korea. The pardon noted in the timeline is frequently interpreted as part of a broader pattern in which high-profile business leaders receive clemency after convictions, sometimes justified publicly on economic grounds.

In media coverage, it is also used as an example of how licensing and regulatory decisions can become focal points for allegations of improper corporate influence.
