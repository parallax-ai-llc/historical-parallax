## Summary

Robert Burns Woodward was an American organic chemist who won the 1965 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his achievements in the art of organic synthesis. He is considered the greatest organic chemist of the 20th century, having synthesized cholesterol, cortisone, strychnine, chlorophyll, and vitamin B12. He also co-developed the Woodward-Hoffmann rules for predicting the outcomes of organic reactions. His total syntheses were masterpieces of chemical strategy.
