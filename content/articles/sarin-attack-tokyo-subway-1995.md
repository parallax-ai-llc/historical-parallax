At a high level, the Tokyo subway sarin attack on **March 20, 1995** involved the release of the nerve agent sarin on multiple subway trains during the morning rush hour. It killed **13 people** and injured thousands (many with acute symptoms and longer-term health effects). The attack is widely recognized as one of the most serious chemical terrorism incidents in modern history.

## Why this event matters
The Tokyo sarin attack became a benchmark case for emergency response to chemical exposure in a dense urban setting. Key themes in retrospective analysis include:

- **Medical triage under uncertainty:** Early symptoms were confusing, and hospitals faced sudden surges of patients.
- **Public-transport vulnerability:** The attack demonstrated how small quantities of toxic agents can be leveraged in crowded transit systems.
- **Domestic extremist threats:** The perpetrators were a domestic cult rather than a foreign state actor, complicating assumptions about who could produce and deploy chemical agents.
