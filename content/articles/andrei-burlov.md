## Summary

Russian serial killer who murdered at least 9 people in Kurgan Oblast between 1993 and 2000. He targeted drifters and alcoholics, killing them in his apartment. Sentenced to life imprisonment.

## Modus and Investigation

Burlov reportedly targeted socially isolated men, often described as drifters or people with alcohol dependence, and lured them to his apartment. The killings were typically committed indoors, which reduced witnesses and delayed discovery.

As patterns emerged, investigators connected multiple deaths and disappearances in Kurgan Oblast to a single offender. Police attention eventually focused on Burlov, leading to his arrest in 2000.

## Trial and Sentence

In court proceedings that followed his arrest, Burlov was convicted of multiple murders. He received a life sentence in the Russian penal system, reflecting both the number of victims attributed to him and the severity of the crimes.

## Historical Context

The case is sometimes discussed within a broader post-Soviet crime context, when economic instability and weak social services increased vulnerability for marginal populations and complicated law-enforcement efforts.

## Notes on Attribution

Public summaries typically report **at least nine** victims, while the precise number can depend on which suspected cases are included in official counts. As with many serial-crime investigations, some deaths may be difficult to confirm due to delayed discovery and limited forensic evidence.
