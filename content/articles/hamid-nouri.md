## Summary

Iranian former prison official convicted by a Swedish court for his role in the 1988 massacre of thousands of political prisoners in Iran. It was the first trial worldwide for the 1988 executions.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1988 | Participated in mass execution of prisoners at Gohardasht Prison |
| 2019 | Arrested in Sweden under universal jurisdiction |
| 2022 | Convicted of crimes against humanity and sentenced to life |

## Trial and Aftermath (High-Level)

- **Universal jurisdiction:** The case is frequently cited as an example of universal jurisdiction being used to prosecute alleged atrocities committed outside the prosecuting state.
- **Scale and dispute:** Public reporting notes wide variance in estimates of the number of victims in the 1988 prison executions, reflecting uncertainty and political contestation.
- **Appeal outcome:** Reporting describes the life sentence being upheld on appeal in Sweden.
- **Prisoner exchange:** Later reporting describes Nouri being returned to Iran in a Sweden–Iran prisoner swap.
- **Documentation:** The trial generated extensive witness testimony and documentary claims; summaries typically emphasize the evidentiary record developed in open court.
