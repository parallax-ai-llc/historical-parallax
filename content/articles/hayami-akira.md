## Summary

Hayami Akira is a Japanese historical demographer who pioneered the study of Japan's pre-modern population using Buddhist temple death registers (shumon aratame cho). His quantitative approach revealed that Tokugawa-era Japan had sophisticated demographic patterns, including population control and urbanization rates comparable to pre-industrial Europe.
