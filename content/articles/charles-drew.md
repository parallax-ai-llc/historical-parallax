## Summary

Charles Drew was an African-American physician and surgeon who developed improved techniques for blood storage and founded large-scale blood banks during World War II. His research on blood plasma preservation saved countless lives. He directed the first American Red Cross Blood Bank but resigned in protest of racial segregation policies in blood donation.
