## Summary

Kateri Tekakwitha was a Mohawk-Algonquin woman who converted to Catholicism and practiced extreme devotion despite opposition from her community. She was the first Native American to be canonized by the Catholic Church in 2012. Orphaned by smallpox, she was raised by her uncle and converted at age 19. She practiced severe asceticism and was known for her deep prayer life. She is the patron saint of ecology and indigenous peoples.
