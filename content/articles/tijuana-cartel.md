## Summary

Mexican drug cartel led by the Arellano-Felix brothers that controlled the Tijuana-San Diego drug corridor. Known for extreme violence including the murder of Cardinal Posadas in 1993.

## Origins and Structure

The organization is commonly associated with the Arellano Félix family and is sometimes referred to as the Arellano Félix Organization (AFO). Its influence grew during a period of fragmentation and competition among Mexico’s major trafficking groups, with control of border crossings and smuggling routes serving as a central source of power.

## Geography and Strategic Importance

Tijuana’s proximity to San Diego made the region a critical corridor for moving narcotics into the United States. Control over plazas (key trafficking territories) along the border has been a recurring driver of violence, alliances, and disputes among rival organizations.

## Rivalries and Conflict

Reporting on Mexico’s drug war frequently situates the Tijuana Cartel in competition with other major groups, including those associated with Sinaloa. Rivalry dynamics have included shifting local alliances, targeted killings, and contested control of trafficking infrastructure.

## Law Enforcement Pressure and Evolution

Arrests, extraditions, and internal fragmentation are often cited as factors that weakened the cartel’s centralized leadership over time. As with many organized crime groups, splinter factions and successor structures have been reported, complicating simple narratives of a single, stable organization.

## Wider Impact

The cartel’s history is referenced in discussions of cross-border crime, corruption risks, and the challenges of binational enforcement. It also illustrates how violence can be amplified when competition concentrates around high-value border routes.

## Terminology

In some sources the group is labeled by geography (“Tijuana Cartel”), while in others it is identified by key leadership figures (“Arellano Félix Organization”). The naming conventions can matter when tracking splinter groups or changes in leadership over time.
