## Summary

Jean-Baptiste de la Salle was a French priest and educator who founded the Brothers of the Christian Schools (De La Salle Brothers), pioneering modern pedagogy. He established free schools for the poor, organized teacher training programs, and wrote foundational works on education. He is the patron saint of teachers and the founder of modern Christian education. His innovations included teaching in the vernacular rather than Latin and grouping students by ability.
