## Background

Noureddine Adam is a Central African figure associated in open-source reporting with the Séléka coalition, a loose alliance of predominantly Muslim rebel groups that advanced on the capital, Bangui, and took power in 2013. The takeover contributed to a wider cycle of violence and displacement in the Central African Republic (CAR), including the formation of opposing militias and prolonged instability.

## Role in the Séléka Period

Summaries commonly describe Adam as a senior coordinator or commander during Séléka's military campaign. After the coalition took power, Séléka figures held key roles in the transitional political and security apparatus, while international organizations documented serious abuses committed by multiple armed actors.

## International Sanctions and Allegations

The United Nations Security Council sanctions regime for CAR has listed individuals alleged to have supported armed groups or been responsible for human rights violations. Adam is widely reported to have been sanctioned under this framework, including measures such as an assets freeze and travel ban.

Sanctions listings generally cite broad categories of alleged conduct (for example, directing forces implicated in killings or other abuses) rather than adjudicated criminal findings.

## Later Developments

In subsequent years, Adam has continued to appear in reporting related to armed group dynamics and negotiations in CAR. As with many figures in the conflict, public accounts of his influence and whereabouts vary over time and across sources.
