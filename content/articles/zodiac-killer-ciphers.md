## Summary

The Zodiac Killer sent four encrypted messages to Bay Area newspapers. Only two have been solved; one in 1969 and another in 2020 by amateur codebreakers using modern computing.

## What the ciphers were

The Zodiac letters included multiple cryptograms sent to newspapers and accompanied by taunting messages demanding publication. The best-known are commonly labeled by their character length:

- **Z408 (1969)**: a 408-character cipher that was solved shortly after publication.
- **Z340 (1969)**: a 340-character cipher that remained unsolved for decades.
- **Z13 (1970)** and **Z32 (1970)**: shorter ciphers/cryptograms that remain disputed or unsolved in mainstream accounts.

## Solution history (high level)

- The 1969 solution demonstrated that the cipher text contained a coherent English message and helped establish patterns in Zodiac's writing style.
- The 2020 solution of the 340-character cipher drew on both classical cryptanalysis ideas and modern computational search to test transposition and substitution hypotheses.
- Even when a cipher is "solved," interpretation can remain cautious: unclear plaintext segments, formatting choices, and the sender's intent can leave room for debate.

## Why the ciphers matter

- **Forensic linguistics**: letter content and word choice are used to compare communications attributed to Zodiac.
- **Cryptography culture**: the long-unsolved Z340 became a famous public puzzle and a touchstone for amateur codebreaking communities.
- **Case history**: public attention to the ciphers influenced media coverage and the investigation's public narrative.

## Notes and limitations

This topic overlaps with an ongoing unsolved case. Public summaries typically distinguish between (a) authenticated correspondence, (b) disputed letters, and (c) later hoaxes, which can complicate timelines and claims.
