## Summary

William Randolph Hearst Jr. continued the media empire of his father, serving as editor-in-chief of the Hearst newspaper chain. He won the Pulitzer Prize for international reporting in 1956 for a series of interviews with Soviet leaders. He helped transition the Hearst Corporation from newspapers to a diversified media company including magazines, television, and cable networks.
