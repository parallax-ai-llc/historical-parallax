## Key Facts

- **Name**: Michael Spavor Kovrig (commonly reported as Michael Kovrig).
- **Nationality**: Canadian.
- **Known for**: Detention in the People’s Republic of China (PRC) from late 2018 to 2021, widely characterized as a case of **hostage diplomacy**.
- **Primary context**: The detention coincided with Canada’s arrest of Huawei executive **Meng Wanzhou** at the request of the United States.

## Background

Michael Kovrig is a Canadian former diplomat who later worked as a senior adviser for the International Crisis Group (ICG). His public profile became internationally prominent after his detention in China in 2018.

## Detention (2018–2021)

- **Detained**: December 2018 (alongside Canadian citizen Michael Spavor).
- **Detaining authority**: Chinese state security / judicial system (as reported by major media and official statements).
- **Allegations**: China stated the men were suspected of activities endangering national security (details were not publicly transparent to outside observers).
- **Access and process**: Canadian officials and observers repeatedly raised concerns about limited consular access and opaque legal proceedings.
- **International response**: The case drew sustained attention from Canada and other governments, and became a frequently cited example in discussions of state-to-state coercion.

## Release and Aftermath

- **Released**: September 2021.
- **Timing**: The release occurred on the same day Meng Wanzhou returned to China after reaching a deferred prosecution agreement in the United States (a timing widely interpreted as linked).
- **After release**: Kovrig and Spavor returned to Canada; Canadian leadership publicly framed the outcome as the end of an unjust detention.

## Significance

- **Hostage diplomacy as a term**: Kovrig’s case is often referenced in analyses of how governments may detain foreign nationals to gain leverage in broader disputes.
- **Canada–China relations**: The episode contributed to a prolonged deterioration in bilateral relations and heightened public scrutiny of travel risks.
- **Policy impact**: The case influenced debates on consular protection, deterrence, and coordinated responses among allied countries.

## Related People and Entities

- **Meng Wanzhou** — Huawei executive whose extradition case in Canada overlapped with the detentions.
- **International Crisis Group** — Kovrig’s employer at the time of detention (as widely reported).
