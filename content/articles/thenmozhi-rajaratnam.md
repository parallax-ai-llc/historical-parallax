## Summary

Sri Lankan LTTE suicide bomber known as 'Dhanu' who assassinated Indian Prime Minister Rajiv Gandhi on May 21, 1991, at an election rally in Sriperumbudur, Tamil Nadu. She detonated a belt bomb while greeting him.

## Background and Context

- Thenmozhi Rajaratnam, often identified by the nom de guerre **Dhanu**, was associated with the **Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE)**, a militant separatist organization involved in the Sri Lankan Civil War.[^1]
- The assassination took place amid complex regional politics in South Asia, including India’s prior involvement in Sri Lanka through the **Indian Peace Keeping Force (IPKF)** (late 1980s), which became a key grievance shaping LTTE attitudes toward the Indian state.[^1]

## The Assassination (High-Level)

- On **21 May 1991**, Rajiv Gandhi attended an election-campaign event in **Sriperumbudur, Tamil Nadu**.
- Dhanu approached him in a greeting gesture and detonated an explosive device (commonly described as a **belt bomb**), killing Gandhi and others nearby.[^1]
- The attack is widely described as one of the most significant political assassinations in modern Indian history due to the target’s status as a former Prime Minister and national leader.[^1]

## Aftermath and Impact

- Investigations and prosecutions following the attack focused on the broader LTTE-linked conspiracy and logistics that enabled the bombing.[^1]
- The assassination had lasting effects on Indian domestic politics and security policy, including heightened attention to VIP protection and counter-terrorism measures.
- In public memory and scholarship, the event is frequently discussed in connection with the Sri Lankan conflict, transnational militancy, and the political consequences of regional intervention.[^1]
