## Summary

Serbian-Australian paramilitary leader known as Captain Dragan who trained and led paramilitaries accused of atrocities during the Croatian War. He was extradited from Australia after a decade-long legal battle.

## Background

Dragan Vasiljković became a prominent public figure during the breakup of Yugoslavia and the wars that followed. The “Captain Dragan” nickname reflects his media presence and association with paramilitary training and operations connected to Serb forces.

## Allegations and Conviction (High Level)

The case against him is typically summarized in terms of responsibility for mistreatment of detainees and other war crimes during the Croatian War of Independence. His prosecution also became notable internationally because it required:

- Long-running **extradition proceedings** in Australia
- Coordination between national courts and post-conflict accountability efforts

## Broader Significance

The Vasiljković case is often cited in discussions of:

- How countries handle extradition for alleged atrocities committed abroad
- The role of charismatic commanders and irregular units in civil conflicts
- Post-war trials as part of transitional justice in the former Yugoslavia

## Notes on Terminology

In summaries of the Yugoslav wars, the term “paramilitary” can cover a wide range of formations, from loosely organized militias to units that coordinated closely with state security structures. This ambiguity is part of why individual cases often focus carefully on command relationships, orders, and the practical control exercised over perpetrators.
