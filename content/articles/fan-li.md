## Summary

Fan Li was an ancient Chinese strategist, politician, and merchant who is revered as the patron saint of business in Chinese culture. After helping the King of Yue defeat the state of Wu, he retired from politics and became a successful merchant three times, giving away his fortune each time. He is known as Tao Zhu Gong (the wealthy merchant) and his business principles are still studied today. He exemplified the ideal of wisdom in both statecraft and commerce.
