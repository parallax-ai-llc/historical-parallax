## Summary

Bosnian Serb police chief convicted of crimes against humanity for persecution of Bosniaks.

## Background

Stojan Župljanin is a Bosnian Serb former police official associated with the Bosnian War period.
During the early 1990s he held senior positions within Bosnian Serb security structures in and around Banja Luka.
In historical summaries, he is often described as a key police figure in the area that became part of Republika Srpska.

## ICTY Indictment and Proceedings

The ICTY charged Župljanin for alleged involvement in a campaign of persecution and other crimes committed against non-Serb civilians.
The case was part of a broader set of prosecutions focused on the use of state and security apparatuses to remove or terrorize targeted populations.
After being indicted, he remained at large for an extended period before being arrested and transferred to the tribunal.

## Conviction (High-Level)

In the tribunal’s findings, Župljanin was held responsible for crimes against humanity related to persecution and other inhumane acts.
Judicial conclusions emphasized the role of policing and detention practices in supporting larger political and military objectives.
Appeal proceedings later affirmed the core findings and sentence.

## Why He Is Notable

Župljanin is notable as an example of a senior police official—rather than a frontline combat commander—being prosecuted and convicted for wartime crimes.
His case is frequently cited in discussions about:

- command and superior responsibility within police structures
- coordinated persecution carried out through security services
- the ICTY’s approach to linking institutional roles to mass-atrocity crimes
