## Summary

C. V. Raman was an Indian physicist who won the 1930 Nobel Prize in Physics for his discovery of the Raman effect, in which light changes wavelength when scattered by molecules. He was the first Asian to receive a Nobel Prize in any branch of science. His discovery provided a powerful tool for studying the molecular structure of materials. Raman founded the Raman Research Institute in Bangalore.
