## Expanded Overview

- **Full name**: Pablo Emilio Escobar Gaviria.
- **Known for**: Leadership role in the Medellín Cartel during the late 20th century cocaine trade.
- **Public profile**: Combined clandestine trafficking operations with highly visible public influence in Colombia.

## Background

- **Birthplace**: Rionegro, Antioquia, Colombia (raised in the Medellín area).
- **Family and early work**: Came from a modest background; engaged in various illicit activities before large-scale drug trafficking.

## Power and Influence (High Level)

- **Medellín Cartel**: Built a major trafficking organization with international routes and networks.
- **Revenue and logistics**: Cocaine production and distribution depended on complex supply chains, corruption, and enforcement.
- **"Plata o plomo"**: Phrase commonly associated with coercion through bribery or violence.

## Politics and Public Activities

- **Political involvement**: Sought legitimacy and influence through public works and political associations.
- **Colombian institutions**: His activities contributed to sustained pressure on the state, judiciary, police, and media.

## Conflict with the State

- **Violence and terror**: Linked to campaigns of intimidation and attacks during efforts to avoid extradition and prosecution.
- **Extradition issue**: Opposition to extradition to the United States was a central strategic objective.

## Imprisonment and Escape

- **La Catedral**: Was held in a custom-built prison arrangement that later became politically untenable.
- **Escape**: Fled custody, leading to an intensified manhunt.

## Death

- **1993**: Located and killed in Medellín after a long search by Colombian forces, with support and intelligence cooperation.

## Legacy and Cultural Impact

- **Polarizing figure**: Remembered both for violence associated with his organization and for local social spending.
- **State capacity**: The period reshaped Colombia’s security strategy and international cooperation against narcotics trafficking.
- **Popular culture**: Subject of extensive journalism, biographies, films, and television dramatizations.
