## Early Career

- Baekeland trained as a chemist in Belgium and later established his career in the United States.
- Before Bakelite, he was also known for work in photographic chemistry, including commercially successful innovations.

## Bakelite (1907)

- Bakelite is often described as the first fully synthetic plastic: a thermosetting polymer that could be molded when heated and then hardened into a durable form.
- Its heat resistance and electrical insulating properties made it attractive for early electrical and consumer applications.

## Industrial Impact

- Bakelite helped define the material culture of the early 20th century: radios, telephones, electrical components, handles, and household goods.
- The success of Bakelite is frequently cited as a milestone in the rise of polymer science and mass manufacturing of plastics.

## Legacy

- Baekeland’s work illustrates how laboratory chemistry, patent strategy, and industrial partnerships combined to create new categories of everyday objects.
- The term “Bakelite” remains a cultural shorthand for early plastics and the aesthetics of the interwar and postwar industrial era.
