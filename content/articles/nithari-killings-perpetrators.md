## Summary

Moninder Singh Pandher and his servant Surinder Koli killed at least 19 women and children in Nithari village, Noida. Body parts were found in a drain near Pandher's house.

## Investigation and Public Reaction

The Nithari killings became a major national news story in India, prompting public outrage and intense scrutiny of local policing and missing-person reporting. The case led to questions about how early warnings were handled and whether institutional failures contributed to delays in identifying the pattern of disappearances.

## Trials and Sentencing

The legal process involved multiple cases and convictions connected to specific victims. Surinder Koli received several death sentences in connection with murder convictions, while proceedings relating to Moninder Singh Pandher included convictions and later legal reversals in some cases.

Because the case spans many charges and appeals, summaries in secondary sources often differ on counts and outcomes across different years.

## Broader Significance

The case is frequently discussed in the context of:

- victim protection and reporting mechanisms for missing children,
- investigative standards and evidence handling,
- media ethics and public panic during high-profile crime reporting.
