## Summary

Akira Toriyama (April 5, 1955 – March 1, 2024) was a Japanese manga artist and character designer whose work reshaped global popular culture. Best known as the creator of *Dragon Ball*, he produced one of the best-selling manga series in history and established visual and narrative conventions that defined the shonen genre for decades. His art style — dynamic action poses, expressive characters, and inventive creature design — became instantly recognizable worldwide.

Beyond manga, Toriyama's influence extended into animation, video games, and merchandise, generating an estimated franchise value exceeding $20 billion. His character design work for the *Dragon Quest* and *Chrono Trigger* video game franchises further cemented his status as one of the most versatile and prolific visual artists of the 20th century.

## Early Life & Background

Toriyama was born on April 5, 1955, in Nagoya, Aichi Prefecture, Japan. From childhood he showed a strong aptitude for drawing, spending much of his school years sketching characters and scenes. He studied at the Prefectural Okasaki Vocational High School and later worked as a graphic designer at an advertising agency in Nagoya for roughly three years after graduation. Dissatisfied with corporate work, he began submitting manga to *Weekly Shonen Jump* in the late 1970s, driven largely by the desire to win prizes and supplement his income.

## Rise to Prominence

Toriyama's first major success came with *Dr. Slump*, which debuted in *Weekly Shonen Jump* in January 1980. The gag manga featuring the robot girl Arale became a cultural phenomenon in Japan, running until 1984 and earning the Shogakukan Manga Award in 1981. Its anime adaptation drew massive television audiences.

In 1984, he launched *Dragon Ball*, loosely inspired by the Chinese novel *Journey to the West*. The series ran in *Weekly Shonen Jump* until 1995, spanning 519 chapters collected into 42 tankōbon volumes. Its sequel serialization, *Dragon Ball Z*, became a defining property of 1990s global animation and introduced concepts — the Super Saiyan transformation, the power-level system, tournament arcs — that countless series would imitate.

## Key Achievements

- *Dragon Ball* has sold over 260 million manga volumes worldwide as of the mid-2020s, making it one of the best-selling manga series ever published.
- The *Dragon Ball* anime franchise has aired continuously in various forms since 1986, including *Dragon Ball Z* (1989–1996), *Dragon Ball Super* (2015–2018), and theatrical films.
- Toriyama served as character designer for *Dragon Quest* (1986–present), the long-running Square Enix RPG franchise, contributing to titles that collectively sold over 84 million units.
- He co-designed characters for *Chrono Trigger* (1995), widely regarded as one of the greatest RPGs ever made.
- He received the Grand Prize at the Japan Media Arts Festival in 2013 for his overall body of work.

## Controversies & Criticism

Toriyama acknowledged in interviews that he frequently improvised *Dragon Ball*'s storyline without long-term planning, a practice some critics argue led to narrative inconsistencies and power-scaling issues in the later arcs of *Dragon Ball Z*. His relative withdrawal from public life and infrequent interviews also left fans with limited insight into his creative process. Some scholars of manga have noted that his female characters received comparatively less development than his male leads.

## Legacy & Influence

Toriyama died on March 1, 2024, from acute subdural hematoma at age 68. His death prompted tributes from artists, game developers, and fans across dozens of countries. Mangaka including Eiichiro Oda (*One Piece*), Masashi Kishimoto (*Naruto*), and Tite Kubo (*Bleach*) publicly cited him as a foundational influence. His visual grammar — the spiky-haired protagonist, escalating power transformations, globe-spanning tournaments — became the structural template for an entire generation of action manga. *Dragon Ball* continues to generate new animated films and merchandise, ensuring his work remains culturally active well beyond his lifetime.
