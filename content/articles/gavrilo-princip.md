## Summary
Gavrilo Princip was a Bosnian Serb nationalist who assassinated Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary and his wife Sophie in Sarajevo on June 28, 1914. His act triggered World War I. Too young for the death penalty, he was sentenced to 20 years and died of tuberculosis in prison.
