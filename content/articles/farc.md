## Summary

Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia, the largest guerrilla group in the Western Hemisphere. They fought a 52-year war, financed by drug trafficking and kidnapping. Signed peace deal in 2016. Some dissidents continue armed activities.

## Background and Ideology

FARC (Fuerzas Armadas Revolucionarias de Colombia) emerged from rural, left-wing self-defense groups and adopted a Marxist-Leninist orientation. Over time it developed a large, decentralized structure with regional blocs and fronts operating across different parts of Colombia.

## Conflict, Financing, and Tactics

The organization became widely known for guerrilla warfare against the Colombian state and for practices that generated revenue, including kidnapping for ransom and involvement in the cocaine economy. These methods, alongside attacks on security forces and infrastructure, contributed to its designation as a terrorist organization by several governments during parts of its history.

## Peace Process and Aftermath

In the 2010s, negotiations culminated in a 2016 peace agreement. The main organization demobilized and transitioned into a legal political movement, while some dissident factions rejected the accord and continued armed activity in certain regions.

## Legacy

FARC remains central to discussions of Colombia's modern conflict: its roots in political violence and inequality, the human toll of decades of war, and the challenges of reintegration, rural development, and security in the post-agreement period.

## Key Terms

- **Guerrilla movement:** An armed non-state force using irregular warfare, often in rural terrain.
- **Demobilization:** The process of disarmament and transition from an armed group into civilian or political life.
- **Dissident factions:** Breakaway elements that continue armed activity after a peace process.
