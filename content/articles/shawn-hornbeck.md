## Summary

American boy kidnapped at age 11 by Michael Devlin and held captive for over four years. He was found when police searched Devlin's apartment for another missing boy.

## Disappearance

Hornbeck disappeared in Missouri as a child, prompting a missing-person investigation and widespread community attention. Cases involving children often generate extensive volunteer search efforts alongside law-enforcement work, including the distribution of photographs, tip lines, and coordination across jurisdictions.

## Investigation and public attention

For years after the disappearance, reporting emphasized the uncertainty faced by families and investigators when leads are limited or unverified. Missing-child investigations typically involve:

- Follow-up on sightings and tips from the public.
- Examination of local and regional offender patterns.
- Long-term case management as new information emerges.

## Recovery and rescue

Hornbeck was ultimately located alive during police actions connected to another missing child. Public accounts of the case highlight how investigative breakthroughs can occur indirectly, when separate cases intersect.

## Legal proceedings

The perpetrator received multiple severe sentences. In many kidnapping cases, legal proceedings focus on securing long-term public safety and documenting the criminal acts for the record.

## Aftermath and broader impact

The Hornbeck case is often referenced in discussions about:

- The importance of sustained attention to long-term missing-person cases.
- The role of community awareness and coordinated reporting.
- Support needs for survivors and families after recovery.
