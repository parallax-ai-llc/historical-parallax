## What this category usually covers

- The term is used as a shorthand for a set of controversies involving a major management consulting firm across multiple jurisdictions.
- Cases typically involve allegations that consulting work enabled improper procurement, conflicts of interest, or policy outcomes with harmful externalities.
- The common thread is less about a single event and more about patterns of risk management, governance, and accountability.

## Common factual themes in major investigations (high level)

- Public-sector consulting contracts can raise corruption risks when bidding processes are opaque, when intermediaries are used, or when deliverables are difficult to audit.
- Corporate clients in regulated industries can create reputational and legal exposure when consultants are involved in strategies linked to public harm.
- Settlements (including opioid-related settlements in the United States) are often structured as financial payments without an admission of wrongdoing, depending on the jurisdiction and agreement.

## Governance and compliance responses

- Large firms under scrutiny typically respond by tightening third-party due diligence, revising client acceptance standards, and strengthening internal compliance functions.
- Regulators and prosecutors may require cooperation, monitoring, or reporting as part of resolutions.
- Reputational consequences can include loss of government contracts, client departures, and heightened oversight.
