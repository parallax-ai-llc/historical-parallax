## Background

Xiao Jianhua (Chinese: 肖建华) is a Chinese-Canadian businessman who became known for managing assets for the descendants of prominent Chinese leaders and for his role in a diversified investment group commonly referred to as Tomorrow Group.[^1]

He was born in Feicheng, Shandong, China, and attended Peking University at a young age on a scholarship, later receiving a law degree.[^1]

## Business Activities

Public reporting describes Xiao as the owner of Tomorrow Holding / Tomorrow Group, with interests spanning multiple sectors (including banking, insurance, real estate development, and resource-related businesses).[^1]

He has been described as holding stakes (often via investment vehicles) in several Chinese financial institutions, a structure that has been cited as contributing to his influence in China’s finance and corporate landscape.[^1]

## 2017 Disappearance and Legal Case

In 2017, Xiao was taken from Hong Kong to mainland China, an event widely covered internationally and often discussed as part of broader concerns about cross-border law enforcement and political risk for business figures.[^1]

A Shanghai court later sentenced him to 13 years in prison and imposed significant fines related to embezzlement and bribery allegations; Canadian diplomats were reportedly denied access to the trial.[^1]
