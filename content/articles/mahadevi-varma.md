## Summary

Mahadevi Varma was an Indian Hindi poet and essayist who was one of the four major pillars of the Chhayavad movement in Hindi poetry. While primarily known as a literary figure, her poetry was deeply imbued with mystical devotion and spiritual longing in the Bhakti tradition. She used the metaphor of divine love to explore feminist consciousness and human suffering. She received the Padma Vibhushan and Jnanpith Award.
