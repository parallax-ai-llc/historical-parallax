## Summary

Justine Henin won seven Grand Slam titles and was ranked world number one for 117 weeks. She won the 2004 Olympic gold.

## Grand Slam Record (High Level)

Henin won **seven** Grand Slam singles titles, with a particularly strong record at the French Open. Across the mid-2000s, she was a consistent contender on clay and hard courts and regularly reached the late stages of majors.

## Playing Style

She was known for an all-court game built around a celebrated one-handed backhand, variety in shot selection, and strong movement. Commentators frequently cite her ability to change pace and direction as a defining strength.

## Rankings and Consistency

Henin held the WTA world No. 1 ranking for **117 weeks** in total, reflecting sustained performance across multiple seasons rather than a single peak year.

## Retirement and Return

She announced a retirement from professional tennis while still among the top-ranked players, then later returned to competition before retiring again. Her career is often discussed as an example of an elite athlete stepping away and attempting a high-level comeback.

## Legacy

In tennis history discussions, Henin is commonly listed among the leading players of her era, especially for her clay-court achievements and the aesthetic influence of her backhand technique.
