## Summary

Ryoji Noyori was a Japanese chemist who shared the 2001 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work on chirally catalyzed hydrogenation reactions. He developed asymmetric catalysis methods using BINAP-ruthenium complexes that enabled the efficient production of single-enantiomer drugs and chemicals. His work revolutionized the pharmaceutical industry by making chiral synthesis more practical and efficient.
