## Background

- Chey Tae-won (최태원) is widely described as a South Korean businessman and the chairman of SK Group.
- Wikipedia reports he was born on December 3, 1960 in Suwon, Gyeonggi Province.
- He studied physics at Korea University (BS) and later pursued (but did not complete) graduate study in economics at the University of Chicago.

## SK Group Leadership (high-level)

- Wikipedia states he became chairman of SK Group in 1998 after the death of his father, Chey Jong-hyun.
- SK Group is typically described as one of South Korea’s largest conglomerates, with major affiliates including SK Telecom, SK Hynix, and SK Innovation.
- Wikipedia notes additional leadership roles across major SK operating companies during his tenure.

## Legal Case and Pardon (overview)

- Wikipedia reports that he was indicted in 2012 for embezzlement related to SK-group companies.
- In 2013, he was found guilty and sentenced to prison, and Wikipedia notes he was later pardoned in 2015.
- Public accounts often cite this case as a prominent example in South Korea of corporate governance controversies involving chaebol leadership.

## Public/Institutional Roles

- Wikipedia states he has served as chair of the Korea Chamber of Commerce and Industry (KCCI) since March 2021.
- Wikipedia also notes involvement in high-profile public initiatives (e.g., support for South Korea’s major international event bids).
