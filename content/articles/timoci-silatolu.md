## Summary

Co-conspirator in the 2000 Fiji coup led by George Speight, convicted of treason.

## Background

Timoci Qiolevu Silatolu (sometimes styled with the chiefly honorific **Ratu**) is a Fijian former politician and telecommunications engineer.

Biographical details commonly reported in coverage of his public career include:

- Origin: Lomanikoro village, Rewa Province, Fiji.
- Family: married, with children.

## Political Career

Silatolu entered national politics in the late 1990s and served as a member of Fiji's House of Representatives.

Key points noted in public sources:

- Elected in the **1999** general election as a candidate of the **Fijian Association Party**.
- Represented the **Rewa Fijian Communal Constituency**.
- His party participated in the **People's Coalition** government formed after the election, but he was not appointed to cabinet.

## 2000 Fiji Coup and Legal Proceedings

Silatolu is described as a co-conspirator in the **2000 Fijian coup d'état** associated with George Speight.

Public reporting and reference summaries describe several related developments:

- In **July 2000**, he was appointed **Assistant Minister of Health** in the interim government organized by Laisenia Qarase.
- He was later **convicted** for involvement in the 2000 coup and **sentenced to imprisonment**.
- Contemporary reporting indicated that coup convicts could face very severe penalties under the law (including capital punishment), even though sentences varied by defendant and case.

## Later Political Activity

After the coup period, Silatolu continued to appear in Fiji's electoral politics.

- He contested the **2001** general election as a candidate of the **Conservative Alliance–Matanitu Vanua**, a party associated in public discourse with Speight sympathizers.
- He lost his seat to Ro Teimumu Kepa.
