## Background

- Peter Navarro (born July 15, 1949) is an American economist and political adviser who became closely associated with U.S. trade policy debates, particularly around China and the use of tariffs.
- He has held academic appointments and authored books and commentary on trade, industrial policy, and globalization.

## Education and Academic Work

- Navarro earned a PhD in economics and worked as a professor, including at the University of California, Irvine (UCI).[^1]
- In addition to academic research, he wrote popular-audience books that argued the United States had been disadvantaged by certain trade practices.

## Role in the Trump Administration (20178)

- Navarro served in the White House as a senior counselor focused on trade and manufacturing policy.
- He is commonly described as a key advocate for a more confrontational U.S. approach to trade with China, including the use of tariffs and tighter controls on supply chains.

## Public Positions and Themes

- Trade with China: Navarro has argued that Chinese industrial policies and intellectual property practices have harmed U.S. manufacturing and workers.
- Industrial policy: He has supported policies intended to expand domestic production capacity for strategic sectors.
- Tariffs: He has been a prominent proponent of tariffs as leverage in trade negotiations.

## Legal and Political Controversies

- January 6 subpoena: As a former Trump aide, Navarro was subpoenaed by the U.S. House committee investigating the January 6, 2021 attack on the U.S. Capitol and did not comply, leading to contempt proceedings.
- Contempt of Congress conviction: In 2023, he was convicted of contempt of Congress in connection with the subpoena dispute.[^1]
