## Summary

Hezbollah member convicted in absentia by the Special Tribunal for Lebanon for the 2005 assassination of former Prime Minister Rafik Hariri using a massive truck bomb that also killed 21 others.

## Background and Context

The assassination of Rafik Hariri on 14 February 2005 was a major political shock in Lebanon and the wider region, triggering mass protests and a long-running international investigation.

The **Special Tribunal for Lebanon (STL)** was a UN-backed court established to prosecute those responsible for the Hariri attack and related connected cases. STL proceedings included extensive analysis of telecoms and other circumstantial evidence described in public summaries of the case.

## Legal Proceedings (High Level)

- **Conviction in absentia** indicates the defendant was not in custody during trial; STL procedure allowed the case to proceed under specific conditions.
- Sentencing in absentia does not by itself resolve custody or enforcement; public reporting has described Ayyash as remaining at large after conviction.

## Why It Matters

This case is frequently cited in discussions of:
