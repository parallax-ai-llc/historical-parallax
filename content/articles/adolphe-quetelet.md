## Summary

Adolphe Quetelet pioneered the application of statistics to social science, developing the concept of the 'average man' (l'homme moyen) and what we now call the body mass index (BMI). He established the Royal Observatory of Belgium and was one of the founders of modern statistics. His work bridging mathematics and social science influenced public policy.
