## Summary

Leo Esaki is a Japanese physicist who shared the 1973 Nobel Prize in Physics for his experimental discovery of tunneling in semiconductors. He discovered the Esaki diode (tunnel diode), which demonstrated quantum mechanical tunneling in solids. This discovery had important applications in electronics and helped advance the understanding of quantum mechanics in solid-state physics.
