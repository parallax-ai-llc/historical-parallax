## Summary

Elsa Schiaparelli was an Italian fashion designer who was one of the most prominent figures in fashion between the two World Wars. Known for her bold, surrealist-inspired designs, she collaborated with artists such as Salvador Dalí and Jean Cocteau. She introduced shocking pink to the fashion world, pioneered the use of zippers as decorative elements, and created iconic designs including the lobster dress and the shoe hat. Her rivalry with Coco Chanel defined the fashion landscape of the 1930s.
