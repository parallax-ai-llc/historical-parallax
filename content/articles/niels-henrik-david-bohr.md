## Clarification

"Niels Henrik David Bohr" is Niels Bohr’s full name. Many sources and indexes list him under the shorter form "Niels Bohr," which can create duplicate or stub entries.

## Scientific Contributions (High Level)

- **Atomic theory:** Proposed the Bohr model of the atom (1913), an early quantum model explaining aspects of atomic spectra.
- **Quantum foundations:** Played a central role in developing the Copenhagen school of quantum mechanics and debates about interpretation.
- **Institution building:** Founded and led the Institute for Theoretical Physics in Copenhagen (now the Niels Bohr Institute), making it a major hub for 20th-century physics.

## World War II and Later Work

During World War II, Bohr fled Nazi-occupied Denmark and later worked with Allied scientific efforts. After the war, he advocated for international cooperation and greater openness around nuclear weapons, reflecting his concern about the political risks of secrecy and arms races.

## Legacy

Bohr remains one of the most influential physicists of the 20th century, with enduring impact across atomic physics, quantum theory, and the culture of research collaboration.

## Core Ideas Often Associated With Bohr

Bohr is closely associated with the principle of **complementarity** and with the Bohr–Einstein debates, which helped define how physicists and philosophers discuss measurement and realism in quantum mechanics.
