## Summary

James Gamble was an Irish-American soapmaker who co-founded Procter & Gamble with William Procter in 1837. He oversaw the manufacturing side of the business, developing soap formulas that made P&G products superior. The company grew from a small Cincinnati operation into a national brand. His partnership with Procter created one of the most enduring corporate dynasties in American history.
