## Summary

Bangladeshi politician and son of Khaleda Zia who was convicted of corruption and money laundering. He was also convicted of plotting a 2004 grenade attack on an opposition rally that killed 24 people. Lives in exile in London.

## Political Background

Rahman is associated with the Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP), one of Bangladesh’s main political parties, and is the son of former prime minister Khaleda Zia. His prominence has been closely tied to dynastic party leadership and the BNP’s long-running rivalry with the Awami League.

## Exile and Party Leadership

Rahman has lived in London for extended periods while facing multiple criminal cases in Bangladesh. Despite being abroad, he has remained influential within BNP circles and has been described in many accounts as a central decision-maker for party strategy and messaging.

## Major Criminal Cases

Public reporting and court proceedings have connected Rahman to high-profile corruption and money-laundering cases. He has also been convicted in relation to the 2004 grenade attack on an opposition rally, a political violence case that became a landmark episode in Bangladesh’s modern history.

## Political Significance

Rahman’s situation sits at the intersection of domestic law enforcement, partisan contestation, and questions about legitimacy and due process in highly polarized political environments. His continued role in BNP leadership while in exile has also shaped debates about accountability, succession, and the party’s future direction.

## Notes

Different political actors and observers dispute narratives about the motivations and fairness of prosecutions in Bangladesh’s political climate. Summaries of his record often distinguish between court judgments and partisan interpretations of those outcomes.
