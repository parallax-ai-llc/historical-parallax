## Summary

Former chief of staff of the Yugoslav Army acquitted by the ICTY on appeal. He was initially convicted for aiding and abetting crimes in Sarajevo and Srebrenica through military support to Bosnian Serbs.

## Military and Political Context

Perišić's career is typically discussed against the backdrop of the Yugoslav Wars of the 1990s and the role of the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia (Serbia and Montenegro) in providing military support to allied forces in Bosnia and Croatia.

## ICTY Proceedings and Legal Significance

His case became well known for legal debates over "aiding and abetting" and the threshold for attributing responsibility when a state provides assistance to another armed force. The appeal outcome is often referenced in commentary about standards of proof and the scope of individual criminal liability in international tribunals.

## Public Legacy

After the tribunal process, Perišić is mainly referenced in legal and historical discussions of command responsibility, military assistance, and accountability during the Yugoslav conflicts.
