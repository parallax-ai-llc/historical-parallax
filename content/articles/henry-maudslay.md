## Summary

Henry Maudslay was an English engineer considered the father of machine tool technology. He developed the first industrially practical screw-cutting lathe, which enabled the precision manufacturing of screws, nuts, and bolts. His workshop trained many of the greatest engineers of the Industrial Revolution, including Joseph Whitworth and James Nasmyth.
