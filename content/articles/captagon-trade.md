## Summary

Syria has become the world's largest producer of captagon amphetamine worth $57 billion. The Assad regime and allied militias have been implicated in manufacturing and trafficking.

## What “Captagon” Refers To

Captagon originally referred to the pharmaceutical fenethylline, but in modern illicit markets the name is widely used for counterfeit stimulant tablets that often contain amphetamine and other substances. Composition can vary by batch and laboratory.

## Drivers and Context

Armed conflict, sanctions, and weakened border enforcement in parts of the region created conditions that analysts say facilitated large-scale production and smuggling. Public reporting frequently attributes involvement to networks with access to territory, logistics, and protection, though specific claims can be contested and are difficult to independently verify.

## Trafficking Routes (Commonly Reported)

- Overland routes into neighboring countries, with onward movement toward Gulf markets.
- Maritime shipments concealed in commercial cargo.
- Use of intermediary hubs and front companies to obscure origin and financing.

## Seizures and Countermeasures

Regional authorities have periodically announced major interdictions of captagon-labeled tablets. These seizures are often used as indicators of scale, but seizure data can be incomplete and may reflect enforcement intensity as much as underlying production.

## Regional and International Responses

Governments have combined policing, border security, and financial sanctions against individuals and entities alleged to be linked to production and trafficking. Diplomatic engagement has also included demands for cooperation to curb cross-border drug flows.
