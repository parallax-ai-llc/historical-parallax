## Overview

Kakuji Inagawa is a Japanese figure associated with organized crime, best known as the founder and long-time leader of the Inagawa-kai, one of Japan’s major yakuza syndicates.

## Significance

His notability is primarily connected to the scale and endurance of the organization he led, and to the broader postwar development of large, structured yakuza groups.

## Historical context

In the decades after World War II, many yakuza groups expanded by consolidating regional gangs, formalizing leadership hierarchies, and navigating rivalries and alliances with other syndicates. Leadership figures often became widely known through organizational roles rather than a single public event.

## Notes on sources

Public accounts of yakuza leadership and internal structure can vary, and many operational details are difficult to verify independently.
