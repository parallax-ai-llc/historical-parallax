## Expanded Overview

### Background
- Also known as **Abu Hamza**.[^1]
- Converted to Islam in **2003** and was associated with the **Buttes-Chaumont network** in Paris; Farid Benyettou is described as a mentor figure.[^1]

### Iraq and early militancy (2004–2007)
- Traveled via **Damascus** in 2004; later captured by U.S. forces near **Fallujah** in **December 2004** and detained at **Camp Bucca**, then transferred to **Abu Ghraib** in 2005.[^1]
- Convicted in **Baghdad** in 2006 for illegal border crossing and sentenced to **15 years**; escaped in a **March 2007** prison break attributed to the Islamic State of Iraq.[^1]

### Return to France and flight (2007–2012)
- After moving through Syria, he was arrested, extradited, and served time in France; he was later released pending trial and subsequently fled to **Yemen**.[^1]
- Sentenced **in absentia** in France in **August 2012** to **five years** in prison for terrorist-organization related charges.[^1]

### Yemen / AQAP period and Charlie Hebdo links
- Described as a member of **Al-Qaeda in Iraq** and later **Al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP)**.[^1]
- Reported by intelligence officials to have helped **Chérif Kouachi** in Yemen (cash and limited training) in the context of planning for the **Charlie Hebdo** attack.[^1]
- Also linked in reporting to AQAP-related activities including interpretation in a 2011 kidnapping case involving French aid workers.[^1]

### Arrest and later proceedings
- Arrested in **Djibouti** on **16 December 2018** and extradited to France on terrorism-related charges.[^1]
- As of late 2024 reporting summarized on Wikipedia, he remained incarcerated and was tried in Paris in 2024, receiving a **life sentence** (October 2024).[^1]
