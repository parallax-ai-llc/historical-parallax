## Summary

William Shockley was an American physicist and inventor who shared the 1956 Nobel Prize in Physics for the invention of the transistor. He developed the junction transistor, which became the most widely used form. He later founded Shockley Semiconductor Laboratory in Silicon Valley, which spawned the semiconductor industry. His later career was marred by his promotion of eugenics and racist theories.
