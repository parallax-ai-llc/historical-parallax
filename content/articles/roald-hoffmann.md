## Summary

Roald Hoffmann is a Polish-American theoretical chemist who shared the 1981 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his theories concerning the course of chemical reactions. He developed the Woodward-Hoffmann rules, which predict the stereochemistry of organic reactions. Born to a Jewish family in Poland, he survived the Holocaust in hiding. He is also a published poet and playwright.
