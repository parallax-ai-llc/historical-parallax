## Summary

Syrian militant who founded Jabhat al-Nusra (al-Qaeda's Syria branch) then rebranded as HTS, claiming to break from al-Qaeda. He led the offensive that toppled Assad in 2024 and became Syria's de facto leader.

## Additional Context

- Often identified by the kunya **Abu Muhammad al-Julani**; sources describe him as the leader of **Hay'at Tahrir al-Sham (HTS)**, an Islamist armed faction that emerged from the Syrian insurgency.
- His public profile is closely tied to the evolution of Jabhat al-Nusra and later organizational rebrandings and coalition politics among Syrian armed groups.
- Coverage about his role varies by source; some accounts emphasize battlefield leadership while others focus on organizational control, governance structures, and external-state designations.

## Organization and Governance (High-Level)

- HTS has at times operated civil-administration bodies and local governance mechanisms in areas under its influence, alongside security services and media outlets.
- Analysts commonly distinguish between **military command**, **political messaging**, and **administrative control** when describing the group's structure.

## International Designations (General)

- Multiple governments have treated HTS and predecessor entities as terrorist organizations; reporting often notes that designation regimes can lag behind or differ from on-the-ground rebrandings.
