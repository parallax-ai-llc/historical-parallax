## Summary

Cornelius Vanderbilt II was the grandson of Commodore Vanderbilt and head of the Vanderbilt family. He served as chairman of the New York Central Railroad and was a prominent philanthropist and socialite. He built The Breakers mansion in Newport, Rhode Island, one of the finest Gilded Age mansions. He oversaw the family railroad empire during a period of consolidation and competition.
