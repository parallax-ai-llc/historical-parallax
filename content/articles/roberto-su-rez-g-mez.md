## Summary

Bolivian drug lord known as the 'King of Cocaine' who controlled Bolivia's cocaine production in the 1980s. He supplied the Medellín Cartel and funded the 1980 Bolivian coup known as the 'Cocaine Coup.'

## Context

Suárez Gómez is often discussed as a key figure in the regional political economy of cocaine in the late 20th century. Descriptions such as “King of Cocaine” reflect an emphasis on the scale of production and trafficking influence attributed to him during the 1980s.

## The “Cocaine Coup” framing

The label “Cocaine Coup” is commonly used to describe a period in which parts of Bolivia’s political power structure were alleged to have been supported by, or entangled with, drug-trafficking money. When expanding this article, it is useful to:
