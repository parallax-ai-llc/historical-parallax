## Summary

He Zehui (Ho Zah-Wei) was a pioneering Chinese nuclear physicist who discovered a new type of nuclear fission — four-body fission of uranium — while working in Germany. She developed nuclear emulsion techniques for particle detection and contributed to China's nuclear physics program. She was one of China's most distinguished female scientists.
