## Key Facts

- Also known as: **Abu Basir**.
- Known for: Leadership roles in **al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP)**.
- Reported roles included: **emir of AQAP** and a senior figure within the wider al-Qaeda network.
- Location of activity: Primarily **Yemen**, with broader al-Qaeda ties.

## Background (High-Level)

Public reporting describes al-Wuhayshi as a Yemeni militant who rose within al-Qaeda-linked organizations after the early 2000s. Sources commonly note that he became prominent as AQAP consolidated in Yemen.

## AQAP Leadership and Notability

- AQAP is widely described as an al-Qaeda affiliate based in Yemen.
- Under al-Wuhayshi’s leadership, AQAP was frequently characterized by analysts and governments as one of the more operationally active al-Qaeda branches.
- Reporting often links AQAP’s public messaging and organizational consolidation in Yemen to this period.

## Death

Al-Wuhayshi was reported killed on **2015-06-15** in **Yemen** in a **US drone strike**, with multiple outlets and official statements cited in later summaries.

## Legacy and Relevance

- Frequently cited in discussions of AQAP’s evolution and leadership.
- Appears in broader histories of al-Qaeda’s networked affiliate model, especially in the Arabian Peninsula.
