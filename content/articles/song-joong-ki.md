## Summary

Song Joong-ki is one of South Korea's most prominent actors, widely recognized for his roles in major Korean dramas and films that have reached global audiences. Born on September 19, 1985, in Daejeon, South Korea, he rose to international stardom through the 2016 mega-hit drama *Descendants of the Sun*, which became a landmark moment in the Korean Wave (Hallyu). His combination of dramatic range, charisma, and consistent output across television and film has made him a defining figure in contemporary Korean entertainment.

Beyond his acting credentials, Song Joong-ki represents the expanding global reach of Korean pop culture. His projects have been broadcast and streamed across Asia, the Middle East, and Western markets, contributing to the normalization of Korean-language content on mainstream platforms. He remains one of the most bankable and internationally recognized Korean actors of his generation.

---

## Early Life & Background

Song Joong-ki was born in Daejeon and showed early academic aptitude, enrolling at Sungkyunkwan University in Seoul to study Business Administration. While at university, he began pursuing acting, signing with a talent agency and gradually transitioning fully into the entertainment industry. His relatively slight build and youthful appearance initially led to him being cast in lighter roles, but he worked consistently to expand his range.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Song made his acting debut in 2008 and gained early recognition through supporting roles in dramas such as *Sungkyunkwan Scandal* (2010) and the popular series *Running Man* (2010–2011), which gave him broad variety-show exposure alongside his dramatic work. His film *A Werewolf Boy* (2012), a fantasy romance, became one of South Korea's highest-grossing films that year and demonstrated his ability to anchor a major production.

His career reached a global inflection point with *Descendants of the Sun* (2016), a KBS2 drama that aired simultaneously in South Korea and China. The show was a commercial phenomenon, breaking viewership records and generating enormous merchandise and endorsement revenue across Asia. Song's portrayal of Special Forces captain Yoo Si-jin made him a household name far beyond Korea.

---

## Key Achievements

- *Descendants of the Sun* (2016) achieved peak ratings above 30% in South Korea and was sold to over 30 countries.
- Won the Grand Prize (Daesang) at the 2016 KBS Drama Awards for his role in *Descendants of the Sun*.
- *A Werewolf Boy* (2012) grossed over 4.2 million admissions at the Korean box office.
- Led the Netflix original series *Vincenzo* (2021), which became one of the platform's most-watched Korean originals at the time of its release.
- Starred in *Reborn Rich* (2022), one of the highest-rated Korean cable dramas of that year.

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Song Joong-ki's 2017 marriage to actress Song Hye-kyo, his co-star from *Descendants of the Sun*, was one of the most publicized celebrity events in Korean entertainment history. Their 2019 divorce drew significant media attention and public commentary. Both parties requested privacy, and Song has since largely kept his personal life guarded. In 2023, he announced a new marriage to British-Italian actress Katy Louise Saunders and the birth of a son, news that was received generally positively by fans and media.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Song Joong-ki's career has helped define what a post-Hallyu Korean leading man looks like on the global stage — capable of anchoring prestige productions, navigating international co-productions, and maintaining relevance across shifting audience tastes. His work on *Vincenzo* and *Reborn Rich* demonstrated a maturation into morally complex roles, signaling durability beyond his earlier romantic-hero image. He continues to be a significant figure in Korean drama's ongoing global expansion.
