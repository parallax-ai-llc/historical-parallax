## Summary

Chinese serial killer who murdered 10 boys in Heilongjiang Province between 1999 and 2004. He lured victims from arcades and killed them. Sentenced to death and executed.

## Background

Gong Runbo was a Chinese serial killer whose crimes were reported in Heilongjiang province in northeastern China. The case is often discussed in the context of late-1990s/early-2000s public-safety reporting in China and the challenges of investigating repeated disappearances of children across multiple incidents.

## Modus operandi (as commonly reported)

Accounts typically describe:

- approaching victims in public entertainment areas (such as arcades)
- luring them away under a pretext
- committing murder and attempting to avoid detection over a multi-year period

## Investigation and arrest

Serial homicide investigations frequently rely on:

- pattern recognition across separate cases (time, location, victim profile)
- tips from the public and families of missing children
- intensified checks of known gathering places and transient populations

Gong was ultimately arrested and prosecuted, receiving a death sentence and being executed.

## Impact and legacy

- **Community fear**: crimes involving child victims often generate intense public attention.
- **Policing practices**: the case is cited in discussions about missing-person response and coordination across jurisdictions.
- **Media narratives**: later retellings focus on victim vulnerability and prevention messaging.
