## Summary

Karl Landsteiner was an Austrian-American biologist and physician who won the 1930 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his discovery of human blood groups (A, B, AB, O). His discovery made safe blood transfusions possible and saved countless lives. He also co-discovered the Rhesus factor (Rh factor) in blood. Landsteiner's work is considered one of the most important in the history of medicine.
