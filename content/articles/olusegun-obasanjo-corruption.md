## Summary

Obasanjo's presidential administration was marked by massive corruption including the $16 billion power sector scandal. His third-term bid was rejected.

## Context

Olusegun Obasanjo served as Nigeria’s civilian president from 1999 to 2007, a period marked by major economic and governance debates, including anti-corruption efforts alongside persistent allegations of misuse of public funds.[^1]

The topic is often discussed in relation to:

- **Large public-sector spending programs**, including energy and infrastructure initiatives,
- **Institutional reforms** (and disputes over their effectiveness), and
- **Political controversies**, including debates around constitutional change and a potential third term.[^1]

## Allegations and Public Debate

Accounts of “corruption” associated with Obasanjo’s administration typically refer to a mix of:

- allegations involving procurement and contracting,
- claims of elite patronage networks,
- criticisms from political opponents and civil society groups,
- and the gap between stated reform goals and outcomes on the ground.[^1]

Because Nigeria’s political environment is highly contested, narratives about responsibility and scale can vary by source; a careful reading usually distinguishes between documented cases, investigative claims, and broader political accusations.[^1]

## Legacy

Obasanjo’s post-presidency public role has kept these issues in the political conversation, as debates over governance, accountability, and state capacity continue to influence Nigerian politics.[^1]
