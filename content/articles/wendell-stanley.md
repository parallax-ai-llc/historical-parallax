## Summary

Wendell Stanley crystallized the tobacco mosaic virus in 1935, proving that viruses were chemical entities that could be studied like other molecules. This was the first virus to be crystallized and demonstrated that viruses are complex molecules rather than tiny organisms. He shared the 1946 Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
