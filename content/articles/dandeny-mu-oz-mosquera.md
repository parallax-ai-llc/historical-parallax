## Summary

Medellín Cartel enforcer known as 'La Quica' convicted of the bombing of Avianca Flight 203 that killed 110 people. He was sentenced to life in the US.

## Additional Context

## Background

- Muñoz Mosquera, known by the alias **“La Quica,”** is described in public sources as an enforcer associated with the Medellín Cartel.
- In the late 1980s, cartel violence in Colombia included high-profile bombings and targeted attacks aimed at intimidating the state and the public.

## Avianca Flight 203 Bombing

- Avianca Flight 203 was destroyed by a bomb in 1989, killing **110 people**.
- The attack is widely referenced as part of a broader campaign of terror attributed to cartel networks during that period.

## Prosecution and Sentence

- He was convicted in the United States in relation to the Flight 203 bombing and received a life sentence (reported as life plus an additional term).
- The case is frequently mentioned in discussions of international cooperation against transnational organized crime.

## Historical Significance

- The Flight 203 bombing remains one of the most notorious incidents in modern Colombian history and is often cited when examining the tactics and reach of cartel-era violence.

- The incident is also referenced in aviation-security discussions as an example of civilian-targeted terrorism tied to organized crime.
