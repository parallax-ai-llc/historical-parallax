## Summary

Russian agent who infiltrated US conservative organizations and the NRA.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2013–2016 | Increased contact-building with U.S. conservative and gun-rights circles (including NRA-linked events) |

## Background

- Russian national who presented publicly as a gun-rights activist and organizer.
- Associated in reporting and court filings with Alexander Torshin, a Russian political figure who was described as a key contact.
- Built networks through conferences, social events, and organizational outreach aimed at influencing U.S. political discourse.

## Activities and Modus Operandi (high level)

- Focused on relationship-building with politically influential groups and individuals rather than formal diplomatic channels.
- Targeted conservative organizations and gun-rights communities to gain access to U.S. political networks.
- Used invitations, introductions, and repeated attendance at events to maintain and expand access.

## Legal Case (United States)

- U.S. authorities alleged she operated under direction/control of Russian officials while in the U.S.
- The case centered on acting as a foreign agent without proper registration, and conspiracy related to that activity.
- The prosecution and sentencing were widely covered as a prominent example of non-traditional influence operations.

## Aftermath

- After her release and deportation, she returned to Russia and remained a notable figure in discussions about foreign influence and political networking.
- The case increased scrutiny of political fundraising, advocacy groups, and conference ecosystems as potential targets for foreign influence.

## Key Entities Mentioned in Public Reporting

- NRA (National Rifle Association)
- U.S. conservative political and advocacy networks
- Russian contacts referenced in reporting and court documents (including Alexander Torshin)
