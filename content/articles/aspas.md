## Summary

Erick "aspas" Santos de Oliveira is a Brazilian professional Valorant player widely regarded as one of the greatest duelists in the history of the game. Born on February 20, 2002, he rose to international stardom as the star fragger of Leviatán and later LOUD, becoming synonymous with Brazil's dominance in competitive Valorant.

Aspas is celebrated for his extraordinary mechanical skill, aggressive playstyle, and ability to produce highlight-reel plays under immense pressure. His performances at multiple international Valorant Champions Tour (VCT) events have cemented his reputation as one of the most individually talented players the tactical shooter genre has ever seen. He is a central figure in Brazil's passionate Valorant community and has helped elevate the region's standing on the global stage.

## Early Life & Background

Erick Santos de Oliveira was born on February 20, 2002, in Brazil. He grew up immersed in competitive gaming culture, developing his aim and game sense through years of playing first-person shooters. Like many Brazilian esports talents, he honed his skills in online ranked play before catching the attention of professional organizations. His natural mechanical ability — particularly his sharp aim and reaction time — stood out even among high-ranked players, leading to opportunities in the emerging Brazilian Valorant scene.

## Rise to Prominence

Aspas first gained attention in the Brazilian Valorant circuit playing for smaller rosters before joining LOUD, one of the most prominent esports organizations in Latin America, in early 2022. With LOUD, he quickly became the team's primary star and one of the most feared duelists in international competition.

His breakout moment came at VCT Masters Reykjavík in April 2022, where LOUD — largely considered underdogs — defeated OpTic Gaming in a thrilling grand final to claim the title. Aspas was a driving force behind the victory, consistently topping the scoreboard and delivering clutch performances that announced him as a world-class talent. His Jett and Raze play in particular drew widespread acclaim from analysts and fellow professionals.

Later in 2022, aspas and LOUD reached the grand final of Valorant Champions Istanbul in September, where they faced OpTic Gaming once again. LOUD secured the world championship title, with aspas earning the tournament MVP award for his dominant individual performances throughout the event.

## Key Achievements

- **VCT Masters Reykjavík 2022 Champion** with LOUD — the organization's first international title
- **Valorant Champions 2022 (Istanbul) Champion** with LOUD — crowned world champions
- **Valorant Champions 2022 MVP** — recognized as the tournament's best player
- **VCT Masters Madrid 2024 Champion** with LOUD
- Multiple VCT Brazil regional titles and consistently ranked among the top players globally by statistical metrics
- Regarded as one of the best Jett and Raze players in competitive Valorant history

## Controversies & Criticism

Aspas has maintained a largely controversy-free career. Some debate has surrounded the relative strength of the Brazilian region compared to EMEA and Pacific, with critics occasionally arguing that LOUD's domestic dominance inflated aspas's statistics. However, his performances at international events — where he consistently produced elite numbers against the world's best teams — have largely silenced such criticism.

Minor discussions have also arisen around the aggressive, aim-heavy playstyle that aspas exemplifies, with some analysts questioning whether it can be sustained long-term or whether it places too much burden on a single player. Nonetheless, LOUD's international results have validated the approach.

## Legacy & Influence

Aspas stands as one of the defining players of Valorant's competitive era. His combination of raw mechanical talent, clutch ability, and big-stage performances has drawn comparisons to legendary fraggers across multiple FPS titles. He is widely considered the best player Brazil has produced in Valorant and among the top handful of players globally in the game's history.

Beyond individual accolades, aspas has been instrumental in establishing Brazil as a powerhouse region in Valorant esports. His success with LOUD inspired a generation of Brazilian players and contributed to the country's enormous viewership numbers for VCT events. In a game still writing its competitive history, aspas has already secured his place among its most iconic figures.
