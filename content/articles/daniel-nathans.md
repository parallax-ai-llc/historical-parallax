## Summary

Daniel Nathans was an American microbiologist who shared the 1978 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for the discovery of restriction enzymes. He was the first to use restriction enzymes to construct a genetic map of a virus (SV40), demonstrating their utility as tools for studying DNA structure and gene organization.
