## Summary

Resort roof collapsed in Gyeongju killing 10 people attending a college orientation. The building had structural defects that were ignored despite warnings.

## Key Facts

- **Date:** February 2014 (widely reported as February 17, 2014)
- **Country:** South Korea
- **Setting:** A large indoor venue used for gatherings (described in reporting as part of a resort complex)
- **Fatalities:** 10
- **Context:** Victims included students attending an orientation event.

## Background

The collapse drew attention to structural safety and building-code enforcement in South Korea, especially for facilities hosting large groups. Public reporting emphasized that prior warnings about the building’s condition had not resulted in timely remediation.

## What Happened

During a college orientation gathering, the roof structure of the venue failed and collapsed, trapping people inside. Emergency services and responders conducted rescue operations and transported injured attendees to hospitals.

## Contributing Factors (High-Level)

Accounts of the incident commonly cite a combination of factors, including:

- **Structural deficiencies** in the roof or supporting elements
- **Maintenance and oversight failures**, where identified risks were not addressed
- **Environmental load** considerations (some reporting discussed weather-related loads at the time)

## Investigation and Accountability

Authorities investigated the design, construction, and management of the facility, focusing on whether safety standards were met and whether negligence contributed to the collapse. Such investigations typically examine permit compliance, inspections, and responsibility among owners, operators, and contractors.

## Aftermath and Impact

The collapse led to renewed scrutiny of public-assembly venues, including inspection regimes and emergency preparedness. In the wake of the tragedy, discussions often focused on strengthening enforcement, improving transparency around inspections, and ensuring that warnings from engineers or staff result in corrective action.
