## Summary

See Hafiz Saeed. Pakistani militant group. Duplicate entry.

### Quick facts

- **Type:** Sunni Islamist militant organization based in Pakistan.
- **Common abbreviation:** **LeT**.
- **Associated front organization (often cited):** **Jamaat-ud-Dawa (JuD)** (commonly described in open-source reporting as a charitable/welfare wing and/or political front).
- **Primary areas of focus in reporting:** Kashmir-related militancy and attacks in India, as well as broader regional security.

### Overview

Lashkar-e-Taiba ("Army of the Pure") is widely described in public sources as a Pakistan-based militant group that emerged in the late 1980s. It has been linked in reporting and official statements to multiple high-profile attacks, and it is listed or designated as a terrorist organization by several governments and international bodies.

### Designations and sanctions (high level)

Public sources commonly note that LeT has been **designated** by bodies such as the **United Nations Security Council (1267/1989/2253 sanctions regime)** and by governments including the **United States** and **India**.

### Notable incidents (non-exhaustive)

- **2008 Mumbai attacks:** LeT is widely reported as being responsible for, or operationally linked to, the coordinated attacks in Mumbai.
- Additional attacks and plots have been attributed to LeT or its networks in open-source reporting; attribution details vary by source and jurisdiction.

## Organization and ideology (high level)

- LeT is generally described as a **Sunni Islamist** militant organization.
- Public reporting frequently discusses **training, recruitment, and logistics networks**; specifics are disputed and vary by time period.
- The relationship between LeT and organizations such as **JuD** is often described as overlapping in personnel and infrastructure in open sources.
