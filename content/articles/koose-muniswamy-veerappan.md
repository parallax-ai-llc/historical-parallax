## Summary

India's most wanted bandit who poached 2,000 elephants for ivory and smuggled $2.6 million worth of sandalwood. Operated with impunity for three decades in dense forests.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1987 | Became most-wanted criminal |
| 2004 | Shot dead in Operation Cocoon |

## Background

- Veerappan operated primarily in the forested border regions of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu.
- His notoriety grew from a combination of poaching, illegal logging/sandalwood smuggling, and armed confrontations with police.
- The terrain and local networks made long-term pursuit difficult and allowed him to evade capture for years.

## Criminal operations (high level)

- Poaching and ivory trafficking were central to his early criminal reputation.
- Sandalwood smuggling leveraged high demand for valuable timber and the complexity of inter-state enforcement.
- His group was also linked to kidnappings and killings, which raised political pressure for decisive action.

## State response

- Multiple task forces and operations were launched over time by state authorities.
- The case became a major public security issue, affecting policing priorities and inter-state coordination.
- Operation Cocoon ended with his death, and is often cited as the culmination of a long manhunt.

## Legacy and public memory

- The story of Veerappan has been retold in journalism and popular media as a symbol of forest banditry and state capacity limits.
- Interpretations vary between portraying him as a criminal profiteer versus emphasizing the socio-economic context of the region.
