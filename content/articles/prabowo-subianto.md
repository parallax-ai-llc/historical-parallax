## Summary

Indonesian president and former general accused of orchestrating the kidnapping and disappearance of pro-democracy activists in 1998. He was dishonorably discharged from the military.

## Military Background

Prabowo Subianto rose through Indonesia’s armed forces and became associated with elite units during the late Suharto era. His military career and networks remained a core part of his public identity even after leaving formal service.

## 1998 Abductions and Human Rights Allegations

In the final months of Suharto’s rule, a number of pro-democracy activists were abducted; some later reappeared while others remained missing. Prabowo has been linked in reporting and official inquiries to these events, which have been a major source of controversy domestically and internationally.

## Political Career

After the end of his military career, Prabowo re-emerged as a prominent political figure. He ran in multiple presidential elections before ultimately winning the presidency in the 2024 election cycle.

## Public Image and Debate

Prabowo’s career is often discussed through two competing lenses:

- **Stability and nationalism**: supporters emphasize leadership, security credentials, and national development.
- **Accountability and democratic norms**: critics focus on alleged past abuses, civil-military relations, and the unresolved status of the disappeared.

## Why He Matters in Regional Politics

As president of the world’s third-largest democracy and a major Southeast Asian economy, Prabowo’s policies on defense, trade, and diplomacy are closely watched, including Indonesia’s posture toward major powers and regional security issues.
