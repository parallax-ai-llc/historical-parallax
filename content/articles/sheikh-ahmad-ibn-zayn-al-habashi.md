## Summary

Sheikh Ahmad ibn Zayn al-Habashi was a Yemeni Islamic scholar and Sufi master of the Ba'Alawi Sufi tradition in the Hadhramaut region. He was a leading authority on Islamic jurisprudence and Sufi spirituality in the 17th-18th century Arabian Peninsula. His scholarly lineage spread Islam to Southeast Asia through the Hadhrami diaspora. His works on fiqh and tasawwuf remain studied in traditional Islamic institutions.
