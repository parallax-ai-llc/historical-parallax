## Summary

Robert Curl was an American chemist who shared the 1996 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with Harold Kroto and Richard Smalley for the discovery of fullerenes. His expertise in microwave spectroscopy was crucial to identifying the C60 buckminsterfullerene molecule. Curl spent most of his career at Rice University.
