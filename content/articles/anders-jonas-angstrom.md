## Summary

Anders Angstrom was a Swedish physicist who founded the science of spectroscopy and studied the solar spectrum. The angstrom unit of length is named after him.

## Scientific work and impact

### Spectroscopy and the solar spectrum

Angstrom is closely associated with early quantitative spectroscopy. His work focused on measuring and cataloging spectral lines, especially in sunlight, and helped establish spectroscopy as a standard method for studying the composition and physical conditions of astronomical objects.

### Publications and academic roles

He worked in Sweden's academic system during the 19th century and produced research in physics and astronomy, including studies that connected observational measurements to physical interpretation.

### Legacy

- The **angstrom (Å)**, a unit of length commonly used in physics, chemistry, and crystallography for atomic-scale distances, is named in his honor.
- Angstrom’s solar spectrum atlas is frequently cited as a milestone in the history of spectral measurement and reference data.
