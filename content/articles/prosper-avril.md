## Summary

Haitian military dictator who ruled from 1988 to 1990 and was found liable for torture by a US federal court. His regime systematically tortured political opponents.

## Political Context

- Avril’s rule occurred during a turbulent period in Haiti following the fall of the Duvalier regime, when the military played a dominant role in national politics.
- Governance during this era was marked by instability, contested legitimacy, and frequent allegations of repression against opponents.

## Human Rights Allegations and Accountability

- Avril has been associated in reporting with detention and torture of political opponents during his time in power.
- A civil case in the United States later found him liable for acts of torture, illustrating how universal jurisdiction–adjacent civil remedies can be used when alleged perpetrators reside abroad.

## Legacy

- Avril is generally remembered as part of Haiti’s late-20th-century cycle of coups and military rule, prior to the country’s later transitions and continued political volatility.
