## Summary

English pirate known as 'Black Sam' who was the wealthiest pirate in recorded history. He captured the slave ship Whydah and over 50 vessels. Known for his democratic leadership. Died when the Whydah sank in a storm off Cape Cod.

## Background

Samuel “Black Sam” Bellamy operated during the **Golden Age of Piracy** in the early 18th century, when European imperial trade and wartime privateering created large flows of valuable shipping in the Atlantic.

## Reputation and Organization (High Level)

Bellamy is often contrasted with more authoritarian pirate captains because popular histories emphasize:

- relatively democratic decision-making aboard ship (common in some pirate crews),
- distribution rules for captured wealth,
- pragmatic alliances with other pirates and opportunistic targeting of merchant vessels.

## The Whydah and Its Legacy

- The *Whydah Gally* (often shortened to *Whydah*) became Bellamy’s flagship after capture.
- The shipwreck off Cape Cod has been the subject of archaeological recovery efforts and museum exhibitions, which have shaped modern public understanding of Bellamy and his crew.
