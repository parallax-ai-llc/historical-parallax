## Summary

Shakira Isabel Mebarak Ripoll, born February 2, 1977, in Barranquilla, Colombia, is one of the best-selling music artists of all time, with over 80 million records sold worldwide. She is the best-selling Latin music artist of her era and has won three Grammy Awards and twelve Latin Grammy Awards. Her ability to blend rock, pop, Latin rhythms, and Middle Eastern influences into a singular sound made her a global phenomenon unlike any other artist of her generation.

Beyond record sales, Shakira reshaped how Latin music penetrated mainstream Western markets. Her 2001 crossover album *Laundry Service* demonstrated that a Spanish-language artist could achieve massive English-language success without abandoning her cultural roots. She has been recognized not only as a musician but as a philanthropist, UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador, and advocate for early childhood education in Colombia.

## Early Life & Background

Shakira was born in Barranquilla, Colombia, to a Colombian mother, Nidia Ripoll, and a Lebanese-American father, William Mebarak Chadid. Her Lebanese heritage significantly shaped her musical identity, introducing her to belly dancing and Arabic music from an early age. She showed artistic talent as a child, writing her first poem at age four and her first rock song at eight.

She signed her first record deal with Sony Music Colombia at age thirteen. Her first two albums, *Magia* (1991) and *Peligro* (1993), were commercial disappointments, and Sony considered dropping her. She retained creative control and spent three years writing and producing her third album entirely independently.

## Rise to Prominence

Shakira's breakthrough came with *Pies Descalzos* (1995), which sold over five million copies across Latin America and Spain. The follow-up, *¿Dónde Están los Ladrones?* (1998), cemented her status as the dominant force in Latin rock, selling over seven million copies and earning two Grammy nominations.

Her English-language crossover arrived with *Laundry Service* (2001), featuring the global hit "Whenever, Wherever," which topped charts in over fifteen countries. The album sold over thirteen million copies worldwide. She became the first artist in history to have a number-one song in both the Spanish- and English-language Billboard charts simultaneously.

Her 2010 FIFA World Cup anthem "Waka Waka (This Time for Africa)" became one of the best-selling singles in history, with over 10 million copies sold, and the official video accumulated over 3 billion YouTube views.

## Key Achievements

- Three Grammy Awards and twelve Latin Grammy Awards
- Over 80 million records sold globally
- First Latin artist to perform at the Super Bowl halftime show (Super Bowl LIV, 2020, alongside Jennifer Lopez)
- "Hips Don't Lie" (2006) became one of the best-selling singles of all time, reaching number one in over 55 countries
- Inducted into the Billboard Latin Music Hall of Fame in 2023
- Her 2023 diss track "BZRP Music Sessions #53" broke streaming records, reaching over 14 million Spotify plays in 24 hours

## Controversies & Criticism

In 2017, Spanish tax authorities charged Shakira with evading €14.5 million in taxes between 2012 and 2014, alleging she was a Spanish resident while claiming Colombian residency. She denied wrongdoing, rejecting an initial settlement offer. The case went to trial in November 2023; she accepted a plea deal, receiving a suspended sentence and paying fines totaling over €7.5 million. The case drew widespread media attention partly because it coincided with her public separation from Spanish footballer Gerard Piqué.

Her 2023 breakup song targeting Piqué was criticized by some as unduly hostile, though many observers noted it reflected a broader cultural moment around accountability in high-profile relationships.

## Legacy & Influence

Shakira is widely credited with opening mainstream Western markets to Latin pop in a way that paved the road for subsequent artists including J Balvin, Bad Bunny, and Karol G. Her fusion of rock instrumentation, Arabic melodic sensibility, and Latin rhythms created an aesthetic template that remains influential. Through the Pies Descalzos Foundation, she has built and funded schools serving over 40,000 displaced children in Colombia. She has been named one of TIME magazine's 100 most influential people on multiple occasions and remains one of the most-followed musicians on social media globally.
