## Summary

Park Chan-ho was the first Korean in MLB, debuting with the Dodgers in 1994. He won 124 MLB games.

## MLB Career Overview

Park Chan-ho was a right-handed pitcher whose Major League Baseball career began with the Los Angeles Dodgers and later included several other teams. His debut in 1994 made him a landmark figure for Korean baseball, helping open an MLB pathway that later players followed.

His win totals and longevity are often highlighted as evidence of sustained performance in a demanding role and environment, including heavy travel and adaptation to different leagues and ballparks.

## Notable Highlights (High-Level)

Commonly cited milestones include:

- establishing himself as a regular starter with the Dodgers in the late 1990s
- a peak period around the turn of the 2000s, including an 18-win season
- serving as a visible cultural figure for Korean fans following MLB from abroad

## Impact on Korean Baseball

Park’s MLB success had effects beyond statistics. It contributed to:

- increased scouting attention on Korean professional and amateur baseball
- greater media coverage and commercialization of overseas Korean athletes
- a “proof of concept” that Korean pitchers could compete at the top level

## Later Career and Legacy

After his MLB years, Park also played in other leagues and remained involved in baseball and public life. In South Korea, he is often remembered as a pioneer whose career shaped expectations for subsequent generations of Korean players abroad.
