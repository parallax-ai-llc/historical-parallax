## Summary

Wacław Sierpiński was a Polish mathematician who made important contributions to set theory, number theory, and topology. He described the Sierpiński triangle, Sierpiński carpet, and Sierpiński space, which became important in fractal geometry. He was one of the founders of the Warsaw school of mathematics and published over 700 papers.
