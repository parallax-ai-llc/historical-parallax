## Summary

Bernie Glassman was an American Zen teacher and social entrepreneur who co-founded the Zen Peacemakers. He was a dharma successor of Taizan Maezumi and pioneered 'engaged Buddhism' in America through street retreats and bearing witness to suffering. He founded the Greyston Bakery in Yonkers, a social enterprise providing jobs for formerly homeless and incarcerated people. His approach integrated Zen practice with social action.
