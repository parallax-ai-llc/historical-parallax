## Summary

Although primarily a political thinker, Kato Hiroyuki was an important figure in introducing Western science and technology to Meiji Japan. As president of Tokyo Imperial University, he established Japan's modern scientific education system and advocated for Social Darwinism and the adoption of Western scientific methods throughout Japanese universities.
