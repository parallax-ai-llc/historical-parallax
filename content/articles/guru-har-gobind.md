## Summary

Guru Hargobind was the sixth Sikh Guru who established the concept of miri-piri (temporal-spiritual authority) in Sikhism. He was the first Sikh Guru to take up arms, wearing two swords representing spiritual and worldly authority. He built the Akal Takht facing the Golden Temple. He fought several battles against the Mughal Empire and was imprisoned in the Gwalior Fort, where he secured the release of 52 Hindu princes.
