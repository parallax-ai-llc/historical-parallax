## Background

Yolanda Chen (Russian: Иоланда Чен) is a retired track-and-field athlete associated with Russia/the former Soviet sports system. She is notable for competing across multiple jumping disciplines over her career, moving from combined events into the long jump and later becoming one of the early elite performers in the women’s triple jump as the event matured internationally.

> Note on biographical details: the frontmatter above is kept as-is for repository consistency, but widely cited sources (including Wikipedia) list her birth as **26 July 1961** in **Moscow**.

## Event progression (high-level)

- **Early career:** began in multi-event athletics (commonly reported as pentathlon) before focusing on horizontal jumps.
- **Long jump specialization:** achieved an elite personal best in the late 1980s (reported as **7.16 m in 1988**).
- **Triple jump switch:** moved to the women’s triple jump in the early 1990s, when the event was still relatively new at the top international level.

## Major highlights

- **1993:** produced one of the leading outdoor marks of the era (reported personal best **14.97 m**), and won a global medal at the **1993 World Championships** (silver).
- **1995:** won **gold** at the **1995 World Indoor Championships** with **15.03 m**, reported as a **world indoor record** at the time.

## After competition

Some biographies describe Chen as remaining involved in athletics media and commentary after retirement, including work connected to European sports broadcasting.

## Legacy

Chen is often mentioned in the early record and medal history of the women’s triple jump, a discipline that underwent rapid performance growth in the 1990s as international championships standardized the event. Her indoor world record performance in 1995 is frequently cited in record-progression summaries.
