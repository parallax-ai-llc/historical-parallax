## Summary

Wu Lien-teh was a Malaysian-Chinese physician who contained the Manchurian plague epidemic of 1910-1911, saving millions of lives. He was the first to identify the pneumonic plague transmission route and invented the precursor to the N95 surgical mask. He was nominated for the Nobel Prize in 1935. He is considered the father of modern Chinese public health.
