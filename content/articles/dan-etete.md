## Summary

Former Nigerian petroleum minister who awarded the OPL 245 oil block to himself through a shell company then sold it to Shell and Eni for $1.3 billion. The deal is subject to ongoing prosecution.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Additional Context

## Background

- Etete served as Nigeria’s petroleum minister during the 1990s, a period when control of oil licenses and revenue allocation were central to state power.
- The OPL 245 case is frequently cited in discussions of resource governance, beneficial ownership, and large-scale corruption risk in extractive industries.

## OPL 245 / Malabu Oil and Gas Deal (High-Level)

- **OPL 245** refers to a major offshore oil block. The controversy centers on how the license was awarded and how proceeds were routed.
- Public reporting describes the license as being assigned to **Malabu Oil and Gas**, a company linked to Etete, and later sold in a deal involving major international firms.

## Why the Case Matters

- It illustrates recurring issues in oil-sector contracting:
  - use of shell entities and opaque ownership
  - political exposure risks for international counterparties
  - the role of banks and intermediaries in large transfers
  - long-running cross-border investigations and litigation

## Legal / Investigative Status

- The deal has been the subject of extensive legal scrutiny in multiple jurisdictions, with proceedings evolving over time.
- Because outcomes can change with appeals and new filings, summaries often focus on the core allegation: self-dealing in the allocation of a public resource.
