## Summary

Herman Bavinck was a Dutch Reformed theologian whose four-volume Reformed Dogmatics is considered one of the most important works of systematic theology in the Reformed tradition. He engaged deeply with modern philosophy and science while maintaining orthodox Reformed theology. He succeeded Abraham Kuyper at the Free University of Amsterdam. His work has experienced a major revival of interest in the 21st century.
