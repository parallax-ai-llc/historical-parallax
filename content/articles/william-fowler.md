## Summary

William Fowler was an American nuclear physicist and astrophysicist who shared the 1983 Nobel Prize in Physics for theoretical and experimental studies of the nuclear reactions of importance in the formation of the chemical elements. He co-authored the landmark B²FH paper on stellar nucleosynthesis, explaining how elements are created inside stars. His experimental measurements of nuclear reaction rates were essential for astrophysics.
