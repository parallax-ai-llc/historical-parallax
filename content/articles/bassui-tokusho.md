## Summary

Bassui Tokusho was a Japanese Rinzai Zen master known for his intense inquiry into the question 'Who is the master?' He wandered for years before establishing a temple and attracting over 1,000 students. His death verse emphasized the emptiness of all phenomena. His letters to students are considered among the finest expressions of Zen teaching in Japanese.
