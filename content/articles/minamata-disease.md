## Summary

Chisso Corporation dumped mercury into Minamata Bay for decades, causing severe neurological disease in thousands. Over 2,000 died; the company and government covered up the contamination.

## Additional Context

### Cause and Mechanism

- Minamata disease is caused by methylmercury poisoning.
- Industrial wastewater contaminated Minamata Bay, and mercury accumulated through the food chain, affecting fish and shellfish consumed locally.

### Symptoms (High-Level)

- Symptoms can include numbness, loss of coordination, impaired speech, hearing and vision problems, and in severe cases paralysis and death.
- Congenital Minamata disease occurred when prenatal exposure affected fetal development.

### Response and Accountability

- The disaster became a landmark case in environmental health, corporate accountability, and long-running victim compensation disputes.
- The Minamata Convention on Mercury is named after the incident and aims to reduce global mercury pollution.
