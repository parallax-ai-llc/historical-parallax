## Background

- Maxim Bakiyev is the son of former Kyrgyz president Kurmanbek Bakiyev.
- He became prominent in Kyrgyzstan during his father’s presidency, particularly in roles connected to investment and state development programs.

## Roles and activities (reported)

- **State development / investment functions (late 2000s):** Reported to have held senior responsibilities connected to investment and development initiatives.

## Allegations and investigations

- Following the 2010 Kyrgyz Revolution, Kyrgyz authorities and political opponents alleged that the Bakiyev family and associates were involved in large-scale corruption and misuse of state resources.
- Maxim Bakiyev has been described in public sources as a target of **criminal investigations** and **asset-recovery efforts** by Kyrgyz authorities after the change in government.

## Exile and current status

- After leaving Kyrgyzstan in 2010, he has been widely described as living abroad.
- Public sources often refer to him as a **fugitive**, reflecting the ongoing legal disputes and outstanding allegations reported by Kyrgyz authorities.
