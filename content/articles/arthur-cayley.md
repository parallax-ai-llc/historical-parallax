## Summary

Arthur Cayley was a British mathematician who helped found the modern school of pure mathematics. He is credited with being the first to define the concept of a group in its modern form and was a pioneer of matrix theory. The Cayley-Hamilton theorem, Cayley graphs, and Cayley numbers are named after him. He published over 900 papers across all areas of mathematics.
