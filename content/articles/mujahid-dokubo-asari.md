## Summary

Nigerian Niger Delta militant leader who threatened oil war against the Nigerian government.

## Background

- **Origin:** Ijaw from Rivers State in Nigeria's Niger Delta region.
- **Education:** Reported to have attended the University of Calabar (did not complete).
- **Name:** Commonly known as **Mujahid Dokubo-Asari** (also rendered **Dokubo Asari**).

## Context: Niger Delta Militancy

The Niger Delta has long been shaped by:

- **Oil production and revenue distribution disputes** ("resource control")
- **Environmental degradation** linked to extraction and spills
- **Local community grievances** over jobs, infrastructure, and compensation

Dokubo-Asari became one of the more visible militant leaders associated with these tensions, often framing demands in terms of regional rights and control over petroleum resources.

## Organizations and Influence

- **Niger Delta People's Volunteer Force (NDPVF):** A militant organization he led, frequently cited in reporting about armed mobilization and threats to oil operations.
- **Public messaging:** Known for media statements that leveraged the economic importance of oil exports to amplify political demands.

## Legal and Political Notes

- **Treason charge (2005):** The Nigerian state charged him with treason amid escalating confrontations.
- **Detention and release:** Subsequent reporting notes a later release from custody, with the case becoming a reference point in discussions of Niger Delta conflict management.

## Why He Matters (High-Level)

- Illustrates how **non-state armed groups** can exert leverage in resource-critical regions.
- Shows the intersection of **local grievances, national politics, and global energy markets**.
- Frequently referenced in analyses of Nigeria's approaches to **amnesty, negotiation, and security** in the Delta.
