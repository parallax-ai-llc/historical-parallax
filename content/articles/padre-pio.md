## Summary

Padre Pio was an Italian Capuchin friar and mystic who bore the stigmata for 50 years. He became one of the most popular Catholic saints of the 20th century, drawing millions of pilgrims to San Giovanni Rotondo. He was reported to have the gifts of bilocation, prophecy, and healing. He was canonized by Pope John Paul II in 2002.
