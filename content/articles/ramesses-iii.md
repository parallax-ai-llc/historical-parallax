## Reign and Historical Context

Ramesses III was the second pharaoh of Egypt’s Twentieth Dynasty and is often regarded as the last great ruler of the New Kingdom. His reign (commonly dated to the early 12th century BCE) unfolded amid regional upheaval at the end of the Late Bronze Age, when migrations, warfare, and trade disruptions affected many Eastern Mediterranean states.

## Major Events and Policies

- **Conflicts with the Sea Peoples:** Egyptian sources describe large-scale incursions by seaborne and land-based groups (often summarized as the “Sea Peoples”). Ramesses III’s inscriptions present these campaigns as decisive defensive victories.
- **Campaigns in the Levant and Libya:** Records also mention fighting in Syria-Palestine and against Libyan groups on Egypt’s western frontier.
- **Building program:** He commissioned major construction, most famously his mortuary temple at **Medinet Habu** in Thebes, whose reliefs and inscriptions are a key source for his reign.
- **Economic strain and labor unrest:** The later New Kingdom faced inflation and shortages. A famous case from his reign is often cited as one of the earliest documented labor strikes, involving workers at **Deir el-Medina**.

## The Harem Conspiracy

Ramesses III is strongly associated with the so-called **Harem Conspiracy**, a palace plot involving members of the royal household. Surviving judicial documents (often discussed under the “Judicial Papyrus” traditions) describe prosecutions of multiple conspirators. Modern forensic studies of the mummy have been cited as supporting evidence for a violent death.

## Legacy and Significance

- He is frequently used as a reference point for the **end-phase of New Kingdom power**, with later rulers facing reduced resources and influence.
- The inscriptions of his reign (particularly at Medinet Habu) are central to historical debates about **Late Bronze Age collapse** dynamics and how Egyptians framed external threats.

## Additional Notes

- Dates for Ramesses III’s reign can vary across scholarly chronologies; many popular summaries place his reign roughly in the **1180s–1150s BCE**.
- The title “Victim of Conspiracy” reflects the enduring prominence of the Harem Conspiracy narrative in reconstructions of his death.
