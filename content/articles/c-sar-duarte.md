## Summary

Mexican former governor of Chihuahua accused of embezzling over $6.5 billion pesos from state coffers. He fled to the US and was extradited back to Mexico in 2022. One of Mexico's most corrupt governors.

## Political Career (High Level)

Duarte was a prominent politician in the Institutional Revolutionary Party (PRI) and built a career through state and federal roles before becoming governor of Chihuahua.
As governor, his administration faced allegations involving misuse of public funds and the diversion of state resources.

## Allegations and Legal Proceedings

Reports and prosecutors’ claims described a pattern of alleged embezzlement and the use of intermediaries to move money from government accounts.
Following an arrest warrant and a period in the United States, Duarte was detained and later extradited to Mexico in 2022.
The case has been referenced in wider discussions about corruption investigations targeting former governors across multiple Mexican states.

## Broader Context

Chihuahua’s politics and finances have been affected by security challenges, federal-state tensions, and the large budgets associated with infrastructure and public safety.
High-profile corruption cases in Mexico often involve parallel issues such as asset recovery, cross-border financial tracing, and the speed of judicial proceedings.
