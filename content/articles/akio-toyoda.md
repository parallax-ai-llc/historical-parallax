## Summary

Akio Toyoda served as president and CEO of Toyota Motor Corporation from 2009 to 2023, leading the company through crises including the 2010 recall crisis, the 2011 tsunami, and the COVID-19 pandemic. He is the grandson of Toyota founder Kiichiro Toyoda. Under his leadership, Toyota remained the world's largest automaker and made major investments in hydrogen fuel cells and electric vehicles. He transitioned to chairman in 2023.
