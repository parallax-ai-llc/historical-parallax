## Summary

French far-right leader convicted of embezzling EU parliament funds by employing party workers as parliamentary assistants. She was sentenced to prison and banned from running for office.

## Additional Context

### What "EU parliamentary assistants" cases involve

In the European Parliament, Members of the European Parliament (MEPs) can employ parliamentary assistants with EU funds for legislative and parliamentary work. Allegations in several European parties over the years have centered on whether some staff paid under these rules were instead working primarily for national party activities.

### Political context

Marine Le Pen is a central figure in French far-right politics and led the National Front (renamed National Rally). Because of that prominence, any legal proceedings connected to EU funding attracted extensive media attention and became part of a broader debate about party finance, compliance rules, and political accountability.
