## Summary

Mass murder-suicide of 918 members of Jim Jones Peoples Temple at Jonestown, Guyana on November 18, 1978. Members drank cyanide-laced punch. It was the largest mass death in American history until 9/11.

## Background

- **Peoples Temple:** A religious movement led by Jim Jones that began in the United States and later established a settlement in Guyana known as **Jonestown**.
- **Relocation to Guyana:** The group’s move outside the United States has often been discussed in reporting and scholarship as connected to internal control, isolation, and conflict with outside scrutiny.

## What Happened (High Level)

- **November 18, 1978:** The deaths at Jonestown involved the consumption of a **poisoned drink**, resulting in a large number of fatalities including children.
- **Violence outside the settlement:** The same period included the killing of U.S. Congressman **Leo Ryan** and others connected to a visit and attempted departures.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- **Scale and symbolism:** The event became a defining reference point in discussions about coercive groups and is often cited as one of the largest single incidents of mass death involving U.S. civilians in the 20th century.
- **Public discourse:** “Drinking the Kool-Aid” entered popular language, though the phrase is widely criticized as trivializing a tragedy and misrepresenting specifics.
- **Ongoing study:** The Jonestown deaths are a continuing subject for historians, sociologists, and journalists examining authority, social influence, and group dynamics.

## Notes on Terminology

- Sources differ on phrasing such as “mass suicide” vs. “mass murder” due to the presence of coercion, armed enforcement, and the deaths of children.

## Key Data Points (Often Cited)

- **Location:** Jonestown, Guyana
- **Date:** 1978-11-18
- **Deaths:** 918 (as commonly reported)
- **Broader relevance:** A major case study in how isolation, fear, and authority can shape collective outcomes.
