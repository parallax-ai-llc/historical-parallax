## Summary

Usman dan Fodio was a Fulani Islamic scholar, reformer, and military leader who led a major jihad in West Africa that established the Sokoto Caliphate, one of the largest states in 19th-century Africa. He was a Sufi scholar of the Qadiriyya order who sought to purify Islamic practice among the Hausa peoples. He wrote extensively on Islamic law, education, and the rights of women. The Sokoto Caliphate endured until British colonization in 1903.
