## Summary

Ei-ichi Negishi was a Japanese-American chemist who shared the 2010 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for palladium-catalyzed cross couplings in organic synthesis. The Negishi coupling, which he developed, is a powerful method for forming carbon-carbon bonds using organozinc compounds. His work enabled the synthesis of complex molecules used in medicine, agriculture, and electronics.
