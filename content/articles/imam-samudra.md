## Summary

Indonesian terrorist and field commander of Jemaah Islamiyah who planned and led the 2002 Bali bombings that killed 202 people. He chose the targets and organized the operation. Executed by firing squad.

## Background and Affiliation

Samudra is widely described as a member and operational figure within Jemaah Islamiyah, a Southeast Asian militant network linked in reporting to al-Qaeda-aligned ideology during the early 2000s. His name is most closely associated with the 2002 Bali bombings, one of the deadliest attacks in Indonesia's modern history.

## The 2002 Bali Bombings (High Level)

The attacks targeted civilian venues and resulted in mass casualties, including many foreign nationals. The event had major political and security consequences for Indonesia and the broader region, accelerating counterterrorism cooperation and prompting changes in Indonesian law-enforcement capabilities and legal frameworks.

## Arrest, Trial, and Sentencing

After the bombings, Indonesian authorities pursued and prosecuted multiple perpetrators and organizers. Samudra was tried in Indonesian courts, convicted, and sentenced to death. His case is frequently discussed in relation to how Indonesia handled terrorism prosecutions in the post-9/11 period.

## Execution and Aftermath

He was executed by firing squad in 2008. The executions of Bali bombing convicts were widely covered and sparked reactions domestically and internationally, including debate about capital punishment, deterrence, and the risk of extremist martyrdom narratives.
