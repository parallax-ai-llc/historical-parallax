## Summary

Yoo Youngkuk was a pioneering Korean abstract painter who is considered the father of Korean abstraction. His bold, hard-edged paintings of mountains and nature, reduced to geometric color planes, bridge Korean landscape tradition and Western geometric abstraction. He was instrumental in establishing abstract art in Korea.
