## Summary

WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange spent 7 years in Ecuador's London embassy and 5 years in Belmarsh Prison. Pled guilty to espionage charges in 2024 and was freed after total 12 years of confinement.

## Background

The case is tied to WikiLeaks' publication of U.S. government documents, including material related to the wars in Iraq and Afghanistan and U.S. diplomatic cables. Assange's legal situation also intersected with separate Swedish investigations in the 2010s, which contributed to his decision to avoid travel and later seek refuge.

## Core Legal Issues

- **Extradition**: The United States sought Assange's extradition from the United Kingdom to face U.S. charges.
- **Espionage Act questions**: The prosecution became a focal point for debate about how espionage-related statutes apply to publishers and journalists.
- **Computer intrusion allegations**: The U.S. case also referenced claims involving attempts to help obtain or access information.

## UK Proceedings (High Level)

Assange's arrest in 2019 began a multiyear sequence of court hearings in the UK related to the extradition request, including decisions in lower courts and further appeals. Health, detention conditions, and assurances about how he would be treated if transferred were repeatedly raised in legal arguments.

## Significance

The prosecution is widely cited in discussions about:

- press freedom and national-security reporting
- the boundary between sourcing/leaking and publishing
- how states pursue cross-border cyber and secrecy cases
