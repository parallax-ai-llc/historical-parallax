## Summary

Ghanaian military head of state who overthrew Acheampong but was himself overthrown and executed by Rawlings just months later. His brief rule failed to address corruption.

## Background

- Career officer in the Ghana Armed Forces who rose through senior command roles before entering national politics.
- Came to power during a period marked by economic stress, shortages, and declining legitimacy for successive military governments.

## Head of State (1978–1979)

- Led the Supreme Military Council after removing Gen. Ignatius Kutu Acheampong in an internal palace coup.
- Promised a return to constitutional rule and signaled willingness to hold elections, while still governing through military structures.
- Faced pressure from:
  - Public anger over inflation and corruption.
  - Fractures inside the armed forces.
  - Demands for accountability for earlier abuses and economic mismanagement.

## Overthrow and Execution

- In June 1979, junior officers led by Jerry John Rawlings overthrew the government.
- Akuffo was tried by a revolutionary tribunal and executed by firing squad alongside other senior figures of the prior regime.

## Historical Significance

- Often cited as an example of how late-stage military transitions can fail when institutions are weak and intra-military trust collapses.
- His short tenure is mainly remembered for the attempted (but incomplete) pivot toward elections before the 1979 coup.
