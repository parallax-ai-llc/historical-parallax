## Summary

Bill Russell won 11 championships with Boston and became the first Black head coach in NBA history.

## Additional context

- Russell anchored the Boston Celtics' defense during a long championship run in the late 1950s and 1960s.
- He was known for elite rebounding, rim protection, and an approach that prioritized team outcomes over individual scoring.

## Significance and legacy

- Often cited as one of the sport's greatest winners because of his championship total.
- His player-coach role is frequently referenced in discussions of leadership and representation in major U.S. leagues.
- The NBA later named the Finals MVP trophy in his honor.
