## Summary

Jack Kilby was an American electrical engineer who invented the integrated circuit (microchip) in 1958 while working at Texas Instruments. This invention is considered one of the most important innovations of the 20th century, enabling the miniaturization of electronics. He shared the 2000 Nobel Prize in Physics for his part in the invention. Kilby also co-invented the handheld calculator and the thermal printer.
