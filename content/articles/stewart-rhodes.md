## Background

Rhodes is a U.S. political activist best known for founding and leading the Oath Keepers. He studied law and was frequently described in media coverage as combining an organizer role with a public-facing spokesperson role for the group.

## Oath Keepers and Public Profile

The Oath Keepers positioned itself around the idea that members should refuse certain government orders they considered unconstitutional. The organization’s membership and messaging drew scrutiny from civil-rights groups, journalists, and law-enforcement officials, especially as armed militia activity and anti-government organizing intensified in the 2010s.

## January 6 and Criminal Case

Federal prosecutors alleged that Rhodes directed and coordinated efforts to stop the peaceful transfer of power following the 2020 election, including communications, travel, and operational planning among Oath Keepers affiliates. He was charged in a broader conspiracy case that included multiple co-defendants.

In 2022, a jury found Rhodes guilty of seditious conspiracy and related offenses. Sentencing and appellate proceedings drew continued public attention because the case was among the most prominent January 6-related prosecutions involving leadership-level defendants.

## Legacy and Significance

Rhodes’s conviction is often cited in discussions of how the U.S. justice system approached extremist organizations and political violence connected to January 6. The case is also used as a reference point for debates about domestic extremism, militia movements, and the boundaries of political speech versus criminal coordination.
