## Summary

John D. Rockefeller Jr. was the only son of Standard Oil founder John D. Rockefeller and a major philanthropist. He oversaw the construction of Rockefeller Center in New York City during the Great Depression. He donated the land for the United Nations headquarters and funded the restoration of Colonial Williamsburg. His philanthropic legacy exceeded even his father's in scope and impact.
