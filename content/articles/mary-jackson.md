## Summary

Mary Jackson was NASA's first Black female engineer. She began as a mathematician in the segregated West Area Computing unit and fought to gain permission to attend whites-only graduate classes. She later became an administrator advocating for the hiring and promotion of women at NASA. She was featured in the book and film Hidden Figures.
