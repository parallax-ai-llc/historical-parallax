## Background

Pedro Martínez is a Dominican former professional baseball pitcher who became one of the most dominant starters of the late 1990s and early 2000s. He is most closely associated with the Boston Red Sox, while also pitching for several MLB clubs across a long career.

## Playing Style

Martínez was known for elite command, a powerful fastball, and swing-and-miss secondary pitches. Analysts often highlight how he sustained exceptional performance despite being smaller than many power pitchers, relying on precision, sequencing, and deception.

## Achievements

- Multiple Cy Young Awards and All-Star selections are frequently cited in career summaries.
- He won a World Series with the Red Sox in 2004, a landmark championship ending a long title drought for the franchise.

## Additional Context

### Peak seasons and context

Martínez’s best-known seasons came during a high-offense era in Major League Baseball, which makes his run-prevention metrics especially notable in retrospective analysis. Statistical measures such as ERA+ are commonly used to compare his dominance across eras.

### Major teams

Over his career, he pitched for the Los Angeles Dodgers, Montreal Expos, Boston Red Sox, New York Mets, and Philadelphia Phillies. His tenure in Boston is often highlighted as the period when he became a league-defining ace.

### Recognition

He was elected to the National Baseball Hall of Fame, reflecting sustained excellence and historical impact at his position.
