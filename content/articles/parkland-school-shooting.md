## Summary

Nikolas Cruz killed 17 people at Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School in Parkland, Florida. Surviving students launched the March for Our Lives gun control movement.

## Key Facts

- **Date:** February 14, 2018
- **Location:** Marjory Stoneman Douglas High School, Parkland, Florida, U.S.
- **Deaths:** 17 people killed
- **Injuries:** 17 people wounded
- **Perpetrator:** Nikolas Cruz (former student)

## Immediate Aftermath

- The school district and local authorities faced intense scrutiny over **campus security**, **law-enforcement response**, and prior interactions with the suspect.
- Multiple **surviving students and families** became prominent public advocates, organizing rallies, voter drives, and school-walkout events.

## Movement and Public Impact

- Survivors helped launch **March for Our Lives**, a youth-led movement advocating for stronger gun-violence prevention policies.
- The event became a major reference point in U.S. debates over:
  - background checks and “red flag”/risk-based firearm removal laws
  - school security measures and threat assessment practices
  - media coverage and political polarization around gun policy

## Legal Proceedings (High Level)

- Nikolas Cruz was arrested shortly after the shooting and later entered guilty pleas.
- The case proceeded to a penalty phase to determine sentencing outcomes.
