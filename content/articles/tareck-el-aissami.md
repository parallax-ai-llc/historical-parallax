## Summary

Venezuelan politician who served as vice president and oil minister. He was designated by the US as a drug kingpin and sanctioned. He was arrested in Venezuela in 2023 on corruption charges in a surprise move by the Maduro government.

## Background

El Aissami rose within Venezuela’s ruling political movement after holding student and interior-security related roles. He later moved into senior government posts, including positions tied to internal affairs, regional governance, and national executive power.

## Allegations, Sanctions, and Designations

In 2017, the United States designated El Aissami under its narcotics sanctions authorities, alleging ties to international drug trafficking networks. The designation led to asset-blocking measures under U.S. jurisdiction and restrictions on transactions with U.S. persons and entities.

Separate sanctions and enforcement actions over the years also focused on Venezuelan state-linked networks involved in corruption and sanctions evasion, with El Aissami often cited as a prominent figure in those narratives.

## 2023 Arrest and Domestic Political Context

El Aissami’s 2023 arrest in Venezuela occurred amid a broader, high-profile anti-corruption drive targeting officials connected to the oil sector and state companies. The move was widely interpreted as both a corruption case and a sign of shifting internal political balances inside the Maduro government.

## Why He Matters

El Aissami is frequently referenced in discussions of state-linked corruption, sanctions policy, and the overlap between political power and illicit economies in Venezuela. His career trajectory—from high office to domestic arrest—illustrates how enforcement actions can come from both international sanctions regimes and internal political realignments.
