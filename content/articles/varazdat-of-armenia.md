## Summary

Varazdat of Armenia was the last known ancient Olympic champion, winning the boxing event around 385 AD. He later became King of Armenia.

## Additional Context

## Historical setting

Varazdat is placed in the late 4th century, a period when Armenia sat between the Roman and Sasanian spheres of influence and experienced frequent changes in leadership and external pressure. Sources for this era are limited and often mediated through later chronicles, so many details are presented cautiously.[^1]

## Olympic tradition

He is commonly described as the last known winner of an ancient Olympic event, associated with boxing, in the waning period of the classical Olympic Games. Modern summaries emphasize that the surviving record is thin, but that his name persists in lists of champions used by later writers.[^1]

## Kingship and politics

Accounts describe his reign as brief. Narratives often focus on the court and military factionalism that could elevate or depose rulers quickly, especially in an Armenia balancing local noble power with demands from larger empires.[^1]

## Legacy

Varazdat’s modern prominence comes less from administrative achievements than from the symbolic pairing of athlete and king. In popular history he appears as a bridge between the classical sporting world and late antique Armenian political history.[^1]
