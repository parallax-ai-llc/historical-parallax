## Summary

South African brothers Ameer and Raees Cajee disappeared with 69,000 Bitcoin worth $3.6 billion from their Africrypt investment platform. It was one of the largest cryptocurrency thefts in history.

## Background

Africrypt was described as a cryptocurrency investment platform operating in South Africa. In early 2021, the company’s founders—brothers Ameer and Raees Cajee—reported a security incident to clients and advised them not to pursue legal action, a message that later drew scrutiny. The episode became widely reported as a major alleged cryptocurrency fraud or theft.

## Scale and Uncertainty

Public reporting often cited very large figures (including tens of thousands of bitcoin), but the true amount involved has been difficult to verify independently. As with other cryptocurrency incidents, market price fluctuations also make USD estimates vary widely depending on the chosen date.

## Investigation and Legal Issues

The case involved private investigators, media reporting, and questions about regulatory jurisdiction. Reports indicated the brothers left South Africa and were later linked to the United Arab Emirates, but legal outcomes and asset recovery efforts have been complex and, in many accounts, inconclusive.

## Why the Case Matters

The Africrypt story is frequently used as an example of:

- The risks of opaque “investment” crypto platforms
- The challenges of cross-border enforcement and asset tracing
- The difficulty of distinguishing hacks from insider fraud when records are limited
