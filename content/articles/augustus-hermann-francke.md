## Summary

Augustus Hermann Francke was a German Pietist theologian and educator who established one of the most remarkable charitable institutions in early modern Europe. His Francke Foundations in Halle included an orphanage, schools, a publishing house, and a pharmacy. He helped make the University of Halle a center of Pietism. His emphasis on practical Christianity and social engagement influenced later evangelical movements worldwide.
