## Summary

Hamas militants broke through the Gaza barrier and attacked Israeli communities, killing 1,200 and kidnapping 250 hostages. The deadliest day for Jewish people since the Holocaust.

## Context and Background

- **Gaza–Israel conflict:** The attack occurred amid decades of Israeli–Palestinian conflict and periodic escalations between Israel and armed groups in Gaza.
- **Immediate framing:** Hamas described the operation as a response to conditions in Gaza and events in Jerusalem; Israel described it as an act of terrorism and a mass-casualty attack on civilians.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Cross-border incursions:** Armed militants crossed the Gaza–Israel boundary and attacked multiple locations in southern Israel, including communities and public sites.
- **Mass casualties and abductions:** Large numbers of people were killed and many were taken hostage into Gaza.
- **Rocket fire:** The operation included large-scale rocket launches toward Israeli population centers.

## Aftermath

- **Israeli response:** Israel declared a state of war and launched extensive military operations against Hamas in Gaza.
- **Regional and global impact:** The events triggered a major escalation with broad humanitarian, political, and security consequences.

## Notes on Figures and Reporting

- **Casualty and hostage numbers** reported in the aftermath have been updated over time as identifications, investigations, and official accounting progressed.
- Many summaries cite approximately **1,200 deaths in Israel** and **about 250 hostages** taken on and after October 7, 2023.

## Related Topics

- **Israel–Hamas war (2023–present):** The attack is widely treated as the trigger for the subsequent Gaza war.
- **Hostage negotiations:** The fate of hostages became a central issue in diplomacy, ceasefire talks, and domestic politics.
