## Summary

Japanese communist militant group that carried out international terrorist attacks in the 1970s-80s including the 1972 Lod Airport massacre in Israel that killed 26 people.

## Overview
The Japanese Red Army (JRA) was a militant far-left organization active mainly from the 1970s through the 1990s. It became internationally known for carrying out and participating in armed attacks, including incidents outside Japan.

## Origins and ideology
The group emerged from the wider landscape of postwar student activism and radical left movements. It advocated revolutionary goals and sought to align with other militant groups abroad, which contributed to its international footprint.

## Notable incidents
The JRA has been linked to multiple high-profile attacks and hijackings during the 1970s and 1980s. Several of these incidents resulted in civilian casualties and drew extensive international attention and counterterrorism responses.

## Decline and aftermath
Over time, arrests, defections, and shifting political conditions reduced the organization’s capacity. Japanese authorities pursued members domestically and through international cooperation, and leaders later announced the group’s disbandment. The JRA remains a case study in transnational political violence during the Cold War era.
