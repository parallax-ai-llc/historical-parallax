## Summary

Syrian ISIS spokesman and senior leader who called for lone-wolf attacks against Western countries. He was one of the most influential ISIS figures and a key founder. Killed in a US airstrike in Aleppo.

## Role in ISIS Propaganda

Al-Adnani served as a principal spokesman and propagandist for ISIS, shaping messaging intended both for internal cohesion and for recruitment abroad. His statements were widely disseminated through ISIS media channels and were treated by analysts as indicators of the group’s strategic priorities.

## Calls for External Attacks

He became particularly notorious for urging sympathizers to conduct attacks in their home countries, encouraging low-complexity violence that did not require direct travel or centralized coordination. This approach aligned with ISIS’s effort to project reach even as it faced military pressure in Iraq and Syria.

## Organizational Responsibilities

Beyond public messaging, he was often described as holding senior roles within the organization’s leadership structure, including oversight functions related to security and external operations (as characterized in open-source reporting and government statements).

## Death and Attribution

Al-Adnani was killed in 2016 near al-Bab in northern Syria. Different actors attributed responsibility for the strike at the time; over subsequent reporting, the event has been commonly associated with U.S.-led coalition operations.
