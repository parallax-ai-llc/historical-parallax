## Summary

Russian serial killer who murdered 6 women in Moscow between 2005 and 2009. He strangled victims in their apartments. Sentenced to life imprisonment.

## Crimes (High-Level)

- Murders of women in Moscow over several years (mid-2000s).
- Reported method: **strangulation**, often in victims' apartments.
- The pattern suggests opportunistic access to victims in private residences.

## Arrest and Legal Outcome

- Police arrested him after investigative work connected multiple cases.
- He was convicted and sentenced to **life imprisonment**.

## Notes on Case Investigation

- Serial homicide investigations typically rely on:
  - linking crime scenes through recurring behavioral indicators,
  - forensic evidence and victimology,
  - and tracing contacts or access routes to victims.
- In urban environments, case linkage can be challenging due to high background crime rates and limited witnesses.

## Public Record

- The case is primarily documented through court reporting and summary accounts.
- Because victims were attacked in private settings, many details come from investigative files and trial proceedings rather than eyewitnesses.
- In many jurisdictions, life sentences for serial murder reflect both the number of victims and the perceived ongoing risk.
