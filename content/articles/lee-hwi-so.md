## Summary

Lee Hwi-so (Benjamin W. Lee) was a Korean-American theoretical physicist who contributed to Standard Model renormalization.

## Academic Background

Benjamin W. Lee was born in Seoul and later studied and worked in the United States. In many biographical summaries, he is presented as part of the post-war generation of Korean scientists who built careers in leading American research universities.

## Research Contributions (High-Level)

Lee is best known for work in quantum field theory and particle physics during the development of what became the Standard Model. His contributions are often discussed in relation to:

- Theoretical foundations of gauge theories.
- Renormalization and the consistent calculation of observable quantities.
- Work that helped make electroweak theory and related frameworks practically usable for precision predictions.

Because this area is highly technical, public summaries typically describe his results in terms of their role in establishing the mathematical consistency of key particle-physics theories.

## Community and Influence

He collaborated with other prominent theorists of the period and mentored younger physicists. His influence is frequently noted through citations and the continued use of methods developed in that era of quantum field theory.

## Death and Legacy

Lee died in a car accident in 1977. Retrospectives often emphasize both the scientific importance of his work and the sense of loss within the physics community given the trajectory of his early-career achievements.
