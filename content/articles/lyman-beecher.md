## Summary

Lyman Beecher was an American Presbyterian minister who was one of the most prominent evangelical leaders of the early 19th century. He was a leader in the temperance movement and opposed Unitarianism in New England. He served as the first president of Lane Theological Seminary in Cincinnati. He was the father of Harriet Beecher Stowe and Henry Ward Beecher.
