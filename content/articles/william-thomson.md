## Identity and Naming

William Thomson is best known under his peerage title, **Lord Kelvin**. Many scientific and historical sources therefore file his major contributions under “Kelvin,” even when referring to work he did before being ennobled.

## Major Contributions (High-Level)

- **Thermodynamics:** Helped formalize the concept of an **absolute temperature scale**, now expressed in **kelvin (K)**.
- **Energy and heat:** Contributed to the development of the **first and second laws of thermodynamics** and to the broader 19th-century synthesis between heat, work, and energy.
- **Electrical science and engineering:** Worked on practical and theoretical problems in electricity, including instrumentation and telegraphy.
- **Transatlantic telegraphy:** Played a prominent role as a scientific adviser and engineer in efforts to lay and operate **submarine telegraph cables**, a major infrastructure project of the era.

## Roles and Institutions

- Associated with the **University of Glasgow**, where he was a central figure in British physics and engineering education in the 19th century.
- Served as a public scientific authority whose work bridged academic physics and industrial-scale engineering.

## Legacy

- The **kelvin** unit (SI base unit of thermodynamic temperature) reflects the lasting influence of his work on temperature and thermodynamics.
- Frequently cited as an exemplar of Victorian-era scientific leadership combining mathematics, experiment, and engineering practice.
