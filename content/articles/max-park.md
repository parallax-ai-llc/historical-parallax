## Summary

Max Park holds multiple Rubik's Cube world records including the 3x3 average record. He is autistic and uses cubing as therapeutic activity.

## Additional Context

- Competitive speedcubing is organized globally through the World Cube Association (WCA), with standardized event formats, scrambles, and record tracking.
- Records are often separated into **single solve** and **average of 5** (or other averages), which reward both peak speed and consistency.

## Training and Technique (High Level)

- Elite 3×3×3 performance typically depends on efficient solving methods, fast recognition, and practiced finger tricks, not just raw turning speed.
- Many top solvers treat practice like a sport: structured drills, review of solve statistics, and gradual optimization of algorithms.

## Representation and Impact

- Park’s visibility has made him a notable figure for autism representation in sports-like competitive spaces.
- Public discussion often highlights how supportive coaching, routine, and an absorbing skill-based activity can intersect with therapeutic goals.

## Legacy

- Beyond individual records, he is often cited as part of a broader era of rapidly improving times driven by technique sharing and community-wide iteration.
