## Summary

Paul Erdős was a Hungarian mathematician who was one of the most prolific mathematicians in history, publishing around 1,500 papers with over 500 collaborators. He made fundamental contributions to number theory, combinatorics, set theory, probability, and graph theory. His collaborative nature led to the concept of the Erdős number, measuring collaborative distance to him. He lived an itinerant lifestyle, traveling from colleague to colleague.
