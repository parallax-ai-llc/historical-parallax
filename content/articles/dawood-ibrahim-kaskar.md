## Summary

Indian crime lord who heads D-Company, one of the world's most powerful criminal organizations based in Mumbai. He masterminded the 1993 Bombay bombings that killed 257 people and is believed to be hiding in Pakistan.

## Background

Dawood Ibrahim Kaskar is an Indian fugitive and underworld figure associated with Mumbai’s organized-crime networks. Public reporting and government statements in India and elsewhere describe him as the leader of **D-Company**, a criminal syndicate linked to illicit finance and trafficking.

## D-Company and Criminal Allegations

High-level descriptions of D-Company commonly include:

- Operations spanning **smuggling and trafficking**, extortion, and other organized-crime activity.
- Use of informal financial channels and cross-border logistics.
- Influence in parts of Mumbai’s underworld during periods of intense gang rivalry.

Because much of the available detail comes from law-enforcement claims and investigative reporting, specific operational assertions are often presented as allegations rather than adjudicated fact.

## 1993 Bombay Bombings (Alleged Role)

Ibrahim has been widely **accused by Indian authorities** of involvement in the **1993 Bombay (Mumbai) bombings**, a coordinated series of attacks that killed hundreds of people. Coverage typically characterizes him as a key organizer or mastermind, while noting that he has not been brought to trial in India due to his fugitive status.

## International Designations and Sanctions

Government actions referenced in summaries of his case include:

- Designation by the **United States Department of the Treasury** under terrorism-related sanctions frameworks.
- Listing or designation by **Indian authorities** in connection with terrorism and organized crime.

Such measures generally aim to restrict travel, freeze assets where possible, and deter financial dealings with affiliated networks.

## Reported Whereabouts and Status

Public sources frequently state that Ibrahim is believed to be outside India, with reporting often pointing to Pakistan as a likely location. These claims are politically sensitive and contested; definitive, independently verified information is limited in open sources.

## Public Impact

- His profile is often cited in discussions about the relationship between organized crime, illicit finance, and regional security.
- The 1993 bombings remain a major reference point in India’s modern history of terrorism and urban violence.
