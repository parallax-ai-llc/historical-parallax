## Summary

Zimbabwean liberation hero turned dictator who destroyed his country's economy and ordered the Gukurahundi genocide. His 37-year rule ended when his own military removed him.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Rise to Power and Consolidation

- **Liberation movement leader**: Mugabe emerged as a major figure in Zimbabwe's anti-colonial struggle and later became the dominant political leader after independence.
- **Centralization of authority**: Over time, his administration is widely described as consolidating power through party control, security services, and restrictions on opposition.

## Major Policies and Crises (High-Level)

- **Gukurahundi**: The early-1980s violence in Matabeleland remains a defining controversy, frequently discussed as mass atrocity and state repression.
- **Land reform and property seizures**: The fast-track land redistribution of the 2000s is commonly linked in summaries to agricultural disruption, capital flight, and long-term economic decline.
- **Economic collapse**: Zimbabwe experienced severe inflation and currency instability; this period is central to assessments of governance and economic management.

## International Relations and Sanctions

- Mugabe's government faced international condemnation and sanctions, while he also pursued alliances that framed Western criticism as neo-colonial pressure.

## Removal and Aftermath

- In 2017, military and party actions ended his presidency; the transition was framed domestically as a political correction rather than a conventional coup.
- Debates about his legacy often split between liberation-era achievements and later authoritarianism and economic harm.

## Key Takeaway

- Mugabe's career is frequently used as a case study of how liberation legitimacy can coexist with, and later be overshadowed by, authoritarian governance.
