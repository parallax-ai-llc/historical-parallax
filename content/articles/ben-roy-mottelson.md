## Summary

Ben Roy Mottelson shared the 1975 Nobel Prize in Physics for discovering the connection between collective motion and particle motion in atomic nuclei. Working with Aage Bohr, he developed the collective model of the nucleus that explained nuclear deformation and rotation. He spent his career at the Niels Bohr Institute in Copenhagen.
