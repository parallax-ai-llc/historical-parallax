Malian military officer who led two coups in 2020 and 2021, declaring himself transitional president. His regime expelled French and UN forces while forming alliances with Russian Wagner Group.

## Background

Assimi Goïta is a career officer in the Malian Armed Forces who rose to national prominence during the political crisis of 2020. He became one of the central figures of Mali’s transition period after the August 2020 coup d’état and the subsequent May 2021 coup.

## Role in Mali’s 2020–2021 Political Transition

- **August 2020 coup:** Goïta emerged as a leading member of the military group that removed President Ibrahim Boubacar Keïta amid mass protests and security concerns.
- **Transitional arrangements:** A civilian-led transition was announced, but the military retained substantial influence through the transitional institutions.
- **May 2021 coup:** After tensions within the transitional government, Goïta led a second power shift and later assumed the title of transitional president.

## Governance and Key Themes

- **Security-first framing:** His administration has often emphasized counterinsurgency and territorial security as primary state priorities.
- **Domestic legitimacy:** The transitional authorities have drawn support from constituencies critical of prior governments, while also facing criticism over delays and constraints associated with prolonged military-led rule.
- **Elections and timelines:** The transition period has been marked by repeated debates over election scheduling and benchmarks for returning to constitutional civilian government.

## International Relations (High-Level)

- **France and Western partners:** Relations with France deteriorated sharply during the transition, culminating in the departure of French forces.
- **United Nations:** Mali’s relationship with the UN mission (MINUSMA) also ended with the mission’s withdrawal after the transitional authorities requested its departure.
- **Regional diplomacy:** Engagement with regional bodies (including ECOWAS) has involved negotiations and pressure regarding the transition timeline.
- **Russia/Wagner:** Mali expanded security cooperation with Russia, including ties widely reported as involving the Wagner Group.
