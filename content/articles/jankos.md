## Summary

Marcin "Jankos" Jankowski is a Polish professional League of Legends player widely regarded as one of the greatest junglers in the history of European competitive play. Born on July 1, 1995, he earned the nickname "The First Blood King" for his hyper-aggressive early-game style that redefined how the jungle role could be played in professional matches.

Over a career spanning more than a decade, Jankos became the most decorated player in LEC (League of Legends European Championship) history. His tenure with G2 Esports from 2018 onward produced an era of European dominance that included a Mid-Season Invitational title in 2019 and a World Championship finals appearance that same year — the first for a European team since Fnatic's victory in Season 1.

## Early Life & Background

Marcin Jankowski was born on July 1, 1995, in Poland. He grew up playing video games competitively and gravitated toward League of Legends during its early years in the European scene. By his mid-teens, he had established himself as a high-ranking solo queue player on the EU West server, where his aggressive jungle pathing and mechanical skill drew attention from professional teams.

## Rise to Prominence

Jankos began his professional career with ROCCAT (later known as Team ROCCAT) in 2014, competing in the EU LCS. His fearless invades and early-game skirmishing quickly earned him a reputation and the "First Blood King" moniker. He secured first blood at a rate far above any other jungler in Europe during his early seasons.

In 2016, he joined H2k-Gaming, where he helped the team reach the semifinals of the 2016 World Championship — a deep run that signaled Europe's resurgence on the international stage. His performance against Samsung Galaxy and Albus NoX Luna cemented his status as a world-class jungler.

The defining move of his career came in late 2017 when he joined G2 Esports alongside Perkz, Wunder, Caps, and Mikyx. This roster became the most successful in European League of Legends history.

## Key Achievements

With G2 Esports, Jankos won eight LEC titles across multiple splits, making him the most titled player in the league's history. The pinnacle came in 2019, when G2 completed the unprecedented "Grand Slam" run in European play and won the Mid-Season Invitational by defeating Team Liquid 3-0 in the finals.

At the 2019 World Championship, G2 advanced to the grand final — the first European team to do so since the modern format was introduced — before falling to FunPlus Phoenix 0-3. Jankos was instrumental throughout the tournament, particularly in the semifinal victory over SK Telecom T1.

His individual accolades include multiple LEC All-Pro Team selections and MVP nominations. By 2023, he held the record for the most games played in LEC history, a testament to his longevity and consistency at the highest level.

## Controversies & Criticism

Jankos has largely avoided major controversy throughout his career. Criticism has occasionally focused on his international performances, with some analysts arguing that his aggressive style could become a liability against disciplined Eastern teams. His 2019 World Championship finals performance against FunPlus Phoenix drew scrutiny, though most of the blame for the 0-3 loss was attributed to G2 being outclassed as a team rather than any individual failure.

His transition to content creation and streaming alongside competitive play also drew mixed reactions, with some questioning whether it divided his focus during later career years.

## Legacy & Influence

Jankos stands as the most accomplished jungler in European League of Legends history and one of the most influential players the region has produced. His aggressive, high-tempo style inspired a generation of European junglers to prioritize early-game pressure and proactive pathing. Beyond competition, his charismatic personality and prolific streaming career made him one of the most popular figures in the European esports ecosystem, bridging the gap between competitive play and entertainment content creation.
