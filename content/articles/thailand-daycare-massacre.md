## Summary

Former police officer killed 37 people including 24 children at a daycare center in Nong Bua Lamphu. One of the deadliest mass killings in Thai history.

## Background

- The attack occurred in **Nong Bua Lamphu Province**, in northeastern Thailand.
- Thai authorities identified the suspect as a **former police officer**.
- Initial reporting described the event as a mass attack focused on a **childcare/daycare facility**, with additional deaths occurring outside the daycare.

## Incident (high-level)

- The attacker used multiple methods reported by authorities and major news outlets, including **firearms** and a **knife**.
- After the daycare attack, the perpetrator traveled to a private residence, where additional deaths were reported.
- Thai police later reported the perpetrator **died by suicide** following the attacks.

## Immediate response and investigation

- Thai police and emergency services secured the scene and began victim identification and next-of-kin notification.
- National leadership publicly acknowledged the event and expressed condolences.
- Investigations focused on the perpetrator’s background, access to weapons, and events leading up to the attack.

## Impact and public discussion

- The incident intensified discussion in Thailand about:
  - **firearms access and enforcement**
  - **mental health and substance-use support** (in cases involving former officials)
  - **community safety** around schools and childcare facilities
- It became a reference point in later reporting on mass violence in Thailand due to its unusually high number of child victims.
