## Summary

Manuel Elkin Patarroyo is a Colombian immunologist who developed SPf66, the first synthetic vaccine against malaria. While field trials showed variable efficacy, his work pioneered the synthetic vaccine approach and demonstrated that chemically synthesized peptides could generate immune responses against parasitic diseases. He donated his vaccine patent to the WHO.
