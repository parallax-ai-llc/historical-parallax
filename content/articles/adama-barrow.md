## Background

Adama Barrow is a Gambian politician who became president after defeating long-time ruler Yahya Jammeh in the 2016 presidential election. His rise to office is commonly described as the product of an opposition coalition that coalesced around a single candidate to end more than two decades of Jammeh’s rule.

## Path to the Presidency (2016–2017)

Barrow’s election victory led to a constitutional crisis when Jammeh initially conceded and later rejected the results. Regional and international pressure—particularly through the Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS)—played a major role in enforcing the election outcome. Barrow was sworn in as president in January 2017 and assumed office as Jammeh departed the country.

## Governance and Reform Agenda

Barrow’s administration has been associated with a stated reform agenda focused on democratic governance after an authoritarian period. High-level themes in reporting and public debate have included:

- Strengthening institutions and the rule of law
- Reviewing constitutional arrangements and term-limit proposals
- Transitional justice initiatives connected to past abuses

## Transitional Justice and Accountability

During Barrow’s presidency, Gambia established processes to document abuses under the previous regime, including a truth and reconciliation mechanism. Recommendations and findings from these processes have influenced national debate over accountability, reparations, and institutional reform.

## Political Challenges and Criticism

Barrow’s government has faced criticism from political opponents and civil society groups regarding the pace and depth of reforms. Public controversies and debates have included:

- Allegations of corruption or patronage in government
- Disputes over constitutional reforms and term limits
- Broader concerns about the consolidation of power by incumbents

## Elections and Continuity in Office

Barrow won re-election in 2021. While his continued tenure reflects electoral support, the political environment has also included opposition claims and public skepticism about whether the post-2017 transition has delivered the full set of promised institutional changes.

## International Relations

Gambia’s post-2017 diplomacy has emphasized engagement with regional partners and international donors, with ongoing attention to governance reforms, economic development, and security cooperation.
