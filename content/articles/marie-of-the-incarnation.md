## Summary

Marie of the Incarnation was a French Ursuline nun and mystic who was one of the founders of the Catholic Church in New France (Canada). She founded the first Ursuline convent in Quebec in 1639 and devoted her life to educating indigenous women and girls. Her extensive correspondence and spiritual writings are among the most important mystical texts of 17th-century Christianity. She was canonized by Pope Francis in 2014.
