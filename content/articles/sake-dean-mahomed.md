## Summary

Sake Dean Mahomed was an Indian surgeon and medical entrepreneur who introduced shampooing (therapeutic massage and steam baths) to Britain. He opened the first Indian restaurant in London (1810) and established Mahomed's Baths in Brighton, becoming 'Shampooing Surgeon' to both King George IV and King William IV. He was a pioneer of Indian therapeutic practices in the West.
