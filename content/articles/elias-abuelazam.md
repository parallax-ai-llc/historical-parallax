## Summary

Israeli-American serial stabber who attacked 18 people in Flint, Michigan in 2010, killing 5. He targeted African-American men in late-night attacks.

## Background

Public reporting identifies Elias Abuelazam as an Israeli-born man who lived in the United States and was connected to multiple violent incidents in 2010. Sources describe a pattern of late-night assaults in Flint, Michigan, which drew significant public attention and a large-scale police response.

## Investigation and Arrest

The Flint attacks prompted investigators to look for links across incidents based on timing, victim profiles, and method. Abuelazam was ultimately taken into custody in 2010 at an airport in Atlanta, Georgia, after authorities developed leads tying him to the Michigan cases.

## Charges and Conviction

Abuelazam faced multiple counts related to homicide and assault. In 2012, he was convicted of murder in Michigan state court. Sentencing and additional case details are summarized differently across secondary sources, but most accounts emphasize that the conviction formally connected him to the Flint series.
