## Summary

Lee Man-hee, founder of the Shincheonji Church, whose sect was responsible for South Korea's largest COVID-19 cluster. He was convicted of obstructing the government's pandemic response by hiding membership lists.

## What the “Cluster” Refers To

The term "Shincheonji Church cluster" is commonly used to describe a major outbreak of COVID-19 linked to Shincheonji Church of Jesus, a new religious movement in South Korea. The best-known early outbreak was associated with services and gatherings in **Daegu** in February 2020.

## Public Health Response and Controversy

South Korean authorities pursued extensive contact tracing and testing during the early wave of the pandemic. In public reporting, controversy centered on whether the church provided complete and timely information about members and meeting attendance that could be used for epidemiological investigation.

## Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

Lee Man-hee, the church’s founder, faced charges connected to alleged obstruction of quarantine measures. The timeline reflects that court outcomes shifted across stages of trial and appeal, and the case is often discussed alongside broader questions about pandemic governance, due process, and the boundaries of state power during public health emergencies.

## Social Impact

The outbreak’s scale and media attention contributed to intense public scrutiny of Shincheonji and heightened debate about religious freedom, minority groups, and stigma during health crises. It also became a reference point in later discussions of superspreading events and institutional transparency.
