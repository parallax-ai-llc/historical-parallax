## Summary

M. S. Swaminathan is known as the father of the Green Revolution in India. He developed high-yielding varieties of wheat that transformed India from a food-deficient nation to a self-sufficient one. His work with Norman Borlaug adapted Mexican dwarf wheat varieties for Indian conditions, saving millions from famine. He received the World Food Prize in 1987.
