## Summary

German industrialist kidnapped and murdered by the Red Army Faction in 1977 during the German Autumn. His driver and three police bodyguards were killed during the abduction.

## Background

- **Industrial leadership:** He held senior roles in major German employer and industry associations and was a prominent public representative of business interests.
- **Political profile:** His prominence and combative public posture in labor disputes made him a symbol for parts of the radical left.
- **Nazi-era past:** Sources describe his earlier involvement with Nazi organizations, including service as an SS officer, which further shaped perceptions in the postwar period.

## Kidnapping and German Autumn (High-Level)

- **Abduction:** He was kidnapped on 5 September 1977 when his convoy was ambushed; the attack killed his driver and police escort.
- **Demands:** RAF demanded the release of imprisoned members, linking the kidnapping to a wider campaign of pressure against the West German state.
- **Crisis:** The episode became central to the "German Autumn," a period remembered as one of the most severe internal security crises in West Germany.

## Aftermath

- **Murder and discovery:** After weeks in captivity, he was killed and his body was later found in France.
- **Policy legacy:** The crisis influenced subsequent debates in Germany over counterterrorism policy, negotiations with hostage-takers, and the balance between civil liberties and state security.
