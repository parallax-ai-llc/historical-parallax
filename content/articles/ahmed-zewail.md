## Summary

Ahmed Zewail was an Egyptian-American chemist who won the 1999 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his studies of chemical reactions using femtosecond spectroscopy. Known as the 'father of femtochemistry,' he developed techniques to observe chemical reactions at the timescale of femtoseconds (10⁻¹⁵ seconds). He was the first Egyptian and Arab scientist to win a Nobel Prize in a scientific field. Zewail also served as a US science envoy to the Middle East.
