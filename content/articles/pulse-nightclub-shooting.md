## Summary

Mass shooting at Pulse nightclub in Orlando on June 12, 2016 killing 49 people.

## Context

- **Location**: Pulse, a gay nightclub in Orlando, Florida, United States.
- **Date**: June 12, 2016 (early morning).
- **Scale**: Widely reported as one of the deadliest mass shootings in modern U.S. history at the time.

## What Happened (High-level)

- The attacker entered the venue and opened fire on patrons.
- The incident escalated into a prolonged standoff with law enforcement.
- Police ultimately breached the building; the attacker was killed.

## Victims and Impact

- **Fatalities**: 49 victims were killed (not including the attacker).
- **Injuries**: 53 people were wounded.
- The attack had a particularly profound impact on **LGBTQ+ communities**, as well as on Orlando’s large **Latino/Hispanic** community.

## Immediate Response

- Emergency medical services transported a large number of patients to area hospitals.
- Local, state, and federal agencies (including the FBI) participated in response and investigation.
- Public vigils and memorials formed quickly in Orlando and internationally.

## Investigation and Public Discussion

- Authorities examined motives and the attacker’s background, including prior contacts with law enforcement.
- The event triggered extensive debate about:
  - **Gun policy** and access to firearms
  - **Counterterrorism** and domestic radicalization
  - **Hate crimes** and targeted violence

## Memorialization

- Pulse became a focal point for remembrance; survivors’ accounts and community advocacy shaped public memory.
- Ongoing discussions have included permanent memorial proposals and the long-term needs of survivors.
