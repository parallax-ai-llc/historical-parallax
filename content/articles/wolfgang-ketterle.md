## Additional Context

- **Field**: Experimental AMO physics and quantum many-body systems using ultracold atoms.
- **What a BEC is**: A Bose–Einstein condensate forms when bosonic atoms occupy the same quantum ground state at extremely low temperatures, producing macroscopic quantum behavior.

## Key Contributions

- **Creation and study of BECs**: Ketterle’s group achieved Bose–Einstein condensation in dilute gases and carried out early experiments that probed coherence and collective properties.
- **Atom optics and interferometry**: BECs enable coherent matter-wave sources, supporting experiments analogous to optical lasers and precision interferometry.
- **Platform for quantum simulation**: Ultracold atomic gases became a versatile laboratory for exploring condensed-matter-like phenomena with high control over interactions and geometry.

## Nobel Prize Context (2001)

The 2001 Nobel Prize in Physics recognized the achievement of Bose–Einstein condensation in dilute gases of alkali atoms and early fundamental studies of the condensates’ properties.

## Related Concepts

- **Evaporative cooling**: A technique to reach nanokelvin temperatures by selectively removing higher-energy atoms.
- **Coherence**: The phase stability that allows interference effects, central to “matter-wave” experiments.
- **Quantum phase transitions**: Changes in quantum states driven by parameters like interaction strength rather than temperature.
