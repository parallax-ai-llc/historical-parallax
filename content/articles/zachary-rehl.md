## Background

Zachary Rehl is an American figure associated with the Proud Boys, a far-right organization that has been involved in political street confrontations in the United States. Public reporting has described Rehl as a leader within the group’s Philadelphia chapter in the period leading up to January 6, 2021.

## Role in January 6 Context

High-level accounts of the January 6 attack on the U.S. Capitol often focus on:

- Pre-event organizing among affiliated groups and local chapters
- Travel and coordination in Washington, D.C.
- Movement of organized contingents toward the Capitol grounds
- Post-event communications and investigation-driven disclosures

Rehl is frequently referenced in this context as one of several Proud Boys leaders prosecuted in a major federal case centered on coordination and intent.

## Legal Case (Overview)

In the United States, “seditious conspiracy” is a federal offense tied to conspiracies to use force to oppose or hinder the authority of the government or the execution of federal law. In the large Proud Boys prosecution connected to January 6:

- Multiple defendants were tried in federal court
- The case featured extensive evidence derived from digital communications and witness testimony
- Convictions (where obtained) were followed by sentencing proceedings that can include substantial prison terms
