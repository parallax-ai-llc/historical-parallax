## Summary

Russian-speaking ransomware group known for mass exploitation of file transfer software vulnerabilities affecting thousands of organizations. Their MOVEit campaign was the largest data theft of 2023.

## Typical Operating Model

Clop is commonly described as a ransomware‑as‑a‑service (RaaS) ecosystem, where core operators develop tooling while affiliates conduct intrusions. Like many contemporary groups, it has been linked to "double extortion": stealing data first and then threatening publication to increase payment pressure.

## Exploitation of Third-Party Software

A distinguishing feature of several Clop campaigns has been the mass exploitation of widely used third‑party products (notably managed file‑transfer platforms). This approach can produce "many‑victim" incidents in which downstream organizations and individuals are affected even if the primary breach occurs at a vendor.

## MOVEit Campaign (2023)

The MOVEit incident became a high‑profile example of supply‑chain‑like impact in cybercrime: a vulnerability in a common tool enabled large‑scale data theft across government agencies, universities, and private companies. Reporting emphasized that the harms included not only operational disruption but also long‑tail privacy impacts from leaked personal data.

## Impact and Response

Responses to Clop incidents typically include emergency patching, incident response, breach notification, and law‑enforcement coordination. The group’s activity has also fueled policy debates about ransomware payments, critical‑infrastructure resilience, and the security obligations of software vendors.
