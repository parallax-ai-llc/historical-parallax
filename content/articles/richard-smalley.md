## Summary

Richard Smalley was an American chemist who shared the 1996 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for the discovery of fullerenes (buckminsterfullerene, C60). Together with Robert Curl and Harold Kroto, he discovered these spherical carbon molecules, opening new fields in chemistry and nanotechnology. He later became a passionate advocate for nanotechnology and sustainable energy solutions.
