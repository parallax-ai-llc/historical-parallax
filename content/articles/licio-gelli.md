## P2 Lodge and Italian Political Scandals

- Propaganda Due (P2) was a clandestine Masonic lodge whose membership list, when discovered, triggered a major political scandal in Italy.
- The exposure raised concerns about covert influence over state institutions, including links alleged or investigated across politics, security services, and business.

## Public Exposure (1981)

- Discovery of P2 membership lists became a focal point for parliamentary inquiries and media reporting.
- The episode contributed to broader debates about transparency, democratic governance, and the persistence of informal networks in postwar Italy.

## Legal and Historical Aftermath

- Gelli faced various legal proceedings over time; public accounts often portray him as a central node in a wider ecosystem rather than the sole driver.
