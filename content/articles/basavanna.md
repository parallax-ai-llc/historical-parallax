## Summary

Basavanna was a 12th-century Indian philosopher, social reformer, and poet who founded the Lingayat movement, which challenged the caste system and promoted social equality. He served as prime minister of the Kalachuri kingdom and used his position to implement social reforms. He composed Vachanas (prose poems) in Kannada that addressed social justice, devotion to Shiva, and the dignity of labor. His movement attracted followers from all castes.
