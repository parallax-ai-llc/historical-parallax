## Summary

Ahmad Sirhindi was an Indian Islamic scholar and Sufi of the Naqshbandi order who is known as Mujaddid Alf-i-Thani (Renewer of the Second Millennium). He opposed Emperor Akbar's syncretic religious policies and advocated for a return to orthodox Sunni Islam. His letters (Maktubat) became a major work of Islamic theology and Sufi thought. He is considered one of the most important figures in South Asian Islamic history.
