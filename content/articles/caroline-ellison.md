## Background

Ellison studied mathematics and worked as a trader before joining the crypto trading firm Alameda Research. Alameda operated closely with FTX, the cryptocurrency exchange founded by Sam Bankman-Fried.

## Alameda Research and FTX Collapse

As Alameda’s CEO, Ellison became a central figure in reporting about the relationship between the trading firm and the exchange. After FTX’s 2022 collapse and bankruptcy, U.S. authorities filed criminal charges against multiple executives.

## Guilty Plea and Cooperation

Public court records describe Ellison pleading guilty to fraud-related charges and entering a cooperation agreement. Her cooperation included providing information to prosecutors and testimony in proceedings connected to the FTX case.

## Sentencing

She was sentenced in federal court after the conclusion of major related trials. Sentencing outcomes in such cases typically consider factors such as acceptance of responsibility, the extent of cooperation, and the scale of financial harm described in the record.

## Broader Significance

The FTX/Alameda scandal became a landmark episode in cryptocurrency regulation and enforcement, influencing public debate on exchange oversight, customer-asset segregation, and risk controls for affiliated trading entities.
