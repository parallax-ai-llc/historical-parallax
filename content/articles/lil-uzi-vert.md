## Summary

Lil Uzi Vert (born Symere Bysil Woods on July 31, 1995) is a Philadelphia-born rapper, singer, and songwriter who emerged as one of the defining voices of the SoundCloud rap and emo rap movements of the mid-2010s. Known for melodic flows, genre-blending production, and an eclectic aesthetic that fuses hip-hop with punk and rock influences, Uzi became a commercial and cultural phenomenon within a few years of releasing their first recordings.

Their 2017 breakthrough single "XO TOUR Llif3" reached number 7 on the Billboard Hot 100 and helped pioneer the emotionally raw, minor-key style that would define a generation of hip-hop. By the early 2020s, Lil Uzi Vert had accumulated multiple platinum certifications, sold-out tours, and a devoted global fanbase, cementing their status as one of the most influential artists of their era.

---

## Early Life & Background

Symere Woods was born and raised in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, growing up in the Francisville neighborhood. They attended Strawberry Mansion High School before dropping out to pursue music. Uzi has cited alternative rock artists such as Marilyn Manson, Fall Out Boy, and Paramore as formative influences alongside hip-hop acts like Wiz Khalifa and Meek Mill. This cross-genre exposure would become central to their artistic identity.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Lil Uzi Vert began gaining traction around 2014–2015 through mixtape releases including *Purple Thoughtz Vol. 1* and collaborations with Philadelphia producer Don Cannon. A co-sign from DJ Drama and Cannon's Generation Now label brought wider industry attention. The 2016 mixtape *Luv Is Rage* and the collaborative project *Lil Uzi Vert vs. the World* generated significant streaming numbers and introduced Uzi's blend of melodic hooks and rapid-fire verses to a national audience.

The 2017 single "XO TOUR Llif3" became a cultural touchstone, driven largely by organic virality on SoundCloud and social media before receiving formal radio promotion. Its success propelled the debut studio album *Luv Is Rage 2* (2017) to number 1 on the Billboard 200.

## Key Achievements

- *Luv Is Rage 2* (2017) debuted at number 1 on the Billboard 200 and was certified platinum multiple times.
- "XO TOUR Llif3" was certified 8× platinum by the RIAA.
- The album *Eternal Atake* (2020) also debuted at number 1 on the Billboard 200, selling over 290,000 album-equivalent units in its first week.
- In 2021, Uzi had a pink diamond implanted in their forehead, valued at approximately $24 million, generating global media coverage.
- Nominated for multiple BET Hip Hop Awards and MTV Video Music Awards throughout their career.

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Lil Uzi Vert has faced criticism over delayed album releases, most notably *Eternal Atake*, which was announced years before its 2020 release amid reported label disputes with Generation Now. The forehead diamond implant drew mixed reactions, with some praising it as bold self-expression and others raising concerns about the medical risks involved. Uzi publicly removed the diamond in 2023 after it was reportedly ripped out during a concert.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Lil Uzi Vert's fusion of emo sensibility, rock aesthetics, and trap production helped legitimize a new emotional register within hip-hop. Artists including Juice WRLD, Polo G, and a wave of subsequent SoundCloud-era rappers have cited Uzi's melodic approach as influential. Their gender-fluid presentation and nonconformist style also contributed to broadening conversations around identity within hip-hop culture.
