## Background

Théogène Rudasingwa is a Rwandan political figure known primarily for his roles within Rwanda's post-1994 government and for his later opposition activities from abroad. Sources describe him as having held influential positions before a public split with the ruling party.

## Defection and Opposition Activity

After leaving government service, Rudasingwa became associated with opposition organizing in exile. His public statements have included criticism of President Paul Kagame's government and allegations about responsibility for major historical events in Rwanda.

Because these claims are politically charged and disputed, accounts often distinguish between:

- what Rudasingwa has alleged in interviews or writings; and
- the official position of the Rwandan government and the views of other investigators and commentators.

## Public Profile

Rudasingwa is frequently referenced in discussions about:

- Rwandan diaspora opposition movements;
- debates over political space, security, and governance in Rwanda; and
- competing narratives about the causes and sequence of events surrounding 1994.
