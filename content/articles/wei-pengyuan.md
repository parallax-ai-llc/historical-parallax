## Summary

Former coal department deputy director found with 200 million yuan in cash weighing two tonnes in his home. Symbol of petty official corruption.

## Position and responsibilities

Wei Pengyuan served in China’s energy and planning bureaucracy, including roles connected to coal-sector policy and project approvals.

He is commonly described as having worked within the National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) system and, after the creation of the National Energy Administration (NEA), as a deputy director-level official in a coal-related department.

## The corruption case

In 2014, authorities investigated Wei for corruption-related offenses.

Media coverage highlighted the unusually large volume of cash reportedly seized from his residence, which became a widely cited example in discussions of Chinese official corruption and illicit wealth concealment.

The case was notable not only for the headline cash figure, but also for what it suggested about access to discretionary approval power in resource and infrastructure sectors.

## Sentencing and public impact

In 2016, Wei received a death sentence with reprieve (a form of suspended death sentence used in China for certain serious crimes).

His case was frequently referenced in the broader context of the Chinese Communist Party’s anti-corruption campaign and the use of exemplary punishments to signal deterrence.

## Broader context

Coal and energy project approvals have historically involved substantial capital flows, long supply chains, and interactions between state-owned enterprises, regulators, and local governments.

In that environment, corruption investigations often focus on approval bottlenecks, contracting decisions, and influence over licensing and expansion projects.
