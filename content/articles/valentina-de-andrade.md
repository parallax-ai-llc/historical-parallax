## Summary

Brazilian cult leader who founded a UFO cult and was accused of ordering the castration and murder of children in a series of ritual killings in Altamira, Brazil. She claimed children born after 1981 were evil.

## Name and identity notes

- Sources often refer to her as Valentina de Andrade; variants such as "Valentina Andrade" appear in some reporting.
- She is described as a Brazilian spiritual leader associated with UFO-oriented beliefs.

## Altamira case (overview)

- The "Altamira" case in Pará involved a series of crimes against boys, including mutilation and murder.
- Public allegations framed the crimes as ritualistic, which amplified national media attention.
- The investigation spanned many years and included multiple suspects and competing theories.

## Allegations and court outcomes

- Andrade was accused of directing or encouraging crimes connected to the Altamira killings.
- Later proceedings reported in mainstream summaries resulted in acquittal due to insufficient evidence.
- The broader case continues to be discussed in Brazil in relation to evidentiary controversy and public hysteria around "satanic" or cult claims.

## Significance

- The case is frequently cited as an example of how sensational narratives can affect investigations and trials.
- It also illustrates difficulties in resolving long-running serial cases where forensic and testimonial evidence is contested.
