## Summary

Sinaloa Cartel enforcer known as 'El Macho Prieto' who was responsible for extreme violence in Sonora and Baja California. He was killed by the Mexican military.

## Background

Gonzalo Inzunza Inzunza was reported in open sources as a senior figure linked to the Sinaloa Cartel.
He has been described as an enforcement leader who coordinated armed personnel and protected high-level cartel leadership.
His activities were associated with northern Mexico, including areas bordering the United States.

## Role and Notoriety

Accounts connected Inzunza to violent conflicts among organized-crime groups and to efforts to control trafficking routes.
In Mexican media and law-enforcement reporting, he was sometimes portrayed as a key operative for internal security and discipline.
Like many figures in Mexican organized crime, reliable public information is limited and often comes from government statements, journalism, and court filings rather than detailed biographies.

## Death

In January 2013, he was killed in a confrontation with Mexican security forces in Puerto Peñasco, Sonora.
Reports describe the incident as part of a broader military and federal campaign targeting cartel leadership and armed wings.
