## Summary

Stefan Banach was a Polish mathematician who is considered one of the founders of functional analysis. He introduced and developed the concept of Banach spaces, which became fundamental to modern mathematics and physics. He published the 'Théorie des opérations linéaires,' one of the first monographs on functional analysis. Despite having an unconventional academic career, he was one of the greatest mathematicians of the 20th century.
