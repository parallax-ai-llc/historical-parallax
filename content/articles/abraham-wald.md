## Summary

Abraham Wald developed sequential analysis during World War II for quality control testing and founded statistical decision theory. His famous analysis of aircraft survivorship bias — recommending armor for the parts of returning planes WITHOUT bullet holes — is one of the most cited examples of clear statistical thinking. He died in a plane crash.
