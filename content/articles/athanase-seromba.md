## Summary

Rwandan Catholic priest who ordered the bulldozing of his own church in Nyange with 2,000 Tutsi refugees sheltering inside during the 1994 genocide. He was convicted of genocide by the ICTR.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2002 | Arrested in Italy where he served as a parish priest |

## Background

- Seromba was a Catholic priest serving at Nyange parish in western Rwanda during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi.
- The Nyange church became a refuge for large numbers of civilians seeking protection from militia violence.

## Crimes and Legal Proceedings

- The case is notable for the allegation that a place of sanctuary was intentionally destroyed to kill those sheltering inside.
- He was prosecuted by the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR), including findings related to ordering or facilitating mass killings.
- His appeal outcome is frequently cited in discussions of individual criminal liability for ordering actions carried out by others.

## Significance

- The conviction is often referenced in broader debates about the role of clergy and institutions during mass atrocities.
- It also illustrates how international tribunals pursued cases years after the events, including arrests outside Rwanda.

## Aftermath

- The Nyange episode remains a stark symbol of the genocide and the failure of protective institutions.
