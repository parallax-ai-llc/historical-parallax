## Summary

Burglary of the Hatton Garden Safe Deposit Company in London over Easter weekend 2015. A gang of elderly career criminals stole 14 million pounds in jewels and cash.

## Method and execution (high level)

The burglary took place during a long holiday weekend, exploiting reduced staffing and the expectation that the premises would be quiet.
Reports describe a method involving forced entry into the building and drilling/cutting through security barriers to access the vault area.
The operation became notable not only for the scale of theft but also for the level of planning and the time spent on-site.

## Investigation and legal outcomes

The case received sustained media coverage and led to arrests and prosecutions of multiple participants.
Court proceedings focused on the planning, the roles played by different individuals, and the valuation and recovery of stolen property.

## Why the event is remembered

- It was one of the most high-profile UK burglaries of the 2010s.
- The participants were widely characterized as experienced, older career criminals.
- It became a reference point in discussions of physical security, insurance exposure, and investigative techniques.
