## Summary

Dutch far-right politician Pim Fortuyn was shot dead by animal rights activist Volkert van der Graaf. First political assassination in the Netherlands in centuries.

## Context

- **Pim Fortuyn** was a Dutch politician and public intellectual who rose rapidly in early 2002 on an anti-immigration, anti-establishment platform.
- His rhetoric and media profile made him one of the most polarizing figures in Dutch politics at the time.
- The killing drew intense attention because political assassinations are rare in modern Dutch history.

## Key Facts

- **Date**: 6 May 2002 (nine days before the 2002 Dutch general election).
- **Location**: Hilversum, Netherlands (after a radio interview).
- **Victim**: Pim Fortuyn.
- **Perpetrator**: Volkert van der Graaf, described as an animal-rights/environmental activist.
- **Basic motive (as reported in public sources)**: Van der Graaf said he acted to stop Fortuyn from “scapegoating” vulnerable groups and targeting Muslims.

## Investigation and Legal Aftermath

- Van der Graaf was arrested shortly after the shooting.
- He was convicted of murder in the Netherlands and received a prison sentence (reported as **18 years**).
- After serving a substantial portion of the sentence, he was released under conditions and supervision.

## Political Impact

- The assassination occurred during the peak of Fortuyn’s electoral momentum and reshaped the campaign atmosphere.
- Fortuyn’s party (LPF) achieved a major electoral breakthrough in the 2002 election shortly after his death.
- The event is widely cited as a turning point in Dutch political culture, accelerating debates on immigration, integration, and the boundaries of political speech.

## Legacy

- The assassination is frequently referenced in later discussions of political violence in the Netherlands.
- It is commonly compared (in Dutch public discourse) with later high-profile murders, including the 2004 killing of filmmaker Theo van Gogh.
