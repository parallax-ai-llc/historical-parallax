## Summary

Theodore Maiman built the first working laser on May 16, 1960, using a synthetic ruby crystal. His invention transformed science, medicine, industry, and daily life. Laser technology is now used in everything from fiber-optic communications to surgery to barcode scanners. Despite its enormous impact, he never received a Nobel Prize.
