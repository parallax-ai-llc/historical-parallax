## Role in the Cali Cartel

- Santacruz Londoño, known as “Chepe,” was identified by authorities and journalists as one of the principal leaders of the Cali Cartel, a Colombian drug-trafficking organization that rivaled the Medellín Cartel.
- The Cali Cartel was often characterized as more networked and businesslike in structure, with emphasis on corruption, logistics, and money movement.

## Pursuit by Colombian authorities

- During the early-to-mid 1990s, Colombian law enforcement increased pressure on major trafficking organizations through specialized units, intelligence cooperation, and extradition-related policies.
- Senior Cali Cartel figures faced arrest campaigns that combined policing operations with political and institutional reforms.

## Escape and death

- Santacruz Londoño escaped from custody in the mid-1990s.
- He was later tracked and killed by police, an endpoint frequently cited as part of Colombia’s broader effort to dismantle top cartel leadership during that period.

## Historical significance

- The fall of the Cali Cartel leadership is often discussed as a turning point that did not end trafficking, but contributed to the emergence of smaller, more fragmented criminal networks.
- The period is also used as a case study in the limits of “kingpin” strategies when illicit markets adapt rapidly to enforcement pressure.

## Notes on sources

- Accounts of internal cartel roles can vary; summaries typically reflect a combination of court records, government reporting, and investigative journalism.
