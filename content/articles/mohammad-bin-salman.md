## Summary

Saudi Crown Prince who US intelligence concluded approved the operation that murdered journalist Jamal Khashoggi in the Saudi consulate in Istanbul in 2018. He has consolidated power and detained rivals while pursuing economic reforms.

## Role and Position

- Mohammad bin Salman (often referred to as **MBS**) is the Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia and a central figure in the country’s contemporary governance.
- He has been associated with major policy initiatives and high-profile political decisions, as well as significant international controversies.

## Power Consolidation and Governance

- Reporting around his rise to dominance frequently emphasizes the concentration of decision-making authority in the Crown Prince’s office.
- Domestic anti-corruption measures and detentions of prominent figures have been portrayed by supporters as reforms and by critics as power consolidation.

## International Relations and Controversies

- The 2018 killing of journalist Jamal Khashoggi generated sustained international scrutiny and diplomatic consequences.
- US assessments reported in public discourse have stated that senior Saudi leadership approved the operation.

## Economic and Social Policy (High-level)

- Publicly promoted economic reform agenda often associated with “Vision 2030.”
- Efforts have included modernization programs and investment initiatives intended to diversify the economy beyond oil.
