## Summary

Helena Petrovna Blavatsky was a Russian occultist and author who co-founded the Theosophical Society in 1875. Her magnum opus The Secret Doctrine presented a synthesis of science, religion, and philosophy drawing on Eastern and Western esoteric traditions. She introduced concepts from Hinduism and Buddhism to Western audiences and influenced the New Age movement. She was a controversial figure, accused of fraud but also admired as a spiritual pioneer.
