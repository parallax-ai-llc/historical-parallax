## Summary

Maurice Wilkins was a New Zealand-born British physicist and molecular biologist who shared the 1962 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his contributions to the discovery of the DNA structure. His X-ray diffraction work at King's College London, along with Rosalind Franklin's, provided crucial experimental evidence for the double helix model. He later became a prominent advocate for the social responsibility of scientists.
