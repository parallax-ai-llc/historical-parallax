## Summary
Gonzalo Rodríguez Gacha, known as 'El Mexicano,' was a Colombian drug lord and leading member of the Medellín Cartel. He was one of the wealthiest and most violent drug traffickers in Colombian history, controlling emerald mining and cocaine trafficking routes. He was killed in a police operation in 1989.

## Early Life
Born May 14, 1947, in Pacho, Cundinamarca, Colombia. He came from humble origins and was originally involved in the emerald trade before moving into cocaine. He earned his nickname 'El Mexicano' for his love of Mexican culture. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Rodríguez Gacha was a key member of the Medellín Cartel alongside Pablo Escobar and the Ochoa brothers. He controlled cocaine trafficking routes through Central America and Mexico. He also maintained control of Colombia's emerald trade through extreme violence. He funded right-wing paramilitary groups. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
On December 15, 1989, Colombian police tracked Rodríguez Gacha and his son to a ranch in Tolú, on Colombia's Caribbean coast. In the ensuing operation, both were killed by police gunfire. His death was considered a major victory in the war against the Medellín Cartel. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His death weakened the Medellín Cartel significantly [^4]
- His case demonstrated that powerful drug lords could be brought down
- Led to increased confidence in Colombia's security forces

## Negative Perspectives
- One of the most violent members of the Medellín Cartel [^5]
- Funded paramilitary groups responsible for massacres
- Used extreme violence to control the emerald trade
- His drug trafficking fueled addiction crises
- Responsible for numerous assassinations of judges and officials
