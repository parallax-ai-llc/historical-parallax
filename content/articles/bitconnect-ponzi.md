## Summary

BitConnect cryptocurrency lending platform was revealed as a Ponzi scheme that defrauded investors of $2.4 billion. Founder Satish Kumbhani was indicted but disappeared.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2018 | BitConnect collapsed as Ponzi scheme was exposed |
| 2022 | Founder Satish Kumbhani indicted by US but disappeared |

## How the Scheme Was Marketed (High Level)

- BitConnect promoted an “investment” product framed as crypto lending with **unusually high, steady returns**.
- Marketing relied heavily on **referral incentives** and aggressive social-media promotion, a common feature of multi-level growth dynamics.

## Why It Collapsed

- When confidence and inflows fell, the structure could not sustain promised payouts—one of the core failure modes of Ponzi-like designs.
- Regulatory warnings and exchange delistings contributed to the loss of access and liquidity.

## Aftermath

- Large investor losses and civil litigation.
- Criminal and regulatory actions focusing on fraud, unregistered securities offerings, and money movement.
- The case is widely cited as a cautionary example in crypto policy debates and investor education.

## Practical Takeaways

- Promises of consistent, outsized returns with vague strategy explanations are a common fraud indicator.
- Referral-heavy growth can mask the underlying absence of sustainable revenue.
- The collapse remains a canonical example used by regulators and educators to explain crypto-related fraud.

- The social dynamics of hype, community pressure, and fear-of-missing-out are central to how similar schemes spread.
