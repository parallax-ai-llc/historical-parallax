## Summary

Norbert Wiener was an American mathematician who founded the field of cybernetics, the study of control and communication in machines and living organisms. A child prodigy who entered college at 11 and earned a PhD at 18, he made important contributions to stochastic processes, information theory, and control theory. His ideas influenced computer science, engineering, biology, and social science.
