## Summary

Royal conspirators including Queen Tiye who plotted the assassination of Pharaoh Ramesses III.

## Historical Context

- **Dynasty / period:** Late New Kingdom, **20th Dynasty** of Ancient Egypt.
- **Target:** **Ramesses III**, a major ruler of the 20th Dynasty, associated with major building programs and documented administrative records.
- The plot is often discussed under the broader "**harem conspiracy**" narratives in which factions within the royal household attempted to change the line of succession.

## What the Conspiracy Refers To

- A court plot attributed to **Queen Tiye** (a secondary wife, distinct from the earlier 18th-dynasty Great Royal Wife Tiye).
- The alleged objective was the **assassination of Ramesses III** and the elevation of a preferred heir.
- Modern summaries typically treat the episode as a named conspiracy case within records describing a judicial response.

## Key Figures (as commonly summarized)

- **Queen Tiye:** Presented as a leading organizer of the plot.
- **Ramesses III:** Pharaoh and alleged target.
- **Court officials and household members:** Often described as a network of participants who supported the attempt through influence and coordination.

## Outcome and Aftermath

- The conspirators are commonly reported as **tried and punished**, with Tiye described as **executed**.
- The episode is cited as an example of how internal palace politics could destabilize succession.
- The narrative survives largely through later summaries and modern secondary references rather than a single, complete contemporary narrative.

## Notes on Evidence

- Many popular retellings compress complex source material; details (exact roles, methods, and the extent of harm to the pharaoh) can vary by reconstruction.
- When writing about the event, it is useful to separate:
  - what is **explicitly claimed** in summarized accounts,
  - what is **inferred** about factional politics,
  - and what remains **uncertain** due to fragmentary or interpretive evidence.
