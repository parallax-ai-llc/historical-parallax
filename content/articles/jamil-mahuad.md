## Summary

Former President of Ecuador convicted of embezzlement for mishandling the 1999 banking crisis. His decisions led to dollarization and massive economic losses for Ecuadorians.

## Political Context

- Mahuad served as president during a period of acute financial and political instability in Ecuador, with banking-sector fragility and currency pressures.
- The late-1990s crisis is often discussed alongside regional shocks and domestic policy constraints, including limited confidence in the sucre.

## Banking Crisis and Dollarization (High Level)

- Policy measures during the crisis involved emergency banking actions and attempts to stabilize the financial system.
- Ecuador's move toward dollarization is generally framed as a drastic stabilization strategy intended to arrest inflation and restore monetary credibility.
- Dollarization can reduce currency risk and inflation volatility, but also limits a country's ability to use independent monetary policy in downturns.

## Removal from Office and Legal Aftermath

- Mahuad was removed from power in 2000 amid mass protests and a breakdown of political support.
- Subsequent legal proceedings and convictions related to the crisis are part of how his presidency is remembered and debated in Ecuadorian public life.

## Legacy

- Supporters and critics differ on whether dollarization was an unavoidable emergency response or a costly policy choice with uneven social impact.
- The episode remains a reference point in Ecuadorian debates over financial regulation, crisis management, and political accountability.
