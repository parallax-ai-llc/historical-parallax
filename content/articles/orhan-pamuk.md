## Summary

Orhan Pamuk is a Turkish novelist who received the Nobel Prize in Literature in 2006. His novels including My Name Is Red, Snow, and The Museum of Innocence explore the tension between East and West, tradition and modernity in Turkish identity. He also founded the Museum of Innocence in Istanbul, based on his novel.
