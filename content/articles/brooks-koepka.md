## Summary

Brooks Koepka is an American professional golfer born on May 3, 1990, in West Palm Beach, Florida. He is widely regarded as one of the most dominant major championship performers of his generation, having won five major titles across the U.S. Open, PGA Championship, and The Open Championship. His ability to elevate his game under the pressure of golf's biggest events has cemented his reputation as a clutch competitor.

Koepka's career trajectory is defined by his fearless approach to majors and an unapologetic, confidence-driven personality. After honing his game on the European Challenge Tour, he broke through on the PGA Tour with back-to-back U.S. Open victories in 2017 and 2018, becoming only the sixth player in history to accomplish that feat. He later became one of the few golfers to hold two different major titles simultaneously.

His decision in 2023 to join LIV Golf — the Saudi-backed breakaway tour — added a controversial chapter to his career, yet he punctuated that transition by winning the 2023 PGA Championship, demonstrating that his major championship form remained intact regardless of the political landscape surrounding professional golf.

## Early Life & Background

Brooks Koepka was born in West Palm Beach, Florida, and grew up in a sports-oriented household. He attended Florida State University, where he played college golf but left after his junior year in 2012 to pursue a professional career in Europe. Rather than attempting to earn his PGA Tour card immediately, he spent several years on the Challenge Tour and European Tour, competing in countries including Turkey, Russia, and Kazakhstan — an unconventional path that he credits with building the mental toughness and course management skills that define his major championship play.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Koepka earned his PGA Tour card for the 2014–15 season and quickly signaled his potential by winning the Turkish Airlines Open in 2014. His breakout came at the 2017 U.S. Open at Erin Hills, where he shot a 16-under-par total to claim his first major. He defended the title in 2018 at Shinnecock Hills, joining an elite list of back-to-back U.S. Open champions. He added PGA Championship titles in 2018 at Bellerive and 2019 at Bethpage Black, becoming the first player in the modern era to win the same major in consecutive years twice.

## Key Achievements

- **5 major championships**: U.S. Open (2017, 2018), PGA Championship (2018, 2019, 2023)
- First player to hold back-to-back titles in two different majors simultaneously (2018–19)
- Reached World No. 1 in the Official World Golf Ranking in 2018, a position he held for 47 weeks
- Runner-up at the 2019 Masters and 2023 Masters, demonstrating consistent major contention
- Member of three U.S. Ryder Cup teams (2016, 2018, 2023)

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Koepka's 2023 move to LIV Golf was one of the most high-profile defections from the PGA Tour, drawing criticism from fellow players and administrators who viewed such moves as destabilizing to professional golf's structure. His public spat with Dustin Johnson and his candid media persona — including a viral stare-down with Rory McIlroy during a 2023 Masters broadcast — generated tabloid attention. Some critics have noted that outside of majors, his PGA Tour win total (8 wins) appears modest relative to his ranking peaks.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Koepka has reshaped how golfers approach major championships mentally, popularizing the notion of deliberately peaking for the four biggest events rather than grinding for consistency throughout the season. His success has influenced a generation of players to prioritize major preparation over weekly results. Regardless of the ongoing PGA Tour–LIV Golf dispute, his five major titles place him among the game's all-time elite.
