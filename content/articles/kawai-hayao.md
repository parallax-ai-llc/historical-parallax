## Summary

Kawai Hayao was a Japanese clinical psychologist and cultural figure who introduced Jungian psychology to Japan. While primarily known as a psychologist, his work deeply explored the intersection of Japanese spirituality, Buddhism, and psychology. He was the first Japanese Jungian analyst and served as Commissioner of the Agency for Cultural Affairs. His books on Japanese mythology and the Japanese psyche bridged psychology and spirituality.
