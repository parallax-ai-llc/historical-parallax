## Background

Thomas Webster is a former New York City Police Department (NYPD) officer. Public reporting about him in connection with the January 6, 2021 attack on the U.S. Capitol has emphasized his prior law-enforcement career and subsequent federal prosecution.

## January 6, 2021 incident

According to court filings and contemporaneous video evidence cited in coverage, Webster was involved in a confrontation outside the U.S. Capitol during the riot. He was accused of using a metal flagpole to assault a U.S. Capitol Police officer during the clash.

## Legal proceedings

Federal prosecutors charged Webster in relation to the incident. A jury later convicted him of assaulting a federal officer and other offenses connected to the attack. Sentencing and related motions are part of the public record in the federal case.

## Significance

The Webster case is frequently referenced in summaries of January 6 prosecutions because it involved a former police officer and an assault on a U.S. Capitol Police officer. It is one of many cases used to illustrate the range of participants and conduct prosecuted after the riot.
