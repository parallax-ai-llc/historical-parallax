## Summary

Britain's richest criminal who laundered Brink's-Mat gold through a smelting operation. Shot dead in his garden in what was initially mistaken for natural death.

## Additional Context

## Brink's-Mat and Gold Laundering

Palmer is best known in connection with the 1983 Brink's-Mat robbery aftermath. According to widely reported accounts, some of the stolen gold was melted down and introduced into legitimate markets, helping obscure its origin. Palmer’s name became closely linked to this laundering pathway.

## Later Criminal Activity and Convictions

After becoming a prominent figure in British criminal reporting, Palmer was also associated with fraud cases later in life, including timeshare-related scams that targeted victims abroad. He faced investigations and legal proceedings connected to these activities, and his case became a reference point in discussions of cross-border fraud and enforcement.

## Death

Palmer was shot at his home in 2015. Media coverage noted that the initial reporting of his death was confused, with later confirmation that he had been murdered.
