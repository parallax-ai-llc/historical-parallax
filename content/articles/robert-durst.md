## Summary

New York real estate heir convicted of murder who was linked to three killings. Famously muttered 'Killed them all, of course' on a hot mic during the HBO documentary 'The Jinx.'

## Background

Robert Durst was a New York real-estate heir connected to the Durst family business. His public notoriety largely stemmed from criminal investigations spanning decades and multiple jurisdictions.[^1]

## Cases commonly associated with Durst

Accounts of Durst frequently reference three deaths in particular:[^1]

- The disappearance of his first wife, Kathleen McCormack Durst
- The killing of his friend Susan Berman
- The killing and dismemberment of his neighbor Morris Black in Texas

The legal status and evidentiary record differ across these cases; summaries often emphasize the unusual scope and longevity of the investigations.[^1]

## Media and public attention

Durst became the subject of extensive media coverage, including the HBO documentary series **The Jinx**, which helped renew public interest and coincided with major legal developments.[^1]

## Conviction and later years

Durst was convicted of murder in California (2021). He later died in custody in 2022.[^1]

## Why the story is notable

The Durst cases are often cited in discussions of:

- How wealth and access to high-quality legal defense can affect the pace and trajectory of investigations
- The role of long-form documentary media in shaping public narratives around criminal cases
- Multi-state investigations and the challenges of cold-case resolution
