## Summary

Colombian drug lord who headed the Cifuentes Villa drug trafficking organization. He was a key supplier to El Chapo's Sinaloa Cartel. He became a cooperating witness for the US government.

## Trafficking Network Context

- Large trafficking organizations often operate as networks rather than strict hierarchies, using brokers, transport coordinators, and financial facilitators to move cocaine across multiple jurisdictions.
- The Sinaloa Cartel has been widely described as a major Mexican trafficking organization with international supply relationships, including with Colombian sources.
- Supplier relationships in transnational trafficking commonly involve negotiated quantities, pricing, credit arrangements, and shared risk management against interdiction.

## Cooperating Witness (General Explanation)

- In U.S. federal prosecutions, a "cooperating witness" is a defendant or detainee who provides information and testimony in exchange for potential consideration at sentencing.
- Cooperation can include debriefings, identification of associates, explanation of operational methods, and sworn testimony in court.
- Such testimony is typically corroborated with independent evidence (communications records, seizures, surveillance, and other witness accounts).

## Extradition and Prosecution Notes

- Extradition transfers a suspect from one country to another to face charges, and can be triggered by an arrest abroad followed by court and diplomatic processes.
- High-profile trafficking cases often proceed in U.S. federal courts, where defendants may face conspiracy-based indictments covering long time spans and multiple acts.
