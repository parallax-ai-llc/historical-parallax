## Summary

Sudanese defense minister indicted by the ICC for crimes against humanity and war crimes in Darfur. He directed military operations against civilians.

## Additional Context

### Position within the al-Bashir government

Abdel Raheem Muhammad Hussein was a senior figure in the government of President Omar al-Bashir, holding key security posts that tied him closely to Sudan’s internal security apparatus and military decision-making. In such roles, he was associated with policies and operations carried out during periods of armed conflict and political repression.

### Darfur allegations and international justice

The International Criminal Court (ICC) has alleged that Hussein bears responsibility for serious crimes committed during the Darfur conflict, including crimes against humanity and war crimes. ICC proceedings in such cases commonly focus on:

- Command responsibility and decision-making authority
- Coordination between military, police, and allied forces
- Targeting patterns and the treatment of civilian populations

### Post-2019 custody and accountability debates

After the 2019 uprising that removed al-Bashir, Hussein was reported to have been arrested. His case became part of wider domestic and international debates over whether former regime officials should be prosecuted in Sudan, transferred to international jurisdictions, or handled through hybrid accountability mechanisms.

### Broader significance

Hussein’s profile is often referenced in discussions of how senior officials can become implicated in mass-atrocity allegations when they oversee or coordinate security operations during internal armed conflicts.
