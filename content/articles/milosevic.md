## Background

- **Full name**: Slobodan Milošević (commonly rendered without diacritics as "Milosevic").
- **Political setting**: Late Cold War and post–Cold War breakup of the Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia, marked by competing national projects, economic decline, and institutional collapse.

## Political Rise and Rule

- Built power through party structures in Serbia and by leveraging mass politics.
- Emphasized Serbian national grievances, including issues in Kosovo, as a mobilizing theme.
- Maintained tight control over state media and security institutions, while opponents and independent outlets faced pressure.

## Role in the Yugoslav Conflicts (High-Level)

- Milosevic is widely regarded as a principal political figure in the regional crises that accompanied Yugoslavia’s dissolution.
- Multiple armed conflicts (Croatia, Bosnia, and Kosovo) involved complex alliances and local command structures; however, Serbian state influence and support networks were central to several Serb military and political projects.
- The period is associated with major war crimes and crimes against humanity committed by multiple parties; the most severe episodes included systematic campaigns of displacement and ethnic cleansing.

## ICTY Indictment and Trial

- The ICTY charged Milosevic with crimes connected to Kosovo, and later expanded allegations to Croatia and Bosnia-Herzegovina.
- His case became one of the most prominent international criminal proceedings of the era.
- The trial ended without a final judgment due to his death, leaving legal responsibility unresolved in court while historical and political assessments remained sharply contested.

## Legacy

- Seen by supporters as a nationalist leader who resisted foreign pressure; seen by critics as an authoritarian responsible for repression and for enabling or directing policies that fueled conflict and atrocity.
- The aftermath of his rule contributed to Serbia’s political transition and to ongoing debates over accountability, national identity, and the interpretation of the 1990s wars.
