## Summary

Mary Lou Retton was the first American woman to win the Olympic all-around gymnastics gold, scoring a perfect 10 on vault at the 1984 LA Games.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1984 | Won all-around gold at LA Olympics with perfect 10 |
| 1984 | Won a total of five medals at the 1984 Summer Olympics (1 gold, 2 silver, 2 bronze) |
| 1984 | Became the first American woman to win Olympic gymnastics all-around gold |
| 1997 | Inducted into the International Gymnastics Hall of Fame |

## Early Life and Training

- Born in Fairmont, West Virginia, and rose through the U.S. junior elite gymnastics ranks.
- Trained under coach Béla Károlyi in Houston, Texas, after relocating for higher-level coaching.

## 1984 Los Angeles Olympics (Key Highlights)

- **All-around gold:** Won the women’s individual all-around, a landmark result for U.S. women’s gymnastics.
- **Perfect 10 moment:** Scored a perfect 10 on vault during the all-around competition, an iconic broadcast highlight of the 1984 Games.
- **Event medals:** In addition to all-around gold, she earned medals across team and apparatus events, finishing the Games with five total medals.

## Recognition and Legacy

- Widely credited with expanding mainstream U.S. interest in women’s artistic gymnastics during the 1980s.
- Became a prominent sports figure in American popular culture through major endorsements and media appearances.
- Remembered for combining power gymnastics with consistency in a high-pressure Olympic final.

## Later Life

- After retiring from elite competition, remained active in public life through speaking, television work, and gymnastics-related appearances.
- In 2023, she was hospitalized with a serious form of pneumonia and later recovered.
