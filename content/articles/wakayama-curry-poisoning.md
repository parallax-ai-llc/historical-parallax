## Summary

Masumi Hayashi poisoned a community curry pot with arsenic at a summer festival in Wakayama, killing 4 and sickening 63. She maintains her innocence on death row.

## Background and Context

- The incident occurred during a local community summer festival where curry rice was served from a shared pot.
- The case is widely discussed in Japan as a major instance of mass poisoning involving arsenic.

## Incident Overview (High Level)

- Arsenic was introduced into the curry served to attendees.
- The immediate outcome reported in major summaries is multiple fatalities and dozens of people becoming ill.
- The event triggered a large-scale police investigation because the poisoning affected a broad cross-section of the community.

## Investigation and Trial (High Level)

- Masumi Hayashi was identified by prosecutors as the primary suspect and was later convicted.
- The case proceeded through Japan’s criminal courts and resulted in a death sentence.
- Public reporting commonly notes that Hayashi has maintained her innocence while incarcerated.

## Aftermath and Public Significance

- The incident influenced public awareness and media discussion around food safety at public events.
- It is frequently cited in conversations about criminal investigations that rely heavily on circumstantial evidence and forensic interpretation.

## Key Entities

- **Location:** Wakayama Prefecture, Japan.
- **Event setting:** A neighborhood festival meal served to many participants.
- **Substance:** Arsenic.
