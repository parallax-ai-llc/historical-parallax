## Background and aliases

Ignacio Coronel Villarreal, widely referred to in press accounts as "Nacho Coronel," was identified by Mexican and U.S. authorities as a senior figure within the Sinaloa Cartel network. Narratives about his career often focus on his alleged role in synthetic drug trafficking.

## Role in trafficking networks (high-level)

Reporting and law-enforcement accounts have associated Coronel with:

- methamphetamine production and distribution routes
- coordination with regional cells in western Mexico (including Jalisco)
- financial and logistical support for cartel operations

Because cartel structures are fluid and contested, precise hierarchies and responsibilities can differ across sources.

## Government pursuit and security context

Coronel's rise is frequently discussed alongside Mexico's broader escalation of anti-cartel operations in the 2000s. The period saw intensified deployments, high-profile arrests, and frequent violence affecting civilians and local institutions.

## Death in 2010

He was killed during a security operation in 2010. Public reporting generally describes the event as a raid that resulted in an exchange of gunfire, though details may vary by outlet and official statement.

## Broader significance

Analysts often cite Coronel as an example of how synthetic drugs (especially methamphetamine) became central to organized crime revenue streams, shaping both cartel competition and law-enforcement priorities.
