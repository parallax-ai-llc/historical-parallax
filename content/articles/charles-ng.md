## Summary

Hong Kong-born serial killer who, with Leonard Lake, kidnapped, tortured, and murdered between 11 and 25 people at a cabin in Northern California between 1983 and 1985. Sentenced to death in California.

## Additional Context

### Crimes and partnership with Leonard Lake

- Ng and Leonard Lake committed a series of abductions, sexual assaults, torture, and murders in California. The crimes were linked to a property/cabin complex where victims were held.
- In many summaries of the case, Lake is described as the primary planner, with Ng participating as an accomplice and co-perpetrator; the investigation drew on physical evidence recovered from the property.

### Capture, extradition, and trial

- After Lake’s death in 1985 (suicide following his arrest), Ng fled and was later detained in Canada.
- Extradition proceedings and pretrial litigation contributed to a long delay before he was tried in California.

### Sentencing and appeals

- Ng was convicted of multiple murders and sentenced to death in California.
- Like many capital cases, the sentence has been subject to extended appellate review and post-conviction litigation over many years.

### Public attention

- The case has been the subject of extensive true-crime coverage, often focusing on the scale of the crimes, the rarity of a prolonged extradition fight, and controversies surrounding evidence and court proceedings.
