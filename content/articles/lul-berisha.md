## Operating context (high-level)

Albania and the wider Western Balkans have been repeatedly cited in European law-enforcement and policy reporting as a transit and distribution corridor for illicit drugs into EU markets. In that context, individuals described as “drug lords” are typically associated with:

- **Cross-border logistics**: coordinating movement of narcotics by land and sea, using layered transport steps to reduce interdiction risk.
- **Brokerage roles**: connecting producers, wholesalers, transport crews, and distributors across multiple jurisdictions.
- **Compartmentalization**: limiting exposure by separating procurement, transport, storage, and street-level distribution cells.

## Commodities and routes (general)

Trafficking networks in the region have been linked (in general reporting) to commodities such as:

- **Cannabis products** (cultivation and bulk movement)
- **Heroin** (transit toward Western/Central Europe)
- **Cocaine** (importation into Europe, then secondary distribution)

Common route characteristics (general, non-person-specific):

- **Maritime legs** across the Adriatic/Ionian Seas
- **Overland corridors** through neighboring Balkan states into EU entry points
- **Use of logistics cover** (legitimate cargo, passenger vehicles, small craft)

## Financial and organizational patterns (general)

At a high level, major trafficking enterprises often rely on:

- **Cash-intensive laundering** via retail, hospitality, construction, or trade businesses
- **Trade-based money laundering** (mis-invoicing, over/under-shipment)
- **Use of intermediaries** and shell entities to distance leadership from transactions
- **International linkages** with suppliers and distribution partners beyond the home country

## Law-enforcement exposure (general)

For figures alleged to sit near the top of a trafficking network, common legal pressure points include:

- Conspiracy/organized-crime statutes
- Asset seizure and unexplained-wealth investigations
- Extradition requests and mutual legal assistance between states
- Long-running investigations where evidence is assembled across multiple jurisdictions
