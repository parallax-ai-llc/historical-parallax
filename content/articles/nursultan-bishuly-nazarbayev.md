## Summary

Kazakhstan's founding president who amassed enormous wealth while ruling for 29 years.

## Background

- Nazarbayev rose through the Soviet-era political system in Kazakhstan and became the republic's top Communist Party leader before the USSR collapsed.
- After independence, he consolidated executive authority and presided over a state built around a strong presidency, a managed political system, and a security-centered apparatus.

## Presidency (high-level)

- **Political system:** Elections and institutions existed, but international observers and rights groups frequently characterized the environment as heavily constrained for opposition parties, independent media, and civil society.
- **Economic model:** The government pursued market reforms while maintaining significant state influence over strategic sectors, particularly hydrocarbons and mining.
- **Capital relocation:** The move of the capital from Almaty to Astana (formerly Akmola) became a signature nation-building project.

## Power, patronage, and allegations

- Nazarbayev and his circle were repeatedly associated with patronage networks and allegations of corruption and illicit enrichment; the scale and specifics have been contested, but the issue has been a persistent theme in reporting and political debate.
- Family members and close associates have been reported to hold prominent positions in politics and business during his tenure.

## Foreign policy posture

- Often described as pursuing a "multi-vector" foreign policy: balancing ties with Russia, China, the United States, and other partners.
- Kazakhstan under Nazarbayev played roles in regional diplomacy and hosted or supported international initiatives, while prioritizing regime stability and strategic autonomy.

## Succession and after resignation

- In 2019, he resigned the presidency but initially retained major influence through institutional roles and informal authority.
- Later political developments, including unrest in 2022 and subsequent reforms, were widely interpreted as reducing the Nazarbayev-era power structure.
