## Summary

Turkish cryptocurrency exchange whose CEO Faruk Fatih Özer fled the country with $2 billion in customer funds. Caught in Albania after being on Interpol's red notice.

## Company overview

Thodex operated as a cryptocurrency exchange serving retail users in Turkey during a period of rapid domestic interest in cryptoassets. Like other exchanges, it handled customer deposits and facilitated trading between cryptocurrencies and fiat-linked balances, creating concentrated custodial risk if internal controls failed.

## Alleged fraud and shutdown

In April 2021, Thodex abruptly halted operations, and reports emerged that users could not withdraw funds. Authorities and media alleged that the exchange’s leadership had misappropriated customer assets, framing the incident as a large-scale fraud rather than a typical liquidity crisis.

The headline figure reported in some accounts—billions of dollars in losses—varied across sources and was debated, reflecting the difficulty of valuing crypto holdings across time and the limited transparency typical of private exchanges.

## Flight, international pursuit, and extradition

Faruk Fatih Özer left Turkey amid the collapse and became the subject of international law-enforcement notices. He was later detained in Albania and extradited to Turkey, illustrating the cross-border nature of crypto-related cases and the role of international cooperation when suspects flee jurisdictions.

## Legal proceedings and sentencing

Turkish courts pursued charges against Özer and others connected to the exchange. Public reporting described an extremely large cumulative prison sentence issued in 2023, a feature sometimes seen in Turkish financial-crime cases where multiple counts are aggregated.

## Significance

The Thodex case is frequently cited in discussions about:

- the risks of centralized custody in crypto markets,
- the need for proof-of-reserves or independent audits,
- consumer protection and licensing regimes for exchanges,
- and how sudden shutdowns can cascade into broader loss of trust in retail trading platforms.
