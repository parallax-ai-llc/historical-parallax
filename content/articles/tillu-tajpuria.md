## Summary

Tillu Tajpuria (an Indian gang figure associated with organized crime in Delhi) was killed in 2023 while in custody at Tihar Jail. His death was widely reported as part of a broader pattern of violent rivalry among gangs operating in and around Delhi, including retaliatory attacks and conflicts that continued even after incarceration.

## Background

The name “Tillu Tajpuria” is most commonly referenced in connection with Delhi’s gang ecosystem, where groups form around neighborhoods, personal rivalries, and alliances that shift over time. These networks have been linked in reporting to extortion, violent assaults, and other serious crimes, with frequent claims and counter-claims about responsibility.

## Reported Criminal Allegations

Open-source reporting (including court and police-related coverage) has described allegations against Tajpuria that included participation in gang-related violence and involvement in organized-crime activity. Specific charges, case outcomes, and the reliability of individual claims can vary across sources; many details are discussed in the context of ongoing investigations and court proceedings.

## Incarceration and Death in Custody

Tihar Jail is one of India’s largest prison complexes and has periodically faced public debate over overcrowding, prisoner classification, and the risk of violence among inmates. In Tajpuria’s case, his death was reported as the result of an attack by rival inmates, and it triggered:

- Internal reviews of security arrangements and inmate supervision.
- Public discussion of how high-risk prisoners are separated and monitored.
- Calls for accountability where lapses in jail management are alleged.

## Aftermath

Media coverage following the killing emphasized the continuing cycle of gang rivalry and retaliation. Authorities typically respond in such cases through a mix of disciplinary action inside prisons, transfers of inmates, and additional charges connected to conspiracy or custodial violence, depending on the investigation’s findings.
