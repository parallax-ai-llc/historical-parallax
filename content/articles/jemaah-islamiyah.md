## Summary

Southeast Asian militant Islamist group responsible for the 2002 Bali bombings and multiple other attacks. It aimed to establish an Islamic caliphate across Southeast Asia.

## Origins

Jemaah Islamiyah (often abbreviated JI) emerged out of networks that developed across Indonesia and neighboring countries.
It is commonly described as having roots in earlier Islamist movements and veteran militant communities.
The organization benefited from personal ties, shared schooling or religious-study environments, and cross-border mobility.

## Ideology and stated goals

Open sources describe JI as seeking a political-religious order aligned with its interpretation of Islam.
In practical terms, this translated into an ambition for a regional “caliphate” spanning parts of Southeast Asia.
Like other extremist movements, the group has used a mixture of propaganda, recruitment, and violent action to pursue its objectives.

## Notable attacks and public impact

JI is most closely associated in the public mind with the 2002 Bali bombings.
That attack killed 202 people and drew intense international attention to the threat of transnational terrorism in the region.
The broader pattern of attacks attributed to JI contributed to fear, political pressure for security reforms, and long-running trauma for affected communities.

## Organization and networks (high level)

Descriptions of JI emphasize that it has operated through:

- Cells that can compartmentalize planning and membership
- Personal networks that cross national borders
- Training and indoctrination pipelines
- Links (formal or informal) to other extremist organizations in the wider jihadist ecosystem

The exact structure has shifted over time in response to arrests, internal disputes, and changes in the security environment.

## State response and counterterrorism

Following major attacks, Indonesian and regional authorities expanded counterterrorism investigations and prosecutions.
A sustained campaign of arrests and trials reduced the group’s operational capacity.
Some high-profile figures were convicted; some were executed, while others received prison sentences.

## Evolution and current status

JI’s activity has not been static.
Analysts often describe phases of disruption, regrouping, and adaptation as leadership changed and networks were dismantled.
Assessments of current capability vary by period, but the historical significance of JI is clear: it shaped regional counterterrorism policy and public awareness of extremist violence.
