## Summary

Russian serial killer who murdered at least 7 women in the Novokuznetsk area of Kemerovo region. He strangled victims and hid their bodies in wooded areas.

## Additional Context

### Pattern of crimes

Reports describe Karelin targeting women in and around Novokuznetsk in Russia's Kemerovo Oblast, killing by strangulation and leaving bodies in wooded or outlying areas.

### Investigation and arrest

The case drew attention due to multiple similar killings over several years. He was ultimately identified and arrested by local law enforcement, and the prosecution attributed at least seven murders to him.

### Trial and sentence

Russian courts convicted him and imposed a life sentence (as reflected in the timeline above). In Russia, life imprisonment typically entails incarceration in a high-security penal colony.
