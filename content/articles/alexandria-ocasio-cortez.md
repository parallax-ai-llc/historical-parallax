## Summary

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez (born October 13, 1989), commonly known as AOC, is an American politician serving as the U.S. Representative for New York's 14th congressional district. First elected in 2018, she became the youngest woman ever elected to Congress at age 29. A member of the Democratic Party and its progressive wing, she is one of the most prominent and polarizing political figures in contemporary American politics.

Her rise from bartender and community organizer to congresswoman captured national attention and signaled a generational shift within the Democratic Party. AOC has used her platform — and a massive social media following — to champion democratic socialist policies, climate legislation, and economic justice, reshaping debates around what mainstream American politics can address.

---

## Early Life & Background

Ocasio-Cortez was born in the Bronx, New York City, to a Puerto Rican family. Her father, Sergio Ocasio, was an architect who moved the family to Yorktown Heights, Westchester County, to access better public schools. His death from lung cancer in 2008 left the family in financial hardship, and AOC worked as a bartender and waitress to help support her mother.

She attended Boston University, graduating cum laude in 2011 with a Bachelor of Arts in International Relations and Economics. While in college, she interned for Senator Ted Kennedy's immigration office, an early exposure to legislative work.

---

## Rise to Prominence

After college, Ocasio-Cortez returned to the Bronx and became involved in community organizing, including work with the Bernie Sanders 2016 presidential campaign. In 2017, Justice Democrats and Brand New Congress recruited her to run against 10-term incumbent Joe Crowley in New York's 14th congressional district primary.

In June 2018, she defeated Crowley — then the fourth-ranking House Democrat and a likely candidate for Speaker — in a stunning upset, winning with roughly 57% of the vote. She went on to win the general election in November 2018 with 78% of the vote. Her victory was widely seen as a landmark moment for progressive grassroots politics.

---

## Key Achievements

- **Youngest woman elected to Congress**: Sworn in January 3, 2019, at age 29.
- **Green New Deal**: Co-introduced the Green New Deal resolution in February 2019 with Senator Ed Markey, a sweeping proposal to address climate change and economic inequality that sparked national debate.
- **Committee assignments**: Served on the House Financial Services Committee and the House Oversight Committee, using both to conduct high-profile questioning of corporate and government witnesses.
- **Electoral influence**: Endorsed numerous progressive candidates and helped expand the "Squad" — a group of progressive congresswomen including Ilhan Omar, Rashida Tlaib, and Ayanna Pressley.
- **Social media reach**: Amassed over 13 million Twitter/X followers and millions more on Instagram, making her one of the most followed politicians in the U.S.

## Controversies & Criticism

AOC has been a frequent target of conservative criticism, particularly over the Green New Deal's projected costs and its perceived scope. Critics from both the right and center-left have questioned the feasibility of policies like Medicare for All and student debt cancellation she has championed.

Within her own party, her primary challenge to incumbent Democrats and her public disagreements with House leadership, including Speaker Nancy Pelosi, generated tension. Her description of U.S. immigration detention facilities as "concentration camps" in 2019 drew sharp rebukes from Jewish organizations and Republican lawmakers. She has also faced scrutiny over campaign finance reporting and use of political action committees.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Ocasio-Cortez has fundamentally altered the landscape of progressive American politics. She demonstrated that a grassroots, small-dollar-funded campaign could defeat a well-connected incumbent, inspiring a new generation of candidates. Her use of social media as a direct political communication tool became a model studied across the political spectrum.

The Green New Deal, though not enacted, shifted the Overton window on climate policy and influenced the climate provisions eventually included in the 2022 Inflation Reduction Act. By 2026, she remains a leading voice for the Democratic Party's progressive wing and a figure whose political trajectory continues to be closely watched nationally.
