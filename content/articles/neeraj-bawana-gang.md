## Background and Overview

- The Neeraj Bawana Gang is generally described in open sources as a Delhi/NCR-area criminal group centered around the figure known as **Neeraj Bawana** (also reported as Neeraj Baliyan/Baliyan).[^1]
- In Indian media and policing discourse, such groups are often framed within the broader pattern of **Delhi gang rivalries** (“gang wars”), where influence can be exercised through intimidation, local networks, and incarceration-era coordination.[^1]

## Reported Activities

Open sources commonly attribute the following categories of activity to the group (language varies by source and may include allegations in specific cases):

- Extortion and coercive collection (“protection” demands)
- Contract killings and targeted violence
- Land/real-estate intimidation ("land grabbing" in common reporting terms)
- Illegal arms possession and trafficking networks

## Rivalries and Gang-War Context

- The group is frequently mentioned alongside rival gangs operating in Delhi and surrounding areas, including the **Jitender Gogi** gang, as part of a wider, long-running rivalry ecosystem.[^1]
- Such rivalries are typically discussed in terms of cycles of retaliation, shifting alliances, and disputes over territory or revenue streams.[^1]
