## Summary

Moses Mendelssohn was a German Jewish philosopher who is considered the father of the Jewish Enlightenment (Haskalah). He argued for the compatibility of Judaism with modern philosophy and advocated for Jewish civil rights and integration into European society. His German translation of the Torah with Hebrew commentary introduced many Jews to modern German culture. He influenced the development of Reform Judaism and Jewish emancipation.
