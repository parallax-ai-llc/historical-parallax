## Summary

Richard Garwin is an American physicist who designed the first hydrogen bomb (Ivy Mike) and later became a prominent advocate for nuclear arms control. He made contributions to detection of gravitational radiation, magnetic resonance imaging, and computer science. Garwin served as a key science advisor to multiple US presidents. He received the National Medal of Science and Presidential Medal of Freedom.
