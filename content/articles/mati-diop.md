## Summary

French-Senegalese filmmaker who became the first Black woman to compete for the Palme d'Or at Cannes with Atlantics (2019). Her film won the Grand Prix. She works between documentary and fiction.

## Background

Mati Diop is a French-Senegalese filmmaker and actor whose work is often discussed in the context of contemporary Francophone cinema and the broader African diaspora. She is the niece of Senegalese director **Djibril Diop Mambéty**, and several of her projects engage—directly or indirectly—with questions of memory, migration, and the afterlives of colonial history.

## Style and Themes

Diop is frequently described as working at the boundary of **documentary and fiction**. Her films commonly feature:

- Nonlinear or hybrid storytelling
- Observational textures drawn from documentary practice
- Social and political themes embedded in personal narratives
- Attention to soundscapes and place, especially coastal and urban environments

## Selected Works

- **A Thousand Suns (2012)**: A film that revisits the legacy of Touki Bouki and its actors, blending reflection, travel, and cinematic history.
- **Atlantics (2019)**: A feature that combines social realism with genre elements; it was recognized at Cannes and brought global attention to her work.
- **Dahomey (2024)**: Reported in public coverage as winning a major prize at the Berlin International Film Festival, and discussed in relation to debates over cultural heritage and restitution.

## Recognition

Diop’s Cannes competition entry with Atlantics was widely noted because it marked a milestone for representation at the festival. Beyond festival “firsts,” she is often cited as part of a new generation of filmmakers expanding the formal language and international visibility of Senegalese and diaspora cinema.
