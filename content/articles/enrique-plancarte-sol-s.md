## Summary

Co-founder of the Knights Templar cartel in Michoacán who controlled methamphetamine production and lime extortion rackets. He was killed by Mexican marines.

## Additional Context

### Knights Templar cartel in Michoacán

- Plancarte Solís was identified as part of the leadership of the Knights Templar cartel (Caballeros Templarios), a criminal organization that emerged in Michoacán after splitting from La Familia Michoacana.[^1][^2][^3]
- The group became known for combining narcotics trafficking—especially methamphetamine—with coercive “taxes” on local businesses and agricultural supply chains in areas under its influence.[^2][^3]

### Criminal economy and community impact

- Accounts of the cartel’s operations commonly describe extortion rackets affecting small businesses, transport, and producers, alongside violence used to enforce compliance and territorial control.[^2]
- The cartel’s activities contributed to local insecurity and were one factor behind the rise of self-defense groups (autodefensas) in parts of Michoacán in the early 2010s.[^4]

### Manhunt and death

- Mexican federal forces pursued top Knights Templar figures as the government intensified operations in Michoacán; Plancarte Solís was reported killed in a confrontation with Mexican marines in 2014.[^1]
- His death was treated by authorities as a significant blow to the group’s leadership, though fragmentation and successor dynamics were widely reported in subsequent years.[^1][^2]

### Aftermath and broader context

- The Knights Templar’s decline is often discussed in parallel with shifts in Mexico’s cartel landscape, where leadership losses can lead to splinter groups and changes in territorial control rather than an immediate end to illicit markets.[^2]
