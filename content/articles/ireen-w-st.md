## Summary

Ireen Wüst is the most decorated Olympic speed skater (13 medals, 6 gold) across five Winter Olympics.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2006 | Won gold at Turin |
| 2014 | Won five medals at Sochi |
| 2022 | Won gold at Beijing; retired |

## Career Overview

- Wüst specialized in long-track speed skating, competing primarily in the 1,000 m, 1,500 m, and 3,000 m events across her career.
- She competed at five Winter Olympics (2006, 2010, 2014, 2018, 2022), a longevity that is rare at the top level of the sport.
- Her Olympic medal total is often cited as a benchmark for consistency across multiple Olympic cycles.

## Notable Achievements (High Level)

- Olympic champion in multiple editions of the Games, including winning gold as a teenager at Turin 2006.
- Strong performances at major championships beyond the Olympics, including World Championships and European Championships.
- Recognized for versatility: successful across middle and longer distances rather than being limited to a single event.

## Style and Reputation

- Known for tactical pacing and efficient technique, traits that are especially important in the 1,500 m.
- Regarded as one of the defining athletes of her era in women’s speed skating.

## Retirement and Legacy

- After Beijing 2022, she announced retirement from competitive speed skating.
- Her medal record and durability across five Olympics made her a prominent figure in Dutch sport.
