## Summary

Canelo Álvarez became the undisputed super middleweight champion and one of the biggest boxing pay-per-view draws. He is considered the best pound-for-pound boxer of his era.

## Additional Context

- **Early career:** Álvarez turned professional as a teenager in Mexico and built a high-volume record before moving into major international bouts.
- **Weight classes:** Over his career he has competed across multiple divisions, ranging from light middleweight up through light heavyweight, with his peak commercial run centered on middleweight and super middleweight.
- **Notable rivalries:** His trilogy with Gennady Golovkin ("GGG") became one of boxing’s most prominent modern rivalries and helped define the middleweight scene of the late 2010s.
- **Undisputed run:** In 2021 he unified the super middleweight titles by collecting the WBA, WBC, IBF, and WBO belts, a milestone that positioned him as an “undisputed” champion in the four-belt era.

## Style and Significance

- Álvarez is widely associated with **counterpunching**, body work, and deliberate pace control, often investing in mid-round adjustments rather than constant volume.
- He has been a central figure in modern boxing economics, serving as a frequent pay-per-view headliner and an example of how global Spanish-language audiences shape major fight promotion.

## Outside the Ring (High Level)

- He is often discussed as part of Mexico’s lineage of elite fighters, with comparisons to prior champions based on drawing power and title achievements.
- Media coverage frequently notes his long-term team structure (training, management, promotion) as a key part of career longevity at the top level.

## Commonly Cited Milestones

- Winning major titles in multiple weight classes is often used to summarize his place among contemporary champions.
- His undisputed championship at super middleweight is a recurring reference point in modern boxing history discussions.
- Analysts frequently highlight the mix of technical refinement and physical strength that allowed him to compete above his early-career weight.
