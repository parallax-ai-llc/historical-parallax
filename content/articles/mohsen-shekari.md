## Summary

First known protester executed during Iran's 2022 Mahsa Amini protest movement.

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

## Background

- Mohsen Shekari was an Iranian man born in 2001.
- He became widely known internationally in connection with Iran’s 2022–2023 protest wave following the death of Mahsa Amini.

## Context

The protests that followed Mahsa Amini’s death involved demonstrations across multiple cities and drew significant attention from international media and human-rights organizations. Iranian authorities responded with arrests, prosecutions, and (in some cases) capital punishment.

## Arrest, Charges, and Proceedings (high level)

Public reporting describes Shekari as being arrested in the context of the 2022 protests and later tried and sentenced to death by Iranian judicial authorities.

Because detailed court records are not consistently accessible to independent observers, summaries in secondary sources may differ on specifics such as:

- the precise allegations and legal characterization of the offense(s)
- the timeline of detention and trial stages
- the evidentiary basis cited by prosecutors and the court

## Execution and Aftermath

- Shekari was executed on 2022-12-08.
- His execution was widely reported as a notable early case of a protest-related execution during the 2022 protest movement.
- The case drew international reactions, including criticism and concern from observers who argued that due-process protections were insufficient.

## Why This Case Is Notable

- Often cited as an early signal that capital punishment could be used as a deterrent against protest participation.
- Became part of broader documentation and debate about legal process, political violence, and state responses during the protest period.
