## Summary

American journalist and Forbes Russia editor shot dead in Moscow for reporting on oligarchs.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2004 | Shot dead in Moscow |

## Background

- Born in 1963 and raised in the United States.
- Became known as an American journalist who reported on post-Soviet political economy, including the rise of oligarchs and organized crime.

## Notable Work and Coverage

- Worked as a journalist and editor; he is widely described as having served as editor of *Forbes Russia*.
- Reported on powerful business figures and the privatization-era accumulation of wealth in Russia.
- Authored books associated with his reporting on Russia, including *Godfather of the Kremlin* (about Boris Berezovsky) and *Conversation with a Barbarian* (based on interviews with Chechen separatist leader Khozh-Akhmed Nukhaev).[^1]

## Death and Aftermath

- Shot in Moscow in July 2004 and later died from his injuries.[^1]
- The killing drew international attention because it involved a foreign journalist working in Russia and because his reporting focused on influential figures.
- Investigations and court proceedings have been reported over the years, but public accounts have described the case as complex and contested.[^1]

## Legacy

- Frequently cited in discussions about press freedom and the risks faced by investigative journalists.
- Remembered for coverage that connected business, politics, and security networks in the post-Soviet period.

## Key Themes

- Post-Soviet political economy and privatization.
- The interplay between business elites, state institutions, and security concerns in Russia.
