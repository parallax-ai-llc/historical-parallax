## Summary

Pedanius Dioscorides wrote De Materia Medica, the most influential pharmacological text in Western history, describing over 600 plants and their medicinal properties. Used as a standard pharmaceutical reference for more than 1,500 years, it was the forerunner of all modern pharmacopeias. He served as a military physician in the Roman army.
