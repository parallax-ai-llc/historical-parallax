## Summary

Cryptocurrency Ponzi scheme that defrauded investors of $2.4 billion by promising guaranteed returns through a lending program. It collapsed in January 2018.

## How the Scheme Was Marketed

- Bitconnect promoted a “lending program” in which users would convert assets into Bitconnect tokens and then “lend” them to the platform.
- The company claimed that a proprietary trading bot and volatility software could generate consistent, high daily returns.
- Returns were presented as unusually predictable for crypto markets, which is a common warning sign for Ponzi-style structures.

## Collapse

- In early 2018, the platform shut down its lending and exchange operations after receiving cease-and-desist actions and regulatory scrutiny.
- The token price fell sharply as confidence evaporated and users rushed to exit.

## Enforcement and Legal Proceedings

- U.S. authorities have described Bitconnect as a large-scale fraud and pursued charges against key figures.
- Reports commonly cite an indictment of founder Satish Kumbhani and related actions against promoters tied to the scheme.

## Broader Significance

- Bitconnect is widely referenced as a defining example of crypto-era Ponzi mechanics: referral incentives, opaque trading claims, and guaranteed-yield marketing.
- The episode is used in investor education as a case study for evaluating yield products and counterparty risk.
