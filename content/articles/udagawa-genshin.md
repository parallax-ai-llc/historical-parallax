## Summary

Udagawa Genshin was a Japanese physician and scholar of Dutch studies who translated and adapted Western anatomy and medical texts. His 1805 work Ihan Teiko (Western Medical Pharmacopoeia) introduced systematic Western pharmacology to Japan. He was the adoptive father of Udagawa Yoan and a pioneer of Western medicine in Japan.
