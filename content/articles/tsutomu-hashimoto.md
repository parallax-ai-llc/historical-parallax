## Summary

Boss of the Kodo-kai, a powerful faction within the Yamaguchi-gumi yakuza organization. Key figure in the 2015 split within the Yamaguchi-gumi.

## Role in the Kodo-kai

- Tsutomu Hashimoto is described in public sources as the head (kaichō) of the Kodo-kai, a major group affiliated with the Yamaguchi-gumi.
- The Kodo-kai has often been characterized as an influential faction with a reputation for organization and operational reach.
- His leadership is commonly discussed as part of the internal power dynamics within Japan’s broader yakuza landscape.

## 2015 Yamaguchi-gumi split

- In 2015, the Yamaguchi-gumi experienced a widely reported organizational split, producing rival groupings.
- Hashimoto’s position in the Kodo-kai made him a key figure in narratives about the dispute, alignments, and the redistribution of influence.
- Such splits can increase law-enforcement attention due to the risk of factional conflict and retaliatory incidents.

## Enforcement and social context

- Japanese authorities have used both prosecutions and regulatory-style ordinances to pressure designated organized-crime groups.
- The public and business environment in Japan has also shifted toward minimizing contact with yakuza organizations, affecting financing and recruitment.
- Leadership figures are therefore often evaluated not only by internal hierarchy but by how they navigate external legal and economic constraints.

## Notes

- Reporting about yakuza leadership structures can vary by source and time period, reflecting the opacity of criminal organizations and ongoing enforcement actions.
- Long-running factional disputes within large syndicates are frequently monitored for their potential impact on public safety.
