## Summary

Steven Shih Chen (born August 18, 1978) is a Taiwanese-American internet entrepreneur best known as the co-founder and former Chief Technology Officer of YouTube. Starting as an early PayPal employee, Chen helped create the world's most popular video-sharing platform, which was acquired by Google for $1.65 billion in 2006, making him one of the most successful entrepreneurs of the Web 2.0 era.

## YouTube (2005-2006)

### The Founding Story

**Origin Myth**:
- Various stories: dinner party, Janet Jackson Super Bowl, zoo videos
- Truth: Recognized opportunity for easy video sharing
- Co-founders: Chad Hurley, Jawed Karim, Steve Chen

**First Office**:
- Above pizzeria in San Mateo
- February 2005
- Very scrappy

**First Video**: "Me at the zoo" (April 23, 2005)
- Jawed Karim at San Diego Zoo
- 18 seconds
- Historic

### Growth Explosion

**Viral Success**:
- Nike Ronaldinho ad (early viral video)
- "Lazy Sunday" SNL skit
- Millions of users within months

**Technical Challenges**:
- Massive bandwidth costs
- Scaling issues
- Chen led technical solutions
- Innovative infrastructure

**Business Model Issues**:
- Copyright problems
- Bandwidth extremely expensive
- No clear revenue model yet
- Burning cash fast
