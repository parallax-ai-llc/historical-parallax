## Summary

Martin Hellman is an American cryptographer who co-invented public-key cryptography with Whitfield Diffie. Their Diffie-Hellman key exchange protocol became fundamental to internet security. He is also known for his work on the nuclear threat and published a risk analysis of nuclear deterrence. Hellman received the Turing Award in 2015 with Diffie.
