## Summary

Abu Marwan Ibn Zuhr (Avenzoar) was one of the most important physicians in medieval Islamic Spain. He was among the first to describe parasitic diseases, perform tracheotomy, and use experimental animal testing before applying treatments to humans. His medical text Kitab al-Taysir influenced European medicine for centuries.
