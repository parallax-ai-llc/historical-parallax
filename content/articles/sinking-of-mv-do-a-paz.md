## Summary

MV Doña Paz collided with an oil tanker killing an estimated 4,386 people, the deadliest peacetime maritime disaster in history. The ferry was overloaded with three times its capacity.

## More Context

### What happened

The **MV Doña Paz** was a Philippine passenger ferry that sank after a collision with the oil tanker **MT Vector** on **20 December 1987**. The collision triggered a major fire, and the ferry sank in a short time.

The disaster is widely cited as the **deadliest peacetime maritime disaster** in recorded history, with an estimated **4,386 deaths**.

### Contributing factors (high level)

Accounts and later reporting frequently highlight several systemic factors that can worsen outcomes in ferry disasters:

- **Overcrowding/overloading** relative to certified capacity
- **Rapid spread of fire** and difficult evacuation conditions
- **Limited time to deploy life-saving equipment** after impact
- **Challenges in emergency communication and coordination** at sea

### Aftermath and legacy

The sinking became a reference point in discussions of passenger-ship safety in the Philippines, especially around:

- enforcement of **capacity limits**
- **fire safety** practices and equipment readiness
- **crew training** and emergency procedures
- broader **regulatory oversight** of domestic maritime transport

## Notes

- Death toll figures are commonly reported as estimates because of uncertainty around passenger manifests and informal/uncounted boarding.
