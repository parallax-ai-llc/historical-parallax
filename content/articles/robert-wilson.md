## Summary

Robert Woodrow Wilson is an American astronomer who shared the 1978 Nobel Prize in Physics with Arno Penzias for the discovery of cosmic microwave background radiation. Their 1964 observation at Bell Labs provided strong evidence for the Big Bang theory. This accidental discovery is one of the most important in the history of cosmology. Wilson later worked on radio astronomy and satellite communications.
