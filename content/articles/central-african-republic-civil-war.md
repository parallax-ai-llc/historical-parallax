## Summary

Multi-sided civil war between Séléka (Muslim) and anti-Balaka (Christian) militias that killed thousands. Both sides committed ethnic cleansing, mass rape, and use of child soldiers.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

## Parties and Dynamics (High-Level)

- The conflict involved multiple armed groups and coalitions, with shifting alliances and fragmented command structures.
- It is commonly summarized through the opposition between **Séléka** and **anti-Balaka**, though the reality included many local militias and splinter factions.

## Key Developments

- The seizure of power by Séléka contributed to a security vacuum and retaliation cycles.
- Anti-Balaka mobilization and reprisals intensified communal violence, displacement, and targeting of civilians.

## Human Rights and Humanitarian Impact

- Reporting on the war frequently highlights patterns of atrocities: killings, sexual violence, forced displacement, and recruitment/use of child soldiers.
- Large-scale internal displacement and refugee flows placed sustained pressure on humanitarian systems.

## International Response

- International actors (including regional organizations and the United Nations) deployed missions aimed at stabilization and civilian protection.
- Despite these efforts, insecurity persisted in many areas, with armed groups maintaining influence through territorial control and resource extraction.

## Ongoing Status

- The conflict is often described as continuing in various forms, including periodic offensives, negotiated arrangements, and localized violence.
- Peace initiatives have repeatedly faced challenges due to fragmentation, incentives for armed control, and weak state capacity.
