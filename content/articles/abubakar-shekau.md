## Summary
Abubakar Shekau was the leader of Boko Haram from 2009 until his death in 2021. Under his leadership, the group conducted mass kidnappings (including 276 Chibok schoolgirls in 2014), bombings, and massacres across northeastern Nigeria. He reportedly killed himself to avoid capture by a rival faction.

## Criminal Career
As a terrorist, Abubakar Shekau became known for criminal activities that drew significant attention from law enforcement and the public. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Abubakar Shekau's criminal career came to an end after internal conflict with a rival jihadist faction, the Islamic State West Africa Province (ISWAP). In May 2021, ISWAP fighters reportedly surrounded him in the Sambisa Forest area; multiple accounts state he detonated explosives to avoid capture.

## Key Actions and Impact
Shekau became internationally known for Boko Haram's mass-casualty attacks and abductions, including the 2014 kidnapping of schoolgirls from Chibok in Borno State. His leadership period coincided with the insurgency's expansion across northeastern Nigeria and into neighboring countries around Lake Chad, contributing to large-scale displacement and a regional humanitarian crisis.

## Communications and Public Persona
He regularly released video messages in which he claimed responsibility for attacks, issued threats, and framed the insurgency in religious terms. The notoriety of these releases amplified fear and helped sustain the group's profile even as it fractured into competing factions.
