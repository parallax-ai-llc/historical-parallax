## Summary

One of India's last major dacoits who operated in UP jungles with a 5 million rupee bounty. Killed in special forces operation.

## Background

"Dadua" was the nickname of an Indian bandit (dacoit) who operated across forested areas of Uttar Pradesh and neighboring regions. In Indian usage, "dacoit" often refers to armed outlaw groups historically associated with rural kidnapping, robbery, and extortion.

## Activities (as reported)

Sources typically associate Dadua with:

- Extortion and protection demands targeting contractors and local businesses
- Kidnappings and armed robberies in remote areas
- A long-running feud with rival gangs and repeated police operations

## Police pursuit

- Indian states have at times used Special Task Forces (STF) and dedicated anti-dacoity units to pursue high-profile outlaws.
- The presence of dense terrain and local support networks can make such operations difficult, leading to long pursuits.
- Dadua was ultimately reported killed in a police operation in 2007.

## Context

Banditry in parts of north-central India has been linked to a mix of geography, weak rural policing, and political patronage. High-profile dacoits often become symbols in public debate about:

- State capacity and law enforcement reforms
- Rural poverty, caste politics, and local power brokers
- The line between criminality and folk-hero narratives in media portrayal
