## Summary

British serial killer who with husband Fred murdered at least 12 women at their Gloucester home. She participated in rape and torture of victims.

## Case Overview (High-Level)

- Rose West is primarily known in connection with a series of murders committed with her husband, Fred West, in England.
- The case became one of the most widely publicized criminal investigations in modern British history due to the number of victims and the discovery of evidence tied to the family home.

## Investigation and Prosecution

- The 1994 investigation led to arrests and extensive forensic work that connected missing persons to the Wests.
- Prosecutors argued that Rose West played an active role, not merely as an accomplice, which became central to the trial narrative.

## Trial and Sentencing

- Rose West was convicted of multiple murders and received a life sentence.
- Public discussion of the case often focuses on how courts assess joint criminal responsibility and the evidentiary challenges when crimes span many years.

## Broader Impact

- The case influenced public awareness around missing-person investigations and victim advocacy in the United Kingdom.
- It remains a frequent reference point in criminology and media analysis regarding sensational coverage, community trauma, and long-running serial investigations.
