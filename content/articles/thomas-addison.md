## Summary

Thomas Addison described Addison's disease (adrenal insufficiency) and pernicious anemia, establishing the study of endocrinology. His 1855 monograph On the Constitutional and Local Effects of Disease of the Suprarenal Capsules was a landmark in medicine. He was one of the great clinical observers of Guy's Hospital, London.
