## Background

Luis Alfredo Garavito Cubillos, often referred to simply as Luis Garavito, is a Colombian serial killer whose crimes made him one of the most notorious offenders in modern Latin American criminal history.

## Crimes

Authorities attributed to Garavito a pattern of child abductions, sexual assaults, and murders across multiple regions. Reports commonly describe him as targeting vulnerable boys and using deception to isolate victims. Because the crimes occurred across jurisdictions and over years, investigators faced significant challenges linking cases and building a complete accounting.

## Arrest and investigation

Garavito was arrested in the late 1990s. His apprehension and subsequent interrogations were closely followed in Colombia and internationally, both because of the scale of the crimes and because of the investigative effort required to connect victims across different areas.

## Prosecution and sentencing

He was prosecuted in Colombia and received a very long prison sentence under Colombian law. The case also became a point of public debate about sentencing limits, plea incentives, and how legal systems handle perpetrators responsible for exceptionally large numbers of victims.

## Public impact

The Garavito case is frequently cited in discussions about child protection, policing coordination between regions, and the long-term effects of violence on communities. It also influenced public awareness campaigns and calls for stronger investigative resources for missing-children cases.
