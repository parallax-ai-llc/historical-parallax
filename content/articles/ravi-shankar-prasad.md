## Technology & Governance Focus (High-Level)

While not an entrepreneur profile, Ravi Shankar Prasad is frequently cited in the context of India’s digital governance era because his cabinet roles sat at the intersection of:

- **Telecom and connectivity** (Communications portfolios)
- **Digital government and IT regulation** (Electronics & IT)
- **Legal frameworks and institutions** (Law and Justice)

From a “historical Parallax” lens, the relevance is **policy leverage**: how state capacity, regulation, and infrastructure shape the operating surface for platforms, startups, and civil society.

## Notable Roles (Selected)

- **Member of Parliament**: Rajya Sabha (Bihar, 2000–2019), then Lok Sabha (Patna Sahib, since 2019).[^1]
- **Union Minister (multiple times)** under NDA governments (Vajpayee and Modi).[^1]
- **Electronics & IT**: served as the first cabinet minister for the reorganized ministry (post-2016 split/reorganization).[^1]

## Themes Associated With His Tenure (Non-exhaustive)

These are broad themes repeatedly referenced in public reporting and parliamentary/ministerial summaries (details vary by policy episode):
