## Summary

Chadian rebel leader who founded the Movement for Democracy and Justice in Chad. He led armed rebellion against President Déby before being killed by Libyan mines.

## Regional Context

The MDJT rebellion is commonly situated within long-running center–periphery tensions in Chad, where remote northern regions have periodically produced insurgent movements challenging the central government.

Northern Chad’s geography (including desert terrain and sparse infrastructure) has historically shaped conflict dynamics by:

- Making state administration and security presence difficult.
- Enabling cross-border movement and supply routes.
- Increasing reliance on local alliances and tribal/communal networks.
