## Summary

South Korean serial killer who murdered at least 9 people in Busan during the early 1980s. He attacked victims at random in a spree that terrorized the city.

## Background (High Level)

The case is associated with a period of heightened public fear in **Busan** in the early 1980s due to repeated, seemingly unconnected attacks. Contemporary reporting and later summaries describe the incidents as creating significant social anxiety and increased police activity.

## Investigation and Arrest

Authorities linked multiple crimes to a single suspect through investigative work and witness information available at the time. Park Choon-shik was arrested in the early 1980s and later prosecuted for a series of murders.

## Sentencing and Aftermath

He was sentenced to death in South Korea. The case is often cited in discussions of the country's criminal justice response to violent serial offenses during that era and the public impact of spree-like violence.
