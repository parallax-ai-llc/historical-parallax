## Summary

Indian military operation to remove Sikh militants from the Golden Temple in Amritsar in June 1984. The operation killed over 500 people and damaged the holiest Sikh shrine.

## Background (High-Level)

- The operation took place amid escalating political conflict in Punjab and a security confrontation involving Sikh militants.
- A major figure associated with the standoff was Jarnail Singh Bhindranwale, who was reported to have used the Golden Temple complex as a base.

## The Operation

- Conducted in June 1984 by the Indian Army to remove armed groups from the Harmandir Sahib (Golden Temple) complex in Amritsar.
- Fighting in and around a major religious site made the operation exceptionally sensitive.
- Reports of casualties vary substantially by source; many accounts describe hundreds to more than a thousand deaths across combatants and civilians.

## Damage, Controversy, and Community Impact

- The operation caused significant damage within the complex and became a lasting source of trauma and political grievance for many Sikhs.
- It is frequently discussed in terms of state force, religious sanctity, and the costs of counterinsurgency operations.

## Aftermath

- The assassination of Prime Minister Indira Gandhi by her Sikh bodyguards later in 1984 is widely linked to the political fallout.
- Anti-Sikh violence in the aftermath became another major historical and legal focus, shaping subsequent politics and communal relations.
