## Summary

Congolese billionaire and former governor of Katanga province who was convicted of hiring mercenaries and fraudulently obtaining property. He claims the charges are politically motivated as he is an opposition leader.

## Political Profile

- Moïse Katumbi is a Congolese businessman and political figure who served as governor of Katanga, a province historically central to the DRC’s mining economy.
- He has been linked with both governance claims of development/administration and allegations of corruption and legal wrongdoing, with supporters framing some cases as politically motivated.

## Legal and Exile Period

- Katumbi’s legal disputes became a major part of his political narrative, intersecting with broader debates about rule of law and political competition in the DRC.
- His departure from the country and later return were treated as notable events in the context of opposition politics.

## Influence and Notability

- His wealth and business ties have made him a high-profile actor in the intersection of politics, mining, and regional power in southern DRC.
- The Katanga governorship is often discussed as an influential base because of the region’s economic importance.
