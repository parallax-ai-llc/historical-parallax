## Summary

Gerty Cori was a Czech-American biochemist who shared the 1947 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine with her husband Carl Cori for their discovery of the course of catalytic conversion of glycogen. She was the first American woman to win a Nobel Prize in science. The Cori cycle, describing how the body converts glycogen to glucose and back, is named after the couple.
