## Summary

Julius Erving, 'Dr. J,' revolutionized basketball with his above-the-rim playing style. He won three scoring titles in the ABA and an NBA championship with Philadelphia.

## Additional Context

### ABA → NBA Bridge Figure

Erving is widely credited with helping **popularize and legitimize the ABA** during the 1970s and then carrying that star power into the NBA after the **ABA–NBA merger**. He played for the Virginia Squires and New York Nets in the ABA before joining the Philadelphia 76ers, and his teams were consistently postseason contenders.

### Style and Influence

His signature was a fluid, airborne style—finishes at the rim, acrobatic layups, and dunks—that helped normalize the modern “above-the-rim” aesthetic. Iconic moments frequently associated with him include the **free-throw-line dunk** in ABA-era showcases and the broader dunk-contest era that followed.

### Awards and Honors (Highlights)

Open statistical summaries commonly list:

- multiple league MVP awards across the ABA and NBA (including **NBA MVP in 1981**)
- an **NBA championship in 1983** with the 76ers
- All-Star selections across both leagues and recognition on NBA anniversary teams

### Legacy

By combining elite production with a visually distinctive style, Erving became a template for later wing scorers and finishers. He is also frequently cited as an early popular example for the idea of a player being "posterized" by an opponent’s dunk.
