## Summary

Sri Lankan former president under whose leadership the civil war ended with massive civilian casualties. His family dominated Sri Lankan politics and was accused of corruption. He fled his official residence during 2022 protests.

## Early Political Career

- Rajapaksa entered national politics as a long-serving member of parliament and held several ministerial roles before reaching the presidency.
- He was associated with the Sri Lanka Freedom Party (SLFP) political tradition and later with a broader Rajapaksa-aligned coalition.

## Presidency (2005–2015)

- **Civil war end**: His presidency is most closely associated with the final phase of the Sri Lankan Civil War and the military defeat of the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE) in 2009.
- **Human-rights scrutiny**: The period has been the subject of significant international attention, including allegations of serious violations and disputes over accountability and civilian harm.
- **Governance and power**: Commentators frequently describe the era as one of strong executive control and expanded influence for members of the Rajapaksa family within state institutions.

## Post-Presidency and Return to Government

- After losing the presidency in 2015, Rajapaksa remained a major political figure and later returned as **Prime Minister**.
- His return coincided with broader shifts in Sri Lankan electoral politics and the consolidation of a Rajapaksa-led party structure.

## 2022 Economic Crisis and Protests

- **Public unrest**: In 2022, Sri Lanka experienced a severe economic and political crisis that triggered large-scale demonstrations.
- **Resignation**: Rajapaksa resigned as prime minister amid the unrest; reporting also described him leaving official premises during the protests.

## Corruption and Accountability Debates

- Allegations of corruption and misuse of state resources have been a recurring feature of reporting and political debate around Rajapaksa-era governance.
- Accountability discussions have taken place domestically and internationally, with ongoing disagreement about facts, responsibility, and the effectiveness of investigations.
