## Summary

American woman who was kidnapped at age 11 by Phillip and Nancy Garrido and held captive for 18 years. She was repeatedly assaulted and bore two children during her captivity.

## Overview
Jaycee Dugard is an American woman who was kidnapped in 1991 and held captive for approximately 18 years before being found in 2009. Her case drew significant public attention in the United States and internationally.

## Disappearance and captivity
Dugard was abducted while walking to a school bus stop in California. Authorities later determined she was held at a residential property, where she lived under coercion and control. The investigation and eventual discovery highlighted challenges in missing-person cases and long-term captivity situations.

## Discovery and legal outcome
In 2009, Dugard was identified after contact with law enforcement connected to her captor. Criminal proceedings resulted in convictions and lengthy prison sentences. The case also led to scrutiny of supervision and monitoring practices for registered offenders.

## Later life and public work
After her recovery, Dugard wrote memoirs describing her experiences and has been associated with advocacy focused on survivors and recovery. Public discussions around her case have emphasized trauma-informed support and the long-term process of rebuilding life after captivity.
