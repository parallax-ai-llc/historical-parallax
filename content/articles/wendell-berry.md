## Summary

Wendell Berry is an American novelist, poet, and environmental activist whose agrarian philosophy is deeply rooted in Christian faith and the stewardship of creation. He has written extensively on the connections between land, community, and spiritual life. His essays, including 'The Unsettling of America' and 'What Are People For?,' critique industrial agriculture and consumer culture from both ecological and theological perspectives. He has farmed in Kentucky for over 50 years.
