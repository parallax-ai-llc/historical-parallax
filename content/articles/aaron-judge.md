## Summary

Aaron Judge set the American League single-season home run record with 62 in 2022, surpassing Roger Maris's 61-year-old mark.

## Early life and development

Judge played multiple sports as a youth and is often noted for uncommon size and athleticism for an outfielder. He attended college at **Fresno State**, where he developed into a prominent MLB prospect.

## Major League career (high level)

- Drafted by the New York Yankees and rose through the minor leagues before debuting in the mid-2010s.
- Known for a power profile built on hard contact, plate discipline, and the ability to play right field and center field.

## Notable seasons and achievements

- **2017**: Breakout year with elite home-run totals and broad recognition across the league.
- **2022**: Home-run record season for the American League; the pursuit of the record became a major national sports storyline.

## Leadership and role

Judge is frequently described as a franchise cornerstone whose public profile extends beyond on-field performance, including team leadership and media attention typical of star Yankees players.

## Style of play (summary)

- Power hitting (home runs, extra-base damage)
- Strong throwing arm in the outfield
- Defensive flexibility and athletic range relative to his size
