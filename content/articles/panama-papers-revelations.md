## Background

The term “Panama Papers” refers to a large-scale leak of internal files from the offshore law firm **Mossack Fonseca**. The materials were shared with journalists and coordinated through the **International Consortium of Investigative Journalists (ICIJ)** and partner newsrooms.

## What the leak contained

- **Millions of records** (emails, PDFs, incorporation documents, passports, contracts) connected to offshore companies and trusts.
- Data covering multiple decades of offshore services, including company formation, nominee directors, and bank introductions.
- Information that helped map relationships between intermediaries (law firms, corporate service providers) and beneficial owners.

## What was “revealed” (high-level)

- How offshore entities can be used to **hold assets**, **route payments**, and **obscure ownership** across jurisdictions.
- The role of professional intermediaries in creating multi-layer corporate structures.
- That many structures were legal in form, but also raised concerns about **tax evasion**, **money laundering**, and **sanctions circumvention** risks depending on use.

## Global impact

- Triggered **investigations** by tax authorities, financial regulators, and law enforcement agencies in numerous countries.
- Accelerated policy discussions on **beneficial ownership transparency**, anti–money laundering enforcement, and corporate registry reforms.
- Led to resignations, political fallout, and public scrutiny of elites and institutions linked to offshore arrangements.

## Common themes in offshore structures

- **Shell companies** registered in low-tax or secrecy jurisdictions.
- Use of **nominee directors/shareholders** to separate control from public records.
- Multi-jurisdiction “chains” designed to complicate tracing ownership.
- Reliance on banks and corporate service providers as gatekeepers.

## Notes on terminology

- “Offshore” is not inherently illegal; it often refers to cross-border corporate or financial arrangements.
- The legal and tax consequences depend on **disclosure**, **residency**, **source of funds**, and compliance with local law.
