## Summary

The 16th Karmapa, Rangjung Rigpe Dorje, was the head of the Karma Kagyu school of Tibetan Buddhism. He was the first major Tibetan lama to tour the West, visiting Europe and America in the 1970s and making Tibetan Buddhism widely known. He established Rumtek Monastery in Sikkim, India, as his seat in exile. He was known for performing the Black Crown ceremony and for his charismatic teaching style.
