## Summary

American respiratory therapist who murdered 6 patients at Glendale Adventist Medical Center in California between 1989 and 1997. He confessed to killing up to 50 patients by injecting them with muscle relaxants. Sentenced to life.

## Context: Harm in Clinical Settings

Cases like Saldivar’s are often discussed under the broader category of healthcare or “angel of death” killings, where a trusted clinician exploits access to vulnerable patients. Such cases raise issues of detection because victims may be medically fragile and deaths can initially appear consistent with underlying illness.

## Investigation (High Level)

Open-source summaries describe the case as developing from retrospective review of patient outcomes and internal concerns. In many hospital-based homicide investigations, evidence can include:

- Mortality pattern analysis (unexpected clusters by shift/unit).
- Medication records and access logs.
- Toxicology and forensic review when exhumations or re-evaluations are performed.

## Victim Count and Attribution

Public accounts note a large discrepancy between the number of murders proven in court and higher numbers mentioned in confessions or allegations. In general, additional claimed victims may be difficult to confirm due to incomplete records, competing medical explanations, and limitations of forensic testing long after death.

## Impact

- Increased attention to patient-safety monitoring, reporting mechanisms, and peer review.
- Use of statistical and auditing approaches to detect anomalous mortality patterns.
