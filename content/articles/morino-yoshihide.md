## Summary

Morino Yoshihide was a Japanese physical chemist who pioneered gas-phase electron diffraction studies of molecular structures in Japan. His precise measurements of molecular geometries advanced understanding of chemical bonding. He established physical chemistry research at several Japanese universities.
