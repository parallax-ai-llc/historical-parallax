## Summary

Claudio Monteverdi was an Italian composer who bridged the Renaissance and Baroque eras. His opera L'Orfeo (1607) is considered the first great opera. His madrigals and sacred music, including the Vespers of 1610, revolutionized the expressive possibilities of Western music.
