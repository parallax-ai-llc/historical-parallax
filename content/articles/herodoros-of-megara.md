## Summary

Herodoros of Megara won ten consecutive Olympic victories in the trumpet competition. His powerful voice was said to be audible from great distances.

## The Olympic “trumpet” contest

Ancient Olympic festivals included not only athletic events but also competitions connected to ceremony and signaling. A trumpet (salpinx) contest is typically described as rewarding volume and clarity, since the winner could be used to announce events and commands.

## Reported achievements

Ancient sources (as summarized in modern reference works) attribute to Herodoros:

- repeated victories in the salpinx competition, sometimes framed as **ten consecutive Olympic wins**
- exceptional breath control and loudness, expressed in anecdotes about his voice carrying over long distances

Because the evidence is largely anecdotal and mediated through later writers, details are generally treated as part-record, part-legend.

## What “Megara” implies

“Of Megara” identifies him with the Greek city-state of Megara. In the classical period, athletes were often remembered through their polis affiliation, which mattered for prestige and for how victories were commemorated.

## Why minor events mattered

Even niche contests could carry status because Olympic victors were celebrated in their home cities, gained public honor, and could be used in civic storytelling about excellence and reputation.

## Notes on chronology

The dates for Herodoros are approximate and are usually inferred from the era in which the relevant Olympic victories were said to occur.
