## Summary

Nicaraguan former president convicted of embezzling $100 million in government funds—making him one of the most corrupt leaders in Central American history. Transparency International named him one of the world's most corrupt leaders.

## Political Career (High-Level)

- Rose through local and national politics, including leadership roles in Managua.
- Led the **Liberal Constitutionalist Party (PLC)** and became a major pole in Nicaragua’s post-1990 party system.

## Corruption Allegations and Case

The case against Alemán is frequently summarized as a large-scale embezzlement and misuse of public funds. It became internationally notable because it involved a former head of state and because it unfolded alongside intense political negotiation in Nicaragua.

## Governance Context

- Nicaragua’s institutions in this period faced challenges around judicial independence, party capture, and enforcement consistency.
- Analysts often frame the episode as illustrating how anti-corruption efforts can be shaped by elite bargains, especially when competing political blocs need accommodation.

## Legacy

Beyond the legal record, Alemán’s presidency is frequently discussed in the context of state modernization efforts, patronage politics, and the long-run effects of corruption scandals on public trust.
