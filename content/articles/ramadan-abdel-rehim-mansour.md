## Summary
Ramadan Abdel Rehim Mansour was an Egyptian serial killer known as 'The Train Station Killer' who murdered at least 32 women in Alexandria and Cairo between 1999 and 2006. He targeted sex workers near train stations, strangling them. He was sentenced to death and executed in 2010.

## Early Life
Born around 1960 in Egypt. Little is publicly known about his early life. He worked menial jobs and lived near train stations in Alexandria and Cairo. He was married and had children. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1999 and 2006, Mansour murdered at least 32 women, primarily sex workers, near train stations in Alexandria and Cairo. He would approach women, lure them to secluded areas, rape them, and strangle them. He sometimes mutilated their bodies. He moved between cities to avoid detection. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Mansour was arrested in 2006 after being identified through witness testimony and forensic evidence. He confessed to 32 murders. He was convicted and sentenced to death by hanging. He was executed in March 2010 at Borg el-Arab Prison near Alexandria. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case led to increased police patrols near train stations [^4]
- Prompted discussions about the safety of women in public spaces in Egypt
- Led to improved forensic capabilities in Egyptian law enforcement

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered at least 32 women over seven years [^5]
- Targeted sex workers, the most vulnerable women
- Operated across two major Egyptian cities undetected for years
- Demonstrated inadequate police investigation of crimes against marginalized women
- His crimes exposed the dangers faced by women near transportation hubs
