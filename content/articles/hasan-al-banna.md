## Summary

Hasan al-Banna was an Egyptian Islamic scholar and activist who founded the Muslim Brotherhood in 1928, one of the most influential Islamic movements of the 20th century. He advocated for an Islamic state governed by sharia and organized a mass movement combining religious education, social services, and political activism. The Brotherhood grew to have millions of members across the Arab world. He was assassinated by Egyptian secret police in 1949.
