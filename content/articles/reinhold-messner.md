## Summary

Reinhold Messner was the first to climb Everest without supplemental oxygen (1978) and the first to solo summit Everest (1980). He climbed all 14 eight-thousanders.

## Climbing Style and Significance

Messner is associated with a “**by fair means**” approach to alpinism: emphasizing minimal equipment, self-reliance, and avoiding heavy expedition support when possible.

- His Everest ascent without supplemental oxygen was important not only as a record, but as evidence that humans could perform at extreme altitude without bottled oxygen under certain conditions.
- Many of his achievements were pursued in a style that prioritized speed, simplicity, and reduced logistical footprint.

## The 14 Eight-Thousanders

Climbing all 14 peaks above 8,000 meters is considered one of the most difficult mountaineering lists.

- Messner completed the set by 1986, contributing to the popularization of the list as a defining goal in high-altitude climbing.
- His record became part of broader debates about recognition, documentation, and the evolution of Himalayan expedition ethics.

## Expeditions Beyond the Himalaya

In addition to high-altitude ascents, Messner undertook major journeys often framed as exploration.

- He is associated with long-distance desert and polar-style treks that emphasized endurance and navigation.
- His writings and lectures helped shape public understanding of risk, survival, and decision-making in extreme environments.

## Public Work

- Messner has authored numerous books on mountaineering and expedition experiences.
- He has been involved in mountain museums and cultural projects in the Alps that document climbing history.
