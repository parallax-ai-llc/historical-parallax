## Summary

Jakob Fugger, known as 'Jakob the Rich,' was the wealthiest European in history and the most powerful banker of the Renaissance. His banking house in Augsburg financed emperors, popes, and kings. He bankrolled Charles V's election as Holy Roman Emperor and controlled the European copper and silver trade. He also built the Fuggerei, the world's oldest social housing complex still in use, where rent has remained at 88 cents per year since 1521.
