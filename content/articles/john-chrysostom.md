## Summary

John Chrysostom, meaning 'Golden-Mouthed,' was Archbishop of Constantinople and one of the greatest preachers in early Christianity. His homilies and commentaries on Scripture remain influential in both Eastern and Western traditions. He was a champion of social justice, criticizing the wealthy and powerful. He was exiled twice for his outspoken criticism of imperial authority.
