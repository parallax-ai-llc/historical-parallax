## Summary

Basque politician convicted of attempting to rebuild the banned political party Batasuna, which was the political wing of ETA. His conviction was later overturned by the European Court of Human Rights.

## Political Profile

Arnaldo Otegi is a Basque politician associated with the Basque nationalist left (izquierda abertzale) in Spain. He has held leadership roles in political organizations connected to that current and has been a prominent public figure in debates about Basque self-determination, political representation, and the legacy of ETA-related violence.

## Legal Controversies and Court Rulings

Otegi has faced multiple legal cases over his political activities. In one prominent case, Spanish courts convicted him of attempting to rebuild or support a banned political structure associated with Batasuna. The European Court of Human Rights later ruled that aspects of the proceedings violated his rights, and the decision is often cited in discussions of judicial impartiality and fair-trial standards.

## Public Debate and Assessment

Supporters have portrayed Otegi as a key actor in moving parts of Basque nationalist politics toward exclusively political methods, while critics have linked his career to organizations that Spanish courts connected to ETA. As with many figures tied to long-running internal conflicts, interpretations of his role vary significantly across Spain's political landscape.
