## Overview

Abu al-Qasim Khalaf ibn al-Abbas al-Zahrawi (often Latinized as Albucasis) was a physician and surgeon from al-Andalus, associated with Córdoba during the Islamic Golden Age. He is widely remembered for consolidating surgical knowledge, describing procedures, and illustrating instruments in a systematic way that influenced later medical writing.

## Names and Identification

The short name “Al-Zahrawi” refers to his nisba, linking him to the area of al-Zahra’ near Córdoba. In European sources he is frequently known as “Albucasis,” a Latinized rendering of “Abu al-Qasim.” This article’s note about duplication (“Already added as Abu al-Qasim al-Zahrawi”) reflects the fact that the same historical figure appears under multiple common name forms.

## Major Work: *Al-Tasrif*

Al-Zahrawi is commonly associated with the medical encyclopedia *Kitab al-Tasrif* (often shortened to *Al-Tasrif*). A portion of this work focuses on surgery and is notable for its practical orientation: it discusses clinical cases, operative techniques, and step-by-step guidance for practitioners.

## Surgical Instruments and Technique

One of the most cited features of his surgical writings is the attention to instruments—how they are shaped, what they are used for, and why particular designs matter. By pairing descriptions with illustrations, the text helped standardize surgical practice and served as a reference point for later physicians.

## Transmission and Influence

Through translation and circulation, al-Zahrawi’s surgical material became influential beyond al-Andalus. Medieval European medical education drew on Arabic and Latin medical compendia, and his reputation as a foundational surgical author is closely tied to this broader translation movement.

## Legacy

In historical overviews of medicine, al-Zahrawi is often presented as an early exemplar of surgical specialization and of medical writing that foregrounds practical technique. His long-term significance is less about a single discovery than about the consolidation of knowledge into a durable, teachable form.
