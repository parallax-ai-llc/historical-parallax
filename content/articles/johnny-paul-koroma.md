## Summary

Sierra Leonean military officer who led the 1997 coup and allied with the RUF rebels. His AFRC junta committed widespread atrocities. He disappeared in 2003 and was declared dead by the Special Court for Sierra Leone.

## Additional Context

- Koroma is closely tied to the Sierra Leone Civil War period, where alliances between military factions and rebel groups contributed to severe violence against civilians.
- The AFRC junta period is described in many accounts as marked by human-rights abuses and instability, with regional forces intervening to restore the elected government.
- The **Special Court for Sierra Leone** pursued prosecutions for serious violations, and Koroma was among figures associated with these proceedings; many summaries note that he was later **declared dead** by the Court.

## Legacy / Ongoing Relevance

- His story is referenced when studying coup dynamics, militia-rebel alliances, and accountability mechanisms in post-conflict justice.
- Because reports describe him as having disappeared before a full judicial resolution, the case is also discussed in terms of the limits of tribunals when suspects cannot be apprehended.
