## Summary

Ugandan cult leader who co-founded the Movement for the Restoration of the Ten Commandments of God. When his prophecy of the end of the world failed, the cult leaders murdered at least 778 followers. He died in the fire or escaped.

## Movement context

The Movement for the Restoration of the Ten Commandments of God emerged in Uganda in the late 1980s as a millenarian Christian sect emphasizing strict observance of the Ten Commandments, apocalyptic prophecy, and communal discipline. Reports about the group describe strong internal control, including isolation from outsiders and demands for material contributions.

## 2000 Kanungu tragedy

On 17 March 2000, a major incident occurred at Kanungu in southwestern Uganda when a building associated with the movement burned while many members were inside. Subsequent investigations and site searches reported additional mass graves connected to other movement locations.

Public accounts generally treat the events as a coordinated mass killing rather than an accidental fire, but details of planning and responsibility have been contested in reporting.

## Fate of leaders

Kibweteere and other senior figures were reported among the dead, but early uncertainty led to speculation that some leaders may have fled. Later reporting commonly lists him as deceased.

## Historical significance

The case is frequently referenced in discussions of:

- Doomsday movements and failed prophecies
- Coercive authority structures in closed groups
- The difficulty of establishing facts immediately after mass-casualty incidents
