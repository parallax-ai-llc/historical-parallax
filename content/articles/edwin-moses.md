## Summary

Edwin Moses won 107 consecutive 400m hurdles races between 1977 and 1987. He won Olympic gold in 1976 and 1984.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1976 | Won Olympic gold at Montreal |
| 1977 | Began 107-race winning streak |
| 1984 | Won second gold at LA |
| 1987 | Streak ended |

## Why He Matters

- **Competitive dominance:** Moses' multi-year winning streak in the 400 m hurdles is one of track and field's best-known records for sustained superiority in a single event.
- **Championship record:** Beyond the Olympics, he won major titles at global championships, frequently setting or approaching world-best times.

## Style and Training (High Level)

- **Technical precision:** The 400 m hurdles rewards rhythm, stride pattern management, and efficient barrier clearance; Moses became emblematic of disciplined, repeatable execution.
- **Consistency:** His streak is often used as a case study in how injury prevention, race planning, and incremental improvement can compound over time.

## Post-Competition Work

- **Sports governance:** Moses has been involved in athletics administration and anti-doping advocacy, representing athletes and promoting integrity in competition.
- **Legacy:** He is commonly cited among the greatest hurdlers in history and remains a reference point for later champions in the event.

## Key Takeaways

- **Longevity under pressure:** His streak is often discussed as a demonstration of maintaining peak performance across seasons and championship cycles.
- **Event difficulty:** The 400 m hurdles combines sprint speed with endurance and barrier technique, amplifying small errors.
- **Athlete leadership:** Moses' post-career roles made him a prominent voice on athlete rights and clean sport.
