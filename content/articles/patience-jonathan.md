## Summary

Former Nigerian first lady who had $31.4 million in cash seized from bank accounts under pseudonyms. She was accused of money laundering during her husband's presidency.

## Context

Patience Jonathan served as Nigeria's First Lady during the administration of President Goodluck Jonathan. In that role she became a prominent political figure and a symbol of the patronage-heavy style of elite politics that has repeatedly collided with anti-corruption enforcement.

## Allegations and enforcement actions

The best-known episode associated with her public image is the seizure of large sums of money held in bank accounts reportedly linked to her through proxies. Nigerian investigators and prosecutors treated the deposits as suspected proceeds of corruption and money laundering connected to the period her husband was in office.

## Why this case is cited

This story is frequently referenced in discussions of:

- How politically exposed persons (PEPs) can use associates and account nominees to move or store funds.
- The difficulty of recovering assets once money is dispersed across multiple institutions and identities.
- The role of courts in balancing due process claims against anti-corruption seizure powers.

## Legacy

Regardless of legal outcomes in any specific proceeding, the episode remains part of Nigeria's broader debate over governance, accountability, and the credibility of anti-corruption campaigns when they intersect with high-status political families.

## Related themes

Asset forfeiture, beneficial ownership, and the political economy of anti-corruption enforcement.
