## Summary

German-American criminal known as the 'Napoleon of Crime' who was the inspiration for Sherlock Holmes' nemesis Professor Moriarty. He stole Gainsborough's painting of the Duchess of Devonshire and ran a global criminal network.

## Additional Context

- Adam Worth is a frequently cited example in histories of **professionalized 19th-century crime**, mixing fraud, theft, and fencing networks.
- The "Napoleon of Crime" label is commonly repeated in secondary sources; the claim that he influenced Conan Doyle's Moriarty is also typically presented as an inspiration rather than a direct one-to-one biography.

## Notable Theft: Duchess of Devonshire (High-Level)

- The Gainsborough portrait theft became famous partly because the painting's disappearance and later recovery illustrated how stolen art could be held for leverage or resale over long periods.
- Accounts often describe negotiations involving intermediaries and reward payments, reflecting the economics of the art underworld.

## Criminal Network (General)

- Biographical summaries portray Worth as operating across the U.S. and Europe, using aliases and coordinating with specialists in burglary, forgery, and logistics.
