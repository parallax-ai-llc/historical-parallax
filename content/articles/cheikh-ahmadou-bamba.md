## Summary

Cheikh Ahmadou Bamba Mbacke was a Senegalese Sufi saint who founded the Mouride brotherhood. He preached a path of peace, work, and spiritual devotion that challenged French colonial authority through non-violent resistance. Exiled by the French to Gabon and Mauritania, his following only grew. His teachings emphasize work as worship and devotion to God. The annual Grand Magal pilgrimage to Touba attracts millions.
