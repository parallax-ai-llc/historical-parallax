## Summary

Nyck de Vries is a Dutch professional racing driver born on February 6, 1995, in Uitwellingerga, the Netherlands. He is best known as the Formula E World Champion for Season 7 (2020–21), a title he secured driving for the Mercedes-EQ factory team. His career trajectory — from karting prodigy through junior formula championships to the pinnacle of both electric and conventional single-seater racing — made him one of the most decorated drivers of his generation in feeder series.

De Vries attracted widespread attention in September 2022 when he made an opportunistic Formula 1 debut as a substitute for the injured Alexander Albon at the Italian Grand Prix, finishing ninth and scoring points on his very first start. That performance earned him a full F1 seat with Scuderia AlphaTauri for 2023, though the season proved difficult and his tenure ended prematurely after just ten races.

---

## Early Life & Background

De Vries grew up in Friesland, a province in the northern Netherlands, in a family that supported his early passion for motorsport. He began karting at a young age and quickly demonstrated exceptional pace, earning him a place in the McLaren Autosport BRDC Award programme and eventually a spot in the McLaren young driver programme. He relocated across Europe to pursue his racing career through the conventional single-seater ladder, later transitioning to the Mercedes junior development programme.

---

## Rise to Prominence

De Vries progressed through Formula Renault and Formula 3 before reaching the top of the junior categories. In 2018, he won the GP3 Series title with ART Grand Prix and also claimed victory at the prestigious Macau Grand Prix, one of the most celebrated non-championship events in motorsport.

In 2019, he stepped up to Formula 2, again with ART Grand Prix, and won the championship, becoming one of a select group of drivers to win both the GP3 and F2 titles. Despite holding a Mercedes superlicence and being widely considered F1-ready, a vacant seat did not materialise immediately, and de Vries channelled his efforts into Formula E.

His Formula E career reached its peak in Season 7 (2020–21), when he clinched the drivers' world championship for Mercedes-EQ, becoming the first Dutch driver to win a major FIA single-seater world championship. His Italian Grand Prix substitute appearance in September 2022 — where he qualified ninth and finished in the points — reset his career trajectory and secured him the AlphaTauri drive for 2023.

## Key Achievements

- **GP3 Series Champion** (2018) with ART Grand Prix
- **Macau Grand Prix winner** (2018)
- **FIA Formula 2 Champion** (2019) with ART Grand Prix
- **Formula E World Champion** (Season 7, 2020–21) with Mercedes-EQ
- **Formula 1 debut points** — 9th place at the 2022 Italian Grand Prix as a substitute driver
- First Dutch driver to win a major FIA single-seater world championship

---

## Controversies & Criticism

De Vries's 2023 Formula 1 season with AlphaTauri generated significant scrutiny. He scored only 2 championship points across 10 races, consistently outpaced by teammate Yuki Tsunoda. Critics pointed to his difficulty adapting to the physical and technical demands of modern F1 machinery compared to Formula E cars. AlphaTauri replaced him with Daniel Ricciardo after the Hungarian Grand Prix, making him one of the quickest mid-season departures in recent F1 history. Some observers argued that he had been promoted prematurely based on the impact of a single substitute outing rather than sustained F1-relevant performance.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Despite his difficult F1 chapter, de Vries's broader career stands as a testament to consistency across categories. Winning both the F2 and Formula E world titles places him in rare company, and his championship in Formula E contributed to legitimising the series as a serious proving ground during the sport's critical growth period. He remains one of the most credentialed Dutch racing drivers outside of Formula 1, operating in an era defined by the dominance of compatriot Max Verstappen.
