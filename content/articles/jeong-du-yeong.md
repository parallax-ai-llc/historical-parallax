## Summary

South Korean serial killer who murdered 9 women in Busan and Gyeongnam between 2006 and 2008 by luring them through online dating.

## Case overview (high level)

Open sources describe Jeong Du-yeong as targeting adult women over a multi-year period.
The crimes received significant attention in South Korea, in part because they were connected to the growing use of online dating at the time.
The pattern underscored how new communication platforms can introduce new vectors for predatory behavior.

## Investigation and arrest

As the case developed, police worked to connect multiple incidents as part of a single series.
Investigations of this kind typically rely on a combination of witness accounts, digital traces, physical evidence, and timeline reconstruction.
Jeong Du-yeong was ultimately identified and arrested in 2008.

## Prosecution and sentencing

He was tried and convicted for multiple murders.
According to reporting summarized in reference materials, a death sentence was imposed in 2009.
South Korea retains capital punishment in law, though executions have not been carried out for many years (a de facto moratorium).

## Public discussion and impact

The case contributed to public debate about:

- Safety risks in online dating and meeting strangers offline
- The responsibilities of platforms and communities to promote safer practices
- How law enforcement adapts investigative techniques to technology-mediated crime
