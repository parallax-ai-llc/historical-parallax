## Summary

Mass shooting at Tree of Life synagogue in Pittsburgh on October 27, 2018 killing 11 worshippers. Deadliest antisemitic attack in US history.

## Background and setting

- Tree of Life is a Jewish synagogue located in the Squirrel Hill neighborhood of Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.
- The attack occurred during Shabbat morning services, when multiple congregations were present in the building.
- The incident is widely described as the deadliest antisemitic attack in the history of the United States.

## Attack overview

- The shooter entered the synagogue on October 27, 2018 and opened fire inside the building.
- Eleven people were killed and several others were injured, including congregants and responding police officers.
- The event prompted a large-scale law-enforcement response and a prolonged scene investigation.

## Investigation and legal proceedings

- Federal authorities brought charges that included hate-crime and firearms counts.
- The case proceeded in federal court, and prosecutors sought the death penalty.
- In 2023, the perpetrator was sentenced to death following conviction.

## Aftermath and broader impact

- The shooting intensified public attention on antisemitism, violent extremism, and security at houses of worship.
- It led to renewed discussions about protecting religious communities and the role of online radicalization.
- Memorials and community support efforts in Pittsburgh became a focal point for solidarity and remembrance.
