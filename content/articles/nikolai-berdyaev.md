## Summary

Nikolai Berdyaev was a Russian religious philosopher who became one of the most important Christian existentialist thinkers of the 20th century. He was expelled from the Soviet Union in 1922 and settled in Paris, where he became a leading voice of Russian Orthodox thought in exile. His works on freedom, creativity, and the destiny of man challenged both communism and bourgeois capitalism. His philosophy emphasized human freedom as the primary spiritual value.
