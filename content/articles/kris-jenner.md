## Summary

Kris Jenner, born Kristen Mary Houghton on November 5, 1955, in San Diego, California, is an American media personality, businesswoman, and talent manager best known as the matriarch of the Kardashian-Jenner family. She rose to global prominence through the long-running reality television series *Keeping Up with the Kardashians* (2007–2021), which chronicled her family's daily lives and business ventures across 20 seasons on E!.

Often self-described as the "momager" of her children, Jenner has been the primary architect behind the Kardashian-Jenner brand empire, managing the careers of her daughters Kim, Kourtney, Khloé, Kendall, and Kylie, as well as son Rob. Her business acumen transformed a family of relative obscurity into one of the most commercially powerful dynasties in modern entertainment, generating billions of dollars in revenue across beauty, fashion, and media.

## Early Life & Background

Jenner grew up in San Diego, California, and attended Clairemont High School. At age 17, she met professional golfer Robert Kardashian while working as a flight attendant. She married Kardashian in 1978; the couple had four children — Kourtney (1979), Kim (1980), Khloé (1984), and Rob (1987) — before divorcing in 1991. Her social circle during the 1970s and 1980s included O.J. Simpson and other Los Angeles celebrities, a connection that would later draw intense public attention during the 1994–1995 Simpson murder trial, in which Robert Kardashian served as defense counsel.

In 1991, Jenner married Olympic gold medalist Bruce Jenner (now Caitlyn Jenner), with whom she had two daughters, Kendall (1995) and Kylie (1996). They separated in 2013 and divorced in 2015.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Jenner's pivot to media began in 2007 when she negotiated a deal with Ryan Seacrest Productions and E! Entertainment to produce *Keeping Up with the Kardashians*. The show premiered in October 2007 and became one of the highest-rated cable reality programs of its era, running for 20 seasons over 14 years. Jenner served as an executive producer throughout its run and negotiated a reported $100 million deal with E! in 2017 to extend the series.

She also brokered major endorsement and brand deals for her children, including Kim Kardashian's SKIMS shapewear (valued at over $4 billion as of 2023) and Kylie Jenner's Kylie Cosmetics, which sold a 51% stake to Coty Inc. in 2020 for approximately $600 million. In 2022, the family transitioned to Hulu with *The Kardashians*, continuing their reality TV presence under Jenner's management.

## Key Achievements

- Executive produced *Keeping Up with the Kardashians* for all 20 seasons (2007–2021)
- Negotiated the family's $100 million E! network renewal deal in 2017
- Managed Kylie Cosmetics' partial sale to Coty Inc. for ~$600 million in 2020
- Co-founded Safely, an eco-friendly cleaning products brand, in 2021
- Produced multiple spin-off series including *Kourtney and Kim Take New York* and *Khloé & Lamar*

## Controversies & Criticism

Jenner has faced persistent criticism for monetizing her family's personal lives, including sensitive events such as Kim Kardashian's 2007 sex tape leak — which many critics alleged she helped publicize to launch Kim's career. She has denied deliberately orchestrating the tape's release. Her management of Kylie's public image, particularly the marketing of Kylie as a "self-made" billionaire, drew widespread scrutiny. Additionally, her handling of family members' personal struggles on camera has been critiqued as exploitative by media commentators.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Jenner redefined the concept of celebrity management by centralizing family branding and turning reality television into a direct-to-consumer marketing vehicle. Her model — blending personal narrative with product promotion — has been widely imitated and studied in business and media circles. She is credited with pioneering the modern influencer-to-entrepreneur pipeline, demonstrated most clearly through Kylie Jenner's cosmetics empire. As of the mid-2020s, the Kardashian-Jenner family's combined business valuations place Jenner among the most commercially influential managers in entertainment history.
