## Summary
Huang Yong was a Chinese serial killer who murdered 17 teenage boys in Henan Province between 2001 and 2003. He lured them to his home with promises of internet access and video games, then tortured and strangled them. He was executed by lethal injection in 2003.

## Early Life
Born October 18, 1974, in Luohe, Henan Province. He grew up in poverty and had limited education. He worked odd jobs and lived alone. He was described as quiet and unremarkable. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 2001 and 2003, Huang lured teenage boys to his home with offers of internet access and video games. He tied them to a rack he had built, tortured them over several days, and strangled them. He buried the bodies near his home. All 17 victims were boys aged 12-17. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Huang's final intended victim, 17-year-old Zhang Liang, escaped and reported him to police on November 9, 2003. Police found evidence of 17 murders at his home. He was quickly tried, convicted, and executed by lethal injection on December 26, 2003. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case led to improved child safety awareness programs in rural China [^4]
- Prompted local authorities to take missing children reports more seriously
- Led to the installation of internet cafes as supervised alternatives for youth

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered 17 teenage boys [^5]
- Lured victims with technology access
- Tortured victims over days before killing them
- Targeted young teens from poor families
- His crimes exposed lack of supervision for rural youth
