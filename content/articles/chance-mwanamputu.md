## Summary

Leader of the Kata Katanga separatist militia who launched an attack on the southern city of Lubumbashi. His forces terrorized communities in southern DRC.

## Background: Katanga and Separatist Politics

- Katanga (often referred to today as the former Katanga region, later divided into multiple provinces) is a mineral-rich area in the southeast of the Democratic Republic of the Congo.
- The region has a history of political separatism dating back to the early post-independence period, with recurring narratives centered on autonomy, resource control, and local identity.

## Kata Katanga Militia (High-level)

- Kata Katanga has been described in reporting as a separatist armed group aiming to revive or advance a Katanga secessionist project.
- Such militias often operate through small, mobile formations and rely on local grievances, coercion, and opportunistic recruitment.
- The presence of valuable mining assets and weak security institutions has contributed to cycles of armed mobilization in parts of the region.

## Lubumbashi Attack

- Lubumbashi is one of the DRC’s largest cities and a major economic hub in the south.
- The 2013 attack linked to Mwanamputu drew attention because it targeted a major urban center rather than remote territory.
- Public accounts emphasize intimidation and violence against civilians as part of the militia’s broader operational footprint.

## Arrest and Legal/Political Aftermath

- Mwanamputu was arrested in 2014 in connection with separatist attacks.
- Cases involving militia leaders in the DRC frequently intersect with broader questions about disarmament, reintegration, and state authority.

## Broader Significance

- The Mwanamputu case illustrates how separatist ideologies, local power struggles, and security vacuums can converge into episodic violence.
- It is also often discussed alongside other armed-group dynamics in the DRC, including competition over territory, cross-border influences, and contested legitimacy.
