## Summary

Assassination of Senator Robert F. Kennedy at the Ambassador Hotel in Los Angeles on June 5, 1968 by Sirhan Sirhan. Kennedy had just won the California Democratic primary.

## Event Overview

- Robert F. Kennedy (RFK) was a U.S. senator and a major candidate for the Democratic presidential nomination in 1968.
- The shooting took place at the **Ambassador Hotel** in Los Angeles shortly after a primary-night speech.

## Shooter and Criminal Case

- **Sirhan Sirhan** was arrested at the scene and later convicted.
- The case has involved repeated legal proceedings over many decades, including parole hearings and post-conviction petitions.

## Immediate Aftermath

- Kennedy died the day after the shooting; his death occurred during a period of intense political violence in the United States.
- The assassination affected the trajectory of the 1968 election and became a defining event in the modern political history of the Kennedy family.

## Investigation and Public Debate (high level)

- Official findings concluded Sirhan acted alone, but alternative theories have circulated in media and popular culture.
- Debates often focus on questions such as ballistic analysis, witness accounts, and the sequence of shots; major claims have been considered in court proceedings.
