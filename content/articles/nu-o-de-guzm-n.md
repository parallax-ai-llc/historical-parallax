## Summary

Spanish conquistador whose conquest of western Mexico was marked by mass enslavement and torture of indigenous people.

## Overview

- Nuño de Guzmán was a Spanish colonial official and conquistador active in early 16th-century New Spain.
- He is most associated with the western Mexican region later known as Nueva Galicia.
- Historical accounts commonly describe his campaigns and governance as exceptionally violent toward Indigenous communities.

## Roles and Offices (high level)

- President of the First Audiencia of Mexico (a governing/judicial body established after the fall of the initial post-conquest administration).
- Governor of Nueva Galicia (title varies by source; generally refers to his leadership in the region he conquered and organized).

## Additional Career Notes

- Guzmán led expeditions west and northwest from central Mexico, pursuing labor, tribute, and territorial control.
- He founded or promoted the establishment of several Spanish settlements in the region (settlement histories vary by city and date).
- He became a political rival of Hernán Cortés’ faction, reflecting broader power struggles among Spanish elites in New Spain.

## Additional Timeline (selected)

- 1520s: Arrived in New Spain and rose into colonial administration.
- 1528–1530: Served as president of the First Audiencia of Mexico (commonly cited range).
- Early 1530s: Led campaigns in western Mexico during the formation of Nueva Galicia.
- Later years: Faced legal actions and imprisonment connected to abuses of power reported by contemporaries and later chroniclers.

## Controversies and Legacy

- Sources frequently attribute to Guzmán:
  - Mass enslavement (including illegal enslavement under Spanish law as it evolved).
  - Torture and coercion to extract information, labor, and valuables.
  - Large-scale disruption and depopulation in areas targeted by his expeditions.
- In many modern historical treatments, he is cited as a prominent example of the violent exploitation that accompanied Spanish expansion.

## Related Terms and Places

- Nueva Galicia — colonial jurisdiction centered in western Mexico.
- Guadalajara — a major city in the region with early colonial foundations and relocations in the 16th century.
- Indigenous polities of western Mexico — diverse communities affected by conquest, raiding, and forced labor systems.
