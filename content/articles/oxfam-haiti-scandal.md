## Summary

Oxfam staff hired sex workers including minors in Haiti following the 2010 earthquake. The scandal exposed widespread sexual exploitation by humanitarian workers in disaster zones.

## Background

- Haiti suffered a catastrophic earthquake in January 2010, prompting a large-scale international humanitarian response.
- Oxfam, an international NGO, operated in Haiti as part of that response, including aid and development programs.

## What the scandal alleged

- Multiple Oxfam-affiliated staff in Haiti were accused of sexual exploitation and abuse, including hiring sex workers.
- Reporting indicated some allegations involved underage victims.
- The controversy expanded beyond individual misconduct to questions about oversight, safeguarding, and institutional accountability.

## Investigations and reporting

- The issue drew major public attention in 2018 following investigative reporting by UK media.
- Accounts described internal reports and disciplinary actions taken earlier, but also criticized the organization’s handling and disclosure.
- The scandal became part of a wider set of concerns about sexual misconduct by aid workers across different organizations and crisis settings.

## Organizational and sector response

- Oxfam publicly apologized and stated it would strengthen safeguarding policies and practices.
- Donors, regulators, and partner governments pressed for clearer standards, transparency, and consequences.
- The episode accelerated sector-wide efforts focused on:
  - Preventing exploitation and abuse
  - Improving whistleblowing channels and survivor-centered reporting
  - Vetting and background checks across organizations
  - Clearer disciplinary processes and cross-NGO information sharing

## Impact

- The scandal damaged trust in humanitarian institutions and triggered reviews of funding and partnerships.
- It highlighted the power imbalance risks present in disaster and poverty contexts.
- It reinforced the need for robust governance, independent investigation pathways, and accountability mechanisms.

## Key takeaways

- Safeguarding is not only a policy issue but an operational requirement with measurable enforcement.
- Transparency and timely disclosure can be critical to maintaining public legitimacy in humanitarian work.
- Strong controls are especially important in high-pressure field environments where supervision is difficult.
