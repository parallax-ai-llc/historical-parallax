## Summary

Iranian serial killer who murdered at least 19 people in Tehran between 1994 and 1997. He drugged victims with spiked drinks before strangling them.

## Additional Context

### Modus operandi

Accounts of the case describe Borujerdi approaching victims and offering drinks that were allegedly drugged, enabling him to subdue them before strangulation. Such details are typically reconstructed from investigation reports, witness statements, and later court findings.

### Investigation and arrest

The killings were reported to have occurred over several years in Tehran. Serial cases can be difficult to connect early, and investigative narratives often focus on how patterns were identified and how suspects were narrowed down.

### Trial and execution

He was convicted in Iran and executed by hanging in 2002. The case is cited as one of Iran’s more widely reported serial murder prosecutions of the 1990s.

### Caution on numbers

Victim counts in serial cases can vary by source (confirmed convictions vs. suspected additional cases). Summaries commonly report “at least” a baseline number tied to the court record.
