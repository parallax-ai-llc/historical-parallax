## Summary

Series of airplane hijackings by the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine in the late 1960s-70s that pioneered political hijacking as a weapon. The 1970 Dawsons Field hijackings were the most dramatic.

## Background

The Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine (PFLP) was involved in a series of high-profile aircraft hijackings, particularly around the late 1960s and early 1970s. These events are commonly discussed as part of a broader period of politically motivated aviation attacks.

## Historical Significance

The hijackings contributed to international attention on the Israeli–Palestinian conflict and led to increased focus on aviation security. Governments and airlines expanded screening, security protocols, and international coordination in response to repeated incidents.
