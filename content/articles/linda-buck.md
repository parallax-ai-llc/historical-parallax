## Summary

Linda Buck is an American biologist who shared the 2004 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine with Richard Axel for discovering the odorant receptor gene family and elucidating the organization of the olfactory system. Her work revealed how 1,000 different receptor genes enable humans to detect and discriminate approximately 10,000 different odors.
