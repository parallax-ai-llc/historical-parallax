## Background

Alexander Vinnik (Russian: Александр Винник) is a Russian national who became widely known in connection with the BTC-e cryptocurrency exchange. Reporting and legal filings have described him as a co-founder and as an alleged operator who directed and supervised aspects of BTC-e’s operations and finances during the exchange’s active years.

## BTC-e and U.S. allegations

BTC-e was a cryptocurrency exchange that operated during the early growth period of bitcoin and other digital assets. U.S. authorities alleged that the platform processed large volumes of cryptocurrency transactions connected to criminal activity and that it functioned as an unlicensed money services business.

High-level themes that commonly appear in coverage of the case include:

- Use of cryptocurrency exchanges to move funds across borders rapidly.
- Compliance failures related to identity verification and anti-money-laundering controls.
- The difficulty of coordinating investigations and prosecutions across multiple jurisdictions.

## Arrest and extradition process

Vinnik was arrested in Greece in July 2017 following a U.S. request connected to money laundering allegations. Over subsequent years, competing extradition requests and legal proceedings involved multiple countries, including Russia and France, before he was ultimately transferred to other jurisdictions.

A simplified sequence (dates vary by jurisdiction and procedure) is:

- 2017: Arrested in Greece after U.S. request.
- 2018–2020: Additional proceedings and extradition developments involving other countries, including France.
- 2022: Extradited from Greece to the United States to face charges.
- 2024: Reported guilty plea in the United States to a money-laundering-related conspiracy charge.

## Later developments

After court proceedings and custody in multiple jurisdictions, public reporting has described Vinnik as being returned to Russia in the context of a prisoner exchange.
