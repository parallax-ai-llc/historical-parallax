## Summary

Bahya ibn Paquda was a Jewish philosopher and judge from Islamic Spain who wrote Hovot ha-Levavot (Duties of the Heart), one of the most influential works of Jewish ethics and spirituality. He argued that Judaism required inner devotion and spiritual intention, not just external observance of commandments. His work combined Islamic Sufi influences with Jewish ethical thought. It remains one of the most widely read Jewish ethical texts.
