## Summary

C. S. Lewis was a British author, theologian, and Christian apologist who became one of the most influential Christian writers of the 20th century. His works include The Chronicles of Narnia, Mere Christianity, and The Screwtape Letters. He converted from atheism to Christianity and became a powerful defender of the faith. He taught at Oxford and Cambridge universities.
