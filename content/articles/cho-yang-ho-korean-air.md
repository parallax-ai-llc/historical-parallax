## Summary

Former Korean Air chairman convicted of embezzling company funds. His family became notorious for the 'nut rage incident' when his daughter forced a plane to return to the gate over macadamia nuts.

## Additional Context

- **Role at Hanjin/Korean Air:** Cho Yang-ho led the Hanjin Group’s flagship airline for decades and was a central figure in South Korea’s chaebol corporate ecosystem.
- **Governance controversies:** Media coverage frequently discussed allegations of weak internal controls, opaque related-party transactions, and the power of controlling families within chaebol structures.
- **Public image and "nut rage":** The 2014 incident involving his daughter (Heather Cho) became a widely cited example of abuse of power by elite families and intensified scrutiny of Korean Air’s leadership.

## Legal and Business Aftermath

- **Criminal proceedings:** Court findings and prosecutions related to alleged embezzlement and breach of trust were part of a broader pattern of enforcement actions involving large conglomerates.
- **Leadership transition:** Following Cho’s death, governance and succession questions continued within the group, including the role of family members and board oversight.
