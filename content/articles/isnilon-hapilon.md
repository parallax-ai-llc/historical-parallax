## Summary

Filipino militant who was designated by ISIS as the 'emir' of its Southeast Asia branch. He led the 2017 Siege of Marawi, a five-month battle that devastated the Philippine city. Killed by Philippine troops during the siege.

## Background and Affiliation

- Hapilon was associated with militant activity in the southern Philippines, a region that has seen multiple insurgent and extremist groups.
- He was linked to Abu Sayyaf, a group known for kidnappings, attacks, and hostage-taking.

## ISIS Alignment

- Reporting described him as a key figure in efforts to align local militant factions with ISIS.
- He was named by ISIS as an "emir" for Southeast Asia in the context of building a regional affiliate structure.

## Siege of Marawi (2017)

- The Siege of Marawi began after clashes around an attempt to capture him, escalating into an extended urban battle.
- The fighting involved multiple militant factions and resulted in significant destruction and displacement.
- The siege became one of the most prominent ISIS-linked conflicts in Southeast Asia.

## Death and Aftermath

- Hapilon was killed by Philippine forces during the later stages of the Marawi fighting.
- The battle’s aftermath included reconstruction efforts and continued security operations against remaining militant networks.

## Designations and Security Context

- Governments and security agencies treated him as a high-priority target due to his role in linking local militant networks to ISIS narratives.
- The Marawi episode influenced regional counterterrorism planning and monitoring of cross-border extremist travel.
