## Summary

Pedro Sánchez Pérez-Castejón (born February 29, 1972) has served as Prime Minister of Spain since June 2018, leading the Spanish Socialist Workers' Party (PSOE). Sánchez came to power through Spain's first successful no-confidence vote in democratic history, ousting Mariano Rajoy. He has since survived multiple elections, formed historic coalition governments, granted controversial amnesty to Catalan separatists, and become one of Europe's most resilient — and polarizing — left-wing leaders.

## National Politics

### Member of Congress (2009-2011, 2013-)

**Spokesperson Roles**:
- Economic spokesperson
- Ascended within PSOE rapidly

### PSOE Leadership Crisis (2014-2017)

**First Election**:
- Won party primary (July 2014) against Eduardo Madina
- Took over weakened PSOE after years of decline

**2015-2016 Elections**:
- Inconclusive results, no government formed
- PSOE worst-ever results in second 2016 election
- Old guard ("barons") forced his resignation October 2016
- Resigned as MP rather than abstain on Rajoy investiture
- "Resistance" tour across Spain rebuilding base

**Comeback** (2017):
- Won unprecedented second primary May 2017
- Defeated establishment candidate Susana Díaz
- Reasserted control of PSOE machine

---

## Path to Power

### No-Confidence Vote (June 2018)

**Gürtel Verdict**:
- Spanish court convicted PP officials in massive corruption case
- Sánchez filed motion of no-confidence against Rajoy
- Won with support from Podemos, Catalan and Basque parties
- First successful no-confidence vote in Spanish democracy
- Became PM June 2, 2018

---

## Premiership

### Minority Government (2018-2019)

**Initial Tenure**:
- Cabinet with majority women (first in Spanish history)
- Exhumation of Franco from Valley of the Fallen
- Limited legislative power
- Failed budget triggered April 2019 election

### Coalition with Unidas Podemos (2020-2023)

**First Coalition Government**:
- First coalition cabinet since Spanish democracy began
- Unidas Podemos junior partner
- COVID-19 pandemic dominated early term
- Strict lockdowns, EU recovery funds
- Labor reform, minimum living income
- Euthanasia legalization, gender violence reforms

### Second Term (2023-Present)

**Inconclusive 2023 Election**:
- PP won most seats but couldn't form majority
- Sánchez negotiated with regional parties
- Junts (Catalan separatist) support secured via amnesty deal
- Re-elected November 2023

**Sumar Coalition**:
- Replaced Unidas Podemos with Sumar movement
- Yolanda Díaz Deputy PM
- Continued left-wing policy agenda

## Catalan Crisis

### Approach

**Dialogue Strategy**:
- Pardoned Catalan independence leaders (2021)
- Repealed sedition law (2022)
- Amnesty law (2024) for those involved in 2017 referendum
- Deeply controversial, accused of enabling separatism

**Junts Support**:
- Carles Puigdemont's party gave Sánchez parliamentary majority
- Right opposition called amnesty unconstitutional power deal
- Massive protests by right and center-right
