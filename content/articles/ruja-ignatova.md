## Summary

Bulgarian fraudster known as the Cryptoqueen who founded OneCoin, a $4 billion cryptocurrency scam. She vanished in 2017 and was placed on the FBIs Most Wanted list.

## Background

- Ruja Ignatova was a prominent public face of OneCoin, appearing at large promotional events and presenting the project as a breakthrough alternative to Bitcoin.
- Her public messaging emphasized rapid growth, exclusivity, and the promise of outsized returns, themes commonly associated with high-risk investment frauds.

## OneCoin and Public Profile

- The OneCoin operation relied heavily on conferences, staged product launches, and a global network of promoters.
- Investigators and later court proceedings described OneCoin as operating without an open, independently verifiable blockchain in the manner of major cryptocurrencies.

## Disappearance

- After 2017, Ignatova disappeared from public view and has remained at large.
- Her disappearance became a central element of the story as law enforcement actions against OneCoin expanded internationally.

## Law Enforcement Actions (High-Level)

- U.S. authorities brought charges alleging fraud and money laundering, and she has been treated as a fugitive.
- In 2022, the FBI placed her on its Ten Most Wanted list.

## Legacy and Public Attention

- The “Cryptoqueen” nickname became widely associated with Ignatova in media coverage of OneCoin.
- The case is frequently cited as a cautionary example of how cryptocurrency-themed marketing can be used to disguise classic investment fraud patterns.
