## Definition and Naming

- **"Unabomber"** is a widely used nickname for **Theodore John Kaczynski (Ted Kaczynski)**.
- The term originated from an FBI case designation associated with early targets involving **universities** and **airlines** (often rendered as **UNABOM**), later popularized in media as "Unabomber".

## Overview (High-Level)

- Kaczynski carried out a **nationwide mail-bombing campaign** in the United States.
- The campaign spanned **1978–1995**.
- Reported totals for the series of attacks include **3 people killed** and **23 injured**.

## Manifesto and Publicity

- Kaczynski authored the manifesto **"Industrial Society and Its Future" (1995)**.
- The text argued against industrial/technological society and became central to public understanding of the case.
- The publication and analysis of the manifesto played a key role in identifying the suspect.

## Investigation and Arrest (High-Level)

- The case is often cited as a major, long-running U.S. federal investigation.
- Kaczynski was arrested in **1996**.
- He was ultimately convicted and received **multiple life sentences**.
