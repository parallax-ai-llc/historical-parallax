## Summary

Ongoing conflict between Mexican security forces and drug cartels that has killed over 350,000 people since 2006. President Calderon deployed the military against cartels, sparking unprecedented violence.

## Context

The “Mexican Drug War” is a commonly used label for overlapping conflicts involving the Mexican state, organized-crime groups, and local actors. Analysts often stress that it is not a single war with a single front, but a shifting set of rivalries and enforcement campaigns.

## Structural factors often discussed

- Fragmentation of cartels into competing groups.
- Geographic importance of trafficking corridors and border crossings.
- Corruption risks and institutional capacity constraints.
- Demand for illicit drugs in regional and international markets.

## Human impact (high-level)

- The conflict is associated with high levels of homicide, disappearances, and displacement.
- Civilian communities can be affected through extortion, forced recruitment, and contested local governance.

## Policy debates

- Debates include the role of military deployments, policing reform, anti-corruption efforts, and harm-reduction approaches.
- Measurement is contested: different datasets and definitions can yield different counts and interpretations.
