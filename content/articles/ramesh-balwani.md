## Background

- Balwani is a U.S.-based former executive who became widely known as the former president and COO of Theranos.
- He was involved in both operations and fundraising as the company promoted a blood-testing system that later proved inaccurate and misleading.

## Role at Theranos (high level)

- Served as a senior operating executive (commonly described as president/COO).
- Worked closely with founder Elizabeth Holmes and was frequently described as a key decision-maker in the company’s day-to-day execution.
- Prosecutors argued that leadership communications and internal knowledge of device limitations diverged from public claims made to investors, partners, and patients.

## Criminal case

- 2018: U.S. federal prosecutors charged Balwani in connection with alleged Theranos fraud.
- 2022: Convicted on multiple counts related to investor and patient fraud (separate trial from Holmes).
- Sentencing: He received a substantial federal prison sentence for his role in the scheme (see Wikipedia for the current figure and any appellate updates).[^1]
