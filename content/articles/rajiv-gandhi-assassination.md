## Summary

Assassination of former Indian PM Rajiv Gandhi by LTTE suicide bomber Thenmozhi Rajaratnam at a campaign rally in Sriperumbudur on May 21, 1991.

## Context

- Rajiv Gandhi was a former Prime Minister of India and a central figure in the Indian National Congress.
- The assassination occurred during an election campaign event in **Sriperumbudur**, near Chennai (Tamil Nadu).
- The attack is widely discussed in connection with India’s involvement in the Sri Lankan civil conflict and tensions involving the **Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE)**.

## Attack and Immediate Aftermath (High-Level)

- The killing is commonly described as a **suicide bombing** carried out at close range in a public setting.
- The incident resulted in the death of Rajiv Gandhi and additional casualties among bystanders.
- It triggered a large national security response and became one of the most consequential political killings in modern Indian history.

## Investigation and Prosecution

- Indian authorities attributed the plot to the **LTTE** and pursued a long-running investigation.
- The case led to arrests, trials, and convictions of multiple individuals tied to the conspiracy.
- The legal process and sentencing outcomes have been periodically revisited in public debate, including discussions about clemency and the duration of imprisonment.

## Historical Significance

- The assassination reshaped:
  - Indian domestic politics in the early 1990s,
  - counterterrorism and protective security practices for political leaders,
  - India’s policy posture toward the LTTE and the Sri Lankan conflict.
