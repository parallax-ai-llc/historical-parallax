## Summary

NSO Group's Pegasus spyware was used by governments to hack the phones of journalists, activists, and politicians including heads of state. Over 50,000 phone numbers targeted.

## Technical and Historical Context

### Background

- Pegasus is a piece of spyware developed and sold by the Israeli company NSO Group.
- It has been marketed to government clients as a tool for law-enforcement and intelligence investigations, and has been the subject of extensive human-rights and press-freedom concerns.

### What it can do (high-level)

- Compromise iOS and Android devices and access data on the device (e.g., messages, call logs, contacts, photos) depending on the version and device state.
- Enable surveillance capabilities such as microphone/camera activation and location tracking once a device is compromised.

### How infections have occurred

- Reporting and forensic write-ups describe both **one-click** (user interaction required) and **zero-click** (no user interaction) attack chains.
- Attack chains have historically leveraged previously-unknown vulnerabilities ("zero-days") in widely used components/apps, then been updated as platforms patched them.

## Notable Public Milestones

- **2016**: Public reporting associated Pegasus with targeted attacks against human-rights defenders, supported by mobile forensic analysis in subsequent investigations.
- **2021**: The **Pegasus Project** investigation (a collaboration involving Forbidden Stories and Amnesty International, with multiple media partners) reported large-scale targeting allegations and published findings based on a leaked list of phone numbers and device forensics.

## Impact and Responses

- **Litigation**: WhatsApp (Meta) sued NSO Group in 2019 over alleged exploitation of WhatsApp for spyware delivery; Apple filed suit in 2021 related to alleged targeting of Apple users and services, then later moved to dismiss its own case while continuing broader support for anti-spyware efforts.
- **Sanctions / restrictions**: In 2021, the U.S. Department of Commerce added NSO Group to the Entity List, increasing restrictions on U.S. exports to the company.
- **Policy debate**: Pegasus became a flagship case in discussions about regulating commercial spyware, export controls, transparency, and safeguards to prevent abuse.
