## Background

- Flores is commonly described in secondary sources as one of the early organizers associated with the formation of the Mexican Mafia (also known as *La Eme*) within the California prison system.
- The Mexican Mafia emerged in the mid-to-late 1950s among Mexican-American inmates and became one of the most influential prison gangs in the United States.

## Historical Context

The gang’s early growth is typically discussed in relation to:

- The post–World War II expansion of the California prison system and the concentration of incarcerated populations.
- Informal inmate social structures that evolved into more formalized groups capable of coordinating protection, retaliation, and contraband activity.
- The later development of alliances and rivalries with other prison and street gangs.

## Role and Significance

High-level descriptions of Flores’s significance generally emphasize:

- Participation in early leadership/organizing efforts during the group’s foundational period.
- Contribution to establishing a durable prison-based organization that later extended influence beyond prison walls through affiliated street networks.

## Legacy

- Accounts of early founders are often used to explain how *La Eme* transitioned from a small inmate group into a long-lasting criminal organization.
- Discussions of Flores frequently appear in broader overviews of Mexican Mafia history rather than detailed, independently sourced biographies.
