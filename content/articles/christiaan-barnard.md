## Summary

Christiaan Barnard was a South African cardiac surgeon who performed the first successful human-to-human heart transplant in 1967. The operation at Groote Schuur Hospital in Cape Town was one of the most significant medical milestones of the 20th century. Though his first patient survived only 18 days, the achievement revolutionized organ transplantation surgery. Barnard became one of the most famous doctors in history.
