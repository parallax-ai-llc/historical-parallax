## Overview

Wirecard AG was a German payment processing and financial services company whose collapse in 2020 became a major European corporate scandal. It is frequently cited as a landmark case of accounting fraud, governance failure, and regulatory shortcomings.

## What the Company Did (high-level)

At a high level, Wirecard’s business was described as providing:

- Card payment processing and acquiring services
- Digital payments infrastructure for merchants
- Financial services offerings linked to payment products

## Alleged/Reported Fraud (high-level)

Public reporting and subsequent investigations centered on claims that significant assets and profits were misstated. A commonly cited figure is **€1.9 billion** allegedly missing or fabricated, often described as cash held in trustee accounts.

Key themes discussed in coverage of the scandal include:

- Use of third-party partners and complex structures to report revenue
- Reliance on supposed trustee accounts and documentation later disputed
- Repeated external warnings and investigative reporting prior to the collapse

## Collapse and Aftermath

In 2020, Wirecard’s credibility and solvency unraveled rapidly following scrutiny of its financial statements. The company entered insolvency proceedings, and the episode triggered:

- Criminal investigations and prosecutions of executives and related parties
- Political and regulatory inquiries into oversight failures
- Corporate governance reforms and debate across Germany and the EU

## Broader Significance

Wirecard is often referenced in discussions about:

- Audit quality and the role of external auditors
- Financial regulator mandates and enforcement capability
- Market incentives that can discourage early whistleblowing
- The risks of opaque corporate structures in fintech and payments
