## Summary

Ardem Patapoutian is a Lebanese-born Armenian-American molecular biologist who shared the 2021 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for discoveries of receptors for pressure and touch. He identified the PIEZO1 and PIEZO2 ion channels, which are the sensors that enable cells to detect mechanical pressure. His discoveries explain how touch and proprioception work at the molecular level.
