## Summary

Kelvin Kiptum set the marathon WR of 2:00:35 at 2023 Chicago. He died in a car accident at age 24.

## Rise to the Elite Level

Kiptum emerged quickly at the top of men’s marathon running, with early international attention focused on how rapidly he progressed from lower-profile road races to major-marathon victories. His breakthrough period is commonly described as unusually fast even by elite-distance-running standards.

## Major Marathon Performances (high level)

- Won the Valencia Marathon (debut at the distance) in 2022
- Won the London Marathon in 2023
- Won the Chicago Marathon in 2023, setting a new world record

## World Record Context

The 2:00:35 performance in Chicago was widely presented as a landmark moment because it moved the men’s marathon world record significantly closer to the two-hour barrier under record-eligible conditions. Record discussions typically reference course certification, pacing and conditions, and ratification processes overseen by World Athletics.

## Training and Team

Public profiles often linked Kiptum to Kenya’s high-altitude training culture and to structured coaching and support teams that guide marathon preparation. Coverage also highlighted how modern marathon success increasingly depends on coordinated planning around training blocks, travel, and major-race scheduling.

## Death and Legacy

Kiptum’s death in a 2024 road accident prompted widespread tributes across athletics, with many emphasizing the sense of unfinished potential given his age and trajectory. He is frequently remembered for the combination of record-level speed and a very brief period at the sport’s peak.
