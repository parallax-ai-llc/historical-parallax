## Summary

Alhazen (Ibn al-Haytham) is the father of modern optics. His Book of Optics established light travels in straight lines.

## Additional Context

### Contributions to optics

In the *Book of Optics* (Arabic: *Kitāb al-Manāẓir*), Ibn al‑Haytham argued that vision occurs when light enters the eye (rather than rays emitted from the eye), helping to overturn older emission theories. He investigated reflection and refraction and analyzed the behavior of light using geometry.

### Experimental method

Alhazen is often highlighted for his emphasis on observation, controlled experimentation, and the careful testing of hypotheses. While modern “scientific method” language post-dates him, his approach is frequently presented as an important precursor to later experimental science.

### Mathematics and astronomy

Beyond optics, he wrote on mathematical problems (including geometry) and engaged with astronomical questions and critiques of earlier models. Many of his works circulated widely in the Islamic world and later influenced European scholarship through translation.

### Legacy

His writings became foundational references in medieval optics and shaped later thinkers working on vision, light, and perspective.
