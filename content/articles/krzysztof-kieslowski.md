## Summary

Krzysztof Kieslowski was a Polish filmmaker whose deeply philosophical works explore moral and metaphysical questions with extraordinary humanity. His Dekalog series and the Three Colors trilogy (Blue, White, Red) are among the most acclaimed works in cinema history. His unexpected death at 54 cut short one of cinema's most profound voices.
