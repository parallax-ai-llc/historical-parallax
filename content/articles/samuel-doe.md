## Summary

Liberian military dictator who seized power in 1980 at age 28 in a bloody coup. His ethnic-based rule sparked the First Liberian Civil War. He was captured, tortured, and executed by rebel leader Prince Johnson.

## Rise to power

Doe was a noncommissioned officer in the Armed Forces of Liberia who led the 1980 coup that ended the long dominance of the True Whig Party. The coup and its aftermath reshaped Liberian politics and introduced a period of military rule.

## Rule and repression

- **Authoritarian governance:** Doe's government was characterized by political repression and recurring violence.
- **Ethnic and factional tensions:** His consolidation of power deepened divisions and contributed to cycles of retaliation.
- **International context:** During the Cold War, Liberia's strategic position shaped aspects of foreign engagement with Doe's regime.

## Civil war and downfall

Armed opposition intensified in the late 1980s, and the First Liberian Civil War began in 1989. Doe was later captured by forces associated with Prince Johnson and killed in 1990, an event that became one of the most notorious episodes of the conflict.

## Legacy

Doe remains a polarizing figure in Liberian history, often discussed in relation to the breakdown of state institutions, the origins of prolonged civil conflict, and the challenges of post-war reconciliation.
