## Background

Kwon Oh-hyuk’s case is discussed in the context of homicide by poisoning, which often creates investigative difficulties because symptoms can mimic natural causes or accidental exposure.

## Poisoning investigations (general points)

- Detecting cyanide and similar toxins depends on rapid testing, preservation of samples, and careful reconstruction of exposure.
- Motive and opportunity can be difficult to establish without digital, financial, or witness corroboration.

## Case significance

- High-profile poisoning cases tend to trigger public discussion about forensic capacity and laboratory resources.
- They also highlight the importance of medical examiner systems and standardized toxicology protocols.

## Why this matters

Poisoning homicides are a recurring blind spot in criminal-justice systems; a small number of technical failures can change the outcome of investigations.
