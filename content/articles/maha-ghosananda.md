## Summary

Maha Ghosananda was a Cambodian Buddhist monk who became known as the 'Gandhi of Cambodia' for his peace activism during and after the Cambodian genocide. He organized annual Dhammayietra (pilgrimage of truth) peace walks across Cambodia's minefields and battlefields. He helped rebuild Cambodian Buddhism after the Khmer Rouge destroyed nearly all monasteries and killed most monks. He was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize multiple times.
