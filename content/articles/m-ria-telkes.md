## Summary

Mária Telkes was a Hungarian-American biophysicist and inventor known as the 'Sun Queen' for her pioneering work in solar energy. She invented the first thermoelectric power generator and designed the first solar-heated house in 1948. Her work on solar distillation provided fresh water to sailors during World War II. Telkes held over 20 patents related to solar energy applications.
