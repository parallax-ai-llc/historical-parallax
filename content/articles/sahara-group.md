## Summary

Indian conglomerate led by Subrata Roy that raised $3.5 billion from 30 million small investors through illegal bond schemes. Roy was jailed for contempt when he refused to return the money. Released on parole, died in 2023.

## What the Group Was Known For

- **Conglomerate structure:** Sahara Group was described as a wide-ranging Indian business group with interests across finance and consumer-facing ventures, alongside high-visibility sponsorships and branding.
- **Regulatory dispute:** The group became internationally known primarily due to a long-running legal and regulatory battle over fundraising and investor refunds.

## Legal and Financial Themes (High Level)

- Large-scale retail fundraising raises questions about disclosure, suitability, and how regulators supervise products sold to millions of small investors.
- Court-supervised refund processes can take years, particularly when records, verification, and asset recovery are contested.

## Public Perception

- The case is often cited in discussions of investor protection in emerging markets and the challenges of enforcing compliance against politically connected business empires.
