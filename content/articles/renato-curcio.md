## Summary

Italian co-founder of the Red Brigades who led the militant leftist group in its early years of kidnappings and kneecappings. He served his sentence and now works as a publisher.

## Historical Context: Italy’s “Years of Lead”

Curcio’s political activity is commonly situated within the period known as Italy’s **Anni di piombo** (“Years of Lead”), marked by political polarization and violence from both far-left and far-right groups.

- The Red Brigades (Brigate Rosse) framed themselves as revolutionary militants.
- Italian authorities and courts treated the organization as a terrorist group responsible for kidnappings, shootings, and assassinations.

## The Red Brigades (Early Phase)

- Curcio is identified as a principal organizer and ideological figure in the group’s early development.
- The organization became known for tactics such as kidnappings and attacks intended to intimidate or punish targets.
- Over time, the group escalated beyond early actions into more lethal violence, becoming a central symbol of political terrorism in 1970s Italy.

## Arrest, Imprisonment, and Release

- Curcio was arrested in the 1970s and served a long prison sentence.
- His eventual release occurred after years of incarceration; by then, the Red Brigades had fragmented and the period of widespread political violence had declined.

## Later Work

- After prison, Curcio became involved in publishing.
- Public discussion of his later work often focuses on rehabilitation, memory of political violence, and how post-conflict societies handle former militants.
