## Summary

German serial killer known as the 'Angel of Bremen' who poisoned 15 people with arsenic over 15 years, including her parents, two husbands, her children, and her fiancé. She was the last person publicly executed in Bremen.

## Modus Operandi

- Contemporary accounts describe the use of arsenic as a poison, historically common in domestic settings because it could be obtained in products used for household and pest-control purposes.
- Many victims were close relatives or household members, which enabled repeated access over long periods without immediate suspicion.

## Trial and Public Memory

- The long timeline and number of deaths made the case a major public scandal in Bremen.
- She remains a notable example in German criminal history of serial poisoning within domestic and family settings.

## Key Points

- Serial poisoning cases often hinge on patterns noticed only after multiple deaths, especially when symptoms resemble common illnesses of the era.
- The execution is remembered as the last public execution in Bremen.
