## Summary

Brazilian businessman and former CEO of Odebrecht, the construction conglomerate at the center of Latin America's largest corruption scandal. The company paid over $3.5 billion in bribes to officials across 12 countries. He cooperated with prosecutors.

## Additional Context

### Odebrecht and the wider scandal

Odebrecht (also known as Odebrecht S.A.) grew from a Brazilian construction business into a diversified conglomerate with major infrastructure projects across Latin America and beyond. The company became synonymous with the *Operation Car Wash* (Lava Jato) era, when Brazilian prosecutors uncovered extensive bribery and illicit political financing tied to public contracts.

### Legal and reputational aftermath

Odebrecht-related cases became a transnational anti-corruption story: investigations and prosecutions spread across multiple jurisdictions, and the company entered into large settlement and leniency agreements. For many observers, the Odebrecht episode is cited as a turning point that showed how procurement, campaign finance, and state-owned enterprises can interact to create systemic corruption risks.

### Why this figure is often referenced

Marcelo Odebrecht is frequently discussed as:

- A symbol of the power (and vulnerability) of large contractors in state-driven development models.
- An example of how corporate compliance failures can scale when projects and political relationships span many countries.
- A high-profile cooperator whose testimony and evidence were used in broader investigations.
