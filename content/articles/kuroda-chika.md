## Summary

Kuroda Chika was a pioneering Japanese chemist who was the first Japanese woman to earn a bachelor's degree in science (1916) and the second to earn a Doctor of Science degree (1929). She studied the chemistry of natural pigments, particularly safflower and shellfish purple dyes, making significant contributions to organic chemistry.
