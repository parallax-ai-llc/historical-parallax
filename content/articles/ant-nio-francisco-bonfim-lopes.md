## Summary

Known as 'Nem da Rocinha,' he was the drug lord of Rocinha, Brazil's largest favela, controlling a $50 million-per-year cocaine operation. His arrest in 2011 required a military-scale operation.

## Additional context

Nem da Rocinha is commonly described as one of the leaders of the **Amigos dos Amigos (ADA)** criminal faction, and as the dominant figure in drug trafficking in Rocinha during the late 2000s and early 2010s.[^1]

Accounts in major press coverage and later reporting describe his rise as tied to **internal power shifts** within local trafficking networks, alongside the availability of transnational cocaine supply routes into Rio de Janeiro.[^1]

### Path to leadership (high level)

- Worked in low-wage jobs before entering the local criminal economy, and reportedly became an **enforcer/security figure** for ADA leadership.[^1]
- After leadership turmoil and killings within the group, he emerged as the **principal leader** in Rocinha in the late 2000s.[^1]

### Operations and control

Reporting attributes to his organization:

- A large, armed security structure used to defend territory and manage local enforcement.
- Revenue derived primarily from cocaine and other drug sales within Rio de Janeiro.
- Connections to upstream suppliers beyond Brazil’s borders.[^1]

### Arrest, prosecution, and state response

He was arrested in November 2011 while leaving Rocinha concealed in a vehicle, in an operation that drew intense public attention.[^1] Subsequent legal proceedings resulted in a prison sentence on drug-related charges, and the arrest was followed by a stronger, sustained state security presence in Rocinha (often discussed in the context of “pacification” policies).[^1]

### Media and biography

Nem’s case became a frequent reference point in discussions of Rio’s organized crime, favela governance, and public security policy. A published biography based on prison interviews has also contributed to international awareness of the case.[^1]
