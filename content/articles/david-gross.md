## Summary

David Gross is an American theoretical physicist who shared the 2004 Nobel Prize in Physics for the discovery of asymptotic freedom in the theory of strong interactions (quantum chromodynamics). This discovery explains why quarks behave as free particles at short distances but are confined at longer distances.
