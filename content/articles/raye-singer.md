## Summary

Raye (born Rachel Keen on October 24, 1997) is a British singer, songwriter, and musician whose career spans both behind-the-scenes songwriting and front-of-stage performance. Of Swiss-Ghanaian heritage and raised in South London, she established herself first as a prolific songwriter for major pop acts before breaking through as a solo artist in her own right.

Her 2023 debut album *My 21st Century Blues* marked a turning point not only in her career but in the broader music industry conversation around artist autonomy and label control. The album earned her six BRIT Award nominations in a single year — a record — and announced her as one of the most compelling voices in contemporary British music.

Raye's impact extends beyond chart performance. Her public dispute with her former label, Polydor Records, over the delay of her debut album sparked widespread discussion about power dynamics in the music industry, earning her the respect of peers and fans alike for her willingness to advocate for herself.

## Early Life & Background

Rachel Keen was born on October 24, 1997, in Croydon, South London, to a Swiss mother and a Ghanaian father. She grew up in Tooting and demonstrated an early aptitude for music, attending the BRIT School of Performing Arts and Technology in Croydon — the same institution that shaped the careers of Adele, Amy Winehouse, and Jessie J. Her training there honed both her vocal abilities and her instincts as a songwriter.

## Rise to Prominence

Raye signed with Polydor Records as a teenager and quickly became a sought-after songwriter, co-writing hits for artists including Beyoncé, John Legend, and Charli XCX. Her solo releases gained traction through a series of singles and collaborative tracks, including "By Your Side" with Jonas Blue (2018) and "Secrets" with Regard (2020), the latter of which reached number four on the UK Singles Chart.

Despite sustained commercial success as a feature artist, her debut album was repeatedly delayed by Polydor. In February 2021, Raye publicly called out the label on Twitter, stating she had been denied the chance to release her own album despite years of work. The outcry from fans and the music industry was significant. She parted ways with Polydor in 2021 and released her debut album independently.

## Key Achievements

*My 21st Century Blues*, released in February 2023, debuted at number three on the UK Albums Chart. The album's lead single "Escapism." featuring 070 Shake reached number one in the UK, making Raye the first unsigned British artist to top the chart in the streaming era. At the 2024 BRIT Awards, she received six nominations — a record for a single artist in one year — winning Best New Artist, Best R&B Act, and Song of the Year for "Escapism." She also won the Ivor Novello Award for Songwriter of the Year in 2024.

## Controversies & Criticism

Raye's public split from Polydor drew significant media attention. While widely praised for her transparency, some critics questioned whether airing label disputes publicly was productive. The label made no public statement. Her later commentary on the industry's treatment of Black women in particular added nuance to the conversation, framing her experience within broader systemic issues.

## Legacy & Influence

Raye's trajectory has made her a symbol of artist empowerment in the streaming age. Her success as an unsigned act demonstrated that major label infrastructure is no longer a prerequisite for chart dominance. Younger artists have cited her willingness to speak out as an influence on their own approach to label negotiations and creative control.
