## Summary

Alban Berg was an Austrian composer of the Second Viennese School who made atonal and twelve-tone music emotionally accessible. His operas Wozzeck and Lulu are landmarks of 20th-century opera, combining serial techniques with Romantic expressiveness. His Violin Concerto is one of the most moving works of the century.
