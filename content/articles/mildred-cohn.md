## Summary

Mildred Cohn pioneered the use of nuclear magnetic resonance (NMR) spectroscopy to study enzyme mechanisms and metabolic processes. Her work using isotopic tracers and NMR provided fundamental insights into how enzymes catalyze biochemical reactions. She was one of the first women elected to the National Academy of Sciences.
