## Summary

Eiichi Shibusawa was a Japanese industrialist known as the 'father of Japanese capitalism.' While not a scientist per se, he founded over 500 enterprises including banks, insurance companies, and industrial firms, and was instrumental in modernizing Japan's economic and industrial infrastructure. He promoted the application of science and technology to Japanese industry.
