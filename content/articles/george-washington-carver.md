## Summary

George Washington Carver was an American agricultural scientist and inventor who developed hundreds of products from peanuts, sweet potatoes, and soybeans. Born into slavery, he became one of the most prominent Black scientists in American history. His work on crop rotation and alternative crops helped transform Southern agriculture. Carver promoted sustainable farming practices and economic self-sufficiency for Black farmers.
