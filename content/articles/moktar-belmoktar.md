## Summary

Algerian militant known as 'Mr. Marlboro' for his cigarette smuggling. He led the 2013 In Amenas hostage crisis in Algeria that killed 39 hostages. Leader of al-Mourabitoun group. Reported killed multiple times.

## Background

Belmoktar was born in Algeria and became active in Islamist militant networks in the early 1990s. He was associated with armed groups operating across Algeria and the wider Sahara-Sahel region during and after Algeria's civil conflict.

## Smuggling and Saharan Networks

In addition to insurgent activity, he built influence through cross-border smuggling routes in the Sahara, including trafficking in cigarettes and other goods. These routes also served as logistical corridors for militant groups, linking remote desert communities, safe houses, and financing mechanisms.

## Militancy and Affiliations

Over time he operated under, or alongside, factions connected to the Salafist Group for Preaching and Combat (GSPC) and later al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb (AQIM). Reporting also links him to the group known as al-Mourabitoun, which was described as active across parts of Algeria, Mali, Niger, and Libya.

## In Amenas Hostage Crisis (2013)

The In Amenas attack targeted a gas facility in Algeria and resulted in a large hostage situation involving Algerian and foreign workers. Public accounts commonly attribute planning and leadership of the operation to Belmoktar's network, and the incident drew international attention to the operational reach of Sahel-based militants.

## Reports of Death

Multiple claims of Belmoktar's death have been reported over the years, including reports connected to airstrikes in Libya. These reports have frequently been disputed or left unconfirmed, contributing to ongoing uncertainty about his status.
