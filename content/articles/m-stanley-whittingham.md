## Summary

M. Stanley Whittingham is a British-American chemist who shared the 2019 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for the development of lithium-ion batteries. He demonstrated the first functional lithium-ion battery using titanium disulfide as the cathode while working at Exxon in the 1970s. His foundational work laid the groundwork for rechargeable battery technology.
