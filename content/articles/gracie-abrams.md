## Summary

Gracie Abrams (born September 7, 1999, in Los Angeles, California) is an American singer-songwriter known for her introspective indie pop and folk-leaning style. The daughter of filmmaker J.J. Abrams, she began releasing music independently in 2020 and built a devoted following through emotionally candid songwriting. Her debut album *Good Riddance* (2023) established her as a significant voice in the bedroom pop and confessional pop landscape, while her second album *The Secret of Us* (2024) brought her first Grammy nomination and a major commercial breakthrough.

---

## Early Life & Background

Gracie Abrams was born in Los Angeles, California, to filmmaker J.J. Abrams and producer Katie McGrath. She grew up in a creative household immersed in the entertainment industry but pursued music largely on her own terms. She studied at Brown University briefly before leaving to focus full-time on her music career. Her early influences included artists such as Taylor Swift, Phoebe Bridgers, and Elliott Smith, whose confessional and minimalist approaches would shape her own songwriting.

## Rise to Fame

Abrams began releasing music in 2020 with her debut EP *minor*, a collection of spare, emotionally raw bedroom pop tracks that circulated widely among indie listeners and earned favorable comparisons to Phoebe Bridgers. A second EP, *This Is What It Feels Like* (2022), produced by Aaron Dessner of The National, expanded her sonic palette while retaining the intimate, diary-like quality of her earlier work. The collaboration with Dessner, who had also worked closely with Taylor Swift on *folklore* and *evermore*, helped raise Abrams's profile considerably.

Her debut full-length *Good Riddance* (2023), also produced with Dessner, was released to strong critical reception. The album's centerpiece single "I Love You, I'm Sorry" became her breakthrough moment, accumulating hundreds of millions of streams and introducing her to a mainstream audience. That same year, she joined Taylor Swift's record-breaking **The Eras Tour** as an opening act for a portion of the North American dates, exposing her to stadium-scale audiences.

## Key Achievements

- *The Secret of Us* (2024) nominated for **Grammy Award for Album of the Year**
- "I Love You, I'm Sorry" surpassed 500 million streams on Spotify
- Opened for Taylor Swift on **The Eras Tour** (2023)
- *Good Riddance* (2023) praised by *Pitchfork*, *Rolling Stone*, and *The Guardian*
- *The Secret of Us* debuted at number four on the Billboard 200

## Artistry & Style

Abrams's songwriting is characterized by its emotional directness and willingness to examine heartbreak, anxiety, and the ambiguities of young adulthood. Her production aesthetic favors understated arrangements — fingerpicked guitar, sparse piano, and delicate layered vocals — that place her lyrics at the center. Critics have noted her ability to capture the internal monologue of grief and longing in language that feels both personal and universally recognizable. Her work with Aaron Dessner brought a more textured, atmospheric quality to her sound without sacrificing its confessional intimacy.

## *The Secret of Us*

Released in 2024, *The Secret of Us* represented a broadening of Abrams's ambitions. The album incorporated more varied production approaches while maintaining the emotional core of her earlier work. Its Grammy nomination for Album of the Year — placing her alongside established superstars — marked a significant milestone for an artist still in the early stages of her career and underscored the critical and commercial momentum she had built over four years.

## Cultural Influence

Gracie Abrams belongs to a generation of artists who built their audiences through streaming platforms and social media rather than traditional radio or label infrastructure. Her music resonates particularly with listeners who prize emotional authenticity over commercial polish, and she has become a reference point in discussions about the resurgence of confessional singer-songwriter traditions in the streaming era. Her association with Taylor Swift and Aaron Dessner has situated her within a broader network of artists redefining the relationship between indie and mainstream pop.
