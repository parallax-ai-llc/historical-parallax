## Summary

Chinese mining tycoon and one of Sichuan's richest men who ran a criminal empire involving murder and gambling. Executed for organizing criminal gang.

## Background (High Level)

- Liu Han was known publicly as a wealthy businessman with significant interests in mining and related industries.
- Reporting on the case framed him as a figure who combined legitimate business activity with organized criminal operations.

## Allegations and Criminal Activity

- Authorities accused Liu and his associates of running an organized gang engaged in violence and illegal businesses, including gambling.
- The case is often described as involving intimidation and killings used to control disputes and protect commercial interests.

## Investigation and Prosecution

- The prosecution presented the case as a major organized-crime investigation tied to broader anti-corruption and anti-gang enforcement campaigns.
- Legal proceedings focused on establishing the gang structure, leadership, and links between business entities and criminal acts.

## Trial, Verdict, and Sentence

- Liu Han was convicted of multiple crimes, including organizing and leading a criminal group.
- He received a death sentence and was executed, making the case one of the most prominent examples of a high-profile organized-crime conviction in China during that period.

## Public Attention and Significance

- The contrast between elite business status and criminal allegations made the case widely discussed.
- It is frequently cited in commentary on the relationship between local power networks, business influence, and criminal enforcement.

## Key Takeaways

- The case is commonly remembered as an example of how organized-crime prosecutions can intersect with high-level business and political attention.
- It highlights the difficulty of distinguishing legitimate commercial operations from coercive or illicit control when violence and intimidation are present.
