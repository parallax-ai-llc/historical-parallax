## Summary

Following the 2021 coup, Myanmar's military junta has killed over 4,000 protesters and civilians. Armed resistance movements have formed across the country.

## Background and Causes

- The movement emerged after Myanmar's February 2021 military coup, which reversed the results of the 2020 general election.
- Civilian opposition included large-scale street protests, labor strikes, and the Civil Disobedience Movement (CDM).
- Over time, resistance shifted from primarily nonviolent protest to a mix of political organizing and armed conflict.

## Key Participants

- The Myanmar military (Tatmadaw) and security forces led repression and counterinsurgency operations.
- Opposition actors included elected lawmakers, pro-democracy groups, and new local defense forces that formed in many regions.
- Long-standing ethnic armed organizations (EAOs) played varying roles depending on local politics and ceasefire dynamics.

## Conflict Dynamics (High Level)

- Fighting has been uneven across Myanmar, with shifting front lines and periodic escalations.
- Information control, internet shutdowns, and restrictions on media have shaped how events are reported and verified.
- The crisis has produced large-scale internal displacement and humanitarian needs, including disrupted education and health services.

## International and Regional Response

- Neighboring states and regional bodies have generally emphasized de-escalation and dialogue, with limited leverage over parties to the conflict.
- Sanctions and diplomatic pressure have been used by some governments, while humanitarian agencies have focused on access and civilian protection.
