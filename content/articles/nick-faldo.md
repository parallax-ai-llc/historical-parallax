## Summary

Nick Faldo won six major championships and was world number one for 97 weeks. He is the most successful European golfer of the modern era.

## Early Life and Development

Faldo turned professional in the mid-1970s and became known for a methodical approach to practice and course management. A defining feature of his career was an extensive swing rebuild early on, undertaken to improve consistency at the highest level.

## Major Championship Record

Faldo’s six majors are split between The Open Championship and the Masters Tournament:

- The Open Championship: 1987, 1990, 1992
- Masters Tournament: 1989, 1990, 1996

His success is frequently associated with strong ball-striking and disciplined strategy, particularly under major-championship pressure.

## Ryder Cup and International Play

As a leading European player of his era, Faldo was a prominent figure in Ryder Cup competition. While his individual record has been debated, he played during a period when Europe emerged as a sustained rival to the United States.

## World Rankings and Peak Years

He reached world number one and maintained that position for extended stretches, reflecting several seasons of elite performance across global tours.

## Post-Playing Career

After his peak competitive years, Faldo remained visible in golf through broadcasting and commentary, and through golf course design and related business ventures.

## Legacy

Faldo is often cited as one of the most accomplished British golfers, and as a prototype for a modern, detail-oriented major champion whose preparation and strategic discipline were central to results.
