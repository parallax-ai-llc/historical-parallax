## Summary

Ivan Sutherland created Sketchpad, the first computer graphics program. Father of computer graphics. 1988 Turing Award.

## Additional Context

- **Sketchpad (1963)**: Sketchpad is widely described as a foundational milestone in interactive computer graphics and human–computer interaction, introducing direct manipulation of graphical objects.
- **Conceptual influence**: Many later ideas in CAD, GUI design, and object-based graphics are discussed as descendants of Sketchpad's approach.
- **Research leadership**: Sutherland is associated with influential labs and with mentoring a generation of computer graphics researchers.

## Key Contributions (High-Level)

- Early interactive graphics and constraints-based drawing.
- Work connected to head-mounted displays and early virtual/augmented reality concepts in the history of computing.
- Advocacy for rigorous engineering approaches in systems and graphics research.

## Recognition

- The Turing Award citation is commonly summarized around his pioneering role in computer graphics.
