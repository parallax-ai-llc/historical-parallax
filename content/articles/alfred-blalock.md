## Summary

Alfred Blalock performed the first successful 'blue baby' operation in 1944 at Johns Hopkins, using the Blalock-Taussig shunt to redirect blood flow in cyanotic infants. Working with Helen Taussig and surgical technician Vivien Thomas, he pioneered cardiac surgery. His work opened the era of open-heart surgery and saved thousands of children's lives.
