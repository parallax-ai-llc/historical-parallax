## Summary

John Henry Newman was a British theologian who converted from Anglicanism to Catholicism and became one of the most influential religious thinkers of the 19th century. He was a leader of the Oxford Movement before his conversion. His works include Apologia Pro Vita Sua and An Essay on the Development of Christian Doctrine. He was created cardinal in 1879 and canonized in 2019.
