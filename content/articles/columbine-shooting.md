## Summary

Mass shooting at Columbine High School in Colorado on April 20, 1999 by Eric Harris and Dylan Klebold who killed 13 people before committing suicide. It was a watershed moment in US school violence.

## Event Overview

The attack unfolded over a short period on school grounds and ended when the perpetrators died by suicide. The incident drew sustained national and international attention and produced extensive investigations into warning signs, planning, and response.

## Immediate Aftermath

- Large-scale emergency response and evacuation.
- Intensive media coverage and prolonged public scrutiny of the community.
- Criminal investigations focused on weapons acquisition, planning, and associated materials.

## Long-Term Impact

Columbine influenced U.S. policy and practice around:

- School security measures and threat assessment programs.
- Active-shooter response doctrine and inter-agency coordination.
- Public debates on firearms regulation, youth violence, and media effects.

## Cultural Legacy

The shooting became a reference point for later school attacks and for research on contagion effects, extremist subcultures, and the ethics of publicizing perpetrators.
