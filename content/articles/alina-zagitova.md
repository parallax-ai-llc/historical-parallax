## Summary

Alina Zagitova won Olympic gold at 2018 PyeongChang at age 15, defeating teammate Evgenia Medvedeva.

## Additional Context

- Zagitova emerged from Russia’s highly competitive women’s singles pipeline and trained under Eteri Tutberidze during her rapid rise to the top tier.
- Her 2017–2018 season included major victories on the Grand Prix circuit and culminated in Olympic success at PyeongChang.
- The 2018 Olympic women’s event was widely framed as a duel between Zagitova and Evgenia Medvedeva, both representing Russia, and remains a defining narrative of that Olympic cycle.

## Competitive Profile

- In the late-2010s scoring environment, program construction and base value strategy (including jump layouts and second-half bonus considerations) were central to winning margins.
- Zagitova is often discussed as an example of how pacing, consistency under pressure, and technical planning can translate into peak-event performance.

## Life After Peak Competition

- After stepping back from full-time competition, she appeared in exhibitions and ice shows and became involved in sports media and public appearances.
- Like many high-profile skaters, her post-competitive period has been shaped by the shift from judged competition to entertainment formats and brand partnerships.

## Why She Matters

Zagitova’s Olympic title at a very young age, and the intense domestic and international focus on Russian women’s figure skating during that era, make her a key figure in modern figure skating history.
