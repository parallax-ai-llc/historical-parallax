## Summary

Russian former military intelligence officer who was a double agent for MI6. He and his daughter were poisoned with Novichok nerve agent in Salisbury, England in 2018 by Russian GRU agents. Both survived. A bystander died.

## Background

- **Sergei Skripal** was a former officer in Russia's military intelligence service (GRU) who later provided information to the United Kingdom.
- After being arrested and convicted in Russia for espionage, he was released in a **2010 spy swap** and settled in the UK.
- The Salisbury incident became one of the most prominent alleged uses of a **military-grade nerve agent** on British soil.

## The 2018 Salisbury incident (high-level)

- Skripal and his daughter, **Yulia Skripal**, were found critically ill in Salisbury, England, in March 2018.
- UK authorities stated that the toxic substance involved was from the **Novichok** class of nerve agents.
- The case also involved broader public-health and decontamination operations in affected areas.

## Investigation and attribution

- The UK government publicly attributed the attack to **Russian state actors**, describing it as an operation by GRU personnel.
- Open-source investigators and UK authorities later identified two suspects traveling under aliases (often reported under the names **"Alexander Petrov"** and **"Ruslan Boshirov"**).
- The incident contributed to renewed scrutiny of covert activity, travel records, and chemical-weapons enforcement frameworks.

## Aftermath and international impact

- The poisoning triggered a major **diplomatic response**, including coordinated expulsions of Russian diplomats by the UK and allied countries.
- It intensified debates about deterrence, sanctions, and the security posture of European states toward alleged Russian intelligence operations.
- A later related poisoning of **Dawn Sturgess** (who died) was widely reported as linked to the same toxic agent, reinforcing the case's public-health dimension.
