## Summary

Algerian jihadist and senior AQIM commander who led forces in northern Mali and was responsible for destroying Timbuktu's historic shrines. He was killed by French forces during Operation Serval.

## Role in AQIM

Abou Zeid became known as a senior field commander within al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb (AQIM), operating across the Sahara and Sahel. His faction was associated with desert-based militant logistics, including movement between Algeria, Mali, Niger, and neighboring areas.

## Kidnappings and Financing

AQIM units in the region were frequently linked in reporting to the kidnapping of foreign nationals for ransom and to other forms of illicit trafficking that helped finance operations. These revenue streams became a central feature of Sahelian militant networks during the 2000s and early 2010s.

## Mali Conflict and Timbuktu

During the 2012 crisis in Mali, jihadist groups seized control of several northern cities, including Timbuktu. The period drew international attention for the imposition of harsh rule and for attacks on cultural heritage sites, including mausoleums and shrines that were part of the city’s historic identity.

## Death Reports

French operations in early 2013 targeted militant strongholds in northern Mali. Abou Zeid was reported killed during fighting in the Ifoghas Mountains; such battlefield reports were later echoed by multiple governments and media outlets.
