## Summary

William Ramsay was a British chemist who won the 1904 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for discovering the noble gas elements: helium, neon, argon, krypton, and xenon. His discovery of an entire new group of elements in the periodic table was one of the greatest achievements in chemistry. He also determined the atomic weight of radium emanation (radon) and co-discovered argon with Lord Rayleigh.
