## Summary

Legendary music producer convicted of murdering actress Lana Clarkson in his mansion. Known for the 'Wall of Sound' technique, he was sentenced to 19 years. Died in prison.

## Music Career (High Level)

- **Producer and songwriter:** Before his criminal conviction, Spector was influential in popular music as a record producer and songwriter.
- **“Wall of Sound”:** He is closely associated with a dense production approach that layered instruments and vocals to create a powerful, reverberant sound, especially prominent in 1960s pop recordings.
- **Studio as instrument:** His reputation reflects a broader shift in postwar popular music where recording technique and arrangement became central to the final “sound” of a record.

## Criminal Case and Later Life

- **Victim:** Actress Lana Clarkson.
- **Trials and conviction:** The case involved a highly publicized legal process that ended in a second-degree murder conviction and a lengthy prison sentence.
- **Death in custody:** He died while incarcerated.

## Legacy

- Spector remains a polarizing figure because his celebrated musical influence coexists with his criminal conviction; summaries often address both dimensions explicitly.

## Notes

- Biographical overviews should distinguish between his artistic impact and the legal findings related to the homicide case.

## Key Works and Associations (High Level)

- Spector’s production style is frequently discussed in relation to 1960s pop and girl-group-era recording, and later retrospectives often credit him with expanding the ambitions of studio production.
- His name remains present in music history discussions even as his conviction shapes how biographies frame his life.
