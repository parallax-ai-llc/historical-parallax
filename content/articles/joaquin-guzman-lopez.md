## Background

Joaquín Guzmán López is commonly identified as one of the sons of Joaquín "El Chapo" Guzmán, the former leader of the Sinaloa Cartel. In reporting and U.S. law-enforcement narratives, he is often discussed in the context of the post–El Chapo leadership landscape and the "Los Chapitos" faction.

## Role in the Sinaloa Cartel Context

After El Chapo's extradition and conviction in the United States, the organization has been described as evolving into a set of competing factions and alliances. Guzmán López's name appears in that broader story as U.S. authorities and journalists map the shifting centers of influence.

## 2024 Allegations and U.S. Custody

Public accounts in 2024 focused on claims that a maneuver involving travel to the United States contributed to the arrest of Ismael "El Mayo" Zambada, a longtime figure associated with the Sinaloa Cartel. The details have been framed as allegations and subject to official statements and ongoing legal processes.
