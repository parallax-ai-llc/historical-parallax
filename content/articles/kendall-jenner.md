## Summary

Kendall Nicole Jenner, born November 3, 1995, in Los Angeles, California, is an American supermodel and media personality who emerged from the Kardashian-Jenner family's reality television franchise to become one of the highest-paid models in the world. A fixture on global runways and magazine covers, she has worked with virtually every major fashion house, cementing herself as a defining figure in 2010s and 2020s fashion culture.

Unlike her siblings, who built brands primarily through social media and beauty ventures, Jenner pursued a traditional high-fashion modeling career. Her success bridges the gap between celebrity culture and the historically insular world of luxury fashion, a transition that was not without friction. By her mid-twenties she had become the world's highest-earning model multiple years running, according to Forbes.

---

## Early Life & Background

Jenner was born to Kris Jenner and Caitlyn Jenner (then Bruce Jenner) and grew up in Calabasas, California, alongside her younger sister Kylie and her half-siblings from Kris's previous marriage to the late Robert Kardashian. The family entered public life with the premiere of *Keeping Up with the Kardashians* on E! in 2007, when Kendall was eleven years old. She attended Sierra Canyon School before homeschooling to accommodate her modeling schedule, graduating in 2014.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Jenner began modeling at age 14, signing with Wilhelmina Models in 2009 before moving to Society Management. Her early work included print campaigns for Forever 21 and Levi's. The pivotal turn came in 2014 when she walked for Marc Jacobs and Giles at New York and London Fashion Weeks, respectively, signaling acceptance by the high-fashion establishment. That same year she appeared on the covers of *Love* and *CR Fashion Book*.

By 2015 she had walked for Chanel, Givenchy, Balmain, and Versace, and closed the Victoria's Secret Fashion Show — a milestone in mainstream modeling visibility. Forbes first ranked her the world's highest-paid model in 2017, with estimated earnings of $22 million that year.

---

## Key Achievements

- Named the world's highest-paid model by Forbes in 2017 and 2018, earning $22.5 million and $22 million respectively.
- Walked for every major luxury fashion house, including Chanel, Versace, Balmain, Givenchy, Burberry, and Alexander Wang.
- Covered *Vogue* editions in the US, UK, France, Italy, Spain, and Australia.
- Co-founded 818 Tequila in 2021, which won multiple awards at the World Tequila Awards and grew into a significant spirits brand.
- Appeared in campaigns for Estée Lauder, Calvin Klein, Adidas, and L'Oréal.

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Jenner has faced recurring accusations of nepotism, with critics arguing her celebrity background fast-tracked access that traditionally trained models are denied. In 2017, a Pepsi advertisement she starred in was pulled within 24 hours after widespread criticism that it trivialized Black Lives Matter protests. She apologized publicly and Pepsi retracted the campaign. She has also faced backlash over the launch of 818 Tequila, with accusations of cultural appropriation and allegations that it was rebranded from an existing Mexican tequila producer — claims the brand disputed.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Jenner's career helped normalize the transition from reality television celebrity to high-fashion credibility, a path that was largely closed before her. Her social media reach — exceeding 290 million Instagram followers as of 2026 — redefined how fashion houses approach audience engagement, accelerating the industry's shift toward influencer-integrated marketing. As a co-founder of 818 Tequila, she has also demonstrated a business acumen extending beyond modeling, following a broader Kardashian-Jenner pattern of brand-building.
