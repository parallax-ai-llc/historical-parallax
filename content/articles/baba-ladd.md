## Summary

Chadian rebel leader who operated from the Central African Republic with his Front Populaire pour le Redressement. His forces committed rapes, killings, and forced recruitment.

## Additional Context

### Background

Baba Laddé (Mahamat Abdoul Kadre Oumar) is a Chadian rebel figure of Fulani ethnicity. Sources describe him as having served as a gendarme before entering rebellion in the late 1990s.[^1]

### Formation of the PFR

He is associated with founding an armed group commonly rendered in English as the **Popular Front for Recovery (PFR)** (French: *Front Populaire pour le Redressement*). Accounts note early arrests/imprisonment and periods of refuge outside Chad prior to later regional activity.[^1]

### Regional Movement and Alliances (2000s)

Reporting summarized by Wikipedia places PFR fighters in **Darfur (2006–2008)** alongside other rebel groups, including a period in which he held a role within the *Forces unies pour le changement (FUC)* coalition.[^1]

### Central African Republic Operations and Displacement (2012)

From 2008 onward, he and his fighters operated in the Central African Republic. In early 2012, joint operations by Chadian and Central African forces targeted PFR bases in north-central CAR; contemporary reporting referenced significant civilian displacement following the fighting.[^1]

### Political Narrative

Academic commentary cited by Wikipedia attributes to him a political argument focused on the perceived marginalization of Fulani/Bororo pastoralist communities, framing his movement as more than purely local banditry.[^1]

### Surrender / Capture (reported)

Open-source summaries report that he **surrendered** in 2012 to peacekeeping forces (often described as regional/AU-linked peacekeepers) after which he was transferred to authorities.[^1]
