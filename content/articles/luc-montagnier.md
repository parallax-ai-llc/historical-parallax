## Summary

Luc Montagnier was a French virologist who shared the 2008 Nobel Prize for the discovery of HIV.

## Scientific work

Montagnier worked at the Pasteur Institute and related French research institutions. His work in retrovirology is most closely associated with the identification of the human immunodeficiency virus (HIV) in the early 1980s, which enabled major advances in diagnostics and treatment research.

## Recognition

- He shared the 2008 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for the discovery of HIV.
- His contributions are often discussed alongside the broader international scientific effort that characterized early AIDS research.

## Later controversies

In later years, Montagnier made public statements on a range of topics that were widely criticized by parts of the scientific community and reported as inconsistent with mainstream scientific evidence. These later positions became a notable aspect of media coverage of his legacy.

## Legacy

Montagnier is remembered both for landmark virology research related to HIV and for the complicated public debate over his later views. Historical summaries often distinguish between his early peer-reviewed contributions and later public commentary.
