## Summary

Artour "Arteezy" Babaev is a Canadian professional Dota 2 player of Uzbek descent, widely regarded as one of the most mechanically gifted carry players in the history of the game. Known for his exceptional farming efficiency and lane dominance, he rose to stardom as a teenager and became one of the most popular figures in competitive Dota 2.

Despite his immense individual talent and consistent top-tier performances, Arteezy's career has been defined by a paradox: sustained excellence without a Valve Major or The International championship victory. His repeated roster moves between Evil Geniuses and Team Secret, and later other organizations, became one of the most discussed storylines in Dota 2 esports. Regardless of titles, his influence on how the carry role is played and his massive fan following have cemented his place in Dota 2 history.

## Early Life & Background

Artour Babaev was born on July 1, 1996, in Tashkent, Uzbekistan. His family emigrated to Canada when he was young, and he grew up in Vancouver, British Columbia. He began playing Dota at an early age, quickly distinguishing himself in pub matchmaking with aggressive, high-tempo play. By his mid-teens, he was already recognized as one of the top-ranked players on the North American ladder.

## Rise to Prominence

Arteezy first gained widespread attention in 2014 when he joined Evil Geniuses at the age of 17. His debut season was remarkable — EG finished third at The International 2014 (TI4), and Arteezy's farming patterns and mid-lane play redefined efficiency standards in professional Dota. His ability to maximize gold from the map while maintaining lane pressure was studied and imitated across all skill levels.

In late 2014, he departed EG to join Team Secret alongside Puppey and s4. He bounced between Secret and EG multiple times between 2015 and 2019, a series of roster swaps that became a running narrative in the community. Notably, he missed EG's TI5 championship win in August 2015 — the team won The International the same year he had left.

## Key Achievements

Arteezy accumulated numerous tournament victories and high placements throughout his career. He won multiple premier LAN events with both EG and Secret, including several Dota Major Championships qualifiers and third-party tournaments. He consistently ranked among the highest-earning Dota 2 players, with career prize earnings exceeding $2 million. His peak MMR and pub dominance made him one of the most-watched Dota 2 streamers on Twitch, regularly drawing tens of thousands of viewers during the mid-2010s.

At The International, he achieved top-three finishes on multiple occasions but never claimed the Aegis of Champions — a fact that became central to his competitive narrative.

## Controversies & Criticism

Arteezy's frequent team changes drew criticism from analysts and fans who viewed the roster instability as detrimental to long-term success. The EG-Secret shuffle, particularly between 2015 and 2016, was seen by some as prioritizing individual comfort over team cohesion. His stream, while enormously popular, occasionally featured tilted behavior that divided opinion. Critics argued that his inability to win TI despite years of top-level play reflected issues beyond mechanics, though supporters maintained that Dota 2's team-dependent nature made such judgments unfair.

## Legacy & Influence

Arteezy's impact on Dota 2 extends beyond tournament results. He popularized farming patterns and carry playstyles that became standard at all levels of play. His streams introduced countless players to high-level Dota, and his blend of skill and personality helped grow the game's Western audience during a critical period. The phrase "the Arteezy effect" — describing a player too talented to ignore but seemingly cursed in championship settings — entered the community lexicon. Whether or not he ever lifts the Aegis, Arteezy remains one of the most influential and beloved figures in Dota 2 history.
