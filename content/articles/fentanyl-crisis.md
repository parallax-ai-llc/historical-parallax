Illicit fentanyl primarily manufactured in Mexico using Chinese precursors has killed over 100,000 Americans annually. The synthetic opioid is 50 times stronger than heroin.

## What fentanyl is (overview)

Fentanyl is a synthetic opioid used medically for severe pain management and anesthesia. In illicit markets, fentanyl and fentanyl analogs are produced outside regulated pharmaceutical supply chains and distributed as powders, pressed pills, or as adulterants mixed into other drugs.

Because potency varies across batches, small dosing errors can be fatal.

## How the crisis evolved

Public health reporting often describes several reinforcing trends:

- Expansion of opioid use disorder populations originally linked to prescription opioids
- Increased availability of illicitly manufactured fentanyl (IMF)
- Mixing of fentanyl into heroin, counterfeit pills, and stimulants, increasing accidental exposure

## Supply chain and trafficking (high level)

Analyses commonly discuss a cross-border supply chain in which:

- Precursor chemicals may be sourced internationally
- Synthesis and pill-pressing occur in clandestine labs
- Distribution uses established smuggling routes and domestic dealer networks

Law enforcement priorities tend to focus on dismantling production cells, intercepting precursor shipments, and targeting trafficking organizations.

## Public health impact

The crisis is frequently framed as both a public health and criminal justice emergency due to:

- Rapid increases in overdose mortality
n- Strain on emergency medical services and treatment capacity
- Secondary harms to families and communities

Harm-reduction measures discussed in policy debates include naloxone distribution, test strips, supervised-use interventions (in some jurisdictions), and expanded access to medication-assisted treatment.

## Policy and international dimensions

Government responses have included combinations of:

- Scheduling and control of fentanyl-related substances
- Sanctions and diplomatic pressure related to chemical supply chains
- Border and postal inspection measures
- Treatment expansion and prevention campaigns

The balance between enforcement and treatment remains a contested policy question.
