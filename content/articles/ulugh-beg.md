## Summary

Ulugh Beg was a Timurid sultan, astronomer, and mathematician who built the great observatory in Samarkand. His star catalog was the most accurate of its time, containing the positions of over 1,000 stars. He calculated the length of the sidereal year with remarkable precision. Ulugh Beg was one of the greatest observational astronomers of the medieval period.
