## Summary

Bulleh Shah was a Punjabi Sufi poet whose verses are among the most popular and widely sung in Punjab. He was a follower of the Qadiri Sufi order and a disciple of Shah Inayat Qadiri. His poetry challenged social hierarchies, religious orthodoxy, and the distinction between Hindu and Muslim. His kafi poems, expressing ecstatic love for the divine, remain central to Punjabi music and culture.
