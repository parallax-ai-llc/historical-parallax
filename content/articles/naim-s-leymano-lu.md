## Summary

Naim Süleymanoğlu won three consecutive Olympic golds (1988-1996), set 46 world records, and defected from Bulgaria.

## Additional Context

- Süleymanoğlu became widely known by the nickname "Pocket Hercules," reflecting his short stature and extraordinary strength-to-bodyweight performance.
- He is often highlighted as one of the most dominant lifters in the history of Olympic weightlifting due to his repeated world records and multi-cycle Olympic success.
- His defection is commonly discussed in the political context of the late Cold War and the experiences of ethnic minorities in Eastern Europe.

## Sporting Significance

- Weightlifting records are typically tracked by weight class and by lift type (snatch, clean & jerk, and total). Comparing records across eras requires attention to rule changes and class restructurings.
- Three consecutive Olympic gold medals across three Games is rare in weightlifting, and it is used as a shorthand measure of longevity at peak performance.
