## Background

QuadrigaCX was a Canadian cryptocurrency exchange that marketed itself as a simple way to buy and sell digital assets. Cotten was publicly presented as the principal operator and a key point of control for customer deposits and withdrawals.

## Collapse and Allegations

After Cotten’s reported death in late 2018, the company stated that it could not access certain “cold wallets” and operational accounts. Subsequent court-supervised proceedings and third-party reviews described patterns consistent with commingling of funds, missing assets, and the use of new customer deposits to satisfy earlier withdrawal requests.

## Aftermath

The case became a widely cited example in the cryptocurrency sector of key-person risk, weak governance, and inadequate segregation of customer assets. It also contributed to broader public and regulatory scrutiny of crypto exchanges, custodial practices, and audit transparency.

## Key Themes

- Single-operator control over critical credentials and processes
- Limited independent oversight and controls around custody
- Difficulty verifying reserves and liabilities without robust disclosure
- Cross-border complications when assets and events span multiple jurisdictions

## Legacy in Crypto Risk Discussions

QuadrigaCX is frequently referenced in discussions of proof-of-reserves, multi-signature custody, and the need for clear corporate governance in exchanges that hold customer assets.
