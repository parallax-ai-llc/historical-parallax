## Summary

Al-Shabaab militants attacked the coastal Kenyan town of Mpeketoni, killing 48 people. Gunmen went door to door targeting non-Muslim men.

## Background

- **Region**: Mpeketoni is a town in Lamu County on Kenya’s coast, an area that has faced periodic militant violence and security operations.
- **Context**: The attack occurred amid broader concerns in Kenya about cross-border militancy and retaliatory attacks linked to Kenya’s security posture in the region.

## Attack Overview

- **Date**: 2014-06-15
- **Primary location**: Mpeketoni (Lamu County, Kenya)
- **Tactics reported**:
  - Armed attackers moved through parts of the town and targeted civilians.
  - Accounts described **door-to-door** assaults and identity-based targeting, including reports that non-Muslim men were singled out.
  - Property damage was reported, including fires set at buildings and vehicles.

## Perpetrators (Attribution)

- The attack has widely been attributed to **al-Shabaab** militants.
- Attribution in incidents of this kind is typically based on a mix of claims, intelligence assessments, and on-the-ground investigations; reporting has included discussion of competing narratives.

## Immediate Response

- Kenyan security forces deployed to the area following the attack.
- Government responses commonly included:
  - heightened security patrols,
  - local restrictions (e.g., curfews),
  - arrests and investigations targeting suspected networks.

## Aftermath and Impact

- **Fatalities**: 48 people were reported killed (as summarized above).
- The incident heightened public concern about security in coastal Kenya and contributed to political and policy debate regarding counterterrorism strategy and community relations.
