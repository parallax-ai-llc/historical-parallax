## Name Variant and Duplication Note

The spelling “Sin-Itiro” is a romanization variant; many sources index the physicist as Tomonaga Shin'ichirō (also rendered Shinichiro). This file appears to be a duplicate entry under a less common transliteration, but the subject is the same Nobel Prize–winning Japanese theoretical physicist.

## Overview

Sin-Itiro (Shin'ichirō) Tomonaga was a leading figure in 20th-century quantum physics. He is best known for foundational work in quantum electrodynamics (QED), including methods that helped address divergences in calculations and supported the development of renormalization techniques.

## Research Contributions (High Level)

- Advanced theoretical approaches to QED that became central to modern particle physics.
- Contributed to the broader postwar reconstruction and internationalization of Japanese physics through research leadership and academic institutions.

## Recognition

Tomonaga shared the 1965 Nobel Prize in Physics for fundamental work in quantum electrodynamics, an award commonly associated with the formal development of renormalized QED alongside contemporaries in the field.
