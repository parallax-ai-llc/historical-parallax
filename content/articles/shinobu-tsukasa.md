## Background

Kenichi Shinoda (篠田 建市) is better known publicly by the name **Shinobu Tsukasa** (司 忍). He is a Japanese yakuza figure who became the **sixth kumicho** (top boss/chairman) of the **Yamaguchi-gumi**, widely described as Japan’s largest yakuza syndicate.[^1]

According to published biographies, he was born on **January 25, 1942** in **Ōita, Ōita Prefecture** (Kyushu). Before entering organized crime, he attended a local fisheries high school and worked for a fisheries company.[^1]

## Role in the Yamaguchi-gumi

- He began his yakuza career in **1962** by joining the **Hirota-gumi**, a Nagoya-based Yamaguchi-gumi affiliate.[^1]
- After the Hirota-gumi was disbanded, he helped form **Kodo-kai** in **1984** as a successor organization, working closely with figures such as **Kiyoshi Takayama**.[^1]
- Reporting on his leadership often emphasizes his roots outside Kansai; he has been described as the first Yamaguchi-gumi kumicho not originating from the Kansai region.[^1]

## Public Profile and Notable Notes

Descriptions of his public persona sometimes contrast with stereotypical images of an underworld “godfather.” For example, accounts of his 2005 induction note that he traveled by **train** to the ceremony rather than using a chauffeur-driven car, and was reported to have stopped at a ramen restaurant en route.[^1]

Biographical summaries also note a serious early conviction: in the 1970s he was convicted of killing a rival yakuza boss and served a long prison sentence.[^1]
