## Summary

Michael Jeffrey Jordan (born 1963) is an American former professional basketball player and businessman widely considered the greatest basketball player of all time. Playing 15 NBA seasons, primarily with the Chicago Bulls, Jordan won six NBA championships (6-0 in NBA Finals), five MVP awards, and ten scoring titles. His combination of athleticism, skill, competitive drive, and global marketing appeal transformed basketball into a worldwide phenomenon. The Air Jordan brand with Nike revolutionized sports marketing. Jordan's cultural impact transcended sports—he became the most marketed athlete ever and remains an icon of excellence, winner mentality, and commercial success.

## Early Life

**Birth:** February 17, 1963, Brooklyn, New York
**Family:** Moved to North Carolina. Father James (murdered 1993). Mother Deloris.

**High School:**
Cut from varsity basketball as sophomore—legendary motivation story. Made team next year, became star.

**University of North Carolina:**
- Freshman, 1982
- Hit game-winning shot in NCAA Championship
- National Player of the Year (1984)

---

## NBA Career

### Chicago Bulls (1984-1993)

**Draft:** 3rd overall (1984) by Chicago Bulls

**Rise:**
- Immediate star
- Scoring titles
- 1988: First MVP
- But couldn't get past Detroit Pistons ("Bad Boys")

**First Three-Peat (1991-1993):**
- Finally beat Pistons (1991)
- Won first title vs. Lakers, Finals MVP
- Three consecutive championships
- Became global icon

### First Retirement (1993-1995)

**Father's Murder (1993):** James Jordan killed in robbery. Devastated Michael.

**Baseball (1994):** Attempted pro baseball (minor leagues). Modest performance.

**"I'm Back" (1995):** Returned to Bulls late season. Lost to Orlando in playoffs.

### Chicago Bulls (1995-1998)

**Second Three-Peat (1996-1998):**
- 1996: Won 72 games (then-record)
- Three more championships
- Six Finals MVPs total
- "The Last Shot" (1998)—hit winner vs. Jazz

### Washington Wizards (2001-2003)

**Second Comeback:** Played two seasons, age 38-40. Team didn't make playoffs. Retired for good 2003.

## Playing Style

**Athleticism:** 
- Aerial ability ("Air Jordan")
- Hang time, dunking
- Defensive intensity

**Skill:**
- Complete scorer (post, mid-range, three)
- Elite defender (Defensive Player of Year, 1988)

**Competitiveness:**
Legendary work ethic, trash talk, will to win. Stories of dominating practices, teammates.

**Stats:**
- 30.1 points per game (career average—highest ever)
- 6 championships
- 6 Finals MVPs
- 5 regular season MVPs
- 10 scoring titles

---

## Business Empire

**Nike/Air Jordan:**
- Signed rookie deal (1984) with Nike
- Air Jordan brand became cultural phenomenon
- Still generates $5+ billion annually
- Revolutionized athlete endorsements

**Charlotte Hornets:**
Majority owner (2010-2023). First former player to own NBA team.

**Net Worth:** ~$3 billion (first athlete to reach billionaire status from sports)

## Controversies

**"Republicans Buy Sneakers Too":**
Avoided political statements; criticized for prioritizing commercial over activism.

**Gambling:**
Heavy gambling on golf, cards—question whether 1993 retirement related. No conclusive evidence.

**Personality:**
Stories of cruelty to teammates, opponents. Ruthless competitor.
