## Summary

Hertha Ayrton was a pioneering British engineer and mathematician who made important contributions to the understanding of electric arcs and sand ripple formation. She was the first woman to read her own paper before the Royal Society and the first to be proposed for Fellowship. Her Ayrton fan was used in World War I to clear poison gas from trenches.
