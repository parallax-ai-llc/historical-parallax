## Background

- Russell Williams was a senior officer in the Canadian Forces and served as commander of CFB Trenton, a major air force base in Ontario.
- His public profile included ceremonial duties and high-trust leadership roles, which later heightened the shock and attention around the case.
- As of publicly available accounts, he is not reported as deceased; he is serving a life sentence.

## Crimes (high-level)

- The case is commonly summarized as involving:
  - multiple sexual assaults,
  - two murders,
  - and a pattern of breaking into homes and stealing personal items.
- Investigators linked incidents using physical evidence and other corroborating details described in public case summaries.

## Investigation and Arrest

- The investigation became widely known for its focus on forensic linkage and police interview strategy.
- Williams was arrested in 2010 after investigators connected him to the crimes, and he was subsequently charged.

## Trial and Sentence

- He pleaded guilty and was convicted in 2010.
- Sentence: life imprisonment, with additional convictions for sexual assault-related offenses.
