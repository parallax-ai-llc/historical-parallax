## Summary

Organized massacre of Sikhs across India following Indira Gandhis assassination. Over 3,000 Sikhs were killed in Delhi alone, with Congress party members accused of organizing the violence.

## Background

The violence followed the assassination of Prime Minister **Indira Gandhi** by her Sikh bodyguards in October 1984, which itself occurred in the aftermath of heightened tensions between the Indian state and Sikh political demands in Punjab.

## Patterns of violence (as commonly summarized)

Public investigations and reporting often describe:

- Targeted attacks on Sikh neighborhoods, businesses, and places of worship
- The use of voter lists or local knowledge to identify Sikh households
- Failures of protection and delayed response in multiple areas

## Aftermath

- Survivors faced displacement, loss of property, and long-term trauma.
- The events became a defining case in debates on communal violence, impunity, and state accountability in India.

## Accountability and inquiries

- Multiple commissions and investigations were established over time.
- Legal proceedings and convictions occurred unevenly over decades, contributing to criticism about delayed justice.

## Memory and commemoration

The riots remain a sensitive subject in Indian public life, with ongoing discussion in politics, civil society, and diaspora communities.
