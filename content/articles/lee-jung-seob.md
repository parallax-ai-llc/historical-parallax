## Summary

Lee Jung-seob was a Korean painter known for his passionate paintings of bulls, children, and family scenes, as well as his unique silver foil drawings. Separated from his Japanese wife and children by the Korean War, his art expresses intense longing and emotional power. He died in poverty at 40 but is now one of Korea's most beloved artists.
