## Summary

Malaysian sovereign wealth fund scandal where $4.5 billion was stolen. PM Najib Razak and financier Jho Low were charged. The looted funds were spent on yachts, art, and Hollywood films.

## What 1MDB was

1Malaysia Development Berhad (1MDB) was established as a Malaysian state fund intended to drive investment and development. In later reporting and court proceedings, it became associated with large-scale financial fraud and misappropriation of funds.

## Core allegations (high level)

Open-source summaries typically emphasize:

- Complex financing structures, including bond issues and joint-venture announcements
- The movement of funds through multiple entities and jurisdictions
- The use of intermediaries and shell companies to obscure beneficial ownership

## International dimension

The scandal became global because:

- Money flows and asset purchases involved banks, regulators, and courts in multiple countries.
- Recovery efforts focused on tracing assets such as real estate, art, luxury items, and proceeds tied to entertainment financing.

## Legal and political consequences

- Malaysian politics were significantly affected, with the issue becoming central to elections and governance debates.
- Banks and financial institutions faced investigations, settlements, or compliance scrutiny in connection with transactions related to 1MDB.

## Broader significance

The case is often cited as an example of how sovereign-linked entities can be exploited when governance, oversight, and transparency controls are weak.
