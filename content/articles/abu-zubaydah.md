## Summary

Palestinian alleged al-Qaeda facilitator who was the first detainee subjected to CIA enhanced interrogation techniques. He was waterboarded 83 times. He has been held at Guantánamo since 2006 without charge.

## Additional Context

- Abu Zubaydah (Zayn al-Abidin Muhammad Husayn) is a central figure in public debates about **CIA detention and interrogation** after 9/11.
- Reporting and declassified material describe him as among the earliest high-value detainees in U.S. custody and a key case in subsequent legal challenges.

## Legal and Human-Rights Significance (High-Level)

- His detention is frequently referenced in discussions of **habeas corpus**, state-secrets arguments, and the evidentiary barriers created by classified programs.
- International bodies and civil-society organizations have cited his case in arguments about torture prohibitions and accountability mechanisms.

## Detention Status (General)

- Open sources note prolonged detention without a criminal trial, with disputes over the extent of his role in al-Qaeda and the reliability of information obtained under coercive interrogation.
