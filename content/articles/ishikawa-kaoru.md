## Summary

Ishikawa Kaoru was a Japanese chemical engineer who developed the cause-and-effect diagram (fishbone diagram or Ishikawa diagram), one of the most widely used tools in quality management. He pioneered quality circles in Japan and promoted company-wide quality control. His methods were crucial for Japan's quality revolution.
