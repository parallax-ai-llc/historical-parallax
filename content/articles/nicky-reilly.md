## Summary

Vulnerable British convert who attempted a suicide bombing in a restaurant in Exeter.

## Incident (2008 Exeter attempted bombing)

- **Date and location**: The attempted bombing occurred on **22 May 2008** in the **Princesshay** shopping precinct in **Exeter, Devon, England**, at the Giraffe cafe and restaurant.[^1]
- **Device**: Reporting describes an improvised explosive device (often characterized as a nail bomb) which detonated prematurely while Reilly was in a toilet cubicle, resulting in **injuries to himself** and **no fatalities**.[^1]
- **Public response**: The city centre was rapidly cordoned off, with **large-scale evacuations** while police and bomb disposal teams searched for additional devices and assessed the scene.[^1]
- **Second device**: Accounts of the incident note that a **second device** was located and made safe by specialists.[^1]

## Legal Outcome

- Reilly **pleaded guilty** in 2008 to an attempted suicide bombing / attempted bombing-related offence connected to the Exeter incident.[^1]
- Contemporary coverage often framed the case around a combination of **radicalization** and **personal vulnerability**, with discussion of his mental health featuring prominently in reporting about the event.[^1]

## Background (High-level)

- Reilly was a British man from **Plymouth** who had converted to Islam prior to the 2008 attack attempt.[^1]
- Public sources characterize him as socially and psychologically vulnerable, which was widely cited as a factor in how he was influenced and recruited.[^1]
