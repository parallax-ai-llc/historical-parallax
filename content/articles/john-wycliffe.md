## Summary

John Wycliffe was an English theologian and proto-reformer who advocated for translating the Bible into English and challenged papal authority and clerical corruption. He inspired the Lollard movement and anticipated many ideas of the Protestant Reformation. He supervised the first complete English translation of the Bible. His remains were exhumed and burned as a heretic after his death.
