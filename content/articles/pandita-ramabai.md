## Summary

Pandita Ramabai was an Indian Christian social reformer, scholar, and champion of women's rights. She founded the Mukti Mission in Pune to rescue child brides, widows, and victims of famine. She translated the Bible into Marathi from the original Hebrew and Greek. She was one of the first women in India to receive the title 'Pandita' for her Sanskrit scholarship.
