## Overview
On April 14, 1865, just days after the end of the American Civil War, President Abraham Lincoln was shot by John Wilkes Booth while attending the play "Our American Cousin" at Fords Theatre in Washington, D.C.

## The Event
Booth, a Confederate sympathizer, entered the presidential box and shot Lincoln in the back of the head. He then leaped onto the stage, shouting "Sic semper tyrannis!" (Thus always to tyrants) before escaping. Lincoln died the following morning.

## Legacy
Lincolns death plunged the nation into mourning and dramatically altered the course of Reconstruction. It remains a pivotal moment in American history, symbolizing the tragic cost of the Civil War.
