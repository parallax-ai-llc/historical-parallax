## Summary

Robbery of the Securitas cash depot in Tonbridge, Kent on February 22, 2006 that netted 53 million pounds. The gang kidnapped the managers family to gain access.

## Key Details

- **Location:** Tonbridge, Kent, England.
- **Target:** The Securitas cash depot (cash storage and distribution facility).
- **Date:** 22 February 2006.
- **Reported haul:** About **£53 million**, often cited as one of the largest cash robberies in UK history.
- **Core tactic:** Coercion of staff via kidnapping and intimidation.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- A gang kidnapped the depot manager and threatened his family to force cooperation.
- The perpetrators gained access to the depot and loaded cash into a vehicle.
- The scale of the theft and the organized nature of the operation led to an intensive police investigation.

## Investigation and Prosecutions

- Multiple suspects were arrested in the months after the robbery.
- Several defendants were tried and convicted in UK courts.
- Sentences included lengthy prison terms, reflecting the seriousness and planning involved.

## Aftermath and Significance

- The robbery became a case study in **cash-in-transit** and **depot security** vulnerabilities.
- It influenced discussions about physical security, staff coercion risks, and procedures for high-value cash storage.
- Only a portion of the stolen money was reported as recovered.
