## Summary

Hashimoto Sanai was a Japanese physician and scholar of Western studies (rangaku) who advocated for adopting Western science and medicine in late Edo period Japan. He studied Western medicine and chemistry and proposed radical modernization reforms. He was executed during the Ansei Purge at age 25 for his reformist activities.
