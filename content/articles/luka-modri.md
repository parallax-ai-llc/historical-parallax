## Summary

Luka Modrić won the 2018 Ballon d'Or after leading Croatia to the WC final. He won six CLs with Real Madrid.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2018 | Won Ballon d'Or |
| 2022 | Won fifth CL |

## Playing Style

Modrić is primarily known as a central midfielder valued for press-resistance, ball progression, and tempo control. He is often described as a complete midfielder: able to defend, carry the ball, and create chances.

## Club Career (High Level)

- Developed in Croatia before moving to top European leagues.
- Became a key part of Real Madrid’s midfield during an era of repeated UEFA Champions League success.
- His longevity at an elite club is frequently highlighted as a sign of professionalism and tactical intelligence.

## International Career

For Croatia, Modrić has been a long-term captain and central figure in the national team’s modern peak, including deep tournament runs.

## Awards and Recognition

- The 2018 Ballon d’Or is often framed as recognition for both club achievements and Croatia’s World Cup performance.
- He has also been recognized through multiple “team of the year” style selections tied to major competitions.

## Notes

The spelling of his surname includes the diacritic (Modrić), though it is often written as “Modric” in contexts where special characters are omitted.
