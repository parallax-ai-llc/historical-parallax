## Summary

Marta is the greatest female footballer, winning six FIFA Best awards and becoming the all-time Women's World Cup scorer.

## Background

Marta Vieira da Silva, widely known simply as **Marta**, is a Brazilian footballer celebrated for her longevity and record-setting performances for both club and country.

## Playing Style

She is commonly described as an attacking player with:

- close control and dribbling in tight spaces,
- creativity in the final third,
- strong shooting and set-piece ability,
- the capacity to lead transitions and carry the ball over distance.

## National Team Career (High-Level)

Marta became a defining figure for the Brazil women’s national team across multiple tournament cycles.

- **FIFA Women’s World Cup:** She set major scoring records, including becoming the tournament’s all‑time leading scorer in widely reported summaries of her career.
- **Olympics:** Brazil’s women’s team repeatedly contended for medals during her era, and her performances helped keep the team among global contenders.

## Club Career (High-Level)

Across her club career, Marta played in major women’s leagues and high-profile teams, contributing to the professionalization and visibility of women’s football.

## Awards and Recognition

- Frequently cited as a record-holder for winning the FIFA world player award multiple times.
- Often placed in “all-time greatest” discussions due to both peak performance and sustained elite output.

## Broader Impact

Marta’s prominence made her an emblematic figure for women’s sports in Brazil and internationally, used in discussions about:

- investment gaps between men’s and women’s football,
- equal opportunity in youth development,
- media coverage and sponsorship disparities.
