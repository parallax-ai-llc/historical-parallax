## Summary

ISIS-inspired terrorists drove a van down Las Ramblas in Barcelona killing 16 people. A related cell planned a larger attack using TATP explosives.

## Additional Context

### Related incidents and cell structure

The 17 August 2017 attack in Barcelona was linked to a broader plot attributed to a small extremist cell operating in Catalonia. Investigations described the group as inspired by ISIS propaganda and intent on causing mass casualties.

### Planned explosives attack

Many accounts emphasize that the cell initially planned a larger-scale operation using TATP (triacetone triperoxide), a highly unstable homemade explosive that has appeared in multiple jihadist terror plots in Europe. The plan was disrupted after an explosion at a property in Alcanar, widely reported as an accidental detonation during bomb-making preparations.

### Cambrils attack

Hours after the Barcelona attack, a related incident occurred in Cambrils, where attackers used a vehicle and knives in an attempted assault and were ultimately shot by police. This contributed to the characterization of the events as a coordinated series rather than a single isolated incident.

### Victims and public response

The Las Ramblas attack struck a crowded tourist area, amplifying psychological impact and global media attention. Spain’s response included heightened security measures, public vigils, and increased counterterrorism coordination.

### Counterterrorism themes

The case is often discussed in relation to:

- small, locally organized cells influenced by transnational propaganda
- the tactical shift toward vehicle-ramming attacks due to low barriers to execution
- the operational risks of homemade explosives and the role of investigative leads after precursor events (such as accidental explosions)

### Source caution

Early reports after terror incidents can change as investigations confirm identities, exact timelines, and organizational links. Later judicial findings and official police reports generally provide the most stable narrative.
