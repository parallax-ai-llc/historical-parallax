## Summary

Narinder Singh Kapany was an Indian-American physicist known as the "father of fiber optics." His experimental work in the 1950s on light transmission through glass fibers laid the foundation for fiber optic communication. He coined the term "fiber optics" and founded several companies to commercialize the technology. Fortune magazine named him one of seven "Unsung Heroes" of the 20th century.
