## Summary

Houston Astros used a camera system to steal signs during their 2017 World Series-winning season. Manager and GM were suspended and fired, but players kept their championship.

## Background and Context

- In baseball, the catcher signals the next pitch (type/location) to the pitcher.
- If the offense can reliably learn those signals, it can anticipate pitches and gain an advantage.
- “Sign stealing” by observation is generally legal; using electronic equipment in real time is prohibited under MLB rules.

## What Was Alleged

Public reporting and MLB’s investigation focused on a system that:

- used a center-field camera feed to decode catcher signs,
- relayed information to the dugout in real time, and
- communicated the expected pitch to the batter (often described as audible cues such as banging on a trash can).

The best-known allegations related to the Astros’ 2017 season, including home games.

## MLB Investigation and Findings (High Level)

- MLB opened an investigation after accounts from former players and media reporting brought details to light.
- A formal report was issued that described rule violations and outlined accountability for the club’s leadership.
- MLB treated the scheme as an organizational failure rather than isolated individual misconduct.

## Discipline and Penalties

Penalties described in MLB’s response included:

- one-year suspensions for the team’s manager and general manager (followed by their dismissal by the club),
- significant fines,
- loss of draft picks, and
- discipline related to clubhouse operations.

## Wider Impact

- The scandal intensified scrutiny of technology use across MLB.
- It influenced subsequent enforcement emphasis on in-game video access and on-field communications.
- It also reshaped discussions about competitive integrity, player accountability, and organizational culture.

## Terms and Related Topics

- **Catcher signs:** hand signals to communicate pitch selection.
- **Real-time electronic assistance:** prohibited use of technology to decode and relay signs during games.
- **Competitive integrity:** the principle that outcomes should be determined by lawful play and fair conditions.
