## Summary

Bjarne Stroustrup is a Danish computer scientist who created the C++ programming language, one of the most widely used programming languages in history. He designed C++ as an extension of C with object-oriented features, combining high-level abstraction with low-level efficiency. C++ is used in systems programming, game engines, browsers, and countless applications. Stroustrup currently teaches at Columbia University.
