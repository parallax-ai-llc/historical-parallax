## Summary

Howard Schultz transformed Starbucks from a small Seattle coffee bean retailer into the world's largest coffeehouse chain. He introduced the Italian espresso bar concept to America and built a company with over 35,000 stores worldwide. Schultz was known for progressive employee policies including health insurance for part-time workers and stock options. He served as CEO in multiple stints and briefly explored a presidential run in 2019.
