## Summary

American hacker known as 'Sabu' who was a leader of LulzSec and Anonymous. After arrest, he became an FBI informant and helped identify and prosecute other hackers. His cooperation led to the arrest of Jeremy Hammond and others.

## LulzSec and Anonymous (context)

Monsegur became widely known during a period when loosely organized online collectives carried out high-profile intrusions and data leaks.
LulzSec, in particular, was associated with attention-seeking attacks framed as “for the lulz,” while Anonymous encompassed a broader range of political and protest-oriented operations.

## Cooperation with law enforcement

After his arrest, Monsegur’s cooperation with the FBI became one of the most consequential informant cases in early-2010s hacking prosecutions.
His role is often discussed in terms of:

- Identifying participants and facilitating arrests.
- The ethical and legal controversy around informant-driven investigations.
- The chilling effect (or deterrent effect) on decentralized online groups.

## Legal outcome

He received a relatively short sentence compared with other defendants, which was widely attributed to the extent of his cooperation.
The case is frequently cited to illustrate how plea agreements and cooperation can reshape outcomes in cybercrime prosecutions.

## Broader impact

The “Sabu” episode helped move public discussion from romanticized hacker narratives toward issues like operational security, infiltration risk, and accountability.
It also became a reference point for journalists, researchers, and law-enforcement agencies analyzing online collective action.
