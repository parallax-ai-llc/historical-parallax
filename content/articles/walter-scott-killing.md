## Summary

North Charleston police officer Michael Slager shot unarmed Walter Scott in the back as he ran away from a traffic stop. A bystander's video contradicted Slager's account; he was convicted.

## Context

The shooting occurred in North Charleston, South Carolina, after a traffic stop.
Initial official statements were disputed shortly after the incident when a bystander video appeared to show Scott running away as shots were fired.
The footage became central to public understanding of what happened and to subsequent legal proceedings.

## Key Facts (High Level)

- The incident involved an officer-involved shooting during an attempted detention following a stop.
- Video evidence played a major role in evaluating competing narratives about threat, use of force, and compliance.
- The case became part of broader national debates about policing, accountability, and the value of independent recordings.

## Legal Proceedings (High Level)

After the shooting, Slager faced criminal charges.
The case proceeded through state court, and later involved federal proceedings.
Public reporting and court filings emphasized how evidentiary review (including video) influenced charging decisions, trial strategy, and outcomes.

## Aftermath and Public Impact

The killing and its documentation contributed to renewed scrutiny of use-of-force policies and training.
It also reinforced calls for body-worn cameras, clearer investigative standards, and transparent release of evidence in critical incidents.
