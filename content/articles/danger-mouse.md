## Summary

Danger Mouse, born Brian Joseph Burton, is an American music producer, singer, and songwriter who became one of the most influential figures in 21st-century popular music. Rising to fame with his unauthorized 2004 mashup album *The Grey Album*, which blended The Beatles' *White Album* with Jay-Z's *The Black Album*, he quickly established himself as a boundary-pushing creative force willing to defy genre conventions.

Over the following two decades, Burton became one of the most sought-after producers in the industry, working across rock, hip-hop, soul, and electronic music. As one half of Gnarls Barkley (with CeeLo Green) and Broken Bells (with James Mercer of The Shins), and as a producer for artists including The Black Keys, Adele, and Beck, he has shaped the sound of modern music with a distinctive aesthetic that blends vintage warmth with experimental ambition. He has won multiple Grammy Awards and earned widespread critical acclaim for his genre-defying approach.

## Early Life & Background

Brian Joseph Burton was born on July 29, 1977, in White Plains, New York, and raised in Spring Valley, New York. He later moved to Athens, Georgia, where he attended the University of Georgia. Growing up, Burton was drawn to a wide range of music, from hip-hop and soul to classic rock, influences that would later define his eclectic production style. He adopted the stage name Danger Mouse from the British animated television series of the same name.

## Rise to Prominence

Burton first gained underground attention in the early 2000s producing hip-hop tracks, but his breakthrough came in February 2004 with *The Grey Album*. The project, which he created in his bedroom and distributed for free online, drew a cease-and-desist order from EMI, which held rights to The Beatles' recordings. The controversy only amplified public interest, and the album was downloaded over a million times. *The New York Times* and other major outlets praised its ingenuity, and it was named the best album of 2004 by *Entertainment Weekly*.

This notoriety led to a partnership with CeeLo Green as Gnarls Barkley. Their 2006 debut single "Crazy" became a worldwide phenomenon, reaching number one in the United Kingdom for nine consecutive weeks and earning Grammy nominations. Their debut album, *St. Elsewhere*, was both a critical and commercial success.

## Key Achievements

Danger Mouse has amassed an extraordinary production discography. He produced Gorillaz's *Demon Days* (2005), which sold over eight million copies worldwide. He collaborated with The Black Keys on five albums, including *El Camino* (2011) and *Turn Blue* (2014), helping transform the duo into arena-headlining rock stars. He produced Beck's *Modern Guilt* (2008) and key tracks on Adele's *25* (2015). With Broken Bells, formed in 2008 with James Mercer, he released three acclaimed studio albums. His production work on The Black Keys' *Brothers* (2010) earned him a Grammy Award for Producer of the Year in 2011. In total, he has won six Grammy Awards across multiple categories.

## Controversies & Criticism

The legal battle over *The Grey Album* remains the most prominent controversy in Burton's career. EMI's cease-and-desist order sparked a wider debate about copyright, fair use, and artistic freedom in the digital age, leading to the "Grey Tuesday" online protest in which over 170 websites hosted the album in defiance. Some critics have also noted that Burton's signature production style, characterized by heavy reverb and lo-fi textures, can at times overshadow the artists he works with, imposing a uniform sonic identity across otherwise diverse projects.

## Legacy & Influence

Danger Mouse is widely regarded as one of the defining music producers of his generation. His work on *The Grey Album* helped legitimize the mashup as an art form and influenced how the music industry approached remix culture. His ability to move seamlessly between genres, from hip-hop to indie rock to soul, set a template for the modern super-producer. Artists and producers continue to cite his layered, analog-influenced production aesthetic as a major inspiration. Burton's career demonstrates how creative risk-taking, even when legally contested, can reshape an entire industry's approach to music.
