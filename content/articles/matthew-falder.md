## Summary

Cambridge-educated British academic convicted of 137 offenses including blackmail and child exploitation.

## Background (Public Record)

- English national; reported to have grown up in Knutsford, Cheshire.
- Educated at the University of Cambridge (Clare College), with graduate work reported in geophysics/oceanography.
- Worked in academia prior to arrest; publicly reported affiliations include the University of Birmingham.

## Modus Operandi (High Level)

Public reporting describes a pattern of online coercion and blackmail that:

- Used multiple aliases and personas to contact victims.
- Pressured victims into providing degrading material or committing harmful acts.
- Leveraged threats of disclosure to sustain control over victims.
- Shared coerced material within illicit online communities to build status.

## Investigation and Arrest

- UK authorities (including the National Crime Agency) conducted a multi-year investigation.
- Falder was arrested in June 2017.
- The case involved a large number of complainants and charges, reflecting a sustained period of offending.

## Conviction and Sentencing

- Pleaded guilty to 137 charges relating to multiple victims.
- Sentenced in 2018 to a lengthy custodial term, alongside an extended licence period after release.
- The reported prison term was later reduced on appeal, while maintaining a long period of post-release supervision.

## Notability

- Frequently cited by investigators as an extreme example of large-scale online coercion and exploitation.
- The case is used in public discussion about cross-border digital investigations and platform cooperation with law enforcement.
