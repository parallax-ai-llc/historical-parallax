## Summary

Wahiduddin Khan was an Indian Islamic scholar, author, and peace activist who wrote over 200 books on Islam, peace, and spirituality. He founded the Centre for Peace and Spirituality (CPS International) in New Delhi. He advocated for a peaceful interpretation of Islam and interfaith harmony. He received the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian honor, and was recognized for his contributions to promoting peace.
