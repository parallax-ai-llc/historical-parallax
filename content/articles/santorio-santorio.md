## Summary

Santorio Santorio (Sanctorius) was the father of quantitative experimental physiology. He invented the clinical thermometer, a pulse clock (pulsilogium), and a weighing chair to study human metabolism. He spent 30 years weighing himself and everything he consumed, discovering insensible perspiration and founding the field of metabolic science.
