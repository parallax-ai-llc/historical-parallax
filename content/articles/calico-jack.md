## Summary

English pirate born John Rackham known for his colorful calico clothing and for having two female pirates, Anne Bonny and Mary Read, in his crew. He designed the famous skull-and-crossbones Jolly Roger flag. Hanged in Jamaica.

## Historical Setting

Rackham operated during the so-called "Golden Age of Piracy" in the Caribbean and Atlantic, when European colonial shipping created valuable targets and naval patrols were uneven.
Pirate crews often shifted between privateering, smuggling, and outright piracy depending on wartime conditions and local enforcement.

## Crew and Notoriety

Rackham is especially remembered because Anne Bonny and Mary Read sailed with him, and their participation became a lasting part of piracy folklore.
The episode also shows that pirate crews could include people who did not fit the era’s typical social roles, even if the historical record is incomplete and filtered through court documents.

## Capture and Execution

In 1720, Rackham’s vessel was captured off Jamaica.
He was tried and executed at Port Royal, and accounts commonly note that the captured women received temporary reprieves because of pregnancy claims.

## Legacy

Rackham’s name remains widely known less for the scale of his captures than for the symbolism of pirate flags and the story of his crew.
He appears frequently in later novels, films, and games that blend historical facts with romanticized pirate imagery.
