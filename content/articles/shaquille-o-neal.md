## Additional Context

- **Profile:** Shaquille O’Neal is an American former professional basketball player, widely regarded as one of the dominant centers in NBA history.
- **NBA career:** He played for multiple franchises, including the Orlando Magic, Los Angeles Lakers, Miami Heat, Phoenix Suns, Cleveland Cavaliers, and Boston Celtics.

## Career Highlights

- **Championships:** O’Neal won **four NBA championships** (three with the Lakers, one with the Heat).
- **Major awards:** He was named **NBA Most Valuable Player (2000)** and earned multiple **Finals MVP** honors.
- **All-Star presence:** He was selected to numerous NBA All-Star teams and was a perennial All-NBA and All-Defensive candidate during his peak.

## Post-Playing Career

- **Broadcasting:** After retirement, he became a prominent basketball analyst and media personality.
- **Business interests:** O’Neal has been associated with endorsements, franchising, and a range of commercial ventures.
- **Public footprint:** His celebrity extends beyond sports into entertainment and philanthropy.

## Key Points

- O’Neal’s career is often used to illustrate the impact a physically dominant center can have on team construction and strategy.
- His post-playing media presence helped keep him among the most recognizable figures in modern basketball.
- His peak years are commonly cited in comparisons of modern big-man play, efficiency, and defensive impact.
