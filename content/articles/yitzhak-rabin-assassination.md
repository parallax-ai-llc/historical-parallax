## Summary

Israeli PM Yitzhak Rabin was assassinated by right-wing Israeli extremist Yigal Amir at a peace rally. The assassination derailed the Oslo peace process.

## Context

- Rabin was the Prime Minister of Israel and a central figure in the 1990s peace efforts with the Palestinians.
- The assassination occurred during a large public rally in Tel Aviv supporting the Oslo Accords and the broader peace process.

## Assassination (High-Level)

- The attacker, Yigal Amir, opposed the Oslo process and targeted Rabin at the conclusion of the rally.
- Rabin was shot and later died from his wounds the same night.
- The event was widely covered in Israel and internationally and prompted immediate security and political responses.

## Immediate Aftermath

- National mourning followed, including a large funeral attended by many foreign leaders.
- The assassination intensified domestic debate about political incitement, polarization, and the boundaries of political protest.
- It also affected the trajectory and public support of the Oslo peace process in the years that followed.

## Legal and Security Response

- Amir was arrested at the scene and later convicted for the assassination.
- The killing triggered scrutiny of security arrangements for Israeli political leaders and reforms to protection protocols.

## Historical Significance

- The assassination is commonly cited as a major turning point in modern Israeli politics.
- Rabin's death became a symbolic marker for the fragility of the peace process and the risks posed by internal extremist violence.
