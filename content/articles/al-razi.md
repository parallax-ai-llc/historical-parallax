## Summary

Al-Razi (Rhazes) was a Persian physician, alchemist, and philosopher who made fundamental contributions to medicine and chemistry during the Islamic Golden Age. He was the first to distinguish between smallpox and measles clinically and wrote extensively on medicine, chemistry, and philosophy. His medical encyclopedia 'Kitab al-Hawi' was one of the most comprehensive medical texts of its time. He is considered one of the greatest physicians in history.
