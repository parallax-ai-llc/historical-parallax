## Summary

Lesslie Newbigin was a British theologian, missionary, and bishop who spent over 35 years as a missionary in India. He was a key figure in the ecumenical movement and the formation of the Church of South India. His later works, including The Gospel in a Pluralist Society, addressed the challenge of mission in a secular Western context. He profoundly influenced the missional church movement.
