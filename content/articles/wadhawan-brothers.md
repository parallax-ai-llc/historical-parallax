Indian builders behind HDIL who caused the collapse of PMC Bank affecting millions of depositors.

## Background

The Wadhawan brothers are associated with **Housing Development and Infrastructure Limited (HDIL)**, a Mumbai-based real-estate developer that grew rapidly during India's mid-2000s property boom. Their business profile and political connections were frequently discussed in Indian financial and media reporting.

## PMC Bank Scandal (High-Level)

Public summaries of the case describe a fraud involving **Punjab and Maharashtra Co-operative (PMC) Bank** in which:

- loan exposures to HDIL were allegedly concealed through **fictitious or proxy accounts**;
- regulatory and auditor disclosures were undermined by **misreporting of non-performing assets**;
- the eventual revelation triggered **restrictions on withdrawals**, creating acute stress for depositors.

## Legal Proceedings

- **Investigations**: multiple agencies investigated the matter, including financial-crime and banking regulators.
- **Charges/Allegations**: reporting commonly references allegations of cheating, criminal conspiracy, and financial misconduct tied to bank lending.
- **Court process**: the case has involved extended custody and bail litigation, with proceedings continuing over time.

## Impact

The PMC Bank episode became a prominent example in India of how concentrated exposures and weak controls at a cooperative bank can cascade into a depositor crisis. For many affected customers, withdrawal limits and delays in resolution became the most visible consequence.
