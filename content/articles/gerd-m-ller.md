Gerd Müller scored 68 goals in 62 internationals and the winning 1974 World Cup final goal.

## Club Career

Müller is most closely associated with Bayern Munich, where he became a central figure in the club’s rise to European prominence.
He won multiple domestic titles and was a key scorer in European competition during the 1970s.

## Scoring Profile

Nicknamed **“Der Bomber”**, Müller was known for exceptional finishing in crowded penalty areas.
Rather than relying on long-range shooting, he often scored from quick reactions, positioning, and one-touch strikes.

## Records and Honors

He set long-standing scoring records in German football, including prolific league tallies that were cited for decades.
Internationally, his goals at major tournaments contributed to West Germany’s European Championship and World Cup successes.

## Later Life and Legacy

After retiring as a player, he remained connected to football through coaching and mentoring roles.
He is widely remembered as one of the defining goal-scorers of the 20th century and a benchmark for striker efficiency.

In statistical comparisons, he is often highlighted for an unusually high goals-per-game rate across both club and international play.
