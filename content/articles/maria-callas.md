## Summary

Maria Callas was a Greek-American soprano who is widely regarded as the most influential opera singer of the 20th century. Her extraordinary vocal range, dramatic intensity, and magnetic stage presence revitalized bel canto opera. Known as 'La Divina,' she transformed opera from a vocal showcase into a total dramatic art form.
