## Summary

Sheldon Glashow is an American theoretical physicist who shared the 1979 Nobel Prize in Physics with Steven Weinberg and Abdus Salam for their contributions to the electroweak unification theory. He proposed the charm quark to explain certain anomalies in particle physics. His work was fundamental to the development of the Standard Model of particle physics.
