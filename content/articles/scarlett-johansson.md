## Summary

Scarlett Johansson (born November 22, 1984) is an American actress and singer who has become one of the highest-grossing and most recognizable performers in Hollywood. Beginning her career as a child actress in the early 1990s, she transitioned into critically acclaimed dramatic roles before achieving global blockbuster fame as Natasha Romanoff / Black Widow in the Marvel Cinematic Universe. She has been the world's highest-paid actress on multiple occasions, with her films collectively grossing over $14 billion worldwide.

Johansson's range spans intimate independent dramas, science fiction, action, and comedy. She has received numerous accolades including BAFTA nominations, Golden Globe nominations, and Tony Award recognition for her Broadway work. Beyond acting, she has recorded music and become a prominent cultural figure whose career choices — and legal battles — have shaped conversations about the film industry's evolving distribution landscape.

## Early Life & Background

Scarlett Ingrid Johansson was born in New York City to Karsten Johansson, a Danish architect, and Melanie Sloan, an American producer. She grew up in Manhattan alongside a twin brother, Hunter, and two other siblings. Her mother enrolled her in acting classes at a young age after Johansson expressed interest in performing. She trained at the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute before attending the Professional Children's School in Manhattan.

Her multicultural background — Danish on her father's side, with Ashkenazi Jewish heritage on her mother's — has informed her public identity and the roles she has pursued throughout her career.

## Rise to Prominence

Johansson made her film debut in *North* (1994) at age nine. Her breakthrough came with *The Horse Whisperer* (1998), directed by Robert Redford. She cemented her critical reputation with two 2003 releases: *Girl with a Pearl Earring*, opposite Colin Firth, and *Lost in Translation*, directed by Sofia Coppola, for which she earned BAFTA and Golden Globe nominations. Her performance in *Match Point* (2005) for Woody Allen further established her as a dramatic force.

In 2010, she joined the Marvel Cinematic Universe in *Iron Man 2*, beginning a role she would reprise across more than ten films. Her standalone film *Black Widow* was released in July 2021 simultaneously in theaters and on Disney+ Premier Access, a decision that led Johansson to file a lawsuit against Disney, alleging breach of contract. The suit was settled out of court in September 2021.

## Key Achievements

- Starred in four of the top-grossing films of all time, including *Avengers: Endgame* (2019), which grossed $2.8 billion globally.
- Won a Tony Award for Best Revival of a Play as a producer for *"'Art'"* in 2023.
- Named the world's highest-paid actress by *Forbes* in 2018 and 2019.
- Received dual Golden Globe nominations in 2020 for *Marriage Story* and *Jojo Rabbit*.
- Recorded two studio albums: *Anywhere I Lay My Head* (2008) and *Break Up* (2009).

## Controversies & Criticism

Johansson faced significant backlash in 2018 when she was cast as a transgender man in *Rub & Tug*; she withdrew from the role following public criticism about casting a cisgender actress in a transgender part. She also drew criticism for her casting as the lead in *Ghost in the Shell* (2017), in which she played a character originally Japanese, reigniting debates about whitewashing in Hollywood. Her Disney lawsuit, while legally resolved, drew divided opinions about actor compensation models in the streaming era.

## Legacy & Influence

Johansson's career arc — from indie critical darling to the anchor of the MCU's female representation — mirrors broader shifts in Hollywood's treatment of women in franchise filmmaking. Her legal action against Disney contributed to industry-wide discussions about how streaming affects talent compensation, with lasting implications for contract structures across the business. As one of the few actresses to sustain A-list status across three decades and genres, she remains a benchmark figure in contemporary cinema.
