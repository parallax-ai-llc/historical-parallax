## Additional Context

- **Field**: Paleoclimatology and geochemistry, especially the use of **stable isotope ratios** (notably oxygen isotopes) as temperature and climate proxies in snow and ice.
- **Why ice cores matter**: Layered ice in Greenland and Antarctica preserves a time-ordered record of past atmospheric conditions and temperature signals, allowing reconstruction of climate variability.

## Key Contributions

- **Greenland ice core leadership**: Dansgaard was central to early major Greenland deep ice-core efforts, including work associated with the **Camp Century** core.
- **Abrupt climate shifts**: His work helped establish that the last glacial period included rapid swings between colder and warmer states, later widely discussed as **Dansgaard–Oeschger events**.
- **Methods and interpretation**: Beyond drilling, he contributed to interpreting isotope records and establishing them as credible quantitative evidence in climate science.

## Legacy

Dansgaard is frequently cited in the history of climate science for helping turn ice cores into one of the most influential archives for understanding Earth's recent climate history, especially the reality of abrupt change on human-relevant timescales.

## Related Concepts

- **Ice-core proxies**: Stable isotopes, trapped gases, and impurities measured to infer temperature, atmospheric composition, and volcanic events.
- **Abrupt climate dynamics**: The idea that climate can switch regimes quickly, rather than changing only gradually.
- **Greenland vs. Antarctic records**: Comparison of hemispheric timing and magnitude of past climate shifts.
