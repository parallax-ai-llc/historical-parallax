## Summary

Norte del Valle Cartel leader known as 'Don Diego' who was one of the FBI's Ten Most Wanted Fugitives. He was arrested hiding behind a false wall.

## Background

Diego León Montoya Sánchez is commonly described as a senior figure associated with the Norte del Valle Cartel, a major Colombian drug-trafficking organization that expanded during the 1990s and early 2000s amid intense competition and fragmentation among trafficking networks.

The cartel era in Colombia is frequently characterized by:

- Rivalries between trafficking groups and shifting alliances.
- High levels of violence connected to control of routes, suppliers, and distribution.
- International law-enforcement cooperation, including extraditions and joint investigations.

## International Wanted Status

Montoya's inclusion on the FBI Ten Most Wanted list reflected the U.S. government's focus on disrupting transnational cocaine trafficking and targeting alleged leaders who could be linked to large-scale shipments, financial networks, and organized violence.

## Arrest and Extradition

Accounts of his capture emphasize concealment in a specially prepared hiding place. Following arrest by Colombian authorities, high-profile detainees in similar cases have often faced extradition proceedings and subsequent prosecution in U.S. federal courts for drug-trafficking and related charges.

## Significance

Cases involving alleged cartel leadership are often treated as benchmarks for bilateral cooperation between Colombia and the United States. They also illustrate the strategic shift from targeting individual kingpins to broader approaches focused on organizations, routes, and financial infrastructure.
