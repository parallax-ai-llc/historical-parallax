## Additional Context

- Maier’s 1998 Olympic story is often remembered for the contrast between a dramatic crash and immediate return to win top events, cementing a “comeback” narrative.
- Alpine skiing rewards a blend of technical precision, risk management, and high-speed decision-making; elite skiers often specialize but can compete across multiple disciplines.
- Serious injury comebacks in skiing are particularly challenging because training requires both physical recovery and rebuilding confidence at speed.
