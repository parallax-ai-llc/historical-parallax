## Summary

Leon Lederman was an American experimental physicist who shared the 1988 Nobel Prize in Physics for the discovery of the muon neutrino. He also co-discovered the bottom quark in 1977 at Fermilab. He served as director of Fermilab and coined the term 'God Particle' for the Higgs boson in his popular book. Lederman was a passionate advocate for science education.
