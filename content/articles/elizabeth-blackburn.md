## Summary

Elizabeth Blackburn is an Australian-American molecular biologist who shared the 2009 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for the discovery of how chromosomes are protected by telomeres and the enzyme telomerase. Her work revealed the mechanisms of cellular aging and has implications for cancer research and understanding age-related diseases.
