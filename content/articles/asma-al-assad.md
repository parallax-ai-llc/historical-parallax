## Summary

British-Syrian former first lady who is married to Bashar al-Assad. She was sanctioned by the US and EU for supporting the Assad regime. Initially portrayed as a modernizing force, she became complicit in the regime's atrocities.

## Expanded overview

Asma al-Assad (born 1975) is the wife of Syrian president Bashar al-Assad and served as Syria’s first lady after his accession in 2000. She was born and raised in the United Kingdom, later returning to Syria after marriage.

## Public role

- In the early years of Bashar al-Assad’s presidency, she was presented internationally as a modernizing public figure.
- She was associated with charitable initiatives and public-facing patronage work typical of a first lady role.

## International scrutiny

After the outbreak of the Syrian civil war in 2011, Asma al-Assad’s public image shifted sharply as the conflict escalated and the Syrian state faced extensive international condemnation and sanctions. Her role is discussed primarily in relation to state legitimacy, elite networks, and the regime’s domestic and international messaging.
