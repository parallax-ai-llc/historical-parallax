## Context

The phrase "Triangle of Death" has been used in media and public discourse to describe parts of Campania where illegal waste trafficking and dumping were alleged to have caused severe environmental harm.

In Italy, organized-crime involvement in waste management is sometimes discussed under the broader concept of "ecomafia"—criminal enterprises profiting from environmental violations.

## Why This Matters

Environmental crime can impose long-term costs that are difficult to remediate, including soil and groundwater contamination, community displacement, and persistent public-health anxiety.

Allegations of elevated disease rates in affected regions often become a focal point for civil society activism and policy reform, even when specific causal pathways are contested or complex.
