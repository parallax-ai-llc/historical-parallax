## Summary

Leader of Jamaat-e-Islami Bangladesh executed for genocide and crimes against humanity in 1971.

## Background

- Bangladeshi Islamist politician associated with Jamaat-e-Islami Bangladesh. [^1]
- Widely known internationally due to the post-2010 prosecutions for crimes committed during the 1971 war. [^1]

## Role in 1971 (overview)

- Prosecutors alleged involvement in the organization and direction of auxiliary forces in 1971, including the Al-Badr militia. [^1]
- The tribunal proceedings focused on specific incidents and allegations tied to killings, abductions, and persecution during the war period. [^1]

## Government and party roles

- Held senior positions in Jamaat-e-Islami Bangladesh’s leadership structure over multiple decades. [^1]
- Served as a cabinet minister in Bangladesh during the early 2000s. [^1]

## War-crimes trial and execution

- Tried by the International Crimes Tribunal (Bangladesh), a domestic court established to prosecute 1971-era crimes. [^1]
- Convicted and sentenced to death in 2014; appeals continued through the Supreme Court. [^1]
- Executed in 2016 after the final judicial process concluded. [^1]
