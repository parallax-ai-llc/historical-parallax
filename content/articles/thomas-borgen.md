## Additional Context

### Identity and Background

Thomas F. Borgen (born 27 March 1964) is a Norwegian banker who became closely associated with Denmark through his leadership at Danske Bank.
He was born in Sarpsborg, Norway, and studied business at Heriot-Watt University (BA) before completing an MBA at Syracuse University.

### Danske Bank Career

Borgen joined Danske Bank in 1997 and rose through senior management.
He became CEO in 2013, succeeding Eivind Kolding, and served until his resignation in September 2018.

### Money Laundering Scandal (High Level)

Borgen’s tenure overlapped with the Danske Bank money laundering scandal, which centered on suspicious transactions routed through the bank’s Estonian branch.
Public reporting has often described the suspected flows as extremely large in scale, and the episode led to regulatory, legal, and reputational consequences for the bank.

### Legal and Civil Proceedings

In addition to criminal investigations connected to the scandal, Borgen has faced civil litigation.
As of 2022, he was reported to be involved in court proceedings in Lyngby, including a lawsuit seeking substantial damages from former executives.
