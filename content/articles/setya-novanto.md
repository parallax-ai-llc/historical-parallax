Former speaker of the Indonesian House of Representatives convicted of embezzling $170 million from the national electronic ID card project. He faked a car accident to avoid arrest.

## Additional Context

- **Profile:** Setya Novanto is an Indonesian politician who served as **Speaker of the People’s Consultative Assembly** and later **Speaker of the House of Representatives (DPR)**.
- **Party affiliation:** He was a prominent figure within **Golkar**, one of Indonesia’s major political parties.

## E-KTP Corruption Case

- **Case overview:** Novanto became a central figure in a major corruption scandal involving procurement for Indonesia’s electronic identity card program, commonly referred to as **e-KTP**.
- **Scale and scrutiny:** The case drew national attention as one of Indonesia’s most significant corruption investigations, involving large alleged state losses and multiple defendants.
- **Anti-corruption enforcement:** Indonesia’s **Corruption Eradication Commission (KPK)** pursued the case as part of wider efforts to prosecute high-profile corruption.

## Legal and Political Consequences

- **Prosecution:** Novanto was prosecuted and convicted in connection with the scandal.
- **Institutional impact:** The case intensified political debate over anti-corruption enforcement, parliamentary accountability, and procurement governance.
- **Public perception:** The scandal became emblematic of concerns about elite corruption and the challenges of reform in Indonesia.

## Key Points

- The e-KTP scandal is frequently referenced as a case study in procurement vulnerability and political patronage.
- The KPK’s role in pursuing senior figures has been central to Indonesia’s modern anti-corruption narrative.
- It also underscored the reputational and governance costs of large-scale identity and IT procurement failures.
