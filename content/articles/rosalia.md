## Summary

Rosalía Vila Tobella, known mononymously as Rosalía, is a Spanish singer, songwriter, and producer who has redefined the boundaries of flamenco by fusing it with electronic music, reggaeton, hip-hop, and experimental pop. Emerging from Catalonia's music scene, she achieved international acclaim with her 2018 concept album *El Mal Querer*, which reinterpreted traditional flamenco through a modern lens and earned widespread critical praise.

Her 2022 album *Motomami* cemented her status as one of the most innovative pop artists of her generation, earning a Grammy Award for Best Latin Rock or Alternative Album and Album of the Year at the Latin Grammys. With her genre-defying approach and striking visual identity, Rosalía has become one of the most influential musicians to emerge from Spain in decades, bringing Spanish-language music to new global audiences.

## Early Life & Background

Rosalía was born on September 25, 1992, in Sant Esteve Sesrovires, a small town near Barcelona in Catalonia, Spain. She grew up in a musical household and developed an early passion for flamenco after being exposed to it as a child. At age 16, she enrolled at the Taller de Músics in Barcelona, and later studied at the Escuela Superior de Música de Cataluña (ESMUC), where she trained under the flamenco guitarist José Miguel "Chiqui" Vizcaya. This formal education in flamenco traditions provided the foundation she would later deconstruct and reimagine in her own work.

## Rise to Prominence

Rosalía released her debut album *Los Ángeles* in 2017, a largely acoustic collection of traditional flamenco pieces that earned critical recognition in Spain, including a Latin Grammy nomination. Her breakthrough came in 2018 with *El Mal Querer*, a concept album loosely based on the 13th-century Occitan romance *Flamenca*. The album blended flamenco vocals and palmas with electronic production, auto-tune, and urban beats. The single "Malamente" became a viral sensation and won two Latin Grammy Awards, including Best Alternative Song. The album was named one of the best of 2018 by publications including *Pitchfork*, *The Guardian*, and *The New York Times*.

Her collaborations with international artists — including Travis Scott on "TKN" (2020) and Billie Eilish on "Lo Vas a Olvidar" (2021) — expanded her reach into the anglophone pop mainstream.

## Key Achievements

Rosalía's third studio album, *Motomami* (2022), was her most ambitious project, incorporating reggaeton, bachata, jazz, and experimental electronic sounds across 16 tracks. It debuted at number one on Spain's album chart and reached number 33 on the US Billboard 200. The album won the Grammy Award for Best Latin Rock or Alternative Album and swept the 2022 Latin Grammys with four wins, including Album of the Year. As of 2024, Rosalía has won eight Latin Grammy Awards and two Grammy Awards. Her Motomami World Tour sold out arenas across Europe and the Americas throughout 2022 and 2023.

## Controversies & Criticism

Rosalía has faced criticism from some flamenco purists who argue that her music dilutes or commercializes a deeply rooted Romani cultural tradition. Some Romani commentators have accused her of cultural appropriation, noting that she benefits commercially from an art form historically tied to marginalized communities without being of Romani heritage herself. Rosalía has responded by emphasizing her years of formal flamenco training and her deep respect for the tradition. Her use of auto-tune and electronic production in flamenco contexts has also divided traditionalists, though many critics view her innovations as a revitalization of the genre.

## Legacy & Influence

Rosalía has been widely credited with sparking a renewed international interest in flamenco and Spanish-language music. Her success has opened doors for other Spanish and Latin artists in global markets and demonstrated that non-English-language music can achieve mainstream pop success without compromising artistic ambition. Her visual artistry, including her music videos and fashion choices, has made her a cultural icon. Publications such as *Time* have named her one of the most influential people in the world, and her genre-blending approach has influenced a generation of artists seeking to merge traditional music with contemporary production.
