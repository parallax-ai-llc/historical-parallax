## Summary

Columba was an Irish abbot and missionary who founded the influential monastery of Iona off the coast of Scotland. He is credited with spreading Christianity among the Picts in Scotland. The Iona community became one of the most important centers of Celtic Christianity and learning. He and his monks produced illuminated manuscripts and sent missionaries throughout northern Britain and Europe.
