## Summary

Robert Noyce was an American physicist and entrepreneur who co-invented the integrated circuit independently of Jack Kilby. He co-founded Fairchild Semiconductor and Intel Corporation, earning him the nickname 'Mayor of Silicon Valley.' His planar process for manufacturing integrated circuits made mass production practical. Noyce's contributions to the semiconductor industry were fundamental to the digital revolution.
