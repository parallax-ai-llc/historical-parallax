## Summary

Girolamo Savonarola was an Italian Dominican friar who became a powerful preacher and briefly the effective ruler of Florence. He denounced clerical corruption, despotic rule, and the exploitation of the poor. He organized the famous 'Bonfire of the Vanities' in 1497. He was excommunicated, tortured, and burned at the stake for heresy and sedition.
