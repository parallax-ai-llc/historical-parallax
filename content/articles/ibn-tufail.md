## Summary

Ibn Tufail was an Andalusian philosopher and physician best known for his philosophical novel Hayy ibn Yaqzan, which describes a child growing up alone on an island who discovers truth through reason and observation. This work influenced Robinson Crusoe, John Locke's tabula rasa, and Enlightenment empiricism. He also served as court physician.
