## Summary

Guion Bluford became the first African American in space when he flew aboard the Space Shuttle Challenger in 1983. He flew four shuttle missions in total, logging over 688 hours in space. An aerospace engineer with a PhD from the Air Force Institute of Technology, he worked on problems of aerodynamics and fluid mechanics.
