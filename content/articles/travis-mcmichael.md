## Expanded Context

### Overview

Travis McMichael is primarily known in public records for his role as one of the perpetrators in the 2020 murder of **Ahmaud Arbery** in Glynn County, Georgia, United States. The killing became a widely covered case in the United States and was prosecuted in both Georgia state court and federal court.

### The Ahmaud Arbery killing (high level)

According to widely reported accounts summarized by Wikipedia, Arbery was pursued by multiple individuals in trucks during what prosecutors later characterized as a racially motivated pursuit. A confrontation occurred, and Arbery was shot and killed.[^1]

Key elements frequently referenced in summaries include:

- The pursuit occurred in February 2020 in the Satilla Shores area near Brunswick, Georgia.
- The confrontation and killing were recorded on a cell phone video (recorded by a third participant) that later became central to public attention and legal proceedings.

### Legal proceedings

The case resulted in multiple layers of legal action:

- **State prosecution (Georgia):** McMichael was convicted on murder-related charges in state court proceedings connected to Arbery’s death.[^1]
- **Federal prosecution:** McMichael was also convicted on federal charges framed as civil-rights violations/hate crimes related to the same events.[^1]

### Sentencing (as summarized by Wikipedia)

Wikipedia’s case summary describes McMichael receiving a sentence of **life imprisonment without the possibility of parole** in connection with the proceedings.[^1]
