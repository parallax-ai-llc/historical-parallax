## Summary
Moses Sithole, the 'ABC Killer,' was convicted of 38 murders and 40 rapes in post-apartheid South Africa between 1994 and 1995. He lured unemployed young women with fake job offers. Sentenced to 2,410 years, his case exposed the vulnerability of women during South Africa's transition to democracy.

## Early Life
Born November 17, 1964, in KwaThema, South Africa. Orphaned as a child and raised in institutions. Convicted of rape in 1987 (he claimed innocence), he served six years. After release, he became involved in youth development as cover for his crimes. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1994 and 1995, Sithole raped and murdered at least 38 women across Gauteng Province. Posing as a businessman, he offered young unemployed women jobs, took them to secluded areas, raped and strangled them. Bodies were found in clusters near Atteridgeville, Boksburg, and Cleveland. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Identified through journalist investigation and police work, Sithole attacked an officer during arrest with a hatchet before being shot and captured on October 18, 1995. Convicted of 38 murders, 40 rapes, and 6 robberies in 1997, he received 50 life sentences plus 2,410 years. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- Led to improvements in South African serial crime investigation [^4]
- Prompted specialized serial murder units
- Drew attention to vulnerability of unemployed women

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered 38 women and raped 40 [^5]
- Exploited desperate unemployment in post-apartheid era
- Posed as a philanthropist while committing crimes
- Contributed to South Africa's severe violence against women problem
