## Economics and global trade

Tobacco has long been traded internationally as an agricultural commodity and as a manufactured consumer product. Industry structure varies by country, but often includes a small number of large multinational manufacturers, large-scale leaf purchasers, and extensive retail distribution networks.

### Common policy tools
- **Excise taxes** designed to raise prices and reduce consumption
- **Packaging rules** including prominent health warnings
- **Advertising and sponsorship restrictions** to reduce youth exposure
- **Smoke-free laws** limiting indoor public smoking

## Harm reduction debates

Public policy discussions sometimes distinguish between combusted tobacco (such as cigarettes) and non-combustion nicotine products. Regulators and health authorities debate how to balance adult harm reduction goals with concerns about youth uptake and long-term population effects.
