## Summary

Jean-Martin Charcot is considered the founder of modern neurology. Working at the Salpêtrière Hospital in Paris, he identified and described multiple sclerosis, amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (Charcot's disease), and Charcot-Marie-Tooth disease. He was also one of France's greatest medical teachers, counting Sigmund Freud among his students.
