## Summary

PSY (born Park Jae-sang on December 31, 1977) is a South Korean singer, rapper, and record producer who became one of the most recognizable Korean entertainers in the world following the global viral success of "Gangnam Style" in 2012. Known for his comedic performance style, satirical lyrics, and energetic stage presence, PSY carved out a distinctive identity in Korean pop music long before international audiences discovered him.

His impact on the music industry extends far beyond a single hit. "Gangnam Style" became the first YouTube video to surpass one billion views and fundamentally demonstrated the potential of social media to propel non-English-language music to global audiences. PSY is widely credited with helping lay the groundwork for the Korean Wave (Hallyu) to reach mainstream Western markets, predating BTS and BLACKPINK's international breakthroughs by several years.

## Early Life & Background

PSY was born in Gangnam, Seoul, South Korea — the affluent district that would later become the subject of his most famous song. He grew up in a wealthy family; his father was a businessman. After high school, he enrolled at Berklee College of Music in Boston in 1997 but left without completing his degree. He later enrolled at Boston University before returning to South Korea. His time abroad gave him exposure to Western pop and hip-hop that would shape his musical sensibility.

## Rise to Prominence

PSY debuted in South Korea in 2001 with his first album, *PSY from the Psycho World!*, which immediately attracted controversy for its explicit content. His early career was marked by multiple run-ins with South Korean authorities over album content deemed inappropriate. Despite this, he built a loyal domestic following through the 2000s with his irreverent humor and sharp social commentary.

In July 2012, PSY released "Gangnam Style," a satirical take on the ostentatious lifestyle associated with Seoul's wealthy Gangnam district. The song's accompanying music video — featuring PSY's now-iconic horse-riding dance — spread virally across the internet. By November 2012, the video had broken YouTube's view counter, forcing the platform to upgrade from a 32-bit to a 64-bit integer to accommodate further views. The song peaked at number 2 on the US Billboard Hot 100 and reached number one in over 30 countries.

## Key Achievements

- "Gangnam Style" held the record as the most-viewed YouTube video from September 2012 to July 2017, reaching over 4 billion views.
- First Korean artist to top the UK Singles Chart.
- Won numerous awards including MTV Europe Music Award for Best Video (2012).
- Signed with Scooter Braun's Schoolboy Records / Island Records for international distribution in 2012.
- Released follow-up international single "Gentleman" (2013), which set a then-record for most YouTube views in a single 24-hour period at 38 million.
- Founded his own South Korean label, P NATION, in 2019, signing artists including HyunA and Crush.

## Controversies & Criticism

PSY's career has not been without controversy. In 2002, he was fined for marijuana possession. He also faced criticism for violent, anti-American lyrics in a 2004 protest song performed at a concert, which resurfaced in 2012 during the height of his international fame. He issued a public apology, acknowledging the lyrics were wrong.

Some critics dismissed "Gangnam Style" as a novelty act, questioning whether PSY could sustain international relevance beyond the viral moment — a concern that proved somewhat valid as his subsequent international releases did not replicate the same commercial heights.

## Legacy & Influence

PSY's legacy is multifaceted. In South Korea, he is respected as a veteran entertainer with a two-decade career predating his global fame. Internationally, he demonstrated that language was not a barrier to global music success, influencing how Korean entertainment companies approached overseas marketing. The Korean government recognized "Gangnam Style" as a cultural export of national significance. His founding of P NATION also established him as a significant figure in the Korean music industry's infrastructure.
