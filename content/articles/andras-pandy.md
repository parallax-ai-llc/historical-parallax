## Summary
András Pándy was a Hungarian-Belgian former pastor who murdered at least six family members between 1986 and 1989 in Belgium. He dissolved some bodies in drain cleaner. He was convicted in 2002 with the help of his daughter Agnes's testimony. He was also convicted of incest.

## Early Life
Born May 1, 1927, in Hungary. He was a reformed church pastor who emigrated to Belgium. He married multiple times and had children from different relationships. He was outwardly respectable and religious. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1986 and 1989, Pándy murdered six family members including two of his wives and four of his children. He dissolved some bodies in drain cleaner. He also committed incest with his daughters Agnes and Andrea. He continued posing as a pastor while committing these crimes. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
His surviving daughter Agnes Pándy eventually reported her father to police in 1997. Investigation revealed the murders. Pándy was convicted in 2002 of six counts of murder and sentenced to life imprisonment. Agnes received 21 years as an accomplice. Pándy died in prison on December 23, 2013. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case led to improved investigation of missing persons in Belgium [^4]
- Demonstrated the importance of survivor testimony in family violence cases
- Prompted reforms in Belgian social services' response to domestic abuse

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered six family members including his own children [^5]
- Dissolved victims' bodies in chemicals
- Committed long-term incest against his daughters
- Used his religious position to maintain a facade of respectability
- Manipulated family members into silence for years
