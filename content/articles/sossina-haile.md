## Summary

Sossina Haile is an Ethiopian-American materials scientist who developed high-performance solid oxide fuel cells.

## Research Focus

- Haile’s work is widely associated with solid-state ionics and electrochemical energy conversion.
- A major theme in her research has been materials that conduct protons or oxygen ions at elevated temperatures.

## Solid Oxide and Protonic Ceramic Devices

- Solid oxide fuel cells (SOFCs) convert chemical energy to electricity through electrochemical reactions, and performance depends strongly on electrolyte and electrode materials.
- Haile is known for contributions to fuel-cell materials that improve conductivity and stability, enabling operation at more practical temperatures.
- Her research is also connected to protonic ceramic fuel cells and related hydrogen technologies.

## Academic Career

- After doctoral and postdoctoral training, she held faculty roles at major U.S. research universities.
- She has led research groups that combine materials synthesis, characterization, and device-level testing.

## Recognition and Service

- Haile has received scientific recognition through awards, invited lectures, and professional society honors.
- She has been involved in mentoring and in broader efforts to support representation in STEM fields.

## Why It Matters

- Improvements in ionic-conducting ceramics and electrodes can lower costs and expand deployment options for fuel cells.
- The same materials principles are relevant to sensors, electrolyzers, and other electrochemical devices.
