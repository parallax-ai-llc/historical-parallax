## Summary

Katsuko Saruhashi was the first woman to earn a PhD in chemistry in Japan and a pioneering geochemist who developed methods for measuring CO2 in seawater. Her research on radioactive fallout from nuclear weapons testing in the Pacific demonstrated the global spread of contamination. She established the Saruhashi Prize for women scientists in Japan.
