## Summary

Xavi was Barcelona's and Spain's tiki-taka architect. He won four CLs and the 2010 World Cup.

## Background

- **Club identity:** A product of FC Barcelona's La Masia academy and a long-term first-team midfielder.
- **Position and role:** Primarily a central midfielder known for short passing, tempo control, and positional play.
- **System fit:** Often described as a key on-field organizer within Barcelona and Spain sides associated with "tiki-taka".

## Major Honors (high level)

- **FC Barcelona:** Multiple La Liga titles and UEFA Champions League titles, including a treble season in 2008–09.
- **Spain:** UEFA Euro 2008 and 2012, plus the **2010 FIFA World Cup**.

## Playing Style and Reputation

- **Ball retention and progression:** Emphasis on keeping possession and advancing play through quick combinations.
- **Chance creation:** Frequently contributed to build-up play and assists rather than high goal totals.
- **Leadership:** Served as a captain for Barcelona and was viewed as an influential senior figure in midfield.

## Coaching / Management

- After his playing career, Xavi moved into coaching and later returned to FC Barcelona as a manager.
