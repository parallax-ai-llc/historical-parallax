## Summary

Al-Shabaab leader also known as Ahmad Umar who directs the group's military and terrorist operations from undisclosed locations in Somalia.

## Role and Significance

Ahmed Diriye (often referred to as Ahmad Umar/Abu Ubaidah in reporting) is described in open sources as the emir of al-Shabaab, the Somali militant group aligned with al-Qaeda. As leader, he is associated with setting strategic direction, approving major operations, and overseeing the group’s military and clandestine activities.

## Designations and Rewards

- Al-Shabaab has been designated a terrorist organization by multiple governments and international bodies.
- The United States’ Rewards for Justice program has publicly offered a reward for information related to Diriye’s location and/or arrest (amounts and terms should be checked against the current announcement).

## Operational Context (high level)

Al-Shabaab has maintained an insurgency against the Somali government and the African Union/partner forces, combining guerrilla tactics with terrorist attacks. Leadership is typically kept opaque, and reporting often notes that Diriye’s public profile and confirmed photographs are limited.

## Organization and Command (context)

Open reporting on al-Shabaab typically describes a layered structure that includes a senior leadership council, regional commanders, and specialized units for security, taxation, media, and external operations. Because leaders are intentionally concealed, attribution for specific attacks is often made at the organizational level rather than to an individual commander.

## International Response (high level)

Governments and multilateral partners have pursued a mix of approaches to counter al-Shabaab, including support for Somali security institutions, military operations, sanctions/terror listings, and financial disruption efforts. Public information about Diriye is therefore often tied to counterterrorism designations and reward announcements rather than routine political coverage.
