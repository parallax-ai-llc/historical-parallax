## Summary

Communal violence in northeast Delhi killed 53 people during protests against the Citizenship Amendment Act. Hindu mobs attacked Muslim neighborhoods with alleged police complicity.

## Background

The clashes took place in the context of nationwide protests and counter-protests over India’s Citizenship (Amendment) Act (CAA) and proposals for a National Register of Citizens. In Delhi, a series of sit-ins and road blockades in late 2019 and early 2020 became flashpoints for political mobilization and communal rhetoric.

## Key Events (High Level)

- **Late February 2020:** Violence escalated in parts of northeast Delhi, including attacks on people, homes, shops, and places of worship.
- **Casualties and displacement:** Dozens of people were killed and many more injured; residents fled affected neighborhoods and relied on relief camps and community support.
- **Damage to property:** Large-scale arson and vandalism were reported across multiple localities.

## Response and Aftermath

Authorities deployed additional police forces and imposed restrictions in certain areas as the situation was brought under control. Relief and rehabilitation became a significant focus, including support for victims, medical care, and rebuilding of damaged homes and businesses.

## Investigations and Legal Proceedings

Multiple investigations and prosecutions followed, including cases related to murder, rioting, arson, and conspiracy allegations. Human-rights organizations and journalists documented accounts from victims and witnesses, while public debate continued over the handling of the violence and the conduct of different actors during the unrest.

## Public Debate and Legacy

The 2020 Delhi riots are widely cited in discussions about communal relations in India, the policing of protest movements, and accountability following episodes of mass violence. The events also remain a reference point in broader debates about citizenship policy and political polarization.
