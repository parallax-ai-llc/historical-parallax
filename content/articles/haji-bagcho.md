## Summary

Afghan drug lord convicted in the US of distributing over $250 million worth of heroin. He was one of the largest heroin traffickers in the world, moving over 500 kg per month. Sentenced to life in US federal prison.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2000 | Became major heroin producer in Nangarhar Province |
| 2009 | Arrested by DEA |
| 2012 | Convicted of narcotics trafficking; sentenced to life |

## Additional Context

- **Aliases and identification:** Reporting has described him as "Haji Bagcho Sherzai" and also as "Haji Bagh Chagul" / "Haji Bagcho"; transliteration varies across sources.
- **Geographic footprint:** He was associated with Nangarhar Province in eastern Afghanistan, with reporting also describing a residence/compound outside Peshawar, Pakistan.
- **Scale allegations:** US prosecutors alleged a large export network spanning many countries, and that seized ledgers documented very large-volume heroin transactions.

## Legal Case (High-Level)

- **Charges in US court:** Coverage of the case describes convictions for conspiracy and heroin distribution intended for importation, with the prosecution framing the case as combining narcotics trafficking with support to insurgent actors.
- **Asset forfeiture:** Court reporting described forfeiture orders in the hundreds of millions of US dollars, reflecting alleged proceeds from trafficking.
- **Narco-terrorism framing:** The case has been cited in media coverage as an early use of the US "narco-terrorism" statute in federal prosecution.
