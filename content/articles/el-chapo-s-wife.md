## Additional Background

Emma Coronel Aispuro is a figure known internationally largely through her marriage to Joaquín “El Chapo” Guzmán, the former leader of the Sinaloa Cartel, and through U.S. federal criminal proceedings that accused her of assisting cartel-related activities. Open sources summarize her case as involving conspiracy allegations related to drug trafficking and money laundering, and her eventual guilty plea.[^1]

## Legal Case (High-Level)

As described in publicly available summaries:

- **Jurisdiction**: The key proceedings occurred in U.S. federal court.
- **Conduct described by prosecutors**: Assistance to cartel operations and facilitation of communications and logistics, including in connection with Guzmán’s prison escape efforts.[^1]
- **Outcome**: Guilty plea followed by a custodial sentence reported in open sources.[^1]

## The 2015 Escape (Context)

The 2015 escape of Guzmán from a Mexican prison via a tunnel became a globally covered event. In open-source reporting, Coronel Aispuro’s alleged role is typically framed as supporting planning and coordination rather than being the primary engineer of the operation, reflecting how family and close associates can function as intermediaries in high-security contexts.[^1]
