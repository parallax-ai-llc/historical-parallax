## Summary

Russian-based ransomware gang that attacked hundreds of organizations including Ireland's Health Service Executive. They supported Russia's invasion of Ukraine, prompting a Ukrainian researcher to leak their internal communications.

## Additional Context

- **Ransomware model:** Conti operated as a profit-driven cybercrime operation associated with extortion, often combining data theft with encryption to pressure victims ("double extortion").
- **Targeting:** Public reporting linked Conti activity to attacks on companies and public institutions, including health and critical-service organizations.
- **Ireland HSE incident:** The 2021 attack on Ireland’s Health Service Executive disrupted clinical and administrative systems and became a high-profile example of ransomware’s real-world societal impact.

## Leaks and Fragmentation

- **Internal chat leaks (2022):** After Conti publicly supported Russia’s invasion of Ukraine, a leak of internal communications provided unusually detailed insight into staffing, tactics, and internal management.
- **Rebranding:** Analysts and journalists reported that operations and personnel dispersed into successor or affiliate groups rather than disappearing entirely.
