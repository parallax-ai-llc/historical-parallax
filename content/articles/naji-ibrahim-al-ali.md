## Summary

Palestinian cartoonist assassinated in London, likely by Mossad, for his critical political cartoons.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1987 | Assassinated in London |

## Background

- Born in the village of al-Shajara in what was then Mandatory Palestine.
- Displaced as a child after the 1948 Arab-Israeli war and grew up in refugee communities in Lebanon.

## Work and Style

- Became known for sharp, often satirical political cartoons focused on Palestinian life, Arab politics, and corruption.
- Developed a distinctive visual style that emphasized plain figures, bold lines, and direct captions.
- Published in multiple Arabic newspapers over his career, building a wide readership across the Arab world.

## Handala

- Created the recurring character **Handala** (often dated to 1969), depicted as a barefoot refugee child.
- Handala is typically shown from behind with hands clasped, a symbol of steadfastness and refusal to compromise until return and justice are achieved.
- The character became one of the most recognized icons in modern Palestinian political art.

## Assassination

- Shot in London in July 1987 and later died of his wounds on 1987-08-29.
- Multiple actors were discussed in reporting and investigation; attribution has remained disputed in public accounts.
- His death is frequently described as politically motivated, given the targets of his work.

## Legacy

- Remembered as one of the most influential Palestinian cartoonists of the 20th century.
- His cartoons and the Handala figure continue to be reproduced in books, exhibitions, and political graffiti.
