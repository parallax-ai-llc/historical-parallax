## Summary

Zhu Yilong (朱一龙) is a Chinese actor born on April 16, 1988, in Wuhan, Hubei Province. A graduate of the Beijing Film Academy, he spent years building his craft in television before achieving widespread recognition through critically acclaimed roles in both drama series and film. He is widely regarded as one of China's most versatile leading actors of his generation.

His breakout came with the 2018 online drama *Guardian* (*镇魂*), which cultivated a devoted fanbase and elevated him to national celebrity status. He subsequently demonstrated range across period dramas, thrillers, and prestige films, earning both commercial success and institutional recognition from China's film industry.

## Early Life & Background

Zhu Yilong was born on April 16, 1988, in Wuhan, Hubei Province. He showed an early interest in performance and pursued it academically, gaining admission to the prestigious Beijing Film Academy, where he studied acting. His time at the academy grounded him in classical technique and connected him to the broader tradition of Chinese stage and screen performance. He graduated in 2010 and began working steadily in television, though recognition was slow to come in his early career.

## Rise to Prominence

After graduation, Zhu took on supporting and secondary roles in various television dramas throughout the early 2010s. His perseverance through a relatively low-profile period distinguished him from peers who sought faster fame. The turning point arrived in 2018 when he co-starred in *Guardian* (*镇魂*), a supernatural drama series released on the streaming platform Youku. The show became a cultural phenomenon, and Zhu's nuanced dual-role performance earned him a massive following known as "Longzai" fans. His popularity surged further with the 2019 period drama *The Story of Ming Lan* (*知否知否应是绿肥红瘦*), in which he played a charismatic male lead opposite actress Zhao Liying.

## Key Achievements

Zhu's most significant critical achievement came with the 2022 film *Lighting Up the Stars* (*人生大事*), in which he played a funeral worker confronting life, death, and unexpected family. The film grossed over 1.7 billion yuan at the Chinese box office and earned Zhu the Best Actor award at the 36th Golden Rooster Awards in 2023 — one of China's most prestigious film honors. The performance was widely praised for its emotional authenticity and marked his definitive transition from television star to serious film actor. He also received recognition at the Hundred Flowers Awards, further cementing his status in Chinese cinema's institutional landscape.

## Controversies & Criticism

Zhu Yilong has largely avoided major public controversies, maintaining a relatively private personal life in an industry where celebrity scandals are common. Some early criticism centered on typecasting concerns as he navigated the transition from idol-adjacent drama roles to more serious material, though his performance in *Lighting Up the Stars* largely silenced such doubts. His management of fan culture — occasionally issuing measured statements to discourage excessive fan behavior — has been noted positively by industry observers.

## Legacy & Influence

Zhu Yilong represents a model of sustained artistic development within the Chinese entertainment industry. His trajectory — from decade-long grind to award-winning lead — has been cited as an example of craft-over-celebrity in an industry often dominated by overnight fame. His Golden Rooster win for *Lighting Up the Stars* placed him among a lineage of respected Chinese film actors, and his continued selection of challenging, character-driven roles suggests a career oriented toward longevity and artistic credibility rather than commercial maximalism.
