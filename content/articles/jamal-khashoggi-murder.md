## Summary

Saudi journalist and Washington Post columnist who was murdered and dismembered by a Saudi hit squad inside the Saudi consulate in Istanbul on October 2, 2018. His death caused global outrage and was attributed to Crown Prince MBS.

## Context

Jamal Khashoggi was a Saudi journalist who had lived in the United States and wrote columns that were critical of aspects of Saudi governance. He entered the Saudi consulate in Istanbul, Turkey, for administrative paperwork and was killed there.

## Investigation and Attribution

Turkish officials stated that they had evidence the killing was carried out by a team that traveled from Saudi Arabia and that it occurred inside the consulate. International reporting and intelligence assessments in multiple countries concluded that the operation was unlikely to have occurred without authorization from senior Saudi leadership.

## International Reaction

The murder generated sustained diplomatic fallout, media scrutiny, and political debate about arms sales, strategic partnerships, and accountability for extraterritorial repression. It also became a reference point in discussions of protections for journalists and dissidents abroad.

## Legal and Political Aftermath

Saudi authorities announced prosecutions and convictions of several individuals connected to the operation, while critics argued that the proceedings did not adequately address command responsibility. International bodies, including the United Nations, produced reports assessing responsibility and calling for further investigation.

## Legacy

The case remains widely cited as a high-profile example of political violence against a journalist and as an event that reshaped how governments and institutions discuss reputational risk and human-rights considerations in relations with Saudi Arabia.
