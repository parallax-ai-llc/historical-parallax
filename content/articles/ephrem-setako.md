## Summary

Rwandan military officer convicted of genocide for directing massacres of Tutsi refugees in Kibuye prefecture. He organized militia and soldiers to attack refugee camps.

## Additional Context

- Setako was prosecuted by the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR), which was created by the UN to try major crimes committed during the 1994 genocide.
- His case focused on command responsibility: how officers used authority over soldiers and local actors to enable mass violence.

## Role in the 1994 Genocide (High Level)

- The Kibuye area experienced large-scale killings at sites where Tutsi civilians sought refuge, including churches, stadiums, and other gathering points.
- ICTR findings emphasized organization and coordinationnot spontaneous violenceincluding mobilization of personnel, provision of weapons, and direction of attacks.

## Legal Significance

- ICTR judgments helped establish precedent on how international courts evaluate leadership roles, orders, and assistance in mass atrocity cases.
- The tribunal's work contributed to the broader development of international criminal law on genocide, crimes against humanity, and war crimes.

## Legacy

Setako's conviction is part of the historical record of accountability efforts after 1994, alongside continuing debates about justice, reconciliation, and the long-term social impacts of the genocide in Rwanda.

ICTR cases like this also informed later international and hybrid tribunals by clarifying evidentiary standards for leadership involvement.
