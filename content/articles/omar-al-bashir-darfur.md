## Summary

Sudanese dictator who oversaw a genocidal campaign in Darfur that killed 300,000 people and displaced 2.5 million. He used Janjaweed militias to carry out ethnic cleansing.

## Context: The Darfur Conflict

- The conflict in Darfur escalated in the early 2000s, involving Sudanese government forces, allied militias, and various rebel groups.
- International attention focused on mass atrocities, large-scale displacement, and humanitarian crisis conditions.

## Role of the Sudanese State (High-Level)

- During Bashir’s presidency, Sudan’s security forces and aligned militias were accused of systematic attacks on civilians.
- The term “Janjaweed” is commonly used for militia groups widely reported to have participated in attacks, forced displacement, and other abuses.

## International Criminal Court (ICC)

- The ICC issued arrest warrants for Omar al-Bashir, including allegations related to war crimes, crimes against humanity, and genocide in Darfur.
- The case became a landmark because it involved an incumbent head of state at the time the warrants were issued.

## Aftermath and Political Developments

- Bashir was removed from power in 2019 amid political upheaval and a military takeover.
- Subsequent Sudanese authorities faced ongoing domestic and international pressure regarding accountability and potential cooperation with international courts.
