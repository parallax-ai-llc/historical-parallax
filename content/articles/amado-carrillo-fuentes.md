## Summary
Amado Carrillo Fuentes, known as 'The Lord of the Skies,' headed the Juárez Cartel and was considered Mexico's most powerful drug trafficker in the 1990s. He earned his nickname by using a fleet of Boeing 727s to transport cocaine. He died during plastic surgery to alter his appearance in 1997.

## Early Life
Born December 17, 1956, in Guamuchilito, Sinaloa. He was the nephew of drug lord Ernesto Fonseca Carrillo. He entered the drug trade young and rose through the Juárez Cartel. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Carrillo Fuentes used a fleet of Boeing 727 jets to transport cocaine from Colombia to Mexico, earning him the title 'Lord of the Skies.' He moved an estimated 200 tons of cocaine worth $25 billion. He corrupted Mexican military and government officials at the highest levels. He pioneered bulk air transportation of narcotics. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
On July 4, 1997, Carrillo Fuentes died during extensive plastic surgery and liposuction intended to alter his appearance. Two of the doctors who performed the surgery were later found dead, encased in concrete inside steel drums. The circumstances of his death remain controversial, with some theories suggesting murder. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
