## Summary

Italian Camorra boss from Nola who led one of the most powerful clans in the Neapolitan criminal organization during the 1980s and 1990s. He turned state's witness after his arrest and exposed widespread Camorra activities.

## Additional Context

- **Camorra context:** The Camorra is a networked set of organized-crime groups rooted in and around Naples, often characterized by multiple clans, shifting alliances, and competition over territory and illicit markets.
- **Alfieri clan:** Alfieri is commonly described in public sources as a leading figure associated with a powerful clan in the Nola area, with influence that expanded during periods of intense conflict among Camorra factions.
- **Organized-crime dynamics:** Coverage of the Camorra in the late 20th century frequently mentions cycles of violence tied to power struggles, contract killings, and disputes over revenue streams.

## Cooperation with Authorities

- **Pentito / collaborator:** After arrest, Alfieri became a state’s witness (a "collaboratore di giustizia"), providing testimony that contributed to investigations and prosecutions.
- **Impact of testimony:** Such cooperation is often significant in Italian anti-mafia efforts because it can map clan structures, financial flows, and operational methods that are difficult to prove through traditional evidence.

## Broader Significance

- Alfieri’s trajectory is frequently cited as an example of how leadership figures in organized crime can shift from violent competition to cooperation when faced with sustained law-enforcement pressure.
- Discussions about the Camorra often use cases like his to illustrate the challenges of dismantling decentralized criminal networks.

## Historical Notes

- Italian anti-mafia policy in this era combined investigative tools, witness protection, and specialized prosecutions targeting criminal associations.
- Public narratives about Camorra figures often emphasize the intersection of local politics, business interests, and coercive control in affected regions.
- Cooperation agreements are also debated for their trade-offs: improved prosecutorial reach versus reliance on insider testimony.
