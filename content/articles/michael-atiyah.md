## Summary

Michael Atiyah was a British-Lebanese mathematician who made foundational contributions to geometry, topology, and mathematical physics. He proved the Atiyah-Singer index theorem, which connects analysis, geometry, and topology. He received the Fields Medal in 1966 and the Abel Prize in 2004, making him one of the few to receive both awards.
