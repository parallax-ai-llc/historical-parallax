## Summary

Malian soldiers and Wagner mercenaries killed an estimated 300 civilians in Moura.

## Additional Context

### Location and context

- Moura is a town in central Mali (Mopti Region) that has been affected by Mali's broader conflict involving state forces and multiple armed groups.
- The incident occurred during a period when Mali's military leadership was cooperating with Russian private military contractors commonly referred to as the Wagner Group in security operations.

### What was reported

- Reporting described a large-scale security operation around Moura in late March 2022, followed by allegations of mass civilian killings.
- Multiple independent outlets and rights organizations reported accounts from survivors and witnesses describing arrests, detentions, and executions during the operation.

### Alleged perpetrators and dispute over accounts

- The primary allegations involved Malian armed forces, with Wagner-linked personnel reportedly present.
- Malian authorities disputed parts of the reporting and framed the operation as an anti-terrorist action, while external reporting emphasized civilian casualties.

### Casualties and victims

- Commonly cited estimates refer to roughly 300 people killed; some reports provide ranges and note uncertainty due to limited access and contested narratives.
- Sources frequently characterize many of the victims as civilians, with some reporting also mentioning suspected militants among those killed.

### International reaction and follow-up

- The incident drew international attention, including calls for investigations and accountability.
- Discussions around Moura have been cited in analyses of civilian protection risks, counterinsurgency practices, and the role of foreign contractors in Mali.
