## Summary

French-Moroccan ISIS sympathizer who attacked a supermarket in Trèbes killing four people including a police officer who volunteered as a hostage exchange. He was shot dead by police.

## Background

Public reporting describes Lakdim as a French citizen of Moroccan origin from the Aude department in southern France. Accounts commonly note a history of criminal activity and subsequent radicalization, with attention from security services prior to the 2018 attack.

## The 2018 Trèbes attack (high-level)

The incident is often grouped with events around Carcassonne and Trèbes on the same day. It culminated in an attack at a supermarket in Trèbes and a hostage situation. A widely reported element of the event was the death of a French gendarme officer who offered himself in exchange for a hostage and later died of injuries, becoming a national symbol of service and sacrifice.

## Response and investigation

French authorities treated the attack as terrorism and investigated potential contacts, planning, and ideological links. As with many lone-actor or small-cell cases, investigations focused on communications history, travel, weapons sourcing, and the attacker’s network.

## Political and social impact

The attack renewed public debate in France about counterterrorism, radicalization pathways, and the balance between security measures and civil liberties. Commemorations highlighted the gendarme officer’s actions and framed the event within a broader period of jihadist-inspired violence in Europe.
