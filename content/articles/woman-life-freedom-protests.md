## Summary

Nationwide protests following Mahsa Amini's death were met with brutal crackdown. Security forces killed over 500 protesters and arrested 22,000; the regime executed several demonstrators.

## Background

The protests began after the death of Mahsa (Jina) Amini in September 2022 following her arrest by Iran's morality police. Demonstrations spread across many provinces and drew participation from diverse age groups, including students and women publicly removing or burning headscarves.

The slogan "Woman, Life, Freedom" ("Zan, Zendegi, Azadi") became a central rallying cry, reflecting demands that mixed immediate accountability with broader calls for civil liberties and gender equality.

## Key Dynamics

- **State response:** Iranian authorities used live ammunition, mass arrests, and heavy surveillance to suppress demonstrations.
- **Digital environment:** Authorities periodically restricted internet access and messaging platforms, while protesters relied on social media to document events.
- **Diaspora activism:** Iranian communities abroad organized solidarity rallies and advocacy campaigns.

## International Response

Governments and human rights organizations condemned violence against protesters and called for investigations. Several countries imposed sanctions targeting Iranian officials and entities linked to repression.

## Legacy and Ongoing Significance

The movement is widely viewed as a major challenge to the Islamic Republic since 1979, with lasting cultural and political impact even after street protests diminished. It also elevated global attention to Iran's enforcement of compulsory hijab laws and the role of women in political dissent.
