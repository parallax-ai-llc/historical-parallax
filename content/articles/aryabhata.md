## Summary

Aryabhata was an ancient Indian mathematician and astronomer who is considered one of the greatest mathematicians of antiquity. He wrote the 'Aryabhatiya,' which covered mathematics and astronomy, introducing the place-value system and approximating pi to 3.1416. He proposed that the Earth rotates on its axis and correctly explained eclipses. His work profoundly influenced Indian and Islamic mathematics.
