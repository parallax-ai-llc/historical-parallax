## Summary

Dafne Schippers won the 2015 World Championship 200m gold and was the fastest European woman in the 200m.

## Additional Context

## Background

- Schippers competed internationally for the Netherlands and is known for successfully moving from combined events/sprints toward elite-level short sprinting.
- In European track and field, her 200m performances in the mid-2010s were widely cited as a benchmark for post-2000 European women’s sprinting.

## Notable Achievements

- **World Championships (2015):** Won the women’s 200 metres title.
- **Olympics (2016):** Won silver in the 200 metres at Rio.
- **European status:** Frequently described as the fastest European woman in the 200 metres during her peak years.

## Event Profile (200m Sprint)

- The 200m combines top-end speed with curve running and speed endurance; medal-level performance typically requires maintaining form through the final straight.
- Major titles in this event often turn on championship-round execution rather than season-best time alone.

## Major Competitions

- Major sprint titles are typically decided across heats, semifinals, and finals, requiring repeated high-quality runs within a short period.
- In global championships, lane draws and curve running ability can materially affect 200m outcomes.
