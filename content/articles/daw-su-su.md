## Summary

Daw Su Su was the mother of General Aung San, the founding father of modern Myanmar. Though historical records about her personal life are limited, she is remembered as the matriarch of an influential Burmese family and the grandmother of Aung San Suu Kyi, Myanmar's pro-democracy leader and State Counsellor.

## Early Life and Background

Little is documented about Daw Su Su's early life. She was married to U Pha, a lawyer, and the couple settled in Natmauk, a town in Magway District, Myanmar.

## Family and Children

Daw Su Su and U Pha had nine children together, with General Aung San being their youngest son. Aung San developed a particularly close attachment to his mother during his childhood, a relationship that would influence his early years.

## Historical Significance

While Daw Su Su herself did not hold public office or lead notable political movements, she is historically significant as the mother of General Aung San, who would go on to lead Myanmar's independence movement and establish the foundation for the modern Burmese nation. Her legacy is further recognized through her granddaughter, Aung San Suu Kyi, whose name honors her grandmother.

## Legacy

Daw Su Su's primary historical legacy rests in her role as a family matriarch whose descendants shaped Myanmar's modern political history. She represents the family foundation from which two of Myanmar's most influential figures emerged.

## Note on Historical Documentation

Detailed biographical information about Daw Su Su's personal life, achievements, or views is scarce in historical records. Most information about this period focuses on her son Aung San and granddaughter Aung San Suu Kyi rather than on her individual contributions or experiences.
