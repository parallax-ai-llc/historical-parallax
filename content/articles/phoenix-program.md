## Summary

CIA-run program to 'neutralize' Viet Cong operatives that killed between 20,000-40,000 suspected communist sympathizers. Many victims were innocent civilians killed based on poor intelligence.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1965 | Program began |

## Purpose and Design (High-Level)

- The Phoenix Program was a counterinsurgency and intelligence effort intended to disrupt the Viet Cong's political and administrative infrastructure.
- "Neutralization" was used as an umbrella term that could include:
  - capture,
  - defection inducement,
  - detention, and
  - killing.

## Methods Commonly Described

- Integration of intelligence collection with local security operations.
- Use of interrogation and informant networks to identify suspected cadre.
- Coordination with South Vietnamese security forces and provincial-level units.

## Controversy and Allegations

- Critics alleged the program incentivized body counts and produced:
  - mistaken identity killings,
  - torture and coercive interrogation,
  - corruption and personal vendettas disguised as counterinsurgency work, and
  - weak legal safeguards.
- The blurred boundary between intelligence targeting and law enforcement contributed to claims of systematic abuse.

## Oversight and Historical Debate

- Supporters argued Phoenix targeted insurgent organizers and reduced Viet Cong influence in some areas.
- Critics argued that the program's structure and incentives enabled serious human rights violations and undermined legitimacy.
- Historians continue to debate how to interpret reported casualty figures and how to apportion responsibility between U.S. and South Vietnamese agencies.
