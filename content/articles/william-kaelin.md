## Summary

William Kaelin is an American physician-scientist who shared the 2019 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for discoveries of how cells sense and adapt to oxygen availability. He discovered the role of the VHL tumor suppressor gene in oxygen sensing, showing how cells detect low oxygen levels and respond accordingly. This work has implications for treating anemia, cancer, and heart disease.
