## Summary

Alexander Schmemann was a Russian-American Orthodox priest and theologian who was one of the most influential liturgical theologians of the 20th century. His works For the Life of the World and Introduction to Liturgical Theology redefined the relationship between worship and theology. He served as dean of Saint Vladimir's Seminary and broadcast radio programs into the Soviet Union for Radio Liberty. His theology of the Eucharist as the center of Christian life influenced all Christian traditions.
