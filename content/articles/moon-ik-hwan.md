## Summary

Moon Ik-hwan was a Korean theologian and democracy activist who translated the Bible into modern Korean. While primarily a religious and political figure, his systematic linguistic analysis required scholarly rigor comparable to scientific methodology. He advocated for Korean reunification and democratic reform.
