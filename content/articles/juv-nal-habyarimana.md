## Summary

Rwandan president whose assassination when his plane was shot down triggered the 1994 genocide. His Hutu-dominated regime had built the infrastructure for mass killing before his death.

## Rise to Power and Presidency

Habyarimana came to power in 1973 through a military coup and led Rwanda for roughly two decades. His administration was characterized by strong centralized control and a political system dominated by a single ruling party and military-linked elites.

## Ethnic Politics and State Structure

Analysts of modern Rwandan history often describe his period in office as one in which ethnic identity became deeply intertwined with access to state power, employment, and security. Political opposition and independent media were constrained, and public life was organized around loyalty networks.

## International Context and the Arusha Accords

In the early 1990s, Rwanda faced civil war pressures and international diplomatic efforts to reach a settlement. The 1993 Arusha Accords aimed to establish power-sharing arrangements and integrate forces, but implementation was contested and fragile.

## 1994 Plane Crash and Aftermath

On 6 April 1994, the aircraft carrying Habyarimana was shot down near Kigali. The killings that followed escalated rapidly into the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi, with large-scale violence also directed at political moderates and others.

## Historical Assessment

Habyarimana’s legacy is widely discussed in scholarship on genocide and state violence, including debates about how governmental institutions, propaganda, and militia organization contributed to conditions that enabled mass atrocities.
