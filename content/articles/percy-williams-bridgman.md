## Summary

Percy Williams Bridgman was an American physicist who won the 1946 Nobel Prize in Physics for the invention of apparatus to produce extremely high pressures, and for discoveries he made in high-pressure physics. His work opened the field of high-pressure physics and led to discoveries about the behavior of matter under extreme conditions. He also wrote influential works on the philosophy and logic of physics.
