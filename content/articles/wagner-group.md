## Summary

Russian private military company that committed war crimes across Africa, Syria, and Ukraine. Led by Yevgeny Prigozhin, it recruited prisoners and staged a brief mutiny against Moscow in 2023.

## Additional Context

### What it is

The Wagner Group is widely described as a **Russian private military company (PMC)** and network of related entities that operated alongside Russian state interests while maintaining a degree of legal and organizational ambiguity.

### Areas of operation

Reporting and open-source research frequently link Wagner personnel to deployments in:

- **Ukraine** (notably intensified after Russia's 2022 full-scale invasion)
- **Syria** (supporting the Assad government)
- Multiple African states, including roles connected to security assistance, resource concessions, and influence operations

### Recruitment and organization

During the Ukraine war, Wagner became known for recruiting from Russian prisons and using penal colonies as a manpower source. The group also developed its own training and command structures, though oversight and ties to Russian security services have been widely debated.

### 2023 mutiny and aftermath

In June 2023, Prigozhin led a short-lived armed rebellion/"march" toward Moscow after escalating disputes with Russia's military leadership. The episode ended with a negotiated stand-down, and Prigozhin later died in an aircraft crash in August 2023.

### Allegations and scrutiny

International bodies, journalists, and human-rights organizations have alleged that Wagner personnel were involved in serious abuses and potential war crimes in multiple theaters. Because Wagner often operated in opaque environments, attribution and documentation vary by incident and source.
