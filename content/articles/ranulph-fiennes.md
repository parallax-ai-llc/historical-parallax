## Summary

Ranulph Fiennes is called the 'world's greatest living explorer.' He was the first to cross Antarctica on foot, first to reach both poles, and first to circumnavigate the world via both poles.

## Background

Sir Ranulph Twisleton-Wykeham-Fiennes is an English explorer and writer with a career that combines military service and expedition leadership. Sources describe him as having served in the British Army (including attachments to special forces units) before focusing on endurance and polar expeditions.

## Records and Recognition

- Often credited (including by Guinness-related commentary) with "greatest living explorer" acclaim in the 1980s.
- Undertook major polar travel achievements, including surface travel to both poles and long-distance Antarctic crossings.
- Has written extensively about expeditions and about other explorers, contributing to public history of polar exploration.

## Selected Themes

His legacy is frequently discussed in terms of logistics and risk management in extreme environments, as well as the use of high-profile expeditions to raise funds and attention for charitable causes.
