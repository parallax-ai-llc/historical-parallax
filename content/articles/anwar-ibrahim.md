## Summary

Anwar bin Ibrahim (born August 10, 1947) became the 10th Prime Minister of Malaysia on November 24, 2022, after a political career spanning more than four decades that included rising to Deputy Prime Minister, two imprisonments on widely disputed sodomy and corruption charges, and serving as the country's most prominent opposition figure. His ascent ended one of the longest political journeys in modern Asian politics.

---

## Early Life and Education

Born August 10, 1947, in Cherok Tok Kun, Penang:

**Background**:
- Father Ibrahim Abdul Rahman, hospital porter who became a politician
- Mother Che Yan Hamid
- Devout Muslim upbringing
- Strong tradition of Islamic scholarship

**Education**:
- Malay College Kuala Kangsar
- University of Malaya, BA in Malay Studies (1971)
- Active in student politics

---

## Activist Beginnings

### Islamic Youth Movement (ABIM)

**Founder** (1971):
- Co-founded Angkatan Belia Islam Malaysia (ABIM)
- Promoted Islamic values in Malaysian politics and society
- Became prominent Muslim youth voice in Southeast Asia
- Critic of UMNO government corruption

### Detention

**Internal Security Act** (1974):
- Arrested for leading peasant protest in Baling
- Detained without trial for nearly two years
- Built reputation as fearless dissident

---

## Government Career

### Joining UMNO (1982)

**Mahathir's Recruitment**:
- Prime Minister Mahathir Mohamad recruited Anwar from ABIM
- Surprised supporters with move into ruling party
- Rapidly promoted through ranks

**Ministerial Posts**:
- Minister of Culture, Youth and Sports (1983-1984)
- Minister of Agriculture (1984-1986)
- Minister of Education (1986-1991)
- Minister of Finance (1991-1998)

### Deputy Prime Minister (1993-1998)

**Heir Apparent**:
- Defeated Ghafar Baba for UMNO Deputy President
- Widely seen as Mahathir's successor
- Lauded internationally for handling Asian Financial Crisis (1997-98) early
- Disagreed with Mahathir on capital controls

---

## 1998 Sacking and Imprisonment

### "Reformasi" Crisis

**Dismissal**:
- Sacked September 2, 1998
- Charges of sodomy and corruption announced
- Accused of being "morally unfit"
- Sparked massive street protests ("Reformasi" movement)

**Trial and Imprisonment**:
- Black eye incident — beaten by police chief during detention
- Convicted of corruption (1999), sodomy (2000)
- Sentenced to total 15 years
- Sodomy conviction overturned 2004; released after six years

### Political Realignment

**People's Justice Party (PKR)**:
- Wife Wan Azizah founded PKR in his absence
- Built broad opposition coalition
- Mobilized urban middle class, minorities, reformist Malays

---

## Opposition Leader

### Returning to Politics

**Sodomy II** (2008-2015):
- New sodomy charge filed shortly after political comeback
- Acquitted 2012, convicted on appeal 2014
- Imprisoned 2015 for five years
- International criticism of trials as politically motivated

**Pakatan Rakyat / Pakatan Harapan**:
- Built multi-ethnic, multi-party opposition coalition
- 2008 election: opposition denied UMNO supermajority
- 2013 election: opposition won popular vote

### Royal Pardon and Release

**2018**:
- Pakatan Harapan won historic election under Mahathir
- King granted full royal pardon
- Released after Mahathir's surprise 2018 victory
- Designated as Mahathir's eventual successor

---

## Political Wilderness

### Sheraton Move (2020)

**Coalition Collapse**:
- Mahathir resigned amid succession dispute
- Defections engineered by Muhyiddin Yassin
- Anwar denied premiership again
- Years of political instability followed

### Long-Awaited Premiership

**2022 Election**:
- Hung parliament after general election
- Days of negotiations
- King appointed Anwar PM on November 24, 2022
- Unity government with former rivals UMNO

---

## Premiership

### Early Priorities

**Reform Agenda**:
- Anti-corruption drive (slower than promised)
- Subsidy rationalization
- "Madani" (civil) governance framework
- Inflation, cost-of-living measures

### Economic Policy

**Approach**:
- Pragmatic, business-friendly
- Foreign investment from China, US, Middle East
- Semiconductor and EV manufacturing push
- Continued race-based affirmative action policies

### Foreign Policy

**Multi-Aligned**:
- Strong support for Palestinians
- Critical of Israel during Gaza war
- Maintained relations with both China and US
- Active in ASEAN

---

## Controversies

### Compromises with UMNO

**Coalition with Former Enemies**:
- Allied with UMNO despite opposing them for decades
- Court cases against UMNO figures dropped or settled
- Disappointment among reformist supporters

### Implementation Pace

**Reform Lag**:
- Slow progress on institutional reforms
- Limits on press freedom remained
- Sedition Act, Communications Act not abolished
- Frustrated long-time supporters

### Family Politics

**Daughter Nurul Izzah**:
- Senior political and personal adviser
- Accusations of nepotism
- Stepped back from official roles after backlash

## Personal Life

**Family**:
- Married Wan Azizah Wan Ismail (1980)
- Six children including political successor Nurul Izzah Anwar
- Wan Azizah served as Deputy PM (2018-2020)
- Devoutly religious

**Intellectual**:
- Author of multiple books on Islam, governance
- Holds visiting professorships at Oxford, Georgetown, Johns Hopkins
- Friendships with Joseph Stiglitz, Al Gore

## Historical Significance

### Achievements Cited

- Largest reformist movement in Malaysian history
- Endured two unjust imprisonments
- Built first viable multi-ethnic opposition
- Eventually achieved democratic transition
- Symbol of reformasi across Muslim world

### Critical Views

- Compromised principles for power
- Slow on promised reforms
- Continued race-based policies
- Lack of accountability for past UMNO abuses
- Extended political instability
