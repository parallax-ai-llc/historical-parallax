## Summary

Indian serial killer who murdered 10 hospital patients in Lucknow by injecting toxic substances between 2004 and 2008.

## Modus Operandi (High-Level)

- The murders were reported as occurring in a medical setting, where victims were vulnerable due to illness or hospitalization.
- Accounts describe the killings as involving the **injection of toxic substances**, leveraging access and the clinical environment to avoid immediate detection.
- Hospital-based crimes of this kind often raise difficult questions about:
  - access control to medications and supplies,
  - record-keeping and auditing,
  - how to detect patterns amid routine patient deaths.

## Investigation and Case Development

- Investigations into serial hospital killings typically depend on:
  - identifying clusters of unusual deaths,
  - reconstructing staff movements and access opportunities,
  - comparing medical records for anomalies.
- In this case, authorities eventually focused on a suspect and proceeded to arrest and prosecution.

## Trial and Conviction

- Public sources describe a conviction in 2010 connected to the sequence of patient deaths.
- Such cases often hinge on:
  - forensic evidence and toxicology,
  - witness testimony and workplace records,
  - building a consistent timeline across multiple incidents.

## Broader Significance

- The case is frequently referenced in the context of patient safety and hospital governance.
