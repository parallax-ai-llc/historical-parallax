## Summary

Abdel Kader was an Algerian Sufi scholar and military leader who led resistance against the French conquest of Algeria. He was also a significant Sufi intellectual who studied and commented on Ibn Arabi's works. After his surrender and exile, he settled in Damascus where he saved thousands of Christians during the 1860 riots. He is considered both a national hero of Algeria and a model of interfaith tolerance.
