## Summary

American sex offender who kidnapped 11-year-old Jaycee Dugard and held her captive for 18 years in a hidden backyard compound. He fathered two children with his captive.

## Additional Context

### Captivity and Concealment

Garrido's case is primarily known for the prolonged captivity of Jaycee Dugard, who was abducted as a child and kept hidden for many years. Public summaries describe the confinement taking place on a residential property in a concealed area that was not readily visible from the street.

### Supervision Failures and Discovery

A recurring theme in coverage is the role of parole supervision and institutional oversight. Reports emphasize that Garrido had prior criminal convictions and was under supervision during parts of the period in question, and that the eventual discovery involved contact with law enforcement and parole officials.

### Criminal Proceedings and Sentencing

After the victim was located, Garrido and his spouse were prosecuted, with outcomes including extremely lengthy prison sentences described in secondary sources. The case is often cited as an example of how sentencing can be structured to ensure effective life imprisonment.

### Public Impact

The case drew significant attention in the United States and contributed to public discussions about missing-person investigations, risk assessment for supervised offenders, and the needs of survivors after long-term trauma.
