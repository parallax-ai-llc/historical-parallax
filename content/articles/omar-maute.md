## Summary

Filipino ISIS-aligned militant who co-led the Maute Group in the siege of Marawi City. He was killed alongside Isnilon Hapilon during the battle.

## Background

Omar Maute was one of the leaders of the Maute Group, a militant organization active in Mindanao in the southern Philippines. The group pledged allegiance to the Islamic State (IS) and operated alongside or in coordination with other extremist factions in the region.[^1]

## The Marawi Siege

The 2017 siege of Marawi became one of the most significant urban battles in the Philippines in recent decades. High-level aspects commonly emphasized include:

- militants seizing parts of the city and taking hostages,
- a prolonged campaign by the Armed Forces of the Philippines to retake territory,
- significant displacement of civilians and extensive damage to the city centre,
- the siege’s role in reshaping counter-terrorism priorities and regional security cooperation.[^1]

## Death and Aftermath

Omar Maute was reported killed during the final phases of the Marawi battle. The collapse of the siege did not end extremist violence in the region, but it was widely treated as a strategic defeat for IS-aligned militants in the Philippines.[^1]

## Broader Context

The Maute Group’s rise is often discussed alongside other militant organizations in Mindanao and the Sulu archipelago, where long-running separatist conflict, clan politics, and criminal economies have sometimes intersected with jihadist mobilization.[^1]

Marawi’s reconstruction and the return of displaced residents became major post-siege policy challenges, highlighting the long tail of humanitarian and governance impacts after urban conflict.[^1]
