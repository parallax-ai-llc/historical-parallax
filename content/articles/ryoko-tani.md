## Summary

Ryoko Tani won two Olympic golds and seven World titles. She was undefeated for 84 matches.

## Competitive significance

Tani was one of the most successful judoka in women’s lightweight divisions, building a long peak that spanned multiple Olympic cycles. Her results at World Championships and the Olympics made her a central figure in Japanese judo during a period when the sport was highly competitive internationally.

The reported 84-match unbeaten streak is often cited as shorthand for her consistency and ability to maintain dominance across different tournament settings.

## Style and strengths (high-level)

While detailed technical breakdowns vary by source, Tani’s reputation was built on:

- Reliable scoring ability in elite competition.
- Tactical discipline across long tournament days.
- Longevity: sustaining top form over many years rather than a single peak.

## Transition to public life

After her competitive career, Tani entered politics in Japan, serving as an elected member of parliament. This shift is frequently noted as an example of high-profile athletes moving into public service roles.

## Legacy

Tani is remembered as an athlete who combined major-championship success with durability. In judo, where rule changes and competitive cycles can reshape divisions quickly, her extended run of medals is a key part of why she remains widely referenced in the sport’s modern history.
