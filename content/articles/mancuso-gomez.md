## Summary

Salvatore Mancuso, AUC paramilitary chief, confessed to directing massacres that killed hundreds of civilians in northern Colombia. He cooperated with transitional justice.

## Background

- Salvatore Mancuso is associated with the United Self-Defense Forces of Colombia (AUC), a right-wing paramilitary umbrella group that emerged amid Colombia’s long internal armed conflict.
- Paramilitary structures in the AUC period combined counterinsurgency rhetoric with territorial control, forced displacement, and involvement in drug trafficking.
- Mancuso became one of the AUC’s prominent regional commanders, particularly linked to northern Colombia.

## Role in the AUC and Human Rights Abuses

- The AUC has been widely accused by Colombian courts and human rights organizations of massacres, targeted killings, and intimidation of civilians.
- Mancuso has publicly acknowledged responsibility for ordering or participating in a range of violent acts attributed to paramilitary units under his command.
- The scale of abuses is often described in terms of both direct killings and broader patterns of displacement and social control.

## Demobilization and Transitional Justice

- In the mid-2000s, parts of the AUC entered demobilization processes under Colombian policy frameworks intended to reduce violence and obtain truth about crimes.
- Cooperation has included testimony about chains of command, victims, and alleged links between armed groups and political or economic actors.
- Such cooperation remains contested in Colombia, balancing truth-telling and reparations against concerns about impunity.

## Legal Proceedings and International Dimension

- Mancuso was extradited to the United States on drug-related charges and later served a prison sentence there.
- After release, his removal from the U.S. involved transfers affecting Colombia and Italy, reflecting questions about nationality, jurisdiction, and outstanding legal matters.
- Ongoing debates have focused on how best to secure full testimony for victims while also pursuing accountability.
