## What “data broker” means in underground markets

In cybercrime ecosystems, a “broker” label typically indicates an actor who:

- Aggregates stolen datasets from multiple sources.
- Markets access or downloads to other criminals.
- Negotiates prices, escrow, and proof-of-life samples.

Some brokers specialize in initial access (credentials or footholds), while others focus on exfiltrated personal data, source code, or internal corporate documents.

## Attribution and verification limits

Public claims attached to a handle like “IntelBroker” can be difficult to verify because:

- Multiple people may use a single account name over time.
- Forum identities can be sold, hijacked, or impersonated.
- Samples shared as “proof” may be partial, outdated, or repackaged.

As a result, responsible incident reporting usually distinguishes between confirmed incidents (validated by affected organizations or independent researchers) and unverified claims.

## Common tactics associated with breach marketing

When a threat actor markets a breach, typical steps include:

- Posting an announcement with a target name and a short description of what was taken.
- Sharing a small sample to establish credibility.
- Setting a deadline or auction format to maximize attention.
- Threatening public release if payment is not made.

These dynamics influence how organizations respond, including legal review, customer notification obligations, and engagement with cybersecurity partners.
