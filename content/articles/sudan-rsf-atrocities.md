## Summary

Rapid Support Forces militia committed widespread atrocities including ethnic cleansing in Darfur during Sudan's civil war. Mass killings, sexual violence, and displacement affected millions.

## Background (High Level)

The Rapid Support Forces (RSF) emerged from earlier Sudanese paramilitary structures and became a powerful armed actor. In 2023, open conflict between the RSF and the Sudanese Armed Forces escalated into a nationwide crisis, with severe violence affecting Khartoum and multiple regions, including Darfur.

## Reported Abuses and Patterns

Human rights reporting and international coverage have described patterns of violence attributed to RSF units and allied militias, including:

- Targeted attacks on civilians and community leaders
- Large-scale killings and destruction of residential areas
- Sexual violence and other forms of gender-based violence
- Forced displacement and obstruction of humanitarian access

## Humanitarian Impact

The conflict has contributed to mass displacement (internally and across borders), collapse of essential services, food insecurity, and high civilian casualty risk. In many settings, the combination of urban fighting and ethnically targeted violence has complicated evacuation and relief operations.

## International Response (High Level)

International bodies and governments have issued condemnations, called for ceasefires, and supported investigations into alleged crimes. Documentation efforts often involve UN reporting, NGO investigations, survivor testimony, satellite imagery, and open-source verification.
