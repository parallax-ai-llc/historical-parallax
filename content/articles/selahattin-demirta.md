## Background

- Selahattin Demirtaş is a Kurdish politician and lawyer from Turkey who rose to national prominence through pro-Kurdish political movements.
- He became one of the most visible contemporary Kurdish political figures in Turkey during the 2010s, including through election campaigns and parliamentary activity.

## Political Roles and Public Profile

- He served as a leading figure in the Peoples' Democratic Party (HDP), a pro-Kurdish and left-wing political party that operated within Turkey's parliamentary system.
- In Turkish politics, he was known for emphasizing minority rights, democratization, and political pluralism, while also becoming a polarizing figure amid the country's security-focused debate over the Kurdish issue.

## Imprisonment and Human-Rights Context

- Demirtaş was detained and later held in pre-trial detention and imprisonment starting in 2016 in connection with terrorism-related accusations.
- The European Court of Human Rights (ECHR) has issued rulings regarding his detention, including calls for his release, which became a prominent point of international criticism and advocacy.
- His case is frequently cited in discussions about judicial independence, political freedoms, and the treatment of opposition figures in Turkey.
