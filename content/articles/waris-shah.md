## Summary

Waris Shah was a Punjabi Sufi poet best known for his epic retelling of Heer Ranjha, considered the greatest love poem in Punjabi literature. His version of this tragic love story is also a profound Sufi allegory of the soul's longing for God. He was educated at a mosque school and later settled in Malka Hans, Punjab. His work continues to be performed, recited, and celebrated across Punjab.
