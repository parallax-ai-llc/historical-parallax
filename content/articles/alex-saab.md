## Summary

Colombian-born businessman accused of being a front man for Venezuelan President Maduro's corruption network. He was extradited to the US from Cape Verde but later exchanged in a prisoner swap.

## Background

- Alex Saab is a Colombian-born businessman who built international commercial ties and later became associated in reporting and U.S. allegations with Venezuela’s government-linked procurement networks.[^1]
- Venezuelan authorities described him as a diplomat or special envoy during parts of the legal dispute, a claim contested by U.S. prosecutors and litigated in Cape Verdean and international fora.[^1]

## Allegations and U.S. Case (High Level)

- The United States accused Saab of participating in schemes involving money laundering tied to Venezuelan government contracts and overseas financial movements.[^1]
- The case became politically charged because it overlapped with U.S. sanctions policy toward Venezuela and broader disputes over the Maduro government’s legitimacy.[^1]

## Arrest in Cape Verde

- In 2020, Saab was detained in Cape Verde while his plane was refueling, based on a U.S. request tied to an American arrest warrant.[^1]
- His detention led to prolonged legal proceedings over jurisdiction, extradition, and the applicability of diplomatic protections claimed on his behalf.[^1]

## Extradition to the United States

- After a sequence of court decisions and appeals, Saab was extradited from Cape Verde to the United States to face charges in federal court.[^1]
- The extradition was accompanied by diplomatic friction and public campaigns by Venezuelan officials who asserted that the detention was unlawful.[^1]

## Prisoner Exchange and Release

- In 2023, Saab was released and returned to Venezuela as part of a prisoner swap involving the United States and Venezuela.[^1]
- His release was widely reported as a notable instance where criminal proceedings, sanctions policy, and diplomatic negotiations intersected in a single high-profile case.[^1]
