## Summary

Sébastien Ogier won eight WRC titles, second only to Loeb. He won six consecutive titles from 2013 to 2018.

## Place in modern rally history

Ogier emerged as a dominant figure immediately after Loeb’s main title streak ended, helping define the competitive narrative of the WRC in the 2010s. His championships across different seasons are often framed as proof of adaptability to evolving regulations and shifting team dynamics.

## High-level competitive strengths

- Season management: accumulating points with a combination of wins and consistent finishes.
- Surface versatility: strong performance on both tarmac and gravel events.
- Ability to integrate with top teams and remain competitive through technical changes.

## Titles and team context

Ogier’s most famous run is the six consecutive titles from 2013 to 2018, which placed him among the very small group of multi-time champions in the sport. His later title(s) reinforced that his success was not limited to a single “peak window.”

## Legacy

In discussions of the greatest rally drivers, Ogier is typically positioned just behind Loeb on total championships while being recognized as the defining champion of the post-Loeb era. His career also illustrates how rallying rewards long-term decision-making—car development, event selection, and risk control—alongside raw speed.

## Additional context

Ogier’s title count and his long span at the top level are often used to illustrate how rally drivers must balance aggression with risk control, especially in championships where a single crash can erase a season’s points.

## Key terms

- **Tarmac vs. gravel rallies:** different surface conditions that demand different driving styles and car setups.
