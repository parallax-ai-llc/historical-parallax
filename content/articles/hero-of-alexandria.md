## Summary

Hero (Heron) of Alexandria was a Greek-Egyptian mathematician and engineer who invented the first known steam-powered device (the aeolipile), an early vending machine, and many other mechanical devices. He formulated Heron's formula for the area of a triangle and made contributions to optics and geodesy. His work represents the height of ancient Greco-Roman engineering and invention.
