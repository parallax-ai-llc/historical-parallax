## Summary

Park Mun-ok was a pioneering Korean marine biologist who studied the biodiversity of Korea's coastal waters. He cataloged hundreds of marine species and contributed to the understanding of Korean fisheries ecology. His research helped establish marine conservation policies in South Korea.
