## Summary

Kagawa Toyohiko was a Japanese Christian reformer who dedicated his life to serving the poor and advocating for social justice. He lived in the slums of Kobe, organized cooperatives and labor unions, and was arrested for his peace activism during World War II. He was nominated for the Nobel Peace Prize multiple times and was one of the most internationally recognized Japanese Christians of the 20th century.
