## Summary

East Area Rapist/Original Night Stalker terrorized California for 12 years. Joseph DeAngelo committed 50 rapes, 13 murders, and 120 burglaries while serving as a police officer.

## Naming and scope

- “EARONS” is a combined label used by investigators and journalists to connect two crime series in California: the East Area Rapist and the Original Night Stalker.
- The crimes spanned multiple jurisdictions, which complicated early efforts to link cases and share investigative leads.

## Investigation

- The case became a well-known example of long-running serial investigations, with periodic task forces and renewed public attention over decades.
- In 2018, investigators identified Joseph James DeAngelo as a suspect; reporting widely attributes the breakthrough to DNA and genetic-genealogy methods combined with traditional police work.

## Legal outcome

- DeAngelo later entered guilty pleas in a California court (2020) in a consolidated set of charges, providing a measure of resolution for some survivors and families.
- Sentencing resulted in multiple life terms, and the proceedings included victim-impact statements that documented the lasting harm of the crimes.

## Broader impact

- The EARONS investigation is frequently discussed in relation to the evolution of forensic DNA databases, inter-agency coordination, and the ethics and governance of genealogy-based policing.
- The case also influenced public understanding of “cold cases” and the role of persistence, data, and modern forensics in historical investigations.
