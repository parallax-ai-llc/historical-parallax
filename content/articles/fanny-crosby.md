## Summary

Fanny Crosby was an American mission worker, poet, lyricist, and composer who was one of the most prolific hymn writers in history. Blind from infancy due to a medical error, she wrote over 8,000 hymns, including 'Blessed Assurance,' 'To God Be the Glory,' and 'Pass Me Not, O Gentle Savior.' Her hymns became central to evangelical worship and revival meetings. She was also active in urban mission work in New York City.
