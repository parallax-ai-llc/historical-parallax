## Summary

Igor Sikorsky was a Russian-American aviation pioneer who designed and built the first successful helicopter in the United States (VS-300) in 1939. He also built the first multi-engine fixed-wing aircraft (Ilya Muromets) in Russia in 1913 and pioneered flying boats for Pan American Airways. His helicopters transformed military operations, search and rescue, and civilian transportation.
