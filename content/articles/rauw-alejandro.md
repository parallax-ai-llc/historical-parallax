## Summary

Rauw Alejandro (born Raúl Alejandro Ocasio Ruiz on January 10, 1993) is a Puerto Rican singer, songwriter, and dancer who has become one of the most prominent figures in contemporary Latin urban music. Known for blending reggaeton with R&B, dancehall, and electronic influences, he has carved out a distinctive sonic identity that sets him apart from peers in the genre.

Since breaking through in the late 2010s under Sony Music Latin, Alejandro has accumulated billions of streams, sold-out international tours, and collaborations with some of the biggest names in Latin and global pop. His polished stage presence, rooted in formal dance training, has made him a compelling live performer in a genre not always defined by choreography.

He represents a new generation of Latin urban artists who have expanded the genre's reach beyond traditional markets, earning recognition from mainstream institutions including the Latin Grammy Awards.

## Early Life & Background

Rauw Alejandro was born and raised in San Juan, Puerto Rico. From a young age he was immersed in music through his family environment, and he developed a parallel passion for dance, training seriously in styles including hip-hop, salsa, and contemporary dance. He relocated to Orlando, Florida for a period during his formative years, where he deepened his exposure to American R&B and pop music. This bicultural experience would later shape the cross-genre sensibility that defines his sound.

## Rise to Prominence

Alejandro began posting music independently before catching the attention of industry figures. He signed with Sony Music Latin and released his debut single "Toda" in 2017, which gained traction on digital platforms. His 2019 debut studio album *Afrodisíaco* — released in late 2020 — cemented his arrival, blending reggaeton tempos with R&B vocal production. Collaborations with Anuel AA, Daddy Yankee, and J Balvin helped expand his profile rapidly. His feature on Camilo's "Tattoo (Remix)" in 2020 became a global Latin hit, reaching the top 10 in numerous countries.

## Key Achievements

- His 2021 album *Vice Versa* debuted at number one on the Billboard Top Latin Albums chart.
- The album *Saturno* (2022) showcased a more experimental direction, incorporating synth-pop and futuristic aesthetics, and earned him a Latin Grammy Award for Best Urban Fusion/Interpretation.
- He headlined major festival stages across North America, Europe, and Latin America between 2022 and 2024.
- His collaboration with Rosalía, "Beso" (2023), released during their high-profile relationship, peaked inside the top 5 on the Hot Latin Songs chart and generated over 400 million streams within months.
- By 2025, Alejandro had accumulated over 30 billion combined streams across platforms.

## Controversies & Criticism

The most publicly discussed episode in Alejandro's personal life was his engagement to Spanish pop star Rosalía, announced in 2023, followed by their separation later that same year. The breakup attracted intense media scrutiny. Some critics have questioned whether his output has prioritized commercial polish over artistic depth, though this is a common critique leveled at artists working in mainstream Latin urban music.

## Legacy & Influence

Rauw Alejandro has helped normalize a more genre-fluid approach within Latin urban music, demonstrating that reggaeton artists can integrate R&B, electronic, and pop structures without losing commercial viability. His emphasis on dance as a component of Latin pop performance has influenced a younger cohort of artists. At 33, he remains an active and evolving artist with a substantial catalog and continued international reach.
