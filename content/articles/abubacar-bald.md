## Summary

Guinea-Bissauan military officer involved in making Guinea-Bissau a narco-state. The country became a major transit point for South American cocaine heading to Europe, with military and government officials facilitating the trade.

## Additional Context

## Regional Trafficking Dynamics

Guinea-Bissau has been described by analysts and international agencies as vulnerable to organized crime because of weak institutions, limited maritime and airspace monitoring, and political instability. In the 2000s, West Africa became a significant corridor for cocaine shipments moving from South America toward European markets.

## Allegations and U.S. Actions

U.S. authorities publicly alleged that senior figures within the security services and political system played roles in facilitating trafficking. Baldé was among individuals targeted through sanctions and law-enforcement actions intended to disrupt networks operating across borders.

## 2013 DEA Sting

Accounts of the 2013 case describe an international operation in which suspects were lured into negotiations and then arrested on drug-trafficking-related charges. The episode was frequently cited as an example of external law-enforcement efforts to reach actors believed to be protected by domestic power structures.

## Broader Impact

The “narco-state” characterization became shorthand for concerns that trafficking revenues could distort governance, undermine rule of law, and intensify competition within armed forces and political elites.
