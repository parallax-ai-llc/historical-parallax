## Summary

Ransomware gang that attacked MGM Resorts, causing $100 million in losses, and Change Healthcare, disrupting US prescription processing for weeks.

## Overview

ALPHV (also known as **BlackCat**) is widely described as a ransomware operation that offers tooling and infrastructure to **affiliates**, who conduct intrusions and deploy the ransomware in exchange for a share of proceeds.[^1]

## Modus Operandi (High-Level)

Public reporting commonly associates the group with:

- **Ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS)**: operators provide the malware and “brand,” while affiliates perform intrusions.[^1]
- **Data theft + extortion** (“double extortion”): victim data is exfiltrated and threatened with publication to increase leverage.[^1]
- **Leak-site pressure**: stolen data may be posted or previewed publicly when negotiations stall.[^1]

## Technical Notes (High-Level)

- The "ALPHV"/BlackCat ransomware has been publicly characterized as being written in **Rust**, a relatively uncommon choice among ransomware families at the time it emerged.[^1]
- Like other major ransomware programs, campaigns have been linked in public reporting to broader criminal ecosystems, including the reuse of personnel, infrastructure, or tactics across successor brands.[^1]

## Notable Incidents

- **MGM Resorts (2023)**: The MGM cyberattack led to widespread disruption (including impacts on hotel operations) and was reported to have caused losses on the order of **$100 million**.[^1]
- **Change Healthcare (2024)**: The incident affected a major U.S. healthcare technology provider, causing significant disruption across pharmacy and claims workflows and drawing national attention to systemic dependency on healthcare intermediaries.[^1]

## Why It Matters

ALPHV/BlackCat is often cited in discussions of ransomware risk because it illustrates how modern ransomware groups combine:

- financially motivated intrusion operations,
- scalable affiliate models,
- and coordinated extortion channels,

to produce real-world consequences that extend beyond IT downtime into critical services and supply-chain dependencies.[^1]
