### Context: what “transnational drug trafficking” usually implies

This label typically refers to drug distribution or smuggling activity that:

- crosses national borders (source, transit, and destination jurisdictions),
- uses networks of couriers, intermediaries, and facilitators rather than a single actor,
- relies on logistics channels such as air passengers, freight, maritime shipping, or postal routes,
- involves money movement and concealment (cash movement, structuring, front businesses), and
- triggers cooperation between multiple law-enforcement agencies.
