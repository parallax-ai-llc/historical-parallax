## Summary

Thomas à Kempis was a German-Dutch canon regular and the probable author of The Imitation of Christ, one of the most widely read Christian devotional works. The book emphasizes inner spiritual life and withdrawal from worldly concerns. He spent most of his life in the Augustinian monastery of Mount Saint Agnes in the Netherlands. His writings represent the pinnacle of the Devotio Moderna movement.
