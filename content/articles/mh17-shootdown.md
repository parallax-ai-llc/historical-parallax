## Summary

Downing of Malaysia Airlines Flight 17 over eastern Ukraine on July 17, 2014 by a Russian Buk missile that killed all 298 people aboard. Dutch investigators determined it was fired by Russian-backed separatists.

## Background

Malaysia Airlines Flight 17 (MH17) was a scheduled passenger flight from Amsterdam to Kuala Lumpur. The aircraft, a Boeing 777-200ER, was flying over eastern Ukraine during the armed conflict in the Donbas region.

## What Happened (High-Level)

On 17 July 2014, MH17 was shot down while cruising at high altitude. All 298 passengers and crew were killed. The incident immediately became a major international crisis because many victims were citizens of the Netherlands and other countries.

## Investigation and Attribution

A multinational Joint Investigation Team (JIT) led by Dutch authorities investigated the incident. Dutch-led investigations concluded that the aircraft was brought down by a Buk surface-to-air missile system and that the system originated from the Russian Federation and was transported into and out of separatist-controlled territory.

## Legal Proceedings

Dutch courts prosecuted suspects in absentia. In 2022, a Dutch court convicted three men for their role in the downing and the handling of the missile system; a fourth defendant was acquitted. The case remains central to ongoing international legal and diplomatic disputes related to responsibility.

## International Impact

The shootdown intensified sanctions and diplomatic pressure related to the Russia–Ukraine conflict and reinforced the risks of civilian overflights in active conflict zones. The incident also contributed to changes in aviation risk assessment and route planning practices for airlines.

## Key Points

- The victims came from multiple countries, making the event a global humanitarian and political issue.
- Investigations focused on identifying the weapon system, its chain of custody, and the parties responsible for deployment.
- Court rulings addressed individual criminal responsibility, while state responsibility continues to be debated in international forums.
