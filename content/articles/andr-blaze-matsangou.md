## Context: “Ill-Gotten Gains” Investigations (France)

The term “Ill-Gotten Gains” is commonly used in English-language reporting to describe long-running French investigations and court proceedings focused on assets suspected to have been acquired through corruption and then held abroad (including through high-value real estate). These cases have frequently centered on politically exposed persons (PEPs) and their networks.

In public discussions, the Republic of the Congo under Denis Sassou Nguesso is often mentioned as part of a broader set of jurisdictions where investigators and NGOs have alleged misuse of public funds and subsequent asset concealment.
