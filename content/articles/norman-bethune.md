## Summary

Norman Bethune was a pioneering thoracic surgeon who developed the world's first mobile blood transfusion service during the Spanish Civil War. He later served with the Chinese Communist forces, providing frontline medical care. He is revered as a national hero in China for his selfless service and died of septicemia while treating wounded soldiers.
