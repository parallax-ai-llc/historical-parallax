## Summary

René Laennec invented the stethoscope in 1816 and pioneered the technique of auscultation — listening to internal body sounds for diagnosis. His 1819 treatise on mediate auscultation described the sounds of various lung and heart diseases. His invention remains the most iconic tool in clinical medicine.
