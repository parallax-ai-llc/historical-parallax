## Summary

Brazilian politician who as Speaker of the House led the impeachment of President Dilma Rousseff while hiding $40 million in Swiss bank accounts. He was convicted of corruption in the Lava Jato investigation. Sentenced to 15 years.

## Political Context

Cunha rose within Brazil’s Chamber of Deputies and became a central power broker during a period of intense political polarization. As Speaker of the House (Presidente da Câmara), he controlled legislative agenda-setting, committee assignments, and procedural decisions that shaped congressional strategy.

## Role in the Rousseff Impeachment Process

As Speaker, Cunha accepted and advanced impeachment proceedings against President Dilma Rousseff. The impeachment itself was formally grounded in alleged fiscal and budgetary irregularities, but it unfolded amid broader disputes over governance, corruption investigations, and coalition politics.

## Corruption Investigations (Operation Car Wash)

Cunha became one of the prominent political figures associated in open-source reporting with the wider “Lava Jato” (Operation Car Wash) investigations into bribery, kickbacks, and illicit financing connected to state-linked enterprises and major contractors. Public summaries often focus on allegations involving offshore structures and foreign accounts.
