## Summary

PNG politician convicted of misappropriating millions in public funds meant for development projects.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2014 | Convicted of misappropriating public funds |

## Background

- Reported as a former Papua New Guinean politician associated with the National Alliance, representing **Pomio Open** in the National Parliament (first elected in **2002**).[^1]
- Public profiles describe prior work in the public sector and development projects, including roles connected to Papua New Guinea’s petroleum/energy administration and a World Bank–funded gas technical assistance project.[^1]
- Education is reported to include:
  - Bachelor’s degree (University of Papua New Guinea).[^1]
  - Master’s in resources law (University of Dundee, Scotland).[^1]

## Government Roles (Reported)

- **Chairman**, Special Parliamentary Committee on Gas and Energy Development (after entering parliament).[^1]
- **Trade and Industry Minister** (appointed 12 Nov 2003).[^1]
- **Minister of Foreign Affairs** (2005–2007), under Prime Minister Michael Somare.[^1]
- **Minister for National Planning and District Development** (after re-election in 2007; later lost the portfolio in 2011 amid political realignment/defections).[^1]

## Allegations and Controversies (High-Level)

- **Taiwan diplomatic scandal (2008):** allegations were aired in a Taiwanese court that funds intended to bolster diplomatic relations were personally diverted by multiple PNG officials; Tiensten is reported to have acknowledged meetings while denying wrongdoing.[^1]
- **Kikori funds controversy (2010):** reported accusations connected to very large public sums and landowner associations; details are often summarized as alleged misuse or irregularities tied to development allocations.[^1]

## Conviction and Sentence

- Widely reported as having been **convicted** for corruption-related offenses involving public funds intended for development projects, receiving a **multi-year prison sentence**.[^1]
- In short-form summaries, the case is commonly described as misappropriation of “millions” in public funding (often framed as district/development funds).[^1]
