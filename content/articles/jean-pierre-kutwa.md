## Summary

Cardinal who controversially sided with Laurent Gbagbo during the 2010 Ivorian political crisis. His stance was criticized for exacerbating ethnic and political divisions.

## Additional Context

### Church role

- Jean-Pierre Kutwa is an Ivorian Roman Catholic cleric who served in senior leadership roles within the Church in Côte d’Ivoire.
- He is commonly associated with the Archdiocese of Abidjan, a key diocese in the country’s religious and civic life.

### 2010–2011 Ivorian political crisis (high level)

- The 2010 presidential election led to a disputed outcome and a broader political crisis that included violence and major social polarization.
- Religious leaders, including Catholic bishops and clergy, were closely watched for public statements because of their influence and perceived moral authority.

### Public perception and criticism

- In public reporting and commentary, Kutwa’s statements during the crisis were sometimes interpreted as aligning with one side of the political dispute.
- Critics argued that such messaging could be read as contributing to political or ethnic tension; supporters and some observers emphasized the complexity of the crisis environment and the pressures on public figures.

### Significance

- The episode illustrates how senior religious leaders can become involved—directly or indirectly—in national political debates during periods of instability.
- It also highlights the reputational risk faced by faith institutions when public statements are perceived as partisan.
