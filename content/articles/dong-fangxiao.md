## Additional Context

### Why the Medal Was Stripped

- Dong Fangxiao competed as part of China’s **women’s gymnastics team** at the **2000 Sydney Olympics**, which originally received the **bronze medal**.
- Later documentation reviewed by the sport’s governing bodies indicated she was **under the minimum age requirement** at the time of competition.
- The case became one of the most prominent examples of how **age eligibility rules** are enforced in international gymnastics.

### Broader Impact on Gymnastics Governance

- The controversy contributed to increased scrutiny of:
  - **Birth record reliability** and documentation standards.
  - The incentives created by elite sport systems that reward early specialization.
  - The processes used by federations to verify athletes’ eligibility.

### Legacy

- Dong Fangxiao’s case is often referenced alongside later investigations involving other athletes, and is frequently cited in discussions about:
  - **Fairness and athlete welfare**.
  - The credibility of international competition records.
  - How retrospective rulings can alter Olympic results years after the event.

### Notes

- The case is also used in sports ethics discussions about how responsibility should be allocated between **athletes, coaches, and federations** when eligibility violations occur.
- It highlights the long time horizons involved: decisions taken in youth sport can be judged years later under international rules.
