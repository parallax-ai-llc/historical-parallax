## Summary

South Korean former interior minister who was impeached for his handling of the 2022 Itaewon Halloween crowd crush that killed 159 people. He was accused of negligence and covering up responsibility.

## Office and Responsibilities

As Minister of the Interior and Safety, Lee Sang-min was associated with national-level coordination for disaster response, public safety administration, and oversight of local government systems. In the aftermath of the Itaewon crowd crush, public debate focused on how responsibilities were divided among central ministries, metropolitan authorities, police, and emergency services.

## Itaewon Crowd Crush (2022)

The Itaewon Halloween crowd crush in Seoul became one of South Korea's deadliest peacetime incidents. Reporting on official investigations and inquiries commonly addresses:

- Crowd-management planning and risk assessment in a dense nightlife district.
- Communications and command structure among police and emergency responders.
- The timing and adequacy of warnings, dispatch, and on-scene response.

## Impeachment Proceedings

The National Assembly voted to impeach Lee in early 2023, framing the case around alleged negligence and failures of duty. The impeachment process in South Korea requires review by the Constitutional Court, which evaluates whether conduct rises to the level justifying removal from office.

## Constitutional Court Decision

In 2024, the Constitutional Court dismissed the impeachment, allowing Lee to remain in office. Summaries of the ruling often distinguish between political responsibility and the legal threshold required for impeachment under the constitution.

## Broader Significance

The episode is frequently discussed in the context of:

- Government accountability after major disasters.
- Institutional reforms to crowd safety and emergency coordination.
- Public trust in disaster-prevention systems and crisis leadership.
