## Summary

Hermann Oberth was one of the founding fathers of rocketry and astronautics, alongside Tsiolkovsky and Goddard. His 1923 book The Rocket into Planetary Space provided the mathematical proof that rockets could travel beyond Earth's atmosphere. He inspired and later worked with Wernher von Braun and was instrumental in the development of modern rocketry.
