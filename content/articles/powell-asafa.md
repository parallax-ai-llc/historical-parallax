## Summary

Asafa Powell held the 100m world record of 9.74 seconds before Usain Bolt and won Olympic relay gold at 2008 Beijing.

## Highlights and Records

- Powell was one of the defining male sprinters of the mid-2000s, regularly running sub-10 seconds in the 100m.
- He set and then repeatedly matched or lowered the men’s 100m world record prior to the record being surpassed by Usain Bolt.

## Major Championships (High-Level)

- Competed for Jamaica across multiple World Championships and Olympic cycles.
- Known for relay performances as part of Jamaica’s sprint program during its rise to global dominance.

## Later Career and Challenges

- Faced periods of injury and form fluctuations that affected consistency at major finals.
- Like many elite sprinters, navigated increased scrutiny around anti-doping compliance and sanctions in the sport.
