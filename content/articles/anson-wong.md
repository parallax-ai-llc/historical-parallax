## Summary

Malaysian wildlife trafficker known as the 'Lizard King' who was one of the world's biggest illegal wildlife traders. He smuggled endangered reptiles, birds, and other animals worldwide. Arrested multiple times in Malaysia and the US.

## Background and Modus Operandi

Anson Wong became notable for large-scale trafficking of protected wildlife, particularly reptiles. The nickname "Lizard King" is associated with his role in moving live animals across borders using concealment and fraudulent paperwork. Such networks typically rely on collectors and dealers in multiple countries, exploiting differences in enforcement and demand for exotic pets.

## Legal and Enforcement Notes

Wildlife trafficking cases involving international shipments can trigger prosecution under national wildlife laws as well as import/export controls. In the United States, cases like Wong's have been prosecuted under the Lacey Act and related wildlife statutes when protected species are smuggled or traded in violation of foreign or domestic law.

## Significance

The case is frequently cited in discussions of how profitable, organized, and repeat-offender wildlife trafficking can be, and how sentencing varies across jurisdictions. It is also used as an example of the conservation impact of illicit trade in endangered species.
