## Summary

Theodor Schwann was a German physiologist who is one of the founders of cell theory, establishing that all animals are composed of cells. He also discovered pepsin, the first enzyme isolated from animal tissue, and coined the term 'metabolism.' His work with Matthias Schleiden established cell theory as one of the foundational principles of biology.
