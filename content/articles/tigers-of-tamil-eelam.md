## Overview

The **Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE)**—sometimes informally referred to as the “Tigers of Tamil Eelam”—was a Sri Lankan Tamil separatist organization that sought an independent Tamil state (“Tamil Eelam”) in the north and east of Sri Lanka.

## Origins and Drivers of Conflict

The LTTE emerged amid longstanding ethnic and political tensions in Sri Lanka, including disputes over language policy, political representation, discrimination claims, and cycles of communal violence. Over time, these tensions escalated into protracted civil war.

## Methods and Organization

Reporting and historical accounts describe the LTTE as an armed insurgent movement that developed:

- a centralized command structure,
- conventional and guerrilla capabilities,
- intelligence and overseas fundraising networks.

It was also widely accused of severe human-rights abuses, including assassinations, forced recruitment, and attacks on civilians. Several governments designated it as a terrorist organization.

## War and Defeat

The conflict lasted for decades and caused large-scale displacement and civilian casualties. In 2009, the Sri Lankan government militarily defeated the LTTE in the final phase of the civil war.

## Aftermath and Legacy

Post-war debates have focused on:

- accountability for abuses by multiple actors,
- the status of Tamil political rights and reconciliation,
- the humanitarian impact of the war’s endgame.
