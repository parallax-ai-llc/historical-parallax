## Summary

Wilayat Sinai is an Islamic State (ISIS) affiliate operating primarily in Egypt’s Sinai Peninsula. It is widely associated with large-scale attacks including the 2015 destruction of Metrojet Flight 9268 and the 2017 al-Rawda mosque massacre, as well as a long-running insurgency targeting Egyptian security forces and local communities.

## Background

The group emerged out of the broader Sinai-based militant milieu that expanded after the 2011 Egyptian revolution and subsequent security crackdowns. In 2014 it pledged allegiance to ISIS and began operating under the “Wilayat Sinai” name, aligning its propaganda and operational branding with the Islamic State’s global network.

## Areas of Operation

- **Primary geography:** Northern and central Sinai (including areas around El Arish, Rafah, and Sheikh Zuweid).
- **Secondary activity:** The group has been linked in reporting to occasional operations or networks connected to mainland Egypt, though its core theater is Sinai.

## Tactics and Targeting Patterns

High-level reporting describes a mix of guerrilla-style tactics and mass-casualty violence, including:

- roadside bombs and ambushes against military and police patrols
- coordinated assaults on checkpoints and bases
- attacks on civilian targets, including religious sites and public infrastructure
- propaganda and intimidation campaigns aimed at controlling local populations

## Designations and International Response

Wilayat Sinai is generally treated in open-source reporting as part of the Islamic State’s organizational ecosystem. Various governments and international bodies have designated ISIS and its branches as terrorist entities, which typically triggers sanctions and counter-financing measures. On the ground, Egypt has pursued prolonged security operations in Sinai aimed at disrupting networks, restricting movement corridors, and reducing access to weapons and explosives.
