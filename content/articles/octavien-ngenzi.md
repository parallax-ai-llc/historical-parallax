## Summary

Former Rwandan mayor convicted of genocide by a French court for organizing the massacre of 2,000 Tutsi refugees at Kabarondo church. He had been living in France as a refugee.

## Additional Context

### 1994 Genocide in Rwanda

The 1994 genocide against the Tutsi in Rwanda involved mass violence across the country over a period of weeks, with participation by state structures, militia groups, and civilian networks in many localities. Mayors and other local officials have been a recurrent focus in later legal proceedings because municipal administrations could influence security forces, public messaging, and the organization of attacks.

### Accountability Outside Rwanda

A number of genocide-related cases have been tried outside Rwanda under principles such as universal jurisdiction or domestic criminal statutes covering crimes against humanity. France has been one venue for such prosecutions, including cases involving suspects who had settled in the country after 1994.

### Evidence and Court Findings

In genocide trials, courts typically rely on a combination of survivor testimony, documentary records, expert historical context, and corroborating witness accounts to assess command responsibility and participation in specific events. Convictions in foreign courts can also contribute to broader historical documentation by creating publicly accessible judicial records.
