## Summary

Fulton J. Sheen was an American Catholic bishop, author, and pioneer of religious broadcasting. His television show Life Is Worth Living drew 30 million viewers weekly in the 1950s, making him the most famous Catholic figure in America. He wrote over 70 books and won an Emmy Award. He was declared Venerable by Pope Benedict XVI in 2012.
