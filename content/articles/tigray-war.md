## Summary

War in Ethiopias Tigray region from 2020-2022 involving Ethiopian and Eritrean forces. The conflict resulted in mass atrocities, sexual violence, and a humanitarian crisis affecting millions.

## Background

The conflict emerged from a political crisis within Ethiopia’s federal system following years of tension between the federal government and the Tigray People’s Liberation Front (TPLF), which had been a dominant force in Ethiopian politics for decades. Disputes over governance, legitimacy, and the timing of elections contributed to a rapid breakdown in relations.

## Main Parties

The war involved multiple armed actors, including Ethiopian federal forces and allied regional units, the TPLF/Tigray Defense Forces, and—according to widespread reporting—Eritrean forces operating in and around Tigray. The participation of different forces and shifting alliances contributed to a complex and highly fluid battlefield.

## Humanitarian Impact

International organizations and human rights groups reported large-scale civilian harm, including killings, sexual violence, forced displacement, and destruction of infrastructure. Access constraints, communications blackouts, and insecurity made independent verification difficult in parts of the conflict, while humanitarian needs expanded as services and supply lines were disrupted.

## Ceasefire and Aftermath

A major cessation of hostilities agreement was reached in late 2022, commonly associated with the Pretoria agreement, and was followed by additional implementation steps. Post-war challenges have included humanitarian recovery, accountability debates, reintegration and demobilization issues, and political negotiations over autonomy and representation.

## Why It Matters

The Tigray War is often discussed as a case study in modern intrastate conflict with significant regional spillover risks. It drew sustained international attention due to allegations of atrocities, the scale of humanitarian need, and the diplomatic efforts required to achieve a negotiated pause in fighting.

## International Response

Foreign governments and multilateral organizations engaged in diplomacy and public advocacy around humanitarian access and civilian protection, while also debating sanctions, leverage, and accountability mechanisms. The conflict also highlighted how information constraints and competing narratives can shape global understanding of events on the ground.
