## Summary

Luis Alvarez was an American experimental physicist who won the 1968 Nobel Prize in Physics for developing the hydrogen bubble chamber. He and his son Walter famously proposed the asteroid impact theory for the extinction of dinosaurs in 1980, based on the iridium anomaly at the K-T boundary. He held over 40 patents.
