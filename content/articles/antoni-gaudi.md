## Summary

Antoni Gaudi was a Catalan architect whose extraordinary organic designs defined the Art Nouveau movement in Barcelona. His masterwork, the Sagrada Familia basilica, begun in 1882, remains under construction and is one of the most visited buildings in the world. His unique vision combined nature, religion, and Catalan identity.
