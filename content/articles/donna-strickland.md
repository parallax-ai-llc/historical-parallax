## Summary

Donna Strickland is a Canadian optical physicist who shared the 2018 Nobel Prize in Physics for her development of chirped pulse amplification with GÃ©rard Mourou. She was only the third woman to win the Nobel Prize in Physics. Their technique for generating high-intensity ultrashort optical pulses revolutionized laser physics and enabled applications in medicine, industry, and science. Notably, she did not have a Wikipedia page until her Nobel Prize was announced.
