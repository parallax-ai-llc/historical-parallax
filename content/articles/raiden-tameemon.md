## Summary

Raiden Tameemon had the highest winning percentage in sumo history (96.2%) with a 254-10 record across 21 years. Despite his dominance, he was controversially never promoted to yokozuna.

## Additional Context

- Raiden competed in the Edo period, when professional sumo was closely tied to patronage, regional identity, and ceremonial culture.
- Records from early sumo are often reconstructed from tournament documents and historical accounts rather than modern statistical archives.

## Style and Reputation

- He is remembered for dominance in top-division competition and is frequently mentioned among the greatest rikishi in sumo history.
- The fact that he was never promoted to yokozuna is typically discussed as an example of how rank could reflect politics, custom, or gatekeeping in addition to performance.

## Understanding the Numbers

- Win-loss figures in historical sumo are sometimes cited to illustrate comparative dominance, but era differences (format, number of bouts, and ranking rules) matter.
- A very high winning percentage over many tournaments suggests both peak strength and sustained consistency.

## Legacy

- Raiden's name appears in popular histories of sumo and is used as a shorthand for unmatched strength in the pre-modern era.
- Often referenced when explaining the evolution of the yokozuna title and the formalization of modern sumo institutions.
