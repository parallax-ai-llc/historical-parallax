## Summary

Noguchi Hideyo was a prominent Japanese bacteriologist who made important contributions to the study of syphilis, yellow fever, and other infectious diseases. Working at the Rockefeller Institute in New York, he demonstrated the syphilis spirochete in the brains of paresis patients. He died of yellow fever while researching the disease in West Africa.
