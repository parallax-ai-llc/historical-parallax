## Overview

Mahendra Singh Dhoni (commonly known as **MS Dhoni**) is one of India’s most prominent cricketers, best known as a wicketkeeper-batter and as a highly successful captain in limited-overs formats. He is frequently associated with a calm leadership style, late-innings finishing, and prominent success in major ICC tournaments.

## Early Life and Domestic Cricket

- Born in Ranchi (then in Bihar, now Jharkhand), Dhoni rose through India’s domestic system before becoming a regular in the national team.
- He played domestic cricket for Bihar/Jharkhand and later became a fixture in the Indian Premier League (IPL) era.

## International Career Highlights

- **Role:** Wicketkeeper-batter; often batted in the middle order and was used as a finisher in ODIs and T20Is.
- **Captaincy:** Known for tactical flexibility, use of bowlers in short spells, and a temperament suited to high-pressure chases.
- **Tournament wins as captain:**
  - ICC T20 World Cup (2007)
  - ICC Cricket World Cup (2011)
  - ICC Champions Trophy (2013)

## Indian Premier League (IPL)

- Long associated with the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) franchise.
- Recognized for leadership continuity in a league format and for closing out matches as a batter.

## Playing Style and Reputation

- **Batting:** Powerful hitter with a focus on run-chases; renowned for finishing innings.
- **Wicketkeeping:** Noted for quick stumpings and efficient glovework.
- **Leadership image:** Widely described as composed under pressure, with an emphasis on process and situational decision-making.

## Legacy

Dhoni’s career is often cited in discussions of modern limited-overs cricket leadership, particularly for captaining India through a period of major ICC successes and for popularizing the role of a wicketkeeper-batter as a central strategic figure.
