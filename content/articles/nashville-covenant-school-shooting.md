## Summary

Shooter killed 3 children and 3 adults at The Covenant School in Nashville, Tennessee. The shooter had legally purchased 7 firearms and conducted detailed surveillance of the school.

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- **Date/Place:** March 27, 2023 — The Covenant School, a private Christian school in Nashville, Tennessee.
- **Fatalities:** 6 people were killed (3 children and 3 adults); the attacker was killed by police at the scene.
- **Weapons:** Reporting and law-enforcement statements described multiple firearms brought to the school; authorities stated the shooter had legally purchased firearms.
- **Targeting/Planning:** Authorities said the attacker conducted planning and surveillance prior to the attack.

## Context

The shooting drew national attention because it occurred at an elementary school and because of the rapid police response that ended the attack. It also became part of broader U.S. debates on school safety, firearm access, and the prevention of targeted violence.

## Immediate Response

- **Emergency response:** Nashville police responded within minutes, entered the building, and engaged the shooter.
- **Public safety measures:** Schools and community organizations in the area increased security and provided counseling and crisis support.
- **Communications:** City officials and law enforcement provided briefings as facts were confirmed.

## Investigation (Overview)

Authorities investigated:

- **Timeline reconstruction:** Entry method, movement inside the school, and sequence of events.
- **Weapon acquisition:** How the firearms were obtained and any relevant purchase history.
- **Digital/physical evidence:** Documents, devices, and any written material related to planning.
- **Motive indicators:** Statements and evidence to understand why the school was targeted.

## Aftermath and Public Debate

- **Policy attention:** The event intensified calls for measures such as enhanced school security, threat-assessment programs, and firearm-related policy proposals.
- **Community impact:** Families, staff, and local faith communities faced prolonged trauma and relied on memorials and long-term support resources.
