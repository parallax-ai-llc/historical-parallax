## Summary

Nhat Chi Mai was a Vietnamese Buddhist peace activist who self-immolated in 1967 to protest the Vietnam War, following the example of Thich Quang Duc. She was a student of Thich Nhat Hanh and a member of the School of Youth for Social Service. She left letters to both the US and South Vietnamese governments calling for peace. Her sacrifice drew international attention and deepened anti-war sentiment.
