## Background

Ibrahim Traoré is a Burkinabè military officer who rose to national prominence during a period of severe insecurity in Burkina Faso, where jihadist insurgency and internal displacement placed sustained pressure on state institutions.

## 2022 coups and leadership change

Burkina Faso experienced multiple rapid political transitions in 2022. Traoré became the figurehead of a leadership change in September 2022, arguing that the existing authorities were failing to address the security situation.

Key points often highlighted in coverage of this period include:

- the centrality of counterinsurgency performance to legitimacy
- rivalry and distrust inside the armed forces
- popular demonstrations and political polarization in the capital

## Governance and security focus

As head of state, Traoré has emphasized military mobilization and internal security. Policies and messaging have frequently centered on expanding recruitment, reorganizing forces, and projecting a narrative of national sovereignty.
