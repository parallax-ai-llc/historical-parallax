## Summary

Philip Kapleau was an American Zen teacher whose book The Three Pillars of Zen became one of the most influential books on Zen in the English language. He studied under Harada Roshi and Yasutani Roshi in Japan for 13 years. He founded the Rochester Zen Center in New York, one of the oldest Zen centers in the West. He was a court reporter at the Nuremberg Trials before turning to Zen practice.
