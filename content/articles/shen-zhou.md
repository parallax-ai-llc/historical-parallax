## Summary

Shen Zhou was the founder of the Wu School of painting and one of the Four Masters of the Ming dynasty. His landscapes and flower paintings combine scholarly refinement with spontaneous brushwork.

## Background and Context

Shen Zhou came from a prosperous Suzhou family and moved within the scholarly-elite culture that valued poetry, calligraphy, and painting as linked arts. He is closely associated with the literati tradition of the Ming period, in which painting functioned as personal expression and cultivated taste rather than as a purely commercial craft.

## Artistic Style

- **Landscape painting:** Often emphasizes broad, readable brush structures and expressive ink modulation, drawing on earlier Yuan dynasty literati models.
- **Bird-and-flower subjects:** Combines careful observation with abbreviated, suggestive strokes.
- **Integration of text and image:** Poems and inscriptions on paintings were a central part of meaning and authorship within the literati ideal.

## Wu School and Legacy

Shen Zhou is regarded as a foundational figure of the **Wu School** (centered around Suzhou), which became influential in later Ming painting. His example helped establish a durable model for scholar-amateur painting that shaped later masters, including artists grouped as the "Four Masters" of the Ming.

## Notable Works (Examples)

Art-historical sources commonly cite Shen Zhou's long handscroll landscapes, album leaves, and bird-and-flower paintings as representative of his range. Titles often referenced in museum and catalogue contexts include works translated as **"Lofty Mount Lu"** and other large-scale landscapes, along with intimate studies that foreground brush texture and inscription.

## Key Themes

- **Scholarship and friendship:** Many paintings were made within networks of friends and patrons in Suzhou’s literati circles.
- **Reverence for earlier masters:** He frequently engaged in conscious dialogue with Yuan and earlier models while adapting them to Ming tastes.
- **Personal voice:** Variation in brush tempo and ink tone is a hallmark of the expressive literati approach associated with his name.
