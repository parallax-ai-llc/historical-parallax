## Summary

Argentine military dictator who launched the disastrous 1982 invasion of the Falkland Islands to distract from domestic problems. The war's failure led to the fall of Argentina's military junta. Later convicted of human rights crimes.

## Background

Leopoldo Galtieri was an Argentine army general and a leading figure in the military government that ruled Argentina during the final years of the National Reorganization Process.

## The Falklands/Malvinas War

- **Political context:** The decision to seize the islands is widely interpreted as an attempt to shore up domestic support amid economic crisis and political unrest.
- **Consequences:** Argentina’s defeat accelerated the junta’s loss of legitimacy and contributed to the country’s return to democratic elections.

## Accountability and later years

Galtieri’s post-presidency years were marked by legal proceedings related to the dictatorship era and to the conduct of the war. His role is typically discussed alongside broader evaluations of state terrorism, disappearances, and civil-military relations in late-20th-century Argentina.
