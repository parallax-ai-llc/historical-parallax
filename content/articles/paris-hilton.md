## Summary

Paris Whitney Hilton (born February 17, 1981) is an American media personality, businesswoman, DJ, and socialite who became one of the most recognizable figures of the early 2000s. Heir to the Hilton Hotels fortune, she transformed celebrity culture by pioneering the concept of "famous for being famous," predating and arguably inventing the modern influencer template. Her cultural footprint spans reality television, music, fashion, and entrepreneurship across more than two decades.

Beyond the tabloid persona, Hilton built a diversified business empire estimated at over $1 billion in lifetime product sales, encompassing more than 50 product lines and 45 branded stores worldwide. In her later career she became an advocate for survivors of institutional child abuse, leveraging her platform to successfully lobby for legislative reform in the United States.

## Early Life & Background

Paris Hilton was born in New York City to Richard Hilton, a hotel developer, and Kathy Richards Hilton. She grew up between New York, the Hamptons, and Beverly Hills, attending a series of private schools including the Dwight School in New York. Her great-grandfather Conrad Hilton founded the Hilton Hotels chain, and she was raised in a world of wealth and high society. During her teenage years she began appearing in fashion circles and signed with Donald Trump's modeling agency T Management at age 19.

## Rise to Prominence

Hilton's celebrity accelerated after she became a fixture of New York nightlife in the late 1990s. In 2003 she starred alongside Nicole Richie in Fox's reality series *The Simple Life*, which ran for five seasons until 2007. The show, which placed the two socialites in working-class situations, became a cultural touchstone and made Hilton a household name. That same year, a leaked private video generated enormous tabloid coverage and complicated her public image for years.

She released a self-titled debut album in 2006, which reached number six on the Billboard 200. Her DJ career gained traction in the late 2000s and into the 2010s, with residencies in Ibiza earning fees reported at up to $1 million per performance.

## Key Achievements

- Launched her debut fragrance *Paris Hilton* in 2004; the fragrance line grew to over $2.5 billion in retail sales by the 2020s.
- Authored *Confessions of an Heiress* (2004), a New York Times bestseller.
- Hosted and produced *Paris in Love* (2021), a Peacock reality series documenting her engagement and wedding to Carter Reum.
- Testified before the Utah State Legislature in 2021 regarding abuse at Provo Canyon School, contributing to the passage of the Utah HB 323 legislation reforming youth residential treatment facilities.
- Married venture capitalist Carter Reum in November 2021; the couple welcomed son Phoenix Barron Hilton Reum via surrogate in January 2023 and daughter London Hilton Reum in November 2023.

## Controversies & Criticism

Hilton served 23 days of a 45-day jail sentence in 2007 for violating probation related to a reckless driving charge, an event that received saturation media coverage. Critics have long questioned the substance behind her celebrity, and her early image was frequently criticized as shallow or hypersexualized. She has since addressed these critiques directly, arguing in interviews and her 2020 YouTube documentary *This Is Paris* that the "dumb blonde" persona was a deliberate, calculated performance masking significant trauma from her adolescence at Provo Canyon School.

## Legacy & Influence

Paris Hilton is widely credited with establishing the blueprint for the modern celebrity influencer: a media-savvy personal brand monetized across fragrance, fashion, television, and social media. Kim Kardashian, who worked as Hilton's stylist, has acknowledged Hilton's influence on her own career trajectory. Hilton's early, prolific use of social media and self-branding anticipated practices that would become standard in the influencer economy of the 2010s and 2020s. Her advocacy work on institutional abuse has given her later career a substantive dimension that reframed public perception of her public persona.
