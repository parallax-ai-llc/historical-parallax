## Background

- **PDVSA** (Petróleos de Venezuela, S.A.) is Venezuela's state-owned oil and gas company and historically the country's main source of export revenue.
- Because PDVSA has operated at the center of Venezuela's economy, allegations of corruption around PDVSA have had outsized political, financial, and humanitarian consequences.

## Commonly Reported Corruption Schemes (High-Level)

The term "PDVSA corruption" is often used as an umbrella for multiple alleged or prosecuted schemes, including:

- **Embezzlement and kickbacks** tied to procurement and service contracts.
- **Overbilling / phantom contracts** for goods and services that were inflated in price or not delivered.
- **Oil trading and payment diversion** (e.g., proceeds routed through intermediaries or offshore structures).
- **Money laundering networks** involving foreign banks and shell companies.

## Investigations and Legal Actions (Overview)

- Multiple investigations have been reported across jurisdictions, reflecting that alleged proceeds moved through international financial systems.
- Court cases have involved **former officials, contractors, and intermediaries**, with outcomes ranging from investigations to convictions.
- Public reporting has described cases connected to **foreign exchange, import programs, and oil-for-payment arrangements**, though details vary by source and case.

## Impact (What It Changes)

- **Fiscal damage:** Loss of state revenue can reduce funding capacity for public services.
- **Operational damage:** Corruption allegations can coincide with weakened governance, reduced maintenance, and impaired production capacity.
- **Sanctions and compliance risk:** International enforcement and sanctions regimes can increase transaction costs and limit financing.
- **Public trust:** Persistent allegations contribute to political instability and institutional distrust.
