## Summary

Bosnian Serb general who was the first person convicted of genocide by the ICTY for his role as Drina Corps commander during the Srebrenica massacre. He directed troops in the mass execution operation.

## Role in the Bosnian War

Krstić served in the Army of Republika Srpska (VRS) during the Bosnian War, a conflict marked by widespread atrocities and forced displacement. In 1995, the fall of the UN-designated “safe area” of Srebrenica was followed by the systematic separation of men and boys from women and children and the subsequent mass killings of thousands of Bosniak victims.

## ICTY Proceedings

The International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) prosecuted senior political and military figures connected to crimes committed during the breakup of Yugoslavia. Krstić’s case became a landmark because it was the first ICTY judgment to enter a genocide conviction at trial level, reflecting findings about intent and the organized nature of the killing operation.

## Appeal Outcome

On appeal, the tribunal modified the legal characterization of his responsibility, reducing the genocide conviction to aiding and abetting genocide while maintaining a lengthy prison sentence. The decision is frequently discussed in international criminal law as an example of how courts distinguish between direct perpetration, command responsibility, and forms of assistance that contribute to mass atrocity crimes.

## Significance

Krstić’s conviction and appeal decisions are often cited in scholarship and teaching on genocide jurisprudence, evidentiary standards for intent, and the development of accountability mechanisms after mass violence.

## After Conviction

Individuals sentenced by the ICTY have served time in a variety of states under enforcement agreements, and proceedings often generate additional litigation concerning detention conditions and prison incidents. Public information about Krstić after sentencing has largely centered on his incarceration and tribunal-related filings rather than post-war political activity.

## Related Cases

The Srebrenica killings were addressed across multiple ICTY cases involving different levels of command and participation. Together, these judgments contributed to an evidentiary record about the planning, coordination, and execution of the operation.
