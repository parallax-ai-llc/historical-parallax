## Summary

Jacques Kallis is the greatest all-rounder in cricket history with over 13,000 Test runs and 292 Test wickets.

## Playing Style

Kallis is remembered as a technically solid right-handed batter who could anchor an innings and accumulate runs over long periods, combined with reliable pace bowling that added balance to South Africa’s teams.

His reputation as an all-rounder rests on sustained performance across many years rather than short peaks: the combination of a very high Test run total with a large wicket tally is rare in modern cricket.

## International Career Highlights

He played in multiple formats (Tests, ODIs, and T20s) during a period when international cricket was expanding and professionalizing further. Within South African cricket history, he is often listed among the country’s greatest players.

Later in his career, he continued in coaching and franchise leagues, representing the broader trend of elite cricketers moving into mentorship roles and global competitions after retirement.

## Legacy

Kallis is frequently used as a benchmark when comparing all-rounders across eras, especially in discussions about consistency, workload, and the difficulty of maintaining elite batting and bowling simultaneously.
