## Summary

Arshad Pappu and Baba Ladla were among the chief figures of the Lyari gang wars that paralyzed parts of Karachi for years. Baba Ladla was killed in a police encounter in 2017.

## Background: Lyari and Karachi’s Criminal-Political Nexus

Lyari is a historic neighborhood of Karachi that has experienced long periods of poverty, political contestation, and violence. The “Lyari gang war” label is commonly used for cycles of conflict among armed groups involved in organized crime.

## Patterns of Violence (High Level)

- Rival factions fought over territorial control and illicit revenue streams.
- Residents were often caught between gangs and security operations, with routine disruption to daily life.
- The conflict’s intensity varied over time, with escalations driven by assassinations, leadership changes, and shifting political protection.

## State Response

Authorities periodically launched policing and paramilitary operations aimed at restoring control. Such operations are frequently debated in Pakistan due to concerns about extrajudicial killings, due process, and the long-term effectiveness of force-centric strategies.

## Why the Topic Matters

The Lyari episodes are often cited in discussions of urban governance: how weak service delivery, unemployment, and politicized law enforcement can create space for armed groups to become quasi-authorities.

## Key Names Mentioned

This article title is used as a collective label; specific figures (such as Arshad Pappu and Baba Ladla) are usually discussed in relation to particular factions and phases of the conflict.

Additional reporting also emphasizes the impact on ordinary residents, including school closures, business shutdowns, and displacement during peak violence.
