## Summary

Nixon administration officials broke into Democratic National Committee headquarters and attempted to cover it up. Led to President Nixon's resignation, the only US president to resign.

## What happened (high level)

The scandal began with a break-in at the Democratic National Committee offices in the Watergate complex, carried out by individuals connected to the Nixon re-election campaign. The criminal act itself was compounded by subsequent efforts to obstruct investigation and conceal the connection to political leadership.

A key feature of Watergate is that it combined operational wrongdoing (the burglary and intelligence-gathering) with institutional corruption (misuse of agencies, pressure on investigators, and deception of the public).

## Investigations and evidence

Congressional investigations and journalism played major roles in uncovering the broader pattern of misconduct. The existence of White House tape recordings became pivotal because they provided contemporaneous evidence about what senior officials knew and when they knew it.

The confrontation over access to the tapes helped define modern debates about executive privilege and accountability. Legal and political processes converged, culminating in impeachment proceedings and, ultimately, resignation.

## Consequences and reforms

Watergate had lasting effects on American political culture, increasing skepticism toward government claims and reshaping expectations for transparency. It also contributed to reforms intended to limit abuse of power, including changes in campaign finance oversight and ethics rules.

In the long run, the term “-gate” became a linguistic template for political scandals, reflecting how deeply Watergate imprinted itself on public discourse.

## Enduring lessons (high level)

Watergate is often taught as a case where the decisive issue was not only the initial illegal act but the subsequent attempt to obstruct oversight. The episode also demonstrates how independent institutions—courts, legislatures, and a free press—can interact to surface evidence and enforce accountability.
