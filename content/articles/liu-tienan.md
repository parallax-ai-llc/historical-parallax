## Summary

Former deputy head of China's National Development and Reform Commission convicted of accepting 35 million yuan in bribes. He was exposed by an investigative journalist.

## Institutional Context (High Level)

- China’s National Development and Reform Commission (NDRC) is commonly described as a powerful macroeconomic and industrial-policy agency, influencing approvals and regulation in key sectors.
- Senior roles within the NDRC can involve oversight of investment projects, pricing, and strategic planning, which can create significant opportunities for rent-seeking if governance fails.

## Allegations and Investigation

- Liu Tienan was accused of leveraging his position to accept bribes, with the case becoming notable due to public allegations and subsequent official action.
- The exposure by an investigative journalist is frequently highlighted as part of the narrative, emphasizing the role of reporting in triggering scrutiny.

## Prosecution and Sentencing

- The legal outcome was a conviction for bribery and a life sentence.
- Cases of this type are often presented as demonstrations of enforcement against high-ranking officials, particularly when tied to large monetary amounts and influential agencies.

## Public Attention and Significance

- The case attracted attention because it involved a senior economic policymaker rather than a local-level official.
- It is sometimes referenced in discussions about transparency, conflicts of interest, and the difficulty of monitoring decision-making in concentrated regulatory institutions.

## Key Takeaways

- High-discretion policy roles can amplify corruption risk when oversight mechanisms are weak.
- The Liu Tienan case is remembered for both its institutional sensitivity (NDRC) and the public reporting that preceded formal discipline and prosecution.
