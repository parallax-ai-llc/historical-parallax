## Summary

Colombian guerrilla leader known as 'Tirofijo' (Sureshot) who co-founded and led FARC for 44 years—one of the longest-running guerrilla commands in history. He died of a heart attack. FARC had 18,000 fighters at its peak.

## Additional Context

- FARC (Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia) was a Marxist-inspired guerrilla organization involved in Colombia's internal armed conflict for decades.
- The conflict featured multiple actors, including guerrillas, state forces, and right-wing paramilitary groups, with significant civilian impact.
