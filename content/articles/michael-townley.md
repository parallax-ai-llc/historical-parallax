## Summary

American-born Chilean secret police agent who carried out the assassination of Orlando Letelier in Washington DC in 1976 using a car bomb. He became a key US government witness against DINA. Living under witness protection.

## Background

Michael Townley is associated with Chile's military dictatorship era and the operations of DINA, the regime's secret police. He has been linked in public accounts to covert activities carried out against political opponents inside and outside Chile.

## Letelier Assassination

In September 1976, former Chilean diplomat Orlando Letelier was killed in Washington, D.C., by a car bomb. The attack became one of the most prominent acts of state-linked political violence on U.S. soil during the Cold War and had significant diplomatic consequences.

## Cooperation With U.S. Authorities

After arrest and extradition-related proceedings, Townley cooperated with U.S. prosecutors and provided testimony about DINA operations and the Letelier case. He entered a witness protection program after serving a sentence, and his later whereabouts have been kept private.

## Historical Context

The case is frequently discussed in the context of Operation Condor, a network of South American security services that targeted dissidents across borders. It illustrates how intelligence services and political repression could extend internationally during the period.

## Key Points

- The Letelier assassination had major diplomatic and legal repercussions.
- Townley's testimony was important in efforts to attribute responsibility within DINA and related networks.
- The episode remains a reference point in discussions of accountability for political violence.
