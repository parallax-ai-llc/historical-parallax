## Summary

British trader known as the Flash Crash Trader who contributed to the 2010 market crash by spoofing futures orders from his parents house. He made $40 million from his scheme.

## Background

Sarao became widely known in connection with market manipulation allegations tied to high-speed and electronic trading in equity index futures. Public reporting has emphasized that his activity was conducted remotely and relied on placing and canceling large orders intended to influence the visible supply-and-demand picture in the order book.

## Spoofing (High-Level)

"Spoofing" in this context refers to entering orders with the intent to cancel them before execution, with the goal of misleading other market participants about true buying or selling interest. The tactic can contribute to short-term price movements, especially in markets where participants react quickly to changes in displayed liquidity.

## 2010 Flash Crash Context

The May 6, 2010 "Flash Crash" was a brief, sharp decline in U.S. equities and related instruments, followed by a rapid recovery. Investigations and later court proceedings discussed the interaction of multiple factors, including market structure, algorithmic trading behavior, and liquidity conditions. Sarao's case is often cited as one prominent enforcement action connected to that day.

## Legal Proceedings and Outcome

Sarao was arrested and faced U.S. and U.K. legal processes related to the allegations. Coverage of the case has highlighted both the cross-border enforcement dimension and the technical nature of proving intent in electronic trading strategies. He ultimately received a sentence that did not involve a long prison term, reflecting case-specific circumstances described in public sources.

## Broader Impact

The case is frequently referenced in discussions about:

- the limits of surveillance in fragmented and high-speed markets
- regulatory definitions of manipulative intent
- the role of order-cancellation behavior in destabilizing conditions
- policy responses after the Flash Crash
