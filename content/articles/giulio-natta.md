## Summary

Giulio Natta was an Italian chemist who shared the 1963 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with Karl Ziegler for their discoveries in polymer chemistry. He developed stereoregular polymers (isotactic polypropylene), enabling precise control of polymer structure. His work was fundamental to the modern plastics and synthetic materials industry.
