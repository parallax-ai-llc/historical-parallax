## Summary

Melvin Schwartz shared the 1988 Nobel Prize in Physics for the discovery of the muon neutrino, proving that neutrinos come in different flavors. His idea to use neutrino beams from accelerators enabled a new method of studying fundamental particles. He later left physics to found Digital Pathways, a computer security company.
