## Summary

Canadian who ran over two soldiers with his car in an ISIS-inspired attack.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2014 | Vehicle-ramming attack in Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu, Quebec; two Canadian Armed Forces members struck. |

## Background

- Couture-Rouleau was a Canadian citizen from Quebec.
- Reporting around the incident described a period of radicalization and interest in extremist material.
- Canadian authorities had been aware of him prior to the attack; his ability to travel abroad was reportedly restricted.

## 2014 Attack (Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu)

- Date: 2014-10-20.
- Method: a vehicle was driven into two uniformed service members.
- Outcome: one service member, Warrant Officer Patrice Vincent, was killed; another was injured.
- Response: police pursued the vehicle; Couture-Rouleau was shot during the confrontation and died at the scene.

## Aftermath and Significance

- The event occurred during heightened concern in Canada about ISIS-inspired violence and "lone-actor" attacks.
- Canadian officials publicly characterized the incident as terrorism motivated by extremist ideology.
- The attack was frequently discussed alongside other 2014 incidents in Canada involving extremist-inspired violence.

## Key Points (High-Level)

- Often cited as an early example in Canada of ISIS-inspired vehicle-ramming attacks.
- Demonstrated the challenge of assessing and managing individuals viewed as potential extremist threats.
- Prompted renewed attention to domestic counterterrorism, community reporting, and threat assessment practices.
