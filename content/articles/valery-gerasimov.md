## Background

Valery Vasilyevich Gerasimov is a senior Russian military officer who rose through the Soviet and Russian armed forces and became one of the most visible figures in Russia's contemporary military leadership.

## Roles and Notability

- **Chief of the General Staff of the Armed Forces of the Russian Federation** (in office since 2012).
- Frequently described in English-language commentary in connection with debates about Russian views on modern warfare (often labeled the "Gerasimov doctrine").

## War in Ukraine (context)

Gerasimov's name is closely linked to Russia's command structure during the war in Ukraine because the Chief of the General Staff is central to operational planning and the coordination of major military campaigns. Public reporting has also highlighted periodic shifts in who is formally designated as overall commander for operations, reflecting Russia's evolving approach to managing a large, prolonged conflict.

## "Gerasimov doctrine" (summary of the debate)

The term "Gerasimov doctrine" is widely used in some Western analysis to describe a view that modern conflict blends military force with non-military tools (information operations, political pressure, cyber activity, and economic levers). Many researchers note that the label is contested and can oversimplify what was originally a discussion of how warfare was changing rather than a declared, fixed doctrine.
