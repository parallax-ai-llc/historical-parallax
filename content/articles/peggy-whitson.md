## Summary

Peggy Whitson holds the record for the most time in space by an American astronaut, with 665 days over three missions. She was the first female commander of the International Space Station and the oldest woman to fly in space. Her extensive research aboard the ISS advanced understanding of the effects of microgravity on the human body.
