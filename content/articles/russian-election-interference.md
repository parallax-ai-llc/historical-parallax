## Summary

Russian governments systematic campaign to interfere in the 2016 US presidential election through hacking, social media manipulation, and disinformation. Mueller investigation documented extensive contacts.

## Methods Commonly Described

- Cyber operations, including theft and publication of emails or documents, were paired with influence operations intended to shape public narratives.
- Social media manipulation and coordinated messaging were frequently described as part of a broader strategy to amplify polarization and distrust.

## 2016 U.S. Election (High-Level)

- U.S. government assessments described Russian state-linked actors as conducting hacks and influence operations during the 2016 presidential campaign.
- The Mueller investigation examined links and contacts between Russian actors and individuals connected to the Trump campaign, as well as the underlying cyber and influence activities.

## Responses and Aftermath

- The U.S. and other governments imposed sanctions and took other measures in response to alleged Russian interference activities.
- Public debate over election security led to expanded scrutiny of political advertising, platform moderation policies, and cyber defense practices.

## Broader Context

- Allegations or findings of Russian interference have also been discussed in connection with other countries’ elections and referendums.
- The topic is often framed as part of modern “information warfare,” combining intelligence operations with mass communication channels.

## Common Entities Discussed

- Accounts of the 2016 influence campaign often reference the Internet Research Agency (IRA) and Russian military intelligence (GRU) as key alleged actors.
