Denis Law won the 1964 Ballon d'Or — the only Scottish winner — and formed United's 'Holy Trinity.'

## Early Life and Development

Law grew up in Aberdeen and began his professional career in Scotland before moving to English football. He became known for sharp movement, quick finishing, and an ability to score a variety of goal types despite not being a typical target forward.

## Manchester United and “Holy Trinity”

At Manchester United, Law played alongside George Best and Bobby Charlton in a celebrated attacking trio sometimes referred to as the club’s “Holy Trinity.” In this period he was among the most prominent forwards in Britain and a key figure in United’s domestic and European ambitions.

## Major Honors (High Level)

- **Ballon d’Or (1964):** Awarded as Europe’s top footballer, making him historically notable as the only Scottish recipient.
- **Domestic titles:** Part of title-winning squads during the 1960s.
- **European success:** Associated with Manchester United’s rise toward European Cup glory in the late 1960s.

## International Career

With Scotland, Law was a long-term regular and became one of the nation’s leading all-time goal scorers. His international career is often discussed in relation to Scotland’s competitive performances in the British Home Championship era and the broader challenges of qualifying for major tournaments.

## Playing Style and Legacy

Law is remembered for instinctive finishing, clever positioning, and high-impact performances in big matches. He remains a central figure in Manchester United and Scottish football history, frequently cited in discussions of the greatest British forwards of his generation.
