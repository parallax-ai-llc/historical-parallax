## Summary

Han Yongun (Manhae) was a Korean Buddhist monk, poet, and independence activist. His poetry collection The Silence of Love (Nim-ui Chimmuk) is a masterpiece of modern Korean poetry, using the metaphor of a departed lover to express longing for Korean independence. He was one of the 33 signers of the Korean Declaration of Independence in 1919.
