## Background

Salva Kiir Mayardit is a South Sudanese political and military leader who rose through the Sudan People's Liberation Movement/Army (SPLM/A) during the long conflict that led to South Sudan's secession from Sudan.

## Presidency and governance

- **Independence era:** Kiir became the first president of South Sudan when the country gained independence in 2011.
- **Security and state-building:** His presidency has been defined by efforts to build national institutions amid weak infrastructure and deep political fragmentation.
- **Allegations of corruption and abuses:** Domestic and international observers have repeatedly accused the government and affiliated forces of corruption and serious human-rights violations.

## Civil war and peace process

The political split within the SPLM leadership escalated into armed conflict in 2013, with violence often framed along ethnic and factional lines. Multiple ceasefires and negotiations followed, culminating in a 2018 peace agreement intended to form a unity government and integrate armed groups.

## Political significance

Kiir is frequently discussed as a central figure in debates over South Sudan's post-independence trajectory: whether the state can transition from liberation-era politics toward accountable civilian governance, credible elections, and durable reconciliation.
