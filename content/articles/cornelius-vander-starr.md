## Summary

Cornelius Vander Starr founded what would become American International Group (AIG), one of the world's largest insurance companies. He started his insurance business in Shanghai in 1919 and expanded throughout Asia before building a global empire. He was a pioneer in selling insurance to local populations in foreign markets. His successor, Hank Greenberg, would later build AIG into a financial giant.
