## Summary

Nevill Mott shared the 1977 Nobel Prize for work on electronic structure of magnetic and disordered systems.

## Additional Context

- Mott was a British physicist associated with the University of Cambridge and the Cavendish Laboratory.
- His work is commonly linked to the physics of solids, especially how electrons behave in materials with disorder and interactions.

## Key Ideas (High Level)

- **Metal–insulator transition:** conditions under which a material behaves like a conductor versus an insulator.
- **Disordered systems:** how impurities and structural disorder can change electronic transport.
- **Magnetism in materials:** how electronic structure relates to magnetic behavior.

## Legacy

- Terms like *Mott transition* and *Mott insulator* are widely used in condensed-matter physics.
- The Nobel Prize recognized foundational contributions that shaped modern understanding of electronic properties of materials.
