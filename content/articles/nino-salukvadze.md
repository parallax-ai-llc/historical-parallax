## Summary

Nino Salukvadze competed at a record nine consecutive Olympic Games from 1988 to 2024. She won gold in shooting at the 1988 Seoul Olympics.

## Sport and Events

Salukvadze is best known for **sport shooting** pistol events. Across multiple Olympic cycles she competed under different national designations (late Soviet era, Unified Team period, and later Georgia), reflecting the political transitions of the region.

## Olympic Longevity

Her nine consecutive Olympic appearances (1988–2024) are notable not only for the count, but for the time span across eras of equipment changes, training methods, and international competition depth.

Commonly cited milestones include:

- **1988 Seoul**: Olympic gold in shooting.
- **2024 Paris**: Ninth Olympics, frequently described as a record for women’s Olympic participation.

## Historical Significance

Salukvadze’s career is often used as a case study in elite athletic longevity, especially in precision sports where:

- consistent technical fundamentals matter over decades,
- experience and mental control can offset age-related physical decline,
- sustained federation and coaching support are critical.

## Legacy

In Georgia, she is recognized as a major figure in Olympic sport and a symbol of continuity through national transitions. Internationally, she is remembered for setting participation records and for remaining competitive across an unusually long period.
