## Summary

Indian founder of BitConnect cryptocurrency Ponzi scheme that stole $2.4 billion from investors worldwide. He was indicted by US authorities but has disappeared.

## Additional Context

### How BitConnect was marketed (high level)

- Promoted a "lending" model promising unusually high returns, often framed as passive income.
- Claimed returns were generated by an automated trading strategy (frequently described as a "trading bot"), which became a focal point of skepticism.
- Used multi-level marketing–style promotion via influencers and regional promoters in multiple countries.

### Collapse and regulatory attention

- As confidence fell, the token price dropped sharply, triggering a feedback loop of withdrawals, liquidity problems, and further price declines.
- Regulators in some U.S. states issued cease-and-desist orders describing the program as an unregistered securities offering and/or a Ponzi scheme (reported).
- Civil litigation and enforcement actions sought restitution, asset freezes, and accountability for promoters and organizers.

### Broader significance

- BitConnect became a widely cited example of how crypto speculation, aggressive marketing, and opaque investment claims can combine into large-scale consumer harm.
- The case is frequently referenced in discussions about investor protection, disclosure standards, and cross-border enforcement challenges in crypto markets.
