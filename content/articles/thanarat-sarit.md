## Summary

Thai military dictator who abolished the constitution and ruled by decree. After his death, officials discovered he had amassed an enormous fortune through corruption.

## Rise to Power

- Sarit Thanarat was a senior Thai military figure who became central to politics during the Cold War period.
- In 1957, he took power via a coup, positioning his rule as a corrective to political instability and corruption.

## Rule and Governance Style

- Sarit is widely described as having governed in an authoritarian manner, including suspension of constitutional politics and expanded executive authority.
- His government emphasized order, hierarchy, and state control, using security measures against perceived subversion.
- The period is often discussed in relation to Thailand's broader pattern of military intervention in civilian politics.

## Cold War Alignment and Development

- Sarit's era coincided with heightened anti-communist policy across Southeast Asia.
- Thailand's alignment with Western powers, including the United States, is often highlighted in accounts of military aid, infrastructure programs, and regional strategy.
- Economic modernization and state-led development initiatives are frequently noted as part of the period's policy narrative.

## Corruption and Posthumous Scandal

- After Sarit's death in 1963, investigations reportedly uncovered large personal wealth and patronage networks linked to his administration.
- The revelations became a lasting reference point in Thai political discourse about elite corruption and accountability.
