## Summary

Claes Oldenburg was a Swedish-American sculptor known for his monumental public sculptures of everyday objects like Clothespin, Spoonbridge and Cherry, and Typewriter Eraser. A leading figure of Pop Art, his soft sculptures of hamburgers and toilets challenged the boundaries between art and life. His playful reimagining of the mundane became iconic public art.
