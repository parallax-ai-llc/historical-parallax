## Background

- Adams is a New Zealand track and field athlete best known for shot put.
- She became one of the most decorated shot putters of her era across Olympic, World, and Commonwealth competitions.

## Career highlights (high-level)

- Won Olympic gold medals in the women’s shot put at the 2008 Beijing and 2012 London Games.
- Won multiple World Championship titles and was a consistent medal contender for more than a decade.
- Returned to Olympic competition after injuries and major life events, adding a bronze medal at the 2020 Tokyo Games (held in 2021).

## Style and reputation

- Known for technical consistency and the ability to produce elite throws in championship finals.
- Frequently ranked among the world’s top shot put athletes during the peak years of her career.

## Legacy

- In New Zealand sport, Adams is often cited as a flagship athlete for track and field in the modern era.
- Her sustained success contributed to the visibility of field events and women’s athletics in Oceania.
- She is also noted for longevity at the top level, competing across multiple Olympic cycles.
