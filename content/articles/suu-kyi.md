## High-Level Overview (Aung San Suu Kyi)

Aung San Suu Kyi became an international symbol of opposition to Myanmar’s military rule, associated with long periods of detention and restrictions on political participation. After partial political liberalization, she led the National League for Democracy (NLD) into government, operating within a system where the military retained constitutionally protected power.

Her global reputation evolved over time: while she was celebrated for pro-democracy advocacy, she later drew heavy criticism for her stance and perceived inaction regarding atrocities committed against the Rohingya minority, as well as for broader human-rights concerns in Myanmar.
