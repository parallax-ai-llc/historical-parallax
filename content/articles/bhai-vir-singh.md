## Summary

Bhai Vir Singh was a Punjabi poet, scholar, and theologian who is considered the greatest modern Sikh literary figure. He played a crucial role in the Singh Sabha reform movement, which sought to revive Sikh identity and practice. His novels, poetry, and theological works profoundly shaped modern Punjabi literature. He was the first Sikh to receive the Sahitya Akademi Award. He is sometimes called the 'Sixth River of Punjab.'
