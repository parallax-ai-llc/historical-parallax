## Summary

Dilgo Khyentse Rinpoche was one of the most important Tibetan Buddhist masters of the 20th century, a teacher of the 14th Dalai Lama and many other prominent lamas. He was a master of the Nyingma tradition and a holder of the Rime (non-sectarian) movement. He spent over 20 years in solitary meditation retreat. He played a crucial role in preserving and transmitting Tibetan Buddhist teachings after the Chinese invasion.
