Ivar Giaever shared the 1973 Nobel Prize for discovering tunneling in superconductors.

## Additional Context

- **Nobel Prize (1973)**: Giaever shared the 1973 Nobel Prize in Physics for work related to tunneling phenomena in superconductors, alongside Leo Esaki and Brian Josephson.
- **Research setting**: He is often associated with industrial research environments (notably IBM) that supported foundational condensed-matter experiments in the mid-20th century.
- **Scientific impact**: Tunneling effects are central to superconductivity research and to devices that rely on quantum-mechanical transport.

## Work (High-Level)

- Experimental demonstrations that helped establish electron tunneling through insulating barriers.
- Contributions that intersect with Josephson-junction physics and superconducting device concepts in the broader literature.
