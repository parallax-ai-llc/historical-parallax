## Summary

Former FARC peace negotiator indicted by the US for drug trafficking. He fled and joined Iván Márquez's dissident FARC before being killed in Venezuela.

## Additional Context

### Role in the Colombian conflict and peace process

- Santrich was a member of the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (FARC), a guerrilla organization involved in decades of conflict with the Colombian state.
- He became publicly prominent as part of FARC’s negotiating team during the peace talks that culminated in the 2016 peace agreement.

### U.S. indictment and extradition controversy

- In 2018, U.S. authorities sought extradition on allegations connected to cocaine trafficking, described as occurring after the peace agreement.
- The case became politically sensitive in Colombia, intersecting with debates over transitional justice, the scope of the peace deal, and how to handle alleged post-agreement crimes.

### Break with the post-peace political project

- After periods of detention and legal proceedings, Santrich left Colombia and was reported to have rejoined armed dissident structures.
- He was linked in reporting to a dissident faction associated with Iván Márquez, often described as part of the “Segunda Marquetalia” grouping.

### Death

- Santrich was reported killed in 2021 in Venezuela, with accounts differing on the circumstances.
- His death was used by observers as a marker of the ongoing volatility surrounding dissident armed groups after the formal peace agreement.

### Significance

- Santrich’s trajectory is often cited in discussions about the fragility of peace processes when combatants face unresolved legal exposure and strong incentives to return to illicit economies.
- The case also highlights how international drug-trafficking enforcement can collide with domestic reconciliation frameworks.
