## Summary

Guinean special forces colonel who overthrew President Alpha Condé in a 2021 coup. His military junta has delayed returning to democratic civilian rule.

## Background

Mamadi Doumbouya is a Guinean military officer associated with the country’s special forces. Public biographies describe him as having received training and served in roles that positioned him within elite units, which later became central to the events of 2021.

## Political context before the 2021 coup

The coup unfolded after a period of political tension and protest in Guinea, including controversy around constitutional changes and presidential term limits. Observers frequently cited a combination of factors:

- Disputes over electoral legitimacy and constitutional interpretation
- Public frustration over governance and economic conditions
- Long-standing civil-military dynamics in Guinean politics

## The September 2021 takeover

In September 2021, Doumbouya and allied forces detained President Alpha Condé and announced a military takeover. The junta presented the move as a corrective response to governance failures, while regional and international bodies generally condemned the seizure of power and demanded a timetable for a return to constitutional rule.

## Transitional governance

Following the coup, the junta established transitional structures and issued promises related to reforms and elections. In practice, transitions after coups often involve contested negotiations over:

- The length of the transition period
- The eligibility of junta members to run in future elections
- The balance between security priorities and civilian political participation

## Domestic and international reactions

Guinea’s transition has been shaped by pressure from regional organizations (including ECOWAS), foreign governments, and international financial institutions. Sanctions, diplomatic engagement, and mediation efforts are commonly used tools to push for elections and to deter coup contagion in the region.

## Key themes during the junta period

Reporting on the post-coup period frequently focuses on several recurring themes:

- Promises of anti-corruption or institutional reform versus concerns about repression
- Management of street protests and political opposition
- The sequencing of elections, constitutional drafting, and security-sector control

## Notes on interpretation

Doumbouya is often described either as a reform-minded leader responding to popular discontent or as a military ruler consolidating power under the language of transition. Which framing dominates depends heavily on evolving events, verified actions, and the transparency of the transition roadmap.
