## Summary

ISIS-affiliated militants planted a bomb on a Russian charter plane departing Sharm el-Sheikh killing all 224 people aboard. It was the deadliest aviation disaster in Russian history.

## Flight and crash context

Metrojet Flight 9268 was a charter flight carrying passengers, many of them Russian tourists, from Sharm el-Sheikh in Egypt to Saint Petersburg in Russia. The crash occurred shortly after departure, and the scale of the loss made it a major international aviation and security incident.

## Investigation

Investigators examined flight data, wreckage patterns, and intelligence reporting to determine the cause. The incident was widely attributed to an onboard explosive device, and the Islamic State's Sinai affiliate claimed responsibility.

## Security and policy response

The attack contributed to intensified scrutiny of airport security procedures and of threats from militant groups operating in Egypt's Sinai Peninsula. Several countries temporarily suspended or restricted flights to Sharm el-Sheikh, affecting tourism and prompting additional security measures.

## Broader significance

The event is frequently discussed in relation to:

- the evolution of militant capabilities and targeting of civilian infrastructure
- how governments assess intelligence and aviation risk
- the economic vulnerability of tourism-dependent regions to security shocks
