## Summary

Noureddine Morceli won the 1996 Olympic 1,500m gold and held world records in the 1,500m, mile, and 2,000m simultaneously.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1991 | Won first World Championship gold |
| 1993 | Set mile world record |
| 1996 | Won Olympic gold at Atlanta |

## Background

Morceli emerged from Algeria’s strong tradition in middle-distance running and became one of the dominant 1,500m athletes of the 1990s. He was known for a combination of front-running strength and a decisive finishing kick, and he frequently raced multiple distances on the European circuit.

## Peak Years and Championships

In global championships, Morceli accumulated medals across multiple editions of the World Athletics Championships, including 1,500m titles. His rivalry with other leading milers of the era helped define the event’s competitive landscape during the early-to-mid 1990s.

The 1996 Atlanta Olympics represented the central highlight of his career, where he won Olympic gold in the 1,500m. The Olympic victory was especially significant given that prior seasons had featured both dominant performances and intense expectations.

## World Records and Range

Morceli is remembered not just for championship success but for record-setting across a range of classic middle-distance events. Public records commonly emphasize that he held world records in the 1,500m, the mile, and the 2,000m during his peak period. This breadth reflects an athlete capable of sustaining high pace over tactical races and time trials alike.

## Style of Racing

Commonly noted strengths include:

- The ability to control races from the front when necessary
- Efficient mechanics that supported high sustained speed
- The tactical patience to position well before launching a final surge

## Legacy

Morceli is frequently cited among the great 1,500m runners of the modern era and is a key figure in Algerian athletics history. His record performances and Olympic title continue to serve as reference points in discussions of the event’s evolution and the 1990s middle-distance "golden" period.
