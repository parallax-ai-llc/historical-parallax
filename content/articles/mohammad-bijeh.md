## Summary

Iranian serial killer known as 'The Tehran Desert Vampire' who kidnapped, sexually assaulted, and murdered at least 21 children in the desert south of Tehran between 2004 and 2005. Publicly executed by hanging.

## Case Context

- The crimes were reported as taking place in areas south of Tehran, with victims targeted over a relatively short time window.
- Public attention to the case intensified as the number of victims and the nature of the offenses became known.

## Investigation and Arrest

- Authorities arrested Bijeh after investigative efforts and survivor testimony contributed to identifying a suspect.
- The case is sometimes cited in discussions of child protection, missing-person reporting, and investigative responsiveness.

## Trial and Execution

- Bijeh was tried, convicted, and sentenced to death.
- His execution was carried out publicly by hanging, an element that drew additional coverage and commentary.

## Broader Impact

- The case contributed to public debate in Iran about violent crime, sentencing, and institutional safeguards for children.
- It remains a well-known criminal case in Iranian media narratives of the 2000s.
