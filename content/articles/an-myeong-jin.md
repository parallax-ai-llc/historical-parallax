## Summary

Former North Korean prison camp guard at Camp 22 who defected and provided testimony about biological and chemical weapon experiments on prisoners.

## Background

Camp 22 (also referred to as Hoeryong concentration camp in some reporting) has been described by defectors and human-rights organizations as a large political prison camp in North Hamgyong Province, North Korea. Public information about such facilities is limited and often relies on defector testimony, satellite imagery analysis, and secondary reporting.

## Testimony and Allegations

After defecting, An Myeong-jin became known primarily for testimony alleging severe abuses in the prison-camp system, including claims that prisoners were subjected to experimentation involving chemical and biological agents. Accounts attributed to him are typically presented as allegations based on his personal experience as a guard, rather than claims independently verified through direct access to the site.

Key elements commonly summarized in secondary sources include:

- Descriptions of harsh detention conditions and punishment practices.
- Allegations of experiments on prisoners and fatalities resulting from exposure.
- Assertions that the camp system operated with strict secrecy and limited outside oversight.

## Impact and Reception

An’s statements have been cited in human-rights discussions about North Korea’s detention system. As with other defector accounts, the evidentiary status is frequently discussed in terms of corroboration: some details may align with broader patterns reported by other witnesses, while specific incidents can be difficult to confirm due to restricted access to the country.
