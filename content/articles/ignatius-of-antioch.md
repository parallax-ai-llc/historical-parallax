## Summary

Ignatius of Antioch was an early Christian writer and Bishop of Antioch, one of the Apostolic Fathers. He wrote influential letters on theology, ecclesiology, and Christian life while being transported to Rome for execution. He was the first to use the term 'Catholic Church' in writing. His martyrdom became a powerful symbol of early Christian faith.
