## Summary

Chinese serial killer who murdered 45 people in Henan and Shandong provinces between 1995 and 2003. He robbed and killed victims in their homes. Executed by lethal injection.

## Overview of crimes

Wang Qiang is described in reports as a serial killer and robber who targeted victims in their homes across multiple provinces in China. The murders were linked to robbery and opportunistic selection of vulnerable targets.

Commonly reported elements include:

- A multi-year crime series spanning the late 1990s and early 2000s.
- Victims attacked in residences, with property taken after the assaults.
- A high number of deaths attributed to the same offender in later investigations.

## Investigation and arrest

The case involved linking multiple crime scenes that occurred across jurisdictional boundaries. Such investigations typically require coordination between local police units, forensic analysis, and review of patterns in method and victim selection.

He was tried and convicted, and was ultimately executed by lethal injection in 2005. The outcome reflects China's use of capital punishment in cases involving multiple murders.
