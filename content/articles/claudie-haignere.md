## Summary

Claudie Haigneré was the first French woman in space and the first European woman to visit the International Space Station. She flew twice: to Mir in 1996 and to the ISS in 2001. A rheumatologist and neuroscientist, she conducted medical experiments in space. She later served as French Minister for Research and European Affairs.
