## Summary

Dutch commodity trading company responsible for the 2006 Côte d'Ivoire toxic waste dumping that affected over 100,000 people. A ship contracted by Trafigura dumped toxic waste at multiple sites around Abidjan. The company paid compensation but denied liability.

## Company Overview

- **Business model:** Trafigura is a major commodities trading firm involved in the buying, selling, transportation, and financing of raw materials such as oil and metals.
- **Industry role:** Commodity traders typically operate globally, managing logistics, storage, shipping, and price risk across complex supply chains.

## 2006 Côte d'Ivoire Waste Dumping (High Level)

- **Core controversy:** The company became widely associated with a toxic waste disposal incident in Côte d'Ivoire involving the offloading and dumping of hazardous material around Abidjan.
- **Human impact:** Public accounts describe large numbers of people reporting symptoms and seeking medical attention following the dumping.
- **Legal and civil outcomes:** The aftermath involved investigations, litigation, and settlements, with the company contesting aspects of responsibility while paying compensation in some proceedings.

## Aftermath and Reputational Impact

- **Compliance focus:** The incident is frequently cited in discussions about environmental compliance, waste handling, and oversight in international shipping and disposal.
- **Broader debate:** It is also used as a case study on how regulatory gaps and cross-border operations can complicate accountability for environmental harms.
