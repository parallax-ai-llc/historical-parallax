## Background

- Iván Márquez is commonly identified as a senior figure in the Revolutionary Armed Forces of Colombia (FARC), rising through the organization during Colombia's decades-long internal armed conflict.
- He became a prominent public face of the group during negotiations, which brought insurgent leadership into sustained international media attention.

## Peace Process and Dissident Trajectory

- The 2016 peace agreement is frequently described as a turning point that demobilized much of the FARC while leaving some dissident factions active.
- Márquez's post-agreement trajectory is often discussed in terms of disagreements over implementation, security guarantees for ex-combatants, and the persistence of illicit economies in former conflict zones.

## Illicit Economies and Violence (High-Level)

- Commentary on FARC dissidents often emphasizes how armed groups can become intertwined with revenue streams such as drug trafficking, illegal mining, and extortion.
- These dynamics are typically associated with continued territorial disputes and risks to civilians, community leaders, and former combatants.
