## Background and Role

Tomomasa Nakagawa was a senior member of Aum Shinrikyo, the Japanese doomsday cult responsible for multiple violent crimes in the 1990s. Public reporting describes him as a technically trained participant in the group’s chemical weapons efforts, including work connected to sarin production.

## Connection to the Tokyo Subway Sarin Attack

The March 1995 Tokyo subway attack involved the coordinated release of sarin on multiple train lines during morning rush hour, killing and injuring commuters and causing widespread panic. Court findings linked Nakagawa to preparations and manufacture associated with the attack rather than to the on-the-ground deployment teams.

## Arrest, Trial, and Sentencing

After Aum Shinrikyo’s crimes came under intensified investigation, Nakagawa was arrested and prosecuted along with other senior members. He was convicted in Japan and ultimately sentenced to death for his role in the organization’s killings and chemical weapons program.

## Execution and Aftermath

Nakagawa was executed in July 2018, part of a set of executions of Aum Shinrikyo leadership following the conclusion of related legal proceedings. The executions marked a final major judicial milestone in Japan’s long-running cases tied to the 1995 attack and other Aum crimes.
