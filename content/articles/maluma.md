## Summary

Maluma, born Juan Luis Londoño Arias on January 28, 1994, in Medellín, Colombia, is one of Latin music's most commercially successful artists of the 2010s and 2020s. Known for his smooth blend of reggaeton, trap, and pop, he has built a global fanbase that extends well beyond Latin America into the United States, Europe, and beyond. His stage name, Maluma, is an acronym derived from the first two letters of the names of his mother, father, and sister.

With a string of chart-topping hits and collaborations with artists ranging from Marc Anthony to Madonna, Maluma has helped push urban Latin music into mainstream global consciousness. He represents a generation of Colombian artists who leveraged digital platforms and cross-genre collaborations to achieve international stardom without following the traditional industry path.

His cultural influence extends beyond music into fashion, film, and activism, making him one of the most recognizable Colombian celebrities in the world. He has consistently ranked among the most-streamed Latin artists on Spotify and holds multiple Billboard Latin chart records.

## Early Life & Background

Juan Luis Londoño Arias was born and raised in Medellín, Colombia, in a middle-class family. He showed an early interest in football and music, and by his early teens had shifted his focus primarily to singing. He began writing and recording music at around age 15, uploading early tracks to YouTube. His upbringing in Medellín — a city with a rich musical culture and a history of producing major Latin stars — shaped both his sound and his ambitions.

## Rise to Prominence

Maluma signed with Sony Music Colombia in 2011 and released his debut studio album *Magia* in 2012. The album gained modest regional attention. His breakthrough came with the 2015 single "Borró Cassette," which became a massive Latin radio hit and demonstrated his ability to craft infectious, radio-ready reggaeton. The follow-up album *Pretty Boy, Dirty Boy* (2015) cemented his status as a rising star. In 2017, his collaboration with Shakira on "Chantaje" became one of the most-viewed Latin music videos on YouTube at the time, surpassing 1 billion views. That same year, *F.A.M.E.* debuted at number one on the Billboard Top Latin Albums chart.

## Key Achievements

- Multiple Billboard Latin Music Award wins, including Latin Rhythm Artist of the Year
- "Hawái" (2020) reached number one on the Billboard Hot Latin Songs chart and became one of the most-streamed Latin songs globally that year
- Collaborated with Madonna on "Medellín" (2019), which was performed at the Billboard Music Awards
- Appeared in the film *Marry Me* (2022) alongside Jennifer Lopez and Owen Wilson
- Consistently ranked among the top-streamed Latin artists on Spotify, with billions of streams across his catalog

## Controversies & Criticism

Maluma has faced criticism for lyrics in some early songs that critics and activists characterized as misogynistic or glorifying violence against women. A 2017 campaign in Colombia called for his concerts to be banned on these grounds. He acknowledged the criticism publicly and stated that he was evolving as an artist and as a person. His later work has generally reflected a shift in lyrical tone.

## Legacy & Influence

Maluma has played a meaningful role in elevating Colombian urban music on the world stage and in broadening the audience for Latin trap and reggaeton. Alongside contemporaries like J Balvin and Bad Bunny, he contributed to the mid-2010s global explosion of Latin urban music. His crossover into Hollywood film and collaborations with pop icons have demonstrated that Latin artists can compete at the highest levels across multiple entertainment industries.
