## Background

- Niels Holck (also known in some reporting as *Kim Peter Davy*) is a Danish national associated with international activism and clandestine logistics.
- He became internationally known in connection with the 1995 Purulia arms drop incident in India, in which weapons were delivered by air to a group linked to insurgency in West Bengal.

## Purulia Arms Drop (1995)

- The incident is commonly described as an unauthorized nighttime airdrop of arms and ammunition near Purulia district, West Bengal, India.
- Reporting attributes operational planning and coordination to Holck, alongside other participants, with the aim of supplying a local group opposed to Indian authorities.
- Indian authorities treated the operation as a serious national-security offense and pursued criminal cases related to arms trafficking and conspiracy.

## Legal Proceedings and Extradition

- Holck was sought by Indian authorities for prosecution connected to the Purulia case.
- Danish courts have been involved in extradition considerations; public coverage highlights debates over fair-trial and detention conditions in the requesting state.
- As reported in open sources, extradition has been contested and has remained a point of diplomatic and legal friction for years after the event.

## Significance

- The case is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - cross-border arms trafficking and covert supply operations,
  - the role of private actors in insurgent logistics,
  - the limits of extradition when human-rights or due-process concerns are raised.
