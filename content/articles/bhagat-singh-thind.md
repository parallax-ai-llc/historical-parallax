## Summary

Bhagat Singh Thind was an Indian-American spiritual teacher and author who was one of the first Sikh Americans. His Supreme Court case United States v. Bhagat Singh Thind (1923) was a landmark in American immigration history. After the court ruled against his citizenship application, he later became a citizen and spent decades lecturing on Sikh philosophy and the spiritual teachings of the Guru Granth Sahib across America.
