## Summary

Criminal gang that stole £53 million from the Securitas depot in Tonbridge, Kent in 2006, the largest cash robbery in British history. They kidnapped the depot manager and his family to gain access.

## Overview of the 2006 Robbery

- The robbery targeted a high-value cash distribution depot in Tonbridge, Kent.
- Public accounts emphasize the use of kidnapping and coercion: the depot manager and family were threatened to compel cooperation.
- The theft total—commonly reported as £53 million—made the case a defining UK organized-crime story of the 2000s.

## Tactics (High-Level)

- The operation is generally described as planned rather than opportunistic, involving surveillance, timing, and control of key personnel.
- Disguises and staged scenarios are frequently referenced in summaries as methods to reduce suspicion and delay police response.

## Investigation and Prosecution

- The rapid arrest of many participants is often attributed to investigative leads from logistics, communications, and the difficulty of moving large volumes of cash unnoticed.
- Sentencing of ringleaders is highlighted in major summaries as an attempt to deter similar large-scale robberies.

## Significance

- The case is routinely cited in discussions of cash-handling security, insider-risk management, and the vulnerabilities created when criminals target employees’ families.
- It also illustrates the practical challenge of recovering stolen cash at scale: even when convictions occur, full recovery is not guaranteed in many major robbery cases.
