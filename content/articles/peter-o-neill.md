## Summary

PNG Prime Minister accused of corruption in connection with fraudulent payments of tens of millions of kina.

## Key Positions

- Member of the National Parliament of Papua New Guinea (Ialibu-Pangia Open) (2002–)
- Prime Minister of Papua New Guinea (2011–2019)

## Background

Peter O'Neill is a Papua New Guinean politician who rose from a business background into national politics, eventually becoming Prime Minister in 2011. He led government through multiple parliamentary terms and remained a central figure in PNG politics after stepping down.

## Political Career (High-Level)

- Entered parliament in the early 2000s and built influence through cabinet and parliamentary leadership roles.
- Became Prime Minister in 2011 during a period of constitutional and parliamentary contestation over government leadership.
- Continued as Prime Minister after the 2012 general election, when his coalition secured governing numbers.
- Resigned as Prime Minister in 2019 amid political pressure and shifting parliamentary alliances.

## Notable Events and Allegations

The O'Neill administration was associated with a number of high-profile political and legal controversies, including:

- Allegations related to government payments and procurement decisions investigated by anti-corruption bodies and police units at various times.
- A prolonged political dispute between branches of government during the early part of his premiership, including court rulings and parliamentary maneuvering.
- Public debate over governance, accountability, and the role of investigative task forces and law enforcement agencies.
