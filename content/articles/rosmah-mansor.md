## Summary

Malaysian former first lady and wife of Najib Razak who was convicted of corruption for soliciting and receiving bribes worth $272 million related to a solar energy project. Known for her luxury handbag collection. Sentenced to 10 years.

## Additional Context

### Public role

- Served as the spouse of Malaysia’s prime minister during Najib Razak’s tenure, attracting sustained domestic and international media attention.
- Frequently discussed in Malaysian politics as a highly visible political spouse rather than a formal elected official.

### 1MDB-era scrutiny

- Her public image became closely tied to broader controversy around governance, corruption allegations, and elite consumption during the period associated with the **1MDB scandal**.
- Reporting and court documents in multiple cases have referenced luxury goods and expenditures; public discussions often focus on symbolism as much as legal findings.

### Legal proceedings (high-level)

- Faced corruption-related charges and subsequent court rulings; summaries in media sometimes quote different figures depending on **currency, conversion rate, and whether fines vs. alleged bribe amounts** are being described.
