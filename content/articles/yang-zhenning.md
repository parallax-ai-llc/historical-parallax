## Summary

Yang Zhenning (Chen-Ning Yang) is a Chinese-American physicist who shared the 1957 Nobel Prize in Physics with Tsung-Dao Lee for their prediction of parity violation in weak interactions. He also developed the Yang-Mills theory, which became the foundation of the Standard Model of particle physics. He later returned to live in China.
