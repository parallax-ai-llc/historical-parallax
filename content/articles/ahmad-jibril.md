## Summary

Palestinian militant who founded the PFLP-General Command. His group was suspected of the 1988 Lockerbie bombing (later attributed to Libya). He carried out numerous attacks against Israel including the 1987 hang glider attack.

## Additional Context

## Organization and Role

Ahmad Jibril founded and led the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine – General Command (PFLP-GC), a militant faction distinct from the PFLP. The group was based primarily in Syria and Lebanon and maintained a close relationship with Syrian authorities over many years.

## Notable Incidents and Allegations

- The PFLP-GC was linked by some investigators and commentators to the 1988 Pan Am Flight 103 (Lockerbie) bombing early on, though later prosecutions focused on Libyan intelligence officials.
- The 1987 “Night of the Hang Gliders” attack became one of the group’s most widely cited operations, influencing Israeli security thinking about unconventional infiltration methods.

## Regional Context

Jibril’s career reflects the Cold War–era and post–Cold War landscape of Palestinian militant politics, including rivalry among factions, patronage by regional states, and the shifting centers of influence between Beirut, Damascus, and Gaza.
