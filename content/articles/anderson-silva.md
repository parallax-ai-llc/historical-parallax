## Summary

Anderson Silva held the longest title reign in UFC history at 2,457 days as middleweight champion. Known as 'The Spider,' his striking and counter-fighting were legendary.

## Career Timeline

## Fighting style and significance

### Style

Silva became widely known for a counter-striking approach that blended boxing, Muay Thai, and movement-heavy defense. Analysts often highlighted his timing, distance control, and ability to draw opponents into mistakes.

### Records and achievements

- Long UFC middleweight championship reign (measured by days) and multiple title defenses.
- A long win streak in the UFC during the peak of his career.
- Considered one of the most influential athletes in the growth and mainstream visibility of mixed martial arts in the late 2000s and early 2010s.

### Later career

After his title reign ended, Silva continued competing across different promotions and weight classes. His post-championship period is frequently discussed in terms of longevity and the physical demands of elite-level combat sports.

## Legacy

Silva is commonly cited in "greatest of all time" debates within MMA, and his highlight-reel finishes and evasive style influenced later generations of strikers.
