## Summary

Former and current US president who was indicted on 91 felony charges across four jurisdictions including federal charges for attempting to overturn the 2020 election. He was convicted of 34 felony counts in New York.

## Scope of the Cases

The set of indictments commonly discussed under this label spans multiple jurisdictions and legal theories. High-level categories include:

- **State-level charges** (for example, New York state charges related to business records)
- **Federal charges** (for example, allegations involving classified records and election-related conduct)
- **State prosecution in Georgia** (often described in public reporting as focused on election interference and use of racketeering statutes)

Because these matters involved separate prosecutors, courts, and procedures, developments in one case did not automatically determine outcomes in the others.

## Why It Mattered Politically

The indictments were historically notable because they concerned a former U.S. president and coincided with an active presidential campaign and later a presidency. This created recurring disputes over:

- The boundary between criminal accountability and political conflict
- Claims of selective prosecution versus equal application of the law
- How court schedules interact with election calendars

## Legal and Institutional Significance

Beyond the individual defendant, the cases became reference points for how modern U.S. legal institutions handle high-profile defendants. They also highlighted the practical importance of:

- Clear venue and jurisdiction rules
- Pretrial publicity management and jury selection challenges
- The distinction between alleged conduct, charged offenses, and proven findings
