## Historical figure (Wikipedia-linked): overview

Lyman Stewart (July 22, 1840 – September 28, 1923) was an American businessman and philanthropist associated with the growth of the oil industry in California and with prominent evangelical institutions in Los Angeles.[^1]

## Business and industry

- **Oil ventures (post–Civil War):** After early attempts at drilling in Pennsylvania, Stewart later relocated to California during the period when U.S. oil production and distribution were rapidly consolidating.[^1]
- **Union Oil:** Stewart is described as a co-founder of the Union Oil Company of California, and served as its president during a period of expansion.[^1]

## Philanthropy and religious initiatives

- **Bible Institute of Los Angeles (Biola):** Stewart helped found the Bible Institute of Los Angeles (later Biola University).[^1]
- **The Fundamentals:** Stewart and his brother Milton are described as anonymous funders of *The Fundamentals*, a publication commonly cited as foundational to Christian fundamentalism.[^1]
- **Los Angeles charity work:** The Wikipedia-linked article also associates him with early support for mission and rescue work in Los Angeles (including what is now known as the Union Rescue Mission).[^1]
