## Summary

Former Vice President of Burundi who served under President Nkurunziza during the 2015 political crisis that resulted in widespread violence and human rights abuses.

## Background and Education

- According to Wikipedia, Prosper Bazombanza was born on **12 February 1960** in **Kizi Hill** (then the Kingdom of Burundi) and graduated from the **University of Burundi**.
- Prior to holding national political office, he worked in education as a **mathematics teacher** and later as an **administrator** in the education sector.

## Political Affiliation

- Wikipedia describes Bazombanza as a member of **Union for National Progress (UPRONA)**.
- UPRONA has historically been a significant party in Burundi’s post-independence political landscape; Bazombanza’s roles are often discussed in the context of coalition and power-sharing arrangements.

## Vice Presidency (2014–2015)

- Bazombanza was appointed Vice President in **February 2014** under President **Pierre Nkurunziza**.
- Reporting summarized on Wikipedia notes opposition criticism of the appointment and links it to debates about **constitutional changes** and **term-limit** questions around Nkurunziza’s continued rule.
- This period overlaps with the lead-up to and aftermath of the **2015 political crisis** in Burundi, which involved major unrest and serious allegations of human rights abuses by various observers and organizations.

## Vice Presidency (since 2020)

- Wikipedia states Bazombanza returned to the vice presidency in **June 2020**, serving under President **Évariste Ndayishimiye**.
- His 2020 appointment is described as receiving strong parliamentary support, with some opposition members reportedly boycotting proceedings.
