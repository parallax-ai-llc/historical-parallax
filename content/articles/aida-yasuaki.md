## Summary

Aida Yasuaki was one of the most prolific Japanese mathematicians of the Edo period who founded the Saijō school of mathematics. He made contributions to determinant theory, algebraic equations, and combinatorics. He wrote over 2,000 mathematical works and was a rival of Seki Takakazu's mathematical school.
