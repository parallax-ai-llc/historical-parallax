## Summary

Known as 'Dr. Death,' headed apartheid South Africa's Project Coast chemical and biological weapons program. Developed poisons to kill anti-apartheid activists and planned mass sterilization of Black people.

## Background

Basson was a South African cardiologist who became associated with the apartheid-era state’s chemical and biological weapons efforts.
Project Coast is generally described as a covert program that researched toxins, delivery methods, and related capabilities.

## Project Coast and Allegations

Public discussion of Project Coast has included allegations of targeted poisoning efforts and research that raised severe ethical and human-rights concerns.
The program is frequently referenced in literature on apartheid security structures and clandestine state violence.

## Legal and Professional Proceedings

- Basson faced lengthy legal proceedings, including a high-profile criminal trial that ended in acquittal on many charges.
- The case remained controversial and continued to be debated in South Africa’s post-apartheid reckoning.
- Separate from criminal liability, professional-regulatory actions and disciplinary reviews have also been part of the broader story.

## Significance

The Basson/Project Coast episode is often used as an example of how medical and scientific expertise can be redirected into abusive state projects.
It also illustrates the difficulty of prosecuting clandestine programs where documentation is limited and operations were designed to be deniable.
In many summaries, the story is framed as part of the broader transition-justice challenges after apartheid.
The topic remains sensitive because it intersects with victims’ rights, public trust, and institutional accountability.
