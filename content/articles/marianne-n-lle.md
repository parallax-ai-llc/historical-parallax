## Summary

German serial killer known as the Angel of Death of Cologne who murdered at least 7 patients and attempted to kill 3 others in hospitals between 1984 and 1992. She poisoned victims with trinitroglycerin.

## Additional Context

### "Angel of death" pattern

Nölle is commonly grouped with so-called *healthcare serial killers*—offenders who target patients under their care. These cases are typically difficult to detect early because:

- Patients may already be critically ill.
- Deaths can appear medically plausible.
- Access to medications provides an easy means of harm.

### Reported method (source note)

Some summaries describe the drug used as an antipsychotic sedative (for example, Truxal / chlorprothixene) rather than the compound listed in some secondary retellings. The high-level point is that the alleged killings involved poisoning patients with a medication while she worked as a nurse.

### Aftermath and public impact

High-profile hospital murder cases in Germany contributed to debates about:

- Medication controls and auditing in clinical settings.
- Staffing, supervision, and whistleblowing systems.
- How hospitals and prosecutors evaluate suspicious clusters of patient deaths.
