## Summary

Irish-American gangster who masterminded the Lufthansa heist and then systematically murdered his associates. Immortalized as 'Jimmy Conway' in the film Goodfellas.

## Criminal Context

Jimmy Burke operated within the ecosystem of U.S. organized crime in the mid-to-late 20th century, where crews blended theft, loansharking, extortion, and union racketeering.
Although closely associated with the Lucchese crime family milieu, Burke himself was not an Italian-American “made” member, which shaped both his opportunities and limits.

## The Lufthansa Heist (High-Level)

The 1978 Lufthansa heist at John F. Kennedy International Airport is frequently described as one of the largest cash robberies in U.S. history.
In many accounts, the aftermath is as consequential as the robbery: paranoia about informants and evidence led to violence within the crew.

## Portrayal in Popular Culture

Burke’s life informed the character “Jimmy Conway” in *Goodfellas*, contributing to a lasting public image of the heist-and-cover-up pattern.
True-crime adaptations often emphasize cinematic elements; primary-source details are typically derived from law-enforcement records and cooperating-witness testimony.

## Law Enforcement Outcomes

Organized crime cases of this era often relied on:

- cooperating witnesses and plea bargains,
- financial trails and fencing networks,
- and long sentences that removed key figures from circulation.
