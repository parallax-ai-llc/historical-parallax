## Summary

Samuel Rutherford was a Scottish Presbyterian theologian whose Lex, Rex argued that the king was subject to the law and could be resisted. His letters, written during exile, became classics of devotional literature. He was a commissioner to the Westminster Assembly and helped draft the Westminster Confession. His political theory influenced later democratic thought, including the American founding.
