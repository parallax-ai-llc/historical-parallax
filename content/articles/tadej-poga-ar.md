Tadej Pogačar won the Tour de France, Giro d'Italia, and World Championship in 2024, one of cycling's rarest achievements.

## Background

Pogačar is a Slovenian professional road cyclist who rose through the UCI development ranks before joining UAE Team Emirates. He is widely regarded as a modern all-rounder, capable of winning high-mountain stages, time trials, and hilly one-day races.

## Racing Style

He is known for repeated high-intensity attacks on climbs, strong finishing speed from small groups, and tactical flexibility across stage racing and classics-style terrain. His palmarès includes overall victories in Grand Tours as well as major one-week stage races.

## Notable Achievements

- Multiple Tour de France overall victories (starting in 2020).
- A rare 2024 season triple of Giro d'Italia, Tour de France, and the UCI Road World Championships.
- Consistent success in prestigious stage races and one-day events across the WorldTour calendar.

## Legacy

Cycling commentators often cite his early Tour de France wins as a sign of a generational talent. His 2024 sweep of Giro–Tour–Worlds is frequently compared with historical “triple crown” style seasons that only a small number of riders have approached.

## Context in Modern Cycling

His success is often discussed alongside changes in training, nutrition, aerodynamics, and data-driven racing strategy that have reshaped elite road cycling in the 21st century. Within that environment, his combination of climbing ability and time-trial strength has made him a consistent favorite in multi-week stage races.
