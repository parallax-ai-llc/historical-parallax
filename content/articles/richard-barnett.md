## Summary

Capitol rioter photographed with his feet on Nancy Pelosi's desk.

The image—taken during the January 6, 2021 United States Capitol attack—became one of the most widely circulated photographs from the day.

Key points (as summarized by public reporting and Wikipedia):
- Barnett was photographed in the Speaker's office with his feet on a desk.
- He left a handwritten note addressed to then–Speaker Nancy Pelosi.
- He was arrested shortly after the attack, later convicted on multiple charges, and sentenced to a prison term.

## Background

- **Full name (reported):** Richard Morris Barnett.
- **Nickname (reported):** "Bigo".
- **Reported origins:** Born in Memphis, Tennessee (per Wikipedia).
- **Work history (reported):** Described in reporting as a window salesman; he also described himself as a retired firefighter, a claim that local services said they could not verify (per Wikipedia).

## January 6, 2021 — Notable Actions

Public reporting (summarized by Wikipedia) describes several actions that made Barnett a recognizable figure from the attack:
- Entered the U.S. Capitol during the breach.
- Was photographed in the Speaker's office with his feet on a desk, a moment that became emblematic of the episode.
- Left a handwritten note addressed to then–Speaker Nancy Pelosi.
- Publicly boasted online afterward about aspects of his conduct, including taking an envelope (per Wikipedia).

## Legal Proceedings and Outcome

- **Arrest:** Barnett was arrested on January 8, 2021 (per Wikipedia).
- **Conviction:** In 2023, he was convicted on eight charges, including four felonies (per Wikipedia).
- **Sentence:** Wikipedia summarizes a sentence of **54 months** of confinement, followed by supervised release/probation and a fine.
- **Pardon and release:** Wikipedia states he was **pardoned on January 20, 2025** and released the following day.

## Why This Case Was Prominent

The photograph from the Speaker's office circulated globally and was repeatedly cited in coverage as a symbol of:
- The breach of restricted federal spaces.
- The perceived brazenness of some participants.
- The threat and disruption to the constitutional transfer-of-power process.
