## Summary

Gunmen attacked St. Francis Catholic Church in Ondo State killing at least 40 worshippers.

## Background

- The attack occurred during a Catholic Mass at St. Francis Xavier Catholic Church in Owo, Ondo State, southwestern Nigeria.
- Owo is a community in Ondo State; the church was holding a service when the assault began.
- Nigerian security and political authorities described the incident as a major mass-casualty attack and launched investigations.

## The Attack (High-Level)

- Gunmen attacked worshippers during the service and used firearms and explosive devices.
- Survivors and early reporting described a sudden assault that triggered panic and a mass escape attempt.
- The attack targeted civilians in a religious setting.

## Casualties and Impact

- Early reports described at least 40 deaths; later counts reported higher totals in some accounts.
- Many additional people were wounded, including children, and were taken to local hospitals for treatment.
- The attack drew condemnation domestically and internationally, with calls for accountability and improved security.

## Suspected Perpetrators and Investigation

- Nigerian authorities pursued leads and attributed the attack to armed actors; public reporting noted the uncertainty and evolving claims early on.
- Investigations included forensic work at the scene, hospital casualty verification, and security operations in the region.
- Officials later announced arrests of suspects connected to the attack.

## Aftermath

- The government declared periods of mourning and increased security presence in affected areas.
- Religious leaders and community members held memorials and funerals for victims.
- The incident intensified public debate about insecurity, organized armed violence, and protection of civilian gatherings.
