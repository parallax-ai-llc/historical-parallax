## Summary

Former Russian intelligence officer poisoned with Novichok nerve agent by GRU assassins in Salisbury, England. The attack also killed a bystander and caused international outcry.

## Additional Context

- **Background:** Sergei Skripal was a former officer of Russia’s military intelligence service (GRU) who later provided information to UK intelligence.
- **Conviction and imprisonment:** In Russia he was convicted of espionage and imprisoned before being released in a prisoner swap.
- **Relocation to the UK:** After the swap, he resettled in the United Kingdom.

## Salisbury Poisoning (2018)

- **Location and timing:** Skripal and his daughter Yulia were found critically ill in Salisbury, Wiltshire, in March 2018.
- **Agent used:** UK investigators identified a *Novichok* nerve agent as the cause.
- **Public health impact:** The incident triggered a large decontamination effort across multiple sites linked to the pair’s movements.

## International Response

- **Attribution:** UK authorities attributed the attack to officers linked to the GRU.
- **Diplomatic fallout:** The incident led to coordinated diplomatic expulsions and a major deterioration in UK–Russia relations.
- **Ongoing investigations:** The case remained a reference point in discussions of state-linked assassination attempts and chemical weapons norms.

## Key Points

- The Skripal case is one of the most prominent modern examples of an alleged state-linked poisoning on foreign soil.
- It reinforced international concern about the proliferation and use of nerve agents and the enforcement of chemical weapons norms.
