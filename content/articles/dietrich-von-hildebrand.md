## Summary

Dietrich von Hildebrand was a German Catholic philosopher and theologian whom Pope Pius XII called 'the 20th century Doctor of the Church.' He was an outspoken opponent of Nazism and fled Germany in 1933. His works on ethics, aesthetics, and personalism influenced Catholic thought profoundly. He later became a traditionalist voice within Catholicism, critiquing certain post-Vatican II changes.
