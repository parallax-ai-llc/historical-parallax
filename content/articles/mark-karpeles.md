## Summary

French CEO of Mt. Gox bitcoin exchange who was convicted of manipulating exchange data. The exchange lost 850,000 bitcoins belonging to customers.

## Mt. Gox and early bitcoin markets

Mt. Gox was one of the most prominent early bitcoin exchanges, at times handling a large share of global bitcoin trading volume. Its rapid growth occurred in an ecosystem with limited regulation, immature security practices, and high operational risk.

## Collapse and losses (high level)

The 2014 failure of Mt. Gox became one of the defining crises of early cryptocurrency history. The reported losses—often summarized as hundreds of thousands of bitcoins missing—triggered:

- bankruptcy and rehabilitation processes
- long-running creditor claims and distribution planning
- intense scrutiny of custody, auditing, and exchange governance

## Legal proceedings

Karpeles was arrested in Japan and faced multiple allegations related to Mt. Gox's operation. Court outcomes have been reported as including a conviction on charges related to record/data manipulation, while other more severe accusations were not upheld.

## Broader impact

The Mt. Gox episode is frequently cited in discussions of:

- why exchanges adopted stronger security and custody practices
- the push for licensing, compliance, and transparency in crypto markets
- the risks of centralized custody compared with self-custody
