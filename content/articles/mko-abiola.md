## Summary

Winner of Nigeria's 1993 presidential election who died in prison after the military annulled the results.

## Background

- Moshood Kashimawo Olawale Abiola (commonly known as **MKO Abiola**) was a Nigerian businessman, philanthropist, and politician.
- He became a central figure in Nigeria’s pro-democracy movement during the era of military rule.

## 1993 Election and Annulment

- Abiola contested the **1993 Nigerian presidential election** under the Social Democratic Party (SDP).
- The election is remembered for its broad national support and for crossing ethnic and regional voting patterns.
- The annulment of the election is often treated as a defining moment in Nigeria’s late-20th-century political history.

## Detention

- After the annulment, Abiola continued to press for recognition of his mandate.
- In 1994, following a public declaration of himself as president, he was arrested and remained incarcerated for years.

## Death

- Abiola died in custody in 1998, shortly before Nigeria’s transition away from military rule.
- His death drew international attention and intensified demands for a return to civilian government.

## Legacy and Significance

- The **June 12** election date became a major symbol of Nigeria’s democratic struggle.
- Abiola is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - electoral legitimacy and civilian rule
  - political prisoners and state repression
  - democratization movements in Africa

## Key Points (High-level)

- Known for winning an election that was **annulled by a military regime**.
- Spent the final years of his life as a **political detainee**.
- Remains an enduring reference point in Nigeria’s modern democratic narrative.
