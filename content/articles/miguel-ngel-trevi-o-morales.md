## Summary

Mexican drug lord known as Z-40 who became leader of Los Zetas and was one of the most feared cartel bosses in Mexico. He was known for ordering mass executions and dissolving victims in drums of acid.

## Los Zetas Context

Los Zetas emerged as a highly militarized organized-crime group in Mexico, known for operating with a paramilitary style and for expanding into a diversified portfolio of illicit activities. Public reporting and government statements about the group have often highlighted its use of extreme violence as both a tactical tool and a reputational signal.

## Role and Reputation

Treviño Morales, commonly referred to by the alias “Z-40,” was identified by Mexican and U.S. authorities as a senior leader within Los Zetas. Accounts of his leadership frequently emphasize:

- centralized command over regional cells;
- aggressive territorial disputes with rivals;
- intimidation campaigns aimed at communities, businesses, and local officials.

## Violence Allegations (High Level)

Open-source descriptions of Los Zetas during this period include allegations of mass killings, forced disappearances, and methods intended to destroy evidence and spread fear. These allegations are typically discussed in the broader context of Mexico’s cartel conflict and the challenges faced by investigators in collecting admissible evidence.

## Arrest and Detention

In 2013, Mexican marines arrested Treviño Morales near the U.S. border. Following his capture, he remained in custody while authorities pursued cases connected to organized-crime activity.

## International Designations and Prosecutions

U.S. agencies publicly linked Treviño Morales to drug-trafficking and organized-crime charges and used sanctions and indictments as part of broader efforts to disrupt cartel leadership networks.

## Legacy and Impact

Treviño Morales is often cited as an example of the cartel leadership style associated with Los Zetas: highly violent, operationally adaptive, and willing to compete across multiple criminal markets.
