## Summary

Vladimir Voevodsky was a Russian-American mathematician who won the Fields Medal in 2002 for developing new cohomology theories for algebraic varieties and proving the Milnor conjecture. He later developed univalent foundations for mathematics based on homotopy type theory, proposing a new foundation for mathematics verified by computer proof assistants.
