## Summary

John Wanamaker was a pioneer of modern advertising and the department store concept. He opened one of the first department stores in the United States in Philadelphia in 1876. He was the first retailer to place a half-page newspaper ad and invented the price tag. He also served as U.S. Postmaster General and introduced innovations including money-back guarantees and the concept of the department store restaurant.
