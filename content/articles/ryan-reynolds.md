## Summary

Ryan Reynolds is a Canadian-American actor, producer, and entrepreneur best known for his portrayal of the wisecracking antihero Deadpool in Marvel's film franchise. Born in Vancouver, British Columbia, Reynolds built a career spanning three decades, transitioning from teen soap operas to blockbuster action-comedies and ultimately becoming one of Hollywood's highest-paid stars.

Beyond acting, Reynolds has established himself as a savvy businessman, acquiring and building brands including Aviation American Gin, which he sold to Diageo in 2020 for up to $610 million, and Mint Mobile, sold to T-Mobile in 2023 for approximately $1.35 billion. His blend of self-deprecating humor, social media presence, and entrepreneurial acumen has made him a distinctive figure in both entertainment and business.

---

## Early Life & Background

Ryan Rodney Reynolds was born on October 23, 1976, in Vancouver, British Columbia, Canada. He is the youngest of four sons of James Chester Reynolds, a food wholesaler and former Royal Canadian Mounted Police officer, and Tamara Lee, a retail sales employee. Reynolds grew up in the Kitsilano neighborhood of Vancouver and attended Kwantlen University College briefly before leaving to pursue acting full-time.

He began performing in local theater and commercials as a teenager, landing his first significant role in the Canadian teen soap opera *Hillside* (known in the US as *Fifteen*) in 1991, at the age of 14.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Reynolds relocated to Los Angeles in the mid-1990s, taking on supporting roles in television before breaking through with the ABC sitcom *Two Guys and a Girl* (1998–2001). His film career accelerated with *National Lampoon's Van Wilder* (2002), which established his comedic persona.

He gained wider recognition with supporting roles in *Blade: Trinity* (2004) and *X-Men Origins: Wolverine* (2009), the latter giving him a brief appearance as Wade Wilson/Deadpool. After years of advocacy for a proper Deadpool film, Reynolds starred in *Deadpool* (2016), which grossed over $782 million worldwide on a $58 million budget — a record for an R-rated film at the time. *Deadpool 2* (2018) and *Deadpool & Wolverine* (2024) further cemented his franchise status.

## Key Achievements

- **Deadpool** (2016): $782 million worldwide gross; record-breaking R-rated release
- **Deadpool & Wolverine** (2024): Over $1.3 billion worldwide, highest-grossing R-rated film ever
- **Aviation Gin acquisition**: Sold to Diageo in 2020 for up to $610 million
- **Mint Mobile acquisition**: Sold to T-Mobile in 2023 for approximately $1.35 billion
- Co-owner of Welsh football club **Wrexham AFC** (acquired 2020), subject of the documentary series *Welcome to Wrexham*
- Named one of *Time* magazine's 100 Most Influential People (2017)

## Controversies & Criticism

Reynolds has faced relatively limited controversy compared to peers. His 2012 marriage to actress Blake Lively drew mild criticism when it emerged the wedding was held at Boone Hall Plantation in South Carolina, a former slave plantation. Reynolds acknowledged the insensitivity in a 2020 interview, calling it "something we'll always be deeply ashamed of."

His public comedic feud with actor Hugh Jackman, while largely staged for promotional purposes, occasionally drew commentary about performative celebrity culture. Some critics have also argued his entrepreneurial ventures represent a broader trend of celebrity-driven brand speculation rather than genuine business creation.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Reynolds has redefined what a modern film star can be — combining blockbuster appeal with digital-native humor and genuine business success. His hands-on approach to brand building, particularly his "Maximum Effort" marketing company, has influenced how celebrities engage with commercial ventures. Wrexham AFC's rise under his and co-owner Rob McElhenney's stewardship became a cultural story that transcended sports, drawing global attention to lower-league football.
