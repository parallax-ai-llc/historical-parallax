## Summary

Abbas Kiarostami was an Iranian filmmaker whose minimalist, deeply humanistic films earned him recognition as one of the greatest directors of modern cinema. His Koker Trilogy, Taste of Cherry (which won the Palme d'Or), and Close-Up blur the boundaries between fiction and documentary. His work influenced filmmakers worldwide.
