## Summary

Margaret Hamilton is an American computer scientist and systems engineer who led the team that developed the on-board flight software for NASA's Apollo program. Her software engineering innovations were critical to the success of the Apollo 11 Moon landing. She coined the term 'software engineering' to give the discipline legitimacy. Her error-detection design saved the Apollo 11 mission from aborting during landing.
