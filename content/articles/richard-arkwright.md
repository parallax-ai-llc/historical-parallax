## Summary

Richard Arkwright invented the water frame for spinning cotton, developed the factory system, and became the wealthiest non-noble in Britain. His Cromford Mill, built in 1771, is considered the world's first successful water-powered cotton spinning factory. He is often called the father of the Industrial Revolution.
