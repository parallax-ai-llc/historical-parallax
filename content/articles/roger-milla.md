## Summary

Roger Milla led Cameroon to the 1990 WC quarter-finals at 38 and scored at the 1994 WC at 42.

## Additional Context

### Club career (high-level)

- Played for leading clubs in **Cameroon** before moving to **France**, where he became associated with multiple Ligue 1 teams.
- Known as a forward with strong positioning and opportunistic finishing rather than purely pace-based play.

### International legacy

- Became a global symbol of African football’s rise on the World Cup stage, particularly during **Cameroon’s 1990 run**.
- Remembered for his goal celebrations at the corner flag, which became part of his public identity.
- Often cited in discussions about longevity in elite sport because of his high-profile World Cup goals in his late 30s and early 40s.

### Recognition

- Included in major retrospective lists and awards recognizing influential footballers from Africa.
