## Summary

Nicholas Christofilos was a Greek-American physicist and engineer who independently invented the strong focusing principle for particle accelerators while working as an elevator engineer. He also conceived Project Argus, testing nuclear explosions in space to create artificial radiation belts. His Astron concept for controlled nuclear fusion was ahead of its time. Christofilos exemplified the self-taught scientific genius.
