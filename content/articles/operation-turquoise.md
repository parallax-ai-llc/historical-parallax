## Summary

French military intervention in Rwanda that arrived too late to prevent the genocide and has been accused of protecting génocidaires rather than victims. France's role remains deeply controversial.

## Context

- **Regional setting**: The 1994 Rwandan genocide unfolded amid a civil war between the Rwandan government and the Rwandan Patriotic Front (RPF), alongside large-scale displacement and mass violence.
- **International response**: The United Nations Assistance Mission for Rwanda (UNAMIR) was present but constrained by mandate and resources, and many foreign nationals were evacuated early in the crisis.

## Mandate and Objectives

Operation Turquoise was launched by France under a UN Security Council mandate as a **time-limited humanitarian intervention**.

Commonly cited aims included:

- establishing a **safe humanitarian zone** to protect civilians,
- supporting delivery of **humanitarian aid** and medical assistance,
- stabilizing routes for **refugee movement** and relief logistics.

## Actions on the Ground (High-Level)

- French forces entered southwestern Rwanda and set up checkpoints and protected areas.
- The operation interacted with a rapidly shifting military situation as the RPF advanced.
- Large refugee flows moved toward neighboring countries (notably Zaire/DRC), creating complex humanitarian emergencies in border regions.

## Controversies and Criticism

Operation Turquoise remains controversial in historical and political debate. Frequent points of dispute include:

- **Timing**: Critics argue the intervention came after the genocide was well underway and could not prevent the bulk of killings.
- **Impartiality**: France was accused of favoring actors associated with the prior Rwandan government.
- **Protection and escape**: Some accounts allege the operation enabled or failed to prevent the flight of individuals responsible for genocide into neighboring states.
- **Narrative conflict**: French official accounts emphasize humanitarian intent, while critics stress geopolitical motives and prior French involvement in Rwanda.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- The operation contributed to ongoing debates over **responsibility**, **humanitarian intervention**, and the limits of military action framed as protection.
- France’s role in Rwanda has been examined through investigations, reports, and diplomatic disputes over historical accountability.
- The events around Operation Turquoise are often discussed in the broader context of post-genocide justice efforts and the regional instability that followed in Central Africa.
