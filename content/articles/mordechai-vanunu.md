## Summary

Israeli nuclear technician who revealed details of Israel's secret nuclear weapons program to the British press in 1986. He was lured to Rome by a Mossad honey trap agent and kidnapped back to Israel.

## Background

- **Work at Dimona**: Vanunu worked as a technician at Israel’s Negev Nuclear Research Center (Dimona), which has been widely associated in open-source reporting with Israel’s undeclared nuclear capabilities.
- **Motivation framing**: Public accounts describe his actions as driven by political and ethical objections to secrecy, while Israeli authorities framed the disclosure as a grave security offense.

## 1986 Disclosure

- **Evidence and publication**: He provided information and photographs to journalists at *The Sunday Times*, which published a report describing the scale and technical aspects of Israel’s program.
- **International impact**: The story intensified debate around nuclear ambiguity, non-proliferation norms, and the limits of press reporting on national security matters.

## Abduction, Trial, and Imprisonment

- **Rendition operation**: According to widely reported accounts, he was lured to Rome, detained by Israeli operatives, and returned to Israel for prosecution.
- **Conviction**: He was convicted of offenses including treason/espionage-related charges and served a lengthy prison sentence, including periods of isolation.

## Post-Release Restrictions

After his release, Israeli authorities imposed restrictions that became a continuing subject of legal and human-rights debate, including limits on:

- foreign travel,
- contact with non-Israelis,
- and speaking to the international press.

## Legacy and Ongoing Debate

Vanunu remains a polarizing figure:

- **Supporters** portray him as a whistleblower who exposed a major proliferation reality.
- **Critics** argue the disclosure endangered national security and deterrence.
