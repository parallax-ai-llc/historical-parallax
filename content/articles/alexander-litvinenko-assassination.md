## Summary

Russian former FSB officer who became a critic of Putin and accused the FSB of terrorism. He was poisoned with polonium-210 in London. A UK inquiry concluded Putin 'probably approved' the assassination.

## Background

Alexander Litvinenko was a former Russian security officer who, after leaving Russia, became associated with allegations about corruption and state-linked violence. His case is often discussed as an early, high-profile example of post–Cold War disputes involving intelligence services, defectors, and political exile in Western Europe.

## The Poisoning (High-Level)

Litvinenko was poisoned in London with **polonium-210**, a rare radioactive substance. The use of a radiological agent made the incident unusual compared with more common poisoning cases and created a complex forensic and public-health response.

Key characteristics often emphasized in summaries include:

- the difficulty of obtaining and handling polonium-210
- the distinct medical course associated with acute radiation exposure
- extensive contamination-tracing as part of the investigation

## Investigation and Attribution

British authorities investigated the poisoning as a homicide. The case generated significant public attention because it involved:

- alleged involvement of Russian nationals
- cross-border evidence and extradition disputes
- intelligence-sensitive claims and contested narratives

## UK Public Inquiry (2016)

A UK public inquiry examined available evidence and produced conclusions that were widely reported in international media. The inquiry’s findings are frequently cited in discussions of state responsibility and accountability mechanisms when conventional criminal processes are constrained by jurisdiction and diplomatic factors.

## Diplomatic and Political Impact

The Litvinenko case contributed to long-running tensions between the United Kingdom and Russia. It is commonly referenced alongside later incidents as part of a broader pattern of concern about:

- security risks to dissidents and defectors abroad
- the limits of legal remedies when suspects remain outside reach
- the role of sanctions, expulsions, and public attribution in foreign policy

## Significance

Beyond the individual case, the assassination is used as a case study in modern security and international relations for how **unconventional weapons**, investigative transparency, and political signaling intersect in high-stakes incidents.
