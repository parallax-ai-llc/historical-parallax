## Summary

Burkinabè military officer who overthrew President Kaboré in January 2022 before being overthrown himself by Ibrahim Traoré just 8 months later.

## Background and Military Service

- Burkinabè military officer (often described as a lieutenant colonel at the time of taking power).
- Rose to prominence during Burkina Faso's escalating security crisis in the Sahel, where the state faced attacks by jihadist insurgent groups and growing displacement.
- Has been associated in open sources with counter-insurgency and command roles within the armed forces.
- In public reporting, he is also noted for authoring a book on the Burkinabè military and terrorism (published prior to his 2022 takeover).

## January 2022 Coup and Head-of-State Period

- **24 Jan 2022:** A group of soldiers announced the removal of President **Roch Marc Christian Kaboré**, citing failures to contain insurgent violence.
- Damiba emerged as the leader of the junta, commonly referred to as the **Patriotic Movement for Safeguard and Restoration (MPSR)**.
- The takeover was accompanied by measures typical of military transitions, including suspending parts of the constitutional order and announcing a return-to-civilian-rule roadmap.

## Governance and Security Context

- Damiba's de facto presidency took place amid continued attacks, military setbacks, and humanitarian pressure, which constrained the junta's legitimacy.
- The central political promise of the period was improved security; the persistence of violence remained a key driver of elite and public dissatisfaction.

## September 2022 Overthrow

- **30 Sep 2022:** Damiba was removed in another military takeover led by **Ibrahim Traoré**, again justified publicly as a response to worsening security conditions.
- Following his ouster, multiple reports indicated Damiba left Burkina Faso; open-source accounts commonly state he went to **Togo**.

## Significance

- His brief tenure (roughly eight months) is frequently cited as an example of the rapid cycle of coups in parts of the Sahel during the early 2020s.
- The episode illustrates the linkage between battlefield outcomes, civil-military relations, and regime durability in states facing prolonged insurgency.
