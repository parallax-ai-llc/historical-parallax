## Summary

Torahiko Terada was a pioneering Japanese physicist who conducted groundbreaking research on X-ray diffraction of crystals (independently of Laue's work), seismology, and the physics of everyday phenomena. He was also a celebrated essayist who made science accessible to the Japanese public. His work on disaster prevention influenced Japanese earthquake preparedness.
