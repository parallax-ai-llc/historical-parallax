## Overview

Nicolas Sarkozy (born 1955) is a French politician who served as President of France from 2007 to 2012. He previously held senior cabinet roles including Minister of the Interior and Minister of Finance.

## Political Profile

Sarkozy was commonly associated with a more security-focused, reform-oriented center-right line in French politics, emphasizing public order, labor-market reforms, and a more visibly active style of presidential leadership.

## Presidency (2007–2012)

High-level themes frequently associated with Sarkozy's presidency include:

- Economic and financial-crisis management during the 2008–2009 global downturn
- Domestic reforms (including pensions) and debates over national identity, immigration, and security
- European Union leadership during the rotating Council presidency in 2008
- Active foreign-policy engagement, including involvement in major international summits and crises

## Post-Presidency and Legal Proceedings

Sarkozy has faced multiple investigations and court cases after leaving office. Some proceedings have resulted in convictions at trial and on appeal in certain instances, and other cases have been dismissed or remained subject to ongoing legal process depending on the matter and jurisdiction.
