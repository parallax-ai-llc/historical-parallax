## Summary

Ariel Castro kidnapped three women - Michelle Knight, Amanda Berry, and Gina DeJesus - and imprisoned them in his Cleveland house for a decade. Berry escaped in 2013 by kicking through a door.

## Additional Context

- **Victims:** The case involved the abduction and captivity of Michelle Knight, Amanda Berry, and Gina DeJesus in Cleveland, Ohio, over roughly a decade.
- **Discovery and rescue:** Public attention focused on the moment Berry escaped and alerted neighbors, leading to police intervention and the rescue of the other victims.
- **Criminal case outcome:** Castro pleaded guilty to multiple charges; the sentence effectively ensured he would never be released.

## Social Impact

- **Public discourse:** The case prompted renewed attention to missing-person investigations, media coverage of long-term abductions, and the needs of survivors after rescue.
- **Victim support:** Survivors’ subsequent public statements and advocacy highlighted trauma recovery, privacy concerns, and the importance of community and institutional support.
