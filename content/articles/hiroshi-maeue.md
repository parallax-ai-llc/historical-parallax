## Summary

Japanese serial killer who used suicide pact websites to lure three victims in 2005, strangling them to death. He exploited Japan's suicide epidemic. Executed by hanging.

## Case Overview (High Level)

Maeue’s crimes were notable for the use of online “suicide pact” forums to contact vulnerable people. The case drew attention to the intersection of internet-mediated contact, mental health vulnerability, and predatory exploitation.

## Investigation and Trial

Japanese authorities investigated communications, meeting arrangements, and forensic evidence related to the killings. Court proceedings examined planning, intent, and responsibility, resulting in a death sentence that was ultimately carried out.

## Societal Context

The case is often discussed alongside Japan’s broader public-health concerns around suicide and social isolation. It also contributed to public debate about moderation and monitoring of websites associated with self-harm risk and the responsibilities of platform operators.
