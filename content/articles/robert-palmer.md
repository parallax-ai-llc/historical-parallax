## Additional Context

### January 6 context (high level)

The January 6, 2021 attack on the United States Capitol involved a large crowd that breached police lines and entered parts of the Capitol complex while Congress was meeting to certify the 2020 U.S. presidential election results. A significant portion of subsequent federal prosecutions focused on assaults on law enforcement, obstruction-related conduct, and unlawful entry into restricted areas.

### Conduct described in the case

In widely reported summaries of his case, Robert Palmer is described as having:

- engaged law enforcement officers during clashes outside the Capitol,
- thrown a fire extinguisher toward police, and
- thrown a wooden plank toward police.

These allegations are typically cited as the basis for the length of the sentence reported in public summaries.

### Sentence (as commonly summarized)

Public accounts of the case describe Palmer as receiving a 63-month (5 years and 3 months) federal prison sentence. Federal sentencing in the United States is generally informed by:

- the statutes of conviction,
- the U.S. Sentencing Guidelines (advisory),
- the facts found by the court or admitted in a plea, and
- statutory sentencing factors (including seriousness of the offense, deterrence, and protection of the public).
