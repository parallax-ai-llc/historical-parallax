## Summary

US Army sergeant who murdered 16 Afghan civilians, mostly women and children, in their homes near Kandahar on March 11, 2012. He left his base at night and attacked two villages. Pleaded guilty to avoid death penalty. Serving life.

## Incident overview

The killings attributed to Robert Bales are commonly referred to as the **Kandahar massacre** (or Panjwai district shootings) and occurred during the U.S.-led war in Afghanistan.[^1]

Key points typically emphasized in summaries include:

- The attacks occurred at night, after Bales left a military outpost.
- Victims were primarily civilians, including women and children.
- The incident became a major political and military crisis for the coalition effort.[^1]

## Investigation and prosecution

Bales was taken into U.S. custody and prosecuted through the U.S. military justice system. He ultimately **pleaded guilty** to multiple counts (including murder) in a case structured to avoid a death-penalty proceeding.[^1]

## Sentence and incarceration

He received a sentence of **life without the possibility of parole** and has been held in U.S. military custody.[^1]

## Impact and aftermath

The case has been cited in broader discussions about:

- Command responsibility and oversight in deployed environments
- Screening, stress, and mental-health support for service members
- The strategic consequences of civilian casualties in counterinsurgency operations
