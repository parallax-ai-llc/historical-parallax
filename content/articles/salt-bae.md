## Summary

Nusret Gökçe, known worldwide as "Salt Bae," is a Turkish chef, restaurateur, and internet personality who became a global phenomenon in January 2017 after a video of his flamboyant meat-seasoning technique went viral. His signature move — theatrically sprinkling salt from his fingertips down his forearm onto a carved steak — turned him into one of the most recognizable memes of the decade and launched him into culinary celebrity.

Beyond the meme, Gökçe built a legitimate restaurant empire under the Nusr-Et brand, operating luxury steakhouses across Istanbul, Dubai, London, New York, Miami, and other major cities. His restaurants became destinations for celebrities, athletes, and wealthy tourists, though they also attracted significant criticism for extreme pricing and questions about food quality relative to cost.

Salt Bae represents a distinctly modern type of fame — one built at the intersection of social media virality, spectacle-driven dining, and personal branding — making him both a cultural phenomenon and a polarizing figure in the culinary world.

## Early Life & Background

Nusret Gökçe was born on August 9, 1983, in Erzurum, a city in eastern Turkey. He grew up in a working-class family and left school after the sixth grade due to financial hardship. At age 14, he began apprenticing as a butcher in Istanbul's Kadıköy district, learning the fundamentals of meat preparation and cutting. He later traveled to Argentina and the United States to study meat preparation techniques, working in various steakhouses to refine his craft before returning to Turkey to launch his own venture.

## Rise to Prominence

Gökçe opened his first Nusr-Et steakhouse in Istanbul in 2010, gradually expanding to additional locations in Turkey. His big break came on January 7, 2017, when a video posted to his Instagram account showed him slicing and salting an Ottoman steak with dramatic, almost sensual flair. The clip amassed over 10 million views within days and was shared across every major social platform. The internet christened him "Salt Bae," and the meme spread to every corner of online culture, spawning countless parodies and imitations.

Capitalizing on his viral fame, Gökçe rapidly expanded the Nusr-Et brand internationally. He opened locations in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Miami, New York, Dallas, Boston, London, and Mykonos between 2017 and 2023. His Instagram following surged past 50 million, making him one of the most-followed chefs on the platform.

## Key Achievements

Gökçe built the Nusr-Et brand into an international chain of over 20 luxury steakhouses across multiple continents. He transformed a single Istanbul butcher shop into a hospitality empire reportedly valued in the hundreds of millions of dollars. His viral moment became one of the most enduring food-related memes in internet history, and he cultivated a celebrity clientele that included David Beckham, Leonardo DiCaprio, and numerous world leaders.

## Controversies & Criticism

Salt Bae has faced persistent criticism over his restaurants' extreme pricing — a gold-wrapped tomahawk steak at the London location was listed at £850, and bills totaling thousands of pounds for a single meal became a recurring subject of public outrage. Food critics have frequently questioned whether the quality justified the cost, with many describing the experience as spectacle over substance.

In 2022, Gökçe attracted backlash during the FIFA World Cup final in Qatar when he appeared on the pitch after Argentina's victory, handling the World Cup trophy and posing with players despite having no official role. FIFA opened an investigation into how he gained access to the restricted area. His London restaurant also faced scrutiny over reported labor practices, including allegations from staff regarding tip distribution and working conditions.

## Legacy & Influence

Salt Bae's impact lies less in culinary innovation than in demonstrating the power of social media to reshape the restaurant industry. He proved that viral personal branding could drive a global dining empire and that spectacle could be as valuable as Michelin stars. His model of performance-as-dining influenced a wave of theatrically presented restaurant concepts worldwide, for better or worse cementing the idea that in the age of Instagram, presentation can eclipse the plate.
