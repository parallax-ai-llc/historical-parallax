## Summary

Mircea Eliade was a Romanian historian of religion and philosopher who was one of the most influential scholars of comparative religion in the 20th century. His concepts of 'sacred and profane,' 'hierophany,' and 'eternal return' shaped the academic study of religion. He served as professor at the University of Chicago for decades. His works, including The Sacred and the Profane and A History of Religious Ideas, are foundational texts in religious studies.
