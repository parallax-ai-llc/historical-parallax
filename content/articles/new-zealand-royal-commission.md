## Mandate and Scope (high-level)

- Commonly referred to as the **Royal Commission of Inquiry into the terrorist attack on Christchurch mosques**.
- Established to examine **what New Zealand’s intelligence and security agencies** could have done to prevent the attack, and what lessons should be learned.
- Focused on systemic questions (policy, priorities, coordination), rather than assigning criminal liability.

## Key Findings (summary-level)

- Public reporting notes the Commission concluded the attacker acted largely alone and that there was **no specific, single point of intelligence failure** that would have reliably stopped the attack.
- The Commission highlighted gaps in **strategic focus** (including limited attention to certain forms of violent extremism), and issues in **capability, resourcing, and information-sharing**.
- It emphasized the importance of **community engagement** and building trust with communities affected by counter-terrorism and national security policy.

## Recommendations and Follow-up

- The final report is widely reported to include **44 recommendations**, covering areas such as:
  - clearer national security priorities and accountability
  - improved coordination across intelligence, security, and policing functions
  - strengthened counter-terrorism capability and preparedness
  - better engagement with affected communities and stronger protections against hate-motivated violence
- The New Zealand Government publicly released an **executive summary** and outlined implementation workstreams following the report’s release.
