## Summary

Tanaka Koichi is a Japanese engineer who won the 2002 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for developing soft laser desorption ionization (SLI) for mass spectrometric analysis of biological macromolecules. Working at Shimadzu Corporation as a salaryman engineer, he was one of the most unexpected Nobel laureates, having no PhD degree.
