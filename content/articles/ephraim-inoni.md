## Additional Context

### Role in Cameroonian politics

- Inoni served as Prime Minister under President Paul Biya, a period in which Cameroon’s political system was characterized by strong executive authority and long-running incumbency at the top of the state.[^2]
- As head of government, the prime minister’s office was often discussed in terms of coordination and implementation rather than independent agenda-setting, given the presidency’s central role.[^2]

### Corruption case and sentencing

- Inoni was convicted in a high-profile corruption case and sentenced to a lengthy prison term, a result frequently cited in reporting on Cameroon’s periodic anti-corruption drives.[^1][^3]
- The case is commonly linked to broader efforts known as Operation Épervier (“Operation Sparrowhawk”), which targeted a number of former senior officials, drawing both praise for accountability and criticism over selectivity and due process concerns.[^3]

### Public impact and debate

- The prosecution of a former prime minister highlighted persistent debates in Cameroon about governance, elite impunity, and the effectiveness of anti-corruption campaigns in improving public services and fiscal transparency.[^2][^3]
