## Context and Constitutional Process

South Korea’s Constitution provides an impeachment mechanism for certain high officials, including the president, when they are alleged to have violated the Constitution or other laws in the course of performing official duties.

A typical impeachment sequence involves:

- A motion introduced and voted on in the National Assembly.
- If passed, an immediate suspension of the official’s powers while adjudication proceeds.
- Review by the Constitutional Court, which determines whether to uphold or dismiss the impeachment.

## Key Institutions Involved

- **National Assembly**: Votes on whether to impeach and sets the formal charges.
- **Constitutional Court**: Acts as the adjudicative body for impeachment cases.
- **Law-enforcement and prosecution**: May pursue separate criminal investigations in parallel, depending on allegations.

## Political and Administrative Impact

Presidential impeachment proceedings can create a prolonged period of political uncertainty, including disputes over executive authority, cabinet continuity, and the conduct of foreign and security policy.

In practice, major impacts often include:

- Shifts in legislative-executive relations.
- Market and diplomatic signaling concerns.
- Intensified public demonstrations and counter-demonstrations.
