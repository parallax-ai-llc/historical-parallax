## Summary
Dawood Ibrahim Kaskar is an Indian crime lord, drug trafficker, and designated global terrorist. He heads D-Company, one of the largest criminal organizations in South Asia. He is accused of orchestrating the 1993 Bombay bombings that killed 257 people. He is believed to be hiding in Pakistan.

## Early Life
Born December 26, 1955, in Khed, Maharashtra, India. Son of a police constable, he grew up in Mumbai's Dongri area. He dropped out of school and entered the criminal underworld, eventually taking over the organization of smuggler Haji Mastan. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Ibrahim built D-Company into a transnational criminal organization involved in drug trafficking, arms smuggling, money laundering, extortion, and contract killing. He is accused of financing the March 12, 1993, serial bomb blasts in Mumbai that killed 257 people and injured 700. He has been designated a global terrorist by the US and India. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Ibrahim fled India in 1986 and is believed to have been living in Karachi, Pakistan, since the early 1990s, though Pakistan denies his presence. He is wanted by Interpol and has a $25 million US bounty. India has repeatedly demanded his extradition. He has never been captured or tried for the Mumbai bombings. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- Some in Mumbai's underworld credit him with maintaining a degree of order among criminal gangs [^4]
- His case has highlighted the nexus between organized crime and terrorism
- International pressure on Pakistan regarding his harboring has been significant
