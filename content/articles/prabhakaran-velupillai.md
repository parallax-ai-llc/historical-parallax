## Background and Role

Velupillai Prabhakaran (1954–2009) was the leader and chief strategist of the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE), an armed separatist organization that sought an independent Tamil state (“Tamil Eelam”) in northern and eastern Sri Lanka.[^1]

The LTTE emerged from a broader milieu of Tamil militancy that expanded amid ethnic tensions, discrimination claims, and escalating political violence in Sri Lanka in the 1970s–1980s.[^1]

## Conflict Context (High Level)

- **Sri Lankan Civil War**: The conflict between the Sri Lankan state and multiple Tamil militant groups intensified in the early 1980s and continued, with intermittent ceasefires and negotiations, until 2009.[^1]
- **LTTE capabilities**: Under Prabhakaran, the LTTE developed conventional and guerrilla capabilities, including a naval wing (often referred to as the “Sea Tigers”) and a history of suicide attacks.[^1]
- **International status**: The LTTE was designated a terrorist organization by several governments during the conflict, affecting diplomacy, fundraising, and diaspora-linked networks.[^1]

## Notable Themes Discussed by Sources

- **Centralized leadership**: Sources commonly describe the LTTE as highly centralized around Prabhakaran’s authority.[^1]
- **Use of suicide bombings**: The group is widely associated with the operationalization of suicide attacks in the region, including high-profile assassinations.[^1]
- **Civilian impact**: Reporting and historical overviews emphasize major civilian casualties and displacement over the course of the war.[^1]

## Legacy and Controversy (Neutral Summary)

Historical accounts typically treat Prabhakaran as a decisive figure in modern Sri Lankan history: viewed by some supporters as a Tamil nationalist leader, and by critics and many governments as the head of an organization responsible for terrorism and widespread atrocities.[^1]
