## Summary

British man known as 'White Beast' who joined Al-Shabaab in Somalia and was killed during an attack on a Kenyan military base. He was one of several British fighters in Al-Shabaab.

## Context

Evans became notable in UK media coverage as an example of a Western "foreign fighter" joining an East African jihadist organization rather than traveling to conflicts in the Middle East. Al‑Shabaab, an Islamist militant group based in Somalia, has conducted attacks in Somalia and neighboring countries, including Kenya.

The nickname "White Beast" was widely reported in connection with recruitment and propaganda narratives that highlighted the presence of non-Somali fighters.

## Recruitment and travel

Public accounts describe Evans leaving the United Kingdom and traveling to Somalia, where he joined al‑Shabaab. Cases like this are often discussed alongside online radicalization, social networks, and the challenges of monitoring outbound travel where the end destination is concealed or routed through third countries.

## Death and reported operations

Evans was reported killed during an attack on a Kenyan military base in 2015. Attacks on military installations in Kenya have been part of al‑Shabaab’s broader strategy to pressure Kenya over its military involvement in Somalia.

## Significance

The Evans case is frequently referenced in discussions of:

- the security risks posed by citizens traveling to join designated terrorist groups,
- how militant groups use foreign recruits for publicity and perceived legitimacy,
- and the policy debates around prevention, surveillance, and prosecution for returnees.
