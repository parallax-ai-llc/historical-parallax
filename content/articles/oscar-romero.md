## Summary

Oscar Romero was the Archbishop of San Salvador who became the voice of the poor and oppressed during El Salvador's civil conflict. Initially conservative, he was transformed by the murder of his friend Rutilio Grande and became a fierce critic of military violence and social injustice. He was assassinated while celebrating Mass in 1980. He was canonized by Pope Francis in 2018.
