## Summary

Barbara Liskov is an American computer scientist known for the Liskov Substitution Principle in object-oriented programming and her pioneering work on data abstraction and programming languages. She led the design of the CLU programming language, which introduced many concepts now standard in modern programming. She was one of the first women to earn a PhD in computer science in the US and received the Turing Award in 2008.
