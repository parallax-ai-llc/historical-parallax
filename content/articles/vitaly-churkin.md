## Summary

Russian UN ambassador who died suddenly in New York, one of several Russian diplomats to die mysteriously.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Background

- Churkin was a career Russian diplomat and foreign-policy official.
- He studied at the Moscow State Institute of International Relations (MGIMO) and entered the Soviet foreign service.

## Roles and Positions

- Permanent Representative of the Russian Federation to the United Nations (2006–2017).[^1]
- Ambassador of Russia to Canada (1998–2003).[^1]
- Held senior roles in the Russian Ministry of Foreign Affairs, including periods as a deputy foreign minister and as an official spokesperson (details and dates vary by source).[^1]

## Notable Moments at the United Nations

Churkin was a prominent public voice for Russia at the UN during multiple high-profile international crises. Public reporting and UN records frequently associate his tenure with:

- Security Council debates on Syria and the use of veto power in resolutions related to the conflict.
- Security Council and General Assembly debates following Russia’s 2014 actions in Crimea and eastern Ukraine.
- Regular press briefings and on-the-record exchanges that made him one of the more visible ambassadors of his period.

## Death and Aftermath

- Churkin died in New York City on 20 February 2017 while serving as Russia’s UN ambassador, one day before his 65th birthday.[^1]
- The death was described as sudden; Russian officials reported a heart-related cause.[^2]
- Given his position and location, U.S. authorities treated the death as a matter requiring standard investigative procedures, and media reporting noted routine involvement by U.S. law enforcement/medical examiner processes.[^3]

## Why He Appears in “Suspicious Death” Lists

Churkin is sometimes grouped—especially in media and online commentary—with other Russian diplomats or officials who died unexpectedly around the same period. These lists typically:

- Note the clustering in time as a point of curiosity, while
- Providing limited publicly verifiable evidence of foul play in any specific case.

This article records the commonly cited framing ("died suddenly" / "mysterious" in some commentary) while prioritizing established facts and mainstream reporting on cause and circumstances.
