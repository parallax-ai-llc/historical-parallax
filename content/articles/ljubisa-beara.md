## Summary

Bosnian Serb military security chief convicted of genocide for his central role in planning and executing the Srebrenica massacre. He directed the buses that transported men to execution sites.

## Background

Ljubisa Beara was a Bosnian Serb military officer who served as a security chief within the Army of Republika Srpska during the Bosnian War. He is most closely associated with the events surrounding the July 1995 Srebrenica massacre.

## Legal proceedings

- Beara was prosecuted at the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY).
- The case is often discussed as part of the broader body of international jurisprudence on genocide, command responsibility, and organized mass violence.

## Historical context

Srebrenica is recognized internationally as a genocide. Beara’s conviction is cited in historical and legal accounts that examine how military and police structures organized detention, transport, and executions.
