## Summary

Aaron Montgomery Ward founded the first mail-order business in the United States in 1872, revolutionizing retail commerce. His catalog, known as the 'Great Wish Book,' brought consumer goods to rural Americans who had limited access to stores. The concept of direct-to-consumer selling through catalogs transformed American retail. He also fought to preserve Chicago's lakefront as public space.
