## Summary

Leonardo Notarbartolo led a team that broke into the Antwerp Diamond Centre vault in 2003, stealing diamonds and other valuables worth over $100 million. It was called the heist of the century.

## The 2003 Antwerp Diamond Heist

The Antwerp Diamond Centre burglary is often described as a highly planned, non-violent vault break-in. The group exploited access to the building and defeated multiple layers of physical security, including locks, motion/heat detection, and other alarm mechanisms, to reach a large number of safety deposit boxes.

## Investigation and Arrest

A key narrative element in public accounts of the case is how seemingly minor mistakes can unravel sophisticated operations. Evidence recovered from discarded materials (including items containing DNA) contributed to identifying and prosecuting members of the group, including Leonardo Notarbartolo.

## Scale and Aftermath

Estimates of the stolen value vary, and a significant portion of the diamonds and valuables were never recovered. The case remains a reference point in security and criminology discussions about "defense in depth" and how attackers adapt to layered safeguards.

## Broader Context

Antwerp is one of the world's major diamond trading hubs, and the heist drew attention to both the concentration of high-value goods and the specialized expertise required to secure such environments.
