## Summary

Abu Bakr al-Siddiq was the first Rashidun (rightly-guided) Caliph and the closest companion of Prophet Muhammad. He was the first free adult male to accept Islam and accompanied Muhammad on the Hijra. As caliph, he suppressed the Ridda (apostasy) wars and began the expansion of Islam beyond Arabia. He compiled the first written collection of the Quran.
