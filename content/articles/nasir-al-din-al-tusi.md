## Summary

Nasir al-Din al-Tusi was a Persian polymath who made important contributions to astronomy, mathematics, and philosophy. He built the Maragheh observatory, one of the most advanced of its time, and his astronomical models influenced Copernicus. He wrote the first major work on trigonometry as an independent mathematical discipline. His philosophical and ethical writings were equally influential in the Islamic world.
