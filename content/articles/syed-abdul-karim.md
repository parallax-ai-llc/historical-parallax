## Summary

Pakistani terrorist who was one of the five attackers in the 2001 Indian Parliament attack. He was killed by security forces during the attack.

## Additional Context

### Background

Open sources describe Syed Abdul Karim as a Pakistani militant who took part in a high-profile attack in India in late 2001. Publicly available reporting on his early life is limited, and most biographical details in English-language sources focus on the events of the attack itself.

### 2001 Indian Parliament attack (overview)

The attack on the Parliament complex in New Delhi on 13 December 2001 involved a small team that attempted to breach security and reach the grounds of the legislature. Security personnel engaged the attackers, and all members of the attacking team were killed during the confrontation. The incident triggered a major security response and heightened tensions between India and Pakistan.

### Aftermath and significance

Because the target was India’s legislature, the incident became a defining reference point in discussions of domestic security, counterterrorism policy, and the protection of high-value government sites. It is also frequently cited in accounts of the 2001–2002 period of regional crisis and military mobilization.
