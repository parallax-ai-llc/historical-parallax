## Summary

Lee Seung-gi was a Korean chemist who invented Vinalon, one of the first synthetic fibers developed outside the West. He synthesized the fiber from polyvinyl alcohol in 1939 while working at Kyoto Imperial University. After the Korean War, he went to North Korea where Vinalon became the national fiber. His achievement demonstrated Korean capability in advanced chemistry.
