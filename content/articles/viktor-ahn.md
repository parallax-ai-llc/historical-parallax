## Summary

Viktor Ahn won gold for two countries — three for South Korea (2006) and three for Russia (2014).

## Background

Ahn is a short track speed skater whose career is notable for spanning **two national teams** at the Olympic level. In short track, where races are tactical and contact is common, sustained success typically requires both elite speed and consistent decision-making under pressure.

## Notability: Competing for Two Countries

His medal record is often summarized as a rare example of an athlete who:

- reached the top tier early,
- later re-established himself after a national-team change,
- and won again at the highest stage.

This makes his story frequently cited in discussions about national selection systems, athlete development, and the personal costs of elite sport bureaucracy.

## Events and Skills (High Level)

Short track is contested across multiple distances and relays. Top athletes tend to combine:

- explosive acceleration out of corners,
- pack awareness and positioning,
- and the ability to avoid penalties while staying aggressive.

## Legacy

In Olympic-era short track summaries, Ahn is frequently listed among the most decorated athletes in the discipline.
