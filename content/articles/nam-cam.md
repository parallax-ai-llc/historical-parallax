## Summary

Vietnamese crime boss who ran Ho Chi Minh City's largest criminal empire with police protection. Trial exposed massive government corruption.

## Criminal Network and Influence

- Nam Cam is commonly described as having operated gambling, protection rackets, and other organized-crime activities in Ho Chi Minh City.
- The case is frequently cited for illustrating how criminal organizations can leverage relationships with officials to reduce enforcement pressure.

## Arrest, Trial, and Political Significance

- The investigation reportedly involved a broad sweep of associates and implicated multiple officials.
- Public attention focused on institutional corruption as much as on Nam Cam's direct crimes.
- The trial is often referenced in Vietnam as a prominent anti-corruption and organized-crime prosecution.

## Aftermath

- The case contributed to public narratives about state capacity, accountability, and the risks of criminal capture of law-enforcement institutions.
- It remains a notable example in regional discussions of organized crime and governance.

## Broader Context

- Organized-crime crackdowns often reveal networks that are partly social (patronage) as well as economic (cashflow).
- High-profile trials can serve both a law-enforcement purpose and a political signaling purpose.

- Such cases are frequently used in comparative criminology to discuss state capture and anti-corruption enforcement.
