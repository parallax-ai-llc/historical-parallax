## Summary

Xu Fu was a Chinese court sorcerer sent by Emperor Qin Shi Huang to find the elixir of immortality. He led two large expeditions to the eastern seas and according to legend, never returned, possibly settling in Japan.

## Historical Context

Xu Fu (also rendered as Hsu Fu) is best known from accounts associated with the late Qin dynasty and Emperor Qin Shi Huang’s well-known interest in immortality practices. In later retellings, he is portrayed as a *fangshi*—a type of court specialist linked to techniques such as alchemy, ritual, and divination.

## Sources and Historicity

Most details about Xu Fu are filtered through later historical compilation and legend. As a result, modern summaries often distinguish between:

- **A probable historical envoy** sent on maritime missions in search of rare medicines.
- **Legendary embellishment** that grows over time, especially around the scale of the expeditions and the destination.

## Legends and Cultural Impact

In East Asian popular history, Xu Fu is frequently connected with stories of travel to islands in the east. One persistent tradition links him to early Japan, where some localities maintain shrines, monuments, or place-name associations that treat Xu Fu as a culture-bringing figure.

These traditions are not uniformly accepted as literal history, but they have had lasting influence in:

- Local folklore and tourism narratives
- Cultural exchange stories between China and Japan
- Modern media depictions of Qin-era quests

## Common Themes in Retellings

Writings about Xu Fu tend to emphasize a few recurring themes:

1. **Imperial patronage and anxiety**: a ruler funding extraordinary searches for longevity.
2. **Maritime exploration**: voyages framed as both political missions and mythic journeys.
3. **Ambiguous ending**: disappearance that enables both historical speculation and legend.
