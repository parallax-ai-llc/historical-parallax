## Background

- The case is one of Austria’s most widely reported kidnappings, involving the abduction of Natascha Kampusch in 1998 and her escape in 2006.
- The perpetrator most commonly identified in reporting is Wolfgang Přiklopil, who kept Kampusch hidden from authorities for years.

## Key Facts (high level)

- **Victim:** Natascha Kampusch (born 1988).
- **Abduction:** Taken on her way to school in Vienna in 1998.
- **Duration:** Held for more than 8 years.
- **Escape:** Escaped in August 2006.
- **Outcome for captor:** Přiklopil died by suicide shortly after her escape.

## Captivity and Discovery

- Public sources describe confinement primarily within a concealed cellar space on the captor’s property.
- The long duration without discovery prompted significant public scrutiny of investigative steps taken during the early years after the abduction.

## Aftermath and Public Impact

- The case sparked debate in Austria about missing-person investigations, media coverage, and victim privacy.
- Kampusch later became a public figure through interviews and public statements about the experience and its aftermath.

## Media and Further Reading

- Kampusch’s story has been widely covered internationally and adapted into books and film.
- One commonly cited work is her memoir *3096 Days* (title refers to the number of days in captivity), which also inspired a film adaptation.
