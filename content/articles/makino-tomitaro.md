## Summary

Makino Tomitaro was Japan's greatest botanist, known as the 'father of Japanese botany.' A self-taught genius, he named and classified over 1,500 new plant species and varieties. He published the Makino Illustrated Flora of Japan, the definitive reference for Japanese plant taxonomy. Despite poverty and lack of formal education, his contributions were immense.
