## Summary

Wayde van Niekerk set the 400m WR of 43.03s at the 2016 Rio Olympics. He was coached by 74-year-old great-grandmother Ans Botha.

## Athletic background

Wayde van Niekerk emerged as a leading South African sprinter in the 200m and 400m, with the 400m becoming his signature event. His rise coincided with a period in which the one-lap sprint was becoming increasingly competitive globally.

## 2016 Olympic world record

At the Rio 2016 Olympics, van Niekerk ran 43.03 seconds in the men's 400m final, a time recognized as the world record. The race is often highlighted because:

- The final included multiple elite 400m runners, making it one of the deepest fields in recent Olympic history.
- Van Niekerk won from an outside lane, which can be tactically challenging in a 400m final.
- The performance combined high early speed with sustained form through the final straight.

## Coaching and training

He is closely associated with coach Ans Botha, whose methods and experience were frequently noted in media coverage. Their partnership is often cited as an example of individualized coaching and athlete trust over many seasons.

## Injury and comeback attempts

A serious knee injury in 2017 disrupted his competition schedule. Subsequent seasons involved rehabilitation and attempts to return to peak form, illustrating the difficulty of sustaining elite sprint performance after major injury.

## Significance

Van Niekerk is commonly referenced as one of the defining 400m athletes of his era, with the Rio world record serving as a benchmark for the event.
