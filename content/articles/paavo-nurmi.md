## Summary

Paavo Nurmi, the 'Flying Finn,' won nine Olympic gold medals and set 22 world records. His scientific training approach revolutionized distance running. At the 1924 Paris Olympics, he won five gold medals.

## Why He Matters

Nurmi became a symbol of Finnish athletic excellence and a defining figure in the modernization of endurance sports. Beyond medals, he is remembered for bringing a disciplined, time-and-pace focused approach that influenced how distance running is trained and raced.

## Style and Approach

- **Pacing as strategy**: He was famous for controlling effort using split times, often described as running "against the clock" as much as against competitors.
- **Training discipline**: Accounts of his preparation emphasize consistency, planning, and repeatable routines rather than improvisation.
- **Legacy in coaching**: Later generations of coaches and runners adopted more systematic methods where workouts are measured and progress is tracked.

## Broader Impact

- His Olympic performances helped popularize long-distance events internationally.
- In Finland, his success contributed to the country’s sporting identity during the early 20th century.
- He remains one of the most decorated athletes in Olympic history, frequently referenced in discussions of all-time great distance runners.
