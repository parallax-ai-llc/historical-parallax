## Summary

Russian spy who was part of the Illegals Program, a network of Russian sleeper agents in the US exposed in 2010. She posed as a New York businesswoman. Deported in a prisoner exchange and became a celebrity in Russia.

## Illegals Program case (2010)

Chapman became publicly known after U.S. authorities dismantled a network of Russian "illegal" (non-official cover) intelligence operatives in 2010, a case often referred to as the **Illegals Program**.

### Arrest and resolution

- Arrested by the FBI as part of a broader counterintelligence operation.
- The case concluded with guilty pleas by the defendants to lesser charges and deportation from the United States.

### Prisoner exchange

Chapman was deported to Russia in a high-profile spy swap that also involved individuals associated with Western intelligence.

## Public profile after deportation

After returning to Russia, she became a media figure and public personality, with coverage often focusing on the unusual transition from clandestine activity to public celebrity.

## Notes

The broader FBI investigation that led to the 2010 arrests is widely reported under the name **Operation Ghost Stories**.
