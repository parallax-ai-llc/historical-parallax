## Summary

FBI program that surveilled, infiltrated, and disrupted civil rights and political organizations. Targeted MLK Jr., Black Panthers, and antiwar groups through illegal wiretaps and provocations.

## Program Scope

COINTELPRO (Counter Intelligence Program) encompassed multiple FBI initiatives aimed at monitoring and disrupting domestic political movements. Targets included civil rights organizations, Black liberation groups, anti‑war activists, socialist and communist organizations, and other groups the Bureau considered subversive.

## Tactics Attributed to COINTELPRO

High‑level descriptions of COINTELPRO activities include:

- Infiltration and use of informants to gather intelligence and influence internal dynamics.
- Psychological operations such as anonymous letters and disinformation intended to sow distrust.
- Pressure campaigns involving employers, landlords, and media contacts.
- Use (and alleged misuse) of surveillance authorities, including wiretaps.

## Exposure and Investigations

The program became widely known after activists burglarized an FBI office and leaked documents. Subsequent inquiries and public debate focused on the legality of domestic intelligence operations, constitutional protections, and the need for durable oversight of law enforcement and intelligence agencies.

## Legacy

COINTELPRO remains a frequently cited historical precedent in debates about state surveillance, political policing, and the risk of mission creep during periods of social unrest.
