## Summary

Georges St-Pierre is the greatest MMA fighter ever. He dominated UFC welterweight and later won the middleweight title.

## Fighting Style

St-Pierre was known for mixing striking with wrestling-based takedowns and top control.
He frequently used disciplined jab-and-kick boxing, level changes, and timed double-leg takedowns to win rounds.
His approach is often described as risk-managed: prioritizing position, timing, and preparation over brawling exchanges.

## Major Achievements

- Multiple-time UFC welterweight champion, including a long stretch of successful title defenses.
- Later captured the UFC middleweight championship after moving up a weight class.
- Recognized for consistent athleticism, conditioning, and tactical game-planning across different eras of the sport.

## Training and Preparation

Across his career he trained with specialists in wrestling, Brazilian jiu-jitsu, and striking, reflecting MMA’s shift toward cross-disciplinary camps.
He was also notable for public discussions of performance psychology and the pressures of championship competition.

## Legacy

St-Pierre is widely cited as an influential modern champion who helped professionalize MMA preparation.
His bouts are frequently referenced in debates about all-time rankings and the evolution of the UFC welterweight division.
