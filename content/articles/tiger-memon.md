## Summary

Indian gangster and key conspirator in the 1993 Bombay bombings. He organized the logistics of the attacks under Dawood Ibrahim's orders. He has been a fugitive for over 30 years, believed to be hiding in Pakistan.

## Background and Context

The 1993 Bombay bombings were a coordinated series of attacks in Mumbai that killed and injured large numbers of civilians and became one of India’s most significant terrorism cases of the 1990s. The investigation and subsequent prosecutions produced extensive court records and long-running appeals, and the case is frequently referenced in discussions of organized crime networks and militant financing in South Asia.

## Allegations and Legal Status

Indian authorities have alleged that Memon played a logistical role in the conspiracy, including arranging support and coordination for the attackers. Public reporting commonly describes him as a fugitive and places his suspected location outside India, though definitive, publicly verified details about his whereabouts are limited.

## Significance

The case remains notable for how it connected criminal syndicates, cross-border movement, and political fallout. It also illustrates the challenges of pursuing suspects who are believed to be outside a state’s jurisdiction, and the reliance on extradition, intelligence cooperation, and international pressure in such investigations.

## Related Figures and Networks

Many summaries of the 1993 bombings reference a broader underworld milieu in Mumbai and alleged links to transnational organized crime. In particular, Dawood Ibrahim and the “D-Company” network are frequently mentioned in reporting and court-related discussions as part of the background to the conspiracy.
