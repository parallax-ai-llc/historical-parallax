## Summary

Former president of Myanmar who served as Aung San Suu Kyi's proxy, as she was constitutionally barred from the presidency. He was sidelined during the Rohingya crisis.

## Political Context (High Level)

- Myanmar’s 2008 constitution included eligibility rules that prevented certain individuals from becoming president.
- In the mid-2010s, the National League for Democracy (NLD) won a major electoral mandate, and Aung San Suu Kyi became the central political figure of the civilian government.

## Presidency

- Htin Kyaw became president in 2016, serving during a period of transition in Myanmar’s political system.
- He is frequently characterized as a close ally of Aung San Suu Kyi and as a consensus figure for the role.
- The civilian government operated alongside continued military influence in key areas of state power.

## Governance and Public Scrutiny

- His term overlapped with major domestic and international attention on Myanmar’s conflicts and humanitarian issues.
- Coverage often focused on how decision-making authority was distributed among the presidency, the NLD leadership, and military institutions.

## Resignation

- Htin Kyaw resigned in 2018, with reporting commonly attributing the decision to health reasons.
- Leadership transition thereafter maintained continuity within the NLD-led civilian administration.
