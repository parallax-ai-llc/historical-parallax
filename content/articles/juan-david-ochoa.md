## Summary

Colombian drug lord and founding member of the Medellin Cartel along with his brothers Fabio and Jorge. He helped build one of the largest cocaine networks in history.

## Additional Context

### Role in the Medellín Cartel

Juan David Ochoa Vásquez is most often described as one of the **founders** of the Medellín Cartel, alongside other major figures from Medellín. Open sources consistently place him as the **eldest** of the Ochoa brothers (Juan David, Jorge Luis, and Fabio), who were influential in the cartel’s rise during the cocaine boom.

### Surrender and Legal Outcome

Public reporting notes that in **January 1991** he and his brother **Jorge Luis** surrendered to the Colombian government as part of a negotiated process intended to secure judicial benefits in exchange for cooperation. Accounts describe him serving time in a **medium-security facility** outside Medellín and being **released around January 1996** following a plea-bargain style arrangement.

### Death

Open sources report that Ochoa Vásquez died on **25 July 2013**, with cause of death described as a **heart attack**, at age 67.

### Notes on Biographical Details

Some sources differ on exact biographical specifics (for example, the precise birth date). When compiling timelines, it is common to treat the most consistently repeated dates as anchors while noting that secondary details may vary across references.

### Portrayal in Media

He has been portrayed in dramatizations related to Pablo Escobar-era narratives; for example, the 2012 TV series *Pablo Escobar, The Drug Lord* included a character based on him.
