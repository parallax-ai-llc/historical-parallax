## Summary

David Rudisha set the 800m world record of 1:40.91 at the 2012 London Olympics in what was called the greatest 800m race ever.

## Athletic Profile

Rudisha is known for front-running tactics in the 800 metres, combining high cruising speed with the ability to sustain pace through the final bend. His 2012 Olympic final is often highlighted for both its record time and its unusually fast overall field, with multiple athletes producing personal bests.

## Training and Background

He emerged from Kenya’s strong middle-distance running tradition, which blends altitude training, high training volumes, and competitive domestic circuits. Rudisha’s development was also shaped by structured coaching and a focus on pacing discipline—critical in an event that sits between sprint and endurance demands.

## Major Achievements (High Level)

- Olympic champion in the 800m (London 2012).
- World champion titles in the 800m.
- Holder of the 800m world record (1:40.91).

## Significance

Rudisha’s world record is widely viewed as one of modern athletics’ landmark performances because it was achieved in a championship final without pacemakers. It became a benchmark for how tactical excellence and sustained speed can align at the highest level.

## Records and Race Craft

In elite 800m racing, athletes must manage positioning, pace surges, and lane breaks; Rudisha’s approach emphasized:

- committing early to a fast pace to avoid traffic;
- maintaining form through the second lap as fatigue rises;
- reducing the opportunity for late-race jostling.
