## Summary

Top executives at FTX and Alameda Research including Caroline Ellison and Gary Wang pleaded guilty to fraud and cooperated against Sam Bankman-Fried. They helped uncover $8 billion in missing customer funds.

## Who "Inner Circle" Refers To

- In reporting and later court proceedings, the "inner circle" typically referred to senior executives and close collaborators across **FTX** and **Alameda Research**.
- Prominent figures repeatedly discussed in public coverage included:
  - Caroline Ellison (Alameda)
  - Gary Wang (FTX co-founder)
  - Nishad Singh (FTX engineering)

## Role in the Criminal Case

- Multiple members of the inner circle entered guilty pleas and provided cooperation to U.S. prosecutors.
- Their testimony and cooperation helped establish a narrative that customer assets were diverted and that internal controls were weak or overridden.

## What the Cooperation Was Used To Prove (High-Level)

- How funds moved between entities (FTX ↔ Alameda) and how risk/overdraft mechanisms operated.
- What senior leadership allegedly knew, approved, or directed regarding customer funds and public representations.

## Broader Significance

- The inner-circle cooperation became central to explaining the collapse of a major crypto exchange and the alleged fraud mechanisms.
- The case contributed to increased regulatory and compliance pressure on crypto exchanges, custody practices, and audit/control expectations.
