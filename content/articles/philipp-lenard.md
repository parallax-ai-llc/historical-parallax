## Summary

Philipp Lenard was a Hungarian-German physicist who won the 1905 Nobel Prize in Physics for his research on cathode rays. His experiments showed that cathode rays could pass through thin metal foils, contributing to understanding atomic structure. He later became a vocal supporter of Nazi ideology and promoted "Deutsche Physik."
