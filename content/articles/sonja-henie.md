## Summary

Sonja Henie won three consecutive Olympic golds (1928, 1932, 1936) and ten World titles, then became a Hollywood star.

## Competitive dominance

Henie’s streak of Olympic and World Championship victories made her one of the most dominant athletes in early 20th-century sport. She combined technical skating with showmanship and helped shift women’s figure skating toward more theatrical presentation and athletic jumps.

## Influence on the sport

She is often credited with popularizing short skating skirts and a balletic style that influenced costume and choreography conventions. Her approach helped broaden figure skating’s appeal beyond competitive arenas and into mass entertainment.

## Film and touring career

After the 1936 Olympics, Henie transitioned to professional skating and became a film star in the United States. She appeared in multiple Hollywood productions and staged large-scale ice shows, turning figure skating into a commercially successful touring spectacle.

## Legacy

Henie’s impact is visible both in the evolution of women’s figure skating and in the later development of ice shows as a distinct entertainment industry. She remains a prominent historical reference point when discussing the intersection of elite sport, celebrity, and commercial performance.

## Key points

- Three-time Olympic champion in women’s singles (1928, 1932, 1936).
- Ten-time World Champion, setting a standard for sustained dominance.
- Helped shape modern presentation and the professional ice show model.
