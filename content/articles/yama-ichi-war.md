## Summary

Yakuza war between the Yamaguchi-gumi and breakaway Ichiwa-kai that lasted over four years. 25 people were killed and over 70 injured in gang violence across Japan.

## Background

- **Organized-crime landscape:** The conflict is described as an internal split within Japan's largest yakuza organization, the **Yamaguchi-gumi**.
- **Triggering event:** Accounts connect the outbreak to leadership succession disputes and an assassination that escalated retaliation cycles.

## Pattern of Violence (high level)

- **Duration:** The war spanned more than four years, involving repeated attacks and counterattacks.
- **Casualties:** Sources commonly cite **25 killed** and **70+ injured**, with incidents occurring across multiple prefectures.

## State Response

- **Police pressure:** Japanese authorities increased surveillance and enforcement efforts against major syndicates during the period.
- **Public order impact:** The conflict raised public concern due to shootings and bombings occurring outside underworld-only settings.

## Resolution

- **Dissolution:** The Ichiwa-kai ultimately disbanded in 1989, ending the conflict.

## Significance

- The Yama-Ichi War is often referenced as a turning point that intensified public and police focus on large-scale yakuza violence.
