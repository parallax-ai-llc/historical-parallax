## Summary

Cha Du-ri, son of Cha Bum-kun, played in the Bundesliga and K-League and represented South Korea at the 2010 World Cup.

## Playing style and positions

Cha Du-ri is commonly described as a versatile wide player, used as a **right back / fullback** and also in more advanced wide roles earlier in his career.[^1] He was noted for pace and direct running, which fit both club play and national-team transition styles.

## Club career overview

A large portion of Cha’s professional career was spent in Germany, alongside later periods in the K League and abroad.[^1] High-level summaries typically highlight:

- experience with multiple Bundesliga and German clubs,
- later domestic success in South Korea,
- a career that spanned both European and Asian leagues.

## International career

Cha represented **South Korea** internationally and appeared in two FIFA World Cups (2002 and 2010).[^1] He is also frequently referenced in Korean football media because of his family connection: he is the son of national-team legend **Cha Bum-kun**.[^1]

## After playing

Following his playing career, Cha moved into football work such as coaching and team staff roles.[^1] This path is common for former internationals, combining on-field experience with player development and tactical preparation.

## Notable themes

Cha’s career is often summarized as a blend of European club experience and long national-team service, with a public profile shaped by both performance and his well-known football family background.[^1]
