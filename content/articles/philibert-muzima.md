## Summary

Former Rwandan official convicted of genocide in Belgium for his role in organizing massacres in Kigali during the 1994 genocide. His trial was part of Belgium's universal jurisdiction cases.

## Context

- The 1994 genocide against the Tutsi involved coordinated mass violence across Rwanda, including killings in and around Kigali.
- In a number of European cases, national courts relied on forms of **universal jurisdiction** to prosecute genocide and related crimes committed abroad.
- Belgium became one of the prominent venues for such prosecutions because of its legal framework and investigative capacity for international crimes.

## Allegations and Case Significance

- The case centered on allegations that Muzima used an official position and local networks to help **identify targets**, **mobilize perpetrators**, and facilitate attacks during the genocide.
- Proceedings in Belgium were significant because they demonstrated that genocide cases could be pursued outside Rwanda, complementing international tribunals and domestic prosecutions.
- Like many genocide-era cases, the trial record depended on witness testimony about events that took place amid widespread displacement and fear, making credibility assessment and corroboration central issues.
