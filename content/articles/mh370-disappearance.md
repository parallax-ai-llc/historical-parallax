## Background

Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 (MH370) was a scheduled passenger flight from Kuala Lumpur to Beijing. It was operated by a Boeing 777-200ER carrying 239 passengers and crew.

## Disappearance (High-Level)

After takeoff on 8 March 2014, the aircraft lost contact with air traffic control and deviated from its planned route. Satellite-based analyses later suggested the aircraft continued flying for hours before ending in the southern Indian Ocean. The exact sequence of events and cause of the disappearance remain unconfirmed.

## Search and Investigation

MH370 prompted one of the largest and most expensive search operations in aviation history, involving multiple countries and the use of satellite communications data, ocean drift analysis, and seabed mapping. Despite the search, the main wreckage has not been conclusively located, and only a limited number of debris pieces have been reported to have washed ashore in the years after the disappearance.

## Aviation and Policy Impact

The case accelerated efforts to improve aircraft tracking, flight data transmission, and emergency location capabilities. It also highlighted challenges in cross-border coordination and the limits of existing surveillance over remote oceanic regions.

## Key Points

- 239 people were aboard; there were no confirmed survivors.
- The aircraft's deviation and loss of communications remain central unresolved facts.
- Investigative conclusions rely heavily on satellite communications and inferential modeling rather than direct recovery of flight recorders.
