## Summary

Pakistani Taliban leader known as 'Radio Mullah' who ordered the shooting of Malala Yousafzai.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2012 | Ordered attack on Malala Yousafzai |
| 2013 | Became emir of Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan (TTP) after the death of Hakimullah Mehsud. |
| 2009 | Pakistan military operations in Swat led to his displacement from the Swat Valley. |
| 2018 | Killed in a U.S. drone strike in Afghanistan (reported in Kunar Province). |

## Background

- Fazlullah (born 1974) emerged as a militant cleric associated with the Pakistani Taliban movement.
- He was widely known by the nickname **"Radio Mullah"** due to the use of FM radio broadcasts to spread messages and mobilize supporters.

## Role in the Swat Insurgency

- He became a prominent figure in the Taliban-aligned insurgency in Pakistan's Swat Valley.
- Reporting and accounts commonly describe campaigns of intimidation and coercion in areas under militant influence, including opposition to girls' education.

## Leadership of Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan (TTP)

- After 2013, he is described as leading the TTP, an umbrella organization for several Pakistani Taliban factions.
- His tenure is frequently linked in reporting to continued militant attacks and the group's broader anti-state campaign.

## Notable Allegations and Widely Reported Actions

- Ordered the shooting of Malala Yousafzai (2012), which drew global condemnation and intensified international scrutiny of the TTP.
- Associated with hardline messaging and public intimidation through broadcasts and statements.

## Death

- Fazlullah was reported killed on **2018-06-14** in a U.S. drone strike in Afghanistan.
- Contemporary reporting framed the strike as part of U.S. counterterrorism operations targeting militant leadership.

## Legacy and Historical Significance

- He remains a notable figure in modern Pakistani militant history due to:
  - His role in the Swat Valley insurgency.
  - His leadership of the TTP during a period of sustained militancy.
  - The international attention triggered by the Malala attack.
