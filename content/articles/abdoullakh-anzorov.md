## Summary

Chechen-born teenager who beheaded French teacher Samuel Paty for showing Charlie Hebdo cartoons to students. He was shot dead by police minutes after the murder.

## Background

- Born in 2002 and raised in Russia, he was of Chechen origin.
- He later lived in France with his family and attended school there.
- Contemporary reporting described him as having been exposed to extremist propaganda online prior to the attack.

## Attack on Samuel Paty (2020)

- On 16 October 2020, Anzorov murdered Samuel Paty, a teacher in Conflans-Sainte-Honorine, near Paris.
- The attack followed an online campaign targeting Paty after he used Charlie Hebdo cartoons in a civics lesson on freedom of expression.
- French police shot and killed Anzorov shortly after the attack, following a confrontation.

## Investigation and aftermath

- French authorities treated the killing as a jihadist terrorist attack and opened a terrorism investigation.
- Multiple people were arrested and investigated in connection with alleged assistance, encouragement, or online incitement related to the crime.
- The case became a major flashpoint in French debates about secularism (laïcité), free speech, online radicalization, and the protection of teachers.

## Context

- The killing triggered nationwide commemorations and official ceremonies in France, including a tribute to Samuel Paty.
- French leaders framed the attack as an assault on the values of the French Republic and on education.
- The event also intensified scrutiny of how social media platforms can amplify harassment campaigns and extremist content.
