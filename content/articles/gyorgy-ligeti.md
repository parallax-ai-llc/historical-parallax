## Summary

Gyorgy Ligeti was a Hungarian-Austrian composer whose innovative works explore micropolyphony, tone clusters, and new timbral possibilities. His Atmospheres, famously used in 2001: A Space Odyssey, and his opera Le Grand Macabre are landmarks of 20th-century music. His late works, including the Etudes for Piano, are considered masterpieces.
