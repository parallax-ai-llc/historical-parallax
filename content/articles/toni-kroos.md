## Summary

Toni Kroos is a German professional footballer widely regarded as one of the greatest central midfielders of his generation. Born on January 4, 1990, in Greifswald, East Germany, he built a career defined by exceptional technical precision, vision, and passing ability. His time at Bayern Munich and Real Madrid placed him at the center of the most dominant club teams in European football during the 2010s.

Kroos's influence on the game extends beyond his individual brilliance. He was instrumental in Germany's 2014 FIFA World Cup victory and won five UEFA Champions League titles with Real Madrid. His metronomic consistency and ability to control tempo made him a foundational piece of some of the most successful squads in modern football history.

---

## Early Life & Background

Kroos was born in Greifswald, a city in the former East Germany, and grew up in Graal-Müritz. His father, Roland Kroos, was a football coach, which gave Toni early exposure to the game. He joined the Hansa Rostock youth academy before being recruited by Bayern Munich's academy at age 14 in 2004. His younger brother Felix Kroos also pursued a professional football career, playing in the German lower divisions.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Kroos made his professional debut for Bayern Munich in 2007 and was loaned to Bayer Leverkusen from 2009 to 2010, where he established himself as a first-team midfielder. He returned to Bayern and became a key figure under Jupp Heynckes, winning the treble — Bundesliga, DFB-Pokal, and UEFA Champions League — in the 2012–13 season.

In July 2014, days after the World Cup final, Kroos signed with Real Madrid for approximately €25 million. At Madrid, he became a cornerstone of the team under Zinedine Zidane, contributing to three consecutive Champions League titles from 2016 to 2018.

---

## Key Achievements

- **5× UEFA Champions League winner** (2013 with Bayern Munich; 2016, 2017, 2018, 2024 with Real Madrid)
- **FIFA World Cup winner** with Germany in 2014, winning the Golden Boot equivalent for midfielders
- **4× La Liga champion** with Real Madrid
- **3× Bundesliga champion** with Bayern Munich
- Named to the UEFA Champions League Squad of the Season multiple times
- Announced international retirement in 2021 before returning for Euro 2024, where Germany reached the quarter-finals
- Retired from professional football in June 2024 following Real Madrid's Champions League triumph

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Kroos was occasionally criticized for his perceived lack of defensive contribution and physical intensity compared to box-to-box midfielders. Critics noted that his style demanded teammates compensate defensively, which suited elite clubs but raised questions about adaptability. His 2021 podcast comments criticizing the German national team's management and direction were considered pointed but reflective of broader frustrations within German football following a string of disappointing tournament exits.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Kroos retired as one of the most decorated midfielders in football history. His passing accuracy — consistently above 90% across top-level competition — set a benchmark that influenced how the deep-lying playmaker role was evaluated. At Real Madrid, he formed part of a midfield alongside Luka Modrić and Casemiro that is considered among the finest in Champions League history. His return from international retirement for Euro 2024 demonstrated his enduring quality at age 34.
