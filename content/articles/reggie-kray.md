## The Kray twins and "The Firm"

Reggie Kray is primarily known as one half of the **Kray twins** (Reggie and Ronnie Kray), who became emblematic of a certain postwar London underworld image.

In many accounts, the twins’ power rested on a blend of:

- violence and intimidation,
- control of clubs and venues,
- a cultivated reputation that blurred crime, celebrity, and tabloid fascination.

The term **"The Firm"** is commonly used to describe their criminal organization and circle of associates.

## Crimes, conviction, and imprisonment

The summary timeline highlights the key public milestone: **conviction for murder in 1969**. For organized-crime figures, major convictions often reflect years of police surveillance and the eventual consolidation of witness testimony and corroborating evidence.

Kray-related histories frequently discuss the tension between:

- the twins’ public visibility and connections to nightlife,
- the private violence used to maintain underworld control,
- the state’s efforts to demonstrate that high-profile gang leaders could be prosecuted successfully.

## Cultural legacy

Reggie Kray (and the Kray twins more broadly) remained prominent in British popular culture after their convictions. Their story has been revisited in books, documentaries, and dramatizations, often as a study in:

- myth-making around gangsters,
- the relationship between media attention and criminal notoriety,
- the changing social geography of East London.
