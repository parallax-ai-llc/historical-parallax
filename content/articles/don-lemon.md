## Summary

Don Lemon is an American journalist and television anchor best known for his long tenure at CNN, where he anchored prime-time programming for over a decade. One of the few openly gay Black men to anchor a major American news program, Lemon became a prominent and often polarizing figure in cable news, known for his direct on-air style and willingness to inject personal perspective into breaking news coverage.

Throughout his career, Lemon has reported from the front lines of some of the most significant news events of the 21st century, including Hurricane Katrina, the Ferguson protests, and the January 6, 2021 Capitol riot. His visibility made him a symbol of diversity in broadcast journalism, even as his commentary style drew both loyal viewers and sharp critics.

---

## Early Life & Background

Donald Carlton Lemon was born on March 1, 1966, in Baton Rouge, Louisiana. He grew up in a working-class family in the South and was raised largely by his mother. He attended Louisiana State University before transferring to Brooklyn College, City University of New York, where he earned a Bachelor of Arts in broadcast journalism in 1996.

Lemon has spoken publicly about experiencing sexual abuse as a child, disclosing this on air in 2014 during coverage of the Bill Cosby allegations — a moment that drew widespread attention and reflected his willingness to share personal experience in a journalistic context.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Lemon began his broadcast career at local television stations, including WBR in Baton Rouge and WCAU in Philadelphia. He joined NBC News in the early 2000s, contributing to programs including the *Today* show and *NBC Nightly News*. In 2006, he moved to CNN, where he would spend the bulk of his career.

At CNN, Lemon anchored *CNN Tonight* and later *CNN This Morning*, co-anchored alongside Poppy Harlow and Kaitlan Collins. His coverage of major breaking news events — including the disappearance of Malaysia Airlines Flight MH370 in 2014 and the killing of George Floyd in 2020 — brought him national prominence.

---

## Key Achievements

- One of the first openly gay Black men to anchor a major American cable news program, coming out publicly in 2011 with his memoir *Transparent*
- Emmy Award-winning journalist with recognition for investigative and breaking news coverage
- Anchored CNN's New Year's Eve coverage for several years alongside various co-hosts
- Authored *Transparent* (2011), a memoir addressing his sexuality, race, and personal history

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Lemon was frequently criticized by conservatives for what they characterized as liberal bias in his commentary. He also drew controversy among progressives on several occasions, including a 2013 segment in which he offered on-air advice to Black Americans that many viewed as tone-deaf victim-blaming.

In April 2023, CNN terminated Lemon's contract abruptly following internal complaints about workplace behavior, particularly regarding conduct toward female colleagues. Lemon denied the specific allegations. He subsequently launched an independent interview show on X (formerly Twitter), interviewing figures including Elon Musk in 2024 — a venture that ended after a single episode when Musk declined to distribute the interview on his platform.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Despite a controversial final chapter at CNN, Lemon's career represents a meaningful milestone in the representation of LGBTQ+ individuals and Black journalists in mainstream American media. His two-decade run at major networks helped normalize diverse anchor representation in prime-time news.
