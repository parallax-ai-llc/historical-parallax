## Summary

John Gibbon invented the heart-lung machine, enabling the first successful open-heart surgery using cardiopulmonary bypass in 1953. His machine oxygenated and circulated blood during surgery, allowing surgeons to operate on a still, bloodless heart. This invention made modern cardiac surgery possible.
