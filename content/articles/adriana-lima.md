## Summary

Adriana Lima is a Brazilian supermodel widely regarded as one of the most successful and recognized figures in the fashion industry. Best known for her nearly two-decade association with Victoria's Secret, she became the brand's longest-running Angel, a distinction that cemented her status as an enduring icon of the modeling world. Her combination of striking looks, business acumen, and global appeal made her one of the highest-earning models of her generation.

Beyond the runway, Lima has served as a brand ambassador for major luxury and beauty companies, built a significant philanthropic profile, and emerged as a cultural ambassador for Brazil. Her career trajectory — from a modest upbringing in Salvador to the heights of international fashion — has made her an aspirational figure across Latin America and beyond.

## Early Life & Background

Adriana Lima was born on June 12, 1981, in Salvador, Bahia, Brazil. Raised by her mother after her father left the family when she was six, Lima grew up in modest circumstances. She has described a devout Catholic upbringing that shaped her personal values. At age 13, she entered the Ford Supermodel of the World competition, finishing as runner-up in the Brazilian national contest in 1996 and second globally the following year — a result that launched her professional path.

## Rise to Prominence

Lima moved to New York at 16 to pursue modeling full-time. She signed with Elite Model Management and quickly attracted attention from high-profile clients. Her breakthrough came in 1999 when she was cast as a Victoria's Secret Angel, a role she would hold continuously until 2018 — a span of 19 years. During that tenure she walked in every annual Victoria's Secret Fashion Show from 1999 through 2018, appearing in more shows than any other model in the brand's history.

She also became the face of Maybelline from 2003 to 2009 and has fronted campaigns for Versace, Prada, Giorgio Armani, and Louis Vuitton, among others. Her appearance on the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue cover in 2006 further broadened her mainstream visibility.

## Key Achievements

- Longest-running Victoria's Secret Angel (1999–2018), appearing in 19 consecutive annual fashion shows.
- Named the world's most valuable Victoria's Secret Angel in 2012, estimated at $2 billion in brand value to the company.
- Ranked among Forbes' highest-paid models multiple times; her annual earnings peaked at approximately $10.5 million in 2012.
- Appeared on the covers of Vogue, GQ, and Sports Illustrated across multiple markets.
- Recipient of the Brazilian Order of Cultural Merit for contributions to the country's international image.

## Controversies & Criticism

Lima's 2011 interview in which she described saving her virginity for marriage and her views on premarital relationships drew considerable public debate, with critics arguing the statements reinforced regressive attitudes. Her comments were widely circulated and generated both support from religious communities and sharp criticism from others. Additionally, like many Victoria's Secret Angels, she has faced scrutiny over the brand's historically narrow beauty standards and lack of inclusivity, though Lima herself has largely deflected these criticisms by pointing to her own origins as an underrepresented Afro-Brazilian woman in elite fashion spaces.

## Legacy & Influence

Lima's career reshaped expectations for Latin American models in global fashion, opening doors at a time when Brazilian talent was underrepresented in top-tier editorial and runway work. Her longevity — maintaining elite status across three decades — is unusual in an industry that typically cycles through faces quickly. She has become a reference point for aspiring models in Brazil and across Latin America. Since retiring from Victoria's Secret, she has continued modeling, expanded into acting, and remained a prominent philanthropic figure, supporting children's charities in Brazil and globally.
