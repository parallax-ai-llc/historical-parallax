## Background

- Also known by the nickname **"Marcola"** (Marcos Willians Herbas Camacho).
- Widely described as a leading figure within Brazil's prison-based criminal organization **Primeiro Comando da Capital (PCC)**.

## PCC and Prison Governance Context

- PCC emerged in the 1990s and became one of Brazil's most influential criminal groups, with a strong presence inside prisons.
- Leadership influence is often described as operating through prison networks, enabling coordination of actions both inside and outside custody.

## 2006 São Paulo Attacks (High-Level)

- In May 2006, a wave of coordinated attacks and prison unrest occurred across São Paulo state.
- The event is commonly referenced as a major demonstration of PCC's operational reach and its ability to trigger simultaneous incidents.
- Camacho is frequently cited in reporting and reference materials as an alleged organizer or key instigator while incarcerated.

## Legal Status

- Camacho has been held in Brazilian custody for decades and is repeatedly characterized as a high-profile prisoner.
- Sources often note transfers and security measures intended to limit communication with PCC networks.

## Significance

- The 2006 attacks are used in public-policy debates as a case study in the relationship between prison administration, organized crime, and public security.
- Camacho's notoriety is tied less to a public-facing political role and more to the alleged capacity to direct criminal activity through prison-based command structures.
