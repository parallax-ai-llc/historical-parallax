## Summary

Nicolai "device" Reedtz is a Danish professional Counter-Strike player widely regarded as one of the greatest AWPers and most consistent players in the history of the franchise. Born on September 8, 1995, he rose to prominence as the star player of Astralis, where he helped build one of the most dominant dynasties competitive CS:GO has ever seen.

Over a career spanning more than a decade, device accumulated four Major championships with Astralis, earned numerous MVP awards, and maintained an elite level of performance across different eras of the game. His combination of mechanical precision, intelligent positioning, and calm under pressure set a standard for the AWPer role that influenced an entire generation of players.

## Early Life & Background

Nicolai Reedtz was born on September 8, 1995, in Denmark. He developed an interest in competitive gaming at a young age, gravitating toward Counter-Strike as a teenager. He began competing in the Danish scene under various teams before catching the attention of top-tier organizations. His early career saw him playing under the alias "device" in smaller Danish lineups, where his raw talent with the AWP quickly became apparent.

## Rise to Prominence

Device first gained international recognition as a member of Team Dignitas and later Team SoloMid (TSM) around 2014–2015, where he played alongside Danish stars such as dupreeh, Xyp9x, and karrigan. The core of this roster would go on to form Astralis in January 2016, a move that proved transformative for competitive Counter-Strike.

Under the Astralis banner, device and his teammates developed a methodical, system-driven approach to CS:GO that prioritized preparation, utility usage, and disciplined teamplay. By 2018, Astralis had entered a period of unprecedented dominance, winning three Majors in the span of roughly a year — the FACEIT Major in September 2018, the IEM Katowice Major in March 2019, and the StarLadder Berlin Major in September 2019. Device was named MVP at the FACEIT Major and consistently posted elite statistics throughout this era.

In April 2021, device made the surprising decision to leave Astralis and join Ninjas in Pyjamas (NIP), citing a desire for a new challenge and personal reasons including a move to Sweden. His time at NIP was hampered by health issues and inconsistent team results. He eventually returned to Astralis in late 2022, seeking to recapture the magic of the earlier dynasty as the team transitioned to Counter-Strike 2.

## Key Achievements

- **4 Major championships** with Astralis (2017 ELEAGUE Atlanta, 2018 FACEIT London, 2019 IEM Katowice, 2019 StarLadder Berlin)
- **Multiple Major MVP awards**, cementing his status as the most decorated player in Major history
- **HLTV Top 20 player** for several consecutive years, including a #1 ranking in some listings
- **Intel Grand Slam Season 1** winner with Astralis in 2019
- Consistently ranked among the highest-rated AWPers in professional CS:GO across multiple years

## Controversies & Criticism

Device's move to NIP in 2021 drew criticism from some fans who viewed it as abandoning a winning project. His extended absences from competition due to health-related issues during his NIP tenure led to speculation and debate within the community about his commitment. Device addressed these concerns publicly, citing legitimate medical challenges. His return to Astralis was met with mixed reactions — some welcomed it as a homecoming, while others questioned whether the old magic could be recaptured.

## Legacy & Influence

Device's legacy is defined by longevity, consistency, and winning. He demonstrated that an AWPer could be both a star fragger and a team-oriented player, blending flashy highlights with reliable, system-friendly performances. His four Major titles place him in an elite tier alongside only a handful of players in Counter-Strike history. The Astralis era he helped build remains the benchmark for organizational excellence in CS esports, and his playstyle — calm, calculated, and devastatingly precise — continues to influence AWPers across the professional scene.
