## Akio Morita (primary subject) — high-level overview

Akio Morita (1921–1999) was a Japanese business leader who helped build Sony from a small post-war startup into a global consumer-electronics brand. He is commonly credited (with colleagues) for Sony’s international expansion and for pushing products that defined categories in portable audio and home electronics.

### Commonly cited contributions

- **Co-founding Sony** (originally Tokyo Tsushin Kogyo) with Masaru Ibuka after World War II
- **Globalization of a Japanese brand**: early focus on export markets, overseas subsidiaries, and global marketing
- **Consumer electronics leadership**: associated with Sony’s transistor radio era and later product lines such as the Walkman
- **Business writing**: co-authored the memoir/business book *Made in Japan* (widely cited in management and innovation discussions)
