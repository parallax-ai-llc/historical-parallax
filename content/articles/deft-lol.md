## Summary

Kim "Deft" Hyuk-kyu (born October 23, 1996) is a South Korean professional League of Legends player widely regarded as one of the greatest AD carries in the history of the game. Across a career spanning over a decade, Deft competed at the highest level in both the LCK and LPL, earning multiple domestic titles and establishing himself as a consistent force in international competition.

Deft's career reached its crowning moment at the 2022 World Championship, where he led DRX through an extraordinary lower-bracket run to claim the Summoner's Cup — fulfilling a dream that had eluded him for nearly a decade of professional play. His victory cemented his legacy as one of the most accomplished and resilient players the game has ever seen.

## Early Life & Background

Kim Hyuk-kyu was born on October 23, 1996, in South Korea. He developed a passion for competitive gaming at a young age and quickly rose through the Korean solo queue ranks in League of Legends. His mechanical talent on marksman champions attracted the attention of professional organizations while he was still a teenager.

## Rise to Prominence

Deft debuted professionally in 2013 with Samsung Blue, part of the Samsung Galaxy organization that dominated Korean League of Legends. In 2014, Samsung Blue reached the semifinals of the World Championship, and Deft established himself as one of the premier bot laners in the world. After Samsung's roster dispersed, Deft moved to China's LPL in 2015, joining Edward Gaming. He won the 2015 Mid-Season Invitational with EDG, defeating SKT T1 in a historic final. He spent three years in the LPL, claiming multiple domestic titles with EDG before returning to Korea in 2018.

Back in the LCK, Deft joined KT Rolster and later Hanwha Life Esports and DRX. Despite consistently strong individual performances, a World Championship title remained out of reach for years, fueling a narrative around Deft as one of the best players never to have won it all.

## Key Achievements

Deft's trophy case is extensive. He won the 2015 Mid-Season Invitational with EDG, multiple LPL titles (2015, 2016), and LCK titles with KT Rolster (2018 Spring). His defining achievement came at Worlds 2022, where DRX entered as the fourth seed from the LCK and fought through the play-in stage and bracket to defeat T1 3-2 in a thrilling five-game grand final. Deft was named Finals MVP, and the victory made him the oldest player to win a World Championship at age 26. He attended Worlds nine times across his career, a record that underscores his remarkable longevity.

## Controversies & Criticism

Deft's career has been largely free of major controversy. The primary criticism leveled at him over the years was his inability to win a World Championship despite playing on stacked rosters, with some analysts questioning his performance under pressure in key international matches. His 2022 Worlds victory effectively silenced that narrative. Some fans debated his decision to play in China during his prime years, arguing he might have achieved more staying in the LCK, though his LPL titles countered that criticism.

## Legacy & Influence

Deft is remembered as a model of perseverance and dedication. His career arc — from prodigy to veteran champion — resonated deeply with fans and fellow players alike. He is frequently cited alongside Uzi, Ruler, and Bang in discussions of the greatest AD carries of all time. His tearful celebration after winning Worlds 2022 became one of the most iconic moments in esports history, symbolizing the reward of years of persistence. Deft's influence extends beyond mechanics; he demonstrated that longevity and mental resilience are as important as raw skill in competitive gaming.
