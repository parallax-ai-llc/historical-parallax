## Summary

Criminal compounds in Myanmar, Cambodia, and Laos where tens of thousands of trafficking victims are forced to conduct online scams. Workers face torture and confinement.

## Overview

The term refers to large, guarded facilities—often described as “compounds”—where trafficked workers are forced to run industrial-scale online fraud. Reporting has linked many of the most prominent sites to border regions with limited state control, where criminal groups can operate with protection from local power brokers.

## How the Schemes Work (High-Level)

Workers are typically compelled to use scripted personas to build trust with targets online. Common scam types described in public reporting include:

- Romance and “relationship” scams
- Investment fraud (including crypto-related pitches)
- Fake customer service or e-commerce schemes
- Impersonation scams (banking, police, or platform support)

## Trafficking and Coercion

Victims have been recruited through deceptive job offers and then transported across borders. Once inside a compound, survivors and investigators have described confiscated passports, physical confinement, beatings, and “sales” or transfers between groups. The coercive labor conditions are central to why the phenomenon is often discussed as human trafficking rather than only cybercrime.

## Geography and Governance

Many accounts focus on areas along the Myanmar–Thailand frontier and other border zones in mainland Southeast Asia. These regions can include overlapping authorities (state forces, local militias, or ethnic armed organizations), which complicates enforcement and can enable protection rackets.

## International Response

UN agencies and non-governmental organizations have documented the scale of trafficking and the transnational nature of the networks involved. Responses discussed in public sources include:

- Cross-border rescues and repatriations
- Financial investigations and sanctions in some jurisdictions
- Law-enforcement operations targeting organizers, money flows, and facilitators

## Why It Matters

The scam-compound model combines cyber-enabled fraud with forced labor at scale. It has become a major driver of both victimization (people scammed online) and exploitation (people forced to perpetrate the scams), making it a multidimensional security and human-rights problem.
