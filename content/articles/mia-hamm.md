## Summary

Mia Hamm scored 158 international goals, a record for over a decade. She won two World Cups and two Olympic golds.

## Early Life and Development

Mariel Margaret "Mia" Hamm developed as an elite football (soccer) player in the United States and became a defining figure of the modern women's game. She played college soccer at the University of North Carolina, a major pipeline for U.S. national-team talent.

## Style of Play and Role

Hamm is widely associated with a fast, direct attacking style, combining pace, technical ability, and high work rate. She played primarily as a forward and was central to the U.S. team's transition from a developing program into a sustained international power.

## Major Achievements

- FIFA Women's World Cup champion (1991, 1999).
- Olympic gold medalist with the United States (1996, 2004).
- Held the women's international goalscoring record (158 goals) for many years, reflecting both longevity and consistent production.

## Legacy and Impact

Hamm's prominence helped expand mainstream visibility for women's soccer in the United States, particularly after the 1999 World Cup. She is often cited as an influence on subsequent generations of players and on the growth of professional and youth participation.

## After Playing

Following retirement, Hamm has been involved in soccer-related philanthropy and advocacy and has remained a public figure associated with U.S. Soccer and women's sports.
