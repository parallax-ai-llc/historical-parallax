## Summary

Endre Szemerédi is a Hungarian-American mathematician who proved Szemerédi's theorem, which states that any sufficiently dense subset of the natural numbers contains arbitrarily long arithmetic progressions. He has made fundamental contributions to combinatorics and theoretical computer science. He received the Abel Prize in 2012.
