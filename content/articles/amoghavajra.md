## Summary

Amoghavajra was an Indian-born Buddhist monk who became one of the most influential esoteric (Vajrayana) masters in Chinese Buddhism. He was one of the 'Three Great Masters of Esoteric Buddhism' in Tang Dynasty China. He translated over 100 texts and ritual manuals, establishing Vajrayana as a major force in Chinese Buddhism. He enjoyed imperial patronage and performed rituals that reportedly helped the Tang dynasty in military conflicts.
