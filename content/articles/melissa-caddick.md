## Summary

Australian financial adviser who defrauded clients of $25 million and disappeared, later found dead.

## Expanded Overview

### Background

- Melissa Caddick was an Australian financial adviser who operated through a Sydney-based practice and attracted clients through personal networks and community connections.[^1]
- She presented herself as managing investments on behalf of clients, typically with promises of steady or above-market returns.[^1]

### Alleged Fraud and Client Losses

- Authorities alleged that client money was misappropriated rather than invested as represented, with losses reported in the tens of millions of Australian dollars (commonly cited around A$23–25 million).[^1]
- The matter is frequently described as a Ponzi-style arrangement, where payments to earlier participants were funded from incoming client funds rather than legitimate investment gains.[^1]

### Regulatory Action and Disappearance

- In November 2020, the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC) and law enforcement executed a search warrant at her home as part of an investigation.[^1]
- The day after the search, Caddick left her residence and did not return, leading to a major search effort and extensive media coverage.[^1]

### Discovery and Identification

- In February 2021, a human foot inside a running shoe washed up on a beach south of Sydney and was later identified as belonging to Caddick through forensic testing.[^1]
- The discovery contributed to the conclusion that she had died sometime after her disappearance, though key questions about the circumstances remained a focus of public attention.[^1]

### Impact

- The case prompted renewed scrutiny of due diligence practices by retail investors and the role of licensing, auditing, and custody arrangements in detecting fraud earlier.[^1]
- It also highlighted the difficulty of recovering assets after alleged misappropriation, especially where funds have been spent or moved through complex transactions.[^1]
