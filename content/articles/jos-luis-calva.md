## Summary

Mexican serial killer and cannibal known as 'The Cannibal Poet' who murdered at least three women and ate parts of their bodies. He was found dead in his cell.

## Case overview

- Calva drew significant tabloid and mainstream media attention in Mexico in 2007 after his arrest in Mexico City.
- Authorities alleged that multiple victims were linked to him and that evidence recovered from his residence supported homicide accusations.

## Arrest and investigation

- Police reporting at the time described the discovery of human remains at his apartment, which led to the nickname sometimes used in the press.
- Investigations into serial violence often rely on a mix of forensic evidence, witness testimony, and reconstructed timelines; public accounts of specific case details can differ across sources.

## Death in custody

- Calva was later found dead in his cell in 2007.
- Reports commonly described the death as occurring while in custody; official findings and public commentary have varied, and the circumstances have been discussed in Mexican media.
