## Summary

Cotton Mather was a New England Puritan minister and one of the most influential figures in colonial American Christianity. He wrote over 400 works, including the monumental Magnalia Christi Americana, a ecclesiastical history of New England. He was involved in the Salem witch trials, though his role remains debated. He also promoted smallpox inoculation, showing an unusual engagement with science.
