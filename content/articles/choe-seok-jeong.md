## Summary

Choe Seok-jeong was a Joseon Dynasty mathematician and prime minister who created orthogonal Latin squares, predating Euler's work by decades. His mathematical text Gusuryak contained the first known example of an orthogonal Latin square of order 9. He combined his mathematical genius with a distinguished political career.
