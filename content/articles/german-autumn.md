## Summary

Period of terrorist crisis in Germany in autumn 1977 when the RAF kidnapped industrialist Hanns Martin Schleyer and hijacked a Lufthansa plane to force release of imprisoned RAF members.

## Historical Context

“German Autumn” is typically used to describe the peak of the Red Army Faction (RAF) campaign and the West German state’s countermeasures.
The crisis unfolded during a decade in which political violence, radicalization, and debates about democratic legitimacy were prominent in Western Europe.

## Key Incidents

- **Kidnapping of Hanns Martin Schleyer:** The abduction aimed to pressure the government to release imprisoned RAF members.
- **Hijacking of Lufthansa Flight 181 (“Landshut”):** The aircraft was seized to reinforce the same demand and broaden international pressure.
- **GSG 9 operation:** A West German special unit intervened to rescue hostages, becoming a major reference point in the country’s modern security history.

## Political and Social Impact

The period intensified public debate about the balance between civil liberties and state security.
It also shaped policing, intelligence coordination, and legal frameworks for dealing with terrorism.

## Legacy

In German memory and scholarship, the episode is often treated as a stress test for the postwar Federal Republic.
It remains a frequently cited case when discussing hostage policy, counterterrorism decision-making, and the long-term effects of political violence on democratic institutions.

The term is also used more broadly to capture the atmosphere of fear and political tension that accompanied the events.
