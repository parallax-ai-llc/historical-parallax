## Summary

Prominent South Carolina attorney convicted of murdering his wife and son to distract from his financial crimes. Stole millions from clients, including paralyzed victims.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2021 | Murdered wife and son |

## Background

- Member of the Murdaugh family, a multigenerational South Carolina legal dynasty connected to the 14th Circuit solicitor’s office and the private law firm formerly known as Peters, Murdaugh, Parker, Eltzroth & Detrick (PMPED).[^1]
- Worked as a lawyer handling personal-injury and related civil matters, where prosecutors later alleged he misappropriated client and settlement funds over many years.[^1]

## Financial Crimes (Overview)

- Authorities described a pattern of theft and fraud involving client settlements, insurance proceeds, and other funds routed through accounts tied to his legal practice.[^1]
- The financial allegations gained wider attention in 2021 as investigators examined discrepancies tied to client payouts and firm accounting, leading to multiple criminal charges.[^1]

## Murders and Investigation

- In June 2021, Murdaugh’s wife Maggie and son Paul were found shot to death at the family’s Moselle property in Colleton County, South Carolina.[^1]
- The case drew national attention due to the family’s prominence, the brutality of the crime, and the overlap with the unfolding financial investigations.[^1]

## Trial and Sentencing

- In 2023, a South Carolina jury convicted Murdaugh of murdering Maggie and Paul, and he received a life sentence without the possibility of parole.[^1]
- The murder trial ran alongside continuing legal proceedings related to alleged financial crimes and civil litigation connected to his conduct as an attorney.[^1]

## Public Impact

- The prosecutions became a widely followed example of how institutional influence and local prominence can intersect with accountability, financial oversight, and criminal justice scrutiny.[^1]
- Media coverage expanded beyond the murders to include the broader set of civil and criminal cases linked to Murdaugh and his professional network.[^1]
