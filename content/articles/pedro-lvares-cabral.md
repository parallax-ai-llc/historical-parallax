## Summary

Pedro Álvares Cabral was the first European to reach Brazil in 1500. He claimed it for Portugal while on a voyage to India, changing South American history.

## Additional Context

### 1500 voyage and landfall in Brazil

Cabral was appointed to lead Portugal’s second major expedition to India after Vasco da Gama’s initial voyage. The fleet sailed westward across the Atlantic and made landfall on the Brazilian coast in April 1500, after which Cabral claimed the territory for the Portuguese Crown.

### Continuation to India

After leaving Brazil, the expedition crossed the Atlantic toward the Cape of Good Hope. Contemporary accounts describe storms and losses at sea, followed by arrivals in Indian Ocean ports where the mission sought to establish trade relations.

### Historical significance

Cabral’s voyage is central to Portuguese imperial history: it is tied to the formal start of Portuguese colonization in Brazil and to the wider expansion of maritime routes linking Europe, Africa, and Asia.
