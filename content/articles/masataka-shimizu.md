## Background and identification notes

- Public sources commonly describe **a Masataka Shimizu (清水 政孝)** as a Japanese businessman who served as **President and CEO of Tokyo Electric Power Company (TEPCO)** from **2008 to 2011**.
- That biography typically lists:

## Roles and affiliations (as described in public biographies)

- **TEPCO (Tokyo Electric Power Company):** President/CEO (2008–2011).
- **Keidanren (Japan Business Federation):** Served as a vice-chairman; stepped down in the aftermath of the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster.
- **Fuji Oil Company:** Later served as an outside director (public reporting places this appointment in 2012 and after).

## Notable context: Fukushima Daiichi and corporate accountability

- Shimizu's tenure as TEPCO president overlapped with heightened scrutiny of nuclear plant risk management in Japan.
- After the **2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami**, TEPCO faced a crisis at the **Fukushima Daiichi Nuclear Power Plant**, which became a defining event for the company and its leadership.
- Public reporting notes that Shimizu:
  - Stayed within TEPCO headquarters for an extended period following the disaster.
  - Took sick leave due to overwork.
  - Resigned in 2011 and was succeeded by another executive.
- In later years, TEPCO executives (including Shimizu, in reporting about shareholder litigation) were associated with discussions of personal/corporate liability for disaster-related damages.

## Why this figure matters (high-level)

- As the head of Japan's largest electric utility at the time, Shimizu became closely associated with:
  - **Energy reliability vs. safety tradeoffs** in a highly regulated sector.
  - The governance challenges of managing complex, high-risk infrastructure.
  - The reputational and legal consequences of catastrophic industrial accidents.
