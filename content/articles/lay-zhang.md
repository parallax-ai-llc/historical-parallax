## Summary

Lay Zhang (张艺兴, Zhang Yixing) is a Chinese singer, dancer, songwriter, and actor born on October 7, 1991, in Changsha, Hunan. He rose to international prominence as a member of the South Korean-Chinese boy group EXO, debuting under SM Entertainment in 2012. Over the subsequent decade, he built a successful parallel solo career in China, becoming one of the most commercially significant Chinese pop artists in global markets.

His influence bridges Korean and Chinese entertainment industries at a pivotal moment of cultural exchange, while his outspoken pride in Chinese identity and culture made him a highly visible public figure domestically. Lay Zhang is widely regarded as a key figure who helped introduce the idol training system to mainland Chinese audiences and demonstrated the viability of cross-market pop careers.

---

## Early Life & Background

Zhang Yixing was born on October 7, 1991, in Changsha, Hunan Province, China. He showed early aptitude in the performing arts, studying piano, violin, and traditional Chinese dance as a child. At age ten, he won a national singing competition, which helped set his trajectory toward a professional entertainment career. In 2008, at age 16, he traveled to South Korea to train under SM Entertainment, spending approximately four years as a trainee before his debut.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Lay debuted with EXO in April 2012, initially as part of the sub-unit EXO-M, which promoted primarily in Mandarin-speaking markets. EXO became one of the best-selling acts in K-pop history throughout the 2010s, winning numerous awards and amassing dedicated fanbases across Asia and beyond.

In 2016, Lay launched his solo career in China, releasing his debut album *Lose Control* in October of that year. The album charted strongly and established him as a bankable solo act independent of EXO. He released subsequent albums including *SHEEP* (2017) and *NAMANANA* (2018), the latter of which he executive produced and for which he performed at the 2018 MTV EMAs in London — the first Chinese artist to perform solo at that ceremony.

He also expanded into acting, appearing in the 2017 Chinese film *The Founding of an Army* and the American science fiction film *Kungfu Yoga* (2017) alongside Jackie Chan.

## Key Achievements

- Member of EXO, which sold over 100 million records collectively across their career
- First Chinese solo artist to perform at the MTV EMAs (2018)
- Founder of his own entertainment label, Chromosom, established in 2019
- Multiple Weibo and Chinese music chart records with solo releases
- Featured on the *Forbes* China Celebrity 100 list multiple times

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Lay Zhang has been a polarizing figure in cross-cultural discourse. He was notably absent from EXO's group activities for extended periods due to his domestic Chinese commitments, which generated friction with some international fans who felt his group participation was inconsistent.

He has made several public statements on Chinese national identity and sovereignty — including remarks on the Taiwan and South China Sea issues — that drew criticism internationally but reinforced his standing within mainland China. Critics have also noted that his public persona became increasingly shaped by nationalist positioning from the mid-2010s onward.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Lay Zhang represents a generation of Chinese entertainers who trained in the Korean idol system and returned to China as cultural ambassadors of that model. His label Chromosom reflects his ambition to shape the next generation of Chinese pop production. He remains one of the most-followed Chinese celebrities on global social media platforms and a touchstone figure in discussions of Sino-Korean cultural exchange.
