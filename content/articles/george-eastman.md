## Background

Eastman grew up in upstate New York and began working at a young age after his father died.
He developed an interest in photography in the late 1870s, when the process was still expensive and equipment-heavy.

## Key Contributions

- **Roll film commercialization:** Eastman helped move photography away from glass plates toward flexible film, enabling simpler cameras and mass use.
- **Consumer product design:** The Kodak camera line emphasized ease of use and standardized processing.
- **Vertical integration:** Kodak built a large-scale manufacturing and processing ecosystem around film, paper, chemicals, and cameras.

## Business and Impact

Kodak’s approach turned photography into a consumer habit rather than a specialist craft.
That shift also influenced journalism, advertising, and personal documentation by making images cheap and repeatable.

## Philanthropy and Legacy

Eastman became one of the major American philanthropists of his era, supporting education and health initiatives.
His legacy is intertwined with both the democratization of photography and the later dominance of Kodak in the film industry.

In the long run, his work is often discussed as a case study in how technical packaging, manufacturing scale, and distribution can matter as much as invention.
