## Summary

Australia's most wanted drug lord who fled overseas before being captured in Greece wearing a wig.

## Background

Tony Mokbel (also reported as having the nickname "The Cousin") became a prominent figure in Melbourne's underworld during the period commonly referred to as the Melbourne gangland war.[^1]

## Notability

He drew national attention in Australia for a combination of:

- Allegations and convictions connected to large-scale drug trafficking
- A high-profile period as a fugitive while facing criminal charges
- An internationally publicized arrest after leaving Australia[^1]

## Flight and Capture

Mokbel left Australia while on bail and was sought by Australian authorities. He was later located and arrested in Greece in 2007, an event widely reported because of his attempt to disguise his appearance.[^1]

## Legal Outcomes

After being returned to Australia, he faced a series of prosecutions in relation to drug trafficking matters. Reporting on his case has often focused on sentencing outcomes and the broader law-enforcement response to organized crime in Victoria.[^1]

## Legacy and Public Interest

Mokbel's notoriety is frequently discussed in the context of:

- Media coverage of organized crime networks and policing in Melbourne
- The role of bail, border controls, and extradition in high-profile fugitive cases
