## Summary

Chinese serial killer and robber who murdered 28 people during armed robberies across four provinces between 1991 and 2000. He led a gang that committed some of China's most brutal crimes. Executed by shooting.

## Additional Context

- Zhang Jun’s crimes are frequently summarized as a series of armed robberies that escalated into repeated killings over many years.
- In public accounts, the case is often presented as an example of highly mobile gang activity spanning multiple jurisdictions.

## Modus Operandi (High Level)

- Sources generally describe the group as targeting robbery opportunities and using extreme violence to eliminate witnesses.
- The violence was not limited to a single location, complicating coordination for law enforcement across provincial boundaries.

## Arrest and Outcome

- After capture, the case moved quickly through prosecution by Chinese authorities, with capital punishment carried out by shooting.
- The severity of punishment is consistent with how China has historically handled major violent-crime campaigns and high-profile cases.

## Key Takeaways

- Multi-province crime sprees can expose coordination limits between local police forces.
- High-profile violent cases often trigger intensified enforcement campaigns and public messaging.
- The story is frequently cited as an extreme example of robbery-driven serial killing.

- The case remains notable in China for the unusually high number of victims attributed to the spree.
