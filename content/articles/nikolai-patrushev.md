## Summary

Secretary of Russia's Security Council considered one of the most influential hardliners in Putin's circle.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2008 | Became Security Council secretary |

## Key Roles and Influence

- **KGB / security services background**: Patrushev is a career security official, associated with the Soviet-era KGB and its post-Soviet successors.
- **FSB leadership (1999–2008)**: He served as Director of the Federal Security Service (FSB), Russia’s main domestic security agency, during the early Putin era.
- **Security Council secretary (2008–2024)**: As Secretary of the Security Council, he held a long-running coordination role over national security policy and was often described as a key hardline voice in elite decision-making.
- **Post-2024 role**: After leaving the Security Council secretary position in 2024, he continued to be referenced in Russian state reporting as holding an advisory/security-policy role.

## Public Profile

- Often characterized by observers as part of the **“siloviki”** (security and military elite) with a worldview emphasizing state security, sovereignty, and perceived external threats.
- Has made public statements that frame Russia’s strategic environment in **zero-sum** terms and highlight Western influence operations as a recurring theme.

## Network / Associations

- **Vladimir Putin**: Widely reported to have longstanding ties to Putin’s St. Petersburg/Leningrad security milieu.
- **Russian security establishment**: His career places him in the continuity line of Russia’s security agencies from late Soviet institutions to modern federal services.
- **Family link frequently noted in reporting**: His son, **Dmitry Patrushev**, has held senior roles in the Russian government and state-linked sectors; this is often cited as an example of elite network continuity.

## International Response

- Patrushev has been included in various **Western sanctions lists** in connection with Russia’s security and foreign policy actions (details and dates vary by jurisdiction).

## Additional Notes

- **Policy coordination role**: The Security Council secretariat is often described as a key venue for inter-agency alignment on defense, intelligence, and internal security priorities.
- **Information environment**: Public assessments of Patrushev’s behind-the-scenes influence rely heavily on media reporting and elite-watcher analysis rather than transparent institutional records.
