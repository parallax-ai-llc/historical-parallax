## Summary

Dutch boy whose 1998 murder was solved 20 years later through DNA evidence.

## Career Timeline

## Background

- **Victim**: Nicky Verstappen, a Dutch boy.
- **Context**: He went missing during a 1998 school camping trip; the case remained unsolved for years and became one of the Netherlands' best-known cold cases.[^1]

## Investigation (high level)

- Early investigation produced evidence, but no definitive suspect was charged for many years.
- The case stayed active as forensic methods improved, with particular attention to biological traces that could later be compared using DNA databases.[^1]

## Breakthrough via DNA

- Advances in DNA profiling and comparison techniques enabled investigators to revisit older evidence.
- A DNA match ultimately led police to identify and arrest a suspect roughly two decades after the crime.[^1]

## Legal proceedings and outcome

- After the arrest, the case moved through the Dutch criminal justice process.
- The matter resulted in a conviction, as summarized in widely available public case overviews.[^1]

## Why the case is often cited

- **Cold-case resolution**: Illustrates how long-unsolved crimes can be reopened when forensic capabilities improve.
- **DNA evidence**: Serves as a frequently referenced example of DNA-driven identification in a decades-old investigation.[^1]
