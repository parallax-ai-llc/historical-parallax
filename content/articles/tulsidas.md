## Summary

Tulsidas was an Indian Hindu poet-saint who composed the Ramcharitmanas, a retelling of the Ramayana in Awadhi language that became the most popular and influential religious text in Hindi-speaking India. His devotion to Lord Rama and his accessible poetry made the epic available to ordinary people. He also wrote the Hanuman Chalisa, one of the most recited Hindu prayers. He is considered one of the greatest poets in Indian literature.
