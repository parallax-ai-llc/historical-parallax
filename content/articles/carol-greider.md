## Summary

Carol Greider is an American molecular biologist who shared the 2009 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for the discovery of telomerase. As a graduate student with Elizabeth Blackburn, she discovered the enzyme telomerase, which maintains telomere length and plays a crucial role in aging and cancer. She was one of the youngest women to receive a Nobel Prize.
