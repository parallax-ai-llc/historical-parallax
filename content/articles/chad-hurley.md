## Summary

Chad Meredith Hurley (born January 24, 1977) is an American co-founder and former CEO of YouTube, the world's largest video-sharing platform. As employee #13 at PayPal, Hurley designed the company's logo before joining Steve Chen and Jawed Karim to create YouTube in 2005. After selling YouTube to Google for $1.65 billion in 2006, Hurley became a prominent entrepreneur and investor in Silicon Valley.

## YouTube (2005-2010)

### Co-founding YouTube

**The Idea**:
- Late 2004/Early 2005
- With Steve Chen and Jawed Karim
- Above pizzeria in San Mateo
- Hurley contributed design vision

**Initial Concept**:
- Easy video sharing
- Simple upload and playback
- Social features
- Clean, accessible interface

**Hurley's Role**:
- CEO from start
- Design and user experience
- Business development
- Public face of company

### Rapid Growth

**Viral Success**:
- Launched February 2005
- Grew explosively
- Billions of views by 2006
- Cultural phenomenon

**Design Philosophy**:
- Simplicity
- Fast loading
- Easy embedding
- "Broadcast Yourself"

### Google Acquisition (2006)

**The Deal**:
- October 2006: $1.65 billion
- 20 months after founding
- Hurley's share: ~$345 million
- Age 29

**Post-Acquisition**:
- Remained CEO until 2010
- Helped integrate with Google
- Scaled to 1 billion+ users
- Stepped down as CEO (2010)
