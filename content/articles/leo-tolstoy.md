## Summary

Count Lev Nikolayevich Tolstoy (1828-1910) was a Russian writer widely regarded as one of the greatest novelists in history. His epic novels *War and Peace* (1869) and *Anna Karenina* (1877) are considered pinnacles of realist fiction, combining vast historical sweep with psychological insight. A wealthy aristocrat who became an ascetic moralist, Tolstoy later rejected organized religion, embraced nonviolent resistance, and influenced Mahatma Gandhi and Martin Luther King Jr. His spiritual crisis, documented in *A Confession*, led him to develop a Christian anarchist philosophy emphasizing love, simplicity, and nonviolence. Excommunicated by the Russian Orthodox Church, Tolstoy died at a remote train station, fleeing his estate in spiritual turmoil.

## Early Life

**Birth:** September 9, 1828, Yasnaya Polyana, Russia
**Family:** Wealthy aristocratic family. Orphaned by age 9 (mother died when he was 2, father when he was 9). Raised by relatives.

**Youth:**
- Attended Kazan University (dropped out)
- Wild youth: gambling, drinking
- Served in army (Crimean War, Caucasus)
- *Sevastopol Sketches* (1855): War reportage, established reputation

## Major Works

### War and Peace (1869)

**Scope:**
Epic novel spanning 1,225 pages, hundreds of characters, Napoleonic invasion of Russia (1805-1812).

**Characters:**
- Pierre Bezukhov (spiritual seeker)
- Prince Andrei Bolkonsky (disillusioned aristocrat)
- Natasha Rostova (vivacious heroine)

**Themes:**
- History vs. free will
- Individual lives within historical forces
- Russian national identity
- Love, death, meaning

**Status:**
Often called greatest novel ever written.

### Anna Karenina (1877)

**Plot:**
Anna, married aristocrat, has affair with Count Vronsky. Tragic consequences.

**Opening:**
"Happy families are all alike; every unhappy family is unhappy in its own way."

**Themes:**
- Marriage, fidelity, passion
- Social hypocrisy
- Spiritual crisis (Levin subplot = Tolstoy's struggles)

**Parallel:**
Levin's search for meaning mirrors Tolstoy's spiritual journey.

### Other Works

- *Childhood, Boyhood, Youth* (autobiographical trilogy)
- *The Death of Ivan Ilyich* (novella on mortality)
- *The Kreutzer Sonata* (marriage, celibacy)
- *Resurrection* (last novel, social critique)
- *Hadji Murad* (Caucasus war)

## Spiritual Crisis and Philosophy

### Crisis (1870s-1880s)

Despite fame and wealth, Tolstoy experienced profound crisis. Contemplated suicide. Could not find meaning in art, science, or philosophy.

**A Confession (1882):**
Documented crisis. Rejected institutionalized Christianity but embraced Jesus's moral teachingsâ€”love, nonviolence, simplicity.

### Tolstoyan Philosophy

**Key Ideas:**
- **Nonviolent Resistance**: Do not resist evil with violence
- **Christian Anarchism**: Reject state, property, institutions
- **Simplicity**: Renounce wealth, luxury
- **Manual Labor**: Work with hands; dignity of simple life
- **Vegetarianism**: Embraced late in life

**Influence:**
Gandhi credited Tolstoy as major influence on satyagraha. Correspondence between them (1909-1910). Tolstoy Farm named by Gandhi.

## Personal Life

**Marriage (1862):**
Married Sophia Behrs (18 years old; he was 34). Thirteen children (8 survived infancy).

**Relationship Strains:**
- Initially happy
- Later: deep conflicts over property, philosophy
- Sophia resented Tolstoy giving away copyrights
- Tensions over his ascetic lifestyle

**Estate:**
Yasnaya Polyana became pilgrimage site. Tolstoy worked fields, made shoes, ate simply (while family lived comfortably).

---

## Excommunication

**1901:**
Russian Orthodox Church excommunicated Tolstoy for rejecting church teachings, denying Christ's divinity, promoting rationalist Christianity.

Public reaction mixed: many supported Tolstoy. He continued writing religious tracts.

---

## Death

**Flight from Home:**
October 1910, age 82, Tolstoy secretly left home, fleeing family conflicts and desire for simple death.

**Death (November 20, 1910):**
Fell ill at Astapovo railway station. Died of pneumonia. Refused sacraments. Crowds gathered. International news event.

Buried at Yasnaya Polyana, as he wished, in simple grave.

## Legacy

**Literature:**
- Pinnacle of realist fiction

**Philosophy:**
- Nonviolent resistance inspired Gandhi, MLK
- Christian anarchism
- Anti-war writings

**Russian Culture:**
- National treasure
- Yasnaya Polyana museum
- Continuous scholarship
