## Overview
Sparked by corrupt local officials, the Donghak Peasant Revolution grew into a massive uprising led by Jeon Bong-jun. The rebels demanded social reforms and the expulsion of foreign influence.

## Consequence
The rebellion led the Joseon governement to ask for Chinese aid, which in turn prompted Japan to send troops, sparking the First Sino-Japanese War fought on Korean soil.
