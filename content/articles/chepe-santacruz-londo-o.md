## Summary

Colombian drug lord known as 'Chepe' who was a leader of the Cali Cartel and managed its operations in New York City. He was renowned for his intelligence network and escaped from a maximum-security prison before being killed.

## Background

José Santacruz Londoño, known by the nickname “Chepe,” was associated with the Cali Cartel, a major Colombian drug-trafficking organization that rose to prominence in the late 1980s and early 1990s. The cartel was often characterized as more businesslike and less openly violent than its rival Medellín Cartel, though it remained deeply involved in corruption and violence.

## Role in the Cali Cartel

Santacruz Londoño was linked to international distribution networks, including operations tied to the United States. Reports commonly describe him as focused on logistics, coordination, and building an intelligence and counterintelligence capability to evade law enforcement pressure.

## Arrest, Escape, and Death

He was arrested in Colombia in 1995. In 1996 he escaped from custody, an event that further underscored the vulnerability of Colombian institutions to corruption and intimidation during the cartel era. He was later killed in 1996 during efforts by Colombian authorities to locate and dismantle remaining cartel leadership.

## Historical Significance

Santacruz Londoño’s trajectory reflects the peak and decline of large-scale Colombian cartels and the intensified collaboration between Colombian and U.S. agencies during the “war on drugs.” The period shaped Colombian politics, policing, and public security policy for decades.
