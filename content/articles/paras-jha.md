## Summary

American college student who co-created the Mirai botnet that launched the largest DDoS attack in history, taking down major websites including Twitter, Netflix, and Reddit in 2016. He created it originally to attack rival Minecraft servers.

## Why He Matters

Jha is primarily noted in cybersecurity history because Mirai demonstrated, at large scale, how insecure consumer IoT devices could be weaponized into botnets. The 2016 incidents are often cited as a turning point that pushed operators, researchers, and policymakers to treat IoT security as critical infrastructure risk.

## Mirai (High-Level)

- **Vector**: Mirai scanned the internet for vulnerable devices (such as cameras and routers) that used default or weak credentials.
- **Botnet behavior**: Compromised devices were coordinated to send massive volumes of traffic to targets, creating distributed denial-of-service (DDoS) attacks.
- **Ripple effects**: Large DDoS events can disrupt downstream services, not only the immediate target, due to dependencies on DNS providers and shared network capacity.

## Legal and Ethical Context

- The public record describes criminal charges and a guilty plea related to Mirai’s creation and use.
- The case is frequently referenced in discussions about how youthful experimentation, competitive online communities, and real-world harm can intersect.
- It also influenced industry attention toward password hygiene, firmware updates, device lifecycle support, and responsible disclosure practices.
