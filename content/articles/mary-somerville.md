## Summary

Mary Somerville was a Scottish mathematician, astronomer, and science writer who was one of the most prominent scientific minds of the 19th century. Her book 'On the Connexion of the Physical Sciences' was enormously influential. She was one of the first two women to be admitted to the Royal Astronomical Society. Somerville College, Oxford, is named in her honor.
