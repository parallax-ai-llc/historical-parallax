## Summary

56 inmates were killed in a prison riot in Manaus as FDN fought Comando Vermelho. Many were beheaded; some had their hearts ripped out. Guards had lost control entirely.

## Context

- **Location:** The incident occurred at the *Anísio Jobim Penitentiary Complex (COMPAJ)* in Manaus, Amazonas state, Brazil.
- **Prison-gang landscape:** Brazilian prisons in the 2010s were heavily influenced by organized criminal groups that operated both inside and outside custody. Control of cellblocks and visitation corridors often tracked gang affiliation.
- **Rivalry cited in reporting:** Contemporary accounts described the violence as tied to a broader feud involving **Família do Norte (FDN)** and **Comando Vermelho (CV)**, two major criminal organizations active in northern Brazil.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Loss of control:** The riot and killings were widely reported as occurring after guards lost effective control of parts of the facility.
- **Mass casualty event:** Dozens of inmates were killed during the violence. Some victims suffered extreme mutilation, which became a defining feature of media coverage.
- **Duration and containment:** Public reporting framed the event as a short, intense outbreak of violence followed by a security response to regain control and separate factions.

## Key Actors

- **Inmate factions:** The main reported perpetrators and targets were aligned with rival gangs (FDN vs. CV), though individual affiliations can be difficult to verify from open sources.
- **State authorities:** Amazonas state prison officials and security forces were responsible for restoring order and later reporting preliminary casualty figures.

## Immediate Aftermath

- **Counting and identification:** After major prison disturbances, early death tolls can change as bodies are identified and missing persons are accounted for; open reports may differ on specifics.
- **Security measures:** Such events typically trigger temporary lockdowns, transfers of inmates, and internal investigations focused on weapons flow, staffing levels, and surveillance gaps.

## Why It Matters

- **Indicator of prison governance failures:** The massacre is often cited as an example of how prison overcrowding, understaffing, and gang governance can combine into catastrophic outcomes.
- **Part of a wider cycle:** The incident was discussed alongside other large-scale Brazilian prison clashes in the same period, reflecting national challenges in custodial security and organized crime.
