## Summary

Sam Snead won a record 82 PGA Tour events and seven major championships. He was known for his fluid, natural swing.

## Playing style

Snead was celebrated for a smooth, athletic swing that many players and instructors later treated as a model of rhythm and balance. His reputation for touch around the greens and confident putting contributed to his longevity at the top level.

## Major championships (high level)

He won multiple major titles across different eras of professional golf, including victories at the Masters Tournament, the PGA Championship, and The Open Championship. His career spanned the transition from pre-war to modern televised golf.

## Records and legacy

- **PGA Tour wins:** Long associated with the all-time record total (later matched).
- **Longevity:** Notable for winning tournaments over a very long period, including victories well into his 50s.
- **Influence:** Frequently cited in discussions of the "classic" golf swing and the sport's mid-century popularization in the United States.

## Post-competition

After his peak competitive years, Snead remained a recognizable figure through exhibitions, teaching, and course-related work, and he continued to be referenced as a benchmark for natural talent in golf.
