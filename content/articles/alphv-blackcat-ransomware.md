## Overview

ALPHV (also branded as **BlackCat**) is commonly described as a **ransomware‑as‑a‑service (RaaS)** operation. In a RaaS model, a core group develops and maintains the malware and leak infrastructure, while **affiliates** conduct intrusions and deploy ransomware in exchange for a share of proceeds.

The group has been associated with high‑profile incidents and has been the target of multiple law‑enforcement disruptions and public advisories.

## Commonly reported operating model

Public reporting has highlighted several patterns often discussed in relation to ALPHV/BlackCat:

- **Double extortion:** theft of data followed by encryption and a threat to leak stolen data if payment is not made.
- **Leak sites:** publication of victim names and samples of data to pressure negotiations.
- **Affiliate ecosystem:** multiple intrusion crews using shared tooling under a revenue‑sharing arrangement.

## Targeting and impact

Ransomware groups in this category typically pursue organizations where downtime is costly and operational disruption creates leverage (for example, healthcare, critical services, and large enterprises). When healthcare and pharmacy supply chains are affected, disruption can extend beyond a single company to downstream providers.

## Law‑enforcement actions and disruptions

The group has faced periodic disruption efforts reported by government agencies and security firms, including:

- Infrastructure takedowns or seizures aimed at reducing the group's ability to operate.
- Public identification of tools, tactics, and indicators designed to help defenders harden systems and detect intrusions.

Because ransomware operations frequently reorganize, rebrand, or migrate infrastructure, disruption events may reduce activity temporarily without fully eliminating the underlying ecosystem.

## Notes on attribution

Attribution in ransomware cases is often based on a mix of malware analysis, infrastructure overlaps, negotiation behavior, and victim‑reported details. Public sources may differ on specific claims, timelines, and responsible individuals.
