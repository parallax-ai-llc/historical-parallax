## Summary

Bob Kahn is an American computer scientist and electrical engineer who co-invented TCP/IP with Vint Cerf, the protocol suite that forms the basis of the Internet. He also originated the concept of open architecture networking and initiated the ARPA Internet program. Kahn founded the Corporation for National Research Initiatives (CNRI) and received the Turing Award in 2004.
