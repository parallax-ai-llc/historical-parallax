## Summary

Frédéric Joliot-Curie was a French physicist who shared the 1935 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with his wife Irène Joliot-Curie for the discovery of artificial radioactivity. He later contributed to France's nuclear energy program and was the first French High Commissioner for Atomic Energy. He was also a prominent peace activist and opponent of nuclear weapons.
