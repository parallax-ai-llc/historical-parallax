## Summary

Iraqi politician who served as foreign minister and deputy PM under Saddam Hussein. He was the public face of the regime to the Western world. Convicted of crimes against humanity and sentenced to death. Died in prison of heart attack.

## Role in the Iraqi State

Aziz was one of Saddam Hussein’s best-known civilian officials and served for decades as a senior spokesman and negotiator. His portfolio included foreign policy messaging, diplomatic outreach, and representing Iraq in high-stakes encounters with Western and regional governments.

## International Profile

Because he regularly appeared in international media and met foreign officials, Aziz became a recognizable face of the Ba’athist government. During periods of conflict and sanctions, he often defended Iraqi state positions and argued against external intervention.

## Detention and Legal Proceedings After 2003

After the 2003 U.S.-led invasion, Aziz surrendered to coalition forces and was detained. Iraqi courts later prosecuted former officials of the regime for abuses connected to state repression.

Aziz received convictions for crimes associated with the Ba’athist era, including crimes against humanity. Sentencing and appeals became politically sensitive issues inside Iraq, and reports over time noted debates over whether and how capital sentences would be carried out.

## Death in Custody

Aziz died in prison in 2015. Accounts commonly describe health complications in custody, including cardiovascular issues.

## Historical Significance

Aziz’s career is often used as a case study of how authoritarian systems rely on internationally legible civilian figures—diplomats and ministers—to communicate legitimacy abroad while operating within a coercive domestic apparatus.
