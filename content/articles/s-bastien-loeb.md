## Summary

Sébastien Loeb won nine consecutive World Rally Championship titles from 2004 to 2012, the most dominant run in rally history.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2004 | Won first WRC title |

## Dominance in the WRC era

Loeb’s nine straight championships are frequently used as a benchmark for sustained excellence in modern rallying. The achievement required both speed and consistency across varied surfaces (asphalt, gravel, snow) and changing rally calendars.

His success is closely associated with the Citroën works program, where continuity in team structure and car development helped create a long competitive window.

## What made the streak notable (high-level)

- Adaptability: performing across different terrains and rally formats.
- Consistency: converting strong pace into points over full seasons.
- Partnership: long-running driver–co-driver pairing and stable team operations.

## Beyond full-time championship campaigns

After stepping back from full-time WRC participation, Loeb continued to compete in selected rally events and in other forms of motorsport. This cross-discipline activity contributed to his broader reputation as a versatile racing driver rather than a single-series specialist.

## Legacy

Loeb’s record is often discussed in debates about the greatest rally drivers, in part because it combines multiple titles with a consecutive-run statistic that is rare in top-level motorsport. His era also helped expand international attention for the championship during the 2000s.
