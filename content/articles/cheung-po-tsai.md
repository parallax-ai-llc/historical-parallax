## Summary

Chinese pirate who commanded 600 ships and 50,000 men in the South China Sea. Surrendered to the Qing dynasty and became a colonel in the Chinese navy.

## Background and rise

- Cheung Po Tsai (張保仔) is commonly described as a fisherman’s son from Guangdong who was reportedly kidnapped by pirates as a teenager.
- He was associated with the pirate confederation led by Zheng Yi (Cheng I) and, after Zheng Yi’s death, with the leadership of Ching Shih (Zheng Yi Sao).
- In popular retellings he becomes one of the best-known commanders of the confederation’s “Red Flag Fleet,” operating around the Pearl River Delta and the South China Sea.

## Surrender and later life

- In 1810, Qing officials negotiated a large-scale amnesty for parts of the pirate confederation, after sustained naval pressure and regional disruption.
- Cheung Po Tsai is described in secondary sources as taking a government post after surrender, serving as an officer in the Qing navy and participating in anti-piracy operations.
- He died in 1822; accounts generally place his death at sea.

## Legacy

- A cave on Cheung Chau, Hong Kong—often called “Cheung Po Tsai Cave”—is associated with legends of pirate treasure, and the story is used as a local folklore motif.
- The figure is a frequent subject of Hong Kong popular culture (television, film, and tourism), blending historical piracy with legend.
