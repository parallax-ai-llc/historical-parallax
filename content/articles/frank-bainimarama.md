## Summary

Fijian military commander who carried out a coup in 2006 and ruled as PM until 2022. He was convicted of perverting the course of justice in 2024 and sentenced to prison. His coup was initially popular but became increasingly authoritarian.

## Governance and Controversy

- Bainimarama justified the 2006 coup as necessary to address corruption and ethnic politics, but critics described it as an illegal seizure of power.
- During his rule, Fiji faced restrictions on media and political activity at various points, alongside efforts to rework institutions and electoral rules.
- Supporters credit his government with infrastructure and social programs; opponents emphasize democratic backsliding and the long-term effects on civil society.

## After Office

After losing power in the 2022 election, Bainimarama remained a major political figure. His 2024 conviction and imprisonment marked a rare instance of a former coup leader facing criminal consequences after leaving office.

## Key Takeaways

- Fiji’s modern political history has been shaped by recurring tension between military influence and democratic institutions.
- Bainimarama’s tenure is often evaluated in two frames at once: policy outcomes versus the legitimacy and methods of rule.
- The post-2022 period illustrates how political accountability can continue after a leader leaves formal office.
