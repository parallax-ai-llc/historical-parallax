## Summary

Russian bombardment of Grozny, the Chechen capital, during the First and Second Chechen Wars. The UN called it the most destroyed city on earth in 2003.

## Context and Background

- Grozny was the political and administrative center of Chechnya and became a focal point in both the First Chechen War (mid-1990s) and the Second Chechen War (late-1990s onward).
- The fighting combined conventional military operations with urban warfare, with dense civilian areas exposed to heavy weapons.

## Nature of the Bombardment

- Accounts commonly describe extensive use of artillery, rockets, and air strikes against urban targets during major phases of the sieges.
- Urban bombardment in Grozny is frequently cited in discussions of the military and humanitarian consequences of large-scale firepower in cities.

## Humanitarian and Political Impact

- Civilian casualties and displacement were central features of the conflict, with large numbers of residents fleeing during escalations.
- Human-rights organizations and international bodies criticized the scale of destruction and harm to non-combatants.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- The destruction of Grozny shaped postwar governance and reconstruction efforts in Chechnya, including a long period of rebuilding.
- The bombardment remains a prominent reference point in debates over laws of war, proportionality, and protection of civilians.
