## Summary

Mary Cartwright was a pioneering mathematician whose work with J.E. Littlewood on the van der Pol oscillator during World War II anticipated chaos theory by decades. She was the first woman elected to the Royal Society's fellowship in mathematics and the first female president of the London Mathematical Society. Her work on nonlinear dynamics was groundbreaking.
