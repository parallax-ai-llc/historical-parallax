## Summary

French-Greek Nazi sympathizer who merged Hinduism with Nazism and became a key figure in post-war neo-Nazism. She worshipped Hitler as an avatar of Vishnu and influenced later white supremacist movements worldwide.

## Additional Details

- **Background**: born Maximiani Portas, later adopting the name “Savitri Devi,” and became known for ideological writing rather than formal political office.[^1]
- **Ideological syncretism**: attempted to fuse elements of Hindu religious concepts with National Socialism, including portraying Adolf Hitler in mythic-religious terms; these interpretations are widely regarded as extremist and historically unfounded.[^1]
- **Post-war activity**: after World War II, she participated in pro-Nazi activism and networking that helped circulate apologetic narratives and propaganda among emerging neo-Nazi circles.[^1]
- **Writings**: *The Lightning and the Sun* is often cited as her most influential text, shaping a strain of esoteric or “spiritual” Nazism referenced by later white supremacist groups.[^1]
- **Reception and impact**: scholars and anti-extremism observers discuss her as an example of how religious language can be repurposed to legitimize racist ideology and historical revisionism.[^1]
