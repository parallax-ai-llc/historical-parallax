## Key Facts (high-level)

- **Full name**: Vladimir Vladimirovich Putin (Владимир Владимирович Путин).
- **Born**: 1952-10-07, Leningrad (now Saint Petersburg), Russian SFSR, USSR.
- **Background**: Trained as a lawyer; served as a KGB foreign intelligence officer.
- **Core roles**: Director of the FSB; Prime Minister; Acting President; President of Russia; Chairman/President of the ruling party bloc at different times.

## Career Timeline (selected)

- **1975**: Graduated from Leningrad State University (law).
- **1975–1991**: Worked in the KGB; later served in East Germany (Dresden).
- **1991–1996**: Worked in Saint Petersburg city administration (Anatoly Sobchak era).
- **1996**: Moved to Moscow; held roles in the Presidential Administration.
- **1998**: Appointed **Director of the FSB**.
- **1999 (Aug.)**: Appointed **Prime Minister** of Russia.
- **1999 (Dec.)**: Became **Acting President** after Boris Yeltsin resigned.
- **2000–2008**: **President** (two terms under then-constitutional limits).
- **2008–2012**: **Prime Minister** while Dmitry Medvedev served as President.
- **2012–present**: Returned as **President**; term lengths and limits were altered/extended via constitutional changes.
- **2014**: Russia annexed Crimea following military intervention and a disputed referendum.
- **2015**: Russia began a major military intervention in the Syrian civil war.
- **2022**: Russia launched a full-scale invasion of Ukraine, dramatically escalating the Russo-Ukrainian war.

## Governance & System

- Russia under Putin has widely been described by researchers and observers as increasingly **authoritarian**, with power concentrated in the executive and security services.
- Major themes include: centralization of regional governance, state influence over key media outlets, and a political system structured around a dominant ruling coalition.

## International Impact (overview)

- Putin’s era has been defined by confrontation with parts of the post–Cold War security order, including disputes over NATO expansion, sanctions regimes, and Russia’s use of military force in neighboring states.
- The Ukraine war has reshaped European security policy, energy markets, and global alignment patterns.
