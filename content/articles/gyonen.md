## Summary

Gyonen was a Japanese Buddhist monk and scholar who wrote one of the most important histories of Japanese Buddhism, the Hasshū Kōyō (Essentials of the Eight Schools). This work systematically presented the doctrines of the eight major schools of Japanese Buddhism. He was a scholar of the Kegon (Huayan) school and also studied Vinaya, Pure Land, and esoteric Buddhism. His works remain essential for understanding Japanese Buddhist history.
