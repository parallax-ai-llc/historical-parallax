## Summary

Rakim Athelaston Mayers, known professionally as A$AP Rocky, is a New York rapper, record producer, and fashion figure who emerged from the Harlem-based A$AP Mob collective to become one of hip-hop's most aesthetically distinctive voices. His debut mixtape *Live. Love. A$AP* (2011) announced a style that fused Southern rap cadences with dreamy, psychedelic production and a high-fashion sensibility rarely seen in mainstream hip-hop at the time.

Beyond his music, Rocky has carved out a parallel identity as a fashion icon, collaborating with luxury brands and sitting front row at major couture shows. His influence extends across music, style, and visual culture, making him one of the most cross-disciplinary figures of his generation. His long-term relationship with Rihanna and the birth of their children have kept him in the public eye well into the 2020s.

## Early Life & Background

Rakim Mayers was born on October 3, 1988, in Harlem, New York City. He grew up in poverty in Harlem's Eastside; his father was incarcerated when Rocky was a child, and his older brother was killed in a street-related incident. After his father's death in 2012, Rocky has cited these early hardships as formative to his artistic worldview.

He began rapping around age nine, influenced by Southern rap artists including Three 6 Mafia, UGK, and Outkast — an unusual orientation for a New York artist that would later define his sonic palette. As a teenager he stayed with friends and in shelters after being evicted, eventually finding stability through the A$AP Mob, a Harlem creative collective he joined in his late teens.

## Rise to Prominence

Rocky released *Live. Love. A$AP* in October 2011. The mixtape circulated widely online, earning critical praise for its atmospheric production — handled largely by A$AP Mob affiliate A$AP Yams — and Rocky's languid, melodic delivery. Within weeks he signed a reported $3 million joint deal with RCA Records and Polo Grounds Music.

His major-label debut, *Long. Live. A$AP* (January 2013), debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, driven by singles "Goldie," "Wild for the Night," and "Fuckin' Problems." The album certified platinum and established him as a commercial force. His second studio album, *AT.LONG.LAST.A$AP* (2015), also debuted at number one and earned widespread critical acclaim for its psychedelic, soul-influenced production featuring contributions from Rod Stewart, Joe Fox, and Mark Ronson.

## Key Achievements

- Two consecutive number-one Billboard 200 albums (*Long. Live. A$AP* and *AT.LONG.LAST.A$AP*)
- Multiple Grammy nominations
- Named creative director of Puma's men's division in 2016
- Collaborations with Dior, Gucci, and Calvin Klein; consistent presence at Paris Fashion Week
- Co-founder of AWGE, a multimedia creative agency
- Featured on Kendrick Lamar's "Fuckin' Problems," Drake's "Fuckin' Problems," and numerous high-profile collaborations

## Controversies & Criticism

In July 2019, Rocky was arrested in Sweden following a street altercation in Stockholm. He was held in pretrial detention for roughly a month before being convicted of assault and given a suspended sentence. The case drew international attention, with U.S. officials including President Trump publicly calling for his release — raising questions about diplomatic involvement in criminal proceedings. Rocky maintained the fight was self-defense.

He has also faced criticism for comments perceived as dismissive of the Black Lives Matter movement in a 2016 interview, drawing backlash from activists and peers.

## Legacy & Influence

Rocky helped normalize a fusion of high fashion and hip-hop at a mainstream level, predating and arguably catalyzing the widespread luxury-streetwear crossover of the late 2010s. His production taste — favoring hazy, sample-rich textures over conventional rap sounds — influenced a generation of artists including Post Malone, Playboi Carti (a fellow A$AP Mob member), and Lil Uzi Vert. Within the A$AP Mob, he has served as the primary ambassador following the death of founding member A$AP Yams in January 2015, keeping the collective's profile elevated.
