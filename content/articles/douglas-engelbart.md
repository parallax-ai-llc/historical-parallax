## Summary

Douglas Engelbart was an American engineer and inventor who is best known for inventing the computer mouse and pioneering many aspects of modern computing. His 1968 'Mother of All Demos' showcased the mouse, hypertext, networked computing, and video conferencing decades before they became mainstream. He envisioned computers as tools for augmenting human intellect. Engelbart received the Turing Award in 1997.
