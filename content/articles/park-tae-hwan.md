## Background

Park Tae-hwan is a South Korean competitive swimmer best known for freestyle events, including the 400-meter freestyle in which he won Olympic gold at the 2008 Beijing Games.[^1]

## Major Championships

- **Beijing 2008**: Won **400m freestyle gold**, a landmark result for South Korean swimming at the Olympic level.[^1]
- **London 2012**: Added two Olympic silver medals, keeping him among the leading male middle-distance freestylers of his era.[^1]

## Context and Impact

Olympic swimming medals are often discussed in terms of national program development; Park's success is commonly referenced in South Korea as a turning point that broadened mainstream attention on elite swimming beyond traditional strength sports.[^1]
