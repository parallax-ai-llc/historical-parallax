## Summary

Allyson Felix is the most decorated American track and field Olympian with 11 medals (7 gold) across five Games.

## Background

Felix emerged as a sprint prodigy in Los Angeles and turned professional as a teenager, choosing to focus on track and field rather than collegiate competition.

## Major Achievements

- Olympic medals across **five** Olympic Games (2004–2020), spanning the 200m, 400m, and 4×400m relay.
- Multiple-time world champion, including titles in the 200m early in her career and later success in the 400m and relays.
- Noted for longevity and adaptability as she transitioned from primarily a 200m sprinter to a 400m/relay specialist.

## Advocacy and Legacy

Felix is also known for advocacy around athlete maternity protections and sponsorship policies, speaking publicly about pregnancy and health risks faced by elite athletes.
