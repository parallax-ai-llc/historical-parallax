## Summary

Yukio Mishima was one of the most important Japanese writers of the 20th century. His novels including The Temple of the Golden Pavilion, Confessions of a Mask, and the Sea of Fertility tetralogy combine aesthetic beauty with psychological extremity. His dramatic public suicide by seppuku after a failed coup attempt shocked the world.
