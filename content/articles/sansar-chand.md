## Summary

Indian wildlife smuggler who was India's most notorious wildlife criminal. He was convicted of killing and trading hundreds of tigers, leopards, and other animals. He was linked to international wildlife trafficking networks. Died of cancer.

## Additional Details

- **Primary focus**: trafficking in big-cat parts and other protected wildlife, including skins and bones that are used in luxury goods and some traditional-medicine markets.[^1]
- **Networked trade**: reports described his operation as a supply chain that moved poached material from hunters to intermediaries and then to downstream buyers across state lines.[^1]
- **Enforcement pressure**: Chand was repeatedly arrested over decades, reflecting the difficulty of dismantling entrenched wildlife-trafficking networks and the persistence of demand.[^1]
- **Prosecution history**: public accounts emphasize multiple cases and convictions rather than a single decisive trial, with seizures (e.g., skins and bones) used as key evidentiary anchors.[^1]
- **Broader significance**: his notoriety is frequently cited in discussions of India’s tiger protection efforts and the role of organized smuggling in accelerating population decline during the late 20th and early 21st centuries.[^1]
