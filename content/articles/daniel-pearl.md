## Summary

American journalist and Wall Street Journal reporter who was kidnapped and beheaded in Pakistan by militants led by Omar Saeed Sheikh. His murder was videotaped and released online.

## Background

Daniel Pearl was a journalist best known for international reporting and for covering stories involving politics, security, and the intersection of finance and geopolitics.

By the early 2000s he was working as a reporter for The Wall Street Journal in South Asia.

## Reporting Focus

Accounts of his work often emphasize a mix of:

- field reporting and source development,
- interest in extremist networks and their financing,
- and attention to regional political dynamics affecting the United States and its allies.

## Kidnapping and Death

Pearl’s abduction and killing became a landmark case because of both the brutality of the crime and the way it was circulated as propaganda.

The episode prompted major diplomatic and security responses, as well as long-running legal proceedings and public debate about responsibility.

## Legacy

Pearl is widely remembered in journalism communities through awards, scholarships, and commemorations intended to promote press freedom and support reporters working under risk.

His case is also frequently referenced in discussions about:

- protections for correspondents,
- the ethics and security of meeting sources,
- and the broader issue of impunity for attacks on journalists.
