## Summary

Mexican serial killer and cannibal known as the Cannibal Poet who murdered at least 3 women in Mexico City. Police found human flesh in his refrigerator and a half-eaten arm in a frying pan.

## Background (As Reported)

- Public reporting has described Calva's childhood as traumatic, including early family disruption and sexual abuse.
- He was described in some sources as a writer/poet in addition to being accused of murder.

## 2007 Arrest and Investigation

- According to widely cited accounts, police arrested him in October 2007 during an investigation into the disappearance of his girlfriend, Alejandra Galeana, who was last seen in early October.
- Investigators reported finding mutilated human remains and evidence of cannibalism in his residence, including cooked human flesh and bones.
- Media coverage linked the "Cannibal Poet" nickname to the juxtaposition of his self-presentation as a writer with the brutality of the allegations.

## Death in Custody

- He died in prison on 11 December 2007, reported as suicide by hanging, before the case proceeded to trial.
- Accounts indicate that no suicide note was found.
