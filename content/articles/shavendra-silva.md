## Summary

Sri Lankan army commander whose 58th Division was accused of shelling civilian no-fire zones and hospitals during the final phase of the civil war. He was sanctioned by the US for human rights violations.

## Military career

Shavendra Silva rose through the Sri Lanka Army and is most closely associated in public reporting with senior command roles during the final phase of the Sri Lankan Civil War. His name is frequently discussed in connection with battlefield command decisions and the conduct of operations in densely populated areas.

## Allegations and scrutiny

A range of international reports and advocacy documentation have alleged violations of international humanitarian and human-rights law during the war’s closing months. These accounts commonly focus on issues such as:

- Indiscriminate or disproportionate fire in civilian areas.
- Attacks on protected sites, including medical facilities.
- Treatment of detainees and civilians in the conflict zone.

As with many conflict-related allegations, public debate often turns on standards of evidence, access to conflict zones, and the gap between investigative findings and legal accountability.

## International responses

Silva has been referenced in diplomatic and policy actions tied to human-rights concerns, including restrictions and sanctions measures by some governments. Such actions are typically framed as responses to credible information rather than criminal convictions.

## Domestic politics and appointments

His appointments to senior military positions drew attention because they intersected with Sri Lanka’s broader post-war reconciliation debates, civil–military relations, and the politics of accountability.

## Notes on terminology

Descriptions such as “war criminal” are often used in political and media contexts; strictly speaking, war-crimes determinations are made through competent judicial processes. Many sources therefore describe the allegations and findings rather than treating them as settled court judgments.
