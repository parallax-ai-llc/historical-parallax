## Summary

Grant Hackett won 1500m freestyle gold at 2000 and 2004 Olympics.

## Background

Grant Hackett became one of Australia's best-known middle- and long-distance freestyle swimmers.
He is closely associated with the 1500m freestyle, an event in which he dominated international competition in the early 2000s.
In addition to Olympic success, he won medals at World Championships and Commonwealth Games and frequently served as a leading figure within Australian swimming teams.

## Major Achievements (High Level)

Hackett won Olympic gold in the 1500m freestyle at Sydney 2000 and Athens 2004.
He also won Olympic relay medals, reflecting Australia's depth in freestyle events.
At various points in his career, he held world records in freestyle distances and was recognized for pacing strategy, endurance, and consistency over multiple seasons.

## Later Career and Public Profile

After stepping back from peak competition, Hackett remained in the public eye through commentary, mentoring, and attempts to return to elite racing.
His career is often discussed in comparison with other notable distance swimmers of his era, and his rivalry narratives helped popularize the 1500m freestyle.

## Notability

Hackett is frequently cited in Australian sport for the longevity of his elite performance and for helping maintain Australia’s dominance in distance freestyle.
His results are also a common reference point in discussions about training volume, race strategy, and the evolution of competitive pacing in the 1500m event.
In retrospective rankings, he is often listed among the defining distance freestylers of his generation.
