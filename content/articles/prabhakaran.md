## Summary

Founder and leader of the Tamil Tigers who pioneered suicide bombing as a tactic and ran a brutal insurgency for over 25 years. He was killed by Sri Lankan forces in the final battle of the civil war.

## Role in the Sri Lankan Civil War

Velupillai Prabhakaran led the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE), an armed separatist organization that sought an independent Tamil state in Sri Lanka. Under his leadership, the LTTE evolved from a militant group into a quasi-state actor in areas under its control, with a disciplined command structure and parallel institutions.

## Tactics and Organization (High Level)

- **Guerrilla warfare** and conventional-style operations at different stages of the conflict.
- Use of **suicide attacks** and high-profile assassinations, tactics that made the LTTE internationally notorious.
- A strong internal security apparatus and strict controls over dissent within the organization.

## International Designations and Controversy

Multiple governments and international bodies designated the LTTE as a terrorist organization. Prabhakaran’s legacy remains highly polarizing: supporters framed the LTTE as an armed resistance movement tied to Tamil grievances, while critics emphasized the group’s violence, coercion, and attacks on civilians.

## Death and Aftermath

Prabhakaran was reported killed in May 2009 during the Sri Lankan military’s final offensive. The end of the LTTE as an armed force closed one phase of the conflict but did not end political disputes over accountability, reconciliation, and the status and rights of Tamil communities.

## Historical Significance

Prabhakaran is often discussed in studies of insurgency and counterinsurgency for how the LTTE combined territorial control, propaganda, fundraising networks, and military innovation over a prolonged conflict.

## Caution on Sources

Accounts of LTTE governance, battlefield events, and civilian impact vary by source, and many claims are contested due to the intensity of wartime propaganda and the difficulty of independent verification.
