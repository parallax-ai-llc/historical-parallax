## Summary

Lata Mangeshkar (28 September 1929 – 6 February 2022) was an Indian playback singer widely regarded as one of the greatest and most influential vocalists in the history of Indian music. Over a career spanning more than seven decades, she recorded tens of thousands of songs in over thirty-six languages, becoming the defining voice of Hindi cinema from the 1940s through the 2000s. Her crystalline soprano and emotive phrasing shaped the sound of Bollywood for generations.

Known as the "Nightingale of India" and "Queen of Melody," Mangeshkar's voice was inseparable from the golden era of Hindi film music. She collaborated with nearly every major composer of her time — S.D. Burman, Naushad, Shankar-Jaikishan, Laxmikant-Pyarelal, and A.R. Rahman among them — lending her voice to performances by actresses including Madhubala, Nargis, and Hema Malini. Her cultural stature transcended entertainment; she was treated as a national institution.

## Early Life & Background

Born in Indore, Central Provinces (present-day Madhya Pradesh), Lata was the eldest child of Pandit Deenanath Mangeshkar, a classical vocalist and theatre actor of the Gwalior gharana, and Shevanti (later Shudhamati). Music was the family's lifeblood; she began learning classical music from her father at age five. Following Deenanath's death in 1942, thirteen-year-old Lata became the primary breadwinner for her mother and four younger siblings — including future singers Asha Bhosle, Usha Mangeshkar, and Meena Mangeshkar.

She relocated to Bombay and studied under Master Vinayak, a family friend and film director, who provided her early acting and singing opportunities in Marathi and Hindi productions.

## Rise to Prominence

Mangeshkar's breakthrough came with the song "Aayega Aanewala" from the film *Mahal* (1949), composed by Khemchand Prakash. The haunting track became a nationwide sensation and established her as the preeminent playback voice in Hindi cinema. Through the 1950s and 1960s she dominated the industry, recording multiple songs daily and becoming the default choice for film producers and composers alike.

Her 1963 performance of "Ae Mere Watan Ke Logon," composed by C. Ramchandra and sung live before Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru in the wake of the Sino-Indian War, moved Nehru to tears and became one of the most celebrated patriotic performances in Indian history.

## Key Achievements

- Recorded an estimated 30,000–50,000 songs across Hindi, Marathi, Bengali, Tamil, Telugu, and over thirty other languages.
- Won three National Film Awards for Best Female Playback Singer (1972, 1974, 1990).
- Awarded the Bharat Ratna in 2001, India's highest civilian honour — only the second vocalist to receive it after M.S. Subbulakshmi.
- Recipient of the Dadasaheb Phalke Award (1989), Padma Bhushan (1969), and Padma Vibhushan (1999).
- The Guinness World Records listed her at one point as the most recorded artist in history.

## Controversies & Criticism

Mangeshkar faced significant professional controversy in the 1950s when she successfully lobbied for playback singers to receive royalties from film songs — a campaign that drew resentment from some composers and producers. Critics, including filmmaker Gulzar on occasion, noted that her dominance of the industry made it difficult for other female singers to find consistent work during the height of her career. Some music historians have argued that the near-exclusive preference for her voice in Hindi film music homogenised the sound of mainstream playback singing for decades.

## Legacy & Influence

Mangeshkar's death on 6 February 2022 prompted a two-day national mourning period declared by the Government of India — a rare honour. She was cremated with full state honours at Shivaji Park, Mumbai. Her voice remains embedded in Indian cultural memory, heard across weddings, festivals, and commemorations. Singers from Shreya Ghoshal to Sunidhi Chauhan have cited her as a foundational influence. She produced music, mentored younger artists, and through her Lata Mangeshkar Foundation supported classical musicians. Her career remains the longest and most prolific in the history of playback singing.
