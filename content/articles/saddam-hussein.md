## Summary

Saddam Hussein Abd al-Majid al-Tikriti (April 28, 1937 â€“ December 30, 2006) was the fifth President of Iraq, ruling from 1979 until his overthrow in 2003. Rising through the Ba'ath Party ranks, Saddam established one of the Middle East's most brutal dictatorships, characterized by extreme violence, personality cult, and regional aggression. His regime committed genocide against Kurds, waged devastating wars against Iran and Kuwait, and maintained power through systematic terror. The 2003 U.S. invasion ended his rule, and he was executed in 2006 after being convicted of crimes against humanity.

## Early Life

### Childhood

Born April 28, 1937, in Al-Awja village near Tikrit:

**Difficult Upbringing**:
- Father died before birth or shortly after
- Mother remarried abusive stepfather
- Sent

 to live with uncle at age 10
- Uncle Khairallah Talfah (nationalist, anti-British)
- Influenced by uncle's Arab nationalism

**Education**:
- Attended school in Tikrit
- Moved to Baghdad for secondary school
- Failed military academy entrance exam
- Joined Ba'ath Party (1957, age 20)

## Rise to Power

### Ba'ath Revolutionary

**1959 Assassination Attempt**:
- Failed plot to kill Prime Minister Abd al-Karim Qasim
- Wounded in leg
- Fled to Syria, then Egypt
- Studied law in Cairo
- Built reputation as tough revolutionary

**1963 Coup**:
- Ba'ath Party

 seized power briefly
- Saddam returned to Iraq
- Regime collapsed after 9 months
- Saddam imprisoned (1964-1967)

**1968 Coup**:
- Ba'athists returned to power
- Ahmed Hassan al-Bakr became president
- Saddam became deputy (real power behind throne)

### Consolidating Control (1968-1979)

**Building Power Base**:
- Controlled security apparatus
- Filled positions with Tikritis and loyalists
- Eliminated rivals systematically
- Used oil revenues for patronage

**Modernization Programs**:
- Infrastructure development
- Universal education
- Healthcare expansion
- Reduced illiteracy
- Women's rights (relative to region)

## Total Power (1979-2003)

### The 1979 Purge

**July 16, 1979**:
- Bakr "resigned"
- Saddam became president
- Immediately staged purge

**Palace of the Republic Purge**:
- Called Ba'ath Party meeting
- Read names of "conspirators"
- Videotaped proceedings
- Named individuals led out and executed
- Survivors forced to participate in executions
- 68 executed, hundreds imprisoned
- Message: Total control, trust no one

### Cult of Personality

**Omnipresence**:
- Statues and murals everywhere
- Schools started day pledging loyalty
- Birthday national holiday
- Multiple palaces
- Portrayed as modern Nebuchadnezzar

**Titles**:
- "Glorious Leader"
- "Direct Descendant of Prophet Muhammad"
- "Father of all Iraqis"

**Image Cultivation**:
- Photos in various costumes (soldier, Bedouin, farmer, businessman)
- Claimed to write novels and poems
- Presented as warrior, intellectual, father figure

## Iran-Iraq War (1980-1988)

### The Conflict

**Invasion** (September 1980):
- Attacked Iran after revolution
- Hoped to exploit Iranian chaos
- Grab oil-rich Khuzestan
- Quick victory expected

**Brutal Stalemate**:
- Iranian resistance fierce
- War bogged down in trenches
- Human wave attacks
- Chemical weapons used
- 1 million casualties total

**Chemical Weapons**:
- Used against Iranian troops
- International condemnation muted
- Western nations supported Iraq against Iran

**Outcome**:
- Eight years, no winner
- Massive debt
- Military not as strong as believed
- Iraq exhausted

## Kurdish Genocide (Anfal Campaign)

### Al-Anfal (1986-1989)

**Systematic Campaign**:
- Targeted Kurdish population
- "Arabization" of Kurdistan
- Chemical Ali (Ali Hassan al-Majid) led campaign

**Halabja Gas Attack** (March 16, 1988):
- Chemical weapons on Kurdish town
- 5,000 civilians killed in hours
- Worst chemical weapons attack on civilians
- Images shocked world
- Saddam denied, blamed Iran

**Anfal Operations**:
- 182,000 Kurds killed
- 4,000 villages destroyed
- Forced relocations
- Mass graves
- Genocide recognized internationally

---

## Gulf War (1990-1991)

### Invasion of Kuwait

**August 2, 1990**:
- Iraqi forces invaded Kuwait
- Claimed Kuwait historically part of Iraq
- Debt from Iran-Iraq War
- Oil price manipulation accusations

**International Response**:
- UN condemned
- Sanctions imposed
- Coalition formed (U.S.-led, 35 nations)
- Deadline for withdrawal: January 15, 1991

### Operation Desert Storm

**Air Campaign** (January-February 1991):
- 42 days of bombing
- Iraqi military devastated
- Infrastructure destroyed

**Ground War** (February 24-28):
- 100-hour ground campaign
- Iraqi forces routed
- Kuwait liberated
- Highway of Death: Fleeing Iraqi column destroyed

**Outcome**:
- Kuwait liberated
- Saddam remained in power
- Sanctions continued
- No-fly zones imposed

## The Sanctions Era (1991-2003)

### Suffering Under Sanctions

**UN Sanctions**:
- Trade embargo
- Oil-for-Food program (limited)
- Humanitarian crisis

**Iraqi People**:
- 500,000 children estimated died from sanctions effects
- Healthcare system collapsed
- Malnutrition widespread
- Saddam blamed West, built support

**Saddam's Response**:
- Built palaces during sanctions
- Elite lived well
- Manipulated Oil-for-Food program
- Used suffering for propaganda

### Containment and Cat-and-Mouse

**UN Weapons Inspectors**:
- Searching for WMDs
- Saddam obstructed
- Inspectors withdrawn (1998)
- U.S./UK bombing (Desert Fox)

**Survival**:
- Saddam outlasted Bush Sr., Clinton
- Maintained grip on power
- Crushed 1991 uprisings (Shia and Kurd)
- Used elite Republican Guard

---

## 2003 Iraq War

### Bush Administration and WMDs

**Post-9/11**:
- Bush labeled Iraq part of "Axis of Evil"
- Claims of WMD stockpiles
- Alleged Al-Qaeda links (false)
- UN inspections resumed (2002)

**March 2003**:
- U.S./UK invaded without clear UN mandate
- "Shock and Awe" bombing
- Ground invasion from Kuwait

### Fall of Baghdad

**April 9, 2003**:
- Baghdad fell to coalition
- Saddam statue toppled (iconic image)
- Saddam fled
- State collapsed
- Looting widespread

**On the Run**:
- Months in hiding
- Moved between safe houses
- Stayed with loyalists
- Evaded capture for 8 months

---

## Capture, Trial, and Execution

### Captured

**December 13, 2003**:
- Found in "spider hole" near Tikrit
- Operation Red Dawn
- Disheveled, bearded
- Armed but did not resist
- "I am Saddam Hussein, President of Iraq, and I am willing to negotiate"
- "President Bush sends his regards"

### Trial

**October 2005 - November 2006**:
- Iraqi High Tribunal
- Charged with crimes against humanity
- Dujail massacre case (148 Shias killed in 1982)
- Defiant in court
- Shouted at judges
- Removed from courtroom multiple times

**Verdict** (November 5, 2006):
- Guilty
- Death by hanging
- Appeals rejected

### Execution

**December 30, 2006**:
- Hanged at Camp Justice, Baghdad
- Eid al-Adha (controversial timing)
- Video leaked showing taunting by guards
- Maintained composure
- Last words praising Palestine
- Body returned to tribe for burial

## Legacy

### Iraq After Saddam

**Immediate Impact**:
- State institutions destroyed
- Sectarian violence exploded
- Al-Qaeda in Iraq emerged (became ISIS)
- Civil war conditions

**Long-Term**:
- Ongoing instability
- Iranian influence increased
- Sunni-Shia divide deepened
- Hundreds of thousands killed post-invasion

### Historical Assessment

**Brutal Dictator**:
- Hundreds of thousands killed
- Genocide against Kurds
- Wars killed millions
- Torture and repression

**Modernization**:
- Developed infrastructure (pre-sanctions)
- Education and healthcare improved (1970s-80s)
- Secularized society relative to neighbors

**Counterfactual Debate**:
- Was Iraq better under Saddam than after?
- Would containment have worked?
- WMD claims false; invasion justified?
- Regional stability vs. human rights
