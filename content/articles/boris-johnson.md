## Summary

British PM who was found to have misled Parliament over 'Partygate'—social gatherings at Downing Street during COVID lockdowns. He received a fixed penalty notice for breaking laws his own government set. He resigned under pressure.

## Additional context

- Before becoming prime minister, Johnson worked as a journalist and served as Mayor of London and as a Member of Parliament.
- He became a prominent national figure during debates over Brexit and the UK's relationship with the European Union.

## Legacy and assessment

- His premiership is commonly discussed in relation to Brexit implementation, COVID-era governance, and standards in public life.
- The scandals that culminated in his resignation renewed attention on parliamentary accountability and ministerial conduct.
