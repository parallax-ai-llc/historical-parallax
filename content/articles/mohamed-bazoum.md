## Additional Context

### Core facts already established above
- Elected President of Niger in 2021.
- Overthrown in a 2023 military coup and detained.
- His removal triggered a broader regional crisis in West Africa.

### Why coups create regional crises
- Neighboring states often fear spillover: refugees, cross-border militant activity, and economic disruption.
- Diplomatic standoffs can delay coordination on security and humanitarian needs.
- Detention of an elected leader raises questions about constitutional order and due process.

### Aftermath questions readers usually track
- Whether negotiations lead to release, trial, exile, or continued detention.
- Whether elections are scheduled and what conditions are set for a return to civilian rule.
- How quickly institutions (courts, parliament, security forces) align with the new order.
