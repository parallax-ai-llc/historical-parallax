## Attack overview

The Westgate shopping mall in Nairobi was attacked on 21 September 2013 by militants associated with al-Shabaab. The assault involved firearms and grenades and rapidly turned into a siege as civilians and security forces became trapped in and around the building.

## Siege and response

The incident lasted several days. Key high-level elements include:

- Attackers moving through the mall and targeting people inside.
- Kenyan police and military units responding and attempting to clear the complex.
- A prolonged standoff as security forces worked to evacuate civilians and locate attackers.

The operation concluded after the siege ended, though early reporting and later investigations debated aspects of the response and command coordination.

## Casualties and impact

The attack killed dozens of people and injured many more, including Kenyan citizens and foreign nationals. It had major effects on public perceptions of security in Kenya and on the country's counterterrorism posture.

## Investigation and accountability

Authorities pursued investigations into the attackers' networks, financing, and logistical support. The event also prompted scrutiny of mall security planning and emergency procedures.

## Broader significance

Westgate is frequently cited as a landmark al-Shabaab attack outside Somalia and as an example of how militant groups can target civilian public spaces to generate international attention and political pressure.
