## Summary

Carly Fiorina became the first woman to lead a Fortune 20 company when she was named CEO of Hewlett-Packard in 1999. She oversaw the controversial $25 billion merger with Compaq Computer in 2002. Despite growing HP's revenue, she was forced out in 2005 amid declining stock price and internal conflicts. She later ran for the U.S. Senate in California and briefly for the Republican presidential nomination in 2016.
