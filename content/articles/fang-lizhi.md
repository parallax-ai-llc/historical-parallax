## Summary

Fang Lizhi was a Chinese astrophysicist who made contributions to cosmology and the physics of the early universe. He became famous as China's most prominent pro-democracy advocate in the 1980s, earning the nickname 'China's Andrei Sakharov.' After the 1989 Tiananmen Square protests, he sought refuge in the US embassy and later taught at the University of Arizona.
