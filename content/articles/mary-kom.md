## Summary

Mary Kom is a six-time World Amateur Boxing Champion and won an Olympic bronze at 2012 London. Known as 'Magnificent Mary,' she is the most decorated female amateur boxer.

## Athletic significance

Mary Kom's record is notable both for its longevity and for spanning multiple weight categories across different eras of amateur boxing. In elite amateur systems, staying competitive over many cycles typically requires adapting to evolving rules, scoring interpretations, and international competition depth.

Her Olympic bronze at London 2012 was especially significant because women's boxing debuted as an Olympic sport at those Games. As a result, her medal is often treated as part of a broader milestone for the sport in India.

## Style and training (high-level)

Boxers who succeed repeatedly at the world level generally combine technical defense, high work rate, and tactical control of distance. Commentary about Mary Kom frequently emphasizes ring intelligence and consistency in tournament settings.

## Public role and recognition

Beyond competition, she has been widely recognized in India as a sports icon, frequently referenced in discussions about women's participation in combat sports and access to training pathways outside major metropolitan centers.

Her career is often used as an example of how individual excellence can shift national attention toward underrepresented sports and regions, and how sustained international performance can translate into institutional support for development programs.

## Legacy

As a multi-time world champion, Mary Kom is commonly cited as a benchmark for subsequent generations of Indian boxers. Her achievements have helped normalize the idea of long-term professional identity and leadership for women athletes in Indian sport.

## Note

Many summaries highlight her as a role model for young athletes, particularly girls entering competitive boxing.
