## Summary

Filipino president and son of dictator Ferdinand Marcos. He was convicted of tax evasion in 1995 but the conviction was overturned. He has been accused of historical revisionism regarding his father's dictatorship and the Marcos family's stolen wealth.

## Additional Context

### Background and family legacy

Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr. is the son of former Philippine president Ferdinand Marcos and former first lady Imelda Marcos. His national political career has often been discussed in the context of the Marcos family's return to power after the 1986 People Power Revolution and the subsequent debates over alleged ill-gotten wealth.

### Presidency (selected themes)

High-level issues commonly associated with his administration and public discourse include:

- Institutional trust and the historical record of martial law under his father.
- Governance and anti-corruption expectations in light of long-running allegations about the Marcos era.
- Foreign policy balancing among regional partners and major powers.

### Public controversy

Critics have accused Marcos Jr. and allied networks of historical revisionism regarding the martial-law period. Supporters have emphasized economic development and political stability narratives. These competing frames have made memory politics a central part of his public image.
