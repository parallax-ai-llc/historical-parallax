## Summary

Brazilian crime boss known as Marcola who leads the Primeiro Comando da Capital (PCC), Brazil's most powerful criminal organization. From prison, he directed the 2006 São Paulo attacks that shut down the city.

## Background and context

- **PCC (Primeiro Comando da Capital)** emerged inside São Paulo's prison system and grew into a nationwide criminal network involved in drug trafficking, extortion, and prison governance.
- Marcola is frequently described in reporting and court narratives as a central strategist and coordinator for PCC activities, including actions ordered from custody.

## 2006 São Paulo attacks (high level)

In May 2006, waves of coordinated violence and disruptions hit the São Paulo region, including attacks on police targets and disruptions to public transit and commerce. The events drew national and international attention and intensified debates over prison control, organized crime, and public security policy in Brazil.

## Incarceration and federal transfers

Brazil periodically uses **federal maximum-security facilities** to isolate high-profile organized-crime leaders from local prison systems, reducing influence over communications and internal prison hierarchies. Camacho's transfer to a federal unit is typically framed in this context: limiting command-and-control from within the prison network.
