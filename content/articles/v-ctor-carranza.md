## Summary

Colombian emerald magnate known as the 'Emerald Czar' who was accused of funding paramilitary death squads and ordering massacres. He was acquitted of all charges.

## Additional Context

## Background

Carranza built his fortune in Colombia’s emerald trade, a sector that for decades mixed legitimate mining and export activity with violent competition for control of deposits and routes.[^1]

## Role in the emerald industry

The term “Emerald Czar” is commonly used to describe his prominence in the Boyacá region’s emerald economy. That prominence was linked to both business influence (mining, buying, exporting) and the ability to shape local security arrangements in areas where the state presence was uneven.[^1]

## Allegations and investigations

During Colombia’s internal armed conflict, multiple actors—including guerrillas, paramilitaries, and private security networks—were implicated in abuses. Carranza was accused by some investigators and witnesses of supporting paramilitary structures or benefiting from their operations, but he repeatedly denied wrongdoing and was not ultimately convicted.[^1]

## Public legacy

Carranza’s legacy is often described as dual: a symbol of wealth and regional power in the emerald sector, and a figure surrounded by persistent allegations connected to violence during the conflict era.[^1]
