## Summary

Dawn Fraser won the 100m freestyle at three consecutive Olympics (1956, 1960, 1964), one of only three swimmers to do so.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1956 | Won 100m freestyle gold at Melbourne |

## Early Life and Training

- Fraser grew up in Australia and developed as a competitive swimmer during the postwar rise of organized sport and international competition.
- Her success is often associated with a training culture that emphasized endurance, race pace, and resilience under pressure.

## Olympic Achievements

Fraser’s most famous accomplishment is winning the **women’s 100 m freestyle** at three consecutive Olympic Games:

- **1956 (Melbourne)**
- **1960 (Rome)**
- **1964 (Tokyo)**

This kind of repeat dominance is rare in sprint swimming, where small changes in form, conditioning, and competition can quickly change results.

## World Records and Event Strength

High-level summaries of her career commonly note:

- She set world records in sprint freestyle events.
- Her 100 m freestyle performances helped define the benchmark for women’s sprint swimming in her era.
- She was known for powerful starts, aggressive pacing, and strong finishing speed.

## 1964 Controversy and Suspension

After the Tokyo Olympics, Fraser was involved in incidents that led to a **four-year ban** from competitive swimming under Australian sporting authorities. Accounts describe this period as a major disruption to her career and a notable episode in Olympic-era sports governance.

## Later Life and Public Role

- Fraser remained a prominent public figure in Australian sport, often referenced in discussions about Olympic history and national sporting identity.
- She has been recognized for her contributions to Australian athletics beyond her medal record.

## Legacy

- Remembered as one of Australia’s most successful and influential swimmers.
- Her three consecutive Olympic titles in the same event remain a widely cited milestone in the sport.
