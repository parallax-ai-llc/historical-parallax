Saudi royal adviser who allegedly oversaw the assassination of journalist Jamal Khashoggi via Skype.

## Background

Al-Qahtani emerged as a prominent media and political operative within the Saudi royal court, becoming closely associated with Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman (MBS). Public reporting has described him as a key figure in shaping messaging, managing online campaigns, and advising on information strategy.

## Role in Saudi media and online influence

Open-source accounts have linked al-Qahtani to efforts to organize pro-government messaging online, including networks of accounts used to amplify official narratives and attack critics. Multiple international outlets and researchers have referred to these as coordinated influence operations.

## Allegations related to the Khashoggi killing

Following the October 2018 killing of Saudi journalist Jamal Khashoggi at the Saudi consulate in Istanbul, Turkish officials and international media reports alleged that al-Qahtani helped direct or coordinate elements of the operation. A widely cited claim was that he was connected to the team via communications during the incident.

Saudi authorities announced his dismissal from official roles in 2018 amid international scrutiny. Subsequent Saudi proceedings against other defendants were criticized by some observers for limited transparency; al-Qahtani was not publicly presented as a defendant in those trials.

## Aftermath and international response

Several governments imposed measures in response to the Khashoggi case, including sanctions and travel restrictions targeting individuals alleged to have been involved. U.S. Treasury statements in 2018 identified al-Qahtani among sanctioned individuals connected to the killing.

## Additional notes

- **Official status**: Reported removed from his position in 2018; later status has been the subject of periodic media reporting.
- **Public profile**: Limited public appearances after 2018 compared with his prior visibility.
