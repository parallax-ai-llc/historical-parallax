Founder of the Shower Posse, one of Jamaica's most violent drug gangs, who died in a suspicious prison fire.

## Background

- **Nickname**: Commonly referred to as **"Jim Brown"**.
- **Organization**: Widely described as a leading figure in the **Shower Posse**, a Jamaican gang associated with drug trafficking and violence.
- **Geographic links**: The Shower Posse is often discussed in connection with **Kingston** (including Tivoli Gardens) and with operations extending into **the United States**.

## Historical Context (High-Level)

- The Shower Posse emerged in a period when Jamaica’s political and community "garrison" dynamics intersected with organized violence.
- In many accounts, the gang’s influence is framed as spanning both **local community control** and **transnational narcotics distribution**.

## Extradition and Death (Overview)

- Coke died in **February 1992** while in custody and awaiting extradition.
- His death occurred in a **prison fire**, which has been described as suspicious in multiple summaries of the case.

## Legacy and Significance

- Coke is frequently cited as a prominent example in discussions of:
  - **Jamaican organized crime** in the late 20th century
  - The linkage between **local gangs** and **U.S. drug markets**
  - The risks and controversies surrounding **extradition** and high-profile detention
