## Summary

Svetlana Khorkina won Olympic gold on uneven bars in 1996 and 2000 and was a two-time World all-around champion. She dominated gymnastics despite being unusually tall for the sport.

## Notable Skills and Style

Khorkina was especially known for her uneven bars work, where her combination of height, long lines, and originality influenced how routines were constructed for the apparatus. She competed across the full range of women’s artistic gymnastics events, but bars became her signature event in major championships.

## Major Achievements (High Level)

- Olympic champion on uneven bars (1996, 2000)
- Multiple-time World champion, including all-around world titles in the late 1990s
- A long senior career that spanned several Olympic cycles, which is relatively uncommon at the highest level of women’s artistic gymnastics

## Post-Competition Life

After retiring from elite competition, she remained a prominent public figure in Russia and continued to be associated with sport and public life. Former Olympic champions often transition into roles such as commentary, sports administration, or public service; Khorkina’s public profile remained notable in the years after her last Olympics.

## Legacy

Khorkina is frequently cited in discussions of late-1990s and early-2000s women’s artistic gymnastics as one of the defining athletes of the era. Her success on uneven bars and longevity at the top level made her a reference point for later generations of gymnasts and for the evolution of routine composition on bars.
