## Summary

Tsung-Dao Lee was a Chinese-American physicist who shared the 1957 Nobel Prize in Physics with Chen-Ning Yang for predicting parity violation in weak interactions. At 30, he was one of the youngest Nobel laureates in Physics. He made important contributions to particle physics, statistical mechanics, and field theory. Lee was instrumental in establishing scientific exchanges between China and the United States.
