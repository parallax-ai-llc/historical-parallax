## Summary

Salvadoran president who declared a state of exception in 2022 that has led to the mass arrest of over 80,000 people with widespread reports of torture and wrongful detention. He consolidated power, packed the courts, and won re-election despite constitutional ban.

## Political Background

Bukele rose to national prominence after serving in local government and positioning himself as an outsider to El Salvador's traditional party system. His political brand emphasized direct communication, media savvy, and a focus on security and anti-corruption messaging.

## Security Policy and the State of Exception

The 2022 state of exception was framed by the government as an emergency response to gang violence and public insecurity. Under the policy, authorities conducted large-scale arrests and expanded detention capacity. Human rights organizations and journalists have reported concerns including due process violations, harsh prison conditions, and wrongful detention, while supporters have credited the policy with sharply reducing visible street violence.

## Governance and Institutions

Bukele's administration has been described as highly centralized, with repeated clashes with institutional checks such as courts and oversight bodies. Critics argue that changes to judicial and electoral institutions weakened separation of powers, while the government has argued it is implementing reforms against entrenched interests.

## Economic and Technology Initiatives

Bukele's government pursued high-profile economic initiatives intended to attract investment and tourism, including policies associated with cryptocurrency and digital-economy branding. These moves drew international attention and debate about fiscal risk, financial inclusion, and regulatory compliance.

## International Relations and Criticism

Bukele has faced scrutiny from foreign governments and international organizations over civil liberties and constitutional norms. At the same time, his administration has used improved security metrics and public approval to defend its approach and to claim a mandate for continued governance.
