## Summary

Christa McAuliffe was selected as the first Teacher in Space by NASA in 1985. She was killed along with six crewmates when the Space Shuttle Challenger disintegrated 73 seconds after launch on January 28, 1986. Her death shocked the nation and led to major changes in NASA's safety culture. She became a symbol of the risks and aspirations of space exploration.

## Additional Context

- **Teacher in Space Project:** NASA’s program aimed to send an educator to orbit to inspire students and the general public, with McAuliffe selected after a highly publicized national search.
- **Challenger (STS-51-L):** The mission was intended to deploy a communications satellite and conduct other activities; the loss occurred shortly after liftoff, with the breakup visible on live television.
- **Impact on NASA:** The disaster triggered the Rogers Commission investigation and a major reassessment of launch decision-making, risk communication, and organizational culture.

## Legacy

- **Education initiatives:** McAuliffe’s name is attached to schools, scholarships, and education centers focused on science education and space-related learning.
- **Cultural memory:** She is frequently referenced as a symbol of civilian participation in spaceflight and the human costs of high-risk exploration.
