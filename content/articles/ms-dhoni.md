## Summary

MS Dhoni captained India to victories in the 2007 T20 World Cup, 2011 World Cup, and 2013 Champions Trophy. He is known for his calm leadership and finishing ability.

## Early Life and Rise

- **Domestic breakthrough**: Dhoni rose through Indian domestic cricket and became known as an aggressive wicket-keeper batter with unusual finishing ability.
- **Rapid elevation**: He became a central figure in India’s limited-overs setup shortly after debut, and was soon appointed captain.

## Captaincy and Playing Style

- **Leadership reputation**: He is widely characterized as calm under pressure and pragmatic about tactics, especially in limited-overs cricket.
- **Finisher archetype**: In many match narratives, Dhoni is associated with chasing targets and controlling the final overs through strike rotation and calculated risk.
- **Wicket-keeping**: He is also known for quick stumpings and efficient glove work, becoming one of the era’s most recognizable wicket-keeper captains.

## Major Team Achievements

- **2007 T20 World Cup**: Captained a young Indian side to the inaugural title.
- **2011 ODI World Cup**: Led India to its first World Cup win since 1983, with Dhoni’s match-winning innings in the final often highlighted.
- **2013 Champions Trophy**: India won the tournament under his leadership, completing a rare set of ICC limited-overs trophies.

## Franchise Cricket (IPL)

Dhoni became strongly associated with the Chennai Super Kings (CSK) in the Indian Premier League, where he:

- served as a long-term captain,
- was credited with building a stable team culture,
- and remained a marquee player beyond his international retirement.

## Legacy

In Indian cricket history, Dhoni is frequently cited for:

- modernizing limited-overs captaincy expectations,
- serving as a transition leader between generations,
- and setting a template for wicket-keeper captains at the highest level.
