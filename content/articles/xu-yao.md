## Background

Xu Yao is known publicly for his involvement in the 2020 poisoning of Lin Qi, the founder of Chinese game company Yoozoo Games.[^1]

Lin Qi was a prominent figure in China’s gaming industry and was associated with the acquisition of rights related to *The Three-Body Problem* franchise through Yoozoo.[^1]

## Poisoning Case (2020)

According to reporting summarized on Wikipedia, Xu Yao was a colleague/business associate of Lin Qi and the poisoning was linked to a business dispute.[^1]

Lin Qi became ill in late 2020 and was hospitalized; he later died.[^1]

## Investigation and Arrest

Chinese authorities investigated Lin Qi’s sudden illness and death and ultimately detained Xu Yao in connection with the case.[^1]

The case drew widespread media attention in China because of Lin Qi’s public profile and the high-value business interests connected to his company.[^1]

## Trial and Conviction

Xu Yao was prosecuted for the poisoning and was convicted in a Chinese court.[^1]

Sentencing details and exact charges can vary by translation and reporting; the core outcome is that Xu Yao was found guilty for Lin Qi’s death.[^1]

## Aftermath and Significance

The death of Lin Qi prompted renewed attention to corporate security, governance, and internal disputes at high-growth technology firms in China.[^1]

The event also intersected with broader public interest in Yoozoo Games’ business activities and entertainment investments during that period.[^1]
