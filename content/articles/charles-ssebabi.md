## Summary

Ugandan serial killer who murdered at least 26 women in Kampala between 2007 and 2008. He strangled victims and disposed of their bodies in swamps.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2007 | Began killing women in Kampala |
| 2008 | Arrested after being identified by a surviving victim |
| 2010 | Convicted and sentenced to death |

## Investigation and Prosecution (High-Level)

Public accounts of the case emphasize the investigative challenges of linking multiple crimes and building prosecutable evidence. Identification by a surviving victim is often described as a pivotal moment that led to arrest and subsequent conviction.

## Social Impact

- Raised public concern about personal safety and crime prevention in Kampala.
- Demonstrated the importance of timely reporting, community awareness, and investigative follow-through in serial crime cases.
- Brought renewed attention to support for victims and their families, including the need for trauma-informed services.

## Legal Context

The conviction and sentencing occurred within Uganda's criminal justice system, where capital punishment has been part of sentencing law even as its application can vary over time.
