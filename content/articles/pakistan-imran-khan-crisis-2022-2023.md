## Summary

In April 2022, Imran Khan became the first Pakistani prime minister removed through a parliamentary no-confidence vote. But that was only the beginning. For the next two years, Khan transformed from deposed premier to iconic opposition leader, leading mass rallies that shook Pakistan's military-dominated political order. His message was simple: he was ousted by a "foreign conspiracy" backed by the United States and enabled by Pakistan's powerful military establishment.[^1]

Khan's Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party mobilized millions. The "Azadi March" to Islamabad became a recurring spectacle. In November 2022, Khan survived an assassination attempt. In May 2023, his arrest triggered the worst violence Pakistan had seen in yearsâ€”PTI supporters attacked military installations, and the state responded with mass arrests. Khan was imprisoned in August 2023, disqualified from politics, and has remained behind bars as of late 2024.[^2]

The crisis exposed Pakistan's fundamental contradiction: a nominal democracy where the military has historically made and unmade governments. Khan, once a military favorite, became its greatest threat. His imprisonment created a martyr figure but left PTI leaderless. The 2024 elections, widely seen as rigged against PTI, produced a fragile government while Khan's millions of supporters simmer. Pakistan's political future remains uncertain.[^3]

## Historical Background

### Pakistan's Political Pattern

**The military's role:**

- Ruled directly 1958-1971, 1977-1988, 1999-2008
- Even in "democratic" periods, sets foreign policy, security
- Makes and breaks civilian governments
- Current Chief: General Asim Munir (2022-present)

**Imran Khan's rise:**

- Cricket legend; national hero
- Founded PTI (1996)
- Won 2018 election (with military support, critics say)
- "Naya Pakistan" (New Pakistan) promised

**His government (2018-2022):**

- Anti-corruption rhetoric
- Economic struggles (inflation, debt)
- Tensions with military grew
- Lost military support by 2022[^4]

### The No-Confidence Vote

**March-April 2022:**

- Opposition filed no-confidence motion
- Khan claimed "foreign conspiracy" (US cipher)
- National Assembly speaker blocked vote (unconstitutional)
- Supreme Court ordered vote proceed

**April 10, 2022:**

- **Khan lost vote 174-0** (PTI boycotted)
- First PM removed by parliament
- Shehbaz Sharif became PM
- Khan claimed coup/conspiracy[^5]

## The Protests

### Immediate Aftermath (April 2022)

**Khan's narrative:**

- "Imported government"
- "Regime change conspiracy"
- US wanted him out for opposing Ukraine policy
- Military ("Establishment") collaborated

**Mass rallies:**

- Huge turnout for PTI
- Lahore, Karachi, Islamabad
- Demanded early elections

### May 2022: "Azadi March" I

**May 25, 2022:**

- Khan led march toward Islamabad
- Tens of thousands participated
- Police clashed with protesters
- Government blocked entry to capital
- Marchers dispersed after 5 days

**The pattern was set: massive PTI mobilization, government resistance.**[^6]

### October-November 2022: "Haqeeqi Azadi March"

**October 28, 2022:**

- Khan began long march from Lahore
- Slow progress toward Rawalpindi/Islamabad

**November 3, 2022:**

- **Khan shot during rally** (Wazirabad)
- Wounded in leg (assassination attempt)
- Rally halted
- Khan accused government, military figures

**The attack:**

- Gunman apprehended
- Murky investigation
- Khan survived; became even more defiant

**The march eventually reached Rawalpindi; ended without achieving elections.**[^7]

### May 9, 2023: The Explosion

**May 9, 2023:**

- Khan arrested from Islamabad High Court
- Paramilitary forces publicly seized him
- Video spread: dramatic, humiliating arrest

**Immediate response:**

- **Unprecedented violence**
- PTI supporters attacked military installations
- Army headquarters (GHQ) in Rawalpindi targeted
- Corps commander's residence in Lahore stormed
- ISI building attacked

**This was unprecedented:** Attacking the army is unthinkable in Pakistan's political culture.

**Casualties:** Several killed; hundreds injured

**State response:**

- Supreme Court declared arrest unlawful (May 11)
- Khan released
- But: Massive crackdown on PTI began
- Thousands arrested
- Military courts for civilians announced[^8]

### August 2023: Imprisonment

**August 5, 2023:**

- Khan arrested again (Lahore)
- Convicted in Toshakhana case (misuse of state gifts)
- Sentenced to 3 years
- **Disqualified from politics for 5 years**

**Since then:**

- Held in Adiala Jail (later shifted)
- Multiple additional cases
- As of late 2024: Remains imprisoned
- Over 100 cases filed against him[^9]

## 2024 Elections and After

### February 2024 Elections

**Context:**

- PTI officially barred (symbols denied)
- Khan imprisoned, couldn't campaign
- Major crackdown on party

**Results:**

- PTI-backed independents won most seats
- But couldn't form government
- Coalition of PMLN + PPP formed government
- Shehbaz Sharif remained PM

**Widespread allegations of rigging:**

- Delayed results
- Mysterious changes
- International observers skeptical

**PTI claimed victory was stolen.**

### Current Situation (Late 2024)

**Khan:**

- Still imprisoned
- Multiple sentences (some overturned on appeal)
- Health concerns reported
- Denies all charges

**PTI:**

- Weakened but not destroyed
- Millions still support Khan
- Party structure damaged
- Future uncertain[^10]

## Historical Assessment

### What Does This Mean?

**For Pakistan:**

- Military's power confirmed
- Democracy's limits exposed
- Popular will can be suppressed
- But: Can't eliminate Khan's support base

**Khan's significance:**

- First leader to directly challenge military publicly
- Mobilized urban middle class
- Made "establishment" speakable
- But: Imprisoned, possibly finished politically

### Comparison

| Pakistani Crisis   | Nature             | Outcome                               |
| ------------------ | ------------------ | ------------------------------------- |
| 1977 (Bhutto)      | Military coup      | Bhutto executed                       |
| 1999 (Sharif)      | Military coup      | Sharif exiled                         |
| **2022-24 (Khan)** | **"Soft" removal** | **Khan imprisoned; party suppressed** |

**Khan's case: Not a formal coup, but similar result.**

### The Question

**Can PTI survive without Khan?**

- Movement depends on him personally
- No clear successor
- But: Millions remember

**Will Khan ever be free?**

- Political decisions, not legal
- Depends on establishment's calculations

## Legacy

### For Pakistan

- Shows democracy's fragility
- Military remains paramount
- Popular movements can be crushed
- But resentment doesn't disappear

### For Khan

- From cricket hero to PM to prisoner
- Polarizing figure
- Either a martyr or a demagogue, depending on view
- His chapter may not be finished[^11]
