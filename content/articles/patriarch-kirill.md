## Background

- Kirill (secular name: Vladimir Mikhailovich Gundyayev) is a senior cleric of the Russian Orthodox Church.
- He became Patriarch of Moscow and all Rus’ in 2009, making him the head of the Church’s central leadership.
- Prior to becoming patriarch, he held senior posts associated with church administration and external relations.

## Key Roles (high level)

- **Patriarch of Moscow and all Rus’ (2009–present)**: chief hierarch of the Russian Orthodox Church.
- **Metropolitan of Smolensk and Kaliningrad (prior to 2009)**: a prominent diocesan leadership role before his election as patriarch.
- **External/Inter-church relations (prior to 2009)**: known internationally for work that involved relations with other churches and religious bodies.

## Public Positions and Controversies

- Widely reported as aligning publicly with the Russian state on major national issues, especially after the 2022 invasion of Ukraine.
- Critics argue his public statements and institutional stance have contributed to legitimizing the war.
- Supporters and some church officials frame his position in terms of religious, historical, and national unity narratives.

## Sanctions (selected)

- **United Kingdom (2022)**: sanctioned following assessments that he supported Russia’s invasion of Ukraine.
- Sanctions coverage is commonly tied to his role as a prominent public figure rather than to operational military involvement.

## Why He Matters in Modern History (context)

- As patriarch, Kirill is one of the most visible religious leaders in the Russian-speaking world.
- The Russian Orthodox Church is an important institution in Russia’s public life; its leadership can influence cultural narratives and legitimacy.
- Internationally, his stance has affected ecumenical relationships and perceptions of Russian religious diplomacy.
