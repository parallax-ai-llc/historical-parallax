## Summary

Park Chung-bin (Sotaesan) was the founder of Won Buddhism (Wonbulgyo), a reformed Buddhist movement in Korea. He experienced a great enlightenment in 1916 and established the Order of Won Buddhism in 1924. He taught that the entire universe is the Buddha and advocated for a practical Buddhism adapted to modern life. Won Buddhism emphasizes social engagement, gender equality, and the integration of spiritual practice with daily life.
