## Summary

Known as 'La Tuta,' leader of the Knights Templar cartel who styled himself as a teacher and revolutionary. Ran his cartel with pseudo-religious rituals and extortion of avocado farmers.

## Additional Context

- **Role:** Servando Gómez Martínez, widely known as **"La Tuta"**, was a prominent leader of the Mexican criminal organization **Knights Templar Cartel** (Caballeros Templarios).
- **Region of influence:** The group was strongly associated with the state of **Michoacán**, where it built local influence through intimidation, extortion, and propaganda.
- **Public profile:** Unlike many cartel figures, Gómez became notable for a relatively visible public persona, including recorded messages used for intimidation and narrative control.

## Criminal Activities (High-Level)

- **Extortion and control:** The cartel was linked to systematic extortion of businesses and local industries, including agriculture and mining-related activities.
- **Violence and coercion:** The organization was associated with targeted killings, forced displacement, and coercion of local officials and communities.
- **Fragmentation and conflict:** Intensifying pressure from the Mexican state and rival groups contributed to internal fractures and shifting alliances.

## Arrest and Aftermath

- **Capture:** Gómez was captured by Mexican security forces in 2015.
- **Legal proceedings:** He faced prosecutions connected to organized crime activities and related offenses.
- **Broader impact:** His arrest was treated as a symbolic blow to the Knights Templar, though organized crime dynamics in the region continued to evolve afterward.

## Key Points

- The Knights Templar combined criminal violence with messaging that portrayed itself as a community-linked movement.
- High-profile arrests weakened leadership structures, but did not by themselves resolve underlying security and governance issues.
