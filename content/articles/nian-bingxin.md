## Summary

Chinese serial killer who murdered 12 women in Henan Province between 1999 and 2006. He lured sex workers and strangled them. One of China's most prolific modern serial killers. Executed.

## Crimes and Modus Operandi

Reporting on Nian Bingxin describes a pattern of targeting vulnerable women, including sex workers, and using deception to isolate victims. Accounts commonly note strangulation as the primary method of killing and, in some reports, additional post-mortem violence and robbery.

## Geographic Scope

The murders were linked to locations in Henan Province, with cases attributed to a multi-year period beginning in the late 1990s and extending into the mid-2000s.

## Investigation and Arrest

Chinese authorities connected multiple cases through forensic and investigative work over several years. Nian was arrested in 2006, after which police attributed additional unsolved killings to him during interrogation and case review.

## Trial and Sentencing

He was tried in China’s criminal court system, convicted of multiple murders, and sentenced to death. He was executed in 2008.

## Significance

The case is often cited in discussions of modern Chinese serial homicide and the challenges of investigating crimes against marginalized victims. It also drew attention to how long-running series can persist when victims have limited social protection and cases are dispersed across jurisdictions.
