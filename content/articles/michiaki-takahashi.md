## Summary

Michiaki Takahashi developed the first varicella (chickenpox) vaccine in 1974, using an attenuated live virus strain called Oka. His vaccine has been administered to hundreds of millions of children worldwide, virtually eliminating deaths from chickenpox. The Oka strain remains the basis of all chickenpox vaccines used today.
