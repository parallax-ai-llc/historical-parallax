## Summary

Lalisa Manobal, known professionally as Lisa, is a Thai singer, rapper, and dancer who rose to global fame as a member of BLACKPINK, the South Korean girl group formed by YG Entertainment in 2016. She is widely regarded as one of the most skilled dancers in the K-pop industry and has become a major cultural ambassador bridging Southeast Asian and global pop music.

Lisa's appeal extends well beyond music. Her fashion-forward image, social media dominance, and solo ventures have positioned her as a standalone global superstar. With hundreds of millions of followers across platforms, she consistently ranks among the most-followed musicians in the world, making her a dominant force in celebrity and pop culture.

Her 2021 solo debut broke records and demonstrated that her individual brand was as powerful as the group's collective identity. Since then, she has expanded into entertainment, fashion, and independent music, cementing her status as a generational talent whose influence spans continents.

## Early Life & Background

Born on March 27, 1997, in Buriram, Thailand, Pranpriya Manobal — later legally renamed Lalisa — showed an early aptitude for dance. She grew up in a middle-class family and began formal dance training as a child, studying at the School of Performing Arts Bangkok. At age 14, she auditioned for YG Entertainment in Thailand, beating out thousands of competitors to become one of the label's trainees in Seoul, South Korea. She trained for approximately five years before debuting, learning Korean, Japanese, and honing her performance skills.

## Rise to Prominence

Lisa debuted with BLACKPINK on August 8, 2016, alongside Jisoo, Jennie, and Rosé. The group quickly became one of the best-selling girl groups in history, and Lisa's dancing and rap verses drew particular attention. BLACKPINK's 2019 Coachella performance marked a turning point, exposing the group to massive Western audiences. Lisa was frequently spotlighted for her stage presence and technical precision.

In September 2021, she released her solo debut EP *LALISA*, which sold over 736,000 copies in its first week — a record for a female K-pop solo artist at the time. The title track debuted at number 84 on the Billboard Hot 100 and the music video accumulated over 73 million YouTube views within 24 hours.

## Key Achievements

- Co-holder of the record for most YouTube views in 24 hours for a K-pop solo artist (at debut)
- *LALISA* EP set first-week sales records for female K-pop soloists in 2021
- First Thai artist to chart on the Billboard Hot 100
- Named global ambassador for luxury brands including Celine, MAC Cosmetics, and Bvlgari
- Judge on *Stage 2049* and participant in the Thai reality competition *The Rapper*
- In 2023, departed YG Entertainment and signed with her own label, LLOUD, while also joining RCA Records for international distribution

## Controversies & Criticism

Lisa has largely avoided major personal controversies. In 2022, her fan club's fundraising platform BLINKS raised concerns about fan spending culture and transparency in K-pop fan economies. Her departure from YG Entertainment in 2023 prompted speculation about internal tensions within BLACKPINK's management, though no parties made public accusations. Some critics noted the heavily packaged nature of K-pop idol culture in discussions of her career trajectory.

## Legacy & Influence

Lisa has become a symbol of Southeast Asian representation in the global entertainment industry, inspiring a generation of Thai and broader Asian youth to pursue careers in music and performance. Her success challenged long-standing assumptions that K-pop's international appeal was limited to East Asian artists. As a solo act and entrepreneur, she has demonstrated that K-pop-trained artists can build independent, globally competitive careers beyond the group format.
