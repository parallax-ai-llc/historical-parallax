## Summary

Kashmiri militant commander of Hizbul Mujahideen who used social media to recruit fighters. His killing by Indian forces sparked months of violent protests in Kashmir.

## Context

Burhan Wani became a widely discussed figure within the long-running conflict in Jammu and Kashmir. Coverage of his life and death often intersects with broader debates about militancy, counterinsurgency, political legitimacy, and human-rights concerns in the region.

## Role and Public Profile (High Level)

Wani was associated with Hizbul Mujahideen, a militant group active in Kashmir. He was notable for maintaining a visible online presence, and discussions of his influence frequently highlight:

- **Use of social media:** Public-facing messaging that amplified attention around local grievances and the insurgency.
- **Symbolic status:** Divergent public perceptions, with some viewing him as a militant leader and others framing him within a political resistance narrative.

## Aftermath of His Death

Wani’s killing in 2016 was followed by widespread unrest, including protests, clashes, and security restrictions. The period is often referenced as a moment that intensified polarization and drew renewed international media attention to Kashmir.
