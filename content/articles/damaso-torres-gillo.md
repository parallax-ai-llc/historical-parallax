## Summary

Colombian drug trafficker known as Don Damaso who led the remnants of the Norte del Valle Cartel after its main leaders were killed or captured. He controlled cocaine routes from Colombia's Pacific coast.

## Key Details

### Norte del Valle Cartel context

The Norte del Valle Cartel was one of several Colombian trafficking organizations that expanded after the decline of the Medellín and Cali cartels. By the early 2000s, it was associated with intense internal violence, fragmentation into competing factions, and sustained pressure from Colombian and U.S. authorities.

### Role as a post-fragmentation leader

Torres Gillo is often described as a figure who consolidated or coordinated parts of the cartel’s remaining structure after key bosses were removed. Public sources emphasize control of coastal corridors and logistics on Colombia’s Pacific side, a region frequently used for maritime shipments and access to international routes.

### Arrest, extradition, and prosecution

He was arrested in Colombia and extradited to the United States, where cartel cases commonly focus on conspiracy, trafficking quantities, and network leadership. Extradition is frequently used as a tool to disrupt organizational command and to increase the legal risk for senior traffickers.

## Significance

The “Don Damaso” narrative is often used to illustrate how cartel organizations persist through succession and decentralization: leadership changes do not necessarily end trafficking, but they can reshape violence patterns, alliances, and enforcement strategies.

### Enforcement themes

Coverage of his case often highlights joint operations, the use of informants, and the strategic importance of targeting transport nodes (ports, coastal safe houses, and intermediaries) rather than only producers.
