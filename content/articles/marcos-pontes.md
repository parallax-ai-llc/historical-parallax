## Summary

Marcos Pontes is the first Brazilian and South American astronaut to go to space. He flew to the International Space Station aboard Soyuz TMA-8 in 2006, spending 10 days conducting experiments in microgravity. A military pilot and engineer, he later served as Brazil's Minister of Science, Technology, and Innovation.
