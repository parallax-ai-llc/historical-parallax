## Summary

Paul Ricoeur was a French philosopher whose hermeneutical philosophy profoundly influenced theology, biblical studies, and the humanities. His works on metaphor, narrative, and interpretation provided tools for understanding religious texts and human meaning-making. He developed a 'hermeneutics of suspicion' alongside a 'hermeneutics of faith.' His major works include The Symbolism of Evil, Time and Narrative, and Oneself as Another.
