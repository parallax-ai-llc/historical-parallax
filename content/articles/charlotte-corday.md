## Summary
Marie-Anne Charlotte de Corday d'Armont was a French woman who assassinated Jean-Paul Marat, a radical journalist and politician of the French Revolution, by stabbing him in his bath on July 13, 1793. She was a Girondin sympathizer who believed Marat was destroying France. She was guillotined four days later.
