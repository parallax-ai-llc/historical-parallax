## Summary

Joseph Murray performed the first successful human organ transplant — a kidney transplant between identical twins — on December 23, 1954, at Peter Bent Brigham Hospital in Boston. He later pioneered immunosuppressive drug therapy to enable transplants between non-identical individuals. He received the 1990 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine.
