## Summary

Josei Toda was the second president of Soka Gakkai who transformed it from a small educational organization into a major religious movement in post-war Japan. He was imprisoned during World War II for refusing to accept Shinto as a state religion. During imprisonment, he experienced an awakening through chanting and studying the Lotus Sutra. After the war, he rebuilt Soka Gakkai into a mass movement. He made a declaration calling for the abolition of nuclear weapons.
