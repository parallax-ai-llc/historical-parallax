## Context: The End of the Sri Lankan Civil War

- The Sri Lankan Civil War ended in 2009 with the defeat of the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE).
- The final phase of the war has been the subject of sustained international scrutiny, particularly around civilian harm, restricted access for observers, and post-conflict accountability.

## “No Fire Zones” (high level)

“ No Fire Zones ” were areas that were publicly described as intended to protect civilians. Allegations around the final offensive often focus on whether:

- civilians were effectively protected,
- humanitarian corridors and medical facilities were respected, and
- both state and non-state actors complied with the laws of war.

## Accountability and Documentation

Post-war accountability debates commonly involve:

- casualty estimation methodologies,
- chain-of-command responsibility,
- the role of independent media and satellite evidence, and
- the constraints faced by investigators during and after active hostilities.
