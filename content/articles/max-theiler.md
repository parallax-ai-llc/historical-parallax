## Summary

Max Theiler was a South African-American virologist who won the 1951 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for developing the yellow fever vaccine (17D strain). His vaccine has been used to immunize hundreds of millions of people worldwide and remains one of the most successful vaccines ever created. Theiler's work at the Rockefeller Foundation was crucial to controlling yellow fever globally.
