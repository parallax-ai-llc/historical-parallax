## Summary

Chloe Kim won Olympic halfpipe gold at 2018 and 2022, the youngest female snowboarding champion at 17.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2018 | Won Olympic gold at 17 |
| 2022 | Won second gold at Beijing |

## Early life and background

- Kim was born in the United States and is widely described as a Korean American athlete.
- She began snowboarding at a young age and progressed through youth competitions into elite halfpipe events.

## Competitive highlights

- Before her Olympic debut, Kim was already a top figure in women’s halfpipe, with major wins on the X Games and World Cup circuits.
- At the 2018 Winter Olympics in PyeongChang, she won gold in the women’s halfpipe, becoming one of the breakout stars of the Games.
- In 2022 at the Beijing Winter Olympics, she defended her title and won a second Olympic halfpipe gold, a rare repeat at the top level.

## Style and significance

- Kim is associated with pushing the technical level of women’s halfpipe, combining amplitude (height), difficult rotations, and consistent execution.
- Her success contributed to increased visibility for women’s snowboarding and to broader mainstream coverage of halfpipe events.

## Off-snow activities

- Media coverage has noted her public profile beyond competitions, including endorsements and appearances that positioned her as one of the most recognizable American winter-sport athletes of her generation.
- Like many elite athletes, she has managed training cycles around Olympics, world championships, and major invitational events.
