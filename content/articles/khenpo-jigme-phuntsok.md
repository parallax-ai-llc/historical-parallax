## Summary

Khenpo Jigme Phuntsok was a Tibetan Buddhist master who founded Larung Gar, one of the largest Buddhist academies in the world, located in Sertar, eastern Tibet. At its peak, the academy housed over 40,000 monks, nuns, and lay practitioners. He was a key figure in the revival of Buddhism in Tibet after the Cultural Revolution. The Chinese government repeatedly pressured the academy to reduce its numbers.
