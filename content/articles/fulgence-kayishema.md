## Summary

Former Rwandan judicial police inspector convicted of genocide for leading attacks on Tutsi refugees sheltering in Nyange church. He was arrested in South Africa after years as a fugitive.

## Background and Position

- Served as a judicial police inspector in Rwanda prior to and during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi.
- Was later accused of using official authority and local networks to organize or direct violence against civilians.

## Nyange Church Massacre (1994)

- Nyange church in western Rwanda became a refuge site for Tutsi civilians.
- Prosecutors and historical accounts describe an attack on the church compound that resulted in mass killings.
- The case is often referenced in discussions of how places of worship and “safe” locations were targeted during the genocide.

## Accountability and International Justice

- Kayishema was indicted in international proceedings connected to post-genocide prosecutions.
- His case illustrates long time horizons in atrocity accountability: suspects may evade capture for years while legal mechanisms continue through international cooperation.

## Fugitive Period and Arrest

- Lived as a fugitive outside Rwanda for an extended period.
- Arrest in South Africa (2023) was reported as part of coordinated efforts to locate remaining genocide suspects.
