## Summary

Rwandan president of the Interahamwe militia that carried out much of the actual killing during the 1994 genocide. Despite being Tutsi himself, he led the Hutu extremist militia.

## Role in the Genocide (High-Level)

- **Interahamwe leadership**: Kajuga is widely reported as having held a national leadership position in the Interahamwe, a youth militia linked to extremist political networks.
- **Mass-violence organization**: Accounts emphasize militia coordination, recruitment, and direction during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi.

## Historical Context

- The genocide took place amid state collapse, propaganda, and organized campaigns of violence, with militias and local officials playing key roles.
- Many leaders and organizers fled after the genocide, contributing to regional instability and prolonged conflict in the Great Lakes region.

## Aftermath and Reporting

- Public sources often describe uncertainty around Kajuga's later fate, including reports of death in the late 1990s.
- The case is sometimes discussed as an example of how post-conflict accountability is complicated by displacement, weak documentation, and cross-border conflict.
