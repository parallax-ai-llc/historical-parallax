## Summary

Former school in Phnom Penh converted by the Khmer Rouge into Security Prison 21 where an estimated 17,000 people were tortured and killed. Only 12 people are known to have survived.

## Additional Context

Tuol Sleng, also known as **Security Prison 21 (S-21)**, was operated during the Khmer Rouge period as part of a broader system of detention, interrogation, and political violence in Democratic Kampuchea.

### What happened there

- The site functioned as a security prison where detainees were interrogated under coercion and torture.
- Prison records, photographs, and confessions were produced as part of the regime’s internal security processes.
- Many detainees were later transferred to execution sites outside Phnom Penh.

### After 1979

- After the fall of the Khmer Rouge, the site was preserved and turned into a museum and memorial.
- It is frequently cited in scholarship and public history as evidence for understanding the mechanics of state terror and bureaucratized violence.
