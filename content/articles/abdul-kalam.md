## Summary

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist who played a leading role in developing India's ballistic missile and nuclear weapons capabilities. Known as the 'Missile Man of India,' he led the development of the SLV-III rocket and the Agni and Prithvi missiles. He later served as the 11th President of India (2002-2007) and was beloved for his inspirational leadership and advocacy for science education.
