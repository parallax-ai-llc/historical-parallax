## Summary

Kim Tae-hyung, known professionally as V, is a South Korean singer, songwriter, and actor who rose to global fame as a member of BTS, one of the best-selling music acts in history. Since debuting with Big Hit Entertainment in June 2013, V has distinguished himself through a deep baritone vocal range unusual among K-pop idols, as well as an eclectic artistic sensibility spanning vintage jazz, classical music, and contemporary pop. His influence extends well beyond music: he is consistently ranked among the most followed individuals on social media and has been named one of the most handsome men in the world by numerous outlets.

As a soloist, V released his debut studio album *Layover* in September 2023, which debuted at number one on Billboard's Top Album Sales chart. His artistic identity — marked by introspective lyricism, film photography, and a fondness for art house cinema — has cultivated a devoted global fanbase known as "V stans" within the broader ARMY community. He has also made inroads into acting, appearing in the Korean historical drama *Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth* (2016–2017).

## Early Life & Background

Born on December 30, 1995, in Daegu, South Korea, Kim Tae-hyung grew up in Geochang County, South Gyeongsang Province, where he was raised partly by his paternal grandparents. He developed an early interest in music, learning the saxophone during middle school. He auditioned for Big Hit Entertainment in 2011 — reportedly accompanying a friend to the audition — and was accepted, training for roughly two years before debuting.

## Rise to Prominence

V debuted with BTS on June 13, 2013, with the single album *2 Cool 4 Skool*. His existence as a BTS member was kept secret until debut day, heightening anticipation. The group's ascent from a small agency act to a global phenomenon accelerated sharply between 2017 and 2020, with albums such as *Love Yourself: Tear* (2018) and *Map of the Soul: 7* (2020) topping charts worldwide. V contributed original compositions throughout, including "Stigma" on *Wings* (2016) and co-writing credits on multiple album tracks. He released a series of solo mixtape-style tracks — "Scenery" (2019), "Sweet Night" (2020), and "Snow Flower" (2020) — that collectively amassed hundreds of millions of streams before any official solo album.

## Key Achievements

- *Layover* (2023) debuted at #1 on Billboard Top Album Sales and produced the single "Rainy Days," which charted in over 30 countries.
- "Sweet Night," from the *Itaewon Class* OST, reached number one on iTunes in 106 countries simultaneously — a record at the time.
- BTS collectively sold over 40 million albums worldwide; V contributed to every charting release, including four Billboard Hot 100 number-one singles.
- Named a cultural envoy for South Korean tourism by the Korea Tourism Organization alongside fellow BTS members.
- His Instagram account surpassed 50 million followers within hours of creation in 2021, setting a Guinness World Record for the fastest account to do so.

## Controversies & Criticism

V has largely avoided major controversies. In 2022, South Korean media reported on his friendship with individuals connected to alleged financial misconduct, briefly drawing public scrutiny; V was not implicated in any wrongdoing. Like all BTS members, he faced criticism from some quarters when the group confirmed mandatory South Korean military service enlistment; V enlisted in December 2023, completing his service obligation in line with Korean law.

## Legacy & Influence

V's artistic output has helped expand the stylistic vocabulary of K-pop, demonstrating that idols can cultivate credible solo careers rooted in personal aesthetics rather than commercial formula alone. His embrace of vintage fashion, film cameras, and painters such as Van Gogh has influenced fan communities and younger Korean artists alike. Within BTS's legacy as a group that opened Western markets to Korean pop music at an unprecedented scale, V stands as a symbol of the genre's capacity for individual artistic expression.
