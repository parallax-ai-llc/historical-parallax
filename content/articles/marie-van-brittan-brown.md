## Summary

Marie Van Brittan Brown invented the first home security system in 1966, featuring a camera, monitor, remote-controlled door lock, and alarm button. Her patent laid the groundwork for all modern home security systems and video intercom systems. She developed it because police were slow to respond to calls in her Queens, New York neighborhood.

## Additional Context

### What the invention included (high level)

The system described in the Browns' patent combined several ideas that later became standard in consumer security products:

- A camera that allowed the occupant to view the entrance.
- A monitor inside the home.
- Two-way audio communication (microphone/speaker).
- A remote-controlled door lock and an alarm mechanism.

### Why it mattered

In historical accounts of "smart home" technology, Brown is frequently mentioned because the invention anticipated key elements of modern:

- Video doorbells and intercoms
- Home surveillance systems
- Remote access control for residential doors

### Attribution note

The work is often credited jointly to Marie Van Brittan Brown and her husband Albert L. Brown, who had relevant electronics expertise, with Marie identifying the practical problem and helping design the solution.
