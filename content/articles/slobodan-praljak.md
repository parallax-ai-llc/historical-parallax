## Summary

Bosnian Croat military commander who dramatically swallowed poison and died in the ICTY courtroom after his 20-year sentence was upheld. He was convicted of war crimes including the destruction of Mostar's Old Bridge.

## Background

Praljak was trained as an engineer and also worked in theater and film before the breakup of Yugoslavia. During the Bosnian War, he became a senior figure associated with the Croatian Defence Council (HVO) and Croatian political-military structures in Bosnia and Herzegovina.

## ICTY proceedings

The International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) prosecuted Praljak in the broader *Prlić et al.* case, which addressed crimes committed during the conflict involving Bosnian Croat forces. The tribunal found him responsible for crimes connected to the treatment of Bosniak civilians and detainees and for acts linked to the siege and destruction in Mostar.

## Death in court

On 29 November 2017, after the ICTY Appeals Chamber upheld his conviction and sentence, Praljak stated he rejected the verdict and drank a liquid later confirmed to be potassium cyanide. He died shortly afterward in a hospital in The Hague, and the incident prompted immediate security reviews of courtroom procedures.

## Legacy and controversy

Praljak’s case remains politically charged in parts of the region: supporters portray him as a wartime defender, while critics emphasize the tribunal’s findings on unlawful conduct and civilian harm. The destruction of the Old Bridge (Stari Most) and its later reconstruction also became an enduring symbol in discussions of wartime cultural loss and postwar reconciliation.

## Key points

- Senior Bosnian Croat commander associated with the HVO during the Bosnian War.
- Convicted by the ICTY; sentence upheld on appeal.
