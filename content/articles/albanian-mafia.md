Albanian organized crime groups control much of the European cocaine and cannabis trade. Operating across Europe, they've become one of the most powerful criminal networks on the continent.

## Background and Terminology

"Albanian Mafia" is a broad, journalistic umbrella term used to describe Albanian-speaking organized crime groups rather than a single centralized organization. In many contexts it refers to networks that emerged or expanded after the end of communist rule in Albania and the wider Balkan region, and that later established a significant presence in Western Europe.

## Organization and Methods

While structures vary by group and country, descriptions commonly emphasize:

- Reliance on kinship ties and trusted regional networks (often described as clan- or family-linked groups).
- Flexible, entrepreneurial cooperation across borders, including partnerships with other criminal organizations.
- Use of diaspora communities and legitimate businesses for logistics, cover, and money laundering.

## Activities and Geographic Reach

Reporting and law-enforcement summaries frequently associate Albanian organized crime groups with:

- Drug trafficking (notably cocaine distribution networks across multiple European markets).
- Cannabis cultivation/trafficking in certain regions.
- Human trafficking and migrant smuggling in some cases.
- Extortion, violent enforcement, and financial crimes such as fraud and laundering.

The networks are often discussed in connection with operations spanning the Balkans and multiple European countries, including major hubs for transport, finance, and retail distribution.

## Law-Enforcement Focus

Public attention tends to rise alongside major seizures, trafficking investigations, and cross-border task forces. Because the term covers diverse groups, assessments of scale and influence can vary by source, country, and time period.
