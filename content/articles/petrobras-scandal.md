## Summary

See Marcelo Odebrecht and Lava Jato. Brazilian state oil company was at center of massive corruption scheme. Duplicate entry.

The **Petrobras scandal** refers to a long-running bribery and kickback network centered on **Petróleo Brasileiro S.A. (Petrobras)**, Brazil’s state-controlled oil company. The scheme involved inflated contracts and cartel behavior among major construction firms, with portions of contract value diverted as illicit payments to intermediaries and political figures.

While commonly discussed as part of **Operation Car Wash (Operação Lava Jato)**, the Petrobras scandal is broader than any single case: it spans multiple investigations, plea bargains, and court actions that unfolded across the 2010s and reshaped Brazil’s politics, corporate governance, and anti-corruption enforcement.

## How the scheme worked (high level)

- **Contract inflation:** Petrobras procurement contracts were allegedly priced above competitive levels.
- **Cartel coordination:** Certain suppliers were accused of coordinating bids and splitting work.
- **Kickbacks:** A share of overcharges was routed as illicit payments through intermediaries.
- **Political financing:** Funds were alleged to have supported political parties and individual officials.

## Key entities frequently discussed

- **Petrobras** — the state-controlled oil company at the center of the contracting system.
- **Major construction firms** — large contractors accused of cartel behavior and bribery.
- **Operators/intermediaries** — brokers who allegedly coordinated payments and placements.
- **Political actors** — officials and party structures alleged to have benefited from kickbacks.
- **Federal investigators and courts** — prosecutors, police, and judges involved in Lava Jato cases.

## Impact

- **Economic:** Reduced investment capacity at Petrobras during periods of financial and governance stress; knock-on effects for supply chains.
- **Political:** Intensified polarization and legitimacy challenges for parties and institutions during the mid-2010s.
- **Governance:** Accelerated adoption of compliance controls, auditing, and procurement reforms in large Brazilian companies.
- **International:** Cross-border cooperation in some cases due to global banking and corporate footprints.
