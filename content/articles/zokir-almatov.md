## Summary

Uzbek interior minister directly responsible for the Andijan massacre and systematic torture in prisons.

Zokir Almatov is an Uzbek government official best known for serving as Minister of Internal Affairs during the presidency of Islam Karimov. His tenure has been associated in international reporting and human-rights advocacy with allegations of severe abuses by security forces and prison systems, particularly around the 2005 Andijan events.

## Role and Controversies (High Level)

- As interior minister, Almatov was associated with the state apparatus responsible for policing, detention, and internal security.
- Human-rights organizations and international observers have alleged that torture and ill-treatment were systematic within Uzbek detention facilities during the period.
- In the context of Andijan in 2005, international coverage and advocacy groups described the crackdown as involving large-scale civilian casualties; responsibility was attributed to senior government and security officials.

## Andijan (2005) — Context

- The Andijan events became a major turning point in Uzbekistan's relations with parts of Europe and the United States.
- International debate centered on casualty figures, access for independent investigation, and whether the response constituted a massacre.
- Uzbek authorities characterized the events differently from many external observers; narratives remain contested in political discourse, though the incident is widely documented as a violent suppression.

## Later Life / Status

- Reporting around his resignation has often mentioned medical treatment abroad.
- Public, definitive information about his later activities has been limited compared to his period in office.
