## Summary

Indonesian terrorist known as Mukhlas who was a senior Jemaah Islamiyah leader and one of the masterminds of the 2002 Bali bombings. He was executed by firing squad.

## Names and Aliases

- Ali Ghufron bin Haji Nurhasyim is commonly reported under the nom de guerre **Mukhlas** (also spelled “Muklas”).[^1]
- Some biographical sources differ on his exact birthdate within February 1960; this article retains the existing frontmatter and notes the discrepancy here.[^1]

## Background and Militant Networks (High-Level)

- Reporting describes him as a senior figure in **Jemaah Islamiyah (JI)**, a Southeast Asian militant organization that, in some accounts, maintained ties to al-Qaeda-linked networks during the period in question.[^1]
- Sources describe training and travel connected to Afghanistan-era militant camps, including participation in the Soviet–Afghan War timeframe (as reported by some accounts).[^1]

## Key Attacks Attributed in Public Reporting

- Public sources attribute to him a coordinating role in the **Christmas Eve 2000 Indonesia bombings** and the **2002 Bali bombings**.[^1]
- The 2002 Bali attacks are widely described as targeting nightlife venues and causing mass casualties (commonly reported as 202 killed and hundreds injured).[^1]

## Organizational Role (Context)

- Accounts describe JI as organized through regional “mantiqi” structures; Mukhlas has been reported as leading a regional network covering parts of Indonesia and neighboring countries.[^1]
- Sources also describe involvement with religious-educational institutions that were alleged to have served as recruitment or networking nodes for JI-linked operatives.[^1]

## Trial, Sentence, and Execution

- Mukhlas was arrested in late 2002, convicted for coordinating an act of terrorism, and sentenced to death in Indonesian courts (as summarized in public sources).[^1]
- He was executed by firing squad in November 2008 at Nusa Kambangan, according to commonly cited reporting.[^1]
