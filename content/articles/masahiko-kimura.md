## Summary

Masahiko Kimura is considered the greatest judoka in history, going undefeated for 15 years. His 1951 victory over Helio Gracie was so famous that the shoulder lock he used was renamed the 'Kimura.'

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1937 | Won All Japan Judo Championship at 20 |
| 1949 | Won All Japan Championship for final time |
| 1951 | Defeated Helio Gracie in Brazil |
| 1993 | Died in Japan |

## The "Kimura" Technique (High-Level)

- In grappling, the name "Kimura" commonly refers to a **figure-four shoulder lock** (a type of *ude-garami*).
- The technique appears across multiple grappling traditions; Kimura's association with the move reflects how iconic matches can shape naming conventions.
