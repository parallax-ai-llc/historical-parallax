## Summary

Luis Walter Alvarez was an American experimental physicist who won the 1968 Nobel Prize in Physics for his contributions to elementary particle physics. He developed the hydrogen bubble chamber technique for detecting subatomic particles. Together with his son Walter, he proposed the asteroid impact theory for the extinction of dinosaurs. He also contributed to the Manhattan Project and radar development.
