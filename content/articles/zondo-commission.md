## Summary

Judicial commission investigated state capture in South Africa, revealing how the Gupta family and corrupt officials systematically looted state institutions during Zuma's presidency.

## Mandate and scope

The commission (formally a judicial commission of inquiry) was convened to investigate allegations of **"state capture"**—the idea that private interests could systematically influence state decisions, procurement, and appointments for personal gain.

Key areas covered in public reporting include:

- Governance and procurement at major state-owned enterprises
- The role of senior public officials and political leadership
- Networks of intermediaries, contractors, and advisers

## Process (high level)

- The commission held extensive **public hearings** and collected testimony and documentary evidence.
- As with many commissions of inquiry, the work combined fact-finding with recommendations, rather than issuing criminal verdicts.

## Outcomes and impact

- The final reporting period culminated in recommendations that informed subsequent investigations and public debate.
- The commission became a central reference point in South Africa's contemporary governance and anti-corruption discourse.

## Commonly discussed themes

- How procurement systems can be exploited via complex contracting structures.
- The difficulty of accountability when influence is exercised through informal networks rather than formal instructions.
- The long institutional recovery required after sustained corruption.
