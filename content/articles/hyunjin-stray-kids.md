## Summary

Hwang Hyunjin, known professionally as Hyunjin, is a South Korean singer, rapper, and dancer who serves as a lead dancer and lead rapper of the K-pop group Stray Kids, formed by JYP Entertainment in 2017. Born on March 20, 2000, in Seoul, South Korea, he became one of the group's most recognizable members through his striking visual presence and expressive performance style.

Stray Kids has grown into one of the top-selling K-pop acts globally, and Hyunjin has played a central role in the group's artistic identity. Known for his fluid dance technique and emotional storytelling through movement, he has been widely praised as one of the most compelling performers in fourth-generation K-pop. Beyond music, Hyunjin has gained recognition for his skills in visual art, frequently sharing paintings and drawings with fans.

His influence extends across music, fashion, and social media, where he commands a massive international following. Hyunjin's artistic versatility and stage charisma have helped Stray Kids build a dedicated global fanbase known as STAY.

## Early Life & Background

Hyunjin was born on March 20, 2000, in Seoul, South Korea. He spent part of his childhood in Las Vegas, Nevada, where he attended elementary school and became fluent in English. After returning to South Korea, he developed an interest in dance during his middle school years. He successfully auditioned for JYP Entertainment and entered the agency's rigorous trainee program, training for approximately two years before his debut.

## Rise to Prominence

In 2017, Hyunjin appeared on the JYP Entertainment reality competition show *Stray Kids*, which aired on Mnet. The show documented the formation of the group under the guidance of leader Bang Chan. Hyunjin secured his place in the final eight-member lineup, and Stray Kids officially debuted on March 25, 2018, with the EP *I Am NOT*. The group quickly distinguished itself through self-produced music led by the producing unit 3RACHA, and Hyunjin's dynamic stage presence became a signature element of their performances. By 2022, Stray Kids had become one of the best-selling K-pop acts in the world, with their album *MAXIDENT* selling over 3.74 million copies in South Korea alone. Their 2023 album *5-STAR* debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, making them only the third K-pop act to achieve this.

## Key Achievements

As part of Stray Kids, Hyunjin has contributed to the sale of over 20 million albums worldwide as of 2024. The group completed multiple world tours, including the "MANIAC" tour (2022) and the "5-STAR Dome Tour" across Japan (2023), performing at major venues including Tokyo Dome. Stray Kids won Daesang (Grand Prize) awards at the 2023 and 2024 MAMA Awards. Hyunjin has been recognized individually for his dance skills, frequently highlighted in fan and industry polls as one of the top dancers in K-pop. He choreographed solo performance stages and released the solo track "Red Lights" (with Bang Chan) in 2021 and "Masquerade" in 2024, both of which showcased his artistic range.

## Controversies & Criticism

In February 2021, Hyunjin faced allegations of school bullying from former classmates who claimed he had been verbally aggressive during middle school. JYP Entertainment acknowledged the situation, and Hyunjin personally met with and apologized to the individuals involved. He went on a temporary hiatus from group activities lasting approximately four months, returning in July 2021. The incident prompted broader public discussion in South Korea about celebrity school bullying allegations, which became a widespread cultural phenomenon in early 2021 affecting multiple entertainers.

## Legacy & Influence

Hyunjin has emerged as a defining figure of fourth-generation K-pop, recognized for elevating the role of dance and visual artistry within idol group performance. His painting and visual art, frequently shared through social media and exhibited at fan events, have broadened perceptions of what K-pop idols can offer artistically beyond music. As a fashion figure, he has collaborated with luxury brands and appeared at international fashion weeks. His combination of performance skill, artistic expression, and visual appeal has made him one of the most followed K-pop idols on Instagram, with over 30 million followers as of early 2025, and a key driver of Stray Kids' global popularity.
