## Summary

Indonesian militia leader in East Timor who commanded the Aitarak militia responsible for massacres during the 1999 independence referendum. He was convicted of crimes against humanity.

## Background: East Timor in the Late 1990s

East Timor (Timor-Leste) was under Indonesian rule for decades and experienced prolonged conflict. In 1999, a UN-backed popular consultation (referendum) on independence became a flashpoint for violence, particularly involving pro-integration militias.

## Aitarak Militia (High Level)

Aitarak was one of the better-known militia groups associated with intimidation and violence around the referendum period. Such groups were linked in public reporting and investigations to campaigns that included:

- threats against civilians,
- forced displacement,
- and attacks on pro-independence communities.

## Legal Proceedings

Guterres became a prominent figure in subsequent accountability efforts. Indonesian courts pursued a limited set of cases connected to the 1999 violence, and his conviction is frequently referenced in discussions of how (and whether) domestic systems can handle crimes tied to political transitions.

## Wider Significance

The events surrounding the referendum are remembered not only for immediate loss of life but also for:

- the mass displacement of civilians,
- the destruction of property and infrastructure,
- and the long-term impact on regional politics and reconciliation.

## Note on Sources

Descriptions of militia structures and responsibility are contested in some narratives; comparative reading across UN reporting, human-rights investigations, and court records is often necessary to understand the period.
