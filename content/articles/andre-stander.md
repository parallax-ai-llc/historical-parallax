## Summary

South African police captain who became a notorious bank robber while still serving on the police force. He robbed banks during his lunch break and then investigated his own crimes. Escaped prison and was shot dead in the US.

## Background and notoriety

Stander was a South African police officer whose criminal activity drew attention because it overlapped with his role in law enforcement. Accounts commonly emphasize the unusual pattern of committing bank robberies while employed as a police captain and then participating in the subsequent investigations.

## Arrest, imprisonment, and escape

After his arrest and conviction, Stander received a lengthy sentence. He later escaped from prison, an event that intensified media coverage and led to an international manhunt.

## Death

Stander was eventually located in the United States and died during a police operation in Fort Lauderdale, Florida.

## Cultural portrayals

His story has been retold in books and film, often as an example of criminal "double life" narratives involving law enforcement insiders.

## Context

Stander’s case is often discussed in South African crime history as an example of insider abuse of authority and the challenges of investigating crimes connected to police institutions.

## Notes

Public accounts vary in minor details (such as counts of robberies and timelines) across interviews, books, and press coverage.
