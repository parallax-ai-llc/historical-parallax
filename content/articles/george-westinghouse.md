## Summary

George Westinghouse was an American entrepreneur and engineer who was a pioneer of the electrical industry. He championed alternating current (AC) power distribution, competing with Thomas Edison's direct current (DC) system in the 'War of Currents.' He founded the Westinghouse Electric Company and acquired many of Nikola Tesla's patents. He also invented the railway air brake, which revolutionized railroad safety.
