## Summary

Danish king who ordered the Stockholm Bloodbath of 1520, massacring over 80 Swedish nobles and clergy. The event triggered a rebellion that led to Swedish independence.

## Additional Context

Christian II ruled in the early 16th century during a period of struggle over control of the Kalmar Union (Denmark, Norway, and Sweden).

### Stockholm Bloodbath (1520)

The Stockholm Bloodbath is typically described as a political purge following Christian II’s coronation in Sweden.

- Executions targeted Swedish nobles, clergy, and political opponents.
- The killings helped unify resistance and became a formative event in Swedish national history.

### Deposition and imprisonment

After losing support among both Swedish opponents and segments of Danish elites, Christian II was deposed.

- He spent much of the remainder of his life in confinement.
- His fall illustrates the limits of royal authority when dependent on noble coalitions and regional legitimacy.

### Historical reputation

Christian II’s legacy varies by historiography:

- In Sweden, he is strongly associated with tyranny due to the massacre.
- In Denmark, some accounts also discuss reformist impulses alongside political failure.
