## Summary

Kisho Kurokawa was a Japanese architect and leader of the Metabolism movement who designed buildings based on biological and scientific principles. His Nakagin Capsule Tower (1972) embodied the concept of replaceable modular living units like biological cells. He integrated ecological and technological thinking into architectural design.
