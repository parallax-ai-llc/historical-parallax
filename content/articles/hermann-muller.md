## Summary

Hermann Muller was an American geneticist who won the 1946 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for the discovery that X-rays cause genetic mutations. His work demonstrated the mutagenic effects of radiation, which had profound implications for genetics, medicine, and public health policy regarding radiation exposure. He was also a vocal critic of nuclear testing due to its genetic effects.
