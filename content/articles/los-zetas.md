## Summary

Mexican drug cartel formed by elite military deserters that became one of the most brutal criminal organizations in history. They pioneered mass kidnapping, beheadings, and car bombings.

## Background and Origins

- Los Zetas originated as an armed wing for the **Gulf Cartel**, reportedly drawing heavily from **former Mexican special forces** and other security personnel.
- In many accounts, their early differentiation was a more **militarized training and discipline** relative to peer organizations, which influenced later operational style.

## Organization and Operating Model (High-Level)

- The group is commonly described as combining **drug trafficking** with diversified criminal revenue (e.g., extortion, fuel theft, and control of local businesses).
- Analysts frequently frame Los Zetas as prioritizing **territorial control** and intimidation, rather than acting solely as a logistics network for narcotics.
- Over time, law-enforcement pressure and internal conflict contributed to **splintering** and the emergence of successor factions (the boundaries between "Los Zetas" and successor brands can be blurred in reporting).

## Geographic Footprint

- Reporting often associates Los Zetas with activity across multiple Mexican states and border corridors, reflecting both trafficking routes and local control strategies.
- Public sources vary on the exact extent of presence by year; many maps represent **best-effort estimates** rather than audited ground truth.

## Notes on Language and Claims

- Terms like "cartel," "terror," or "most brutal" are often used in media coverage; for an analytical profile, it is useful to separate **documented acts**, **attributed patterns**, and **rhetorical framing**.
- Where possible, higher-confidence detail should be sourced to: government indictments, credible human-rights documentation, and well-cited investigative reporting.
