## Summary

Cambodian Khmer Rouge military commander known as 'The Butcher' who oversaw massacres of civilians. He was the last major Khmer Rouge leader to be captured. He died in detention before trial.

## Background and Role in the Khmer Rouge

Ta Mok was a senior Khmer Rouge commander associated with the movement’s military and security apparatus. In Khmer Rouge governance, regional and military commanders often wielded broad authority over territory, including forced population movements, local detention sites, and the enforcement of policies that targeted perceived “enemies” of the revolution.

## Alleged Crimes and Reputation

He was widely reported to have been responsible for severe violence in areas under his control, contributing to the nickname “The Butcher.” Allegations associated with senior Khmer Rouge figures commonly include mass killings, forced labor, and the operation of detention and execution sites during Democratic Kampuchea (1975–1979).

## Capture, Detention, and Unresolved Trial

Ta Mok was captured in 1999 after the Khmer Rouge’s remaining forces fragmented and lost territorial control. He was held in Cambodian custody and later linked to plans for legal proceedings connected to accountability efforts for Khmer Rouge-era crimes. He died in detention in 2006 before a final verdict could be reached in court.

## Historical Context

The Khmer Rouge period is associated with widespread atrocities and catastrophic loss of life in Cambodia. Understanding figures such as Ta Mok typically involves placing individual responsibility alongside the broader command structure and ideology of the regime, which combined centralized directives with harsh local enforcement.
