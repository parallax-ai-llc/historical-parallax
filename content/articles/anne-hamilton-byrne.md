## Summary

Australian cult leader who led The Family, a sect that collected children through adoption fraud and raised them under her control with bleached hair, claiming they were her own. Children were drugged with LSD.

## The Family cult

Hamilton-Byrne led a group commonly referred to as **The Family**, which operated in Australia and attracted followers through a mixture of spiritual claims and strict internal discipline.

### Control and abuse allegations

Investigations and survivor accounts described severe control over members and children, including isolation, punishment, and drug use. The case is often cited in discussions of coercive control and child welfare failures.

## Law enforcement actions and aftermath

Authorities raided properties linked to the group in the late 1980s. Hamilton-Byrne later faced criminal proceedings connected to adoption fraud; broader allegations of abuse were documented in journalism and later investigations.

## Legacy

The Family remains one of Australia’s most well-known cult cases, referenced in books, documentaries, and public debate about regulation and oversight of closed communities.

## Investigations and reporting

The case has been documented through investigative journalism and later retellings, contributing to public awareness of coercive groups and the long-term effects on survivors.

## Notes

Dates and organizational details sometimes differ across sources because the group operated with secrecy and informal structures.
