## Summary

Pakistani Taliban leader who succeeded Baitullah Mehsud and directed attacks that killed thousands across Pakistan. He personally appeared in the video of the Camp Chapman attack on the CIA.

## Background

- **Role in TTP:** He is widely described as the second emir (leader) of Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan (TTP), a Pakistani militant umbrella organization.
- **Reputation:** Contemporary reporting often characterized him as a young, aggressive field commander within the Mehsud network.

## Militant Activity (High-Level)

- **Operational command:** Accounts describe him as having held command responsibilities in multiple tribal agencies (including Khyber, Kurram, and Orakzai) prior to becoming overall TTP leader.
- **Propaganda and signaling:** His appearance in the Camp Chapman video was widely interpreted as an effort to claim credit and project operational reach.
- **Network ties:** Reporting frequently discusses TTP links to other jihadist organizations and to al-Qaeda-aligned networks operating in the Afghanistan–Pakistan border region.

## Death and Succession

- **Drone strike:** He was confirmed killed in a US drone strike in 2013.
- **Leadership transition:** After his death, leadership of TTP was reported to have passed to Maulana Fazlullah.
