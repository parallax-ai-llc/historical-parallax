## Summary

Peruvian serial killer known as the Monster of La Merced who murdered at least 24 women in the Junín region. He killed women from indigenous communities who had gone missing over many years.

## Additional Context

### Location and Victim Profile

The murders associated with Covenas Ramírez were linked to the **Junín** region in central Peru, with repeated references to **La Merced** as the focal area. Public descriptions of the case commonly emphasize that many victims were **women from indigenous communities**, and that disappearances accumulated over an extended period before a single suspect was identified.

### Investigation and Arrest (High-Level)

Open-source summaries of the case generally describe a long-running pattern of missing persons reports, followed by a **community-led effort** and police action that resulted in his arrest in the early 2010s. Because many details are reported second-hand, timelines and counts are often presented as **minimum confirmed figures** rather than complete totals.

### Trial and Sentencing

He was ultimately **convicted** in Peru and received a **life imprisonment** sentence. In Peruvian criminal justice reporting, life sentences in high-profile violent-crime cases are typically framed as a response to repeated aggravated offenses and the scale of harm to victims and communities.

## Challenges highlighted by the case

High-level summaries of the “Monster of La Merced” case are often used to illustrate structural problems that can arise in investigations of repeated disappearances:

- **geography and mobility**: remote or forested terrain can delay discovery of evidence
- **underreporting**: marginalized victims may receive less sustained institutional attention
- **case linkage**: disappearances over many years may not be treated as connected until patterns become unmistakable
