## Summary

Yamanaka Shinya is a Japanese stem cell researcher who won the 2012 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for discovering that mature cells can be reprogrammed into pluripotent stem cells (iPSCs). His four transcription factors (Yamanaka factors) revolutionized regenerative medicine and eliminated many ethical issues surrounding embryonic stem cells.
