## Overview

Zhang Yimou (张艺谋) is a Chinese film director and producer widely associated with the "Fifth Generation" of Chinese filmmakers. In many datasets he is better categorized under **arts / culture / media** rather than as a conventional technology or finance entrepreneur.

## Notability

High-level reasons Zhang is frequently included in modern cultural histories include:

- Internationally recognized feature films that helped introduce contemporary Chinese cinema to global audiences.
- Large-scale, state-level event direction (most notably the opening and closing ceremonies of the **2008 Beijing Olympic Games**).

## Selected works (commonly cited)

These titles are regularly referenced in summaries of his career (see Wikipedia for full filmography and dates):

- *Red Sorghum* (often cited as a breakthrough work; it received major international recognition)
- *Raise the Red Lantern*
- *To Live*
- *Hero*
- *House of Flying Daggers*

## Themes and style (high-level)

Commentary on Zhang Yimou’s work often highlights:

- visually stylized cinematography and color symbolism,
- historical settings used to explore modern social questions,
- an emphasis on spectacle in both film and event direction.

## Relationship to "entrepreneurship"

While Zhang is primarily a director, there are a few ways cultural creators are sometimes framed as entrepreneurial in knowledge bases:
