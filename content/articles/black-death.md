## Summary

The Black Death (1347-1353) was the deadliest pandemic in human history, killing an estimated 75-200 million people—roughly one-third of Europe's population. The bubonic plague devastated societies, caused labor shortages that ended feudalism, and profoundly transformed European civilization.
