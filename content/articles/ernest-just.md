## Summary

Ernest Everett Just was a pioneering African American biologist who made significant discoveries about cell fertilization, cell division, and the role of the cell surface in development. He published over 70 scientific papers and two books on cell biology. He faced severe racial discrimination in the US and spent much of his later career in European laboratories.
