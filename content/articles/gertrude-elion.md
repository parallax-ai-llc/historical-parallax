## Summary

Gertrude Elion was an American biochemist and pharmacologist who shared the 1988 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for developing drugs using innovative methods of rational drug design. She developed the first effective drugs for leukemia, herpes, malaria, and organ transplant rejection (azathioprine). Despite never receiving a PhD, she was one of the most productive drug developers in history. Her approach to drug design revolutionized the pharmaceutical industry.
