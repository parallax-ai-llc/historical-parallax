## Summary

Indonesian Jemaah Islamiyah member who helped assemble the bomb for the 2002 Bali bombings. He expressed remorse and cooperated with authorities. His death sentence was commuted to life imprisonment.

## Additional Context

- Ali Imron is associated with Jemaah Islamiyah (JI), the Southeast Asian militant network linked to a series of attacks in Indonesia during the early 2000s.
- The 2002 Bali bombings targeted nightlife districts in Kuta and involved coordinated explosive devices; the attack drew intense domestic and international scrutiny and led to a wide counterterrorism crackdown.
- In many accounts, Imron’s case is notable because he provided information to investigators after arrest and publicly expressed regret, contrasting with some co-conspirators who defended the attack.

## Legal Outcome

- Indonesian courts sentenced Imron to death, but that sentence was later reduced; he ultimately served a long-term prison sentence.
- His cooperation and public statements are often cited in discussions about sentencing, rehabilitation, and the practical value of intelligence obtained from perpetrators.

## Broader Significance

Imron is frequently referenced in Indonesian counter-extremism work as an example of a perpetrator who later participated—directly or indirectly—in messaging about the costs of violence and the realities of militant recruitment.
