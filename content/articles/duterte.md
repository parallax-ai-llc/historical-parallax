## Overview

Rodrigo Duterte is best known internationally for his presidency (2016–2022) and the Philippine “war on drugs,” a policy and policing campaign associated with thousands of deaths and extensive criticism from human-rights organizations. Before national office, he built a political base in Davao City and cultivated an image of strict law-and-order governance.

## Key Themes

### Anti-drug campaign ("war on drugs")
- Elevated anti-narcotics enforcement into a defining national policy agenda.
- Characterized by large-scale police operations and killings linked to both police actions and vigilante-style violence.
- Produced enduring domestic political support among some constituencies alongside sustained allegations of extrajudicial killings.

### Governance style and rhetoric
- Known for combative, populist rhetoric and public statements that often generated international backlash.
- Positioned himself as an outsider willing to break with elite norms, framing critics as obstructing public safety.

### Foreign policy posture
- Sought a more independent posture between major powers, including public criticism of the United States at times.
- Also pursued pragmatic engagement with China and Russia while maintaining treaty ties with the U.S.-led security architecture.

## Criticism and legacy

Duterte’s legacy is widely debated: supporters credit reduced visible street crime in some areas and decisive leadership, while critics emphasize damage to institutions, due process, and human rights norms. The long-term legal and political consequences of the anti-drug campaign—including investigations and accountability efforts—remain a central topic in assessments of his administration.
