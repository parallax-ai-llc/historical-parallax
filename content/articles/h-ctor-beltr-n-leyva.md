## Summary

Mexican drug lord known as 'El H' who took over the Beltrán Leyva Cartel after his brother Arturo's death. He was arrested disguised as an art dealer. Died of cardiac arrest in prison.

## Additional Context

## Role in the Beltrán Leyva Organization

Héctor Beltrán Leyva was part of the Beltrán Leyva family network that became prominent within Mexican organized crime during the 2000s. The group’s trajectory is often discussed in connection with broader cartel fragmentation and shifting alliances, including disputes with other major trafficking organizations and violent competition over routes and territory.

## Leadership After Arturo Beltrán Leyva

After the death of his brother Arturo in 2009, Héctor was widely described as assuming a senior leadership role. Observers commonly portray this period as one in which the organization faced both state pressure and internal rivalries, contributing to splintering and intensified conflict among successor factions.

## Arrest (2014)

Beltrán Leyva’s 2014 arrest in San Miguel de Allende drew attention because it reflected long-running intelligence and surveillance efforts, and because of reports that he attempted to maintain a low-profile lifestyle and business cover while evading capture.

## Detention and Death (2018)

He remained in Mexican custody after arrest. His death in prison was reported as cardiac arrest, and it occurred amid ongoing public scrutiny of conditions, security, and governance challenges within Mexico’s penitentiary system.

## Broader Significance

The case is often referenced as an example of:

- the succession dynamics of family-linked criminal organizations
- the effects of leadership decapitation strategies on cartel fragmentation
- how arrests can reshape alliances and violence patterns in organized crime
