## Summary

Former Russian defense minister whose leadership during Ukraine invasion was widely criticized.

Shoigu (Сергей Шойгу) is a senior Russian state official who served as **Minister of Defence (2012–2024)** and later became **Secretary of the Security Council of the Russian Federation (from May 2024)**.
Before the defense portfolio, he was best known for building and leading Russia’s emergency management apparatus as **Minister for Emergency Situations** for many years.

## Background

- Born in 1955 and trained as an engineer by education.
- Rose to national prominence through disaster-response and civil-defense roles, which helped shape a public image focused on crisis management and state capacity.

## Roles & Significance

- **Emergency management:** Associated with the development of federal emergency response structures and nationwide civil-defense/disaster-response operations.
- **Defense leadership:** As defense minister, he oversaw major military modernization and procurement efforts and became one of the most visible faces of Russia’s security establishment.
- **Security Council role (2024–):** The Security Council is a key coordinating body for national security policy; the secretary role is a senior position within Russia’s security bureaucracy.

## International Context

Shoigu has been a prominent figure in Russia’s security policy during a period that included:

- Intensified confrontation between Russia and Western governments.
- Broad international scrutiny of Russia’s military actions, with widespread condemnation and sanctions directed at senior officials (coverage and specifics vary by jurisdiction).
