## Summary

Sudanese army general who led the 2021 military coup dissolving the civilian transitional government. His forces are fighting the RSF in a devastating civil war that has created one of the world's worst humanitarian crises.

## Additional Context

### Role in Sudan’s post-2019 transition

After the 2019 overthrow of long-time ruler Omar al-Bashir, al-Burhan emerged as a leading figure in the military component of Sudan’s transitional arrangement. He became chair of the Sovereignty Council, a body intended to oversee a path toward civilian rule, but the transition remained contested and fragile.

### 2021 coup and political fallout

In October 2021, al-Burhan led a military takeover that dissolved key civilian institutions and arrested or sidelined political leaders. The move drew domestic protests and international condemnation, and it disrupted negotiations over constitutional arrangements, security-sector reform, and accountability for past abuses.

### Relationship with the RSF and the 2023 war

A central fault line in Sudan’s security landscape has been the relationship between the Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF), a powerful paramilitary organization. Disputes over command structures, integration of forces, and political authority escalated into open conflict in 2023, with fighting concentrated in Khartoum and spreading to other regions.

### Humanitarian and regional implications

The war has produced mass displacement, severe disruptions to basic services, and widespread humanitarian need. It has also heightened regional instability through refugee flows and cross-border security risks, while diplomatic efforts by neighboring states and international actors have struggled to secure a sustained ceasefire.
