## Summary

Rwandan transport minister acquitted by the ICTR of genocide charges. Despite his acquittal, his trial revealed the transport ministry's role in facilitating militia movements during the genocide.

## Background

André Ntagerura served as Rwanda’s Minister of Transport during the period leading up to and including the 1994 genocide. As transport minister, he was situated within the state apparatus that controlled movement, logistics, and administrative coordination.

## ICTR Proceedings (High-Level)

- **Forum:** International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR).
- **Charges:** Genocide-related counts linked to alleged facilitation and coordination.
- **Outcome:** Acquitted of all charges.

## Why the Case Is Discussed

Even with an acquittal, the proceedings are often referenced because they illuminated how ministries and administrative channels could be implicated in mass-violence logistics, including movement of personnel and supplies.

## Aftermath

Publicly available summaries typically note the acquittal and release; detailed information about his later life is limited in common reference sources.

## Significance

Ntagerura’s case is part of the broader record created by the ICTR, which helped define legal standards and historical narratives about responsibility during the 1994 genocide. Tribunal decisions—both convictions and acquittals—continue to be studied in international criminal law and transitional justice.
