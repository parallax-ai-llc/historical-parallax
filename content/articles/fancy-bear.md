## Summary

Russian military intelligence (GRU) hacking group also known as APT28. They hacked the DNC in 2016, the World Anti-Doping Agency, and carried out cyberattacks against governments worldwide. Identified as GRU Unit 26165.

## Additional Context

“Fancy Bear” is one of several labels used by security researchers to track a long-running set of intrusions attributed by multiple governments to Russia’s military intelligence service (GRU). In vendor reporting it is commonly mapped to **APT28**, and is also associated with names such as “Sofacy” in some threat-intelligence taxonomies.

### Common Tradecraft (High Level)

- **Spearphishing** campaigns to capture credentials and establish initial access.
- Use of **custom malware** families and infrastructure that are periodically refreshed to evade detection.
- **Targeting** aligned with geopolitical and military interests, including political organizations, defense entities, international institutions, and media.

### Information Operations Linkage

Public discussion often connects the group’s intrusions with subsequent leak-and-amplify dynamics, where stolen materials are selectively released to shape narratives. Attribution debates typically distinguish between (a) technical intrusion activity and (b) downstream dissemination by other actors or platforms.

### Attribution and Indictments

The 2018 U.S. indictment of 12 GRU officers is frequently referenced as a major public attribution step. Analysts also cite government statements from multiple countries as part of the broader attribution record, alongside technical indicators published by security firms.

### Naming Note

Threat-actor names like “Fancy Bear” are conventions used by researchers; different vendors may use different labels for overlapping activity clusters.
