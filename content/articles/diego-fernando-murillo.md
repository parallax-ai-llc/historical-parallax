## Summary

Colombian paramilitary leader known as Don Berna who controlled Medellín's criminal underworld and the Office of Envigado. He was a former Pablo Escobar ally who later worked with the AUC paramilitaries.

## Background and Aliases

- Commonly known by the nickname **"Don Berna"**.
- Rose in Medellín’s criminal ecosystem during and after the era of large cartel power.

## Paramilitary and Criminal Influence

- Associated with paramilitary structures connected to the **United Self-Defense Forces of Colombia (AUC)**.
- Linked to the **Office of Envigado**, a network that has operated as a broker for organized crime in the Medellín region.
- Used a combination of armed force, intimidation, and negotiated arrangements to consolidate local control.

## Legal Proceedings and Extradition

- In Colombia, his trajectory intersected with demobilization and transitional-justice debates tied to paramilitary groups.
- Extradition to the United States shifted the focus to drug-trafficking prosecutions in U.S. federal courts.

## Historical Significance

Murillo is often cited as an example of how Colombia’s armed conflict and organized crime became intertwined, with actors moving between ideological militias, paramilitary formations, and profit-driven criminal networks.
