## Summary

Timothy Radcliffe is a British Dominican friar who served as Master of the Order of Preachers (Dominicans) from 1992 to 2001. He was the first English-speaking Master in over 700 years. He is known for his engaging preaching and writing on contemporary Christian life. His books, including What is the Point of Being a Christian? and Alive in God, address faith, doubt, and joy with warmth and intellectual depth. He was created a cardinal by Pope Francis in 2024.
