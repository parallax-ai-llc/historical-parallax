## Summary

ISIS twin bombings of Coptic churches on Palm Sunday killing 49 people.

## Context

The attacks occurred amid a period of heightened insurgent and terrorist activity in Egypt, including violence linked to ISIS-affiliated groups operating in the Sinai and a broader campaign targeting state institutions and religious minorities.

Egypt’s Coptic Christians—one of the oldest Christian communities in the Middle East—have periodically been targets of sectarian attacks, and church holidays and large public gatherings have been treated as elevated-risk occasions.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Date:** 9 April 2017 (Palm Sunday)
- **Locations:**
  - **Tanta:** St. George’s Church (Gharbia Governorate)
  - **Alexandria:** St. Mark’s Cathedral complex
- **Attack type:** Coordinated bombings, widely reported as **suicide attacks**

Public reporting summarized the incident as twin explosions striking Coptic churches during Palm Sunday services, resulting in dozens of deaths and injuries.

## Casualties

Counts vary by source and over time; reporting commonly cites roughly **45–49 fatalities** and **100+ injuries** across both sites.

When documenting casualty figures for reference, it is often best to cite the specific source and date of the count because official totals can be revised as victims succumb to injuries.

## Responsibility

ISIS claimed responsibility in public statements reported by major media outlets and summarized in secondary references.

As with many terror incidents, attribution in early reporting can mix claims, investigative findings, and later confirmations; the most stable citation for a short profile is the linked Wikipedia summary.

## Immediate Aftermath

The bombings prompted a major security response, public mourning, and political condemnation.

Egypt’s government announced additional emergency and security measures in the period following the attacks (including widely reported declarations of a state of emergency).

## Why It Matters

The Tanta and Alexandria bombings are frequently cited as a defining moment in the mid-2010s wave of attacks on Egypt’s Coptic community.

They also illustrate a pattern common to high-casualty terrorism: targeting symbolic sites and peak-attendance events to maximize psychological impact, media attention, and societal polarization.
