## Summary

John Stewart Bell was an Irish physicist who devised Bell's theorem, proving that no physical theory of local hidden variables can reproduce all predictions of quantum mechanics. Bell's inequalities provided a way to experimentally test quantum entanglement. His work is one of the most profound results in quantum physics.
