## Summary

Pakistani gang lord who controlled criminal operations in Karachi before being killed by police.

## Background

- Rehman Dakait is widely reported as a Karachi-based gangster from the Lyari neighborhood.
- Some sources identify him by the name **Abdul Rehman Baloch** and describe Lyari as a historically marginalized, densely populated area that has experienced long-running criminal gang activity.
- Reported aliases include *Khan Bhai*, *Khan Saheb*, and *Sardar*.[^1]

## Reported affiliations

- He is described as the founder of the **Peoples' Aman Committee (PAC)**, which has been characterized in reporting as having political links with the **Pakistan Peoples Party (PPP)**.[^1]
- A monetary reward (reported as PKR 5 million) was stated by the Government of Sindh in connection with efforts to capture him.[^1]

## Allegations and criminal activities (high-level)

Public reporting and police claims summarized by secondary sources have attributed a wide range of alleged offenses to him, including:

- dacoity/armed robbery
- murder and attempted murder
- drug trafficking
- kidnapping for ransom
- illegal arms trafficking
- involvement in Lyari gang violence

These are allegations reported in secondary sources and are not reproduced here as detailed case-by-case claims.[^1]

## Context: Lyari and Karachi gang conflict

- Lyari has often been discussed in the context of Karachi's underworld and periodic cycles of armed conflict among local groups.
- Accounts commonly situate Dakait within broader "Lyari gang war" dynamics involving competing factions and retaliatory violence.[^1]

## Death

- He is reported to have been killed in a **police encounter** in Karachi (often specified as the Steel Town area) in 2009.[^1]

## Notes on dates and identifiers

- Different sources may list differing birth/death dates for Dakait; the most widely cited summary references 1980–2009 and a death date in August 2009.[^1]
