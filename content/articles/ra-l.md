## Summary

French cult leader born Claude Vorilhon who founded the Raëlian Movement, claiming aliens created humanity. The group made headlines by falsely claiming to have produced the first human clone in 2002. The movement has thousands of followers worldwide.

## Beliefs and Organization

- The Raëlian Movement presents itself as a new religious movement centered on the idea that extraterrestrials (“Elohim”) created humanity through advanced science.
- Raël’s writings describe a future in which humans achieve scientific immortality (e.g., cloning, mind transfer), themes that the group connects to its spiritual narrative.
- The organization has operated internationally, with varying levels of visibility in different countries and periodic media attention.

## Public Controversies

- The 2002 claim of a successful human clone (announced by a related entity) drew global coverage but was never independently verified.
- Critics and authorities have at times characterized the movement as a cult; supporters describe it as a minority religion advocating sexual freedom and scientific progress.
- Like many new religious movements, it has faced scrutiny about recruitment practices, internal dynamics, and sensational public claims.

## Activism and Messaging

- The movement has promoted campaigns on topics such as atheism, sexual liberation, and freedom of expression.
- It has also leaned into provocative publicity, which has helped it remain in the public conversation despite limited mainstream acceptance.

## Why It Matters

- The Raëlian Movement is frequently cited in discussions of how modern media ecosystems can amplify fringe claims.
- It is also used as a case study in the sociology of religion: charismatic leadership, identity formation, and the boundaries between belief, science, and spectacle.
