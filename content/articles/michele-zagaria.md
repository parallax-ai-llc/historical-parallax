## Summary

Italian Camorra boss of the Casalesi clan who was one of Italy's most wanted fugitives for 16 years. He controlled construction contracts worth billions. Captured in a secret bunker beneath a house in Casapesenna.

## Role in the Casalesi Clan

Zagaria was identified by Italian authorities as a senior leader within the Casalesi clan, a powerful Camorra organization operating primarily in the province of Caserta. The clan has been described as having significant influence over local business activity through intimidation, extortion, and control of procurement in sectors such as construction and waste-related services.

## Alleged Business Control and Criminal Methods

Reporting on the Casalesi clan frequently emphasizes how organized crime groups in Campania can combine violence with economic strategy:

- exerting pressure on firms to win or lose contracts;
- inserting trusted intermediaries into subcontracting chains;
- laundering proceeds through nominally legitimate enterprises.

## Fugitive Years and Capture

Zagaria remained a fugitive for many years, a status that elevated him to Italy's most-wanted lists. His 2011 capture in Casapesenna, in a concealed underground space, was widely publicized as an example of the logistics and local protection networks that can sustain long-term fugitives.

## Aftermath

Following his arrest, coverage typically focuses on the disruption of local clan leadership and the continued efforts by prosecutors to dismantle the Casalesi clan's economic holdings through arrests, trials, and asset seizures.

## Broader Context

The Casalesi clan is often cited in discussions of how Camorra groups blend territorial control with participation in formal markets, especially where public works and permitting create leverage points for corruption.
