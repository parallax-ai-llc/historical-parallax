## Summary

Cult members released sarin nerve gas on five Tokyo subway trains during morning rush hour, killing 13 and injuring thousands. The attack exposed the danger of chemical weapons terrorism.

## Background

- **Perpetrator**: The attack was carried out by members of the Japanese doomsday cult **Aum Shinrikyo**.
- **Precedent incidents**: Aum had already experimented with chemical agents prior to March 1995, including the **1994 Matsumoto sarin attack** (also involving sarin) and other violent crimes linked to the organization.
- **Motives (high level)**: Contemporary reporting and later investigations describe a mix of apocalyptic ideology, paranoia about state suppression, and an attempt to destabilize authorities.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Date / setting**: Morning rush hour in Tokyo on **1995-03-20**.
- **Method**: Operatives carried **liquid sarin** onto multiple subway lines, puncturing containers so the agent evaporated into train cars and stations.
- **Scale**: Five coordinated releases across major commuter routes.
- **Immediate outcome**: **13 deaths** and **thousands injured**, with many more seeking medical evaluation due to exposure fears.

## Health and Emergency Response

- **Symptoms**: Sarin exposure can cause blurred vision, difficulty breathing, convulsions, and respiratory failure; effects can be rapid depending on dose.
- **On-scene challenges**: First responders and hospital staff initially faced uncertainty about the agent, creating risks of **secondary exposure**.
- **Medical countermeasures (general)**: Treatment for nerve agents commonly includes decontamination and antidotes such as **atropine** and **pralidoxime**, alongside supportive respiratory care.

## Aftermath and Consequences

- **Investigation**: The attack triggered extensive raids and investigations into Aum Shinrikyo’s facilities, finances, and procurement networks.
- **Prosecutions**: Numerous members were arrested and tried; several senior figures were sentenced to death or long prison terms.
- **Policy impact**: The incident accelerated Japanese reforms around policing, emergency preparedness, and monitoring of dangerous organizations.
- **Security significance**: It became a landmark case for **chemical terrorism**, shaping global thinking about mass-casualty attacks using toxic industrial and warfare agents.

## Expanded Timeline (Selected)

- **1994**: Matsumoto sarin attack (linked to Aum) foreshadowed nerve-agent use inside Japan.
