## Summary

Tessa Virtue, with Scott Moir, won five Olympic medals including three gold.

## Career Overview

- Virtue is best known as one half of the Canadian ice dance team Virtue/Moir, one of the most decorated pairs in the discipline.
- Their programs were noted for technical consistency and performance quality across multiple Olympic cycles.

## Major Championships and Honors

- Across the 2010, 2014, and 2018 Winter Olympics, the pair accumulated multiple medals, including gold in ice dance.
- They also won world-level titles, contributing to Canada's prominence in figure skating during the period.

## Style and Influence

- Elite ice dance blends required elements (lifts, step sequences, twizzles) with interpretation and timing; Virtue and Moir were often praised for musicality.
- Their visibility helped broaden mainstream interest in ice dance, especially in Canada.

## Life After Competition

- Like many top skaters, Virtue has been involved in skating shows, media projects, and broader sport promotion.
- Retirements in figure skating are sometimes staged over multiple seasons; public communication has typically balanced personal plans with partnership decisions.

## Partnership

- Ice dance results reflect long-term partnership coordination; Virtue and Moir's sustained success over multiple Olympic cycles is relatively rare.
- Their return for the 2018 Olympic cycle is often cited as an example of how elite teams manage rebuilding competitive form after time away.
