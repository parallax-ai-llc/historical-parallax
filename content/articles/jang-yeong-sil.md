## Summary

Jang Yeong-sil was a Korean scientist, inventor, and engineer during the Joseon Dynasty who, despite being born into the slave class, became one of Korea's greatest inventors. He created the world's first rain gauge (cheugugi), an automated water clock (jagyeongnu), and improved metal movable type printing. King Sejong recognized his genius and elevated his social status so he could pursue his inventions.
