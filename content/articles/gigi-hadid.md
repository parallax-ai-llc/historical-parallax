## Summary

Jelena Noura "Gigi" Hadid is an American supermodel who has become one of the most recognizable faces in the global fashion industry since her mainstream breakthrough in 2014. Born into a family with deep ties to the entertainment and real estate worlds, she leveraged both opportunity and relentless work ethic to build a career spanning runway, editorial, and commercial campaigns across every major fashion house.

Hadid's influence extends well beyond modeling. She has shaped beauty standards, championed body diversity in an industry notorious for its rigidity, and used her platform to speak on political and humanitarian issues. With over 75 million Instagram followers as of 2026, she remains a dominant force at the intersection of fashion, culture, and social media.

Her longevity in a notoriously short-lived field, combined with consistent placements at the top of industry rankings, cements her status as a defining figure in 21st-century fashion.

## Early Life & Background

Gigi Hadid was born on April 23, 1995, in Los Angeles, California, to real estate developer Mohamed Hadid and Dutch-American model Yolanda Hadid (née van den Herik). She was raised primarily in Santa Barbara alongside siblings Bella and Anwar, both of whom also pursued modeling careers. Her father is of Palestinian descent, and Hadid has been open about her Arab heritage and its significance to her identity.

Hadid began modeling at age two for Baby Guess before stepping back to focus on academics. She attended Malibu High School, where she was a competitive horseback rider and volleyball player. In 2013, she enrolled at The New School in New York City to study criminal psychology, though she ultimately left to pursue modeling full-time.

## Rise to Prominence

Hadid signed with IMG Models in 2011 and relaunched her professional career in earnest around 2013–2014. Her breakthrough came when she walked in her first New York Fashion Week in February 2014. That same year, she appeared in the Sports Illustrated Swimsuit Issue, dramatically raising her public profile.

By 2015, she had landed major campaigns with Tom Ford, Maybelline, and Tommy Hilfiger. Her collaboration with Tommy Hilfiger — "Tommy x Gigi" — produced co-designed collections in 2016 and 2017 that sold out rapidly and signaled her transition from model to creative collaborator. She closed the Victoria's Secret Fashion Show in 2015 and walked in it again in 2016, the most-watched fashion event annually.

In 2016, she was named International Model of the Year by the British Fashion Council.

## Key Achievements

- Named to the Forbes list of highest-paid models multiple times, earning an estimated $9.5 million in 2016 alone
- Walked for Chanel, Versace, Fendi, Valentino, Marc Jacobs, and virtually every major luxury house
- Appeared on over 35 international Vogue covers by 2025
- Launched Gigi x Maybelline makeup collections beginning in 2017
- Co-designed multiple capsule collections with Tommy Hilfiger and Stuart Weitzman
- Co-founded Guest in Residence, a cashmere knitwear brand, in 2022

## Controversies & Criticism

Hadid faced criticism early in her career from industry figures who questioned whether her physique conformed to traditional runway standards — a critique she addressed publicly, revealing her diagnosis of Hashimoto's disease, which affects metabolism and body composition. Her transparency helped destigmatize the condition.

In 2017, she was criticized for an impersonation of Melania Trump at the American Music Awards that some viewed as culturally insensitive. She also faced backlash in 2017 when a video surfaced of her squinting her eyes in a manner deemed offensive to Asian communities; she issued a public apology.

Her relationship with former One Direction member Zayn Malik attracted intense tabloid scrutiny. The couple's on-and-off relationship and their co-parenting of daughter Khai, born September 2020, has remained a persistent subject of media coverage.

## Legacy & Influence

Hadid arrived at a pivotal moment when social media began dismantling the gatekeeping power of traditional fashion institutions. Her ability to translate runway credibility into massive digital engagement helped redefine what a supermodel could be in the 21st century. She normalized discussing health conditions in the industry, pushed back against critics of her body, and used her platform to advocate for Palestinian rights and humanitarian causes.

Her Guest in Residence venture signals an evolution toward brand-building that may define the next chapter of her career. Younger models frequently cite her as a primary influence, and her career arc — from child model to supermodel to entrepreneur — has become a template studied across the industry.
