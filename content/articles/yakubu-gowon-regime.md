## Summary

Gowon's military government presided over the Biafran War that killed 1-3 million people through combat and engineered famine. Overthrown while attending a summit.

## Background

- **Leadership:** Yakubu Gowon led Nigeria as a military head of state after the 1966 counter-coup, during a period of repeated coups and deep regional tension.
- **Central issue:** The regime is primarily remembered for overseeing the **Nigerian Civil War (Biafran War)** from 1967 to 1970.

## Civil War and Humanitarian Crisis

- **Mass casualties:** The war caused large-scale deaths through combat and a severe humanitarian crisis, including famine conditions in the secessionist region.
- **International attention:** Images of starvation and humanitarian appeals shaped global perceptions of the conflict.

## Governance and Economy (high level)

- **Post-war slogan:** The government promoted post-war reintegration under the idea of "no victor, no vanquished" in official rhetoric.
- **Oil-era state:** The early 1970s coincided with expanding oil revenues, growing state expenditure, and increased scrutiny of corruption and administrative capacity.

## End of the Regime

- **1975 coup:** Gowon was overthrown in a bloodless coup while abroad, reflecting dissatisfaction within the military leadership.

## Legacy

- The Gowon era is debated for its handling of national unity, wartime conduct, and post-war reconstruction.
