## Summary

Bulgarian organized-crime figure widely known by the nickname "Madzho" (Маджо).
Public accounts typically describe him as influential in Sofia’s criminal underworld during the post-communist transition period.

## Background and Identity

- **Nickname**: "Madzho" is commonly used in Bulgarian-language reporting and in public discussion.
- **Name (Latin / Cyrillic)**: Mladen Mihalev / Младен Михалев (spellings may vary in English sources).
- **Source coverage note**: Detailed biographical information is uneven in English-language sources; many summaries rely on secondary reporting.

## Notability (High-level)

- Often described as a key figure associated with the rise of private "security" companies and protection rackets in 1990s Bulgaria.
- Commonly portrayed as part of the broader ecosystem of post-1990 organized crime networks (extortion, smuggling, and control over night-life venues).
- Frequently referenced in Bulgarian media as a symbol of the era’s blurred lines between legitimate business, sports circles, and criminal entrepreneurship.
