## Notes on Naming / Duplication

- “Li Zhengdao” is a romanization that may refer to **Tsung-Dao (T. D.) Lee** (李政道), the Nobel Prize–winning physicist.
- This page was initially marked as a duplicate; the content below treats the subject as **T. D. Lee** and summarizes widely known, high-level facts.

## Key Contributions (High Level)

- **Parity violation in weak interactions (1956–1957):** With **Yang Chen-Ning**, argued that parity conservation had not been established for weak processes and proposed concrete experimental tests; subsequent measurements supported parity nonconservation.
- **Foundational impact:** The parity-violation work helped reshape the modern understanding of fundamental symmetries in particle physics and influenced the development of the electroweak theory.
- **Broader work:** Published across particle physics and related theoretical areas over multiple decades, including topics connected to statistical physics and many-body methods.

## Legacy and Context

- **Nobel Prize (1957):** Shared the Physics Nobel with **Yang Chen-Ning** for the theoretical work that led to the discovery of parity violation.
- **Institutional home:** Closely associated with **Columbia University** for much of his career.
- **Historical significance:** Parity violation is now a standard, textbook example of how an assumed symmetry can fail in nature, motivating careful experimental tests.
