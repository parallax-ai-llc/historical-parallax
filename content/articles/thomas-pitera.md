## Summary

American mobster known as 'Tommy Karate' who was a hitman for the Bonanno crime family. He murdered at least 6 people, dismembering victims and burying them in bird sanctuaries. Trained in martial arts in Japan. Serving life.

## Background

- Pitera became associated with the **Bonanno crime family**, one of the “Five Families” that historically dominated organized crime in New York City.
- His nickname “Tommy Karate” reflected his reputation for physical violence and a personal interest in martial arts.

## Criminal Activity (High-Level)

- Prosecutors described him as an **enforcer/hitman**, responsible for murders connected to internal discipline and criminal disputes.
- The case drew attention for the **dismemberment** of victims and efforts to conceal remains, which contributed to his notoriety.
- His crimes are frequently referenced in discussions of the extreme violence used to maintain fear and control within some organized-crime structures.

## Investigation, Trial, and Sentence

- Federal investigators built the case through a combination of witness testimony and corroborating evidence.
- In 1992, he was convicted of multiple murders and received a **life sentence** in federal prison.
- The prosecution and media coverage emphasized both the number of killings and the unusual disposal methods.

## Notoriety and Legacy

- Pitera is often cited as one of the more violent figures associated with the Bonanno family during the late 20th century.
- His case is used in true-crime writing to illustrate how reputations for brutality can function as a form of “enforcement” within criminal enterprises.
