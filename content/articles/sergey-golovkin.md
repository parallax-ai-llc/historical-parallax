## Summary

Russian serial killer known as Fisher who murdered 11 boys near Moscow between 1986 and 1992. He was the last person executed in Russia.

## Crimes (High-Level)

- Victims were boys, with attacks occurring in the Moscow region.
- The crimes spanned multiple years, which is typical of serial cases where detection is delayed.
- He became known by the moniker **"Fisher"**.

## Arrest and Conviction

- Authorities arrested him in the early 1990s.
- He was tried, convicted of multiple murders, and sentenced to death.

## Execution and Historical Note

- He was executed in 1996.
- He is often described as the **last person executed in Russia**, making the case a reference point in discussions of Russia's later moratorium on executions.

## Investigation (General)

- Long-running child disappearances and murders typically trigger inter-regional coordination, victimology work, and forensic comparison.
- In cases involving repeated offenses, investigators often look for patterns in geography, timing, and offender access to victims.

## Significance

- The case remains widely referenced in Russian true-crime and criminology discussions due to its severity and the timing of the execution.
