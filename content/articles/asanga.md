## Summary

Asanga was an Indian Buddhist monk and philosopher who co-founded the Yogacara (Mind-Only) school of Mahayana Buddhism. He is traditionally said to have received teachings from the future Buddha Maitreya. His major works include the Yogacarabhumi-sastra. Along with his brother Vasubandhu, he developed one of the two most important philosophical systems in Mahayana Buddhism.
