## Summary

OceanGate's Titan submersible imploded during a dive to the Titanic wreck killing all 5 aboard. CEO Stockton Rush had ignored safety warnings and used a modified game controller.

## Overview

The Titan submersible was operated by OceanGate Expeditions as part of commercial deep-sea tourism trips to view the wreck of the RMS *Titanic* in the North Atlantic. On 18 June 2023, the submersible suffered a catastrophic loss during a descent, later assessed by authorities as an implosion consistent with a sudden structural failure under extreme pressure.

## People Aboard

Public reporting identified five people on the dive:

- Stockton Rush (OceanGate CEO and pilot)
- Paul-Henri Nargeolet (Titanic researcher and former French naval officer)
- Hamish Harding (British businessman and adventurer)
- Shahzada Dawood (Pakistani-British businessman)
- Suleman Dawood (Shahzada Dawood's son)

## Search and Confirmation

After contact was lost, a large international search effort was launched involving U.S. and Canadian agencies and private assets. Search operations included surface vessels, aircraft, and remotely operated vehicles (ROVs). Debris consistent with the Titan submersible was later located on the seafloor near the Titanic wreck site, and authorities stated that all five occupants were believed to have died.

## Safety, Engineering, and Oversight Context

The incident prompted widespread scrutiny of:

- **Design choices**: Discussion focused on the risks of novel materials and design approaches for repeated deep dives.
- **Testing and certification**: Commentary emphasized the role of independent classification and safety verification practices in maritime engineering.
- **Organizational culture**: Reporting highlighted internal and external concerns that had been raised prior to the incident, including warnings from industry participants and former staff.

Because many technical details are subject to investigation and expert analysis, high-level descriptions (loss of contact, debris field, implosion conclusion) are more stable than early speculation about the precise failure sequence.

## Investigations and Aftermath

Multiple entities announced or conducted investigations and hearings to understand what happened, identify contributing factors, and evaluate regulatory gaps around privately operated submersibles. Outcomes from these processes have been discussed as potential inputs to future safety standards, insurance requirements, and oversight of commercial deep-sea expeditions.
