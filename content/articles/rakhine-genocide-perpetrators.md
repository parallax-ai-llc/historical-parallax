## Background

- The 201617 wave of violence in Myanmars Rakhine State occurred in the context of long-running discrimination against the Rohingya minority, including restrictions on movement, citizenship status, and access to services.
- International reporting and human-rights documentation describe large-scale attacks on Rohingya villages, mass displacement into Bangladesh, and widespread abuses during clearance operations.

## Perpetrator Profile (High-Level)

This article uses the label perpetrators in a broad, structural sense: individuals and units within Myanmars security apparatus (especially the Tatmadaw / Myanmar Armed Forces and associated forces) alleged to have participated in or enabled atrocities.

Commonly cited categories include:

- Senior commanders responsible for planning or authorizing operations.
- Field commanders and units conducting raids, arrests, or village burnings.
- Security personnel accused of extrajudicial killings, sexual violence, or forced displacement.
- Administrative or logistical actors enabling operations (transport, intelligence, detention systems).

## Documented Allegations (Recurring Themes)

Human-rights investigations and journalistic accounts commonly describe the following patterns during the peak period of violence:

- Coordinated attacks on villages (including arson and destruction of homes).
- Killings and mass graves reported by survivors.
- Sexual and gender-based violence during raids.
- Forced displacement and persecution on ethnic/religious grounds.
- Obstruction of humanitarian access and independent investigation.

## Accountability and International Response

- The Rohingya crisis prompted major international scrutiny, including UN reporting and investigations, and legal/political pressure on Myanmars military leadership.
- Accountability efforts have included sanctions by some governments, calls for international criminal investigation, and proceedings in international courts.
- Because attribution of responsibility can vary by source and by the level of command discussed, reliable summaries typically distinguish between:
  - direct perpetrators (on-the-ground actors),
  - commanders exercising effective control,
  - and institutions that planned or enabled operations.

## Notes on Naming and Attribution

- Many sources focus on organizational responsibility (specific battalions, commands, or leadership roles) rather than naming every individual actor.
- Survivor testimony, satellite imagery, and forensic-style documentation are frequently used to corroborate patterns of violence, but the evidentiary standard for individual criminal liability is higher and may require court findings.
