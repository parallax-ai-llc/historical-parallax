## Summary

Ryokan was a Japanese Zen monk and poet who lived as a hermit and is one of the most beloved figures in Japanese literature. He lived in extreme simplicity in a small hut on Mount Kugami, begging for food and playing with village children. His poetry in both Japanese and Chinese is characterized by its simplicity, warmth, and deep humanity. He embodied the ideal of the Zen poet-monk.
