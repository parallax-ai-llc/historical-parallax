## Summary

Lillian Gilbreth was a pioneer of industrial engineering and ergonomics, applying psychology to time-and-motion studies to improve workplace efficiency and worker welfare. She designed many modern kitchen appliances including the foot-pedal trash can and the shelves inside refrigerator doors. She was the first woman elected to the National Academy of Engineering.
