## Summary

American mobster who was boss of the Trafficante crime family in Tampa, Florida. He controlled gambling operations in pre-revolution Cuba and was recruited by the CIA to assassinate Fidel Castro.

## Additional Details

- **Tampa base**: led the Tampa-based Trafficante organization, which historically derived influence from gambling, racketeering, and labor-related leverage in Florida.[^1]
- **Cuba interests (pre-1959)**: maintained substantial gambling and casino interests in Havana before the Cuban Revolution, alongside other U.S. organized-crime figures involved in the island’s tourism economy.[^1]
- **Castro assassination plots**: public records and later investigations describe Trafficante as one of several mob-linked figures contacted in CIA-backed efforts to target Fidel Castro; accounts vary on the operational details and outcomes.[^1]
- **Congressional scrutiny**: appeared before the House Select Committee on Assassinations (HSCA) in the late 1970s as part of broader inquiry into possible organized-crime connections and intelligence contacts.[^1]
- **Reputation**: often portrayed as a comparatively discreet, long-tenured boss whose influence extended beyond Florida through relationships with other criminal groups and shared gambling interests.[^1]
