## Summary

22-year-old Mahsa Amini died in morality police custody after being arrested for allegedly wearing her hijab improperly. Her death sparked the 'Woman, Life, Freedom' protests across Iran.

## Context

- **Mahsa (Jina) Amini** was a 22-year-old Iranian Kurdish woman.
- She was detained in Tehran by Iran’s **Guidance Patrol** (often described in English-language reporting as the “morality police”) in September 2022.
- Her death rapidly became a focal point for longstanding tensions around **mandatory hijab enforcement**, civil liberties, and state accountability.

## What is publicly reported about the incident

High-level points that are widely reported (details vary by source and remain contested):

- Amini was arrested for alleged non-compliance with Iran’s dress code rules.
- She became unwell while in custody and was taken to a hospital.
- She died shortly afterward, prompting widespread public anger and grief.

## Aftermath: protests and political significance

- Protests spread across many Iranian cities and were commonly associated with the slogan **“Woman, Life, Freedom”**.
- Demonstrations included students, workers, and diverse social groups; they also faced significant crackdowns in many locations.
- The events became an internationally recognized marker of a new phase of Iranian protest activity in the 2020s.

## Why this entry matters

- The death is often cited as a trigger event that transformed local enforcement controversies into a national and global news story.
- It illustrates how a single custody death can catalyze broad-based mobilization when paired with pre-existing social, political, and economic grievances.
