## Summary

American cult leader who founded the Lord Our Righteousness Church and claimed to be the Messiah. He was convicted of sexual contact with minors at his compound in New Mexico.

## Organization and beliefs

Bent led the Lord Our Righteousness Church, a small religious group based in New Mexico. The group drew attention for apocalyptic teachings and for Bent's claim to a unique spiritual status, including statements that he was the Messiah.

## Community structure

Accounts describe the church as operating around a remote compound with a core membership. Such settings can increase dependence on leadership and reduce outside scrutiny, especially when members live or spend extended time on-site.

## Criminal case

Bent was investigated and prosecuted for sexual offenses involving minors. He was convicted in New Mexico state court and received a multi-year prison sentence.

## Aftermath

The case is often cited in discussions of:

- Abuse of authority in closed or high-control communities.
- The role of law enforcement and social services in responding to allegations involving religious leaders.
- Long-term impacts on victims and former members, including the challenge of reintegration after leaving insular groups.

## Related themes

Cases like Bent's are sometimes discussed alongside broader topics such as coercive control, spiritual abuse, and safeguarding policies for minors in communities where leadership authority is treated as unquestionable.
