## Summary

Former Mayor and Governor of São Paulo placed on Interpol's most wanted list for corruption. Embezzled over $300 million from public works projects.

## Political profile

Maluf was a long-running figure in São Paulo and national Brazilian politics, associated with a style of machine politics built around public-works promises, patronage networks, and personalistic campaigning.

## Corruption allegations

He has faced repeated allegations and court proceedings involving diversion of public funds and the laundering of money through offshore structures. These cases became emblematic in Brazil of how infrastructure spending can be paired with kickback systems and procurement manipulation.

## International dimension

Because some allegations involved funds moved through international financial centers, his name appeared in cross-border legal cooperation efforts. Interpol notices and foreign court actions have been part of the public narrative around attempts to locate and repatriate assets.

## Why it is cited

The Maluf story is often used to illustrate:

- How corruption investigations can extend across decades.
- The gap between political influence at home and vulnerability to prosecution later in life.
- The practical challenges of asset recovery, especially when money is routed through layers of companies and intermediaries.

## Legacy

In Brazilian political culture, Maluf became a byword for alleged corruption in public life. Even where specific claims were litigated case by case, the broader reputational association has remained durable.
