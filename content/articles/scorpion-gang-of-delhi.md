## Summary

Indian kidnapping gang led by Sonu Punjaban (Geeta Arora) that abducted and trafficked young women in Delhi's red light district GB Road. The gang forced hundreds of girls into prostitution.

## Background and Context

- Delhi’s G.B. Road (Garstin Bastion Road) is widely reported as a hub for sex work and trafficking networks, and police actions against trafficking have periodically focused on the area.
- The “Scorpion Gang” name became known through reporting and subsequent police cases describing organized kidnapping and coercion of young women.

## Modus Operandi (High-Level)

- Victims were typically lured or abducted and then moved across locations to reduce the chance of rescue.
- Coercion methods described in public reporting include threats, violence, and restricting contact with families.
- The group is commonly characterized as having operated with multiple accomplices involved in recruitment, transport, and confinement.

## Key Figure

- Sonu Punjaban (also reported as Geeta Arora) is identified in major accounts as a leader of the gang and the central defendant in later prosecutions.

## Legal Proceedings and Aftermath

- Arrest, custody escape, and re-arrest are frequently highlighted as turning points in the case narrative.
- Conviction and sentencing (as reported in major summaries) are treated as the culmination of a long-running investigation and trial process.

## Significance

- The case is often cited in broader discussions of trafficking enforcement in India, including the challenges of victim identification, witness protection, and long-term rehabilitation.
- Media attention to the gang contributed to public awareness of trafficking patterns linked to urban red-light districts.
