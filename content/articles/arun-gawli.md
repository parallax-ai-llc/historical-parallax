## Summary

Indian gangster known as Daddy who led BRA gang in Mumbai. Later entered politics but convicted of ordering a murder.

## Underworld Role (High-Level)

- Associated with Mumbai’s organized-crime landscape that emerged alongside labor disputes, real-estate conflicts, and intense competition for local influence.
- Was linked to a gang often described in media accounts as the **B.R.A. gang**, operating during an era when rival factions fought for territory and protection rackets.

## Public Persona and Politics

Gawli cultivated a public image that mixed fear, patronage, and localized “welfare” style support, which helped him transition into formal politics and win an assembly seat.

## Conviction

He was later convicted in a high-profile murder case, illustrating the porous boundary that can exist between criminal networks and electoral politics in some urban contexts.

## Context

The broader story is frequently used to discuss:

- The relationship between organized crime and real-estate development.
- How local patronage and intimidation can shape voting blocs.
- The challenges of policing and witness protection in gang-related prosecutions.
