## Summary

Indian media tycoon raided by enforcement agencies for alleged tax evasion and money laundering.

## Background

- Indian media entrepreneur and investor best known for building the Network18 media group and later co-founding Quintillion Media (The Quint).[^1]
- Worked across television, digital media, and media-related investments/partnerships in India, including business news and general entertainment properties associated with Network18-era assets.[^1]

## Education

- Schooled at St. Xavier's School, Delhi.[^1]
- Studied Economics (Honours) at St. Stephen's College, Delhi.[^1]
- Completed an MBA at the Faculty of Management Studies (FMS), Delhi University.[^1]

## Key Organizations and Roles (high-level)

- **Network18 Group**: described as a media group started in 1993 that grew into a large collection of media properties in India; Bahl is described as its founding/controlling shareholder and managing director.[^1]
- **TV18 / CNBC-TV18**: associated with TV18 and the launch of CNBC-TV18 (1999) per Wikipedia; also associated with other channels/properties during the Network18 period.[^1]
- **Quintillion Media / Quint Digital**: co-founded with Ritu Kapur after departing Network18; associated with TheQuint.com and, later, the BloombergQuint joint venture (launched 2016; partnership ended 2022).[^1]

## Notes on Reported Scrutiny and Allegations

- Wikipedia describes an Income Tax Department raid in October 2018 related to alleged tax-evasion-linked issues (including claims about bogus LTCG) and notes that Bahl provided a statement disputing the allegations.[^1]
- As with many such cases, the article-level takeaway is simply that enforcement scrutiny and allegations were publicly reported; readers should consult primary documents and reliable reporting for procedural status and outcomes.
