## Summary

First President of Mali who established a socialist one-party state. Overthrown by Moussa Traoré and died under suspicious circumstances while in detention.

## Independence-era context

Keïta's presidency sits in the first wave of post-colonial governance in West Africa, when newly independent states were building institutions, national identities, and economic strategies under Cold War pressure.

## Governance and ideology (high level)

He is commonly associated with:

- a strong central state and single-party rule
- socialist-oriented economic planning and state-led development
- efforts to reduce external dependency while seeking international partnerships

## Overthrow and detention

His removal from power is frequently discussed as part of a broader pattern of military coups in the region, where economic stress, political opposition, and security-sector dynamics reshaped leadership.

## Historical significance

Keïta is referenced in debates about:

- the trade-offs between rapid state-building and political pluralism
- development models in the Sahel and their constraints
- how early post-independence decisions influenced later instability
