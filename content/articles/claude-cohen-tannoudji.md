## Additional Context

### Background and education

Cohen-Tannoudji was born in **Constantine (French Algeria)** and later moved to Paris for higher education, including study at the **École normale supérieure (ENS)**. He is often noted for bridging rigorous theoretical training with influential experimental work.

### Scientific contributions

His name is closely associated with **laser cooling** and the ability to cool atoms below classical Doppler limits (often discussed under **sub-Doppler** and **recoil**-scale temperatures). These techniques became foundational for modern atomic physics and helped enable precision measurements and quantum-control experiments.

### Nobel Prize and collaborators

He shared the **1997 Nobel Prize in Physics** with **Steven Chu** and **William D. Phillips** for developing methods to cool and trap atoms with laser light.

### Teaching and influence

Beyond research papers, Cohen-Tannoudji is widely recognized for educational influence through advanced teaching and widely used texts/lecture material in quantum mechanics. His academic lineage also includes prominent students and collaborators in the French atomic-physics community.

### Awards and recognition

In addition to the Nobel Prize, biographies frequently list major international physics awards (e.g., the Harvey Prize and other society medals) as markers of the broad impact of his work.
