## Summary

Fernando Botero was a Colombian figurative artist known for his distinctive style of inflated, voluptuous forms in paintings and sculptures. His rotund figures, applied to still lifes, landscapes, and political subjects including the Abu Ghraib series, created one of the most instantly recognizable styles in contemporary art. His monumental bronze sculptures are displayed in public spaces worldwide.
