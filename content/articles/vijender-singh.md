## Summary

Vijender Singh won Olympic bronze at 2008 Beijing, the first Indian boxer to win an Olympic medal.

## Background

- **Origin**: Indian boxer from Haryana, who rose through the national amateur system into international competition.[^1]
- **Weight classes**: Competed across multiple divisions in amateur boxing, with notable results in the middleweight range in major tournaments.[^1]

## Amateur Career Highlights

Singh’s Olympic medal was part of a longer amateur track record commonly highlighted in summaries of Indian boxing:

- **2006**: Won a medal at the Commonwealth Games (Melbourne), helping raise visibility for Indian boxing on a multi-sport stage.[^1]
- **2008**: Olympic bronze medal in Beijing — the first Olympic boxing medal for India.[^1]
- **2009**: Won a medal at the World Amateur Boxing Championships, adding a world-championship podium to his résumé.[^1]

## Professional Boxing

- **Turned professional**: After his amateur career peak, Singh entered professional boxing and promoted bouts in India, where mainstream attention to boxing has historically been limited compared with cricket and a few other sports.[^1]
- **Public profile**: As an Olympic medalist, he became one of the more recognizable faces of Indian boxing, which can influence sponsorship, youth participation, and media coverage beyond immediate sporting results.[^1]

## Impact and Significance

- **Milestone for Indian boxing**: The 2008 Olympic bronze is often treated as a landmark result that helped validate boxing as an Olympic-path sport in India and encouraged investment in training and talent identification.[^1]
- **Symbolic role**: Singh’s career is frequently referenced as evidence that Indian athletes can achieve podium finishes in combat sports on the biggest stages with sustained institutional support.[^1]
