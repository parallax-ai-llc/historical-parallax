## Summary

Known as 'El Rey,' brother of Sinaloa Cartel leader El Mayo. Became a key government witness testifying against El Chapo at his US trial.

## Additional Context

### Family and cartel affiliation

- Zambada García is widely known by the nickname “El Rey” and is reported as a brother of Ismael “El Mayo” Zambada, a long-time Sinaloa Cartel leader.
- In accounts of Mexican organized crime, the Zambada family is frequently described as part of the Sinaloa Cartel’s upper leadership network.

### Arrest and legal path

- He was arrested in Mexico and later became involved in U.S. legal proceedings.
- Public reporting emphasizes that his case was notable not only for the cartel connection but for the extent of his cooperation.

### Cooperation and testimony

- Zambada García became a cooperating witness and later testified in U.S. court, including at Joaquín “El Chapo” Guzmán’s trial.
- Testimony from cooperating cartel insiders is often used to outline trafficking routes, organizational hierarchy, and operational methods, while also being scrutinized due to incentives created by plea agreements.

### Broader significance

- His cooperation is frequently referenced in discussions of how U.S. prosecutions of transnational criminal organizations rely on insider witnesses.
- The high-profile nature of the Guzmán trial turned “El Rey” into a well-known figure beyond typical law-enforcement circles.
