## Summary

ISIS-linked militants attacked the town of Palma near a massive TotalEnergies gas project, killing dozens. The attack led to evacuation of foreign workers and suspension of the $20 billion project.

## Background

- Palma is a coastal town in **Cabo Delgado** province, northern Mozambique, near the Afungi peninsula where the **Mozambique LNG** project (led by TotalEnergies) was being developed.
- The attack occurred amid the broader **Cabo Delgado insurgency**, in which armed groups including an ISIS-aligned faction (often referred to as **Islamic State–Mozambique/ISCAP**) carried out raids and seized territory.

## Attack Overview

- Militants launched coordinated assaults around **24 March 2021**, targeting Palma’s urban center and key routes.
- The attack triggered intense fighting over multiple days, including ambushes and attacks on convoys attempting to evacuate civilians and foreign workers.
- Reported casualty figures vary by source; many accounts describe **dozens** of deaths, with significant uncertainty due to the chaotic conditions and delayed access.

## Immediate Aftermath

- Evacuation efforts moved people by **road and sea** to safer areas, with additional air evacuation reported for some foreign personnel.
- The violence and insecurity led TotalEnergies to declare **force majeure** and suspend construction activities on the LNG project.
- The event drew heightened international attention to security risks in Cabo Delgado and the vulnerability of large infrastructure projects in conflict zones.

## Longer-Term Impact

- The Palma attack is frequently cited as a key inflection point that contributed to:
  - prolonged delays in restarting LNG construction,
  - intensified regional security involvement,
  - and broader displacement and humanitarian pressures in northern Mozambique.
- Subsequent security operations in 2021 included foreign deployments (notably **Rwandan** forces and a **SADC** mission), which reported gains against insurgent positions; the security situation, however, remained complex.

## Key Entities

- **Armed group:** ISIS-linked militants operating in Cabo Delgado (terminology varies across reporting).
- **Government:** Mozambique security forces.
- **Industry:** TotalEnergies and partners associated with the Mozambique LNG development.
- **Affected populations:** residents of Palma and surrounding areas, plus workers and contractors connected to nearby projects.
