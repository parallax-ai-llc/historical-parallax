## Summary

Palestinian militant known as the 'Master' who organized PFLP's aircraft hijacking operations. He planned the Entebbe hijacking and coordinated with international terrorist groups. Israel's Mossad allegedly poisoned him with contaminated chocolate.

## Additional Context

### Role in the PFLP

Haddad was a founding figure in the **Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine (PFLP)** and became associated with its most internationally visible operations, particularly aircraft hijackings intended to secure prisoner releases and global attention.

### International operations

Open-source histories often link him to planning or coordinating multiple hijackings in the late 1960s and 1970s. The **Entebbe hijacking (1976)**, which led to an Israeli rescue operation in Uganda, is one of the most widely cited events connected to networks around the PFLP.

### Internal splits and control

Accounts describe tensions between Haddad and other PFLP leadership over strategy, including the political costs of high-profile international attacks. Such disagreements contributed to factional dynamics within Palestinian militant organizations during the period.

### Death and allegations

He died in 1978 in East Germany (then the GDR). Some sources allege that Israel's Mossad assassinated him via poisoning (commonly described as contaminated food such as chocolate), though the specific operational details are difficult to independently verify from public records.

### Historical significance

Haddad is often discussed in studies of the evolution of international terrorism in the 1970s, when airline hijackings became a prominent tactic with global media impact.
