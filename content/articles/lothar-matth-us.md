## Summary

Lothar Matthäus won 150 caps, captained Germany to the 1990 WC, and won the first FIFA World Player award.

## Club career

Matthäus developed into an elite midfielder in the German Bundesliga, becoming known for his athleticism, ball-winning, and ability to drive play forward. He is strongly associated with Bayern Munich, where he won major domestic honors across multiple stints, and with Inter Milan, where he was a key figure during a successful period that included major European and Italian achievements.

## International career

A long-serving member of West Germany and later unified Germany, Matthäus was renowned for his durability and leadership. He played in multiple World Cups and European Championships and is remembered for captaining the national team to victory at the 1990 FIFA World Cup. His international record includes a very high number of appearances and a significant scoring contribution for a midfielder.

## Style of play

Often described as a complete midfielder, Matthäus combined defensive intensity with long-range shooting and passing range. Over his career he also played in more defensive roles, including as a sweeper/libero, adapting his game as his physical profile changed.

## Awards and recognition

In 1991 he won the inaugural FIFA World Player of the Year award. He is frequently included in discussions of the greatest German footballers and among the most influential midfielders of his era.

## Post-playing career

After retiring as a player, Matthäus worked as a coach and football commentator/pundit, with managerial roles in several countries. His media presence kept him prominent in German-speaking football coverage.
