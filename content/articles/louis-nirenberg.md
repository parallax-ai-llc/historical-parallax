## Summary

Louis Nirenberg was a Canadian-American mathematician who made fundamental contributions to partial differential equations and their application to complex analysis and geometry. His Gagliardo-Nirenberg interpolation inequality and work on elliptic regularity are cornerstones of modern analysis. He shared the Abel Prize in 2015 with John Nash.
