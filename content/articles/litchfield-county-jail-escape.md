## Summary

Ted Bundy escaped from Pitkin County Courthouse by jumping from a second-story window during a recess while representing himself. He escaped again later from Garfield County Jail.

## Background (Ted Bundy escapes referenced in this article)

The summary above describes two well-documented 1977 escapes by serial killer Ted Bundy while he was facing murder charges in Colorado. These incidents are commonly cited as examples of custody failures involving:

- A defendant representing himself (greater courtroom access and reduced restraints)
- Reliance on minimal staffing during routine recesses
- Physical security gaps in older facilities (windows, stairwells, and unsecured corridors)

## Key Events (high-level)

- **Courthouse escape (Pitkin County, Aspen, Colorado)**: Bundy escaped during a court proceeding by leaving a second-story area and fleeing on foot.
- **Jail escape (Garfield County, Glenwood Springs, Colorado)**: Bundy later escaped from a county jail facility, after which he traveled across state lines.

## Immediate Aftermath

Public reporting and later retrospectives generally emphasize that the escapes triggered:

- Intensive manhunts and expanded interagency coordination
- Review of courtroom and detention security procedures
- Increased attention to transport and supervision standards for high-risk detainees
