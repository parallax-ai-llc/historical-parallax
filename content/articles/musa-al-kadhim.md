## Summary

Musa al-Kadhim was the seventh Imam in Twelver Shia Islam, known for his patience and forbearance during Abbasid persecution. He was a scholar of Islamic law and theology who established an extensive network of agents to manage the Shia community. He was imprisoned by the Abbasid caliphs for years and reportedly died of poisoning in prison. His shrine in Kadhimiya, Baghdad, is one of the holiest sites in Shia Islam.
