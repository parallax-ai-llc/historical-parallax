## Summary

Chief of the Babbar Khalsa International militant organization who led one of Punjab's most violent separatist groups during the Khalistan movement. He was killed in a police encounter.

## Background (High Level)

Babbar is commonly described in sources as a leading figure associated with militant Sikh separatism during a period of intense political conflict and insurgency in Punjab. The era involved targeted attacks, heavy security responses, and long-lasting political and social impacts in the region.

## Organization and Activity

Babbar Khalsa International (BKI) has been described as a militant/separatist organization linked in public reporting to violent operations during the 1980s and early 1990s. Accounts of leadership roles typically focus on organizing cadres, propaganda, and operational planning, alongside networks that extended beyond Punjab.

## Conflict Context

The Khalistan movement encompassed a range of political and militant currents advocating for Sikh autonomy or an independent state. In many summaries, the period is characterized by cycles of violence and counterinsurgency, including accusations of abuses by multiple actors.

## Aftermath and Designations

Militant groups from the period have been subject to later scrutiny, bans, and terrorism-related designations in some jurisdictions. Historical narratives often distinguish between political grievances and the use of violence against civilians.

## Terminology Note

In Indian policing contexts, the phrase “police encounter” is commonly used for armed confrontations leading to deaths; in some cases it has also been debated in public discourse, so incident details are often presented with caution unless independently verified.

## Terms (Quick Glossary)

- **Separatism**: Political advocacy for secession or creation of a new state.
- **Insurgency / counterinsurgency**: Armed rebellion and the state’s response to suppress it.
