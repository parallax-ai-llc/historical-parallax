## Summary

English pirate who captured a rich Arab merchant vessel and retired wealthy in France. One of the few pirates to successfully retire from piracy with his fortune intact.

## Additional Context

Christopher Condent (also spelled "Congdon" in some sources) operated during the so-called Golden Age of Piracy, when European imperial competition created lucrative maritime targets.

### Operations in the Indian Ocean

- Indian Ocean piracy targeted heavily laden merchant ships traveling between the Middle East, India, and European ports.
- Capturing a single rich vessel could yield life-changing wealth, which explains why some pirates sought "one big prize" rather than long careers.

### Retirement and reintegration

Condent is often cited as a rare case of a pirate who successfully exited the profession.

- Accounts emphasize that he retired with substantial proceeds.
- His later life in France reflects how pirates sometimes used pardons, bribes, or legal gray zones to re-enter society.

### Why his story stands out

- Many pirates were killed in combat, captured, or executed; Condent’s survival and retirement is atypical.
- His career connects to broader themes: maritime trade routes, colonial rivalry, and the thin line between privateering and piracy.

### Notes

- Historical details about individual pirates can be fragmentary; modern biographies often reconcile court records, newspapers, and later secondary accounts.
