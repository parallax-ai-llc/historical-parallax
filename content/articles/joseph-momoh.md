## Summary

President of Sierra Leone whose corrupt and incompetent rule led directly to the RUF civil war. Overthrown in a military coup while the country descended into chaos.

## Political career

- Momoh was a military officer before entering Sierra Leone’s top political office.
- He succeeded Siaka Stevens and led the country during a period marked by economic strain, governance challenges, and growing public dissatisfaction.

## Governance and reforms

- His administration faced criticism over corruption allegations, patronage politics, and the capacity of state institutions.
- Sierra Leone’s one-party political framework and pressures for political liberalization were major themes of the era.

## Road to civil war

- The Revolutionary United Front (RUF) insurgency began in 1991.
- Analysts typically point to multiple contributing factors, including regional conflict spillovers, control of diamond resources, weak state capacity, and grievances related to governance and inequality.

## 1992 coup and aftermath

- In 1992, Momoh was overthrown by a military coup amid worsening security conditions.
- The coup did not end the conflict; Sierra Leone’s civil war continued for years and became one of the most severe crises in the region during the 1990s.

## Legacy

- Momoh’s presidency is often assessed in the context of state fragility, the political economy of natural resources, and the difficulty of reform under entrenched patronage systems.
