## Summary

Indian political figure known as 'Chinnamma' who was the close aide and companion of Tamil Nadu CM Jayalalithaa. She was convicted in the disproportionate assets case and sentenced to 4 years. She attempted to become CM after Jayalalithaa's death.

## Additional Context

### Relationship to AIADMK power structure

- Sasikala's influence was widely discussed in connection with Jayalalithaa's long tenure and the centralized control of party decision-making.
- Her role was often described as informal but consequential, particularly in personnel decisions and access to the chief minister.

### Disproportionate assets case (high level)

- The case concerned allegations of assets disproportionate to known income, a common legal framing in Indian anti-corruption prosecutions.
- Conviction and imprisonment significantly curtailed her immediate political ambitions, especially the attempt to become chief minister after 2016.

### Post-2016 succession and factional splits

- After Jayalalithaa's death, AIADMK experienced factional competition and realignment.
- Sasikala and associated figures were central to one pole of the split, while other leaders consolidated control of the government and party apparatus.
- Court rulings and party disciplinary actions later shaped her formal standing within party structures.
