## Summary

The 41st Sakya Trizin, Ngawang Kunga Theckchen Palbar Samphel, is the head of the Sakya school of Tibetan Buddhism. He is considered one of the most learned Tibetan Buddhist masters alive. He fled Tibet in 1959 and has been based in India and internationally. He passed the throne to his elder son in 2017 while continuing to teach worldwide. The Sakya tradition is known for its emphasis on scholarship and the Lamdre (Path and Fruit) teachings.
