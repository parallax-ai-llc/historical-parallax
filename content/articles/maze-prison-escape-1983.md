## Summary

38 IRA prisoners escaped from the supposedly escape-proof Maze Prison in Northern Ireland, the largest prison break in British history. One guard died of a heart attack.

## Context

- **Date & place:** 25 September 1983, HM Prison Maze (Long Kesh), County Antrim, Northern Ireland.
- **Setting:** The prison held paramilitary prisoners during the Troubles, with separate areas for loyalists and republicans.
- **Security posture:** It was widely described as a maximum-security facility and marketed as exceptionally hard to escape.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Seizure of H-Block 7 (H7):** Prisoners took control by taking prison officers hostage and preventing alarms from being raised early.
- **Weapons:** Contemporary accounts describe handguns being smuggled into the prison and used to control hostages.
- **Use of a supply vehicle:** A lorry delivering food supplies was taken over and used as part of the escape attempt.
- **Gatehouse incident:** A group in officers’ uniforms attempted to take over the gatehouse to open the main gate.
- **Perimeter breach:** The situation escalated to a rapid, chaotic breach of the prison perimeter once alarms were finally raised.

## Casualties and Injuries

- **One prison officer died** after suffering a heart attack during the escape.
- **Multiple staff were injured** during the takeover and fighting, including reports of stabbings and shootings.

## Immediate Aftermath

- The escape was treated as a major security and political crisis in the United Kingdom.
- It was widely regarded as a significant propaganda victory and morale boost for the Provisional IRA.
- An official inquiry was ordered, with security procedures and prison management coming under intense scrutiny.

## Inquiry and Findings (Overview)

- An official investigation reported extensive operational failures and issued recommendations to tighten security.
- Public debate included whether the breakdown was primarily due to staff error, institutional design limits, or political constraints on prison operations.

## Recapture (Broad Outline)

- Security forces launched an immediate manhunt with roadblocks and coordinated searches.
- A substantial number of escapees were recaptured quickly, while a smaller group remained at large for longer periods.
