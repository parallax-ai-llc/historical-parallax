## Summary

Daniel Humm is a Swiss-born chef widely regarded as one of the foremost culinary figures of his generation. Best known as the chef and owner of Eleven Madison Park in New York City, Humm has earned some of the most prestigious accolades in the restaurant world, including three Michelin stars and a ranking of number one on the World's 50 Best Restaurants list. His cooking is defined by a philosophy that prizes precision, restraint, and the emotional resonance of a meal.

Beyond technical mastery, Humm has distinguished himself through bold reinvention. In 2021, he transformed Eleven Madison Park into a fully plant-based restaurant, a decision that generated global debate about the future of fine dining. His willingness to risk commercial and critical standing on a principled vision marks him as one of the most consequential — and polarizing — voices in contemporary gastronomy.

## Early Life & Background

Daniel Humm was born on April 16, 1976, in Strengelbach, Switzerland. He showed an early aptitude for cooking and began his culinary apprenticeship at age 14, training in Switzerland's classical European tradition. He worked in some of Switzerland's finest kitchens during his formative years, gaining foundational skills in French technique and discipline. At 24, he became the executive chef of Gasthaus zum Gupf in the Swiss Alps, where he earned his first Michelin star — the youngest chef in Switzerland to do so at that time.

## Rise to Prominence

In 2003, Humm moved to the United States, taking a position as executive chef at Campton Place in San Francisco, where he quickly earned a Michelin star. In 2006, he was recruited to lead the kitchen at Eleven Madison Park in New York City, then a brasserie-style restaurant struggling to define itself. Humm partnered with restaurateur Will Guidara to reposition the restaurant as a destination for refined, ingredient-driven American cuisine. The transformation was swift and decisive: Eleven Madison Park earned three Michelin stars by 2012 and was named the World's Best Restaurant by the World's 50 Best in 2017.

In 2011, Humm and Guidara purchased the restaurant from Danny Meyer's Union Square Hospitality Group. The pair also opened NoMad restaurant and bar, expanding their hospitality footprint. Guidara departed the partnership in 2019, leaving Humm as sole owner.

## Key Achievements

- Three Michelin stars, maintained continuously since 2012
- World's Best Restaurant (World's 50 Best), 2017
- James Beard Award for Outstanding Chef, 2012
- Youngest chef to receive a Michelin star in Switzerland
- Authored several acclaimed cookbooks, including *Eleven Madison Park: The Cookbook* (2011) and *I Love New York* (2013)

## Controversies & Criticism

Humm's 2021 decision to convert Eleven Madison Park to an entirely plant-based menu drew both admiration and sharp criticism. Many longtime patrons and food critics questioned whether the restaurant could sustain its reputation without the animal proteins central to its celebrated dishes, including its signature lavender honey duck. Some critics argued the pivot was ideologically motivated at the expense of culinary excellence. The restaurant lost one of its Michelin stars in 2022, reigniting debate. Others viewed the move as courageous leadership on sustainability. Humm has also faced scrutiny over workplace culture at his restaurants, though no formal legal actions have been widely reported.

## Legacy & Influence

Humm's influence on American fine dining is substantial. His tenure at Eleven Madison Park helped rehabilitate New York's standing in global gastronomy and demonstrated that American cuisine could compete with Europe's most storied institutions. His plant-based pivot, regardless of its commercial outcome, shifted industry conversations around sustainability and chef responsibility. Humm continues to operate at the intersection of culinary ambition and ethical conviction, a combination that has made him one of the most discussed chefs of his era.
