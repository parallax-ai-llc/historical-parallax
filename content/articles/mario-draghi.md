## Summary

Mario Draghi served as President of the European Central Bank from 2011 to 2019 and later as Prime Minister of Italy. His 2012 pledge to do 'whatever it takes' to save the euro is credited with preventing the breakup of the eurozone. He implemented quantitative easing and negative interest rates to stimulate the European economy. He also served as Italian Prime Minister from 2021 to 2022.
