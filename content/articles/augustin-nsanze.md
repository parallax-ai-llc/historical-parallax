## Context

The 1993 political crisis in Burundi followed the assassination of President Melchior Ndadaye and a failed coup attempt. The period escalated into widespread intercommunal violence and contributed to the start of the Burundian Civil War (1993–2005).

## Allegations and Accountability

Nsanze is described in secondary summaries as having been implicated in violence associated with the 1993 crisis. Publicly available accounts typically frame this as allegation/implication rather than as a fully adjudicated historical finding.

When documenting figures associated with mass violence, it is common to distinguish between:

- **Operational command** (whether a person held authority over units present at crimes)
- **Direct participation** (whether a person is alleged to have personally carried out acts)
- **Legal responsibility** (whether a court or tribunal has made findings)

## Why This Matters

The Burundi case is frequently cited as an example of how political assassinations and contested transitions can rapidly trigger mass atrocities, especially when security forces fracture and civilian militias mobilize.
