## Summary

Derek Barton was a British chemist who shared the 1969 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his contributions to the development of the concept of conformation and its application in chemistry. His conformational analysis showed how the three-dimensional arrangement of atoms affects molecular behavior and chemical reactivity, revolutionizing organic chemistry.
