## Summary

A section of the Seongsu Bridge over the Han River collapsed during morning rush hour, dropping a bus and cars into the river. 32 people died due to shoddy construction and poor maintenance.

## Background

The Seongsu Bridge opened in 1979 and became one of the busiest crossings over the Han River, linking Seongdong District (north) to Gangnam District (south). Rapid urban growth increased traffic loads and elevated the importance of routine inspection and maintenance.

## What Happened

On October 21, 1994, during morning rush hour, a section of the bridge's upper structure failed, causing vehicles to fall into the river. The disaster resulted in:

- **32 deaths**
- **17 injuries**

## Contributing Factors (as reported in investigations)

Accounts of the disaster cite a combination of engineering and governance failures, including:

- Faulty or inadequate welding during construction
- Components degraded by corrosion and fatigue over time
- Insufficient maintenance and inspection practices
- Delayed or ineffective responses to earlier warning signs

## Accountability and Aftermath

The collapse triggered major public scrutiny and legal consequences. Reporting on the case notes:

- Multiple officials and construction-related personnel were convicted on negligence-related charges
- Senior city leadership faced political repercussions, including resignations
- The incident became a reference point in South Korea for strengthening safety oversight on critical infrastructure

## Legacy

The Seongsu Bridge disaster is frequently cited alongside other modernization-era accidents in South Korea as an example of how fast development can amplify systemic risk when construction quality control and long-term maintenance are under-resourced.
