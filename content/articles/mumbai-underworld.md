## Summary

Network of organized crime gangs that dominated Mumbais criminal landscape from the 1970s to 2000s. Major gangs included D-Company, Chhota Rajan gang, and the Pathan gangs.

## Additional Context

### Origins and structure

The term “Mumbai underworld” generally refers to a shifting ecosystem of organized-crime groups rather than a single organization. It grew alongside the city’s port economy and informal labor markets, with early profits often tied to smuggling, protection rackets, and control of local unions.

Over time, the underworld developed characteristics typical of mature organized crime:

- Hierarchical gangs with disciplined enforcement and networks of intermediaries.
- Cross-border finance and logistics, including hawala-style money movement.
- Deep ties to legitimate businesses used for laundering and influence.

### Crime markets and influence

Activities commonly associated with Mumbai’s underworld across decades include:

- Extortion, kidnapping, and contract violence.
- Smuggling (gold, electronics, narcotics) and later more complex financial crimes.
- Influence operations in real estate, construction, and certain entertainment sectors.

Public discourse also frequently highlighted alleged links between some underworld figures and parts of the film industry, especially during periods when informal financing and intimidation were widely reported.

### Gang rivalries and fragmentation

The “underworld” label often masks an ongoing pattern of factional conflict:

- Rival groups competed for territory, revenue streams, and political protection.
- Splits within major networks produced cycles of retaliatory killings.
- Crackdowns, extradition efforts, and international sanctions reshaped leadership and pushed parts of the ecosystem offshore.

### Relationship to political and communal violence

Analysts and investigators have periodically described overlap between organized crime and broader political violence, including the use of criminal networks for procurement, logistics, and intimidation. The 1990s in particular are often cited as a period when crime, communal tensions, and terrorism-related cases intersected in Mumbai.
