## Summary

Padmasambhava (Guru Rinpoche) was an Indian tantric Buddhist master who is credited with bringing Buddhism to Tibet in the 8th century. He subdued local spirits and demons, converting them into protectors of the Dharma. He founded the Nyingma (Ancient) school of Tibetan Buddhism. He is venerated as a second Buddha in Tibetan Buddhism, and his hidden treasure texts (terma) continue to be discovered.
