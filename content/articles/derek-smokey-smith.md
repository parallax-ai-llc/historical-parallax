## Summary

British bank robber who was part of the Securitas depot robbery gang that stole £53 million from a Kent cash depot in 2006. It was the largest cash robbery in British history.

## Context

The Securitas depot robbery is frequently cited in discussions of organized robbery in the UK because of its scale and because it targeted a cash-handling facility rather than a conventional bank branch.

Large cash robberies typically involve surveillance, logistics, and multiple roles (planning, access, transport, and laundering), which is why investigators often treat them as organized-crime cases rather than opportunistic theft.

## Aftermath and Significance

The case is remembered as a benchmark for "largest cash robbery" in British history at the time, and it contributed to public scrutiny of cash-depot security practices.

Sentencing outcomes in such cases often reflect both the amount stolen and the degree of organization and coercion involved.
