## Summary

Bruce Beutler is an American immunologist who shared the 2011 Nobel Prize for discoveries concerning innate immunity.

## Context and Significance

Beutler is best known for work that helped clarify how the innate immune system detects microbial components and initiates inflammatory responses. In broad terms, this line of research connected molecular recognition (how the body “senses” pathogens) to downstream signaling pathways that coordinate early immune defenses.

## Research Contributions (High Level)

- **Innate immune sensing:** His research is widely associated with identifying key receptors involved in detecting pathogen-associated molecules.
- **Inflammation and signaling:** The work helped anchor later studies of cytokine signaling, immune activation, and dysregulated inflammation.
- **Biomedical impact:** These insights contributed to a clearer conceptual basis for understanding septic shock and other inflammatory conditions, and they supported subsequent translational research efforts.

## Awards and Recognition

- **Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine (2011):** Shared for discoveries concerning activation of innate immunity.
