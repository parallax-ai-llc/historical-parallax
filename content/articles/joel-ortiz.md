## What SIM Swapping Is

SIM swapping is a form of account-takeover fraud in which an attacker convinces or tricks a mobile carrier into transferring a victim's phone number to a SIM card controlled by the attacker. Once the attacker controls the number, they can intercept SMS-based password resets and two-factor authentication codes.

## The Ortiz Case in Brief

Ortiz's prosecution has been widely noted because it was among the first major criminal cases in the United States to focus on SIM swapping as the core mechanism of theft, particularly as it related to cryptocurrency accounts and exchanges.

## Investigation and Prosecution

Cases of this type typically involve digital evidence (logins, IP history, exchange records), carrier account activity, and victim reports. Law enforcement may also rely on cooperation from telecommunications companies and cryptocurrency services to tie number transfers to subsequent account access.

## Sentencing and Deterrence

Ortiz received a lengthy prison sentence in California, reflecting both the financial losses and the view that the conduct involved planning and repeated victimization. The case is often referenced in security guidance urging stronger authentication methods than SMS.

## Broader Impact

SIM swapping has influenced the adoption of authenticator apps, hardware security keys, and carrier-level protections (such as account PINs and port-out locks). It has also prompted debate about the responsibilities of carriers, platforms, and users in preventing number-based identity attacks.
