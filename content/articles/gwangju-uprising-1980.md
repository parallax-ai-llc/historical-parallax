## Overview
From May 18 to May 27, 1980, citizens of Gwangju took up arms after local Chonnam University students demonstrating against the martial law government were fired upon, killed, and beaten by government troops.

## Legacy
The event is now recognized as a cornerstone of South Korean democracy. May 18 is a national day of commemoration, and the uprising is often compared to the Tiananmen Square protests for its significance in Asian democratic history.
