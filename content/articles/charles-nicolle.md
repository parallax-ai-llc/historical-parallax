## Summary

Charles Nicolle discovered that typhus is transmitted by body lice, a finding that saved countless lives during World War I and subsequent epidemics. Working at the Pasteur Institute in Tunis, he observed that patients ceased being infectious after being bathed and given clean clothes. He received the 1928 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine.
