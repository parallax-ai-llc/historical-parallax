## Summary

Konrad Zuse was a German civil engineer, inventor, and computer pioneer who built the world's first programmable computer, the Z3, in 1941. He also designed the first high-level programming language, Plankalkül, in the 1940s. Working in isolation in Nazi Germany, he independently developed many concepts of modern computing. Zuse is considered one of the most important pioneers of computer science.
