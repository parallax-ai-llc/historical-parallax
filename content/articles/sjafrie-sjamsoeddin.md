## Background

- Wikipedia describes Sjafrie Sjamsoeddin as an Indonesian army officer and politician.
- He is reported to have been born in Makassar and to have graduated from the Indonesian Military Academy in 1974.
- His career is commonly associated with elite Indonesian Army units (including Kopassus) and senior command roles.

## Allegations and Public Scrutiny

- He has been accused in various reports of involvement in human-rights abuses during Indonesia’s late-New Order and post-New Order transition period.
- Wikipedia notes allegations connected to:
  - the 1997–1998 kidnappings/disappearances of activists,
  - the May 1998 Jakarta riots,
  - and events in East Timor (including reporting that he was present around the time of the 1991 Santa Cruz massacre).
- Wikipedia also states that Indonesian authorities cleared him regarding the 1998 riots and that he was not formally charged over East Timor or the kidnappings, though he was dismissed from the military connected to the kidnapping issue.
- In 2009, Wikipedia reports he was denied a U.S. visa while serving as an adviser to Indonesia’s president at the time.
