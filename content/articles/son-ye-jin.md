## Summary

Son Ye-jin is a South Korean actress widely regarded as one of the most accomplished performers of her generation. Born on January 11, 1982, in Daegu, South Korea, she built a career spanning over two decades across film and television, earning a reputation for emotional depth and versatility. Her ability to move between melodrama, romantic comedy, and thriller has made her one of the most bankable stars in Korean entertainment.

She rose to international recognition through the 2020 Netflix drama *Crash Landing on You*, which became a global phenomenon and introduced her to audiences far beyond South Korea. Her on-screen chemistry with co-star Hyun Bin translated into a real-life relationship, culminating in their marriage in 2022, which drew enormous media attention worldwide.

## Early Life & Background

Son Ye-jin was born in Daegu and raised in Seoul. She attended Konkuk University, where she studied film arts. She made her acting debut in 2000 with the television series *Secret Departure* and quickly transitioned to film, finding her footing in the early 2000s Korean cinema wave.

## Rise to Prominence

Her breakout came with the 2003 melodrama film *A Moment to Remember*, in which she played a woman diagnosed with early-onset Alzheimer's disease. The film was a commercial and critical success, establishing her as a leading dramatic actress. She followed this with *April Snow* (2005) alongside Bae Yong-joon, further cementing her status in melodrama.

She expanded her range with the action-thriller *The Berlin File* (2013) and the romantic comedy *Be With You* (2018), demonstrating consistent box-office pull across genres. By the late 2010s, she had starred in over 20 films and numerous television dramas.

## Key Achievements

- *Crash Landing on You* (2020) peaked at No. 1 on Netflix in multiple Asian countries and remains one of the highest-rated Korean dramas in cable television history, recording a peak rating of 21.7% in South Korea.
- Won the Grand Prize (Daesang) for Television at the 2020 Baeksang Arts Awards, one of South Korea's most prestigious honors.
- Named a UNESCO Honorary Ambassador for the Advancement of Women in 2019.
- Her film *The Classic* (2003) and *A Moment to Remember* are frequently cited in lists of essential Korean romantic films.
- Forbes Korea has repeatedly listed her among the most influential celebrities in the country.

## Controversies & Criticism

Son Ye-jin's career has been largely free of major controversy. In 2022, some media outlets reported on a property purchase in Gangnam prior to a government announcement of development plans in the area, raising questions about insider knowledge. Both she and her representatives denied any wrongdoing, and no formal legal action was taken. The episode received tabloid coverage but did not materially affect her public standing.

## Legacy & Influence

Son Ye-jin's career helped shape the global appetite for Korean romantic drama. Her performances in the early 2000s contributed to the first wave of the Korean Wave (*Hallyu*) reaching Southeast Asia and beyond. *Crash Landing on You* represented a second-generation Hallyu moment, arriving on a streaming platform and reaching audiences in over 190 countries. She is frequently cited by younger Korean actresses as a reference point for sustaining a long-form dramatic career with consistent critical credibility.
