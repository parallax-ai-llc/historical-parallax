## Summary

Brutal exploitation of the Congo Free State under King Leopold II from 1885-1908. Forced labor, mutilation, and murder killed an estimated 10 million Congolese. Workers had hands cut off for failing rubber quotas.

## Congo Free State and Forced Labor

The Congo Free State (1885–1908) was effectively controlled by King Leopold II as a personal domain. Economic extraction—especially rubber and ivory—was pursued through coercive systems that relied on forced labor, hostage-taking, and violent punishment.

## Mechanisms of Abuse

Historical accounts frequently describe:

- **Quota enforcement** for rubber collection under threat of violence.
- **Mutilation and terror** used to control communities.
- **Widespread deaths** from murder, starvation, disease, and the disruption of local societies.

## International Exposure and Reform Pressure

Reports by missionaries, journalists, and investigators helped bring international attention to abuses. The resulting outcry contributed to political pressure that ended Leopold’s personal control, leading to Belgium taking over administration in 1908.

## Legacy and Historical Debate

The atrocities remain a major reference point in discussions of colonial violence, the ethics of resource extraction, and the long-term social and economic consequences of colonial rule in Central Africa.
