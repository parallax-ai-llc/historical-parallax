## Expanded Notes

### Identity and background

- Public reporting identifies him as a British volunteer fighter known by the nom de guerre **"Macer Gifford"**.[^1]
- Some sources describe his birth year as **1987** and report his given name as **Harry Rowe**; the exact biographical details may vary by source.[^1]
- He has been described as being from **Cambridge, England**.[^2]
- Reporting has described him as a **former currency trader** prior to travelling to Syria.[^1]

### Syria / anti-ISIS involvement (high-level)

- He travelled to **Syria in 2015** to join Kurdish-led forces (often reported as the **YPG** / **SDF**) against the Islamic State (ISIS/ISIL).[^1]
- He has been associated in reporting with the broader anti-ISIS campaign in northern Syria, including operations linked to the **Manbij offensive (2016)**.[^1]
- Multiple reports describe his service as spanning **2015–2017** (with more than one tour), framed as volunteer participation rather than formal UK military service.[^1]

### Public visibility and speech controversies

- After returning to the UK, he received media attention and public invitations to speak about his experiences.
- Reporting notes an episode where a university union initially barred a talk over concerns it could encourage others to travel to fight, and later reversed the decision following public pressure and police advice.[^1]

### Ukraine / later involvement (high-level)

- Wikipedia and associated reporting describe him as later involved in **Ukraine** from **2022 onward**, including activity described as volunteer medical support and/or recruiting for foreign volunteers.[^1]
