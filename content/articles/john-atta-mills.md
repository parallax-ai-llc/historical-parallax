Ghanaian president who died in office under mysterious circumstances. His 'Better Ghana' agenda was undermined by corruption within his own party.

## Early Career

John Evans Fiifi Atta Mills was trained as a lawyer and academic and became known in Ghanaian public life before ascending to national executive office. He served as vice president prior to winning the presidency.

## Presidency (2009–2012)

Mills took office in 2009 following a competitive election and led the National Democratic Congress (NDC) government. His period in office overlapped with major economic and policy issues, including management of public finances and the early years of Ghana's oil era.

## Governance and Political Debate

As with other Ghanaian administrations, Mills's presidency faced intense domestic scrutiny, including disputes over appointments, investigations, and allegations of wrongdoing by political actors. Commentary about the "Better Ghana" agenda often focuses on the gap between campaign messaging and the practical constraints of governing.

## Death in Office and Succession

Mills died in office in July 2012. Under Ghana's constitutional procedures, Vice President John Dramani Mahama succeeded him, and the transition became a notable test of institutional continuity.

## Legacy

Mills is frequently discussed as part of Ghana's broader record of democratic consolidation, including peaceful transfers of power and adherence to constitutional succession. Assessments of his leadership vary across partisan lines and emphasize both policy choices and the political environment he navigated.
