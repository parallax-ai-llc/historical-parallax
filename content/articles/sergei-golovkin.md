## Summary

Russian serial killer known as 'Fisher' who abducted, tortured, and murdered 11 boys near Moscow between 1986 and 1992. He worked at a horse-breeding farm and lured victims there. Last person executed in Russia.

## Background (High-Level)

- Golovkin’s crimes are typically placed in the late Soviet / early post-Soviet transition period, a time when public institutions and policing capacity were under strain.
- Major summaries describe him as employed at a horse-breeding farm, using access to secluded property as part of his offending pattern.

## Offending Pattern and Investigation

- Public accounts emphasize the targeting of boys and the use of abduction and confinement.
- The fact pattern commonly described includes luring victims to a rural or semi-rural location, which complicated discovery and delayed intervention.
- The investigation and subsequent confession-based narrative are often cited in Russian true-crime coverage as a landmark case.

## Trial, Sentencing, and Historical Significance

- He was sentenced to death and executed by shooting.
- He is often described as the last person executed in Russia, making the case notable not only for the crimes but also for its placement in Russian penal history.
