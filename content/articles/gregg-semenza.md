## Summary

Gregg Semenza is an American physician-scientist who shared the 2019 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for discoveries of how cells sense and adapt to oxygen availability. He discovered hypoxia-inducible factor (HIF-1), the master switch that controls cellular response to low oxygen. His work explains how organisms adapt to changes in oxygen levels.
