## Summary

Four-time Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh who spent billions of public money building statues of herself and memorials to her party symbol. Accused of massive disproportionate assets.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1984 | Joined the Bahujan Samaj Party (BSP) after being mentored by Kanshi Ram |
| 1989 | Entered Parliament (served in both Lok Sabha and Rajya Sabha at different times) |
| 1995 | First term as Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh (coalition politics; short tenure) |
| 1997 | Returned as Chief Minister of Uttar Pradesh (another short tenure) |
| 2002–2003 | Served again as Chief Minister (coalition government) |
| 2007–2012 | Fourth term as Chief Minister after BSP won a majority in the UP assembly |
| 2007–2010 | Major memorial/park construction period in Lucknow and Noida associated with BSP symbolism and Dalit iconography |

## Background

- Born in Delhi and identified with the Dalit (Scheduled Caste) community.
- Trained and worked as a teacher before entering full-time politics.
- Became a prominent protégé of Kanshi Ram, the BSP founder, and later one of the party’s central leaders.

## Political Profile

- Known for building a voter coalition around Dalit politics and broader social-group alliances in Uttar Pradesh.
- Served multiple times as Chief Minister of India’s most populous state, making her one of the most influential regional leaders of the 1990s–2010s.
- The BSP’s election symbol is an elephant; party branding and iconography were commonly reflected in public-facing installations during her tenure.

## Memorials, Statues, and Public Spending Criticism

- During her administrations, large-scale parks, memorials, and statues were commissioned in Uttar Pradesh, including installations featuring Dalit leaders (e.g., B. R. Ambedkar) as well as BSP-associated symbolism.
- Critics argued that some projects resembled a personality cult and represented wasteful use of public funds; supporters framed them as symbolic recognition of historically marginalized communities.

## Allegations and Investigations (High-Level)

- Mayawati has faced recurring allegations of corruption and disproportionate assets in Indian political discourse and media coverage.
- She has publicly denied wrongdoing; outcomes have varied across different inquiries and legal proceedings over time.

## Notes on Impact

- Widely regarded as a major figure in modern Dalit electoral politics, particularly in North India.
- Reputation remains polarized: credited by supporters with representation and visibility, while criticized by opponents for patronage politics and monument-focused governance.
