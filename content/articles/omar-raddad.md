## Summary

Moroccan gardener convicted of murdering his employer in France in a case many believe was a miscarriage of justice.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1994 | Convicted in controversial murder case |

## Case Overview

- **Victim**: Ghislaine Marchal, a wealthy widow in Mougins (Alpes-Maritimes), France.
- **Defendant**: Omar Raddad, a Moroccan-born gardener employed by Marchal.
- **Charge**: Murder.
- The case became widely known in France due to disputed evidence and a broader public debate about investigative errors and judicial reliability.

## Why the Case Was Controversial

- The prosecution’s narrative relied heavily on a message written in blood at the crime scene that was interpreted as implicating Raddad.
- Critics argued the phrase’s grammar and spelling were unusual, and that alternative explanations (including the possibility of a staged message) were not fully resolved.
- The investigation and trial have been discussed as an example of how circumstantial interpretation can dominate when direct evidence is contested.

## Legal Proceedings and Key Milestones

- **1991**: Ghislaine Marchal is killed; investigators focus on her employee, Omar Raddad.
- **1994**: Raddad is convicted in a high-profile trial.
- **1996**: He receives a **presidential pardon** (which typically reduces or ends imprisonment but does not automatically erase the conviction).
- Subsequent years include repeated legal efforts and public campaigns seeking a retrial or full exoneration.
