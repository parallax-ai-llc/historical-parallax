## Summary

Chairman of Korean Air and Hanjin Group convicted of embezzlement. His entire family became known for abusive behavior toward service workers, including the 'nut rage' incident.

## Additional Context

Cho Yang-ho led the Hanjin Group conglomerate, best known internationally for Korean Air.

### Corporate governance and legal issues

- South Korean prosecutors pursued multiple cases involving alleged embezzlement and breach of trust tied to group affiliates.
- His tenure became a frequent example in domestic debate about chaebol governance, family control, and accountability.

### Family-related public scandals

The "nut rage" incident involving his daughter (a Korean Air executive) became a widely covered symbol of elite misconduct toward employees.

- The episode triggered public backlash and investigations into workplace culture and management practices.
- It also widened scrutiny of the family’s role in the airline and the group’s succession plans.

### Legacy

In coverage after his death, commentary often focused on a split legacy:

- Business expansion and global visibility for Korean Air.
- Reputational damage from repeated allegations, trials, and high-profile scandals.
