## Incident Overview

EgyptAir Flight 990 was a scheduled passenger flight that crashed into the Atlantic Ocean after departing the New York area en route to Cairo. The loss of the aircraft resulted in the deaths of all passengers and crew on board.

## Investigation (High Level)

Major aviation accidents are typically reconstructed using multiple evidence streams, including:

- Flight Data Recorder (FDR) parameters such as altitude, airspeed, and control inputs.
- Cockpit Voice Recorder (CVR) audio capturing crew communications and cockpit sounds.
- Wreckage recovery and analysis, including component failures and impact patterns.
- Air traffic control recordings and radar tracks.

## Divergent Conclusions

Open sources describe a significant dispute between investigative authorities over the most plausible cause. The U.S. National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) publicly concluded the aircraft was intentionally downed by a flight crew member, while Egyptian authorities contested that interpretation and emphasized alternate explanations.
