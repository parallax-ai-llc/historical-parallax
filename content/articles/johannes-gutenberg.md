## Summary

Johannes Gutenberg was a German inventor who introduced movable type printing to Europe, revolutionizing the production of books and enabling the spread of knowledge. His printing press, developed around 1440, is considered one of the most transformative inventions in human history. The Gutenberg Bible, printed around 1455, was the first major book produced using movable type in Europe. His invention catalyzed the Renaissance, Reformation, and Scientific Revolution.
