## Summary

Italian master thief who led the team that pulled off the $100 million Antwerp Diamond Heist in 2003. Rented office space in the diamond centre for years to case the vault.

## The Antwerp Diamond Heist (High Level)

- The 2003 Antwerp operation is often described as one of the largest diamond thefts ever reported.
- Notarbartolo is commonly identified as the leader and organizer, coordinating a small team and maintaining a low profile.
- Accounts emphasize long-term preparation, including establishing a credible presence in the Antwerp diamond district.

## Target and Security Context

- The targeted vault in Antwerp has been described as protected by multiple layers of security (physical barriers and detection systems).
- The case is frequently cited in security discussions because it highlights how procedural gaps and human factors can matter as much as technology.

## Planning and Execution Themes

- **Patience and cover**: maintaining a believable reason to be in the building over an extended period.
- **Reconnaissance**: understanding access patterns, schedules, and points of failure.
- **Team coordination**: dividing roles so the group could move quickly during the narrow operational window.

## Investigation and Aftermath

- Arrests and convictions followed, but reporting often notes that a significant portion of the stolen goods were never recovered.
- The heist became widely covered in books and documentaries as a modern example of high-end property crime.

## Key Takeaways

- The heist is remembered less for violence and more for meticulous planning and operational discipline.
- It remains a reference point for physical-security professionals evaluating layered defenses.
- Notarbartolo’s public profile is largely defined by association with the “heist of the century” narrative.
