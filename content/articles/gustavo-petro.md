## Summary

Gustavo Francisco Petro Urrego (born April 19, 1960) became the first left-wing President of Colombia on August 7, 2022, ending centuries of conservative-liberal alternation in one of Latin America's most stable democracies. A former member of the M-19 guerrilla group who later served as senator, congressman, and Mayor of Bogotá, Petro won the 2022 election in alliance with Afro-Colombian environmental activist Francia Márquez, Colombia's first Afro-descendant Vice President.

## Early Life and Education

Born April 19, 1960, in Ciénaga de Oro, Córdoba, Colombia:

**Background**:
- Father Gustavo Petro Sierra, agronomist
- Mother Clara Nubia Urrego, teacher
- Family moved to Zipaquirá near Bogotá when he was a child
- Middle-class family of modest means

**Education**:
- Externado University, Bogotá — Economics
- Catholic University of Louvain, Belgium — postgraduate studies in economics
- Universidad Externado — specialization in public administration
- Salamanca University, Spain — PhD in Economic and Political Sciences

## M-19 Guerrilla Years

### Joining M-19

**Movimiento 19 de Abril**:
- Joined M-19 urban guerrilla group as a teenager
- Group emerged from disputed 1970 election
- Pursued more political/symbolic actions than mass violence
- Famously stole Bolívar's sword (1974)

**Activities**:
- Used pseudonym "Aureliano"
- Active in Zipaquirá
- Focused on political organizing and publications
- Not involved in major violent operations

### Imprisonment

**Arrested** (1985):
- Caught with weapons in Zipaquirá
- Tortured during initial detention
- Imprisoned for 18 months
- Released after M-19 peace process began

### Demobilization (1990)

**M-19 Disarmament**:
- Group laid down arms in 1990 peace deal
- Joined legal politics as M-19 Democratic Alliance
- Petro entered electoral politics

## Political Career

### Constituent Assembly

**1991 Constitution**:
- M-19 won seats in 1991 Constituent Assembly
- New constitution recognized social rights, ethnic minorities
- Major democratic reform Petro participated in

### Diplomatic Exile

**Brussels** (1994-1996):
- Threatened by paramilitaries after exposing corruption
- Posted as diplomatic attaché
- Studied while abroad
- Returned to lead political career

### Congress

**Chamber of Representatives** (1991-1994, 1998-2006):
- Represented Cundinamarca
- Built reputation as fierce opposition figure
- Investigated corruption, paramilitary ties

**Senate** (2006-2010):
- "Parapolítica" investigation
- Exposed ties between politicians and paramilitaries
- Forced multiple senators to face criminal charges
- Confronted President Uribe in televised debate

### Presidential Runs

**2010 Election**:
- First presidential bid for Polo Democrático
- Finished fourth (9.1%)
- Built national profile

### Mayor of Bogotá (2012-2015)

**Colombia Humana**:
- Won 2011 mayoral election
- Public housing for displaced people
- Free healthcare for poor districts
- Trash collection nationalization (controversial)
- Removed by Inspector General Alejandro Ordóñez (later overturned)
- Restored to office after Inter-American Court ruling
- Final term marked by conflicts with city council

## 2018 and 2022 Presidential Elections

### 2018 Runner-up

**First Major Bid**:
- Lost runoff to Iván Duque
- Nearly 8 million votes (42%)
- Best left-wing performance in Colombian history at the time
- Established as leading opposition figure

### 2022 Victory

**Pacto Histórico Coalition**:
- Broad left coalition
- Vice presidential pick: Francia Márquez (Afro-Colombian environmentalist)
- Promised "human" politics, peace, environmental change

**Election**:
- Won runoff against Rodolfo Hernández with 50.4%
- First left-wing president in Colombian history
- Inaugurated August 7, 2022

## Presidency

### "Total Peace" Policy

**Comprehensive Negotiations**:
- Talks with ELN guerrillas
- Engagement with FARC dissidents (Estado Mayor Central)
- Negotiations with Gulf Clan paramilitary
- Mixed results, some breakdowns
- Murder of community leaders continued

### Economic and Social Reforms

**Tax Reform** (2022):
- Increased taxes on high earners and oil/coal
- Generated funds for social programs
- Boosted minimum wage substantially

**Health Reform**:
- Major restructuring proposed
- Targeted private health insurers (EPS)
- Faced congressional resistance
- Led to multiple cabinet reshuffles

**Pension Reform** (2024):
- Restructured pension system
- Greater public component
- Constitutional Court partial review

### Environmental Agenda

**Anti-Extractivism**:
- Halted new oil and gas exploration contracts
- Pushed transition to renewable energy
- "Petro for the planet" rhetoric at UN
- Conflict with traditional oil industry

**Amazon Protection**:
- Strengthened deforestation enforcement
- Initiated "Coalition for a Just Energy Transition"

### Foreign Policy

**Regional Approach**:
- Restored relations with Venezuela
- Critical of US drug policy
- Strong support for Palestinians during Gaza war
- Suspended diplomatic ties with Israel (2024)
- Friction with Trump administration on migration

## Controversies

### Cabinet Instability

**Frequent Reshuffles**:
- Multiple ministers dismissed or resigned
- Coalition partners broke with government
- Difficulty advancing legislative agenda

### Campaign Finance

**Allegations**:
- Son Nicolás Petro investigated for accepting illicit campaign funds
- Referred to drug traffickers
- Petro denounced "judicial coup"
- Investigation ongoing

### Personal Style

**Communication**:
- Heavy Twitter/X use, often controversial
- Conflicts with attorney general, judiciary
- Accusations of authoritarian tendencies
- Late-night ramblings
- Health concerns and missed appointments

### Implementation Challenges

**Reform Limits**:
- Failed to pass health reform in original form
- Difficulty managing Congress
- Frustrations among base
- High homicide rates persisted in many regions
