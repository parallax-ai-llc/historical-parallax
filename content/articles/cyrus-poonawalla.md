## Summary

Cyrus S. Poonawalla is an Indian billionaire businessman and the founder and chairman of the Serum Institute of India, the world's largest vaccine manufacturer by number of doses produced. Under his leadership, the Serum Institute has produced billions of vaccine doses against diseases such as measles, polio, influenza, and COVID-19, supplying affordable vaccines to over 170 countries. His company played a critical role during the COVID-19 pandemic by manufacturing the Covishield vaccine (the Oxford-AstraZeneca vaccine) at unprecedented scale. Poonawalla is also a noted philanthropist and horse-racing enthusiast who has significantly contributed to public health worldwide.

## Historical Significance

Cyrus Poonawalla transformed a small biotech venture into a global public health powerhouse. The Serum Institute of India under his stewardship became the backbone of immunization programs in developing nations, providing vaccines at a fraction of the cost charged by Western manufacturers. His commitment to affordable vaccine production helped eradicate polio in several countries and dramatically reduced infant mortality from preventable diseases across Africa, Asia, and Latin America. During the COVID-19 pandemic, the Serum Institute's ability to produce vaccines at scale was instrumental in the global vaccination effort, particularly for low- and middle-income countries. Poonawalla's legacy lies in demonstrating that large-scale pharmaceutical manufacturing can be both commercially viable and a force for global health equity.
