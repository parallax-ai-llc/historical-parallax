## Summary

Scott Dixon is a New Zealand professional racing driver widely regarded as one of the greatest IndyCar drivers of all time. Competing primarily in the NTT IndyCar Series since 2001, he has accumulated more championships and victories than any active driver in the series. His combination of technical precision, consistency, and longevity has made him the benchmark against which modern IndyCar talent is measured.

Born in Brisbane, Australia, but raised in Auckland, New Zealand, Dixon moved through the ranks of open-wheel racing with remarkable speed before establishing himself as the dominant force in American open-wheel racing across two decades. His career with Chip Ganassi Racing, which began in 2002, became one of the most successful driver-team partnerships in motorsport history.

---

## Early Life & Background

Scott Dixon was born on July 22, 1980, in Brisbane, Queensland, Australia, and grew up in Auckland, New Zealand. He was introduced to motorsport at a young age through karting and quickly demonstrated exceptional talent. By his early teens he was competing in Formula Ford in New Zealand, where he won multiple championships. At 16, he traveled to the United States to race in Formula Atlantic, signaling an early ambition to compete at the highest levels of North American open-wheel racing.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Dixon made his Champ Car debut in 2000 and joined the Indy Racing League full-time in 2001 with PacWest Racing. He secured his first IndyCar victory at Nazareth Speedway that year, announcing his arrival emphatically. In 2002 he moved to Chip Ganassi Racing, a partnership that has defined his career. His first IndyCar championship followed in 2003, the same year he won the Indianapolis 500 for the first time. That 2008 season, widely considered his finest, saw him claim a second championship with dominant consistency across the full calendar.

---

## Key Achievements

Dixon's list of accomplishments is unmatched in modern IndyCar racing. He has won six IndyCar Series championships (2003, 2008, 2013, 2015, 2018, 2020), tying A.J. Foyt's all-time record of six titles. He has won the Indianapolis 500 twice, in 2008 and 2022. His career victory total surpassed A.J. Foyt's record of 67 wins in 2022, making him the all-time wins leader in IndyCar/CART/Champ Car combined history. He has competed in over 350 IndyCar races, a record for the modern era. In 2018 he was named the Asahi Shimbun Athlete of the Year in New Zealand, reflecting his standing in his home country.

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Dixon's career has been largely free of major controversy. Some observers have pointed to the resources afforded by Chip Ganassi Racing as a factor in his success, arguing that his dominance reflects team infrastructure as much as individual brilliance. He was involved in a dramatic crash at the 2017 Indianapolis 500 where his car vaulted over Jay Howard's stopped vehicle, though he walked away uninjured. The incident raised ongoing discussions about oval safety rather than generating personal criticism.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Dixon's sustained excellence over more than two decades has reshaped expectations for career longevity in IndyCar. He has mentored younger teammates including Tony Kanaan and Marcus Ericsson and is widely respected within the paddock for his professionalism. His success has helped raise the profile of New Zealand motorsport internationally. As the all-time wins and championships leader, his legacy is assured as the defining IndyCar driver of the 21st century.
