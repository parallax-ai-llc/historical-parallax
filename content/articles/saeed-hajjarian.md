## Summary

Iranian reformist shot in the head in an assassination attempt by hardliners.

## Background

- Saeed Hajjarian (سعید حجاریان) is associated with Iran's reform movement as a political strategist and journalist.
- He served in multiple state institutions earlier in his career, including intelligence-related roles, before becoming publicly identified with reformist politics.
- He was an advisor to Iranian President Mohammad Khatami (served as president 1997–2005).

## Political and Public Roles

- Member of the City Council of Tehran (elected in the 1999 local elections).
- Vice Chairman of the City Council of Tehran during his council tenure.
- Often described in reporting and scholarship as an important reformist strategist and commentator in late-1990s/early-2000s Iranian politics.

## Assassination Attempt (2000)

- Date: 12 March 2000.
- He was shot in the face/head at close range and survived.
- The attack left him severely disabled.
- The incident occurred in a period of intense factional conflict between reformist and conservative/hardline forces in Iran.

## Significance

- The attack became emblematic of the risks faced by high-profile reformist figures during that era.
- Contemporary accounts frequently connect the attempted assassination to broader struggles over political liberalization and accountability.
