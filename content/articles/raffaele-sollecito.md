## Summary

Italian who was initially convicted then acquitted of the 2007 murder of British student Meredith Kercher in Perugia along with Amanda Knox. The case became a major international cause célèbre.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

| 2009 | Convicted; sentenced to 25 years |

## Additional Context

- The Meredith Kercher case became internationally prominent due to its cross-border dimensions, extensive media coverage, and repeated changes in verdicts.
- Sollecito was one of multiple defendants associated with the investigation; public attention also focused on prosecutorial theory and forensic interpretation.

## Legal Process (High-Level)

- In Italy, criminal cases can proceed through multiple stages, including trials, appeals, and review by the Court of Cassation.
- The final 2015 acquittal is commonly treated as the definitive outcome for Sollecito in relation to the murder charge.

## Key Issues Often Discussed

- Forensics: how DNA evidence is collected, tested, and presented, and what standards of reliability are applied.
- Media influence: the role of tabloid narratives and international press in shaping public opinion.
- Procedural complexity: how differing rulings across levels of court can confuse non-specialists.

## Broader Significance

- Frequently cited in discussions of wrongful conviction risk, evidentiary standards, and the interaction between courts and public narratives.
- Remains a case study in how high-profile criminal investigations can become long-running legal and cultural controversies.
