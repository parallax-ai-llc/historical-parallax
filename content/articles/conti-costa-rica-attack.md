## Summary

Conti ransomware group attacked Costa Rica's government systems forcing the country to declare a national emergency. Multiple government agencies were crippled for months.

## Background

Conti was a prominent ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) operation that rose to global attention in 2020–2022, combining malware development, affiliate recruitment, and extortion operations. The group was associated in reporting with the broader Russian-speaking cybercrime ecosystem.

## What Happened

Public reporting describes the Costa Rica incident as a ransomware intrusion affecting multiple government entities. The attackers attempted to disrupt operations, pressure officials, and extract payment by encrypting systems and threatening data exposure.

## Impact

The attack disrupted public services and administrative workflows across agencies, including delays in tax and customs processing and knock-on effects for businesses that depend on government digital systems. The incident became notable as one of the first cases where a national government declared an emergency primarily due to a ransomware campaign.

## Government Response

Costa Rican authorities publicly acknowledged the incident, coordinated incident response across agencies, and worked with domestic and international partners. Response efforts included system restoration, containment measures, and public communications while services were gradually brought back online.

## Aftermath and Significance

The case is often cited in discussions of ransomware risk to critical government services, including the need for resilient backups, incident-response planning, and cross-agency coordination. It also contributed to broader scrutiny of major ransomware groups during 2022 as Conti’s operations fractured following internal leaks and law-enforcement pressure.
