## Summary

Ch√∂gyam Trungpa was a Tibetan Buddhist meditation master who was one of the most influential and controversial figures in bringing Tibetan Buddhism to the West. He founded Naropa University in Boulder, Colorado, and the Shambhala meditation centers worldwide. His books, including Cutting Through Spiritual Materialism, introduced thousands of Westerners to Buddhist practice. His unconventional lifestyle, including alcohol use and sexual relationships with students, generated ongoing controversy.
