## Summary

Kenyan al-Qaeda operative killed in a US commando raid in Somalia.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2006 | Named by U.S. authorities in terrorism-related charges connected to the 2002 attacks |
| 2009 | Killed in a U.S. commando raid in Somalia |

## Background

- Nabhan was a Kenyan national described in open sources as an al-Qaeda operative active in East Africa.
- He was frequently discussed in reporting and government statements in relation to al-Qaeda’s regional networks and later militant activity in Somalia.

## Alleged Activities and Notability

High-level points commonly attributed to him in public reporting:

- Association with al-Qaeda-linked operations in East Africa.
- Alleged involvement in planning or supporting attacks in Kenya, especially those occurring in 2002 in Mombasa.
- Later presence in Somalia, where he was reportedly connected to militant groups operating in the region.

## Death

- Nabhan was killed on 14 September 2009 during a U.S. special operations raid in Somalia.
- The operation was widely reported as targeting a senior al-Qaeda figure and was notable for being conducted inside Somalia.

## Context

- The 2002 Mombasa attacks and subsequent investigations were a key episode in the history of transnational jihadist violence in East Africa.
- Nabhan is often referenced in discussions of the evolution of al-Qaeda-affiliated networks in Kenya and Somalia during the 2000s.
