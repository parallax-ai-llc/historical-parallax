## Background and Reputation

- Sreten Jocić is a Serbian figure frequently described in media and court reporting as a major organized-crime boss, nicknamed “Joca Amsterdam,” associated with transnational trafficking networks.
- Public accounts often place him within a broader post–Cold War Balkan organized-crime landscape that connected supply routes, logistics hubs in Western Europe, and networks in the former Yugoslavia.

## Allegations and Notable Cases (High Level)

- Jocić has been linked in reporting and legal proceedings to narcotics trafficking and violent organized-crime activity.
- One of the most prominent cases associated with him in Serbia concerns the killing of Croatian journalist Ivo Pukanić in 2008, which led to prosecutions of multiple defendants and extensive public attention.

## Legal Proceedings and Sentencing

- Serbian courts have tried Jocić in connection with organized-crime allegations; outcomes have been reported as including convictions and long prison sentences in at least one major case.
- Coverage of his legal history includes extradition, detention, and repeated court proceedings, reflecting the difficulty of prosecuting cross-border criminal enterprises.

## Broader Context

- The Jocić story is often discussed alongside regional efforts to dismantle organized-crime structures, strengthen witness protection, and improve cross-border policing and judicial cooperation.
- His profile illustrates how high-level criminal networks can combine legitimate business fronts, corruption risks, and violence to maintain influence.
