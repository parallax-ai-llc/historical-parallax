## Summary

Isaiah Shembe was a Zulu prophet who founded the Nazareth Baptist Church (ibandla lamaNazaretha), one of the largest African-initiated churches in southern Africa. He blended Zulu cultural traditions with Christian biblical elements, creating a distinctive religious movement. He established the holy village of Ekuphakameni and the annual Nhlangakazi mountain pilgrimage. His movement has millions of followers in South Africa today.
