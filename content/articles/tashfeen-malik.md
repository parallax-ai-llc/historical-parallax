## Summary

Pakistani citizen who, with husband Syed Rizwan Farook, carried out the San Bernardino shooting that killed 14 people. She had pledged allegiance to ISIS. Both were killed in shootout with police.

## Additional Context

### San Bernardino attack (high level)

The San Bernardino shooting became one of the most consequential U.S. mass-casualty attacks of the 2010s and was widely treated as both a terrorism investigation and a mass-violence case. Reporting emphasized the attackers’ planning, the attack site, and the subsequent law-enforcement response.

### Online propaganda and allegiance statements

Public discussion of Malik frequently notes that a statement pledging allegiance to ISIS was posted online around the time of the attack. In analysis of the incident, this element is often used to illustrate how contemporary extremist propaganda can intersect with “homegrown” violence, even when operational ties are unclear or limited.

### Aftermath and policy debate

The attack contributed to renewed debate about counterterrorism, firearms, and the screening and vetting of visa applicants. It also prompted extensive media scrutiny of the couple’s background, travel history, and social-media presence.
