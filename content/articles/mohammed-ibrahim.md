## Summary

Mohammed Ibrahim (known as Mo Ibrahim) founded Celtel International, which brought affordable mobile telecommunications to sub-Saharan Africa. He sold Celtel for $3.4 billion in 2005. He then founded the Mo Ibrahim Foundation, which publishes the Ibrahim Index of African Governance and awards the Ibrahim Prize — the world's largest individual prize — to African leaders who govern well and leave office democratically.
