## Summary

Angelina Jolie is an American actress, filmmaker, and humanitarian widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in contemporary Hollywood. Over a career spanning three decades, she has earned Academy Award, Screen Actors Guild, and Golden Globe recognition while commanding some of the highest salaries in the film industry. Beyond entertainment, she is equally known for her extensive work with the United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR), logging over 60 field missions to conflict zones and refugee camps across more than 30 countries.

Her cultural footprint extends well beyond the screen. Jolie has shaped public conversations around adoption, women's health advocacy, and international humanitarian law. Her 2013 op-ed in The New York Times disclosing her preventive double mastectomy following a BRCA1 genetic test sparked a measurable global surge in genetic cancer screening — a phenomenon researchers dubbed the "Angelina Effect."

## Early Life & Background

Born on June 4, 1975, in Los Angeles, California, Angelina Jolie Voight is the daughter of Oscar-winning actor Jon Voight and actress Marcheline Bertrand. Her parents separated when she was one year old, and she was largely raised by her mother. She attended the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute from age 11, developing an early passion for performance.

Her adolescence was marked by emotional instability and self-described struggles with identity. She attended Beverly Hills High School but found greater belonging in acting classes. By her late teens she had begun modeling in London and appeared in music videos, setting the stage for her film debut.

## Rise to Prominence

Jolie made her feature film debut in *Lookin' to Get Out* (1982) as a child, but her career effectively began with the cable film *Gia* (1998), for which she received a Golden Globe. Her breakthrough came with *Girl, Interrupted* (1999), earning her the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress at age 24. The role of Lara Croft in *Lara Croft: Tomb Raider* (2001) transformed her into a global action star and one of the most bankable names in Hollywood.

Her subsequent two decades included critically acclaimed roles in *Monster's Ball* (2001), *A Mighty Heart* (2007), and *Changeling* (2008), the latter earning her an Academy Award nomination for Best Actress. She expanded into directing with *In the Land of Blood and Honey* (2011), *Unbroken* (2014), and *First They Killed My Father* (2017).

## Key Achievements

- Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress, *Girl, Interrupted* (2000)
- Three Golden Globe Awards and two Screen Actors Guild Awards
- Named UNHCR Goodwill Ambassador in 2001; elevated to Special Envoy in 2012
- *Forbes* ranked her Hollywood's highest-paid actress multiple times between 2009 and 2013
- Received the Jean Hersholt Humanitarian Award from the Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences in 2013
- Co-founded the Preventing Sexual Violence in Conflict Initiative with UK Foreign Secretary William Hague in 2012

## Controversies & Criticism

Jolie has navigated significant public scrutiny throughout her career. Her early public persona — including wearing a vial of then-husband Billy Bob Thornton's blood and making comments about a close relationship with her brother — invited tabloid sensationalism. Her high-profile relationship with Brad Pitt, which began on the set of *Mr. & Mrs. Smith* (2005) while Pitt was married to Jennifer Aniston, generated years of media controversy. Their marriage in 2014 ended in a contentious separation filed in 2016, followed by prolonged legal disputes over custody of their six children that continued well into the 2020s.

Her directorial work received mixed critical reception, with *Unbroken* in particular criticized for emotional distance from its subject matter.

## Legacy & Influence

Angelina Jolie occupies a rare dual legacy as both a defining Hollywood star of the 2000s and a significant humanitarian actor. Her UNHCR work helped elevate refugee issues in mainstream Western media at a time when such visibility was limited. The "Angelina Effect" demonstrated that a single public health disclosure by a celebrity could materially shift population-level medical behavior. Her advocacy for survivors of wartime sexual violence contributed to an international policy agenda that led to the 2013 G8 Declaration on Preventing Sexual Violence in Conflict.

In film, her portrayals of complex, physically capable female protagonists helped reshape expectations for women in action cinema during a period when such roles were scarce.
