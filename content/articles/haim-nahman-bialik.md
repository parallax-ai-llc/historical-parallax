## Summary

Haim Nahman Bialik was a Jewish poet who is considered the national poet of Israel and one of the most important modern Hebrew poets. His poetry drew on traditional Jewish sources while expressing the anguish and aspirations of the Jewish people in the modern era. His poem 'In the City of Slaughter' (responding to the Kishinev pogrom) galvanized Jewish self-defense movements. He helped revive Hebrew as a living literary language.
