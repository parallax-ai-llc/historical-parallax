## Summary

Ethiopian PM and Nobel Peace Prize laureate whose government waged a devastating war in Tigray from 2020-2022. An estimated 600,000 people died from fighting, famine, and disease. He was accused of ethnic cleansing and using starvation as a weapon.

## Early Career

Abiy Ahmed served in Ethiopia’s security and intelligence institutions and later entered electoral politics. He became a prominent figure during a period of significant political protest and leadership transition within the ruling coalition.

## Rise to the Premiership

He became Prime Minister in 2018 and pursued a reform agenda that included changes to political restrictions, releases of some political prisoners, and efforts to restructure governing institutions. He also led a diplomatic opening with Eritrea, a process cited in the Nobel Committee’s decision to award him the 2019 Nobel Peace Prize.

## War in Tigray and Aftermath

From 2020, Ethiopia experienced a major armed conflict in the Tigray region involving federal forces, regional forces, and allied actors. Reporting on the war has included allegations of war crimes and serious human-rights abuses by multiple parties; casualty estimates vary widely across sources, and access constraints have complicated independent verification.

A cessation-of-hostilities agreement was signed in 2022, and subsequent efforts focused on disarmament, restoration of services, and humanitarian recovery, while political tensions and security challenges have continued in parts of the country.

## International Attention

Abiy’s tenure has been characterized by a sharp contrast between early international praise for reforms and later condemnation and concern over the humanitarian toll of internal conflicts.
