## Summary

Thierry Henry was Arsenal's all-time top scorer and led the 'Invincibles' unbeaten season. He won the 1998 World Cup.

## Playing style

Henry was known for pace, close control, and finishing from the left channel as a forward who frequently drifted wide before cutting inside. He combined goal scoring with chance creation, and became a focal point for Arsenal’s attack under Arsène Wenger.

## Club career highlights (high level)

- **Arsenal:** Multiple Premier League and FA Cup titles, including the unbeaten 2003–04 league season. He is widely recognized as one of the club’s defining modern-era players.
- **FC Barcelona:** Part of the squad that won major domestic and European honors in the late 2000s, including the UEFA Champions League.
- **Other clubs:** Began with Monaco and had spells at Juventus and later MLS with New York Red Bulls.

## International career

With France, Henry won the 1998 FIFA World Cup and UEFA Euro 2000. He also earned a large number of caps and goals, spending long stretches as a leading scorer for the national team.

## Post-playing work

After retiring as a player, Henry worked in coaching and media roles, including managerial and assistant-coach positions in professional football.

## Legacy

Henry is often cited among the top forwards of his era, particularly for his peak years in the Premier League and his influence on how modern wide-forward roles developed.
