## Summary

Camille Corot was a pivotal French landscape painter who bridged Neoclassicism and Impressionism. His plein-air oil sketches introduced a freshness that profoundly influenced the Impressionists. His generous nature and prolific output made him one of the most beloved figures in 19th-century French art.
