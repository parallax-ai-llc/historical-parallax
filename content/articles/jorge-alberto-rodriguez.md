## Summary

Colombian drug trafficker associated with the Norte del Valle Cartel who was one of the most wanted drug lords in the 2000s. He controlled cocaine production and distribution networks across Colombia and into the United States.

## Additional Context

- The Norte del Valle Cartel was one of the largest Colombian cocaine-trafficking organizations during the late 1990s and early 2000s, emerging after the fragmentation of earlier major cartels.
- U.S. and Colombian investigations into cartel-linked trafficking commonly relied on conspiracy and continuing-criminal-enterprise style charges, as well as extradition requests for defendants indicted in U.S. federal courts.
- Large-scale cocaine distribution networks typically involved layered logistics (production, transport corridors, storage, money laundering, and corruption) rather than a single centralized operation.

## Legal and Enforcement Notes

- High-profile cartel prosecutions in the 2000s frequently involved international cooperation, including intelligence sharing and coordinated arrests.
- Indictments in the United States often centered on trafficking quantities and organizational role, with cases supported by wiretaps, informants, and seizures.
