## Summary

Kenyan opposition leader who has run for president multiple times and contested results. The 2007 election dispute led to violence that killed over 1,100 people.

## Additional Context

- Odinga has been a central figure in Kenyan politics across multiple decades, often associated with opposition movements and pro-democracy advocacy.
- His political career is intertwined with the country's transitions in party systems, constitutional debate, and electoral reform.

## Elections and Disputes (High-Level)

- Kenya's presidential elections have periodically produced major disputes, with court challenges and negotiated political settlements playing important roles.
- The 2007–2008 crisis is frequently cited as a turning point that intensified attention to electoral administration, violence prevention, and accountability.

## Policy and Coalition Dynamics

- Kenyan politics is often coalition-driven; alliances and realignments are common as parties assemble nationwide support.
- Public discussion around Odinga often focuses on governance reform, devolution, and the balance between executive authority and institutions.

## Legacy and Significance

- Seen by supporters as a long-standing reform figure and by critics as a polarizing competitor in high-stakes elections.
- Remains an important reference point for understanding opposition politics and democratic consolidation in Kenya.
