## 개요

Jenna Ortega is an American actress born on September 27, 2002, in Coachella Valley, California. She rose to prominence as a child actress before achieving global recognition through her lead role as Wednesday Addams in Netflix's *Wednesday* (2022), which became one of the most-watched English-language series in the platform's history. Her ability to portray complex, deadpan characters with emotional depth has made her one of the most sought-after young actresses in Hollywood.

Ortega is of Mexican and Puerto Rican descent and has been vocal about Latina representation in mainstream media. Beyond acting, she has cultivated a significant presence in fashion and pop culture, becoming a face for major brands and a recurring fixture at global events such as the Met Gala. Her influence extends to a generation of young fans who identify with her outspoken, unconventional public persona.

## 초기 생애 및 배경

Jenna Marie Ortega was born on September 27, 2002, in Coachella Valley, California, the fourth of six children in a family of Mexican and Puerto Rican heritage. She expressed interest in acting from an early age, reportedly asking her parents to find her an agent around age six. Her family relocated to support her career pursuits, and she began auditioning professionally by the time she was nine years old.

Ortega completed much of her schooling around an active filming schedule, relying on on-set tutors during productions. She has spoken in interviews about the challenges of balancing education with a demanding career during her formative years.

## 부상

Ortega landed her first significant role as Young Jane on The CW's *Jane the Virgin*, appearing from 2014 onward. She gained wider recognition playing Harley Diaz in Disney Channel's *Stuck in the Middle* (2016–2018), for which she received an Imagen Award nomination. Film roles in *Iron Man 3* (2013) and *Insidious: Chapter 2* (2013) had given her early screen experience, but it was the horror genre that accelerated her profile — she starred in *Scream* (2022) and its 2023 sequel, as well as *X* (2022).

Her breakout moment came with Netflix's *Wednesday*, directed by Tim Burton, which premiered in November 2022. The series accumulated over 1.2 billion viewing hours within its first 28 days, setting a record for an English-language Netflix series at the time. Her viral "Rave'N" dance sequence became a global social media phenomenon.

## 논란 및 비판

Ortega drew attention in early 2023 when she disclosed in interviews that she had rewritten some of her own dialogue and choreography on *Wednesday* without prior showrunner approval, including the iconic dance sequence. The comments sparked debate about on-set creative boundaries, though Netflix and producers responded diplomatically. She has also faced tabloid scrutiny regarding her personal relationships, which she has consistently declined to address publicly.

## 유산 및 영향

Ortega has become a defining young actress of the 2020s, credited with revitalizing gothic and horror aesthetics for mainstream audiences. Her Latina heritage and willingness to discuss representation have positioned her as a cultural touchstone for young viewers of color. With *Wednesday* Season 2 in production and continued film commitments, her trajectory points toward enduring relevance in both prestige television and genre cinema.
