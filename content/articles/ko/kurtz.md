## Kurtz as a Literary Archetype

- "Kurtz" is best known as a fictional figure in Joseph Conrad’s *Heart of Darkness* (1899).
- The character is commonly read as an emblem of how unchecked power and distance from oversight enable brutality.
- Many interpretations treat Kurtz less as a single biographical person and more as a composite drawn from colonial reality.
- The name appears in later adaptations and cultural references as shorthand for "going native" and moral collapse.
