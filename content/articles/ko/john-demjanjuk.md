## 개요

Nazi camp guard convicted of being an accessory to the murder of 27,900 Jews at the Sobibor extermination camp. His case established the precedent of convicting guards without evidence of specific acts.

## Additional Context

## 배경

Demjanjuk was born in 우크라이나 and served in the Soviet Red Army during 제2차 세계대전 before being captured by German forces. In postwar decades he immigrated to the 미국, where questions later emerged about his wartime activities and immigration disclosures.

## Major Legal Proceedings

- **이스라엘 (1980s–1990s):** He was extradited and tried in 이스라엘 after being identified by some witnesses as "Ivan the Terrible" of Treblinka. He was initially convicted and sentenced to death, but 이스라엘’s 대법원 later acquitted him after new evidence raised substantial doubt about the Treblinka identification.
- **미국 (multiple proceedings):** U.S. authorities pursued denaturalization and deportation cases tied to allegations of wartime service as a guard at Nazi camps.
- **독일 (2009–2012):** German prosecutors argued that service at Sobibor as a guard made him an accessory to mass murder, even without proof of a specific killing act. He was convicted in 2011; he died while the appeal process was ongoing.

## Significance

The German case is widely discussed for its legal theory: treating camp guard service at an extermination camp as sufficient participation to establish complicity in mass murder. The case is often cited in discussions of late-stage Holocaust prosecutions and evidentiary standards.
