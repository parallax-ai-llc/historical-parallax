## 개요

Kobe Steel admitted to falsifying quality data on aluminum, copper, and steel products for decades. Products went into cars, planes, and trains worldwide, raising safety concerns.

## 배경

- **Company**: Kobe Steel (Kobe Steel, Ltd. / Kobelco) is a major 일본ese producer of steel and nonferrous metals.
- **Why the data mattered**: Customer specifications for strength, durability, and other properties are commonly verified through inspection certificates and test results.

## What Was Falsified (High-Level)

Reports described manipulation of:

- **Strength and durability test data** for aluminum and copper products
- **Inspection and quality certification records** provided to customers

## Products and Supply Chain Exposure

While exact downstream usage varies by customer, affected materials were reported as being supplied into multiple industries, including:

- **Automotive** components and materials
- **Aerospace** supply chains (materials used by contractors and manufacturers)
- **Rail and industrial equipment**

## Timeline (Additional Detail)

- The scandal became public in **2017**, when Kobe Steel acknowledged that some quality data had been falsified.
- The company indicated the practice had occurred over an extended period (often described as **years to decades** in coverage).

## Consequences and Response

- **Customer reviews and audits**: Major customers reviewed compliance with their specifications and sought clarification on safety margins.
- **Regulatory and legal scrutiny**: The incident triggered investigations and broader questions about quality governance.
- **Reputation and governance impact**: Corporate governance reforms and enhanced compliance programs are commonly reported outcomes in such cases.

## Why This Case Is Cited

고베제강 데이터 사기 is frequently referenced as an example of:

- How **documentation and certification systems** can fail when incentives and oversight are misaligned
- The need for **independent verification** and strong internal controls in safety- and reliability-critical supply chains
- The systemic risk that arises when a supplier serves **many global customers** across industries
