## 개요

Luxury 음악 festival promoted by influencers that collapsed spectacularly. Attendees arrived to find no artists, no luxury, and disaster relief tents. Became emblematic of social media fraud.

## 배경

- Marketed as a luxury destination festival in the Bahamas with an emphasis on premium accommodation and celebrity/influencer promotion.
- Promoted alongside (and partly to advertise) the Fyre mobile app, described as a tool for booking musical acts.

## What Went Wrong (High Level)

- Logistics and infrastructure were not in place for the number of attendees and the level of service advertised.
- 예술가 and vendor arrangements were incomplete, leading to cancellations and unmet obligations.
- The gap between marketing claims and deliverable operations became visible only ~ 후에 arrival, when basic needs (housing, food, transport) were not met.

## 여파

- The event triggered widespread media coverage and ~이 되었다 shorthand example of hype-driven fraud.
- Legal actions followed, including civil lawsuits and criminal proceedings connected to the festival’s promotion and financing.
- The story later reached a broader audience ~을 통해 major documentaries, reinforcing its cultural footprint.

## Notable Themes

- Influencer marketing as a force multiplier for credibility—effective for reach, dangerous when paired with weak 처형.
- “Luxury” as a promise: expectations rise faster than operational reality, leaving little room for partial success.
- Due diligence failures: customers often rely on brand signals and social proof rather than verifiable plans.
