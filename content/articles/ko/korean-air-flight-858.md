## 개요

북한n agents bombed a Korean Air passenger jet killing 115 people to disrupt the 1988 Olympics.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- **Flight**: Korean Air Lines Flight 858 (KAL 858), a Boeing 707 operating an international passenger route.
- **Attack**: A bomb was planted aboard the aircraft and detonated in flight.
- **Outcome**: The aircraft was destroyed and **115 people** were killed.
- **Motivation (as commonly reported)**: 대한민국n investigators concluded the attack aimed to create fear and disrupt or undermine 대한민국 ahead of the **1988 서울 Olympics**.[^1]

## Background and Context

- The late 1980s featured heightened inter-Korean political tension and extensive international attention on 대한민국 due to Olympic preparations.
- Civil aviation attacks were viewed as strategically significant because they targeted civilians and generated global media coverage.

## Investigation and Attribution (Summary)

- The investigation focused on identifying who placed the device, how it was smuggled onboard, and who ordered the operation.
- Widely cited accounts state that **북한n operatives** were identified as responsible, with 대한민국n authorities presenting the incident as a state-directed act of terrorism.[^1]

## Aftermath and Legacy

- The incident became one of the most well-known cases of aviation terrorism linked to the Korean peninsula.
- It is frequently discussed in the context of:
  - the security environment surrounding the 1988 Olympics,
  - changes in aviation security practices,
  - and inter-Korean relations during the period.
