## 맥락

The Savar building collapse (commonly associated with the Rana Plaza disaster) became one of the deadliest industrial accidents in the history of the global garment supply chain.[^1]

Bangladesh's ready-made garment sector employs large numbers of workers and produces clothing for international brands, making the disaster a major event in discussions about workplace safety and corporate responsibility.[^1]

## Building conditions and warnings

Reporting about the incident emphasizes that the building's structural integrity was compromised before the collapse.[^1]

- Cracks were observed in the structure prior to the disaster.
- The building's use and modifications were alleged to be inconsistent with safe construction practices, including additions that were not authorized.

These warning signs became central to later investigations and public scrutiny.[^1]

## Collapse and immediate response

The building collapsed on 24 April 2013, resulting in a very large number of deaths and injuries among garment workers and others present.[^1]

After the collapse:

- Rescue and recovery operations continued for an extended period.
- The event received intensive local and international media coverage.

## Investigations and legal proceedings

Authorities pursued investigations and criminal cases related to responsibility for the collapse.[^1]

High-level issues raised in the aftermath include:

- Accountability for building safety oversight and enforcement.
- Liability of building owners and factory operators.
- The role of supply-chain pressure and purchasing practices in shaping factory conditions.

## Industry reforms and broader impact

The disaster accelerated efforts to improve factory safety standards in Bangladesh, including increased inspections and new frameworks involving brands, factories, unions, and regulators.[^1]

It is frequently cited in debates about:

- Supply-chain transparency
- Labor rights and worker representation
- The responsibilities of multinational companies sourcing from low-cost manufacturing hubs
