## 요약

Japanese mass murderer who drove a truck into a crowd in Akihabara, Tokyo, then stabbed pedestrians, killing seven. He was executed by hanging.

## 배경

- Kato was born in Japan in 1982.
- Contemporary reporting around the case discussed his employment history and personal grievances leading up to the attack.

## Akihabara attack (2008)

- On 8 June 2008, Kato drove a rented truck into pedestrians in the Akihabara district of Tokyo.
- After the vehicle attack, he exited and used a knife to stab additional victims.
- The incident caused widespread shock in Japan and prompted renewed public debate about street crime and weapon access.

## 수사, trial, and sentence

- Kato was arrested at the scene and later indicted on multiple counts, including murder.
- A Japanese court sentenced him to death; appellate proceedings upheld the sentence.

## 처형 and legacy

- Kato was executed by hanging in 2022.
- The case is frequently referenced in discussions of mass violence in Japan, including the role of media coverage and the social impact on victims’ families.
