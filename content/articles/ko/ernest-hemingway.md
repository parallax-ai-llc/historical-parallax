## Paris Years (1920s)

**Expatriate Life:**
Moved to Paris as foreign correspondent. Part of "Lost Generation" (Gertrude Stein's term).

**Mentors/Friends:**
- Gertrude Stein (advice on writing)
- Ezra Pound (editing)
- F. Scott Fitzgerald (friend/rivalry)
- James Joyce, Pablo Picasso (acquaintances)

**Early Works:**
- *In Our Time* (1925): Short stories
- *The Sun Also Rises* (1926): Expatriates, bullfighting, lost generation

**"Iceberg Theory":**
Hemingway's style: omit everything possible. Surface action implies deeper emotion. Minimalist, declarative sentences.

## Decline and Death

**Health:**
- Multiple accidents (plane crashes, car accidents)
- Alcoholism
- Depression
- Liver disease
- Possible CTE from injuries

**Mental Health:**
Paranoia, depression worsened. Electroconvulsive therapy at Mayo Clinic (1960-1961)—damaged memory, couldn't write.

**Death (July 2, 1961):**
Suicide by shotgun at home in Ketchum, Idaho. Age 61.

**Family History:**
Father, brother, sister, granddaughter also died by suicide.
