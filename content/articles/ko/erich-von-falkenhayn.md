The strategic dispute with Hindenburg and Ludendorff over Eastern Front strategy continues to generate criticism. ~하는 동안 현대의 scholars note that Falkenhayn's caution had merit, critics argue that the opportunity to knock Russia out of 전쟁 in 1915 was real and that Falkenhayn's refusal to pursue Ludendorff's bold operational concepts cost Germany its best chance for victory.[^117] Had Russia been decisively defeated in 1915, Germany could have concentrated its full strength against France and Britain in 1916, potentially achieving the breakthrough that eluded it ~ 내내 전쟁.

Falkenhayn's relationship with his subordinates and peers has been criticized as politically inept. His dependence on the Kaiser's favor rather than building broad support within the officer corps left him vulnerable when his strategies failed.[^118] The bitter factional disputes between Falkenhayn and the Hindenburg-Ludendorff partnership damaged German strategic coherence and unity of command at a 비판적인 period of 전쟁.[^119]

His defeat in Palestine by 장군 Allenby in October 1917 demonstrates that Falkenhayn's later commands were not uniformly 성공적인, even if the Romanian campaign had been brilliant.[^120] Critics note that a truly first-rate commander should have been able to achieve better results even with the limited forces available in the Ottoman 극장.

Some historians have questioned whether Falkenhayn's strategic concept for Verdun was even coherent. The ambiguity about whether the goal was to capture Verdun or merely to inflict casualties by threatening it suggests unclear strategic thinking.[^121] When Crown 왕자 Wilhelm and his staff shifted to prioritizing territorial gains, Falkenhayn apparently failed to effectively reassert control over the operation's objectives, allowing it to devolve into exactly the kind of mutual bloodbath he had supposedly wanted to avoid.[^122]

The 현대의 criticism Falkenhayn received from German sources cannot be entirely dismissed as political scapegoating. Many professional German officers who worked with him genuinely questioned his strategic judgment and operational concepts.[^123] ~하는 동안 post-전쟁 politics certainly amplified this criticism, the underlying concerns about his leadership were rooted in real failures and disappointments ~ 동안 his tenure as Chief of the 장군 Staff.

Finally, Falkenhayn's overall strategic record as supreme commander must be judged by results. Germany's position deteriorated ~ 동안 his tenure from September 1914 to August 1916. The initial failures to achieve victory in 1914 were not his fault, but his strategies in 1915 and 1916 failed to improve Germany's strategic situation and in some respects worsened it.[^124] The entry of Romania into 전쟁 against Germany in August 1916, which occurred partly ~ 때문에 Germany appeared to be losing ~ 후에 Verdun and the Brusilov Offensive, represented a strategic failure that Falkenhayn's later tactical success in defeating Romania could not entirely erase.[^125]
