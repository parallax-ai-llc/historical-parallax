## 개요

Both sides committed extensive atrocities during the 한국전쟁. Notable incidents include the No Gun Ri massacre by US forces and systematic execution of prisoners by 북한n forces.

| 1953 | War ended |

## 맥락

- The 한국전쟁 involved multiple armed forces and rapidly shifting front lines, creating conditions for widespread civilian vulnerability.
- Ideological polarization and fears of infiltration contributed to harsh treatment of suspected collaborators and prisoners.
- Documentation of atrocities comes from survivor testimony, military records, journalism, and later investigations.

## Types of atrocities (high level)

- Mass killings of civilians, including during evacuations, screening operations, and anti-guerrilla campaigns.
- Executions of prisoners of war and political detainees.
- Sexual violence, looting, and forced displacement in contested areas.

## Accountability and memory

- Some incidents were investigated years later, sometimes producing official acknowledgments or contested findings.
- The politics of memoryhow events are taught, commemorated, and debatedremains sensitive in Korea and among involved states.
- Ongoing research continues to refine understanding of scale, responsibility, and the experiences of different communities.

## Significance

- The legacy of wartime atrocities shapes modern discourse on 인권, reconciliation, and national identity.
- It also affects diplomatic narratives among Korea, the 미국, 중국, and other parties connected to the conflict.
