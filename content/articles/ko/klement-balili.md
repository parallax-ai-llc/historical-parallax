## 개요

알바니아n drug lord who ran one of 유럽's largest cannabis and cocaine trafficking networks.

## 경력 연표

| 연도 | 사건 |
|------|-------|
| 2016 | Greek police issued an arrest warrant while Balili held a public post in Sarandë (Director of Road Transportation).[^2] |
| 2016–2018 | Reported to have remained at large; 알바니아n police did not arrest him at the time of the Greek warrant.[^2] |
| 2018 | 알바니아n authorities changed the law to prohibit extraditions (context cited in coverage of Balili’s case).[^2] |
| Jan 2019 | Turned himself in to 알바니아n police after more than two years at large.[^2] |
| May 2019 | Sentenced in 알바니아; later reporting describes a 10-year sentence connected to an abbreviated trial process.[^2] |
| 2020s | 알바니아n appeals court reporting: Serious Crimes Court of Appeal upheld a 10-year sentence.[^2] |

## Background and Public Role

Balili is an 알바니아n figure widely described in regional media as an international drug trafficker, sometimes labeled “the Balkans’ Escobar”.[^2]

According to reporting, he worked for the 알바니아n government in Sarandë as **Director of Road Transportation** around the time a Greek arrest warrant was issued in 2016.[^2]

## Criminal Allegations and Charges (as Reported)

Coverage of his 알바니아n case has referenced the following charges:[^2]

- Drug trafficking
- Membership in a criminal group
- Lack of justification for assets
- Money laundering

## Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- Prosecutors reportedly sought a longer sentence (18 years) than the final term described in later reporting.[^2]
- Reporting states the first-instance sentence was reduced due to an **abbreviated trial** request, resulting in a lower final sentence.[^2]

## Extradition Context

Reporting notes that Balili was tried in 알바니아 despite being wanted by Greek police, in a context where the two countries reportedly lacked an extradition agreement and 알바니아n law changes in 2018 were described as limiting extraditions.[^2]
