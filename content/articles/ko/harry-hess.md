## 배경

Hess was an American geologist whose ideas helped shape the modern theory of plate tectonics.
His career is often discussed both in terms of his academic work and the mid-20th-century oceanographic research boom that made new seabed data available.

## Seafloor spreading (1962)

Seafloor spreading proposed that new oceanic crust is created at mid-ocean ridges and moves outward over time.
This mechanism provided a physical explanation for how continents could appear to drift while remaining part of larger moving plates.

## Why it mattered

Before plate tectonics became broadly accepted, evidence existed (matching coastlines, fossil distributions, paleomagnetism), but the driving process was contested.
Hess’s synthesis connected multiple lines of geophysical observation to a coherent model of ocean-basin evolution.

## 유산

- The seafloor-spreading concept became a cornerstone of plate tectonic theory.
- It influenced how scientists interpret mid-ocean ridges, trench systems, and the recycling of oceanic lithosphere.
- It helped motivate further systematic mapping of the seafloor and measurement of magnetic anomalies.
