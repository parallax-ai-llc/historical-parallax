## 개요
Joel David Rifkin was an American serial killer who murdered 17 women, mostly sex workers, in the 뉴욕 City area between 1989 and 1993. He was caught during a routine traffic stop when police noticed a foul odor from his truck and found a body in the bed.

## 초기 생애
Born January 20, 1959, in 뉴욕 City. He was adopted and raised on Long Island. He was a poor student who was bullied in school. He worked as a landscaper and handyman. [^1]

## 범죄 경력
Between 1989 and 1993, Rifkin murdered 17 women, primarily sex workers he picked up in 뉴욕 City. He strangled most victims and disposed of bodies across Long Island and New Jersey. He kept trophies from his victims. [^2]

## 체포 / 몰락
On June 28, 1993, police attempted to pull Rifkin over for driving without a license plate. After a high-speed chase, they stopped his truck and found the decomposing body of his last victim. He confessed to 17 murders and was convicted, receiving 203 years to life. [^3]

## 긍정적 관점
- His case led to improved investigation of crimes against sex workers [^4]
- Prompted formation of multi-agency task forces for serial crimes in the NYC area
- Advanced forensic identification of decomposed remains

## 부정적 관점
- Murdered 17 women [^5]
- Targeted sex workers, knowing police often deprioritized such cases
- Kept trophies from victims
- Operated for four years without detection
- His case highlighted systemic neglect of violence against sex workers
