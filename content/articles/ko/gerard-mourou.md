## Chirped Pulse Amplification (CPA)

CPA is a method for producing extremely intense, ultrashort laser pulses without destroying the amplifier.
The basic idea is to **stretch** a short pulse in time (reducing peak power), **amplify** it safely, then **recompress** it to regain a very high peak intensity.

## Scientific and Practical Impact

High-intensity, short-pulse lasers enabled by CPA are used across research and industry, including:

- nonlinear optics and laser–matter interaction experiments
- precision micromachining and materials processing
- medical procedures that rely on short pulses and controlled energy delivery (often discussed in the broader ultrafast-laser context)
