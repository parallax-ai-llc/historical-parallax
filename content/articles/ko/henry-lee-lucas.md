## 개요
Henry Lee Lucas was an American serial killer convicted of 11 homicides. He falsely confessed to hundreds of murders, exposing problems with police handling of confessions. His death sentence was commuted by Governor George W. Bush.

## 초기 생애
Born August 23, 1936, in Blacksburg, Virginia. He suffered extreme abuse from his mother, a sex worker. He lost an eye in childhood. In 1960 he murdered his mother. [^1]

## 범죄 경력
After release in 1975, Lucas partnered with Ottis Toole, committing murders across the south. He was convicted of 11 murders. He initially confessed to 600+ murders, leading police to close hundreds of cases, though most confessions were later proven false. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Arrested in 1983 in Texas. A task force investigated his claims; journalist Hugh Aynesworth proved many confessions impossible. Sentenced to death for one murder, commuted to life by Governor Bush. Died in prison March 12, 2001. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- Exposed serious problems with false confession handling [^4]
- Led to confession verification reforms
- Some wrongly accused were exonerated

## Negative Perspectives
- Convicted of 11 murders [^5]
- False confessions caused hundreds of cases to be closed incorrectly
- Murdered his teenage common-law wife
- Manipulated the justice system
