## 개요

The Cho family running Korean Air became infamous for abusive behavior: nut rage incident, water-throwing at a meeting, and smuggling luxury goods. Highlighted 재벌 entitlement.

## 경력 연표

| 연도 | 사건 |
|------|-------|
| 2014 | Nut rage incident |
| 2018 | Water-throwing incident |

## 배경

- Korean Air is part of the Hanjin Group, a major 대한민국n conglomerate (재벌).
- Family-controlled governance structures in 재벌 groups have long drawn scrutiny over accountability and workplace power dynamics.
- The scandals became a focal point for public debate about "gapjil" (abuse of power by elites) and corporate culture.

## Notable incidents (high level)

- The "nut rage" incident involved an in-flight service dispute that escalated into a public controversy.
- The "water-throwing" incident referred to alleged abusive behavior toward staff during a business meeting.
- Separate allegations included improper handling or smuggling of luxury goods, contributing to legal and reputational consequences.

## Public and institutional response

- Media coverage amplified the events and spurred calls for corporate governance reforms.
- Authorities investigated specific allegations, and some involved parties faced legal proceedings or penalties.
- The controversy influenced discussions on shareholder rights, executive conduct, and protections for employees.

## Broader significance

- The Korean Air family scandals are often cited as emblematic of tensions between economic power and social legitimacy.
- They also illustrate how high-visibility incidents can trigger wider attention to labor conditions and compliance practices.
