## 개요

Jan Ernst Matzeliger invented the shoe lasting machine in 1883, which mechanized the process of attaching the upper part of a shoe to the sole. His machine could produce up to 700 pairs of shoes per day compared to 50 by hand, cutting shoe prices in half and making affordable footwear available to ordinary people. He died of tuberculosis at age 36.
