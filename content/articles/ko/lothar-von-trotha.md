## 개요

German colonial general who commanded the genocide of the Herero and Nama peoples in German South West 아프리카 (Namibia) in 1904-1908. His extermination order led to the deaths of up to 100,000 people, considered the first genocide of the 20th century.

## 배경

Von Trotha was a career officer in the German Empire who served in several colonial and overseas postings before being sent to German South West 아프리카 during the Herero uprising.

## Herero and Nama genocide (1904-1908)

- In October 1904, von Trotha issued an "extermination order" (Vernichtungsbefehl) directed at the Herero population.
- After the Battle of Waterberg, German forces pursued Herero groups into the Omaheke region of the Kalahari Desert, where many died from dehydration and starvation.
- The campaign later expanded to include the Nama, involving forced displacement, mass killings, and the establishment of concentration camps.

## Consequences and historical assessment

Historians widely describe the actions carried out under von Trotha's command as genocidal. The Herero and Nama genocide is frequently cited as an early example of 20th-century state violence in a colonial context.

## Later life

Von Trotha was eventually recalled to 독일. He continued to be discussed in German political and military circles, but his colonial record has become a central focus of modern scholarship and remembrance debates.
