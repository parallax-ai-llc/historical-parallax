From a progressive foreign policy perspective, the Gulf 전쟁 represented unnecessary militarism that could have been avoided ~을 통해 more patient diplomacy, and the decision not to remove Saddam ~하는 동안 encouraging uprisings showed either incompetence or callousness.[^121] The brutal suppression of Kurds and Shiites ~ 후에 전쟁, following American encouragement to rebel, ~의 결과를 가져왔다 humanitarian catastrophe that Bush bears responsibility for. His invasion of Panama, ~하는 동안 achieving its narrow objective of capturing Noriega, ~의 결과를 가져왔다 중요한 civilian casualties and questionable legal justification.[^122]

Conservative critics argue Bush betrayed the Reagan revolution by agreeing to tax increases and failing to advance a conservative domestic agenda.[^123] His broken "no new taxes" pledge represented not responsible governance but political weakness and a fundamental misunderstanding of conservative principles. The subsequent rise of more ideologically committed Republicans reflected grassroots fury at Bush's perceived betrayal. His reluctance to celebrate the Cold 전쟁's end struck some as a failure to fully articulate Western values and 민주주의's triumph over 공산주의.[^124]

Economic critics note that Bush presided over a recession and failed to respond effectively to economic concerns that 궁극적으로 cost him reelection.[^125] His apparent lack of vision for domestic policy—he famously struggled to articulate what he called "the vision thing"—suggested a leader more comfortable managing existing systems than imagining new directions. The electoral defeat by Bill Clinton represented a rejection not just of Bush personally but of his entire approach to governance, suggesting 대중 found his leadership inadequate.[^126]
