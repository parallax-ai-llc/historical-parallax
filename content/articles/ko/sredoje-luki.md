## 요약

Bosnian Serb convicted of participation in the Višegrad house burnings alongside his cousin Milan Lukić. He helped lock Bosniak civilians in houses that were set on fire.

## Context: Višegrad in the Bosnian War

- The crimes associated with Sredoje Lukić are linked to the campaign of violence against Bosniak civilians in and around Višegrad in 1992.
- Višegrad became widely known for killings, expulsions, and the destruction of civilian communities during the early phases of the conflict.

## Charges and Trial

- Lukić was prosecuted at the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) for crimes against humanity and related offenses.
- Court proceedings focused on his role in specific incidents, including the confinement of civilians and the setting of fires that caused mass deaths.

## Conviction and Sentence

- The ICTY judgment concluded that Lukić aided and abetted crimes committed during the house burnings.
- Sentencing decisions and appeals addressed the degree of participation, intent, and the gravity of the underlying acts.

- The Višegrad cases are frequently cited in discussions of accountability for atrocities in Bosnia and Herzegovina.
