## Notable Works

### Manga / Comics

- *Akira* — dystopian science-fiction manga widely credited with helping popularize manga internationally.
- *Dōmu* (*Domu: A Child's Dream*) — earlier manga noted for its cinematic paneling and psychological tension.

### Animation / Film

- *Akira* (1988) — feature film (writer/director) and a global reference point for cyberpunk aesthetics.
- *Roujin Z* (1991) — animated film (screenplay).
- *Steamboy* (2004) — animated feature film (co-writer/director).
- *Short Peace* (2013) — anthology project (creator/director roles).

## Themes and Style

- Visual storytelling often described as highly cinematic, with dense urban detail and dynamic motion.
- Recurring interest in near-future technology, social breakdown, political power, and mass urban life.
- Blends grounded mechanical realism with heightened, sometimes surreal, dramatic escalation.

## Influence and Legacy

- Frequently cited as a key figure in late-20th-century 일본ese pop culture, especially for the impact of *Akira*.
- Helped shape international perceptions of anime as a medium capable of large-scale, adult-oriented science fiction.
- His work is commonly referenced in discussions of cyberpunk imagery and modern animation craft.
