## 개요

카우스, born Brian Donnelly on November 4, 1974, in Jersey City, New Jersey, is an American artist, designer, and painter whose work spans fine art, street art, graphic design, and collectible toys. Beginning his career as a graffiti artist in the 1990s, he evolved into one of the most commercially successful and culturally influential contemporary artists of his generation. His instantly recognizable imagery — cartoon-derived figures with X-ed-out eyes — bridges the worlds of pop art, street culture, luxury fashion, and mainstream entertainment.

His significance lies in his ability to dissolve boundaries between high and low culture, bringing fine art into contact with streetwear, toy collecting, and brand collaborations. 카우스 has worked with Nike, Dior, Uniqlo, and Sesame Street, among dozens of others, while simultaneously exhibiting at major institutions and auction houses. His work commands millions at auction and draws massive crowds to outdoor installations worldwide, making him a defining figure in 21st-century visual culture.

After graduating, 카우스 began subverting street-level advertising. He would obtain unused bus shelter posters and phone booth displays, alter them by inserting his skull-and-crossbones motifs and X-eyed characters, then return them to public display. ## Controversies & Criticism

카우스 has faced persistent criticism from fine art circles who question whether his work represents genuine artistic depth or sophisticated brand management. Critics note that the volume of commercial collaborations — with fast-fashion brand Uniqlo releasing mass-market T-shirts at under $20 — sits in tension with auction prices that position him alongside blue-chip contemporary artists. Some street art purists have argued his corporate partnerships represent a betrayal of graffiti's anti-establishment roots. Others have raised concerns about the speculative collectibles market his work fuels, drawing comparisons to market manipulation seen in other hypebeast-adjacent commodities.

## Legacy & Influence

카우스 has permanently reshaped how contemporary art intersects with consumer culture. He demonstrated that an artist could maintain critical and commercial credibility simultaneously, influencing a generation of artists working across art, design, and fashion — from Daniel Arsham to Virgil Abloh. His model of accessible merchandise alongside limited editions and fine art originals has become a template for artist-brand partnerships. His work helped legitimize toy collecting as an art-adjacent practice and contributed to the global expansion of the "art toy" market.
