## 유명한 인용문

> "Love is the crowning grace of humanity."[^138]
— On the supreme importance of love in human experience

> "And men go about to wonder at the heights of the mountains, and the mighty waves of the sea, and the wide sweep of rivers, and the circuit of the ocean, and the revolution of the stars, but themselves they consider not."[^139]
— From Augustine's Confessions, read at the summit of Mont Ventoux, reflecting on the importance of self-knowledge

> "To be able to say how much love, is love but little."[^140]
— On the inadequacy of words to express profound emotion

> "I freeze and burn, love is bitter and sweet, my sighs are tempests and my tears are floods, I am in ecstasy and agony, I am possessed by memories of her and I am in exile from myself."[^141]
— Expressing the contradictions of passionate love

> "It is better to will the good than to know the truth."[^142]
— Prioritizing moral action over intellectual knowledge

> "Events appear sad, pleasant, or painful, not because they are so in reality, but because we believe them to be so and the light in which we look at them depends upon our own judgment."[^143]
— On the subjective nature of experience

> "Man has not a greater enemy than himself."[^144]
— On the challenge of self-mastery

> "Books have led some to learning and others to madness."[^145]
— On the double-edged nature of knowledge

> "A short cut to riches is to subtract from our desires."[^146]
— Advocating contentment over accumulation

> "All pleasure in the world is a passing dream."[^147]
— Reflecting on the transitory nature of earthly satisfaction
