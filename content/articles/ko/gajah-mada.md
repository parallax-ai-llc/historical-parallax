## 역사적 평가

### 긍정적 평가

Academic scholarship has consistently recognized Gajah Mada's exceptional political, military, and administrative capabilities. Historians credit him in 나이든 Javanese manuscripts, poems, and inscriptions with bringing the Majapahit Empire to its absolute peak of glory[^70]. ~ 동안 왕 Hayam Wuruk's reign (1350–1389), Majapahit attained unprecedented power 구체적으로 ~을 통해 Gajah Mada's strategic vision and administrative excellence, which he maintained throughout his tenure from 1329 to 1364[^71].

Scholarly research emphasizes his leadership skills and strategic intelligence in the context of 14th-세기 Southeast Asian geopolitics. His ability to rise from common origins to become the most 강력한 minister in the empire demonstrates the meritocratic aspects of Majapahit's political system and Gajah Mada's exceptional personal capabilities.

Military historians acknowledge his sophisticated understanding of naval power and amphibious operations. ### 부정적 평가

비판적인 historical assessment focuses primarily on Gajah Mada's methods and their human costs. Some historians question the extent of Majapahit's actual territorial control, suggesting that many regions listed in the Nagarakretagama may have been tributary states with considerable autonomy rather than directly governed territories[^80]. This scholarly debate challenges maximalist interpretations of Gajah Mada's achievements, suggesting his empire may have been more fragile and less integrated than nationalist narratives claim.

Archaeological and historical controversies also surround his representation. Many scholars disagree with Mohammad Yamin's 1945 interpretation of terracotta artifacts as depicting Gajah Mada, considering such identification speculative and potentially inaccurate[^81]. These debates reflect broader concerns about how nationalist ideology has shaped historical interpretation of Gajah Mada, potentially creating a mythologized figure that obscures the complex historical reality.
