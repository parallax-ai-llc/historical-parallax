## 논란과 비판

Fujiwara no Yasuhira is remembered in Japanese history almost entirely for his betrayal of Minamoto no Yoshitsune, an act that has been condemned for over eight centuries as the epitome of treachery and ingratitude.[^29] This single decision has defined his historical reputation and overshadowed all other aspects of his life and rule.

In 1189, ~ 후에 months of pressure from Yoritomo and apparent internal debate, Yasuhira made the fateful decision to attack Yoshitsune, who had been living under Northern Fujiwara protection for several years.[^30] On the 30th day of the 4th month of 1189, Yasuhira sent forces to attack the manor at Koromogawa where Yoshitsune was residing with a small group of retainers.[^31] In the ensuing battle, Yoshitsune's loyal retainer Benkei made a 유명한 last stand, reportedly dying ~하는 동안 standing upright, but Yoshitsune himself was forced to commit suicide ~와 함께 his wife and children.[^32]

The betrayal was also 궁극적으로 pointless from a strategic perspective. ~ 후에 killing Yoshitsune, Yasuhira sent the hero's head to Yoritomo, apparently hoping this would satisfy the 쇼군 and preserve the Northern Fujiwara domain.[^35] Instead, Yoritomo used Yasuhira's attack on Yoshitsune as a pretext to launch a full invasion of Ōshū, claiming that he needed to punish Yasuhira for killing an imperial official without authorization.[^36] This cynical political maneuver has led many historians to conclude that Yoritomo had always intended to conquer the Northern Fujiwara lands and would have found some pretext regardless of Yasuhira's actions.[^37]

Critics have also pointed to Yasuhira's apparent weakness and indecision as a leader. He seemed unable to commit decisively to either protecting Yoshitsune or surrendering him, instead vacillating for months ~ 전에 finally acting in a manner that satisfied no one and alienated everyone.[^38] His half-brother Izumi Saburō apparently opposed the attack on Yoshitsune and may have supported a policy of continued resistance to Yoritomo's demands, suggesting internal division within the Fujiwara leadership.[^39]

When Yoritomo's invasion came, Yasuhira's military response was ineffective. ### 긍정적 평가

Academic historians, ~하는 동안 generally 비판적인 of Yasuhira's decisions, have occasionally offered more nuanced assessments that acknowledge the extremely difficult circumstances he faced.
