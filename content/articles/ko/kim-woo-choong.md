## 개요

Founder of Daewoo Group who perpetrated a $43 billion accounting fraud, the largest in Korean history. Fled abroad for 6 years before returning and being convicted.

## Daewoo Group and the Chaebol Model

김우중 founded **Daewoo Group**, which grew into one of 대한민국's major conglomerates (재벌). Daewoo expanded aggressively across manufacturing, shipbuilding, automobiles, and international trading, reflecting a broader 대한민국n development strategy that combined export-led growth with large, diversified corporate groups.

## Rapid Expansion and Risk Accumulation

Accounts of Daewoo's growth frequently emphasize heavy use of leverage and ambitious overseas investments. In the late 1990s, 대한민국 faced severe macroeconomic stress during the 아시아n financial crisis period, and highly indebted corporate structures became more fragile under tightening credit and shifting market conditions.

## Collapse and Legal Aftermath

Daewoo's collapse in 1999 became one of the defining corporate failures in modern 대한민국n economic history. Subsequent investigations and court proceedings focused on allegations of:

- large-scale accounting manipulation,
- embezzlement and breach of trust,
- improper financing practices.

김우중 left 대한민국 and later returned to face trial; he was convicted and sentenced, and later reports note subsequent reductions or pardons as part of 대한민국's periodically debated approach to white-collar crime and economic policy.

## Historical Significance

The Daewoo case is often discussed in relation to:

- post-crisis corporate restructuring,
- governance reforms and transparency standards,
- the economic and political influence of 재벌 in 대한민국.
