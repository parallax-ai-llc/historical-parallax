## 개요

French fortune teller and poisoner born Catherine Monvoisin who was central to the Affair of the Poisons at the court of Louis XIV. She supplied poison to French aristocrats and performed black masses involving infant sacrifice.

## Additional Context

### The Affair of the Poisons

- The Affair of the Poisons (late 1670s–1680s) was a major scandal involving accusations of poisoning, fortune-telling, and alleged occult practices among segments of the French elite.
- 라 부아쟁 (Catherine Monvoisin) became one of the most prominent figures in the investigations and later popular histories.
- Sources describe a network of clients seeking poisons, love potions, and services that blended folk medicine, divination, and criminal opportunism.

### Court politics and investigation

- The scandal occurred under Louis XIV, where court intrigue and patronage created both motive and opportunity for clandestine plots.
- The French state response included special investigative bodies and secret proceedings, and the episode has been studied as an example of early modern policing and state power.

### Historical interpretation

- Some of the most sensational allegations (such as the details of "black masses") are debated in terms of evidentiary reliability, because testimony was gathered under intense pressure and within a politically charged environment.
- Regardless of exact details, the case remains a touchstone for understanding fear, rumor, and moral panic in early modern 유럽.

### Why she is remembered

- Symbol of how informal occult economies could intersect with elite anxieties about inheritance, marriage, and status.
- A recurring figure in literature and popular culture about poisoners, witches, and clandestine court scandals.
