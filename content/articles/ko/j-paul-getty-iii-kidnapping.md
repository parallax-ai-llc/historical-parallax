## 개요

Kidnapping of J. Paul Getty III by the Ndrangheta in Rome in 1973. After his grandfather refused to pay, the kidnappers sent the boys severed ear to a newspaper. A $2.9 million ransom was eventually paid.

## Additional Context

- **Criminal group**: The kidnapping is attributed to the 'Ndrangheta, a Calabrian organized-crime network that has been involved historically in extortion and kidnapping.
- **Negotiation dynamics**: Accounts emphasize the prolonged negotiations and the family's internal dispute over paying a ransom.
- **Media attention**: The case became internationally notorious, in part due to the proof-of-life tactic reported in the press.

## Aftermath (High-Level)

- Often cited in later discussions of ransom policy and the incentives created by payment.
- Revisited in books and screen adaptations focusing on wealth, family conflict, and organized crime.
