~에도 불구하고 his eventual canonical status, Hopper faced 중요한 criticism throughout his career, particularly ~ 동안 rise of Abstract Expressionism. ## 역사적 평가

### 긍정적 평가

Scholarly reassessment has elevated Hopper's reputation considerably ~ 이래로 the mid-세기 criticisms from Abstract Expressionist advocates. He is now widely acknowledged as the most 중요한 realist 화가 of twentieth-세기 America, encapsulating aspects of American experience so authentically that "we can hardly see a tumbledown house near a deserted road or a shadow slipping across a brownstone facade except through his eyes"[^90].

Hopper's philosophical approach to 예술 has been praised for its depth. His statement that "Great art is the outward expression of an inner life in the artist, and this inner life will result in his personal vision of the world" from his 1933 "Notes on Painting" encapsulates his core artistic philosophy[^94]. His belief that "No amount of skillful invention can replace the essential element of imagination" reflected his conviction that technical skill alone is insufficient for creating meaningful art[^95]. His aim, as he stated, was "the most exact transcription possible of my most intimate impression of nature," emphasizing personal interpretation over mere representation[^96].

Critics have noted Hopper's prescient critique of abstract 예술: "One of the weaknesses of much abstract painting is the attempt to substitute the inventions of the intellect for a pristine imaginative conception. The inner life of a human being is a vast and varied realm and does not concern itself alone with stimulating arrangements of color, form, and design"[^97]. This philosophical grounding has gained appreciation as the dominance of Abstract Expressionism has waned.

### 부정적 평가

~에도 불구하고 posthumous acclaim, negative assessments of Hopper's work persist in some quarters. The mid-세기 criticism that dismissed him as technically limited continues to influence certain scholarly perspectives. Hopper's conservative approach to pictorial composition and his rejection of modernist formal experimentation are seen by some as artistic limitations rather than strengths.

Critics have pointed to Hopper's narrow thematic range as a weakness. His persistent focus on isolation and loneliness, ~하는 동안 distinctive, has been characterized as repetitive and psychologically constrained[^98]. Some have argued that Hopper's work reflects his own emotional limitations and troubled marriage rather than a broader vision of American life.

The controversy surrounding the racialized promotion of Hopper as the "most inherently Anglo-Saxon painter" has led some scholars to examine how his work was complicit in exclusionary visions of American identity[^99]. This nationalist appropriation of his imagery has complicated his legacy, raising questions about whose America Hopper depicted and whose experience was excluded from his vision.

Additionally, some feminist 예술 historians have criticized how Josephine Hopper's own artistic career was sacrificed to Edward's success, with her talents subordinated to her roles as model, muse, and record-keeper[^100]. The controlling and belittling aspects of their marriage, including Edward's forbidding her from discussing their relationship and cutting her off from friends, reveal troubling power dynamics that cast a shadow over the romantic mythology surrounding the artistic couple.

Technical criticisms persist as well. Some printmakers have noted that Hopper's preference for wiping etching plates completely clean, rather than using residual ink for tonal effects, represented a more conservative approach to the medium[^101]. His 후기의 work has been characterized by some as repetitive and lacking the vitality of his earlier achievements.
