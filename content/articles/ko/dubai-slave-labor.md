## 개요

Dubai's gleaming skyline was built largely by migrant workers living in squalid labor camps. Workers from South Asia face debt bondage, confiscated passports, and dangerous conditions.

## Key Context (High-Level)

- **Migrant labor scale:** The UAE’s construction, domestic work, and service sectors have relied heavily on migrant workers, particularly from South Asia and other parts of Asia and Africa.
- **Common vulnerability factors:** Recruitment debt, lack of bargaining power, language barriers, and dependency on employer-controlled visas have been repeatedly cited by observers as drivers of exploitation risk.

## Mechanisms Often Reported

- **Recruitment fees and debt bondage:** Workers may borrow at high interest to pay brokers/agents, creating pressure to accept abusive conditions to repay debts.
- **Passport confiscation and restricted mobility:** Rights groups have reported practices where workers’ documents are held by employers or labor suppliers, limiting 자유 to leave or change jobs.
- **Wage withholding and delayed pay:** Reports commonly describe 후기의 payment, non-payment, or deductions that workers struggle to contest.
- **Crowded labor accommodation:** Labor camps have been criticized for overcrowding and 가난한 sanitation in some cases, especially for lower-wage workers.
- **Workplace safety risks:** Construction work has been associated with heat stress and accident risks; enforcement and standards are frequently debated by regulators and advocates.

## Governance and Legal Framework (Overview)

- **Kafala-style sponsorship:** A sponsorship-based residency and work-permit structure in Gulf states has been widely discussed as enabling employer leverage over workers’ legal status.
- **Labor dispute resolution:** The UAE has labor courts and complaint mechanisms; critics argue that practical access, timelines, and worker protections can be uneven.
- **Reform efforts (general):** Over the years, authorities have announced changes to labor rules, wage payment systems, and inspection regimes; observers differ on how consistently reforms are implemented and enforced.

## Why the Issue Drew Global Attention

- **Mega-project construction boom:** Dubai’s rapid expansion and large-scale real estate and infrastructure projects intensified scrutiny of labor sourcing, subcontracting chains, and conditions on worksites.
- **International NGO reporting:** Organizations such as 인권 Watch and Amnesty International have published accounts highlighting coercive practices, recruitment debt, and barriers to remedy.
- **Media investigations:** International press coverage has periodically amplified worker testimonies and documentation about accommodation, pay practices, and the ability to change employers.
