## Context (High Level)

Ríos Montt is one of the most internationally recognized figures of Guatemala’s late-20th-century military governments. His brief presidency is closely associated with the most violent period of the Guatemalan 내전, particularly in Indigenous regions where the state fought guerrilla organizations.

## Why He Matters

- **내전 policy**: His administration is frequently cited in discussions of “scorched earth” counterinsurgency campaigns.
- **Transitional justice**: The 2013 genocide trial was a landmark case in 라틴아메리카 for prosecuting a former head of state in domestic courts.
- **Political legacy**: Even after leaving power, he remained influential through party politics and congressional leadership.

## Governance and Counterinsurgency (Summary)

- Emphasized military control and internal security as the core state priority.
- Framed the conflict as a struggle against subversion/communism, which shaped policy and rhetoric.
- Counterinsurgency operations in rural areas were widely alleged to have targeted civilian populations alongside armed opponents.

## Trial and Legal Aftermath (Summary)

- Charges centered on atrocities committed against Maya Ixil (and related communities) during the early 1980s.
- The case produced extensive testimony and documentation about military operations, command responsibility, and the human cost of the conflict.
- The annulment and subsequent retrial efforts became a major point of debate about judicial independence, due process, and accountability.
