## 역사적 평가

### 긍정적 평가

Scholarly assessments of Steinem generally credit her with extraordinary skill at mass communication and coalition-building ~ 동안 a 비판적인 period of social change. 역사학자 Ruth Rosen argues that Steinem's ability to translate complex feminist theory into accessible language without condescension was crucial to building a broad-based women's movement in the 1970s.[^71] 정치학자 Jo Freeman emphasizes Steinem's organizational talents and her understanding that sustainable movements require both grassroots energy and institutional infrastructure.[^72]

Supporters also note that Steinem has consistently supported younger feminists and adapted her positions in response to legitimate criticisms, particularly regarding intersectionality, transgender inclusion, and sex workers' rights.[^77] Unlike some second-wave feminists who became reactionary with age, Steinem attended Black Lives Matter protests, supported marriage 평등, and condemned police 폭력, maintaining her progressive credentials.[^78]

### 부정적 평가

Critics from the left argue that Steinem's liberal feminism has been complicit in neoliberal transformations that have worsened conditions for most women ~하는 동안 celebrating token female representation in elite institutions.[^79] Nancy Fraser and other theorists contend that second-wave feminism's emphasis on professional achievement and market participation dovetailed with corporate interests in expanding the labor force and undermining family wage structures, leaving working-class women worse off.[^80]

The CIA controversy continues to generate suspicion among radical activists who see Steinem as potentially compromised by intelligence connections and hostile to genuinely 혁명가 feminism.[^81] Some critics interpret her conflicts with more radical feminists like Ti-Grace Atkinson and her criticisms of separatist and lesbian feminist approaches as evidence of deliberate movement management on behalf of establishment interests.[^82]

Postcolonial feminists criticize Steinem's international advocacy as sometimes reflecting American imperial feminism that imposes Western frameworks on non-Western contexts.[^83] Her support for U.S. military interventions justified by women's rights concerns (as in Afghanistan) has been cited as evidence that liberal feminism can serve as handmaiden to American empire.[^84]
