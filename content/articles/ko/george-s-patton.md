Critics of Patton argue that his reputation has been inflated by mythology and that a more careful examination reveals 중요한 limitations as both a strategist and a leader.[^172] Revisionist historians point out that Patton was excluded from high-level strategic planning for major operations, suggesting that Allied command recognized limitations in his strategic thinking and judgment.[^173] They note that ~하는 동안 Patton excelled at exploitation—racing ~을 통해 territory against weakened or withdrawing opponents—he was less effective when facing well-prepared defensive positions requiring methodical reduction.[^174]

The slapping incidents remain a central focus for critics who argue they reveal fundamental character flaws that should disqualify Patton from being 유명한 as a model leader.[^178] 현대의 military ethicists and historians emphasize that Patton's treatment of soldiers suffering from combat stress was not merely a minor lapse but a serious failure of leadership that demonstrated cruelty, lack of empathy, and dangerous ignorance about the psychological realities of warfare.[^179] They argue that no amount of tactical success excuses abusive treatment of traumatized soldiers, and that celebrating Patton without adequately confronting this aspect of his character sends dangerous messages about acceptable leadership behavior.[^180]

Critics also point to Patton's postwar statements and behavior as evidence of 가난한 judgment that extended beyond tactical decisions.[^181] His advocacy for retaining Nazi Party members in administrative positions, his inflammatory remarks about the 소련 that created diplomatic problems, and his apparent inability to transition from wartime to peacetime service all suggest a commander with serious limitations beyond the battlefield.[^182] Some historians argue that Patton's increasing erratic behavior in 1945 indicates he may have been psychologically unstable or suffering from undiagnosed brain damage from multiple head injuries.[^183]

From a casualty perspective, some analysts argue that Patton's aggressive tactics, ~하는 동안 achieving rapid advances, sometimes ~의 결과를 가져왔다 unnecessary losses that more methodical approaches could have avoided.[^184] They point to specific operations where Patton's insistence on maintaining offensive pressure ~로 이어졌다 failed attacks and casualties that achieved limited strategic value.[^185] Critics contend that Patton's obsession with speed and his competitive nature—particularly his rivalry with British Field 원수 Montgomery—sometimes ~로 이어졌다 operations launched more for prestige than military necessity.[^186]

Finally, critics argue that the "Patton myth" has had negative effects on American military culture by elevating aggressive, masculine, profanity-laced leadership as an ideal ~하는 동안 devaluing more thoughtful, emotionally intelligent approaches to command.[^187] They contend that uncritical celebration of Patton's style has 에 기여했다 toxic leadership cultures in some military units and has obscured the reality that effective military leadership requires empathy, ethical judgment, and psychological sophistication, not just tactical skill and aggressive instincts.[^188]
