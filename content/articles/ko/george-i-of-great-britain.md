George I's legacy is paradoxical: though widely disliked personally and dismissed by contemporaries as a mediocre 왕, his reign proved transformative for British constitutional development.[^116] His greatest legacy was unintentional—by his absence and disengagement, he allowed the evolution of cabinet government and parliamentary supremacy that would define 현대의 British 민주주의.[^117]
