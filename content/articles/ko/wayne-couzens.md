## 배경

Wayne Couzens was a serving officer in London's Metropolitan Police Service. Public reporting described prior allegations and complaints about his conduct before the murder, alongside questions about vetting and internal oversight within the force.[^1]

## Kidnapping and Murder of Sarah Everard

In March 2021, Couzens used his status as a police officer to detain Sarah Everard while she was walking home in London. He abducted her, drove her away from the city, and later raped and murdered her.[^1]

The case drew intense public attention because it involved abuse of police authority, and it contributed to broader debate in the UK about women's safety in public spaces and trust in policing.[^1]

## 수사 and Arrest

Couzens was identified as a suspect after investigative work that included reviewing CCTV and other evidence, leading to his arrest within days of Everard's disappearance. Subsequent searches and forensic analysis supported the prosecution case.[^1]

## 재판 and Sentencing

Couzens pleaded guilty to kidnapping, rape, and murder. He received a whole-life order (a sentence intended to keep the offender in prison for the rest of their life) for the murder of Sarah Everard.[^1]

## Public and Institutional Impact

The murder and the fact that it was committed by a police officer prompted internal reviews and external scrutiny of the Metropolitan Police. It also triggered calls for reforms related to officer conduct, accountability, and the handling of complaints.[^1]

## Notes

Most published summaries emphasize the case as an example of an offender leveraging institutional authority to facilitate a violent crime, which is one reason it became a touchstone for policy and cultural discussion in the UK.[^1]
