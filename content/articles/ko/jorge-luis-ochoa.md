## 개요
호르헤 루이스 오초아 Vásquez was a co-founder of the Medellín Cartel. He surrendered in 1991, served five years, and was released. The US unsuccessfully sought his extradition.

## Additional Context

### Background
- 호르헤 루이스 오초아 Vásquez is frequently described as coming from a family involved in cattle and other legitimate businesses prior to (and alongside) cocaine trafficking.
- Biographical summaries often emphasize a "businessman" profile compared to more visibly violent cartel figures.

### Role in the Medellín Cartel
- Commonly listed as a founding member of the Medellín Cartel, alongside figures such as Pablo Escobar, Carlos Lehder, 호세 곤살로 로드리게스 가차, and Gustavo Gaviria.[^2]
- In many accounts, the Ochoa network is framed as part of the cartel’s logistics and commercial backbone (production, distribution, and money movement), rather than solely street-level enforcement.

### Extradition Pressure and Legal Strategy
- During the 1980s, U.S. indictments and extradition requests became a major pressure point for cartel leaders.
- Ochoa’s case is often cited as an example of how 콜롬비아n domestic sentencing, surrender deals, and political constraints around extradition could limit U.S. prosecution outcomes.[^2]

### Notes on Dates and Names
- This file retains the frontmatter birth field as-is, but many sources cite his birth date as 30 September 1950 (Medellín).[^2]
- Spanish naming conventions: Ochoa (paternal surname) and Vásquez (maternal surname) may be inconsistently rendered across English-language sources.
