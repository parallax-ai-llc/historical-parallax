### The Polygamy Crisis

The most 중요한 controversy of Fatmawati's life centered on her husband's decision to practice polygamy. ## 역사적 평가

### 긍정적 평가

Scholars and historians have increasingly recognized Fatmawati's contributions beyond the symbolic act of sewing the flag. ### 부정적 평가 and Criticisms

Some critics argue that Fatmawati's contributions have been romanticized beyond their actual significance. Feminist historians have expressed disappointment with Fatmawati's response to Sukarno's polygamy, viewing her withdrawal from the palace as a missed opportunity for advocacy. Women's organizations at the time had hoped she would use her position as First Lady to publicly champion marriage law reform and women's rights.[^97] Instead, her personal solution – maintaining her title ~하는 동안 living separately – addressed her individual situation but did little to advance legal protections for other Indonesian women who faced similar circumstances without her privileges.

Critics note that Fatmawati was "not initially in favor of reforming marriage laws until she found out about her husband's intentions to marry again," suggesting her concern for women's rights was prompted by personal grievance rather than principled commitment.[^98] This interpretation portrays her as someone who only recognized injustice when she personally experienced it, rather than as a true advocate for systemic change.

Some historians argue that Fatmawati's role exemplifies how Indonesian 민족주의 incorporated women primarily in supportive, domestic capacities rather than as equal participants. The fact that her most 유명한 contribution involved sewing – 전통적인 women's work – rather than political or military action reflects gendered limitations on women's participation in the 독립 movement.[^99]

Her absence from Sukarno's funeral, ~하는 동안 understandable on personal grounds, is viewed by some as petty and beneath the dignity expected of a National Hero, especially one whose status derived largely from her relationship with the deceased.[^100] Critics suggest that a truly statesmanlike figure would have attended to represent national unity and show respect for Indonesia's founding 대통령, regardless of personal grievances.

Finally, some observers question whether Fatmawati's numerous commemorations – airports, hospitals, streets, statues – are proportionate to her actual contributions, or whether they reflect Indonesia's tendency toward cult-of-personality politics and dynastic thinking, especially given that her daughter later became 대통령.[^101]
