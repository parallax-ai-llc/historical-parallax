## 개요

Mexican drug lord named Isidro Meza Flores who controls the Mazatlán faction of the Beltrán Leyva Organization. He is a major methamphetamine and fentanyl trafficker in Sinaloa with a multi-million dollar US bounty.

## 배경 and Identity

The nickname “El Chapo Isidro” is associated with Isidro Meza Flores, a figure described in open reporting as operating in and around the Pacific coast region of Sinaloa, Mexico. Sources frequently link him to the broader ecosystem of splinter groups that emerged ~ 후에 leadership disruptions within major trafficking organizations.

## Reported Role in Organized Crime

Public sources generally describe Meza Flores as:

- a regional organizer connected to the **Beltrán Leyva Organization**’s fragmentation,
- involved in logistics for synthetic drugs, and
- competing with rival factions for control of corridors and port access on Mexico’s northwest coast.

~ 때문에 much of the available information comes from law-enforcement reporting and journalism, details can vary by source and over time.

## Broader Context

The Sinaloa region’s trafficking landscape has often been characterized by shifting alliances, where local enforcement pressure, rival competition, and leadership disputes can rapidly change which groups control specific territories. In that environment, individuals with regional influence may be described as “faction leaders” even when organizational structures are fluid.
