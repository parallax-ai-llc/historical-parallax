## Background and Identification

카비루 소코토 has been described in reporting and court coverage as a Boko Haram-linked figure associated with the 2011 Christmas Day bombing in Madalla.

He is also commonly referenced under the name **카비르u Umar** in 나이지리아n media coverage and legal proceedings related to the case.

## 2011 Madalla Christmas Day Bombing (Context)

The Madalla attack is widely cited as one of the deadliest early incidents in Boko Haram’s insurgency period and drew national and international attention.

High-level details frequently cited in sources include:

- **Date:** 25 December 2011
- **Target:** St. Theresa Catholic Church, Madalla (Niger State)
- **Method:** Bombing (explosive device)

## Arrest, Custody, and Trial

Public reporting on the case emphasizes that he was arrested in 2012, after which there were widely reported developments involving custody and re-arrest.

The case proceeded through 나이지리아n courts, culminating in a 2013 conviction.

## Conviction and Sentence

He was convicted in 2013 for his role in the Madalla church bombing.

Sources commonly report a **life sentence** in connection with the conviction (some reporting varies on the precise counts and sentencing details).

## Significance

The conviction was treated by 나이지리아n authorities and many observers as a notable counter-terrorism prosecution tied to a high-profile mass-casualty attack.
