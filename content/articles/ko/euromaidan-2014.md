## 개요

The Euromaidan, also known as 혁명 of Dignity, was Ukraine's second major democratic uprising in a 10년—and the one that changed everything. Beginning in November 2013 when 대통령 Viktor Yanukovych abruptly rejected an association agreement with the 유럽연합 in favor of closer ties with Russia, the protests grew from a pro-European demonstration into a mass movement against corruption, authoritarianism, and Russian dominance. For three months, hundreds of thousands occupied Kyiv's Maidan Nezalezhnosti (독립 Square).[^1]

혁명 turned bloody in February 2014. Government snipers opened fire on protesters, killing over 100 people in what ~이 되었다 deadliest political 폭력 in 현대의 Ukrainian history. The victims are remembered as the "Heavenly Hundred" (Nebesnaya Sotnya). On February 22, 2014, Yanukovych fled to Russia, and 의회 voted to remove him from power. A new government oriented toward Europe took over.[^2]

But Russia's response was swift. Within weeks, unmarked Russian troops seized Crimea; a sham referendum followed, and Putin annexed the peninsula—the first forcible border change in Europe ~ 이래로 World 전쟁 II. Pro-Russian separatists, backed by Moscow, then launched an insurgency in eastern Ukraine. Euromaidan thus ignited the Russo-Ukrainian 전쟁, which in 2022 ~이 되었다 full-scale invasion. 혁명's legacy is being written in blood and resistance.[^3]

## Historical Background

### Ukraine's Orange Revolution (2004)

In 2004, massive protests overturned a fraudulent election:

- Viktor Yushchenko vs. Viktor Yanukovych
- Russian-backed Yanukovych initially declared winner
- Millions protested; new election; Yushchenko won
- But subsequent years disappointed: corruption continued; economy stagnated[^4]

### Yanukovych's Return (2010)

In 2010, Yanukovych won a fair election:

- Defeated Yulia Tymoshenko
- Imprisoned Tymoshenko on dubious charges
- Consolidated power; increased corruption
- Maneuvered between EU and Russia[^5]

### EU Association Agreement

For years, Ukraine negotiated an Association Agreement with the EU:

- Would deepen economic ties and require reforms
- Popular aspiration for European integration
- Russia pressured Ukraine to join Eurasian Union instead
- **November 21, 2013:** Yanukovych suspended the EU agreement

This triggered the protests.[^6]

## The Protests

### Phase 1: Euromaidan Begins (November 2013)

**November 21:** First protests at Maidan ~ 후에 EU deal suspended
**November 24:** Large march (~100,000)
**November 30:** Berkut riot police brutally attacked peaceful protesters at night—the "Rozgon" (dispersal). This 폭력 radicalized 운동.
**December 1:** 500,000+ marched; protesters occupied government buildings

The slogans shifted from "Sign the EU deal" to "Yanukovych must go."[^7]

### Phase 2: Occupation and Escalation (December 2013 - January 2014)

Maidan ~이 되었다 fortified protest camp:

- Self-organized; thousands slept on the square
- Tents, barricades, field kitchens
- Volunteer medical units
- Diverse participants: students, pensioners, nationalists, liberals
- 음악, speeches, solidarity

**January 16, 2014:** 의회 passed "Dictatorship Laws"—banning protests, restricting media. This fueled outrage.

**January 22, 2014:** First shooting deaths—Serhiy Nigoyan and others killed by security forces.[^8]

### Phase 3: The Bloodiest Days (February 2014)

**February 18-20:** Yanukovych ordered a massive assault:

- 폭력 erupted; protesters pushed toward government buildings
- Security forces used live ammunition
- **February 20: The Massacre**—snipers fired on crowds from rooftops
- 48+ protesters killed in hours

**February 21:** EU-mediated agreement signed—but Yanukovych had lost support
**February 22:** Yanukovych fled Kyiv; 의회 voted to remove him; protesters entered his abandoned residence and found evidence of lavish corruption[^9]

## The Heavenly Hundred

The 108 protesters killed between January and February 2014 are honored as the "Heavenly Hundred":

- Youngest: 16 years 나이든
- Oldest: 83 years 나이든
- Included Ukrainians, Belarusians, Armenians, Georgians

The term emerged ~ 동안 funeral ceremonies on Maidan. February 20 is now commemorated as Day of the Heavenly Hundred Heroes.[^10]

### The Snipers Question

**Ukrainian investigations:**

- Concluded Berkut (riot police) officers fired on protesters
- Forensic evidence matched bullets to Berkut rifles
- 27 Berkut officers identified
- 2023: Three convicted in absentia

**Contested narratives:**

- Some researchers (Ivan Katchanovski) argue some fire came from opposition-controlled buildings—a claim amplified by Russian propaganda but not supported by mainstream investigations
- Debate continues over command responsibility[^11]

## 여파

### Interim Government

- Yatsenyuk became interim 총리
- 의회 restored 2004 헌법 (limiting presidential power)
- 초기의 presidential election called
- **May 2014:** Petro Poroshenko elected

### Crimea Annexation

**Late February - March 2014:**

- Unmarked Russian troops (the "little green men") seized government buildings
- Ukrainian military bases blockaded
- **March 16:** Referendum under Russian occupation—96.77% for joining Russia (fabricated)
- **March 18:** Putin signed annexation decree

The first forcible change of European borders ~ 이래로 WWII.[^13]

### 전쟁 in Donbas

**April 2014:**

- Pro-Russian separatists seized buildings in eastern Ukraine
- "People's Republics" declared in Donetsk and Luhansk
- Russia provided weapons, troops, financing
- 전쟁 began; ~14,000 killed by 2022

### 2022 Full-Scale Invasion

The Russo-Ukrainian 전쟁, which began with Euromaidan's aftermath, escalated dramatically:

- **February 24, 2022:** Russia launched full-scale invasion
- Ongoing 전쟁; hundreds of thousands killed/wounded
- Ukraine fights for survival—invoking Maidan's legacy[^14]

## 역사적 평가

### Ukrainian/Western Perspective

**Revolution of Dignity:** Euromaidan was a genuine popular uprising for 민주주의, European values, and an end to corruption. Ukrainians chose their future ~에도 불구하고 deadly repression. The Heavenly Hundred are martyrs for 자유. Russian intervention proves the threat Ukraine faced was real.[^15]

**Unfinished Revolution:** Post-Maidan Ukraine has made progress (anti-corruption reforms, decentralization, EU candidacy) but problems remain. The 2022 invasion united 국가 like never ~ 전에.

### Russian Perspective

**Western-Backed Coup:** Russia portrays Euromaidan as an illegal overthrow of a legitimate 대통령, orchestrated by the US and EU. Far-right nationalists allegedly dominated 운동. Russia's actions in Crimea were defensive—protecting Russian speakers from a "fascist" government.

This narrative is rejected by most Western scholars and Ukrainians but shapes Russian policy.[^16]

### Critical Analysis

**Complexity:** 혁명 was genuinely popular but also included far-right elements (Right Sector, Svoboda). Their presence was exaggerated by Russian propaganda but existed. Post-Maidan governments struggled with corruption ~에도 불구하고 reforms.

**Consequences:** Euromaidan won 전투 but triggered a 전쟁 that continues. Ukraine's European aspirations are now paid for in blood.[^17]
