## 개요

Lord Elgin removed marble sculptures from the Parthenon in Athens, claiming Ottoman permission. Greece has demanded their return for 200 years; the British Museum refuses.

- Thomas Bruce, 7th Earl of Elgin, served as British 대사 to the Ottoman Empire.

## Why the Dispute Persists

- **Cultural patrimony:** Greece argues the sculptures are integral parts of a single monument (the Parthenon) and should be reunited in Athens.
- **Legal/administrative ambiguity:** The British Museum and UK-side arguments often emphasize that Elgin acted under lawful authority as understood at the time and that the Museum later acquired them.
- **Ethical standards changed:** 현대의 norms about archaeological context, heritage protection, and colonial power dynamics complicate "legal at the time" defenses.

## Common Arguments (Side-by-Side)

### Arguments for Return (Greece)

- Reunification: the Parthenon is a coherent architectural and artistic whole; separation distorts meaning.
- Local stewardship: Greece maintains facilities intended for proper conservation and interpretation.
- Historical 정의: the removal occurred under imperial asymmetry and should be remedied.

### Arguments Against Return (British Museum / UK-side)

- Universal museum framing: major museums present world cultures in comparative context.
- Conservation claim: a long-running argument is that the sculptures were preserved and made accessible.
- Precedent concern: returning them could trigger broad repatriation demands across many collections.

## Present-Day Context

- Greece has repeatedly requested repatriation (or long-term return) and has built dedicated museum infrastructure in Athens to display Acropolis antiquities.
- The British Museum’s position has historically been that it cannot permanently deaccession the sculptures under existing legal constraints.
- Public and scholarly debate remains active, and the issue is frequently cited as a leading case in global repatriation discussions.

## Quick Facts

- The sculptures date to the 5th 세기 BCE, from the period of Periclean building projects in Athens.
- The term "Elgin Marbles" is contested; many prefer "Parthenon Sculptures" to emphasize origin.
- The controversy is not only about ownership, but also about interpretation, context, and the ethics of acquisition.
