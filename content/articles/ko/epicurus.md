### 긍정적 평가

Scholars and defenders of Epicurus have emphasized his profound humanism, scientific outlook, and therapeutic approach to 철학. 고전적인 philologist Diskin Clay argues that Epicurus created "the most humane of ancient philosophical schools," centered on friendship and genuine concern for human flourishing rather than abstract metaphysical speculation.[^84] His materialism and rejection of supernatural causation are seen as anticipating the scientific worldview, earning him praise as a proto-과학자 who sought natural explanations for phenomena that others attributed to divine intervention.[^85]

### 부정적 평가

Critics of Epicurus, both 고대의 and 현대의, have raised substantial objections to his 철학 and its implications. Rationalist philosophers argue that relying exclusively on sensation cannot justify mathematical, logical, or metaphysical knowledge, and that Epicurus provided no adequate account of abstraction, reasoning, or scientific theory formation.[^102] His claim that all sensations are true seems to conflate sensory states with the propositions we form about them, leading to confusion about the nature of truth and error.[^103]

The ethical implications of Epicureanism have drawn fire from various quarters.
