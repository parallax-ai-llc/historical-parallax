## 개요

Soviet dictator whose totalitarian rule killed an estimated 6-20 million people through purges, forced collectivization, and the Gulag system. He ruled through terror from 1924 to 1953.

## 권력의 부상

- Stalin (born Ioseb Jughashvili) was a Bolshevik revolutionary who rose within the Communist Party apparatus.
- In 1922 he became General Secretary of the Communist Party, a position that allowed him to shape appointments and build a loyal power base.
- After Vladimir Lenin's death, Stalin outmaneuvered rivals inside the party leadership and consolidated authority.

## Domestic Policy and Repression

- Stalin oversaw rapid industrialization and the collectivization of agriculture, policies that were enforced through state coercion.
- The Soviet state expanded a vast security apparatus and a system of forced-labor camps (the Gulag).
- The Great Purge (including show trials, executions, and mass arrests) targeted real and perceived political opponents as well as broad segments of society.

## 제2차 세계대전 Leadership

- During 제2차 세계대전, Stalin became the central wartime leader of the 소련, directing the mobilization of the economy and armed forces.
- The 소련 played a decisive role in the defeat of Nazi 독일, at enormous human and material cost.

## Postwar Period and Legacy

- After 1945, the 소련 emerged as a superpower and established a sphere of influence in Eastern 유럽, contributing to the early 냉전 order.
- Stalin's death in 1953 was followed by power struggles and, later, official "de-Stalinization" that criticized aspects of his rule.
- Historical assessments emphasize both the USSR's transformation into a major industrial and military power and the extensive human suffering caused by state terror and policy-driven catastrophes.
