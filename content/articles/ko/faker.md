## Additional Context

Lee Sang-hyeok, known competitively as **Faker**, is most closely associated with the mid lane role and with long-term play for the SK Telecom T1 / T1 organization. His career is often presented as a case study in sustained elite performance in an esport with rapid strategic and mechanical evolution.

### Competitive Profile

- Known for a deep champion pool and the ability to adapt to changing game “metas.”
- Frequently described as combining mechanical precision with strong decision-making around objectives and team fights.
- Over multiple roster eras, he has been portrayed as both a central carry threat and a stabilizing veteran presence.

### Organization and Era

Faker’s rise coincided with the professionalization and global broadcast scale of League of Legends. T1’s championship runs helped define 초기의 international storylines, and later titles reinforced the narrative of longevity across competitive generations.

### Cultural and Industry Impact

- Widely used as a reference point for “GOAT” (greatest of all time) debates in esports.
- ~이 되었다 major figure in sponsorship and media, helping normalize esports celebrity beyond gaming audiences.
- In many retrospectives, his career is framed as evidence that consistent fundamentals (discipline, practice, adaptation) can outlast patch-to-patch volatility.

### Leadership Note

Team narratives often emphasize his in-game leadership and communication, especially in high-pressure international matches.
