## 개요

Sekarmadji Maridjan 카르토수위르요 (1905-1962) was an 인도네시아n 이슬람ic political leader who led the Darul 이슬람 rebellion against the 인도네시아n government from 1949 to 1962[^1]. He proclaimed the establishment of Negara 이슬람 인도네시아 (인도네시아n 이슬람ic State) on August 7, 1949, seeking to establish an 이슬람ic theocracy based on sharia law in opposition to the secular Pancasila ideology of the 인도네시아n Republic[^2]. A former medical student who became a protégé of nationalist leader Tjokroaminoto, 카르토수위르요 led one of the most prolonged and bloody insurgencies in post-independence 인도네시아n history before his capture and execution in 1962[^3]. His legacy remains controversial, viewed by some as a religious martyr and by others as a separatist rebel who threatened national unity[^4].

---

## 초기 생애와 배경

Sekarmadji Maridjan 카르토수위르요 was born on January 7, 1905, in Cepu, a small oil-producing town in North Central Java[^5]. He was one of seven siblings born to a family of modest means; his father, also named 카르토수위르요, worked as a mantri candu (opium overseer), a minor position within the Dutch colonial administration[^6]. Despite his humble origins, 카르토수위르요 received an education that was exceptional for indigenous 인도네시아ns of his generation, attending Dutch-medium schools that were typically reserved for the colonial elite and their select protégés[^7].

His early education began at the Tweede Inlandsche School, and after graduating, he was sent to Rembang to attend the Hollandsch Inlandsche School[^8]. This exposure to Western education would profoundly shape his worldview, providing him with the analytical tools and linguistic capabilities that would later serve him as a political writer and organizer. In his youth, 카르토수위르요 displayed considerable intellectual promise, which led him to pursue higher education at the prestigious Nederlands-Indische Artsen School (NIAS), the 네덜란드 Indies Medical College in Surabaya, beginning around 1923[^9].

During his time in Surabaya, 카르토수위르요's life took a decisive turn that would redirect his trajectory from medicine to politics. While attending medical school, he boarded at the house of Hadji Oemar Said Tjokroaminoto (1882-1934), one of the most prominent 이슬람ic nationalist leaders of the early twentieth century and a leading figure in Sarekat 이슬람[^10]. Tjokroaminoto's organization counted over two million members at its peak and represented the most powerful expression of 이슬람ic political consciousness in colonial 인도네시아[^11]. Living in Tjokroaminoto's household placed 카르토수위르요 at the epicenter of 인도네시아n nationalist ferment, where he encountered other future leaders of the independence movement, including the young Sukarno, who would later become 인도네시아's first president[^12].

The influence of Tjokroaminoto proved decisive. 카르토수위르요 became deeply involved in the activities of the Partai Sarekat 이슬람 인도네시아 (PSII), the political party that emerged from the earlier Sarekat 이슬람 movement[^13]. He abandoned his medical studies entirely to dedicate himself to political activism, a decision that reflected both his ideological commitment and his recognition that the struggle for 인도네시아n independence required full-time dedication[^14]. He became a regular writer for the SI newspaper Fadjar 아시아, where he demonstrated his commitment to 이슬람ic ideology through articles that criticized colonial policies, socio-economic injustices, police abuses of power, and Dutch claims of religious and political "neutrality"[^15]. His writings also addressed the international dimension of the nationalist struggle for merdeka (independence), revealing his awareness of global anti-colonial movements[^16].

카르토수위르요 participated in the famous Youth Pledge of 1928, a watershed moment in 인도네시아n nationalism that brought together diverse youth organizations to proclaim a unified 인도네시아n identity transcending ethnic and regional divisions[^17]. By the mid-1930s, his dedication and organizational abilities had elevated him to a high rank within the PSII[^18]. His political formation under Tjokroaminoto's mentorship during the 1920s would fundamentally shape his career in the service of political 이슬람, instilling in him the conviction that 이슬람ic principles should form the foundation of an independent 인도네시아n state[^19].

## 권력의 부상

카르토수위르요's ascent within the 이슬람ic nationalist movement accelerated following Tjokroaminoto's death in 1934. Together with Abikusno Tjokrosujoso, 카르토수위르요 assumed control of the PSII, and under their leadership, the party adopted a much more militant anti-colonial stance[^20]. This period marked the beginning of 카르토수위르요's gradual radicalization, as he became increasingly convinced that compromise with Dutch colonial authorities was both morally unacceptable and strategically counterproductive[^21]. His uncompromising views on non-cooperation with the colonial government made him a controversial and polarizing figure within the broader nationalist movement[^22].

In 1929, 카르토수위르요 relocated to Malangbong, a town between Garut and Tasikmalaya in West Java, where he intensively studied 이슬람 with local religious leaders including Kiai Jusuf Tauziri and Kiai Ardiwisastra[^23]. This immersion in traditional 이슬람ic learning complemented his earlier exposure to modernist 이슬람ic thought in urban centers. While in Malangbong, he met and married the daughter of a local PSII leader, establishing family ties that would anchor him in the region for the remainder of his life[^24]. He settled permanently in the Garut area and established a madrasa (이슬람ic school), positioning himself as both a political organizer and a religious educator[^25].

In 1937, 카르토수위르요 made the decisive break that would set him on the path toward armed rebellion. He resigned from the PSII to establish his own political movement explicitly advocating for a future 이슬람ic State of 인도네시아 based on 이슬람ic law[^26]. This split reflected his growing frustration with what he perceived as insufficient commitment to 이슬람ic principles within the mainstream nationalist movement. Throughout the late 1930s and early 1940s, 카르토수위르요 consistently advocated for an 이슬람ic state, seeking to implement Tjokroaminoto's teaching that 이슬람ic principles should be applied in the widest possible manner to all aspects of national life[^27].

The 일본ese occupation of the Dutch East Indies (1942-1945) provided 카르토수위르요 with new opportunities to build his organizational and military capacity. During this period, he established armed militias in the Garut area, one of many such groups that the 일본ese supported and armed to help resist any future Allied invasion[^28]. These militias would later form the core of his rebel forces. The 일본ese also created Hizbullah, an 이슬람ic youth volunteer force established on December 8, 1944, with an estimated 25,000 personnel consisting of Muslim youths organized as a reserve force for the Defenders of the Homeland (PETA)[^29]. Unlike PETA, which was under 일본ese Army command, Hizbullah's command structure lay with the Masyumi Party, giving 이슬람ic political leaders significant influence over armed formations[^30].

Following 인도네시아's Proclamation of Independence on August 17, 1945, 카르토수위르요 initially participated in the new Masyumi political party, serving as a charismatic leading figure alongside other 이슬람ic nationalists[^31]. Masyumi's chief aims were the achievement of 인도네시아n independence and the establishment of a state governed by 이슬람ic principles, and it played a dominant role in 인도네시아n politics from 1945 to 1949 alongside the 인도네시아n National Party (PNI) and the Socialist Party of 인도네시아 (PSI)[^32]. However, 카르토수위르요 became increasingly disillusioned with the Republican government's willingness to compromise with returning Dutch colonial forces and its adoption of the secular Pancasila ideology rather than 이슬람ic law as the foundation of the new state[^33].

The breaking point came with the Renville Agreement of January 17, 1948, an unsuccessful attempt to resolve disputes with the Dutch that required Republican forces to withdraw from much of West Java[^34]. Members of the Republican Army who had come from 이슬람ic militias like Hizbullah and Sabilillah felt betrayed by the 인도네시아n government for ratifying the agreement, which recognized many areas behind the van Mook Line as de jure Dutch territory[^35]. While the Republican government characterized this withdrawal as a strategic hijrah (migration) to preserve military strength for future struggle, 카르토수위르요 rejected this interpretation, arguing that such tactical retreat was not in the spirit of 이슬람ic jihad but rather represented an unacceptable compromise with colonial oppression[^36]. He chose to remain in West Java and continue guerrilla warfare against the occupying Dutch forces, even as official Republican forces withdrew[^37].

## 주요 업적

On August 7, 1949, in Cimambang, West Java, 카르토수위르요 proclaimed the establishment of the Negara 이슬람 인도네시아 (인도네시아n 이슬람ic State), with himself as Imam and commander-in-chief[^38]. This proclamation represented the culmination of his decades-long advocacy for an 이슬람ic state and marked the beginning of what would become a thirteen-year armed struggle against the 인도네시아n Republic. The declaration came at a moment when the Dutch colonial administration was collapsing and the Republican government was consolidating its control over the archipelago following the formal transfer of sovereignty in December 1949[^39]. 카르토수위르요's refusal to acknowledge Republican authority positioned him as the leader of the most significant armed opposition to the new 인도네시아n state[^40].

Under 카르토수위르요's leadership, Darul 이슬람 (the common name for his movement, meaning "House of 이슬람") developed a sophisticated organizational structure and ideology. He made 이슬람 the foundation of his political program, arguing that every political struggle must be grounded in political faith, which he equated with ideology and realism[^41]. The movement implemented the concept of hijrah (migration for faith) and jihad (struggle in the path of God) as fundamental organizing principles[^42]. 카르토수위르요's writings during this period revealed interesting contradictions in his intellectual orientation: on one hand, he was mystically inclined and deeply immersed in Sufism, which drew criticism from some of his Sarekat 이슬람 colleagues; on the other hand, he was a hard-nosed, ideologically fervent 이슬람ic politician with clear revolutionary objectives[^43].

During the 1950s, a weak central government and uncoordinated military response allowed Darul 이슬람 to flourish, eventually controlling approximately one-third of West Java[^44]. The movement launched raids that reached as far as the outskirts of Jakarta, demonstrating its military capabilities and the vulnerability of the Republican government[^45]. 카르토수위르요's success in maintaining this insurgency owed much to his understanding of rural Javanese society and his ability to mobilize peasant grievances against both colonial exploitation and Republican centralization. The rebellion spread beyond West Java to four other provinces, as 이슬람ic rebels in South Sulawesi and Aceh joined Darul 이슬람 and acknowledged 카르토수위르요 as their highest authority, though in practice there was little coordination between the rebels in different provinces[^46].

카르토수위르요 established governmental institutions within his 이슬람ic state, including 이슬람ic courts, taxation systems, and educational facilities based on religious instruction. He positioned himself not merely as a rebel leader but as the legitimate Imam of an alternative 인도네시아n state founded on 이슬람ic law rather than secular nationalism[^47]. In 1950, he sent a letter to President Sukarno and Prime Minister Mohammad Natsir, arguing that political 이슬람 represented the only effective weapon to fight against communism and proposing cooperation on this basis, though his proposal was ignored by the 인도네시아n government[^48]. This outreach demonstrated 카르토수위르요's view of himself as a legitimate political actor rather than merely an insurgent, and his conviction that the Republican government was making a strategic error in rejecting 이슬람ic governance.

The Darul 이슬람 movement under 카르토수위르요's leadership became the most bloody and intractable of the many regional conflicts that beset 인도네시아 during the 1950s and early 1960s[^49]. The insurgency's resilience demonstrated both the depth of support for 이슬람ic political alternatives in certain regions and the organizational and ideological coherence that 카르토수위르요 had managed to instill in his movement. His ability to sustain this rebellion for over a decade against an increasingly consolidated state represents a significant, if controversial, achievement in the context of post-colonial state formation.

## 논란과 비판

카르토수위르요's rebellion resulted in enormous human suffering and loss of life across multiple provinces of 인도네시아. The Darul 이슬람 insurgency was characterized by violent tactics that targeted both government forces and civilian populations, contributing to a death toll that numbered in the tens of thousands over the course of the thirteen-year conflict[^50]. As military pressure intensified and his movement became increasingly isolated, 카르토수위르요 declared "total war" in 1961, leading to an escalation in which Darul 이슬람 guerrillas increasingly employed terror tactics and banditry against civilians[^51]. These actions further alienated the rural populations that had previously provided the movement with shelter and support, undermining the rebellion's claim to represent genuine popular aspirations.

Critics, both during his lifetime and subsequently, accused 카르토수위르요 of placing religious ideology above national unity, thereby contributing to the fragmentation of the newly independent 인도네시아n state during its most vulnerable formative period. The rebellion diverted military resources and governmental attention away from economic development and nation-building at a critical juncture in 인도네시아n history[^52]. Secular nationalists viewed his rejection of the Pancasila ideology—which attempted to accommodate religious, nationalist, and socialist principles within a unified framework—as dangerously sectarian and incompatible with 인도네시아's religious and ethnic diversity[^53].

The official 인도네시아n historical narrative, constructed particularly during the New Order regime of President Suharto, characterized 카르토수위르요 as a separatist rebel and enemy of national unity rather than as an 이슬람ic nationalist leader[^54]. This displacement of his 이슬람ic motivations served nation-building purposes by portraying political 이슬람 as a negative and divisive force incompatible with 인도네시아n nationalism[^55]. The government's characterization emphasized the accusation that 카르토수위르요 had attempted to assassinate President Sukarno, for which he was convicted at his military court-martial[^56]. This charge reinforced the official narrative that positioned him as a traitor to the nation rather than as a political opponent with a competing vision of 인도네시아n statehood.

Many 인도네시아n nationalists, including former comrades from the independence struggle, viewed 카르토수위르요's rebellion as a betrayal of the unified anti-colonial movement. In his autobiography, Sukarno recalled their earlier friendship, noting that "in 1918 카르토수위르요 was a dear friend. We worked side by side with Tjokro [Cokroaminoto] for our country. In the '20s in Bandung we lived, ate, and dreamed together"[^57]. This personal dimension to their later antagonism added a tragic element to the conflict between the 이슬람ic state and the secular Republic, suggesting that what emerged as a fundamental ideological divide had not been predetermined but resulted from choices made during the tumultuous post-independence period[^58].

The brutality employed by both sides in the conflict—government forces as well as Darul 이슬람 fighters—contributed to cycles of violence and reprisal that devastated communities throughout West Java and other affected regions. The "fence of legs" strategy introduced by the 인도네시아n military in 1957, which involved encircling guerrilla mountain bases and cutting off supply and escape routes, forced rebels to surrender or face annihilation[^59]. While militarily effective, such tactics also resulted in significant hardship for civilian populations caught between the two sides.

## 개인 생활

카르토수위르요's personal life remained largely subordinated to his political and religious mission, with available historical sources focusing primarily on his public role rather than his private affairs. What is known suggests a man whose personal choices were consistently guided by his ideological commitments and whose family relationships were intertwined with his political network.

His marriage to the daughter of a local PSII leader in Malangbong reflects the typical pattern of political marriages within 인도네시아's 이슬람ic nationalist movement, cementing alliances between different nodes of the organizational network while simultaneously integrating him into the social fabric of rural West Java[^60]. His wife remained with him throughout the insurgency; when 인도네시아n forces captured 카르토수위르요 at his hideout on Mount Geber in June 1962, his wife and personal commander, Kurnia, were captured alongside him[^61]. This detail suggests her enduring loyalty and her willingness to share the hardships and dangers of his fugitive existence during the final years of the rebellion.

Evidence suggests that 카르토수위르요 had children, as one of his grandsons has been documented describing him vaguely as an "intellectual Muslim"[^62]. However, specific information about the number and names of his children, their experiences during the rebellion, or their subsequent fates after his execution remains largely absent from available historical sources. This absence reflects both the deliberate obscuring of connections to a figure officially designated as a rebel and traitor, and the broader pattern in which 인도네시아n historiography has focused on 카르토수위르요's political role rather than his personal relationships.

His intellectual character combined mystical spirituality with political rigor. Contemporaries noted his immersion in Sufism, which drew criticism from some Sarekat 이슬람 colleagues who favored a more rationalist approach to 이슬람ic reform[^63]. This mystical orientation coexisted with his reputation as a hard-nosed political organizer and ideologue, suggesting a complex personality that integrated spiritual depth with practical revolutionary commitment[^64]. His establishment of a madrasa in Malangbong indicates that he saw himself not merely as a political leader but also as an educator responsible for transmitting 이슬람ic knowledge to future generations[^65].

The final years of 카르토수위르요's life were spent in hiding, moving between mountain hideouts as 인도네시아n military pressure intensified. The declaration of martial law in 1957 and the establishment of Guided Democracy by Sukarno in 1959 marked a turning point that strengthened the government's counter-insurgency capabilities[^66]. By the time of his capture in 1962, 카르토수위르요 was an aging rebel leader whose movement had been systematically dismantled. In captivity, he issued orders for his followers to surrender, suggesting either pragmatic recognition that further resistance was futile or a desire to prevent additional bloodshed[^67].

There were reports that President Sukarno wept when informed that his former friend had been executed by firing squad on September 5, 1962[^68]. If true, this detail adds poignant complexity to their relationship, suggesting that despite their profound political antagonism, some emotional connection persisted from their earlier shared struggles in the nationalist movement.

## 유산

The legacy of Sekarmadji Maridjan 카르토수위르요 remains deeply contested, reflecting ongoing debates about the role of 이슬람 in 인도네시아n politics and the contested narratives of the nation's founding period. His transformation from nationalist leader to "separatist rebel" and, for some, into a "martyr for 이슬람," illustrates how historical memory is constructed and reconstructed according to changing political circumstances[^69].

In official 인도네시아n historiography, particularly during the New Order regime (1966-1998), 카르토수위르요 was portrayed primarily as a separatist who threatened national unity, with his 이슬람ic motivations systematically downplayed or dismissed[^70]. This narrative displacement served the state-building project by characterizing political 이슬람 as inherently divisive and positioning the secular Pancasila ideology as the only legitimate foundation for 인도네시아n national identity[^71]. The elision of 이슬람 from reconstructed accounts of 카르토수위르요's intentions allowed for two reconstitutive processes: one that portrayed political 이슬람 negatively, and another that could paradoxically transform 카르토수위르요 into a martyr figure for those who rejected the official narrative[^72].

Even though the Negara 이슬람 인도네시아 and Darul 이슬람 were crushed by the 인도네시아n military more than half a century ago, the movement's ideological legacy persisted. Some splinter groups and cells of the NII continue to remain active underground[^73]. More significantly, veterans of Darul 이슬람 later helped establish Jemaah 이슬람iyah (JI), the still-extant regional militant network that evolved from the long-established Darul 이슬람 movement into an organization with links to Al-Qaeda and strongly influenced by Osama bin Laden's ideology[^74]. The connection between 카르토수위르요's movement and later terrorist organizations became tragically concrete with the 2002 Bali bombings and other devastating attacks conducted by Jemaah 이슬람iyah[^75].

The ideological and organizational connections linking Darul 이슬람 to Jemaah 이슬람iyah operated through intermediate phases. Following 카르토수위르요's execution in 1962, his ideas were reinterpreted by subsequent leaders, resulting in the emergence of the Komando Jihad movement of the 1970s and 1980s, led by former Darul 이슬람 members[^76]. Key figures like Abdullah Sungkar and Abu Bakar Bashir, who became widely known as leaders of Jemaah 이슬람iyah, emerged during this period, demonstrating the continuity of networks and ideological frameworks across generations[^77]. This trajectory from 카르토수위르요's anti-colonial 이슬람ic nationalism to contemporary jihadist terrorism represents one of the most controversial aspects of his legacy, though scholars debate the extent to which he bears responsibility for these later developments that occurred decades after his death.

In post-1998 인도네시아, following the fall of Suharto and the democratic reformation era, reassessments of 카르토수위르요 have become possible. Recently, he and Darul 이슬람 have become heroic symbols of local 이슬람ist struggle for some constituencies, offering an alternative historical vision to the secular nationalist narrative[^78]. Scholarly perspectives have also evolved significantly. While earlier scholarship between 1949 and the 1980s largely rejected the religious claims of the Darul 이슬람 movement, interpreting the antagonism as primarily a fight for power, self-assertion, or land rights, more recent scholars have challenged these interpretations[^79]. Historian Chiara Formichi and others have criticized the failure of earlier scholars like van Dijk and Jackson to take serious consideration of the role of 이슬람 in the movement, arguing that such approaches failed to understand 카르토수위르요 on his own terms[^80].

Contemporary historical inquiry has revealed a more complex figure—neither simply a separatist rebel nor purely a religious martyr, but rather a politician whose legacy has been shaping the role of 이슬람 in 인도네시아n politics for over fifty years[^81]. The contingencies of the past that transformed a nationalist leader into multiple contested symbols continue to influence debates about 인도네시아n national identity, the relationship between religion and state, and the legitimacy of different political visions for the nation[^82]. 카르토수위르요's rebellion contributed to stricter government measures against separatist movements and influenced 인도네시아's approach to handling religious and political dissent throughout the subsequent decades[^83].

## 역사적 평가

### 긍정적 견해

Recent scholarship, particularly the work of historian Chiara Formichi, has rehabilitated aspects of 카르토수위르요's historical reputation by taking his 이슬람ic motivations seriously rather than dismissing them as mere pretexts for power struggles or regional grievances[^84]. This revisionist approach argues that Darul 이슬람 was, first and foremost, an 인도네시아n nationalist and anti-colonial movement dedicated to the full independence of the new nation, with 이슬람 providing the frameworks, ideas, and inspirations for its institutional and ideological development[^85]. From this perspective, 카르토수위르요 represented a legitimate alternative vision of 인도네시아n statehood that deserved serious consideration rather than dismissal as separatist rebellion.

Supporters and sympathetic scholars emphasize that 카르토수위르요 remained consistent throughout his life in advocating for the implementation of 이슬람ic principles in governance, a position he developed under the tutelage of Tjokroaminoto and maintained through the turbulent decades of colonial resistance and post-independence conflict[^86]. His establishment of schools, courts, and administrative structures within Darul 이슬람-controlled territories demonstrated a commitment to building functional 이슬람ic governance rather than mere destructive opposition[^87]. His 1936 pamphlet distinguishing between positive jihad (jihad of the tongue and heart, jihad al-akbar led by the Imam) and negative, destructive jihad (jihad of the sword, jihad al-asghar) reveals a more nuanced understanding of 이슬람ic struggle than the crude militant characterization suggests[^88].

The view of 카르토수위르요 as an "intellectual Muslim," as articulated by his grandson, reflects an interpretation that emphasizes his educational background, his prolific writing in 이슬람ic newspapers, his theological studies with traditional religious scholars in Malangbong, and his role as an educator who established madrasas[^89]. This perspective presents him as a religious intellectual who translated 이슬람ic principles into a political program, rather than as a mere military insurgent. His continued resistance against Dutch forces even after the Republican withdrawal from West Java in 1948 can be interpreted as principled anti-colonialism rather than opposition to 인도네시아n independence itself[^90].

Some analysts argue that the Republican government's rejection of 카르토수위르요's 1950 proposal to cooperate against communism represented a missed opportunity for national reconciliation and demonstrated the secular nationalist elite's unwillingness to accommodate 이슬람ic political aspirations[^91]. From this viewpoint, the subsequent thirteen years of conflict resulted partly from the rigidity of the Republican leadership rather than solely from 카르토수위르요's intransigence. The personal dimension of the conflict, particularly Sukarno's reported tears at 카르토수위르요's execution, suggests recognition even from his opponents of the tragic nature of this fratricidal struggle between former comrades in the independence movement[^92].

### 부정적 견해

The dominant assessment of 카르토수위르요 in 인도네시아n historiography and among secular nationalists emphasizes the destructive consequences of his rebellion for national unity and development. Critics argue that his refusal to accept the democratic consensus embodied in the 1945 Constitution and Pancasila ideology represented a fundamental rejection of 인도네시아's pluralistic character[^93]. In a nation encompassing extraordinary religious, ethnic, and cultural diversity, his insistence on establishing an 이슬람ic state based on sharia law would have relegated non-Muslims to second-class citizenship and potentially fractured the archipelago into competing religious polities[^94].

The enormous human cost of the Darul 이슬람 rebellion—tens of thousands of deaths over thirteen years—represents its most damning indictment. Scholars like van Dijk have emphasized the frustration of regional military commanders and popular discontent with agrarian reforms and political centralization as primary motivations, suggesting that religious ideology served primarily as mobilizing rhetoric for more mundane grievances[^95]. The escalation to "total war" in 1961 and the increasing use of terror tactics and banditry against civilians undermines claims that the movement represented principled 이슬람ic governance, revealing instead the desperation of a failing insurgency[^96].

카르토수위르요's conviction for attempted assassination of President Sukarno, whether historically accurate or not, positioned him in official narratives as not merely a political opponent but as a would-be assassin who sought to destroy the legitimate leadership of the nation[^97]. His execution following a military court-martial was thus presented as appropriate punishment for rebellion and attempted murder rather than as political persecution of an ideological rival. The characterization of 카르토수위르요 as an enemy of the state who rejected the diplomatic and political processes that other 이슬람ic parties like Masyumi had engaged with reinforced this negative assessment[^98].

The connection between Darul 이슬람 and later terrorist organizations, particularly Jemaah 이슬람iyah, has profoundly colored contemporary negative assessments of 카르토수위르요's legacy. While he died in 1962, decades before the rise of global jihadism, the organizational networks and ideological frameworks he established provided foundations that later militant 이슬람ists built upon[^99]. The 2002 Bali bombings and other terrorist attacks conducted by groups claiming descent from his movement have associated his name with contemporary religious violence in the minds of many 인도네시아ns and international observers[^100]. Critics argue that even if 카르토수위르요 himself did not advocate the indiscriminate terrorism practiced by his ideological descendants, his uncompromising rejection of secular nationalism and his sanctification of armed jihad established precedents that facilitated later extremism.

Analysts who emphasize material and political motivations over religious ideology argue that 카르토수위르요's movement exploited religious sentiment to pursue fundamentally secular objectives of power and regional autonomy. This interpretation, dominant in earlier scholarship, characterized the religious rhetoric as instrumental rather than sincere, suggesting that 카르토수위르요 manipulated 이슬람ic symbols to mobilize support for what was essentially a rebellion against central government authority[^101]. While recent scholarship has challenged this reductionist approach, it remains influential in shaping negative assessments of his motivations and legacy.
