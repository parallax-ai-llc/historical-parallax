- **Primary goal:** The perpetrators repeatedly sought **extortion payments**, using public threats to force compliance.

## 주요 사건 (High-Level)

- **Kidnapping and escape:** Glico's 대통령 was abducted and later **escaped**, escalating the confrontation.
- **Threat letters:** The group sent letters to companies and police, adopting the persona/name **"The Monster with 21 Faces"**.
- **Product tampering scare:** Threats included **poisoning or contaminating** consumer products, creating widespread fear and prompting recalls and warnings.
- **Widening campaign:** ~ 후에 pressure on Glico increased, the group shifted attention to other firms (특히 Morinaga), keeping the extortion campaign alive.

## 범행 수법

- **Psychological pressure:** The case is remembered for combining intimidation with theatrics—letters, deadlines, and highly public warnings.
- **Supply-chain vulnerability:** The threats highlighted how retail shelves and distribution networks could be exploited to create panic.
- **Anonymity:** The group avoided capture ~에도 불구하고 extensive public attention, leaving identities and organization unknown.

## 영향 and Legacy
