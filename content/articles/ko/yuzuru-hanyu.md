## 요약

Yuzuru Hanyu won consecutive Olympic golds in 2014 and 2018, setting 19 world records. He was the first to land a quad loop.

## 경력 연표

| 연도 | 사건 |
|------|-------|
| 2014 | Won Olympic gold at Sochi |
| 2018 | Won second gold at PyeongChang |
| 2022 | Retired from competitive skating |

## 배경

- Hanyu trained in Sendai and was part of the generation that helped drive a global boom in men’s figure skating difficulty.
- He is also closely associated with the 2011 Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami period, which affected his home region during his development years.

## Competitive Profile

- His competitive reputation combines high-difficulty jumps with musical interpretation and distinctive program construction.
- He is frequently cited as a leading figure in the transition to the “quad era,” where multiple quadruple jumps became standard for top contenders.

## 영향 and Public Recognition

- In Japan, he became one of the most prominent modern athletes, drawing large audiences and significant media attention.
- Internationally, he is often discussed as an athlete whose results and style influenced judging-era trends and rival training strategies.

## After Competitive Retirement

- After stepping away from competition, he has performed in professional ice shows and other exhibition formats.
- His post-competitive work is commonly framed as extending the sport’s audience beyond the competitive calendar.

## Key Takeaways

- His career is commonly referenced as a blend of technical innovation and performance-driven artistry.
- Two consecutive Olympic titles placed him in a rare historical category within men’s singles.
- His popularity helped broaden mainstream attention to figure skating in Japan and abroad.
