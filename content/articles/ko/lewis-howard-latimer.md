## Why He Matters

- Latimer was a key late-19th-century electrical pioneer whose work helped move electric lighting from experimental demonstrations toward scalable, manufactured products.
- He is frequently noted for bridging invention and production: detailed mechanical drawings, patent work, and practical improvements that made devices easier to build and maintain.

## 초기 생애와 배경

- Born in Chelsea, Massachusetts (1848), Latimer grew up in the 보스턴 area and is commonly described as largely self-taught in technical drafting.
- He worked as an office boy and later as a draftsman; this path—documentation, drawings, and patent preparation—became central to his engineering career.

## Major Technical Contributions (High Level)

### Drafting and Patent Work

- Latimer produced technical drawings and assisted with patent-related documentation in an era when precise drawings were essential for protecting and communicating inventions.
- He is widely associated with drafting work connected to the early telephone period and later to electric lighting.

### Electric Lighting and Manufacturing

- Latimer is commonly credited with improvements related to carbon-filament electric lamps and with work on manufacturing processes that supported more reliable, longer-lasting bulbs.
- He also contributed to the broader ecosystem around electric light systems (not only the bulb, but also the practical steps required for production and deployment).
