## Additional Context

- **Model organism approach:** Kandel is well known for using the sea slug **Aplysia** to study learning and memory. Its relatively simple nervous system allowed researchers to connect behavioral learning to identifiable neurons and synapses.
- **Synaptic plasticity:** A central theme in his work is that memory formation involves changes in **synaptic strength** (plasticity), with different mechanisms contributing to short-term versus long-term memory.
- **Molecular mechanisms:** Widely cited summaries describe how biochemical signaling cascades and gene expression changes can stabilize long-term synaptic changes, linking psychology-level concepts (learning) to cellular and molecular processes.
- **Nobel Prize (2000):** The 2000 노벨상 in Physiology or 의학 recognized discoveries concerning **signal transduction in the nervous system**. Kandel shared the prize with **Arvid Carlsson** and **Paul Greengard**.

## Why It Matters

Kandel’s research helped shape 현대의 neuroscience by:

- Providing an experimental bridge between **behavior**, **neurons**, and **molecules**.
- Helping establish synaptic plasticity as a foundational mechanism for **learning and memory**.
- Influencing approaches in **psychiatry and neurology** that look for molecular explanations and interventions.
