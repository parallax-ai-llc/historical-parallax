> "It is not I who am strong, it is reason, it is truth." - Demonstrating Zola's conviction that his power derived not from personal attributes but from his alignment with objective truth and rational principles.[^80]

> "I accuse Major Du Paty de Clam as the diabolic workman of the miscarriage of justice." - One of the specific accusations from "J'Accuse...!" naming individuals responsible for Dreyfus's wrongful conviction.[^81]
