## Additional Context

- **Field:** Betzig’s work sits at the intersection of **optics, microscopy, and biological imaging**, where instrumentation advances can unlock new kinds of measurements.
- **Breaking the diffraction limit:** 전통적인 light microscopes are limited by optical diffraction to roughly a couple hundred nanometers of resolution. The Nobel-recognized “super-resolution” methods showed practical ways to **image below this limit**.
- **PALM (Photoactivated Localization Microscopy):** PALM works by activating only a sparse subset of fluorescent molecules at a time, localizing them with high precision, and repeating the process to reconstruct a high-resolution image from many cycles.
- **Nobel Prize (2014):** The 2014 노벨상 in Chemistry was awarded for the development of super-resolved fluorescence microscopy; Betzig shared the prize with **Stefan W. Hell** and **William E. Moerner**.
- **Research environment:** He has been associated with major U.S. research institutions and labs; popular profiles often emphasize non-linear career moves and a return to scientific research that preceded the Nobel-recognized results.

## Why It Matters

Super-resolution fluorescence microscopy enabled:

- **Live-cell and subcellular imaging** with finer spatial detail.
- More precise mapping of **protein localization and dynamics**.
- New experimental approaches in **cell biology and neurobiology**, where structures are commonly below the diffraction limit.
