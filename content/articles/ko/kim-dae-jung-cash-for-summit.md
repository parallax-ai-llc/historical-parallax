## 개요

President 김대중's government transferred $500 million to 북한 through Hyundai Group to arrange the first inter-Korean summit. Several officials were convicted.

## 배경

The "cash-for-summit" scandal emerged from efforts to arrange the first inter-Korean summit in 2000, during 대한민국n President 김대중's 햇볕 정책 era. The policy emphasized engagement, economic cooperation, and reduced tensions with 북한.

## Alleged payment and channels

Investigations found that large sums were transferred to 북한 through corporate and financial intermediaries connected to the Hyundai Group. Supporters framed the transfers as part of broader economic cooperation needed to open talks, while critics argued they were a covert inducement for the summit.

The controversy centered on transparency, the use of public or private funds, and whether the arrangement violated laws governing inter-Korean transactions.

## Legal and political consequences

Several 대한민국n officials and business figures were investigated and some were convicted. The scandal complicated public debate over the 햇볕 정책: it did not erase the diplomatic significance of the summit, but it became a persistent critique of how engagement was conducted.

## Significance

The case is frequently cited in 대한민국n politics as a high-profile example of the tension between strategic diplomacy and domestic accountability. It also shaped later discussions about the governance of inter-Korean projects and the role of large conglomerates in national strategy.
