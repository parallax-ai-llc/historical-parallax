## Modus Operandi and Investigation

Public reporting typically characterizes Seo Chang-deok as an offender who targeted women over an extended period and across multiple incidents. As with many serial-murder investigations, identifying a consistent pattern and linking cases can take years due to:

- Limited forensic evidence in older cases
- Geographic dispersion of incidents
- Delays in reporting or discovery
- Evolving investigative methods over time

## Arrest and Prosecution

After arrest, the case moved through South Korea's criminal justice process, including interrogation, indictment, and trial. High-profile serial murder cases often involve:

- Verification of confessions against independent evidence
- Reconstruction of timelines for each alleged incident
- Evaluation of motive and opportunity
- Sentencing determinations based on the full set of proven crimes
