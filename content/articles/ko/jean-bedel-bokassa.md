## 개요
Jean-Bédel Bokassa was a Central African military officer and political leader who ruled the Central African Republic from 1966, later declaring himself emperor of the Central African Empire. His rule is remembered for authoritarian governance, extravagance, and allegations of severe human-rights abuses.

## Rise and rule
Bokassa seized power in a 1965–1966 coup and consolidated authority through a one-party system and a strong security apparatus. In 1976 he proclaimed the Central African Empire and, in 1977, held an elaborate coronation that drew global attention.

## International relations and downfall
His government maintained close ties with France for much of his rule, though relations became strained amid reports of abuses and domestic unrest. In 1979, he was removed from power during a French-supported operation following intensified controversy and instability.

## Trials and historical assessment
Bokassa later returned to the country and faced trial, receiving convictions on multiple charges; some sentences were later reduced or altered. Historians and commentators commonly treat his reign as an emblematic case of postcolonial authoritarianism, personalist rule, and the political consequences of weak institutions.
