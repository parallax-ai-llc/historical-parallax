## 개요
Javed Iqbal murdered 100 boys in Lahore, Pakistan between 1998 and 1999, dissolving their bodies in acid. He confessed by letter to a newspaper. Sentenced to a symbolic death mirroring his crimes, he was found dead in his cell in 2001 before execution.

## 초기 생애
Born October 8, 1956, in Lahore, into a middle-class business family. He ran an electronics business. He had prior charges for sexual abuse of minors that didn't result in conviction. He claimed motivation from being assaulted in police custody. [^1]

## 범죄 경력
Between 1998 and 1999, Iqbal murdered 100 street children in Lahore, luring them with food and money. After strangling them, he dissolved bodies in hydrochloric acid and dumped remains in the Ravi River. He kept photographs and records of each victim. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
He sent a confession letter and evidence to a newspaper in December 1999, then surrendered to a journalist rather than police. In March 2000, he was sentenced to death by strangulation and dismemberment. Found dead in his cell October 7, 2001, in apparent suicide. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- Brought attention to vulnerability of street children in Pakistan [^4]
- Led to calls for child protection laws
- Prompted discussion of police and welfare system reform

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered 100 boys, primarily homeless street children [^5]
- Dissolved victims in acid
- Targeted the most marginalized children
- Previous abuse charges were not properly pursued
- Exposed catastrophic child protection failures
