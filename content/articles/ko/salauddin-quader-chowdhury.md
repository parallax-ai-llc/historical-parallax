## 요약

Bangladeshi politician convicted of genocide during the 1971 Liberation War. He assisted Pakistani forces in targeting Hindu minorities.

## 추가 맥락

### Political Career (High-Level)

- Salauddin Quader Chowdhury was a Bangladeshi politician commonly associated with the **Bangladesh Nationalist Party (BNP)** and served as a member of parliament in multiple terms (as reported in major summaries).
- In Bangladeshi politics, he was often described as a prominent and sometimes polarizing figure.

### 1971 Liberation War Allegations

- The charges centered on alleged collaboration with Pakistani forces during the **Bangladesh Liberation War (1971)**.
- Public reporting often frames the allegations as involving violence against civilians, including persecution of minority communities.

### Tribunal, Appeals, and Controversy

- His conviction is associated with proceedings of Bangladesh’s **International Crimes Tribunal (ICT)**, established to prosecute war crimes related to 1971.
- The ICT process has been the subject of domestic and international debate, with criticism and support discussed in Bangladeshi and global media.
- Chowdhury reportedly maintained his innocence and pursued legal appeals; courts ultimately upheld the sentence.

### Execution and Aftermath

- He was executed in **2015**, an event widely covered as part of a broader set of ICT-related executions.
- The case remains politically and emotionally charged in Bangladesh, reflecting the continuing national significance of the 1971 war and accountability efforts.
