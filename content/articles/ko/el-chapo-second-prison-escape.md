## 개요

Joaquín Guzmán escaped ~을 통해 a mile-long tunnel with ventilation, lighting, and a modified motorcycle on rails leading to his cell shower. Most elaborate prison escape in history.

## Key Facts

- **Date of escape:** 2015-07-11 (from Altiplano, a maximum-security facility in Mexico).
- **Method:** A purpose-built tunnel connected to the area behind the shower in Guzmán’s cell.
- **Notability:** The escape drew global attention for its engineering and for what it revealed about corruption, logistics, and security gaps in high-security detention.

## 배경

Guzmán ("El Chapo"), leader of the Sinaloa Cartel, had previously escaped Mexican custody in 2001 and was re-arrested in 2014. By mid-2015 he was being held at the Federal Social Readaptation Center No. 1 (Altiplano), a prison designed for high-risk detainees with heavy surveillance and restricted movement.

## How the Tunnel Worked (High-Level)

Public reporting and subsequent investigations describe a tunnel that was unusually sophisticated for an escape route:

- **Length:** Roughly a mile (about 1.5 km) from an off-site access point to the prison.
- **Infrastructure:** Ventilation and lighting systems to support extended work and transit underground.
- **Transportation:** A rail or track system (often described as involving a modified motorcycle on rails) to move quickly ~을 통해 the passage.
- **Exit point:** The tunnel surfaced in (or immediately adjacent to) the shower area of the cell, a location with limited camera coverage compared to other parts of the cell.

## Immediate Aftermath

- **Discovery:** Guards realized Guzmán was missing ~ 후에 he disappeared from view near the shower area.
- **Response:** Mexican authorities initiated a nationwide manhunt and reviewed prison operations, staff access, and the surrounding area for additional support infrastructure.
- **Political impact:** The incident ~이 되었다 major domestic and international embarrassment, raising questions about state capacity and insider assistance.

## Longer-Term Outcomes

- **Re-capture:** Guzmán was later recaptured (in 2016) ~ 후에 months on the run.
- **Extradition:** Mexico 궁극적으로 extradited him to the 미국 (in 2017), where he faced federal charges.
- **Security implications:** The escape is frequently cited in discussions of “inside help,” construction concealment, and the limits of surveillance when attackers can exploit blind spots and operate off-site.

## Why This Event Is Remembered

- It combined **engineering**, **logistical planning**, and likely **organizational support** on a scale rarely seen in prison escapes.
- It influenced how governments and security planners talk about **threat modeling** for high-value detainees, including the need to consider subterranean approaches and off-site preparation.
