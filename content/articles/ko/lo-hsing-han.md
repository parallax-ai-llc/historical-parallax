## 개요
로싱한 was a Burmese drug lord who was one of the largest opium and heroin producers in the Golden Triangle. He used his drug profits to become a legitimate businessman in 미얀마, building one of the country's largest conglomerates. He was described as the 'Godfather of Heroin.'

## 초기 생애
Born around 1935 in Kokang, northern Shan State, Burma (미얀마). He was of ethnic Chinese origin. He gained control of a local militia and used his military position to enter the opium trade. [^1]

## 범죄 경력
로싱한 became one of the most powerful opium warlords in the Golden Triangle during the 1960s and 1970s. He led the Ka Kwe Ye militia, ostensibly fighting communist insurgents but primarily involved in the opium trade. After being pardoned by the 미얀마 military, he built the 아시아 World conglomerate, one of the country's largest companies. [^2]

## 체포 / 몰락
로싱한 was arrested by the Thai government in 1973 and sentenced to death for drug trafficking. He was extradited to Burma in 1980 and pardoned by the military government. He then transitioned into legitimate business. He died of natural causes on July 6, 2013, in Yangon. The US sanctioned him and his family's businesses. [^3]

## 긍정적 관점
- Invested in 미얀마's economic development through 아시아 World [^4]
- Built infrastructure including ports and highways
- His transition to legitimate business was seen as a model by some

## 부정적 관점
- Was one of the world's largest heroin producers [^5]
- His drugs caused widespread addiction globally
- Used military connections to protect his drug operations
- His 'legitimate' businesses were built on drug money
- Escaped justice through political connections
