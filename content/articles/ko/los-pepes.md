## 개요
로스 페페스 (People Persecuted by Pablo Escobar) was a vigilante group that targeted Escobar's associates during 1992-93. Allegedly backed by the Cali Cartel and aided by 콜롬비아n security forces. They killed hundreds of Escobar associates.

## 배경
- **Origin of the name**: “로스 페페스” is commonly expanded as *Perseguidos por Pablo Escobar* (“People Persecuted by Pablo Escobar”), framing the group as victims or avengers of Escobar’s violence.
- **Context**: The group emerged during the final phase of the state’s pursuit of Pablo Escobar, when violence between cartels, the government, and armed actors escalated in 콜롬비아.

## Formation and Composition (high-level)
- Reported to be a **loose network** rather than a single formal organization.
- Frequently described as including **former Medellín Cartel associates**, **paramilitary-linked actors**, and **rivals of Escobar**, though the exact membership is disputed.
- Many accounts describe **financial and operational support** from Escobar’s enemies, especially rival trafficking groups.

## Operations and Tactics (1992–1993)
- Targeted **Escobar’s infrastructure**: lawyers, accountants, messengers, and suspected collaborators.
- Conducted **intimidation campaigns** (e.g., threats, pamphlets, and “lists”) aimed at isolating Escobar.
- Reported involvement in **killings and property destruction** against individuals believed to be tied to Escobar’s network.

## Alleged Links to Other Actors
- Multiple journalistic and historical accounts allege **coordination or deconfliction** with elements of 콜롬비아n security forces during the hunt for Escobar.
- Other accounts emphasize the role of **rival cartels** (notably the Cali Cartel) in enabling 로스 페페스’ reach.
- These claims remain **contested**, with varying degrees of evidentiary support depending on the source.

## Impact and Aftermath
- Contributed to **pressure on Medellín Cartel remnants** by accelerating defections and fear among associates.
- The campaign is often discussed as an example of **extrajudicial violence** intersecting with state counter-cartel efforts.
- After Escobar’s death in 1993, 로스 페페스 as a named entity is generally described as **dissipating**, while some participants are alleged to have migrated into other armed or criminal structures.

## Why It Matters (historical framing)
- 로스 페페스 is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - the **blurring of lines** between law enforcement, intelligence, and illicit armed actors,
  - the ethics and consequences of **vigilante/paramilitary collaboration** against a common enemy,
  - and how cartels leveraged **proxy violence** to reshape the competitive landscape.

## Ethical and legal debates (high level)
- In many countries, vigilantism and paramilitary action raise questions about:
  - due process and the rule of law,
  - civilian harm and collective punishment,
  - and long-run institutional consequences when non-state violence is tolerated.
- Discussions of 로스 페페스 often sit inside broader debates about whether “ends justify means” in counter-cartel campaigns.

## Notes on Sources
- Public narratives rely heavily on **journalistic reporting**, **testimony**, and **retrospective investigations**, which can conflict.
- For a careful read, compare **multiple independent accounts** and distinguish between documented events and attribution claims.
