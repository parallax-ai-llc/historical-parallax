## 개요

ISIS-inspired suicide bombers attacked three churches and three luxury hotels in Sri Lanka on Easter Sunday killing 269 people. The attacks were carried out by local jihadist group NTJ.

## Major attack locations

- Widely reported targets included churches in Colombo, Negombo, and Batticaloa, as well as hotels frequented by international guests.
- The attacks were timed to coincide with Easter services, maximizing casualties and psychological impact.

## Security and political consequences

- The bombings prompted a national security crackdown and renewed focus on intelligence coordination across Sri Lankan agencies.
- Public debate also centered on whether prior warnings were missed or mishandled, and on the balance between civil liberties and emergency powers.

## Long-term legacy

- Survivors’ accounts and memorial services have kept the events in public memory, with ongoing calls for victim support and accountability.
