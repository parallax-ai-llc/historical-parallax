## 개요

Karl Heinrich Marx (1818-1883) was a German philosopher, economist, historian, and revolutionary socialist whose ideas fundamentally shaped modern political and economic thought. His critique of capitalism, developed in *Das Kapital* and with Friedrich Engels in *The Communist Manifesto*, argued that capitalism alienates workers, exploits their labor, and will inevitably be overthrown by proletarian revolution leading to socialism and ultimately communism—a classless society. While regimes claiming Marxist inspiration (소련, Maoist 중국) resulted in millions of deaths, Marx's analysis of class conflict, ideology, and capitalism's contradictions continues to influence economics, sociology, history, and politics.

## Key Ideas

### Historical Materialism

History driven by material/economic conditions, not ideas. Mode of production (how society produces goods) determines social structures, politics, culture.

**Stages:** Primitive communism → slavery → feudalism → capitalism → socialism → communism

### Class Struggle

"The history of all hitherto existing society is the history of class struggles."

Society divided into classes with conflicting interests:
- **Bourgeoisie**: Owners of means of production
- **Proletariat**: Workers who sell labor

### Critique of Capitalism

**Exploitation:**
Workers produce more value than they receive in wages. The difference (surplus value) goes to capitalists as profit.

**Alienation:**
Under capitalism, workers alienated from:
- Their labor (forced, not fulfilling)
- Products of labor (owned by capitalists)
- Fellow workers (competition)
- Human nature (creative potential suppressed)

**Contradictions:**
Capitalism contains internal contradictions—crises, concentration of wealth, growing proletariat—that will lead to its collapse.

### Revolution and Communism

Proletariat will overthrow bourgeoisie through revolution. "Dictatorship of the proletariat" transitions to communism: classless, stateless society where means of production are commonly owned.

## 주요 작품

### The Communist Manifesto (1848)

Written with Engels. Short pamphlet:
- "A specter is haunting 유럽—the specter of Communism"
- History as class struggle
- Call to workers: "Workers of the world, unite!"

### Das Kapital (1867, Volume 1)

Marx's masterwork on political economy:
- Analysis of capitalism's mechanics
- Commodity, value, money
- Labor theory of value
- Surplus value and exploitation
- Capital accumulation

Volumes 2 and 3 published posthumously by Engels.

### Other Works

- *Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts* (1844)—alienation
- *The German Ideology* (with Engels)—historical materialism
- *The Eighteenth Brumaire of Louis Bonaparte* (1852)—political analysis

## 개인 생활

**Jenny von Westphalen:**
Married 1843. Seven children (three survived). Devoted despite poverty.

**Friedrich Engels:**
Lifelong collaborator, friend. Engels supported Marx financially, co-authored works.

**Exile:**
Expelled from multiple countries for radicalism:
- 파리 (1845)
- Brussels (1848)
- 런던 (1849—remained until death)

**Poverty:**
Lived in poverty in 런던 (Soho). Lost children to disease. Engels's financial support essential.

**Character:**
- Brilliant, voracious reader
- Difficult personality
- Poor health
- Devoted to family despite neglect
- Quarreled with many socialists

## 논란

**Violence:**
Marx advocated revolution; whether this requires violence debated.

**Authoritarianism:**
"Dictatorship of proletariat" criticized. Regimes claiming Marx were brutal.

**Anti-Semitism:**
*On the Jewish Question* contains anti-Semitic language (complex context—Marx was Jewish).

**Economic Predictions:**
Many predictions (capitalism's imminent collapse) didn't occur as expected.
