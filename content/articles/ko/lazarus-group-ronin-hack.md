## What the Ronin bridge is (high level)

Ronin is a 블록체인 network associated with the game Axie Infinity, and the “Ronin bridge” was designed to allow users to move assets between chains. Bridges typically rely on smart contracts plus a validation mechanism (often a set of keys or validators) that can authorize withdrawals.

Because bridges can concentrate large pooled balances, they are often treated as high-value targets. A successful compromise can allow attackers to authorize transfers that appear valid at the protocol level.

## The 2022 incident (overview)

Public reports describe a March 2022 theft in which assets were removed from the Ronin bridge, and the incident was later attributed by various sources to 북한–linked actors commonly labeled “라자루스 그룹.” As with many cyber events, attribution is based on a combination of technical indicators and investigative assessments.

## Aftermath and response themes

Incidents of this type commonly trigger:

- Emergency pauses or shutdowns of bridge functionality
- Coordination with exchanges to identify and freeze flows where possible
- On-chain tracing and public reporting of attacker addresses
- Security reviews (key management, validator thresholds, monitoring, and audits)

## Why it mattered

The Ronin hack is often cited as a watershed moment for bridge security because it combined a large dollar impact with a mechanism (bridge authorization) that sits at the boundary between ecosystems.

It also reinforced two broader lessons:

- **Operational security matters:** validator key compromise can be as damaging as code vulnerabilities.
- **Laundering is part of the playbook:** rapid movement across services and assets can reduce recoverability.
