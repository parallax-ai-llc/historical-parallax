## 개요

Former vice chairman of China's Central Military Commission convicted of accepting $12.3 million in bribes for military promotions. He was the highest-ranking military officer convicted in PRC history.

## 경력 연표

| 연도 | 사건 |
|------|-------|
| 2002 | Became CMC vice chairman |
| 2015 | Arrested for corruption |
| 2016 | Convicted and sentenced to life |

## 군사 경력

- Guo Boxiong rose through the People’s Liberation Army (PLA) and reached the Central Military Commission (CMC), one of the most powerful bodies in China’s military system.
- As CMC vice chairman, he was positioned near the top of the command hierarchy during a period of modernization and organizational change.

## Corruption Case (High-Level)

- Chinese authorities accused Guo of trading promotions and assignments for bribes, framing the case as part of a broader effort to curb corruption within the armed forces.
- His prosecution was widely interpreted as politically and institutionally significant because it targeted an exceptionally senior military figure.

## Broader Context

- The case occurred during a sweeping anti-corruption campaign under Xi Jinping that also affected other senior officials.
- PLA reform efforts have been discussed alongside such cases, with the argument that rooting out patronage networks can enable structural reorganization and tighter party control.

## 유산

- In public narratives, Guo’s conviction is often cited as a signal that corruption investigations can extend into the highest ranks.
- The episode remains relevant to discussions of civil-military relations in China and the mechanisms used to enforce party discipline.
