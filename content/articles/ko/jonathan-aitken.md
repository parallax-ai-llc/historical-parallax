## 개요

British politician who was convicted of perjury and perverting the course of justice after lying in a libel case. He had claimed The Guardian's allegations of secret arms deals and Saudi bribes were false. Sentenced to 18 months.

## Additional Context

## Political Roles

Aitken was a Conservative (Tory) politician who served as a Member of Parliament and held junior ministerial posts, reaching cabinet-level office in the mid-1990s. His career was part of the broader political environment of the Major government era, when questions about "sleaze" and public integrity received sustained media focus.

## Libel Action and Criminal Case

The legal saga stemmed from a libel lawsuit Aitken brought against *The Guardian* over reporting related to alleged financial support and meetings linked to arms-related business. During the libel proceedings he gave accounts of travel and hospitality arrangements that were later found to be untrue. The subsequent criminal prosecution resulted in convictions for perjury and perverting the course of justice.

## 말년

After serving his sentence, Aitken’s post-politics life has been covered for its sharp contrast with his earlier career; he became associated with religious and charitable work and is often described as having undergone a public personal transformation.
