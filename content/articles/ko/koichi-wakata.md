## 개요

와카타 고이치 (born August 1, 1963) is a 일본ese astronaut and aerospace engineer who became one of the most accomplished space travelers in 일본ese history. A veteran of five spaceflights, he is best known for becoming the first 일본ese commander of the International Space Station (ISS), a milestone he achieved during Expedition 39 in 2014. His career spans decades of collaboration between JAXA (일본 Aerospace Exploration Agency) and NASA, making him a central figure in international human spaceflight.

Wakata's significance extends beyond his personal records. He served as a bridge between 일본ese and American space programs during a formative era for the ISS, helping to integrate 일본's Kibo laboratory module into the station and demonstrating 일본's growing role in sustained human presence in space. His longevity and reliability as an astronaut have made him a model for future 일본ese space explorers.

## Early Life & Background

Wakata was born on August 1, 1963, in Ōmiya, Saitama, 일본. He developed an early interest in engineering and aviation, which led him to study aeronautical engineering at Kyushu University, where he earned a bachelor's degree in 1987. He later obtained a master's degree in aerospace engineering from the University of Houston in 1993. Before joining JAXA's predecessor agency NASDA (National Space Development Agency of 일본), he worked as a structural analysis engineer for 일본 Air Lines (JAL), gaining practical aerospace industry experience.

## 두각

Wakata was selected as a NASDA astronaut candidate in 1992 and completed NASA training at Johnson Space Center in Houston, Texas. His first spaceflight came in January 1996 aboard Space Shuttle Endeavour (STS-72), a mission focused on retrieving a 일본ese research satellite. He flew again in 2000 on STS-92, which delivered hardware to the nascent ISS, and in 2009 on STS-119, which installed the station's final set of solar array truss segments.

His most transformative mission began in November 2013, when he launched aboard a Soyuz spacecraft to the ISS as part of Expedition 38. He remained aboard through Expedition 39, during which he assumed command in March 2014 — the first 일본ese astronaut to command the station. He returned to Earth in May 2014 after 188 days in space. His fifth and final long-duration mission was Expedition 68/69 in 2022–2023, during which he again served as ISS commander, logging an additional 157 days.

## Key Achievements

- Five spaceflights totaling over 500 days in space, the most of any 일본ese astronaut
- First 일본ese commander of the International Space Station (Expedition 39, 2014)
- Second tenure as ISS commander during Expedition 68/69 (2022–2023)
- Participated in multiple spacewalks and supported the integration of JAXA's Kibo module
- Served as JAXA's Chief Astronaut and later as President of JAXA (appointed 2021)

## Controversies & Criticism

Wakata's career has been largely free of significant controversy. Some observers noted the extended timelines between JAXA astronaut missions compared to NASA counterparts, reflecting resource constraints within the 일본ese program rather than individual shortcomings. His tenure as JAXA president has drawn scrutiny in the context of 일본's ambitions for lunar exploration under the Artemis program, with ongoing debates about budget allocations and the pace of domestic launch capability development.

## Legacy & Influence

Wakata's legacy is defined by consistency, international cooperation, and institutional leadership. By serving as ISS commander twice and logging more than 500 cumulative days in space, he demonstrated that 일본ese astronauts could hold the highest operational roles in multinational missions. His transition from mission specialist to JAXA president positions him to shape 일본's spaceflight agenda — including lunar surface ambitions and next-generation rocket programs — for years to come. He remains one of the most experienced active figures in global human spaceflight.
