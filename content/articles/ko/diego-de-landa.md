## 배경

- Worked amid Spain’s broader campaign of Christianization that often treated indigenous religious objects and writings as “idolatry.”

## Notable Actions and Allegations

- Associated with an inquisition-style investigation in Yucatán that used coercion and 고문 against Maya communities.
- Best known for overseeing the destruction of Maya religious items and the burning of many indigenous books/codices in 1562.
- The event is often referenced as a major loss for pre-Columbian Maya written history, ~ 때문에 only a handful of Maya codices survive today.

- Served as 주교 of Yucatán (appointed later in life), giving him substantial authority in the region’s colonial church hierarchy.

## Writings and Historical Impact

- Authored *Relación de las cosas de Yucatán*, a colonial-era account describing Maya society, 종교, and practices as he understood them.
- The work includes a flawed but historically 영향력 있는 “alphabet” (often called the Landa alphabet) that later helped scholars in the decipherment of Maya script.
- His legacy is 따라서 dual: a figure linked to cultural destruction and abuse, but also a source text used by later historians and epigraphers.
