## 2001 Spaceflight (Soyuz / ISS)

- In 2001, Tito flew to the International Space Station (ISS) aboard a Russian Soyuz mission as a privately funded participant.
- The trip is widely reported as costing about US$20 million and lasted roughly eight days in orbit.
- The flight was a landmark moment for “space tourism” and for the broader commercialization of human spaceflight.

## Significance and Legacy

- Tito’s mission helped demonstrate that private individuals could finance and complete orbital spaceflight under existing government space programs.
- It catalyzed public discussion about regulatory oversight, training standards, and the role of commercial partners in crewed missions.
- Later private astronaut programs and commercial spaceflight initiatives frequently cite his 2001 trip as an 초기의 precedent.
