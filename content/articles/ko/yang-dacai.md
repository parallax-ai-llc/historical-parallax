## 배경과 맥락

The nickname “Brother Watch” (表哥) emerged during a period when Chinese social media users increasingly scrutinized officials’ lifestyles and perceived mismatches between public salaries and conspicuous consumption.

The episode began after Yang was photographed at the scene of a serious traffic accident in Shaanxi Province, where his expression and accessories drew public attention.

## What the Online Investigation Focused On

Public discussion centered on:

- Multiple luxury watches seen in different photos of Yang.
- Whether the watches and other visible signs of wealth could be explained by lawful income.
- How local discipline and supervisory bodies would respond to public allegations.

## 수사 and Legal Outcome

According to publicly reported summaries (including Wikipedia), disciplinary investigations concluded that Yang had accepted bribes and possessed assets inconsistent with legal income.

He was ultimately convicted of bribery and received a prison sentence reported as 14 years.

## Why the Case Became Notable

This case is often cited as an example of:

- The role of online “human flesh search” style investigations in surfacing corruption allegations.
- How imagery and small details (e.g., a watch) can become catalysts for wider accountability demands.
- The interaction between public opinion, internal party discipline processes, and criminal prosecution.

## 여파

The “Brother Watch” label remained a cultural shorthand in China for officials whose visible consumption triggers corruption suspicions.
