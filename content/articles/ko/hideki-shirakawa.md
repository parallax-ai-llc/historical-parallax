## 개요

Hideki Shirakawa is a Japanese chemist who shared the 2000 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for discovering and developing conductive polymers. He accidentally produced a silvery polyacetylene film in 1977 that could conduct electricity when doped with iodine. This discovery created the field of organic electronics, enabling flexible displays and organic solar cells.
