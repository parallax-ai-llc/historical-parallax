## 요약

Japanese yakuza boss who led the Kudo-kai, a particularly violent yakuza syndicate based in Kitakyushu. He became the first yakuza boss sentenced to death for ordering attacks on civilians.

## 배경

Satoru Nomura is primarily known through Japanese reporting and court proceedings related to the Kudo-kai, a yakuza group based in Kitakyushu that gained a reputation for intimidation and violence.

## Kudo-kai and Local Impact

Kudo-kai’s activities were frequently discussed in the context of:

- Pressure on local businesses and civic institutions.
- Attempts by authorities and community groups to disrupt organized crime influence in Fukuoka Prefecture.
- A broader nationwide trend of anti-yakuza ordinances that increased legal and social constraints on syndicates.

## Criminal Cases and Sentencing

The cases associated with Nomura drew attention because they centered on alleged command responsibility: prosecutors argued that attacks were ordered and coordinated at leadership level rather than being isolated acts by individual members.

His death sentence was widely reported as a landmark outcome, reflecting an unusually severe judicial response toward an alleged top-level yakuza leader.

## 중요성

In discussions of Japanese organized crime policy, the Nomura case is often referenced as an example of how law enforcement and prosecutors sought to hold leadership directly accountable, especially where victims included civilians and public officials.
