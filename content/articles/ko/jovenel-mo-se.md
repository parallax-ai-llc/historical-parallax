## Political background

- Moïse was a Haitian businessman who entered politics with backing from the political movement associated with former president Michel Martelly.
- He won the presidency after an election cycle marked by delays, disputes, and questions about legitimacy among opponents.

## Governance and protests

- His term faced persistent political unrest, including large street protests tied to economic conditions, fuel shortages, and allegations of corruption.
- Haiti’s institutional instability—frequent changes in government leadership and contested constitutional interpretations—formed the backdrop for many of the crises during his presidency.

## Constitutional and electoral disputes

- A major controversy concerned the end date of Moïse’s presidential term, with competing interpretations of election timing and constitutional provisions.
- The dispute contributed to political paralysis and intensified tensions between the executive branch, opposition parties, and civil society.

## Security situation

- Armed gang influence expanded in many parts of Haiti, affecting daily life, commerce, and the state’s ability to provide security.
- Analysts commonly describe a cycle in which political fragmentation, weak policing, and economic hardship created conditions for non-state armed groups to gain leverage.

## Assassination and aftermath

- Moïse was assassinated at his residence in July 2021.
- The killing triggered international investigations and deepened uncertainty over Haiti’s transition plans, governance arrangements, and the timeline for elections.
