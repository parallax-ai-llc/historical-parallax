## 배경

- Like many conquistadors, his wealth and political influence were closely tied to control over land, tribute, and coerced indigenous labor systems.

## Expedition to the Amazon Basin ("El Dorado" search)

- He is widely associated with a costly eastward expedition from the Andean highlands into the Amazon basin, driven by Spanish rumors of 부유한 kingdoms.
- The journey became notorious for logistical collapse: difficult terrain, shortages of food, and heavy casualties among both Spaniards and the indigenous people compelled to support the expedition.
- In broader histories of exploration, this episode is often cited as an example of how conquest-era ambitions collided with the realities of geography, supply, and resistance.

## Rebellion and the New Laws

- The Spanish Crown issued the New Laws (1542) in part to reduce the worst abuses of the encomienda system and to limit the hereditary transfer of encomiendas.
- Opposition among many settlers and encomenderos in Peru helped spark armed 분쟁; Gonzalo Pizarro ~이 되었다 저명한 leader of this resistance.
- The rebellion is commonly framed as both:
  - a political struggle over authority between royal officials and colonial elites, and
  - a material struggle over labor, extraction, and the distribution of conquest spoils.
