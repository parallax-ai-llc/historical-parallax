## 개요

## Additional Context

### 배경 and Roles

- **Legal name**: Diego Fernando Murillo Bejarano, commonly known as **"Don Berna"**.
- ~이 되었다 저명한 figure in Medellín’s underworld, with influence spanning **drug trafficking**, **contract killing networks**, and **paramilitary structures**.
- Was associated with Colombia’s **United Self-Defense Forces of Colombia (AUC)**, a right-wing paramilitary federation that was later designated as a terrorist organization by multiple governments.

### Medellín Criminal Structure

- Often linked to the **Oficina de Envigado**, a criminal network that historically operated as a brokerage for 폭력 and illicit commerce in the Medellín region.
- His notoriety reflects how post-cartel criminal power in Medellín shifted from a single "cartel" model to **networked groups** competing for territory and rents.

### Demobilization and Extradition

- Participated 중반에2000s **paramilitary demobilization** process, which drew controversy over questions of truth-telling, reparations, and the persistence of successor criminal groups.
- In **2008**, he was **extradited to the United States** on drug-related charges, a step that also affected ongoing Colombian judicial processes connected to paramilitary 폭력.

### Significance

- Don Berna is frequently cited in discussions of:
  - The blurred boundary between **political paramilitarism** and **organized crime**.
  - The evolution of **urban armed governance** in Medellín ~ 후에 the decline of the Medellín Cartel.
  - The role of extradition as a tool in the Colombian state’s strategy against high-level traffickers.
