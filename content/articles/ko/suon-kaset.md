## 요약

Khmer Rouge prison commander who oversaw detention facilities where prisoners were tortured and executed. He operated provincial-level security centers during the genocide.

## 역사적 맥락

Suon Kaset is associated with the Khmer Rouge security apparatus during Democratic Kampuchea (1975–1979), a period marked by mass imprisonment, forced labor, and systematic killings that have been widely characterized as genocide.

## Role in the Security System

Provincial and regional detention centers functioned as nodes in a broader mechanism of internal surveillance and purges. Commanders were responsible for interrogations, record-keeping, transfers, and coordination with higher-level security organs.

## Accountability and Aftermath

Many Khmer Rouge officials blended into border areas or joined insurgent remnants after 1979. Later accountability efforts in Cambodia focused on documenting chains of command and establishing individual responsibility for crimes committed at specific facilities.

## Why This Matters

Cases involving prison commanders are often used to illustrate how mass atrocities rely on administrative routines—arrest lists, confessions, and local facilities—rather than only on battlefield violence.
