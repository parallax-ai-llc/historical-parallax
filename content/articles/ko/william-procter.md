## 요약

William Procter was an English-American candlemaker who co-founded Procter & Gamble with James Gamble in 1837. The company became one of the largest consumer goods companies in the world. Starting with candles and soap in Cincinnati, the partnership grew through Union Army contracts during the Civil War. The company's Ivory soap, introduced after Procter's death, became one of America's most iconic products.

## Company Formation and Strategy

Procter & Gamble began as a partnership between relatives by marriage in Cincinnati, a growing river city with access to transportation, raw materials, and expanding markets. The early business focused on candles and soap—staples with steady demand—which helped the firm survive business cycles.

During the American Civil War, military supply contracts provided scale and revenue that accelerated industrial capabilities. Over time, P&G developed a reputation for manufacturing consistency and brand-building, contributing to its later expansion into mass consumer goods.

## Historical Significance

William Procter is often cited as an example of 19th-century immigrant entrepreneurship in the United States. His career reflects broader trends in American industrialization: partnership-based firms, expansion through logistics networks, and growth enabled by institutional purchasers (including government).

## 유산

Although the company’s most famous products emerged over a long period and through many leaders, Procter’s role as a co-founder places him at the start of a corporate lineage that became influential in marketing, product standardization, and consumer packaged goods.

## Further Notes

Histories of American consumer capitalism often cite Procter & Gamble as an early example of scaling manufacturing and branding together, beginning with basic household necessities.

This founder-era origin story is often contrasted with the company’s later global diversification into many product categories.
