## 개요

Jan Železný holds the javelin WR of 98.48m and won three consecutive Olympic golds (1992-2000).

## Athletic Profile

Železný is widely regarded as one of the greatest javelin throwers in history, combining elite speed on the runway with strong technical control at release. His world record throw of 98.48 m (set in 1996) remains one of the most notable marks in track and field.

## Major Championships

In addition to his Olympic success, Železný won multiple World Championship titles and was a consistent medal contender across the 1990s and early 2000s. His longevity at the top level is often cited as a hallmark of his career.

## Technique and Influence

Analysts and coaches frequently reference Železný's efficient throwing mechanics, including his transition from approach to delivery stride and his ability to maintain javelin alignment through the release. His performances helped shape modern expectations for elite distances in the redesigned javelin era.

## Post-Competition Career

After retiring from top-level competition, Železný became involved in coaching and mentoring, including work with leading Czech throwers. In this role he has contributed to the continuation of technical traditions in the event.

## 유산

Železný's combination of record-setting distance and championship consistency made him a central figure in men's javelin history, often used as a benchmark when evaluating later Olympic and world champions.

## Notability

His 98.48 m world record is frequently highlighted as a rare example of an athletics record that has remained intact for decades.
