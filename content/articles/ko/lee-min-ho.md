## 개요

이민호 is a 대한민국n actor and singer who rose to international stardom through the Korean Wave (Hallyu), becoming one of the most recognized 아시아n celebrities worldwide. Born on June 22, 1987, in 서울, 대한민국, he built a reputation across drama, film, and music over a career spanning two decades.

His breakthrough in 2009 transformed him from a struggling actor into a household name across 아시아 and beyond. 이민호's appeal — combining leading-man looks with consistent dramatic range — helped Korean entertainment penetrate markets in 중국, 동남아시아, the 중동, and 라틴아메리카 at a pivotal moment in the genre's global expansion.

By the mid-2010s, 이민호 had amassed over 40 million social media followers and commanded some of the highest per-episode fees in Korean television, cementing his status as a bankable star whose projects consistently attract large international viewership.

## Early Life & Background

이민호 was born on June 22, 1987, in Heukseok-dong, Dongjak District, 서울. He attended Konkuk University, where he studied film arts. His path to acting was not immediate — he faced repeated rejection from agencies and endured minor roles for several years before his career gained traction.

In 2006, he suffered a serious car accident that left him hospitalized for months and raised questions about whether he would return to the industry. His recovery and subsequent persistence became a defining element of his personal narrative.

## 두각

이민호's career pivoted sharply with the 2009 drama *Boys Over Flowers*, a Korean adaptation of the 일본ese manga *Hana Yori Dango*. The series aired on KBS2 and became a pan-아시아n phenomenon, drawing record viewership across 대한민국, 중국, and 대만. His portrayal of the charismatic but arrogant Gu Jun-pyo made him a regional star overnight.

He followed this with *Personal Taste* (2010) and *City Hunter* (2011), the latter earning strong critical reception for his action-oriented performance. *The Heirs* (2013) on SBS drew massive simultaneous audiences across 아시아 and further expanded his reach into 중동ern and 라틴아메리카n markets. In 2020, *The King: Eternal Monarch* on Netflix marked his return from mandatory military service (2017–2019) and introduced him to a broader streaming audience.

## Key Achievements

- *Boys Over Flowers* (2009) averaged 28.5% viewership ratings in 대한민국, one of the highest for a Monday-Tuesday drama at the time.
- Named a Goodwill Ambassador for UNICEF Korea in 2013.
- Estimated brand endorsement income placed him among the top-earning Korean celebrities throughout the 2010s, with deals including Samsung, KB Kookmin Bank, and international luxury brands.
- His fan club, MINOZs, organized coordinated charitable donations in multiple countries tied to his birthdays, a model that influenced Hallyu fan culture broadly.
- Released the single "My Everything" (2015) and the EP *Song For You* (2015), demonstrating crossover into music.

## Controversies & Criticism

이민호 has largely maintained a clean public image. In 2017, his military service assignment to a social service position rather than active duty drew mild public scrutiny, though this was consistent with his medical exemption status following his 2006 accident. No major legal or ethical controversies have been attached to his name.

## Legacy & Influence

이민호 is frequently cited alongside peers like 김수현 and Song Joong-ki as part of the actor generation that normalized Korean drama consumption outside 아시아 in the early 2010s. His career helped establish the commercial template — high-production romances with aspirational aesthetics — that later productions followed when targeting global streaming platforms. His sustained popularity across nearly two decades reflects both disciplined project selection and the enduring reach of the Hallyu wave he helped amplify.
