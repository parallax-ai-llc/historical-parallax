## 개요

British colonial administrator whose indirect rule system facilitated exploitation across Nigeria and East Africa.

Often associated with the British doctrine of **indirect rule**, Lugard argued that colonial governments should govern ~을 통해 existing local authorities ~하는 동안 retaining decisive control over taxation, policing, and economic policy.[^1] In practice, the approach helped lower the administrative costs of empire ~하는 동안 consolidating British power and enabling extraction.

## 초기 생애 and Military Background

- Commissioned into the British Army and served in 후기의-19th-세기 imperial campaigns.
- Built a reputation as a frontier officer and administrator in a period when military force and civil governance were closely intertwined in British expansion.

## Work in East Africa

- Served in areas including **Nyasaland** (present-day Malawi) and **Uganda**, where British authorities were establishing protectorates and negotiating (or imposing) political arrangements with local leaders.
- Helped shape 초기의 protectorate administration, combining treaty-making, coercion, and the creation of colonial revenue systems.

## Nigeria and Indirect Rule

Lugard ~이 되었다 central figure in Britain’s consolidation of what would become 현대의 Nigeria:

- As **High Commissioner of Northern Nigeria** (초기의 1900s), he promoted governance ~을 통해 emirs and established a framework in which customary institutions were recognized selectively and subordinated to colonial priorities.
- As **Governor of Nigeria**, he oversaw the **1914 amalgamation** of Northern and Southern Nigeria into a single colonial entity, a decision with long-term political and administrative consequences.

Indirect rule varied significantly across regions:

- In the north, colonial administration leaned on existing emirate structures.
- In many southern areas, where centralized authorities were different or contested, colonial governance often created or empowered “chiefs” in ways that reshaped local politics.

## Major Publications

- **The Dual Mandate in British Tropical Africa** (1922) articulated a justification for empire framed as a balance between metropolitan interests and purported responsibilities to colonized populations, ~하는 동안 also defending continued economic development under imperial control.[^1]

## 유산 and Criticism

- 영향력 있는 within British colonial policy circles and later debates on governance in Africa.
- Widely criticized by historians and postcolonial scholars for providing an intellectual framework that rationalized coercive rule, unequal economic structures, and the political re-engineering of African societies.
