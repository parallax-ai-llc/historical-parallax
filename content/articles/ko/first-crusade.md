## 개요

The campaign drew tens of thousands of knights, peasants, clergy, and pilgrims from across Western Europe, united by religious fervor, papal authority, and the promise of spiritual reward.

The legacy of the First Crusade is vast and contested. It initiated a cycle of crusading that would occupy European attention for the next two hundred years and shaped Christian-Muslim relations in ways still felt today.

It provoked one of the earliest systematic waves of anti-Jewish 폭력 in European history. It permanently strained relations between the Latin West and the Byzantine East.

## Multiple Perspectives

### Christian Crusader View

For the crusaders and their contemporaries in Western Christendom, the First Crusade was an act of sacred warfare — not merely a military campaign but a penitential pilgrimage armed with swords.

교황 Urban II had framed the expedition in the language of spiritual 의학: the knights of Europe, prone to 폭력 and sin, could redirect their martial energy toward a holy purpose and earn remission of their sins in the process.

The cry "Deus lo vult" — God wills it — expressed a genuine belief that the expedition was divinely ordained and that the suffering and sacrifice of the march were part of God's providential design.

The Council of Clermont in November 1095 was the formal launching point, but the crusade's spiritual momentum was built by preachers across Europe in the months that followed.

Figures like Peter the Hermit drew crowds of thousands with vivid descriptions of Muslim atrocities against Jerusalem's Christians and the desecration of the Holy Sepulchre.

현대의 chronicles — the *Gesta Francorum*, Fulcher of Chartres's history, Raymond of Aguilers's account — present the crusade as an unfolding divine drama, with God's hand visible in every victory and miraculous intervention.

The discovery of the Holy Lance at Antioch was interpreted as divine confirmation that the enterprise was blessed, and it restored the crusaders' morale at a 비판적인 moment of crisis.

When Jerusalem fell on July 15, 1099, crusader chroniclers wept with joy, describing the 학살 as a righteous purging of a sacred space rather than an act of brutality to be ashamed of.

Godfrey of Bouillon ~이 되었다 iconic crusader hero: pious, selfless, refusing the royal title in Jerusalem. The establishment of the four Crusader states was understood as the fulfillment of God's promise.

For generations of European Christians, the First Crusade was a founding myth, a demonstration that a united Christendom, guided by the papacy, could achieve the impossible.

This narrative shaped crusading recruitment, crusading 문학 — the *chansons de geste* — and the political theology of the 중세의 papacy for two hundred years.

The crusader ideal — the 기사 as Christian 군인 — shaped chivalric culture across Western Europe, from epic poetry to the founding of military orders like the Knights Templar and the Hospitallers.

### Muslim Perspective

For the Muslim world, the arrival of the crusaders was an unexpected and deeply traumatic shock. The Arabic sources of the period call the crusaders "Franj" — Franks — regardless of their actual origin.

The initial reaction in the Muslim world was one of fragmentation and disarray: the Seljuk empire was deeply divided by dynastic 분쟁, the Fatimid Caliphate was a rival power, and local Syrian rulers had little interest in coordinating a unified defense.

### Jewish Perspective

The First Crusade inaugurated a catastrophic era for the Jews of Western Europe. As the crusading armies assembled and marched ~을 통해 the Rhineland in spring 1096, a question arose among many participants: why travel thousands of miles to fight the enemies of Christ when enemies of Christ lived in their midst?

This logic — brutal in its simplicity — drove crusader mobs and local participants to attack the thriving Jewish communities of Speyer, Worms, and Mainz.

The Rhineland Massacres of 1096 were among the worst episodes of anti-Jewish 폭력 in European history to that point. In Worms, over 800 Jews were killed. In Mainz, a community of several thousand was almost entirely annihilated.

Some Jews chose martyrdom — the *kiddush Hashem*, or sanctification of God's name — killing their own children and themselves rather than submitting to forced baptism.

The sources for these events include both Latin chronicles and Hebrew martyrologies like the *Mainz Anonymous*, which preserve the Jewish community's own account of the horror.

The 주교 of Speyer attempted to protect his Jewish community and largely succeeded; the Bishops of Worms and Mainz were overwhelmed by the mobs. The massacres were not officially sanctioned by the crusading leadership or the papacy, but they were a direct product of the social and religious hysteria the crusade unleashed.

### Byzantine View

The relationship between the Byzantine 법원 and the crusading princes was difficult from the start. Alexios insisted that each crusading leader swear an oath to restore to Byzantine 주권 any former imperial territories they recovered — a requirement most accepted grudgingly and few honored.

## 유산 & Consequences

The First Crusade's most immediate consequence was the establishment of four Latin Christian states in the Levant: the Kingdom of Jerusalem, the County of Edessa, the Principality of Antioch, and the County of Tripoli.

These states, defended by a thin layer of European settlers and the military orders of the Templars and Hospitallers, created a permanent Latin Christian presence in the eastern Mediterranean that would endure for nearly two centuries.

They also necessitated a continuous investment of crusading energy from Western Europe: the loss of Edessa in 1144 prompted the Second Crusade, Saladin's reconquest of Jerusalem in 1187 triggered the Third, and so on ~을 통해 seven or more major expeditions.

Contact with the sophisticated Islamic civilization of the Levant exposed Western Europeans to 고대의 Greek 철학 preserved in Arabic translation, to advances in mathematics, 의학, and astronomy, and to luxury goods that transformed European material culture.

The military orders founded to protect the Crusader states became major economic and political powers in their own right. The papacy emerged from the First Crusade with dramatically enhanced authority and prestige.

The human cost of the First Crusade was staggering. Tens of thousands of crusaders died on the march from disease, battle, and starvation. The populations of Antioch, Jerusalem, and numerous smaller cities suffered 학살 or enslavement.

The Jewish communities of the Rhineland were devastated in the opening months of the campaign. The long-term geopolitical consequences — the deepening rift between Latin and Greek Christianity, the entrenchment of a narrative of civilizational 분쟁 between Islam and the West, the acceleration of anti-Jewish 폭력 in Europe — resonate across the centuries.

The First Crusade was a moment when religious idealism and brutal 폭력 fused in a combination that would define the 중세의 world's most intense conflicts.
