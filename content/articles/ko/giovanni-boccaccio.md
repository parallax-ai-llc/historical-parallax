### 부정적 평가

Criticism of Boccaccio has focused on several areas: moral objections to his content, perceived limitations in his artistic vision, and questions about the depth of his humanism and scholarship.[^112]

Religious and moral critics from Boccaccio's time forward have condemned *The Decameron* for its licentiousness, anti-clericalism, and perceived celebration of vice.[^113] Conservative Catholic critics argued that the work corrupted readers by presenting sin attractively, undermining moral instruction ~을 통해 entertaining stories of adultery, deception, and clerical corruption. 교회's placement of *The Decameron* on the Index of Prohibited Books reflected institutional recognition of the work's challenge to religious authority and conventional morality.[^114]

Some critics have argued that Boccaccio lacks the philosophical depth of Dante or the spiritual refinement of Petrarch, positioning him as primarily an entertainer rather than a profound thinker.[^115] This view holds that ~하는 동안 Boccaccio excels at storytelling and social observation, his work lacks the theological, philosophical, and moral vision that elevates Dante's *Divine Comedy* to supreme status. ~에 따르면 this critique, Boccaccio's celebration of earthly life, human cleverness, and social maneuvering reflects limited spiritual ambition.[^116]

Literary critics have sometimes found Boccaccio's prose style overly elaborate, ornate, and artificial, particularly in his 초기의 romances.[^117] The long, complex periodic sentences that 르네상스 theorists admired can strike 현대의 readers as verbose and distant from natural speech rhythms. Some tales in *The Decameron* are criticized for mechanical plotting or conventional treatment of stock situations, suggesting uneven artistic achievement within the collection.[^118]

Feminist critics have identified 중요한 problems in Boccaccio's representation of women and gender relations.[^119] ~하는 동안 acknowledging his creation of complex female characters and his 선구적인 *De Mulieribus Claris*, feminist scholars note that his work 궁극적으로 reinforces patriarchal assumptions about women's nature, sexuality, and proper social roles. Some scholars question the depth and originality of Boccaccio's humanist scholarship, noting that his 고전적인 knowledge, ~하는 동안 impressive for his time, was limited and sometimes erroneous.[^121] His Greek learning was second-hand and fragmentary; his Latin works often relied on 중세의 sources and traditions as much as 고전적인 texts. Later 르네상스 scholars with better linguistic training and access to superior manuscripts revealed errors and limitations in Boccaccio's 고전적인 scholarship, suggesting that his reputation as a humanist may be somewhat inflated.[^122]

Critics have also noted contradictions and inconsistencies in Boccaccio's work and life, particularly his apparent later repudiation of his vernacular writings.[^123] Some view this as intellectual inconsistency or failure of nerve, a retreat from his artistic achievements into conventional piety. The tension between his celebration of earthly life in *The Decameron* and his later religious anxieties suggests an unresolved 분쟁 between humanist and Christian values that he never successfully integrated.[^124]

~에도 불구하고 these criticisms, scholarly consensus maintains Boccaccio's importance as a foundational figure in Western 문학, with his limitations understood within historical context rather than as disqualifying flaws.[^125] His achievements in developing vernacular prose, establishing narrative forms, and contributing to 르네상스 humanism outweigh the objections, securing his place among 문학's major figures.[^126]
