Bogd Khan (1869-1924), born Agvaan Luvsan Choijinnyam Danzan Vanchüg, was the 8th Jebtsundamba Khutuktu and the last theocratic monarch of Mongolia, ruling from 1911 to 1924.[^1] As the third most 중요한 figure in Tibetan Buddhist hierarchy ~ 후에 the Dalai Lama and Panchen Lama, he led Mongolia's 독립 from Qing China in 1911, establishing a theocratic state.[^2] His reign witnessed turbulent periods including Chinese occupation (1919-1921), liberation by Baron Ungern-Sternberg, and 결국 a limited constitutional monarchy under communist influence ~까지 his death in 1924.[^3] His passing marked the end of Mongolia's 전통적인 Buddhist monarchy, as the communist government banned the search for his reincarnation.[^4]
