Political historians 비판적인 of Washington note that his administration became increasingly partisan ~에도 불구하고 his rhetoric of standing above party politics. His strong support for Hamilton's financial programs and the Jay Treaty with Britain alienated the emerging Democratic-Republican opposition and 에 기여했다 the very partisan divisions his Farewell Address warned against.[^97] Critics argue that Washington's claims to nonpartisanship were disingenuous and that he actively supported Federalist policies ~하는 동안 presenting himself as a neutral arbiter.

Some scholars question Washington's intellectual contributions, noting that his most 유명한 written works—including the Farewell Address—were largely drafted by others, particularly Alexander Hamilton and James Madison. 역사학자 Gary Wills argues that Washington's reputation for wisdom often derived from his ability to recognize and appropriate the ideas of more intellectually gifted advisors rather than from original thinking.[^98] His limited formal 교육 and tendency toward brevity in his own writing suggest a mind more practical than philosophical.

Recent 비판적인 scholarship emphasizes Washington's harsh policies toward Native Americans, including his approval of the destructive Sullivan Expedition ~ 동안 혁명가 전쟁 and his administration's facilitation of westward expansion at Native peoples' expense.[^99] The Iroquois name for Washington, "Town Destroyer," reflects the reality that his leadership often meant dispossession and 폭력 for indigenous peoples who stood in the way of American expansion.

Critics also note that the cult of Washington worship, particularly in the nineteenth 세기, created a mythologized figure that bore little resemblance to the actual man, making it difficult for Americans to understand their history honestly. 역사학자 François Furstenberg argues that the apotheosis of Washington into a quasi-religious figure served ideological purposes by creating a unifying national symbol, but this came at the cost of historical truth and made it harder for Americans to critically examine their founding.[^100] The challenge, these scholars argue, is to see Washington as he actually was—a talented but flawed individual whose accomplishments and failures both merit serious examination.
