## 개요

Co-pilot Andreas Lubitz deliberately crashed Germanwings Flight 9525 into the French Alps on March 24, 2015, killing all 150 people aboard. He had a history of depression and suicidal tendencies.

## 수사 Findings

Investigations concluded the crash was an intentional act, with cockpit voice and flight data evidence indicating deliberate descent.
The incident shifted attention from technical failure modes to **pilot mental health, screening, and oversight**.

## Aviation Safety Responses

Regulators and airlines reviewed cockpit procedures and introduced or reinforced measures such as:

- “two-person” cockpit rules in some jurisdictions and carriers
- additional reporting and support pathways for mental health concerns
- reassessment of medical certification practices and follow-up requirements

## Privacy, Medicine, and Duty to Warn

The case raised difficult policy questions about how to balance medical confidentiality with public safety in safety-비판적인 professions.
European discussions included whether physicians and employers had sufficient mechanisms to act on warning signs.

## Broader Impact

Germanwings Flight 9525 ~이 되었다 widely cited example in aviation risk management of how rare, intentional actions can drive system-wide policy change.
It also influenced research and training discussions related to resilience, peer reporting, and organizational safety culture.
