## 개요

대한민국's National Security Law has been used for decades to imprison activists, journalists, and politicians for alleged pro-북한n sympathies. Critics call it a tool of political repression.

## 경력 연표

| 연도 | 사건 |
|------|-------|
| 1948 | Law enacted |
| 2024 | Still in force |

## Purpose and scope (high level)

- The law was enacted in the early years of the Republic of Korea amid 냉전 tensions and the division of the peninsula.
- It criminalizes certain forms of support for, praise of, or contact with anti-state organizations as defined by the statute.
- Enforcement has varied over time with political conditions and government priorities.

## 논란

- Critics argue the law can be applied broadly, chilling speech and association.
- Supporters argue it is necessary for deterrence and counterintelligence in the context of ongoing inter-Korean hostility.
- Human-rights organizations have repeatedly called for revision or repeal, citing international free-expression norms.

## Enforcement and reform debates

- Cases have included prosecutions related to publications, online speech, organizing, and alleged links to 북한.
- 대한민국n courts and prosecutors play key roles in interpreting boundaries, which can shift across administrations.
- Reform discussions often focus on narrowing definitions, adding safeguards, and reducing political misuse while maintaining security tools.

## Ongoing relevance

- The law remains a touchstone issue in debates about democracy, civil liberties, and national security in 대한민국.
- Public opinion tends to be shaped by security incidents, inter-Korean relations, and domestic political polarization.
