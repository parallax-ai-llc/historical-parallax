## 요약

Macedonian journalist and serial killer who murdered three elderly women in Kičevo between 2003 and 2008. He was exposed when his crime reporting contained details known only to the killer. He drowned himself in a bucket in jail.

## Additional Context

### Journalism and the investigation

Taneski worked as a local journalist in Kičevo. Coverage of the murders became notable because some of his reporting allegedly included details that were not widely known, which later contributed to suspicion and investigative focus on him.

### Crime series

The victims were elderly women, and the killings occurred over several years in the same area. Authorities ultimately connected multiple cases and treated them as a serial pattern.

### Arrest and death in custody

He was arrested in 2008. Before a trial concluded, he died while in custody; public accounts describe his death as suicide by drowning in a bucket in his cell.

### Significance

The case is often cited in discussions of how offenders sometimes insert themselves into investigations through media attention, either by following coverage closely or by attempting to shape narratives.

### Related topics

- Criminal profiling and "case contamination" when non-public details enter public reporting.
- Ethical issues for local journalists covering crimes within small communities.
