## 배경

BitConnect was widely described by regulators and prosecutors as a classic Ponzi-style scheme wrapped in crypto marketing, with heavy emphasis on:

- **High, consistent returns** marketed as low-risk
- **Referral commissions** and multi-level promotion incentives
- **Vague or unverifiable trading/"bot" claims** used to justify yield narratives

Within that ecosystem, promoters played an 중요한 operational role by:

- Driving new user acquisition ~을 통해 events, videos, and social channels
- Normalizing the product via repeated testimonials and “success stories”
- Creating social pressure ~을 통해 affiliate-style incentives

## Public Allegations and Case Outcome (High Level)

Glenn Arcaro is commonly referenced in reporting as a leading BitConnect promoter who was later **convicted of fraud** in connection with the broader scheme. Public summaries typically frame the case around promoter-led solicitation and the flow of funds from new participants to earlier participants and insiders.

## Why Promoter Cases Matter

Promoter-focused prosecutions are often used to communicate that:

- Marketing and solicitation can be treated as part of the fraudulent conduct, not “just speech.”
- Distribution networks (affiliates, influencers, sales funnels) can be as central as the underlying product.
- Even when a token/project is global, enforcement can still occur via jurisdiction over individuals and payment rails.
