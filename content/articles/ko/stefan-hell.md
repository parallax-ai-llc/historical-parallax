## 요약

Stefan Hell is a Romanian-German physicist who shared the 2014 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for developing super-resolved fluorescence microscopy.

## Scientific contribution (high level)

Classical light microscopy is limited by optical diffraction, which constrains the smallest resolvable features. Hell’s work helped show practical ways to surpass this limit in fluorescence microscopy, enabling imaging of biological structures at substantially finer scales.

## STED microscopy

Stimulated Emission Depletion (STED) microscopy uses carefully shaped light patterns to selectively switch off fluorescence in parts of the sample, leaving a much smaller effective region emitting light. By scanning this reduced spot, researchers can build images with improved resolution.

## Why it mattered

Super-resolution microscopy expanded what could be observed inside cells, including the organization of proteins and other subcellular structures that were previously below the diffraction limit. This opened new avenues for cell biology, neuroscience, and biomedical research.

## 유산

Hell’s contributions are part of a broader transformation in imaging, where optical and computational techniques together pushed microscopy into regimes once thought impossible for visible light.
