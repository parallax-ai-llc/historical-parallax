## 두각

Holt joined NBC News in 2000, initially working as a correspondent and weekend anchor. He became anchor of Weekend Today and Weekend Nightly News, building a reputation for reliability and composure under pressure. His profile rose significantly after he substituted for Brian Williams during breaking news events, demonstrating an ability to lead coverage of major stories.

In 2015, following Brian Williams's suspension over fabricated war reporting claims, NBC named Holt the permanent anchor of NBC Nightly News. The transition was smooth, and under Holt's stewardship the broadcast maintained strong ratings. A defining moment came in May 2017 when he conducted an exclusive interview with President Donald Trump, during which Trump stated he had the 러시아 investigation in mind when he fired FBI Director James Comey—a revelation that generated significant political fallout.

## Controversies & Criticism

Holt's 2016 presidential debate moderation drew mixed reviews. Some critics argued he was too passive in fact-checking candidates in real time, while others praised his restraint in letting candidates speak. His Trump interview in 2017, while journalistically significant, also prompted scrutiny over whether follow-up questioning was sufficiently probing. Additionally, as with most network anchors, Holt has faced broader criticism about the limitations of commercial broadcast news in covering complex issues with adequate depth.

## Legacy & Influence

Holt's tenure at NBC Nightly News represents a landmark in American broadcast history. As a Black anchor leading a flagship evening newscast, he has expanded representation in a field long dominated by white men. His steady journalism during crises—from Hurricane Harvey in 2017 to the COVID-19 pandemic—has reinforced public trust in institutional media at a time of widespread skepticism. Holt is widely regarded as a model of professional broadcast journalism, and his career has inspired a generation of journalists from underrepresented backgrounds.
