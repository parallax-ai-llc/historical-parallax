## 개요

Frederick Griffith 영국의 bacteriologist who discovered bacterial transformation, the process by which bacteria can incorporate genetic material from their environment. His 1928 Griffith experiment showed that a non-virulent strain of Streptococcus pneumoniae could be transformed into a virulent strain, demonstrating that genetic information could be transferred between bacteria. This experiment ~로 이어졌다 the eventual identification of DNA as the genetic material.

## Griffith Experiment (Why It Mattered)

- Griffith’s work implied that a “transforming principle” could carry heritable information between organisms.
- This finding set up later experiments (특히 by Avery, MacLeod, and McCarty) that identified DNA as the material responsible for transformation.
- The transformation concept became foundational for genetics, microbiology, and 현대의 biotechnology techniques that rely on introducing genetic material into cells.

## Key Takeaways

- The transformation result was empirical: it showed a reproducible change in bacterial traits under specific conditions.
- The experiment helped shift debate toward the existence of a physical, transferable genetic substance.
- It remains a canonical example of how careful controls can reveal unexpected mechanisms.
