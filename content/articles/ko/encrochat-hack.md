## 배경

- **EncroChat** was an encrypted communications service that sold modified smartphones marketed for privacy. Authorities alleged it was widely adopted by organized crime networks for coordinating trafficking and other illicit activity.[^1]
- Investigators targeted the platform ~ 후에 years of difficulty obtaining evidence from end‑to‑end encrypted messaging at scale.

## What was unusual about the operation

- Rather than breaking encryption in transit, the operation is commonly described as a **device-side compromise**: malware was reportedly deployed to EncroChat handsets to access messages **before encryption / after decryption** on the device.[^1]
- This approach meant investigators could collect large volumes of communications content without needing to decrypt network traffic.

## Scale and outcomes (high level)

- Reports describe **hundreds of millions of messages** being collected across Europe and used to drive investigations.[^1]
- The takedown is associated with **thousands of arrests** and the seizure of drugs, firearms, and cash in multiple countries (figures vary by reporting and jurisdiction).[^1]

## Legal and evidentiary debates

- Courts in several countries have considered questions such as:
  - whether defendants can challenge the technical method used to collect EncroChat data,
  - how cross-border evidence sharing was authorized and documented,
  - and what disclosure is required for a fair 재판 when the underlying technique involves sensitive investigative capabilities.
- Public reporting summarizes that outcomes have differed by country and by case posture, reflecting differences in criminal procedure and disclosure standards.[^1]

## Why it mattered

- The EncroChat case is often cited as a milestone in 현대의 policing ~ 때문에 it:
  - demonstrated that **platform-centric** encrypted services could be disrupted at the infrastructure or device level,
  - accelerated cooperation among European law enforcement agencies,
  - and shifted criminal communications away from single-purpose encrypted phone services toward mainstream apps and operational security practices.
