French woman known as 'The She-Devil of Nancy' convicted of murdering her lover and dismembering his body.

## 배경

- Simone Weber was a French criminal case figure who became nationally known through intense media coverage in France.
- She was nicknamed *"La Diablesse de Nancy"* (often translated as "the She-Devil of Nancy"), reflecting the notoriety and sensational framing of the case.

## Case Overview

- The case centered on the disappearance and death of a man described in sources as her partner/lover.
- Investigators linked the disappearance to evidence suggesting the victim had been killed and his body dismembered, which became a defining and widely reported aspect of the case.
- Weber denied responsibility during the investigation and proceedings, and the case remained controversial in public discussion even after conviction.

## Trial and Sentencing (High Level)

- In 1991, a French court convicted Simone Weber of murder.
- The verdict established her responsibility for the killing; sentencing resulted in a long prison term under French criminal law.
- Subsequent years included continued public interest, commentary, and repeated retellings in crime reporting and documentaries.

## 여파 및 유산

- The case is frequently cited in French true-crime media as an example of a high-profile domestic homicide prosecution.
- It has also been referenced in discussions about media sensationalism, public perception of defendants, and the impact of notoriety on criminal proceedings.
