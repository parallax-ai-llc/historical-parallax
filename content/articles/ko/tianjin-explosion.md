## 요약

Massive explosions at a chemical storage facility in Tianjin, China on August 12, 2015 that killed 173 people. Illegal storage of hazardous chemicals caused the blast equivalent to 21 tons of TNT.

## What Happened

- The explosions occurred at a hazardous-chemicals warehouse in the **Binhai New Area** of Tianjin, a major port and industrial zone.
- The blast sequence included multiple detonations and a large fire, damaging nearby buildings, port infrastructure, and vehicles.
- Many of the casualties were **firefighters and emergency responders** who arrived shortly after the initial fire began.

## 원인 and Contributing Factors

- Investigations reported **improper and illegal storage** of dangerous chemicals.
- Safety and distance requirements for hazardous storage near residential and commercial areas became a major focus after the disaster.
- The incident highlighted risks created when regulatory enforcement does not match the scale of industrial growth.

## Emergency Response and Environmental Concerns

- Large-scale firefighting and evacuation efforts followed, with significant disruption to the port and surrounding neighborhoods.
- Public attention also focused on potential contamination from stored chemicals and the management of runoff and debris.
- Authorities conducted cleanup operations and monitoring, while residents raised concerns about longer-term health and environmental impacts.

## Legal and Administrative Aftermath

- Chinese authorities pursued criminal and administrative accountability, including prosecutions tied to safety violations and corruption.
- By 2016, dozens of individuals—including company executives and officials—had been convicted in connection with the disaster.

## Broader Significance

- The Tianjin explosions became a landmark industrial-safety case in China.
- The disaster is often cited in discussions about hazardous-materials regulation, emergency-response preparedness, and transparency after major accidents.
