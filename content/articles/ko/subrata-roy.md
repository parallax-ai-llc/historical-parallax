## 요약

Chairman of the Sahara Group ordered to refund $3.5 billion to investors in illegal bond schemes. Jailed for contempt of court by the Indian Supreme Court.

## Sahara Group and the Dispute (High Level)

Subrata Roy led Sahara India Pariwar, a conglomerate with businesses spanning finance, real estate, and media. The most widely reported legal conflict centered on fundraising instruments associated with Sahara entities and regulatory findings that investors were owed refunds. The matter became a long-running, high-profile case involving Indian courts and securities regulation.

## Regulatory and Legal Context

Large-scale investor refund cases typically involve questions about:

- Whether the instruments were properly registered and disclosed under securities rules
- The size and verifiability of the investor list (including identity and contact details)
- The mechanism for returning funds, including escrow or court-supervised processes

In India, such disputes can involve the Securities and Exchange Board of India (SEBI) and the Supreme Court, especially when enforcement and compliance become contested.

## Detention and Court Oversight

Roy’s jailing for contempt is commonly discussed as an example of the judiciary’s role in compelling compliance with refund orders. Release conditions in such cases can be tied to partial payments, guarantees, or other court-imposed requirements.

## Later Years

Public reporting (including Wikipedia summaries) states that Roy died in 2023. The Sahara-related litigation and refund process is often discussed independently of his later personal status because it involves institutional records, investor claims, and court-supervised compliance.

In investor-protection discussions, the case is frequently cited as a stress test of verification, enforcement, and restitution capacity at very large scale.
