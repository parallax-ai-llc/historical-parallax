## 개요
The Dnepropetrovsk maniacs were three Ukrainian teenagers — Viktor Sayenko, Igor Suprunyuk, and Alexander Hanzha — who murdered 21 people in 2007. They filmed their killings. Two received life sentences.

## 배경 and Modus Operandi (high-level)
- The group is commonly referred to as the **“Dnepropetrovsk maniacs”** in English-language sources.
- Crimes were committed over a concentrated period in **2007**.
- Victims were typically attacked in public or semi-public spaces; accounts emphasize opportunistic targeting.
- Some killings were **filmed**, and a portion of the material circulated online, drawing international attention.
