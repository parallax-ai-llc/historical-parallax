Both admirers and critics agree that he was pompous, arrogant, highly valuing his opinions and showing little patience with anyone who disagreed with him.[^158] His use of bitter invective, dismissing critics as "jackasses," "pigs," "beasts," "idiots," and "blockheads," suggests intellectual arrogance rather than the humility appropriate to genuine scientific inquiry.[^159] His stubborn personality and pathological taste for controversy repeatedly got him into trouble, and his wanderings ~을 통해 Europe had as much to do with his difficult character as with 박해 for his ideas.[^160]

Some scholars argue that Bruno's diplomatic awkwardness and inability to moderate his behavior—such as dedicating to the virgin 여왕 Elizabeth a text stating that women without offspring "should be considered more useless than a poisonous mushroom"—demonstrates 가난한 judgment that undermined his own causes.[^161] ~하는 동안 he was fairly 성공적인 at finding sympathetic patrons, he invariably alienated them ~을 통해 his behavior, suggesting that personal failings rather than systematic 박해 explain much of his troubled career.[^162]

Critics also question whether Bruno's philosophical system was coherent, noting tensions between his monistic metaphysics and his dualist magical theory, and between his materialistic atomism and his spiritual pantheism.[^163] Some scholars characterize his thought as representing "incoherent materialistic pantheism" rather than a systematic 철학 that could serve as a foundation for 현대의 과학.[^164]

Finally, critics note that few of Bruno's specific scientific claims were actually correct or 영향력 있는 on subsequent scientific developments.[^165] ~하는 동안 the universe did turn out to be heliocentric and vastly larger than 중세의 cosmology supposed, Bruno's specific theories about how this universe functioned, the nature of matter, the role of soul in physical processes, and countless other details were either wrong or too vague to be empirically testable.[^166] In this view, Bruno deserves recognition as a bold speculative thinker but not as a founder of 현대의 과학 or a scientific martyr.[^167]

## 유명한 인용문

> "Perhaps your fear in passing judgment on me is greater than mine in receiving it."

Said to his inquisitors upon receiving his death sentence on January 20, 1600, this defiant statement exemplifies Bruno's refusal to be intimidated by the prospect of 처형 and his conviction that those who condemned free thought had more to fear than those who practiced it.[^189]

> "There is no law governing all things."

A reflection of Bruno's philosophical rejection of rigid Aristotelian categories and his vision of an infinite, dynamic universe not constrained by the finite laws proposed by 전통적인 natural 철학.[^190]

> "Believe that nothing is impossible for you, think yourself immortal and capable of understanding all, all arts, all sciences, the nature of every living being. Mount higher than the highest height; descend lower than the lowest depth."

From Bruno's hermetic writings, this quote encapsulates his belief in humanity's capacity for infinite knowledge and spiritual ascent, reflecting his Neoplatonic vision of the soul's potential to comprehend the cosmos.[^191]

> "Time is the father of truth, its mother is our mind."

A philosophical reflection on epistemology, suggesting that truth emerges ~을 통해 the combination of temporal development and human intellect, rather than being revealed ~을 통해 divine authority alone.[^192]

> "It is proof of a base and low mind for one to wish to think with the masses or majority, merely because the majority is the majority. Truth does not change because it is, or is not, believed by a majority of the people."

This statement reflects Bruno's conviction that truth is independent of popular opinion and his rejection of arguments from authority or consensus—a position that characterized his entire intellectual life and 에 기여했다 his conflicts with religious and academic establishments.[^193]
