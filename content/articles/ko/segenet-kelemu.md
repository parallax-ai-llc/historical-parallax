## 요약

Segenet Kelemu is an Ethiopian plant pathologist who became first woman to lead ICIPE.

## 경력 연표

| 연도 | 사건 |
|------|-------|
| 1957 | Born in Ethiopia |
| 2013 | Became ICIPE director general |
| 2014 | L'Oreal-UNESCO Award |

## Field and Contributions (High-Level)

- **Discipline:** Plant pathology and related agricultural biosciences.
- Work commonly associated with:
  - understanding plant diseases and their economic impact,
  - improving crop resilience and yield stability,
  - and strengthening research institutions that support food security.

## 지도부

- She became the first woman to lead the **International Centre of Insect Physiology and Ecology (ICIPE)**.
- As director-general, she was associated with:
  - expanding scientific programs,
  - building international collaborations,
  - and increasing the visibility of African-led research.

## Recognition

- She received international recognition including the **L'Oréal–UNESCO For Women in Science** award.
- Such awards typically highlight both research excellence and leadership that broadens participation in science.

## 중요성

- Her career is frequently cited as an example of:
  - Ethiopian and African scientific leadership at global institutions,
  - the role of applied bioscience in development outcomes,
  - and the importance of supporting women in STEM at senior levels.
