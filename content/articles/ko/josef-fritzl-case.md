오스트리아n incest and captivity case where 요제프 프리츨 imprisoned his daughter Elisabeth in a cellar for 24 years from 1984-2008, fathering seven children through rape.

## Discovery of the Case (2008)

- The case became public in 2008 after one of Elisabeth Fritzl's children, Kerstin, became seriously ill and was taken to a hospital, prompting scrutiny of the family's accounts.
- Investigators learned that Elisabeth had been held captive for decades in a concealed cellar area at the family home in Amstetten, Lower 오스트리아.

## Charges and Conviction

- 요제프 프리츨 was prosecuted for multiple crimes reported to include rape, false imprisonment (deprivation of liberty), incest, coercion, and negligent homicide.
- He pleaded guilty in 2009 and received a life sentence, with 오스트리아n law allowing a parole review after a minimum period.

## Victims and Aftermath

- The captivity and abuse resulted in the birth of seven children; reports indicate that some children were raised in captivity while others were raised in the household above.
- The case drew international attention to issues of family violence, victim isolation, and long-term trauma, as well as the challenges of detection when abuse occurs within private spaces.

## Institutional and Social Impact (High-Level)

- The case prompted broader public discussion in 오스트리아 and beyond about safeguarding, social services, and the warning signs of coercive control.
- Media coverage raised ethical questions about privacy and the protection of victims, particularly regarding identification, long-term care, and reintegration.
