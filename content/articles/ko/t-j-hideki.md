## 요약

Prime Minister of Japan who led the country into WWII and oversaw atrocities across Asia. He was convicted of war crimes by the Tokyo Tribunal and executed by hanging.

## 추가 맥락

### Early life and military career

Tōjō Hideki was a career officer in the Imperial Japanese Army who rose through the ranks in the interwar period. His advancement occurred alongside Japan’s increasing militarization and expansion on the Asian continent, and he became associated with hardline security and wartime mobilization policies.

### Prime minister during World War II

As prime minister during a critical phase of the Pacific War, Tōjō led a wartime cabinet and was closely tied to Japan’s strategic decisions and the conduct of the war. Histories of the period often discuss the role of cabinet government, military influence, and imperial authority in decision-making, with Tōjō positioned as a central figure within that system.

### Defeat, arrest, and trial

After Japan’s defeat, Tōjō was arrested by Allied occupation authorities. He was prosecuted before the International Military Tribunal for the Far East (commonly called the Tokyo Trials), convicted of war crimes, and executed in 1948.

### 유산

Tōjō remains a controversial historical figure. In Japan and internationally, he is frequently referenced in debates over war responsibility, historical memory, and how wartime leaders are portrayed in education and public commemoration.
