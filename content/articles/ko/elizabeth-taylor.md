## 개요

Elizabeth Taylor's literary career spanned three decades and produced a remarkable body of work consisting of twelve novels, four short story collections, and one children's book. ## 논란과 비판

~에도 불구하고 the consistently positive 비판적인 reception of her work, Elizabeth Taylor never achieved the widespread recognition that many believed she deserved. Taylor "never earned a literary reputation that would reflect the generally good reviews of her work," with Kingsley Amis attributing her lack of recognition to her concentration on domestic life.[^34] Some critics labeled her works as "women's novels" that were "frequently vilified" by the literary establishment.[^35]

In the "Angry Decade" of the 1950s and the socially and politically conscious 1960s, when the prevailing fashion in fiction emphasized "size, scope, relevance and Importance," Taylor's work was too often condescended to as high-class "women's novels."[^36] She was part of a generation of "women writers" working in post-First World 전쟁 Britain whose reputations remained trapped within the prison of "domestic romance," and most were overshadowed by a younger generation of Angry 젊은 Men who dominated the literary scene.[^37]

Her shrewd but affectionate portrayals of middle-class and upper-middle-class English life won her an audience of discriminating readers and loyal friends in 세계 of letters, even if the broader literary establishment failed to canonize her ~ 동안 her lifetime.[^42]

## 유산

Elizabeth Taylor's literary legacy has experienced a remarkable posthumous revival, transforming her from an underrated 작가 to one increasingly recognized as among the best British novelists of the twentieth 세기.[^58] Her work has been championed by major publishers including Virago 현대의 Classics and New York Review Books, which have reissued her novels with new introductions by 뛰어난 writers.[^59]
