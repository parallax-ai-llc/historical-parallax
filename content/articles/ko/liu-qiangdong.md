## 개요

류창둥, also known as Richard Liu, is the founder and executive chairman of JD.com (Jingdong), one of 중국's largest e-commerce companies and a Fortune Global 500 enterprise. Born in rural Jiangsu province in 1974, he built JD.com from a small electronics counter in 베이징 into a technology and logistics powerhouse that competes directly with Alibaba across retail, cloud computing, and supply chain services.

His significance lies not only in JD.com's scale — serving hundreds of millions of customers across 중국 — but in his insistence on building a proprietary logistics network at a time when competitors outsourced fulfillment. This decision defined JD's reputation for speed and reliability and reshaped expectations for e-commerce delivery across 아시아.

Liu's story is one of dramatic upward mobility, strategic boldness, and serious personal controversy. He remains one of the most influential and polarizing figures in Chinese technology business.

## Early Life & Background

류창둥 was born on February 14, 1974, in Suqian, Jiangsu province, into a family of boat operators. His parents worked the Grand Canal trade routes, and the family lived in relative poverty. He showed academic promise early and in 1992 was admitted to Renmin University of 중국 in 베이징, where he studied sociology. To support himself and send money home, he learned computer programming and took on freelance coding work during his university years.

After graduating in 1996, he worked briefly at a 일본ese pharmaceutical company, gaining exposure to corporate operations before deciding to strike out on his own.

## 두각

In 1998, Liu opened a small counter selling magneto-optical products in 베이징's Zhongguancun technology district, naming the business Jingdong. The venture expanded steadily into electronics retail, eventually operating twelve physical stores. The 2003 SARS outbreak forced him to close his stores and pivot online — a turning point that proved transformative. He launched jdlaser.com (later JD.com) and never returned to brick-and-mortar retail.

By 2007, recognizing that third-party delivery in 중국 was unreliable, Liu made the controversial decision to build JD's own warehousing and last-mile delivery infrastructure. The move required enormous capital and skepticism from investors, but it became JD's defining competitive advantage. JD.com went public on the Nasdaq in May 2014, raising approximately $1.78 billion in its IPO — one of the largest U.S. listings by a Chinese company at that time.

## Key Achievements

- Founded JD.com in 1998; grew it into a Fortune Global 500 company with revenues exceeding $130 billion annually by the mid-2020s.
- Built one of 중국's most extensive proprietary logistics networks, with hundreds of warehouses and a delivery fleet covering nearly all Chinese counties.
- Secured a strategic investment from Tencent in 2014, embedding JD into the WeChat ecosystem and reaching hundreds of millions of additional consumers.
- Expanded JD into healthcare (JD Health), technology services (JD Technology), and industrial supply chains.

## Controversies & Criticism

In August 2018, Liu was arrested in Minneapolis, Minnesota, on a felony rape allegation made by a Chinese graduate student at the University of Minnesota. U.S. prosecutors declined to file charges, and Liu returned to 중국. The accuser subsequently filed a civil lawsuit in the 미국; in 2022, a federal judge allowed the case to proceed. The litigation attracted sustained international media coverage and raised questions about corporate governance at JD.com. Liu stepped back from day-to-day CEO responsibilities in 2022, transitioning to executive chairman, though he retained significant control over the company.

## Legacy & Influence

류창둥 fundamentally altered 중국's retail and logistics landscape. JD's logistics subsidiary, JD Logistics, became an independent listed company in 2021, demonstrating that the infrastructure-first model he championed had standalone commercial value. His approach influenced competitors and pushed the entire Chinese e-commerce sector toward faster, more reliable delivery standards. Despite personal legal troubles, his business legacy — transforming a street-market stall into a global-scale technology enterprise within two decades — remains a defining example of 중국's private-sector economic rise.
