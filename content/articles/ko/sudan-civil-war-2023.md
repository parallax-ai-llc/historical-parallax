## 요약

Civil war between the Sudanese Armed Forces and the Rapid Support Forces that began on April 15, 2023. The conflict has killed thousands, displaced 9 million, and caused a humanitarian catastrophe.

## 추가 맥락

### 배경

The April 2023 outbreak followed a prolonged power struggle between Sudan’s regular military leadership and the Rapid Support Forces (RSF), a powerful paramilitary organization that grew out of earlier militia structures. Disagreements over security-sector reform, command hierarchy, and the integration of forces into a single national army contributed to escalating tensions.

### Pattern of Fighting

The conflict has featured urban battles (including in Khartoum), fighting over key infrastructure (airports, bridges, and military bases), and shifting control across multiple regions. As the war continued, violence expanded beyond the capital into other parts of Sudan, including areas that were already fragile after years of political instability.

### Humanitarian Impact (High-Level)

- Large-scale displacement inside Sudan and cross-border refugee flows into neighboring countries.
- Severe disruption of hospitals, water systems, electricity, and supply chains in major cities.
- Food insecurity worsened by conflict-related access constraints and damage to markets and transport corridors.

### 국제적 대응

Diplomatic efforts have included attempted ceasefires, mediation initiatives, and calls for protection of civilians and humanitarian corridors. International organizations and aid agencies have repeatedly emphasized the need for sustained access for relief operations.
