## 요약

Sara Josephine Baker was a pioneering public health physician who dramatically reduced infant mortality in New York City's tenements. She established the Bureau of Child Hygiene, introduced pasteurized milk stations, trained midwives, and developed the baby health stations that reduced infant deaths by over 40%. Her model was adopted nationwide.
