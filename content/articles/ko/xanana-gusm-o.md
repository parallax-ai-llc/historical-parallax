## 배경

- **José Alexandre "Xanana" Gusmão** is a central political figure in Timor-Leste (East Timor), associated with the country’s independence struggle and post-independence state-building.
- Widely recognized internationally as a leading voice of East Timorese self-determination during the Indonesian occupation.

## Role in the Independence Movement

- Became a prominent leader in the resistance, including leadership associated with armed and political wings of the movement as it evolved.
- Was **captured and imprisoned by Indonesian authorities** in the 1990s, but continued to serve as a symbolic and practical leader of the resistance while incarcerated.
- After the 1999 referendum process and transition to independence, moved into formal national leadership.

## 정치 경력 (high-level)

- **President (2002–2007):** Served as the first president of the independent Democratic Republic of Timor-Leste.
- **Prime Minister (2007–2015):** Returned to executive government leadership after the presidency.
- Remained an influential figure in coalition-building and national policy debates, including security, governance, and development priorities.

## 유산 and Significance

- Often described as a bridge between the resistance generation and the institutions of the new state.
- His career is frequently used as a lens for understanding Timor-Leste’s transition from occupation to independence and the challenges of consolidating democratic governance.
