## 개요

북한n commando who led the failed 1968 raid on the 대한민국n presidential Blue House.

## 배경

- 김신조 (김신조) was a member of a 북한n special operations unit tasked with infiltration into 대한민국.
- His name is primarily associated with the January 1968 "Blue House raid" attempt, aimed at assassinating 대한민국n President Park Chung-hee.

## 1968 Blue House Raid (Overview)

- A team of 북한n commandos infiltrated across the Demilitarized Zone (비무장지대) and moved toward 서울.
- The operation failed before reaching the presidential compound (the Blue House).
- The incident triggered a major security response in 대한민국 and became one of the most widely cited 냉전-era infiltration attempts on the peninsula.

## Capture and Aftermath

- 김신조 was captured alive during the manhunt that followed the failed raid.
- According to widely reported accounts, most of the infiltrators were killed during subsequent engagements, while a small number were not captured.
- His capture was used in 대한민국 as a high-profile example of 북한n commando operations and as part of broader counter-infiltration efforts.

## Later Life (대한민국)

- After his capture, 김신조 later became known in 대한민국 as a public figure connected to security education and anti-infiltration awareness.
- He is often described in secondary sources as having lived in 대한민국 for decades following the incident.
- Public reporting on his exact later-life details varies by source, and biographies commonly rely on press and interview material rather than primary documents.

## Historical Significance

- The Blue House raid is frequently discussed alongside other late-1960s incidents that increased tensions on the 한반도.
- It contributed to heightened domestic security measures in 대한민국 and reinforced the perception of ongoing unconventional threats.
- The event remains a reference point in discussions of 북한n special forces doctrine and inter-Korean confrontation during the 냉전.
