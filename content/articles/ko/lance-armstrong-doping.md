## 배경

The 랜스 암스트롱 doping scandal centers on allegations and subsequent findings that Armstrong and associates used performance-enhancing drugs (PEDs) and blood-manipulation methods during professional cycling competition, including years in which Armstrong won the Tour de 프랑스. The case became one of the most prominent and consequential anti-doping episodes in modern sport.

## Alleged methods

Public reporting and anti-doping summaries commonly describe a program that involved multiple tactics rather than a single banned substance. These have included:

- Use of erythropoietin (EPO) and other substances intended to increase oxygen-carrying capacity.
- Blood transfusions (autologous or otherwise) designed to boost performance and evade detection windows.
- Coordinated timing around testing regimes and racing schedules.

Specific details have been contested over time, but the narrative of systematic, organized doping is central to most mainstream accounts.

## Investigation and sanctions

The U.S. Anti-Doping Agency (USADA) pursued a case that relied in part on witness testimony and non-analytical evidence, reflecting how anti-doping enforcement can proceed even when direct positive tests are limited or absent. In 2012, sanctions included disqualification of results and loss of titles recognized by governing bodies. The episode highlighted the role of whistleblowers and former teammates in anti-doping investigations.

## Public admission

In 2013, Armstrong admitted to doping in a televised interview with Oprah Winfrey, an event that shaped public perception of the scandal and clarified that the case was not limited to isolated incidents. The admission became a reference point in discussions about athlete accountability and media narratives.

## Wider consequences

The scandal had ripple effects beyond Armstrong as an individual:

- It intensified scrutiny of cycling’s governance and team medical practices.
- It contributed to reforms in testing strategy, biological passports, and investigative approaches.
- It influenced how sponsors, charities, and sports organizations evaluate reputational risk.

## Cultural and institutional impact

Armstrong’s rise and fall is often discussed as a case study in the tension between extraordinary athletic achievement and the incentives created by competitive, commercialized sport. It is also used in ethics and governance contexts to examine how institutions respond when a high-profile figure is implicated in systemic rule-breaking.
