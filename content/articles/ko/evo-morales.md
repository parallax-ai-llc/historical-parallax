## 주요 업적

Morales's presidency brought transformative changes to Bolivia, particularly in the economic and social spheres. Upon taking office in January 2006, he immediately moved to fulfill his campaign promise of nationalizing Bolivia's natural gas reserves, the second-largest in South America. Morales's government also prioritized indigenous rights and cultural recognition. On the international stage, Morales positioned Bolivia as a leader of Latin America's "Pink Tide" of leftist governments, forming close alliances with Venezuela's Hugo Chávez, Cuba's Fidel Castro, and Ecuador's Rafael Correa. He challenged U.S. influence in the region, expelled the U.S. 대사 and Drug Enforcement Administration in 2008, and withdrew Bolivia from an international arbitration convention he viewed as favoring corporations over states.[^22]

Evo Morales's legacy is deeply contested, reflecting Bolivia's profound polarization and the complex, contradictory nature of his presidency.
