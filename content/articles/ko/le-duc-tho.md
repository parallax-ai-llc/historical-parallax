## 개요

베트남ese communist leader who directed the war effort and was offered but declined the 노벨 평화상.

## 배경

- Born in what is now Nam Định Province in French Indochina.
- Joined the Indochinese Communist movement in the early 20th century and spent periods imprisoned by French colonial authorities.

## Roles in the 베트남ese Communist Party

- Became a senior organizer within the Lao Dong (Workers') Party / Communist Party of 베트남.
- Often worked on party organization, security, and strategy rather than holding a single long-lived public-facing government portfolio.
- Served on top leadership bodies (including the Politburo) during the 베트남전쟁 era.

## 베트남전쟁 and Negotiations

- Widely associated with directing and coordinating the North 베트남ese war effort and political strategy.
- Was a chief negotiator for North 베트남 in the 파리 Peace talks with the 미국.
- Negotiations culminated in the 1973 파리 Peace Accords, which provided for U.S. withdrawal and a framework for ceasefire arrangements.

## 노벨 평화상 (1973)

- The 노벨 평화상 for 1973 was awarded jointly to U.S. Secretary of State Henry Kissinger and 레 득 토 for the 파리 Peace Accords.
- 레 득 토 declined the prize, stating that peace had not actually been achieved in 베트남.
- The award was highly controversial due to continued fighting and the broader conduct of the war.

## After 1975

- Continued as a high-level figure in 베트남's post-war leadership.
- Remained influential in party affairs into the late 1980s.

## 논란

- 레 득 토's legacy is contentious; he is praised in 베트남 for wartime leadership and diplomacy but criticized internationally for the communist party state's security apparatus and wartime/post-war repression.
- Descriptions such as "war criminal" appear in some political or activist contexts; assessments vary by source and legal forum.

## 유산

- Remembered primarily for his role in the 파리 Peace negotiations and for being the only person to have formally declined a 노벨 평화상.
- A prominent example in debates about whether peace prizes should be awarded amid ongoing conflict.
