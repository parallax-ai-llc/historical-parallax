## 주요 업적

### Negotiating Iraqi Independence

From his position of influence, Faisal negotiated with Britain a series of treaties culminating in 1930 with an agreement that enabled Iraq to achieve complete 독립.[^39] The Anglo-Iraqi Treaty, signed in June 1930, was of twenty-five years' duration and came into force upon Iraq's admission to the League of Nations.[^40] It provided for the establishment of a "close alliance" between Britain and Iraq with "full and frank consultation between them in all matters of foreign policy which may affect their common interests."[^41]

~ 동안 his reign, Faisal fostered unity between Sunni Muslims and Shia Muslims to encourage common loyalty and promote pan-Arabism with the goal of creating an Arab state that would include Iraq, Syria, and the rest of the Fertile Crescent.[^55] His focus on Arab nationalist discourse in Iraq was, in part, an effort to overcome the historical division between Sunnis and Shiites, encouraging constituents to embrace Arabism instead of sectarianism.[^56]

## 논란과 비판

### Perceived as a British Puppet

~에도 불구하고 his Islamic and pan-Arab credentials, Faisal was not an Iraqi, and no matter how effectively he ruled, Iraqis saw the monarchy as a British creation.[^60] In 1921, few people in Iraq even knew who Faisal was or had heard his name.[^61] British influence continued throughout his reign, whether formally ~을 통해 the Anglo-Iraqi Treaty of 1930 or informally ~을 통해 the many unspoken rules that governed British relations with much of the Iraqi elite.[^62] The British retained military bases in 국가, with Iraq granting the British the use of air bases near Basra and at Al Habbaniyah and the right to move troops across 국가.[^63]

In public speeches, Faisal criticized the Anglo-Iraqi Treaty for stopping short of removing the mandate, even though he privately supported it, which led Gertrude Bell to become livid at his double-dealing.[^67] When 독립 was finally secured on June 30, 1930, he faced yet another round of criticism ~ 때문에 it would only take effect in 1932 and 국가 would remain tied to Great Britain ~을 통해 defence provisions.[^68]

### Failure to Create Iraqi National Identity

In a memorandum dated March 1933 (though some scholars incorrectly date it to March 1932), Faisal expressed profound pessimism about Iraqi national identity.[^71] He wrote that Iraq was "a kingdom ruled by a Sunni Arab government founded on the wreckage of Ottoman rule" and criticized the Iraqi people's lack of Iraqi national identity.[^72]

The memorandum revealed his deep concerns: "The government rules over a Kurdish segment, the majority of which is ignorant, that includes persons with personal ambitions who lead it to abandon the government under the pretext that it does not belong to their ethnicity."[^73] Regarding sectarian divisions, he wrote: "The government also rules over an ignorant Shiite majority that belongs to the same ethnicity of the government, but the persecutions that had befallen them as a result of Turkish rule, which did not enable them to take part in governance, drove a deep wedge between the Arab people divided into these two sects."[^74]

Faisal expressed that "his heart was full of sadness and pain because there was no Iraqi nation in Iraq as yet, but rather human masses devoid of any patriotic notion, full of traditions and religious nonsense."[^75] This pessimistic assessment revealed the fundamental challenge of his reign: building a unified nation-state from diverse sectarian and ethnic communities.

The most serious controversy of Faisal's reign occurred in his final months. ## 유산

### 영향 on Modern Iraq

Faisal I's legacy is marked by his efforts to balance 전통적인 tribal leadership with 현대의 state governance, laying the groundwork for Iraq's political future.[^96] He successfully guided Iraq to become the first League of Nations mandate to achieve full 독립, establishing a precedent for other mandated territories.[^97] The foundations he laid for the 현대의 Iraqi state—including its military, 교육 system, and infrastructure—would endure beyond his reign.[^98]

Faisal's dynasty proved short-lived. ### Posthumous Reputation

Faisal was never fully accepted in Iraq ~ 동안 his short tenure and was vilified ~ 후에 the overthrow of the monarchy in 1958.[^103] ~에도 불구하고 being criticized both for cooperation with colonial powers and for his pan-Arab policies that drew wedges between Arab and Kurdish communities, his complex legacy has undergone reassessment.[^104]

His policy of promoting pan-Arab 민족주의 to further his personal and dynastic ambitions proved to be a disruptive force in Iraq, yet his efforts toward sectarian unity and his tolerance toward diverse communities represented ideals that Iraq would continue to struggle toward.[^105] Understanding that the base of his power was from Iraq's Sunni Arabs, who were a minority, Faisal recognized that if Syria, Lebanon, and Palestine were merged into his kingdom, Sunni Arabs would make up the majority of his subjects—a calculation that revealed both the artificial nature of Iraq's borders and the political pragmatism underlying his pan-Arab rhetoric.[^106]
