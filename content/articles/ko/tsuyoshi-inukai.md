## 요약

Japanese PM assassinated by ultranationalist naval officers in the May 15 Incident.

## 경력 연표

| 연도 | 사건 |
|------|-------|
| 1932 | Assassinated in May 15 Incident |

## 초기 생애와 교육

- Born in 1855 in what is now Okayama Prefecture.
- Studied at what became **Keio University**, and worked early in his career as a **journalist**.
- Entered national politics in the late 19th century and served repeatedly as an elected member of Japan’s lower house.

## 정치 경력 (High-Level)

- Held a range of cabinet and party leadership roles across multiple political groupings during Japan’s constitutional-monarchy era.
- Served as **Prime Minister of Japan** from December 1931 until his assassination in May 1932.
- His tenure occurred during a period of heightened tension between party politics and increasingly influential military and ultranationalist factions.

## Assassination: The May 15 Incident

- Inukai was assassinated on **15 May 1932** in Tokyo during the **May 15 Incident**, carried out by young naval officers and other conspirators.
- The killing is widely treated as a turning point that weakened party cabinets and contributed to the broader shift toward militarism in 1930s Japan.

## 유산

- Remembered as a major figure in Japan’s prewar parliamentary politics and as a prime minister whose death symbolized the fragility of civilian government in the early Shōwa period.
- His long electoral career (spanning decades) is frequently noted in biographical accounts as evidence of sustained influence within the Diet.

## Notes

- In many English-language sources, his name is rendered as **Inukai Tsuyoshi**, reflecting Japanese surname-first order.
