## 요약

Polish serial killer known as 'the elegant murderer' who robbed and murdered at least 30 people.

## 배경

Mazurkiewicz was a Polish murderer active in the post–World War II period. Contemporary reporting and later summaries describe him as socially polished and well-dressed, which contributed to the nickname "the elegant murderer."

## 범죄 and Modus Operandi

High-level patterns commonly attributed to the case include:

- **Target selection**: victims were often approached through social and business circles, enabling him to gain trust.
- **Robbery motive**: killings were associated with theft of money and valuables.
- **Use of deception**: he allegedly relied on charm and plausible cover stories to lure victims into vulnerable situations.

## 수사 and Trial

- **Case building**: investigators linked multiple disappearances and deaths through financial traces and witness accounts.
- **Charges**: sources commonly cite responsibility for at least 30 murders, though exact totals vary by account.
- **Outcome**: he was convicted and ultimately executed in 1957.

## 유산

Mazurkiewicz remains one of Poland's most notorious 20th-century serial killers. The "elegant murderer" label is often used in summaries to highlight the contrast between his public persona and the brutality of his crimes.
