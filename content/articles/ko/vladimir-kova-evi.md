## 요약

Serbian military officer found unfit to stand trial at the ICTY for the shelling of Dubrovnik's Old Town in 1991. He suffered severe PTSD and was returned to Serbian custody.

## Additional Context

## Siege and Shelling of Dubrovnik

Kovačević is discussed in connection with the 1991 attack on Dubrovnik during the breakup of Yugoslavia.

The shelling of Dubrovnik’s Old Town drew particular condemnation because:

- The area is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.
- Cultural heritage sites are protected under the laws of armed conflict.
- The attack became a prominent example of damage to civilian and cultural infrastructure.

## ICTY Indictment and Fitness to Stand Trial

International tribunals must determine whether an accused can meaningfully participate in proceedings.

In this case, the finding that Kovačević was unfit to stand trial illustrates:

- The legal requirement for mental competence in criminal proceedings.
- The practical limits of international justice when defendants suffer severe illness.
- The procedural outcome of transferring custody back to national authorities.

## Broader Significance

The Dubrovnik cases are often referenced in discussions of:

- Accountability for attacks on protected sites.
- The documentation of wartime events through media, observers, and later investigations.
- The relationship between individual responsibility and broader command structures.
