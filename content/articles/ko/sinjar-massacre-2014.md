## 요약

ISIS attacked the Yazidi people of Sinjar, killing 5,000 men and abducting 7,000 women and girls as sex slaves. Recognized as genocide by the UN.

## 배경

- In 2014, the Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant (ISIL/ISIS) conducted a rapid offensive across parts of northern Iraq.
- The Yazidis, a distinct ethno-religious minority with deep roots in the Sinjar region, were specifically targeted during this campaign.

## What Happened (High Level)

- ISIS forces attacked communities in and around Sinjar, resulting in large-scale killings and the capture of civilians.
- Many Yazidi families fled toward Mount Sinjar, where thousands became stranded in harsh conditions and faced an acute humanitarian crisis.
- Abductions—particularly of women and girls—were accompanied by systematic abuses, including sexual slavery, forced conversion, and trafficking.

## Humanitarian and International Response

- The crisis prompted urgent international attention and relief operations, including efforts to deliver food, water, and medical aid to people trapped or displaced.
- The events around Sinjar became a major driver of wider displacement from northern Iraq, with survivors seeking safety in other parts of Iraq and abroad.

## Recognition, Accountability, and Aftermath

- International bodies and multiple governments have described the ISIS campaign against the Yazidis as genocide.
- Documentation efforts have included survivor testimony, forensic investigations, and the identification of mass graves.
- Legal proceedings related to ISIS crimes have occurred in several jurisdictions, contributing to the broader record of crimes committed during the conflict.

## 유산

- The Sinjar massacre is widely referenced as one of the clearest examples of ISIS’s mass-atrocity violence and of the vulnerability of minority communities during state collapse and war.
- Commemoration and survivor advocacy have remained central to calls for justice, reparations, and the safe return of displaced Yazidis.
