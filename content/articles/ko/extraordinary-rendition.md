## 개요

## Definition and Mechanics

- “Extraordinary rendition” generally refers to the secret transfer of detainees outside normal judicial or extradition processes.
- In the post-9/11 context, it is closely associated with CIA detention and interrogation operations, including the use of undisclosed facilities (“black sites”).
- Transfers often relied on foreign partners, air transport logistics, and classification that limited public oversight.

## Legal and Ethical Controversy

- Critics argued the program undermined due process and facilitated 고문 or cruel, inhuman, or degrading treatment.
- Supporters framed it as a counterterrorism tool aimed at disrupting plots and interrogating high-value suspects.
- The controversy intersected with U.S. and international legal obligations, including human-rights law and prohibitions on 고문.

## Public Exposure and Oversight

- Investigative reporting and subsequent government inquiries brought details of the program into 대중 record.
- The subject became central to debates about intelligence accountability, executive power in wartime, and transparency.

## International Impact

- Allegations of detention sites and transit operations in multiple countries created diplomatic fallout and domestic investigations abroad.
- Human-rights organizations pursued documentation and legal remedies for detainees who alleged abuse.
