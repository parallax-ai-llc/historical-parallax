## 개요

Korean Central Intelligence Agency kidnapped dissidents abroad, tortured suspects, and assassinated President Park Chung-hee. Its director 김재규 shot Park at a dinner in 1979.

## Origins and Mandate

The Korean Central Intelligence Agency (중앙정보부) was established after the 1961 military coup and became a central instrument of the Park Chung-hee government’s internal security and political control. In practice, the agency combined intelligence collection with domestic surveillance and investigative powers.

## Role in Authoritarian-Era Politics

In historical accounts of 대한민국’s developmental state, the 중앙정보부 is frequently described as a major actor in:

- monitoring and suppressing political opposition
- controlling information and influencing media
- coordinating security policy during periods of martial-law politics

## Notable Allegations and Incidents (high level)

International attention to the 중앙정보부 increased due to allegations of overseas operations against dissidents. One widely cited incident is the 1973 kidnapping of opposition politician 김대중 in 일본, which became a diplomatic and human-rights controversy.

## 1979 Assassination of Park Chung-hee

On 26 October 1979, President Park Chung-hee was shot and killed by 중앙정보부 director 김재규. The event triggered a major political crisis and is often treated as a turning point in 대한민국’s modern political history, preceding another period of military rule and subsequent democratization movements.

## Reorganization and Successor Agencies

The 중앙정보부 was later reorganized and renamed (including periods as the Agency for National Security Planning, and later the National Intelligence Service). These institutional changes are often discussed alongside debates about oversight, democratization, and the boundary between foreign intelligence and domestic political policing.
