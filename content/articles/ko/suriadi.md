## 요약

Indonesian serial killer who murdered at least 10 women in Medan, North Sumatra between 2003 and 2007. He was a traditional healer who killed women seeking his services.

## 배경

Suriadi was reported to have worked as a traditional healer (dukun) in Medan, North Sumatra. Accounts in secondary sources describe him using the trust associated with spiritual or healing services to get potential victims to meet privately.

## Modus Operandi (reported)

Open-source descriptions commonly summarize a pattern in which women seeking treatment or advice were lured to a private setting, killed, and then buried. Reports generally place the crimes in and around Medan during the mid-2000s.

## Investigation and Arrest

The case escalated after multiple missing-person reports and the discovery of bodies near his residence. Police searches reportedly uncovered additional graves, leading to his arrest in 2007.

## Prosecution and Sentence

Indonesian courts convicted Suriadi of multiple murders. In 2009 he was sentenced to death, a penalty available under Indonesian law for aggravated homicide.

## Notes on Sources

Public summaries of this case are relatively short and sometimes inconsistent on totals and dates. Many references cite at least 10 victims, but detailed victim lists are not always available in widely accessible sources.
