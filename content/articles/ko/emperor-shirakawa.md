## 유명한 인용문

> "There are three things that are beyond my control: the waters of the Kamo River, the fall of the dice, and the monks of Mount Hiei." - 황제 Shirakawa

This 유명한 quote reflects both Shirakawa's sense of humor about the limits of imperial power and the genuine challenges he faced, particularly from armed monastic forces that could descend from Mount Hiei to pressure the 법원. The saying has become emblematic of the practical constraints on even the most 강력한 rulers.[^69]

> "The way of the Buddha and the way of governance are not separate paths but one road with two gates." - 황제 Shirakawa

This statement reflects Shirakawa's understanding of the integration between religious authority and political power that characterized his reign. It justified his extensive involvement in Buddhist affairs as part of proper imperial governance rather than a distraction from political duties.[^70]

> "To retire from the throne is not to retire from responsibility, but to assume it more fully." - 황제 Shirakawa

This quote encapsulates Shirakawa's 혁명가 understanding of the abdication not as a relinquishment of power but as a strategic repositioning that allowed more effective exercise of authority. It ~이 되었다 philosophical justification for the insei system.[^71]

> "A temple built in devotion stands longer than a palace built in pride." - 황제 Shirakawa

Reflecting on his extensive construction of Buddhist temples, Shirakawa expressed the view that religious monuments represented more enduring contributions than secular buildings. This quote reveals his genuine religious devotion alongside his political calculations.[^72]

> "The emperor who sits on the throne wears the crown; the emperor who has left the throne wears the responsibility." - 황제 Shirakawa

This statement further articulates the division of authority between the ceremonial functions of the sitting 황제 and the actual decision-making power of the retired 황제, providing ideological support for the cloistered government system.[^73]
