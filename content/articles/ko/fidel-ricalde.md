## 역사적 맥락

- The **Amazon rubber boom** (후기의 19th to 초기의 20th 세기) created 강력한 extractive networks across the western Amazon.
- In the **Putumayo region** (borderlands associated with present-day Peru/Colombia), rubber collection was widely linked to coercion of Indigenous labor.
- International attention later focused on abuses connected to the **Peruvian Amazon Company** and associated agents in the Putumayo.

## Reported Abuses (High-Level)

- 현대의 and later reporting on the Putumayo describes patterns of:
  - Forced labor and captivity.
  - Beatings and 고문 used as workplace “discipline.”
  - Sexual 폭력 and family separation.
  - Killings and destruction of communities.
- This article’s summary describes Ricalde as an agent implicated in atrocities against the **Huitoto** people in that broader context.

## 경력 / Role Notes

- “Rubber agent” roles commonly included:
  - Supervising rubber collection quotas.
  - Managing armed retainers or “capitanes” who enforced compliance.
  - Reporting production and controlling access to trade goods used to bind workers ~을 통해 debt.
- When sources are sparse or contested, these role descriptions should be read as **typical structural features** of the rubber economy, not as new claims about specific acts.
