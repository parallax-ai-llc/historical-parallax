## 배경

Wirathu is a Burmese Buddhist monk who became internationally known for nationalist rhetoric directed against Muslim minorities in Myanmar. His public profile rose in the 2010s as communal tensions and political liberalization created new channels for mass mobilization and media amplification.

## 969 Movement and Nationalist Messaging

- Associated with the **969** branding campaign, which encouraged Buddhist-majority consumer and social solidarity framed in opposition to Muslims.
- His speeches and interviews were widely criticized for portraying Muslims as an existential threat, using language that human-rights observers described as incitement.

## Relationship to Communal Violence

While causal attribution in mass violence is complex, Wirathu’s prominence in nationalist networks and the dissemination of anti-Muslim narratives made him a symbol in international reporting about the conditions that preceded and accompanied violence against the **Rohingya**.

## State Response and Legal Issues

Myanmar authorities at times moved to restrict or prosecute nationalist figures, and Wirathu’s legal status and public activity have fluctuated with changing political conditions.

## Broader Significance

Wirathu is often referenced in studies of modern hate propaganda: how religious authority, identity politics, and social media ecosystems can interact to normalize dehumanizing narratives and facilitate real-world violence.
