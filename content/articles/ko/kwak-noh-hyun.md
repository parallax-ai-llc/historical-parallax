## 개요

Former superintendent of 서울's education system convicted of illegally influencing school admissions. His case highlighted widespread corruption in 대한민국's education system.

## 경력 연표

| 연도 | 사건 |
|------|-------|
| 2010 | Became 서울 superintendent of education |
| 2013 | Convicted of election law violations |
| 2014 | Sentenced to prison |

## Additional Context

### Public role and policy domain

- As superintendent of education in 서울, Kwak held an influential administrative position affecting public schools, budgeting, and admissions-related policy.
- The office is politically salient in 대한민국 because education is closely linked to social mobility and household spending.

### Legal and political controversy

- Kwak's most widely reported legal trouble involved election-law violations connected to his 2010 campaign (including allegations around campaign funding and coordination).
- The case became part of a broader set of 대한민국n public debates about political finance, anti-corruption enforcement, and the governance of education offices.
- His situation is often cited alongside other high-profile prosecutions as an example of how electoral rules are strictly policed in the country.

### Broader significance

- Illustrates how education administration can become a focal point for political conflict in societies where schooling is a major driver of status competition.
- Shows the reputational and institutional fragility of public education governance when corruption allegations emerge.

### Notes

- The key public significance of the case is usually framed around integrity of the electoral process and public trust in education administration.
