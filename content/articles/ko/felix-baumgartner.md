## 개요

Felix Baumgartner jumped from 24 miles above Earth in 2012, breaking the sound barrier ~ 동안 freefall and setting records for highest jump and fastest freefall.

## Additional Context

- **Project framing:** The 2012 jump was conducted as part of the *Red Bull Stratos* project, combining aerospace engineering, pressure-suit design, and high-altitude balloon operations.
- **Key technical constraints:** High-altitude freefall requires managing low pressure, extreme cold, limited aerodynamic control, and the risk of uncontrolled spin.
- **Test program:** Public summaries of the effort describe multiple test ascents and incremental jumps to validate equipment and procedures prior to the record attempt.

## 영향 and Legacy

- **Public science communication:** The event was widely followed as a demonstration of human factors and aerospace safety systems, not only as an athletic stunt.
- **Records and successors:** Subsequent high-altitude jumps by other teams are often discussed in the context of refining techniques and safety margins rather than purely “beating” the numbers.
