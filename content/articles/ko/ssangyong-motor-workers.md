## Expanded Overview

This article concerns the workers and union members involved in the 2009 labor conflict at SsangYong Motor in Pyeongtaek, South Korea, widely remembered for a prolonged factory occupation and a large-scale police operation to end it.[^1]

### 배경

- The dispute emerged in the context of corporate restructuring and proposed mass layoffs.
- Workers and union representatives framed the action as a defense of jobs and a response to what they viewed as unfair cuts.

### 2009 Factory Occupation

- The occupation lasted approximately 77 days and involved barricading parts of the plant.
- Confrontations included the use of improvised defenses by occupiers and forceful tactics by authorities to regain control.
- The episode became one of the most visible labor struggles in South Korea in the late 2000s.

### State Response and Public Attention

- Police and other security forces carried out a major crackdown to end the standoff.
- Media coverage and political debate focused on proportionality, worker safety, and the broader condition of labor rights.

### 여파 및 유산

- The conflict had lasting consequences for participants, including legal proceedings and long-term social and economic hardship for some affected workers.
- The event is often referenced in later discussions of industrial relations, the policing of labor protests, and the human costs of rapid restructuring.
