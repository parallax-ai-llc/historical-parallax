## How the fraud worked (high level)

- Enron used complex accounting structures and special purpose entities (SPEs) to move debt and losses off its balance sheet.
- Aggressive revenue recognition and opaque disclosures 에 기여했다 an inflated perception of profitability and financial health.

## Key actors and institutions

- Senior executives were central to strategy and messaging to investors and analysts.
- External auditors and market intermediaries faced scrutiny for oversight failures and conflicts of interest.

## Collapse and legal consequences

- As confidence eroded, Enron's stock price fell and access to financing deteriorated, culminating in bankruptcy in 후기의 2001.
- Criminal trials later ~의 결과를 가져왔다 major convictions for top executives, and related civil litigation produced large settlements.

## Regulatory and market impact

- The scandal was a major driver of U.S. corporate governance reforms, including the Sarbanes–Oxley Act of 2002.
- It reshaped expectations around internal controls, audit 독립, and disclosure practices.

## 유산

- Enron is frequently used as a case study in ethics, accounting, and organizational culture.
- The name remains shorthand for systemic governance failure and the risks of financial opacity.
