## 배경

- Erdem Gündüz is described as a Turkish performance 예술가 and choreographer associated with 현대의 dance and performance work.[^1]
- He became internationally known in 2013 ~ 동안 Gezi Park protests for a silent, still performance in Istanbul that was widely shared online.[^1]

## The “Standing Man” protest (2013)

- In mid-June 2013, Gündüz stood silently for an extended period in Taksim Square (Istanbul), facing the Atatürk Cultural Center (AKM).[^1]
- The act was intentionally non-violent and wordless: the performance relied on stillness, duration, and public space as its medium.[^1]
- The image and idea spread quickly on social media, and the phrase/hashtag “duran adam” (“standing man”) was used to describe similar copycat demonstrations in other locations.[^1]

## Significance and reception

- The protest is often cited as an example of how minimalist performance can function as political expression, especially under conditions where gatherings are restricted.[^1]
- Observers highlighted the contrast between the protest’s calm posture and the surrounding tension of the Gezi Park period, which amplified its symbolic impact.[^1]
- The event is frequently discussed as a moment where digital circulation (photos, short videos, memes) helped transform a local act into an international news story.[^1]

## Themes (high-level)

- **Silence and visibility:** using quiet presence to attract attention without slogans.
- **Public space as stage:** turning an ordinary square into a site of performance.
- **Replication and participation:** encouraging others to repeat the gesture as a decentralized form of protest.
