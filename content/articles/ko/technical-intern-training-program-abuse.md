## 요약

Japan's Technical Intern Training Program has been widely criticized as a cover for cheap foreign labor exploitation. Workers face abuse, confiscation of passports, and unsafe conditions.

## 추가 맥락

### Program purpose vs. outcomes

The Technical Intern Training Program (TITP) was framed as a skills-transfer and development initiative. Critics argue that, in practice, parts of the program functioned as a labor supply channel for industries facing domestic workforce shortages, creating incentives to prioritize low costs over training and worker protections.

### Commonly reported abuse patterns

Public reporting and advocacy work have highlighted recurring issues such as:

- Excessive recruitment fees and debt bondage risks
- Contract substitution and wage underpayment
- Restrictions on changing employers
- Withholding of documents or coercive workplace control
- Unsafe conditions and inadequate access to grievance mechanisms

### Governance and oversight

The program has involved multiple layers of intermediaries (sending organizations, supervising organizations, and host companies). This structure has been criticized for diffusing accountability and making enforcement uneven, particularly when language barriers and immigration status create additional vulnerability for interns.

### Reform efforts

Announcements of overhauls and replacement frameworks are generally discussed in terms of tightening oversight, reducing intermediaries’ power, strengthening labor rights protections, and aligning the scheme more closely with its stated training mission.
