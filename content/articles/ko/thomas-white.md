## 요약

British operator of the Silk Road 2.0 darknet marketplace who took over after the original Silk Road was shut down. He ran the site under the pseudonym Dread Pirate Roberts 2.

## 맥락: Darknet Marketplaces

- “Darknet markets” are online marketplaces typically accessed via anonymity networks (such as Tor).
- They became widely known for facilitating the sale of illicit goods and services, with payments commonly made using cryptocurrencies.
- The original **Silk Road** drew global attention and was shut down by U.S. authorities in 2013; many successor markets attempted to replicate its model.

## Silk Road 2.0 (High-Level)

- Silk Road 2.0 positioned itself as a continuation of the original marketplace after its takedown.
- The “Dread Pirate Roberts” identity served as a symbolic brand; multiple individuals were alleged or confirmed to have used it across different phases.
- Like other markets of its kind, Silk Road 2.0 faced persistent risks from undercover operations, technical vulnerabilities, and operational mistakes.

## 수사 and Arrest

- The UK National Crime Agency (NCA) arrested White in 2014 in connection with his role operating the site.
- Darknet marketplace cases often involve a mix of digital forensics, blockchain analysis, and traditional investigative methods.

## Sentence and Significance

- In 2019, White received a multi-year prison sentence.
- His case is commonly referenced in discussions about the lifecycle of darknet markets: rapid re-creation after takedowns, followed by further enforcement action.
