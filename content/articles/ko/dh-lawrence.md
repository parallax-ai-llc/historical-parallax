Lawrence's literary output was prodigious and remarkably diverse, encompassing novels, short stories, poetry, plays, essays, travel writing, literary criticism, and even paintings.[^32] His major novels represent some of the most 중요한 achievements in twentieth-세기 English 문학, revolutionizing the novel's treatment of sexuality, psychology, and social relationships.[^33]

Lawrence's literary career was marked by repeated clashes with censorship authorities and moral guardians who found his frank treatment of sexuality obscene and dangerous.[^48] Four of his most 유명한 novels—*Sons and Lovers* (1913), *The Rainbow* (1915), *Women in Love* (1920), and *Lady Chatterley's Lover* (1928)—were subjects of censorship trials or suppression for their radical portrayals of sexuality and their use of explicit language.[^49]

## 역사적 평가

### 긍정적 평가

Lawrence's defenders argue that his exploration of sexuality represented a courageous and necessary challenge to Victorian repression and hypocrisy.[^101] E.M. Critics, particularly feminist scholars, have mounted sustained challenges to Lawrence's reputation ~ 이래로 the 1970s.[^112] Kate Millett's *Sexual Politics* (1970) launched the most 영향력 있는 feminist critique, arguing that Lawrence's novels promoted phallocentric ideology in which women find fulfillment only ~을 통해 submission to male sexual dominance.[^113] Millett's analysis "knocked Lawrence off his Priest-of-Love pedestal, since when he hasn't nearly managed to clamber back on."[^114]
