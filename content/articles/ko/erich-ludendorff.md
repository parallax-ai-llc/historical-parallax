## 유명한 인용문

> "The English soldiers fight like lions." / "True. But don't we know that they are lions led by donkeys." - Exchange allegedly between Ludendorff and 장군 Max Hoffmann ~ 동안 WWI, though the authenticity and exact wording of this quote is disputed by historians. The phrase "lions led by donkeys" became 유명한 as a criticism of British military leadership.[^60]

> "We must ruthlessly make every effort to bring every man and woman capable of work into a job." - From his advocacy for the Auxiliary Services Act of 1916, demonstrating his commitment to total mobilization of German society for 전쟁 effort.[^61]

> "We can no longer win this war, but we must not lose it either." - Statement reportedly made ~ 동안 discussions about unrestricted submarine warfare in 1917, reflecting the increasingly desperate German strategic position.[^62]

> "The army has been stabbed in the back." - Ludendorff's adoption and propagation of the phrase suggested by British 장군 Sir Neill Malcolm, which ~이 되었다 foundation of the destructive stab-in-the-back myth that poisoned Weimar German politics.[^63]

> "I refuse to have anything to do with an armistice. The responsibility must be borne by those circles who have brought us to this pass." - Ludendorff's statement when demanding that civilian politicians negotiate the armistice in October 1918, shifting blame from military to civilian leadership ~에도 불구하고 his own role in Germany's strategic situation.[^64]
