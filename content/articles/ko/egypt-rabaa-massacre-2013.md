## 개요

On August 14, 2013, Egyptian security forces did something that would have been unthinkable two 년 전: they surrounded a protest camp and opened fire on unarmed civilians for hours. By the time the smoke cleared at Rabaa al-Adawiya Square in Cairo, at least 817 people were dead—and likely over 1,000. It was the largest mass killing of demonstrators in a single day in 현대의 history.[^1]

The victims were supporters of Mohamed Morsi, Egypt's first democratically elected 대통령, who had been removed by the military in a coup just weeks earlier. They had gathered in the tens of thousands, camping in the square for six weeks to demand his return. When the security forces came with bulldozers and armored vehicles, they came to kill. Snipers shot from rooftops. Protesters were gunned down as they ran. Bodies piled in makeshift morgues.[^2]

The Rabaa 학살 marked the final death of the Arab Spring in Egypt. The generals who had deposed Morsi—led by Abdel Fattah el-Sisi—would face no consequences. The Muslim Brotherhood was banned, its members imprisoned by the thousands. And 세계, which had once 유명한 Tahrir Square, responded with muted concern and continued arms sales. Rabaa showed that some lives matter less than geopolitics.[^3]

## Historical Background

### The Arab Spring in Egypt

**2011:**

- January 25: Protests began at Tahrir Square
- February 11: Hosni Mubarak resigned ~ 후에 30 years
- Military council (SCAF) took interim control

**The promise:** 민주주의, dignity, 자유

### Morsi's Presidency

**June 2012:**

- **Mohamed Morsi** (Muslim Brotherhood) elected 대통령
- First freely elected civilian leader in Egyptian history

**His government:**

- 논란이 많은 from the start
- November 2012: Decree granting himself broad powers
- Rushed ~을 통해 new 헌법
- Economic struggles continued
- Secular-Islamist divide deepened
- Millions protested against him (Tamarod movement)

**By June 2013:**

- Mass protests demanding his removal
- Military issued ultimatum[^4]

### The Coup

**July 3, 2013:**

- 장군 Abdel Fattah el-Sisi announced Morsi's removal
- 헌법 suspended
- Morsi arrested
- **Military coup (or "revolution" in official terminology)**

**Morsi supporters refused to accept it.** They gathered at Rabaa al-Adawiya Square, near a mosque in Cairo's Nasr City, and at al-Nahda Square near Cairo University.[^5]

## The Sit-In

### Six Weeks of Occupation

**July 3 - August 14, 2013:**

- Tens of thousands of Morsi supporters occupied Rabaa Square
- Tents, stages, medical facilities
- Families with children
- Daily prayers and speeches
- Demanded Morsi's reinstatement

**The government called them terrorists.** The protesters called themselves defenders of 민주주의.

### Escalating Tensions

**July 8:** 초기의 morning attack on protesters at Republican Guard headquarters

- At least 51 killed
- Government claimed soldiers attacked first
- Set the pattern

**International warnings:**

- 인권 Watch, Amnesty warned of impending bloodshed
- EU, US called for restraint
- But Egypt's interim government moved toward confrontation[^6]

## August 14: The Massacre

### The Operation

**5:30 AM:**

- Security forces surrounded Rabaa Square
- Armored vehicles, bulldozers moved in
- Announcements (disputed) told protesters to leave
- Many exit points blocked

### The Killing

**Morning:**

- Police and military advanced from all sides
- Live ammunition from the start
- Snipers on surrounding buildings
- Protesters tried to flee but many routes blocked

**Methods:**

- Automatic weapons
- Snipers
- Armored vehicles running over people
- Teargas (massive amounts)
- Fire (tents burned with people inside)

**Witness accounts:**

- "They were shooting everyone"
- "Dead bodies everywhere"
- "No place to run"

**The mosque:**

- Rabaa al-Adawiya Mosque ~이 되었다 morgue
- Bodies stacked inside
- Hundreds killed in and around it[^7]

### The Hours of Killing

**The operation lasted most of the day:**

- Not a brief dispersal but sustained assault
- Wounded shot again
- Medical volunteers targeted
- Journalists caught in crossfire

**By evening:** Square cleared; bodies removed

### Al-Nahda Square

**Same day:**

- Smaller protest camp at al-Nahda Square also attacked
- Dozens more killed
- Combined death toll exceeded 1,000[^8]

## 여파

### Immediate

**State of emergency declared:**

- Curfews
- Continued 폭력 across Egypt
- Hundreds more killed in following days
- Churches and government buildings attacked (by some Morsi supporters)

**Crackdown on Muslim Brotherhood:**

- Declared terrorist organization (December 2013)
- Thousands arrested
- Death sentences for hundreds (many in mass trials)
- Complete suppression[^10]

### Long-Term

**For Egypt:**

- Sisi became 대통령 (2014)
- Authoritarian rule consolidated
- Tens of thousands political prisoners
- Press 자유 eliminated
- Arab Spring hopes extinguished

**For Morsi:**

- Imprisoned
- Multiple trials
- **Died in courtroom June 2019** (heart attack; UN called for investigation)

### 국제 반응

**Remarkably muted:**

- US suspended some military aid briefly
- Then resumed
- No sanctions on Sisi
- Egypt remained key ally
- Western governments prioritized "stability"

**Human Rights Watch:**

> "One of the world's largest killings of demonstrators in a single day in recent history... The security forces' actions likely amounted to crimes against humanity."

**No one was prosecuted.**[^11]

## 역사적 평가

### Why Did It Happen?

**Immediate:**

- Military unwilling to tolerate prolonged protest
- Morsi supporters refused to disperse
- Mutual escalation

**Structural:**

- Deep state never accepted Morsi
- Military-civilian divide
- Islamist-secular polarization
- Counter-혁명가 sentiment

### Was It Planned?

**Evidence suggests:**

- Security forces prepared for mass casualties
- Hospitals cleared; morgues prepared
- Exit routes deliberately blocked
- Snipers pre-positioned

**This was not crowd control. It was elimination.**[^12]

### Comparison

| Massacre                | Deaths             | Accountability         |

| Tiananmen (1989)        | Hundreds-thousands | None (China)           |
| Bloody Sunday NI (1972) | 14                 | Inquiry 38 years later |

**Rabaa may be the largest single-day massacre of protesters in modern history.**

### The Rabaa Symbol

**Four-fingered salute:**

- R4bia (Arabic for "four" + abbreviation)
- Yellow hand with four fingers raised
- Used by Morsi supporters globally
- Symbol of resistance and memory
- Banned in Egypt[^13]
