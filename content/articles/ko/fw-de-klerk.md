F.W. de Klerk's legacy remains fiercely contested, reflecting the fundamental disagreement about whether he should be remembered primarily as the last leader of apartheid or the facilitator of 민주주의.[^78] This debate intensified following his death, with reactions ranging from international tributes praising his courage to South African activists celebrating his demise.[^79]
