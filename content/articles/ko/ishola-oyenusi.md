## 배경

- Ishola Oyenusi was a Nigerian criminal who became widely known in the early 1970s for violent armed robberies.
- Contemporary accounts describe him as part of a broader wave of urban armed robbery that drew intense public attention and government response.

## Crimes and Public Notoriety

- He was linked in reporting to multiple robberies and killings, contributing to a reputation for extreme violence.
- His case was treated as emblematic of the state’s effort to deter armed robbery through swift prosecution and highly visible punishment.

## Arrest, Trial, and Execution

- Oyenusi was tried and convicted under Nigeria’s armed robbery laws of the period.
- He was executed by firing squad at the Lagos Bar Beach on 8 September 1971.
- The execution was carried out publicly, with large crowds present, and was photographed and reported internationally.

## 역사적 배경

- Public executions at Bar Beach in Lagos were used at the time as a deterrent measure against violent crime.
- The publicity around Oyenusi’s execution is frequently cited in discussions of Nigeria’s 1970s criminal justice policies.

## 유산

- Oyenusi’s name remains a reference point in Nigerian popular memory and media when discussing armed robbery and state responses to violent crime.
- His story is sometimes referenced in later commentary on deterrence, policing, and the ethics of public punishment.
