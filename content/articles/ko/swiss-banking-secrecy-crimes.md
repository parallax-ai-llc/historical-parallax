## 요약

Swiss banking secrecy laws have enabled tax evasion, money laundering, and hiding of stolen assets for decades. Dictators, criminals, and corporations have used Swiss banks to hide billions.

## What Bank Secrecy Did

Swiss bank secrecy has historically limited the disclosure of client information, creating an attractive environment for asset protection. Critics argue that the same confidentiality also reduced friction for illicit finance, including tax evasion, corruption proceeds, and money laundering.

## International Pressure and Reforms

Over time, coordinated pressure from other states and international bodies pushed Switzerland toward greater transparency, including information-sharing agreements intended to reduce offshore tax evasion.

## Commonly Cited Abuse Patterns

- Use of numbered accounts and intermediaries to obscure beneficial ownership
- Placement of politically exposed persons’ assets outside their home jurisdictions
- Complex structures (foundations, trusts, shell companies) used to distance funds from their origin

## Why the Topic Remains Controversial

Debates often balance privacy and financial stability against the social harm of enabling large-scale tax avoidance and the concealment of stolen public wealth.
