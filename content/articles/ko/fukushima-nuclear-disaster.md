## 배경 and Causes

The Fukushima Daiichi accident occurred ~ 후에 the Tōhoku earthquake and tsunami (11 March 2011). The earthquake caused a loss of off-site power, and the subsequent tsunami flooded backup diesel generators and electrical switchgear. With power and many cooling systems disabled, operators struggled to remove decay heat from the reactor cores.

## Emergency Response (High-Level)

In the days ~ 후에 the tsunami, emergency actions included venting to reduce pressure, injecting seawater as an improvised cooling measure, and establishing exclusion zones. ## Longer-Term Impacts

- **Decontamination and waste management:** Cleanup included removal of contaminated topsoil and extensive monitoring, producing large volumes of radioactive waste requiring storage and processing.
- **Decommissioning:** Work has involved stabilizing damaged reactors, treating contaminated water, and planning for retrieval of fuel debris—an engineering effort expected to take decades.
- **Policy effects:** The accident influenced nuclear safety regulation in Japan and internationally, including stress tests, reassessment of severe-accident management, and changes in energy policy debates.
