## 주요 업적

### Economic Growth and the "Brazilian Miracle"

Médici's presidency coincided with a period of extraordinary economic expansion that ~로 알려지게 되었다 the "Brazilian Miracle." From 1969 to 1974, Brazil's GDP grew at an average annual rate of 10.9%, with some sources citing growth as high as 11.16% per year.[^27][^28] This explosive growth was orchestrated primarily by Economics Minister Antônio Delfim Netto, who implemented policies of export-led industrialization, foreign investment attraction, and massive public infrastructure projects.[^29] ~ 동안 this period, GDP expanded by 55.84% and per capita income increased by 42.15%.[^30]

Brazil's stock market soared, and a new consumer culture emerged among the middle and upper classes who benefited from the boom.

### Infrastructure Megaprojects

The Médici administration embarked on ambitious infrastructure projects that transformed Brazil's physical landscape. The Trans-Amazonian Highway (BR-230), inaugurated by Médici in 1972, was conceived as a 4,000-kilometer road cutting ~을 통해 the Amazon rainforest to integrate the interior with Brazil's northeastern coast.[^33] The project was promoted as opening up the Amazon for development and providing land for landless peasants from the drought-stricken Northeast, though its actual impact was far more complex and 논란이 많은.

### International Diplomacy and Anti-Communist Coordination

In December 1971, Médici made a state visit to the 미국, where he met with 대통령 Richard Nixon.[^37] ~ 동안 this meeting, the two leaders discussed coordinating their efforts to combat leftist governments in Latin America. ~에 따르면 declassified memoranda, Nixon asked Médici whether the Chilean military was capable of overthrowing Salvador Allende, and Médici replied that he felt they were and made clear that Brazil was working toward this end.[^38] Nixon stated that "we must try and prevent new Allendes and Castros and try where possible to reverse these trends."[^39]

The two presidents agreed to establish a secret back channel for communication outside normal diplomatic channels, with Henry Kissinger representing Nixon and Brazilian Foreign Minister Gibson Barbosa representing Médici.[^40] Nixon offered discreet aid and money for Brazilian operations against the Allende government and supported Cuban exile efforts to overthrow Castro, "as long as our hand did not appear."[^41] Two years ~ 후에 this December 1971 meeting, the Chilean military under 장군 Augusto Pinochet overthrew Allende in a coup on September 11, 1973.[^42]

### The "Years of Lead" and Systematic Torture

Médici's presidency is historically referred to as the "Years of Lead" (Anos de Chumbo) due to its extreme political repression.[^50] A hardliner among military leaders, Médici sponsored the regime's greatest 인권 abuses, with 박해 and 고문 of dissidents, harassment of journalists, and press censorship becoming ubiquitous.[^51] The military's national security apparatus committed the majority of 고문, murders, and disappearances ~ 동안 his presidency.[^52] ~에 따르면 the Brazilian Amnesty Committee, 170 political opponents were killed ~ 동안 Médici's term.[^53]

Accusations of 고문 increased dramatically ~ 동안 Médici's presidency: there were 308 documented cases between 1964 and 1968, 1,027 in 1969, and 1,206 in 1970.[^59] A report by the International Jurists Commission argued that the premeditated infliction of pain was "systematic and scientific," documenting how medical practitioners collaborated by administering medicines to keep prisoners conscious ~ 동안 extended 고문 sessions.[^60] In the 1980s, the Catholic Vicariate of São Paulo and Protestant ministers obtained thousands of classified documents that detailed the use of 고문 ~ 동안 Médici's term—revelations that shocked average Brazilians who had been unaware of the extensive use of 고문.[^61]

The Trans-Amazonian Highway, initially promoted as a symbol of national progress, brought lasting environmental and social consequences.[^68] Its construction opened previously inaccessible regions to logging, cattle ranching, and land grabbing, sparking widespread deforestation and biodiversity loss.[^69] It also facilitated illegal mining and 에 기여했다 the displacement of Indigenous and 전통적인 communities, many of whom continue to suffer 폭력 and territorial invasions.[^70]

비록 ~이지만 Institutional Act No. 5 (AI-5) was issued in December 1968 ~ 전에 Médici took office, he used its extraordinary powers extensively.[^75] AI-5 suspended habeas corpus for political crimes, closed the National 의회, granted the 대통령 dictatorial powers, imposed prior censorship on media and the arts, suspended constitutional guarantees and political rights, revoked congressional mandates, prohibited popular demonstrations, authorized summary dismissals in public administration, and transferred trials of political crimes to military courts.[^76] Under Médici, these powers were wielded with particular ruthlessness to crush all opposition.

## 유산

Emílio Garrastazu Médici's legacy remains deeply contested in Brazilian society and historiography. His four-year presidency is remembered primarily for two contradictory national trends: Brazil's "economic miracle" and widespread 인권 violations.[^86] This duality makes him perhaps the most 논란이 많은 of Brazil's military presidents, embodying the trade-offs that authoritarian modernization entails.

In the decades following the dictatorship's end, Brazilian society has gradually confronted the repressive legacy of the military regime. In 2011, twenty-six years ~ 후에 the dictatorship ended, the Brazilian government created a National Truth Commission to examine serious 인권 violations practiced by government agents from 1946 to 1985.[^87] The commission worked for almost three years, establishing partnerships with other countries to investigate international networks created by dictatorships for monitoring and persecuting political opponents across borders.[^88]

The Truth Commission's work aimed to create narratives that would allow, ~을 통해 the right to memory and truth, a reconnection between historical understanding and the traces left by the military dictatorship's legacy.[^89] 그러나 over thirty years into the redemocratization process, 중요한 gaps persist between Brazil's past and present, as the political transition occurred under the tutelage of the army with no visible break from 과거, making it difficult for 국가 to establish deep transformations and fully confront the dictatorship's legacy.[^90]

In 2015, the Federal University of Rio de Janeiro (UFRJ) revoked the honorary doctorate degree it had awarded Médici in the 1970s.[^91] In 2024, the Federal University of Pelotas (UFPel) 마찬가지로 revoked its honorary degree.[^92] These symbolic acts represented Brazilian universities' belated acknowledgment of Médici's role in systematic 인권 violations and their refusal to continue honoring someone associated with state terror.

The infrastructure projects initiated ~ 동안 Médici's presidency continue to shape Brazil's physical and economic landscape, though their legacy is mixed. The Itaipu Dam remains a crucial source of electricity for Brazil and Paraguay. The Trans-Amazonian Highway, though never completed as originally envisioned, opened vast areas of the Amazon to settlement, development, and unfortunately, environmental destruction. The economic growth of the "miracle" years laid foundations for Brazil's industrial base, but the foreign debt accumulated ~ 동안 this period and the exacerbated inequality created social problems that persist into the 21st 세기.

### 긍정적 평가

Some defenders of Médici's presidency argue that he successfully navigated a period of intense internal 분쟁 and prevented Brazil from falling to communist insurgency. ### 부정적 평가

The overwhelming consensus among historians, 인권 organizations, and democratic activists is that Médici presided over the darkest period of Brazil's military dictatorship. 현대의 Brazilian historians emphasize that the political transition occurred "under the tutelage of the army with no visible break with the past," meaning that military leaders like Médici were never held accountable for 인권 violations.[^105] This lack of accountability has made it difficult for Brazil to fully reckon with its authoritarian past and represents a continuing challenge to Brazilian 민주주의.
