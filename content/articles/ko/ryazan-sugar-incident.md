## 요약

FSB agents were caught planting what appeared to be a bomb in a Ryazan apartment building. The FSB claimed it was a training exercise, but residents believed it was a real attack.

## 배경

- The incident occurred during the broader context of the 1999 Russian apartment bombings, a series of explosions in multiple cities that killed many civilians and helped drive public support for renewed war in Chechnya.
- Ryazan is often discussed because, unlike the earlier blasts, local residents and police intervened while an apparent device was being planted.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- Residents of an apartment building reported suspicious activity and alerted authorities.
- Police discovered sacks in the basement that appeared consistent with an explosive setup and treated the situation as an urgent terrorism threat.
- A related vehicle and individuals connected to the operation were reportedly traced and detained; the individuals were later identified as FSB personnel.

## Official Explanation vs. Public Doubts

- Russian authorities stated that the Ryazan episode was a training exercise and that the material in the sacks was not live explosive.
- Critics and some observers argued the episode looked like an attempted attack that was only stopped because locals acted quickly.
- Because the event sits amid a larger, politically consequential bombing campaign, disputes about intent and accountability have remained prominent in commentary.

## Why It Remains Notable

- It is frequently cited as a case where a suspected terror act was interrupted before detonation, creating unusually detailed contemporaneous reporting and official statements.
- The subsequent reclassification as an "exercise" is central to why the incident continues to be debated.
