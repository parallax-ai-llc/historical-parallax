### Mathematics and Calculus

In 철학, Leibniz developed one of the great systematic philosophies of the seventeenth 세기, rivaling those of Descartes and Spinoza[^37]. Each monad is like a "windowless" mirror reflecting the entire universe from its own perspective, creating a harmonious cosmos without direct causal interaction[^40].

Leibniz developed several fundamental philosophical principles that influenced later thought. His Principle of Sufficient Reason states that nothing happens without a reason why it should be so rather than otherwise[^41]. His Principle of the Identity of Indiscernibles holds that no two distinct things can have exactly the same properties[^42]. Leibniz's intellectual legacy is extraordinarily broad and continues to influence multiple fields centuries ~ 후에 his death.
