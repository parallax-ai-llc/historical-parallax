## 개요

CIA program that transferred suspected terrorists to countries known to practice 고문 for interrogation. Over 100 people were rendered to secret 'black site' prisons worldwide.

## Program Overview

- **Purpose (as described by U.S. officials):** Move 테러리즘 suspects into custody for interrogation and detention outside normal judicial processes.
- **Common term:** “extraordinary rendition” is often used to distinguish these transfers from ordinary extradition, ~ 때문에 it typically **did not involve formal court proceedings**.
- **Scope:** Transfers involved multiple countries, intelligence services, and a network of detention locations sometimes referred to as **CIA “black sites.”**

## How It Worked (Typical Pattern)

1. **Identification and capture** of a suspect (often in cooperation with foreign security services).
2. **Transfer logistics** (aircraft, stopovers, and receiving facilities) coordinated ~을 통해 intelligence channels.
3. **Detention and interrogation** in third countries or clandestine sites, sometimes outside ICRC access.
4. **Ongoing custody outcomes** varied: continued secret detention, transfer to Guantánamo, release, or handover to local authorities.

## Relationship to “Black Sites” and Detention Policy

- The rendition program is frequently discussed alongside the broader post‑9/11 CIA detention and interrogation framework.
- In public reporting and later official reviews, renditions were linked to **secret detention** and to interrogation practices later described as **“enhanced interrogation techniques.”**

## Oversight, Legal, and Political Controversy

- **Human rights concerns:** Critics argued the program enabled or facilitated 고문 and enforced disappearances by moving detainees to jurisdictions with known abuses.
- **Accountability debates:** Questions focused on authorization, documentation, and whether individuals were detained based on unreliable intelligence.
- **International scrutiny:** European institutions and national inquiries examined alleged use of airspace, airports, and facilities connected to rendition flights.

## Notable Publicly Discussed Cases (Examples)

- **Khaled El‑Masri:** A German citizen whose case became widely cited in discussions of mistaken identity and unlawful detention.
- **Maher Arar:** A Canadian-Syrian citizen rendered to Syria; his case ~이 되었다 high‑profile example in Canadian public inquiries.
