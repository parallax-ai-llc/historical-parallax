## Additional Context

## What “Platform Role” Means Here

- The central allegation is not that Facebook initiated the 폭력, but that its recommendation, engagement, and distribution dynamics amplified dehumanizing narratives and coordinated harassment.
- In fragile contexts, rapid spread of inflammatory content can interact with existing state 폭력, social tensions, and misinformation.

## Findings and Criticism (High-level)

- UN investigators and civil-society groups argued that online hate speech 에 기여했다 offline harms against Rohingya communities.
- Critics highlighted low levels of Myanmar-language moderation capacity ~ 동안 key periods, and the difficulty of enforcing policies in fast-evolving local dialects.

## Corporate and Regulatory Aftermath

- Facebook/Meta has publicly acknowledged being too slow in addressing abuse in Myanmar and later removed accounts linked to Myanmar’s military.
- The controversy is frequently referenced in debates about:
  - content moderation resourcing in non-English languages,
  - algorithmic amplification and virality,
  - transparency and access for independent researchers,
  - legal liability for platform-enabled harms.

## Ongoing Relevance

- The episode is a canonical case study in “platform governance” and in the limits of reactive moderation ~ 동안 political crises.
