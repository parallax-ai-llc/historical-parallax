## 요약

Mexican cartel hitman known as 'El Pozolero' (The Stewmaker) who confessed to dissolving approximately 300 bodies in acid for the Tijuana Cartel. His confession shocked Mexico.

## 추가 맥락

### What the case represented

- The case became emblematic of the scale of enforced disappearances and clandestine body disposal linked to organized crime in Mexico.
- Public attention focused not only on the killings but also on the methods used to conceal evidence.
- The confession was cited as a rare, detailed account of how criminal groups managed logistics around disappearance and intimidation.

### Tijuana Cartel environment (high level)

- The Tijuana/Arellano Félix Organization was one of the major trafficking organizations operating along the U.S.–Mexico border.
- Competition over plazas (trafficking corridors) contributed to cycles of violence, retaliation, and fragmentation among groups.
- Local corruption risks and fear of reprisals complicated investigations and witness cooperation.

### Legal outcomes

- Mexican prosecutions in such cases often involve a mix of homicide-related charges and organized-crime statutes.
- Sentencing can be driven by the number of victims attributed, the nature of the acts, and evidentiary standards.
