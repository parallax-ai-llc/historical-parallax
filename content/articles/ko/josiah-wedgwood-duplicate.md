## High-Level Context (조사이어 웨지우드)

조사이어 웨지우드 was an English potter and entrepreneur who helped industrialize the production and marketing of ceramics in 18th-century Britain.

### What he is widely known for

- Building the **Wedgwood** pottery business into a major manufacturer of high-quality earthenware.
- Developing and popularizing distinctive ceramic bodies and finishes, including **Jasperware** (noted for its matte, colored body with classical relief decoration).
- Combining **design, quality control, and branding** with scale production—an early example of modern industrial management in consumer goods.

### Business and industry

- Wedgwood is often cited in discussions of the **산업 혁명** for applying systematic production methods to ceramics.
- He pursued elite patronage and international markets, using product lines and consistent standards to support growth.

### Science, networks, and legacy

- He interacted with leading scientific and industrial circles of the period (commonly associated with the Midlands innovation networks).
- The Wedgwood family later became connected to the Darwin family through marriage, linking his legacy to prominent figures in British intellectual history.
