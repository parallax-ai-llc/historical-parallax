## 개요

Former Syrian intelligence officer convicted in Germany for aiding and abetting crimes against humanity. He helped arrest and deliver protesters to the Al-Khatib detention center where they were tortured.

## Additional Context

Al-Gharib’s case is frequently cited as part of the 초기의 wave of **universal-jurisdiction** prosecutions connected to the Syrian civil 전쟁. German courts pursued several cases involving Syrian state detention facilities and intelligence branches, relying on refugee testimony, defectors, documentary evidence, and NGO investigations.

### Role in Branch 251 (Al-Khatib)

Public reporting and 법원 summaries describe Branch 251 as a detention and interrogation center in Damascus associated with systematic abuse. The prosecution theory in al-Gharib’s 재판 emphasized participation in a chain of custody: helping deliver detainees to a site where 고문 was occurring.

### Significance of the Conviction

- Demonstrated that mid-level participants could be prosecuted even without proving direct commission of 고문.
- 에 기여했다 the broader evidentiary record on detention conditions and practices.
- Highlighted Germany’s role as a venue for accountability efforts where domestic remedies in Syria were not available.

### 여파

The case is often discussed alongside other German proceedings as part of an emerging model for accountability: using national courts to address atrocities committed abroad when international tribunals or domestic prosecutions are blocked.

### Sentencing Note

The 4.5-year sentence is commonly characterized as reflecting an aiding-and-abetting role rather than leadership responsibility for the detention system.
