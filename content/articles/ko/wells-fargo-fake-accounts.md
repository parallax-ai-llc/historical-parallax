## 요약

Wells Fargo employees created millions of fake bank accounts without customer consent to meet sales quotas. Over 3.5 million fake accounts were opened; the bank paid $3 billion in fines.

## What happened (high level)

The scandal centered on employees opening deposit and credit accounts without customer authorization and, in some cases, moving customer funds or issuing cards that generated fees. Investigations described a sales culture focused on aggressive "cross-selling" targets, where workers faced intense pressure to meet daily and monthly quotas.[^1]

## Scale and customer impact

Commonly reported impacts included:

- Millions of unauthorized accounts created over multiple years.
- Customers charged fees or affected by unwanted products (for example, account maintenance fees, overdraft fees, or credit inquiries), followed by remediation and refunds as the scandal expanded.[^1]

## Regulatory and legal actions

Authorities at the federal and state level brought enforcement actions that resulted in large penalties and ongoing oversight.

Notable milestones (high level):

- 2016: Public enforcement actions and penalties brought the issue into national focus.[^1]
- Later years: Additional settlements and consent orders increased total penalties and required governance, compliance, and risk-management reforms.[^1]

## Leadership and governance fallout

The scandal led to significant reputational damage and changes in leadership. Senior executives and board members faced scrutiny over incentive systems, internal controls, and the handling of whistleblower complaints and employee terminations.[^1]
