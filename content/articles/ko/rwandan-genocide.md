## 요약

Genocide of Tutsis by Hutu extremists in 1994 that killed 800,000 in 100 days. Fastest genocide in recorded history.

## 배경

- The genocide occurred against a backdrop of long-running political and ethnic tensions, shaped in part by colonial-era policies and post-independence power struggles.
- In the early 1990s, Rwanda experienced civil war and negotiations that culminated in the Arusha Accords, though the political situation remained highly unstable.

## 1994 Mass Violence

- After the assassination of President Juvénal Habyarimana in April 1994, extremist networks and militias rapidly organized mass killings.
- The violence targeted Tutsi civilians and also included the killing of many moderate Hutu opponents of the extremist agenda.

## International Response and UN Role

- The genocide is often cited as a major failure of the international community to prevent or halt mass atrocities despite warning signs.
- The UN Assistance Mission for Rwanda (UNAMIR) was present but operated under constrained mandates and limited resources.

## 여파

- The Rwandan Patriotic Front (RPF) ended the genocide militarily, after which large refugee flows and regional instability followed.
- Accountability efforts included the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR) and domestic mechanisms such as gacaca courts.

## 유산

- The Rwandan genocide remains a central case study in genocide prevention, transitional justice, and post-conflict reconstruction.
