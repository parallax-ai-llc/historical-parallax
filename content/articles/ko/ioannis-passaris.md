## 개요

Greek criminal known as the 'Greek Houdini' who committed murders and spectacular prison escapes.

## Notability

Passaris is primarily remembered in Greek and European media for a combination of violent crime and high-profile escapes. The "Greek Houdini" label reflects the public fascination with his ability to evade guards and exploit security weaknesses rather than any legitimate expertise.

## Crimes (high-level)

Accounts commonly attribute to him:

- **Homicide**: killings connected to robberies and flight from law enforcement.
- **Armed robbery**: bank and cash-related robberies, often involving firearms.
- **Flight and evasion**: repeated attempts to escape confinement and avoid recapture.

## Arrests, Escapes, and Recapture

Public summaries emphasize a pattern:

- **Capture after investigations** into robbery-related violence.
- **Detention and court proceedings** leading to a major conviction.
- **Escape(s)** that drew national attention and prompted security reviews.
- **Recapture** after manhunts and inter-agency coordination.

## Legacy and Public Discussion

- **Security lessons**: his escapes are frequently cited in discussions of prison transport and facility security.
- **Media portrayal**: coverage tends to focus on the dramatic elements (escapes, manhunts) alongside the seriousness of the underlying crimes.
