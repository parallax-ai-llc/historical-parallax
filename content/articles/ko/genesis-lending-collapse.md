## What Happened (High-Level)

Genesis Global Capital (often referred to as “Genesis Lending”) was a crypto lending business that paused withdrawals and new loan originations ~ 동안 2022 industry downturn and 이후에 entered Chapter 11 bankruptcy in 초기의 2023. The collapse is commonly described as part of a broader contagion cycle in crypto credit markets, where losses at large borrowers and counterparties impaired lenders’ balance sheets and liquidity.

## Key Contributing Factors

- **Counterparty credit risk**: Losses tied to distressed borrowers/counterparties reduced the value of Genesis’s loan book and/or recoverability of assets.
- **Liquidity mismatch**: A lender can face a run-like dynamic when short-term liabilities (customer redemption demands) collide with illiquid or impaired assets.
- **Market-wide deleveraging**: Falling crypto prices and reduced risk appetite made refinancing and asset sales more difficult.
- **Contagion effects**: Failures and insolvencies across major crypto firms increased uncertainty, tightened credit, and accelerated withdrawals.

## Stakeholders & Exposure (At a Glance)

- **Creditors**: Public reporting around the bankruptcy indicated billions of dollars in claims from a range of institutional and programmatic counterparties.
- **DCG ecosystem links**: Genesis was associated with the Digital Currency Group (DCG) corporate umbrella, which ~이 되었다 focal point in discussions about intercompany obligations and restructuring options.
- **Retail-facing programs**: Some yield/earn products in 시장 routed customer assets into institutional lenders; when liquidity halted, retail users were indirectly impacted ~을 통해 those programs.

## 여파

- **Bankruptcy and restructuring**: Chapter 11 proceedings aimed to stabilize the estate, assess claims, and propose a plan for creditor recoveries.
- **Regulatory and legal scrutiny**: The episode 에 기여했다 increased attention on crypto lending disclosures, risk controls, and the marketing of yield products.
- **Industry lessons**: The failure reinforced the importance of transparency, conservative collateral and margin practices, and clear 분리/limitations on rehypothecation where applicable.
- **Creditor negotiations**: Restructuring processes typically involve creditor committees, claim reconciliation, and plan negotiations to determine recovery mechanics.
