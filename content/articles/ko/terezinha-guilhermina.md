## 요약

Terezinha Guilhermina is a visually impaired Brazilian sprinter who won multiple Paralympic gold medals and set world records in sprint events.

## 경력 연표

| 연도 | 사건 |
|------|-------|
| 2004 | Won Paralympic medals at Athens |
| 2012 | Won three gold medals at London Paralympics |

## Athletic Profile

- Guilhermina competes in Paralympic track sprint events, commonly in the T11 visual-impairment classification.
- Her racing is typically supported by a guide runner, a standard feature of T11 competition, with athlete and guide linked by tether.

## 주요 업적

- Across multiple Paralympic Games, she has won medals in sprint distances, building a reputation as one of Brazil's leading para-athletes.
- Her London 2012 performance is frequently cited as a career peak, including multiple gold medals.

## Records and Competitive Context

- Paralympic sprinting places a premium on start reaction, acceleration, and coordination with a guide; performance margins are often small at the elite level.
- World record and Paralympic record progressions in sprint events have been shaped by improvements in coaching, training systems, and professionalism in para-sport.

## 유산

- In Brazil, high-profile Paralympic champions have played an outsized role in raising visibility for para-sport and disability inclusion.
- Guilhermina is often referenced in media coverage as part of the generation that expanded Brazil's Paralympic medal competitiveness.

## Notes on Classification

- Paralympic athletics classifications group athletes by functional impact; T11 is for athletes with very low visual acuity who compete with a guide.
- Classification and guide-runner rules are central to how results are compared across para-sport events.
