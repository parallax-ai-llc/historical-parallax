## 개요

Future 노벨 평화상 winner who was kidnapped by Korean CIA agents in 도쿄 in 1973 and nearly assassinated. Only saved by American intervention.

## 배경

김대중 was a prominent 대한민국n opposition politician and a leading critic of authoritarian rule in the 1960s–1970s. His growing domestic popularity and international profile made him a high-value target for political intimidation.

## The 1973 도쿄 Kidnapping (High-Level)

- **Date and place:** August 1973, 도쿄, 일본.
- **Perpetrators:** Agents associated with 대한민국's intelligence services (commonly described as the 중앙정보부 in contemporary accounts).
- **Method:** Kim was seized from a 도쿄 hotel and taken to a boat, in an apparent attempt to remove him from 일본 and return him to 대한민국.
- **Near-fatal outcome:** Reports describe an intended killing at sea, which was not carried out.

## International Pressure and Aftermath

일본 protested the violation of its sovereignty, and the incident drew attention from allied governments. Kim ultimately survived and later returned to political life, remaining a central figure in 대한민국's democratization movement.

## Historical Significance

The kidnapping is often cited as an emblematic case of 냉전–era authoritarian repression in 대한민국 and of the risks faced by opposition leaders. In later decades, Kim's career trajectory—from dissident to president and 노벨 평화상 laureate—shaped how the episode is remembered.
