## 개요

Student killed 21 people at a polytechnic college in Kerch, Crimea using a shotgun and a homemade bomb. Inspired by the Columbine massacre.

| 연도 | 사건 |

## Incident Overview

- **Date:** 17 October 2018
- **Place:** Kerch, Crimea (internationally recognized as part of 우크라이나; administered by 러시아 since 2014)
- **Site:** Kerch Polytechnic College
- **Casualties:** 21 people killed (including the perpetrator), dozens injured[^1]

## Perpetrator (High-Level)

Wikipedia describes the attacker as a student at the college who carried out the assault with a shotgun and an improvised explosive device, and who was influenced by the Columbine school massacre.[^1]

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The incident involved **both** a firearm attack and an explosion inside the campus.[^1]
- Reports commonly summarize the event as a **mass shooting** with an accompanying **bombing**.[^1]
- The attacker died during the incident; accounts describe the death as self-inflicted.[^1]

## Immediate Aftermath

- Authorities treated the event as a major security incident and launched an investigation into planning, procurement of weapons, and possible accomplices.[^1]
- The case became one of the deadliest school/college attacks in the region in recent years and prompted renewed discussion around access to weapons, school security, and copycat risk.[^1]

## Why It Matters (Context)

- The event is frequently referenced in discussions of **school violence** and **mass-casualty attacks** in Eastern 유럽.
- The stated inspiration by Columbine is often cited as an example of **transnational copycat dynamics** in mass attacks.[^1]
