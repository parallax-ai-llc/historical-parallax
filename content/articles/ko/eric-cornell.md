## Additional Context

- **Bose–Einstein condensation (BEC):** A BEC is a state of matter that can occur when a gas of bosonic atoms is cooled to extremely low temperatures, causing a large fraction of atoms to occupy the same quantum ground state.
- **1995 breakthrough:** Cornell and colleagues at JILA (University of Colorado/NIST) are widely credited with producing one of the first BECs in a dilute atomic gas (commonly reported as **rubidium-87**), using laser cooling and evaporative cooling techniques.
- **Nobel Prize (2001):** The 2001 노벨상 in Physics recognized experimental realization of BEC and 초기의 studies of condensates. Cornell shared the prize with **Carl E. Wieman** and **Wolfgang Ketterle**.
- **Ultracold atom platform:** BEC experiments helped establish ultracold atoms as a versatile platform for probing **quantum many-body physics**, coherence, and controlled interactions.

## Why It Matters

Work on BEC 에 기여했다:

- Improved understanding of **macroscopic quantum phenomena**.
- Experimental techniques used in **atom interferometry** and precision measurement.
- The broader field of **quantum simulation**, where cold atoms emulate complex quantum systems.
