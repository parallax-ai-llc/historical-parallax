## 요약

Dutch filmmaker Theo van Gogh was shot and stabbed on an Amsterdam street by Islamist Mohammed Bouyeri for making a film critical of Islam. A death threat was pinned to his body with a knife.

## 배경과 맥락

- **Theo van Gogh** was a Dutch filmmaker and public commentator known for provocative criticism, including work addressing religion and integration debates in the Netherlands.[^1]
- The murder occurred during a period of heightened public discussion in Europe about **immigration**, **religious freedom**, and **political violence**, particularly in relation to Islamist extremism.[^1]

## 가해자와 동기 (개요)

- The attacker, **Mohammed Bouyeri**, was described as an Islamist extremist who targeted van Gogh over his expressive works and public statements.[^1]
- Reporting commonly links the attack to van Gogh’s involvement in the short film **"Submission"**, which criticized the treatment of women in some interpretations of Islam, intensifying controversy around his work.[^1]

## 공격과 즉각적인 여파

- Van Gogh was attacked in **Amsterdam** on **2 November 2004**, suffering gunshot wounds and stab wounds; a written threat was left pinned to his body.[^1]
- The incident prompted a major police and security response, as well as immediate political reactions across the Netherlands.[^1]

## 사회적·정치적 영향

- The killing is frequently cited as a pivotal moment in Dutch public life, contributing to debates on:
  - freedom of speech versus incitement and hate,
  - integration policy and social cohesion,
  - the threat posed by violent extremism.
- It also intensified scrutiny of extremist networks and influenced discussions about protecting public figures and cultural producers.[^1]
