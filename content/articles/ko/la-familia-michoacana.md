## 개요

Mexican drug cartel from Michoacan known for combining drug trafficking with pseudo-religious ideology. They distributed Bibles and claimed to protect citizens while controlling meth production.

## Additional Context

### Origins and operating model

- 라 파밀리아 미초아카나 emerged in the context of 멕시코's fragmented cartel landscape, with Michoacán as a key production and trafficking region.
- The group became associated with methamphetamine production and distribution, alongside extortion and territorial control.
- Public reporting often emphasizes its use of moralistic or religious rhetoric as a form of internal discipline and external messaging.

### Relationship to successors and splinters

- The Knights Templar cartel is commonly described as an offshoot that inherited networks and territory after La Familia's decline.
- Fragmentation, leadership losses, and shifting alliances contributed to a pattern of rebranding and splintering that is common among Mexican organized-crime groups.

### State response and social effects

- Mexican federal and state operations targeted leadership and infrastructure, while communities faced violence, intimidation, and economic disruption.
- The broader security environment in Michoacán later contributed to the rise of local self-defense forces (autodefensas) in some areas, although dynamics varied by locality.

### Why it is notable

- Frequently cited as an example of a cartel attempting to combine criminal enterprise with a quasi-ideological identity.
- Illustrates how narrative, propaganda, and social control can be integrated into organized crime strategies.
