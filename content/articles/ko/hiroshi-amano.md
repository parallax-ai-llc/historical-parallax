## 개요

Hiroshi Amano is a Japanese physicist and engineer who shared the 2014 Nobel Prize in Physics for the invention of efficient blue LEDs. Working with Isamu Akasaki, he developed the p-type gallium nitride that made blue LEDs possible. Blue LEDs enabled the creation of white LED lighting, which uses a fraction of the energy of incandescent bulbs.
