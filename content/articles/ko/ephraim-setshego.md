## 개요

South African serial killer known as the 'Atteridgeville Mutilator' who murdered and dismembered multiple victims in Pretoria. Convicted of multiple murders and sentenced to life.

## Additional Context

### Nickname and geography

- Setshego was dubbed the “Atteridgeville Mutilator,” a reference to Atteridgeville, a township west of Pretoria (Tshwane) in Gauteng province.[^1][^2]
- Media coverage of the case focused on a cluster of killings in and around the township ~ 동안 후기의 1990s, contributing to public fear and pressure for arrests.[^1]

### 수사 and arrest

- Serial murder investigations typically involve linking cases ~을 통해 victimology, time and location patterns, and forensic evidence; Setshego’s prosecution was presented as connecting multiple incidents in the same area and period.[^1]
- As with many high-profile serial cases, the investigation drew intense attention from residents and local authorities, with concerns about policing capacity and community safety.[^1][^2]

### 재판 and sentencing

- Setshego was convicted of multiple murders and received life sentences, reflecting South Africa’s approach to serious violent crime ~을 통해 lengthy 투옥 rather than capital punishment.[^1][^3]
- The case is sometimes referenced in discussions about post-apartheid crime trends in South Africa and the social conditions that shape both victimization and law enforcement responses.[^2][^3]
