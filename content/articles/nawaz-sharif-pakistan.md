## Summary

Three-time Pakistani PM disqualified from office after the Panama Papers exposed his family's offshore wealth. His daughter Maryam Nawaz continued his political dynasty.

## Political Context

Nawaz Sharif is often analyzed through the lens of Pakistan’s recurring tensions between elected civilian leadership, the judiciary, and other powerful institutions. His career is also closely tied to party organization, patronage networks, and the durability of family-led political brands.

## Panama Papers and Accountability Politics

- The Panama Papers episode became a major public symbol of offshore wealth and elite financial practices.
- In Pakistan, corruption allegations and disqualifications are frequently contested as both legal questions and political instruments.
- As a result, Sharif’s disqualification and later political activity are referenced in debates about rule of law, selective accountability, and democratic stability.

## Dynasty and Succession

The Sharif family’s continued role in politics is commonly framed as part of a broader South Asian pattern in which prominent families maintain influence through party leadership, regional bases, and succession to younger relatives.
