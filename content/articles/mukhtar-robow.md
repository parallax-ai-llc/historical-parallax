## Summary

Somali militant and former deputy leader of al-Shabaab who trained at al-Qaeda camps in Afghanistan. He later defected and attempted to run for office before being arrested.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2017 | Defected from al-Shabaab |
| 2018 | Arrested while running for regional presidency |

## Role in al-Shabaab

- **Senior leadership**: Robow (often known by a nom de guerre) was reported as a senior commander and spokesman within al-Shabaab during the group’s rise.
- **Relationship to al-Qaeda**: Open-source reporting has described ideological and training links between leadership figures in al-Shabaab and al-Qaeda networks.

## Split, Defection, and Re-Entry Attempts

- **Leadership dispute**: Accounts describe a break with the faction led by Ahmed Abdi Godane, reflecting internal struggles over strategy, violence, and control.
- **Defection**: After leaving al-Shabaab, Robow entered a period of negotiation and protection under Somali authorities.
- **Electoral ambitions**: He attempted to participate in regional politics, an unusual path for a former militant leader and a politically sensitive test for reintegration policy.

## Arrest and Aftermath

- **2018 arrest**: He was detained during an attempt to run for a regional presidency, prompting unrest and controversy.
- **Later public role**: Subsequent reporting has at times described him being incorporated into government structures, illustrating the difficult balance between security concerns and political reconciliation.

## Broader Significance

Robow is frequently discussed in analyses of:

- insurgent movement fragmentation,
- deradicalization and reintegration pathways,
- and the risks and incentives involved when former militant leaders enter formal politics.
