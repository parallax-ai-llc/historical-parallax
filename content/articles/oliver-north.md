## Summary

US Marine officer who orchestrated the Iran-Contra affair, secretly selling weapons to Iran and funneling money to Nicaraguan Contras. His conviction was overturned on appeal.

## Early Life and Education

- Born in 1943 in San Antonio, Texas, and raised in the United States.
- Attended the U.S. Naval Academy, graduating in 1968, before beginning a career as a Marine Corps officer.

## Military and National Security Roles

- Served in the U.S. Marine Corps, including combat service in the Vietnam War.
- Later worked on the staff of the U.S. National Security Council (NSC) during the Reagan administration.
- Became associated with covert and sensitive policy operations tied to U.S. foreign policy in the 1980s.

## Iran–Contra Affair (High-Level)

The Iran–Contra affair involved two linked, controversial efforts:

1) Secret sales of U.S. arms to Iran (despite restrictions), partly framed as a way to pursue hostage releases.
2) Diversion of proceeds to support the Contra forces in Nicaragua, intersecting with U.S. legal and congressional constraints on such aid.

North became a central figure because of his operational role in coordinating and documenting aspects of these activities.

## Investigations and Legal Proceedings

- North testified before Congress in highly publicized hearings.
- He was convicted in 1989 on multiple counts, but the convictions were later vacated/overturned on appeal due to issues related to immunized congressional testimony.

## Later Activities

- Remained a public political figure, including media appearances and involvement in advocacy and organizational leadership roles.
- Ran for U.S. Senate in Virginia in the 1990s but did not win.
