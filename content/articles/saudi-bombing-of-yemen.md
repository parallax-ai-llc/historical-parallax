## Summary

Saudi-led coalition airstrikes have killed thousands of Yemeni civilians including attacks on hospitals, schools, buses, and weddings. Used US and UK-supplied weapons.

## Background

- The air campaign began in March 2015 as part of the wider **Yemeni civil war**, with a Saudi-led coalition intervening against the Houthi movement.
- The conflict quickly became a regionalized war involving multiple Yemeni factions and outside state support.

## Patterns Alleged by Investigators

- Human rights organizations and UN-related reporting have documented repeated strikes on civilian sites and critical infrastructure.
- Reported targets have included:
  - medical facilities and ambulances
  - schools and school transportation
  - markets, weddings, and funerals
  - water, sanitation, and other civilian infrastructure

## Humanitarian Impact

- Airstrikes compounded a broader humanitarian crisis marked by displacement, food insecurity, and disease outbreaks.
- Damage to ports, roads, and fuel access affected the delivery of aid and basic services.

## International Response and Controversy

- The campaign has drawn scrutiny over compliance with international humanitarian law (distinction, proportionality, and precautions in attack).
- The coalition's use of foreign-made aircraft and munitions became a focal point in debates about arms sales and accountability.

## Notes on Terminology

- The label "Saudi Bombing of Yemen" is commonly used to describe the coalition air campaign, but it sits within a multi-party conflict with shifting front lines and actors.
