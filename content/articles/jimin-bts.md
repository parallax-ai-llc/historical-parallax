## Summary

Park Jimin, known mononymously as Jimin, is a South Korean singer, dancer, and songwriter who rose to global fame as a member of the K-pop group BTS. Born on October 13, 1995, in Busan, South Korea, he is widely regarded as one of the most talented performers of his generation, celebrated for his technical dance precision and expressive vocal style. His influence extends well beyond the K-pop genre, reshaping perceptions of South Korean music and male artistry worldwide.

As a member of BTS since the group's debut in 2013, Jimin has been a central figure in the band's ascent from a small Korean label to a global cultural phenomenon. His solo work, launched in earnest in 2023, confirmed his standing as a versatile artist capable of sustained commercial and critical success independent of the group.

## Early Life & Background

Jimin was born and raised in Busan, South Korea's second-largest city. He attended Busan High School of Arts, where he majored in modern dance and graduated at the top of his class. His training there gave him a rigorous foundation in contemporary movement that would distinguish him among his peers. He was scouted by Big Hit Entertainment (now HYBE) and relocated to Seoul to undergo trainee preparation before BTS's debut.

## Rise to Prominence

Jimin debuted with BTS on June 13, 2013, under Big Hit Entertainment. The group's early years were marked by modest domestic success, but by 2015–2016 their fanbase, known as ARMY, began expanding rapidly across Asia and into Western markets. Jimin's performances — particularly his solo stages at BTS concerts, including the emotionally charged "Lie" from the 2016 *Wings* era — drew significant attention to his individual artistry.

BTS broke into the U.S. mainstream around 2017–2018, and Jimin became one of the group's most recognized faces globally. He was consistently cited in major media as a standout performer, and his social media presence helped drive BTS's record-breaking digital footprint.

## Key Achievements

- **Solo debut:** Jimin released his first solo album, *FACE*, on March 24, 2023. It debuted at No. 2 on the Billboard 200, making him the first solo Korean artist to achieve that chart position at the time.
- **"Like Crazy":** The album's lead single entered the Billboard Hot 100 at No. 1, making Jimin the first Korean solo artist to top that chart.
- His second solo album, *MUSE*, was released in July 2024 and debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200, extending his record as the highest-charting Korean solo artist in that ranking's history.
- As part of BTS, he contributed to the group's multiple Grammy nominations, two consecutive No. 1 albums on the Billboard 200 (*Map of the Soul: 7* and *BE*), and stadium-level world tours.
- Named to *Time* magazine's 100 Most Influential People list in 2023.

## Controversies & Criticism

Jimin, like other BTS members, has faced targeted harassment and coordinated malicious rumors — a widespread issue affecting K-pop artists on social media platforms. HYBE and Big Hit Music have taken legal action in multiple cases involving defamatory content. His rapid commercial success as a solo artist has also drawn some criticism around chart manipulation allegations, though no substantiated evidence of improper activity has been established.

## Legacy & Influence

Jimin's career represents a pivotal chapter in the globalization of Korean popular music. His chart achievements as a solo artist demonstrated that Korean-language performers could compete at the top of Western commercial music without crossover concessions. Beyond metrics, his performances are frequently studied by dancers and choreographers for their technical and emotional depth. He has also been open about mental health struggles and personal growth, contributing to broader conversations around well-being among young fans.
