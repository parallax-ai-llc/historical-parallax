## Summary

Bosnian Serb politician convicted by the ICTY for persecution, torture, and cruel treatment of Bosniak and Croat civilians in the Bosanski Šamac municipality during the Bosnian War. Sentenced to 17 years.

## Background

- Blagoje Simić was a Bosnian Serb political figure associated with authorities in the Bosanski Šamac area during the early phases of the Bosnian War.
- The municipality became one of many localities where non-Serb civilians were subjected to detention, abuse, and forced removal as control shifted during 1992.

## ICTY Proceedings (high level)

- The **International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY)** prosecuted a number of cases related to crimes committed in Bosnia and Herzegovina during the 1990s.
- Simić was charged in relation to persecutions and mistreatment of Bosniak and Croat civilians in Bosanski Šamac.
- ICTY cases typically distinguish between direct participation, superior responsibility, and involvement in joint criminal enterprises; the specific findings depend on the judgment and appellate decisions.

## Conviction and Sentence

- The ICTY found him responsible for crimes including persecution and forms of inhumane treatment as summarized in the case record.
- Sentencing in ICTY cases reflects factors such as the gravity of the crimes, the role of the accused, and aggravating/mitigating circumstances.
- The widely cited sentence for Simić in reporting is **17 years’ imprisonment** (as reflected in common summaries).

## Why the Case Matters

- The Bosanski Šamac case is often referenced in discussions of how local political control and policing structures enabled systematic abuses.
- It also illustrates how international tribunals assembled evidence from municipal-level events to establish patterns of persecution.
