## Summary

Muhammad Nasiruddin al-Albani was an Albanian-born Islamic scholar based in Syria and Jordan who became the most influential hadith scholar of the 20th century. He meticulously authenticated and classified thousands of hadith, publishing Silsilat al-Ahadith al-Sahiha and al-Da'ifa. He was a leading figure in the modern Salafi movement and challenged traditional madhhab-following, advocating direct reliance on authenticated hadith. He was controversial but enormously influential.
