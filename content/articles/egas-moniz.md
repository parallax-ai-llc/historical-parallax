## Summary

Egas Moniz received the 1949 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for developing cerebral angiography and prefrontal leucotomy (lobotomy). Cerebral angiography remains a crucial diagnostic tool, while his promotion of lobotomy is now considered one of medicine's most controversial chapters. He was also a notable politician and diplomat.
