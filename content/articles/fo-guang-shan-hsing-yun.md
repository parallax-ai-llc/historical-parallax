## Summary

Hsing Yun was a Chinese Buddhist monk who founded Fo Guang Shan, one of the largest and most influential Buddhist organizations in the world. He promoted 'Humanistic Buddhism,' emphasizing the application of Buddhist principles to everyday life and social engagement. He founded over 200 temples worldwide, the Buddha Museum in Taiwan, and several universities. He wrote over 100 books and was one of the most influential Chinese Buddhist leaders of the modern era.
