## Summary

The UN's largest peacekeeping mission in Congo has been plagued by sexual exploitation scandals and failures to protect civilians. Despite 18,000 troops, massacres continue.

## Additional Context

### Core facts already established above
- UN mission began in 1999 and became one of the largest peacekeeping deployments.
- The mission has faced sexual exploitation scandals.
- It has been criticized for failures to protect civilians despite a large troop presence.
- Protests in 2023 demanded that MONUSCO leave.

### Why “failure” is a recurring debate in peacekeeping
- Civilian protection depends on intelligence, mobility, political consent, and clear rules of engagement.
- Scandals undermine legitimacy, reducing cooperation from communities the mission relies on.
- Even large missions can be stretched thin when violence is dispersed and armed groups are fragmented.
