## Summary

Sin-Itiro Tomonaga was a Japanese physicist who shared the 1965 Nobel Prize in Physics with Richard Feynman and Julian Schwinger for their fundamental work in quantum electrodynamics. He independently developed a relativistically covariant formulation of QED. His work provided the theoretical framework for understanding electromagnetic interactions at the quantum level. Tomonaga also served as president of Tokyo University of Education.
