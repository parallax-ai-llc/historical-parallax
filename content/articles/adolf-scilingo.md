## Summary

Argentine naval officer who confessed to throwing drugged political prisoners from aircraft into the Atlantic Ocean during the Dirty War 'death flights.' His confession was the first public acknowledgment by a perpetrator. Convicted in Spain.

## Additional Context

### Historical Context

Adolfo Scilingo was an Argentine naval officer associated with Argentina’s last military dictatorship (1976–1983), a period marked by widespread human rights abuses and forced disappearances.

### “Death Flights”

He became internationally known after publicly describing the practice commonly referred to as “death flights,” in which detainees were drugged and thrown from aircraft into the sea. His statements contributed to broader efforts to document abuses from the period.

### Prosecution

Scilingo was prosecuted in Spain under principles that can allow courts to hear cases involving crimes against humanity. He was convicted and sentenced in a case that drew attention to transnational accountability for human rights violations.
