## Background

Ntabo Ntaberi Sheka is associated with armed group activity in eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC), a region marked by long-running conflict involving state forces, local militias, and foreign-linked armed groups. He is most commonly linked in reporting to the Nduma Defense of Congo (NDC), which operated largely in North Kivu.

## Leadership of the NDC (High Level)

Accounts describe Sheka as a political and military leader figure within the NDC, leveraging local grievances, control of territory, and conflict-era economic networks. As with many militia structures in the DRC, leadership and alliances are often described as fluid and shaped by local power struggles.

## 2010 Walikale Atrocities and Legal Proceedings

The Walikale area became internationally known in 2010 after reports of mass sexual violence and other abuses against civilians. Open-source summaries frequently attribute responsibility for ordering or enabling those crimes to militia leadership, including Sheka.

Sheka was later arrested and prosecuted in the Congolese military justice system. In 2020, he was reported convicted for war crimes and crimes against humanity in connection with the Walikale events and other abuses.

## Significance

The conviction has been cited as a notable example of domestic accountability for conflict-related atrocities in the DRC. Observers have also highlighted the continuing challenges of witness protection, security conditions, and the broader environment of impunity that has affected many cases in the region.
