## Summary

Sri Lankan Tamil arms dealer known as KP who was the LTTE's chief arms procurer and international operations head. He built a global weapons procurement network spanning Asia, Europe, and Africa.

## Additional Context

### Role inside the LTTE

- Pathmanathan is widely described as the LTTE's key overseas logistics figure, responsible for sourcing weapons and moving materiel across borders.
- Reporting and court accounts commonly characterize the network as relying on front companies, diaspora fundraising, and brokers in multiple countries.
- The role mattered because the LTTE operated as a hybrid of insurgent force and international procurement organization during the Sri Lankan Civil War.

### After 2009: leadership claim, capture, and detention

- After the LTTE's battlefield defeat in 2009 and the death of its leader Velupillai Prabhakaran, KP publicly claimed to be the new head of the organization.
- In mid-2009 he was detained in Malaysia and transferred to Sri Lanka, where he was held by authorities.
- Public reporting has described his post-arrest status as involving detention and interrogation, with periodic controversy over transparency and legal process.

### Why he is notable

- Illustrates how non-state armed groups can develop long-distance procurement channels that mirror state-like supply chains.
- Often cited in discussions of sanctions evasion, illicit arms brokerage, and transnational finance used to sustain insurgencies.

### Notes on sources and naming

- "KP" is a commonly used alias in Sri Lankan reporting; many accounts refer to him primarily by initials.
- Public information about precise dates, locations, and alleged transactions is often fragmented due to the clandestine nature of arms brokering.
