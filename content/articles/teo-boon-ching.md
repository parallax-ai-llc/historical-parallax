### Pangolin trafficking (context)

Pangolins are among the most-trafficked mammals globally. Illicit trade typically involves:

- poaching or capture in source regions
- consolidation through intermediaries and warehouses
- cross-border smuggling by land/sea routes
- distribution to end markets for meat and scales

Because supply chains are transnational, investigations often involve multiple jurisdictions and coordination across customs, wildlife authorities, and financial-crime units.

### Common indicators in large-scale wildlife trafficking cases

Investigations into high-volume wildlife trafficking networks frequently reference:

- repeat shipment patterns (similar routing, concealment methods, or paperwork)
- use of front companies or third-party logistics providers
- communications and broker networks linking collectors, consolidators, and buyers
- movement of proceeds through cash-intensive channels or layered transactions
