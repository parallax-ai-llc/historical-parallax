## Summary

Rocky Marciano is the only heavyweight champion to retire undefeated at 49-0 with 43 knockouts.

## Boxing Style and Reputation

Marciano was known for relentless pressure, exceptional conditioning, and an ability to absorb punishment while continuing to advance.
His power—often associated with his overhand right—contributed to a knockout rate that defined his public image.
Rather than relying on finesse, he frequently won by wearing opponents down over rounds.

## Championship Era

He captured the heavyweight title in 1952 and defended it multiple times before retiring.
His title reign occurred during a transitional period in heavyweight boxing, following the peak years of earlier legends and before later television-era superstars.
The undefeated record has remained a central talking point in debates about all-time great heavyweights.

## Notable Bouts

Marciano fought and defeated several prominent heavyweights of his time, including former champions and major contenders.
Many of his victories were marked by late-round rallies, reinforcing the narrative of durability and persistence.
His fights are still revisited for their intensity and for what they reveal about mid-20th-century heavyweight competition.

## Legacy

Marciano’s 49–0 record is one of the most recognizable statistics in boxing history.
He has been inducted into boxing halls of fame and remains a reference point in comparisons of undefeated champions.
Outside the ring, his life and early death in a plane crash contributed to a lasting mythic status in American sports culture.
