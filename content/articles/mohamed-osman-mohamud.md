## Summary

Somali-American convicted of attempting to bomb a Christmas tree lighting ceremony in Portland.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2010 | Arrested after attempting to detonate what he believed was a vehicle bomb at Portland's holiday tree-lighting ceremony (device was inert in an FBI sting operation).[^1] |
| 2011 | Federal prosecution proceeds; pretrial litigation focuses on evidence and the government's undercover operation.[^1] |
| 2012 | Convicted in U.S. federal court on charges related to attempting to use a weapon of mass destruction.[^1] |
| 2014 | Sentenced to a lengthy federal prison term (reported by Wikipedia as 30 years).[^1] |

## Background

- Described in reporting and court filings as Somali-American and living in the Portland, Oregon area at the time of the plot.[^1]
- His case is commonly cited as an example of a U.S. domestic terrorism prosecution that involved an FBI undercover sting operation.[^1]

## Plot, Investigation, and Arrest

- The attempted attack was linked to Portland's annual Christmas tree lighting event at Pioneer Courthouse Square.[^1]
- According to the widely reported account summarized by Wikipedia, undercover FBI operatives interacted with Mohamud and ultimately provided an inert device intended to simulate a car bomb.[^1]
- The arrest occurred after he attempted to trigger the detonation, at which point law enforcement took him into custody.[^1]

## Trial and Sentencing

- The case proceeded in federal court and resulted in a conviction.[^1]
- Defense arguments discussed in coverage included claims of entrapment and challenges to the government's role; the prosecution maintained the defendant's intent and actions supported guilt.[^1]
- The sentence is reported in public summaries as a multi-decade prison term.[^1]

## Significance and Aftermath

- The incident is frequently referenced in discussions about:
  - Counterterrorism tactics and the use of informants/undercover agents
  - Legal standards for entrapment defenses
  - Security practices around large public events

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- Target/event: public holiday tree-lighting ceremony in Portland, Oregon.[^1]
- Method alleged: attempted detonation of a vehicle bomb (inert device supplied during a sting).[^1]
- Outcome: arrest at the scene, federal conviction, and long prison sentence.[^1]
