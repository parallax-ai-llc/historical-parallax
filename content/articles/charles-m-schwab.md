## Summary

Charles M. Schwab was a steel magnate who served as the first president of U.S. Steel Corporation and later founded Bethlehem Steel. Under his leadership, Bethlehem Steel became the second-largest steel producer in the United States. He played a crucial role in American industrial capacity during World War I. Despite earning immense wealth, he died nearly bankrupt during the Great Depression.
