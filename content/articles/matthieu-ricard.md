## Summary

Matthieu Ricard is a French-born Tibetan Buddhist monk, author, and photographer who has been called 'the happiest person in the world' based on neuroscience research on his brain. He left a promising career in molecular biology to become a Buddhist monk in Nepal. He serves as the French interpreter for the Dalai Lama. His books, including Happiness and Altruism, have been translated into many languages.
