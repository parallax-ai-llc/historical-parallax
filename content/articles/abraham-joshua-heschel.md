## Summary

Abraham Joshua Heschel was a Polish-American Jewish rabbi and philosopher who was one of the most important Jewish theologians of the 20th century. He marched with Martin Luther King Jr. at Selma and was a leader in interfaith dialogue and social justice. His books The Sabbath and God in Search of Man articulated a philosophy of radical amazement. He opposed the Vietnam War and championed the Soviet Jewry movement.
