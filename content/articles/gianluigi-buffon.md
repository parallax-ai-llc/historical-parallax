## Summary

Gianluigi Buffon, born January 28, 1978, in Carrara, Italy, is widely regarded as one of the greatest goalkeepers in the history of football. His career spanning over two decades at the highest level—primarily with Juventus and the Italian national team—defined an era of goalkeeping excellence characterized by extraordinary reflexes, commanding presence, and remarkable longevity.

Buffon's importance to Italian football cannot be overstated. He was the last line of defense for Italy's 2006 FIFA World Cup triumph and the backbone of Juventus's domestic dominance throughout the 2010s. His combination of athleticism, leadership, and tactical intelligence elevated the goalkeeping position and inspired generations of players worldwide.

---

## Early Life & Background

Born in Carrara, Tuscany, Buffon came from an athletic family—his mother was a discus thrower, his father a weightlifter, and both sisters were volleyball players. He initially played as a field player before switching to goalkeeper at age 13. He joined Parma's youth academy, where his development accelerated rapidly under professional coaching structures.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Buffon made his Serie A debut for Parma on November 19, 1995, at just 17 years old, becoming one of the youngest goalkeepers to feature in Italy's top flight. He won the UEFA Cup with Parma in 1999 and the Coppa Italia in 1999 and 2002. His performances earned him a world-record transfer fee for a goalkeeper at the time—€52.8 million—when Juventus signed him in 2001.

At Juventus, he won nine Serie A titles (2002, 2003, and then consecutively from 2012 to 2020, minus the 2006 title stripped in the Calciopoli scandal) and reached two UEFA Champions League finals (2003 and 2015). His crowning international achievement came at the 2006 World Cup in Germany, where Italy conceded only two goals—both own goals—throughout the tournament, with Buffon keeping five clean sheets.

## Key Achievements

- 2006 FIFA World Cup winner with Italy
- 10 Serie A titles with Juventus
- UEFA Cup winner with Parma (1999)
- UEFA Champions League runner-up (2003, 2015)
- FIFA World Player of the Year runner-up (2006)
- IFFHS World's Best Goalkeeper award multiple times (2003, 2004, 2006)
- Over 1,000 professional appearances across his career
- Italy's most capped player with 176 international appearances

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Buffon's career was not without controversy. He received a 13-match ban in 2005 for his involvement in the Calciopoli match-fixing scandal at Juventus, though he personally was never accused of involvement in the manipulation. He chose to stay with Juventus when the club was relegated to Serie B in 2006, a decision widely praised as loyal but which nonetheless connected him to that tainted period.

In 2017, he received a one-match suspension after confronting referee Michael Oliver following a Champions League exit to Real Madrid, and his subsequent public criticism of the official drew widespread debate about player conduct.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Buffon retired from professional football in May 2023 with Parma, completing a deeply symbolic return to the club where he began. His legacy rests on redefining modern goalkeeping—combining physical excellence with psychological resilience and tactical sophistication. Younger goalkeepers including Gianluigi Donnarumma, his successor in the Italian national team, have cited Buffon as a primary influence. He remains a global ambassador for the sport and a symbol of loyalty, professionalism, and longevity.
