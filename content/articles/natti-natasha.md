## Summary

Natti Natasha (born Natalia Alexandra Gutierrez Batista on December 10, 1986, in Santiago de los Caballeros, Dominican Republic) is one of the most prominent female voices in Latin urban music. Blending reggaeton, dembow, and R&B, she has carved out a distinct identity in a genre historically dominated by male artists, earning recognition as a leading figure in the global Latin music explosion of the late 2010s and early 2020s.

Her collaborations with artists such as Bad Bunny, Ozuna, and Daddy Yankee helped propel her to mainstream success, while her solo work demonstrated commercial staying power. With billions of streams across platforms and multiple Latin Billboard chart-toppers, Natasha has become a defining voice for Dominican artists on the world stage.

---

## Early Life & Background

Natalia Gutierrez Batista was born on December 10, 1986, in Santiago de los Caballeros, Dominican Republic's second-largest city. She developed an interest in music at a young age and relocated to New York City as a teenager, where she worked to establish herself in the competitive Latin music scene. Her early years in New York exposed her to a diverse range of musical influences and industry contacts that would prove critical to her later success.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Natasha's early career included collaborations with rapper Daddy Yankee, who signed her to his Royalty Records label. Her 2017 collaboration with Bad Bunny, "Amantes de una Noche," and her feature on Ozuna's "Criminal" (2017) significantly raised her international profile. "Criminal" became a massive global hit, accumulating over 1.7 billion views on YouTube and reaching the top of the Billboard Hot Latin Songs chart.

Her debut studio album, *Illuminati*, was released in 2019 and debuted at number one on the Billboard Top Latin Albums chart. The album featured hit singles including "Toca Toca" and "Phillygreeze," consolidating her status as a solo act rather than solely a featured artist.

## Key Achievements

- "Criminal" with Ozuna (2017) surpassed 1.7 billion YouTube views and topped multiple Latin charts.
- *Illuminati* (2019) debuted at number one on Billboard Top Latin Albums.
- Won the Premio Lo Nuestro award for Urban Female Artist of the Year multiple times.
- Received multiple Latin Grammy nominations throughout her career.
- Named among Billboard's top Latin artists of her era and recognized as a trailblazer for women in urban Latin music.

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Natasha has faced scrutiny common to artists in the reggaeton genre, including criticism over the explicit or sexually suggestive content in some of her music videos and lyrics. Some commentators have noted tensions between her self-empowerment messaging and the visual presentation of certain projects. These debates reflect broader conversations within the Latin music industry about gender, agency, and artistic expression.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Natti Natasha's career represents a significant shift in Latin urban music, demonstrating that female artists can achieve headline-level commercial success in a genre long dominated by men. Her success has opened doors for subsequent Dominican and Caribbean female artists and broadened the global audience for dembow-influenced music. She remains an active and influential figure in the Latin music industry as of 2026.
