## Background

- **Belden Namah** (born 1960) is a Papua New Guinean politician associated with the **Vanimo-Green River** electorate in **West Sepik (Sandaun) Province**.
- He became nationally prominent as a senior opposition figure and later served in high-profile government and parliamentary roles.

## Political Roles (high-level)

- **Member of Parliament** for Vanimo-Green River (commonly reported as first elected in the 2000s).
- **Leader of the Opposition** (early 2010s), positioning him as one of the best-known critics of the sitting government.
- Served as a **Deputy Prime Minister** in a later government period (reported in multiple public summaries of his career).

## 2012 Sedition Charge (context)

- In 2012, Namah was **charged with sedition**, a serious political offense in PNG law.
- Contemporary reporting described the allegation as involving an **alleged plot** or actions framed as threatening to the government.
- The case was widely discussed because it involved a prominent opposition leader and raised public debate about political accountability and the use of criminal charges in political conflict.
