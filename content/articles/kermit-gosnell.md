## Summary

American doctor convicted of murdering three infants born alive during illegal late-term abortions at his Philadelphia clinic. His clinic was called a house of horrors.

## Additional Context

### Clinic and Investigation

- Gosnell operated a clinic in Philadelphia that became the subject of major criminal and regulatory scrutiny.
- Investigators described unsafe medical conditions and poor recordkeeping, with allegations of repeated violations of basic standards of care.

### Criminal Charges and Trial

- The case centered on allegations that infants were killed after being born alive, alongside related charges connected to illegal late-term abortions.
- The trial drew significant local and national attention, including debates about oversight, media coverage, and how medical regulators responded.

### Legal and Policy Aftermath

- The case prompted renewed discussion about inspection regimes, enforcement capacity, and how complaints are handled in healthcare settings.
- It was also cited in political debates in the United States, where different groups framed the case within broader arguments about abortion policy.

### Public Impact

- The details presented in court contributed to lasting public shock and reputational damage to involved institutions.
- The case remains an often-referenced example in discussions of medical malpractice, criminal accountability, and regulatory failure.
