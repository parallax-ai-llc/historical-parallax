## Summary

Indian serial killer who murdered 6 members of his own family and 5 others in Pune, India. He poisoned victims with cyanide and stole their property. Sentenced to death.

## Overview of the case

Accounts of the Kantrole case typically emphasize three linked elements:

1. **Victim targeting within personal circles**, including members of his own family.
2. **Poisoning as the method of killing**, with cyanide frequently mentioned.
3. **Financial motive**, with theft of property/valuables described as part of the series.

## Modus operandi (high-level)

Poisoning cases are often challenging to detect quickly because early symptoms can resemble natural illness. In reports about Kantrole, cyanide is described as the key agent. Cyanide is a fast-acting poison that interferes with cellular respiration; in criminal investigations, proof typically relies on toxicology, procurement evidence, and witness testimony.

## Investigation and prosecution (general notes)

Serial murder investigations in India frequently combine:

- missing-person and suspicious-death reports consolidated over time,
- forensic work (including post-mortem toxicology),
- financial records and property transfers,
- reconstruction of contact patterns between suspect and victims.

This article’s summary indicates that the courts ultimately treated the crimes as sufficiently grave to impose a **death sentence**.

## Geographic context

**Pune** is a major city in the Indian state of Maharashtra. When a case spans multiple victims and includes property theft, investigators typically coordinate across local police stations and courts to link incidents into a single pattern.
