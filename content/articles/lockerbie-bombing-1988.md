## Summary
Pan Am Flight 103 was destroyed by a bomb over Lockerbie, Scotland, on December 21, 1988, killing all 259 on board and 11 on the ground. Libyan intelligence officer Abdelbaset al-Megrahi was convicted. It was the deadliest terrorist attack in Britain.

## Investigation and Legal Proceedings
Investigators linked the bombing to an improvised explosive device concealed in luggage and traced elements of the device through forensic and intelligence work across multiple countries. A long-running international investigation led to indictments of Libyan officials, and a special Scottish court convened in the Netherlands heard the case.

## Policy and Aviation Security Effects
The attack accelerated changes in airline baggage screening, interline baggage handling rules, and broader international coordination on aviation security. It also became a reference point in debates about how intelligence warnings are communicated and acted upon in civil aviation.

## Memorials and Commemoration
Lockerbie and other affected communities created memorials and annual commemorations for the victims, and the event remains a major case study in victim support, cross-border investigations, and the long tail of terrorism-related prosecutions.
