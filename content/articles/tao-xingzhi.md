## Summary

Tao Xingzhi was a Chinese educator and reformer who pioneered mass education and science literacy in China. Influenced by John Dewey at Columbia University, he developed the concept of 'life education' integrating learning with practical experience. He founded numerous schools for the poor and advocated for scientific thinking in Chinese education.
