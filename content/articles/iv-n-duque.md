## Summary

Colombian former president and protégé of Álvaro Uribe whose administration was marked by violent crackdowns on protests in 2021 that killed dozens. Security forces committed extrajudicial killings during the National Strike.

## Political Background

- Duque is a Colombian politician who rose nationally through party politics and alliances associated with former president Álvaro Uribe.
- Before the presidency, he held roles that helped position him as a technocratic and party-backed candidate.

## Presidency (2018–2022)

- His term included continued debate over the implementation of Colombia’s peace process and security policy.
- Economic and social pressures, including inequality and fiscal debates, shaped the domestic political environment.

## 2021 Protests and Human Rights Concerns

- In 2021, large protests (often referred to as part of a "National Strike") saw confrontations between demonstrators and security forces.
- Human rights organizations documented allegations of excessive force and other abuses during the unrest.
- The protest period became a defining controversy of his administration and influenced public opinion about governance and policing.

## Post-Presidency

- After leaving office in 2022, he remained a public figure in Colombian and international political circles.

## International Context

- His presidency coincided with heightened regional attention to Venezuela’s political and humanitarian crisis, an issue central to Colombian foreign and border policy.
- Like many leaders, his administration also faced major governance challenges during the COVID-19 pandemic period.
