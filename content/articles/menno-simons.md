## Summary

Menno Simons was a Dutch Anabaptist leader from whom the Mennonites take their name. He renounced his Catholic priesthood and became a leader of peaceful Anabaptists in the Low Countries and northern Germany. He emphasized believers' baptism, nonviolence, and separation from the world. His writings helped consolidate the Anabaptist movement after its near-destruction.
