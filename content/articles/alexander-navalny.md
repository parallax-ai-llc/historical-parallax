## Summary

Russian opposition leader who survived a Novichok poisoning and returned to Russia knowing he'd be imprisoned. Died in an Arctic penal colony under suspicious circumstances.

## Notes on Naming

Many English-language sources refer to the Russian opposition politician as **Alexei Navalny** (Алексей Навальный). This article uses the identifier and title as stored in the repository, but readers should verify naming and transliteration against primary references.

## Background

- Born in 1976 in the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic (USSR).
- Became known internationally for activism, investigative work, and organizing political opposition.

## Public Profile and Work

### Anti-corruption and Investigations

Navalny became widely associated with anti-corruption messaging and investigations that circulated online, often focusing on alleged corruption among Russian officials and state-linked elites.

### Political Activity

His political activity included campaigning, organizing demonstrations, and creating networks that supported opposition candidates and civic engagement.

## Arrests, Imprisonment, and International Attention

- Faced multiple arrests and criminal cases in Russia over the years.
- In 2020, he was poisoned with a nerve agent widely reported as **Novichok**; international investigations and responses followed.
- After returning to Russia, he was detained and later held in penal colonies under harsh conditions.

## Death (2024)

He died in custody in February 2024 in an Arctic penal colony. International observers, governments, and human-rights organizations called for transparent investigation and accountability.
