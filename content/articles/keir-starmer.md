## Summary

Sir Keir Rodney Starmer (born September 2, 1962) became Prime Minister of the United Kingdom on July 5, 2024, after leading the Labour Party to its largest parliamentary majority since Tony Blair's 1997 victory. A former human rights barrister and Director of Public Prosecutions (2008-2013), Starmer entered Parliament in 2015 and replaced Jeremy Corbyn as Labour leader in April 2020, repositioning the party toward the political center after years of left-wing opposition.

## Early Life and Education

Born September 2, 1962, in Southwark, London:

**Background**:
- Father Rodney, toolmaker
- Mother Josephine, nurse with Still's disease
- Named after Labour founder Keir Hardie
- Working-class upbringing in Surrey

**Education**:
- Reigate Grammar School (became fee-paying during attendance)
- University of Leeds, BA in Law (1985)
- St Edmund Hall, Oxford, BCL (1986)

## Legal Career

### Human Rights Barrister

**Doughty Street Chambers**:
- Founding member (1990)
- Specialized in human rights and criminal law
- Acted in death penalty cases worldwide
- Represented McDonald's libel defendants ("McLibel")

**Distinctions**:
- Queen's Counsel (2002)
- Knighted KCB (2014) for legal services
- Adviser to Northern Ireland Policing Board

### Director of Public Prosecutions

**Tenure** (2008-2013):
- Led Crown Prosecution Service for England and Wales
- Handled phone hacking, MPs' expenses, 2011 London riots prosecutions
- Reformed approach to rape and domestic violence cases
- Established guidelines on assisted suicide prosecution

## Entry into Politics

### Parliamentary Election (2015)

**Holborn and St Pancras**:
- Won safe Labour seat
- Succeeded Frank Dobson
- Re-elected 2017, 2019, 2024

### Shadow Ministerial Roles

**Under Corbyn**:
- Shadow Brexit Secretary (2016-2020)
- Architect of Labour's second referendum policy
- Walked tightrope between Remain and Leave voters
- Position blamed by some for 2019 election defeat

## Labour Leadership (2020-2024)

### Replacing Corbyn

**Leadership Election** (April 2020):
- Won on first round with 56.2%
- Defeated Rebecca Long-Bailey, Lisa Nandy
- Pledged "10 commitments" honoring Corbyn-era policies
- Promised to unite the party

**Reshaping Labour**:
- Suspended Jeremy Corbyn from Labour whip
- Confronted antisemitism within party
- Expelled hard-left factions
- Abandoned several leadership campaign pledges
- Drew criticism from left-wing supporters

### Building Towards Power

**Polling Recovery**:
- Labour fell behind in early COVID period
- Overtook Conservatives in late 2021 (Partygate)
- 20-point lead through 2022-2023
- Tory chaos under Truss reinforced lead

**Policy Repositioning**:
- "Mission-driven government" framework
- Fiscal conservatism, "iron-clad" rules
- Workers' Rights Plan
- Limited tax pledges
- "Five missions" for change

## 2024 General Election

### Landslide Victory

**Results**:
- Labour: 411 seats (gain of 211)
- Conservatives: 121 seats (loss of 251)
- Largest Labour majority since 1997
- Lowest Tory seat count in modern history
- Vote share 33.7% — won big with relatively low share

**Campaign**:
- Ran on "change" against 14 years of Tory rule
- Disciplined messaging
- Avoided controversial commitments
- Liz Truss's economic legacy and Sunak's struggles

### Becoming Prime Minister

**July 5, 2024**:
- Sworn in by King Charles III
- Cabinet of experienced figures
- Rachel Reeves first female Chancellor
- Angela Rayner Deputy PM

## Premiership

### Early Months

**Initial Decisions**:
- Released early prisoners to ease overcrowding
- Cut winter fuel payments to many pensioners (controversial)
- New investment summit
- Tightened immigration enforcement

**Priorities**:
- NHS reform and reduced waiting lists
- Housing target: 1.5 million homes
- "Great British Energy" public energy company
- Workers' Rights Bill

### Foreign Policy

**Approach**:
- Closer alignment with EU on security
- Continued strong Ukraine support
- "Trans-Atlantic" relations recalibrated under Trump second term
- Resumed engagement with China

### Controversies

**"Freebies" Row** (2024):
- Donations of clothing, glasses, concert tickets disclosed
- Lord Alli donations attracted scrutiny
- Starmer paid back some gifts
- Damaged early "different politics" image

**Winter Fuel Cut**:
- Means-tested winter fuel payments
- Pensioner backlash
- Backbench unease

**Riots Response** (August 2024):
- Far-right riots after Southport attack
- Strong law-and-order response
- Fast-track sentencing
- "Two-tier policing" criticism from right

## Political Style

### Cautious Pragmatism

**Approach**:
- Lawyer-like attention to detail
- Avoids ideological positioning
- Process and management over rhetoric
- Reluctant to make uncosted promises

**Communication**:
- Less charismatic than predecessors
- Often described as "wooden"
- Improved on the campaign trail
- Five Missions framework for clarity

### Centrist Repositioning

**From Soft Left to Centre**:
- Backed by Tony Blair-era figures
- Distance from Corbynism
- Pro-business messaging
- Patriotic imagery (Union Jack)
