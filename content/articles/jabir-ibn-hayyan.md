## Summary

Jabir ibn Hayyan (Geber) was an Arab-Persian polymath who is considered the father of chemistry. He introduced the experimental method to alchemy, transforming it into chemistry. He discovered numerous chemical processes including distillation, crystallization, and the synthesis of hydrochloric and nitric acids. His systematic approach to chemical experimentation influenced European science for centuries.
