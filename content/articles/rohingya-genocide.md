## Summary

Genocide against the Rohingya Muslim minority in Myanmar beginning in 2017. Military operations killed over 10,000 and forced 740,000 to flee to Bangladesh. The ICJ ordered Myanmar to prevent genocide.

## Background

The Rohingya are a predominantly Muslim minority in Myanmar’s Rakhine State, long affected by discrimination and restrictions on movement, work, and access to services.
A key political factor has been contested citizenship and statelessness, especially under Myanmar’s post-independence nationality policies.
Tensions in Rakhine also involve communal conflict, historical narratives about migration and identity, and competition over political power and resources.

## Escalation and 2017 Operations

Violence and displacement affecting Rohingya communities intensified over multiple years, including major episodes prior to 2017.
In 2016–2017, attacks by a Rohingya insurgent group were followed by large-scale “clearance operations” by Myanmar’s security forces.
International organizations and human rights groups documented patterns consistent with systematic targeting of civilians and mass forced displacement.

## Humanitarian Impact

The crisis produced one of the largest refugee movements in Asia in recent decades, with the majority of those fleeing entering Bangladesh.
Large refugee camps formed around Cox’s Bazar, creating sustained challenges in shelter, health, education, and long-term legal status.
Repatriation efforts have faced major obstacles, including security concerns and disputes over citizenship and rights.

## International Legal and Political Response

The situation prompted investigations and legal proceedings in international forums, including efforts linked to the International Criminal Court regarding cross-border crimes.
In 2020, the International Court of Justice ordered provisional measures requiring Myanmar to take steps to prevent acts of genocide and preserve evidence.
The crisis has also shaped sanctions, diplomatic pressure, and debates over accountability, military power, and civilian governance in Myanmar.

## Terminology and Ongoing Debate

Use of the term “genocide” reflects assessments that the violence involved intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a protected group.
Myanmar’s authorities have disputed many allegations, while international bodies have continued to document abuses and displacement.
The broader issue remains tied to questions of minority rights, citizenship, and the role of the military in Myanmar’s political system.
