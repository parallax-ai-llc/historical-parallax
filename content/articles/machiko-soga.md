## Summary

Machiko Soga was a Japanese polymer chemist who made pioneering contributions to Ziegler-Natta catalysis and polyolefin research at the Tokyo Institute of Technology. She developed improved catalysts for polypropylene production that were adopted by the chemical industry. She was one of the few female professors in Japanese chemistry departments.
