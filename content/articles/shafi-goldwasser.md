## Summary

Shafi Goldwasser is an Israeli-American computer scientist who has made fundamental contributions to cryptography, computational complexity theory, and computational number theory. She co-invented zero-knowledge proofs, which are now used in blockchain technology and privacy-preserving systems. She received the Turing Award in 2012 with Silvio Micali. She is a professor at MIT and the Weizmann Institute.
