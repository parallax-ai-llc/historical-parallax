## Summary

Mexican factory worker who assassinated presidential candidate Luis Donaldo Colosio.

## Background

- Aburto Martínez was a Mexican civilian who worked in a factory prior to the 1994 assassination.[^1]
- He became publicly known only after the killing of Luis Donaldo Colosio, the Institutional Revolutionary Party (PRI) presidential candidate in Mexico's 1994 election cycle.[^1]

## Assassination of Luis Donaldo Colosio (1994)

- The assassination occurred during a campaign event in Tijuana, Baja California, in March 1994.[^1]
- Aburto Martínez was detained at the scene and identified by authorities as the shooter.[^1]
- The killing became a major political shock in modern Mexican politics, given Colosio's status as the ruling party's candidate.[^1]

## Investigation and Legal Proceedings

- Mexican authorities prosecuted Aburto Martínez for the assassination; he was convicted and sentenced to imprisonment.[^1]
- Public discussion has periodically included claims and controversy about whether he acted alone, but the basic case record centers on Aburto Martínez as the convicted assassin.[^1]

## Incarceration

- Aburto Martínez has remained in custody following his conviction, and his status has continued to be covered in Mexican media and public debate as the case resurfaced over the years.[^1]

## Historical Significance

- The Colosio assassination is frequently cited as a pivotal event that intensified scrutiny of political violence and security around elections in Mexico.[^1]
- Aburto Martínez's name is closely tied to the episode because the case became a recurring reference point in debates about state institutions, accountability, and political transitions in the 1990s.[^1]
