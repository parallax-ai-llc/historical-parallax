## Context: Nigerian trafficking networks

From the 1990s onward, law-enforcement and media reporting frequently described Nigerian-run courier networks operating on long-haul air routes. These networks were often portrayed as:

- Relying on recruiters and handlers who organized travel and concealment
- Using small-to-medium quantities per courier to reduce losses from seizures
- Maintaining connections to wholesale suppliers and foreign distributors

## Alleged role in the supply chain

Where she is described as a “queenpin,” the implication is that her role went beyond couriering and involved planning, financing, or managing multiple participants. Typical responsibilities attributed to organizers include:

- Selecting routes and coordinating handoffs

- Managing cash proceeds and paying participants
