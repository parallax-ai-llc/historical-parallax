## Summary

John Owen was an English Nonconformist theologian who is often considered the greatest Puritan theologian. He served as Dean of Christ Church, Oxford, and Vice-Chancellor of the University under Cromwell. His works on the Holy Spirit, mortification of sin, and communion with God are theological classics. He was a champion of religious toleration and congregational church government.
