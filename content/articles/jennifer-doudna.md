## Summary

Jennifer Doudna is an American biochemist who shared the 2020 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for the development of CRISPR-Cas9 gene editing technology. Together with Emmanuelle Charpentier, she adapted the bacterial immune system CRISPR into a precise genome editing tool. This technology has revolutionized biology by making gene editing faster, cheaper, and more accurate. CRISPR has implications for medicine, agriculture, and biotechnology.
