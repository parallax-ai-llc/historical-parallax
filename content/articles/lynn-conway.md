## Summary

Lynn Conway was an American computer scientist, electrical engineer, and transgender activist who made foundational contributions to VLSI chip design. Her collaboration with Carver Mead on the Mead-Conway approach democratized chip design and enabled the fabless semiconductor industry. She was fired from IBM in 1968 for transitioning and rebuilt her career, eventually becoming a professor at the University of Michigan.
