## Summary

Sushruta was an ancient Indian physician and surgeon who is considered the father of surgery. His text "Sushruta Samhita" describes over 300 surgical procedures, 120 surgical instruments, and is one of the most comprehensive medical texts from antiquity. He pioneered rhinoplasty (nose reconstruction), ophthalmology, and many other surgical techniques that were centuries ahead of their time.

## Additional context

### Text and tradition

- Sushruta is traditionally linked to the *Sushruta Samhita*, a foundational text in Ayurveda that focuses heavily on surgery and trauma care.
- The work is structured to cover anatomy, diagnosis, instruments, operative techniques, and post-operative care, reflecting an attempt to systematize surgical knowledge.
- Scholars debate the precise dating and authorship layers of the text, with many viewing it as a compilation that evolved over time rather than the product of a single moment.

### Surgical education and methods

- Accounts associated with the tradition emphasize hands-on training, including practice on models and simulated materials before operating on patients.
- The text describes approaches to wound management, suturing, and the classification of injuries, alongside discussions of hygiene and preparation.
- Descriptions of reconstructive techniques—especially nasal reconstruction—are frequently cited in the global history of plastic surgery.

### Legacy

- In South Asian medical history, Sushruta is often invoked as an emblem of early surgical sophistication.
- Modern narratives sometimes compare the *Sushruta Samhita* to other ancient medical corpora, highlighting how different civilizations preserved and transmitted clinical knowledge.
