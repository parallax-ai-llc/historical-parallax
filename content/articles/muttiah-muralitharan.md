## Summary

Muttiah Muralitharan took 800 Test wickets, the all-time record. His unique bowling action generated extreme spin.

## Playing Style and Legacy

- Muralitharan was best known as an off-spin bowler whose deliveries produced unusually large turn, making him one of the most difficult bowlers to face in Test cricket.
- His career is frequently discussed in the context of debates over bowling action legality. His action was scrutinized repeatedly, and the controversy became part of his public narrative.
- The 800 Test wickets milestone is widely treated as one of the most durable records in the sport.

## Context

- Sri Lanka’s rise as a major cricketing nation in the 1990s and 2000s is often associated with a core group of players, with Muralitharan’s wicket-taking playing a central role.
- Beyond Tests, he also had a significant One Day International (ODI) career, contributing to Sri Lanka’s competitiveness across formats.

## After Competitive Cricket
