## Summary

Justin Bieber is a Canadian singer, songwriter, and musician who became one of the best-selling music artists of all time after being discovered on YouTube as a teenager in 2008. His rapid ascent from small-town Ontario to global superstardom redefined how artists could be found and launched in the digital age.

Bieber has sold over 150 million records worldwide, won two Grammy Awards, and holds numerous Billboard and Spotify records. His career has spanned teen pop, R&B, EDM, and gospel-influenced music, demonstrating a remarkable ability to evolve artistically across more than a decade in the public eye. Beyond music, his personal struggles and public redemption arc have made him one of the most scrutinized and discussed celebrities of his generation.

## Early Life & Background

Justin Drew Bieber was born on March 1, 1994, in London, Ontario, Canada, and raised in Stratford, Ontario. He was brought up primarily by his mother, Pattie Mallette, who was 18 years old at the time of his birth. Growing up in modest circumstances, Bieber taught himself to play piano, drums, guitar, and trumpet. As a child, he entered a local singing competition in Stratford in 2007, placing second. His mother began uploading videos of his performances to YouTube so that family and friends could watch, inadvertently setting the stage for his discovery.

## Rise to Prominence

Talent manager Scooter Braun stumbled upon Bieber's YouTube videos in 2008 and arranged for the 13-year-old to audition for Usher in Atlanta. Bieber soon signed with RBMG Records, a joint venture between Usher and Island Records. His debut EP, *My World*, released in November 2009, was certified platinum in the United States, making him the first solo artist to have seven songs from a debut album chart on the Billboard Hot 100. The follow-up, *My World 2.0* (2010), debuted at number one, driven by the hit single "Baby," which became one of the most-viewed videos in YouTube history.

His transition from teen idol to mature artist was marked by *Purpose* (2015), which debuted at number one and produced three chart-topping singles: "What Do You Mean?," "Sorry," and "Love Yourself." In 2021, *Justice* continued his commercial dominance, with "Peaches" reaching number one on the Hot 100.

## Key Achievements

Bieber has accumulated over 75 billion streams on Spotify, making him one of the most-streamed artists on the platform. He has won two Grammy Awards, 21 Billboard Music Awards, 18 American Music Awards, and 22 MTV Europe Music Awards. He was the youngest solo male artist to debut at number one on the Billboard 200 since Stevie Wonder in 1963. His *Purpose World Tour* (2016–2017) grossed over $250 million.

## Controversies & Criticism

Bieber's rapid fame brought significant personal and legal challenges. Between 2013 and 2014, he faced arrests for DUI and vandalism, and incidents involving reckless driving and public altercations drew widespread media criticism. His behavior during this period led to a petition on the White House website calling for his deportation from the United States, which gathered over 270,000 signatures. Bieber has spoken publicly about his struggles with depression, anxiety, and substance abuse during this time. In 2022, he was diagnosed with Ramsay Hunt syndrome, which caused partial facial paralysis and forced him to cancel the remaining dates of his *Justice World Tour*.

## Legacy & Influence

Bieber's discovery via YouTube fundamentally changed the music industry's approach to talent scouting, proving that social media could rival traditional pathways to stardom. He pioneered the model of the internet-born pop star that artists like Shawn Mendes and Billie Eilish would later follow. His willingness to discuss mental health publicly helped destigmatize these conversations among younger audiences. As both a cautionary tale and a comeback story, Bieber's career remains one of the defining narratives of 2010s pop culture.
