## Background

Svetlana Aleksandrovna Masterkova (born 17 January 1968, Achinsk—then in the Russian SFSR of the Soviet Union) is a Russian former middle-distance runner.[^1] She is best known for winning two Olympic gold medals at the 1996 Atlanta Games and for setting world records in the 1000 metres and the mile.[^1]

*Note:* The frontmatter field `death: "Unknown"` reflects the current state of this repository record; Wikipedia describes her as living.[^1]

## Major Achievements

- **Olympic Games (1996, Atlanta):** Gold in **800 m** and **1500 m**.[^1]
- **World Athletics Championships (1999, Seville):** Gold in **1500 m**; Bronze in **800 m**.[^1]
- **European Athletics Championships (1998, Budapest):** Gold in **1500 m**.[^1]
