## Summary

Belgian man who threw grenades and shot into a crowd in Liège, killing 6 and injuring 125.

## Context and background

- Nordine Amrani was a French-speaking Belgian man of Moroccan origin, born in Brussels (Ixelles/Elsene), and trained as a welder.[^1]
- Reporting describes a difficult upbringing, including being orphaned young and raised in foster homes.[^1]
- He had a documented interest in firearms and a history of convictions related to weapons possession.[^1]
- He had been released from prison in 2010, prior to the 2011 attack.[^1]

## 2011 Liège attack (high-level)

- Date/time: Tuesday, 13 December 2011, around 12:34–12:40 local time (CET).[^1]
- Location: Place Saint-Lambert, central Liège, near the courthouse and a public square with heavy pedestrian traffic.[^1]
- Method: Witness accounts and later reporting describe stun grenades being thrown followed by gunfire directed at civilians in the square.[^1]
- Weapons reported: an FN FAL rifle and a Smith & Wesson Model 57 revolver, along with multiple M84 stun grenades.[^1]
- The incident ended when Amrani died by suicide at the scene.[^1]
- Earlier the same day, he also killed a woman at his home; this death is commonly included in totals associated with the event.[^1]

## Casualties and reporting notes

- Public reports vary on precise totals across early coverage versus later, consolidated accounting.
- Wikipedia’s summary reports 7 deaths (including the perpetrator and the woman killed earlier that day) and 147 injured.[^1]

## Immediate response and aftermath

- Police sealed off the area shortly after the attack, and emergency services treated large numbers of injured people in and around the square.[^1]
- The event prompted national mourning and international media coverage, given the public setting and scale of injuries.[^1]
