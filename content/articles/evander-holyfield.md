## Summary

Evander Holyfield was a four-time heavyweight champion who defeated both Tyson and Lennox Lewis. He is known for Tyson biting his ear.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1984 | Won Olympic bronze at LA |

| 1996 | Defeated Mike Tyson |
| 1997 | Tyson bit his ear in rematch |

## Additional Context

Holyfield’s career is notable for success across weight classes and for a long period at the top of heavyweight boxing during a transitional era. He first gained major attention as a cruiserweight champion before moving up to heavyweight, where he faced several of the most prominent fighters of the late 1980s and 1990s.

### Style and Public Image

- Known for durability, conditioning, and a high work rate in longer fights.
- Built a reputation for competitiveness in high-profile championship bouts.
- Became a major figure in mainstream sports culture during boxing’s pay-per-view peak.

### Notable Rivalries and Fights (High Level)

- **Mike Tyson**: Their bouts became widely remembered not only for the competitive matchup but for the 1997 disqualification incident.
- **Riddick Bowe**: A defining trilogy frequently cited in accounts of heavyweight boxing in the early 1990s.
- **Lennox Lewis**: Two fights in 1999 that are often discussed in debates about heavyweight rankings of the period.

### Legacy

Holyfield is commonly referenced in discussions of the modern heavyweight era as an example of a champion who repeatedly returned to title contention. His name remains strongly associated with the “four-time heavyweight champion” achievement and with some of boxing’s most-viewed events.

### Early Career Note

His 1984 Olympic medal and subsequent rapid rise in the professional ranks are often used to illustrate how quickly he moved from amateur success to world-title contention.
