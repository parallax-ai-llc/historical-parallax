## Summary

Syrian governments sarin nerve agent attack on the rebel-held town of Khan Shaykhun on April 4, 2017 that killed over 80 people. The US responded with cruise missile strikes.

## Background

The Khan Shaykhun chemical attack occurred during the Syrian civil war, in a phase marked by intensified fighting in Idlib Governorate and repeated international allegations regarding the use of prohibited chemical agents.

Khan Shaykhun was a rebel-held town at the time, and its location in northwest Syria meant it sat along contested lines of control.

## What happened

Victims and first responders reported symptoms consistent with exposure to a nerve agent, including respiratory distress and convulsions. The incident rapidly drew global attention due to the scale of civilian casualties and the visibility of medical response efforts.

Investigations by international bodies attributed responsibility to Syrian government forces, while the Syrian government and its allies rejected those findings and offered alternative explanations.

## International response

The United States launched cruise-missile strikes against Shayrat Airbase on April 7, 2017, describing the action as a response to the chemical attack and a deterrent against future use. The strike increased U.S. direct military involvement in the conflict and intensified diplomatic confrontation at the UN Security Council.

## Significance

The event became a major reference point in debates over enforcement of the Chemical Weapons Convention, the credibility and limits of international accountability mechanisms during wartime, and the strategic calculus of external powers operating in Syria.
