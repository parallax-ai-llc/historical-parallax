## Summary

Massive South Korean internment camp in Busan where 4,000 homeless, disabled, and orphaned people were enslaved. Guards beat inmates to death, raped women, and sold children for adoption.

## Additional Context

- **Location:** Busan, South Korea.
- **Stated purpose:** Social welfare and “rehabilitation” for vagrants and vulnerable people.
- **Population:** Reports describe thousands of detainees, including homeless people, people with disabilities, and children.

## Documented Abuses

- **Forced confinement:** People were detained involuntarily, often via police roundups.
- **Forced labor:** Inmates were compelled to work under coercion.
- **Violence and intimidation:** Beatings and harsh discipline were widely reported.
- **Sexual violence and trafficking allegations:** Reporting includes accounts of sexual abuse and illegal adoptions.

## Exposure, Investigations, and Aftermath

- **Public exposure:** The facility became widely known after incidents and allegations surfaced in the late 1980s.
- **Accountability:** Legal and political responses have been criticized as limited relative to the scale of abuse.
- **Victims’ advocacy:** Survivors and civil-society groups have continued to push for truth-finding and reparations.

## Why It Matters

Brothers Home is often cited in discussions of how state power, policing, and welfare institutions can combine to produce large-scale human-rights abuses when oversight is weak.
