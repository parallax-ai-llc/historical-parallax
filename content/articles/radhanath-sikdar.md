## Summary

Radhanath Sikdar was an Indian mathematician who computed the height of Mount Everest, determining it to be the world's highest peak. Working as a chief computer (calculator) for the Great Trigonometric Survey of India under Andrew Waugh, he processed the survey data and first identified 'Peak XV' as the tallest mountain in 1852.
