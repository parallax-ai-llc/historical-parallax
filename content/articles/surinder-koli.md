## Summary

Indian serial killer and cannibal from Noida who murdered at least 19 children between 2005 and 2006. He worked as a domestic servant and lured child victims. Sentenced to death multiple times.

## Additional context

### Nithari killings (Noida) context

- Koli is associated with the “Nithari killings” case, a series of child disappearances and murders that drew national attention in India in 2006.
- The case became highly public after human remains were discovered near a residence in the Nithari area of Noida, leading to an intensive investigation and extensive media coverage.

### Investigation and allegations

- Reporting on the case included disturbing allegations, including claims of sexual violence and cannibalism; these details have been widely discussed in coverage but also contested in parts of the legal and public debate.
- The investigation involved multiple agencies and raised questions about earlier missing-person reports, local policing, and how patterns of disappearances were handled.

### Trials and legal proceedings

- Koli was tried in multiple cases connected to different victims and received multiple death sentences from trial courts.
- Appellate proceedings and judicial reviews unfolded over years, reflecting the complexity of the case record and the number of charges.

### Public impact

- The Nithari case became a reference point in conversations about child protection, urban safety, and the responsiveness of institutions to vulnerable communities.
