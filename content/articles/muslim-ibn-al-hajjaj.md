## Summary

Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj was a Persian Islamic scholar who compiled Sahih Muslim, considered the second most authentic collection of hadith in Sunni Islam. His methodology differed from al-Bukhari's in organization, grouping all narrations of a hadith together. He was a student of al-Bukhari and traveled widely to collect traditions. His collection contains approximately 7,500 hadith.
