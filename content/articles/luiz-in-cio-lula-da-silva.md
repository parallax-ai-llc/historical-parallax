Additional context: Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva (commonly known as **Lula**) is a Brazilian politician and former trade union leader who served as **President of Brazil** from **2003–2010** and again beginning in **2023**.

## Key Facts

- **Full name:** Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva
- **Political affiliation:** Workers' Party (Partido dos Trabalhadores, **PT**)
- **Known for:** Rise from industrial worker to union leadership; national political prominence; multiple presidential terms.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1970s | Prominent labor leader in the ABC industrial region (Greater São Paulo), associated with major strikes during Brazil’s military dictatorship period. |
| 1980 | Helped found the **Workers' Party (PT)**. |
| 1989 | Ran for president and lost in the second round (a key early national campaign). |
| 2002 | Elected President of Brazil (took office in 2003). |
| 2003–2010 | Served two consecutive terms as President of Brazil; associated with economic growth periods and large social programs. |
| 2018 | Imprisoned following a corruption conviction connected to Operation Car Wash (Lava Jato); later released. |
| 2021 | Convictions annulled by Brazil’s Supreme Federal Court, restoring political rights. |
| 2022 | Won the presidential election and returned to office in 2023. |

## Presidency (2003–2010)

High-level themes often associated with Lula’s first two terms include:

- **Social policy:** Expansion of anti-poverty and cash-transfer programs (notably **Bolsa Família**), with reductions in poverty and inequality reported in various analyses.
- **Economy:** A period that included commodity-driven growth and efforts to maintain macroeconomic stability.
- **Foreign policy:** Increased international engagement, including South–South cooperation and a more visible role in multilateral forums.

## Legal and Political Controversies (high level)

- Lula’s political era includes major corruption scandals that affected Brazilian institutions and parties, including the **Mensalão** scandal (mid-2000s) and **Operation Car Wash (Lava Jato)** in the 2010s.
- Lula was convicted and later imprisoned in relation to Lava Jato; the decisions were later overturned/annulled by the Supreme Federal Court, allowing him to run again.
