## Summary

Sinaloa Cartel leader known as 'El Licenciado' who helped El Chapo escape prison and later tried to take over the cartel. He was arrested and extradited to the US.

## Background

Dámaso López Núñez, known by the nickname "El Licenciado," is associated with the Sinaloa Cartel, one of Mexico's most powerful drug-trafficking organizations. He has been described as a former official within Mexico's prison system who later became aligned with cartel leadership.

## Role in the Sinaloa Cartel

- López was linked to the inner circle around Joaquín "El Chapo" Guzmán.
- After Guzmán's 2015 escape from a high-security prison via a tunnel, López was widely reported as a key facilitator.
- Following Guzmán's subsequent recapture and extradition, López was described as attempting to position himself as a leading figure, amid factional conflict.

## Arrest and extradition

- Mexican authorities arrested López in 2017.
- He was extradited to the United States in 2018 to face federal charges.
- In US proceedings, he was reported to have cooperated with prosecutors, a common dynamic in major organized-crime cases where testimony is used to dismantle networks.

## Significance

The case is frequently cited in discussions of:

- How prison corruption and official access can enable high-profile escapes
- The internal succession struggles that can follow the removal of a cartel leader
- Cross-border cooperation between Mexican and US law enforcement on trafficking cases
