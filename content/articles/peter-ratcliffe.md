## Summary

Peter Ratcliffe is a British physician and cell biologist who shared the 2019 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for discoveries of how cells sense and adapt to oxygen availability. He identified the molecular machinery that cells use to detect oxygen levels, including the hypoxia-inducible factor (HIF) pathway. His work revealed fundamental mechanisms of oxygen regulation.
