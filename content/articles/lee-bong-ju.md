## Background

Lee Bong-ju is a South Korean marathon runner known for success at major international competitions in the 1990s and early 2000s. Marathon careers are often evaluated by a mix of championship outcomes (where tactics and weather matter) and big-city marathon wins (where deep fields and pacing strategies are central).

## Notable achievements (high level)

- **Olympics:** His silver medal in the marathon at the 1996 Atlanta Olympics is a defining result and is frequently cited in South Korean athletics history.
- **Major marathons:** Winning the Boston Marathon in 2001 placed him among a relatively small set of athletes who have won one of the sport’s most prominent annual races.

## Competitive profile

Marathon runners at Lee’s level typically combine:

- High weekly endurance volume over long training cycles
- Threshold and tempo work to sustain fast pace for long durations
- Race-day fueling and hydration planning

Performance can be strongly affected by course profile and conditions; Boston, for example, is known for its net downhill start and variable wind, which can influence pacing and late-race outcomes.

## Place in South Korean sport

In popular and media accounts, Lee is often discussed as part of a period when South Korea produced internationally competitive marathoners and increased the visibility of distance running for domestic audiences.
