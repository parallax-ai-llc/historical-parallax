## Summary

Nao Deguchi was the co-founder of Oomoto, one of Japan's most influential new religious movements. An impoverished widow, she began receiving divine revelations in 1892 from the deity Ushitora no Konjin. She wrote over 200,000 pages of automatic writing (Ofudesaki). Her apocalyptic teachings about the reconstruction of the world attracted many followers and alarmed the Japanese government.
