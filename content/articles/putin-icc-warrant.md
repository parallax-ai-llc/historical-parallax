## Summary

The International Criminal Court issued an arrest warrant for Russian President Vladimir Putin in March 2023 for the war crime of unlawful deportation of Ukrainian children to Russia.

## Background

The arrest warrant was issued in the context of Russia’s full-scale invasion of Ukraine and subsequent international investigations into alleged violations of international humanitarian law. The ICC can investigate certain crimes when jurisdictional conditions are met, including through referrals or acceptance of jurisdiction by a state.

## Legal basis and allegations

The ICC announcement referenced alleged war crimes relating to the unlawful deportation and transfer of protected persons (children) from occupied areas. Public-facing summaries emphasize that an arrest warrant reflects a judicial finding of reasonable grounds at that stage, not a final determination of guilt.

## Jurisdiction and enforcement limits

The ICC does not have its own police force and relies on cooperation by states, especially ICC member states (States Parties), to execute warrants. Questions about jurisdiction and cooperation are politically charged, particularly where major powers reject the court’s authority.

## Diplomatic implications

The warrant had practical implications for international travel and diplomacy, since some countries are obligated under the Rome Statute to arrest individuals subject to ICC warrants if they enter their territory, while others are not.

## Broader significance

The episode is often discussed as a high-profile test of the ICC’s capacity to pursue accountability in active conflicts and to sustain long-running cases despite geopolitical constraints.
