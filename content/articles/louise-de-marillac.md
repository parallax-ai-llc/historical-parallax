## Summary

Louise de Marillac was a French noblewoman who co-founded the Daughters of Charity with Vincent de Paul, creating the first non-cloistered religious community of women in the Catholic Church. The Daughters of Charity served the poor in hospitals, orphanages, and schools. She is the patron saint of social workers and those who care for the sick. Her innovative model of religious life for women profoundly influenced Catholic charitable work.
