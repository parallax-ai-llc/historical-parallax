## Summary

Former Indonesian national police chief accused of involvement in extrajudicial killings during anti-drug operations. He later became interior minister.

## Context: Policing and the “War on Drugs”

Indonesia has periodically pursued aggressive anti-narcotics campaigns. Human-rights organizations and investigative reporting have criticized some operations for excessive use of force, weak accountability, and blurred lines between law enforcement and militarized tactics.

## Controversies (High-Level)

Public allegations and criticism around anti-drug operations during periods of Karnavian’s senior police leadership have included claims of:

- **extrajudicial killings** or unlawful lethal force,
- incentives that encourage “shoot-on-sight” behavior,
- limited transparency in incident reporting and investigations.

As with many policing controversies, the specifics depend on case-by-case investigations, internal accountability mechanisms, and judicial outcomes, which may not be uniformly available in public sources.

## Government Roles

Karnavian is known for holding high-level state positions that span security and civil administration, including:

- senior roles within the Indonesian National Police,
- service as **National Police Chief**,
- later appointment as **Interior Minister**, a role that includes oversight of domestic governance, regional administration, and aspects of electoral administration.

## Significance

Debates about Karnavian’s tenure reflect broader Indonesian governance questions:

- how to balance public security goals with human-rights safeguards,
- how police oversight and internal discipline operate in practice,
- and how security officials transition into civilian executive roles.
