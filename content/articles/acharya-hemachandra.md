## Summary

Acharya Hemachandra was a Jain scholar, polymath, and one of the most brilliant intellectuals of medieval India. He wrote on grammar, logic, rhetoric, yoga, and Jain philosophy. He was the court scholar of the Chalukya kings of Gujarat and composed the Trishashtishalakapurusha Charita, a comprehensive Jain universal history. His Sanskrit grammar Siddhahemachandra rivaled Panini's. He influenced King Kumarapala to adopt vegetarianism and Jain principles.
