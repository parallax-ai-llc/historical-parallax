## Summary

Rwandan first lady accused of being a key member of the Akazu inner circle that planned and executed the 1994 genocide. She fled to France where she has been protected from extradition.

## Background

Agathe Kanziga married Juvénal Habyarimana, who later became President of Rwanda. As First Lady during the 1970s–1990s, she was associated in some accounts with elite political networks close to the presidency.

## Allegations Related to 1994

Following the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi in Rwanda, she was accused by Rwandan authorities of involvement with the so-called “Akazu” (an alleged inner circle around the presidential family) and of participation in planning or incitement. She has denied wrongdoing.

Because allegations of genocide and related crimes involve complex evidentiary records and multiple jurisdictions, public reporting often distinguishes between claims made by prosecutors and the outcomes of court proceedings.

## Exile and Legal Proceedings

After the death of President Habyarimana in April 1994, Agathe Habyarimana left Rwanda and was evacuated to France. Over subsequent years, Rwanda issued requests and warrants seeking her prosecution or extradition. French legal authorities have considered aspects of these requests within French procedural frameworks.

## Public Significance

Her case is frequently discussed in relation to:

- The role of political elites and networks in the lead-up to mass violence.
- International jurisdiction and extradition practices for genocide allegations.
- The long timeline often involved in post-conflict accountability processes.
