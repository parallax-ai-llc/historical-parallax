## Summary

Terrorist attack at Lod Airport (now Ben Gurion) in Israel on May 30, 1972 by three Japanese Red Army members. They killed 26 people and wounded 80 by spraying gunfire in the terminal.

## Background

- The attack took place at **Lod Airport** near Tel Aviv, which was later renamed **Ben Gurion Airport**.
- The operation is widely described as a collaboration in which **Japanese Red Army** members carried out an attack aligned with Palestinian militant objectives.

## Attack (30 May 1972)

- Three assailants arrived in the arrivals area and opened fire with automatic weapons; accounts also describe the use of grenades.
- The victims included local civilians and international travelers; many of those killed were **Puerto Rican Christian pilgrims**.
- Two of the attackers were killed during the incident, while one survived and was captured.

## Perpetrators and Accountability

- The surviving attacker is commonly identified in reporting as **Kōzō Okamoto**, who was arrested, tried, and imprisoned in Israel.
- Okamoto was later released as part of a prisoner exchange and subsequently lived abroad.

## Impact and Legacy

- The incident became a prominent example of transnational terrorism in the early 1970s, including the involvement of non-Middle Eastern militants in attacks related to the Arab–Israeli conflict.
- Airport security practices internationally were tightened over time, and the attack is frequently discussed in analyses of aviation security and counterterrorism.
