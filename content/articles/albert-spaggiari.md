## Summary

French bank robber who masterminded the 1976 heist of the Société Générale bank in Nice through the sewers, stealing $10 million. He left a note: 'Without weapons, without hatred, without violence.' Escaped from court by jumping out a window.

## The 1976 Nice bank heist

Spaggiari is most closely associated with a high-profile burglary of a Société Générale branch in Nice. The heist became famous for its method: burglars accessed the vault via underground tunnels connected to the city’s sewer system, allowing a prolonged entry without an armed confrontation at the bank counter.

## Planning and execution (high level)

Accounts generally emphasize careful reconnaissance, time spent working underground, and the use of nonviolent techniques to open deposit boxes and remove valuables. The note left behind—often quoted as "Sans armes, ni haine, ni violence" (without weapons, without hatred, without violence)—helped shape the public image of the crime as an "ingenious" rather than overtly violent operation, even though it was still a major felony.

## Arrest and courtroom escape

After the robbery, Spaggiari was arrested but later escaped in a dramatic incident during a court appearance, jumping from a window and fleeing with assistance. His escape added to the legend of the case and complicated French law-enforcement efforts to bring him back into custody.

## Later life and legacy

Spaggiari remained a fugitive until his death. The heist has been repeatedly referenced in popular culture and true-crime writing as a template for "tunnel" or "sewer" bank burglaries and as an example of how a single event can dominate a criminal’s public reputation.
