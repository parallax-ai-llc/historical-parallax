## Summary

Sudanese Arab militia that carried out genocide in Darfur on behalf of the Sudanese government. They attacked villages, killing men and raping women in a campaign of ethnic cleansing.

## Overview
Janjaweed is a term commonly used for Arab militia groups that operated primarily in the Darfur region of western Sudan in the early 2000s. The label has been used in reporting and human-rights documentation in connection with attacks on civilian communities during the Darfur conflict.

## Background
The Darfur conflict escalated in 2003 amid fighting between Sudanese government forces, allied militias, and rebel groups. Multiple investigations and international bodies described patterns of violence that included village raids, displacement, and widespread humanitarian impacts.

## International response and legal proceedings
The crisis prompted major humanitarian operations and diplomatic initiatives, including African Union and United Nations peacekeeping efforts. The International Criminal Court opened cases related to Darfur, and the term “Janjaweed” often appears in accounts describing the armed actors alleged to have carried out abuses.

## Legacy
In later years, some militia elements were reported to have been reorganized or integrated into other security structures. The Darfur conflict and associated allegations of atrocities remain a central subject in discussions of Sudan’s contemporary history and accountability efforts.
