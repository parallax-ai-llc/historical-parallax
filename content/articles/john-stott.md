## Summary

John Stott was a British Anglican clergyman and one of the most influential evangelical leaders of the 20th century. He served as rector of All Souls Church, Langham Place, in London for decades. He was a principal author of the Lausanne Covenant of 1974, which defined evangelical Christianity's mission. Time magazine named him one of the 100 most influential people in the world in 2005.
