## Summary

Leader of Los Valencia cartel in Michoacán who ran a methamphetamine empire. His organization was one of the precursors to La Familia Michoacana.

## Context: methamphetamine trafficking in Michoacán

Michoacán became an important region in Mexico’s synthetic-drug economy as criminal groups shifted from predominantly plant-based drugs to industrial-scale production of methamphetamine.

This shift required access to precursor chemicals, clandestine laboratories, and distribution corridors to domestic and U.S. markets.

## Los Valencia and successor dynamics

Los Valencia (also referred to in some reporting as the Valencia cartel) is often described as an early power in Michoacán’s methamphetamine trade.

Narratives about later organizations—such as La Familia Michoacana—frequently describe an evolution where earlier trafficking networks provided personnel, local influence, and logistics that successor groups adapted and expanded.

## Arrest and disruption

Barragan’s reported arrest in the early 2000s is typically framed as part of wider efforts by Mexican authorities to target leadership figures tied to synthetic-drug production.

Leadership removals sometimes fragment organizations, but they can also generate splinter groups that compete violently for remaining assets and territory.

## Broader significance

This period is often used by analysts to explain how localized trafficking groups in western Mexico grew into larger, more ideologically framed and territorially embedded organizations.

It also illustrates how the economics of synthetic drugs—high value-to-weight and scalable production—can accelerate organizational growth and conflict.
