## Summary

Lilian Thuram is France's most capped player (142). He scored twice in the 1998 WC semi-final.

## Club Career (High Level)

- Thuram built a long European club career, playing for major teams in **France** and **Italy** and later in **Spain**.
- He was valued for defensive versatility, often deployed at **right-back** or **center-back** depending on team needs.

## International Career Highlights

- A key member of France’s golden era squads that won **1998 World Cup** and **Euro 2000**.
- His two-goal performance in the **1998 World Cup semi-final** is one of the most memorable moments of his playing career.
- Known for defensive consistency across tournament football and long runs of qualification and finals appearances.

## Style of Play

- Renowned for athleticism, tactical discipline, and the ability to handle elite wingers one-on-one.
- Combined pace with positional intelligence, making him effective both in open play and defensive transitions.

## Post-Playing Work

- After retirement, Thuram became widely recognized for **anti-racism** and social-justice advocacy, including educational and public-speaking work.

## Key Takeaways

- Thuram is remembered for defensive excellence and unusual longevity at the top international level.
- His 1998 World Cup semi-final goals remain a defining highlight.
- Post-retirement, he became prominent as a public intellectual and advocate on racism.
