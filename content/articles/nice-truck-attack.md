## Context and Background

- The attack occurred on **14 July 2016 (Bastille Day)** in Nice, France.
- Large public celebrations and fireworks on the **Promenade des Anglais** created dense crowds and a high-impact target.
- The event is commonly analyzed as a case study in vehicle-ramming attacks and public-event security.

## What Happened (High Level)

- A cargo truck was driven into pedestrians along the seafront promenade.
- The incident resulted in **86 fatalities** and **hundreds of injuries** (sources vary by counting method and later deaths).
- French authorities treated the attack as terrorism-linked violence, and it became part of a broader wave of attacks in France in the 2010s.

## Aftermath

- The attack triggered immediate national mourning, emergency response mobilization, and extended investigations.
- Public debate followed regarding perimeter controls, policing levels, and the challenges of securing open urban promenades.
- Memorials and annual commemorations have been held in Nice to honor victims and survivors.

## Significance

- Demonstrated how everyday vehicles can be weaponized with limited logistics.
- Influenced security planning for mass gatherings, including use of physical barriers, vehicle exclusion zones, and crowd-flow management.
- Remains one of the deadliest attacks in modern French history.
