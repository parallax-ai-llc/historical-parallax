## Summary

Derek Walcott was a Saint Lucian poet and playwright who received the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1992. His epic poem Omeros reimagines Homer's Iliad in a Caribbean setting. His work explores the cultural complexity of the Caribbean, bridging European literary tradition and Caribbean experience with extraordinary linguistic virtuosity.
