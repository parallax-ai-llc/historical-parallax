## Summary

David Julius is an American physiologist who shared the 2021 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his discoveries of receptors for temperature and touch. He identified the TRPV1 receptor that senses heat and capsaicin (the compound that makes chili peppers hot) and the TRPM8 receptor for cold. His work revealed how the nervous system senses temperature.
