## Summary

Minneapolis police officer convicted of killing Justine Damond who had called 911 for help.

## Incident (2017)

- The shooting occurred in Minneapolis, Minnesota, in July 2017.
- Justine Damond (also reported as Justine Ruszczyk Damond) called 911 to report a possible assault near her home.
- Noor and his partner responded to the call; Damond approached their police vehicle.
- Noor fired a single shot that killed Damond.

## Legal Proceedings

- Noor was charged in connection with Damond's death.
- In 2019, a jury convicted Noor of:
  - third-degree murder, and
  - second-degree manslaughter.
- Sentencing followed the 2019 convictions.
- Subsequent appeals and court rulings altered the status of some convictions (see Wikipedia for the chronology).

## Aftermath and Public Attention

- The case received substantial public attention because the victim had called police for assistance.
- It also drew attention internationally, including in Australia, due to Damond's background.
- The incident and trial were widely covered in local and national U.S. media.
