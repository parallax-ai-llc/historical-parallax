## Summary

The Sidemen are a British YouTube collective formed on October 19, 2013, consisting of seven members: KSI (Olajide Olatunji), Miniminter (Simon Minter), Zerkaa (Josh Bradley), TBJZL (Tobi Brown), Behzinga (Ethan Payne), Vikkstar123 (Vikram Barn), and W2S (Harry Lewis). Originally brought together through their shared love of gaming and online content creation, the group grew into one of the most successful and recognisable YouTube collectives in the world, accumulating hundreds of millions of subscribers across their individual and collective channels.

The Sidemen's significance extends well beyond YouTube metrics. They represent a generation of British internet culture that transformed how young people consume entertainment, and they pioneered a model of collaborative content creation that influenced countless creators worldwide. Their ability to evolve from FIFA gaming videos into a multi-platform entertainment and business empire demonstrated that online creators could build sustainable, diversified brands.

## Early Life & Background

The members of the Sidemen grew up primarily in England, with backgrounds spanning London, Essex, and beyond. Several members, including KSI, attended schools in the Home Counties before pursuing YouTube full-time. The group coalesced through the gaming community, with many members initially connecting through online interactions around FIFA and Call of Duty content in the early 2010s. The shared house model — members living together in London — became central to their early identity and content output, fostering a chemistry that translated naturally on screen.

## Rise to Prominence

The Sidemen's collective channel launched in October 2013, building on the pre-existing audiences of its individual members. Their early content centred on FIFA Ultimate Team challenges and gaming videos, but the group rapidly diversified into vlogs, challenges, and sketch comedy. By 2016, the channel had reached tens of millions of views, and individual members like KSI were already household names in UK internet culture.

A significant turning point came with the Sidemen's move into large-scale charity football matches. Beginning in 2016, the Sidemen Charity Match pitted the group against fellow YouTubers in front of tens of thousands of fans at professional stadiums including Wembley Arena and Stamford Bridge. These events sold out rapidly, signalling the group's crossover appeal beyond purely online audiences.

## Key Achievements

- The Sidemen YouTube channel surpassed 20 million subscribers, with combined individual subscriptions exceeding 100 million.
- Their charity football matches raised significant sums for causes including children's hospices and food poverty organisations.
- In 2019, the group launched **Sides**, a clothing and lifestyle brand that expanded into a successful e-commerce operation.
- They established **Sides restaurant**, a fried chicken chain with locations across London, growing into a nationally recognised fast-food brand by the mid-2020s.
- KSI's boxing career, supported and promoted partly through the Sidemen platform, culminated in high-profile professional bouts, bringing mainstream sports media attention.

## Controversies & Criticism

The Sidemen have faced scrutiny on several occasions. In 2017, a public falling-out between KSI and other members — particularly documented in diss tracks and public social media exchanges — temporarily fractured the group before a reconciliation. Individual members have faced criticism for insensitive remarks in older content, prompting public apologies and content removal. The group's charity match events have occasionally attracted criticism regarding ticket pricing and accessibility for younger fans.

## Legacy & Influence

The Sidemen fundamentally shaped British YouTube culture and demonstrated that a collaborative group model could sustain and amplify individual creator careers over more than a decade. Their business ventures — from clothing to restaurants — served as a blueprint for content creators seeking to build tangible commercial enterprises. As of 2026, they remain one of the longest-running and most commercially successful YouTube collectives globally, influencing a new generation of British creators who cite the Sidemen as a primary inspiration.
