## Summary

Mary Anning was a British fossil collector and paleontologist who made some of the most important fossil discoveries in history. She found the first correctly identified ichthyosaur skeleton, the first two plesiosaur skeletons, and the first pterosaur skeleton found in Britain. Despite her lack of formal education and the gender discrimination of her era, her discoveries transformed scientific understanding of prehistoric life and Earth's history.
