## Summary

Andre-Marie Ampere is the founder of electrodynamics. He formulated Ampere law relating magnetic fields to electric currents. The SI unit of electric current is named after him.

## Background

Ampere was born in Lyon in the late 18th century and is remembered as a largely self-taught polymath who worked across mathematics, physics, and natural philosophy.

## Scientific contributions

### Electrodynamics

Ampere played a central role in establishing **electrodynamics**, the study of forces and fields associated with electric currents. His work helped formalize relationships between electric current and magnetic effects, building on experimental discoveries of his era.

### Ampere's law and related concepts

Ampere's law is a foundational statement connecting magnetic fields to electric currents. In later formalisms, it is incorporated into Maxwell's equations, which are core to classical electromagnetism.

### Naming legacy

- The SI unit of electric current, the **ampere (A)**, is named after him.
- Many physics and engineering texts treat Ampere as a key figure linking experimental observation to mathematical description in early electromagnetic theory.
