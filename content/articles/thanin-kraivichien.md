## Summary

Thai prime minister appointed by the military after the 1976 Thammasat University massacre. His brief government served as civilian cover for the military junta that committed atrocities against student protesters.

## Background

- Thanin Kraivichien was trained in law and is commonly described as a conservative jurist prior to entering executive office.
- He rose to national leadership during a period of intense political conflict in Thailand, shaped by Cold War anti-communism and domestic polarization.

## Appointment and Political Setting (1976)

- Following the October 1976 violence surrounding Thammasat University and the subsequent military intervention, Thanin was appointed prime minister.
- His government is often characterized as closely aligned with military and royalist conservative forces that sought to reverse earlier democratic opening.

## Governance and Policies (High Level)

- Thanin's administration pursued a hardline stance against leftist movements and perceived communist influence.
- Accounts of the period emphasize restrictions on political activity, censorship, and expanded state security measures.
- The government faced legitimacy challenges and opposition from multiple factions, including elements within the military.

## Removal and Aftermath

- In 1977, Thanin was removed by another coup, illustrating the unstable balance between civilian governments and military power in that era.
- The brief tenure is frequently discussed in Thai political history as part of a cycle of repression, backlash, and repeated interventions.
