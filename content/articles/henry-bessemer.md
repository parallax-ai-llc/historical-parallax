## Summary

Henry Bessemer developed the Bessemer process for mass-producing steel.

## Additional Context

- The **Bessemer process** was a major step in industrial steelmaking, enabling much larger volumes of steel at lower cost compared to earlier methods.
- Cheaper, more abundant steel supported 19th-century infrastructure and manufacturing expansion, including railways, bridges, and machinery.
- As with many industrial breakthroughs, Bessemer’s work sits in a broader ecosystem of metallurgical innovation (with later processes improving quality control and alloy consistency).

## Why It Matters

- **Industrial scale:** helped shift steel from a relatively costly material to a foundational input for mass industry.
- **Second-order effects:** widespread steel availability shaped urbanization, transportation networks, and military-industrial capacity.
- **Innovation pattern:** a single process improvement can unlock entirely new economic frontiers when it reduces the cost of a general-purpose material.
