## What "Modern Slavery" Means

In contemporary policy and human-rights reporting, "modern slavery" is an umbrella term that can include forced labor, debt bondage, human trafficking for labor exploitation, and other situations where people cannot refuse or leave due to threats, coercion, or deception.

## How It Appears in Supply Chains

Risk often concentrates in labor-intensive, subcontracted, or informal segments of production where visibility is low and accountability is diffused across layers of intermediaries.

Common patterns include:

- Recruitment fees and debt that lock workers into abusive conditions
- Confiscation of identity documents
- Withholding of wages and threats of violence or deportation
- Excessive overtime and restrictions on movement

## Sectors Frequently Cited

Investigations and NGO reports repeatedly highlight exposure risks in fishing and seafood processing, agriculture, mining and mineral extraction, construction, garment manufacturing, and domestic work connected to commercial services.

## Regulation and Corporate Due Diligence

Responses have included transparency requirements, import controls, and "due diligence" expectations aimed at mapping suppliers, auditing labor conditions, and remediating harms when forced labor is identified.

## Why Enforcement Is Hard

Even when a brand commits to ethical sourcing, complex subcontracting, cross-border jurisdiction, and retaliation risks for whistleblowers can make detection and remedy difficult.
