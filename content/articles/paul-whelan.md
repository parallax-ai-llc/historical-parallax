## Summary

American detained in Russia on espionage charges widely regarded as politically motivated.

His case became one of the most prominent U.S. wrongful-detention disputes with Russia, spanning his 2018 arrest, a 2020 conviction and long sentence, and eventual release in a major 2024 prisoner swap.

## Background

- Former U.S. Marine and later worked in corporate security.
- Holds multiple citizenships in addition to the United States (commonly reported as Canadian, British, and Irish).

## Detention in Russia

- **Arrest (Dec 2018):** Detained in Moscow and accused by Russian authorities of espionage.
- **Prosecution narrative:** Russian officials alleged he was caught receiving classified information.
- **Whelan’s position:** He and U.S. officials said he was visiting for a wedding and that the case was politically motivated.

## Trial and Sentence

- **Conviction (2020):** A Russian court found him guilty of espionage.
- **Sentence:** Reported as a **16-year** prison term in a high-security penal colony.
- **International reaction:** The U.S. government repeatedly described him as wrongfully detained.

## Diplomatic Context

- His detention became a recurring issue in U.S.–Russia relations and was frequently discussed in the context of prisoner-exchange negotiations.
- U.S. officials and his family periodically criticized the lack of consular access and the pace of diplomatic efforts.

## Later Developments

- **Release (2024):** Whelan was released as part of a large multi-country prisoner swap involving the United States and Russia.
- After his release, he continued to be cited in public discussions about wrongful detention cases and prisoner-exchange policy.
