## Summary

Greek anti-fascist rapper stabbed to death by a Golden Dawn supporter, triggering the party's prosecution.

## Background

- **Stage name**: Killah P.
- **Origin**: From the Piraeus area (Athens–Piraeus metropolitan region), Greece.
- **Public profile**: Known for explicitly anti-fascist, anti-racist themes and for speaking publicly against the far-right party Golden Dawn.

## Assassination (2013)

- On **17 September 2013**, Fyssas was attacked and **stabbed** in **Keratsini** (Piraeus, Athens metropolitan area).
- The attacker, **Giorgos Roupakias**, was identified in court proceedings as a supporter/member connected to **Golden Dawn**.
- Fyssas’s death rapidly became a national flashpoint, with widespread demonstrations and international attention.

## Investigation and Prosecution Impact

- The killing acted as a catalyst for a major Greek state response toward Golden Dawn, including investigations that treated the party as an organized criminal enterprise.
- In **2020**, a Greek court ruled that **Golden Dawn operated as a criminal organization**, convicting multiple senior figures; Roupakias was convicted for Fyssas’s murder and sentenced to **life imprisonment**.

## Legacy

- Fyssas is widely remembered in Greece as a symbol of resistance to political violence and neo-Nazism.
- Commemorations, memorial gatherings, and public art referencing Killah P have persisted as part of the broader anti-fascist movement.
