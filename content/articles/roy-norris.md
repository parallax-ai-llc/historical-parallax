## Summary

American serial killer who partnered with Lawrence Bittaker as the 'Toolbox Killers.' He testified against Bittaker in exchange for a life sentence. His detailed testimony about their crimes was considered among the most disturbing ever given.

## Background and Partnership

Roy Norris became known primarily through his association with Lawrence Bittaker in a series of murders in California in 1979. In most reporting, the pair are identified together under the label "Toolbox Killers," a name derived from evidence discussed in the case.

## Arrest, Prosecution, and Testimony

After the crimes, Norris was arrested and later cooperated with prosecutors, providing testimony about the offenses and his co-defendant. This cooperation is commonly described as a factor in his receiving a life sentence rather than the death penalty.

## Incarceration and Death

Norris remained incarcerated for decades and died in prison in 2020. The case is frequently referenced in discussions of violent crime, criminal investigations, and how plea agreements and cooperation can shape outcomes in major prosecutions.
