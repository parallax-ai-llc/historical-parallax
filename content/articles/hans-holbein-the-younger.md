## Summary

Hans Holbein the Younger was a German-Swiss painter who became the greatest portrait painter of the Northern Renaissance. As court painter to Henry VIII, he created definitive images of the Tudor court. His painting The Ambassadors is renowned for its virtuosic detail and famous anamorphic skull.
