## Summary

Son-in-law of Kazakh president, accused of kidnapping, torture, and murder of political opponents.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2007 | Charged with kidnapping and murder of bankers |
| 2007–2008 | Removed from power and later tried in Kazakhstan in absentia over the Nurbank case |
| 2011 | Published a memoir/accusatory book ("The Godfather-in-Law") about Kazakhstan's political elite |
| 2014 | Held in Austria during extradition and related legal proceedings |
| 2015 | Found dead in an Austrian prison while awaiting trial |

## Background

- Aliyev was a prominent Kazakh figure who held senior roles across business, law enforcement, and diplomacy.
- He was married to Dariga Nazarbayeva, the eldest daughter of President Nursultan Nazarbayev, which made him a politically sensitive figure inside Kazakhstan.

## Notable Allegations and Legal Cases

- **Nurbank case (2007):** He was accused by Kazakh authorities of involvement in the abduction, torture, and killing of two bank executives connected to Nurbank.
- **Kazakhstan proceedings:** After leaving the country, he faced criminal charges and was later convicted in absentia in Kazakhstan on multiple counts related to the 2007 allegations.
- **Austria / international dimension:** Aliyev spent years abroad (notably in Austria) while facing legal actions, extradition requests, and investigations tied to the Kazakh cases.

## Death

- Aliyev died on **2015-02-24** in detention in Austria.
- He was found hanged in his cell; Austrian investigators publicly treated the death as a suicide, though the circumstances were politically controversial.

## Key Points

- Often described in reporting as a symbol of post-Soviet elite corruption and factional infighting.
- His rise and fall is frequently discussed in the context of Kazakhstan's consolidation of power in the 2000s.
