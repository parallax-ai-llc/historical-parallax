## Summary

Mary Beatrice Davidson Kenner was the most prolific African American female inventor in US history, holding five patents. Her most significant invention was the sanitary belt with an adjustable moisture-proof pocket (1957), which improved menstrual hygiene. Due to racial discrimination, companies initially refused her inventions until decades later.
