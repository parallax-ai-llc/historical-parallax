## Background

Ramesh "Sunny" Balwani is best known as the former president and chief operating officer of Theranos, the Silicon Valley blood-testing startup founded by Elizabeth Holmes. He was also romantically involved with Holmes, a relationship that became a recurring point of public and courtroom interest because of its overlap with company leadership and decision-making.

## Role at Theranos

As COO, Balwani was portrayed by prosecutors as a central executive in day-to-day operations, including oversight of laboratory processes, internal reporting, and interactions with partners and investors. Theranos claimed it could run a broad menu of lab tests from very small blood samples; subsequent investigations and litigation asserted that the company's technology did not perform as advertised and that many tests relied on conventional analyzers.

## Fraud Case and Conviction

Balwani was tried in US federal court on charges related to wire fraud and conspiracy. The government alleged he helped mislead investors and business partners about the capabilities and performance of Theranos' technology and operations. In 2022, a jury convicted him on multiple counts.

## Sentencing

In 2023, Balwani was sentenced to a prison term of nearly 13 years. Court proceedings also addressed financial penalties and restitution in connection with losses attributed to the fraud.

## Significance

The Theranos prosecutions are frequently cited as a landmark case in startup accountability, highlighting the risks of hype-driven claims in regulated health and diagnostics settings and the legal exposure executives face when representations to investors and partners are found to be materially false.
