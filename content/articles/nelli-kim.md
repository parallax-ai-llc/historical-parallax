## Summary

Nelli Kim won five Olympic medals including three gold at 1976 Montreal, scoring two perfect 10s alongside Nadia Comăneci.

## Competitive Era

Nelli Kim competed during a period when women’s artistic gymnastics was rapidly evolving in difficulty and international popularity. The 1976 Montreal Olympics are especially remembered for the emergence of perfect-10 scoring, with Kim and Nadia Comăneci becoming central figures in that narrative.

## Major Achievements

High-level achievements commonly associated with Kim include:

- multiple Olympic medals across the 1976 and 1980 Games
- world-level titles and strong all-around competitiveness
- recognition for both execution and difficulty on key apparatus

## Coaching and Governance Roles

After retiring from competition, Kim became involved in gymnastics administration and judging. Former elite athletes often transition into governance roles because of their sport-specific expertise and international networks; in Kim’s case, her post-competitive work is frequently mentioned alongside her competitive record.

## Legacy

Kim’s legacy is frequently summarized through:

- her contribution to the public visibility of women’s gymnastics in the 1970s
- the iconic association with perfect-10 performances
- her long-term influence in the sport beyond competition
