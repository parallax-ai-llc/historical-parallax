## Context

Yoni Palmier is commonly associated with the "serial murders in Essonne" case, a series of killings in the Essonne department (Île-de-France) spanning late 2011 through early 2012. Reporting on the case often emphasizes that the incidents occurred within a relatively small geographic area, which contributed to a large and highly publicized investigation.

> Note on basic biographical data: the frontmatter above is preserved as-is for repository consistency, but widely cited sources (including Wikipedia) list Palmier as **born 24 December 1978**.

## Case overview (high-level)

- **Pattern and investigation:** Authorities linked multiple crime scenes to a single perpetrator based on consistent forensic indicators (notably the weapon type cited in public reporting).
- **Public response:** The concentration of incidents in time and place led to intensified policing efforts and substantial media attention.
- **Arrest and charging:** Palmier was arrested in **April 2012** and subsequently indicted/charged in connection with the series.

## Proceedings and sentence (summary)

According to widely cited summaries, Palmier was tried in a French cour d'assises and received a sentence of **life imprisonment**, with a reported minimum term ("lock-in period") of **22 years** before eligibility for certain forms of release could be considered. An appeal trial later maintained the same sentence.
