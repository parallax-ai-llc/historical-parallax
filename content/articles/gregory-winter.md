## Summary

Gregory Winter is a British biochemist who shared the 2018 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for the phage display of peptides and antibodies. He used phage display to evolve new antibodies for therapeutic use, leading to the development of adalimumab (Humira), one of the best-selling drugs in history. His work revolutionized the development of antibody-based medicines.
