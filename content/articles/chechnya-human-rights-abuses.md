## Summary

Systematic human rights violations by Russian and Chechen security forces during and after the Chechen wars including torture, disappearances, and extrajudicial killings. Kadyrovs regime continues abuses.

## Context

The term refers broadly to documented allegations and findings of serious abuses committed during the conflicts in Chechnya (including the First and Second Chechen Wars) and in the subsequent period of governance and security operations in the republic. Multiple actors have been accused, including Russian federal forces, pro-Moscow Chechen security units, and armed groups.

## Commonly reported patterns

Reports by human-rights organizations and journalists have described patterns such as:

- enforced disappearances and secret detention
- torture and cruel or degrading treatment in custody
- extrajudicial killings and collective punishment
- intimidation of lawyers, activists, and independent media

During the wars, “filtration” or screening facilities were widely discussed in reporting as sites of abuse; terminology and the precise structure of such facilities varies across sources.

## Postwar governance and accountability

After large-scale fighting decreased, critics argued that abuses continued under local security structures with limited domestic accountability. International bodies and NGOs have periodically called for investigations, protection for witnesses, and access for monitors.

## 2017 persecution of LGBTQ people

In 2017, international media and human-rights groups reported a campaign of detention and abuse targeting LGBTQ people in Chechnya. Russian federal authorities faced pressure to investigate; Chechen officials denied the allegations. The episode became a focal point for broader discussion of impunity and political control in the region.

## Notes on sources

Because the topic spans active conflicts and politically contested reporting environments, specific incident claims can be difficult to independently verify. High-level summaries typically rely on NGO documentation, court records where available, and investigations by international organizations.
