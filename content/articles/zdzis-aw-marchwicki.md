## Summary

## Background

Marchwicki became widely known in Poland under the nickname **“Wampir z Zagłębia”** (“the Vampire of Zagłębie”), referencing the Zagłębie Dąbrowskie / Dąbrowa Basin region in southern Poland.

Public accounts typically describe:

- a series of attacks on women over multiple years
- intense public fear and extensive policing efforts
- a high-profile prosecution that later became controversial

## Crimes (Commonly Reported Overview)

Secondary summaries frequently attribute to this case:

- **Victims:** 14 women killed
- **Additional attacks:** several non-fatal assaults
- **Time period:** mid-1960s through 1970

Descriptions of the offender’s method often emphasize sudden assault with blunt force and a pattern of post-assault staging or sexualized violence. Specific forensic and case-by-case details should be verified against reliable primary reporting.

## Investigation and Trial (High-Level)

Accounts of the investigation commonly note that the case received extraordinary attention, including large-scale mobilization of law enforcement resources and heightened political pressure to identify and punish the perpetrator.

Marchwicki was ultimately convicted and sentenced to death. Over time, the conviction has been the subject of dispute in some commentary, with claims that the evidentiary basis and investigative methods should be re-examined.
