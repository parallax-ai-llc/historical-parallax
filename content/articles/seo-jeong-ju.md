## Summary

Seo Jeong-ju (Midang) was one of the most important Korean poets of the 20th century. His early sensuous verse drew on shamanistic and Buddhist imagery, while his later work embraced Korean folk traditions. His poetry collection Beside a Chrysanthemum is considered a masterpiece of Korean literature. His reputation was complicated by collaboration during the Japanese occupation.
