## Summary

Tsuguharu Foujita was a Japanese-French painter who became one of the most successful artists in Paris during the 1920s. His distinctive milky white backgrounds, fine ink lines, and cats became his trademark. He bridged Japanese artistic traditions with the School of Paris.
