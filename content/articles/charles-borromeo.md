## Summary

Charles Borromeo was an Italian cardinal and Archbishop of Milan who was one of the leading figures of the Counter-Reformation. He implemented the reforms of the Council of Trent in his diocese and became the model of the ideal post-Tridentine bishop. He established seminaries, catechetical schools, and charitable institutions. During the plague of 1576, he personally ministered to the sick. He is the patron saint of seminarians.
