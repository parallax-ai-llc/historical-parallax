## Summary

Margaret Court won a record 24 Grand Slam singles titles and completed the Grand Slam in 1970.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1960 | Won first Australian Open at 17 |
| 1970 | Won all four Grand Slams in one year |
| 1973 | Retired with 24 Grand Slam titles |

## Records and achievements

- Court is widely cited as holding the **all-time record for Grand Slam singles titles** (24) across women's and men's tennis in the Open Era statistical conversation, with most of her majors won before the Open Era.
- She also accumulated significant **doubles and mixed-doubles** success, making her one of the most decorated players in major-tournament history.
- The 1970 season is notable for the **calendar-year Grand Slam** in singles (winning all four majors in one year).

## Era and competition (context)

Court's peak years bridged the transition into the **Open Era** (professional players allowed to compete in majors, starting in 1968). Comparisons across eras often note differences in training, travel, surfaces, and depth of field, which affect how records are interpreted.

## Post-playing career

After retiring from top-level competition, Court became involved in ministry and is known publicly as a Christian pastor in Australia.

## Public debate and legacy

Beyond tennis, Court has been a subject of public debate due to her outspoken social and political views, including statements about LGBTQ+ issues. These controversies have periodically resurfaced in discussions about honors and naming within Australian tennis venues.
