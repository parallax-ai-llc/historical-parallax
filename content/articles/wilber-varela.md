## Summary

Colombian drug lord known as 'Jabón' (Soap) who led the Norte del Valle Cartel after internal wars killed several rival leaders. He was found dead in Venezuela.

## Cartel role

Wilber Alirio Varela Fajardo, known as “Jabón,” was identified as a leader within the Norte del Valle Cartel, a major cocaine trafficking organization that operated after the fragmentation of the Cali Cartel.

The cartel was associated with large-scale cocaine exports from Colombia to the United States, often routed through intermediary networks in other countries.

## Indictments and reward

U.S. authorities indicted Varela on drug trafficking charges in the early 2000s, and the U.S. State Department publicized a reward offer for information leading to his arrest and/or conviction.

These legal actions reflected the broader U.S. strategy of targeting transnational cartel leadership through extradition and international law-enforcement cooperation.

## Internal conflict

Accounts of the Norte del Valle period emphasize violent internal rivalries and a cycle of retaliatory killings among factions.

Varela’s rise is often described alongside conflicts with other cartel figures, including a prolonged feud with Diego León Montoya Sánchez (“Don Diego”).

## Death

Varela was found dead in Venezuela in early 2008, after years of evading capture.

His death was widely interpreted as another episode in the cartel’s internal violence and the instability created by leadership decapitation and factional competition.
