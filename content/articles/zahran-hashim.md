## Summary

Sri Lankan Islamist who led the 2019 Easter Sunday bombings that killed 269 people at churches and hotels. He was the leader of National Thowheeth Jama'ath. He detonated himself at the Shangri-La Hotel in Colombo.

## Easter Sunday Bombings (High-Level)

The 2019 Sri Lanka Easter attacks targeted churches during services and several hotels, producing mass casualties and global media attention. The incident triggered a large counterterrorism operation and extensive political fallout.

Publicly reported themes from subsequent investigations and inquiries include:

- The presence of advance warnings and disputes over how they were handled.
- Coordination challenges across intelligence and police institutions.
- The rapid identification of a network rather than a lone actor.

## Network and Ideology

Zahran Hashim has been described as associated with National Thowheeth Jama'ath (NTJ). International reporting also discussed links or pledges of allegiance to transnational jihadist organizations; specific operational links should be treated carefully and verified against official findings.
