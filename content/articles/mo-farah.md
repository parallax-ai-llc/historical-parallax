## Summary

Mo Farah won the 5,000m/10,000m double at both 2012 and 2016 Olympics. In 2022, he revealed childhood trafficking to the UK.

## Early life and identity

- Farah was born in **Somalia** and later moved to the United Kingdom.
- In 2022, he publicly described being trafficked to the UK as a child, which changed public understanding of his early life story.

## Athletic profile

- Specialized in long-distance track events, particularly the **5,000 m** and **10,000 m**.
- Known for tactical racing and a strong finishing kick, especially in championship finals.

## Major achievements

- Completed the Olympic **"distance double"** (5,000 m and 10,000 m) at **London 2012** and repeated it at **Rio 2016**.
- Won multiple World and European titles, becoming one of Britain’s most decorated modern track athletes.

## Later career and public role

- Transitioned to road racing and the marathon later in his career.
- Received prominent UK honors and became a major public figure in British sport.
- His later public statements contributed to broader conversations about migration, identity, and exploitation.

## Historical significance

- Farah’s Olympic doubles place him among a small set of athletes who dominated both 5,000 m and 10,000 m in the same Games.
- His career is often discussed in the context of modern endurance training systems and elite competition strategy.
