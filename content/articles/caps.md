## Summary

Rasmus Borregaard Winther, known professionally as **Caps**, is a Danish professional League of Legends player widely regarded as one of the greatest mid laners in the history of European competitive League of Legends. Born on November 17, 1999, he rose to international prominence as the star mid laner of Fnatic and later G2 Esports, helping define the golden era of European League of Legends from 2018 onward.

Caps is celebrated for his aggressive, mechanically gifted playstyle and his willingness to take risks that few other professional players would attempt. His back-to-back World Championship finals appearances in 2018 and 2019 — with two different teams — cemented his status as one of the most talented players the Western scene has ever produced. He has won multiple LEC (League of Legends European Championship) titles and remains a central figure in European esports.

## Early Life & Background

Rasmus Winther was born on November 17, 1999, in Denmark. He developed an interest in competitive gaming at a young age, climbing the European solo queue ladder in League of Legends during his early teenage years. His exceptional mechanical skill and aggressive laning drew attention from professional scouts while he was still underage. Before joining the top level of European competition, Caps competed in minor leagues and the Turkish league, playing for Dark Passage in 2016, where he began to build his reputation as a rising talent.

## Rise to Prominence

Caps joined **Fnatic** ahead of the 2017 LEC Spring Split, replacing Fabian "Febiven" Diepstraten. Though initially inconsistent — earning the community nickname "Craps" for his volatile performances alongside his "Claps" moniker when playing at his peak — he quickly matured into one of Europe's most dominant mid laners. In 2018, Caps led Fnatic to an LEC title and a historic run to the **World Championship Grand Final**, where they fell to Invictus Gaming 0–3.

In a shocking offseason move, Caps left Fnatic to join rival organization **G2 Esports** in November 2018, forming a superteam alongside Luka "Perkz" Perković, Marcin "Jankos" Jankowski, Mihael "Mikyx" Mehle, and Martin "Wunder" Hansen. The 2019 G2 roster became one of the most dominant teams in Western League of Legends history.

## Key Achievements

- **2x World Championship Finalist** (2018 with Fnatic, 2019 with G2 Esports)
- **Mid-Season Invitational Champion** (2019 with G2 Esports, defeating Team Liquid 3–0)
- **Multiple LEC Champion** — winning numerous splits with both Fnatic and G2 Esports
- **2019 Grand Slam Attempt** — G2 won both LEC splits and MSI in 2019, entering Worlds as favorites before falling to FunPlus Phoenix 0–3 in the final
- Only Western player to reach back-to-back World Championship finals with two different rosters

## Controversies & Criticism

Caps' departure from Fnatic to join G2 Esports at the end of 2018 was one of the most polarizing roster moves in European League of Legends history. Many Fnatic fans viewed the decision as a betrayal, particularly since G2 had been Fnatic's primary rival. His inconsistency in high-pressure international matches, particularly the two 0–3 World Finals losses, has drawn criticism from analysts who question whether he elevated his play sufficiently at the most critical moments. Additionally, his brief role-swap to bot lane during the 2020 season, allowing Perkz to return to mid lane temporarily, was seen as an unusual experiment with mixed results.

## Legacy & Influence

Caps remains the defining mid laner of European League of Legends. His mechanical ceiling, willingness to innovate champion picks, and flair for highlight-reel outplays inspired a generation of European mid lane talent. His career trajectory — from volatile rookie to two-time Worlds finalist — encapsulates the ambition and potential of the European competitive scene. Whether or not he ultimately lifts the Summoner's Cup, Caps has already secured his place as one of the most impactful Western players in League of Legends history.
