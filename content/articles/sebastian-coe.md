## Summary

Sebastian Coe won 1,500m gold at both 1980 and 1984 Olympics. He set eight outdoor world records and later became president of World Athletics.

## Athletic Profile

- Coe is best known for elite middle-distance running, particularly the 800 m and 1,500 m events.
- His record-setting period in the late 1970s and early 1980s is often used as a benchmark era for British middle-distance dominance.

## Notable Achievements (High-Level)

- Two-time Olympic champion in the 1,500 m (1980, 1984).
- Multiple Olympic medals across middle-distance events.
- Credited in major summaries with eight outdoor world records during his competitive career.

## Later Roles

- After retirement from competition, Coe transitioned into public-facing roles that combined sport, leadership, and governance.
- He is widely recognized for senior administrative leadership within international athletics, including serving as president of World Athletics.

## Legacy and Public Perception

- In athletics history, Coe is frequently discussed alongside other leading runners of his era, with emphasis on tactical racing and record pace capabilities.
- His administrative tenure is commonly associated with governance and integrity debates within track and field, reflecting the broader pressures on the sport in the 2010s.
