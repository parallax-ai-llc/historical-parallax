## Summary

Former Rwandan army chief of staff who fled to South Africa after falling out with President Kagame. He survived an assassination attempt in Johannesburg widely attributed to Rwandan agents.

## Military and Government Career

Nyamwasa rose through the ranks of the Rwandan Patriotic Front (RPF) and later served in senior roles in the post-1994 Rwandan state, including leadership positions within the Rwanda Defence Force. As a high-ranking official, he was associated with the consolidation of Rwanda’s security institutions during a period of intense regional conflict and reconstruction.

## Break with the Rwandan Leadership

By the late 2000s, he fell out with President Paul Kagame’s government and left Rwanda. His departure and subsequent opposition activities were portrayed very differently by his supporters and by Rwandan authorities, reflecting broader polarization around Rwanda’s governance and security policies.

## Exile in South Africa

After relocating to South Africa, Nyamwasa became a prominent figure in disputes involving alleged extraterritorial targeting of Rwandan dissidents. The 2010 shooting incident in Johannesburg received international attention and contributed to diplomatic friction, with commentators linking it to broader patterns of cross-border political violence in the region.

## Court Cases and Attribution

South African prosecutions related to the attempted killing resulted in convictions (as noted in the timeline). Public reporting frequently discussed alleged links to Rwandan state actors, while official positions and evidentiary claims varied across sources and legal contexts.
