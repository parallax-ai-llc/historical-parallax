## Sport Context (Race Walking)

- Race walking is an endurance discipline governed by technique rules requiring continuous ground contact and a straightened supporting leg through part of the stride.
- At elite levels, performance depends on aerobic capacity, pace control, efficient biomechanics, and avoiding penalties for form violations.

## Career Profile (High Level)

- Liu Hong is associated with the modern era of Chinese success in race walking, particularly in the women’s 20 km event.
- Her career is often described through a combination of global medals, fast times, and sustained competitiveness across championship cycles.

## Major Results and Recognition

- Her Olympic gold in 2016 is typically cited as her signature achievement on the largest stage.
- Holding a world record (at least for a period) positioned her as one of the leading athletes in her event internationally.

## Training and Competition Themes

- Elite race walkers frequently manage fine margins between maximum sustainable speed and technique compliance; strategic pacing is central in major championships.
- Championship races can differ from record attempts, often emphasizing tactical moves, positioning, and response to surges.

## Legacy

- Liu Hong is frequently referenced in summaries of top women’s race walkers of her era.
- Her success contributed to the visibility of race walking and reinforced China’s standing in the discipline.

## Key Takeaways

- Race walking’s technical constraints make consistency and rule compliance as important as raw endurance.
- Liu Hong’s career is remembered for a peak that included world-leading times and an Olympic title.
