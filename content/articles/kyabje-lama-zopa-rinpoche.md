## Summary

Lama Zopa Rinpoche was a Nepali Buddhist teacher who was the spiritual director of the Foundation for the Preservation of the Mahayana Tradition (FPMT), a worldwide network of over 160 Buddhist centers, projects, and services. Along with Lama Thubten Yeshe, he was one of the first Tibetan lamas to teach Westerners in Nepal. He was known for his compassion, his extremely long teachings, and his emphasis on the bodhisattva ideal.
