## Summary

Yves Klein was a French artist known for his monochrome paintings in his patented International Klein Blue and his radical performance works. His Anthropometries, using nude models as living paintbrushes, and Leap into the Void photograph are iconic works of postwar art. He died at 34 but profoundly influenced conceptual and performance art.
