## Summary

Kudo-kai yakuza leader sentenced to death for ordering attacks on civilians.

## Background and Context

- **Kudo-kai** is a Japanese yakuza organization that has been associated in media and law-enforcement reporting with violent incidents in and around Kitakyushu, Fukuoka Prefecture.
- In the 2000s–2010s, Japan expanded **anti-organized-crime measures** (often discussed under the umbrella of *bōryokudan* countermeasures), including ordinances that limited business dealings with designated groups.
- High-profile prosecutions in this period frequently focused on **command responsibility**—whether senior figures could be held accountable for attacks carried out by subordinates.

## Criminal Case (High-Level)

- Inamatsu was identified by prosecutors as a senior leader within Kudo-kai.
- The case that drew international attention centered on whether he **ordered or directed a series of attacks** targeting civilians.
- A Japanese court ultimately imposed a **death sentence** in 2021, reflecting the court’s finding that leadership involvement had been proven to a very high standard.
- As with other major criminal cases in Japan, the process included multiple stages of investigation and trial, with outcomes that can be subject to appeal and post-sentencing proceedings.

## Why the Case Matters

- It is often cited as a notable example of Japan’s willingness to pursue **top-level accountability** in organized-crime violence.
- The case also illustrates the broader societal aim of **reducing intimidation and violence** linked to organized crime, particularly against ordinary residents and businesses.
