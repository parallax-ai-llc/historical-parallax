## Summary

Mass killings carried out by Yahya Jammeh's Junglers death squad at his hometown of Kanilai. Victims included political opponents, journalists, and suspected coup plotters.

## Background

- Yahya Jammeh ruled The Gambia for more than two decades after taking power in a 1994 coup, presiding over a security apparatus accused of widespread abuses.
- The "Junglers" are widely described in reporting and testimony as a paramilitary unit linked to the presidency and used for intimidation and lethal operations.

## Kanilai and the Alleged Killings (High Level)

- Kanilai, Jammeh's hometown, is frequently mentioned in accounts of regime-era abuses as a location connected to security operations and clandestine detentions.
- Allegations around the Kanilai killings describe extrajudicial violence targeting perceived opponents or suspected threats to the regime.

## Truth-Seeking and Accountability

- The Gambia's Truth, Reconciliation and Reparations Commission (TRRC) gathered testimony from victims, witnesses, and former security personnel about Jammeh-era crimes.
- Truth commissions generally aim to establish an authoritative public record, identify patterns of abuse, and recommend reforms and reparations.
- Follow-on accountability can include prosecutions, sanctions, and institutional changes, though outcomes vary by political will and legal capacity.
