## Summary

Mastermind of the Great Train Robbery of 1963 who planned the heist in meticulous detail. Served 10 years in prison and later wrote his memoirs.

## Additional Context

- **Notability:** Reynolds became internationally known as the leading organizer of the 1963 Great Train Robbery in England.
- **Role:** He was described as the planner and coordinator rather than a front-line participant.

## The Great Train Robbery (High-Level)

- **Target:** A Royal Mail train carrying cash.
- **Approach:** The gang interfered with railway signals to stop the train, then removed money from the mail car.
- **Scale:** The robbery was one of the most high-profile crimes in modern British history.

## Flight and Capture

- **Years on the run:** After the robbery, Reynolds evaded arrest for several years.
- **Arrest and imprisonment:** He was ultimately captured and served a substantial prison sentence.

## Later Life and Legacy

- **Public interest:** The case remained a staple of British crime history, generating books and television coverage.
- **Memoir and media:** Reynolds later wrote about his life, contributing to the enduring fascination with the heist.
