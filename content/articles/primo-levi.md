## Summary

Primo Levi was an Italian Jewish chemist and writer who survived Auschwitz and wrote some of the most important literature about the Holocaust. His book 'The Periodic Table' uses chemical elements as a framework for autobiography. He is considered one of the most important Italian writers of the 20th century.
