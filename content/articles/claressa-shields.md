## Summary

Claressa Shields won Olympic boxing gold at 2012 London and 2016 Rio, the first American boxer to win consecutive Olympic golds.

## Additional Context

### Early life and amateur background

Shields is from **Flint, Michigan**, and rose through U.S. amateur boxing to become one of the most decorated American women boxers. Her Olympic achievements are often paired with her medal record at world championships and Pan American competition.

### Professional boxing achievements (high-level)

In the professional ranks, Shields is widely discussed for rapidly collecting major world titles and for becoming an **undisputed champion** (holding the major belts simultaneously) in her weight class. Coverage of her career often emphasizes her willingness to move across divisions and pursue "all the belts" rather than staying in one weight class.

### Nicknames and public profile

She has been billed with nicknames such as **"T-Rex"** and **"GWOAT"** ("Greatest Woman of All Time"), reflecting her marketing persona and competitive claims.

### Crossover to MMA

Shields has also competed in **mixed martial arts (MMA)**, a relatively uncommon transition for elite boxers. Her MMA appearances are typically framed as an experiment in becoming a multi-sport combat athlete while continuing to box.

### Significance

Shields is frequently cited in discussions of the growth of women's boxing in the U.S., including the visibility gained from Olympic success and the subsequent push for professional pay-per-view opportunities and main-event status.
