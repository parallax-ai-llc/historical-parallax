## Summary

Beltrán-Leyva Cartel leader who was killed along with eight associates in a marine operation in Tepic, Nayarit. The operation was later found to have involved corrupt military officers.

## Context

Patrón Sánchez is typically described in open-source reporting as a regional figure within Mexico’s fragmented organized-crime landscape, where alliances and splinters can shift rapidly after arrests, internal disputes, or leadership changes. In this environment, “cartel leader” often denotes operational control over a corridor or state-level network rather than a single national hierarchy.

## Role and Notability (High-Level)

In accounts that associate him with the Beltrán-Leyva organization, Patrón Sánchez is presented as a key node for activities in and around Nayarit. Discussions of his role frequently highlight:

- the importance of coastal and transit routes for trafficking and logistics
- the use of local networks to support concealment, movement, and enforcement
- the overlap between criminal power and vulnerabilities in local governance

## The 2017 Operation and Aftermath

The death of Patrón Sánchez is commonly framed as part of Mexico’s broader security strategy of targeting high-value criminal leaders. Subsequent reporting about corruption in relation to the operation is often used as an example of a recurring pattern in counter-cartel efforts:

- operational successes can coexist with compromised decision-making
- cases can expose bribery, collusion, or leakage of sensitive information
- accountability and transparency are essential for legitimacy

## Why It Matters

The episode is cited in debates about the effectiveness and risks of militarized approaches to organized crime, particularly when corruption undermines institutional trust.
