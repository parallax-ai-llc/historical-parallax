## Summary

Former President of Namibia whose government was implicated in the Fishrot corruption scandal involving Icelandic fishing company Samherji. Massive bribes paid for fishing quota access.

## Political background

Pohamba is a long-serving Namibian politician associated with SWAPO (South West Africa People's Organization), the dominant party in Namibia since independence.

## Presidency (2005–2015)

As president, he oversaw a period often characterized by continuity in policy and leadership within SWAPO, with attention to public administration, social programs, and regional diplomacy.

## Fishrot scandal (context)

The "Fishrot" scandal refers to allegations that officials and intermediaries received improper payments connected to access to Namibian fishing quotas, including dealings involving the Icelandic company Samherji.
