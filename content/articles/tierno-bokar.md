## Summary

Tierno Bokar was a Malian Sufi teacher and mystic of the Tijaniyya order who was called the 'Sage of Bandiagara.' He emphasized love, tolerance, and the inner dimension of Islam. His teachings were recorded by his student Amadou Hampâté Bâ and became influential in promoting interfaith understanding. He lived in poverty and was marginalized by French colonial authorities but is now recognized as one of West Africa's greatest spiritual teachers.
