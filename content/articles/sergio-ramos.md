## Summary

Sergio Ramos García is one of the most decorated defenders in the history of football. Born on March 30, 1986, in Camas, Seville, Spain, he built his career primarily at Real Madrid, where he spent 16 years and became club captain. Known for his aggressive defending, aerial prowess, and extraordinary goal-scoring ability from set pieces, Ramos redefined the modern centre-back role.

His impact extends beyond individual brilliance. He was a cornerstone of the dominant Spanish national team that won two UEFA European Championships (2008, 2012) and the FIFA World Cup (2010), achieving an unprecedented international treble. At club level, he won four UEFA Champions League titles with Real Madrid, cementing his status as one of the greatest defenders ever to play the game.

## Early Life & Background

Sergio Ramos was born in Camas, a small town near Seville, into a working-class family with deep footballing roots. His older brother René also pursued a professional career in football. Ramos joined Sevilla FC's youth academy at a young age, developing rapidly through the ranks and making his La Liga debut for Sevilla on March 1, 2004, aged just 17, becoming one of the youngest defenders to appear in the top flight of Spanish football.

## Rise to Prominence

Ramos's performances at Sevilla attracted immediate attention from Spain's biggest clubs. In the summer of 2005, Real Madrid signed him for €27 million — a then-record fee for a Spanish defender — when he was just 19 years old. He became a regular starter almost immediately under various managers, and by 2011 he was appointed club captain, a role he held until his departure in 2021.

His rise on the international stage mirrored his club form. He earned his first senior Spain cap in March 2005 and became an indispensable part of the squad that dominated world football between 2008 and 2012 under managers Luis Aragonés and Vicente del Bosque.

## Key Achievements

- **4 UEFA Champions League titles** with Real Madrid (2014, 2016, 2017, 2018)
- **5 La Liga titles** with Real Madrid
- **2 UEFA European Championship medals** with Spain (2008, 2012)
- **1 FIFA World Cup medal** with Spain (2010)
- **180 caps** for Spain — a national record at the time of his retirement from international football in 2021
- Over **100 career goals**, an extraordinary tally for a defender, many coming from penalties and headers in crucial moments
- Scored in the 93rd minute of the 2014 Champions League final to force extra time against Atlético Madrid

## Controversies & Criticism

Ramos accumulated more red cards than any other player in La Liga and Champions League history, a reflection of his win-at-all-costs mentality that sometimes crossed disciplinary lines. He was widely criticized for an apparent deliberate foul on Liverpool's Mohamed Salah in the 2018 Champions League final that forced Salah off with a shoulder injury. His physicality and occasional cynical play drew consistent criticism throughout his career, even as they were admired by supporters.

## Legacy & Influence

Ramos leaves behind a legacy as the template for the modern attacking centre-back — a defender equally comfortable initiating play, leading a backline, and arriving in the opposition box at decisive moments. His leadership, particularly during Real Madrid's four-title Champions League era between 2014 and 2018, was considered instrumental. After leaving Real Madrid in 2021, he had spells at Paris Saint-Germain and Sevilla before moving to Monterrey in 2024. His record cap tally and trophy haul ensure his place among the all-time greats of the sport.
