## Summary

Beijing imposed a national security law on Hong Kong, effectively ending its autonomy. Over 10,000 arrested during pro-democracy protests; media shut down and opposition jailed.

## Background

Large-scale protests in 2019 were initially triggered by a proposed extradition bill and broadened into a wider pro-democracy movement. The events occurred within the "one country, two systems" framework established at the 1997 handover.

## National Security Law (2020)

In 2020, China enacted a National Security Law for Hong Kong covering offenses such as secession, subversion, terrorism, and collusion with foreign forces. Authorities argued it was needed to restore order; critics argued it curtailed civil liberties and political pluralism.

## Policing and prosecutions

The crackdown has been associated with mass arrests during protests, changes in policing tactics, and a legal environment in which protest-related charges and national-security prosecutions became more common.

## Effects on civil society and media

Many civil-society organizations disbanded or reduced activities, and several outlets faced closures or legal pressure according to widely reported accounts. The long-term effect has been a contraction of the space for dissent and independent organizing.
