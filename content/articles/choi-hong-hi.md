## Summary

Choi Hong-hi is considered the founder of taekwondo. He established the International Taekwondo Federation in 1966 and worked to spread the martial art worldwide.

## Early Life and Military Career (High Level)

- Choi served as an officer in the South Korean military in the post–World War II period.
- His role in institutionalizing training helped connect martial-arts practice with military and national programs.

## Taekwondo Development

- The development of taekwondo involved multiple schools (kwan), instructors, and organizations; Choi is widely associated with naming and systematizing the art.
- He promoted standardized forms (tul/patterns) and instructional materials to support international teaching.

## International Organizations

- In 1966 he founded the **International Taekwondo Federation (ITF)**, which became one of the major global bodies associated with taekwondo.
- Over time, taekwondo governance and competition also developed through other organizations, contributing to different rulesets and traditions.

## Legacy

- Choi’s influence is frequently discussed in the broader history of modern Korean martial arts.
- His life and organizational choices are also part of the debate about how taekwondo’s identity evolved across politics, sport, and cultural diplomacy.
- He is often cited in comparisons between ITF-style and Olympic-sport taekwondo, which diverged in rules and emphasis.
- Instructional texts attributed to him helped codify terminology and curriculum for many practitioners.
