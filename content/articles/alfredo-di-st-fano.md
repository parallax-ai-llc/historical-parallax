## Summary

Alfredo Di Stéfano led Real Madrid to five consecutive European Cups (1956-1960). He scored in all five finals.

## Additional Context

### Playing style and reputation

Di Stéfano was known for a complete, all-action style: he contributed to buildup play, pressed defensively, and finished attacks, often dropping deep and then arriving in the box. In accounts of the era, he is frequently described as the central engine of Real Madrid’s dominance in European competition during the late 1950s.

### Club and international career

Before becoming a Real Madrid icon, he played in South America and Spain during a period when international transfers were less standardized than today. He is also notable for representing more than one national team in different contexts, reflecting eligibility and administrative realities of mid‑20th‑century football.

### Major honors (high level)

- European Cup titles with Real Madrid in the 1950s.
- Multiple domestic league titles.
- Individual recognition as one of the defining players of his generation.

### Later life

After retirement he remained involved in football, including work in management/coaching roles, and stayed strongly identified with Real Madrid’s institutional history.
