## Summary

Argentine admiral who oversaw the ESMA detention center where 5,000 people disappeared during the Dirty War.

## Role in Argentina's military dictatorship

- Massera was a senior naval officer and one of the leading figures of Argentina's 1976 military coup and subsequent junta.
- During the period known as the "Dirty War", state forces carried out widespread illegal detention, torture, and forced disappearances.
- ESMA (the Navy Mechanics School) became one of the most notorious clandestine detention and torture centers, and Massera is commonly associated with its command structure.

## Trials and accountability

- After the return of democratic rule, legal proceedings sought to establish responsibility for crimes committed during the dictatorship.
- Massera was convicted in major human-rights trials; later legal and political developments affected sentences and custody conditions.
- Subsequent court actions in Argentina revisited dictatorship-era cases as the country expanded prosecutions for crimes against humanity.

## Historical significance

- Massera is frequently cited as a symbol of institutional responsibility for state terror.
- His legacy is central to Argentina's ongoing debates about justice, memory, and accountability for dictatorship-era abuses.
