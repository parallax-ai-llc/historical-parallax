## Summary

Lotte Group founder Shin Kyuk-ho and family members were convicted of embezzlement and breach of trust. The patriarch created slush funds worth billions through circular shareholding.

## Context and Allegations

This article summarizes widely reported legal cases involving senior figures at Lotte and the Shin family. In many sources, the issues are described as a mix of corporate governance concerns and alleged financial misconduct.

Commonly discussed themes include:

- **Embezzlement and breach of trust** allegations tied to group governance and transactions.
- **Bribery-related** allegations in the context of broader South Korean political and corporate investigations in the mid-to-late 2010s.
- Questions around **control and succession** inside the conglomerate structure.

## Notable Court Outcomes (High-Level)

- **Shin Kyuk-ho** (founder) was convicted of embezzlement; in **December 2017** he was **sentenced to four years in prison** for embezzling **128.6 billion won**, while being allowed to remain free due to poor health (per Wikipedia). 
- **Shin Dong-bin** (chairman) received a **two-year suspended jail sentence** on **22 December 2017** relating to embezzlement and breach of trust (per Wikipedia).
- On **13 February 2018**, Shin Dong-bin was **sentenced to 30 months in prison** by the Seoul Central District Court in a separate ruling referenced by Wikipedia.

> Note: Specific charges, defendants, and sentence details can vary by case and reporting. This page keeps the coverage intentionally high-level.

## Why It Mattered

- The cases became a reference point in discussions of **chaebol governance** and accountability.
- They highlighted how **family-controlled conglomerates** can face heightened scrutiny during leadership transitions.
- The outcomes affected leadership availability and reputational risk for one of South Korea’s largest business groups.
