## Summary

Hajime Isayama is a Japanese manga artist best known as the creator of *Attack on Titan* (*Shingeki no Kyojin*), one of the best-selling manga series in history. Born on August 29, 1986, in Ōyama, Ōita Prefecture, Japan, Isayama conceived the series as a dark, high-stakes story about humanity's struggle for survival against monstrous giants called Titans. Serialized in Kodansha's *Bessatsu Shōnen Magazine* from September 2009 to April 2021, the series ran for 139 chapters across 34 collected volumes.

The manga has sold over 140 million copies worldwide as of 2023, making it one of the highest-selling manga franchises ever produced. Its anime adaptation, produced by Wit Studio and later MAPPA, became a global phenomenon, drawing tens of millions of viewers and establishing Isayama as one of the most influential manga creators of his generation.

---

## Early Life & Background

Isayama was born in the rural town of Ōyama (now part of Hita City) in Ōita Prefecture, on the island of Kyushu. Growing up in a small, isolated community, he described feeling a sense of claustrophobia and confinement — an experience that directly shaped the walled-city setting of *Attack on Titan*. He developed an interest in manga from a young age and enrolled at the Osaka Designers' College after high school to study design and illustration. During this period, he worked part-time at an internet café, an experience he later cited as influential in shaping his understanding of social dynamics and human vulnerability.

## Rise to Prominence

Isayama submitted an early prototype of *Attack on Titan* to Shōnen Jump in 2006 but was rejected. He subsequently brought the concept to Kodansha, where editors recognized its potential. The series officially launched in *Bessatsu Shōnen Magazine* in September 2009, when Isayama was just 23 years old. Within its first few years, the manga attracted massive readership both domestically and internationally. The anime adaptation's first season in 2013, produced by Wit Studio, catapulted the franchise into global mainstream recognition. By 2013, the manga had sold over 30 million copies — an extraordinary milestone for a series only four years old.

---

## Key Achievements

- *Attack on Titan* sold over 140 million copies worldwide as of 2023, ranking among the top-selling manga series of all time alongside *One Piece* and *Naruto*.
- The series was published in over 30 countries and languages.
- Isayama won the Kodansha Manga Award in the shōnen category in 2011.
- The anime adaptation earned widespread critical acclaim, with multiple seasons produced between 2013 and 2023.
- In 2013, *Time* magazine listed *Attack on Titan* among its best comic books and graphic novels of the year.

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Isayama faced significant controversy in 2013 when critics noted that a character design in *Attack on Titan* bore a resemblance to a real-life figure associated with far-right ideology. Isayama apologized and stated the resemblance was unintentional. Additionally, the manga's ending in 2021 was divisive among fans, with many criticizing the resolution of major character arcs and the moral framing of the story's conclusion, sparking extensive online debate. Some critics also raised concerns about the manga's treatment of fictional ethnic and national groups and potential allegorical implications.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Isayama reshaped expectations for what mainstream shōnen manga could achieve thematically, introducing morally ambiguous characters, large-scale political intrigue, and bleak narrative consequences into a format often associated with more straightforward heroism. His work influenced a wave of darker, more complex manga that followed throughout the 2010s. The *Attack on Titan* franchise generated billions of dollars in merchandise, licensing, and media revenue, and the anime finale in 2023 drew record streaming numbers globally. Isayama announced plans to pursue new creative projects following the series' conclusion, with the manga community watching closely for his next work.
