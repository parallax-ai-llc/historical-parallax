## Additional Background

The El Paso shooting refers to an attack at a Walmart in El Paso, Texas on August 3, 2019. Open-source summaries describe the incident as one of the deadliest mass shootings in modern U.S. history, with victims including both U.S. and Mexican nationals.[^1]

## Attack Overview (High-Level)

Key high-level elements consistently noted in public summaries include:

- **Location and timing**: A retail store during daytime shopping hours.
- **Targeting**: Authorities described the attack as directed at Hispanics/Latinos, framing it as racially motivated.[^1]
- **Casualties**: 23 people were killed, with additional people injured.[^1]

## Investigation and Legal Proceedings

Wikipedia summaries describe the case as involving both state and federal processes. Publicly reported outcomes include:

- **Federal case**: A sentence of 90 consecutive life terms in U.S. federal court (reported in 2023).[^1]
- **State proceedings**: Separate state-level prosecution activity has been covered in open sources, reflecting the dual-jurisdiction nature of major violent-crime cases in the U.S.[^1]

## Community Impact and Cross-Border Dimensions

Because El Paso sits on the U.S.–Mexico border (adjacent to Ciudad Juárez), the incident has often been described as having a cross-border social and political impact. Open sources note that victims included people with ties to both countries, and that the attack drew responses from institutions in the U.S. and Mexico.[^1]
