## Summary

Russian poet whose passionate, linguistically innovative verse is among the greatest in the Russian language. Exile, poverty, and the deaths of family members marked her tragic life.

## Additional Context

### Themes and style

Tsvetaeva's poetry is often described as emotionally intense and formally inventive. Critics frequently point to:

- Compressed, associative imagery and abrupt turns of thought.
- Strong rhythmic drive and unconventional syntax.
- A voice that moves between lyric intimacy and historical witness.

### Exile and return

Her biography is tightly intertwined with twentieth-century Russian history: she left Soviet Russia in the early 1920s, lived in European exile, and returned to the USSR in 1939. The period around her return was marked by political repression that affected her family.

### Place in literature

In surveys of Russian modernism, she is commonly placed alongside other major poets of the era. Her reputation grew substantially after her death, as more of her work became widely published and studied.
