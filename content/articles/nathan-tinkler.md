## Additional Context

### Background

- Nathan Tinkler (born 1 February 1976) is an Australian mining industry executive.
- He trained as an electrician at Muswellbrook TAFE and entered the coal industry as an apprentice electrical fitter at Bayswater Coal (Hunter Valley, NSW).
- He later founded a mine machinery maintenance business called **Custom Mining** (established when he was 26).

### Mining and investment milestones (high-level)

- **Middlemount coal mine**: paid a deposit in 2006 to buy the Middlemount coal mine in Central Queensland; later sold the stake to **Macarthur Coal** in 2007 for a reported A$275 million (largely in shares), and then sold Macarthur shares in 2008 for a reported A$422 million in cash.
- **Aston Resources / Maules Creek**: purchased the Maules Creek coal deposit in NSW from **Rio Tinto** in 2008; floated Aston Resources in 2010 (reported valuation ~A$1.2 billion).
- **Whitehaven Coal transaction (2012)**: merged Aston Resources and Boardwalk Resources with Whitehaven Coal in 2012; his Whitehaven shareholding was subsequently sold to financial backers in 2013.

### Other business interests

- **Patinack Farm (thoroughbred racing/breeding)**: established in 2008 and later sold (reported in 2014). Reports describe it as a large-scale racing and breeding enterprise with significant Group 1 success.
- **Sports ownership/administration**:
  - Involvement with the **Newcastle Jets** (A-League) via Hunter Sports Group; the club entered voluntary administration in 2015 and the league licence was terminated.
  - Assumed ownership of the **Newcastle Knights** (NRL club) in 2011.

### Legal, regulatory, and financial issues (public record)

- Appeared before the NSW **Independent Commission Against Corruption (ICAC)** in 2014 concerning allegations around political donations; he denied wrongdoing and reporting notes no corruption finding against him.
- Was declared **bankrupt** by the Federal Court in 2016; reporting notes the bankruptcy was later **annulled** in 2018.
