## Summary

Allied Democratic Forces, originally a Ugandan rebel group, has been massacring civilians in eastern Congo. Responsible for the deadliest attacks in the Beni territory killing hundreds.

## Background

The ADF insurgency refers to violence associated with the Allied Democratic Forces (ADF), a group that originated as a Ugandan rebel movement in the 1990s and later entrenched itself in eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC). Over time, the conflict became closely associated with persistent insecurity in North Kivu and Ituri provinces.

## Geography and Actors

- **Primary area of operations:** The insurgency is often discussed in relation to the Beni area and surrounding territories in eastern DRC, where repeated attacks on civilians have been reported.
- **State forces and partners:** Congolese security forces have conducted repeated operations against the ADF; Uganda has also been involved at various points, including cross-border security cooperation.
- **Civilians:** Civilian communities and displaced populations are central to the humanitarian impact, as attacks, abductions, and insecurity disrupt daily life and local economies.

## Patterns of Violence

High-level reporting frequently emphasizes:

- **Mass casualty attacks** on villages and towns, including killings of civilians.
- **Abductions and coercion,** including forced recruitment.
- **Retaliatory dynamics,** where military operations are followed by further attacks on nearby communities.

## International Framing

In some public claims and reporting, the ADF’s attacks have been linked to the Islamic State (ISIS), including instances where ISIS claimed responsibility. Analysts and official statements have debated the nature and extent of organizational ties, but the “ISIS-ADF” framing has become part of how the conflict is discussed internationally.

## Humanitarian Impact

The insurgency has contributed to:

- **Large-scale displacement** within eastern DRC.
- **Severe protection concerns,** including insecurity affecting travel, farming, and market activity.
- **Constraints on aid delivery** due to ongoing violence and access limitations.
