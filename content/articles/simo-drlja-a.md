## Summary

Bosnian Serb police chief in Prijedor who organized the ethnic cleansing of Bosniaks and Croats. He oversaw the Omarska, Keraterm, and Trnopolje camps. Killed while resisting arrest by NATO SFOR troops.

## Role in Prijedor (1992)

- Drljača served as a police leader in the Prijedor area during the Bosnian War.
- International reporting and later tribunal records describe coordinated campaigns of detention, abuse, and forced removal targeting non-Serb communities.
- The Omarska, Keraterm, and Trnopolje camps became emblematic of mass detention and atrocities in northwestern Bosnia.

## Indictment and attempted arrest

- Drljača was among individuals sought in connection with war crimes investigations.
- In 1997, NATO-led Stabilisation Force (SFOR) attempted to detain him; he was killed during the operation after reportedly resisting.

## Historical significance

- The Prijedor atrocities are central to the documentation of ethnic cleansing during the conflict.
- Drljača is frequently referenced in accounts of how police structures were used to implement persecution and detention policies at the local level.

## Related judicial record

- Even where an individual suspect was not tried to verdict, the camps and events in the Prijedor region were examined across multiple cases.
- Tribunal findings and witness testimony from those proceedings are often used to reconstruct chains of command and patterns of detention and abuse.
- The Prijedor camps have been documented in journalism, survivor accounts, and subsequent historical syntheses of the war.
