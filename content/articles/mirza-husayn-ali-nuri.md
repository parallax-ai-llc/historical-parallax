## Summary

Baha'u'llah (Glory of God), born Mirza Husayn-Ali Nuri, was the founder of the Baha'i Faith. He declared himself the Manifestation of God foretold by the Bab and spent 40 years in prison and exile. He wrote the Kitab-i-Aqdas (Most Holy Book), the Kitab-i-Iqan (Book of Certitude), and thousands of other works. He taught the essential unity of all religions, the oneness of humanity, and the need for a world commonwealth.
