## Summary

Karl Ziegler was a German chemist who shared the 1963 Nobel Prize in Chemistry with Giulio Natta for their discoveries in the field of polymer chemistry and technology. He developed Ziegler catalysts for polymerization of ethylene at low pressures, enabling mass production of polyethylene. His work revolutionized the plastics industry.
