## Summary

Seven Taliban militants attacked Army Public School in Peshawar, killing 149 people including 132 children aged 8-18. Pakistan's deadliest terrorist attack.

## Career Timeline

## Background

- The target was Army Public School (APS) in Peshawar, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, a school associated with Pakistan’s military community.
- The attack took place amid a broader insurgency and militant violence involving the Pakistani Taliban (Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan, TTP).

## Attack Overview (High-Level)

- Assailants: the attack was attributed to TTP militants.
- Method: an armed assault on a school campus during a school day, resulting in a prolonged and widely reported siege and rescue operation.
- Victims: 149 people were killed, including 132 children (school students).

## Immediate Response

- Pakistani security forces conducted an operation to end the attack and regain control of the campus.
- The incident triggered nationwide mourning, emergency medical response, and heightened security at schools and public institutions.

## Aftermath and Policy Impact

- The attack became a major inflection point in Pakistan’s counterterrorism policy debate.
- Reported outcomes included:
  - stronger enforcement actions against militant groups,
  - renewed political consensus around counter-extremism measures,
  - intensified security planning for educational institutions.

## Public Memory

- The APS attack is widely commemorated in Pakistan as a national tragedy and is frequently referenced in discussions of terrorism, child victims of conflict, and security policy.
