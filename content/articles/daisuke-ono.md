## Summary

Daisuke Ono (小野大輔) is one of Japan's most prominent voice actors, born on May 4, 1978, in Kochi Prefecture. Represented by Music Ray'n, he has built a reputation over two decades for his rich, versatile baritone voice and his ability to portray complex, intense characters across a wide range of anime genres. His performances consistently rank among the most celebrated in the industry.

Ono rose to national recognition through a series of landmark roles in the 2000s and 2010s, establishing himself as a leading figure in the seiyuu (voice acting) world. Beyond anime, he has extensive credits in video games, drama CDs, and live events, and has pursued a parallel career as a musical artist. His influence extends to a generation of voice actors who cite him as a formative inspiration.

## Early Life & Background

Daisuke Ono was born on May 4, 1978, in Kochi City, Kochi Prefecture, Japan. He developed an interest in performance arts during his school years, eventually pursuing formal training in voice acting after completing his general education. He studied at the Osaka Prefectural Tondabayashi High School before moving toward professional training, joining the talent agency that would help launch his career. His deep, expressive voice became his defining asset from early in his training.

## Rise to Prominence

Ono made his professional debut in the early 2000s, accumulating supporting roles before landing breakthrough parts. His career pivoted significantly in 2008 when he voiced Sebastian Michaelis in *Black Butler* (*Kuroshitsuji*), a composed and enigmatic butler whose dry wit and menace required precise tonal control. The role became iconic and introduced Ono to a massive international fanbase.

That same year, he voiced Shizuo Heiwajima in *Durarara!!* (2010 broadcast), a physically imposing yet emotionally volatile character that showcased a different dimension of his range. These dual high-profile roles in quick succession cemented his A-list status in the industry.

## Key Achievements

Ono's most celebrated roles include:

- **Sebastian Michaelis** – *Black Butler* (2008–ongoing), his signature role
- **Shizuo Heiwajima** – *Durarara!!* (2010)
- **Jotaro Kujo** – *JoJo's Bizarre Adventure* (2014–2019), one of the franchise's most iconic protagonists
- **Erwin Smith** – *Attack on Titan* (2013–2022), the commanding Survey Corps leader

He won the **Best Voice Actor award at the 6th Seiyu Awards in 2012**, one of the industry's most prestigious recognitions. He has also released music singles and albums, performing at solo live concerts and fan events throughout Japan.

## Controversies & Criticism

In 2019, Ono faced significant public backlash following allegations related to his personal conduct, which were reported in Japanese tabloid media. The controversy led to a temporary suspension of his public activities and appearances. His agency issued a formal statement, and Ono subsequently returned to work after a period away. The episode drew scrutiny regarding accountability standards within the Japanese entertainment industry.

## Legacy & Influence

Daisuke Ono's body of work spans more than two decades and includes some of the most culturally significant anime titles of the 2000s and 2010s. His portrayal of Jotaro Kujo in *JoJo's Bizarre Adventure* in particular is considered definitive, shaping how a generation of fans understands the character. Younger seiyuu frequently cite his technical precision and emotional depth as benchmarks for the craft. His career demonstrates the expanding global reach of Japanese voice acting as an art form.
