## Summary

Kenenisa Bekele holds the 10,000m world record and won both 5,000m and 10,000m at the 2008 Olympics. He won 11 World Cross Country titles.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2008 | Won 5,000m and 10,000m at Beijing |

## Additional Context

### Early Life and Rise

- Bekele is from Ethiopia, a country with a strong tradition in distance running, and emerged internationally in the early 2000s.
- He became known for combining track speed with cross-country strength, enabling success across multiple championship formats.

### Major Achievements

- He won Olympic gold medals and multiple world titles, and set world records on the track.
- His dominance in World Cross Country included both short and long race formats during the era when the championships held multiple distances.

### Transition to Road Racing

- Later in his career he moved toward road events, including the marathon.
- His marathon performances placed him among the top contenders of his era, drawing attention for how his track credentials translated to the longer distance.

### Running Style and Reputation

- He has been noted for tactical awareness, strong finishing speed, and the ability to surge late in races.
- Injuries and intermittent absences were recurring themes, but his peak performances remained highly regarded.

### Legacy

- Bekele is often cited as one of the greatest long-distance runners, especially for his rare combination of track, cross-country, and road success.
- His records and championship results are frequently used as benchmarks in discussions of modern distance-running greatness.
