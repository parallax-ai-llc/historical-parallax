## Summary

Hermann von Helmholtz was a German physician and physicist who made significant contributions to thermodynamics, optics, electrodynamics, and the philosophy of science. He formulated the conservation of energy principle and invented the ophthalmoscope for examining the eye. His work on the perception of sound and vision bridged physics and physiology. Helmholtz was one of the greatest scientists of the 19th century.
