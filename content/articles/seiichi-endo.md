## Summary

Aum Shinrikyo's biological weapons chief executed for producing anthrax and sarin gas.

## Background

Seiichi Endo is commonly described in reporting as one of the technically trained members of Aum Shinrikyo who supported the group’s weapons efforts. Accounts emphasize that Aum sought both **chemical agents** (notably sarin) and **biological agents**, and that the group attempted to recruit people with relevant scientific backgrounds.

## Role in Aum Shinrikyo

High-level summaries of Endo’s responsibilities typically highlight:

- **Chemical production support:** involvement in preparing sarin used in the 1995 attack.
- **Biological weapons efforts:** association with Aum’s attempts to develop biological agents, often referenced in connection with anthrax-related experimentation efforts attributed to the group.
- **Operational enablement:** a behind-the-scenes technical role rather than a public-facing leadership position.

## Legal Proceedings and Execution

Endo was convicted in Japan for participation in mass-casualty terrorism and related crimes linked to Aum Shinrikyo’s attacks. Japan’s legal process for capital cases can involve long appeal timelines; for Aum cases, executions occurred decades after the 1995 attacks.

In **July 2018**, Japanese authorities carried out a series of executions of Aum members, including Endo, by hanging.
