## Summary

Georgian crime boss known as Shakro Molodoy (Young Shakro) who became one of the most powerful Vory v Zakone in Russia. He controlled vast criminal enterprises across Europe and the former Soviet Union.

## Background

- Kalashov is associated with the “vory v zakone” (thieves-in-law) tradition, a term used for elite status within parts of post‑Soviet criminal subcultures.
- Reporting on his influence typically emphasizes networks spanning multiple countries, including money laundering and protection rackets.

## International Dimension
