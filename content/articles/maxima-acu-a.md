## Correction / Context

This stub contains an explicit note that the original entry was "completely incorrect." The core correction is that **Máxima Acuña (also written Máxima Acuña de Chaupe) is widely described as a Peruvian campesina and environmental defender**, not an "environmental criminal." She became internationally known for resisting efforts to remove her from land near the **Conga gold mining project** in Cajamarca, Peru.

To preserve the historical record of this repository while improving factual context, the inaccurate label remains in the frontmatter above; the statements in this section reflect the commonly cited biographical description in reputable sources.

## Background (high-level)

- **Origin/region:** Cajamarca region in the northern Andes of Peru.
- **Public profile:** Known for a long-running land dispute and advocacy related to mining impacts on water and land.
- **Human rights framing:** Coverage often places her case in the context of environmental justice, rural land rights, and the safety of community defenders.

## Why she is notable

- Her story is frequently cited in discussions about **resource extraction** and **community resistance** in Latin America.
- She is often referenced as an example of the risks faced by people who oppose large-scale projects in rural areas.
- The Goldman Prize recognition helped amplify her case globally.
