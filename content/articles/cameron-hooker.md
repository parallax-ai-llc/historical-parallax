## Summary

American kidnapper known as the 'Girl in the Box' case who kidnapped Colleen Stan in 1977 and held her captive for 7 years, keeping her in a coffin-like box under his bed. His wife eventually helped Stan escape. Serving 104 years.

## Additional Context

- **Case setting:** The kidnapping of Colleen Stan began after she accepted a ride while traveling in Northern California (1977). The crimes became widely known in media coverage as the "Girl in the Box" case.
- **Control and coercion:** Reporting on the case emphasizes long-term coercive control, including threats, isolation, and forced compliance, rather than a single confined incident.
- **Investigation and prosecution:** The case was ultimately resolved through law-enforcement involvement and testimony that described the pattern of captivity and abuse over multiple years.
- **Sentencing:** Hooker received a sentence intended to keep him imprisoned for life (commonly summarized as 104 years), reflecting multiple counts tied to kidnapping and related offenses.
