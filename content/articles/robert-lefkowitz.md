## Summary

Robert Lefkowitz is an American physician and biochemist who shared the 2012 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for studies of G-protein-coupled receptors (GPCRs). He discovered and characterized the beta-adrenergic receptor and other GPCRs, which are the targets of about half of all modern medications. His work with Brian Kobilka revolutionized understanding of how cells respond to chemical signals.
