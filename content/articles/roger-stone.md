## Background

- Roger Jason Stone Jr. is an American political consultant and lobbyist known for decades of Republican campaign work.
- He began political activism at a young age and later worked in and around national Republican politics in Washington, D.C.

## Political Consulting and Lobbying

- Stone is commonly associated with the rise of modern U.S. political consulting, including aggressive messaging and opposition research.
- He co-founded or worked at firms involved in campaign consulting and lobbying, including work alongside other Republican operatives.
- Over his career, he advised or associated with multiple Republican candidates and political committees.

## Relationship with Donald Trump

- Stone had a long-running association with Donald Trump that predates the 2016 campaign, including informal political advising and media promotion.
- During the 2016 U.S. presidential election cycle, Stone publicly positioned himself as connected to the campaign orbit and as an outside advocate.

## Legal Issues and Conviction

- In 2019, Stone was indicted in federal court; charges included obstruction of a congressional proceeding, witness tampering, and making false statements.
- A jury found him guilty in 2019 on multiple counts related to the House Intelligence Committee investigation.
- The case centered on communications and testimony connected to inquiries into Russian interference and contacts involving WikiLeaks.

## Later Developments

- In 2020, Stone received executive clemency related to his sentence, followed later by a full pardon.
- After the case, he remained a prominent media figure and continued to appear in U.S. political commentary.
