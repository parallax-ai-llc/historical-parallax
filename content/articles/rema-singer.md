## Summary

Divine Ikubor, known professionally as Rema, is a Nigerian singer and songwriter born on May 1, 2000, in Benin City, Edo State. He emerged as one of the defining voices of Afrobeats' global expansion in the early 2020s, blending Afropop, trap, and emo influences into a distinctive sound that defied easy categorization. His crossover appeal — rooted equally in West African rhythms and global pop sensibilities — positioned him as a central figure in the generation of artists carrying Afrobeats beyond its regional origins.

Rema rose to mainstream attention in 2019 after being signed to Jonzing World, a subsidiary of Mavin Records, and quickly became a critical and commercial force. His 2022 single "Calm Down" became a watershed moment for Nigerian pop on the global stage, eventually reaching number one in over 30 countries after a remix with Selena Gomez in 2023. By his mid-twenties, Rema had established himself not only as a hit-maker but as a genre-shaping artist with a genuinely international following.

## Early Life & Background

Rema was born Divine Ikubor on May 1, 2000, in Benin City, Nigeria. He grew up in a musically rich environment; his grandfather was a highlife musician, and his late brother was also involved in music. Rema credits his upbringing in Benin City — a city with deep cultural and artistic traditions — as foundational to his artistic sensibility. He attended Emotan College in Benin City before relocating to Lagos to pursue music professionally. He lost both his father and his brother at a young age, experiences that shaped the emotional depth visible in his songwriting.

## Rise to Prominence

Rema's career accelerated dramatically in 2019. After posting freestyle videos on social media, he caught the attention of Don Jazzy, the founder of Mavin Records, and was signed to Jonzing World. In February 2019, he released a self-titled EP featuring the track "Iron Man," which was included in Barack Obama's 2019 summer playlist — a moment that brought immediate international attention. He won the Next Rated award at the 2019 Headies Awards, confirming his status as Nigeria's most anticipated new talent.

His debut studio album *Rave & Roses* was released in March 2022 and debuted at number two on the Billboard World Albums chart. The album contained "Calm Down," which grew steadily through streaming before exploding globally. The Selena Gomez remix, released in August 2022 and promoted heavily through 2023, reached the top 40 in the United States and peaked inside the top 10 in the United Kingdom, making Rema one of the few African artists to achieve such sustained Western chart penetration.

## Key Achievements

- "Calm Down" (remix with Selena Gomez) peaked at number 3 on the UK Singles Chart and number 18 on the US Billboard Hot 100 in 2023.
- The song spent over 90 weeks on the Billboard Global 200, one of the longest runs by an Afrobeats track.
- *Rave & Roses* debuted at number two on the Billboard World Albums chart upon release in March 2022.
- Won the Headies Next Rated Award in 2019.
- Featured on Barack Obama's 2019 Summer Playlist with "Iron Man," weeks after his debut.
- His second studio album *HEIS*, released in 2024, debuted to critical acclaim and further cemented his experimental approach to Afropop.

## Controversies & Criticism

Rema has been relatively free of major personal controversies. However, he has publicly addressed mental health pressures related to early fame, discussing burnout and the industry's demand for constant output. Some critics noted that the commercial dominance of "Calm Down" overshadowed the broader range of *Rave & Roses*, reducing public perception of his artistic scope. He has also navigated industry commentary about the role of Western collaborations in Afrobeats artists' crossover success, a broader debate about authenticity versus commercial strategy that touched his Selena Gomez remix.

## Legacy & Influence

Rema represents a generation of Nigerian artists for whom Afrobeats is not a niche export but a dominant global genre. His willingness to incorporate emo, trap, and global pop textures into his work has expanded the sonic vocabulary associated with Nigerian pop. "Calm Down" demonstrated that an Afrobeats track could sustain long-term global chart presence — not just peak and fade — signaling a structural shift in how the genre performs internationally. At 25, he remains one of the youngest artists to have achieved this scale of cross-continental commercial success, and his trajectory suggests a career with significant runway ahead.
