## Summary

Indonesian cleric and spiritual leader of Jemaah Islamiyah who was linked to multiple terrorist attacks including the 2002 Bali bombings. He was convicted of funding a terrorist training camp. Released from prison in 2021.

## Background

Bashir was an influential Islamist cleric in Indonesia who became associated with networks advocating for a stricter interpretation of Islamic law and, according to multiple legal proceedings, with militant organizations in Southeast Asia.

## Jemaah Islamiyah and Militant Networks

He was widely described as a spiritual figure linked to Jemaah Islamiyah (JI), a group blamed for major attacks in Indonesia, including the 2002 Bali bombings. The degree and nature of his operational involvement has been debated in public discourse, but Indonesian courts convicted him in several cases related to conspiracy and support for militancy.

## Trials, Sentences, and Release

Across the 2000s and 2010s, Bashir faced repeated arrests and trials. A later conviction centered on alleged support for a militant training camp. He was eventually released in 2021 after serving a substantial portion of his sentence.

## Broader Significance

His public profile illustrates how religious authority, political activism, and militant ecosystems have intersected in post-Suharto Indonesia, and how counterterrorism policy has evolved through prosecutions, surveillance, and organizational bans.
