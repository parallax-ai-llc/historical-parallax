## Summary

Social engineering attack on July 15, 2020 that hijacked Twitter accounts of Barack Obama, Joe Biden, Elon Musk, and other celebrities to promote a bitcoin scam. Three teenagers were charged.

## Additional Context

The 2020 Twitter Bitcoin scam was a high-profile social engineering incident in which attackers gained access to internal tools and posted fraudulent cryptocurrency messages from prominent accounts.

### How the scam worked (at a high level)

- Attackers leveraged social engineering to compromise accounts with elevated access.
- They posted messages promising to “double” money sent to a Bitcoin address, a classic advance-fee fraud pattern adapted to cryptocurrency.

### Why it mattered

- The incident highlighted risks in privileged-access management and internal account administration systems.
- It also reinforced the importance of multi-factor authentication, employee security training, and strict auditing of administrative actions.
