## Summary

Ethiopian regional president of Somali Region who oversaw widespread human rights abuses including extrajudicial killings, torture, and ethnic cleansing of Oromos. His Liyu police committed atrocities. Arrested in 2018.

## Additional Context

### Somali Region politics and security forces

The Somali Region is Ethiopia’s eastern regional state, bordering Somalia and Djibouti, and has long been shaped by conflicts involving insurgency, counterinsurgency, and competition over resources and political control. Regional security structures have been central to governance, with armed police forces often operating alongside (or in tension with) federal institutions.

### The Liyu Police

The Liyu Police ("special police") became widely associated with allegations of severe abuses. In reporting on the region during Abdi Illey’s tenure, human rights organizations and media accounts described patterns of:

- Arbitrary detention and torture
- Extrajudicial killings
- Collective punishment and retaliatory violence
- Abuses linked to ethnic targeting and displacement

### Removal from power and arrest

Abdi Illey lost his position during a period of political change and reform in Ethiopia. He was later arrested amid investigations and public pressure for accountability related to violence and abuses in the Somali Region.

### Why this matters

The case is frequently referenced in debates over decentralization and federalism in Ethiopia, including how regional leaders can accumulate coercive power, and what mechanisms exist (or fail) to constrain security forces and protect civilians.
