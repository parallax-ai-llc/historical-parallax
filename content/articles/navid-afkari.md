## Summary

Iranian wrestler executed despite international protests for allegedly stabbing a security guard during protests.

## Background

- Afkari was known primarily as a competitive wrestler from Iran.
- His case became widely cited by international human-rights organizations and sports bodies as an example of concerns about due process in Iran.

## Arrest and Charges

- Afkari was arrested after nationwide protests in Iran in 2018.
- Iranian authorities accused him of involvement in the death of a security guard in Shiraz.
- Afkari and supporters disputed the allegations; reporting on the case frequently highlighted claims that confessions were coerced.

## Trial and Human-Rights Concerns

- International observers and advocacy groups raised concerns about fair-trial standards, including access to counsel and the reliability of evidence.
- The case drew particular attention because Afkari was an athlete and because capital punishment was imposed amid public and international scrutiny.

## International Response

- The case prompted calls for clemency from human-rights organizations and public figures.
- The World Players Association and other sports-linked groups publicly appealed to Iranian authorities to halt the execution.
- International media coverage framed the case within broader debates about the treatment of protesters and detainees in Iran.

## Execution and Aftermath

- Afkari was executed in September 2020.
- The execution drew renewed condemnation and intensified advocacy campaigns focused on due process and the use of the death penalty.

## Notes

- Articles about Afkari often mention his brothers in connection with the same protest-related period and legal proceedings.
- Because much reporting is based on official statements and contested accounts, summaries typically distinguish between the government’s allegations and the defense’s claims.
