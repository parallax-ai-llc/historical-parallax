## Summary

Bede Griffiths was a British Benedictine monk who spent the latter half of his life in India, establishing a Christian ashram that integrated Hindu and Christian spirituality. He adopted Indian dress and customs, living as a Christian sannyasi. His books, including The Golden String and A New Vision of Reality, explored the convergence of Eastern and Western mysticism. He is considered one of the most important figures in Hindu-Christian dialogue.
