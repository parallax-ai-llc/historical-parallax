## Summary

Belarusian journalist arrested after Lukashenko forced his Ryanair flight to land in Minsk.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2021 | Arrested after forced landing of Ryanair flight |

## Background

- Protasevich (born 1995 in Minsk) became involved in Belarusian opposition activism in the early 2010s.
- He studied journalism at Belarusian State University and was later reported to have been expelled.
- He was associated with the youth opposition movement **Young Front**.

## Work and public profile

- Protasevich worked as a journalist, photographer, and blogger, including reporting for Belarusian media outlets.
- He was reported to have worked with Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty’s Belarus service (mid-to-late 2010s).
- In exile (notably in Poland), he became publicly associated with opposition information efforts on Telegram.

## NEXTA and 2020 Belarus protests (high-level)

- Protasevich was reported to have served as editor-in-chief of the Telegram channel **NEXTA** and as an editor for other opposition-oriented channels.
- During the 2020 Belarus presidential election period and subsequent protests, NEXTA became a major source of updates and protest coordination content for many Belarusians.

## 2021 Ryanair Flight 4978 incident

- On **23 May 2021**, the Ryanair flight he was on (Athens → Vilnius) was diverted to Minsk after Belarusian authorities cited a bomb threat.
- Protasevich and his partner at the time, **Sofia Sapega**, were detained after landing.
- The diversion triggered significant international condemnation and contributed to additional sanctions and aviation restrictions targeting Belarus.

## Detention, prosecution, and later developments

- After his detention, Protasevich appeared in Belarusian state media in highly publicized statements; supporters and international observers raised concerns about coercion.
- In **May 2023**, he was reported to have been sentenced to **eight years** in prison.
- Shortly afterward (May 2023), Belarusian authorities announced he had been **pardoned**.

## Why this matters

- The flight diversion and arrest became a prominent example cited in discussions about state coercion, civil aviation security, and the risks faced by journalists/activists in authoritarian contexts.
