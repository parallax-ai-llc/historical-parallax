## Summary

Indian serial killer from Gujarat who murdered at least 17 women in Ahmedabad. He targeted sex workers and was known as the 'Psycho Serial Killer' of Ahmedabad.

## Background

Public summaries describe Patel as an Indian offender associated with a series of killings in Ahmedabad, Gujarat. Reporting on the case has often highlighted the vulnerability of marginalized victims and the difficulty of investigating crimes where victims may be less likely to be formally reported missing.

## Pattern of Crimes (High Level)

Accounts commonly state that victims were women, including sex workers, and that the killings occurred over multiple years. Specific numbers and attribution can vary by source and by what investigators ultimately charged and proved.

## Investigation and Arrest

Police investigations consolidated multiple cases and led to an arrest in the mid-2010s, after which additional cases were linked through statements, evidence, and case reviews. Media coverage often refers to the case as a notable example of serial crime in the region.

## Legal Proceedings

As with many serial-crime investigations, the legal process typically involves:

- linking individual incidents to a single suspect,
- validating confession evidence and corroboration,
- establishing timelines and victim identification,
- presenting forensic and witness evidence where available.

## Public Impact

The case drew attention to safety issues for women in urban areas, policing practices, and the ways social stigma can affect both reporting and public understanding of victimization.
