## Summary

Morio Tanimoto set fire to a mental health clinic in Osaka killing 26 people including himself. He had been a patient at the clinic and used gasoline to start the fire.

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- **Type:** Arson attack on a medical facility (mental health clinic).
- **Location:** Osaka, Japan.
- **Date:** 2021-12-17.
- **Fatalities:** 26 people (including the perpetrator), as commonly reported in initial coverage.
- **Method:** Gasoline was used to start the fire.

## What Happened (Overview)

- The perpetrator entered the clinic and started a fire using gasoline.
- Victims included clinic patients and staff.
- The perpetrator died as a result of the incident.

## Immediate Response & Investigation (Overview)

- Fire and emergency services responded to extinguish the blaze and evacuate survivors.
- Police treated the incident as a deliberate arson attack.
- Public reporting described the perpetrator as having been a patient of the clinic.

## Why It Matters

- The incident was widely described as one of Japan's deadliest single-building fires in recent years.
- It prompted renewed public attention to building egress, fire safety practices, and risks of targeted violence in public facilities.
