## Summary

Nigel Mansell won the 1992 F1 World Championship and the 1993 CART IndyCar title, the only driver to hold both simultaneously.

## Formula One Career

Mansell competed in Formula One across multiple seasons and teams, including high-profile stints with Williams and Ferrari. He became known for aggressive racecraft and a willingness to push through physical discomfort, contributing to a public image as a hard-charging competitor.

## 1992 Championship Season

His 1992 title with Williams is often remembered as a dominant campaign, combining a strong car with consistent qualifying pace and race execution. The season stands as the peak of his Formula One results.

## Move to CART / IndyCar

After winning in F1, Mansell moved to the American open-wheel series (CART) and won the championship in his rookie season in 1993. Holding the reigning F1 and CART titles at the same time remains a rare cross-discipline achievement.

## Later Racing and Public Life

He returned to Formula One for subsequent spells and continued to appear in motorsport-related events and media. In later years he remained an emblematic figure from an era when drivers more commonly moved between major international series.

## Legacy

Mansell’s legacy is shaped by the combination of an F1 world championship, extensive race wins, and the unusual CART crossover success. He is frequently cited among the notable British drivers of the late 20th century.
