## Summary

Jamal ad-Din al-Afghani was a political activist and Islamic modernist who advocated for Muslim unity (pan-Islamism) against Western colonialism. He argued that Islam was compatible with reason and modern science. He traveled widely, influencing reform movements from Egypt to India to Iran. He co-founded the influential journal al-Urwa al-Wuthqa with Muhammad Abduh. His ideas inspired both modernist and revivalist Islamic movements.
