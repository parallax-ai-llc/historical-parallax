## Summary

Founder of the Genyōsha (Dark Ocean Society), a powerful ultranationalist secret society that promoted Japanese expansionism. Supported political assassinations and organized crime.

## Background

- Toyama Mitsuru was active in Japanese nationalist politics from the late Meiji period through the early Shōwa era.
- He is frequently discussed in connection with secret-society style political networks that blended ideology, patronage, and coercion.

## Genyōsha (Dark Ocean Society)

- Genyōsha was an ultranationalist organization associated with anti-liberal politics and support for Japanese expansion abroad.
- Accounts of the group describe involvement in political intimidation, support for violent activism, and links to underworld figures.
- The organization’s activities were part of a broader ecosystem of nationalist societies that sought influence over parties, bureaucrats, and military-linked circles.

## Influence and legacy

- Toyama’s name is sometimes connected to the later Black Dragon Society (Kokuryūkai), though sources differ on the exact institutional lineage and roles.
- In modern historiography, he is often presented as an emblematic figure in the intersection of nationalism and organized coercion.
