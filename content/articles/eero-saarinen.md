## Summary

Eero Saarinen was a Finnish-American architect known for his neofuturistic designs including the Gateway Arch in St. Louis, the TWA Flight Center at JFK Airport, and the Dulles International Airport terminal. His furniture designs, including the Tulip Chair, are also icons of mid-century modern design.
