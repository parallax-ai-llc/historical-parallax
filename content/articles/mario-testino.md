## Summary

Mario Testino is a Peruvian-born fashion and portrait photographer who became one of the most influential image-makers in the global fashion industry over a four-decade career. His work has appeared in virtually every major fashion publication, including Vogue, Vanity Fair, and GQ, and he has collaborated with the world's leading luxury brands including Gucci, Versace, Burberry, and Chanel. His photographs are celebrated for their warmth, sensuality, and ability to capture an almost cinematic glamour in his subjects.

Testino's cultural impact extends beyond magazine pages. His intimate portraits of Diana, Princess of Wales — taken months before her death in 1997 — remain among the most widely reproduced royal photographs in history. He helped define the visual language of 1990s and 2000s luxury fashion while launching and cementing the careers of numerous models and celebrities. His accessibility as a collaborator and his particular skill with natural light and spontaneous energy set his aesthetic apart from contemporaries.

## Early Life & Background

Mario Testino was born on October 30, 1954, in Lima, Peru, into a middle-class family of Italian descent. He studied at the Universidad del Pacífico in Lima before moving to London in the mid-1970s, where he attended the London School of Economics and then studied at St. Martin's School of Art. Initially he pursued photography with almost no formal training, selling his first images to small fashion magazines while developing his distinct approach through persistence and self-teaching. His outsider status as a South American arriving in the heart of the British fashion world sharpened his ambition and gave him a distinctive perspective.

## Rise to Prominence

Testino began building his reputation in London in the late 1970s and early 1980s, shooting for modest editorial clients before his work attracted the attention of major fashion editors. By the late 1980s he was contributing regularly to British and Italian Vogue. His career accelerated sharply when he was commissioned to shoot campaigns for Gucci under creative director Tom Ford in the 1990s — images that were provocative, bold, and enormously influential in repositioning the brand. The Princess Diana portraits, published in Vanity Fair in 1997, brought his name to a mainstream global audience. Throughout the 2000s he was among the highest-paid and most sought-after photographers in fashion.

## Key Achievements

- Shot the landmark 1997 Vanity Fair portrait series of Diana, Princess of Wales, just months before her death
- Produced defining campaign imagery for Gucci, Burberry, Versace, Dolce & Gabbana, and Chanel across multiple decades
- Published several acclaimed monographs, including *Any Objections?* (1998) and *Let Me In* (2007)
- Founded MATE (Museo de Arte de Lima — Testino), a photography museum in Lima, Peru, opened in 2012 to promote Latin American photography
- Shot official portraits of the British royal family, including Prince William and Catherine Middleton ahead of their 2011 wedding

## Controversies & Criticism

In January 2018, during the #MeToo movement, multiple male models and assistants published accounts in The Boston Globe alleging sexual misconduct by Testino spanning several decades. Following the allegations, Condé Nast, Burberry, and other major clients suspended their professional relationships with him. Testino denied the allegations. The accusations significantly altered his public standing and halted his mainstream commercial career, prompting broader discussion about power dynamics in the fashion industry.

## Legacy & Influence

Despite the controversies that clouded his later career, Testino's photographic output from the 1990s through the 2010s remains a defining archive of fashion's most commercially potent era. His founding of MATE represents a lasting institutional contribution to photography in Latin America. His visual style — characterized by sun-drenched spontaneity and relaxed glamour — influenced a generation of fashion photographers and shaped public expectations of luxury brand imagery.
