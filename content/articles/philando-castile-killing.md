## Summary

Minnesota police officer shot Philando Castile during a traffic stop while his girlfriend livestreamed the aftermath on Facebook. The officer was acquitted of all charges.

The shooting and the widely shared video became a major flashpoint in U.S. debates about policing, use of force, and accountability, and it prompted sustained public protests and official reviews.

## Incident Overview

- **Setting:** A traffic stop in Falcon Heights, Minnesota.
- **Immediate aftermath:** Castile’s girlfriend recorded and livestreamed the moments after the shooting, accelerating public awareness and media coverage.
- **Key issues raised:** The interaction’s escalation, the officer’s stated perception of threat, and the standards used to evaluate use-of-force decisions.

## Investigation and Legal Proceedings

- **Charging decisions:** Prosecutors filed criminal charges against the officer involved.
- **Trial:** The case proceeded to trial, with extensive focus on body of evidence, officer testimony, and legal thresholds for criminal liability.
- **Outcome:** The officer was acquitted of all charges, a result that intensified public scrutiny of how police shootings are adjudicated.

## Public Reaction and Impact

- **Protests and civic response:** The killing catalyzed protests, community organizing, and calls for reform.
- **Media and technology:** The livestreamed video highlighted the role of smartphones and social platforms in documenting incidents and shaping narratives in near real time.
- **Policy discussions:** The case is frequently cited in conversations about traffic-stop procedures, de-escalation training, and transparency in investigations.

## Legacy

- The event remains a frequently referenced example in discussions of U.S. policing and racial justice.
- It contributed to broader attention on the gap between public expectations of accountability and the legal standards applied in court.
