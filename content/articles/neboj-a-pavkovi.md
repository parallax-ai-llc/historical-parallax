## Summary

Serbian military commander convicted of crimes against humanity for his command of the 3rd Army during atrocities committed in Kosovo in 1999. He directed military operations that led to mass deportations.

## Background: Kosovo War (1998–1999)

The Kosovo War involved fighting between Yugoslav/Serbian forces and Kosovo Albanian insurgents, followed by a NATO air campaign and a major displacement of civilians. The period is widely associated with allegations of systematic abuses, including forced expulsions and attacks on civilians.

## Role and Command Responsibility

Pavković was a senior officer associated with the Yugoslav Army's operations in Kosovo. In international criminal law, command responsibility refers to a superior's liability when crimes are committed by subordinates and the superior knew or had reason to know and failed to prevent or punish. ICTY cases often turned on evidence about operational control, orders, reporting chains, and patterns of conduct on the ground.

## ICTY Proceedings

The International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) prosecuted multiple military and political figures for crimes committed during the conflicts of the 1990s. Pavković surrendered to the tribunal and was tried on charges including crimes against humanity and violations of the laws or customs of war, with sentencing reflecting findings on his role, the scale of crimes, and applicable legal standards.

## Legacy and Debate

Judgments involving Kosovo remain politically contested in the region, with disputes over narratives of victimhood and responsibility. In historical and legal literature, ICTY decisions are also used as case studies for how tribunals document events, assess evidence, and apply doctrines like joint criminal enterprise and command responsibility.
