## Summary

Former superintendent of Seoul's education system convicted of illegally influencing school admissions. His case highlighted widespread corruption in South Korea's education system.

## Additional Context

### Public role and policy domain

- As superintendent of education in Seoul, Kwak held an influential administrative position affecting public schools, budgeting, and admissions-related policy.
- The office is politically salient in South Korea because education is closely linked to social mobility and household spending.

### Legal and political controversy

- Kwak's most widely reported legal trouble involved election-law violations connected to his 2010 campaign (including allegations around campaign funding and coordination).
- The case became part of a broader set of South Korean public debates about political finance, anti-corruption enforcement, and the governance of education offices.
- His situation is often cited alongside other high-profile prosecutions as an example of how electoral rules are strictly policed in the country.

### Broader significance

- Illustrates how education administration can become a focal point for political conflict in societies where schooling is a major driver of status competition.
- Shows the reputational and institutional fragility of public education governance when corruption allegations emerge.

### Notes
