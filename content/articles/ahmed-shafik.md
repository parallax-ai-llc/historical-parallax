## Summary

Egyptian former PM and military officer who was Mubarak's last PM and narrowly lost the 2012 presidential election. He fled Egypt under corruption charges, was later briefly detained in the UAE. Charges are considered politically motivated by some.

## Additional Context

## Military and Aviation Career

Before entering top-level politics, Ahmed Shafik was a career officer in the Egyptian Air Force and later served as Minister of Civil Aviation, a portfolio that drew attention because of airport modernization and the strategic importance of aviation security.

## Role in Egypt’s Political Transition

- Shafik became prime minister during the final days of Hosni Mubarak’s presidency in 2011, amid mass protests.
- In 2012 he ran for president and lost to Mohamed Morsi in a close second-round result.
- In later years he remained an emblematic figure for supporters of the pre-2011 establishment and for critics of Islamist politics.

## Legal and Political Disputes

Shafik faced corruption-related allegations and legal proceedings at various points; interpretations of these cases vary and are often framed within Egypt’s broader post-2011 political struggles.
