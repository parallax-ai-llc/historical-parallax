## Summary

American career bank robber who committed robberies from the 1950s into his 70s and escaped from prison 18 times. He pulled off his last heist at age 78 and inspired the film The Old Man & the Gun.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 1999 | Arrested for bank robbery at age 78 |
| 2004 | Died in prison |

## Modus Operandi

- **Nonviolent presentation**: Tucker was often described as relying on charm, disguises, and calm demeanor rather than overt violence during robberies.
- **Target selection**: His crimes primarily involved banks, contributing to his reputation as a persistent, long-running offender.
- **Repeat offending**: The pattern of arrest, incarceration, escape, and reoffending shaped much of his public notoriety.

## Prison Escapes and Incarceration

- **Frequent escapes**: He became widely known for repeatedly escaping from custody, an unusual feature even among career criminals.
- **High-profile facilities**: Accounts of his incarceration include time in major state and federal prisons, with escapes used by authorities as examples of security challenges.
- **Later-life crimes**: His late-age robberies drew attention because they contrasted with typical age patterns in criminal offending.

## Cultural Impact

- **Public fascination**: Media portrayals often emphasized the tension between his polite demeanor and his long criminal record.
- **Film adaptation**: His story helped inspire *The Old Man & the Gun* (2018), which dramatized aspects of his later-life robberies and prison breaks.
- **Legacy**: In criminology and popular culture, he is sometimes cited as an extreme example of recidivism paired with repeated custodial escapes.
- **Aging and crime**: His case is frequently mentioned in media discussions about how rare it is for older offenders to continue committing high-risk crimes.
- **Narrative framing**: Retellings often focus on the contrast between his courteous behavior and the persistence of his criminal career.
- **Historical note**: The documented number of escapes is often highlighted as an outlier in U.S. prison-escape histories.
