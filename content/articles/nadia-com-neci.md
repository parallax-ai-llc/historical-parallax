## Summary

Nadia Comăneci scored the first perfect 10 in Olympic gymnastics at 1976 Montreal. She won five Olympic golds.

## Early Life and Training

Comăneci was born in Onești, Romania, and trained from a young age in the Romanian gymnastics system. She is closely associated with coaches Béla and Márta Károlyi, who played a major role in her development and in Romania’s rise as a powerhouse in women’s artistic gymnastics.

## 1976 Montreal Olympics

At the 1976 Summer Olympics, Comăneci became an international phenomenon after receiving the first recorded perfect 10.0 score in Olympic gymnastics. Multiple perfect scores followed during the same Games, and she left Montreal with a haul of medals that helped define the event.

Key points often noted in overviews of 1976:

- The scoring display equipment was not designed to show “10.0” and reportedly displayed “1.00” for her perfect routines.
- Her performances on uneven bars and balance beam are frequently cited as signature routines of the era.

## Continued Competition and 1980 Olympics

Comăneci continued to compete at the top level through the late 1970s and into 1980, winning additional Olympic titles in Moscow. Her career is commonly summarized as spanning two Olympic cycles in which she remained a central figure in women’s gymnastics.

## Life After Elite Sport

After retiring from competition, Comăneci remained connected to gymnastics through public roles and organizational involvement. In 1989 she left Romania and later settled in the United States.

## Public Profile and Legacy

Comăneci is widely remembered for elevating global attention on women’s gymnastics and for becoming a symbol of technical precision in the sport. Her “perfect 10” is often treated as a turning point in how audiences and broadcasters engaged with gymnastics, helping to popularize it as a marquee Olympic event.
