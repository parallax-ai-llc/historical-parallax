## Summary

Kurdish leader and former president of Iraqi Kurdistan whose family has been accused of nepotism and corruption. His 2017 independence referendum led to Iraqi military action and Kurdish territorial losses. His family controls key business interests.

## Background

Masoud Barzani is a leading figure in Kurdish politics and the head of the Kurdistan Democratic Party (KDP). He is the son of Mullah Mustafa Barzani, an influential Kurdish nationalist leader, and his political identity is closely tied to the modern history of Kurdish autonomy movements in Iraq.

## Political Role and Influence

As president of the Kurdistan Region of Iraq (KRI), Barzani was associated with the consolidation of KDP influence across regional institutions and security structures. Analysts and critics have described Kurdish politics in the KRI as heavily shaped by party patronage networks, with rival power centers often aligned with major parties such as the KDP and the PUK.

## 2017 Independence Referendum (Aftermath)

In 2017 the KRI held a referendum on independence. While the vote produced a strong “yes” result among participants, it was opposed by the Iraqi federal government and several regional states. After the referendum, Iraqi forces moved to reassert control over disputed territories, and Kurdish authorities lost positions in areas that had been held since the fight against ISIS, making the referendum widely viewed as a strategic setback.

## Allegations and Criticism

Public allegations concerning Barzani and the broader KDP leadership have included:

- Nepotism and the concentration of senior political and security roles among closely connected elites.
- Claims that business opportunities, licensing, and contracts in the region favored networks linked to the ruling parties.
- Criticism that institutional checks on executive and party power were weak compared to the practical influence of party structures.

These claims are contested in Kurdish politics; supporters often emphasize stability, security coordination, and the region’s role as a partner for international actors.
