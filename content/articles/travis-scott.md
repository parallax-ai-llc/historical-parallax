## Summary

Travis Scott, born Jacques Berman Webster II, is an American rapper, singer, songwriter, and record producer who became one of the most influential figures in hip-hop during the late 2010s. Known for his heavily auto-tuned vocals, psychedelic production style, and immersive live performances, Scott has blurred the lines between rap, pop, and experimental music.

Beyond music, Scott has established himself as a cultural force through landmark brand collaborations with Nike, McDonald's, and Fortnite, redefining how artists engage with commercial partnerships. His ability to merge music, fashion, and digital experiences has made him a blueprint for the modern multi-platform artist, though his career has also been marked by significant controversy.

## Early Life & Background

Jacques Berman Webster II was born on April 30, 1991, in Houston, Texas. He grew up in Missouri City, a middle-class suburb south of Houston, living with his grandmother until age six before moving in with his parents. His father was a musician and his mother worked for Apple. Scott attended Elkins High School and briefly enrolled at the University of Texas at San Antonio before dropping out to pursue music full-time, a decision that strained his relationship with his parents, who cut him off financially.

Houston's chopped and screwed scene, pioneered by DJ Screw, heavily influenced Scott's sonic aesthetic. He moved to New York and then Los Angeles in his late teens, sleeping on friends' couches while producing beats and recording tracks.

## Rise to Prominence

Scott's early mixtape *Owl Pharaoh* (2013) caught the attention of rapper T.I., who signed him to Grand Hustle Records. He later signed with Kanye West's GOOD Music and Epic Records. His second mixtape, *Days Before Rodeo* (2014), built significant underground buzz with tracks like "Mamacita" and "Don't Play."

His debut studio album, *Rodeo* (2015), was released to critical acclaim, featuring the platinum single "Antidote," which peaked at number 16 on the Billboard Hot 100. His second album, *Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight* (2016), debuted at number one on the Billboard 200. Scott cemented his superstar status with *Astroworld* (2018), named after the defunct Houston amusement park. The album's lead single "Sicko Mode" reached number one on the Hot 100 and became a cultural phenomenon.

## Key Achievements

Scott's *Astroworld* debuted at number one with 537,000 album-equivalent units in its first week. "Sicko Mode" was certified Diamond by the RIAA. His 2020 virtual concert within Fortnite attracted over 12.3 million concurrent players, setting a record for the platform. His Nike collaboration on the Air Jordan 1 Retro High became one of the most sought-after sneakers in history, routinely reselling for over $1,500. His McDonald's partnership in September 2020 was the first celebrity meal collaboration since Michael Jordan's in 1992, generating widespread shortages. His album *Utopia* (2023) debuted at number one on the Billboard 200.

## Controversies & Criticism

On November 5, 2021, a crowd surge at Scott's Astroworld Festival in Houston resulted in the deaths of ten concertgoers, with hundreds more injured. The tragedy prompted extensive scrutiny of festival safety practices and Scott's role in encouraging aggressive crowd behavior at his shows. Numerous lawsuits were filed against Scott, Live Nation, and other organizers. Scott denied responsibility but expressed condolences and offered to cover funeral costs. The incident led to broader industry conversations about concert safety and artist accountability. In 2024, a $2 billion lawsuit was settled, though specific terms were not fully disclosed.

## Legacy & Influence

Travis Scott's impact on hip-hop production — layered, atmospheric, and psychedelic — has influenced a generation of producers and artists. His concept of "rage music" shaped festival culture throughout the 2010s and 2020s. His approach to brand partnerships, treating collaborations as creative projects rather than endorsements, fundamentally changed celebrity marketing. Despite the shadow of Astroworld, Scott remains one of the most commercially successful and culturally relevant artists of his generation.
