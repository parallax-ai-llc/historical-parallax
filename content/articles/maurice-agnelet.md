## Context

Maurice Agnelet is primarily known in relation to the disappearance and presumed murder of Agnès Le Roux, a wealthy French heiress connected to a casino business. The case is notable for its long duration, repeated legal proceedings, and sustained public attention.

## High-level case outline

- **Relationship**: Agnelet was a French lawyer and was reported to have been in a relationship with Le Roux.
- **Allegations**: Prosecutors alleged that Le Roux was killed and that Agnelet was responsible.
- **Outcome**: He was ultimately convicted of murder (notably in 2014), after years of investigation and court hearings.

## Why the case drew attention

- **No body found / evidentiary challenges**: Cases built largely on indirect evidence tend to generate controversy and multiple appeals.
- **Business and money**: The allegations were intertwined with inheritance, control of assets, and high-stakes disputes.
- **Long timeline**: The span between the alleged crime and final conviction is frequently cited as a defining feature of the matter.

## Notes on legal process (France)

This article is intentionally high-level; French criminal procedure has features that often appear in long-running cases:

- Serious felonies can be tried in an assize court setting.
- Appeals may result in retrials rather than only a review of legal errors.
- High-profile investigations can cycle through new evidence, new testimony, and renewed charges over time.
