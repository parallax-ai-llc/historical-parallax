## Summary

Bawa Muhaiyaddeen was a Sri Lankan Sufi teacher who was found by followers in the jungles of Sri Lanka in the 1930s and spent the last 15 years of his life in Philadelphia, establishing the Bawa Muhaiyaddeen Fellowship. His exact origins are unknown. He taught a universal form of Sufism emphasizing God's qualities of compassion and love. He built one of the first mosques in America designed specifically for American Muslims.
