## Background

Changpeng Zhao (commonly known as "CZ") is a Chinese-born Canadian business executive who co-founded Binance, a cryptocurrency exchange that grew into one of the largest global trading platforms by volume.

## Legal case (US)

- In 2023, US authorities announced a settlement with Binance that included a multi‑billion‑dollar penalty and compliance commitments.
- Zhao personally pleaded guilty to a criminal charge related to failures in anti‑money‑laundering (AML) controls.
- As part of the resolution, Zhao agreed to step down as Binance's CEO, and Binance agreed to implement enhanced compliance measures.

## Why it mattered

Regulators and prosecutors framed the case as a landmark enforcement action in the crypto industry, emphasizing:

- Bank Secrecy Act / AML expectations for exchanges serving US customers
- Sanctions compliance and customer‑screening controls
- The role of corporate governance and "tone from the top" in high‑risk financial platforms

## Aftermath and industry impact

- The settlement accelerated a broader industry shift toward formal compliance programs, including transaction monitoring, KYC/identity verification, and independent audits.
- The case is frequently cited in discussions about the regulation of centralized exchanges and the risks of operating across jurisdictions.
