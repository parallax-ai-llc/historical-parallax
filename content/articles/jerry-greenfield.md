## Summary

Jerry Greenfield co-founded Ben & Jerry's ice cream with Ben Cohen. Together they built a brand that combined premium ice cream with social activism. He served as the company's primary ice cream maker and was involved in community relations. After Unilever's acquisition, he continued as an advocate for social justice and responsible business. The duo proved that companies could be both profitable and socially responsible.
