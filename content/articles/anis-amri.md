## Summary

Tunisian terrorist who drove a truck into a Christmas market in Berlin on December 19, 2016, killing 12 people. He pledged allegiance to ISIS. He fled across Europe and was shot dead by Italian police near Milan.

## Background

- Amri was born in Tunisia and later moved to Italy, where he was imprisoned for arson and violence-related offenses before eventually leaving the country.[^1]
- He later traveled to Germany and sought asylum; German authorities had assessed him as a potential threat prior to the Berlin attack.[^1]

## Berlin Christmas market attack (2016)

- **Date:** 2016-12-19
- **Location:** Breitscheidplatz, Berlin (near the Kaiser Wilhelm Memorial Church)
- **Method:** A hijacked heavy truck was driven into crowds at a Christmas market.
- **Casualties:** 12 people killed and dozens injured (figures vary slightly by source and later accounting).[^1]
- The Islamic State (ISIS) claimed responsibility and released material presenting Amri as the attacker.[^1]

## Flight and death

- After the attack, Amri fled Germany and traveled through multiple European countries.
- On 2016-12-23, he was shot and killed by police in Sesto San Giovanni near Milan, Italy, after a routine stop and subsequent confrontation.[^1]

## Aftermath and significance

- The attack intensified European debates over intelligence sharing, border controls, and the monitoring of suspected extremists.
- It also led to scrutiny of how threat assessments were handled in Germany in the months prior to the attack.[^1]
