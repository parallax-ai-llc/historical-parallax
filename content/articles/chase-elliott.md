## Summary

Chase Elliott is an American professional stock car racing driver who competes full-time in the NASCAR Cup Series, driving the No. 9 Chevrolet Camaro ZL1 for Hendrick Motorsports. The son of NASCAR Hall of Famer Bill Elliott, Chase has carved out his own legacy as one of the premier drivers of his generation, winning the 2020 NASCAR Cup Series championship. Known for his consistent road course excellence and his deep connection with fans, he has been voted NASCAR's Most Popular Driver every year since 2018.

Elliott represents a new generation of NASCAR talent that combines technical precision with marketability. His championship run in 2020 — achieved during a shortened, pandemic-affected season — cemented his status as a bonafide title contender and validated years of strong but winless playoff performances. He is widely regarded as one of the most complete drivers in the sport.

## Early Life & Background

Chase Elliott was born on November 28, 1995, in Dawsonville, Georgia, the same hometown as his father, NASCAR legend Bill Elliott. Growing up immersed in motorsport culture, Chase began karting at a young age and quickly demonstrated exceptional talent. He attended school in Dawsonville while spending much of his youth racing in various regional and national series. His upbringing in a racing family gave him direct access to professional knowledge and mentorship that accelerated his development as a driver.

## Rise to Prominence

Elliott rose through the ranks of NASCAR's developmental ladder with remarkable speed. In 2012, at age 16, he began competing in the NASCAR K&N Pro Series. His breakout came in 2014 when he won the NASCAR Nationwide Series (now Xfinity Series) championship with JR Motorsports at just 18 years old, becoming the youngest champion in series history at the time.

In 2016, he was tabbed to replace the retiring Jeff Gordon in the iconic No. 24 — later renumbered to No. 9 — at Hendrick Motorsports. He made his Cup Series debut that year and earned Rookie of the Year honors. Despite accumulating strong finishes and multiple runner-up results over his first few seasons, his first Cup win did not come until 2018 at Watkins Glen International, ending a stretch of agonizing close finishes. From there, victories followed steadily, particularly on road courses where Elliott established himself as the class of the field.

## Key Achievements

- **2020 NASCAR Cup Series Champion** — clinched the title at Phoenix Raceway in November 2020
- **NASCAR Xfinity Series Champion (2014)** — youngest champion in series history at the time
- **NASCAR Cup Series Most Popular Driver** — awarded every year from 2018 through the mid-2020s, continuing a legacy his father held for many years
- **Seven road course victories** in the Cup Series by 2023, including multiple wins at Watkins Glen, Road America, and the Charlotte Motor Speedway Roval
- **2020 NASCAR Cup Series regular season champion**

## Controversies & Criticism

Elliott's career has been largely free of major controversy. His most difficult stretch came in 2023 when a snowboarding accident in January fractured his tibia, sidelining him for multiple races — an unusual off-track setback that frustrated fans and the team. Some critics have pointed to uneven oval-track performance relative to his road course dominance as a limitation, though his championship and consistent playoff appearances counter that narrative.

## Legacy & Influence

Chase Elliott has become the face of NASCAR's next generation, bridging the legacy of his Hall of Fame father with his own hard-won accomplishments. His popularity with fans, particularly younger audiences, has helped NASCAR maintain relevance during a period of evolving media consumption. His road course mastery has also influenced how teams approach road circuits strategically. As he enters his prime years, Elliott is positioned to add further championships and secure a place among the sport's all-time greats.
