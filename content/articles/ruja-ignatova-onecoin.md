## Summary

Bulgarian businesswoman known as the Cryptoqueen who ran OneCoin, a $4 billion cryptocurrency Ponzi scheme that defrauded millions of investors worldwide. She disappeared in 2017 and is on the FBI's Ten Most Wanted.

## Background and Context

- OneCoin was promoted as a cryptocurrency but was widely described by investigators and journalists as lacking the typical features of a public, independently verifiable blockchain.
- The project used aggressive multi-level marketing style sales tactics, relying on a global network of distributors to recruit new participants.

## How the Scheme Was Presented

- Marketing materials emphasized education packages and “tokens” that could be used to “mine” OneCoins within a controlled internal system.
- Trading was often described as taking place on company-controlled platforms rather than on open, third-party exchanges.

## Disappearance and Investigations

- In 2017, Ignatova stopped appearing publicly after traveling in Europe; her whereabouts have been unknown since.
- Authorities in multiple countries pursued related investigations, and several OneCoin associates were later arrested or prosecuted.

## Legal Status (High-Level)

- U.S. prosecutors charged Ignatova in connection with alleged fraud and money laundering, and she has been treated as a fugitive in U.S. proceedings.
- In 2022, the FBI added her to its Ten Most Wanted Fugitives list and offered a reward for information leading to her arrest.
