## Summary

Khvicha Kvaratskhelia is a Georgian professional footballer who plays as a left winger for Paris Saint-Germain and the Georgian national team. Born on February 12, 2001, in Tbilisi, Georgia, he is widely regarded as one of the most exciting attacking players of his generation, known for his explosive dribbling, sharp finishing, and creativity in tight spaces.

He rose to global prominence during his debut season at Napoli in 2022–23, where his performances were central to the club winning its first Serie A title in 33 years. His impact was so immediate and emphatic that Napoli fans coined the nickname "Kvaradona" — a portmanteau of his surname and Diego Maradona — reflecting the weight of expectation and admiration he attracted within his first season in Italy.

## Early Life & Background

Kvaratskhelia was born in Tbilisi, Georgia, and grew up in a football family — his father, Badri Kvaratskhelia, was a former professional footballer. He began his youth career at Dinamo Tbilisi before moving through the Georgian football system. His technical development in Georgia's domestic leagues, combined with a loan spell at Rustavi, gave him the foundation for a professional career at a young age.

## Rise to Prominence

Kvaratskhelia made his senior debut for Dinamo Tbilisi and later joined Lokomotiv Moscow's youth structure before signing with Rubin Kazan in Russia. Following Russia's invasion of Ukraine in February 2022, FIFA permitted foreign players to suspend their contracts with Russian clubs, and Kvaratskhelia departed, joining Georgian club Dinamo Batumi on a temporary basis.

His performances for Dinamo Batumi attracted widespread European interest, and in July 2022, Napoli signed him for a reported fee of approximately €10 million. The 2022–23 Serie A season proved transformative: Kvaratskhelia scored 12 league goals and contributed 13 assists, forming a devastating partnership with striker Victor Osimhen. Napoli finished the season with 90 points, clinching the Scudetto by a wide margin. Kvaratskhelia won the Serie A Young Player of the Year award and received widespread recognition across European football.

In January 2025, PSG signed Kvaratskhelia for a reported fee of around €70–75 million, marking a significant move to one of Europe's wealthiest clubs.

## Key Achievements

- Serie A title with Napoli (2022–23)
- Serie A Young Player of the Year (2022–23)
- Multiple UEFA Champions League appearances with Napoli and PSG
- Key player for Georgia's historic qualification for UEFA Euro 2024 — the nation's first-ever major international tournament appearance
- Scored at Euro 2024 as Georgia reached the Round of 16, defeating Portugal 2–0 in the group stage

## Controversies & Criticism

Kvaratskhelia's departure from Napoli in January 2025 drew criticism from some supporters who felt the club sold him prematurely. Contract negotiations had reportedly stalled over salary demands, and while the transfer was conducted professionally, sections of the Napoli fanbase expressed frustration at losing a player who had become a symbol of the club's recent success.

## Legacy & Influence

Kvaratskhelia has already demonstrated a transformative impact on Georgian football, elevating the national team's profile and inspiring a generation of young players in the country. His combination of technical skill, physical directness, and consistent big-game performances have established him as a top-tier European talent. At 25, his career trajectory suggests his most significant achievements may still lie ahead.
