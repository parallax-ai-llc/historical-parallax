## Summary

The Prague Spring of 1968 was an attempt to liberalize communist Czechoslovakia under Alexander Dubček's 'socialism with a human face.' Soviet and Warsaw Pact forces invaded in August, crushing the reforms and establishing the Brezhnev Doctrine.
