## Summary

Colombian paramilitary leader who founded the AUC, Colombias largest right-wing paramilitary organization. The AUC committed massacres of civilians while fighting FARC.

## Additional Context

- **Role in Colombia’s conflict:** Castaño became one of the most visible leaders associated with right-wing paramilitary forces during Colombia’s long internal armed conflict, positioning his organization as anti-guerrilla while operating outside state command structures.
- **AUC (high level):** The United Self-Defense Forces of Colombia (AUC) functioned as a federation of paramilitary groups and was widely linked in reporting and judicial processes to massacres, targeted killings, and displacement of civilians.
- **International designation:** The AUC’s designation as a terrorist organization by the United States in the early 2000s reflected concerns about violence and criminal activity connected to the group.

## Allegations and Legal Attention

- Public accounts of the period include allegations of collaboration among some local power brokers, armed actors, and illicit economies; coverage often emphasizes the blurred boundary between political violence and organized crime.
- Castaño has been described in secondary sources as involved in or connected to severe human-rights abuses attributed to paramilitary forces during the conflict.

## Death and Legacy

- He was reported killed in 2004, with later narratives commonly describing internal disputes within paramilitary networks as a contributing factor.
- In historical summaries of Colombia’s conflict, his name appears frequently in discussions of paramilitarism, demobilization processes, and accountability for violence against civilians.

## Broader Historical Notes

- The AUC’s later demobilization and the subsequent legal processes are commonly discussed as attempts to reduce paramilitary violence while addressing victims’ rights.
- Analysts of the period often focus on how armed groups sustained themselves through extortion, territorial control, and links to narcotics trafficking.
- The long-term impact includes displacement, trauma, and ongoing debates about truth, reparations, and political responsibility.
