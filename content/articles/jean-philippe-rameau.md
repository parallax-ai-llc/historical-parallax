## Summary

Jean-Philippe Rameau was the most important French composer of the 18th century and a major music theorist. His operas and opera-ballets, including Les Indes galantes and Castor et Pollux, brought new harmonic richness and dramatic power to French stage music. His Treatise on Harmony fundamentally shaped Western music theory.
