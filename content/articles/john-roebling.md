## Summary

John Roebling designed the Brooklyn Bridge, one of the most iconic structures in American engineering history. He pioneered the use of wire rope (steel cable) for suspension bridge construction. He died from tetanus after a construction-site injury before the bridge was completed; his son Washington Roebling finished the project.
