## Summary

James Chadwick was a British physicist who discovered the neutron in 1932, for which he won the 1935 Nobel Prize in Physics. His discovery of the electrically neutral particle in the atomic nucleus explained isotopes and made nuclear fission possible. He later led the British contribution to the Manhattan Project.
