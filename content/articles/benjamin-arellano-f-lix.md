## Summary

Mexican drug lord who led the Tijuana Cartel (Arellano Félix Organization) with his brothers. The cartel controlled the Tijuana-San Diego border crossing and was one of Mexico's most powerful drug organizations. Arrested in 2002.

## Organization and Role

The Arellano Félix Organization (AFO) was a trafficking group historically associated with the Tijuana plaza, a strategic corridor for moving narcotics into the United States. As one of the leading figures, Arellano Félix was commonly described by authorities and journalists as a central decision-maker in the group’s operations and its relationships with rival organizations.

## Law Enforcement Pressure

By the late 1990s and early 2000s, Mexican and U.S. agencies increased cooperation and targeted major cartel leadership figures. Arellano Félix’s 2002 arrest was widely treated as a milestone in those efforts, even as the broader dynamics of cartel fragmentation and succession continued.

## Extradition and Sentencing (High Level)

- After a period of imprisonment in Mexico, he was extradited to the United States.
- In U.S. federal court, he received a multi-decade sentence.
- Reporting on the case often emphasizes that prosecution focused on organized trafficking activities rather than portraying a single individual as solely responsible for the organization’s actions.

## Historical Context

The Tijuana corridor has been a recurring focal point in Mexico’s drug war history because of geography, cross-border commerce, and the scale of demand in the U.S. market. The AFO’s prominence in this area is frequently referenced in studies of how territorial control, corruption, and violence can shape criminal competition.

Arellano Félix’s case is often cited in discussions of how leadership arrests can disrupt a specific organization while not necessarily reducing overall trafficking capacity, as other groups may fill the vacuum.

In historical summaries, he is therefore treated as one node in a larger, evolving network of cross-border organized crime.
