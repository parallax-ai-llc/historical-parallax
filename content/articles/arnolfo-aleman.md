## Summary

See Arnaldo Aleman.

Arnolfo (often rendered **Arnaldo**) Alemán Lacayo is a Nicaraguan politician who served as **Mayor of Managua** and later as **President of Nicaragua (1997–2002)**. He became one of the most prominent figures in the Liberal Constitutionalist Party (PLC) era, and his post-presidency was marked by major corruption proceedings that resulted in a conviction.

This article uses the spelling "Arnolfo" as a variant; many sources use "Arnaldo" for the same individual.

## Background

Alemán was born in 1946 and rose through liberal political networks during Nicaragua's transition periods. His national profile increased during the 1990s as municipal politics in Managua became a major platform for national leadership.

## Presidency (1997–2002)

As president, Alemán led a center-right administration during a period focused on market-oriented reforms, international assistance, and reconstruction after natural disasters. His presidency is also associated with intense party bargaining and institutional reforms that shaped Nicaragua's political landscape in the late 1990s.

One frequently noted aspect of this era is the political arrangement sometimes referred to as **"El Pacto"**, a power-sharing understanding between leading liberal and Sandinista figures that influenced electoral and institutional rules.

## Corruption Case and Legal Outcomes

After leaving office, Alemán faced allegations related to misuse of public funds and was convicted in 2003. Accounts of subsequent legal developments vary by source and time period, including changes to sentencing conditions and political-legal controversies surrounding the case.

## Reputation and Legacy

Alemán remains a polarizing figure in Nicaragua:

- Supporters credit him with rebuilding efforts and strengthening liberal electoral machinery.
- Critics emphasize corruption allegations and the long-term institutional effects of elite political deals.
