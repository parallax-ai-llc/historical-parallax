## Summary

Prince Harry, Duke of Sussex, born Henry Charles Albert David on September 15, 1984, is the younger son of King Charles III and the late Princess Diana. Once one of Britain's most popular royals, he served a decade in the British Army, including two combat tours in Afghanistan, before stepping back from royal duties in 2020 alongside his wife, Meghan Markle. He now resides in Montecito, California, pursuing independent ventures in media, philanthropy, and advocacy.

Harry's significance extends beyond his royal lineage. His candid disclosures about mental health struggles, racial bias within the royal institution, and the psychological toll of royal life have ignited global conversations about the monarchy's modernization. His 2023 memoir *Spare* became the fastest-selling nonfiction book in UK publishing history, cementing his role as a polarizing yet culturally consequential figure.

## Early Life & Background

Harry was born at St Mary's Hospital, London, and raised primarily at Kensington Palace and Highgrove House. His childhood was defined by public scrutiny and private upheaval — most profoundly the death of his mother, Princess Diana, in a Paris car crash on August 31, 1997, when Harry was 12. He has spoken extensively about suppressing grief for nearly two decades afterward.

He attended Ludgrove School and then Eton College, graduating in 2003 with two A-levels. After a gap year in Australia and Lesotho — where he co-founded the charity Sentebale in 2006 — he enrolled at the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst in May 2005, commissioning as a second lieutenant in April 2006.

## Rise to Prominence

Harry served two tours in Afghanistan: a frontline deployment in Helmand Province in 2007–08, cut short when his presence was leaked, and a second tour as an Apache helicopter co-pilot and gunner from 2012–13. He left the army in June 2015 after ten years of service, having risen to the rank of Captain.

In 2016 he began dating American actress Meghan Markle. Their marriage at Windsor Castle on May 19, 2018, drew a global television audience estimated at 1.9 billion. Their son Archie was born in May 2019, followed by daughter Lilibet in June 2021.

In January 2020, Harry and Meghan announced their intention to step back as senior royals, relocating first to Canada and then to California. The formal separation from royal duties was confirmed in February 2020.

## Key Achievements

- Co-founded **Sentebale** (2006), supporting children affected by HIV/AIDS in Lesotho and Botswana.
- Founded the **Invictus Games** in 2014, an international Paralympic-style competition for wounded servicemembers; the games have since been held in eight cities across four continents.
- Published memoir ***Spare*** (January 2023), which sold over 1.4 million copies in the UK and US on its first day, breaking multiple sales records.
- Signed multi-year content deals with Netflix and Spotify (both announced 2020), producing the documentary series *Harry & Meghan* (2022).

## Controversies & Criticism

Harry's decision to give a candid interview to Oprah Winfrey in March 2021 — in which he and Meghan alleged a royal family member had expressed concern about Archie's skin color — triggered intense debate. Buckingham Palace issued a rare public rebuttal, calling the claims "concerning" but noting "recollections may vary."

*Spare* drew criticism from royal commentators and family members for detailed allegations against King Charles III, Queen Camilla, and Prince William, including claims of a physical altercation with William in 2019. Critics accused Harry of airing private grievances for commercial gain; supporters argued the book exposed systemic failures of the institution.

His 2023 visa application to the United States became a legal controversy when it was questioned whether past drug use disclosed in *Spare* had been properly declared.

## Legacy & Influence

Harry's most durable legacy may be his normalization of mental health discourse among men, particularly veterans. The Invictus Games have directly engaged over 1,000 competitors across multiple nations and generated sustained funding for veteran rehabilitation programs. His public break with the monarchy accelerated broader debate about the future relevance and reform of the British royal institution. Whether viewed as a whistleblower or a disruptor, his trajectory represents an unprecedented rupture in modern royal history.
