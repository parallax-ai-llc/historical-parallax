## Summary

Femke Bol (born February 23, 2000) is a Dutch track and field athlete specializing in the 400 meters and 400 meters hurdles, widely regarded as one of the most dominant middle-distance sprinters of her generation. Hailing from Amersfoort, Netherlands, she burst onto the international scene as a teenager and rapidly ascended to the top of world athletics with a combination of raw speed, tactical intelligence, and remarkable versatility.

Bol has become particularly celebrated for her performances in relay events, most notably her extraordinary anchor legs that have repeatedly turned near-certain defeats into stunning victories for the Netherlands and European teams. Her ability to perform under pressure in championship settings has made her one of the most compelling figures in contemporary athletics.

## Early Life & Background

Femke Bol was born on February 23, 2000, in Amersfoort, a city in the Utrecht province of the Netherlands. She began athletics at a young age and showed early aptitude for sprint and hurdle events. Coached by Laurent Meuwly, she developed rapidly through the Dutch youth athletics system, demonstrating exceptional talent across both the flat 400m and the 400m hurdles disciplines — a rare dual-event capability that would define her senior career.

## Rise to Prominence

Bol announced herself on the global stage at the 2019 European Athletics Indoor Championships in Glasgow, where she won the 400m title at just 19 years old. She followed this with strong performances through 2020 and 2021, establishing personal bests that placed her among the world's elite.

At the 2020 Tokyo Olympics, she won bronze in the 400m hurdles and contributed to a silver medal in the mixed 4×400m relay. Her profile exploded in 2022 when she delivered one of the most memorable relay legs in athletics history at the European Championships in Munich, storming from fourth place to first on the 4×400m anchor to secure gold for the Netherlands. That same year, she won the Diamond League title and set a world indoor 400m record of 49.26 seconds in February 2023 at the World Athletics Indoor Tour in Liévin, France.

At the 2023 World Athletics Championships in Budapest, Bol won gold in the 400m hurdles and anchored the Netherlands to gold in the 4×400m mixed relay. She entered the 2024 Paris Olympics as one of the most anticipated athletes of the Games, where she won gold in the 400m hurdles with a time of 52.03 seconds and powered the Dutch mixed relay team to a gold medal.

## Key Achievements

- World Athletics Championships gold, 400m hurdles (2023)
- Olympic gold, 400m hurdles — Paris 2024
- Olympic bronze, 400m hurdles — Tokyo 2020
- World indoor 400m record: 49.26 seconds (February 2023, Liévin)
- Multiple European Championships gold medals (individual and relay)
- Diamond League 400m hurdles champion (2022, 2023)

## Controversies & Criticism

Bol has largely avoided significant controversy throughout her career. Her dominance has occasionally prompted discussion about the competitive balance in women's 400m hurdles, though such observations reflect admiration as much as critique. No doping violations or conduct controversies have been associated with her career.

## Legacy & Influence

Femke Bol has reinvigorated Dutch athletics and elevated the profile of the 400m hurdles globally. Her relay heroics have made her a national icon in the Netherlands and a symbol of clutch performance in team events. Still only in her mid-twenties as of 2026, she is positioned to further redefine the boundaries of her events and likely ranks among the all-time greats of the discipline before her career concludes.
