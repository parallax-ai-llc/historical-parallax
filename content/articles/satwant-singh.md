## Context

- Satwant Singh served in the Indian Army and was posted as part of Prime Minister Indira Gandhi's personal security detail.
- The assassination occurred against the backdrop of escalating conflict between the Indian state and Sikh separatist militancy in Punjab.
- A key precipitating episode was **Operation Blue Star** (June 1984), the Indian Army's assault on the Golden Temple complex in Amritsar.

## Assassination of Indira Gandhi (1984)

- On 31 October 1984, Satwant Singh and fellow bodyguard Beant Singh shot Indira Gandhi at her residence in New Delhi.
- Beant Singh was killed in the immediate aftermath; Satwant Singh was captured after being shot and later tried.

## Trial, Conviction, and Aftermath

- Satwant Singh was convicted for his role in the assassination and sentenced to death.
- The killing was followed by widespread anti-Sikh violence in multiple Indian cities in 1984, often referred to as the 1984 anti-Sikh riots.

## Legacy and Significance

- The assassination remains a pivotal event in modern Indian political history, reshaping security practices and national politics.
- Satwant Singh is primarily remembered in relation to the assassination and the broader cycle of violence surrounding Punjab in the 1980s.
