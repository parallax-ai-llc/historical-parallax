## Summary

American woman known as the Girl in the Box who was kidnapped by Cameron Hooker in 1977 and held captive for 7 years. She was kept in a coffin-like box under her captor's bed for up to 23 hours a day.

## Additional Context

- **Nickname:** The case became widely known as **"The Girl in the Box"**, referencing the confinement method described in reporting and testimony.
- **Method of control:** Accounts emphasize a mix of **physical restraint**, **sexual violence**, **isolation**, and **psychological coercion** used to maintain captivity over years.
- **Public misconceptions:** Later coverage often highlights how long-term captivity can involve coercive control that complicates escape, reporting, and victim behavior.

## Legal and Aftermath Notes

- **Criminal proceedings:** Cameron Hooker was prosecuted and received a lengthy prison sentence (commonly reported as **104 years**).
- **Victim impact:** The case is frequently discussed in the context of trauma, survival strategies, and long-term recovery needs after prolonged captivity.
- **Media coverage:** Books and television documentaries have revisited the case, contributing to its notoriety in U.S. true-crime media.

## Related Themes

- **Hitchhiking risk:** The abduction is often cited in discussions of the dangers associated with hitchhiking in the 1970s.
- **Captivity narratives:** The case is referenced alongside other high-profile captivity cases to illustrate patterns of offender manipulation and victim isolation.
