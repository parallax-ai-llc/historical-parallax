## Summary

Former President of Congo-Brazzaville who was overthrown by Sassou Nguesso in a civil war. Convicted in absentia of treason and embezzlement of public funds.

## Political Career (high level)

Lissouba was a Congolese politician and academic who rose to national leadership during periods of significant political transition in the Republic of the Congo (Congo-Brazzaville). He became president following the reintroduction of multi-party elections in the early 1990s.[^1]

## 1997 Civil War and Overthrow

The 1997 conflict in Congo-Brazzaville culminated in Lissouba being removed from power and Denis Sassou Nguesso returning to the presidency. Accounts of the period typically emphasize the role of armed factions, control of the capital (Brazzaville), and regional involvement in the fighting.[^1]

## Exile and Legal Proceedings

After losing power, Lissouba spent extended periods outside the country. He was later convicted in absentia on charges reported as including treason and embezzlement, which became part of the broader political and legal contestation following the civil war.[^1]

## Historical Significance

Lissouba's presidency is often referenced in discussions of post–Cold War democratization efforts in Central Africa and the fragility of new electoral institutions when confronted with armed political competition.[^1]
