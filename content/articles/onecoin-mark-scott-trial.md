## Context: OneCoin and related prosecutions

- **OneCoin** was marketed as a cryptocurrency but prosecutors described it as a large-scale fraud/Ponzi-style operation.
- US investigations also targeted **individual facilitators**, including lawyers and financial intermediaries accused of moving proceeds.
- The Mark Scott case is frequently cited as an example of how money laundering charges can attach to professionals who help route or disguise funds.

## What the trial centered on (high level)

- Allegations that Mark Scott helped **launder hundreds of millions of dollars** tied to OneCoin.
- Use of **investment funds and offshore structures** is commonly described in public summaries of the case.
- The prosecution narrative emphasized **concealment of source and ownership** of proceeds rather than the marketing of the scheme itself.

## Outcome and significance

- **Conviction (2019)** is widely reported in public references.
- The case helped establish a public record around:
  - How OneCoin funds allegedly moved through financial vehicles.
  - How prosecutors frame the role of professional services providers in large fraud cases.

## Key entities often mentioned alongside this case

- **Ruja Ignatova** (OneCoin founder; widely reported as missing / fugitive).
- **Konstantin Ignatov** (associated with OneCoin; publicly reported to have pleaded guilty in the US).
- US DOJ and SDNY press materials are often referenced in secondary reporting about OneCoin-related cases.
