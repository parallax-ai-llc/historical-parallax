## Summary

British ISIS recruiter known as the 'White Widow of ISIS' who traveled to Syria with her young son. She was killed in a US drone strike near the Iraq-Syria border.

## Background

Sally‑Anne Jones was a British national reported to have traveled to Syria and joined the Islamic State (ISIS). She became known in media coverage for online propaganda and recruitment efforts, particularly messaging aimed at encouraging travel and participation in ISIS activities.

## Role in Propaganda and Recruitment

Her notoriety reflected how ISIS leveraged social platforms to reach international audiences. Public accounts describe her as participating in English-language messaging and using personal narratives to lend credibility to recruitment appeals, a pattern widely documented in analyses of ISIS’s media strategy during the mid-2010s.

## Death and Aftermath

She was reported killed in a U.S. strike in 2017. Her case is often cited in discussions of foreign fighter flows, online radicalization, and the challenges governments faced in monitoring and countering extremist content originating from conflict zones.
