## Summary

Half-brother of North Korean leader Kim Jong-un who was assassinated at Kuala Lumpur airport with VX nerve agent. Two women smeared the agent on his face in a brazen public assassination.

## Incident Overview

The assassination occurred in February 2017 at **Kuala Lumpur International Airport (KLIA2)**. Kim Jong-nam collapsed shortly after being approached in public and later died. Malaysian authorities identified the toxic agent as **VX**, a highly lethal nerve agent that is also covered under international chemical weapons controls.

## Perpetrators and Modus Operandi (High-Level)

Reporting and court proceedings described two women who approached Kim and applied substances to his face in quick succession, after which they left the area. The case drew attention because:

- the attack was conducted in a crowded, heavily surveilled international airport,
- the agent involved was a weapon of mass destruction–associated chemical,
- the operation appeared to involve additional logistical support beyond the two on-camera assailants.

## Investigation and Legal Proceedings

Malaysian police arrested multiple suspects and pursued additional individuals believed to have left Malaysia shortly after the incident. The two women were charged in Malaysia; their cases evolved over time amid diplomatic scrutiny and questions about intent and deception (including claims that they believed they were participating in a prank or television segment).

## Diplomatic and Security Impact

The assassination triggered a significant diplomatic rift between **Malaysia and North Korea**, including disputes over the handling of Kim's remains and broader concerns about North Korean clandestine operations abroad. It also raised renewed international attention on the risks of chemical agent use in civilian public spaces.

## Historical Significance

The killing of a high-profile member of the North Korean ruling family abroad is frequently cited in analyses of North Korean internal politics, succession dynamics, and the external reach of state-linked security services.
