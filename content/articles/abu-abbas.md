## Summary

Palestinian militant who masterminded the 1985 Achille Lauro hijacking. He was captured by US forces in Iraq in 2003 and died in custody.

## Additional Context

## Organizational Affiliation

Abu Abbas (commonly identified as Muhammad Zaidan) was associated with the Palestine Liberation Front (PLF), a faction linked historically to the broader Palestinian political-militant landscape. He was known internationally primarily because of the Achille Lauro incident.

## Achille Lauro Hijacking (1985)

The hijacking of the Italian cruise ship *Achille Lauro* became a major international crisis. The incident is widely remembered for the killing of passenger Leon Klinghoffer, which drew global condemnation and long-running legal and diplomatic consequences.

## International Pursuit and Legal Issues

Following the hijacking, Abu Abbas was sought by multiple governments. Public accounts often describe periods in which he lived or traveled under shifting political protection and amid disputes over jurisdiction, extradition, and the handling of terrorism-related cases.

## Capture in Iraq and Death

He was captured by U.S. forces in Iraq in 2003. In 2004 he died while in custody; reporting and official statements discussed medical causes, while the circumstances remained the subject of public attention.
