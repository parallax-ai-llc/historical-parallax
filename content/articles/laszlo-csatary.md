## Summary

Hungarian war criminal accused of complicity in the deaths of 15,700 Jews deported from Košice.

## Background

- Csatáry was a Hungarian police officer active during World War II.
- He was associated with Košice (Kassa), a city that was under Hungarian administration during part of the war.

## Allegations and Legal Proceedings

- He was accused of involvement in the deportation of Jews from Košice to Auschwitz in 1944.
- Allegations centered on his role in policing and the handling of detainees during roundup and deportation operations.
- A Czechoslovak court convicted him in absentia after the war (reported as a death sentence).
- Decades later, he was located living in Hungary after having emigrated and lived abroad.
- In the early 2010s, Hungarian authorities brought war-crimes-related charges against him.
- He died in 2013 before a full trial could be concluded.
