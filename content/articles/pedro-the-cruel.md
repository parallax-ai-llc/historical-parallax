## Summary

King of Castile known for his cruelty who murdered his wife, brother, and numerous nobles. He was overthrown and killed by his half-brother Henry of Trastámara.

## Reputation and contested narratives

Pedro I of Castile is remembered under sharply divergent epithets: "the Cruel" versus "the Just." The split reflects medieval factional propaganda as well as genuine disputes over his methods of rule, including the use of executions, confiscations, and personal violence against rivals.

## Political environment

His reign unfolded amid dynastic conflict, noble factionalism, and shifting alliances with neighboring kingdoms. Royal authority in Castile depended heavily on managing powerful aristocratic houses, and periods of civil conflict could quickly become personalized vendettas.

## Violence and court politics

Accounts commonly emphasize a pattern of:

- Severe punishment for perceived betrayal.
- Targeting of political competitors and their supporters.
- Marital and succession disputes that became intertwined with power struggles.

## Downfall

The war that ended his rule is often framed as both a dynastic contest and a broader struggle over the distribution of power between crown and nobility. His death at the hands of his half-brother symbolized the triumph of a rival line and helped shape later narratives about legitimacy.

## Legacy

Pedro's story endures as a cautionary tale about personal rule in a fragmented political order: brutality can produce short-term compliance but also create coalitions of enemies who are willing to replace the sovereign entirely.
