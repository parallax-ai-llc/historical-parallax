## Summary

Swami Dayananda Saraswati was an Indian Hindu reformer who founded the Arya Samaj, a movement promoting a return to the Vedas and opposing idol worship, caste discrimination, and superstition. He was one of the first modern Hindu leaders to advocate for women's education and against child marriage. His book Satyarth Prakash (Light of Truth) presented his vision of reformed Hinduism. He influenced the Indian independence movement and Hindu reform.
