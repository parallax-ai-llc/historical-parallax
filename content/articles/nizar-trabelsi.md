## Background

- Nizar ben Abdelaziz Trabelsi (born 2 July 1970) is a Tunisian former professional footballer.
- He is often described as having played as an attacking midfielder.
- Public reporting frequently frames his later notoriety around terrorism-related prosecutions in Belgium and subsequent proceedings in the United States.

## Football Career (high level)

According to Wikipedia, Trabelsi began his career with CS Sfaxien in Tunisia before moving to Germany.

Notable clubs listed in public sources include:

- CS Sfaxien
- Fortuna Düsseldorf (including a reserve side stint)
- Wuppertaler SV (Borussia Wuppertal)
- Amateur clubs including 1. FC Wülfrath, SV 09/35 Wermelskirchen, and VfR Neuss

## Arrest and Allegations

Key points commonly cited in summaries of the case:

- He was arrested in Belgium on 13 September 2001 (in Uccle, near Brussels).
- Belgian prosecutors connected him to al-Qaeda-related networks and to an alleged plan targeting Kleine Brogel Air Base in Belgium.
- Some reports also mention allegations relating to other disrupted plots in the early 2000s; details vary by source and jurisdiction.

## Legal Proceedings and Custody

- In 2003, a Belgian court convicted Trabelsi and sentenced him to a 10-year prison term in connection with terrorism-related offenses (including the Kleine Brogel Air Base plot).
- After completing his Belgian sentence, Belgium extradited him to the United States on 3 October 2013 for additional prosecution.
- In 2014, the European Court of Human Rights ruled that Belgium’s extradition violated Article 3 of the European Convention on Human Rights and awarded damages.
- In July 2023, a U.S. federal jury found him not guilty on the charges tried in the United States.
- Public reporting indicates he remained in custody under U.S. immigration detention after the acquittal and was later returned to Belgium in 2025, where he was released.
