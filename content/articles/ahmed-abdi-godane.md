## Summary

Somali terrorist who led al-Shabaab and ordered the 2013 Westgate shopping mall attack in Nairobi that killed 67 people. Under his leadership, al-Shabaab pledged allegiance to al-Qaeda. Killed by US airstrike.

## Background

Ahmed Abdi Godane (also known as Mukhtar Abu Zubair in some sources) was a senior leader within al-Shabaab, the Islamist militant group that emerged from the Islamic Courts Union era in Somalia. He rose within the organization during a period of insurgency against Somalia’s transitional authorities and regional forces.

## Leadership of al-Shabaab

Under Godane’s leadership, al-Shabaab consolidated parts of its command structure and expanded its external operations narrative. Public reporting has described internal disputes and factional conflict within the group, including crackdowns on rivals and suspected dissent.

In 2012, al-Shabaab publicly pledged allegiance (bay‘a) to al-Qaeda, tying the group more explicitly to the global jihadist network.

## Attacks and Regional Impact

Godane was linked in public sources to major attacks, including the 2013 Westgate shopping mall attack in Nairobi. The group’s operations contributed to regional security concerns across Somalia and neighboring countries, particularly Kenya and Uganda.

## Targeting and Death

Godane was the subject of international counterterrorism efforts, including designations and reward programs reported by governments. He was killed in a U.S. airstrike in Somalia in 2014, after which al-Shabaab announced a successor.
