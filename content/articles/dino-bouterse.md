## Summary

Son of Surinamese president Desi Bouterse convicted of cocaine trafficking and providing support to Hezbollah. He was sentenced to over 16 years in US prison.

## Background

Dino Bouterse is a Surinamese national who became internationally known through U.S. federal prosecutions connected to narcotics trafficking and terrorism-related allegations. He is also notable due to his family relationship to Desi Bouterse, a dominant figure in Suriname's modern political history.

## Arrest and U.S. Prosecution

Public reporting on the case commonly describes an international law-enforcement operation leading to his arrest in Panama and transfer to U.S. custody. The prosecution emphasized alleged plans and agreements involving:

- Facilitating cocaine shipments and using Suriname-linked routes.
- Providing material or operational support to designated militant groups.
- Access to official or quasi-official roles that could enable logistics and protection.

## Sentencing and Aftermath

After conviction and sentencing in the United States, the case was frequently referenced in discussions of transnational crime in the Caribbean and northern South America, including the intersection between drug trafficking networks, state capacity, and politically exposed persons.

## Significance

The Dino Bouterse case is often used as an example of how U.S. authorities pursue extraterritorial narcotics and terrorism-related cases through undercover operations, international arrest coordination, and federal court proceedings.
