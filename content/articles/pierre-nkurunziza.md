## Summary

Burundian president who sparked a political crisis in 2015 by running for a disputed third term. His security forces killed over 1,000 people and displaced 400,000 in the ensuing crackdown. Died suddenly of cardiac arrest.

## Additional Context

### Background and Rise to Power

Nkurunziza emerged from Burundi's civil-war era and became a leading figure in the CNDD-FDD, a former rebel movement that transitioned into a dominant political party. His ascent to the presidency in 2005 is often situated within the post-conflict settlement frameworks intended to stabilize Burundi's institutions.

### Governance and Party-State Consolidation

Over his tenure, reporting described increasing concentration of power around the presidency and ruling party, along with heightened politicization of security institutions. Critics and international organizations raised concerns about civil liberties, political pluralism, and accountability for abuses.

### 2015 Constitutional Dispute

The 2015 crisis centered on whether a third presidential term was permitted under Burundi's constitutional arrangements and the Arusha peace framework. The resulting unrest, attempted coup, and subsequent crackdown drove large-scale displacement and strained relations with regional and Western partners.

### International Response

The crisis triggered sustained international scrutiny, including UN reporting, sanctions and diplomatic pressure by some governments, and debates about peacekeeping or protection mechanisms. The episode is frequently referenced as a case study in post-conflict constitutionalism and term-limit politics.

### Final Years and Succession

Nkurunziza indicated he would not seek another term in 2020, though he remained influential within the ruling coalition. After his death in 2020, Burundi's leadership transition proceeded under his designated successor, with ongoing debate about continuity versus change.
