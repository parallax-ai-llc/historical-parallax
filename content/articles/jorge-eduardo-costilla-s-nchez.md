## Summary

Known as 'El Coss,' successor to Osiel Cárdenas as Gulf Cartel leader. His arrest destabilized the cartel and intensified factional violence in northeastern Mexico.

## Profile

- Costilla Sánchez, commonly known by the nickname “El Coss,” was identified by authorities as a senior figure within the Gulf Cartel, a major Mexican organized-crime group.
- Reporting and government statements describe him as part of a leadership layer that operated amid intense competition with rival organizations, including groups associated with Los Zetas.

## Role in the Gulf Cartel

- After the imprisonment of earlier leaders, Costilla Sánchez was widely described as a successor within the cartel’s hierarchy.
- His period of prominence overlapped with fragmentation and internal disputes that affected control of trafficking routes and local power in northeastern Mexico.

## Capture and legal proceedings

- Mexican security forces captured Costilla Sánchez in 2012 in Tamaulipas state.
- Following his arrest, Mexican and U.S. authorities publicly linked him to drug-trafficking allegations; cross-border cooperation on extradition and prosecutions has been a recurring feature of Gulf Cartel cases.

## Broader context

- The Gulf Cartel’s evolution illustrates how arrests of top figures can contribute to splintering, succession conflicts, and localized spikes in violence.
- The organization’s activity is often discussed in the context of Mexico’s broader security challenges, including corruption risks and the militarization of counter-narcotics operations.
