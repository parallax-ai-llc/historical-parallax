## Summary

Palestinian militant leader who founded the PFLP-General Command and orchestrated numerous attacks against Israel. He was accused of the 1970 Swissair bombing and various cross-border raids.

## Background

Ahmed Jibril was a Palestinian militant and political figure who became known for leading a factional organization within the broader Palestinian movement. He is commonly described as having had a long-term base in Syria and close ties with Syrian authorities.

## PFLP–General Command (PFLP-GC)

Jibril founded the Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine – General Command (PFLP-GC) after splitting from the PFLP. The group was often characterized as emphasizing military operations and maintaining an independent command structure compared with other factions.

## Notable Incidents and Allegations

Public reporting and historical accounts have linked Jibril and the PFLP-GC to multiple attacks and plots over decades, including airline-related incidents and cross-border operations. Some attributions are framed as allegations, reflecting the contested and politicized nature of militant-violence reporting.

## Prisoner Exchanges and Political Role

Jibril’s name is also associated with prisoner exchange negotiations, including the mid-1980s exchange commonly referred to as the “Jibril Agreement.” Such exchanges have been politically significant in the Israeli–Palestinian conflict, affecting domestic politics and armed-group legitimacy.

## Later Years

He remained a prominent factional leader into the 2000s and 2010s, with continued influence from Damascus. Jibril died in 2021.

## Notes

- The PFLP-GC is distinct from the PFLP despite similar naming and overlapping historical origins.
