## Summary

Prominent Laotian civil society leader and recipient of the Ramon Magsaysay Award who was abducted at a police checkpoint in Vientiane in 2012 and never seen again. His enforced disappearance is believed to be state-sponsored.

## Public work

Somphone was known for community development initiatives and youth leadership programs in Laos. He helped build networks that connected rural development, education, and civil society activity, and became a prominent figure in the country’s limited non-governmental sector.

## Ramon Magsaysay Award

In 2005, he received the Ramon Magsaysay Award for Community Leadership, an honor often described as Asia’s equivalent of the Nobel Prize. The award recognized a long-term record of development work and civic engagement.

## Disappearance (2012)

Somphone was last seen on 15 December 2012 in Vientiane, where he was stopped at a police checkpoint and taken away in an incident captured on CCTV. The publicly available footage became central to international calls for a transparent investigation.

## International response

Human rights organizations and several foreign governments repeatedly urged Laos to investigate the case and clarify Somphone’s fate and whereabouts. The case is frequently referenced in discussions of enforced disappearance and the risks faced by civil society leaders in restrictive political environments.

## Continuing significance

Somphone’s disappearance remains a notable unresolved case in Southeast Asia and is often cited by advocates pressing for accountability mechanisms, protection of activists, and access to information for families of the disappeared.
