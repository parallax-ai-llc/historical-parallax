## Summary

Moroccan serial killer who murdered at least 36 people in Casablanca during the 1990s. He targeted homeless individuals sleeping on the streets.

## Additional Context

### Modus operandi and victimization

Accounts of the case describe a pattern of attacks against highly vulnerable people, particularly individuals experiencing homelessness who were sleeping outdoors. Crimes against marginalized victims can be under-reported and under-investigated, which is one reason such cases sometimes accumulate large numbers of victims before authorities identify a suspect.

### Investigation and arrest

Serial crime investigations typically combine witness reports, patrol observations, and forensic work to link incidents that initially appear unconnected. In this case, Moroccan authorities ultimately arrested Abdelali Hadi and attributed multiple killings to him.

### Trial and sentencing

Hadi was convicted and sentenced to death. Morocco retains the death penalty in law, though executions have historically been rare for extended periods; as a result, death sentences can function in practice as long-term incarceration while still carrying significant political and social symbolism.

### Broader significance

The case is frequently cited in discussions of:

- Public safety for people living on the streets
- The social invisibility of homeless victims
- The challenges of detecting repeat-offender violence early
