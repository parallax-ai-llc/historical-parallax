## Summary

Guru Tegh Bahadur was the ninth Sikh Guru who was executed by the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb for defending the right of Kashmiri Hindus to practice their faith. He is known as 'Hind di Chadar' (Shield of India) for his sacrifice. His hymns on detachment and divine devotion are included in the Guru Granth Sahib. His martyrdom directly led to the militarization of the Sikh community under his son Guru Gobind Singh.
