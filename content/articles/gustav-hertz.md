## Summary

Gustav Hertz was a German experimental physicist who shared the 1925 Nobel Prize in Physics with James Franck for the Franck-Hertz experiment demonstrating energy quantization in atoms. He was a nephew of Heinrich Hertz. After WWII, he worked on isotope separation in the Soviet nuclear program before returning to East Germany.
