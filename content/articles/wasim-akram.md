## Summary

Wasim Akram is the greatest fast bowler in limited-overs cricket with 502 ODI wickets, an all-time record. He was famous for his lethal reverse swing.

## Playing style

Akram is widely associated with left-arm fast bowling, particularly in one-day international cricket. He was known for combining pace with late swing, including reverse swing, and for variation in length and angle that made him effective with both the new and older ball.

Notable attributes frequently cited include:

- Left-arm angle that created different lines of attack compared to right-arm fast bowlers.
- Ability to swing the ball conventionally and to generate reverse swing.
- Strong performance in both opening spells and death-overs bowling in limited-overs formats.

## Leadership and major tournaments

Akram captained Pakistan and played in multiple World Cups, with the 1992 tournament being a defining moment for Pakistan cricket. His contributions in that era are part of why he is treated as a foundational figure for later generations of Pakistani fast bowlers.

## Records and recognition

In addition to wicket tallies, he is often mentioned in all-time lists of ODI bowlers and as a key figure in the modernization of fast bowling tactics in limited-overs play.

## Post-retirement work

After retirement, he remained active as a commentator and cricket analyst, and has been involved in coaching or advisory roles in different contexts. His public profile has continued through media work and cricket-related appearances.

## Legacy

Akram's legacy is frequently described in terms of influence: his style and success helped establish a model for Pakistani pace bowling, and his technical skill with swing remains a reference point for players and coaches.
