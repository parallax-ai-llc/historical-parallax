## Summary

Suicide car bombing by Jaish-e-Mohammed that killed 40 Indian CRPF personnel in Pulwama, Kashmir on February 14, 2019. It was the deadliest attack on Indian security forces in Kashmir.

## Background

The Pulwama attack occurred amid a long-running insurgency and counterinsurgency in Jammu and Kashmir, where militant groups, security forces, and civilians have been involved in recurring cycles of violence. The region’s disputed status and heavy security presence form the broader context for understanding the event.

## Attack and immediate response

Public reporting describes a vehicle-borne explosive attack on a convoy of India’s Central Reserve Police Force (CRPF). The incident prompted a rapid security response, heightened alert levels, and an intense national debate about intelligence failures and force protection.

## Diplomatic and military escalation

In the weeks after the bombing, India and Pakistan experienced a sharp rise in tensions, including cross-border claims and counterclaims and a brief period of military escalation. The episode is frequently cited as a major crisis point between the two nuclear-armed neighbors.

## Domestic political impact

The attack influenced public discourse inside India on terrorism, national security, and policy toward Kashmir. It also became a prominent reference point in political messaging and media coverage during that period.

## Ongoing significance

Pulwama remains a key case study in discussions of militant violence in Kashmir, crisis management between India and Pakistan, and the challenges of preventing high-casualty attacks on security convoys.
