## Summary

Canadian filmmaker who murdered a man imitating the TV show Dexter.

## Additional Context

### Background

- Mark Andrew Twitchell (born July 4, 1979) is from Edmonton, Alberta, Canada.
- He studied Radio and Television Arts at the Northern Alberta Institute of Technology (NAIT) and pursued work as an aspiring filmmaker.

### Filmmaking Work (pre-crime)

- He was associated with a production company called Xpress Entertainment.
- He worked on a Star Wars fan-film project, *Star Wars: Secrets of the Rebellion* (reported as unreleased).
- In 2008 he shot a short horror film project at a rented garage in Edmonton.

### Murder of John Brian Altinger (2008)

- In October 2008, John Brian Altinger (38) disappeared after arranging a date.
- Reporting describes Twitchell posing online on the dating site Plenty of Fish to lure victims to the rented garage.
- Altinger was killed at the garage (reported as bludgeoning and stabbing).
- The killing drew attention because it was alleged to have been influenced by the TV series *Dexter* and its protagonist Dexter Morgan.

### Aftermath, Investigation, and Trial

- Reporting describes emails sent from Altinger’s accounts after his disappearance, raising suspicions among friends.
- Twitchell was arrested as the investigation focused on the garage and his communications.
- The case proceeded to trial in Alberta; the proceedings were widely covered in Canada.

### Conviction and Sentence

- In April 2011, Twitchell was convicted of first-degree murder.
- He was sentenced to life imprisonment, with parole ineligibility set at 25 years (as commonly applied to first-degree murder in Canada).

### Why the Case Was Notable

- The case became a high-profile example of a homicide facilitated by online dating and luring.
- Media attention focused on the alleged attempt to reenact fictional tropes (particularly *Dexter*).
