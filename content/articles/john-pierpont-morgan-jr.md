## Summary

J.P. Morgan Jr., known as 'Jack,' succeeded his father as head of J.P. Morgan & Co. and was one of the most powerful bankers during World War I and the interwar period. He organized massive loans to Britain and France during WWI and was a target of a 1920 bombing on Wall Street. He navigated the firm through the 1929 crash and the subsequent banking reforms of the New Deal era.
