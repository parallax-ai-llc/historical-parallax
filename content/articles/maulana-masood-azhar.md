## Summary

Founder of Jaish-e-Mohammed who was released from an Indian prison after militants hijacked Indian Airlines Flight 814 to Kandahar. His organization has carried out multiple major attacks against India.

## Background

Maulana Masood Azhar is a Pakistani cleric associated with militant Islamist politics in South Asia. He became internationally prominent after his release from an Indian prison as part of negotiations following the hijacking of Indian Airlines Flight 814.

## Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM)

JeM is a Pakistan-based militant organization that has been blamed by Indian authorities and other governments for major attacks in India. It has been linked in public reporting to high-profile incidents over multiple decades, and it has faced international sanctions and terrorist designations.

## International Designations and Pressure

Azhar’s designation as a global terrorist in 2019 followed years of diplomatic efforts and disputes at international forums. The designation reflects a broad international assessment of JeM’s role in regional militancy and the risks of escalation between nuclear-armed states.

## Regional Impact

The organization and its leadership have been cited in discussions of:

- Cross-border militancy and insurgency dynamics between India and Pakistan.
- The use of suicide attacks and mass-casualty tactics in the region.
- The political constraints involved in dismantling or curbing militant networks.

## Further Notes

Attribution for specific attacks is politically sensitive and often disputed, but JeM’s name appears repeatedly in Indian charge sheets, diplomatic statements, and international terrorism reporting.
