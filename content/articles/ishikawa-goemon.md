## Summary

Legendary Japanese outlaw boiled alive for attempting to assassinate Toyotomi Hideyoshi. Became folk hero depicted as Japanese Robin Hood.

## Historical Context

- Ishikawa Goemon is commonly situated in Japan's late Sengoku period, a time associated with political consolidation and frequent conflict among competing leaders.
- Accounts of Goemon blend historical fragments with later folklore, so many details are presented as legend rather than fully verifiable biography.

## In Popular Culture

- He is often portrayed as a thief who steals from elites and aids the poor, a narrative that resembles "Robin Hood" archetypes.
- The execution-by-cauldron story is one of the most repeated elements of the legend and central to his enduring notoriety.

## Interpretation Notes

- When cited historically, Goemon is frequently used as a case study in how outlaw figures are mythologized in national storytelling.
- Dramatic retellings (theatre, film, manga, games) may diverge from encyclopedia-style summaries.
