## Summary

Ottoman special operations agent who carried out political assassinations for the Committee of Union and Progress. Eventually executed by his own government.

## Historical Context

- **Late Ottoman politics:** Yakub Cemil is discussed in sources within the turbulent period of Committee of Union and Progress (CUP) dominance and coup politics.
- **Security apparatus:** He is often portrayed as a figure connected to covert or force-based political action rather than conventional governance.

## Notable Actions (high level)

- **1913 power shift:** Accounts link him to violent events around the 1913 CUP coup, including the killing of senior officials during the takeover.
- **Role within CUP:** He is described as an operative used for intimidation and enforcement during intense factional struggle.

## Downfall

- **Insubordination / plotting:** Reporting describes a later break with leadership and alleged involvement in conspiracies.
- **Execution:** He was executed in 1916, reflecting how revolutionary factions sometimes purged even loyal enforcers.

## Interpretation
