## Summary

Nellie Bly was an American journalist who set a world record by traveling around the world in 72 days in 1889-1890, beating the fictional Phileas Fogg's 80-day record.

## Additional Context

- Bly (born Elizabeth Jane Cochran) became famous for investigative reporting and undercover work.
- Her 1887 asylum investigation is often cited as an early, influential example of undercover reporting used to expose institutional abuse.
- The 1889–1890 trip around the world was a highly publicized media event and a notable moment in late-19th-century mass-circulation journalism.

## Why She Matters

- Demonstrated how firsthand reporting and narrative pacing could mobilize public attention.
- Expanded the perceived scope of what women reporters could do in major newsrooms of the era.
- Helped popularize travel writing as a competitive, headline-driven form of reporting.
