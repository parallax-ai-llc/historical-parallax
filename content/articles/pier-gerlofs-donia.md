## Summary

Frisian pirate and rebel leader known as 'Grutte Pier' who fought against the Habsburgs. Famous for reputedly beheading multiple men with a single sword stroke.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1515 | Began pirate rebellion |
| 1520 | Died, possibly poisoned |

## Historical Setting

- Early 16th-century Frisia was affected by regional power struggles involving local factions and expanding Habsburg influence.
- Maritime raiding in the North Sea and Zuiderzee was intertwined with politics, privateering, and revenge cycles between rival groups.

## Rebel Activity and Reputation

- Donia became associated with armed resistance and seaborne raids against opponents and occupying forces.
- Accounts emphasize his role as a charismatic symbol of Frisian defiance, combining local grievance with broader anti-Habsburg sentiment.
- The nickname “Grutte Pier” ("Big Pier") reflects later memory as much as contemporary documentation.

## Legend vs. Record

- Many popular stories focus on extraordinary physical strength and a massive sword.
- Some claims (such as dramatic feats of beheading multiple men in one strike) function as folklore and are hard to verify with primary sources.
- Even when embellished, the legend illustrates how rebels and pirates can become cultural icons long after the underlying conflict ends.

## Legacy

- Donia is remembered in Frisian cultural memory as a figure of resistance.
- His story is often used to discuss how regional identities are shaped through mythmaking, songs, and commemorations.
