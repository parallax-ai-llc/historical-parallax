## Summary

Fumihiko Maki is a Japanese architect who won the 1993 Pritzker Prize for his synthesis of Eastern and Western architectural philosophies. His engineering innovations in metallic surfaces and lightweight structures advanced architectural technology. He designed the 4 World Trade Center in New York and the Tokyo Metropolitan Gymnasium.
