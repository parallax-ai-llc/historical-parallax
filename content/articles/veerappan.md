## Summary

Indian bandit and ivory smuggler who terrorized Karnataka and Tamil Nadu forests for three decades. Killed 184 people.

## Operations and Environment

Veerappan operated primarily in forested border regions of the Indian states of Karnataka and Tamil Nadu. Accounts of his criminal enterprise commonly mention poaching and smuggling networks, and note that rugged terrain and local knowledge made sustained pursuit difficult for law enforcement.

## Manhunts and State Response

His activities led to long-running, multi-agency efforts to capture him, including specialized police units and extensive search operations. The costs and political pressures associated with these operations became a recurring theme in public discussion, particularly when high-profile incidents drew national attention.

## Kidnapping of Rajkumar (Public Significance)

The 2000 kidnapping of popular Kannada film star Rajkumar is often treated as a turning point in how widely the case was followed beyond the immediate region. Reporting on the episode typically highlights the role of negotiations and the broader public anxiety generated by the abduction.

## Death and Aftermath

Veerappan was killed in a 2004 police operation that has been described as an ambush. After his death, debate continued about the effectiveness of policy responses to illegal wildlife trade, as well as the social conditions that can enable long-term outlaw figures to persist.

## Cultural Impact

His life and the pursuit of him have been portrayed in books, documentaries, and films, and he remains a reference point in discussions of organized crime connected to environmental exploitation.
