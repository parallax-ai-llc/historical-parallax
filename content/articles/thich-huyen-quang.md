## Summary

Thich Huyen Quang was a Vietnamese Buddhist monk who served as the Supreme Patriarch of the Unified Buddhist Church of Vietnam, a position he held while under house arrest by the Vietnamese government for decades. He was a prominent dissident who repeatedly called for religious freedom in Vietnam. He spent over 26 years under house arrest or detention for his opposition to government control of religion.
