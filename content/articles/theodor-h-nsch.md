## Summary

Theodor Hänsch is a German physicist who shared the 2005 Nobel Prize for contributions to laser-based precision spectroscopy.

## Scientific contributions (high level)

Hänsch is best known for advancing tools that allow extremely precise measurements of light and atomic transitions.

- **Laser spectroscopy:** Techniques that use lasers to probe the energy levels of atoms and molecules with very fine resolution.
- **Optical frequency combs:** A method to create a spectrum of evenly spaced “teeth” (frequencies) that can link optical frequencies to microwave standards, enabling precise counting and comparison of optical frequencies.

These developments strengthened the connection between fundamental physics, metrology (the science of measurement), and practical instrumentation.

## Nobel Prize context

The 2005 Nobel Prize in Physics recognized contributions that enabled **precision spectroscopy** and frequency-comb technology. This work underpins:

- improved optical atomic clocks,
- tests of fundamental constants,
- high-resolution measurements in atomic and molecular physics.

## Institutional roles

Hänsch has been associated with major German research institutions, including leadership and senior research roles that helped shape programs in quantum optics and precision measurement.

## Legacy

Frequency-comb technology became a standard tool across physics and related fields, influencing timekeeping, calibration of spectrometers, and experiments requiring accurate optical referencing.
