## Summary

FBI and US Marshals siege of Randy Weaver's Idaho cabin resulted in deaths of Weaver's wife, 14-year-old son, and a US Marshal. The incident fueled the militia movement.

## Background

- **Location:** Ruby Ridge, near Naples, Boundary County, Idaho.
- **Central figure:** Randy Weaver, a former U.S. Army Green Beret who moved his family to a remote cabin.
- **Triggering charges:** A federal indictment related to an alleged sale of illegally shortened shotguns to an ATF informant; Weaver failed to appear in court, leading to a bench warrant.

## 1992 Standoff (High-Level Sequence)

- **Reconnaissance and initial firefight (Aug 1992):** U.S. Marshals conducting surveillance encountered Weaver's family and a friend; a gunfight resulted in deaths on both sides.
- **FBI Hostage Rescue Team deployment:** After the initial incident, the FBI took command of the operation.
- **Rules of engagement controversy:** The FBI issued aggressive rules that later became a focal point of criticism and investigation.
- **Siege duration:** The confrontation lasted roughly **11 days**.

## Casualties

- **Samuel Weaver (14):** Killed during the initial firefight.
- **Vicki Weaver:** Killed by an FBI sniper shot while inside/near the cabin doorway area (circumstances were heavily contested).
- **Deputy U.S. Marshal William Degan:** Killed in the initial firefight.

## Legal Aftermath

- **Federal trial outcomes:** Randy Weaver was acquitted of the most serious charges and convicted on lesser offenses related to failing to appear in court and violating bail conditions (details vary by charge framing).
- **Civil settlements:** The Weaver family later received a federal settlement; additional settlements were reached with other parties involved.

## Investigations and Policy Impact

- **Government reviews:** Multiple inquiries examined the conduct of federal agencies, including the FBI's rules of engagement and command decisions.
- **Operational reforms:** Ruby Ridge is commonly cited alongside the 1993 Waco siege as a catalyst for changes in federal law-enforcement policies on use of force, negotiation, and command oversight.

## Historical Significance

- **Public trust and extremism narratives:** The event became a prominent symbol in debates over federal authority, civil liberties, and law-enforcement accountability.
- **Militia movement:** It is widely referenced as a galvanizing episode for segments of the U.S. militia movement in the 1990s.
