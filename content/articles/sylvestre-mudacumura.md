## Summary

Rwandan military commander who led the FDLR rebel group in eastern Congo and was wanted by the ICC for nine counts of war crimes. He was killed by Congolese military forces after evading capture for years.

## Background: FDLR in Eastern DRC

After the 1994 genocide in Rwanda, various armed elements fled into what is now the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC). Over time, these networks evolved into insurgent formations, including the Democratic Forces for the Liberation of Rwanda (FDLR). The FDLR became a persistent security problem in eastern DRC, contributing to cycles of violence involving local communities, other armed groups, and state forces.

## Command Role

Mudacumura is commonly described in open sources as the FDLR’s military commander. In that role, he was linked to operational decisions and to the conduct of fighters in areas of North and South Kivu, where conflict dynamics have included attacks on civilians, forced displacement, and predatory taxation.

## ICC Arrest Warrant (2012)

The International Criminal Court (ICC) issued an arrest warrant alleging war crimes in eastern DRC. Public summaries often describe the accusations as involving responsibility for acts such as:

- Attacks directed against civilians
- Murder and inhumane treatment
- Destruction of property and pillage

The ICC warrant framed the allegations around command responsibility and the organization of armed operations.

## Evasion and Regional Operations

For years after the warrant, Mudacumura was reported to have avoided capture despite military operations and international pressure. The difficulty of arrest operations in eastern DRC is frequently attributed to terrain, fragmented armed-group networks, and fluctuating alliances.

## Death in 2019

Mudacumura was reported killed in 2019 by Congolese security forces during an operation in South Kivu. His death was widely noted because it effectively ended the ICC case against him without a trial, while leaving broader accountability questions around armed-group violence in the region.
