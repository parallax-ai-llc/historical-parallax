## Summary

Peter Debye was a Dutch-American physicist and physical chemist who won the 1936 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his contributions to understanding molecular structure through X-ray diffraction and dipole moments. The Debye model of heat capacity and the Debye-Hückel theory of electrolyte solutions are named after him.
