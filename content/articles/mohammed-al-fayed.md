## Summary

Mohamed Al-Fayed was an Egyptian-born billionaire who owned Harrods department store in London, the Ritz Hotel in Paris, and Fulham F.C. He purchased Harrods in 1985 and transformed it into the world's most famous luxury department store. His son Dodi Al-Fayed died alongside Princess Diana in a Paris car crash in 1997. He sold Harrods to Qatar Holdings for £1.5 billion in 2010. After his death, numerous allegations of sexual abuse emerged.
