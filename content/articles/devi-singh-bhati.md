## Summary

One of the most feared dacoits of Rajasthan's Chambal region who terrorized the India-Pakistan border areas. He was killed in a police encounter after decades of dacoit operations.

## Additional Context

Dacoit activity in north and central India has historically been associated with rural banditry operating across difficult terrain and administrative boundaries. In Rajasthan and neighboring regions, organized gangs have been reported to exploit remote areas, border routes, and gaps in local policing.

In reporting about Devi Singh Bhati, sources generally describe:

- Operations conducted over a long period rather than a single incident.
- Mobility across districts and border-adjacent areas.
- A sustained law-enforcement focus culminating in an encounter-style operation.

## Law-Enforcement Response

Indian policing responses to dacoit networks typically include coordinated efforts across jurisdictions, the use of intelligence from local informants, and special operations to arrest or neutralize armed gang leaders. Encounter killings have also been a recurring and controversial feature of anti-dacoit campaigns in multiple states, raising broader questions about due process and the circumstances under which lethal force is used.

## Legacy and Public Memory

Individuals described as major dacoit figures often remain part of regional crime narratives long after their deaths, appearing in journalistic retrospectives and local accounts about security, policing, and the social conditions that allow rural banditry to persist.
