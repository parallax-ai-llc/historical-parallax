## Summary

Missy Franklin won four gold medals at the 2012 London Olympics at age 17, including the 100m and 200m backstroke.

## Additional Context

### Early Career

- Became a standout in U.S. swimming as a teenager, particularly in backstroke events.
- Established herself in international competition during the early 2010s.

### Olympic and World Competition

- Won multiple medals at the 2012 London Olympics, including individual backstroke golds.
- Also won medals at World Aquatics Championships and contributed to relay teams.

### Later Career and Retirement

- Continued through the Rio 2016 Olympic cycle.
- Retired from competitive swimming in the late 2010s after struggles that included injuries.

### Legacy

- Remembered as one of the defining U.S. swimmers of her peak period, with major Olympic success at a young age.
