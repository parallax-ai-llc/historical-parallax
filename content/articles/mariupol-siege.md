## Summary

Russian siege of Mariupol, Ukraine in 2022 that killed over 20,000 civilians. Russian forces bombed a maternity hospital and the Mariupol Drama Theatre where hundreds of civilians sheltered.

## Background

Mariupol is a strategic port city on the Sea of Azov. During Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine in 2022, the city became one of the earliest and most severe urban battlefields, with intense shelling and a rapid deterioration of humanitarian conditions.

## Humanitarian impact (high level)

- Large-scale damage to housing, utilities, and medical infrastructure.
- Long periods with limited access to electricity, water, heat, and communications.
- Civilian casualty reporting during the siege was difficult; estimates varied and were constrained by access and verification challenges.

## Key sites and symbols

- **Mariupol maternity hospital** bombing and the **Mariupol Drama Theatre** strike became widely reported internationally as emblematic of the threat to civilians during the battle.
- The **Azovstal steelworks** complex served as a final defensive position and sheltering area before the defenders' surrender and evacuation in May 2022.

## Aftermath and accountability

Following the city's capture, questions about civilian deaths, deportations, and treatment of prisoners became part of broader international investigations and debates. Multiple governments and international bodies have discussed potential war-crime allegations related to attacks on civilian infrastructure; legal determinations depend on evidence gathering and judicial proceedings.
