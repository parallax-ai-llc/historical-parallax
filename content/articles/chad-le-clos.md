## Summary

Chad le Clos upset Michael Phelps to win the 200m butterfly gold at the 2012 London Olympics.

## Background

- Chad le Clos is a South African swimmer from Durban who became internationally prominent in the early 2010s.
- He is best known for butterfly events, while also competing in freestyle at elite level.

## London 2012 Breakthrough

- The 2012 Olympic final in the men’s 200m butterfly became one of the signature races of that Games.
- Le Clos won gold ahead of Michael Phelps, who had dominated the event across the previous two Olympics.
- The victory was widely framed as a generational shift and one of South Africa’s standout Olympic moments of the period.

## Major Competitions and Medals (High-level)

- After 2012, le Clos remained a regular finalist and medal contender at World Championships and other major meets.
- His resume includes Olympic medals beyond London, reflecting sustained performance across multiple cycles.
- In addition to individual events, he has also been part of relay squads representing South Africa.

## Strengths as an Athlete

- Commentators often highlight le Clos’s finishing speed, race tactics, and capacity to handle high-pressure finals.
- His ability to contest both butterfly and freestyle contributed to meet-to-meet versatility.

## Later Career

- Like many elite swimmers, le Clos’s post-breakthrough years involved managing training loads, injuries, and the changing competitive landscape.
- His career is commonly used as an example of how a single iconic Olympic race can become an enduring reference point even as an athlete continues competing.

## Legacy

- Le Clos is frequently cited among South Africa’s most accomplished swimmers in the modern era.
- The London 2012 upset remains central to his public identity and to broader narratives about Phelps-era dominance meeting new challengers.
