## Summary

Mexican drug lord known as 'El Azul' (The Blue One) who was a key leader of the Sinaloa Cartel. He was known as a diplomat and mediator between rival cartels. Reported dead in 2014 but status unconfirmed.

## Additional Context

### Early Background and Law-Enforcement Ties

Open-source biographies often describe Esparragoza Moreno as having had early ties to Mexican federal security structures, including reported association with the now-defunct **Dirección Federal de Seguridad (DFS)**. This background is frequently cited as part of his reputation for operating through **relationships and negotiation** rather than public displays of violence.

### Guadalajara Cartel → Sinaloa Cartel Arc

Accounts commonly place him among figures connected to the **Guadalajara Cartel** era in the 1970s–1980s, and then describe a later transition into leadership roles across successor organizations as Mexico’s trafficking landscape fragmented. In this framing, "El Azul" is often characterized as a **connector** across groups, eventually aligning with and helping shape the **Sinaloa Cartel**.

### Nickname

The nickname **"El Azul"** ("The Blue One") is widely explained as referencing his **dark complexion**, sometimes described in sensationalized terms in popular reporting.

### Fugitive Status and Rewards

He has been described as a long-term **fugitive** and as a target of both Mexican and U.S. authorities, with rewards publicly offered for information leading to his capture.

### Unconfirmed Death Reports

Reports in 2014 claimed he died of a **heart attack**, but coverage has repeatedly emphasized that the claim was **not conclusively confirmed**, contributing to continuing uncertainty about his ultimate fate.
