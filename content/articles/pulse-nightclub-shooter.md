This page is kept as a compatibility stub for references that use the generic label “Pulse nightclub shooter.” The individual widely identified as the shooter in the 2016 Pulse nightclub attack is **Omar Mateen**.

## Identity / Naming

- **Common label:** “Pulse nightclub shooter”
- **Identified individual:** Omar Mir Seddique Mateen (commonly “Omar Mateen”)
- **Event association:** Pulse nightclub shooting (Orlando, Florida, 2016)

## Background (high-level)

Public reporting describes Mateen as a U.S.-born man raised in Florida who worked as a security guard. He was previously investigated by U.S. federal authorities and later closed out prior to the attack.

## Pulse nightclub attack (overview)

- **Date:** 2016-06-12
- **Location:** Pulse nightclub, Orlando, Florida, United States
- **Nature of incident:** Mass shooting and hostage situation
- **Outcome:** The attacker was killed during a law-enforcement response.

### Casualties (commonly reported)

- **Killed:** 49 victims (excluding the attacker)
- **Wounded:** 53 victims

## Aftermath and impact (high-level)

- The attack became a major national and international news event and was widely described as one of the deadliest mass shootings in modern U.S. history at the time.
- The incident prompted extensive debate and policy discussion around firearm access, domestic extremism, terrorism framing, and LGBTQ+ community safety.
- Vigils and memorials were held in many locations, and Pulse became a symbol within broader discussions of hate-motivated violence.
