## Summary

Bernard Hinault won the Tour de France five times and is the last French winner (1985). Known as 'The Badger,' he was one of cycling's fiercest competitors.

## Racing Style and Reputation

Hinault’s nickname, **“The Badger,”** reflects an image of relentless competitiveness. In historical cycling writing, he is often portrayed as a rider who combined:

- strong time-trial ability
- aggressive climbing
- a willingness to race for time gains even when defending a lead

## Major Results (High-Level)

Beyond his five Tour de France victories, Hinault’s palmarès is commonly summarized as including wins in other Grand Tours and major one-day races. This breadth is part of why he is regularly listed among the most accomplished riders of the modern era.

## Place in French Cycling

Hinault’s 1985 Tour win remains a reference point in French cycling culture because he is still the last Frenchman to win the Tour de France. Discussions about later French contenders often use Hinault as the benchmark for:

- tactical toughness in stage races
- expectations for leadership and team dynamics
- national hopes and media pressure

## Post-Retirement

After retiring from competition, Hinault remained a visible figure in cycling, including appearances connected to the Tour de France and commentary on the sport’s evolution.
