## Summary

Richard Sears co-founded Sears, Roebuck and Company, which became the largest retailer in the United States for much of the 20th century. He started by selling watches and expanded into a general merchandise mail-order business. His aggressive marketing and catalog innovations made consumer goods accessible to rural America. The Sears catalog became a cultural institution in American life.
