## Summary

British neonatal nurse convicted of murdering 7 babies and attempting to murder 6 more at the Countess of Chester Hospital between 2015 and 2016. She injected air, overfed milk, and poisoned with insulin. Sentenced to whole life order.

## Background

Letby worked as a neonatal nurse at the Countess of Chester Hospital in England. The case drew extensive attention because it involved unexplained collapses and deaths of premature and newborn babies in a hospital setting.

## Investigation

- Concerns were raised internally after a cluster of incidents on the neonatal unit.
- Police investigations focused on timelines, clinical records, and patterns across multiple events.
- The prosecution case relied heavily on circumstantial evidence and expert medical testimony, with the defense disputing interpretation of the medical evidence.

## Trials and convictions

Letby was tried in the UK criminal justice system and convicted of multiple counts relating to infant deaths and attempted murders. Sentencing included a whole-life order, meaning no ordinary prospect of release.
