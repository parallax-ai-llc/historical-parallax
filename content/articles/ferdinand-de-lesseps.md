## Summary

Ferdinand de Lesseps oversaw the construction of the Suez Canal, completed in 1869, connecting the Mediterranean and Red Seas and revolutionizing global trade. He later attempted the Panama Canal but the project failed due to engineering challenges and disease. The Suez Canal remains one of the most important waterways in the world.
