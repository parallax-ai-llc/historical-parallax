## Summary

Tadao Kashio was one of four brothers who founded Casio Computer Company. He invented the world's first compact all-electric calculator in 1957, which launched Casio into the electronics industry. Casio later became famous for its digital watches, electronic musical instruments, and cameras. The 14-A calculator was a breakthrough that replaced mechanical calculators with electronic ones, transforming office work worldwide.
