## Summary

Former mayor of Dakar convicted of embezzling city funds. His imprisonment was widely seen as politically motivated to prevent him from challenging President Macky Sall.

## Background

Khalifa Ababacar Sall is a Senegalese politician associated with the Socialist Party and long active in municipal politics in Dakar. His career rose during a period when Senegal's politics featured competitive elections but also intense contestation over the use of state institutions.

## Mayor of Dakar

Sall served as mayor of Dakar and built a political base around city governance and local patronage networks. Dakar's municipal budget and social-assistance programs made the mayoralty an influential platform.

## Embezzlement case and conviction

Senegalese authorities accused Sall and associates of misusing municipal funds, including money intended for local support programs. The case became a national political flashpoint: supporters argued it was part of a strategy to sideline a potential presidential contender, while the government presented it as a straightforward anti-corruption prosecution.

He was convicted and sentenced to prison, and later received a presidential pardon.

## Political impact

The prosecution and imprisonment affected opposition coalition-building and became part of wider debates about judicial independence, selective enforcement, and the boundaries between accountability and political competition in Senegal.
