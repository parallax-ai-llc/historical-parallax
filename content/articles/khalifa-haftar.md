## Summary

Libyan military commander who launched a civil war to take over Libya and whose forces are accused of extrajudicial killings and airstrikes on civilian targets. He commands the Libyan National Army in eastern Libya.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2014 | Launched Operation Dignity against Islamist militias |
| 2019 | Attacked Tripoli, triggering renewed civil war |
| 2020 | Ceasefire agreed |

## Additional Context

- Haftar is a prominent figure in the post-2011 Libyan conflict and is most closely associated with armed forces aligned with authorities in eastern Libya.
- He has been described in many reporting and diplomatic sources as seeking to unify Libya under a single military command, while opponents frame his campaigns as power consolidation.
- The Libyan National Army (LNA) is an umbrella label for a coalition of regular units, tribal forces, and allied armed groups rather than a single, standardized national institution.

## Notability

- Central actor in the broader Libyan Civil War and its shifting front lines.
- Internationally consequential because control of territory, oil infrastructure, and migration routes in Libya has regional spillover effects.

## Allegations and Controversies

- Multiple human rights organizations and international observers have accused LNA-affiliated forces of abuses, including extrajudicial killings and attacks impacting civilians.
- These allegations are part of wider documentation of abuses committed by multiple armed actors during Libya’s conflict.
