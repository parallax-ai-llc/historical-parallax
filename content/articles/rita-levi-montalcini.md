## Summary

Rita Levi-Montalcini was an Italian neurobiologist who shared the 1986 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for her discovery of nerve growth factor (NGF). She conducted research in her bedroom during World War II when banned from university as a Jew. Her discovery of NGF opened new fields of research in neuroscience and developmental biology. She became a senator for life in Italy and lived to 103.
