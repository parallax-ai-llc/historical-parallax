## Summary

Kylian Mbappé won the 2018 World Cup at 19 and scored a hat-trick in the 2022 final. He joined Real Madrid in 2024.

## Additional Context

### Playing profile

- Mbappé is commonly described as a forward valued for acceleration, off-ball runs, and high-volume goal scoring.
- He has played both as a central striker and from wide starting positions, depending on club and national-team tactics.

### Club career arc (high level)

- After breaking through at AS Monaco, he became a global star at Paris Saint-Germain, where he was a focal point of the attack for multiple seasons.
- His time at PSG coincided with the club's era of heavy investment and frequent domestic titles, alongside repeated UEFA Champions League campaigns.
- A move to Real Madrid has long been a major storyline in European football, reflecting the player's market value and the club's global profile.

### International career arc (high level)

- France's 2018 World Cup win featured Mbappé as one of the tournament's standout young players.
- His scoring in the 2022 final is frequently referenced as one of the most notable individual performances in a World Cup final.

### Broader significance

- Often used as a case study for modern elite-player career management: contract leverage, image rights, and the interaction of sporting goals with commercial power.
- A representative figure for the post-2010s generation of global football superstars shaped by social media, sponsorship ecosystems, and club-state geopolitics.
