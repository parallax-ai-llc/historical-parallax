## Summary

Archibald McIndoe pioneered reconstructive surgery for severely burned Royal Air Force pilots during World War II at Queen Victoria Hospital. He developed innovative skin grafting techniques and insisted on psychological rehabilitation alongside physical reconstruction. His patients, known as the Guinea Pig Club, credited him with restoring their lives.
