## Summary

Willis Carrier was an American engineer and inventor who is considered the father of modern air conditioning. In 1902, he designed the first modern air-conditioning system to control humidity in a printing plant. His invention transformed manufacturing, architecture, and daily life in hot climates. Carrier founded the Carrier Corporation, which remains a major HVAC company today.
