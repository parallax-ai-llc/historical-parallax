## Summary

Vasyl Lomachenko won Olympic gold at 2008 and 2012 with a 396-1 amateur record, then became a three-division pro champion.

## Amateur Excellence

Lomachenko is widely regarded as one of the most accomplished amateur boxers of the modern era, with his two Olympic gold medals often used as shorthand for his international success. His amateur record is frequently cited in profiles as an unusually dominant run for an elite competitor.

## Professional Career (High-Level)

In professional boxing, he became known for winning major titles across multiple weight classes in a relatively small number of fights. Coverage of his pro career often emphasizes his rapid rise to championship level and his willingness to take difficult matchups early compared with many contemporaries.

## Boxing Style and Reputation

Analysts commonly describe Lomachenko’s style as movement-heavy and angle-focused, with an emphasis on footwork, timing, and combination punching. His reputation is closely tied to technical skill and ring IQ rather than single-punch power.

## Public Profile and Broader Context

Beyond results, his career has been covered in the context of Ukrainian sport and national identity, particularly during periods of heightened geopolitical tension. Public reporting notes his involvement with Ukraine’s territorial defense in 2022.

## Legacy

He is frequently referenced in discussions about how amateur fundamentals translate to professional success, and his performances are often used as teaching examples in boxing media. Evaluations of his legacy typically focus on technical quality, the speed of his title achievements, and the depth of opposition he faced.
