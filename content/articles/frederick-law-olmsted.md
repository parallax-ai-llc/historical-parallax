## Summary

Frederick Law Olmsted is considered the father of American landscape architecture, designing Central Park in New York City, Prospect Park, the Biltmore Estate grounds, and the grounds of the U.S. Capitol. His designs emphasized naturalistic landscapes that provided public recreation spaces and improved urban quality of life. He co-founded the profession of landscape architecture.
