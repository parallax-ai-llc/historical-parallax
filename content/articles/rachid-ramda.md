## Summary

Algerian convicted of financing the 1995 Paris Metro bombings that killed 8 people.

## Background (high-level)

- Commonly described as an Algerian national associated in reporting with Islamist networks active in the 1990s.
- Biographical accounts describe time spent outside Algeria (including the UK), with public claims and counter-claims about affiliations and roles.

## Case Context (1995 bombings)

- The 1995 bombings in France targeted public transportation and public spaces and caused multiple deaths and injuries.
- In public reporting about the investigations, Ramda has been characterized as a figure accused of facilitating or financing the network rather than being a direct perpetrator.
- Ramda has publicly denied involvement in the bombings.

## Legal Proceedings (selected points)

- Arrested in London in November 1995; subsequent proceedings included a prolonged UK extradition process.
- Handover to French custody occurred in December 2005 after UK appeals were exhausted.
- French proceedings described in sources include:
  - A 2006 conviction for criminal association with a terrorist organisation (10-year sentence reported).
  - A later assize-court trial concluding in October 2007 with a life sentence reported, including a minimum term (often cited as 22 years).
