## Summary

French-Algerian terrorist who attacked the Charlie Hebdo offices in Paris with his brother Chérif killing 12 people. They claimed to avenge the Prophet Muhammad.

## Context

Said Kouachi was one of the perpetrators of the January 2015 attack on the French satirical magazine Charlie Hebdo in Paris. The incident became a defining moment in contemporary European debates about extremist violence, freedom of expression, and domestic security.

## Attack and Immediate Aftermath

The attack resulted in multiple deaths and triggered a large-scale manhunt. Kouachi and his brother Chérif were later killed by police following a standoff. The events unfolded alongside related attacks and hostage situations during the same period, intensifying public fear and political pressure for rapid security responses.

## Broader Significance

In the weeks that followed, France saw mass public demonstrations and heightened discussion about counterterrorism policy, community relations, and the balance between civil liberties and surveillance. Media coverage also highlighted how the attackers framed their actions ideologically, while many public responses emphasized solidarity with the victims and press freedom.
