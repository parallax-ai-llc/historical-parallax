## Summary

Margaret Burbidge was a British-American astronomer who co-authored the landmark B²FH paper on stellar nucleosynthesis, explaining how elements are created in stars. She served as director of the Royal Greenwich Observatory and was the first woman elected president of the American Astronomical Society. She also pioneered the study of galaxy rotation curves and quasar spectra. Burbidge fought against gender discrimination in astronomy throughout her career.
