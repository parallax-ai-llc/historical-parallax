## Summary

Theodor Kocher was a Swiss physician and surgeon who won the 1909 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his work on the thyroid gland. He developed safe thyroid surgery techniques and understood the importance of the thyroid in human physiology. He performed over 5,000 thyroid operations with remarkably low mortality rates.
