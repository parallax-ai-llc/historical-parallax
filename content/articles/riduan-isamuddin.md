## Identity and Aliases

Riduan Isamuddin is widely known in open-source reporting by the alias **Hambali**. Many summaries about “Hambali” and “Riduan Isamuddin” refer to the same individual; this page exists mainly for completeness because some datasets list both names separately.

## Why He Appears in Counterterrorism Reporting

High-level points commonly described in public sources (details and attribution vary):

- **Network role:** Often characterized as a facilitator/connector across Southeast Asia, rather than a field-level attacker.
- **Regional focus:** Associated with **Jemaah Islamiyah (JI)**, an organization implicated in major attacks in Indonesia in the early 2000s.
- **International links:** Repeatedly described as a liaison between regional militants and **al-Qaeda** leadership.

## Capture and Detention (Publicly Reported)

- **2003 capture:** Reported captured in Thailand and transferred into U.S. custody.
- **Pre-trial detention:** Reported held in secret CIA detention facilities (“black sites”) prior to Guantánamo.
- **Guantánamo Bay:** Reported transferred to Guantánamo Bay in 2006 and held there for years; open sources through 2025 commonly describe ongoing detention and legal proceedings without a widely publicized final resolution.
