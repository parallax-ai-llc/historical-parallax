## Summary

Tintoretto was a Venetian painter known for his dramatic use of perspective and light. His monumental Paradise in the Doge's Palace is one of the largest canvas paintings in the world. His energetic style bridges the High Renaissance and Baroque periods, earning him the nickname Il Furioso.
