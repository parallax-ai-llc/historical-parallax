## Summary

Rwandan sub-prefect convicted by the ICTR of genocide for directing massacres in Gisagara commune. He organized and supervised the killing of thousands of Tutsi civilians.

## Role as a Local Official (High-Level)

- As a sub-prefect, Ntawukulilyayo held administrative authority at a local level during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi.
- ICTR cases often focus on how state and local officials used offices, resources, and security structures to enable mass violence.

## ICTR Proceedings

- The **International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR)** prosecuted senior figures as well as regional administrators.
- Convictions at the ICTR typically rely on witness testimony, documentary evidence, and findings about command responsibility or direct participation.

## Historical Context

- The events in Gisagara commune occurred within the nationwide campaign of killings and persecution in 1994.
- Scholarship and tribunal records emphasize the importance of local coordination—roadblocks, lists, and mobilization—in translating national incitement into ground-level atrocities.

## Why the Case Matters

Ntawukulilyayo’s conviction is frequently referenced in discussions of accountability for civil administrators and the ways bureaucratic authority can be instrumentalized for mass violence.
