## Summary

Eritrean human trafficker who ran a smuggling network through the Sinai Peninsula, holding migrants captive and torturing them for ransom. His organization enslaved thousands of East African migrants.

## Background

Smuggling routes through the **Sinai Peninsula** became a major humanitarian and security concern in the late 2000s and early 2010s, with migrants and asylum seekers targeted for kidnapping and extortion. Reports describing “ransom camps” emphasized torture, confinement, and coercion to extract payments from families.

## Alleged Modus Operandi (High Level)

Public descriptions of Sinai trafficking networks often include:

- moving people across multiple borders using intermediaries,
- holding captives in improvised detention sites,
- demanding ransom via phone calls to relatives, sometimes under duress,
- exploiting weak policing, corruption, and conflict dynamics.

## Information Gaps

Details about individual traffickers and the ultimate outcomes of arrests can be difficult to verify because:

- victims may be displaced or afraid to testify,
- networks operate across jurisdictions,
- public records and reporting can be incomplete or inconsistent.
