## Summary

Ji Seok-yeong was a Korean physician who introduced smallpox vaccination to Korea in 1879, saving countless lives. He learned the vaccination technique from Japanese physicians and implemented vaccination programs across Korea. He is considered the pioneer of modern preventive medicine in Korea.
