## Summary

Ibn Hazm was an Andalusian Muslim scholar, theologian, and literary figure who was one of the most original thinkers in Islamic history. He founded the Zahiri (literalist) school of jurisprudence and wrote on comparative religion, logic, and ethics. His Ring of the Dove is a celebrated work on the nature of love. His al-Fasl fi al-Milal is one of the earliest works of comparative religion.
