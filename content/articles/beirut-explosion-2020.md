## Summary

Massive explosion at Beirut port on August 4, 2020 when 2,750 tonnes of ammonium nitrate detonated, killing 218 people and devastating the city. Government negligence in storing the chemicals was blamed.

## What Happened

The Beirut port explosion on **4 August 2020** was triggered by the detonation of a large stockpile of **ammonium nitrate** that had been stored for years. The blast caused widespread damage across Beirut and produced shockwaves felt far beyond the immediate port area.

## Immediate Consequences

- **Mass casualties and injuries:** Hundreds were killed and thousands injured.
- **Infrastructure damage:** Homes, hospitals, and businesses were heavily damaged, contributing to a broader humanitarian emergency.
- **Displacement:** Large numbers of residents were left without habitable housing.

## Governance and Investigation

The disaster led to intense scrutiny of port management and government oversight. Investigations and judicial processes have been repeatedly delayed, with reporting frequently pointing to political and institutional obstacles.

## Broader Impact

The explosion intensified Lebanon’s overlapping crises—economic instability, political distrust, and strains on public services—while also prompting international aid efforts and calls for accountability reforms.
