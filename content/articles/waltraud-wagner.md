## Summary

Austrian serial killer nurse who led a group of four nurses at Lainz General Hospital in Vienna that murdered at least 49 patients between 1983 and 1989. They killed using overdoses or forced water ingestion.

## Lainz Hospital murders ("Lainz Angels")

Wagner is associated with a group of nurses sometimes referred to in media coverage as the "Lainz Angels." The crimes occurred at Lainz General Hospital (part of Vienna's hospital system) over several years in the 1980s.

Reported features of the case include:

- Victims were primarily elderly or seriously ill patients.
- Methods included administering overdoses of medication or forcing water into patients, causing fatal complications.
- The group operated over time without immediate detection, highlighting weaknesses in oversight and reporting.

## Discovery and investigation

The case came to wider attention after a colleague reportedly overheard nurses speaking about killings and informed authorities. Investigators then reviewed patient deaths and patterns that were inconsistent with expected medical outcomes.

## Trial and sentencing

Wagner and other members of the group were prosecuted; Wagner received a life sentence after conviction for multiple murders and attempted murders. The proceedings emphasized both criminal responsibility and the institutional context in which the crimes were able to continue.

## Aftermath and significance

The case is frequently cited in discussions about:

- Patient safety and the need for effective monitoring in long-term care wards.
- Whistleblowing channels and protections for healthcare staff.
- Hospital governance practices that can detect unusual mortality patterns earlier.
