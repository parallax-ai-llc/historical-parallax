## Background

- Pedro Espinoza was a Chilean intelligence and military figure associated with the Pinochet-era security apparatus.
- Public reporting and court records about the Orlando Letelier case generally link the operation to Chile's secret police (DINA) and its external operations.

## The Letelier Assassination (1976)

- Orlando Letelier, a former Chilean diplomat and minister under President Salvador Allende, was killed by a car bomb in Washington, D.C. in 1976.
- The attack also killed Ronni Karpen Moffitt, an American colleague who was with Letelier at the time.
- The case is widely regarded as a prominent example of state-linked political violence carried out beyond Chile's borders during the Cold War period.

## Legal Case and Conviction

- Chilean courts later prosecuted multiple participants and officials connected to the operation.
- This article's summary reflects that Espinoza was convicted in Chile for his role in the assassination (listed here as a 1993 conviction).
- Specific sentencing details and procedural history can vary across sources; consult primary court documents and reputable case summaries for exact outcomes.

## Why the Case Matters

- The Letelier-Moffitt assassination has been cited in historical accounts as:
  - a milestone in international attention to Chile's human-rights abuses under military rule,
  - a major diplomatic incident involving an attack in the capital of the United States,
  - a key case in documenting how intelligence services coordinated covert operations internationally.
