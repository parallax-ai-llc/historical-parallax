## Summary

Patrick Manson is called the father of tropical medicine for his discovery that mosquitoes transmit filarial parasites (elephantiasis). His research in China and Hong Kong established vector-borne disease transmission as a fundamental principle. He founded the London School of Tropical Medicine in 1899.
