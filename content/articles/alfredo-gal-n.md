## Summary

Spanish serial killer known as the 'Playing Card Killer' who shot 6 people dead in Madrid in 2003, leaving playing cards at crime scenes. He was a former Spanish army soldier. Sentenced to 142 years.

## Additional Context

### 2003 Madrid shootings

Galán was linked to a series of shootings in the Madrid area in 2003 in which victims were attacked at close range. A distinctive element reported in the case was the placement of a playing card at scenes, which shaped the media nickname “Playing Card Killer.”

### Arrest and confession reports

Following his arrest, Spanish investigators attributed multiple attacks to him. Public accounts of the case describe admissions and details that were tested against forensic evidence and witness information during the judicial process.

### Trial and sentencing

He was convicted and received a long sentence (reported as 142 years). Under Spanish law, effective time served can be limited by statutory caps, which often differs from headline sentence totals reported in individual cases.

### Public impact

The case drew wide attention in Spain and is often cited in discussions of serial violence, investigative profiling, and the interaction between media narratives and criminal proceedings.
