## Summary

Jeffrey Preston Bezos (born 1964) is an American entrepreneur and investor who founded Amazon.com in 1994, transforming it from an online bookstore into the world's largest e-commerce and cloud computing company. At various points the world's richest person (net worth exceeding $200 billion), Bezos expanded Amazon into streaming, groceries, devices (Kindle, Alexa), and Amazon Web Services. His customer-obsessed philosophy, long-term thinking, and willingness to experiment made Amazon one of the most valuable companies ever. After stepping down as CEO (2021), Bezos focuses on Blue Origin (space company) and philanthropy. He also owns *The Washington Post*.

## Amazon

### Founding (1994)

**"Regret Minimization Framework":**
Bezos asked whether at 80 he'd regret not trying internet business. Left Wall Street. Drove to Seattle, writing business plan in car.

**1994:** Founded Amazon in garage. Named after the river (largest in world).

**1995:** Launched as online bookstore. Advantage: unlimited selection vs. physical stores.

### Growth

**Strategy:**
- Customer obsession
- Long-term thinking (sacrifice short-term profits for growth)
- "Day 1" culture (always startups mindset)
- Willingness to fail, experiment

**Expansion:**
- Music, DVDs, electronics, everything
- Marketplace (third-party sellers)
- Prime membership (2005)
- Kindle (2007)
- Amazon Web Services (2006)—cloud computing giant
- Alexa/Echo (2014)
- Whole Foods acquisition (2017)

### Dominance

By 2020s, Amazon:
- Largest e-commerce company
- Largest cloud provider (AWS)
- Major in streaming, advertising, devices
- One of most valuable companies globally

## Blue Origin

Founded Blue Origin (2000), space company. Goal: millions of people living and working in space.

**New Shepard:** Suborbital tourism rocket
**New Glenn:** Orbital rocket (development)

**2021:** Bezos flew to space on New Shepard.

## Washington Post

## Leadership Philosophy

**Principles:**
- Customer obsession
- Long-term thinking
- Invent and simplify
- High hiring bar
- Frugality
- Bias for action
- Disagree and commit

**"Day 1" vs. "Day 2":**
Day 2 is stasis, irrelevance, decline. Always maintain Day 1 startup mentality.
