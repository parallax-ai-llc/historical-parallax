## Summary

Peter Canisius was a Dutch Jesuit theologian and the most important Catholic figure of the Counter-Reformation in Germany. His catechisms were published in over 200 editions during his lifetime and became the standard Catholic teaching tool in German-speaking countries. He founded numerous colleges and was instrumental in preventing the complete triumph of Protestantism in southern Germany and Austria. He was named a Doctor of the Church in 1925.
