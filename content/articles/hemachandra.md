## Summary

Hemachandra was a medieval Indian Jain scholar who independently described what is now known as the Fibonacci sequence in 1150, before Leonardo of Pisa. He applied the sequence to problems in Sanskrit prosody. He also made contributions to mathematics, grammar, philosophy, and logic, and was one of the most prolific scholars of medieval India.
