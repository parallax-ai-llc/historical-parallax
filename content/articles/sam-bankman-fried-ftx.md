## Background

Sam Bankman-Fried (often referred to as SBF) emerged from quantitative trading and became a prominent figure in the cryptocurrency industry during the rapid expansion of crypto exchanges and hedge funds.

## FTX and Alameda Research

- **FTX:** Built as a major crypto-asset trading venue offering spot and derivatives products to global users.
- **Alameda Research:** Closely associated trading firm that was widely reported to have had intertwined finances and risk exposure with FTX.
- **Risk concentration:** The collapse highlighted how leverage, illiquid exchange-issued tokens, and weak internal controls can amplify systemic risk in lightly regulated markets.

## Collapse and legal aftermath

FTX filed for bankruptcy after a liquidity crisis and loss of market confidence. Investigations focused on the handling of customer assets, lending/borrowing practices, and the accuracy of public statements made to investors and users. The case became a reference point for calls to strengthen governance, auditing, and consumer protections in crypto markets.

## Broader impact

The FTX scandal is frequently cited in discussions about:

- exchange custody standards and segregation of client funds
- transparency and conflicts of interest between exchanges and affiliated trading firms
- the role of celebrity/VC endorsements in retail risk-taking
