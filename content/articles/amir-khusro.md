## Summary

Amir Khusro was an Indo-Persian Sufi poet, musician, and scholar who is considered the father of Qawwali music and Hindustani classical music. He was the devoted disciple of Nizamuddin Auliya and one of the most important cultural figures of medieval India. He invented several musical instruments including the sitar and tabla (according to tradition). He wrote in Persian, Arabic, Turkish, and Hindi, producing over half a million verses.
