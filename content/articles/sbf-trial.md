## Summary

Sam Bankman-Fried's federal trial revealed how he stole $8 billion from FTX customers. His inner circle testified against him; convicted of seven federal charges and sentenced to 25 years.

## Case Context

- The trial followed the rapid collapse of **FTX**, once one of the largest cryptocurrency exchanges, and associated entities including **Alameda Research**.
- Prosecutors argued that customer funds were misused and that public statements obscured the true financial condition and risk.

## Evidence and Testimony (High-Level)

- The government's case relied heavily on testimony from former close colleagues and executives who described internal decision-making.
- Key themes in evidence included:
  - customer-asset custody and commingling allegations
  - the relationship between FTX and Alameda
  - internal controls, accounting practices, and risk management failures

## Outcome

- Sam Bankman-Fried was convicted on multiple federal counts related to fraud and conspiracy.
- The sentence (reported as 25 years) became one of the most consequential outcomes in U.S. crypto-related criminal enforcement to date.

## Impact

- The proceedings accelerated debate about exchange governance, proof-of-reserves claims, and the responsibilities of crypto intermediaries.
- The case is frequently referenced as a turning point in mainstream scrutiny of the crypto industry.
