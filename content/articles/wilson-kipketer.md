## Background

Wilson Kipketer was born in Kenya and later represented **Denmark** in international athletics. His career is often discussed in the context of the globalization of middle-distance running and athlete migration between national federations.

## 800 m World Record

Kipketer is most associated with the men’s **800 metres**, where he set the world record in 1997 (improving it across multiple races). That record stood as a benchmark for the event for many years and is a defining achievement of the modern era of middle-distance track.

## Championships and Competitive Era

- Competed across major championships in the late 1990s and early 2000s, an era featuring strong rivalry among elite 800 m runners.
- Won major medals, including an **Olympic silver** (Sydney 2000), reflecting sustained top-tier performance beyond the record-setting season.

## Running Profile (High-Level)

- Noted for tactical intelligence in paced championship racing as well as the ability to run exceptionally fast times in record-attempt conditions.
- Often cited as a model of consistent, technically efficient 800 m racing.

## Legacy

Kipketer’s record progression and international representation make him a prominent figure in both Danish sport history and the global narrative of African-born middle-distance excellence.

## Key Takeaways

- One of the defining **800 m specialists** of his generation.
- Combined record-setting speed with championship-level consistency.
- Frequently referenced in discussions of nationality changes in elite athletics.
