## Summary

Babe Didrikson Zaharias won two Olympic track golds in 1932 then became the greatest female golfer of her era with 10 LPGA majors.

## Background

Didrikson Zaharias is frequently cited as one of the most versatile athletes of the 20th century. She rose to prominence in track and field during an era when women’s sport had fewer professional pathways and less institutional support than men’s sport.

## Multi-Sport Significance

- **Track and field:** Her Olympic success in 1932 helped cement her public reputation as an exceptional all-around competitor.
- **Golf:** In later years she became a central figure in elevating women’s professional golf, including major-tournament wins that were foundational to her era’s competitive standard.

## Style and Public Profile

Her career combined elite performance with a strong media presence. Contemporary accounts often emphasize her confidence and willingness to challenge expectations about what female athletes could do in both competition and public life.

## Legacy

Didrikson Zaharias is often discussed in the context of:

- the growth of women’s professional sport in the United States
- the early history of major championships in women’s golf
- the broader cultural visibility of women athletes before modern sponsorship and league infrastructures

## Additional Notes

In historical rankings and retrospectives, she is frequently used as a reference point for “crossover” athletic greatness—elite performance across materially different sports with different skill demands and competitive circuits.
