## Summary

Alexander Beilinson is a Russian-American mathematician who has made fundamental contributions to algebraic geometry, representation theory, and mathematical physics. He introduced Beilinson's conjectures relating algebraic K-theory and special values of L-functions. He received the Wolf Prize in Mathematics in 2018.

## Research Areas

Beilinson’s work is often described as connecting several major “languages” in modern mathematics:

- **Algebraic geometry**, especially the study of sheaves and derived categories on varieties.
- **Representation theory**, including geometric approaches to representations of Lie algebras and algebraic groups.
- **Arithmetic geometry**, where questions about motives and regulators interact with special values of L-functions.

## Notable Contributions (High-Level)

### Beilinson’s conjectures

Beilinson proposed influential conjectures linking:

- algebraic **K-theory** and **motivic cohomology**,
- **regulator maps** from algebraic cycles to analytic invariants,
- and the expected structure of **special values of L-functions** for algebraic varieties.

These conjectures sit in the broader program of understanding “motives” as a unifying object behind cohomology theories.

### Geometric representation theory

In geometric representation theory, Beilinson is associated with techniques that relate representation-theoretic questions to geometry on flag varieties and sheaves. This viewpoint helped make sheaf-theoretic and derived-category methods standard tools in the field.

## Impact and Legacy

Beilinson’s name is attached to multiple constructions and ideas (for example, conjectures and categorical frameworks) that are used as reference points in contemporary research. His work is frequently cited in contexts where modern algebraic geometry interfaces with number theory and mathematical physics.
