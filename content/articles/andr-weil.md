## Summary

André Weil was a French mathematician who was one of the most influential mathematicians of the 20th century. He made fundamental contributions to algebraic geometry, number theory, and group theory. He was a founding member of the Bourbaki group. The Weil conjectures, later proved by others, were among the most important unsolved problems in mathematics for decades.
