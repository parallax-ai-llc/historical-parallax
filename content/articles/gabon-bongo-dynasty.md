## Summary

The Bongo family controlled Gabon for 56 years, amassing billions through oil corruption. Omar Bongo owned 39 properties in France; son Ali was overthrown in a 2023 coup.

## Overview

- The term “Bongo dynasty” commonly refers to the long period in which Gabon was led by Omar Bongo and later his son Ali Bongo Ondimba.
- Their rule was closely linked to the Parti Démocratique Gabonais (PDG) and to state management of oil revenue, with persistent allegations of patronage and corruption.

## Omar Bongo Era

- Omar Bongo governed from 1967 until his death in 2009, making him one of Africa’s longest-serving heads of state.
- Gabon’s oil wealth enabled comparatively high GDP per capita, but critics argued that benefits were unevenly distributed and that political competition was constrained.
- The period is frequently discussed within the broader “Françafrique” framework: dense political and economic ties between France and its former colonies.

## Ali Bongo and Succession Politics

- Ali Bongo succeeded his father in 2009 after elections that were contested by opponents.
- His presidency faced recurring controversy, including disputes over election integrity and governance, and periods of uncertainty related to health and succession.

## 2023 Coup and Aftermath

- In 2023, military officers announced a takeover after an election, citing irregularities and institutional breakdown.
- The coup was widely interpreted as both a rejection of entrenched rule and an example of renewed coup risk across parts of West and Central Africa.
