## Summary

Indian serial killer who with her sister Renuka Shinde kidnapped and killed 13 children in Maharashtra. They were sentenced to death, making them the first women to receive death sentences in Maharashtra.

## Case overview

Reporting and court records summarized in secondary sources describe a pattern in which Seema Gavit and her sister Renuka Shinde targeted young children in public places, used the kidnappings to support theft and begging schemes, and killed victims after the crimes. The case is frequently cited in India as a rare instance of women receiving capital sentences for serial murder.

## Investigation and prosecution (high level)

The investigation focused on connecting multiple disappearances to a single pattern and establishing identity through witness accounts and recovered evidence. Prosecution narratives emphasized repetition across incidents and the pair’s coordinated role in the crimes.

## Trial and sentencing

- **Conviction:** The sisters were convicted on multiple counts relating to kidnapping and murder.
- **Death sentence:** Indian reporting notes that the death sentences were historically notable within Maharashtra.
- **Appeals and legal process:** As with other capital cases in India, sentences can involve extended appellate review and petitions, and the public status of such proceedings can change over time.

## Public attention and context

The case attracted significant media coverage due to the number of victims, the social vulnerability of the targets, and the unusual profile of the perpetrators. It is sometimes discussed in broader debates about child safety, policing capacity, and the rarity and administration of the death penalty.
