## Summary

Arthur Guinness founded the Guinness brewery at St. James's Gate in Dublin in 1759, signing a 9,000-year lease. He developed the distinctive dark stout that became one of the world's most recognized beer brands. The brewery became the largest in the world by 1838. Today, Guinness is sold in over 150 countries and approximately 10 million glasses are consumed daily worldwide. His legacy represents one of the longest-lasting consumer brands in history.
