## Summary

Mutaz Essa Barshim shared 2021 Olympic gold with Gianmarco Tamberi. He cleared 2.43m, joint second-highest ever.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2016 | Olympic silver medal (Rio de Janeiro) |
| 2017 | World champion (London) |
| 2019 | World champion (Doha) |
| 2021 | Shared Olympic gold with Gianmarco Tamberi at Tokyo |

## Key Achievements

- **Olympic medals across three Games**: bronze (2012), silver (2016), and gold (2021).
- **Two-time World Champion**: won the men’s high jump at the World Athletics Championships in **2017** and **2019**.
- **Elite all-time clearance**: a **2.43m** personal best places him among the top clearances ever recorded in the event.

## The 2021 Shared Gold Moment

At the Tokyo Olympics (held in 2021), Barshim and Italy’s Gianmarco Tamberi finished the final tied after flawless jumping through 2.37m and identical miss patterns at 2.39m. With gold on the line, they were offered a jump-off; after confirming with officials that a tie was permitted, they chose to **share the gold**, a rare and widely celebrated outcome.

## Style and Strengths (High-Level)

- **Consistency at championship meets**: Barshim has been known for delivering his best performances under pressure, turning qualification form into final-round success.
- **Explosive takeoff**: his technique emphasizes vertical lift and a strong approach rhythm to maximize clearance at the bar.
- **Injury resilience**: he has returned to world-class heights after periods of injury setbacks, including major foot/ankle issues earlier in his career.

## Context and Legacy

Barshim is often cited as a defining high jumper of his era, combining top-end height (2.43m) with repeated podium finishes at the Olympics and World Championships. His decision to share Olympic gold in 2021 is frequently referenced as a sportsmanship moment that broadened mainstream attention for the high jump.
