## Summary

Idaho mother convicted of murdering her two children and her husband's first wife. Believed she was a goddess chosen to prepare for the Second Coming of Christ.

## Background

Lori Vallow (also known as Lori Vallow Daybell) became the central figure in a highly publicized U.S. criminal case involving the disappearance and deaths of her children and the deaths of additional individuals connected to her family.

## Disappearance of the children

In 2019, Vallow's two youngest children, Joshua "JJ" Vallow and Tylee Ryan, were last seen in Idaho. Concerns escalated when relatives and authorities could not confirm the children's whereabouts. The case drew national attention as investigators alleged that Vallow and her then-husband, Chad Daybell, provided misleading statements and resisted efforts to locate the children.

## Associated deaths and investigation

The investigation broadened to include the deaths of other people in the couple's orbit, including Daybell's previous wife, Tammy Daybell. Prosecutors alleged that a mix of personal relationships, financial motives, and apocalyptic religious beliefs influenced the group's actions.

## Trial and conviction

Vallow was charged and later tried in Idaho. In 2023, she was convicted of murder and conspiracy-related charges connected to the deaths of her children and Tammy Daybell. The proceedings featured extensive testimony about timelines, digital evidence, and the religious ideology discussed among a small circle of adherents.

## Public impact

The case is frequently discussed in the context of true-crime media, child welfare concerns, and the role that extremist or delusional belief systems can play in escalating family violence. It also prompted public scrutiny of how missing-children cases are handled across jurisdictions.
