## Background

Mukhlas, also known as Ali Gufron, was linked to the Indonesian militant organization Jemaah Islamiyah (JI). JI was responsible for multiple attacks in Southeast Asia and sought to build a broader militant network in the region.

## Bali Bombings (2002)

The 2002 Bali bombings targeted nightlife districts and killed more than 200 people, many of them foreign tourists. Mukhlas was widely described as an operational planner and commander for the attack, alongside other key figures in JI.

## Trial and Sentencing

Indonesian courts convicted Mukhlas for his role in the bombings and sentenced him to death. The proceedings were closely watched domestically and internationally as Indonesia confronted major terrorist violence and strengthened counterterrorism capabilities.

## Execution and Aftermath

Mukhlas was executed in 2008 along with other convicted Bali bombing perpetrators. The attacks and the subsequent trials became a turning point for Indonesian security policy, law enforcement coordination, and regional counterterrorism efforts.

## Significance

The case is frequently cited in studies of Southeast Asian militant networks, including recruitment, financing, and cross-border linkages.
