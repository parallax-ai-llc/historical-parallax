## Summary

Naguib Mahfouz was an Egyptian novelist who became the first Arabic-language writer to receive the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1988. His Cairo Trilogy chronicles three generations of a Cairo family amid Egypt's modernization. His prolific output of over 30 novels established the Arabic novel as a major literary form.
