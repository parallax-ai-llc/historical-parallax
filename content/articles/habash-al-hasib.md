## Summary

Habash al-Hasib al-Marwazi was a Persian astronomer and mathematician who introduced trigonometric functions including tangent, cotangent, secant, and cosecant. Working at the Abbasid court in Baghdad, he compiled astronomical tables and conducted observations used for calendar reform. His trigonometric innovations were foundational for Islamic and later European mathematics.
