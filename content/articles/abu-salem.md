## Summary

Indian gangster and hitman for Dawood Ibrahim's D-Company who was involved in the 1993 Bombay bombings. He was extradited from Portugal to India with a condition against the death penalty. Sentenced to life.

## Additional Context

- Abu Salem is widely described in Indian and international reporting as a **member/associate of D-Company**, a criminal network historically linked to Dawood Ibrahim.
- His extradition from Portugal became a notable case in **international criminal law and extradition practice**, particularly around assurances related to the death penalty.

## Legal and Judicial Notes (High-Level)

- In accounts of the case, courts and prosecutors discussed evidentiary links between organized-crime networks and terror attacks in early-1990s Mumbai.
- Public sources frequently mention that extradition assurances can affect **sentencing options** and the scope of charges that can be pursued.
