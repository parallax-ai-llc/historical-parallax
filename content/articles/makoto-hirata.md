## Summary

Aum Shinrikyo member who was a fugitive for 17 years before surrendering to police on New Year's Day.

## Context

Aum Shinrikyo (also known later as Aleph) was a Japanese religious movement that became internationally notorious after members carried out the 1995 Tokyo subway sarin attack. In the years following the attack and subsequent investigations, Japanese authorities pursued a number of suspects connected to the group's activities.

## Why He Matters

Makoto Hirata is often referenced in overviews of post-1995 Aum Shinrikyo investigations because his long period as a fugitive became one of the best-known late developments in the case. His surrender in 2012 drew renewed media attention to the broader history of the group and the continuing legal process around unresolved suspects.

## Key Points (High-Level)

- Identified as an Aum Shinrikyo member and sought by Japanese authorities for many years.
- Spent an unusually long period avoiding capture (commonly summarized as about 17 years).
- Surrendered voluntarily in early 2012, which triggered renewed investigation and court proceedings.
