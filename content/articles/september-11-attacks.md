## Summary

Al-Qaeda attacks on September 11, 2001 using hijacked airliners. Nearly 3,000 killed when planes struck WTC, Pentagon, and crashed in Pennsylvania.

## Targets and Immediate Outcomes

- **World Trade Center (New York City):** Two hijacked aircraft struck the North and South Towers, which later collapsed.
- **Pentagon (Arlington, Virginia):** A third aircraft struck the U.S. Department of Defense headquarters.
- **Pennsylvania crash site:** A fourth aircraft crashed after passengers attempted to regain control.

## Perpetrators

- The attacks were carried out by **al-Qaeda** operatives.
- They involved coordinated hijackings of commercial passenger flights.

## Death Toll and Human Impact

- Nearly **3,000 people** were killed, including civilians, first responders, and passengers/crew.
- The attacks produced long-term health impacts for many survivors and responders due to debris exposure.

## Aftermath

- The United States launched the **"War on Terror"** and initiated military operations in Afghanistan.
- Domestic security policy and aviation screening changed significantly.
- The attacks reshaped global counterterrorism cooperation and intelligence priorities.

## Commemoration

- Annual memorial ceremonies are held, and major memorial sites include the **National September 11 Memorial & Museum** in New York.
