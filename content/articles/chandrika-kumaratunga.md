## Summary

Former president of Sri Lanka whose administration was plagued by corruption allegations and the escalation of the civil war with Tamil Tigers. She survived a suicide bomb attack that blinded her in one eye.

## Political background

Kumaratunga came from a prominent Sri Lankan political family and became a significant figure in the country’s center-left politics.[^1] She served in senior government roles before becoming president and was also associated with the Sri Lanka Freedom Party (SLFP) and related coalitions.[^1]

## Presidency (1994–2005)

Her presidency coincided with an intense period of the **Sri Lankan Civil War** involving the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE).[^1] Public accounts of her tenure often focus on two parallel tracks:

- efforts at **negotiation and peace initiatives** at various points, and
- renewed military escalation when talks broke down.[^1]

## 1999 assassination attempt

In 1999, Kumaratunga survived a suicide bombing attributed to the LTTE; reports note that she lost vision in one eye as a result.[^1] The attack became one of the defining public events of her presidency.

## Governance, controversy, and allegations

Her administration faced recurring controversy and allegations of corruption in Sri Lankan politics.[^1] As with many heads of government, assessments of her record vary by political perspective, with debates often centered on wartime decision-making, economic policy, and institutional reform.

## Later public life

After leaving office in 2005, Kumaratunga remained active in public affairs, at times commenting on reconciliation, governance, and post-war politics.[^1]
