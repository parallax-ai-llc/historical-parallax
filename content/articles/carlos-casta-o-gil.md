## Summary

Co-founder of the AUC paramilitary group that committed some of Colombia's worst massacres. He disappeared and is believed to have been murdered by his own brother.

## Context: Colombia’s Armed Conflict

Castaño emerged during a long-running internal conflict involving guerrilla organizations, state forces, and right-wing paramilitaries. Paramilitary groups often claimed to fight insurgents, while also being accused of targeting civilians, engaging in forced displacement, and controlling territory for economic and political purposes.

## Formation and Role in the AUC

The United Self-Defense Forces of Colombia (AUC) functioned as a federation of paramilitary blocs. Castaño was a prominent spokesperson and organizer and became associated with mass violence attributed to AUC units and affiliates.

## Links to Illicit Economies

Analysts and judicial proceedings have frequently described the intersection between paramilitarism and narcotrafficking in Colombia, including revenue streams from drug production, trafficking corridors, and extortion. Specific allegations around individuals and networks have varied across cases and reporting.

## Public Statements and Controversies

Castaño sometimes sought political legitimacy in public messaging while facing accusations of grave human-rights abuses. The gap between political claims and documented violence became part of the broader debate about the paramilitary phenomenon and state responsibility.

## Disappearance and Reported Death

He disappeared in 2004, and later accounts—including judicial findings reported in public sources—have described his killing as connected to internal disputes. Narratives commonly point to conflicts with other paramilitary leaders and to tensions around demobilization and exposure of criminal links.
