## Background

Wikipedia describes Thomas "Pinocchio" Rung as a German serial killer who was active in Germany across the 1980s and 1990s and later received a life sentence.[^1]

The article characterizes his early life as unstable and violent, including repeated conflict with the law prior to the murders.[^1]

## Crimes and Investigation (high-level)

According to Wikipedia, authorities eventually linked multiple killings to Rung after his arrest in 1995, and he confessed soon afterward.[^1]

Because the cases involved different methods and occurred over many years, Wikipedia notes that connections between incidents were not immediately recognized at the time.[^1]

## Trial and Sentencing

Wikipedia reports that a Berlin regional court sentenced Rung to life imprisonment in 1996, with additional measures described in German reporting as preventive detention.[^1]

Later prison-violence convictions are also described in the same source, resulting in additional sentences on top of the original life term.[^1]
