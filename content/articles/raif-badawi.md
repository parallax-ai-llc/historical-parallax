## Summary

Saudi blogger sentenced to 1,000 lashes and 10 years in prison for 'insulting Islam' on his website.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2015–present | Remains a prominent symbol in debates over freedom of expression, blasphemy laws, and the treatment of dissidents in Saudi Arabia (per Wikipedia and human-rights reporting). |

## Why He Matters

- **Freedom of expression:** His case is frequently cited in global discussions about online speech and state punishment.
- **Rule of law & due process:** International observers have criticized the severity of the sentence and the handling of the proceedings (per Wikipedia).
- **Human rights advocacy:** NGOs and writers’ organizations have used the case to campaign against corporal punishment and for the release of prisoners of conscience.

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- **Known for:** Blogging and public commentary; association with the website/community often described as *Free Saudi Liberals* (per Wikipedia).
- **Charges/convictions:** Related to “insulting Islam” / apostasy-related allegations in public reporting; legal framing varies across sources (see Wikipedia for specifics).
- **Sentence (reported):** A multi-year prison term plus fines and a corporal punishment component of 1,000 lashes administered in installments (per Wikipedia).

## International Response (Examples)

- Condemnation and advocacy from press-freedom and human-rights groups.
- Public campaigns by writers and civil-society organizations calling for his release.
- Diplomatic and media attention that elevated the case beyond Saudi domestic politics.
