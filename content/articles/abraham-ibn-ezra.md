## Summary

Abraham ibn Ezra was a medieval Spanish Jewish scholar, poet, and biblical commentator who was one of the most brilliant and versatile Jewish intellectuals of the Middle Ages. His Torah commentary is included alongside Rashi's in standard Jewish Bibles. He wrote on grammar, astronomy, mathematics, and astrology. After fleeing the Almohad invasion of Spain, he traveled across Europe, spreading Spanish Jewish learning to Ashkenazi communities.
