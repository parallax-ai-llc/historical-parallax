## Summary

Italian playboy criminal who committed the £60 million Knightsbridge safe deposit robbery. Caught through a fingerprint, he was shot dead by Italian police while on day release from UK prison.

## Additional Context

## Knightsbridge robbery overview

Viccei is best known for the 1987 robbery of safe deposit boxes at the Knightsbridge Safe Deposit Centre in London. Accounts commonly describe the operation as carefully planned: robbers gained entry during business hours, subdued staff, and accessed numerous boxes, producing one of the most prominent UK robberies of the era.[^1]

## Investigation and arrest

He was later identified and arrested; reporting often highlights forensic evidence, including fingerprints, as a key factor in connecting him to the crime. The case became a reference point in discussions of how physical evidence and investigative persistence can unravel high-value thefts.[^1]

## Imprisonment and death

Viccei was convicted in the UK and later died in Italy after being shot by police. His death is often summarized as occurring during a confrontation with law enforcement while he was temporarily released from prison.[^1]

## Cultural footprint

The Knightsbridge robbery has remained part of popular crime history in the UK, frequently cited in documentaries and retrospectives on notable robberies. Viccei’s image as a “playboy” criminal is also part of the narrative that media coverage built around the case.[^1]
