## Summary

Brazilian former judge who led the Lava Jato anti-corruption investigation. He was later found to have colluded with prosecutors, undermining the impartiality of the trials. He became Bolsonaro's justice minister, then a senator.

## Operation Lava Jato (Car Wash)

Sérgio Moro became internationally prominent as a federal judge associated with Operation Lava Jato, a sprawling anti-corruption investigation that targeted bribery and kickback networks connected to major firms and political figures. Supporters framed the effort as a watershed for accountability; critics argued it relied on aggressive tactics and blurred legal boundaries.

## Controversies and Court Rulings

Moro’s role became more contested after reporting based on leaked communications suggested improper coordination between the judge and prosecutors. Brazil’s Supreme Federal Court later issued decisions affecting cases linked to Moro, including rulings related to jurisdiction and judicial impartiality in the prosecution of former president Luiz Inácio Lula da Silva.

## Transition to Politics

After leaving the bench, Moro joined President Jair Bolsonaro’s government as Minister of Justice and Public Security, a move that intensified debate about whether his earlier judicial actions had political implications. He later entered electoral politics and became a senator.

## Public Significance

In contemporary Brazil, Moro is a polarizing figure: for some he symbolizes anti-corruption enforcement; for others he represents the politicization of the judiciary. The Lava Jato era remains central to debates about legal process, institutional trust, and the relationship between law enforcement and politics.
