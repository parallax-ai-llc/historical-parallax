## Summary

Mark Elliot Zuckerberg (born 1984) is an American entrepreneur who co-founded Facebook (now Meta Platforms) in 2004 from his Harvard dorm room, becoming one of the world's youngest billionaires. Facebook grew to become the largest social network with nearly 3 billion users, fundamentally changing how humans communicate, share information, and consume media. Following controversies over privacy, misinformation, and political manipulation, Zuckerberg renamed the company Meta (2021) to focus on building the "metaverse." He remains one of the most powerful and controversial figures in technology.

## Facebook

### Founding (2004)

**"TheFacebook":**
Created from Harvard dorm room (February 4, 2004). Initially Harvard-only network.

**Co-founders:**
Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, Chris Hughes

**Growth:**
- Expanded to other Ivies, then colleges, then everyone
- 2006: Open to public (age 13+)
- Rapid global growth

### Legal Disputes

**Winklevoss Twins:**
Cameron and Tyler Winklevoss claimed Zuckerberg stole their idea (ConnectU). Settled for $65 million (2008).

**Eduardo Saverin:**
Co-founder sued over diluted shares. Settled confidentially.

**Film:**
*The Social Network* (2010) dramatized founding and disputes.

### Scale

By 2020s:
- Facebook: ~3 billion users
- Instagram: ~2 billion users (acquired 2012)
- WhatsApp: ~2 billion users (acquired 2014)
- Oculus VR (acquired 2014)

## Controversies

### Privacy

- 2018 Cambridge Analytica scandal: User data harvested for political manipulation
- Repeated privacy violations
- FTC fines ($5 billion, 2019)

### Misinformation

- Platform used to spread fake news
- Role in 2016/2020 elections
- COVID misinformation
- Myanmar genocide facilitation accused

### Mental Health

- Instagram's effect on teen mental health (Frances Haugen whistleblower)
- Addictive design criticized

### Political

- Both sides criticize: Too much censorship vs. too little
- Content moderation challenges
- Hate speech, extremism

### Antitrust

- Monopoly concerns
- Acquisitions (Instagram, WhatsApp) scrutinized
- Break-up proposals

## Leadership Style

**Founder Control:**
Maintains majority voting control through dual-class shares despite owning ~13% of stock. Difficult to remove.

Early motto (later modified to "Move fast with stable infrastructure").
