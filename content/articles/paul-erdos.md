## Summary

Paul Erdős was the most prolific mathematician of the 20th century, publishing over 1,500 mathematical papers with more than 500 collaborators. He made fundamental contributions to number theory, combinatorics, and probability. The Erdős number measures collaborative distance to him. He lived an itinerant life, owning virtually nothing and devoting himself entirely to mathematics.
