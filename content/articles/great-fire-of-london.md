## Summary

The Great Fire of London from September 2-5, 1666, destroyed 13,200 houses and 87 churches, including St. Paul's Cathedral. The fire displaced 100,000 people but killed relatively few. It led to building regulations and Christopher Wren's reconstruction of London.
