## Summary

Leader of the Séléka rebel coalition who overthrew President Bozizé and became the first Muslim leader of the CAR. His forces committed widespread atrocities against Christians.

## Background

Michel Djotodia emerged as a leader within the Séléka coalition, an alliance of rebel groups that challenged the government of President François Bozizé. The Central African Republic had faced repeated cycles of rebellion and weak state capacity, creating conditions for armed groups to gain territory.

## Seizure of power

In 2013 Séléka forces entered Bangui and Bozizé was ousted, after which Djotodia declared himself head of state. The takeover triggered regional and international concern about security, governance, and humanitarian conditions.

## Violence and fragmentation

During and after the takeover, armed factions were accused of serious abuses, and retaliatory violence escalated among competing militias. Analysts often describe the period as one of state collapse in which command-and-control over fighters was limited and authority fragmented across local power centers.

## Resignation and later status

Djotodia resigned in 2014 under pressure from regional mediators and international actors. Subsequent political arrangements in the CAR involved transitional authorities and later elections, while armed groups continued to influence security conditions in parts of the country.

## Significance

Djotodia's brief presidency is typically discussed as part of the CAR's broader crisis, including questions about coalition politics among rebel groups, the role of neighboring states, and the challenges of rebuilding state institutions after armed conflict.
