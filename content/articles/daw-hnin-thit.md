## Summary

Khin Hnin Thit (ဒေါ်ခင်နှင်းသစ်) is a Bamar politician from Myanmar who represented Padaung township in Bago Region in the country's parliament. Born on January 1, 1968, she has been involved in various parliamentary activities and policy initiatives related to agricultural development.

## Early Life and Background

Khin Hnin Thit was born on January 1, 1968, as a member of the Bamar ethnic group, the largest ethnic group in Myanmar. She hails from the Padaung township region in Bago Region.

## Political Career

### Parliamentary Representation

Khin Hnin Thit served as a representative for Padaung township in Bago Region in Myanmar's parliament, where she participated in legislative activities affecting her constituency.

### Policy Initiatives

In 2019, she was involved in government efforts related to poppy substitution programs. The Myanmar government initiated plans to invite investors for alternative crops to replace poppy cultivation, with Khin Hnin Thit participating in these policy discussions.
