## Summary

Martin Niemöller was a German Lutheran pastor and anti-Nazi dissident famous for his poem 'First they came...' He initially supported Hitler but became a leader of the Confessing Church opposing Nazi control of Protestantism. He spent seven years in concentration camps as Hitler's personal prisoner. After the war, he became a prominent pacifist and ecumenist.
