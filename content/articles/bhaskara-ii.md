## Summary

Bhaskara II (Bhaskaracharya) was an Indian mathematician and astronomer who is considered the greatest mathematician of the medieval period. His work 'Lilavati' on arithmetic and 'Bijaganita' on algebra advanced Indian mathematics significantly. He discovered some principles of differential calculus centuries before Newton and Leibniz. He also accurately calculated the sidereal year.
