## Summary

Todd Phillips is an American film director, screenwriter, and producer best known for helming some of Hollywood's most commercially successful comedies of the 2000s and for his dramatic reinvention with the 2019 psychological thriller *Joker*. Born on December 20, 1970, in Brooklyn, New York, Phillips built a reputation as a reliable architect of raunchy, male-driven ensemble comedies before pivoting to prestige filmmaking in a move that surprised the industry.

His career spans two distinct phases: a run of blockbuster R-rated comedies including *Road Trip*, *Old School*, and *The Hangover* trilogy, and a later pivot toward darker, more character-driven work. *Joker* (2019) earned over $1 billion at the global box office and won two Academy Awards, cementing Phillips as a filmmaker capable of operating well beyond his comedic origins.

---

## Early Life & Background

Todd Phillips was born in Brooklyn, New York, and grew up on Long Island. He studied film at New York University's Tisch School of the Arts, where he directed the 1993 documentary *Hated: GG Allin and the Murder Junkies*, a raw portrait of the provocateur punk musician GG Allin. The film premiered at the Sundance Film Festival and established Phillips early as a filmmaker willing to engage with transgressive subject matter. His NYU background and early documentary work gave him a foundation in unscripted chaos and counterculture storytelling that would inform his later fiction work.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Phillips broke into mainstream filmmaking with *Road Trip* (2000), a low-budget comedy that grossed over $68 million worldwide against a $16 million budget. He followed it with *Old School* (2003), starring Will Ferrell, Luke Wilson, and Vince Vaughn, which grossed $87 million domestically and became a defining comedy of its era. His ascent peaked with *The Hangover* (2009), which grossed $467 million worldwide on a $35 million budget, making it one of the highest-grossing R-rated comedies in history at the time of its release. It won the Golden Globe for Best Motion Picture – Musical or Comedy in 2010.

---

## Key Achievements

Phillips directed all three films in *The Hangover* trilogy, with the franchise collectively grossing over $1.4 billion worldwide. His most significant achievement came with *Joker* (2019), co-written with Scott Silver and starring Joaquin Phoenix as Arthur Fleck. The film won the Golden Lion at the Venice Film Festival — the first comic book-adapted film to do so — and was nominated for eleven Academy Awards, winning two: Best Actor for Phoenix and Best Original Score for Hildur Guðnadóttir. *Joker* grossed over $1.07 billion worldwide, making it one of the highest-grossing R-rated films ever made. Phillips followed it with *Joker: Folie à Deux* (2024), a musical sequel also starring Lady Gaga.

---

## Controversies & Criticism

Phillips has attracted criticism for the humor and gender dynamics in his comedies, particularly regarding the treatment of women in *The Hangover* films. In a 2019 interview, he controversially claimed he stopped making comedies because "woke culture" made it impossible to be funny, remarks widely criticized as deflective. *Joker: Folie à Deux* (2024) received a strongly negative critical and commercial reception, grossing approximately $58 million domestically against a reported $200 million production budget, representing one of the more significant box office disappointments of that year.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Phillips reshaped the landscape of R-rated studio comedy in the 2000s alongside filmmakers like Judd Apatow, demonstrating that adult-oriented comedies could reliably generate blockbuster returns. His work on *Joker* challenged assumptions about what superhero-adjacent films could accomplish artistically, opening critical and commercial space for darker, auteur-driven takes on comic book IP. Whatever the mixed reception of his sequel, the original *Joker* remains a landmark in how studios approach prestige genre filmmaking.
