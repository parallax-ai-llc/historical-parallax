## Summary

William Law was an English clergyman and theological writer whose A Serious Call to a Devout and Holy Life profoundly influenced John Wesley, Samuel Johnson, and many others. He advocated rigorous Christian living and later turned to mysticism influenced by Jakob Böhme. His emphasis on inner spiritual life bridged Anglican and mystical traditions.
