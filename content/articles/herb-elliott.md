## Summary

Herb Elliott won the 1960 Olympic 1,500m gold and was never beaten over that distance in his career.

## Additional Context

- Elliott’s peak coincided with an era when the mile and 1,500m were among the sport’s highest-profile middle-distance events.
- “Undefeated” in a distance typically refers to head-to-head races during a defined competitive window; it underscores dominance rather than implying perpetual competition.
