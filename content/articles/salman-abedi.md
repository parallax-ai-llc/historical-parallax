## Background

Salman Abedi was the perpetrator of the 2017 Manchester Arena bombing in the United Kingdom. The attack targeted a crowded public venue immediately after a concert, killing 22 people and injuring many more, and it had a lasting impact on public life and security practices around major events.

## Attack and Response

The bombing prompted a major counterterrorism investigation, arrests of associates, and extensive scrutiny of intelligence and policing processes. The Islamic State claimed responsibility in public messaging, and the incident became a focal point for debates about domestic counterterrorism, community protection, and the safeguarding of public spaces.

## Longer-Term Impact

In the years following, the attack was repeatedly referenced in policy discussions about inter-agency coordination, information sharing, and the management of extremist threats. It also led to major public commemorations and support efforts for victims and families in the Manchester area.
