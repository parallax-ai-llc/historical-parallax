## Summary

E. O. Wilson was an American biologist, naturalist, and entomologist known as the 'father of sociobiology' and the 'father of biodiversity.' His work on island biogeography with Robert MacArthur became foundational to conservation biology. His controversial book 'Sociobiology' proposed a biological basis for social behavior. Wilson was also one of the world's leading experts on ants and a passionate advocate for biodiversity conservation.
