## Summary

Major Chinese triad society founded in 1945 with an estimated 20,000 members worldwide. It is involved in drug trafficking, human smuggling, and money laundering across Asia and globally.

## Overview

The "14K" is commonly described as one of the largest and most widely recognized Chinese triad organizations. Public sources often portray it less as a single centralized hierarchy and more as a **network of affiliated groups** operating across regions.

## Activities described in open sources

Reports and academic/law-enforcement summaries often associate triad groups with:

- Narcotics trafficking and distribution networks
- Extortion and protection rackets
- Illegal gambling and bookmaking
- Smuggling (including people and goods)
- Money laundering through legitimate fronts

## Geographic presence (high level)

While historically linked to Hong Kong and southern China, open sources frequently mention diaspora and transnational activity in parts of:

- East and Southeast Asia
- North America
- Europe and Oceania

## Organizational characteristics (general)

- Reliance on personal ties and compartmentalized cells
- Use of intermediaries and front businesses to reduce exposure
- Adaptation to local enforcement pressure by shifting routes and methods

## Note

Specific attributions to named groups can vary by jurisdiction and time period, and media reporting may blend multiple "14K"-labeled factions together.
