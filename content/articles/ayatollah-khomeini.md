## Summary

Iranian revolutionary and cleric who founded the Islamic Republic of Iran. He led the 1979 revolution, sanctioned the US embassy hostage crisis, and ordered the mass execution of political prisoners. His fatwa against Salman Rushdie sparked global controversy.

## Political Role and Governance

Khomeini became the central religious and political authority of the post-revolutionary state as the first Supreme Leader. Under his leadership, Iran adopted a new constitution and institutions designed to embed clerical oversight in government.

## Ideology (High Level)

He promoted the concept of *velayat-e faqih* (guardianship of the Islamic jurist), arguing that qualified Islamic jurists should have ultimate authority over political matters. In practice, this shaped Iran into a theocratic republic with elected bodies operating under the supervision of unelected clerical institutions.

## Major Events and Controversies

- **US embassy hostage crisis (1979–1981):** The takeover and prolonged detention of diplomats became a defining rupture in US–Iran relations.
- **Iran–Iraq War (1980–1988):** The war dominated Iran’s early revolutionary period, with enormous casualties and lasting regional consequences.
- **Political repression:** His era is associated with severe restrictions on opposition groups and human-rights abuses, including executions of political prisoners.
- **Rushdie fatwa (1989):** The religious decree calling for the killing of author Salman Rushdie internationalized Iran’s revolutionary posture and generated a long-running diplomatic and security controversy.

## Legacy

Khomeini is remembered by supporters as the leader who toppled the Pahlavi monarchy and asserted Iranian sovereignty and Islamic governance. Critics emphasize authoritarian consolidation, violent repression, and enduring consequences for civil liberties and regional stability.

## Additional Notes

In many accounts of modern Iranian history, his leadership marks the decisive shift from monarchy to a revolutionary state structure that continues to shape Iran’s domestic politics and regional posture decades later.
