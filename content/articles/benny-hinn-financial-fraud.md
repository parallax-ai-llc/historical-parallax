## Summary

Israeli-born televangelist accused of financial fraud and living a lavish lifestyle funded by donations from followers who believed he could heal the sick. Senate investigations revealed millions spent on luxury travel.

## Public Profile

Benny Hinn became widely known through televised “faith healing” services and a style of charismatic preaching closely associated with the broader televangelism ecosystem.

## Financial Scrutiny and Criticism

Public controversies around Hinn’s ministry have often focused on:

- **fundraising practices** and donation appeals
- the relationship between donations, organizational spending, and personal lifestyle
- the broader debate over “prosperity gospel” teaching and its effect on vulnerable audiences

Some reporting and official scrutiny (including U.S. Senate attention to certain ministries in the televangelism sphere) contributed to sustained public debate, even as legal outcomes and factual claims vary across sources and time.

## 2019 “Renunciation” in Context

In 2019, Hinn drew attention for statements interpreted as rejecting or distancing himself from aspects of prosperity-gospel teaching. Commentators noted that such statements can be framed differently depending on whether the emphasis is theological (doctrine) or institutional (fundraising and accountability).
