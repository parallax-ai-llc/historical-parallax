## Summary

Zhang Shouzheng (Shoucheng Zhang) was a Chinese-American physicist who made groundbreaking contributions to topological insulators and predicted the quantum spin Hall effect. His work at Stanford on topological states of matter was widely expected to earn a Nobel Prize. He also proposed the existence of the angel particle (chiral Majorana fermion). He died in 2018.
