## Summary

Nam Joo-hyuk is a South Korean actor and model born on February 22, 1994, in Busan, South Korea. He first gained recognition as a fashion model before transitioning into acting, building a reputation as one of his generation's most bankable leading men in Korean television dramas. His ability to portray emotionally nuanced characters across genres — from romance and coming-of-age to sports and fantasy — has earned him a substantial domestic and international fanbase.

His breakout role in the 2016 sports drama *Weightlifting Fairy Kim Bok-joo* demonstrated both his comedic timing and emotional depth, cementing his status beyond his model origins. Since then, he has consistently headlined high-profile productions, and his mainstream crossover appeal has made him a prominent figure in the global Korean Wave (Hallyu).

## Early Life & Background

Nam Joo-hyuk was born and raised in Busan, South Korea's second-largest city. He relocated to Seoul to pursue opportunities in the entertainment industry, enrolling at Hanlim Multi Art School, a performing arts high school that has produced numerous Korean celebrities. He was scouted by YG Entertainment around 2013, initially signing as a model rather than an actor.

## Rise to Prominence

He made his runway and commercial modeling debut in 2013 under YG Entertainment's modeling division and quickly attracted attention for his tall frame — standing approximately 187 cm — and distinctive features. He made his acting debut in 2014 with a supporting role in the drama *The Idle Mermaid* and followed it with appearances in *Who Are You: School 2015* (2015) alongside Kim So-hyun.

His profile rose considerably with the fantasy romance *Scarlet Heart: Goryeo* (2016), in which he played a prince alongside IU and Lee Joon-gi. Later that same year, *Weightlifting Fairy Kim Bok-joo* — in which he played a swimming athlete — became a cult favourite, praised for its warm storytelling and his chemistry with co-star Lee Sung-kyung.

He continued to headline major productions, including the time-travel romance *Moon Lovers* and the acclaimed drama *Start-Up* (2020), where he played Nam Do-san, a startup founder. *Start-Up* was a Netflix co-production that expanded his international visibility significantly. In 2022 he starred in *Twenty-Five Twenty-One*, another widely praised coming-of-age drama that won him further critical recognition.

## Key Achievements

- Lead role in *Weightlifting Fairy Kim Bok-joo* (2016), one of the most beloved Korean rom-coms of the decade
- Starring role in Netflix co-production *Start-Up* (2020), which reached audiences across Asia, North America, and Europe
- Lead in *Twenty-Five Twenty-One* (2022), which earned strong ratings and international streaming attention
- Multiple Brand of the Year and popularity award wins at major Korean television award ceremonies
- Active brand ambassador for global fashion and lifestyle labels

## Controversies & Criticism

In 2022, Nam Joo-hyuk was named in a school bullying (학교폭력) allegation circulated anonymously online. His agency, Soop Entertainment, denied the claims and stated they would take legal action against those spreading false information. The controversy drew significant public attention but did not result in verified findings or formal proceedings. He fulfilled his mandatory military service, enlisting in 2022 and completing his service in 2024.

## Legacy & Influence

Nam Joo-hyuk represents a generation of Korean entertainers who successfully bridged the modeling and acting worlds. His roles in streaming-era dramas contributed to the international normalisation of Korean content on platforms like Netflix. His work in youth-oriented narratives helped define the aesthetic and emotional register of Korean coming-of-age drama in the late 2010s and early 2020s.
