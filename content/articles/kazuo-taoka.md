## Summary
Kazuo Taoka was the third kumicho of the Yamaguchi-gumi. Under his leadership from 1946 to 1981, the organization grew from a small Kobe gang to Japan's largest criminal syndicate with 13,000 members. Known as 'The Bear,' he survived an assassination attempt in 1978.
