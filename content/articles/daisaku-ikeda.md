## Summary

Daisaku Ikeda was a Japanese Buddhist leader who served as the third president of Soka Gakkai International (SGI), a lay Buddhist organization with 12 million members worldwide. He promoted Nichiren Buddhism's practice of chanting Nam-myoho-renge-kyo. He founded Soka University and numerous cultural institutions. He conducted over 70 published dialogues with world leaders, intellectuals, and religious figures. He received numerous honorary degrees and peace awards.
