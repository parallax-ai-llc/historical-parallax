## Summary

GSPC leader who pledged allegiance to al-Qaeda before being killed by Algerian forces.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

## Background

- Sahraoui was an Algerian Islamist militant associated with the Salafist Group for Preaching and Combat (GSPC).
- He was also known by the alias **Mustapha Abou Ibrahim**.[^1]
- Public sources disagree on his exact birth date/year; some profiles list **1966** while other reporting has cited **26 September 1969**.[^1]

## Role in the GSPC

- He led the GSPC from **August 2003** until his death in **June 2004**.[^1]
- His appointment followed internal disagreements within the group, including debates about reconciliation with the Algerian government.[^1]

## Alignment with al-Qaeda (2003)

- In **October 2003**, Sahraoui (as GSPC leader) publicly pledged allegiance to **Osama bin Laden’s al-Qaeda**.[^1]
- Some reporting also notes an oath of allegiance to **Mullah Mohammed Omar** (Taliban leader) during the same period.[^1]
- These moves are commonly described as part of the GSPC’s effort to frame its insurgency within a broader transnational jihadist narrative.[^2]

## Death

- Sahraoui was reported killed on **20 June 2004** during clashes with Algerian security forces in the **Kabylie** region (often described as within **Béjaïa Province**).[^1]
- Multiple news and security outlets reported his death and described it as occurring during an Algerian army sweep/operation.[^2]

## Related Entities

- **GSPC**: An Algerian armed Islamist group that later became closely associated with al-Qaeda; it is often discussed in connection with the later formation/branding of **Al-Qaeda in the Islamic Maghreb (AQIM)**.[^1]
- **Hassan Hattab**: Predecessor as GSPC leader.[^1]
- **Abu Musab Abdel Wadoud**: Reported successor as GSPC leader.[^1]
