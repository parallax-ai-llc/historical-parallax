## Summary

Bjarni Tryggvason was an Icelandic-Canadian astronaut and engineer who flew on the Space Shuttle Discovery in 1997. He designed the Microgravity Vibration Isolation Mount used on the International Space Station. His engineering contributions helped control vibrations in microgravity that affect scientific experiments.
