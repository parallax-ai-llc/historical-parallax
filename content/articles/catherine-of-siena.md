## Summary

Catherine of Siena was an Italian mystic, activist, and Doctor of the Church who played a crucial role in persuading Pope Gregory XI to return the papacy from Avignon to Rome. Despite having no formal education, she wrote the Dialogue, one of the great mystical works of Christianity. She was a political mediator and spiritual advisor to popes and rulers. She was only the second woman named a Doctor of the Church.
