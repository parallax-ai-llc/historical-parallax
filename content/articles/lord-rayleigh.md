## Summary

Lord Rayleigh (John William Strutt) was a British physicist who won the 1904 Nobel Prize in Physics for discovering the element argon. He made fundamental contributions to acoustic theory (Rayleigh scattering explains why the sky is blue), fluid dynamics, optics, and electromagnetism. His textbook 'The Theory of Sound' remained the standard reference for decades.
