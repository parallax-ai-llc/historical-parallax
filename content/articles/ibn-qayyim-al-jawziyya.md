## Summary

Ibn Qayyim al-Jawziyya was a Syrian Islamic scholar who was the most prominent student of Ibn Taymiyyah. He wrote extensively on Islamic jurisprudence, theology, and spirituality. His works like Madarij al-Salikin (Ranks of the Seekers) combined rigorous legal scholarship with Sufi-influenced spiritual guidance. He was imprisoned along with Ibn Taymiyyah for their controversial views. His writings remain widely read in conservative Islamic circles.
