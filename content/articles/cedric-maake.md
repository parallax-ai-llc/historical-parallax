## Summary
Cedric Maake, the 'Wemmer Pan Killer,' was a South African serial killer convicted of 27 murders, 26 rapes, and 14 robberies committed in Johannesburg between 1996 and 1997. He was sentenced to 1,340 years and 13 life sentences, making his case one of the largest serial murder convictions in South African history.

## Early Life
Born around 1964 in South Africa. Little is publicly known about his early life. He was a security guard who used his knowledge of the areas he patrolled to commit crimes undetected. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1996 and 1997, Maake committed a series of murders, rapes, and robberies in the greater Johannesburg area, particularly around Wemmer Pan. He attacked women walking alone or couples in parks. He used a hammer and knife as weapons. His crimes created widespread fear in Johannesburg communities. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Maake was arrested in December 1997 after being linked to the crimes through fingerprint evidence and witness testimony. He was convicted in 2000 of 27 murders, 26 rapes, and 14 robberies. He received 1,340 years in prison and 13 life sentences. [^3]
