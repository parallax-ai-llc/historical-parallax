## Summary

Louis Kahn was an American architect whose monumental buildings are characterized by massive geometric forms and a masterful integration of natural light. His Salk Institute, Kimbell Art Museum, and National Assembly Building in Dhaka are considered among the greatest buildings of the 20th century. His philosophical approach to architecture influenced generations of architects.
