## Summary

Lee Jong-suk is a South Korean actor and model born on September 14, 1989, in Yongin, Gyeonggi Province. He first gained widespread recognition as a fashion model — becoming the youngest male model to walk the Seoul Collection runway at age 16 — before transitioning into acting and establishing himself as one of South Korea's most bankable dramatic leads. His lean frame, distinctive features, and emotive screen presence made him a dual-threat talent in the Hallyu wave.

Over the course of the 2010s, Lee built an impressive television résumé through a string of high-rating melodramas and romance series. His ability to balance vulnerable, intellectual, and charismatic character types earned him a devoted fanbase across East and Southeast Asia, earning him consistent recognition at major Korean entertainment awards. He remains one of the few performers to maintain top-tier status in both the modeling and acting industries simultaneously.

## Early Life & Background

Lee Jong-suk was born in Yongin, South Korea, and grew up with an early interest in performance and fashion. He attended Hyangnam High School and later enrolled at Konkuk University, majoring in theater and film. His entry into modeling came in his mid-teens; at 16, he became the youngest male model to walk the Seoul Collection, South Korea's premier fashion runway event, setting an early record that foreshadowed his later prominence.

## Rise to Prominence

Lee made his acting debut in 2010 with a minor television role, gradually taking on more substantial parts in films and dramas. His breakout came with the 2012 school drama *School 2013*, where his portrayal of a rebellious but emotionally complex student earned wide critical praise and a Best New Actor award at the KBS Drama Awards. He followed this with *I Hear Your Voice* (2013), a fantasy-legal drama that became one of the highest-rated cable series of that year, cementing his leading-man status.

Subsequent hit dramas — including *Pinocchio* (2014–2015), *W* (2016), and *While You Were Sleeping* (2017) — consistently drew strong ratings and reinforced his reputation for choosing projects with inventive, genre-blending narratives. *W*, a meta-fictional romance between a webtoon character and a real-world woman, was particularly noted for its unconventional storytelling and attracted significant international viewership on streaming platforms.

## Key Achievements

- Youngest male model to walk the Seoul Collection (2005), at age 16.
- KBS Drama Awards Best New Actor for *School 2013* (2012).
- Multiple nominations and wins at the Baeksang Arts Awards, MBC Drama Awards, and SBS Drama Awards throughout 2013–2018.
- *While You Were Sleeping* (2017) achieved peak ratings above 12% on SBS, a strong figure for primetime drama.
- Completed mandatory South Korean military service (2019–2021) and returned to acting with the thriller series *Big Mouth* (2022), which posted competitive viewership numbers on MBC.

## Controversies & Criticism

In 2017, Lee faced allegations of workplace harassment made by a former model. His agency denied the claims, and no legal action resulted, but the accusations generated significant media coverage. Critics have also occasionally noted a tendency toward similar character archetypes across projects — the brooding, misunderstood intellectual — suggesting limited dramatic range, though supporters argue his consistent chemistry with co-stars sustains audience engagement.

## Legacy & Influence

Lee Jong-suk is widely credited as a key figure in the mid-2010s expansion of Korean drama viewership beyond traditional East Asian markets. His projects were among the early titles to gain strong traction on global streaming platforms, contributing to the infrastructure of international Hallyu fandom. As both model and actor, he helped normalize dual-industry careers for Korean male entertainers, influencing a generation of idol-actors who followed similar paths.
