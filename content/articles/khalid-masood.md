## Background

- Khalid Masood (also known by other names reported in press coverage) was a British man who carried out the 2017 Westminster attack in London.
- Public reporting describes a criminal history earlier in life and periods of contact with the justice system.
- UK authorities stated after the attack that he was not the subject of a live counter-terrorism investigation at the time of the incident.

## Westminster attack (22 March 2017)

- Masood drove a vehicle along Westminster Bridge, striking pedestrians.
- He then crashed the vehicle near the Palace of Westminster and proceeded on foot.
- He stabbed a police officer at the perimeter of Parliament.
- He was shot by armed police at the scene and later died from his injuries.

## Casualties

- Five people were killed in total, including the police officer and the attacker.
- Dozens of others were injured during the vehicle attack on the bridge.

## Investigation and official response

- UK counter-terrorism police led the investigation in the immediate aftermath.
- Authorities conducted searches and arrests in the days following the attack as they worked to establish motive, contacts, and any supporting network.
- Officials treated the incident as terrorism; public statements emphasized rapid response and the protection of Parliament and the public.

## High-level incident characteristics

### Attack mode

- The incident is widely categorized as a **vehicle-ramming** attack followed by a **knife assault**, a pattern seen in other low-complexity attacks.
- Such attacks can be carried out with limited planning and without specialized equipment.

### Location significance

- Westminster is a symbolic and high-security area given the presence of Parliament and major government institutions.
- The attack contributed to public and policy discussions about protecting crowded public spaces near national landmarks.
