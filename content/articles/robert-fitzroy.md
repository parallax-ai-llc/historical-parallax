## Summary

Robert FitzRoy was a British vice-admiral and meteorologist who captained HMS Beagle during Charles Darwin's famous voyage. He later pioneered weather forecasting, coined the term 'forecast,' and established the Meteorological Office (now Met Office). He developed early weather prediction charts and storm warnings that saved many lives at sea.
