## Summary

Malaysia Airlines suffered two major disasters in 2014: Flight MH370 disappeared with 239 aboard in March, and Flight MH17 was shot down over Ukraine killing 298 in July.

## Flight MH370 (Disappearance)

- **Nature of event**: MH370 is widely described as an aviation mystery involving a loss of contact and an aircraft that was not recovered during initial search phases.
- **Search effort**: The disappearance led to a multinational search across large areas of ocean, involving multiple governments and specialized maritime and aviation resources.
- **Debris findings**: Later, debris consistent with the aircraft was reported found in the Indian Ocean region, including on Réunion, informing hypotheses about the likely search area.

## Flight MH17 (Shootdown)

- **Conflict-zone risk**: MH17 was shot down while flying over eastern Ukraine during an active conflict, highlighting risks to civil aviation routing over contested airspace.
- **International investigation**: The incident prompted international investigative and legal efforts focused on determining responsibility and accountability.

## Broader Impact on Malaysia Airlines

- **Operational and reputational shock**: The two incidents in the same year created extraordinary reputational and operational pressure on the airline.
- **Corporate restructuring**: Public reporting frequently notes subsequent restructuring and changes in the airline’s ownership and operating model as part of recovery efforts.

## Aviation Safety and Policy Aftermath

- **Airspace management**: MH17 intensified debate about how airlines and regulators assess and communicate conflict-zone airspace risks.
- **Tracking and communications**: MH370 contributed to renewed focus on aircraft tracking, data communications, and the timeliness and transparency of incident information sharing.
