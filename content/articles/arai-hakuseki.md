## Summary

Arai Hakuseki was a Japanese Confucian scholar and statesman who advanced rational and empirical approaches to knowledge during the Edo period. He interviewed a captured Italian priest, Giovanni Battista Sidotti, producing Seiyo Kibun (Record of Western Things), one of the first Japanese works systematically documenting Western geography, religion, and technology.
