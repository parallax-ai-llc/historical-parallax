## Background and Education

- Panamanian lawyer and public figure who also worked as a novelist.
- Studied law and political science at the **University of Panama** and later attended the **London School of Economics** (per Wikipedia).[^1]

## Mossack Fonseca (Firm)

- In **1977**, co-founded the Panama City law firm **Mossack Fonseca** with Jürgen Mossack.[^1]
- The firm became a major global provider of offshore corporate services, operating through a network of international offices.[^1]
- In **March 2018**, the firm announced it would shut down in the aftermath of sustained legal and reputational fallout connected to the Panama Papers.[^1]

## Political and Public Roles

- Served as a **minister-counselor** to Panamanian President **Juan Carlos Varela** (per Wikipedia).[^1]
- Was associated with the **Panameñista Party** and at one point led the party, until removal in 2016 (per Wikipedia).[^1]

## Panama Papers and Legal Proceedings (High-Level)

- In **2016**, the leak of internal firm documents (“Panama Papers”) triggered global reporting on offshore structures and their use across jurisdictions.[^1]
- Panamanian authorities investigated the firm on suspicions including **money laundering** and related financial crimes (as described by Wikipedia).[^1]
- Wikipedia reports that Fonseca and Jürgen Mossack were **arrested in February 2017** and later **released on bail** in April 2017 while investigations proceeded.[^1]

## Literary Work

- Wikipedia describes Fonseca as an **award-winning novelist** who wrote novels, plays, and short stories.[^1]
- Reported to have won Panama’s **Ricardo Miró Prize** (a national literary award) for novels including *Dance of the Butterflies* (1994) and *Dream City* (1998).[^1]
