## Summary

Michael Johnson was the first to win the 200m/400m double at one Olympics (1996 Atlanta). He held both world records and won five Olympic golds.

## Early Career

Johnson competed for Baylor University and became one of the defining sprint athletes of the 1990s. His approach emphasized controlled speed and strong finishing strength, particularly in the 400 meters.

## Signature Performances

- 1996 Atlanta Olympics: achieved the rare 200m and 400m double, setting a then-world record in the 200m.
- 1999: set a 400m world record of 43.18 seconds, a mark that stood for many years.

## Major Championships

Across Olympics and World Championships, Johnson accumulated multiple gold medals and was recognized for consistent performance in global finals. His medal count and record-setting times made him a reference point for later quarter-milers and 200m specialists.

## Legacy

Johnson's records and championship double helped redefine expectations for sprint versatility. After retirement, he worked in media and commentary and has been involved in track-and-field promotion and athlete support.

## Notable Traits

Johnson was known for an upright running posture, powerful stride mechanics, and the ability to maintain form late in races. He also benefited from an era of increasing professionalism in training, sports science, and global competition.

## Context in Sprint History

His 200m/400m achievements are often used as a benchmark when comparing athletes who attempt to combine speed endurance (400m) with pure sprint speed (200m). Even as world records were later broken, Johnson's championship resume remained unusually complete.
