## Background

Steve Bannon is an American political operative and media figure who became nationally prominent through right-wing media and later through his work with Donald Trump’s 2016 presidential campaign and early administration.

Before entering frontline politics, he worked across business, finance, and entertainment-related projects, and was later associated with outlets and organizations that positioned themselves as part of a broader right-populist or nationalist movement.

## Role in the Trump Orbit

Bannon is most widely known for:

- senior involvement with the 2016 Trump campaign, particularly in the late stage of the race,
- serving in a senior White House role early in the Trump administration,
- advocating for a confrontational populist strategy on issues such as immigration, trade, and anti-establishment messaging.

His tenure and public commentary are frequently discussed in the context of ideological factionalism inside the administration, as well as the relationship between partisan media ecosystems and governing.

## Media and Political Organizing

Public reporting often links Bannon to right-wing media projects and to efforts aimed at movement-building, including support for various nationalist or populist-aligned candidates and initiatives in the United States and abroad.

These efforts have been controversial, with critics associating them with the normalization of extremist-adjacent rhetoric, while supporters describe them as insurgent politics against established institutions.

## Legal Issues (High-Level)

Bannon has faced multiple legal controversies in the United States. High-level summaries of widely reported issues include:

- a federal contempt of Congress conviction connected to non-compliance with a congressional subpoena related to the January 6 investigation,
- allegations and charges connected to fundraising efforts for private border-wall initiatives.
