## Summary

Kapil Dev captained India to their first Cricket World Cup victory in 1983 and became the leading Test wicket-taker. His 175 not out against Zimbabwe in the 1983 World Cup is legendary.

## Playing style

- Kapil Dev was a fast-bowling all-rounder known for athletic fielding and the ability to change a match with either wickets or quick scoring.
- He was often described as a natural hitter, effective in accelerating an innings rather than building slowly.
- As a captain, he is associated with an attacking approach that emphasized taking wickets and backing players under pressure.

## Records and recognition

- His career totals and longevity placed him among the most impactful Indian cricketers of his era.
- The 1983 World Cup win is widely treated as a turning point for cricket's popularity and commercial growth in India.
- Kapil Dev remains a reference point in discussions about the role of all-rounders and leadership in Indian cricket history.

## After retirement

- He continued to be visible in cricket through commentary, administration, and public appearances.
- His legacy is frequently invoked when comparing later Indian teams and captains to the 1983 generation.
