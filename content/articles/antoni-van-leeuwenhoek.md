## Summary

Antoni van Leeuwenhoek was a Dutch businessman and scientist who is known as the 'father of microbiology.' He was the first to observe and describe microorganisms using single-lens microscopes that he made himself. He observed bacteria, spermatozoa, blood cells, and many other microscopic structures for the first time. His meticulous observations opened an entirely new world of microscopic life to science.
