Former chief of the Joint Staff Department of China's Central Military Commission convicted of bribery. He was the highest-ranking active-duty military officer to fall in Xi's anti-corruption drive.

## Background

Fang Fenghui is a Peoples Liberation Army (PLA) general who rose through command roles in ground forces to senior posts within the Central Military Commission (CMC).

In Chinese civilmilitary governance, the CMC controls the armed forces; the Joint Staff Department (formerly the General Staff Department) is a key operational and planning body.

## Role in the PLA and CMC

As chief of the Joint Staff Department, Fang would have been associated with:

- Operational planning and readiness coordination across services
- Joint training, staff work, and military-to-military engagement mechanisms
- Implementation of structural reforms as the PLA reorganized theater commands in the mid-2010s

## Anti-corruption case (high level)

Fangs investigation and conviction occurred during a wide anti-corruption campaign that affected multiple senior PLA figures.

Typical elements emphasized in official reporting on PLA graft cases include:

- Abuse of authority connected to personnel appointments and promotions
- Receipt of bribes or illicit benefits
- Confiscation of illegal gains and loss of political status within the party-military system

## Significance

Fangs downfall was notable because it involved an active-duty, top-level staff officer rather than a retired figure, reinforcing the campaigns reach inside the operational command structure.

It also drew attention to persistent incentives for corruption in military personnel and procurement systems, issues Chinese defense reforms have repeatedly aimed to address.
