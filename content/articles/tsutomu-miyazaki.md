## Summary
Tsutomu Miyazaki, 'The Otaku Murderer,' murdered four girls aged 4-7 near Tokyo between 1988 and 1989. He committed necrophilia, cannibalism, and sent victims' remains to families. His crimes caused a moral panic about otaku culture in Japan. He was executed by hanging in 2008.

## Early Life
Born August 21, 1962, in Tokyo with deformed hands. Shunned by classmates, he withdrew into manga and anime. He was close to his grandfather, whose death in 1988 deeply affected him. His room contained thousands of videotapes. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between August 1988 and June 1989, Miyazaki kidnapped, murdered, and mutilated four young girls. He committed necrophilia and cannibalism. He sent charred bones and teeth of one victim to her family with taunting postcards. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Caught July 23, 1989, while assaulting a child in a park. A search of his home revealed extensive evidence. Convicted and sentenced to death in 1997, upheld in 2006. Executed by hanging June 17, 2008. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- Led to stronger child protection laws in Japan [^4]
- Improved prefectural police coordination
- Contributed to research on social isolation effects

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered four young girls [^5]
- Committed necrophilia and cannibalism on children
- Sent remains to grieving families
- Created unjust stigma against otaku culture
- Caused widespread parental fear
