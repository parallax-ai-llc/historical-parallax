## Additional Context

## Causes and Context (High Level)

World War I is commonly explained through a combination of alliance commitments, militarization, imperial competition, and nationalist movements. The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand in 1914 acted as a trigger within an already unstable geopolitical environment.

## Major Characteristics

- **Industrialized warfare:** heavy artillery, machine guns, and mass mobilization.
- **Trench systems:** especially on the Western Front, producing long stalemates and high casualties.
- **New weapons:** poison gas and early armored vehicles and aircraft.
- **Global dimension:** fighting extended beyond Europe through empires and colonies.

## Consequences

The war contributed to the collapse or transformation of major empires and reshaped borders and political systems. It also influenced economic instability, social change, and diplomatic arrangements that affected the interwar period.

## Memory and Legacy

WWI left a lasting cultural imprint through literature, memorials, and public commemoration, and is frequently studied as a precursor to later conflicts in the twentieth century.
