## Summary

Shuji Nakamura is a Japanese-American engineer and physicist who shared the 2014 Nobel Prize in Physics for the invention of efficient blue LEDs. Working at Nichia Corporation, he independently developed the first commercially viable blue LED and blue laser diode. He sued Nichia over compensation for his invention and later became a professor at UC Santa Barbara.
