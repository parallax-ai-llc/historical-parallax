## Summary

Yi I (Yulgok) was a Joseon Dynasty Neo-Confucian philosopher and reformer who proposed a 100,000-strong military preparedness plan years before the Japanese invasion. His systematic approach to governance, education, and military technology reform demonstrated analytical thinking that anticipated scientific policy. He appears on the South Korean 5,000 won banknote.
