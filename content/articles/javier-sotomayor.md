## Summary

Javier Sotomayor holds the high jump world record of 2.45m. He won 1992 Olympic gold.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1992 | Won Olympic gold at Barcelona |
| 1993 | Set world record of 2.45m |

## Athletic Profile

- **Event**: Men's high jump.
- **Known for**: Exceptional combination of speed on the approach and explosive takeoff, producing record-level clearance heights.

## Major Achievements

- Olympic gold (1992) and additional major medals at world-level championships (often highlighted in career summaries).
- World record: 2.45 m (set in 1993), still the outdoor men's high jump world record.

## Record Significance

Sotomayor's 2.45 m mark is frequently cited as one of track and field's most iconic records because:

- it has endured across multiple generations of elite high jumpers,
- it sits at the extreme end of human performance for the event, and
- it serves as a practical "ceiling" benchmark in comparisons of technique and athleticism.

## Controversy and Later Career

Public accounts also note a doping suspension period late in his competitive years. Overviews typically treat this as part of the complexity of his legacy while still recognizing his competitive results and the official record status.
