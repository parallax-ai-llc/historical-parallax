## Additional Context

### Name and Duplicate Entry Note

- **Dorothy Johnson Vaughan** is commonly referred to as **Dorothy Vaughan**.
- This page functions as a **full-name variant** entry; details overlap with other Dorothy Vaughan records in the set.

### Work at NACA/NASA

- Vaughan worked at the **National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics (NACA)** and later **NASA**, at the Langley Research Center.
- She became a supervisor in the West Area computing group, leading teams of mathematicians who performed complex calculations supporting aeronautics and early spaceflight research.

### Transition to Electronic Computing

- As NASA shifted from human "computers" to electronic computers, Vaughan is noted for proactively learning new skills.
- She is associated with the adoption of programming (including **FORTRAN**) to remain effective during this technological transition and to help her team adapt.

### Cultural Recognition

- Vaughan’s career became widely known to the general public through the book and film **"Hidden Figures"**, which highlighted the contributions of Black women mathematicians and engineers in the U.S. space program.

### Legacy

- Vaughan is often remembered for combining **technical excellence** with leadership in a segregated workplace, and for helping her teams navigate institutional change.
- In educational contexts, her story is frequently used to illustrate how computing history includes essential contributions from people who were long under-credited.
