## Summary

Wilhelm Ostwald was a Baltic German chemist who won the 1909 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his work on catalysis, chemical equilibria, and reaction velocities. He is considered one of the founders of physical chemistry alongside van 't Hoff and Arrhenius. He developed the Ostwald process for manufacturing nitric acid, which became industrially important. He also made significant contributions to color theory and philosophy of science.
