## Summary

Brigham Young was the second president of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints and led the Mormon migration to Utah. He organized the settlement of over 350 communities in the American West. He served as the first governor of Utah Territory. He was a controversial figure known for his leadership ability, polygamy practices, and colonization efforts.
