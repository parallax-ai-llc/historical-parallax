Algerian Berber singer and activist assassinated during Algeria's Black Decade civil war.

## Background

- Matoub Lounès (also spelled Matoub Lounes) was a Kabyle (Amazigh/Berber) singer-songwriter from Algeria.
- He became widely associated with advocacy for Amazigh language and cultural rights, and with secular, democratic politics.
- His public prominence grew during periods of intense political conflict in Algeria.

## Historical Context

- The 1990s Algerian Civil War is often referred to as the "Black Decade" due to widespread violence.
- Artists, journalists, and political figures were frequently targeted, and public speech carried significant risk.

## Activism and Public Role

- Beyond music, he was a visible activist and commentator on identity, language policy, and civil liberties.
- He is commonly remembered as a cultural symbol for many in Kabylia and the wider Amazigh diaspora.

## Key Events (selected)

- 1994: Abducted by an armed Islamist group and later released (widely reported as the GIA in major accounts).
- 1998-06-25: Killed in an attack while traveling by car in the Tizi Ouzou region.

## Legacy

- His death prompted large public demonstrations and remains a major reference point in modern Kabyle/Amazigh cultural history.
- His work is often cited in discussions of minority-language recognition and cultural resistance.
