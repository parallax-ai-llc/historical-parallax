## Summary

Sierra Leonean rebel commander known as 'Mosquito' who was one of the most brutal leaders of the RUF during the civil war. He ordered mass amputations and the use of child soldiers. Killed in Liberia, reportedly on Charles Taylor's orders.

## Background

Sam Bockarie rose to prominence during the **Sierra Leone Civil War (1991–2002)** as a senior commander in the Revolutionary United Front (RUF). The conflict became notorious for widespread atrocities against civilians, including forced labor, sexual violence, and the recruitment of children as fighters.

## Role in the Conflict (High Level)

Public accounts typically associate Bockarie with:

- command responsibility for RUF operations during some of the war’s most brutal phases,
- campaigns intended to terrorize populations and disrupt state control,
- internal factional dynamics and shifting alliances across Sierra Leone and neighboring Liberia.

## Accountability and Aftermath

- The post-war period included the creation of the **Special Court for Sierra Leone (SCSL)** to prosecute those bearing the greatest responsibility for serious crimes.
- Reporting about Bockarie’s death has often connected it to political and security calculations within Liberia during Charles Taylor’s presidency.

## Significance

Bockarie is frequently referenced as an example of how armed groups can sustain control through extreme violence, and how accountability efforts can be undermined when key suspects die or are killed before arrest and trial.
