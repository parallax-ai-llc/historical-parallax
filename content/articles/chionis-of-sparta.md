## Summary

Chionis of Sparta was an ancient Greek athlete who won Olympic titles in the stadion and long jump at three consecutive Olympiads (664-656 BC).

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 664 BC | Won first Olympic stadion title |
| 660 BC | Won second Olympic title |
| 656 BC | Won third consecutive title |

## Events and Records (High Level)

- Ancient sources associate Chionis with sprint victories (notably the stadion), and sometimes with additional events depending on the tradition cited.
- He is also linked in later accounts to an exceptional long-jump distance, often discussed by historians as difficult to interpret because ancient measurement and event formats differ from modern track and field.

## Historical Sources

- Knowledge of early Olympic victors comes primarily from later compilations and antiquarian writers.
- As with many archaic-period athletes, details beyond victories (training, biography, exact performances) are sparse and sometimes contested.

## Significance

- Chionis is frequently mentioned among the earliest well-known Spartan Olympic champions.
- His repeated victories are used to illustrate the prestige of Panhellenic games and the competitive prominence of certain city-states.

## Legacy

- Modern discussions of ancient athletics often cite Chionis when explaining the limits of reconstructing performances from literary sources.
- He appears in lists of notable early Olympians as a symbol of Sparta’s athletic tradition.
- Dates are typically given in BC and rely on reconstructed Olympiad chronologies.
- Surviving accounts reflect later historical memory as much as contemporaneous record-keeping.
