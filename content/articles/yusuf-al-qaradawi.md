## Summary

Yusuf al-Qaradawi was an Egyptian-Qatari Islamic scholar who was one of the most influential Sunni clerics of the modern era. He was president of the International Union of Muslim Scholars and hosted the widely watched Al Jazeera program Sharia and Life. He wrote over 120 books, including The Lawful and the Prohibited in Islam. He balanced between traditional Islamic scholarship and modern reform, though his views on various issues provoked controversy.
