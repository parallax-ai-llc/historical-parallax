## Summary

South African private security guard who shot and killed at least 39 people, mostly Black South Africans, while supposedly guarding properties in East London during apartheid. He was convicted of 7 murders.

## Context

Van Schoor's crimes were committed in the late apartheid era, a period marked by systemic racial segregation and violence in South Africa. Accounts of the case have often highlighted the racialized environment in which his actions occurred.

## Crimes and convictions

- Investigators linked a series of shootings in the East London area to van Schoor.
- He was convicted in court of multiple murders (commonly reported as seven), while other deaths were widely alleged but not all proven to the criminal standard required for conviction.
- Sentencing reports typically describe a very long prison term, followed by parole after serving a portion of the sentence.

## Public attention and later discussion

The case has been referenced in discussions about private security, vigilantism, and how apartheid-era institutions treated violence against Black South Africans. Later media coverage has revisited the case as an example of impunity and structural injustice.
