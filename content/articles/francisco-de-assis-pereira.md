## Summary

Brazilian serial killer known as the Park Maniac who raped and murdered at least 11 women in Sao Paulos Parque do Estado in 1998. He posed as a modeling agent to lure victims.

## Additional Context

### Background and deception

- Pereira exploited the aspirations of young women by presenting himself as a talent scout or modeling agent.
- This approach reduced suspicion and created opportunities for private meetings, a common feature in crimes involving impersonation or “confidence” tactics.

### Crimes and location

- The attacks were associated with Parque do Estado (State Park) in São Paulo, an area where isolated terrain increased vulnerability and hindered immediate discovery.
- Public reporting typically emphasizes the combination of sexual violence and murder, with multiple victims in a short time frame.

### Investigation and sentencing (high level)

- His arrest followed intensified policing and public attention as disappearances and murders accumulated.
- Brazilian sentencing in such cases can include very long aggregate prison terms, reflecting multiple counts and aggravating circumstances.
