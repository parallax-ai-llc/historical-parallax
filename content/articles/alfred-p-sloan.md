## Summary

Alfred P. Sloan served as president, chairman, and CEO of General Motors from 1923 to 1956, transforming it into the world's largest corporation. He invented the modern concept of a decentralized corporation with multiple divisions. His strategy of offering 'a car for every purse and purpose' helped GM surpass Ford. His management principles, documented in 'My Years with General Motors,' influenced business education worldwide.
