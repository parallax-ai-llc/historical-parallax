## Context

Yoshimi Tanaka is described in public sources as a member associated with the **Japanese Red Army**, a militant left-wing organization active during the late 1960s and 1970s. The group became internationally known for violent actions, including attacks and high-profile incidents involving aviation and hostage situations.

## Japan Airlines Flight 351 (“Yodogō” hijacking)

High-level accounts of the 1970 incident describe:

- **Target:** Japan Airlines Flight 351 (a domestic flight)
- **Action:** Hijacking and forced diversion of the aircraft
- **Outcome:** The aircraft was flown out of Japan and ultimately reached **North Korea**

The event is notable in Japanese postwar history as one of the first major airline hijackings involving Japanese militants, and it became a reference point in later discussions of domestic extremism and aviation security.

## Aftermath and significance

- The incident helped accelerate **security responses** around civil aviation.
- It remains a widely cited episode in the broader narrative of 20th-century **Japanese political violence**.
- Public reporting often focuses on the group’s fate after arriving in North Korea and the long-running political and legal complexities that followed.
