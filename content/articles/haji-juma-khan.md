## Summary

Afghan drug lord considered one of the biggest heroin and opium traffickers in the world. He was linked to both the Taliban and Afghan government officials. Arrested in Indonesia and extradited to the US in 2008.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Additional Context

- **Name and sourcing note:** The Wikipedia link in the frontmatter ("Haji_Juma_Khan") may not resolve as an article at all times; a closely related profile is published under the title **"Juma Khan"**.
- **Region:** Reporting commonly places him in Afghanistan's southwest (including Nimruz Province) and in cross-border trafficking corridors linking Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Iran.
- **Smuggling hub:** One widely reported account describes Bahramcha (near the tri-border area) becoming an important node for narcotics and arms smuggling after 2001.

## Detention and Status (High-Level)

- **US Treasury/OFAC designation:** The "Haji Juma Khan Organization" has been listed under US sanctions authorities, reflecting a view of the network as a significant trafficking entity.
- **Custody without public trial details:** Some reporting emphasizes the unusual nature of his detention and the scarcity of publicly available court proceedings compared to other high-profile trafficking cases.
- **Public reporting of release:** Media accounts have described a quiet release after years in US custody.
