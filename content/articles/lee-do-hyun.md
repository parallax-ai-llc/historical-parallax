## Summary

Lee Do-hyun is a South Korean actor born on April 6, 1995, who has rapidly established himself as one of his generation's most compelling dramatic performers. Since his debut in 2017, he has built a reputation for emotional range and an ability to inhabit psychologically complex characters, earning recognition across both television and film. His work spans romantic dramas, psychological thrillers, and period pieces, demonstrating a versatility rare among actors of his age.

His career trajectory accelerated sharply in the early 2020s, driven by a string of critically acclaimed series that attracted large domestic and international audiences on streaming platforms. Lee's performances have consistently drawn praise for their nuance and authenticity, helping elevate the projects he joins beyond conventional genre expectations. He is widely regarded as a key figure in the ongoing global expansion of Korean screen entertainment.

## Early Life & Background

Lee Do-hyun was born in Ulsan, South Korea, and later relocated to Seoul to pursue an acting career. He studied at Seoul Institute of the Arts, one of South Korea's most prestigious performing arts institutions, where he trained formally in acting before entering the industry. His early interest in performance developed during his teenage years, and he pursued the discipline with focused commitment rather than stumbling into the profession by chance.

## Rise to Prominence

Lee made his acting debut in 2017 with minor television roles, gradually accumulating experience across several dramas. His first significant breakthrough came with the 2019 fantasy romance series *Hotel Del Luna*, in which he played a supporting role that introduced him to a wider audience. The following year, he secured his first leading role in *18 Again* (2020), a body-swap romantic comedy in which he played a 37-year-old man inhabiting his 18-year-old body. The performance required him to convincingly portray a middle-aged man's emotional interior within a youthful physicality — a challenge he met to considerable critical acclaim.

His international profile surged with *Youth of May* (2021), a tragic romance set against the backdrop of the 1980 Gwangju Uprising, and more dramatically with *Melancholia* (2021), in which he played a high school student in a morally fraught relationship with a teacher. The psychological complexity of his roles in this period cemented his standing as a serious dramatic actor.

## Key Achievements

Lee's most acclaimed role to date came in *The Glory* (2022–2023), Netflix's revenge thriller in which he played Joo Yeo-jeong, a surgeon entangled in an elaborate scheme of retribution. The series became a global phenomenon on Netflix, ranking among the platform's most-watched non-English titles. His performance alongside Song Hye-kyo drew particular praise. In 2024, he starred in the period drama *When the Phone Rings*, further broadening his dramatic range.

## Controversies & Criticism

Lee Do-hyun's career has been largely free of significant controversy. Some observers have noted that his early typecasting in romantic leading-man roles initially obscured his deeper dramatic capabilities, a perception his subsequent project choices have systematically dismantled.

## Legacy & Influence

Lee Do-hyun represents a generation of Korean actors who have benefited from and contributed to the global streaming era. His willingness to take on morally ambiguous, emotionally demanding roles has helped shape audience expectations for Korean drama. As of 2026, he remains among the most in-demand actors in the Korean industry, with his profile continuing to grow internationally.
