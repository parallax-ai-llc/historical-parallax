## Summary

Tina Turner (born Anna Mae Bullock; November 26, 1939 – May 24, 2023) was an American singer, songwriter, and dancer widely regarded as the "Queen of Rock 'n' Roll." Her career spanned more than six decades, encompassing R&B, soul, rock, and pop, and she became one of the best-selling music artists of all time with an estimated 100 million records sold worldwide.

Turner's story is one of extraordinary resilience. After escaping an abusive marriage and professional partnership with Ike Turner in the mid-1970s, she staged one of the most celebrated comebacks in music history, becoming a global superstar in her own right during the 1980s. Her 1984 album *Private Dancer* and its singles redefined her as a solo artist and cemented her cultural status.

Her influence extended beyond music into fashion, live performance, and popular culture. She broke barriers as a Black woman achieving mainstream crossover success in rock and pop at a time when such paths were rarely available, and her autobiography and stage productions have inspired millions around the world.

## Early Life & Background

Anna Mae Bullock was born on November 26, 1939, in Nutbush, Tennessee, to Floyd Richard Bullock and Zelma Priscilla Currie. She grew up in rural poverty, and her childhood was marked by instability — her parents separated when she was young, and she was largely raised by her grandmother. She sang in her local church choir from an early age, developing the powerful vocal style that would define her career.

After relocating to St. Louis, Missouri, as a teenager to be near her mother, she encountered Ike Turner and his Kings of Rhythm at a nightclub. She joined the group in the late 1950s, quickly becoming its lead vocalist.

## Rise to Prominence

The duo Ike & Tina Turner rose to prominence in the 1960s with a high-energy live act that built a devoted following on the R&B circuit. Their 1971 cover of Creedence Clearwater Revival's "Proud Mary" became their biggest hit, reaching No. 4 on the Billboard Hot 100 and winning a Grammy Award for Best R&B Vocal Performance by a Duo or Group. They were nominated for Grammy Awards multiple times and released dozens of albums throughout the 1960s and early 1970s.

In 1976, Tina left Ike following years of physical and emotional abuse. She rebuilt her career through relentless touring and television appearances, and in 1984, *Private Dancer* — featuring the singles "What's Love Got to Do with It" and "Better Be Good to Me" — sold over 20 million copies globally. "What's Love Got to Do with It" reached No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100 and won three Grammy Awards, including Record of the Year.

## Key Achievements

- Eight Grammy Awards, including a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award (1991)
- Inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame twice: with Ike Turner (1991) and as a solo artist (2021)
- Her 1987–1988 *Break Every Rule* World Tour grossed over $28 million and set attendance records
- Her 2008–2009 *Tina!: 50th Anniversary Tour* grossed over $318 million, one of the highest-grossing concert tours ever at that time
- *Private Dancer* (1984) and *Break Every Rule* (1986) both achieved multi-platinum status across multiple countries

## Controversies & Criticism

Turner's marriage to Ike Turner was marked by well-documented domestic violence, details of which she disclosed publicly in her 1986 autobiography *I, Tina* and the 1993 biopic *What's Love Got to Do with It*. The revelations brought significant attention to domestic abuse and her courage in speaking out was widely praised, though some critics noted that the biopic's portrayal of Ike was one-dimensional. In her later years, Turner expressed that she preferred to focus on healing rather than revisiting the trauma in public forums.

## Legacy & Influence

Tina Turner's influence on popular music and performance is immeasurable. Artists including Beyoncé, Mick Jagger, and Oprah Winfrey have cited her as a primary inspiration. Her stage presence — defined by powerful vocals, athleticism, and charisma — set a new standard for live performance. The 2018 Broadway musical *Tina – The Tina Turner Musical* extended her story to new generations.

She became a Swiss citizen in 2013 and renounced her U.S. citizenship in 2013, settling in Küsnacht, Switzerland. She died on May 24, 2023, at age 83, following a long illness. Tributes poured in from across the music world, affirming her status as one of the most enduring and transformative artists of the twentieth century.
