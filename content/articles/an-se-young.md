## Summary

An Se-young won the Olympic gold medal in women's singles badminton at the 2024 Paris Olympics. She is one of the youngest world-class badminton players.

## Background

An Se-young emerged from South Korea’s competitive junior badminton system and quickly transitioned to senior-level international tournaments. Early results on the BWF circuit helped establish her as a leading women’s singles player.

## Playing Style (High-Level)

- **Speed and coverage:** Known for quick movement and the ability to sustain long rallies.
- **Defensive consistency:** Frequently turns defense into offense through patient shot selection.
- **Tactical discipline:** Adjusts tempo and placement rather than relying on raw power alone.

## Notable Achievements

Beyond the 2024 Olympic title, she has been recognized for winning or contending in major international events and for holding a top global ranking during her early 20s.

## Significance

Her Olympic win is often discussed as part of South Korea’s broader strength in badminton, alongside a tradition of elite singles and doubles players. For many observers, her rise also reflects the increasing depth and athleticism in modern women’s singles.
