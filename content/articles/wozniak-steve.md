## Background

- Commonly known as **Steve Wozniak** (Stephen Gary Wozniak), an American computer engineer and inventor.
- Grew up in the San Jose, California area and developed an early interest in electronics and computing.

## Key Contributions

- **Apple I (1976):** Designed and hand-built the hardware that became one of the first single-board personal computers.
- **Apple II (1977):** Led much of the engineering for the Apple II, a mass-market personal computer widely credited with helping popularize home computing.
- Known for practical, cost-effective engineering choices that made early personal computers more accessible and reliable.

## Career Notes

- **Apple Computer co-founder (1976):** Co-founded Apple with Steve Jobs (and Ronald Wayne in the earliest formation).
- After Apple’s early success, stepped away from day-to-day engineering leadership and pursued independent projects and philanthropy.
- Has been active as a public speaker and advocate for technology education.

## Legacy and Impact

- Frequently cited as a foundational engineer of the personal-computer era, especially for the Apple I and Apple II designs.
- Remembered for emphasizing engineering craftsmanship and user-focused product simplicity.
