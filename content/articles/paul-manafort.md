## Summary

Trump's former campaign chair convicted of tax fraud, bank fraud, and failing to register as foreign agent.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2000s–2014 | Advises Ukrainian political figures, including work connected to Viktor Yanukovych and the Party of Regions. |
| 2016 | Serves as chairman of Donald Trump's presidential campaign for part of the election cycle. |
| 2017 | Faces scrutiny and indictment related to lobbying disclosure (FARA) and financial activity. |
| 2018 | Convicted in federal court on counts including tax fraud and bank fraud; later pleads guilty in a separate federal case. |
| 2019 | Sentenced in federal court; begins serving prison time. |

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- **Role in U.S. politics:** Manafort became a prominent Republican political consultant and lobbyist, later taking a senior role in the 2016 Trump campaign.[^1]
- **Foreign consulting/lobbying:** His work included political consulting tied to Ukrainian interests, which later became a focus of U.S. legal scrutiny, including questions about disclosure under U.S. foreign-agent rules.[^1]
- **Criminal convictions:** In 2018 he was convicted in federal court on multiple financial counts (commonly summarized as tax and bank fraud-related offenses). He also entered a guilty plea in a separate federal case.[^1]
- **Sentencing and outcome:** He received federal sentences and ultimately received a presidential pardon in 2020.[^1]

## Why He Matters

Manafort is often cited as a high-profile example of how **political consulting, international influence work, and financial arrangements** can collide with U.S. disclosure requirements and federal financial crime enforcement.
