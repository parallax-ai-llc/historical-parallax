## Summary

Yi I (pen name Yulgok) was a Korean Neo-Confucian philosopher and statesman who is considered one of the two greatest Korean Confucian thinkers alongside Yi Hwang. He emphasized the inseparability of li (principle) and qi (material force), differing from Toegye's emphasis on li. He also served in government, proposing practical reforms including military strengthening. He passed all the civil service examinations with the highest scores nine times.
