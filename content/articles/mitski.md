## Summary

Mitski Miyawaki, known professionally as Mitski, is an American singer-songwriter born in Japan whose emotionally raw indie rock and art pop have earned her critical acclaim and a devoted following since the mid-2010s. Her music explores themes of loneliness, identity, desire, and cultural displacement with unusual directness, drawing comparisons to artists like Liz Phair and PJ Harvey. Over six studio albums, she has built a reputation as one of the most distinct voices in contemporary indie music.

Her 2016 album *Puberty 2* and 2018's *Be the Cowboy* elevated her from college radio darling to mainstream critical recognition, with the latter debuting at number 51 on the Billboard 200. The 2022 single "Never Gonna Give It Up" and album *Laurel Hell* further expanded her audience, while "My Love Mine All Mine" became a viral phenomenon in 2023, reaching number 19 on the Billboard Hot 100.

## Early Life & Background

Mitski was born on September 27, 1990, in Mito, Ibaraki, Japan, to a Japanese mother and an American father who worked for the U.S. government. Her upbringing was nomadic — she lived in roughly 13 countries before settling in the United States, including stints in Japan, Turkey, Malaysia, the Democratic Republic of Congo, and China. This itinerant childhood, often marked by cultural isolation and language barriers, became foundational to her artistic perspective.

She studied music at the State University of New York at Purchase, where she recorded her first two albums — *Lush* (2012) and *Retired from Sad, New Career in Business* (2013) — as part of her senior thesis project. Both were self-released and demonstrated an early command of orchestral arrangement alongside personal songwriting.

## Rise to Prominence

Mitski signed with Double Double Whammy and released *Bury Me at Makeout Creek* in 2014, a guitar-heavy record that gained significant traction in indie circles. *Puberty 2* (2016), released on Dead Oceans, was named album of the year by several publications including Pitchfork, which gave it a rare 9.0 rating. The album's track "Your Best American Girl" became a defining song of the era, its music video accumulating millions of views.

*Be the Cowboy* (2018) saw Mitski working with producer Patrick Hyland to craft a more controlled, theatrical sound. It debuted at number 51 on the Billboard 200 and cemented her as a critical favorite. After extensive touring, she announced a hiatus from social media in 2019, citing burnout.

She returned with *Laurel Hell* in February 2022, which debuted at number 5 on the Billboard 200 — her highest chart position to date.

## Key Achievements

- Six studio albums released between 2012 and 2022
- *Laurel Hell* debuted at number 5 on the Billboard 200 (2022)
- "My Love Mine All Mine" reached number 19 on the Billboard Hot 100 (2023)
- Pitchfork named *Puberty 2* among the best albums of the 2010s
- NPR included her in its list of the 200 greatest songs by 21st-century women and nonbinary artists

## Controversies & Criticism

Mitski has spoken publicly about industry pressures and emotional exhaustion from touring, particularly in a 2019 tweet announcing her social media break, which prompted significant fan discussion about artist sustainability. Some critics noted that her 2019 hiatus raised questions about parasocial dynamics between artists and audiences online. Her music has also occasionally been reduced to meme formats on TikTok and other platforms, a phenomenon she has addressed with ambivalence in interviews.

## Legacy & Influence

Mitski is widely credited with influencing a generation of indie and alternative artists, particularly women and artists of Asian descent navigating American identity. Her unflinching lyrical style and theatrical live performances have set a template for emotional authenticity in indie rock. "My Love Mine All Mine" achieving mainstream chart success without major label backing was widely cited as a marker of changing music industry dynamics.
