## Summary

Ibuka Masaru was a Japanese electronics engineer who co-founded Sony Corporation with Morita Akio. He was the technical genius behind Sony's early innovations, including Japan's first tape recorder, transistor radio, and the Trinitron color television. His engineering vision drove Sony's transformation from a small repair shop to a global electronics giant.
