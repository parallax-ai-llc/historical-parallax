## Summary

Last Shah of Iran whose authoritarian rule and SAVAK secret police suppressed dissent. His modernization programs alienated the clergy. Overthrown in the 1979 Islamic Revolution. His admission to the US triggered the Iran hostage crisis.

## Historical Context

Mohammad Reza Pahlavi ruled Iran during the Cold War era, a period in which Iran held strategic importance in regional geopolitics and energy markets. His reign is commonly discussed alongside modernization efforts, state centralization, and the political role of the monarchy.

## The 1979 Iranian Revolution

His overthrow in 1979 is a central turning point in modern Iranian history. The revolution replaced the monarchy with an Islamic Republic and reshaped Iran's domestic politics and its relations with the United States and many regional actors.

## Politics, Reform, and Opposition

Accounts of the Shah's rule often highlight both development initiatives and political repression. Debates over the period typically focus on governance, civil liberties, and the effectiveness and social consequences of rapid economic and cultural change.
