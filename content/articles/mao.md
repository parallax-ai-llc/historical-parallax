## Overview

Mao Zedong (often written as Mao Tse-tung in older Western romanization) was the paramount leader of the People's Republic of China from its founding in 1949 until his death in 1976. He was a revolutionary theorist and political leader whose ideas were codified as "Mao Zedong Thought" and became central to the Chinese Communist Party's governing ideology.

## Major Policies and Campaigns

- **Land reform (early 1950s):** Redistribution of land and suppression of "landlord" classes; accompanied by widespread political violence.
- **Collectivization:** Consolidation of agriculture into cooperatives and then communes, reducing private farming.
- **Great Leap Forward (1958–1962):** Rapid industrialization and communal agriculture drive; policy failures, coercion, and inflated reporting contributed to a famine with tens of millions of deaths in scholarly estimates.
- **Cultural Revolution (1966–1976):** Attempt to reassert ideological control and purge perceived "capitalist roaders"; empowered radical youth (Red Guards), disrupted education and institutions, and led to persecution, imprisonment, and deaths.

## Governance and Political Style

- Emphasized continuous revolution, class struggle, and mass mobilization as tools of governance.
- Used party campaigns, political labeling, and self-criticism sessions to enforce conformity.
- Maintained a strong personal cult of personality, particularly during the Cultural Revolution.

## International Role

- Led China through the Korean War period and early Cold War alignment with the Soviet Union.
- Relationship with the USSR deteriorated into the Sino-Soviet split.
- U.S.–China rapprochement occurred in the early 1970s, including Nixon's 1972 visit, reshaping Cold War geopolitics.

## Legacy (High-Level)

Mao remains one of the most consequential and polarizing figures of the 20th century. He is credited by supporters with unifying China after decades of war and upheaval and establishing a modern state framework, while critics emphasize the immense human suffering and social damage linked to his campaigns and political repression.

## Key Terms

- **Maoism:** A variant of Marxism–Leninism emphasizing peasant-based revolution, protracted people's war, and mass-line politics.
- **Mao Zedong Thought:** Official ideological contribution attributed to Mao and incorporated into CCP doctrine.
