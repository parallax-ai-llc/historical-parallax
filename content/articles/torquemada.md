## Summary

First Grand Inquisitor of the Spanish Inquisition who oversaw the torture and execution of thousands of alleged heretics. He engineered the expulsion of Jews from Spain affecting 200,000 people.

## Background

- Torquemada was a Dominican friar who rose to prominence in the court of Ferdinand II of Aragon and Isabella I of Castile.
- His influence grew during a period of state centralization and heightened religious scrutiny in late-medieval Spain.

## Spanish Inquisition and methods

- As the first Grand Inquisitor, he helped formalize inquisitorial procedures across multiple tribunals.
- The institution used investigation, denunciations, and interrogation, including torture in some cases, within a legal-religious framework of the era.
- Public ceremonies known as autos-da-fé were used to announce sentences and impose punishments.

## 1492 expulsion and broader policy

- The 1492 Alhambra Decree ordered Jews to convert or leave the kingdoms; Torquemada is often described as an influential advocate of the policy.
- Estimates for the number affected vary across sources and depend on how historians count departures, conversions, and later expulsions.

## Legacy and historiography

- Torquemada’s name became synonymous with religious persecution in later European polemics and popular culture.
- Modern historians distinguish between Torquemada’s personal role and the broader political, social, and religious forces driving the Inquisition.
