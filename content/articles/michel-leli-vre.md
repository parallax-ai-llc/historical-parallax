## Context

- Michel Lelièvre is widely known in connection with the **Marc Dutroux affair**, one of Belgium’s most notorious criminal cases of the 1990s.
- In that case, Dutroux was arrested in 1996 and later convicted in 2004 for kidnappings, sexual abuse, and murders; several associates were tried as accomplices.

## Role in the Dutroux case (high-level)

- Sources describing the case generally characterize Lelièvre as an **accomplice** who assisted Dutroux in the abduction and captivity of victims.
- Public reporting on the trial emphasized the distinction between the principal offender (Dutroux) and accomplices who supported logistics, concealment, or detention.

## Legal outcome

- Lelièvre was convicted in **2004** as an accomplice in the Dutroux case.
- He received a **multi-decade prison sentence** (commonly reported as **25 years** in summaries of the case’s accomplices).

## Why the case mattered in Belgium

- The Dutroux investigation and trial became a national trauma and a major stress test for Belgian policing and justice institutions.
- Public anger at investigative failures and perceived systemic issues contributed to large-scale civic protest (often referenced as the **White March**) and later reforms.
