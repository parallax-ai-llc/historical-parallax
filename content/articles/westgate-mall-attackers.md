## Summary

Al-Shabaab terrorists who attacked the Westgate shopping mall in Nairobi killing 67.

## Career Timeline

## Background and Context

- **Group attribution:** The attack was widely attributed to **al-Shabaab**, a Somalia-based militant Islamist group that has carried out attacks in Somalia and neighboring countries.
- **Stated rationale:** Al-Shabaab framed the assault as retaliation for Kenya’s military involvement in Somalia and Kenya’s role in regional counterterrorism efforts.
- **Target selection:** Westgate was a high-profile commercial center in Nairobi, and the attack aimed to create mass casualties, fear, and extensive media attention.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- The assault began on **21 September 2013**, with attackers entering the mall and opening fire.
- A large-scale emergency response followed, including evacuations, medical response, and security operations.
- The siege ended after several days; public reporting differed on the number of attackers, whether they were killed, and the precise sequence of events during the final clearance.

## Aftermath and Significance

- **Human impact:** The attack resulted in dozens of deaths, many injuries, and trauma for survivors and first responders.
- **Security and policy:** Westgate influenced public debate in Kenya on intelligence coordination, crisis response, and preparedness for mass-casualty attacks.
- **Ongoing uncertainty:** Because this entry groups the perpetrators as a collective (“attackers”), it is best read as a pointer to the broader Westgate incident and its documentation rather than a definitive roster of individuals.
