## Summary

British spree killer who shot three people in Northumberland in 2010 after release from prison, killing one. He led police on a week-long manhunt before shooting himself.

## Spree and Manhunt (High Level)

- The 2010 attacks in Northumberland involved targeted shootings connected to Moat’s personal relationships and grievances.
- The incident included the shooting of a police officer and generated a major multi-day search operation.
- The manhunt became a widely covered UK media event, with large-scale police deployment and extensive public attention.

## End of the Standoff

- Moat was eventually located near Rothbury, where police contained the area and negotiated while maintaining a security perimeter.
- The situation ended when Moat shot himself; he later died from his injuries.
- The case prompted debate about media reporting during active police operations and about the management of high-risk offenders after release.

## Public and Institutional Response

- The incident prompted reviews of police tactics, negotiation strategies, and firearms response procedures.
- It also raised questions about risk assessment for individuals with histories of violence and instability.
- Media coverage during the unfolding manhunt became a major point of criticism, including concerns about sensationalism and operational security.

## Notes

- The case became shorthand in the UK for an intense, high-profile armed manhunt.
- Official reviews and commentary emphasized balancing public information needs with the risks of live operational coverage.
