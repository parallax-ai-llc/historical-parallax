### Khmer Rouge Foreign Relations (1975–1979)

The Khmer Rouge (Democratic Kampuchea) maintained a foreign ministry and external representations while it carried out mass violence inside Cambodia. In broad terms, the regime’s diplomatic activity included:

- Managing relationships with key state sponsors and counterparts (most notably the People’s Republic of China).
- Maintaining formal ties, recognition, and state-to-state communications.
- Supporting propaganda efforts abroad and controlling the regime’s narrative.
- Liaising with aligned parties, movements, and sympathetic organizations internationally.

### Diplomatic Roles Under Authoritarian Regimes

Diplomats serving highly centralized, violent regimes can function less as independent policy actors and more as **implementers** of party directives. Typical responsibilities (as documented in scholarship on similar systems) can include:

- Transmitting official positions and negotiating recognition, aid, or trade.
- Coordinating visits, delegations, and protocol.
- Performing information work (including curated reporting and messaging).
- Enforcing internal discipline and secrecy requirements within overseas offices.
