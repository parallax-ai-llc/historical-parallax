## Summary

Frederic Edwin Church was the most celebrated American landscape painter of the mid-19th century. His monumental canvases of Niagara Falls and icebergs drew massive crowds. Inspired by Humboldt's scientific vision, he captured nature's grandeur with scientific precision and Romantic sublimity.
