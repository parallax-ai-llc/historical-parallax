## Summary

Rwanda-backed M23 rebels have repeatedly seized territory in eastern Congo. The group has committed mass killings, rape, and displacement of hundreds of thousands.

## Origins and Name

“M23” is commonly understood to reference the March 23 agreement associated with earlier rebel integration arrangements in the region. The rebellion is part of a broader history of armed groups operating in eastern DR Congo.

## Regional Dimension

The conflict is frequently described as having cross-border dynamics, with regional states accused of backing armed actors. Such allegations are politically sensitive and often addressed through UN reporting, regional diplomacy, and peace processes.

## Humanitarian Impact

Recurring offensives in North Kivu and surrounding areas have been associated with:

- large-scale displacement
- disruptions to local economies and humanitarian access
- allegations of serious abuses against civilians

## Peace Efforts and Cycles of Violence

Eastern Congo has seen repeated cycles: ceasefires, negotiations, partial demobilization, and renewed fighting. The persistence of armed groups is often linked to security dilemmas, weak state control, and competition over territory and resources.

## International Response

The crisis has repeatedly drawn involvement from the UN peacekeeping mission (MONUSCO) and regional forums seeking ceasefires and withdrawal or demobilization commitments.
