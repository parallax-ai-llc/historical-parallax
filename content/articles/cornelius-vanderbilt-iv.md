## Summary

Cornelius Vanderbilt IV was a fourth-generation member of the Vanderbilt family who chose journalism over business. He founded several newspapers and wrote multiple books. He lived to be 101 years old, witnessing the entire arc of the 20th century. His life demonstrated the transition of American dynastic families from industrial power to cultural influence.
