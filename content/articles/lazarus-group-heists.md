## Background and Attribution

The name "Lazarus Group" is commonly used by security researchers and government agencies to describe a set of North Korea-linked cyber operators. Attribution is typically based on a combination of technical indicators (malware tooling and infrastructure), victimology, and strategic alignment with North Korean state interests.

## Common Tactics and Targeting

Operations attributed to the group have included:

- Bank-focused theft through fraudulent international transfers and manipulation of payment systems.
- Cryptocurrency theft via compromises of exchanges, wallets, and cross-chain bridges.
- Ransomware and destructive malware campaigns used for disruption, coercion, or to create cover for theft.

## Notable Incidents (High-Level)

While incidents vary by sub-group and time period, reporting frequently links Lazarus-affiliated activity to:

- The Bangladesh Bank heist (2016), involving attempted transfers via the SWIFT network and a confirmed loss of $81 million.
- The WannaCry ransomware outbreak (2017), which disrupted organizations worldwide and drew broad public attribution.
- Large-scale cryptocurrency thefts in the 2020s, including bridge and exchange compromises that produced hundreds of millions of dollars in losses.

## Impact

Analyses of Lazarus-linked theft often emphasize its strategic role in generating foreign currency for North Korea under international sanctions. The sustained focus on cryptocurrency is frequently described as a response to the relative speed of laundering, the global reach of exchanges and mixers, and the availability of high-value targets.
