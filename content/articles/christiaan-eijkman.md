## Summary

Christiaan Eijkman was a Dutch physician and professor of physiology who shared the 1929 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his discovery that beriberi is caused by a nutritional deficiency. His experiments with chickens in the Dutch East Indies showed that polished rice caused beriberi-like symptoms, leading to the discovery of vitamins. His work was foundational to the field of nutritional science.
