## Summary

Charli D'Amelio is an American social media personality and dancer who rose to become the most-followed creator on TikTok, reaching 100 million followers in November 2020 faster than any other user on the platform. Born on May 1, 2004, in Norwalk, Connecticut, she began posting dance videos in mid-2019 and within months had captured a global audience with her approachable style and technical skill.

Her influence extends well beyond TikTok. D'Amelio has leveraged her platform into television, brand partnerships, and entrepreneurship, becoming a central figure in the first generation of creators who built careers entirely through short-form video. She is widely credited with shaping the aesthetics and trends of early TikTok culture.

## Early Life & Background

Charli Grace D'Amelio was born on May 1, 2004, in Norwalk, Connecticut, to Marc D'Amelio, a former Connecticut state senate candidate and businessman, and Heidi D'Amelio, a photographer. She has one older sister, Dixie D'Amelio, who also became a prominent social media personality.

D'Amelio trained competitively in dance from age three, focusing on hip-hop and contemporary styles. She attended King School in Stamford, Connecticut, before transitioning to online schooling after her career demanded a full-time commitment. Dance training played a direct role in the precision and fluidity that distinguished her early content.

## Rise to Prominence

D'Amelio joined TikTok in June 2019 and posted her first widely shared video that summer. A duet video with user @move_with_joy in October 2019 accelerated her growth significantly. By December 2019 she had surpassed 5 million followers, and by March 2020 she had crossed 40 million, aided by pandemic-driven surges in TikTok usage.

In November 2020 she became the first TikTok creator to reach 100 million followers. Her family relocated to Los Angeles in early 2020 to support their growing media careers. She joined the Hype House creator collective briefly before stepping back to pursue independent projects.

## Key Achievements

- First TikTok creator to reach 100 million followers (November 2020)
- Starred in Hulu's reality series *The D'Amelio Show* (2021–2023), which followed her family
- Co-founded Charli and Dixie's podcast and co-authored *Essentially Charli: The Ultimate Guide to Keeping It Real* (2020)
- Launched a shoe collaboration with Puma and a cosmetics line, Born Dream, with sister Dixie
- Competed on *Dancing with the Stars* Season 31 (2022), finishing as runner-up
- Named to Time magazine's 100 Most Influential People list in 2020

## Controversies & Criticism

In November 2020, a dinner video posted by her family sparked backlash when viewers perceived her as rude to a personal chef and dismissive about her follower count. The incident led to a temporary loss of roughly a million followers and a public apology. Critics argued the episode revealed entitlement amid rapid fame.

D'Amelio has also faced scrutiny over eating disorder commentary from viewers, a topic she addressed publicly and through her series. She has spoken openly about struggling with anxiety and body image pressures, lending credibility to ongoing debates about the mental health costs of early social media fame.

## Legacy & Influence

D'Amelio became the defining face of TikTok's mainstream breakout period, demonstrating that a teenage creator could build a multi-platform media enterprise within two years. Her trajectory influenced how brands approach influencer marketing for Gen Z audiences and how talent agencies scout short-form video creators. The D'Amelio family's reality series further normalized creator families as entertainment subjects. She remains one of the most-followed individuals on TikTok as of 2026.
