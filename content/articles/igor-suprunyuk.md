## Summary

Ukrainian serial killer and core member of the Dnepropetrovsk maniacs who filmed brutal murders of 21 people for a snuff film collection. He was the primary perpetrator of the killings.

## Background

Suprunyuk became known internationally in connection with a 2007 spree of violent attacks in the Dnipropetrovsk region of Ukraine. Reporting typically describes him as part of a small group of young men who recorded assaults and killings and circulated the footage online, which drew significant public attention and aided subsequent investigation.

## Crimes and Investigation (High Level)

Accounts of the case describe multiple victims and a pattern of opportunistic attacks in public places. Investigators used a combination of victim reports, forensic evidence, and digital traces connected to the circulated videos to identify suspects. The case is sometimes referenced in discussions of how online distribution of violent material can accelerate both public alarm and law-enforcement response.

## Trial and Sentence

Following arrest, Suprunyuk was tried in Ukraine. He is widely reported to have received a life sentence in 2009 for murder and related offenses tied to the 2007 killings.

## Notes and Legacy

The case is commonly cited in media and criminology commentary because of the filmed nature of the crimes and their online spread. It has also been used as an example in debates about content moderation, digital forensics, and the societal impact of "shock" media.
