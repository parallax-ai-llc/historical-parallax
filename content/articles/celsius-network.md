## Summary

Crypto lending platform that froze $4.7 billion in customer funds before filing for bankruptcy. CEO Alex Mashinsky was arrested for fraud.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2022 | Froze customer funds; filed bankruptcy |

## Business Model (High-Level)

- Celsius marketed itself as a crypto "earn" and lending platform, attracting deposits by advertising comparatively high yields.
- The firm’s model depended on deploying customer assets into loans and other strategies while maintaining confidence that withdrawals would remain available.

## 2022 Crisis and Bankruptcy

- During broader crypto market stress, Celsius **paused withdrawals**, framing the move as necessary to stabilize operations.
- The pause triggered liquidity and solvency concerns and preceded a formal bankruptcy filing.

## Allegations and Legal Actions

- After the collapse, regulators and prosecutors pursued investigations and charges related to misrepresentation, fraud, and the handling of customer assets.
- The arrest of CEO Alex Mashinsky became a focal point in the case and in public discussion of accountability in crypto markets.

## Industry Impact

- Celsius is widely cited as part of a cluster of 2022-era crypto failures that reduced trust in yield products and centralized lending platforms.
- The case contributed to renewed emphasis on risk disclosure, custody practices, and the difference between marketed "yield" and underlying leverage.

## Notes

- Celsius’s failure is often discussed alongside other crypto collapses as evidence that advertised yields can mask correlated, systemic risks.
- The case also sharpened public attention on distinctions between on-chain transparency and the opacity of centralized intermediaries.
