## Summary

Egyptian supreme guide of the Muslim Brotherhood who was sentenced to death and later life imprisonment after the 2013 military coup. He oversaw the Brotherhood's brief period in power and subsequent crackdown.

## Role in the Muslim Brotherhood

- Badie became the **General Guide (Supreme Guide)** of the Muslim Brotherhood in 2010, a position combining ideological and organizational authority.
- His tenure overlapped with the Brotherhood’s rise after the 2011 uprising and its subsequent confrontation with the Egyptian state.

## 2013 coup and crackdown

- Following the July 2013 military takeover that removed President Mohamed Morsi, Badie was arrested amid a broad crackdown.
- Egyptian authorities later designated the Muslim Brotherhood a terrorist organization, and many leaders faced prosecution.

## Trials and sentences

- Badie was tried in multiple cases; Egyptian courts issued severe sentences including **life imprisonment** and, in some proceedings, **death sentences** that were later appealed or revisited.
- These cases were widely reported as part of the post-coup campaign against the Brotherhood’s leadership.

## Historical significance

- Badie’s period as leader is often used to frame the Brotherhood’s transition from an underground opposition movement to brief electoral power and back to repression.
- His legal cases illustrate how post-2013 Egyptian politics intertwined security policy, mass trials, and the restructuring of political space.
