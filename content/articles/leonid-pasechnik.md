## Background

- Leonid Pasechnik is a political and security figure associated with the Russian-backed Luhansk People's Republic (LNR) in eastern Ukraine.
- Open-source biographies commonly describe him as having served in Ukraine's Security Service (SBU) in the Luhansk region prior to the 2014 conflict.

## Role in the Luhansk People's Republic (LNR)

- He has been linked to the LNR's internal security apparatus, including senior positions often described as state security or intelligence roles.
- After the 2017 leadership change in the LNR, he served as the de facto / acting head, and was later confirmed as leader following the LNR's 2018 leadership vote.

## Political Context

- The LNR is a self-proclaimed entity that emerged during the 2014 conflict in eastern Ukraine and has been supported by the Russian Federation.
- It is not recognized as a sovereign state by most countries and has been treated by Ukraine and many international bodies as an illegal armed/separatist administration.

## Positions and Public Stances (high-level)

- In public statements, LNR leadership under Pasechnik has generally emphasized alignment with Russia and coordination with other Russia-backed separatist authorities in the region.
- In 2022, Russia declared the annexation of parts of Ukraine including the Luhansk region; these claims remain internationally disputed and are not recognized by many states.
