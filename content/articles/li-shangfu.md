## Key Facts

- **Full name**: Li Shangfu (李尚福).
- **Known for**: Senior People’s Liberation Army (PLA) officer who served briefly as China’s Minister of National Defense before being removed from office in 2023.
- **International note**: Was reported to have been under U.S. sanctions related to China’s acquisition of Russian military equipment (per widely reported public accounts summarized on Wikipedia).[^1]

## Background

Publicly available English-language summaries describe Li as a career PLA officer whose work included senior roles tied to **military equipment development and procurement**, including leadership responsibilities in organizations connected to China’s defense industry and space/armaments establishment.[^1]

## Career (Expanded)

### Selected roles and themes

- **Equipment and procurement**: Frequently associated with China’s military equipment development system and institutions that oversee procurement and modernization.[^1]
- **Senior leadership track**: Reached high rank and was elevated into senior national defense leadership prior to his brief ministerial tenure.[^1]

## Disappearance and Removal (2023)

Accounts summarized in open sources describe a period in 2023 in which Li **stopped appearing in public**, followed by official announcements that he had been **removed from his ministerial post**. The episode was widely interpreted in the context of China’s internal party-state discipline and anti-corruption enforcement mechanisms, though many specific details were not publicly disclosed in official statements.[^1]
