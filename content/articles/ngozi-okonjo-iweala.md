## Summary

Ngozi Okonjo-Iweala became the first woman and first African to serve as Director-General of the World Trade Organization in 2021. She previously served as Nigeria's Finance Minister and Foreign Affairs Minister. At the World Bank, she served as managing director. Though primarily a public official rather than entrepreneur, she has been instrumental in shaping economic policy, trade, and development finance across Africa and globally.
