## Summary

Suicide bombing at Manchester Arena after concert on May 22, 2017 killing 22 including children.

## Background

- Manchester Arena is a large indoor venue in Manchester, England.
- The attack targeted the crowd as people were leaving the concert, a moment of high density and vulnerability.
- UK authorities classified the incident as a terrorist attack.

## Attack (22 May 2017)

- A suicide bomber detonated an improvised explosive device in the arena’s public foyer/concourse area.
- The blast occurred shortly after the concert ended, as attendees (including many children and families) exited.
- Casualties included **22 people killed** and **hundreds injured**.

## Perpetrator

- The attacker was **Salman Ramadan Abedi**, a British citizen.
- Abedi died at the scene in the suicide bombing.

## Immediate Response

- Emergency services treated it as a mass-casualty incident; hospitals in Greater Manchester activated major incident protocols.
- Police quickly secured the area and initiated a counter-terrorism operation.

## Investigation and Legal Aftermath

- UK counter-terrorism police investigated Abedi’s network and support.
- Multiple arrests and prosecutions followed for roles related to assisting or preparing for the attack.

## Public Impact

- The bombing triggered national mourning and large public vigils in Manchester.
- It intensified public discussion about venue security, intelligence sharing, and counter-terrorism preparedness.

## Memorials and Remembrance

- Victims have been commemorated through memorial events and ongoing public remembrance efforts.
- The phrase “We Stand Together” became associated with Manchester’s civic response.
