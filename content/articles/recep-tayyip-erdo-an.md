## Summary

Turkish president accused of massive corruption after leaked recordings revealed alleged bribery. His government imprisoned more journalists than any other country. He has centralized power and weakened democratic institutions.

## Background

Recep Tayyip Erdoğan is a central figure in modern Turkish politics and has held national executive office for decades.

- He served as **Mayor of Istanbul** in the 1990s.
- He became **Prime Minister** in 2003 after his party, the Justice and Development Party (AKP), consolidated power.
- In 2014 he was elected **President of Turkey**.

## Domestic Politics and Governance

Observers and analysts often describe Erdoğan’s tenure as a period of major institutional change.

- Supporters credit his governments with large infrastructure projects and periods of economic growth, especially in the 2000s.
- Critics argue that checks and balances have weakened over time, citing constraints on media, civil society, and political opposition.

## 2013 Corruption Allegations and Political Fallout

- In late 2013, corruption investigations and leaked audio recordings triggered significant political controversy.
- The government disputed aspects of the allegations and framed some disclosures as politically motivated.
- The episode remains a major reference point in discussions of state institutions, policing, and judicial independence in Turkey.

## Shift to an Executive Presidency (2017)

- A 2017 constitutional referendum approved changes that expanded the powers of the presidency.
- The reforms altered the balance between parliament, the executive, and the judiciary; they are often cited as a turning point toward a more centralized executive system.

## Foreign Policy (High-Level)

Turkey’s foreign policy under Erdoğan has involved balancing relationships across NATO, neighboring states, and regional conflicts.

- Ongoing engagement with **NATO** alongside periodic tensions with allies.
- Active involvement in regional issues affecting Syria, migration flows, and security.
