## Summary

Italian serial killer who murdered 17 people in Liguria between 1997 and 1998. He killed prostitutes, train passengers, and acquaintances using a stolen .38 revolver. Sentenced to 13 life terms.

## Crimes and Investigation (High Level)

Bilancias murders occurred primarily in the Italian region of Liguria and included attacks on both strangers and acquaintances. Accounts describe the use of a firearm in several killings, and the case became a high-profile manhunt during 19971998.

Investigators connected incidents through forensic evidence and patterns in victimology and location. Public reporting highlights DNA evidence as one of the factors that contributed to his arrest.

## Prosecution and Sentence

He was tried and convicted in Italy for multiple murders and received multiple life sentences. In Italian criminal law, life imprisonment (ergastolo) can include additional measures that restrict parole eligibility depending on the specifics of the conviction.

## Media and Public Attention

The case received extensive media coverage in Italy, in part because of the number of victims in a short period and the apparent variety of targets.
