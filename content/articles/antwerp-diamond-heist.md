## Summary

Robbery of the Antwerp Diamond Centre on February 16, 2003 that netted over $100 million in diamonds, gold, and jewels. It was called the heist of the century.

## Setting and Target

The Antwerp Diamond Centre is located in Antwerp, Belgium, a city long associated with the international diamond trade. The burglary targeted a high-security vault system used by multiple clients, making the crime notable not only for the value stolen but also for the operational complexity of attacking a shared facility.

## Method (High-Level Summary)

Public accounts describe a carefully planned burglary that took place over a weekend when the surrounding offices were closed. Reporting has emphasized the team’s preparation and their ability to circumvent layers of security features designed to deter or delay intrusion.

## Investigation and Arrest

Leonardo Notarbartolo was among those identified in connection with the burglary. Investigative reporting and court proceedings describe how physical evidence and post-heist activity contributed to arrests and prosecutions.

## Aftermath

- The event became a reference case in discussions of physical security, insider risk, and the limits of “defense in depth” when attackers have time to plan.
- Many accounts note that the bulk of stolen items were not recovered, contributing to the heist’s enduring reputation.

## Legacy

The Antwerp Diamond Heist is frequently cited in popular and security-oriented retellings as an example of high-value theft involving careful reconnaissance, operational discipline, and exploitation of procedural gaps.
