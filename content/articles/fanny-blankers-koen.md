## Summary

Fanny Blankers-Koen, the 'Flying Housewife,' won four gold medals at the 1948 London Olympics at age 30. She held seven world records simultaneously and was named Female Athlete of the Century.

## Background

- Blankers-Koen competed in an era when women’s track and field received far less institutional support and media attention than men’s events.
- She was already an established international athlete before the 1948 Games, with versatility across sprints, hurdles, and jumps.

## 1948 London Olympics

- She won four gold medals at age 30, an achievement that stood out in a period when athletes’ careers were often shorter and opportunities for women were limited.
- The “Flying Housewife” nickname reflected contemporary media framing, emphasizing her role as a mother while she dominated elite sport.
- Her victories helped expand the public imagination of what women could achieve in Olympic athletics.

## Records and Recognition

- Holding multiple world records at the same time contributed to her reputation as one of the most complete athletes of her generation.
- She later received major honors, including recognition as Female Athlete of the Century.

## Legacy

- Her 1948 performance is commonly cited as one of the greatest single-Olympics achievements in track and field.
- She remains an enduring figure in discussions of women’s sport, longevity, and the historical evolution of elite competition.
- Later generations of athletes and historians have treated her London results as a turning point for women’s visibility in athletics.
