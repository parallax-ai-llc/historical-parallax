## Summary

Swedish serial shooter who targeted immigrants in Malmö, killing three and injuring several.

## Background

- Raised in the Lindängen area of Malmö by a single mother.
- Had immigrant background via a father born in Närpes, Finland.
- Was reported to have been formally diagnosed with Asperger syndrome in May 2009 (as described in media coverage of the trial).

## Attacks (high-level)

- Active period reported as June 2003 to October 2010 in Malmö, Sweden.
- Targeted people he perceived as non-white, as well as people he viewed as "race traitors".
- Commonly described as a lone-actor, racially motivated serial shooter.
- Wikipedia attributes at least three deaths and multiple attempted murders/injuries to the shooting series.

## Investigation and Trial

- Arrested by Swedish police in November 2010 in connection with the Malmö shootings.
- In 2012, found guilty on multiple counts including murder and attempted murder and sentenced to life imprisonment.
- In April 2013, the Court of Appeal upheld the life sentence and convicted him of additional attempted murders.
- In June 2013, Sweden's Supreme Court reportedly denied further appeal.

## Notability / Context

- Sometimes referred to in Swedish media as "Laser Man II" (Lasermannen II), drawing a comparison to John Ausonius ("Lasermannen") who carried out similar racially motivated attacks in the early 1990s.
- The shootings created fear within Malmö's immigrant communities and prompted public safety warnings.
