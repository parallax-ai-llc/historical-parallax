## Summary

Walter Reed was an American army physician who led pioneering research that confirmed the theory that yellow fever is transmitted by mosquitoes. His Yellow Fever Commission experiments in Cuba proved that the disease was carried by the Aedes aegypti mosquito, leading to effective prevention measures. The Walter Reed Army Medical Center in Washington D.C. was named in his honor.
