## Summary

Yi Gwangsu was a pioneering Korean modern novelist often called the father of modern Korean literature. His novel Heartless (Mujong, 1917) is considered the first modern Korean novel. He championed the use of vernacular Korean in literature and advocated for Korean independence, though his later collaboration with Japan remains controversial.
