## Summary

Martin Luther King Sr. was an American Baptist pastor and early civil rights leader who served as pastor of Ebenezer Baptist Church in Atlanta for over 40 years. He was the father of Martin Luther King Jr. and an influential figure in the African American church and civil rights movement. He led voter registration drives and desegregation efforts in Atlanta before his son became famous.
