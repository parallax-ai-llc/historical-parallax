## Summary

Russian national identified by the US, UK, and Australia as the leader of the LockBit ransomware gang, the world's most prolific ransomware operation. LockBit extorted over $120 million from over 2,000 victims worldwide.

## Attribution and Allegations

Khoroshev has been publicly identified by multiple governments as a leading figure behind the LockBit ransomware operation. Such attributions are typically based on law-enforcement investigations, technical indicators, and intelligence-sharing across jurisdictions.

## LockBit in Context

LockBit operated as a ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS) program, meaning developers and administrators provided tooling and infrastructure while "affiliates" carried out intrusions and deployed the malware. Victims were typically pressured to pay to restore access to systems and to prevent data publication.

## International Response

In early 2024, coordinated action by international law-enforcement disrupted LockBit infrastructure (often referred to as Operation Cronos). Subsequent actions included sanctions designations and public reward offers intended to incentivize information leading to identification or arrest.

## Impact (High Level)

Public reporting around LockBit emphasizes the scale of victims and the financial harm from extortion demands, as well as secondary effects such as operational downtime, incident-response costs, and long-term security remediation.
