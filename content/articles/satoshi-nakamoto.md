## Summary

Satoshi Nakamoto is the pseudonymous person or group who created Bitcoin, the first decentralized cryptocurrency. In 2008, Nakamoto published a whitepaper describing Bitcoin and its underlying blockchain technology. In 2009, the Bitcoin network was launched. Nakamoto's true identity remains unknown, making them one of the most mysterious figures in technology. Their creation launched the cryptocurrency revolution.

## Additional Details

- **Pseudonymous authorship**: “Satoshi Nakamoto” is widely treated as a pseudonym; no definitive public documentation has established whether it refers to one individual or a group.[^1]
- **Core design**: the Bitcoin white paper combined existing cryptographic primitives (hashing, digital signatures, proof-of-work) into a system for decentralized consensus over a ledger of transactions.[^1]
- **Early implementation**: Nakamoto wrote and released the initial Bitcoin software as open source, collaborated with early contributors, and iterated on protocol details in public forums and email.[^1]
- **Genesis and early mining**: the network began with a “genesis block” and an early period where a small number of participants mined blocks; analysis has suggested large early holdings associated with Nakamoto, though exact attribution is debated.[^1]
- **Departure**: Nakamoto gradually reduced involvement and ultimately stopped communicating publicly, leaving stewardship to other developers and the broader community.[^1]
- **Cultural impact**: beyond the technical system, the Nakamoto identity became a symbol within cryptocurrency culture and a focal point for recurring speculation and investigative journalism.[^1]

## Notes

Claims about Nakamoto’s identity, location, and total bitcoin holdings vary across sources; high-confidence statements generally come from contemporaneous posts, code history, and careful chain-analysis rather than rumor.[^1]
