## Summary

Rulon Gardner achieved one of the greatest upsets in Olympic history by defeating unbeaten Alexander Karelin in Greco-Roman wrestling at 2000 Sydney.

## 2000 Sydney: the Karelin upset (context)

- Gardner met Aleksandr Karelin in the **130 kg Greco-Roman** final at the 2000 Olympics.
- Karelin entered the match as a **three-time reigning Olympic champion** and had been **unbeaten in international competition for 13 years**.
- Gardner won the final **1–0**, a result widely described as one of the biggest upsets in Olympic wrestling.

## Major medals and results

- **Olympic Games**
  - 2000 (Sydney): **Gold** — 130 kg Greco-Roman
  - 2004 (Athens): **Bronze** — 120 kg Greco-Roman
- **World Championships**
  - 2001 (Patras): **Gold** — 130 kg Greco-Roman
- **Pan American competition** (selected)
  - Pan American Games: **Gold** (2003)
  - Pan American Championships: medals in the late 1990s and 2000

## Background and training

- Born in **Afton, Wyoming (USA)**; raised on a family **dairy farm**, which he has credited for developing his strength.
- Wrestled in college at the **University of Nebraska–Lincoln** and later trained with elite U.S. Greco-Roman programs and clubs.

## After competitive wrestling

- Worked as a **motivational speaker** and has appeared in U.S. media, including reality television.
- Published an autobiography, *Never Stop Pushing*, describing his athletic career and personal adversity.

## Notable incidents (documented)

- Survived a **2002 snowmobile accident** that led to hypothermia/frostbite and ultimately the loss of a toe.
- Survived a **2007 light-aircraft crash** into **Lake Powell (Utah)**; he and two others swam to shore and endured an overnight wait for help.

## Legacy

- Remembered most for the 2000 Olympic final, which disrupted one of the sport’s most dominant championship streaks.
- His career is often cited in wrestling as a case study in how match strategy, conditioning, and composure can overturn overwhelming pre-match expectations.
