## Summary

Italian general and anti-Mafia prefect who was assassinated by Cosa Nostra in Palermo in 1982. His murder galvanized Italys anti-Mafia movement.

## Early Service and Counter-Terrorism Work

Carlo Alberto dalla Chiesa served as an officer in the Carabinieri and rose through senior command roles. In the 1970s, he became known for efforts against political violence, including work targeting the Red Brigades (Brigate Rosse) during Italy’s “Years of Lead.”

## Appointment in Palermo

In 1982, he was appointed Prefect of Palermo, a position intended to strengthen the state’s response to organized crime in Sicily. Contemporary reporting described tensions between expectations placed on him and the resources and legal tools available at the time.

## Assassination

Dalla Chiesa was killed in an ambush in Palermo in September 1982. His wife, Emanuela Setti Carraro, and a police escort officer were also killed. The attack was attributed to Cosa Nostra and became one of the most prominent mafia assassinations of the period.

## Impact and Legacy

The murder intensified public and political pressure for stronger anti-mafia measures. It is often discussed alongside other landmark killings of officials, journalists, and magistrates that shaped Italy’s modern anti-mafia movement and legal reforms.

## Commemoration

In Italy, his name is frequently invoked in civic remembrance of anti-mafia and rule-of-law institutions, and memorials and dedications have been used to symbolize resistance to organized crime.
