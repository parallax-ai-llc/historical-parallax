## Summary

American kidnapper who imprisoned three women in his Cleveland home for over a decade, subjecting them to repeated sexual assault. He was sentenced to life plus 1,000 years.

## Case Overview

Ariel Castro abducted and held three young women—Michelle Knight, Amanda Berry, and Gina DeJesus—at his home in Cleveland, Ohio. The case became widely known because the captivity lasted for roughly a decade and because the victims were able to escape alive.

## Discovery and Rescue (2013)

The imprisonment ended when Amanda Berry was able to alert neighbors and law enforcement after forcing access through part of the home's entryway. Police then entered the residence and found the other two women. The rescue prompted major media attention and renewed scrutiny of missing-person investigations and community awareness.

## Legal Proceedings

Castro faced multiple charges related to kidnapping, rape, and other offenses. He entered a guilty plea and received a sentence designed to ensure he would spend the remainder of his life in prison, with an additional term symbolically reflecting the scale of the crimes.

## Aftermath and Public Impact

The case led to extensive public discussion about victim support, long-term trauma recovery, and the ways that prolonged captivity can go undetected in residential settings. It also became a reference point in broader conversations about reporting systems for missing persons and coordination between agencies.
