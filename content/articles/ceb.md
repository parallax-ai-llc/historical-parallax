## Summary

Sébastien "Ceb" Debs is a French professional Dota 2 player and coach, best known for his role in OG's back-to-back victories at The International 2018 and The International 2019 — a feat no other organization has achieved. Initially a veteran player and analyst who struggled to break through at the highest level, Ceb transitioned into coaching before returning to active play at a pivotal moment for OG, becoming one of the most celebrated offlane players in Dota 2 history.

His career arc — from a long-tenured competitor who spent years on the fringes of tier-one Dota to a two-time world champion — is widely regarded as one of esports' greatest redemption stories. Ceb's game sense, leadership, and clutch performances under pressure cemented his legacy as a defining figure in competitive Dota 2.

## Early Life & Background

Sébastien Debs was born on November 17, 1992, in France. He grew up immersed in competitive gaming, entering the Dota scene during the original Defense of the Ancients era. By his late teens, he was already competing in European tournaments, developing a reputation as a knowledgeable and strategic player well before Dota 2's release in 2013.

## Rise to Prominence

Ceb competed under various team banners throughout the early-to-mid 2010s, including stints with teams like 4 Anchors + Sea Captain and Team Tinker. Despite being respected within the community for his game knowledge, consistent top-tier results eluded him. By 2017, he shifted to a coaching and managerial role with OG, the organization co-founded by Johan "N0tail" Sundstein and Tal "Fly" Aizik.

The turning point came in mid-2018 when Fly and s4 departed OG just weeks before The International 2018, leaving the roster in crisis. Ceb stepped back into an active player role, filling the offlane position. What followed was one of the most improbable runs in esports history: OG, widely considered underdogs, fought through the lower bracket to win TI8, claiming the Aegis of Champions and approximately $11.2 million in prize money.

## Key Achievements

- **The International 2018 Champion** — OG defeated PSG.LGD in the grand finals, with Ceb delivering iconic performances on heroes like Axe and Magnus.
- **The International 2019 Champion** — OG became the first and only team to win two consecutive Internationals, earning over $15.6 million. Ceb's play was instrumental, particularly his legendary Axe call against PSG.LGD in the upper bracket.
- **Total career earnings** exceeding $6 million in prize money individually, placing him among the highest-earning esports players in history.
- After stepping back from active competition following TI9, Ceb returned to OG's roster periodically, including stints in 2022 when the team needed roster reinforcements.

## Controversies & Criticism

Ceb faced scrutiny in 2019 when screenshots surfaced of him making xenophobic remarks in a pub game directed at Russian-speaking players. He publicly apologized, acknowledging the comments were unacceptable. Valve did not issue a formal punishment, but the incident sparked broader conversations about toxicity in professional Dota 2. Critics argued that prominent players should be held to higher behavioral standards, while supporters noted his swift apology and subsequent conduct.

## Legacy & Influence

Ceb's legacy is defined by resilience and reinvention. His journey from a journeyman competitor to a two-time world champion — achieving this after initially stepping away from playing — resonates as a narrative about persistence and seizing opportunity. Within OG, he served as both a strategic mind and an emotional anchor, contributing to a team culture that thrived under pressure.

His clutch plays, particularly the TI8 lower bracket run and the TI9 Axe moments, remain among the most replayed highlights in Dota 2 history. Ceb demonstrated that deep game understanding and leadership can rival raw mechanical skill at the highest level of competition.
