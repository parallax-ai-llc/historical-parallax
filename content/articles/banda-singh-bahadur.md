## Summary

Banda Singh Bahadur was a Sikh military leader who established the first Sikh sovereignty in Punjab. Appointed by Guru Gobind Singh before his death, Banda Singh led the Sikh forces to remarkable victories against the Mughal Empire. He captured Sirhind and established Sikh rule in parts of Punjab. He was eventually captured and executed in Delhi along with hundreds of his followers.
