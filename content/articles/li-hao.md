## Context and Identification

The name "Li Hao" is shared by multiple people; the Wikipedia URL in the frontmatter is a disambiguation page. This article’s summary and timeline refer to the perpetrator described in Wikipedia as the *Luoyang sexual slavery case* (also titled "Li Hao (murderer)").[^2]

## Key Facts (high-level)

- **Birth**: Wikipedia reports Li Hao (李浩) was born **February 1977** in **Xinye County**, **Nanyang**, **Henan**, China.[^2]
- **Employment**: Wikipedia describes him as having held roles including work connected to **Luoyang’s fire bureau** and later as a **clerk** in a **quality-control** sector within a Henan technology bureau (as reported by Wikipedia’s cited sources).[^2]
- **Criminal case**: Wikipedia describes a pattern of **abduction and prolonged captivity** of multiple young women in Luoyang, Henan, between **2009 and 2011**, involving sexual violence and other crimes.[^2]
- **Discovery and arrest**: Wikipedia states the survivors were freed in **September 2011** after one victim escaped, leading to his arrest.[^2]

## Legal Outcome

Wikipedia reports that a Luoyang court convicted Li Hao in **2012**, including offenses such as **rape**, **murder**, **organizing prostitution**, **producing illegal pornography**, and **illegal detention**, and sentenced him to **death**. Wikipedia further reports the sentence was approved by China’s Supreme People’s Court and that he was **executed in 2014**.[^2]
