## Summary

Former Banco do Brasil director convicted in the Mensalão vote-buying scandal. Fled to Italy using his dead brother's passport before being extradited.

## Mensalão context (high-level)

The **Mensalão** scandal refers to a major Brazilian political corruption case from the mid-2000s involving allegations of monthly payments to legislators in exchange for support in Congress.

## Role and conviction

Pizzolato is commonly described as having worked as a director at **Banco do Brasil**, with prosecutors alleging that he helped facilitate illicit financing connected to the scheme.

High-level points frequently noted in coverage:

- the case became a landmark in Brazil for the prosecution of senior political figures
- convictions were handed down by Brazil’s Supreme Federal Court (STF) in a widely watched process

## Flight and extradition

After conviction, he left Brazil and was detained in Italy. Reporting around the case notes that he traveled using documents connected to a deceased brother, which became part of the legal and diplomatic dispute.

He was ultimately **extradited to Brazil** after Italian proceedings.

## Why the case is remembered

The Pizzolato episode is often cited in discussions of:

- the difficulty of enforcing white-collar sentences across borders
- the role of dual nationality and residency claims in extradition fights
- Brazil’s shifting anti-corruption politics in the 2000s–2010s
