## Summary

French police employee who stabbed four colleagues to death at Paris police headquarters.

## Background

- Harpon was an IT specialist employed at the Paris Police Prefecture (Préfecture de Police) and worked in an intelligence-related unit.[^1]
- Reports describe him as having held security clearance, providing access to sensitive police information.[^1]
- He was reported to have been deaf since childhood.[^1]

## 2019 Paris Police Headquarters Attack

- **Date/Place:** 3 October 2019, Paris Police Prefecture on the Île de la Cité (central Paris).[^1]
- **Victims:** Four colleagues were killed; additional colleagues were injured. The attacker was shot and killed at the scene.[^1]
- **Weapon:** Accounts report use of a ceramic knife.[^1]

## Investigation & Reported Motive

- Early reporting treated the incident as a workplace dispute, but a counter-terrorism investigation was opened shortly after.[^1]
- Media and official reporting discussed possible radicalization and jihadist motivation; investigators examined communications and digital materials connected to the attacker.[^1]

## Aftermath

- The attack prompted scrutiny of internal security and reporting procedures within the police administration, including how prior concerns were handled.[^1]
- The incident became a major reference point in French discussions about insider threats, vetting, and information-access controls within public safety institutions.[^1]
