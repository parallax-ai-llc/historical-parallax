## Summary

Paracelsus was a Swiss-German physician, alchemist, and philosopher who founded the discipline of toxicology. He pioneered the use of chemicals and minerals in medicine and challenged the traditional Galenic medical system. His famous dictum 'the dose makes the poison' remains a fundamental principle of toxicology. Paracelsus is considered the father of toxicology and a pioneer of pharmaceutical chemistry.
