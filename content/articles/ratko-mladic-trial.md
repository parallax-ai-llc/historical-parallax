## Summary

Trial of Bosnian Serb military commander Ratko Mladic at the ICTY for genocide at Srebrenica and war crimes. After hiding for 16 years, he was convicted and sentenced to life.

## Tribunal and Charges (High Level)

- The case was heard at the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY), a UN-established court created to prosecute serious crimes committed during the Balkan conflicts.
- Prosecutors charged Mladić with crimes including genocide, crimes against humanity, and violations of the laws or customs of war.
- The allegations covered both the Srebrenica events and the wider campaign of violence during the Bosnian War.

## Proceedings and Outcome

- Mladić was arrested in 2011 after a long period in hiding and was transferred to The Hague.
- The trial featured extensive documentary evidence and testimony from survivors, insiders, and experts.
- In 2017 the tribunal convicted him and sentenced him to life imprisonment.
- In 2021, the appeals process concluded with the life sentence upheld.

## Significance

- The trial was among the most prominent ICTY proceedings because it addressed senior-level command responsibility.
- It contributed to the historical record through the compilation of evidence and formal findings about the Bosnian War.
- The case is often cited in international criminal law discussions about genocide standards, witness protection, and long-duration prosecutions.

## Notes

- ICTY trials commonly span many years due to the scale of evidence, the number of witnesses, and the need for translation and procedural safeguards.
- The Mladić proceedings are often compared with other ICTY cases to evaluate consistency in sentencing and findings.
