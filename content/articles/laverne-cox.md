## Summary

Laverne Cox is an American actress and LGBTQ activist best known for her Emmy-nominated role as Sophia Burset on Netflix's *Orange Is the New Black*. Born on May 29, 1972, in Mobile, Alabama, Cox became the first openly transgender person to be nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award in an acting category, a milestone that marked a turning point in mainstream visibility for transgender people in Hollywood.

Beyond acting, Cox has become one of the most prominent advocates for transgender rights in the United States. She uses her platform to speak out against discrimination, violence targeting transgender women of color, and systemic inequity. Her appearances on magazine covers, in documentaries, and before legislative bodies have helped shift public conversation around gender identity in meaningful ways.

Cox's influence extends well beyond entertainment. She has served as a cultural touchstone at a moment when transgender rights moved to the center of American political and social debate, consistently offering a voice grounded in both personal experience and political urgency.

## Early Life & Background

Laverne Cox was born and raised in Mobile, Alabama, as one of twin brothers. From an early age she showed a passion for the performing arts, studying dance and theater. She was bullied as a child for her gender nonconformity. After high school, Cox moved to New York City to pursue a career in performance, attending Marymount Manhattan College, where she studied acting and dance.

## Rise to Prominence

Cox first gained wider attention through early television appearances, including the VH1 reality series *TRANSform Me* (2010), which she also co-produced. Her breakthrough came in 2013 when she was cast as Sophia Burset, a transgender inmate, in *Orange Is the New Black*. Her nuanced portrayal earned her a Primetime Emmy nomination in 2014 for Outstanding Guest Actress in a Comedy Series — a historic first for an openly transgender performer. She appeared on the cover of *Time* magazine in June 2014, with the headline "The Transgender Tipping Point," cementing her status as a symbol of a cultural shift.

## Key Achievements

- First openly transgender person nominated for a Primetime Emmy Award in an acting category (2014).
- Featured on the cover of *Time* magazine (June 9, 2014).
- Executive produced and appeared in the documentary *Laverne Cox Presents: The T Word* (2014), which won an Emmy Award.
- Received a Daytime Emmy nomination for her role in the TV movie *Grandma* (2019).
- Named one of *Time*'s 100 Most Influential People multiple times.
- Became the first openly transgender person to appear on the cover of *Cosmopolitan* magazine (2015).

## Controversies & Criticism

Cox has largely avoided personal controversy, though she has been a target of harassment and threats tied to her public advocacy. She has spoken publicly about an incident in November 2021 in which she and a friend were targeted in a transphobic attack in Los Angeles. Some criticism has come from conservative commentators opposed to her advocacy work, but these criticisms are broadly reflective of wider political disagreements over transgender rights rather than conduct-based concerns.

## Legacy & Influence

Cox's career reshaped what was possible for transgender performers in Hollywood and changed the terms of mainstream transgender representation. Her Emmy nomination in 2014 opened doors for greater inclusion of transgender actors and stories in major productions. As an activist, she has testified before legislatures, partnered with organizations fighting anti-transgender legislation, and mentored younger LGBTQ advocates. Her legacy is one of visibility leveraged into sustained political and cultural advocacy.
