## Summary

Toxic chemicals in Oxy and other humidifier disinfectants killed over 100 people and injured 1,500 in South Korea, including many infants. Companies covered up the dangers for years.

## Background

- In South Korea, household humidifiers are commonly used, especially during winter and in homes with young children.
- Several companies marketed "humidifier disinfectants" intended to be added to water tanks to prevent microbial growth.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- Consumers aerosolized the disinfectant along with water vapor, enabling inhalation of chemical particles deep into the lungs.
- After a cluster of severe lung injuries (including among pregnant women and infants) was investigated, authorities linked cases to humidifier disinfectant use.
- The scandal became a major public-health and corporate accountability issue due to the scale of harm and the long period before hazards were acknowledged.

## Key Facts (Commonly Cited)

- Reported outcomes include deaths and a much larger number of injured people, with children disproportionately affected.
- The products were sold for years before the connection to lung injury was publicly recognized.
- Multiple brands and manufacturers were implicated, with Oxy Reckitt Benckiser among the most visible due to market presence and public response.

## Corporate & Regulatory Response

- Public outrage led to investigations into product safety testing, labeling, and corporate knowledge of risks.
- The case triggered scrutiny of:
  - chemical safety governance and pre-market testing standards,
  - consumer product oversight and recall mechanisms,
  - accountability for harm caused by everyday household products.

## Legal Proceedings & Accountability (Overview)

- Criminal investigations and civil claims followed as victims and families sought recognition and compensation.
- The case is frequently referenced in discussions of corporate negligence, scientific evidence standards, and the responsibilities of multinational firms operating in domestic markets.

## Public Impact & Legacy

- The humidifier disinfectant tragedy is often cited as one of South Korea's worst consumer product disasters.
- It contributed to broader reforms and heightened public awareness about chemical exposure risks in consumer products.
