## Case overview

Wei Yixin is described in press summaries as a Chinese Communist Party official whose case culminated in a conviction for bribery and an execution.

The case has been cited as an example of how China’s anti-corruption campaign has reached both senior and mid-level officials, especially where prosecutors alleged unusually large bribe totals.

## Anti-corruption campaign context

Since the early 2010s, China has pursued a long-running discipline and prosecution effort led by party inspection bodies and state prosecutors.

Cases typically involve a party disciplinary investigation, transfer to criminal authorities, trial and sentencing, and then a separate review stage for capital punishment where applicable.

## Sentencing and deterrence framing

Chinese state messaging around high-profile corruption cases often emphasizes deterrence, institutional discipline, and “clean governance.”

Executions for corruption are comparatively rare relative to the overall volume of corruption convictions, which is why cases described as resulting in execution tend to attract attention.
