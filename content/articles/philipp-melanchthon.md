## Summary

Philipp Melanchthon was a German reformer and close collaborator of Martin Luther. He authored the Augsburg Confession of 1530, the primary confession of the Lutheran Church. Known as the 'Praeceptor Germaniae' (Teacher of Germany), he reformed the German university system. He sought to mediate between Lutherans and Catholics and between various Protestant factions.
