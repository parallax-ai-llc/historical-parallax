## Summary

Alexis Carrel received the 1912 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his pioneering work in vascular suturing and organ transplantation techniques. He developed methods for connecting blood vessels end-to-end and kept animal tissues alive outside the body. His surgical innovations made modern organ transplantation possible.
