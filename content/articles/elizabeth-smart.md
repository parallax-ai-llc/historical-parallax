## Summary

American teenager kidnapped from her Salt Lake City bedroom at knifepoint in 2002 and held by Brian David Mitchell for 9 months. She was found alive walking with her captors in Sandy, Utah.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2011 | Published memoir My Story |

## Additional Context

- **Public search and recovery**: The case drew extensive national attention in the United States during the search period, and her recovery was widely reported.
- **Legal proceedings**: Brian David Mitchell was ultimately convicted; the case involved complex questions around competency and criminal responsibility.

## Advocacy and Public Work

- Smart later became an advocate focused on child safety and preventing sexual violence, emphasizing survivor-centered support and education.
- She has worked with organizations and public campaigns that address exploitation and abuse and has spoken publicly about the long-term impact of trauma.

## Key Takeaways

- The case is often referenced in discussions about missing-person investigations, media attention, and the importance of post-recovery support for survivors.

## Aftermath and Public Significance

- The case remained prominent in American public consciousness for years, both because of the intense search effort and because Smart later chose to speak publicly.
- Her advocacy work has been cited in conversations about improving survivor support, reducing stigma, and strengthening prevention education.
