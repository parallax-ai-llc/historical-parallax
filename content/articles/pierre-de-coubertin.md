## Summary

Pierre de Coubertin founded the modern Olympic Games in 1896 and served as president of the International Olympic Committee for 29 years. He believed sport could promote international understanding.

## Early Interests and Reform Agenda

Coubertin was an aristocratic French educator and writer who became interested in educational reform, particularly the role of physical education. He argued that organized sport could develop character and civic virtues, and he promoted athletic programs in schools.

## Revival of the Olympic Games

Coubertin is widely credited as the principal architect of the modern Olympic movement:

- He advocated for an international, recurring multi-sport event modeled (loosely) on ancient Greek precedents.
- He helped create organizational structures that became the **International Olympic Committee (IOC)**.
- The 1896 Athens Games provided a symbolic starting point, after which the Olympics expanded in scale and global participation.

## Olympic Ideals and Symbols

Coubertin promoted "Olympism" as an ideal that linked sport to education and internationalism. Ideas and symbols associated with the early Olympic movement include:

- The emphasis on **amateurism** (a dominant value in elite sport at the time).
- International competition framed as peaceful rivalry.
- Ceremonial traditions and narratives that helped present the Games as a global event.

(Several well-known Olympic phrases and symbols are popularly associated with Coubertin, though specific origins can be complex and are sometimes attributed through later IOC tradition.)

## Legacy and Criticism

Coubertin’s influence on global sport institutions is enduring, but his views have also been criticized. Historians note that some of his positions reflected the social norms of his era, including elitist assumptions and restrictive attitudes toward women’s participation in sport.

Despite these controversies, his central role in founding and shaping the modern Olympics makes him a major figure in the history of international sport.
