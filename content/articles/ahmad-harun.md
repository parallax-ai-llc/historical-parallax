## Summary

Sudanese government minister indicted by the ICC for 51 counts of war crimes and crimes against humanity in Darfur. He organized and armed Janjaweed militia forces.

## Background

Ahmad Harun is a Sudanese political figure who held senior roles within the government during the period of the Darfur conflict. Public accounts frequently describe him as a key state official involved in security and administrative coordination.

## Darfur Conflict and ICC Allegations

The ICC issued an arrest warrant alleging that Harun played a role in mobilizing, funding, or coordinating forces implicated in attacks on civilian populations in Darfur. The allegations involve counts of war crimes and crimes against humanity connected to the early years of the conflict.

Descriptions of the case often emphasize the relationship between formal state structures and irregular militia forces, including the Janjaweed, in patterns of violence and displacement.

## Political Roles and Domestic Detention

Harun continued to hold public office after the ICC warrant, including positions within the ruling party and government. Following political upheaval in Sudan in 2019, he was reported to be in custody under Sudanese authorities.

## Transfer and Accountability Issues

A recurring issue in reporting on Harun has been whether Sudan would transfer ICC suspects to The Hague, pursue domestic prosecutions, or adopt a hybrid approach. The status of such processes has been affected by shifting political conditions and later conflict dynamics.
