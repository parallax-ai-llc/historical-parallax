## Summary

Crush at Hillsborough football stadium in Sheffield on April 15, 1989 that killed 97 Liverpool fans. Police blamed fans for decades before an inquiry found the crush was caused by police failures.

## What Happened (High Level)

The disaster occurred during an FA Cup semi-final at Hillsborough Stadium. An overcrowding and crush developed in fenced standing pens, leading to fatal asphyxiation and traumatic injuries. Subsequent investigations focused on crowd management, stadium design, and emergency response.

## Investigations and Accountability

Multiple inquiries and legal proceedings unfolded over decades. The Hillsborough Independent Panel (2012) published extensive documentation and challenged long-standing public narratives. In 2016, a new inquest jury concluded the victims were unlawfully killed and rejected claims that supporters were responsible for the disaster.

## Lasting Impact

Hillsborough became a turning point in UK stadium safety and policing practices. It contributed to reforms in ground design and match-day operations and remains a major reference point in debates about public accountability, media ethics, and the treatment of victims’ families.
