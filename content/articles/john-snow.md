## Summary

John Snow is considered the father of modern epidemiology for his work tracing the 1854 Broad Street cholera outbreak in London to a contaminated water pump. He pioneered the use of mapping and statistical analysis to identify disease sources. He also administered chloroform anesthesia to Queen Victoria during childbirth.
