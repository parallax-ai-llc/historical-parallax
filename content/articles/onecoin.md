## Summary

Cryptocurrency fraud that defrauded investors of $4 billion worldwide. Founded by Ruja Ignatova, known as the Cryptoqueen, who disappeared in 2017 and is on the FBIs Most Wanted.

## How the Scheme Worked (High-Level)

- Marketed as a cryptocurrency, OneCoin was promoted through conferences, motivational events, and a global distributor network.
- A common description in investigations is that OneCoin lacked the characteristics of a real, independently verifiable blockchain-based currency.
- Sales often centered on “education packages” and affiliated marketing commissions, resembling a multi-level marketing structure.

## Key Figures and Enforcement Actions

- Ruja Ignatova (“Cryptoqueen”) served as the most visible leader and promoter and later disappeared from public view in 2017.
- Law enforcement agencies in multiple jurisdictions pursued prosecutions related to wire fraud, money laundering, and conspiracy allegations.
- Co-founders and major promoters faced indictments and sentencing in U.S. federal court and elsewhere, as reported in public records.

## Investor Impact and Aftermath

- Loss estimates vary by source; reporting frequently cites multi‑billion‑dollar totals across many countries.
- The case is widely referenced as a cautionary example for retail investors of high-pressure sales tactics and opaque “crypto” products.
