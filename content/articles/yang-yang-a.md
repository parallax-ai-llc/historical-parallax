## Background

Yang Yang (A) (Chinese: 杨扬) is a retired Chinese short track speed skater who competed primarily in the 500 m and 1000 m events, as well as team relays. She is widely cited as one of the most accomplished athletes in the history of short track speed skating.

She is commonly written as “Yang Yang (A)” to distinguish her from another Chinese skater romanized as Yang Yang (sometimes labeled “Yang Yang (S)”). In international results, the “(A)” label is associated with her birth month (August) and became part of her competitive identity.

## Olympic highlights

- **2002 Salt Lake City:** Won **gold** in the **500 m** and **1000 m**, achievements often described as China’s first Winter Olympic gold medals.
- She also earned additional Olympic medals across her Olympic appearances (including relay and individual events), contributing to China’s rise in short track speed skating.

## World Championship record (high level)

Across the late 1990s and early 2000s, Yang Yang (A) accumulated an unusually large set of medals and titles at major international competitions.

- She is credited with multiple **overall World Championship** titles in short track speed skating during the period **1997–2002**.
- Her results include medals in sprint distances (500 m, 1000 m) as well as relays, reflecting both individual speed and team strength.

## Later roles and public service

After her top-level competitive career, Yang Yang (A) became active in sport governance and athlete representation.

- She served as a member of the **International Olympic Committee (IOC)** in the 2010s.
- She later held senior roles in **anti-doping governance**, including a vice-presidential position at the **World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA)**.

## Legacy

Yang Yang (A) is frequently referenced as a turning point figure for Chinese winter sports: her Olympic success in 2002 is treated as a symbolic breakthrough, and her later administrative work reflects a transition from elite performance to institutional leadership in global sport.
