## Summary

Two-time President of Uganda whose second regime was as brutal as Amin's, with an estimated 300,000 killed in the Luwero Triangle during the Ugandan Bush War.

## Additional Context

### Early Political Career

- Emerged as a leading figure around Uganda's independence and served as Prime Minister before becoming President.
- His first period in power included escalating conflict with political rivals and Uganda's traditional kingdoms.

### Consolidation of Power

- Moved to centralize authority in the 1960s, contributing to prolonged political instability.

### Second Presidency and Conflict

- Returned to power after the fall of Idi Amin; his second administration coincided with the Ugandan Bush War.
- Widespread abuses were reported during this period, including violence in the Luwero Triangle.

### Overthrow and Exile

- Overthrown again in 1985 and spent subsequent years in exile.

### Historical Significance

- His legacy remains controversial and is often discussed within the broader pattern of coups and civil conflict in post-independence Uganda.
