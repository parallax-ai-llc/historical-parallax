## Summary

Japanese woman convicted of poisoning a community curry pot with arsenic at a summer festival in Wakayama, killing 4 and sickening 63. Maintains her innocence on death row.

## Case overview

The incident is commonly referred to as the Wakayama curry poisoning case.
It took place at a community event where food was prepared and shared in a large batch.
Because the victims were ordinary attendees, the case drew significant national attention.
The suspected poison was arsenic, a substance that can cause severe acute poisoning.

## Investigation and trial (high level)

Public reporting emphasizes the difficulty of linking a single suspect to a communal food source.
Cases like this often rely on circumstantial evidence, opportunity, and forensic findings.
Hayashi was prosecuted and convicted, and her sentence was ultimately upheld.
She has continued to deny responsibility, which is part of why the case remains discussed.

## Public impact

The case influenced Japanese public perceptions of random mass harm in everyday settings.
It also fed broader debates about the death penalty and long appellate timelines in Japan.
Community-food events and school or festival meals can become focal points for safety concerns after such incidents.
