## Summary

Richard Stallman is an American programmer and software freedom activist who launched the GNU Project in 1983 and founded the Free Software Foundation in 1985. He developed the GNU Compiler Collection (GCC), GNU Emacs, and the GNU General Public License (GPL). His philosophy of free software — that users should have the freedom to run, study, distribute, and modify software — profoundly influenced the open-source movement.
