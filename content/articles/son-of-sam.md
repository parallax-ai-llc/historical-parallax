"**Son of Sam**" is the media nickname used for **David Berkowitz**, who carried out a series of shootings in New York City in 1976–1977. The case became a major U.S. news story and had lasting cultural and legal impact.

## Background and Identification

- David Berkowitz (born 1953) was identified as the perpetrator after investigative work connected evidence from multiple crime scenes and led police to him.
- Berkowitz was arrested in 1977 and later pleaded guilty in court proceedings.

## Cultural and Legal Impact

### Media Coverage

The "Son of Sam" case is frequently cited as an example of how high-profile violent crime investigations can shape public fear, media practices, and policing narratives in a major city.

### "Son of Sam" Laws

In the United States, the case is closely associated with so-called **"Son of Sam" laws**—statutes intended to limit or regulate a criminal’s ability to profit from publicity, such as book or movie deals about their crimes. Variants of these laws have been debated and revised over time, including legal challenges involving free-speech and constitutional questions.
