## Summary

Xu Guangqi was a Chinese scientist, agronomist, and mathematician who served as a senior official in the Ming Dynasty. He collaborated with Matteo Ricci to translate Euclid's Elements into Chinese, introducing Western mathematics to China. He wrote the comprehensive agricultural treatise 'Nongzheng Quanshu' and reformed the Chinese calendar. Xu bridged Chinese and Western science during a crucial period.
