## Summary

Slovak serial killer who murdered at least 8 women between 2009 and 2011 in Bratislava region. He dismembered victims.

## Modus Operandi (Reported)

Accounts of the case describe Curko as targeting women and using extreme violence. Investigators linked him to multiple killings through forensic work, and reporting described post-mortem dismemberment in several cases.

## Investigation and Identification

Curko was identified during an investigation in the Bratislava area that relied on physical evidence, including DNA. As with many serial cases, authorities reconstructed timelines and victim movements to connect separate incidents into a single pattern.

## Death During Arrest Attempt

Curko died in 2011 during an attempted arrest. Public reporting describes the incident as a confrontation with police rather than a standard arrest, ending the possibility of a full trial that could have established a comprehensive public record of the crimes.

## Impact

The case received attention in Slovakia as an example of how modern forensic methods can connect a series of crimes that initially appear unrelated. It also raised public discussion about victim protection, investigative resources, and the challenges of detecting serial violence quickly.

## Further Notes

Because Curko died during the attempted arrest, much of the public narrative has been shaped by investigative findings and media reporting rather than courtroom testimony. As a result, exact counts and details are often reported with qualifiers such as “at least” or “suspected” in secondary sources.

Where available, primary-source documentation (police statements, forensic summaries, and contemporaneous reporting) is generally needed to distinguish confirmed victims from suspected additional cases.
