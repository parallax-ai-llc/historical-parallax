## Summary

Sofia Kovalevskaya was the first woman to receive a PhD in mathematics (1874) and the first to be appointed a full professor of mathematics in Europe. She made important contributions to analysis, partial differential equations, and mechanics, including the Cauchy-Kovalevskaya theorem. She proved the existence of solutions to certain partial differential equations.
