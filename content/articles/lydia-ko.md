## Summary

Lydia Ko became the youngest LPGA event winner at 15 and won Olympic silver at 2016 Rio and gold at 2024 Paris.

## Early life

Ko was born in South Korea and raised in New Zealand, representing New Zealand in international competition. She emerged as a standout junior golfer and quickly transitioned to elite professional events.

## Professional achievements

- Ko is known for reaching major milestones at an unusually young age, including early LPGA wins.
- She has won multiple LPGA Tour events across different seasons, maintaining a long period of relevance at the top level.
- Rankings milestones (including reaching world number one) contributed to her reputation as one of the defining players of her generation.

## Olympic record

Ko's Olympic medals (silver in 2016 and gold in 2024) are often cited as signature achievements, placing her among the most decorated women's golfers in Olympic competition.

## Playing style

Observers frequently note her consistency, course management, and short-game skill as core strengths, alongside strong competitive composure in high-pressure events.

## Legacy

Ko is commonly referenced as a benchmark for early excellence in women's golf and as a prominent figure in New Zealand sport.
