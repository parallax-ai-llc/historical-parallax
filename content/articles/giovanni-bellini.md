## Summary

Giovanni Bellini was a Venetian Renaissance painter who transformed the art of Venice and established it as a rival to Florence. Known for his luminous use of oil painting and atmospheric landscapes, he pioneered the Venetian colorist tradition. His innovations directly influenced his pupils Giorgione and Titian.
