## Summary

King Solomon was the third king of Israel, son of David, renowned for his wisdom, wealth, and building the First Temple in Jerusalem. He is traditionally credited with authoring Proverbs, Ecclesiastes, and the Song of Solomon. His reign represented the golden age of the united Israelite monarchy. His Temple became the center of Jewish worship and identity for centuries.
