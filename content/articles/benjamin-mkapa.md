## Background

Benjamin William Mkapa served as the third President of Tanzania (1995–2005) and was a leading figure in the ruling party, Chama Cha Mapinduzi (CCM).

Before the presidency, he held senior cabinet and diplomatic posts, including foreign affairs and ambassadorial assignments.

## Policy orientation (high level)

Mkapa’s presidency is commonly summarized as combining:

- Market-oriented reforms and privatization of state-owned enterprises.
- Efforts framed as anti-corruption and public-sector reform.
- Engagement with international financial institutions on debt relief and macroeconomic policy.

Supporters argue these measures helped stabilize the economy and attract investment; critics argue benefits were uneven and that corruption remained entrenched.

## Corruption allegations and the central bank scandal

Mkapa’s post-presidency reputation has been affected by recurring public controversies over governance and corruption.

One major episode discussed in Tanzanian public life is the Bank of Tanzania’s External Payment Arrears (EPA) account scandal, in which large sums were fraudulently paid out through a mechanism intended to settle arrears.

The scandal became publicly prominent after his presidency and fueled criticism of oversight during his administration.

## Public scrutiny and political legacy

Mkapa is often evaluated in comparison with earlier eras of Tanzanian governance, including the post-Nyerere period of liberalization.

His tenure is also noted for debates over state capacity, the role of donors, and the tradeoffs between liberalization and accountability.

## Later life

After leaving office, Mkapa remained an influential elder statesman in regional and international forums until his death in 2020.
