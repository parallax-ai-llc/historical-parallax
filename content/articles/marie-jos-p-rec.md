## Summary

Marie-José Pérec won 200m/400m double at 1996 Atlanta and held the 400m WR for 23 years.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1992 | Won 400m gold at Barcelona |

## Background

Born in Basse‑Terre, Guadeloupe, Pérec emerged as one of France’s premier long sprinters, specializing in the **400 metres** and later becoming a world-class **200 metres** runner as well.

## Major Achievements (High-Level)

- **Olympic champion:** Gold in the **400 m (1992)** and a rare **200 m / 400 m double (1996)**.
- **World and European medals:** A consistent medal contender across global championships during the 1990s.
- **World record era:** Her 400 m world record is often cited as one of the standout marks of the decade, illustrating the peak level of her prime.

## Competitive Style

Pérec was known for combining top-end speed with strong speed endurance, enabling her to close races powerfully and also compete effectively over 200 m.

## Atlanta 1996

The 1996 Olympics remain the signature moment of her career, where she won both the 200 m and 400 m. The double is historically difficult because it demands both explosive sprint capacity and the ability to tolerate high lactic load in the one-lap event.

## Later Career and Sydney 2000

Her later career included high public expectations for the 2000 Olympics. She ultimately withdrew from the Sydney Games, which became a heavily discussed episode in French sports media and shaped how her career’s final chapter is remembered.

## Legacy

- Widely regarded as one of the greatest French track athletes of the modern era.
- Frequently referenced in discussions about the “long sprint” (200/400) specialization and the rarity of elite performance across both distances.
