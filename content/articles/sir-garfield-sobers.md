## Summary

Garfield Sobers is considered the greatest all-rounder in cricket history. He hit six sixes in an over and held the Test batting record of 365* for 36 years.

## Playing Style and Reputation

Sobers’ historical standing rests on rare excellence in **multiple disciplines**:

- **Batting**: a stylish left-handed batter capable of both long, defensive innings and aggressive scoring.
- **Bowling**: effective with pace and spin depending on match conditions, which increased his tactical value.
- **Fielding**: regarded as an exceptional fielder and safe catcher.

The term “all-rounder” is often used broadly, but Sobers is frequently singled out as a benchmark because he influenced matches in more than one way at the highest level.

## Major Milestones (Expanded)

### 365* Test Record

His 365 not out (1958) became a long-standing record for the highest individual score in a Test innings. The innings is regularly cited as a defining moment in West Indies cricket and in the history of record-setting batting performances.

### Six Sixes in an Over

Sobers famously hit six consecutive sixes in an over in first-class cricket (1968). The feat became iconic partly because it was rare and demonstrated controlled power-hitting within a formal match context.

## Leadership and Team Impact

Sobers captained the West Indies in Tests and was a central figure during a period when Caribbean cricket was gaining global prestige. While West Indies dominance is often associated with the later pace-bowling era, Sobers’ career helped establish a tradition of excellence and star power that shaped international perceptions of the team.

## Honors and Recognition

He was knighted, reflecting both sporting achievement and cultural significance in Barbados and the wider Caribbean. In retrospectives, he is frequently included in “greatest XI” selections and ranked among the most complete cricketers in the sport’s history.

## Legacy

Sobers is commonly referenced in debates about:

- what constitutes a true all-rounder,
- the evolution of batting ambition and power-hitting,
- West Indies cricket as a cultural and post-colonial sporting force.
