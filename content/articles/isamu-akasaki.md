## Summary

Isamu Akasaki was a Japanese engineer and physicist who shared the 2014 Nobel Prize in Physics for the invention of efficient blue light-emitting diodes (LEDs). His decades-long research on gallium nitride semiconductors enabled the development of blue LEDs, which made possible energy-efficient white LED lighting. This invention is transforming global energy consumption.
