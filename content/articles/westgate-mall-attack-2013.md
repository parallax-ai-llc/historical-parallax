## Summary

Al-Shabaab militants attacked the Westgate shopping mall in Nairobi, killing 67 people in a four-day siege. Attackers asked victims trivia about Islam to identify non-Muslims.

## Key Facts (High-Level)

- **Date:** 21–24 September 2013
- **Location:** Westgate shopping mall, Westlands, Nairobi, Kenya
- **Perpetrator:** al-Shabaab (claimed responsibility)
- **Motivation as stated by al-Shabaab:** retaliation for Kenya's military involvement in Somalia (including Operation Linda Nchi / subsequent deployments)

## What happened

- Four masked gunmen attacked the mall, shooting civilians and taking hostages.
- The incident developed into a prolonged siege involving multiple Kenyan security units.
- Part of the mall collapsed during the crisis, and reporting on casualty totals has varied in part because of the fire and structural damage.

## Casualties (reporting varies)

- Some accounts report **67** killed (often cited in early summaries).
- Other reporting (including later summaries on the Wikipedia page for the attack) has cited **71 total deaths**, including civilians, Kenyan security personnel, and the four attackers.
- Approximately **200** people were reported wounded in the attack.

## Aftermath (selected)

- Kenyan authorities arrested multiple people in the aftermath.
- In November 2013, a Kenyan court charged four Somali nationals with allegedly harboring the gunmen (all pleaded not guilty, per the same summary source).
