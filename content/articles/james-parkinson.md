## Summary

James Parkinson first described the condition that would later be named Parkinson's disease in his 1817 publication An Essay on the Shaking Palsy. He was also a noted paleontologist who contributed to understanding fossils. His clinical description of the disease's symptoms remained the definitive account for many decades.
