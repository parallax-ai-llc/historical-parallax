## Summary

See earlier entry. Subrahmanyam Chandrasekhar was a theoretical astrophysicist whose work shaped modern understanding of stellar structure and evolution, especially the physics of white dwarfs, neutron stars, and black holes.

## Key Contributions (High Level)

- **Chandrasekhar limit**: He calculated an upper mass limit for stable white dwarf stars supported by electron degeneracy pressure. Above this threshold, a star cannot remain a white dwarf and must collapse to a denser state.
- **Stellar structure theory**: He produced foundational mathematical treatments of radiative transfer, hydrodynamic stability, and the equilibrium of self-gravitating bodies.
- **Mathematical methods in astrophysics**: Chandrasekhar was known for rigorous use of applied mathematics, often publishing monographs that became standard references.

## Notable Historical Context

Early in his career, Chandrasekhar’s conclusions about white dwarf collapse met strong skepticism from parts of the astronomical establishment, including a well-known disagreement with Arthur Eddington. Over time, observational discoveries and improved theory established the importance of compact objects and validated key elements of the collapse picture.

## Recognition

Chandrasekhar received major scientific honors, including the **Nobel Prize in Physics (1983)** for theoretical studies of the physical processes important to the structure and evolution of stars.

## Legacy

Chandrasekhar’s work helped connect quantum statistics, relativity, and thermodynamics to astrophysical phenomena, providing a framework for later research on supernova mechanisms and compact remnants. His limit is routinely used in astrophysics as a reference scale when discussing end stages of stellar evolution.

## Terms (Quick Glossary)

- **White dwarf**: A dense stellar remnant supported by electron degeneracy pressure.
- **Degeneracy pressure**: Quantum-mechanical pressure arising from the Pauli exclusion principle.
- **Compact object**: A high-density remnant such as a white dwarf, neutron star, or black hole.
