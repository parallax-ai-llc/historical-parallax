## Summary

Kidnapping of 14-year-old Elizabeth Smart from her Salt Lake City home in 2002 by Brian David Mitchell. She was held for 9 months before being found walking with her captors.

## Background and context

- Elizabeth Smart was abducted from her family home in Salt Lake City, Utah, in June 2002.
- The case received extensive national media coverage and became a prominent missing-person investigation in the United States.
- Smart was recovered alive in March 2003 after being spotted in public with her captors.

## Investigation and recovery

- The investigation involved local and federal law-enforcement resources and generated numerous public tips.
- Public recognition of the victim and suspects played a role in the eventual identification.
- After recovery, prosecutors pursued criminal charges against the abductors.

## Legal aftermath

- The primary abductor, Brian David Mitchell, was tried and convicted; he received a life sentence in federal court.
- Proceedings included competency and mental-health questions that extended the timeline to sentencing.

## Significance

- The case contributed to broader public discussion about child abduction, victim support, and media attention in missing-person cases.
- Elizabeth Smart later became a public advocate focused on child safety and victim assistance.
