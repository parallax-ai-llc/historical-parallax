## Field context: History of science

Historians of science commonly contribute by:

- Reconstructing the development of theories and experiments using archives, publications, and correspondence.
- Analyzing the interaction between science and society (funding, institutions, politics, education).
- Comparing how scientific concepts travel across languages and cultures, including translation and reception.
- Clarifying how “standard narratives” form (e.g., how certain figures become canonical while others are overlooked).

This kind of work is often interdisciplinary, drawing methods from history, philosophy of science, sociology, and area studies.

## Why this matters in scientific biographies

Even when someone is not known for producing new experimental results, historians of science can shape how later generations understand:

- What counted as evidence in a given era.
- Which controversies were central versus peripheral.
- How scientific communities formed consensus.
