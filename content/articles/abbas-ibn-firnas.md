## Summary

Abbas ibn Firnas is credited with making one of the first attempts at human flight, using a wing-like apparatus to glide from a height near Córdoba around 875 CE. He also developed a process for manufacturing colorless glass, created a chain of rings that simulated planetary motion, and designed a water clock. He is considered a pioneer of aviation.
