## Background and Identity

- **Full name**: Jacob Anthony Chansley (also known as **Jake Angeli**).[^1]
- Known publicly for adopting a "shaman" persona associated with the QAnon conspiracy movement, including a horned fur headdress and face paint.[^1]

## Role in the January 6 Attack

- Chansley entered the U.S. Capitol during the January 6, 2021 attack and was photographed in the Senate chamber, which helped make him an emblematic image of the event.[^1]
- The visibility of the imagery around him (costume, flag, and media photos) amplified his notoriety well beyond other individual participants.[^1]

## Criminal Case and Sentence

- Federal prosecutors charged Chansley in connection with the attack; he later pleaded guilty to a felony count of **obstruction of an official proceeding**.[^1]
- In November 2021, he was sentenced to **41 months** in federal prison and ordered to pay restitution as part of his judgment.[^1]

## Public Impact

- The "QAnon Shaman" image became a shorthand in global news coverage for the riot and for conspiracy-driven political extremism in the United States.[^1]
- His case is frequently cited in discussions of how online conspiracy subcultures can translate into offline political violence and spectacle.[^1]

## Additional Notes

- The nickname "QAnon Shaman" is a media label rather than an official title; it reflects how he was popularly identified during and after January 6.[^1]
