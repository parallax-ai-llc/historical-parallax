## Summary

Somali pirate kingpin known as Afweyne (Big Mouth) who was the godfather of modern Somali piracy. He masterminded the hijacking of over 50 ships including the MV Pompei.

## Additional Context

- Mohamed Abdille Hassan was a Somali religious and political leader associated with the Dervish movement.
- He led a long anti-colonial resistance campaign in the Horn of Africa during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

## Historical Context

- The region was shaped by competition among colonial powers and local polities, alongside religious and clan dynamics.
- The Dervish movement is often discussed as an early, organized form of Somali resistance to external rule.

## Legacy

- Interpretations of Hassan vary: he is viewed by some as a nationalist symbol and by others through the lens of conflict and coercion.
- His campaigns influenced later political narratives about sovereignty and resistance in Somalia.
