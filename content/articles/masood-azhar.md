## Summary

Pakistani militant who founded Jaish-e-Mohammed, responsible for attacks on Indian Parliament (2001), Pathankot airbase (2016), and Pulwama (2019). He was released from Indian prison in exchange for hijacked plane passengers in 1999.

## Background

Masood Azhar is a Pakistani Islamist militant leader best known for founding **Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM)**, a group based in Pakistan that has been linked by multiple governments to attacks in India-administered Kashmir and elsewhere.

Azhar first gained wider public attention outside Pakistan after his **arrest in India in 1994** and subsequent imprisonment. In **1999**, he was released as part of a prisoner exchange following the hijacking of **Indian Airlines Flight IC-814**.

## Jaish-e-Mohammed (JeM)

JeM was formed in **2000** and has been described in open sources as advocating armed jihad in Kashmir. The group has been associated with a range of high-profile attacks and attempted attacks, including incidents cited by Indian authorities such as the **2001 Indian Parliament attack**, the **2016 Pathankot airbase attack**, and the **2019 Pulwama bombing**.

## International Designations and Responses

- **United Nations (2019)**: After sustained diplomatic efforts, Azhar was listed by the UN as a **global terrorist** (a designation that typically triggers sanctions-related measures such as asset freezes and travel restrictions).
- **Government designations**: Various countries have also designated JeM and/or Azhar under their domestic counterterrorism frameworks.
