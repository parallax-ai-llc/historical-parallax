## Summary

Chinese pirate lord who commanded a fleet of over 400 ships and attempted to conquer Manila in the Philippines. His massive invasion force was eventually repelled by Spanish defenders.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Historical Setting

- Limahong is typically described in the context of **16th-century maritime raiding** in East and Southeast Asia.
- His activities overlapped with early Spanish colonial efforts in the Philippines and the contest for regional trade routes.

## Raid on Manila (High Level)

- The 1574 attack is remembered as a major early test of Spanish defenses in Manila.
- Accounts emphasize the scale of his force and the intensity of fighting before the attackers were repelled.

## Aftermath and Later Activity

- Following the failure to take Manila, Limahong and his followers continued operating in the region.
- Historical narratives about his later years vary, and many summaries treat the details as uncertain compared with the better-documented Manila episode.

## Legacy

- In Philippine and regional history writing, Limahong appears as a prominent example of a pirate leader whose campaigns intersected with colonial-era state-building and fortification.

## Key Takeaways

- Limahong is best remembered for the attempted seizure of Manila in 1574.
- The episode highlights the vulnerability of early colonial settlements to large-scale maritime raiding.
- Later details of his fate are often summarized cautiously due to uneven historical documentation.
