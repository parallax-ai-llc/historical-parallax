## Summary

Slovak serial killer who murdered at least 9 women across Europe while living as a vagrant.

## Background

- According to Wikipedia, Rigo was born in Modra (then Czechoslovakia) and later lived in Bratislava.[^1]
- Wikipedia reports that as a teenager he and his siblings were placed into a youth corrective institution and later an orphanage for about a year, following family disruption related to his father's arrest.[^1]
- Prior to the murders, he had multiple earlier convictions (primarily property crimes) and intermittently served prison sentences.[^1]

## Crimes and Modus Operandi (reported)

- Wikipedia describes a spree spanning roughly 1990–1992, with attacks reported in Bratislava as well as abroad (including Munich and Amsterdam).[^1]
- A consistently cited element of his modus operandi is breaking into residences at night or early morning, often via ground-floor windows, basements, or balconies.[^1]
- He reportedly used socks over his hands to reduce fingerprints, which contributed to the nickname “Sock killer” in some coverage.[^1]
- Sources summarized by Wikipedia link the case to sexual violence and necrophilia allegations, alongside psychiatric diagnoses discussed during proceedings.[^1]

## Arrest, Trial, and Sentence

- Wikipedia states he was apprehended in March 1992 and later convicted of multiple murders, receiving a life sentence in Slovakia.[^1]
- Wikipedia further notes that he served his sentence in Leopoldov Prison and died in custody in 2022.[^1]
