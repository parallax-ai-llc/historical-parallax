## Summary

Zhao Liying (赵丽颖) is one of China's most commercially successful actresses, known for her prolific output in Chinese television dramas across romance, historical, and fantasy genres. Born on October 16, 1987, in Langfang, Hebei province, she rose from modest beginnings to become a household name across mainland China and a prominent figure in pan-Asian pop culture. Her ability to portray both delicate and fiercely independent heroines made her a defining actress of the 2010s Chinese drama landscape.

Her cultural footprint extends beyond acting — Zhao commands an enormous social media following, consistently ranks among China's highest-paid entertainers, and holds considerable influence over fashion and consumer trends. She was the first actress to surpass 100 million followers on Weibo, cementing her status as one of the most influential celebrities in the Chinese-speaking world.

## Early Life & Background

Zhao Liying was born on October 16, 1987, in Langfang, Hebei province, into an ordinary working-class family. She showed an early interest in performance and participated in local talent competitions as a teenager. In 2006, she was discovered through a Sina.com beauty contest, which served as her entry point into the entertainment industry. She signed with Tangren Media and began taking on minor television roles while developing her craft.

## Rise to Prominence

Zhao's early career consisted largely of supporting roles in historical and palace dramas throughout the late 2000s and early 2010s. Her breakout came with the 2013 drama *The Glamorous Imperial Concubine* and more significantly with *The Journey of Flower* (花千骨, 2015), a fantasy xianxia drama in which she played the lead role of Hua Qiangu. The series drew peak ratings of over 2.8 billion views online and became one of the highest-rated Chinese dramas of that year, transforming Zhao into a top-tier star virtually overnight.

She followed this with *Princess Agents* (楚乔传, 2017), which accumulated over 50 billion online views, and *Story of Minglan* (知否知否应是绿肥红瘦, 2018–2019), which earned critical praise for its nuanced portrayal of a woman navigating Song dynasty social hierarchies. In 2021, she returned with the spy thriller *The Oath of Love* and the romance drama *Who Rules the World* (2022).

## Key Achievements

- First Chinese actress to surpass 100 million followers on Weibo.
- *Princess Agents* (2017) accumulated over 50 billion online views, a record-breaking figure at the time of broadcast.
- Won multiple industry awards including the Weibo Night Best Actress awards and Chinese TV Drama Festival honors.
- Recognized by Forbes China on its Celebrity 100 list multiple times, ranking as high as 4th in 2018.
- Served as brand ambassador for major international labels including Dior Beauty and L'Oréal Paris.

## Controversies & Criticism

Zhao's marriage to actor Feng Shaofeng in 2018 and the announcement of their son drew intense public and media scrutiny. The couple announced their separation in 2021, which attracted widespread tabloid coverage in China. She has also faced occasional criticism regarding the quality control of her drama projects, with some viewers noting uneven pacing in certain productions. In 2021, broader Chinese government crackdowns on celebrity culture led to increased scrutiny of high-profile entertainers, though Zhao herself remained largely unaffected compared to peers.

## Legacy & Influence

Zhao Liying's career helped legitimize the xianxia and historical romance drama genres as mainstream entertainment vehicles capable of generating massive viewership. Her portrayals of resilient, self-determined female characters resonated strongly with younger Chinese audiences and contributed to shifting expectations around female lead archetypes in Chinese television. She remains one of the most bankable stars in the Chinese entertainment industry and a significant cultural export figure in Southeast Asia and diaspora communities worldwide.
