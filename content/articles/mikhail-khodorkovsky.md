## Summary

Once Russia's richest man as head of Yukos oil company, imprisoned for 10 years on fraud and tax evasion charges widely seen as politically motivated by Putin.

## Additional Context

### Background

- Became prominent during the post-Soviet privatization era and built wealth through banking and energy assets.
- Led the oil company Yukos during a period when major Russian firms expanded and sought international investment.

### Yukos and the State

- Yukos was among Russia's largest oil companies; conflict with authorities escalated in the early 2000s.
- The company was later broken up, and major assets were taken over by state-controlled entities after legal and tax proceedings.

### Legal Proceedings and Imprisonment

- The prosecution drew extensive international attention and was widely debated in terms of political influence and rule-of-law.
- Served roughly a decade in prison before receiving a presidential pardon and leaving Russia.

### Post-Release Activities

- Became an outspoken critic of Vladimir Putin and supported civil-society and opposition-linked initiatives.
- His trajectory is frequently referenced in discussions of political risk, property rights, and corporate governance in Russia.
