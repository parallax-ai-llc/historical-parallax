## Summary

Charles Babbage was a British mathematician and inventor who originated the concept of a programmable computer. He designed the Difference Engine and Analytical Engine, which contained many features of modern computers including memory, a processor, and input/output. Although never fully built during his lifetime, his designs anticipated modern computing by over a century. He is considered the 'father of the computer.'
