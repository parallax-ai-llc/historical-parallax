## Summary

The Terracotta Army is a collection of terracotta sculptures depicting the armies of Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China. Buried with the emperor in 210-209 BCE to protect him in the afterlife, the army consists of over 8,000 soldiers, 130 chariots with 520 horses, and 150 cavalry horses, most of which remain buried. Discovered in 1974 by local farmers digging a well, it's one of the greatest archaeological discoveries of the 20th century and a UNESCO World Heritage Site, revealing the scale and artistry of ancient China.

---

## Discovery (1974)

Farmers digging well near Xi'an discovered pottery fragments and bronze weapons. Archaeological excavation revealed massive underground complex.

---

## The Army

**Scale**: 8,000+ life-sized warriors, 130 chariots, 520 horses, 150 cavalry horses
**Individuality**: Each figure has unique facial features, expressions, hairstyles
**Detail**: Armor, weapons, ranks depicted accurately
**Height**: Warriors average 5'11" (180 cm)
**Pits**: Three main pits covering 20,000+ square meters

**Purpose**: Protect Emperor Qin Shi Huang in afterlife

## Emperor Qin Shi Huang

- First Emperor of unified China (221-210 BCE)
- Unified warring states
- Built Great Wall
- Standardized writing, currency, measurements
- Buried in massive mausoleum (still unexcavated)
