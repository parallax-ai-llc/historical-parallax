## Summary

German far-right terrorist who killed 9 people at two shisha bars in Hanau, Germany on February 19, 2020. He targeted people of immigrant backgrounds and posted a racist manifesto. He then killed his mother and himself.

## Investigation and public debate

In the wake of the Hanau shootings, public discussion in Germany included questions about prior contacts with authorities, how threats are assessed, and what preventive interventions are effective against far-right radicalization. The case also intensified debate about the protection of communities that are disproportionately targeted by racist violence.

### Topics often discussed
- **Recognition of extremist violence** and consistent classification of motives
- **Victim-centered commemoration** and the role of civic remembrance
- **Prevention and early intervention** for radicalization and conspiracy thinking
