## Summary

Indian bodyguard who assassinated PM Indira Gandhi on October 31, 1984 in retaliation for Operation Blue Star.

## Background

Beant Singh served in the Indian Prime Minister's security detail during a period of intense political conflict in Punjab. Tensions between the Indian state and Sikh militants escalated in the early 1980s, culminating in Operation Blue Star, an Indian Army action at the Golden Temple complex in Amritsar.

## Assassination of Indira Gandhi

On 31 October 1984, Beant Singh and another bodyguard, Satwant Singh, shot Prime Minister Indira Gandhi at her residence. The assassination was widely interpreted as motivated by anger over Operation Blue Star and its consequences.

## Immediate Aftermath

Beant Singh was shot and killed shortly after the attack. The assassination triggered a major political and security crisis and was followed by large-scale anti-Sikh violence in multiple locations, resulting in many deaths and deep communal trauma.

## Historical Significance

The killing of Indira Gandhi reshaped Indian politics and security practices, including protective protocols for senior officials. It remains central to modern Indian debates about state violence, insurgency, accountability for mass violence, and the long-term impact of sectarian polarization.
