## Summary

Rwandan gendarme chief who was convicted of crimes against humanity for failing to prevent his subordinates from participating in the genocide. He was acquitted of genocide itself.

## Expanded overview

Augustin Ndindiliyimana is a Rwandan former senior police official whose name appears in historical accounts and international legal proceedings related to the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi in Rwanda.

## Role and historical context

- Served in a senior position within Rwanda’s national police structures prior to and during 1994.
- The period is defined by the rapid escalation of mass violence, the collapse of political agreements, and the mobilization of state and militia structures.

## International tribunal proceedings

Ndindiliyimana was among individuals prosecuted at the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR). Tribunal cases and appeals addressed questions of command responsibility, knowledge, and actions taken or not taken during the genocide.

## Significance

His case is cited in broader discussions of how international criminal law evaluates responsibility within security institutions, and how evidence is weighed across complex chains of command.
