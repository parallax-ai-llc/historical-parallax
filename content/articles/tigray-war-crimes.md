## Summary

Ethiopian and Eritrean forces committed widespread atrocities in Tigray including mass killings, sexual violence, and deliberate starvation. An estimated 600,000 people died.

## Background

The Tigray War began in November 2020 after escalating political tensions between Ethiopia’s federal government and the Tigray People’s Liberation Front (TPLF). The conflict expanded beyond a conventional battlefield and involved multiple armed actors, including Ethiopia’s federal forces, Eritrean forces, regional forces, and various allied militias.

## Nature of the Allegations

Public reporting and investigations have described a pattern of alleged violations that include:

- Mass killings of civilians in towns and villages.
- Widespread conflict-related sexual violence.
- Large-scale destruction and looting of civilian infrastructure, including hospitals, schools, and religious sites.
- Obstruction of humanitarian assistance and the use of starvation-like conditions as a method of warfare.

Because access to affected areas was often restricted, casualty figures and incident-level details have varied by source and have been difficult to independently verify in real time.

## Investigations and Documentation

Multiple institutions and organizations have investigated or documented alleged abuses, including:

- Joint and parallel reporting by UN-affiliated bodies and Ethiopia’s national human-rights institutions.
- International human-rights organizations that collected witness testimony, satellite imagery, and open-source material.
- Media investigations describing specific incidents and identifying potential perpetrators or chains of command.

These reports commonly emphasized that abuses were attributed to more than one side, though the scale and attribution of particular incidents remains politically contested.

## Humanitarian Impact

The war created a severe humanitarian crisis, including large-scale displacement, disruption of agriculture and markets, and prolonged interruptions to banking, fuel, telecommunications, and health services. Aid agencies warned repeatedly about acute food insecurity and elevated mortality risks, particularly during periods when humanitarian access was constrained.

## Political and Legal Responses

International responses included diplomatic pressure, sanctions discussions in some jurisdictions, and calls for:

- Independent, credible investigations.
- Protection of civilians and restoration of basic services.
- Accountability mechanisms (domestic, hybrid, or international) depending on legal and political feasibility.

The 2022 cessation-of-hostilities agreement reduced major combat, but questions about accountability, missing persons, and survivor assistance remained central to post-conflict recovery.
