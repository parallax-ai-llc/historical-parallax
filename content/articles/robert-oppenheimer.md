## Summary

J. Robert Oppenheimer was an American theoretical physicist who served as the scientific director of the Manhattan Project during World War II. Known as the 'father of the atomic bomb,' he led the team that developed the first nuclear weapons. After the war, he became chairman of the Atomic Energy Commission and advocated against nuclear proliferation. His security clearance was controversially revoked in 1954 during the McCarthy era.
