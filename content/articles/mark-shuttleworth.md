## Summary

Mark Shuttleworth became the first African and first South African in space when he flew to the ISS in 2002 as a space tourist aboard a Soyuz rocket. He conducted science experiments during his 10-day mission. He is also the founder of Ubuntu Linux and Canonical Ltd, making significant contributions to open-source software.
