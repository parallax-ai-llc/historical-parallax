## Summary

Ratan Tata served as chairman of Tata Group from 1991 to 2012, transforming it from a domestic conglomerate into a global powerhouse. He led Tata's acquisitions of Tetley Tea, Jaguar Land Rover, and Corus Steel. He launched the Tata Nano, the world's cheapest car. Under his leadership, Tata Group revenues grew from $4 billion to over $100 billion. He was awarded India's second-highest civilian honor, the Padma Vibhushan.
