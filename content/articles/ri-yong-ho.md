## Background

- Ri Yong-ho (리영호) is a North Korean senior military figure who rose during the transition from Kim Jong-il to Kim Jong-un.
- Public biographical details are limited; most reporting relies on North Korean state media announcements and subsequent foreign analysis.

## Roles and Standing (as publicly reported)

- Held the post of **Chief of the General Staff** of the Korean People's Army (KPA).
- Promoted to the rank of **Vice Marshal** and associated with the top military leadership circle during 2010–2012.
- Appeared at high-profile state events during the early Kim Jong-un period, suggesting strong political standing at the time.

## Purge / Removal (2012)

- In mid-2012, North Korean media announced that Ri was **relieved of his duties**, initially attributing the move to "illness".
- After the announcement, Ri largely **disappeared from state media**, which is commonly interpreted as an indicator of removal from power.
- **His ultimate fate has not been confirmed** in open sources; outside claims (including execution rumors) remain unverified.

## Why Ri Yong-ho Matters

- Ri's sudden removal is frequently cited as one of the earliest major elite shake-ups under Kim Jong-un.
- Analysts often view the episode as evidence of:
  - consolidation of the new leader's authority,
  - rebalancing between military and party influence,
  - intolerance for independent power bases within the senior command.

## Notes

- The Summary above reflects common reporting and speculation; the key confirmed point is the 2012 removal, not the manner of death.
