## Summary

Felix Arvid Ulf Kjellberg, known online as PewDiePie, is a Swedish YouTuber and content creator who became one of the most influential figures in the history of online video. Born on October 24, 1989, in Gothenburg, Sweden, he built his channel into a cultural phenomenon, holding the title of most-subscribed individual creator on YouTube for nearly a decade.

His content evolved from Let's Play gaming videos in the early 2010s to commentary, meme reviews, and lifestyle content, reflecting broader shifts in internet culture. PewDiePie's conversational, unfiltered style helped define the template for modern YouTube entertainment and demonstrated that independent creators could rival traditional media in reach and cultural impact.

Despite his success, his career has been marked by significant controversies that forced ongoing public reckonings about creator responsibility and platform accountability. He remains a pivotal figure in understanding how online media matured through the 2010s.

## Early Life & Background

Felix Kjellberg was born in Gothenburg, Sweden, and grew up in a middle-class family. He studied industrial economics and technology at Chalmers University of Technology but dropped out in 2011 to pursue his YouTube channel full-time. Before committing to content creation, he briefly worked selling hot dogs to fund his early equipment. His parents were reportedly skeptical of his choice to leave university.

## Rise to Prominence

Kjellberg created his YouTube channel in 2010, initially posting gaming commentary — particularly horror games such as *Amnesia: The Dark Descent*. His energetic, reactive commentary attracted a rapidly growing audience. By 2012, his channel was among the fastest-growing on the platform. In August 2013, he surpassed Smosh to become the most-subscribed YouTube channel in the world, a position he held until 2019, when the channel T-Series overtook him.

At his peak, PewDiePie commanded over 100 million subscribers. The 2019 subscriber race between his channel and T-Series became a major internet event, with fans mounting billboard campaigns and coordinated promotions to keep him ahead.

## Key Achievements

- Reached 100 million YouTube subscribers in 2019, the first individual creator to do so.
- Held the title of most-subscribed YouTube channel from 2013 to 2019.
- His channel has accumulated over 29 billion total views.
- Published a book, *This Book Loves You* (2015), which reached the New York Times bestseller list.
- Launched the *Scare PewDiePie* virtual reality series with YouTube Red in 2016.

## Controversies & Criticism

PewDiePie faced serious controversies that damaged his reputation and led to commercial consequences. In January 2017, The Wall Street Journal published an investigation highlighting antisemitic imagery and phrases in his videos, leading Disney's Maker Studios and YouTube to sever ties with him. In September 2017, he used a racial slur during a live stream, drawing further condemnation.

In March 2019, the Christchurch mosque shooter referenced PewDiePie in a statement before the attack. Kjellberg publicly condemned the attack and asked fans to stop using his name in that context. Critics and commentators debated the degree to which his content and community had cultivated extremist adjacency.

## Legacy & Influence

PewDiePie fundamentally shaped YouTube's creator culture, demonstrating that a single individual with a camera could accumulate an audience larger than most television networks. His success influenced a generation of creators to pursue full-time content creation. He also helped normalize gaming as mainstream entertainment, predating and arguably accelerating the rise of platforms like Twitch.

In 2020, he relocated to Japan with his wife, Swedish YouTuber Marzia Bisognin, signaling a quieter phase of his career. His channel continues to post intermittently as of 2026.
