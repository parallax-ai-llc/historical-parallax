## Summary

Eusébio won the 1965 Ballon d'Or and the 1966 World Cup Golden Boot with 9 goals. He scored 473 goals for Benfica.

## Additional Context

Eusébio da Silva Ferreira became one of the defining figures of Portuguese football in the 1960s. Born in Portuguese Mozambique, he moved to Lisbon as a teenager and spent the core of his career at Benfica, where he was central to the club’s domestic dominance and European success.

### Playing Style and Reputation

- Often described as a powerful, direct forward with an explosive shot and strong acceleration.
- Known for his goal-scoring consistency across league play and European competition.
- Widely associated with Benfica’s rise in the European Cup era and Portugal’s best World Cup performance of the 20th century.

### Key Achievements (High Level)

- **Benfica**: Long-term first-team star during a trophy-rich period.
- **Ballon d’Or (1965)**: Recognized as Europe’s top footballer that year.
- **1966 World Cup**: Finished as top scorer and helped Portugal place third.

### Legacy

Eusébio’s legacy in Portugal extends beyond statistics: he is treated as a national sporting icon and a benchmark for later generations of Portuguese attackers. After retiring, he remained publicly linked to Benfica and Portuguese football as an ambassadorial figure.

### Note on Name

In English-language sources he is commonly referred to simply as **Eusébio**, though his full name appears in formal records.
