## Summary

Ben Stokes played one of the greatest innings in cricket history at Headingley in 2019, scoring 135* to win an impossible Test match for England.

## Background

Stokes was born in Christchurch, New Zealand, and later moved to England, qualifying to play for England through residency. He developed as an all-rounder, contributing with both bat and ball, and became known for match-shaping performances in high-pressure situations.

## Playing Style and Reputation

- **Role:** Batting all-rounder (a top-order or middle-order batter who also bowls fast-medium).
- **Strengths:** Power hitting, counterattacking innings, and the ability to change the momentum of a match.
- **Leadership:** He has been widely noted for aggressive field settings and a willingness to take tactical risks as captain.

## Notable Matches (High Level)

- **2019 Headingley Test (Ashes):** His unbeaten 135 became a defining moment of the series and a modern classic in Test cricket.
- **2019 Cricket World Cup final:** England won the title in a dramatic final that included a super over; Stokes played a central role in the closing stages.

## Legacy

Stokes is frequently cited as one of England’s most influential modern cricketers because his peak performances often came in matches with large historical or competitive stakes. Beyond individual statistics, much of his public reputation rests on resilience, competitiveness, and the capacity to produce decisive moments.

In biographies and match retrospectives, the 2019 summer (World Cup final and Ashes) is often treated as the period that cemented his global profile. His later captaincy is commonly discussed as a continuation of that high-intensity, momentum-driven approach.
