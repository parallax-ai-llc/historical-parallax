## Summary

Christopher "GeT_RiGhT" Alesund is a Swedish former professional Counter-Strike player widely regarded as one of the greatest players in the history of the franchise. Born on May 29, 1990, he dominated both Counter-Strike 1.6 and Counter-Strike: Global Offensive across a career spanning more than a decade, earning recognition for his lurking playstyle, clutch ability, and extraordinary game sense.

GeT_RiGhT's peak years with Ninjas in Pyjamas (NiP) from 2012 to 2019 cemented his status as a legend. He was named HLTV's Player of the Year in both 2013 and 2014—the first player ever to win the award in consecutive years. His influence on Counter-Strike extends beyond statistics; he helped define the lurker role and demonstrated that intelligent positioning could be as impactful as raw aim.

As a pioneer of modern competitive CS, GeT_RiGhT bridged the gap between the 1.6 and CS:GO eras and remains one of the most decorated and respected figures in esports history.

## Early Life & Background

Christopher Alesund was born on May 29, 1990, in Stockholm, Sweden. He grew up during the golden age of Swedish Counter-Strike and began playing competitively as a teenager. Sweden's strong LAN culture and deep talent pool in the mid-2000s provided an ideal environment for his development. He adopted the handle "GeT_RiGhT" early in his career and quickly stood out in the Swedish amateur scene.

## Rise to Prominence

GeT_RiGhT first gained recognition in Counter-Strike 1.6, joining Fnatic in 2007 at age 17. He competed with Fnatic through several roster changes before moving to SK Gaming in 2010. With SK, he won Intel Extreme Masters Season V in 2011, solidifying his reputation as one of the world's best 1.6 players.

When CS:GO launched in August 2012, GeT_RiGhT transitioned to the new game as part of the newly formed Ninjas in Pyjamas roster alongside f0rest, Xizt, friberg, and Fifflaren. NiP immediately dominated the early CS:GO scene, winning an unprecedented 87 consecutive maps on LAN between 2012 and 2013—a record that still stands. The team won the first CS:GO Major at DreamHack Winter 2013.

## Key Achievements

GeT_RiGhT's list of accomplishments is among the most extensive in CS history. He was awarded HLTV's #1 player in the world in both 2013 and 2014, making him the only player to claim the honor in back-to-back years. He won the first CS:GO Major at DreamHack Winter 2013 and reached Major finals at ESL One Katowice 2014 and ESL One Cologne 2014.

With NiP, he accumulated over 20 tournament victories between 2012 and 2016, including titles at major events such as Fragbite Masters, ESEA LAN, and multiple DreamHack events. His individual statistics during the 2012–2015 period consistently placed him among the top-rated players globally, with an HLTV rating regularly above 1.15.

## Controversies & Criticism

GeT_RiGhT's career was largely free of major controversy. The most significant criticism he faced came during NiP's gradual decline from 2016 onward, as some analysts suggested the team held onto its veteran roster too long. In 2019, after being benched by NiP, he joined Dignitas' reunion roster alongside other Swedish legends, though the project produced limited competitive results before disbanding.

He was open about his struggles with Crohn's disease, which he was diagnosed with in 2016 and which periodically affected his ability to compete. His public discussion of the illness drew widespread support from the community and raised awareness about chronic health conditions in esports.

## Legacy & Influence

GeT_RiGhT is universally acknowledged as one of the greatest Counter-Strike players of all time. His mastery of the lurker role—playing independently to gather information, secure flanks, and capitalize on rotations—became a blueprint studied by aspiring professionals worldwide. His clutch rounds, particularly his famous plays on maps like Dust2 and Mirage, remain iconic moments in CS history.

Beyond his in-game contributions, GeT_RiGhT helped legitimize esports as a career during a critical growth period. NiP's early CS:GO dominance drew mainstream media attention in Sweden, and GeT_RiGhT's calm demeanor and sportsmanship made him an ideal ambassador for competitive gaming. He was inducted into the Esports Awards Hall of Fame, and his legacy continues to influence how the lurker position is played at the highest level.
