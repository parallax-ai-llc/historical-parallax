## Summary

Former Libyan intelligence chief convicted of crimes during the 2011 uprising and of ordering the 1989 UTA Flight 772 bombing that killed 170 people. He was sentenced to death by a Libyan court.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

| 2011 | Directed attacks on civilians during uprising |
| 2012 | Captured in Mauritania and returned to Libya |

## Overview

Abdullah Senussi (born 1949) is a Libyan former intelligence official who was a senior figure in the security apparatus during the rule of Muammar Gaddafi. He has been frequently mentioned in relation to Libya’s internal security operations and high-profile cases connected to the pre-2011 state.

## Role in Libya's security apparatus

- **Intelligence leadership:** Senussi held senior posts associated with internal intelligence and security functions, roles that typically involve surveillance, counterintelligence, and political security.
- **International legal attention:** He has been sought or discussed in connection with investigations and proceedings related to alleged crimes during the Gaddafi era.
- **Post-2011 developments:** Libya’s political fragmentation after 2011 complicated custody, extradition debates, and the venues in which cases involving former officials might be tried.

## Significance

His case is often cited in discussions about transitional justice, accountability, and the challenges of rebuilding legal institutions amid conflict.
