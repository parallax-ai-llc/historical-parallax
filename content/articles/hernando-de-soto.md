## Summary

Hernando de Soto led the first European expedition deep into what is now the southeastern United States. He was the first European to document the Mississippi River.

## Historical Context

De Soto’s expedition occurred during Spain’s rapid expansion into the Americas, when conquistadors pursued both imperial claims and personal fortunes. His earlier experience in Central and South America shaped the tactics and expectations he brought to North America.

## Significance

- Helped produce some of the earliest European written accounts of societies in the American Southeast.
- Confirmed (to Europeans) the scale of the Mississippi River system and its importance as an interior waterway.
- Left an archaeological and documentary trail that continues to inform research into early contact-era history.

## Legacy and Debate

Modern assessments often distinguish between the expedition’s geographic and documentary impact and the severe human costs associated with conquest, including violence, coerced labor, and the spread of epidemic diseases. The expedition is therefore studied both for what it recorded and for the disruptions it intensified.
