## Overview
On March 1, 1919, 33 Korean cultural and religious leaders signed a Declaration of Independence and read it aloud at Tapgol Park in central Seoul. This act sparked nationwide protests involving over 2 million Koreans.

## Significance
Although the movement was brutally suppressed by Japanese forces, it catalyzed the formation of the Korean Provisional Government and influenced non-violent resistance movements worldwide, including Gandhis Satyagraha in India.
