## Summary

Serena Jameka Williams (born 1981) is an American former professional tennis player widely considered the greatest female tennis player of all time. With 23 Grand Slam singles titles (Open Era record), she dominated women's tennis for over two decades. Along with sister Venus, Serena emerged from Compton, California, to transform tennis with her powerful serve (fastest ever recorded by a woman), athleticism, and mental toughness. Beyond sports, Williams became a cultural icon, fashion entrepreneur, and venture capitalist. Her excellence, outspokenness on race and gender, and longevity cement her status as one of history's greatest athletes.

## Early Life

**Birth:** September 26, 1981, Saginaw, Michigan
**Family:** Fourth of five daughters. Richard Williams (father) and Oracene Price (mother). Raised in Compton, California.

**Richard's Plan:**
Father determined Venus and Serena would become tennis champions. Taught them on public courts despite lack of tennis background. Famously mapped out career before they were born.

**Compton:**
Trained amid gang activity. Stories of drive-by shootings during practice. Moved to Florida for tennis academies.

## Professional Career

### Early Years

**Pro Debut (1995):** Age 14.

**First Grand Slam (1999):** U.S. Open—defeated Martina Hingis. Age 17.

### Dominance

**"Serena Slam" (2002-2003):**
Held all four Grand Slam titles simultaneously across 2002-2003.

**"Second Serena Slam" (2014-2015):**
Achieved again at ages 33-34.

### Grand Slam Titles (23 Singles)

| Slam | Titles |
|------|--------|
| Australian Open | 7 |
| French Open | 3 |
| Wimbledon | 7 |
| U.S. Open | 6 |

**Doubles:** 14 Grand Slam doubles (all with Venus)

### Career Statistics

- 73 WTA singles titles
- 319 weeks at world No. 1
- Four Olympic gold medals
- Over $94 million career prize money (most ever for female athlete)

## Playing Style

**Power:**
- Serve up to 128 mph (fastest ever by woman)
- Groundstrokes: heavy, flat, aggressive
- Movement despite muscular build

**Mental Toughness:**
Famous comebacks. Fighter in big moments. "Clutch" player.

**Evolution:**
Adapted game over 25-year career. Less mobile later but smarter.

## Venus Partnership

**Doubles:**
- 14 Grand Slam doubles together
- 3 Olympic gold medals
- Undefeated in Grand Slam doubles finals

**Head-to-Head:**
31 professional matches (Serena leads 19-12).

## Controversies

**2009 U.S. Open:**
Famous outburst at lineswoman. Fined, penalized point.

**2018 U.S. Open Final:**
Dispute with umpire Carlos Ramos over coaching violation. Called him "thief." Lost final to Naomi Osaka. Sparked debate over double standards.

**Catsuit:**
French Open initially banned her compression catsuit; rules later clarified.

## Retirement

**August 2022:** Announced "evolution away from tennis" at U.S. Open. Final match: lost third round to Ajla Tomljanović.

## Legacy
