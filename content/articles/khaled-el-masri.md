## Summary

German citizen of Lebanese descent who was kidnapped by the CIA and held in a secret prison in Afghanistan for five months due to mistaken identity. His case is one of the most documented CIA extraordinary rendition cases.

## Additional Context

### Extraordinary Rendition Context

- His case is frequently cited in reporting and human-rights documentation about “extraordinary rendition,” where suspects were secretly transferred and detained outside normal legal processes.
- The central feature of his case was mistaken identity, highlighting how errors can propagate through intelligence and detention systems.

### Detention and Release

- Reports describe months of detention in Afghanistan before he was released without charges.
- His release and the circumstances around it became a major public controversy in Europe, raising questions about accountability and inter-state cooperation.

### Legal Proceedings and Findings

- Efforts to pursue legal remedies involved multiple jurisdictions and faced obstacles such as state-secrets claims and diplomatic sensitivities.
- The European Court of Human Rights decision became a landmark ruling emphasizing state responsibility for unlawful detention and ill-treatment.

### Broader Impact

- The case influenced public debate about counterterrorism policy, oversight, and the balance between security and civil liberties.
- It remains a reference point for discussions about transparency, reparations, and the legal limits of intelligence operations.
