## Summary

Philip Emeagwali is a Nigerian computer scientist who won the 1989 Gordon Bell Prize for using a Connection Machine to perform 3.1 billion calculations per second, then one of the fastest computation speeds achieved. His work on using massively parallel computers for petroleum reservoir modeling demonstrated the potential of parallel computing. He has been called a pioneer of the internet for his contributions to parallel computing.
