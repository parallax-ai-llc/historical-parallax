## Expanded Context

### What Happened

- The attack took place on **24 May 2014** at the **Jewish Museum of Belgium** in **Brussels**.
- A gunman opened fire with a long gun and a handgun, killing **four people**.
- The incident was widely treated as an **antisemitic terrorist attack** and drew international condemnation.

### Victims (High-Level)

- The victims included museum staff and visitors; public reporting emphasizes the attack’s impact on the Jewish community in Belgium and on cultural institutions generally.

### Suspect and Motive (High-Level)

- The case is associated with **Mehdi Nemmouche**, a French national described in reporting as an **ISIS returnee**.
- Accounts commonly describe him as having traveled to **Syria** prior to the attack.
- Public summaries often frame the motive in terms of **terrorism** and **targeting a Jewish institution**.

### Investigation and Arrest (High-Level)

- Nemmouche was arrested in **Marseille** shortly after the shooting, following travel in Europe.
- Reporting described weapons and related items recovered at the time of arrest.

### Legal Outcome (High-Level)

- Belgian proceedings later resulted in a conviction and a **life sentence** being reported in mainstream coverage.
- The case is frequently cited in discussions about **foreign fighter returnees** and domestic security responses in Europe.

### Broader Significance

- The attack contributed to heightened security concerns for **Jewish sites** and public venues.
- It is sometimes referenced alongside other mid-2010s European terrorism cases in analyses of radicalization pathways and cross-border mobility.
