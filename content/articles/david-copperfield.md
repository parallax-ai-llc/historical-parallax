## Summary

David Copperfield (born David Seth Kotkin) is an American magician described as the most commercially successful magician in history. His name appeared in Epstein-related court documents through witness testimony describing a dinner where Copperfield performed magic tricks and allegedly made comments about having access to young women through an agency. There are no criminal charges or direct allegations of sexual misconduct against Copperfield related to Epstein, but the testimony raises questions about his awareness of and comments related to Epstein's activities.

### Early Life

Born September 16, 1956, in Metuchen, New Jersey:

**Early Start**:
- Started magic at age 10

Copperfield became the world's most famous magician:

**Television Specials**:

- Reached billions of viewers globally

**Iconic Illusions**:
- Making the Statue of Liberty disappear (1983)

**Commercial Success**:

- Running since 1996

## Lessons

### Guilt by Association Limits

**What We Know**: Present at dinner, made comment
**What We Don't Know**: Intent, knowledge, involvement
**What We Can't Conclude**: Criminal activity or direct complicity

### The Importance of Evidence

**Standards**:
- Allegations ≠ proof
- Presence ≠ participation
- Comment ≠ criminal conduct
- Suspicion ≠ guilt

### Grey Areas

Copperfield's case shows:
- Not everything is black and white
- Questionable judgment ≠ criminality
- Some associations raise questions without providing answers
- Impossible to resolve all ambiguity
