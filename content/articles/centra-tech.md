## Summary

Fraudulent cryptocurrency startup promoted by Floyd Mayweather and DJ Khaled that raised $32 million through fake partnerships with Visa and Mastercard. Founders convicted.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2017 | $32 million raised through fraud |

## Overview

- Centra Tech was presented as a cryptocurrency and payments startup, and it raised funds through an initial coin offering (ICO).
- The project became a prominent example used to illustrate how marketing and celebrity promotion can amplify a fraudulent offering.

## Promotion and Misrepresentation (High-Level)

- The company claimed partnerships and capabilities that were later described by authorities as fabricated or misleading.
- Celebrity endorsements helped drive attention and fundraising, even though endorsements do not necessarily verify underlying claims.

## Enforcement and Outcomes

- U.S. authorities brought charges tied to securities and wire fraud.
- The founders were arrested and later convicted, and the case became a reference point in debates over ICO regulation and disclosure standards.

## Broader Significance

- Often cited as an early, high-profile warning about due diligence in token sales.
- Helped shape a narrative that regulatory enforcement would extend to crypto fundraising structures that functioned like unregistered securities offerings.

## Notes

- The Centra Tech episode is frequently cited in investor education materials as a "red flags" case study.
- It is also used to illustrate how claims of major corporate partnerships should be independently verified.
