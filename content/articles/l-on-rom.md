## Summary

Belgian Force Publique officer in the Congo Free State who decorated his garden with severed human heads. Inspiration for Kurtz in Joseph Conrad's Heart of Darkness.

## Background

Léon Rom is discussed in histories of the Congo Free State, a period widely documented for severe exploitation, coercive labor systems, and large-scale human rights abuses under colonial rule.

## Force Publique context

- The Force Publique functioned as a colonial military and policing apparatus.
- Violence and hostage-taking were used to enforce extraction policies, including rubber quotas.

## Cultural legacy

Rom is sometimes referenced in discussions of how colonial brutality influenced European literature and public debate, including comparisons to characters in *Heart of Darkness*.

## Why this matters

The Congo Free State remains a central case study in the consequences of unaccountable colonial governance and extractive political economy.
