## Summary

Albert Starr, together with engineer Lowell Edwards, developed the first successful artificial heart valve (Starr-Edwards valve) in 1960. The mechanical ball-and-cage valve design saved hundreds of thousands of lives and opened the field of prosthetic heart valve surgery. He performed the first successful mitral valve replacement in 1960.
