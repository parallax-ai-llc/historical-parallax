German police officer who committed serial murders while serving as a detective.

## Background

- Served as a police officer in Baden-Württemberg (Germany), including work in Stuttgart; later described as serving in a canine unit.
- Reported personal and financial stressors preceded the crime spree, including heavy debt and the death of a young daughter from a brain tumour (as reported in press and Wikipedia summaries).

## Crimes (1984–1985)

High-level pattern described in reporting:

- Lured victims at remote rest areas or parking areas.
- Shot victims with a police-issue pistol (often cited as a Walther P5).
- Used the victims' vehicles as part of the escape logistics.
- During robberies, smashed bank counter/teller windows with a sledgehammer, leading to the nickname "Hammer-Killer" (Der Hammermörder).

Commonly cited victims connected to the robberies:

- Siegfried Pfitzer (found shot at a rest stop in May 1984; vehicle linked to a bank robbery the same day).
- Eugene Wethey (found shot in December 1984; vehicle later linked to a bank robbery).
- Wilfried Scheider (found shot in July 1985; case linked to another bank robbery).

## Investigation and Exposure

- A special commission (often referred to as SOKO "Hammer") was formed and conducted a large-scale investigation with many interviews and leads.
- Public appeals were made, including a reconstruction segment on the German TV program *Aktenzeichen XY… ungelöst*.
- Suspicion turned toward Poehlke after police linked a discovered uniform to him and found inconsistencies in his explanation.

## Family Murders and Death

According to widely reported accounts:

- As investigators closed in, Poehlke killed his wife and one of his sons at their home.
- He fled Germany with his younger son and was found days later in Torre Canne (near Brindisi, Italy).
- He killed the child and then died by suicide (murder–suicide).
