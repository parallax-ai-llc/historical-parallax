## Summary

Medellín Cartel hitman known as 'Popeye' who confessed to killing 300 people and ordering 3,000 more on Pablo Escobar's behalf. He became a YouTube celebrity before dying of cancer.

## Additional Context

### Medellín Cartel context

- Velásquez is most associated with the Medellín Cartel era, when Colombia faced intense violence tied to cocaine trafficking and state–cartel conflict.
- The Medellín Cartel’s campaign included assassinations, bombings, and intimidation aimed at influencing policy and law enforcement.

### Relationship to Pablo Escobar

- He became publicly known through accounts describing him as a close subordinate and operative for Pablo Escobar.
- His notoriety has been amplified by later interviews and media portrayals of the cartel period.

### Legal consequences and imprisonment

- Public reporting describes him as surrendering to authorities and serving a lengthy prison sentence for crimes linked to cartel activity.
- After release, he remained a controversial public figure, drawing criticism for the way cartel-era violence was discussed or narrated in popular media.

### Media presence

- In the late 2010s, he gained attention through online videos and interviews in which he recounted cartel history.
- This phenomenon is often cited in debates about "narco-culture" and the ethics of monetizing or glamorizing criminal violence.

### Death

- He died in 2020, with reporting indicating cancer as the cause.
- His life story is frequently used as a window into the broader human and political costs of Colombia’s drug war period.
