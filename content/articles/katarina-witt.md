## Summary

Katarina Witt won consecutive Olympic golds in 1984 and 1988 for East Germany. After reunification, Stasi surveillance files about her were revealed.

## Early Life and Training

Witt was born in Staaken (then in East Germany) and developed as an elite skater within the East German sports system. She trained under coach Jutta Müller, one of the most prominent figure-skating coaches in the German Democratic Republic (GDR).

## Competitive Profile

Witt was known for combining strong technical content with expressive, theatrical presentation. In the 1980s, women’s figure skating often emphasized artistry and musical interpretation alongside jumps and spins, and Witt became one of the era’s most recognizable performers.

### Major Titles (high level)

- Olympic champion (two-time)
- Multiple-time European champion
- World champion (at least once during the 1980s)

## Post-Competitive Career

After her eligible-competition peak, Witt performed in professional skating shows and media appearances. She also worked in entertainment projects connected to figure skating, which helped keep her public profile prominent beyond her competitive years.

## Stasi Files and Public Discussion

Following German reunification, archives from the Ministry for State Security (Stasi) became available, and reports about surveillance practices around high-profile athletes—including Witt—became part of broader public conversations about state control in the former GDR. The topic is typically discussed in terms of how elite sports intersected with politics, security services, and international image-making during the Cold War.

## Legacy

Witt is often cited as a defining star of 1980s women’s figure skating, both for results and for cultural impact. Her Olympic victories are regularly referenced in retrospectives on the sport’s evolution and on the history of East German athletics.
