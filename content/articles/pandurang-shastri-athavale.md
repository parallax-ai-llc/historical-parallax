## Summary

Pandurang Shastri Athavale (Dada) was an Indian philosopher and social reformer who founded the Swadhyaya movement, a self-study movement based on the Bhagavad Gita that transformed millions of lives across India. He taught that the divine resides in every person and that selfless devotion expressed through work could transform society. He received the Templeton Prize in 1997 and the Magsaysay Award. His movement established self-sufficient communities across India.
