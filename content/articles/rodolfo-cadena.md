## Summary

Leader of the Mexican Mafia whose murder in prison triggered the formation of rival gang Nuestra Familia.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Additional Context

- Cadena is commonly identified by the nickname **"Cheyenne"** and is described in sources as a prominent early figure in the **Mexican Mafia (La Eme)**.
- Accounts of his role emphasize the period when California prison-gang structures were consolidating, with La Eme becoming influential among Mexican-American inmates.

## Incarceration and Influence (High Level)

- Sources describe Cadena as having gained status while incarcerated, becoming associated with violent enforcement and internal discipline within La Eme.
- Narratives of his influence often connect him to efforts to expand La Eme’s reach beyond a purely in-prison identity, including coordination, alliances, and control of illicit activity.

## Death and Aftermath

- The killing of Cadena is widely cited as a flashpoint event in escalating conflict between **Nuestra Familia** and the Mexican Mafia.
- Later histories commonly frame this period as a turning point leading to a longer-running cycle of retaliations inside California prisons.
