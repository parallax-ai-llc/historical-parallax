## Summary

Seve Ballesteros won five major championships and was one of golf's most charismatic players. He popularized European golf and was a Ryder Cup legend.

## Early life and rise

- Ballesteros was born in Cantabria, Spain, and became a prominent figure in the first wave of modern European golf stars.
- He turned professional young and quickly established himself on the European Tour, gaining attention for creativity and shot-making.

## Playing style

- Known for an imaginative **short game** and recovery shots from difficult lies, he was often described as a player who could "manufacture" shots.
- His willingness to take aggressive lines and attempt low-percentage escapes contributed to both highlight-reel moments and occasional volatility.

## Major championships (high-level)

- Ballesteros won **five major championships**, including victories at The Open Championship and the Masters Tournament.
- In addition to majors, he won numerous European Tour events and was a consistent contender in the late 1970s through the 1980s.

## Ryder Cup legacy

- He became an emblem of European competitiveness in the Ryder Cup era when Europe increasingly challenged and then often defeated the United States.
- Beyond playing, he also served as a **captain**, reinforcing his reputation as a leader and emotional catalyst for the European team.

## Illness and legacy

- Ballesteros later battled **brain cancer** and died in 2011.
- His influence is frequently framed in terms of making elite golf feel more international and inspirational, particularly for Spanish and European players who followed.
- Memorial efforts and charitable initiatives associated with his legacy have been used to promote cancer awareness and support.
