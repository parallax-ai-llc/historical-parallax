## Summary

Military commander of M23 rebel movement who led the capture of Goma in 2012. Wanted for war crimes including summary executions and forced recruitment.

## Background: Eastern DRC Conflict (High Level)

Eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo has experienced protracted armed conflict involving state forces, rebel movements, and local militias, with cross-border dynamics and competition over security and resources. The humanitarian consequences have included repeated displacement, civilian killings, and chronic insecurity.

## M23 and Command Role

M23 (March 23 Movement) is commonly described as a rebel group that became internationally prominent during the 2012 offensive that briefly seized Goma, a major city in North Kivu province. Makenga has been portrayed in reporting as a senior military commander associated with the group’s operations during that period.

## Allegations and Human Rights Concerns

Public reporting has linked M23 units to alleged abuses, including summary executions, forced recruitment, and other violations of the laws of war. Documentation of such allegations typically draws on UN reporting, NGO investigations, and witness testimony, with incident-level details sometimes contested.

## Regional and Diplomatic Dimension

The conflict has often been discussed in connection with regional security politics involving neighboring states, peace negotiations, and disarmament/reintegration efforts. Periods of battlefield escalation have frequently been followed by ceasefire attempts, mediation, and talks aimed at demobilization.

## Why Goma Matters

Goma is a strategic hub near international borders and key transport routes, so control of the city has outsized political and humanitarian significance. Its capture in 2012 drew major international attention because it highlighted state fragility, the vulnerability of civilians, and the limits of existing peacekeeping and diplomatic efforts.

## Terms (Quick Glossary)

- **Laws of war**: Rules under international humanitarian law governing conduct in armed conflict.
