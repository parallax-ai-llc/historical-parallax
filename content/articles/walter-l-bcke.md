## Summary

German politician assassinated by a neo-Nazi for supporting refugee policies.

## Background

Walter Lübcke was a German Christian Democratic Union (CDU) politician and public administrator.
He served for many years in regional politics in the state of Hesse, and became known nationally after public remarks defending Germany’s acceptance of refugees during the 2015–2016 European migrant crisis.

## Political and Administrative Work

Lübcke held roles in local and state-level public administration, and was associated with the Kassel regional government (Regierungspräsidium Kassel) in Hesse.
His work included responsibilities typical of regional authorities, such as oversight and coordination across municipalities and state agencies.

## Refugee Policy Controversy

During public events related to refugee accommodation and integration, Lübcke spoke in favor of Germany’s constitutional obligations and humanitarian approach.
His comments were circulated online and became a focal point for far-right agitation, including threats and targeted harassment.

## Assassination

Lübcke was shot at his home in 2019.
German authorities treated the killing as a politically motivated act with links to far-right extremism.
The case became a prominent example in debates over political violence and extremist networks in Germany.

## Investigation and Trial (High Level)

Investigators identified and charged suspects connected to the far-right scene.
Court proceedings examined evidence around planning, motive, and possible accomplice involvement.
The case drew sustained national attention due to the victim’s public role and the broader context of extremist violence.

## Legacy

The assassination intensified public discussion about threats faced by elected officials and civil servants, and about the role of online incitement.
It also prompted renewed focus on monitoring and prosecuting violent far-right extremism.
