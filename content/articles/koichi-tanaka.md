## Summary

Koichi Tanaka is a Japanese engineer who shared the 2002 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for developing soft desorption ionisation methods for mass spectrometric analyses of biological macromolecules. His technique enabled the analysis of large proteins and other biomolecules by mass spectrometry. Remarkably, he was a salaryman at Shimadzu Corporation with only a bachelor's degree when he won the Nobel Prize.
