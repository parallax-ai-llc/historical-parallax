## Summary

Jack Welch served as CEO of General Electric from 1981 to 2001, growing the company's market value from $12 billion to $410 billion. He was known for his aggressive management style, including ranking employees and cutting the bottom 10%. He transformed GE from a manufacturing company into a financial and services conglomerate. Fortune magazine named him 'Manager of the Century' in 1999.
