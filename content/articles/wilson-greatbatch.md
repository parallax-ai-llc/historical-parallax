## Summary

Wilson Greatbatch invented the implantable cardiac pacemaker in 1958, saving millions of lives. He accidentally discovered that a circuit he was building emitted electrical pulses similar to the human heartbeat. He also developed the lithium-iodide battery that powers modern pacemakers, extending their lifespan to over 10 years.
