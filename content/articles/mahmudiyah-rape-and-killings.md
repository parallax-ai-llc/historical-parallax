## Summary

US soldiers raped a 14-year-old Iraqi girl and murdered her and her family, then set the house on fire. Ringleader Steven Green was sentenced to life in prison.

## Context

- The incident occurred during the Iraq War near Mahmudiyah, south of Baghdad, while U.S. forces were operating in the area.
- It became one of the most widely reported U.S.-perpetrated war crimes of the conflict and drew international condemnation.

## What Happened (High-Level)

- A group of U.S. soldiers planned and carried out an assault on an Iraqi family.
- They raped a 14-year-old girl, killed her, and murdered several members of her family.
- The perpetrators attempted to destroy evidence by setting the home on fire.

## Perpetrators

- The crime was attributed to several U.S. Army soldiers from the same unit.
- Steven Dale Green was identified as a central participant and later faced federal prosecution.

## Investigation and Prosecution

- The case was investigated and prosecuted in U.S. courts.
- Multiple participants received long prison sentences.
- Green was sentenced to life in prison; he later died in custody.

## Why It Matters

- The case intensified scrutiny of military discipline, rules of engagement, and accountability for abuses against civilians.
- It is frequently cited in discussions of war crimes, civilian protection, and the legal treatment of crimes committed in conflict zones.

## Key Facts

- **Location:** Mahmudiyah area, Iraq
- **Date:** March 2006
- **Victims:** A 14-year-old girl and members of her family
- **Outcome:** U.S. convictions and life sentence for the primary defendant
