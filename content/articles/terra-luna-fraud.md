## Overview

The term “Terra Luna fraud” is commonly used to describe alleged misconduct and disputed representations surrounding the Terra ecosystem, including the TerraUSD (UST) stablecoin and the LUNA token.
The episode became globally notable because the collapse was rapid, highly public, and financially large, affecting retail and institutional participants.

## How the System Worked (High-Level)

TerraUSD (UST) was marketed as a stablecoin, meaning it aimed to maintain a relatively stable price (often referenced to the U.S. dollar).
Its stability mechanism was tied to LUNA through algorithmic and market-incentive design rather than traditional reserves held in cash equivalents.
This design choice is central to both the project’s early growth narrative and the later criticism.

## Collapse and Immediate Aftermath

When confidence in UST’s peg weakened, market dynamics accelerated the decline.
As UST de-pegged, LUNA’s market price and supply dynamics also deteriorated, producing a feedback loop that amplified losses.
The event triggered emergency measures inside the project and intense public scrutiny across crypto markets.

## Investigations, Litigation, and Regulatory Attention

In the aftermath, regulators and prosecutors in multiple jurisdictions examined whether investors were misled and whether securities or fraud laws were violated.
Civil lawsuits and enforcement actions focused on issues such as disclosures, marketing claims, and the adequacy of risk communication.

## Broader Impact

The Terra collapse is often cited as a turning point that:

- accelerated stablecoin and crypto-market regulation debates
- increased skepticism toward “algorithmic stablecoin” designs
- contributed to broader market contagion and loss of confidence
