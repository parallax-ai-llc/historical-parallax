## Summary

Antonio Stradivari was an Italian luthier whose violins, violas, and cellos are considered the finest string instruments ever made. His instruments, known as Stradivarius, are prized for their exceptional tonal quality and remain the standard against which all others are measured. Approximately 650 of his instruments survive today, each worth millions of dollars.
