## Overview

The **Tamil Tigers**, more formally the **Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE)**, were a Sri Lankan Tamil militant and separatist organization that fought for an independent Tamil state in the north and east of Sri Lanka. Wikipedia summarizes the LTTE as one of the most prominent armed groups in the Sri Lankan Civil War and notes that it operated a sophisticated military and administrative apparatus for a non-state actor.

## Historical Context

- **Origins (1970s):** The LTTE emerged in a broader context of Sri Lankan ethnic politics and Tamil grievances, with the organization commonly dated to the mid-1970s.
- **Civil war era:** The conflict intensified in the 1980s and persisted for decades, marked by insurgency, counterinsurgency, and periodic negotiations.
- **End of the organization (2009):** Wikipedia reports that the LTTE was militarily defeated by the Sri Lankan government in 2009, effectively ending its control over territory.

## Tactics and International Designations

Wikipedia and related public reporting describe the LTTE as using guerrilla warfare and maritime capabilities, and as being associated with political violence including assassinations and suicide attacks. Several governments designated the LTTE as a terrorist organization during the conflict (the details and dates vary by jurisdiction).
