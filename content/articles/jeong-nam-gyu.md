## Summary

South Korean serial killer who murdered 14 people between 2004 and 2006 in the Gyeonggi Province area. He targeted both men and women. Sentenced to death.

## Case overview (high level)

Jeong Nam-gyu’s crimes spanned roughly two years and affected multiple victims across the wider Gyeonggi area.
The fact that victims included both men and women contributed to broad public anxiety, as it reduced the ability to define the risk as limited to a single demographic.
Cases with multiple incidents across a region often place sustained pressure on investigative resources and public confidence.

## Investigation and identification

Serial cases typically require investigators to determine whether separate incidents share a common perpetrator.
This can involve timeline analysis, linking of evidence across jurisdictions, and careful evaluation of tips and witness statements.
Jeong was arrested in 2006, ending the active series.

## Trial and sentencing

He was convicted and sentenced to death in 2007 according to commonly cited summaries.
As with other death sentences in South Korea, the distinction between a legal sentence and the reality of a long-standing execution moratorium is a frequent point of discussion.
