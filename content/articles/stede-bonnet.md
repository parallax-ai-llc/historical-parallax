## Summary

Barbadian plantation owner known as 'The Gentleman Pirate' who became a pirate reportedly to escape his wife. He was one of the few pirates who actually purchased his ship. Sailed with Blackbeard before being captured and hanged.

## Background

Bonnet was a wealthy landowner in Barbados, which made his decision to turn to piracy unusual compared with most seafarers who became pirates through maritime labor and wartime privateering. Contemporary accounts emphasize his social status and relative inexperience at sea.

## Piracy and association with Blackbeard

After acquiring the sloop *Revenge*, Bonnet attacked ships in the Atlantic and Caribbean. He later sailed in proximity to Edward Teach (Blackbeard), and sources describe a period in which Bonnet’s command was effectively subordinated while his ship and crew operated alongside Blackbeard’s larger force.

## Capture and execution

In 1718, colonial authorities pursued Bonnet’s crew, and he was eventually captured near the Cape Fear region and taken to Charles Town (Charleston). He was tried and convicted of piracy and was executed by hanging in December 1718.

## Legacy

Bonnet’s life is remembered in piracy folklore as a story of social transgression: a man of wealth abandoning plantation society for an outlaw career. His nickname “the Gentleman Pirate” continues to appear in popular histories, novels, and screen portrayals.
