## Summary

Secretary of Rajneesh who orchestrated the 1984 bioterror attack and attempted murder of a US attorney.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1970s | Became a close associate of Bhagwan Shree Rajneesh (Osho) and took the name Ma Anand Sheela.[^1] |
| 1981 | Moved with the Rajneesh movement to the United States and helped manage the Oregon commune (Rajneeshpuram).[^1] |
| 1984 | Orchestrated the Rajneeshee salmonella poisoning in The Dalles, Oregon (the largest bioterror attack on U.S. soil at the time).[^1] |
| 1984 | Linked to planning for additional attacks and to attempts to influence local elections around Wasco County, Oregon.[^1] |
| 1985 | Left the United States amid escalating investigations and internal conflict within the movement.[^1] |
| 1985–1986 | Pleaded guilty to charges including assault and conspiracy related to the 1984 poisoning and other offenses; served federal prison time.[^1] |
| 2010s | Became the subject of renewed public attention via documentaries and journalism about Rajneeshpuram and the 1984 attacks.[^1] |

## Background

- **Origin**: Born Sheela Ambalal Patel in India (1949).[^1]
- **Name**: Adopted the honorific "Ma Anand Sheela" after joining Rajneesh's community.[^1]
- **Role**: Often described as Rajneesh's secretary and a key administrator/spokesperson for the movement in Oregon.[^1]

## Why She Is Historically Notable

- **Public health / bioterrorism**: Associated with the deliberate contamination of restaurant salad bars with *Salmonella* in 1984, sickening hundreds of people in The Dalles, Oregon.[^1]
- **Political influence operations**: The Rajneesh movement pursued local political power in Wasco County, leading to intense conflicts with residents and authorities.[^1]
- **Legal precedent**: The case is frequently cited in discussions of domestic bioterrorism and cult-associated violence in the United States.[^1]

## Key Events (High Level)

- **Rajneeshpuram**: The movement built a large commune in rural Oregon, which became the focal point of legal, political, and social disputes.[^1]
- **1984 salmonella poisoning**: The poisoning campaign targeted multiple restaurants in The Dalles to reduce voter turnout and sway local elections.[^1]
- **Aftermath**: Investigations and prosecutions followed; Sheela later faced criminal charges and imprisonment.[^1]

## Legacy and Interpretations

- **Media portrayal**: Modern documentaries and reporting often focus on the Oregon conflict, the bioterror plot, and Sheela’s public persona as a disciplined, confrontational spokesperson.[^1]
- **Broader theme**: The episode is used as a case study in how insular movements can escalate from social conflict to organized criminal activity under charismatic leadership and intense in-group loyalty.[^1]
