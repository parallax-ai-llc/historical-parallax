## Summary

Park Seo-joon (박서준) is a South Korean actor born on December 16, 1988, in Seoul, South Korea. He is one of the most bankable leading men in the Korean entertainment industry, recognized for his versatility across romantic comedies, action dramas, and prestige film. His consistent ability to top viewership ratings and drive international streaming numbers has made him a defining figure of the Korean Wave's second decade.

His appeal extends beyond domestic audiences — Park has cultivated a massive fanbase across Southeast Asia, Japan, and the Americas through streaming platforms, particularly Netflix and Disney+. He is represented by Awesome ENT and has leveraged his fame into brand ambassadorships, music releases, and a breakout Hollywood appearance.

---

## Early Life & Background

Park Seo-joon was born Park Young-gyu in Seoul and grew up with an interest in athletics and performance. He attended Hyungseong High School and later studied at Kyung Hee University. Before pursuing acting, he completed his mandatory military service as a social service worker. His entry into entertainment came through modeling and small commercial appearances, which built the foundation for his television debut.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Park made his acting debut in 2011 with a minor role in the drama *Dream High 2*. He gained wider recognition through supporting roles in *Pots of Gold* (2013) and *Witch's Romance* (2014). His breakout came with the 2015 romantic comedy *Kill Me, Heal Me*, where his charismatic performance earned him a dedicated following.

The 2015–2016 period proved pivotal: he starred in *She Was Pretty* (2015), which recorded strong ratings and cemented him as a leading man. His role in *Hwarang: The Poet Warrior Youth* (2016–2017) alongside Park Hyung-sik and BTS's V introduced him to an even broader international audience.

The 2019 romantic comedy *What's Wrong with Secretary Kim* was a commercial and critical peak — it averaged 8.96% viewership nationally and topped streaming charts across Asia, earning Park a Baeksang Arts Award nomination.

---

## Key Achievements

- **Itaewon Class** (2020, JTBC): Averaged 16.1% viewership; ranked No. 1 on Netflix in multiple countries simultaneously. Park's portrayal of the resilient entrepreneur Park Saeroyi is considered his career-defining performance.
- **The Marvels** (2023, Marvel/Disney): Park appeared as a supporting character in a notable Hollywood crossover role, marking one of the most high-profile entries by a Korean actor into the MCU.
- **Baeksang Arts Awards**: Multiple nominations across television categories between 2018 and 2021.
- Music: Released several OST tracks that charted on Melon and Gaon, demonstrating crossover appeal.

## Controversies & Criticism

Park Seo-joon has maintained a largely controversy-free public image. In 2019, minor criticism arose over his agencies' management of fan event logistics, though this was resolved without lasting reputational damage. He has been open about the pressures of celebrity and the physical demands of his roles, which generated sympathetic public discourse rather than backlash.

---

## Legacy & Influence

Park Seo-joon represents a generation of Korean actors who built careers on the convergence of domestic television, global streaming, and social media fandom. His trajectory — from supporting roles to MCU casting — illustrates the expanding pathways for Korean talent in global entertainment. He is frequently cited as a style and fitness icon among Korean men in their 20s and 30s, and his endorsement work with brands including Laneige and Puma underscores his cross-industry influence.
