## Summary
Peter Kürten, the 'Vampire of Düsseldorf,' was a German serial killer who committed a series of murders and sexual assaults in Düsseldorf in 1929. He drank the blood of some victims. His case was studied extensively by psychiatrists and inspired Fritz Lang's film 'M' (1931). He was executed by guillotine.

## Early Life
Born May 26, 1883, in Mülheim am Rhein, Germany, into a large, impoverished family. His father was a violent alcoholic who abused the family. Kürten was exposed to violence and sexual deviance from a young age. He committed petty crimes as a youth and spent years in prison. [^1]

## Criminal Career
In 1929, Kürten committed a series of murders and attacks in Düsseldorf, using hammers, scissors, and knives. He attacked men, women, and children, killing at least nine. He claimed to drink blood from some victims' wounds. His crimes caused mass panic in the city. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Kürten's wife turned him in after he confessed the murders to her in May 1930. He was arrested and confessed to multiple murders. Convicted in April 1931, he was sentenced to death nine times. He was guillotined at Klingelpütz Prison in Cologne on July 2, 1931. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- His case was extensively studied by psychiatrists, advancing criminal psychology [^4]
- Inspired Fritz Lang's classic film 'M' (1931)
- Contributed to understanding of serial murder and sadistic psychology

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered at least nine people [^5]
- Attacked men, women, and children indiscriminately
- Drank blood from victims
- Caused mass panic in Düsseldorf
- His crimes terrorized an entire city for months
