## Summary

South African serial killer known as the Muldersdrift Strangler who murdered at least 10 women in the West Rand area. He targeted sex workers and hitchhikers.

## Career Timeline

## Victims and Vulnerability (High-Level)

The case is often summarized as involving attacks on women in vulnerable situations, including sex workers and hitchhikers. Criminology discussions frequently note that such victim groups can face elevated risk because of reduced protection, stigma, and lower likelihood of rapid reporting or sustained investigative attention.

## Investigation and Evidence

Serial cases in geographically spread areas like the West Rand can require linking multiple incidents through forensic evidence, behavioral patterns, and timeline reconstruction. Public reporting highlights that arrests and convictions in such cases often rely on a combination of physical evidence, witness statements, and suspect interviews rather than a single decisive clue.

## Sentencing

Griffin was sentenced to multiple life terms. In South African court practice, multiple life sentences are sometimes used to reflect the scale of harm across numerous counts and to reduce the chance of early release.

## Broader Context

- **Community impact:** The killings contributed to fear and changes in behavior, especially among those who relied on hitchhiking or worked in precarious environments.
- **Public discourse:** Coverage of cases involving sex workers can shape debates about policing priorities, social support, and the safety consequences of marginalization.

## Name and Location

He is referred to as the “Muldersdrift Strangler,” a label derived from the area associated with some of the crimes. Such monikers are common in media coverage of serial cases, functioning as shorthand for geographically linked patterns, though they can also oversimplify the details of where incidents occurred and how investigations unfolded.

In case summaries, the West Rand setting is often noted as a mix of urban edges and semi-rural routes that can create opportunities for predatory crimes.
