## Summary

Vallabhacharya was a Hindu philosopher and theologian who founded the Pushti Marg (Path of Grace) devotional tradition and the Shuddhadvaita (pure non-dualism) school of Vedanta. He taught that the world is real and is a manifestation of Brahman, rejecting the illusionistic view. He traveled across India debating scholars and establishing his movement. His devotional approach centered on Krishna bhakti through service and love.
