## Summary

Thich Thien-An was a Vietnamese Zen master who was one of the first Vietnamese Buddhist teachers in America. He founded the International Buddhist Meditation Center in Los Angeles in 1970. He taught at UCLA and wrote Zen Philosophy, Zen Practice. He helped Vietnamese refugees settle in the United States and promoted understanding of Vietnamese Buddhism in America. He died of cancer at age 54.
