## Additional Context (High-Level)

### Why unlicensed practice and impersonation cases matter
- Medical impersonation and unlicensed practice can harm patients through misdiagnosis, inappropriate procedures, or unsafe prescriptions.
- Even where harm is not documented, deception undermines trust in credentialing and clinical oversight.

### Patterns investigators commonly look for
- Claims of training or licensure that cannot be verified through official registries.
- Use of falsified documents, aliases, or borrowed professional identities.
- Practice settings with weaker verification or fragmented recordkeeping.

### Legal / regulatory framing (general)
- Most countries regulate the practice of medicine through licensing bodies and protected professional titles.
- Allegations may lead to criminal charges, civil liability, and professional sanctions against any collaborators.
- Outcomes depend on jurisdiction and the documented scope and duration of the conduct.
