## Summary

Rwandan military intelligence officer who orchestrated the targeted killing of Queen Rosalie Gicanda, the last Tutsi queen, and organized massacres at roadblocks during the 1994 genocide. He was convicted of genocide by the ICTR.

## Allegations and Historical Context

Nizeyimana is described as an officer connected to military intelligence structures during the 1994 genocide against the Tutsi in Rwanda. Allegations addressed in international proceedings included involvement in killings at roadblocks and responsibility for attacks on civilians in the Butare area, where violence escalated as local administrative and military authorities coordinated or enabled mass हत्या.

A particularly prominent allegation involved the killing of Rosalie Gicanda, often described as the last queen of Rwanda. The episode is frequently cited because of its symbolic significance and the way it illustrates targeted violence against prominent Tutsi figures.

## Arrest and International Prosecution

He was arrested outside Rwanda and transferred to the International Criminal Tribunal for Rwanda (ICTR). Such transfers typically follow cooperation between national police forces and international tribunals to locate suspects who left the country after the conflict.

## Judgment and Sentence

The ICTR convicted him of genocide and related crimes and imposed a life sentence. The judgment is often referenced in summaries of ICTR jurisprudence concerning command structures, participation at roadblocks, and accountability for discrete high-profile killings during the genocide.
