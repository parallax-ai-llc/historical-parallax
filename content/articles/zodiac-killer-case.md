## Summary

Unidentified serial killer who terrorized the San Francisco Bay Area from 1968-1969 killing at least five people. He taunted police with cryptographic letters, one of which was only solved in 2020.

## Confirmed and Claimed Victims

Investigators generally attribute at least five murders and two surviving victims to the Zodiac Killer, based on a combination of crime-scene evidence and contemporaneous communications. The perpetrator also claimed responsibility for additional killings in letters, but many of those claims remain unverified or disputed.

## Letters, Ciphers, and Media Strategy

A central feature of the case was the killer’s use of letters to newspapers and law enforcement. These communications included threats, demands for publication, and cryptograms. The letters helped keep public attention on the case and complicated investigative work by producing a steady stream of tips and speculation.

One of the better-known ciphers (often referred to as the “340 cipher”) was publicly reported as solved in 2020 by independent codebreakers. Other ciphers and symbolic markings associated with the Zodiac remain subjects of analysis and debate.

## Investigation and Unresolved Status

Multiple police departments and agencies were involved due to the geographic spread of the crimes and the public nature of the communications. Over time, the case has been revisited with evolving forensic methods, including fingerprint analysis, DNA-related claims, and renewed review of suspects. Despite periodic renewed attention, the identity of the Zodiac Killer has not been definitively established in the public record.

## Cultural Impact

The Zodiac case has had lasting influence on true-crime media and popular culture, partly because of its combination of violent attacks, coded messages, and the long-running uncertainty about the offender’s identity. It is often referenced in discussions of serial-offender profiling, public communication strategy in criminal investigations, and the limits of forensic certainty in historical cases.

## Notes

Because the case spans decades of reporting and reinterpretation, key details (including the attribution of specific incidents and the authenticity of particular communications) can vary between sources.
