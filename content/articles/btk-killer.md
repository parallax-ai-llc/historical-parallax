## Summary

Dennis Rader terrorized Wichita, Kansas for decades as the BTK (Bind, Torture, Kill) strangler. Caught after sending a floppy disk to police that contained church metadata.

## Additional Context

- **Identity:** Dennis Lynn Rader.
- **BTK:** Acronym for “Bind, Torture, Kill,” used in communications attributed to the perpetrator.
- **Area:** Wichita, Kansas and surrounding communities.

## Modus Operandi (High-Level)

- **Victim targeting:** Reporting describes a pattern of selecting victims and conducting surveillance.
- **Control and restraint:** The BTK label reflects the use of binding and strangulation.
- **Communication:** The case is notable for letters and messages sent to media and police.

## Capture and Investigation Lessons

- **Re-engagement:** After years of inactivity, communications resumed, creating new investigative opportunities.
- **Digital trace:** The floppy-disk episode is often used as a cautionary tale about metadata and operational security.
- **Interdisciplinary work:** The investigation combined traditional detective work, behavioral analysis, and digital forensics.

## Cultural and Legal Aftermath

- **Public impact:** The case influenced public perceptions of serial offenders and long-term investigations.
- **Media coverage:** Numerous books, documentaries, and dramatizations have been produced.
