## Summary

Ethnic violence between Meitei and Kuki communities in Manipur killed over 200 people and displaced 60,000. The conflict included sexual violence and destruction of thousands of homes.

## Background

- Manipur is a northeastern Indian state with a complex mix of ethnic communities and a geography split between an Imphal valley and surrounding hills.
- Political representation, land access, and affirmative action classifications have long been sensitive issues in the region.
- Tensions between Meitei and Kuki-Zo communities have periodically flared, and the 2023 crisis escalated into widespread communal violence.

## Triggers and Early Escalation

- The violence is widely reported to have intensified in early May 2023 after protests and counter-protests related to community status and perceived threats to rights.
- Local incidents rapidly spread, aided by rumor, fear of attacks, and breakdowns in inter-community trust.
- Authorities implemented emergency measures at various points, including restrictions on movement and communications.

## Humanitarian Impact

- Large numbers of people were displaced into relief camps or across district lines, often separated by community identity.
- Reports described the burning of homes, attacks on villages, and the destruction of places of worship and community infrastructure.
- Allegations of sexual violence and other grave abuses prompted national attention and calls for investigations.

## Security, Politics, and Ongoing Challenges

- The crisis involved deployments of state and central security forces, as well as debates over policing capacity and neutrality.
- Political controversy included criticism of response times, coordination failures, and the difficulty of restoring normal civic life.
- Longer-term challenges include reconciliation, rehabilitation of displaced families, accountability for crimes, and rebuilding destroyed property.
