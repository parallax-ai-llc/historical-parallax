## Summary

Ignatius of Loyola was a Spanish priest and the founder of the Society of Jesus (Jesuits), one of the most influential religious orders in Catholic history. His Spiritual Exercises became a foundational text for Catholic spirituality and retreats. After a military career ended by injury, he devoted his life to spiritual discipline and education. The Jesuits became leaders in education, missionary work, and the Counter-Reformation.
