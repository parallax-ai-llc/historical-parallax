## Summary

Russian-based ransomware gang that attacked the Colonial Pipeline in May 2021, causing fuel shortages across the US East Coast. The gang operated a ransomware-as-a-service model. They disbanded after the attack drew massive law enforcement attention.

## Operating Model (High Level)

- DarkSide was commonly described as a **ransomware-as-a-service (RaaS)** program: operators maintained the malware and infrastructure while affiliates conducted intrusions and ran deployments.
- Public reporting often framed DarkSide as a financially motivated group that emphasized profitability and “business-like” negotiation tactics.

## Typical Tactics

- Initial access in ransomware cases is frequently associated with stolen credentials, remote access services, or exploiting exposed systems; DarkSide campaigns were discussed in this broader pattern.
- Operations generally involved **data theft + encryption** to increase pressure ("double extortion").

## Aftermath and Legacy

- The Colonial Pipeline incident elevated ransomware to a major national security topic in the United States.
- Following the intense attention in 2021, DarkSide was widely reported to have shut down and later been linked (by some reporting) to successor branding such as BlackMatter.
