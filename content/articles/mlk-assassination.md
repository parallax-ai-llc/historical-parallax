## Summary

Assassination of civil rights leader Martin Luther King Jr. on April 4, 1968 in Memphis, Tennessee by James Earl Ray. His death sparked riots in over 100 US cities.

## Setting and immediate events (1968)

- King traveled to **Memphis, Tennessee** to support the city’s sanitation workers’ strike.
- He was shot on April 4, 1968, while standing on the balcony of the **Lorraine Motel**.

## Suspect and prosecution

- **James Earl Ray** fled after the killing and was later arrested abroad.
- Ray pleaded guilty in 1969 and was sentenced to prison, though he later attempted to withdraw the plea and claimed he was not the shooter.

## Public impact

- King’s assassination triggered widespread grief and unrest, including riots in many U.S. cities.
- It reshaped U.S. politics and accelerated debates over civil rights, policing, and political violence.

## Investigations and contested narratives

- Over decades, multiple investigations examined claims of conspiracy.
- The assassination became a focal point for public skepticism about official accounts, with recurring allegations involving organized crime or government agencies.

## Memorialization

- The Lorraine Motel later became part of the **National Civil Rights Museum**.
- The event remains a pivotal marker in U.S. 20th-century history and in the historiography of the civil rights era.
