## Summary

Already covered.

Teddy Riner (Teddy Pierre-Marie Riner) is a French heavyweight judoka from Pointe-à-Pitre, Guadeloupe. He is widely regarded as one of the most dominant athletes in modern judo, with multiple Olympic and world titles in the +100 kg division.

Key notes (high-level):
- Weight class: +100 kg (heavyweight)
- Known for: long unbeaten streak in international competition (2010–2020)
- Major titles: Olympic champion (2012, 2016, 2024) and record-setting World Judo Championships gold totals

## Major Achievements

- Olympic Games: three-time +100 kg gold medalist (2012, 2016, 2024)
- World Championships: twelve World Judo Championships gold medals (across heavyweight, open weight, and team events)
- European Championships: multiple-time champion in +100 kg

## Competitive Style

Riner is known for combining elite grip fighting with exceptional balance and timing for a heavyweight. His matches often revolve around controlling distance and forcing opponents into unfavorable gripping exchanges before committing to high-percentage throws.

## Legacy

In discussions of judo’s greatest heavyweights, Riner is frequently cited for both longevity at the top level and consistency across multiple Olympic cycles.
