## What Was P2?

Propaganda Due (P2) was a clandestine Masonic lodge in Italy that attracted powerful figures from politics, the military, intelligence, media, and business. The scandal erupted when investigators found documents and membership lists suggesting a coordinated, secret network operating outside normal democratic oversight.

## Discovery and Political Fallout

The 1981 discovery of the membership list triggered a major crisis of confidence in Italian institutions. It contributed to government instability and intensified scrutiny of:

- Intelligence and security-service governance
- Patronage networks and political corruption
- The role of secret societies in postwar Italian public life

## Alleged “Plan of Democratic Rebirth”

Accounts of the scandal often reference a political blueprint attributed to P2 leadership that proposed reshaping Italian institutions and media. This element became central to the narrative that P2 aimed at influencing, or even steering, the state from the shadows.

## Legacy

The P2 scandal remains a recurring reference point in modern Italian history because it illustrates how informal elite networks can undermine transparency. It is also used comparatively in discussions of “deep state” claims—both when substantiated by documentation and when invoked rhetorically.
