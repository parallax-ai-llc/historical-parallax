## Summary

Commander of the Presidential Guard during the Rwandan genocide who ordered the assassination of PM Agathe Uwilingiyimana. Remained one of the most wanted genocide fugitives until confirmed dead.

## Background

Mpiranya was an officer associated with Rwanda’s pre-1994 state security structures. In many accounts, his prominence is tied to the Presidential Guard, a force that played a central role in the first days of violence after the plane crash that killed President Juvénal Habyarimana.

## Role in 1994 events

He is widely described as having exercised command responsibility during the onset of the genocide, including involvement in attacks on political leaders and international peacekeepers. Specific allegations and the evidentiary record are discussed in international-criminal-law reporting and tribunal-related documentation.

## Fugitive status

After 1994, Mpiranya became a long-term fugitive. His case is often cited as an example of how alleged perpetrators evaded arrest for years through cross-border networks, false identities, and gaps in international enforcement.

## Confirmation of death

Later investigative work and official announcements reported that he had died years earlier, with identification confirmed long after the fact. This outcome underscored the difficulty of resolving outstanding cases when suspects disappear and records are incomplete.

## Significance

The history of Mpiranya’s case is frequently referenced in discussions of post-genocide justice, including the challenges of locating suspects and the broader effort to document command structures behind mass atrocities.
