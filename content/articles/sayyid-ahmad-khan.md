## Summary

Sir Sayyid Ahmad Khan was an Indian Muslim reformer and educator who founded the Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College at Aligarh (later Aligarh Muslim University). He advocated for modern scientific education for Muslims and sought to reconcile Islamic theology with Western rationalism. He promoted Hindu-Muslim cooperation and British-Muslim relations. His educational movement profoundly shaped the Muslim elite of South Asia and contributed to the Pakistan movement.
