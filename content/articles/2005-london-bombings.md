## Summary

Four suicide bombers attacked London's public transport during morning rush hour killing 52 people. The attackers were British citizens inspired by Al-Qaeda.

## Background

- The attacks occurred against the backdrop of the Iraq War and heightened concern about transnational jihadist violence in Europe.
- London’s transport system (Underground and bus network) was a high-density target during weekday commuting hours.

## The Attacks (7 July 2005)

- **Method:** Coordinated suicide bombings using improvised explosive devices.
- **Targets:** Three London Underground trains and one double-decker bus.
- **Casualties:** 52 victims were killed (excluding the four bombers) and hundreds were injured.
- **Disruption:** Central London experienced major transport shutdowns and emergency response mobilization.

## Perpetrators and Investigation

- The four bombers were identified as British citizens; investigators reconstructed their travel, communications, and movements leading up to the attacks.
- The investigation drew on CCTV, forensic analysis, witness accounts, and phone/financial records to establish timelines and support networks.

## Immediate Response

- Emergency services carried out mass-casualty triage, evacuation, and scene management across multiple sites.
- Authorities issued public safety guidance while attempting to confirm the nature and scope of the incidents.

## Aftermath and Impact

- The bombings influenced UK counterterrorism policy and public debate about domestic radicalization and prevention.
- Memorials and annual commemorations became part of London’s civic remembrance, honoring victims and first responders.
