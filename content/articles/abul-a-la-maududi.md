## Summary

Abul A'la Maududi was a Pakistani-Indian Islamic scholar, journalist, and political leader who founded the Jamaat-e-Islami party. He developed a comprehensive Islamic political ideology advocating for an Islamic state governed by sharia. His prolific writings, including Tafhim ul-Quran (a Quran commentary), influenced Islamist movements worldwide. He was sentenced to death (later commuted) for his political activities in Pakistan.
