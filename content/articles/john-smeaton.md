## Summary

John Smeaton was the father of civil engineering who rebuilt the Eddystone Lighthouse.

## Additional Context

- Smeaton is widely associated with the professionalization of **civil engineering** in Britain; later engineering institutions often reference him as an early exemplar of the field.
- His work on the **Eddystone Lighthouse** is notable as an early demonstration of engineering-as-applied-science under severe environmental constraints.
- Historical accounts frequently mention his interest in materials and hydraulics, including use of **hydraulic lime** for durable mortar.

## Impact

- The lighthouse project influenced subsequent coastal and maritime engineering by showing how material choice and structural design can address wave impact and erosion.
- Smeaton's broader career is linked to water and transport infrastructure (mills, canals, harbors), reflecting the era's push for industrial and commercial expansion.
- Many summaries credit him with helping establish norms of measurement, experimentation, and documentation that later became standard engineering practice.
