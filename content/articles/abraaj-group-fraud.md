## Summary

Dubai-based Abraaj Group, the developing world's largest private equity firm, collapsed amid $780 million fraud. Founder Arif Naqvi used investor money including Bill Gates' foundation funds for personal use.

## Background

The Abraaj Group was a Dubai-based private equity firm known for investing across emerging markets, and it marketed itself as a bridge between global capital and developing-world growth opportunities. Its reputation and access to prominent limited partners made the collapse notable beyond the Gulf region.

## Core Allegations

High-level summaries of the scandal often emphasize:

- **Misuse of fund cash:** accusations that investor money was diverted for purposes inconsistent with fund mandates.
- **Misleading reporting:** concerns that financial statements, cash balances, or fund governance information were presented in a way that obscured actual uses of money.
- **Governance and controls:** questions about internal controls, compliance oversight, and the independence of fund administration.

## Fundraising and LP Exposure

- The controversy drew global attention in part because limited partners reportedly included large institutions and well-known philanthropic organizations.
- The situation became a case study for LP due diligence in emerging-market private equity, including monitoring of fund-level cash management and related-party transactions.

## Investigations and Proceedings

- The group’s collapse led to multiple investigations across jurisdictions.
- Legal outcomes reported in major summaries focus on founder Arif Naqvi and alleged fraud involving fund investors.
- The timing and venue of proceedings have been shaped by cross-border enforcement, extradition questions, and coordination among regulators.

## Market and Industry Impact

- The Abraaj episode is frequently cited in discussions about the reputational risk of emerging-market investing when governance is weak.
- It reinforced calls for stronger independent fund administration, clearer valuation practices, and more robust disclosure standards.
