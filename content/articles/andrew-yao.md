## Key Contributions

Yao is widely known for work that shaped modern theoretical computer science, especially in computational complexity theory.

- **Yao's minimax principle (communication complexity):** a foundational tool for proving lower bounds by relating randomized and deterministic models.
- **Yao's garbled circuits (secure computation):** an early and influential construction enabling two-party computation, later central to practical cryptography.
- **Complexity and algorithms:** contributions spanning data structures, algorithmic game theory, and lower-bound techniques.

## Career and Institutions

Born in Shanghai, Yao studied in the United States and held academic positions at major research universities. He later returned to China and became associated with Tsinghua University, where he helped build research capacity in computer science.

## Recognition

Yao received the **ACM Turing Award (2000)** for contributions to the theory of computation, reflecting the impact of his ideas across both complexity theory and cryptography.

## Influence

Many of Yao's results are taught as standard tools in graduate theory courses, and "Yao's principle" is frequently used when analyzing randomized algorithms and communication protocols.

His work also helped connect abstract complexity models to practical security goals, particularly through secure computation constructions that later became building blocks for real-world privacy-preserving systems.
