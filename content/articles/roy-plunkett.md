## Summary

Roy Plunkett accidentally discovered polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE), marketed as Teflon, while researching refrigerants at DuPont in 1938. The non-stick, heat-resistant material found applications in cookware, industrial coatings, aerospace, and medical devices. Teflon became one of the most commercially successful accidental discoveries in chemistry.
