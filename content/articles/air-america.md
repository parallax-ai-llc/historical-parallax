## Summary

CIA-operated airline in Southeast Asia during the Vietnam War era that was linked to opium trafficking in the Golden Triangle. It transported supplies for anti-communist operations.

## Additional Context

## Operations in Southeast Asia

Air America was used to support U.S. covert and paramilitary operations, particularly in Laos and surrounding areas, during the broader Indochina conflicts. Flights included cargo, personnel, and logistical support in regions with limited infrastructure.

## Relationship to the “Secret War” in Laos

- The airline is often discussed in connection with the CIA’s clandestine campaign in Laos.
- Its activities intersected with local militias and shifting alliances, contributing to long-running debates about accountability in covert operations.

## Opium Trafficking Claims

Allegations that Air America was connected to narcotics trafficking are part of a larger historical argument about the Golden Triangle drug trade and wartime logistics. Accounts vary in how direct or institutional the involvement was portrayed.

## Notes
