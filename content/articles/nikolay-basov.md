## Context

Basov’s Nobel-recognized work is typically presented as part of the scientific foundations of **quantum electronics**—the study and engineering of devices that exploit quantum properties of matter to generate, amplify, or control electromagnetic radiation.

A **maser** (microwave amplification by stimulated emission of radiation) and a **laser** (light amplification by stimulated emission of radiation) rely on *stimulated emission*, where an incoming photon can prompt an excited system to emit an additional photon with matching energy and phase. This concept enables exceptionally coherent, narrowly directed beams.

## Why Masers and Lasers Matter

- **Precision sources**: Coherent radiation is crucial for measurement, spectroscopy, and frequency standards.
- **Amplification with low noise**: Early masers were important in contexts where signal quality was critical.
- **Platform technology**: Lasers became a general-purpose tool across science, industry, and medicine.

## Typical Applications (High Level)

- Materials processing and manufacturing (cutting, welding, marking)
- Communications and data transmission via optical systems
- Scientific instrumentation (spectroscopy, interferometry)
- Medicine (imaging and certain surgical procedures)
