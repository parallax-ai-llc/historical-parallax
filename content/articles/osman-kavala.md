## Summary

Turkish philanthropist and civil society figure who became one of the most prominent political-prisoner cases in Turkey.

He has been held for years in connection with allegations related to the 2013 Gezi Park protests and other charges, despite repeated international calls for his release.

## Background

- Known for supporting arts, cultural dialogue, and civil society initiatives in Turkey.
- Associated with Anadolu Kültür, a cultural organization focused on supporting cultural projects and cross-regional exchange.

## Detention and Legal Case (High Level)

- **2017**: Detained and arrested amid accusations tied to the Gezi Park protests.
- **2019**: The European Court of Human Rights (ECtHR) ruled that his detention violated rights and called for his release; Turkish authorities did not release him.
- **2020**: He was briefly acquitted in one case and then re-detained on new/related allegations.
- **2022**: Sentenced to aggravated life imprisonment in the Gezi Park-related trial; co-defendants received prison sentences.

## Why the Case Matters

- Often cited by rights groups as an example of prolonged pretrial detention and politicized prosecution.
- Became a focal point in Turkey’s relations with European institutions due to ECtHR compliance obligations.
