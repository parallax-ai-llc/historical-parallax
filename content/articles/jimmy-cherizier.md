## Summary

Haitian gang leader known as 'Barbecue' who leads the G9 alliance of armed gangs that has terrorized Port-au-Prince. He is a former police officer accused of multiple massacres.

## Background and Rise

Jimmy “Barbecue” Chérizier is widely reported to have served as a police officer before emerging as a central figure in armed-group politics in Haiti’s capital.
The consolidation of multiple gangs into an alliance such as “G9” reflects a shift from fragmented neighborhood control toward coordinated territorial and political bargaining.

## Allegations and Humanitarian Impact

- **Mass violence:** He has been accused by human-rights groups and international observers of involvement in massacres and political violence.
- **Control of infrastructure:** Armed groups have repeatedly affected ports, fuel terminals, roads, and neighborhoods, worsening shortages and displacement.
- **Political leverage:** Gang coalitions have at times sought to influence government decisions, appointments, and negotiations.

## Sanctions and International Attention

He has been the subject of international scrutiny, including sanctions and calls for accountability, amid broader efforts to address Haiti’s security collapse.
The crisis is intertwined with weak institutions, economic shocks, and a long history of political instability.
