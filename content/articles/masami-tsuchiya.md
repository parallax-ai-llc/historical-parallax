## Role within Aum Shinrikyo

Tsuchiya is described as a chemist associated with Aum Shinrikyo's chemical weapons program. The group invested in technical capability to produce toxic agents, and the Tokyo subway sarin attack became one of the most notorious cases of chemical terrorism in modern history.

## The 1995 Tokyo subway attack (context)

The attack involved the release of sarin on Tokyo subway lines during the morning commute, causing deaths and widespread injuries. It also produced long-term psychological and medical effects among survivors and exposed limitations in emergency preparedness for chemical incidents.

## Criminal proceedings and execution

After Aum's crimes, Japanese authorities prosecuted numerous members of the organization, including those involved in planning and production. Tsuchiya was ultimately sentenced to death and executed in 2018, years after the original attack, reflecting the lengthy appeals and legal processes typical in capital cases.

## Significance

The case is often referenced in studies of extremist organizations with technical expertise, highlighting how access to scientific knowledge and industrial precursors can increase the destructive potential of non-state actors.

It is also used in policy discussions about chemical security, public health readiness, and the challenges of detecting clandestine production in a civilian setting.

## Aftermath

The Tokyo subway attack prompted major changes in Japan's emergency response planning and public awareness of chemical hazards, and it remains a frequently cited reference point in counterterrorism and public-health preparedness.
