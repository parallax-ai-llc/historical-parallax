## Summary

Second known protester executed during Iran's 2022 protests, publicly hanged from a crane.

## Background

- Rahnavard's execution occurred amid nationwide unrest in Iran in 2022 following the death of Mahsa Amini in police custody.
- The protests included demonstrations across many cities and were met with a security crackdown, mass arrests, and prosecutions in Revolutionary Courts.

## Case (high-level)

- Iranian authorities accused Rahnavard of involvement in violence during the protest period.
- Reporting about his case described allegations connected to the killing of security personnel in Mashhad.
- He was identified in international coverage as one of the first protesters executed in relation to the 2022 protest movement.

## Legal process and due-process concerns

- The proceedings were reported as having moved quickly from arrest to sentencing.
- Human-rights organizations and international observers criticized the use of Revolutionary Courts for protest-related cases and raised concerns about access to counsel and fair-trial guarantees.
- In overviews of the crackdown, Rahnavard's case is frequently cited as an example used to deter further protests.

## Execution

- Rahnavard was executed by public hanging, described in reports as being carried out using a crane.
- His execution was widely reported as the second protest-related execution publicly confirmed during the 2022 crackdown.

## Public and international reaction

- The execution drew condemnation from international officials and human-rights groups.
- Commentary about the case often focused on the symbolic impact of a public execution during a period of political unrest.

## Significance

- Rahnavard is remembered in summaries of the 2022 protests as an "execution victim" linked to protest-related prosecutions.
- His name is frequently listed alongside other individuals executed or sentenced to death in connection with the 2022–2023 protest period.
