## Political Context

- Tuđman rose to prominence during the collapse of Yugoslavia and the emergence of multi-party politics in Croatia.
- His presidency coincided with the Croatian War of Independence and wider conflicts in the former Yugoslavia.

## Governance and Leadership Style

- Commentators frequently describe his leadership as highly centralized, with strong presidential influence over state institutions and wartime decision-making.
- Domestic politics in the period involved debates about nationalism, media freedom, and the consolidation of executive power.

## War and Accountability Debates

- Operation Storm (1995) was a decisive military offensive that resulted in the displacement of large numbers of Serb civilians.
- International legal and historical debates have focused on command responsibility, patterns of crimes during and after the operation, and the political objectives of wartime leadership.

## Legacy

- In Croatia, Tuđman is viewed by many supporters as a founding figure of statehood, while critics focus on authoritarian governance and wartime abuses.
- The competing narratives continue to shape memorialization, education, and political rhetoric in the region.
