## Summary

Harald zur Hausen is a German virologist who won the 2008 Nobel Prize for discovering HPV causes cervical cancer.

## Scientific context

Zur Hausen’s work sits at the intersection of virology, oncology, and epidemiology: it treats certain cancers as diseases that can have identifiable infectious causes.
A key implication of this framing is that prevention (for example, vaccination) can reduce cancer incidence when the causal pathogen is well established.

## Key contributions

- Helped establish human papillomaviruses (HPV) as a central causal factor in cervical cancer.
- Advanced the broader concept that specific viral subtypes can be linked to specific cancer risks.
- Contributed to the scientific foundation that later supported population-scale HPV vaccination programs.

## Recognition and impact

The 2008 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine recognized discoveries about HPV and cervical cancer, which influenced research priorities and public-health strategies.
In practice, the HPV–cervical cancer link helped unify screening (Pap tests / HPV testing) with preventive vaccination as complementary approaches.
