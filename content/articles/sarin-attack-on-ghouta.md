## Summary

Sarin nerve agent attack on rebel-held suburbs of Damascus killed an estimated 1,400 people including hundreds of children. The attack was attributed to the Syrian government.

## Additional Context

### What is sarin

- Sarin is a highly toxic nerve agent that disrupts normal nervous system function.
- Exposure can cause rapid symptoms, including breathing failure, without timely treatment.
- The use of nerve agents is prohibited under international chemical weapons norms.

### Location and conflict setting

- Ghouta refers to a region of suburbs east and south of Damascus.
- In 2013, the area included rebel-held districts and was subject to siege dynamics and heavy fighting.
- The attack occurred amid the broader Syrian civil war, with multiple armed actors and international involvement.

### Attribution and investigation (high level)

- International reporting and later assessments debated responsibility, munitions types, and command-and-control implications.
- The UN mission at the time was focused on determining whether chemical weapons were used, not formally attributing blame.
- Subsequent investigations and government statements in various countries argued over evidence chains and battlefield access.

### Impact

- The incident became one of the deadliest chemical weapons attacks of the 21st century.
- It influenced international diplomacy, public opinion, and policy decisions regarding Syria.
- It also intensified scrutiny of chemical weapons stockpiles, verification regimes, and enforcement mechanisms.
