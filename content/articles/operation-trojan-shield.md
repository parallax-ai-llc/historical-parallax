## Background

Operation Trojan Shield (also described by authorities as an international law-enforcement operation targeting organized crime communications) culminated in June 2021 after several years of covert work.

## How the Operation Worked (High Level)

- Law enforcement distributed **ANOM** encrypted devices into criminal networks through intermediaries.
- The platform was operated in a way that allowed authorities to **collect and decrypt messages** sent by users.
- Intercepted communications were shared through a coordinated, multi-country investigative effort.

## Scope and Outcomes

Public reporting and official statements describe the operation as unusually large in scale:

- ~**12,000** ANOM devices monitored
- **300+** criminal organizations implicated across many jurisdictions
- ~**27 million** messages intercepted
- Seizures reported at the time included **tens of tonnes of drugs** (often cited as **~32 tonnes**) as well as cash and firearms.

## International Coordination

The operation involved collaboration among multiple agencies and countries (including the United States and Australia), with intelligence sharing enabling local arrests and seizures.

## Significance

- Demonstrated a model of **targeting criminal communication platforms** rather than attempting to break encryption at scale.
- Prompted renewed debate about **lawful access**, cross-border evidence sharing, and operational transparency.
