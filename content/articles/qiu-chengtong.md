## Summary

Qiu Chengtong (Shing-Tung Yau) is a Chinese-American mathematician who won the Fields Medal in 1982 for proving the Calabi conjecture, which became fundamental to string theory and theoretical physics. Calabi-Yau manifolds, named after him and Eugenio Calabi, describe the extra dimensions in string theory. He has made profound contributions to geometry and mathematical physics.
