## Summary

Massive romance and cryptocurrency fraud operations run from compounds in Cambodia, Myanmar, and Laos using trafficked workers. Victims lost over $75 billion globally with thousands of workers enslaved.

## How the Scheme Works (High-Level)

- “Pig butchering” is a fraud model where scammers **build trust over time** (“fattening”) and then push victims into large transfers (“butchering”).
- Common elements include:
  - romance or friendship-style chat relationships,
  - investment pitches (often crypto trading platforms or fake exchanges),
  - staged “profits” shown on manipulated dashboards, and
  - escalating pressure to deposit more money or pay “fees” to withdraw.

## Organizational Structure and Compounds

- Reporting has linked many operations to industrial-scale compounds where large numbers of workers run scripted conversations.
- A major controversy is the use of **human trafficking and forced labor**, where people are recruited under false job offers and coerced to scam targets.

## Geographic Footprint

- The scheme has been associated with networks operating across multiple jurisdictions, including Cambodia, Myanmar, and Laos.
- Cross-border movement of people, money, and digital infrastructure complicates enforcement and victim recovery.

## Victims and Impact

- Victims can lose life savings due to prolonged manipulation and repeated deposits.
- Losses are hard to measure precisely because:
  - many victims do not report,
  - money moves through layered accounts and crypto addresses, and
  - scams span multiple languages and platforms.

## Law Enforcement and Policy Challenges

- Investigations often require international cooperation (financial intelligence, extradition, asset tracing).
- Crackdowns can be uneven, and perpetrators may relocate operations.
- Survivors and rescued workers frequently require protection, legal assistance, and repatriation support.
