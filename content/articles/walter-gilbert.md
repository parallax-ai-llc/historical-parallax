## Summary

Walter Gilbert is an American physicist and biochemist who shared the 1980 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for his development of a method for DNA sequencing. The Maxam-Gilbert method was one of the first practical techniques for reading the sequence of DNA bases. He also made important contributions to understanding gene expression and the role of introns. Gilbert later became a pioneer in the biotechnology industry.
