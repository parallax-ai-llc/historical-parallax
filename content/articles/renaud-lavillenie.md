## Background

Lavillenie is a French pole vaulter from the Clermont-Ferrand area who emerged in the late 2000s as one of the leading figures of the post-Sergey Bubka era.

## Major Achievements (high level)

- **Olympic gold (2012, London):** won the men’s pole vault final with a best clearance of 5.97 m.
- **Indoor world record (2014):** cleared **6.16 m** indoors, surpassing Bubka’s longstanding indoor mark.
- **World and European medals:** earned multiple medals across World Championships, World Indoor Championships, and European competitions, remaining a top-tier vaulter for more than a decade.

## Style and Reputation

Lavillenie has often been described as a technically efficient, speed-based vaulter, combining a fast runway approach with precise takeoff mechanics.

## Context and Legacy

His 2014 indoor record performance is frequently cited as a landmark moment in modern pole vaulting, showing that the 6.00 m barrier could be approached repeatedly in a new generation of athletes.

He is also notable for maintaining elite-level competitiveness across multiple Olympic and World Championship cycles.
