## Summary

Former Mozambican Finance Minister extradited to the US for his role in the $2 billion hidden debt scandal. Signed secret guarantees for loans that were diverted to personal enrichment.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Background

- Mozambique’s “hidden debts” scandal involved state-linked companies taking on large loans that were not transparently disclosed to the public or to international partners.
- The loans were connected to maritime and security projects, and were backed by government guarantees.
- When the debts became public, Mozambique faced severe financial strain and international criticism over governance failures.

## Role and Allegations

- As finance minister, Chang is alleged to have authorized or signed guarantees enabling borrowing outside normal parliamentary oversight.
- Prosecutors have alleged that portions of proceeds were misused, including through kickbacks and inflated contracts.
- The scandal has been described as implicating networks of officials, intermediaries, and international financial actors.

## Arrest and Extradition Dispute

- Chang was arrested abroad and became the subject of competing extradition requests.
- The case attracted attention because it involved cross-border corruption allegations, financial crime, and questions about where trial should occur.
- His eventual extradition to the United States placed the matter within U.S. federal court proceedings tied to international lending and bribery allegations.

## Impact on Mozambique

- The hidden debts episode contributed to currency instability, austerity pressures, and a loss of investor confidence.
- It also spurred investigations, trials, and policy debates about transparency, public debt management, and corruption enforcement.
