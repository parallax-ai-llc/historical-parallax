## Summary

Bosnian Serb interior minister convicted of crimes against humanity for overseeing ethnic cleansing.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2013 | Convicted of crimes against humanity |

## Background

- **Full name**: Mićo Stanišić (Serbian Cyrillic: Мичо Станишић).[^1]
- **Known for**: Serving as a senior Bosnian Serb interior/security official during the Bosnian War and later being convicted at the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY).[^1]

## Role in the Bosnian War (high-level)

- Stanišić was associated with the Bosnian Serb Ministry of the Interior (MUP/Police structures) during the period in which widespread persecution and forcible removal of non-Serb civilians occurred in multiple municipalities.[^1]
- ICTY cases involving Bosnian Serb leadership describe coordinated policies that included arrests, detention, and deportation/forcible transfer; Stanišić’s conviction relates to responsibility for crimes committed in that broader campaign.[^1]

## Legal proceedings

- **Court**: International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY), The Hague.[^1]
- **Outcome**: Convicted of crimes against humanity (persecution) and war crimes; later sentenced (ICTY judgement and appeals).[^1]

## Notes

- Many public summaries focus on the *institutional role* (interior/security apparatus) rather than direct battlefield command; the ICTY findings address command/superior responsibility and participation in a joint criminal enterprise framework, depending on the judgement stage.[^1]

## Additional timeline (approximate)

- **1992**: Bosnian War begins; policing and detention systems expand rapidly in areas under Bosnian Serb control.[^1]
- **1990s**: Crimes in multiple municipalities (detention, persecution, expulsions) form the factual background in several ICTY prosecutions involving interior/security structures.[^1]
- **2000s–2010s**: ICTY trial and appeals process proceeds over many years, reflecting the complexity of multi-accused leadership cases.[^1]
