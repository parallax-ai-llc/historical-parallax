## Summary

Chinese military cyberespionage unit that hacked hundreds of companies and government agencies worldwide.

## What It Is

Unit 61398 is commonly described as a People's Liberation Army (PLA) unit associated with cyberespionage. In public reporting, it is linked to long-running campaigns targeting foreign companies, government agencies, and critical sectors, primarily for intelligence collection.

## How It Has Been Described in Public Reporting

High-level themes that appear repeatedly across open sources include:

- **Large-scale targeting** across multiple industries (technology, manufacturing, energy, and defense-related supply chains).
- **Long dwell times**, with attackers maintaining access for extended periods to exfiltrate data.
- **Tradecraft that blends** spearphishing, credential theft, and exploitation of exposed services to gain initial access, followed by lateral movement and persistence.

These are general patterns discussed in the literature on state-linked cyberespionage, not a definitive checklist for attribution in any single incident.

## Significance

Unit 61398 became a widely cited example in the public debate over:

- attribution of state cyber operations,
- the boundary between national security espionage and commercially motivated theft,
- and the role of indictments and naming-and-shaming in deterring cyber activity.

## Additional Notes

- **Attribution caveat:** Public attributions generally combine technical indicators, victimology, infrastructure overlap, and intelligence assessments. Open-source reporting can be incomplete or revised over time.
- **Naming conventions:** "Unit 61398" is often discussed alongside labels like "APT1" used by security firms. These labels may not map 1:1 across vendors.
- **Operational intent:** Public discussions frequently distinguish between classic espionage (state intelligence collection) and theft of commercial trade secrets; the alleged activities tied to Unit 61398 are often cited in that debate.
