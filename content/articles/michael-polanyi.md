## Summary

Michael Polanyi was a Hungarian-British polymath who made important contributions to physical chemistry, philosophy of science, and economics. He pioneered the theory of adsorption, transition state theory in chemical kinetics, and X-ray diffraction of fibers. His philosophical work on tacit knowledge and personal knowledge profoundly influenced epistemology. He was the father of Nobel Prize-winning chemist John Polanyi.
