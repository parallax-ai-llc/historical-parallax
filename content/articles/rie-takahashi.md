## Summary

Rie Takahashi (高橋李依) is a Japanese voice actress born on February 27, 1994, in Saitama Prefecture. Represented by Mausu Promotion, she rose to prominence in the mid-2010s and quickly established herself as one of the most versatile and sought-after voice talents in the anime industry. Her ability to portray a wide emotional range — from deadpan comedic delivery to intense dramatic expression — has made her a defining voice of her generation.

Her breakthrough came in 2016 with dual high-profile roles: Emilia in *Re:Zero − Starting Life in Another World* and Megumin in *KonoSuba: God's Blessing on This Wonderful World!* — two of the most popular isekai anime of the decade. Landing lead roles in two concurrent major franchises is exceptionally rare, and it cemented her reputation as a performer of exceptional range and consistency.

Since then, Takahashi has built an extensive catalogue of beloved characters, earning numerous industry awards and a devoted international fanbase. She is also active as a solo music artist, regularly performing theme songs and original releases tied to her anime work.

## Early Life & Background

Takahashi was born and raised in Saitama Prefecture, Japan. She developed an interest in performance from a young age and pursued voice acting as a career path. She trained under Mausu Promotion's affiliated school before making her professional debut. Her early career consisted of minor supporting roles, which she used to refine her craft before landing leading parts.

## Rise to Prominence

Takahashi's career trajectory shifted dramatically in 2016. She was cast as Emilia, the gentle half-elf heroine of *Re:Zero*, and simultaneously as Megumin, the eccentric explosion-obsessed mage in *KonoSuba*. Both series became cultural phenomena, with Megumin in particular earning widespread recognition for Takahashi's comedic timing and her iconic "explosion" incantation delivery. She reprised both roles in subsequent seasons, OVAs, and films, including *Re:Zero* Season 2 (2020–2021) and the *KonoSuba* theatrical film (2019) and Season 3 (2024).

## Key Achievements

- **Emilia** (*Re:Zero*, 2016–present): A flagship role across multiple seasons and specials, one of the most prominent heroine roles in modern isekai anime.
- **Megumin** (*KonoSuba*, 2016–present): Widely regarded as one of anime's most iconic comedic characters; reprised in the 2024 third season.
- **Hu Tao** (*Genshin Impact*, 2021–present): Japanese voice for the popular pyro character in miHoYo's globally successful game, expanding her reach into the video game industry.
- **Retsuko** (*Aggretsuko*, 2016–present): The lead role in the Netflix/Sanrio original series, showcasing her dramatic and musical abilities.
- Won the **Best Lead Actress** award at the 12th Seiyu Awards (2018), one of the most prestigious honors in the Japanese voice acting industry.
- Active music career, with singles and albums released through Pony Canyon.

## Controversies & Criticism

Takahashi has maintained a notably clean public profile. No significant controversies have surrounded her career. Some early criticism pointed to a perceived similarity in vocal tone across certain roles, though subsequent diverse castings have largely countered that view.

## Legacy & Influence

Takahashi's success in securing dual lead roles simultaneously in 2016 set a benchmark for young voice actresses in the competitive industry. Her work on globally distributed platforms — including Netflix's *Aggretsuko* and *Genshin Impact* — has introduced her performances to audiences well beyond traditional anime viewership. She represents a generation of voice actors whose work transcends regional media boundaries, influencing how the industry approaches international co-productions and game tie-ins.
