## Summary

Francis Collins is an American physician-geneticist who led the Human Genome Project to its completion in 2003 and served as director of the National Institutes of Health from 2009 to 2021. He discovered the genes responsible for cystic fibrosis, neurofibromatosis, and Huntington's disease. Collins is known for his advocacy of the compatibility of faith and science.
