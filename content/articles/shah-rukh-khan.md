## Summary

Shah Rukh Khan, widely known as "SRK" or the "King of Bollywood," is one of the most recognizable and commercially successful actors in the history of cinema. Born on November 2, 1965, in New Delhi, India, he has starred in over 90 films spanning four decades and has won 14 Filmfare Awards — the most by any actor in the award's history. His influence extends far beyond Indian borders, with an estimated global fanbase of over 4 billion people.

Khan's career is defined by his ability to transition across genres — from romantic leads to complex villains — while maintaining extraordinary commercial appeal. His production company, Red Chillies Entertainment, has expanded his footprint into visual effects, film production, and streaming content. He is widely cited as a transformative figure who helped globalize Bollywood cinema during the 1990s and 2000s.

Beyond film, SRK is a philanthropic figure and a co-owner of the Indian Premier League cricket franchise Kolkata Knight Riders. His rags-to-riches story — an orphan from a middle-class Delhi background who became one of the world's wealthiest entertainers — has made him a symbol of aspiration across South Asia and its diaspora.

## Early Life & Background

Shah Rukh Khan was born in New Delhi to Meer Taj Mohammed Khan, a Pakistani-origin independence activist, and Lateef Fatima, a magistrate's daughter. He grew up in the Rajendra Nagar neighborhood and attended St. Columba's School, where he excelled in academics and sports. He pursued a bachelor's degree in Economics from Hansraj College, Delhi University, and later studied Mass Communication at Jamia Millia Islamia before dropping out to pursue acting.

His father died in 1981 and his mother in 1991, leaving him effectively orphaned before his film career had fully taken hold. He began his acting career on Delhi's theatre circuit and gained early television recognition in the 1989 serial *Fauji*, playing an army officer.

## Rise to Prominence

Khan relocated to Mumbai in 1991 after his mother's death. His Bollywood debut came in *Deewana* (1992), which won him the Filmfare Best Male Debut Award. He quickly distinguished himself by taking on unconventional antagonist roles in *Darr* (1993) and *Baazigar* (1993) — both commercial hits that broke the mold of the traditional Bollywood hero.

The mid-1990s cemented his superstar status. *Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge* (1995), directed by Aditya Chopra, became the longest-running Bollywood film in history, playing continuously at Mumbai's Maratha Mandir cinema for over 25 years. He followed it with a string of blockbusters including *Dil To Pagal Hai* (1997), *Kuch Kuch Hota Hai* (1998), and *Kabhi Khushi Kabhie Gham* (2001), establishing himself as the definitive romantic hero of his generation.

## Key Achievements

- **14 Filmfare Awards**, including 8 Best Actor wins — a record.
- *Dilwale Dulhania Le Jayenge* ran for **1,278 weeks** at Maratha Mandir (1995–2022).
- Named one of **Time magazine's 50 Most Powerful People in the World** in 2008.
- Recipient of the **Padma Shri** (2005) and France's **Ordre des Arts et des Lettres** (2007) and **Légion d'honneur** (2014).
- His 2023 comeback film *Pathaan* grossed over ₹1,050 crore ($127 million) worldwide, the highest-opening for a Bollywood film at the time.
- Estimated net worth of approximately $770 million, making him one of the wealthiest actors globally.

## Controversies & Criticism

Khan has faced several public controversies. In 2002, he was involved in an altercation at a Mumbai nightclub that led to legal proceedings, though charges were eventually dropped. His 2007 film *Chak De! India* drew some criticism for its portrayal of Pakistan, while his 2023 blockbuster *Pathaan* faced Hindu nationalist protests over a costume worn in a song sequence, leading to temporary boycott calls and edits to the film.

His son Aryan Khan was arrested in October 2021 during a drug raid on a cruise ship; the case drew intense media scrutiny for months before charges were dropped in May 2022 due to insufficient evidence. The episode sparked broader debate about celebrity treatment in Indian law enforcement and media.

## Legacy & Influence

Shah Rukh Khan's legacy is inseparable from the globalization of Indian popular culture. His romantic films of the 1990s and early 2000s shaped the aspirations of an entire generation of South Asian audiences worldwide, and his brand of expressive, emotionally accessible stardom influenced countless successors. He is frequently credited alongside Salman Khan and Aamir Khan as part of the "Three Khans" who collectively dominated Bollywood for three decades.

His 2023 comeback — after a five-year gap — with *Pathaan*, *Jawan*, and *Dunki* demonstrated a sustained capacity for commercial reinvention rare among actors in their late fifties. Internationally, academic works on diaspora identity and postcolonial culture frequently cite his films as primary texts.
