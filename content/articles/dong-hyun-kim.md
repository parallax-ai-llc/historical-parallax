## Background

Im Dong-hyun is a South Korean recurve archer known internationally for competing at the highest level while having significant visual impairment. His case is frequently cited in sports media as an example of elite performance under physical constraints.

## Olympic Participation

He represented South Korea at multiple Olympic Games and was part of teams that won medals in recurve archery events. South Koreas national program is widely regarded as one of the strongest in the sport, with intense domestic competition for Olympic selection.

## World Records

He has been credited with setting world-record scores in major competition settings, including on large stages such as the Olympics. Such records reflect consistency across long distances and changing weather and light conditions.

## Significance

Beyond medals, his story is often used to illustrate the role of technique, repetition, and mental focus in archery, where marginal differences in release, alignment, and timing can determine outcomes.
