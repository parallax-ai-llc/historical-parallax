## Summary

Peruvian former president investigated for money laundering related to alleged illegal campaign contributions from Odebrecht and Venezuela. He and his wife spent 9 months in pretrial detention. Charges ongoing.

## Background

- Trained as a military officer and became nationally known through political activity connected to the Peruvian armed forces.
- Entered electoral politics and built a political coalition that eventually won the presidency.

## Presidency (2011–2016)

- Served as President of Peru from 2011 to 2016.
- Positioned as a center-left nationalist during campaigning, while facing pressure to balance social programs with macroeconomic stability.
- Oversaw government actions amid debates over mining, environmental policy, and social conflict in resource-producing regions.

## Corruption and Campaign-Finance Allegations (High-Level)

Public reporting and investigations have focused on allegations of illegal campaign contributions, including claims tied to:

- Odebrecht-related funding networks referenced in wider Latin American investigations.
- Alleged foreign contributions (often described in media and filings as linked to Venezuela).

Humala and his wife, Nadine Heredia, were held in pretrial detention for months during the investigation phase. Court rulings later allowed release while proceedings continued.
