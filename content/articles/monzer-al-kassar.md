## Summary

Syrian arms dealer known as the 'Prince of Marbella' who sold weapons to conflicts worldwide for decades. He was linked to the Iran-Contra affair and the Achille Lauro hijacking. Convicted in US for conspiring to sell arms to Colombian FARC.

## Additional Context

- **Base of operations**: Al-Kassar was associated with a long period of residence and business activity in Spain, particularly around Marbella, which contributed to the media nickname “Prince of Marbella.”
- **Client network**: Reporting about his career commonly describes a broker role connecting state and non-state buyers to suppliers across Europe and the Middle East, with deals tied to multiple conflicts.
- **Notable allegations**: He was repeatedly named in press and investigative accounts connected to high-profile episodes of international arms trafficking, even when not always charged in each case.

## Legal Case (United States)

- **DEA sting operation**: His U.S. conviction followed an undercover operation alleging a conspiracy to sell weapons with the understanding they would be used by the FARC against U.S. personnel.
- **Extradition and trial**: After arrest in Spain, he was extradited to the United States, tried in federal court, and convicted on conspiracy-related counts.
- **Sentence and custody**: U.S. courts imposed a long prison sentence; subsequent reporting focuses on the case as a prominent example of extraterritorial enforcement against alleged arms brokers.

## Why He Is Notable

Monzer al-Kassar is often cited in discussions of:

- the globalization of illicit arms markets,
- the role of intermediaries in sanction-evading supply chains,
- media portrayals of “jet-set” trafficking figures operating across jurisdictions,
- and the use of sting operations to build prosecutable conspiracies in transnational crime cases.
