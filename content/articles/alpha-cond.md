## Background

Alpha Condé (born 1938) is a Guinean politician who became the country's first president elected in a competitive, multi‑party vote after decades of authoritarian rule and military involvement in politics.

He spent significant periods in opposition politics and was known internationally as a long‑time critic of Guinea's previous governments.

## Presidency (2010–2021)

Condé took office in 2010. His presidency was marked by:

- Efforts to consolidate civilian rule and rebuild state institutions after years of instability.
- Contentious elections and periodic unrest, including clashes involving security forces and opposition supporters.
- Ongoing debate over governance, corruption allegations, and the balance between political order and civil liberties.

## Constitutional controversy and third term

A major turning point was the constitutional reform process that enabled Condé to seek another term in office. Supporters argued the change reset term limits under a new constitutional framework, while critics viewed it as a strategy to extend personal power.

The period around the referendum and subsequent election saw intensified polarization and protests, with reported casualties and arrests.

## 2021 military coup

In September 2021, Condé was removed from power in a military coup led by officers who dissolved the government and suspended aspects of the constitutional order. The coup was widely condemned internationally, while some domestic supporters of the takeover cited frustration with the third‑term bid and broader governance concerns.

## Aftermath and legacy

Condé's political legacy is often assessed in two opposing frames:

- As a figure associated with Guinea's first peaceful transfer into an elected presidency and a decade of civilian leadership.
- As a leader whose third‑term push and governing style contributed to democratic backsliding and set conditions for renewed military intervention.

Any long‑term judgment of his tenure in Guinea tends to focus on institutional durability, human rights conditions during episodes of unrest, and whether constitutional norms were strengthened or weakened.
