## Summary

French bank robber and repeat prison escapee who used a helicopter to escape from Réau Prison in 2018, making it one of the most spectacular prison breaks in French history. He modeled his criminal career on Hollywood heist films.

## Background

Faïd became widely known in France for a pattern of serious robbery convictions combined with repeated escapes from custody. Media coverage often highlighted his fascination with American crime films and the way he presented his actions using cinematic language and imagery.

## Notable Escapes

- **2013 (Sequedin prison):** Escape involving explosives and outside assistance, which intensified scrutiny of prison security and coordination.
- **2018 (Réau prison):** Helicopter escape carried out with accomplices; the operation was notable for speed and planning, and it triggered a national debate about high-security incarceration.

## Recapture and Legal Aftermath

After the 2018 escape, Faïd was recaptured following a large-scale manhunt. French courts later pursued charges connected to the escape network, including accomplices and logistical support. Reporting on the case frequently emphasized the costs and risks created by repeated escapes, both for law enforcement and for public safety.

## Public Profile

Unlike many career criminals who avoid publicity, Faïd's case became a cultural reference point in France, often framed as an example of how media narratives can intersect with crime, notoriety, and security policy.

## Legacy

In contemporary French crime reporting, Faïd is often cited as a symbol of "spectacular" escape tactics in the modern era and as a driver for reforms and procedural reviews within the prison system.
