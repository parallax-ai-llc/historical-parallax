## Summary

James Sinegal co-founded Costco Wholesale and served as its CEO for nearly three decades. He built Costco into the world's third-largest retailer with a membership warehouse model that prioritized low prices and employee welfare. Costco's starting wage was consistently above competitors, and Sinegal kept his own salary modest. The company became famous for its $1.50 hot dog combo and high customer loyalty.
