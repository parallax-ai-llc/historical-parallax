## Summary
Héctor Luis Palma Salazar, known as 'El Guero' Palma, was a Mexican drug lord and co-founder of the Sinaloa Cartel with Joaquín 'El Chapo' Guzmán. He was known for extreme violence. He was imprisoned in Mexico and the US, and released in 2022.

## Early Life
Born July 29, 1960, in Mocorito, Sinaloa, Mexico. He entered the drug trade through the Guadalajara Cartel. His personal life was marked by tragedy when his wife and children were murdered by a rival cartel member. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Palma co-founded the Sinaloa Cartel after the breakup of the Guadalajara Cartel. He controlled significant cocaine and marijuana trafficking routes. His rivalry with the Arellano Félix brothers of the Tijuana Cartel resulted in extreme violence. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Palma was arrested on June 23, 1995, after a plane crash while fleeing authorities. He was imprisoned in Mexico and later extradited to the US in 2007 for drug trafficking. He was convicted and served his US sentence before being returned to Mexico. He was released from Mexican prison in 2022. [^3]
