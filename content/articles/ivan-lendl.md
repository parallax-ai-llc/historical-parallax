## Summary

Ivan Lendl won eight Grand Slam titles and reached 19 consecutive Grand Slam semi-finals. He revolutionized fitness in tennis.

## Additional Context

- **Era and rankings**: Lendl was a defining player of the 1980s and spent significant time ranked world No. 1, helping set the template for modern baseline power and professionalism in training.
- **Major-tournament consistency**: He is often highlighted for repeatedly reaching the later rounds of majors, including an extended run of Grand Slam semifinal appearances.
- **Fitness and preparation**: Contemporary accounts frequently credit him with pushing elite tennis toward year-round conditioning, nutrition, and structured match preparation.

## Playing Style (High-Level)

- Baseline-first offense built around heavy topspin and depth.
- Serve + first-strike patterns that helped him control rallies.
- A percentage-oriented approach focused on reducing errors and sustaining pressure.

## After Playing Career

- **Coaching**: After retiring, he became known for coaching and mentoring top players, emphasizing scheduling discipline and incremental improvement.
- **Recognition**: He is widely cited as a key figure in the transition from the classic serve-and-volley era to the power-baseline era.
