## Incident Overview (Capital One, 2019)

The Capital One breach became one of the most widely reported U.S. financial-sector data incidents of the late 2010s. Public reporting described the intrusion as leveraging cloud infrastructure weaknesses rather than a novel cryptographic break.

## Misconfiguration and Cloud Security Lessons

The case is often summarized as a “configuration failure” story:

- A web application firewall (WAF) configuration was alleged to allow unintended access.
- Cloud-hosted environments made it possible to move from an exposed service to sensitive data if identity and access controls were not tightly scoped.
- Logging and anomaly detection helped support attribution after the incident.

## Legal Process

Thompson was arrested in 2019 and later convicted in U.S. federal court. The legal proceedings drew attention because the breach involved a major consumer bank and affected a very large population.

## Broader Significance

Security teams frequently cite the incident when discussing:

- Defense-in-depth for cloud deployments
- The importance of least-privilege access policies
- The difference between “hacking” narratives and the practical reality of misconfigurations
