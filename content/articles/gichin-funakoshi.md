## Summary

Gichin Funakoshi is the father of modern karate, bringing the art from Okinawa to mainland Japan. He founded Shotokan karate and established karate as a recognized martial art.

## Role in Modern Karate

- Funakoshi is commonly credited with helping adapt Okinawan karate for wider instruction in Japan, including in educational settings.
- His approach emphasized etiquette, character development, and disciplined practice alongside physical technique.

## Cultural Impact

- The spread of karate in the 20th century was shaped by public demonstrations, university clubs, and the standardization of training methods influenced by early pioneers.
- Shotokan later became one of the most widely practiced karate styles internationally, with many organizations tracing lineage to Funakoshi's teaching.

## Key Points

- Karate's modernization involved changes in terminology, pedagogy, and public presentation as it moved from Okinawa to mainland Japan.
- Funakoshi is frequently referenced in discussions of martial arts as a lifelong practice rather than a purely competitive pursuit.
