## Summary

Mathilde Krim was a pioneering AIDS researcher and activist who founded the American Foundation for AIDS Research (amfAR) in 1985. As a geneticist, she conducted important early research on interferon as a cancer treatment. She was instrumental in mobilizing the scientific and political response to the AIDS epidemic.
