## Summary

Alfred Sisley was an Impressionist landscape painter who remained the most consistently devoted to plein-air Impressionism. His serene depictions of the Seine valley and snow scenes are among the purest expressions of Impressionist light. His subtle sensitivity earned him posthumous recognition.
