## Summary

Albert Hustin performed the first successful non-direct blood transfusion using sodium citrate as an anticoagulant in 1914. His technique of preventing blood from clotting during storage and transfusion was a breakthrough that made blood banks possible. This innovation was critical for saving lives during both World Wars.
