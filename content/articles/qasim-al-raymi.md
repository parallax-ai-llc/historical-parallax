## Background

Qasim al-Raymi (also spelled al-Raymi/al-Rimi in some reporting) was a Yemeni militant who rose through the leadership ranks of **Al-Qaeda in the Arabian Peninsula (AQAP)**.

High-level points (per Wikipedia):

- AQAP formed in 2009 through the merger of Saudi and Yemeni al-Qaeda branches.
- al-Raymi is described as an early AQAP figure and later its top leader.

## Role in AQAP

- Reported as a **military commander** within AQAP in its earlier years.
- Became **emir (leader) of AQAP** after the death of his predecessor, Nasir al-Wuhayshi (reported in 2015).
- As leader, he was associated with AQAP’s broader insurgent posture in Yemen during the country’s prolonged civil conflict.

- AQAP has historically shown an ability to **reconstitute leadership** after losses; assessments of impact typically distinguish short-term disruption from longer-term organizational resilience.
