## Background

- Peter R. de Vries (often styled with a middle initial) was a Dutch journalist known for covering organized crime and high-profile criminal cases.
- He worked as an investigative crime reporter (misdaadverslaggever) and became a prominent media figure in the Netherlands through reporting and television appearances.

## Work and Public Profile

- De Vries reported on major criminal investigations and trials, often focusing on witness intimidation, the criminal underworld, and the justice system’s response.
- He was widely recognized for bringing complex cases to the public in an accessible way, including through long-running crime-focused TV formats and commentary.

## Connection to the Ridouan Taghi / Marengo Case

- In 2021, de Vries was publicly associated with the “Marengo” trial, a major Dutch prosecution targeting an alleged organized-crime network linked to Ridouan Taghi.
- He served as a confidant/adviser to a key witness in that case, which increased public attention and security concerns around participants.

## Shooting and Death

- De Vries was shot in Amsterdam on 6 July 2021 after leaving a television studio.
- He was hospitalized in critical condition and died from his injuries on 15 July 2021.

## Impact and Legacy

- His killing was widely condemned in the Netherlands and internationally as an attack on press freedom and the rule of law.
- The case intensified debate about the safety of journalists, lawyers, and witnesses in organized-crime prosecutions.
