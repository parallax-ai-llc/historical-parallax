## Summary

Erna Schneider Hoover invented the first computerized telephone switching system in 1971, which used a computer to monitor incoming calls and automatically adjust call acceptance rates. Her invention prevented overloading during peak periods and is considered one of the most important advances in telecommunications. She was one of the first women to receive a software patent.
