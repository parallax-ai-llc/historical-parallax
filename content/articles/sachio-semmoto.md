## Summary

Sachio Semmoto is a Japanese serial entrepreneur known as 'the father of Japan's telecom revolution.' He co-founded three major telecom companies: DDI (now part of KDDI), eAccess, and EMOBILE, each challenging established monopolies. His persistent entrepreneurship across decades demonstrated how competition could lower prices and improve services in telecommunications.
