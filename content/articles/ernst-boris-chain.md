## Summary

Ernst Boris Chain was a German-born British biochemist who shared the 1945 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for his work on developing penicillin. He isolated and purified penicillin with Howard Florey, making it available as a practical antibiotic. Born to a Jewish family, he fled Nazi Germany in 1933. Chain's biochemical expertise was essential in understanding penicillin's structure and mechanism.
