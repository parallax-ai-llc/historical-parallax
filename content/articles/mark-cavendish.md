## Summary

Mark Cavendish holds the record for most Tour de France stage wins with 35, surpassing Eddy Merckx's long-standing record.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2008 | Won four Tour de France stages |

| 2024 | Won record 35th Tour de France stage |

## Rider profile

Cavendish is primarily known as a **sprinter**, specializing in flat finishes decided by high-speed lead-outs and positioning. His strengths are often described as:

- explosive top-end speed
- strong race craft in crowded finales
- ability to win repeatedly across a three-week Grand Tour

## Tour de France achievements (high level)

- Stage wins across many editions of the Tour, making him one of the defining sprinters of his generation.
- Frequent contention for the **points classification** (green jersey), which rewards consistent high placements and intermediate sprint points.

## World Championship and classics

In 2011, Cavendish won the UCI Road World Championship road race, a one-day title that is distinct from stage-race achievements and is often viewed as a peak indicator of form and tactical execution.

## Career narrative

Cavendish's career is often summarized as a cycle of dominance, adversity, and resurgence: sprinting success at a young age, setbacks from crashes and illness, and later returns to top-level stage victories.

## Legacy

By surpassing a long-standing Tour stage-win benchmark, Cavendish became a reference point in discussions of sprinting longevity and specialization within modern professional cycling.
