## Summary

Lee Iacocca was an automotive industry executive who saved Chrysler Corporation from bankruptcy in the early 1980s. At Ford, he led the development of the Ford Mustang and Pinto. After being fired by Henry Ford II, he became CEO of near-bankrupt Chrysler, securing a controversial government loan guarantee. He introduced the minivan concept and restored Chrysler to profitability, becoming a symbol of American business resilience.
