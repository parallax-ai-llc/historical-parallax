## Overview

Nai-in Wongsiri is described in public sources as a Thai serial killer whose victims were children in Bangkok during the 1990s. Accounts typically emphasize that he used routine public spaces—such as parks, markets, and transit areas—to approach children, making the crimes particularly alarming to families and communities.

## Modus Operandi (High-Level)

Summaries generally report a pattern of:

- Targeting children in public places
- Luring victims away from guardians or crowded areas
- Committing murder after abduction

Specific victim counts and details can vary by source; the article’s overview commonly cites “at least five” child victims.

## Investigation and Arrest

Coverage notes that Thai police linked multiple cases through similarities in circumstances and witness information. His arrest in the late 1990s is usually presented as the result of investigative pressure as missing-children cases accumulated and public concern intensified.

## Trial and Sentencing

According to widely cited summaries, Wongsiri was convicted and received a death sentence. The case is often referenced in discussions of Thailand’s criminal-justice response to violent crimes against children.

## Impact and Legacy

The murders contributed to heightened fear around child safety in urban public settings and are frequently recalled in Thai crime reporting as an example of how serial offenses can exploit everyday environments. The case also appears in broader regional lists of notable serial offenders.
