## Summary

Granville Woods was an African American inventor who held over 60 patents, including the multiplex telegraph (allowing communication between moving trains and stations), the third rail for electric trains, and the automatic air brake. He was known as the 'Black Edison' and sold several patents to General Electric, Westinghouse, and Bell Telephone.
