## Summary

William Herschel was a German-born British astronomer who discovered the planet Uranus in 1781, the first planet found with a telescope. He also discovered infrared radiation and cataloged thousands of double stars and nebulae. He built the largest telescopes of his time and fundamentally expanded our knowledge of the solar system and the Milky Way galaxy. His sister Caroline Herschel was also a noted astronomer.
