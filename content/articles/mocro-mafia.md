## Summary

Moroccan-Dutch organized crime network controlling much of Europe's cocaine trade. Responsible for a wave of murders, bombings, and kidnappings in the Netherlands.

## Origins and label

"Mocro Mafia" is a media and policing label commonly used for networks associated with Dutch-Moroccan criminal figures, rather than a single formal organization with a public structure.

## Criminal activities (high level)

Reporting typically links these networks to:

- trafficking and wholesale distribution of cocaine via European logistics hubs
- contract violence connected to disputes over routes, shipments, and debts
- intimidation of witnesses and threats against legal professionals

## Dutch response

The Netherlands has responded with a mix of:

- large-scale investigations and prosecutions
- witness protection and security measures for high-risk targets
- debates about the resilience of institutions under organized-crime pressure

## Societal impact

The topic is often discussed in the context of:

- public safety concerns and spectacular violence incidents
- organized crime's influence on ports, freight, and cash-based economies
- trust in the rule of law when journalists, lawyers, or witnesses are attacked
