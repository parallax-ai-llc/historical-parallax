## Summary

Cy Young won 511 MLB games, an all-time record that will almost certainly never be broken. The annual award for best pitcher bears his name.

## Early Life and Path to the Majors

Young (born Denton True Young) grew up in rural Ohio and developed his pitching reputation in the late 19th century, a period when professional baseball was stabilizing into major leagues. His long career spanned major rules and equipment changes, including the evolution of the pitcher’s mound distance and the structure of leagues and schedules.

## Pitching Style and Reputation

Accounts of Young’s pitching emphasize durability and control, with contemporaries describing him as a workhorse who could shoulder heavy innings loads. His 511-win total is often contextualized by the era’s expectations for starting pitchers, who commonly completed games far more frequently than modern pitchers.

## Notable Records and Milestones

Commonly cited achievements include:

- 511 career wins (MLB record)
- 7 career no-hitters (as commonly reported)
- Participation in the first modern World Series (1903)
- A perfect game in 1904 (often described as the first of the modern era)

## Legacy: The Cy Young Award

Major League Baseball’s annual Cy Young Award is named in his honor and is awarded to the best pitchers in each league in the modern format. The award’s association with excellence in pitching has reinforced Young’s name as a shorthand for elite performance, even as the sport’s usage patterns (bullpens, pitch counts, rotations) have changed significantly.
