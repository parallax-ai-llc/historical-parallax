## Summary

American private military company whose contractors killed 17 Iraqi civilians at Nisour Square in Baghdad in 2007. Four contractors were convicted of murder. Founder Erik Prince later faced scrutiny for various deals.

## Background

- Blackwater USA was founded in the late 1990s in the United States and grew as a provider of private security, training, and logistical services.
- The company became especially prominent during the post-9/11 era, when U.S. government agencies relied heavily on contractors for protective services in conflict zones.

## Role in Iraq and Public Scrutiny

- Blackwater contractors were deployed in Iraq for diplomatic security and related missions.
- The company drew intense criticism following shootings involving civilians, most notably the **Nisour Square** incident in Baghdad (2007).
- The episode became a focal point in broader debates about accountability for private military and security contractors operating alongside state forces.

## Legal and Political Aftermath

- U.S. criminal prosecutions and appeals followed the Nisour Square case, and convictions were ultimately obtained for several contractors.
- In 2020, the convicted contractors received presidential pardons, a decision that generated significant controversy domestically and internationally.

## Rebranding and Corporate Continuity

- After reputational damage, the firm underwent rebranding and restructuring; "Blackwater" was later associated with successor names including **Xe Services** and **Academi**.
- Discussion of corporate continuity often distinguishes between the original brand, legal entities, and later ownership and management changes.

## Founder and Broader Controversies

- Founder **Erik Prince** remained a prominent figure in public reporting on private security, foreign policy, and proposed security arrangements.
- Allegations and investigations reported in the press have included questions about contracting practices, oversight, and proposed deals; specific claims vary by source and have been disputed.

## Legacy

- Blackwater is frequently cited in analyses of the privatization of military functions and the challenge of regulating armed contractors.
- The company’s story is used in policy discussions about rules of engagement, jurisdiction, transparency, and victim compensation.
