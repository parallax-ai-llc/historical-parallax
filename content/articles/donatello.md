## Summary

Donatello was the greatest sculptor of the Early Renaissance whose innovations in naturalism and emotional expression transformed European sculpture. His bronze David was the first free-standing nude male sculpture since antiquity. His works including the Gattamelata equestrian statue pioneered new forms of sculptural expression.
