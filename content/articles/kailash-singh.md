## Summary

Indian wildlife smuggler known as the 'Tiger Man' who was one of India's most notorious poachers and wildlife traffickers. He organized the killing and smuggling of tigers, leopards, and other endangered species across India and Nepal.

## Wildlife Trafficking Context

Wildlife trafficking networks in South Asia have historically targeted high-value species such as tigers and leopards, with demand linked to illegal trade in skins, bones, and other body parts. Law-enforcement reporting and conservation groups often describe these markets as transnational, moving products across porous borders and relying on intermediaries.

## Modus Operandi (High Level)

Accounts of Singh’s activities describe a networked operation: coordination of poachers, procurement of contraband, and movement of goods through multiple states and into neighboring countries. Such operations typically depend on concealment methods, cash payments, and the exploitation of remote protected areas.

## Enforcement and Prosecution

Singh’s arrests and later court outcomes are frequently cited in discussions of anti-poaching enforcement in India, including the role of specialized wildlife crime units and inter-state coordination.

## Conservation Impact

The killing of large predators has ecological effects beyond population counts, influencing trophic dynamics and tourism economies in regions dependent on protected-area conservation. High-profile cases are often used to illustrate the scale and organization of illegal wildlife trade.
