## Summary

Powerful Sicilian Mafia clan from Siculiana that built a global criminal network spanning Canada, Venezuela, Brazil, and Europe. Known as the 'Rothschilds of the Mafia' for their financial sophistication.

## Origins and Base

The Cuntrera–Caruana clan is associated with Siculiana (province of Agrigento) in Sicily. In many accounts, the group is described as a family-based Mafia network that leveraged kinship ties and migration routes to establish influence beyond Italy.

## International Network

The clan is often discussed as an example of transnational organized crime, with reported links across:

- Canada (including Montreal and Toronto)
- Latin America (including Venezuela and other countries used for logistics and finance)
- European nodes used for coordination, money movement, and contacts

The “Rothschilds of the Mafia” nickname appears in reporting as a reference to alleged financial and money-handling capabilities rather than a single territorial stronghold.

## Criminal Activities (high-level)

Descriptions of the clan commonly include involvement in:

- International drug trafficking (notably heroin and cocaine supply chains)
- Money laundering and the use of legitimate businesses for concealment
- Brokerage and coordination roles between different Mafia factions and partners

## Law Enforcement Attention

Multiple members have been the subject of investigations and arrests in different jurisdictions, reflecting the cross-border nature of the network. Publicly reported cases in Canada and Italy have been used to illustrate how Mafia families can operate through diaspora communities while retaining links to Sicily.
