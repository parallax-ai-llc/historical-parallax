## Summary

Huston Smith was a Chinese-born American scholar of comparative religion whose book The World's Religions (originally The Religions of Man) is the most widely used textbook on world religions, selling over 3 million copies. He personally practiced Hinduism, Buddhism, and Sufism alongside his Christian faith. His empathetic approach to the world's religious traditions influenced generations of students and the broader public understanding of religion.
