## Summary

Sugar Ray Robinson is the greatest pound-for-pound boxer ever. He won the middleweight title five times.

## Additional context

### Style and significance

- Robinson’s blend of speed, footwork, timing, and punching variety made him a benchmark for “complete” boxing skill.
- The phrase “pound-for-pound” is commonly associated with assessments of Robinson’s excellence across weight classes and eras.
- He competed in an unusually high number of professional bouts, reflecting the norms of mid-20th-century boxing.

### Major phases of his career

- Robinson first reached prominence as an elite welterweight and later moved up to middleweight, where he won the world title multiple times.
- Rivalries with top contemporaries helped define his era and contributed to his enduring reputation among historians and fighters.
- Late-career comebacks were part of his public narrative and showed both the physical costs and competitive drive typical of long boxing careers.

### Legacy

- Many later champions and trainers cite Robinson’s footage and tactical choices as a reference point for offensive and defensive fundamentals.
- Beyond records and titles, Robinson’s influence is often measured in how boxing audiences and analysts describe greatness and skill.
