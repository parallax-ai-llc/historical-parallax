## Summary

Russian serial killer known as the 'Granny Ripper' who murdered and dismembered at least 3 people in Saint Petersburg over a 12-year period. She was arrested in 2015 at age 68 after CCTV footage.

## Background

Samsonova lived in Saint Petersburg and was associated in media reports with a series of killings involving people she knew or lived with. Her case drew attention in Russia because of her age and the sensational framing used by tabloids.

## Investigation

Authorities connected her to at least one homicide after surveillance footage and forensic findings were reported to have tied her to the case. Public reporting also described police reviewing older missing-person files and suspicious deaths to determine whether they were related.

## Alleged Victims and Confessions

News coverage frequently cited claims that she confessed to more killings than were formally proven in court. Such confessions and statements can be difficult to verify, and investigators generally distinguish between suspected cases and those supported by physical evidence.

## Legal Proceedings

After her arrest, she underwent evaluations and was held in connection with murder charges. Reporting noted that the case involved questions about competence and psychiatric assessment, which can influence trial process and sentencing in Russian criminal law.

## Media and Public Reaction

The nickname “Granny Ripper” reflected a broader pattern of sensational labels in crime reporting. The case also prompted discussion about social isolation, informal roommate arrangements, and the challenges of monitoring vulnerable adults in large cities.
