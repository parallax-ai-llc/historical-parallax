## Summary

American businessman and founder of Blackwater (now Academi), a private military company. His contractors committed the Nisour Square massacre. He has been linked to secret meetings with Russian officials and arms deals in Africa.

## Additional Context

- Prince is closely associated with the post2001 expansion of private military and security contracting, particularly in Iraq and Afghanistan.
- Blackwater provided protective security services under US government contracts, placing contractors in combat-adjacent roles with significant political and legal controversy.

## Major Controversies (High Level)

- The 2007 Nisour Square killings by Blackwater contractors became a defining incident, raising questions about contractor accountability, rules of engagement, and oversight.
- Subsequent investigations and prosecutions highlighted the difficulty of applying consistent legal frameworks to private contractors operating in war zones.
- Prince has also been linked in reporting and investigations to proposals for privatized security and military activities in multiple countries.

## Business and Political Influence

- Prince has pursued ventures in logistics, security, and aviation, often marketed to governments seeking rapid capacity without large standing forces.
- He has been a prominent advocate for privatization approaches to security policy, which critics argue can dilute democratic accountability.

## Legacy

Prince remains a polarizing figure in debates over the outsourcing of force, the relationship between commerce and conflict, and the long-term consequences of privatized security industries.

His career is frequently cited when assessing how states manage contractor oversight in high-risk environments.
