## Summary

Bombing of Air India Flight 182 on June 23, 1985 by Sikh extremists that killed all 329 people aboard. It was the deadliest aviation terrorism incident before 9/11.

## Additional Context

## Incident Overview

Air India Flight 182 (operated by Air India on a route that included Canada and India) was destroyed by a bomb on 23 June 1985 over the Atlantic Ocean, killing all 329 people on board. The attack is commonly attributed to Sikh extremists linked to the Khalistan movement.

## Related Events and Investigation

- On the same day, a separate bomb exploded at Japan’s Narita Airport while being transferred, an event often discussed alongside the Flight 182 attack.
- The investigation and prosecutions stretched over decades and became a case study in intelligence sharing, aviation security, and the challenges of prosecuting terrorism cases.

## Public Inquiry and Legacy

In Canada, the bombing is frequently described as one of the country’s most consequential acts of mass murder and led to extended public discussion about institutional failures and community impact.

## Notes

- Casualty figures and attribution are widely reported, but legal responsibility and evidentiary narratives differ by source.
- The case is frequently used in aviation-security training as an example of long-tail investigative complexity.
