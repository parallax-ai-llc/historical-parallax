## Summary

Pafnuty Chebyshev made major contributions to probability theory, statistics, and number theory. He proved Bertrand's postulate, developed the Chebyshev inequality and Chebyshev polynomials, and advanced the law of large numbers. He also invented over 80 mechanical devices and is considered the founder of the Russian school of mathematics.
