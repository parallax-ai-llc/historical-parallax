## Summary

Eric Lander is an American geneticist and mathematician who was one of the leaders of the Human Genome Project and founding director of the Broad Institute of MIT and Harvard. A former mathematician, he developed computational methods for gene mapping and was instrumental in sequencing the human genome. He briefly served as White House science advisor.
