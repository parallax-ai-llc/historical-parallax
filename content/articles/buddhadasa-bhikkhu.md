## Summary

Buddhadasa Bhikkhu was a Thai Buddhist monk who was one of the most influential Theravada thinkers of the 20th century. He founded the Suan Mokkh forest monastery and reinterpreted traditional Theravada teachings in light of direct experience rather than ritual. His concept of 'Dhammic Socialism' attempted to apply Buddhist principles to social and political issues. He engaged with other religions and influenced interfaith dialogue.
