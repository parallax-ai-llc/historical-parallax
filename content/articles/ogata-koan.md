## Summary

Ogata Koan was a leading Japanese physician and educator who established the Tekijuku school in Osaka, which trained many of Japan's future leaders in Western medicine and science during the late Edo period. He translated Dutch medical texts and promoted vaccination against smallpox. His school produced numerous influential figures of the Meiji Restoration.
