## Summary
Kwame Malik Kilpatrick was the mayor of Detroit who was convicted of corruption, racketeering, and extortion. He ran a pay-to-play scheme and used city funds for personal expenses. He was sentenced to 28 years in 2013 and pardoned by President Trump in 2021.
