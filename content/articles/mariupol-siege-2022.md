## Summary

Russian forces besieged the Ukrainian city of Mariupol for 86 days, bombing a maternity hospital and a theater sheltering 1,300 civilians. An estimated 25,000 civilians were killed.

## Context

Mariupol is a port city on the Sea of Azov in southeastern Ukraine. After Russia launched its full-scale invasion of Ukraine in February 2022, Mariupol became a major target due to its location between Russian-controlled areas and Crimea.

## Notable events (selected)

- **Late Feb 2022:** Fighting intensified around the city as Russian and aligned forces advanced and attempted to encircle Mariupol.
- **9 Mar 2022:** An airstrike hit a maternity hospital and children’s hospital complex, becoming one of the best-known early incidents of the siege.
- **16 Mar 2022:** The Mariupol Drama Theatre, widely reported to be used as a civilian shelter, was destroyed; the number of people inside at the time was disputed in later reporting.
- **Apr–May 2022:** Remaining Ukrainian defenders were concentrated in and around the Azovstal steelworks as the siege continued.
- **May 2022:** Ukrainian forces at Azovstal surrendered and were taken into Russian custody, marking the end of organized resistance inside the city.

## Humanitarian impact

- The siege involved prolonged disruption of **water, electricity, heating, food supply, and medical care**, contributing to a humanitarian emergency.
- Civilian casualty figures varied widely by source; Ukrainian authorities and international observers described large-scale loss of life and extensive destruction of residential areas.
- Damage to infrastructure and housing led to large-scale displacement from the city during and after the siege.

## International response and accountability

- International organizations and governments condemned attacks on civilian sites and called for humanitarian corridors and access.
- The events of the siege were referenced in discussions about potential **war crimes** and the need for independent investigations, including documentation of strikes on civilian infrastructure.

## Aftermath

- Mariupol remained under Russian control following the siege.
- Post-siege reporting focused on reconstruction efforts, population movement, and the difficulty of independently verifying conditions inside the city due to restricted access.
