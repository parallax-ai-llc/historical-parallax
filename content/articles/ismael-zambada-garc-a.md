## Summary

Mexican drug lord known as 'El Mayo' who co-led the Sinaloa Cartel for decades without ever being imprisoned. He was one of the most powerful and elusive drug traffickers in history. Arrested in the US in 2024.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1980 | Began drug trafficking in Sinaloa |
| 2003 | Became co-leader of Sinaloa Cartel |
| 2024 | Arrested in the United States |

## Role in the Sinaloa Cartel (High Level)

- Zambada García ("El Mayo") is widely described as a longtime top figure within the Sinaloa Cartel.
- He was often portrayed as a strategic organizer focused on logistics, alliances, and maintaining operational continuity.
- In accounts of cartel leadership, he is frequently mentioned alongside Joaquín "El Chapo" Guzmán as a co-leader during key periods.

## Reputation and Evasion

- For many years, he avoided capture, contributing to an image of being unusually elusive relative to other high-profile traffickers.
- Analyses often attribute this to cautious movement, reliance on trusted networks, and a preference for low visibility.

## U.S. Law Enforcement Focus

- U.S. authorities have treated him as a major trafficking figure, with investigations tied to drug distribution networks and cartel operations.
- Public reporting in 2024 described his arrest in the United States, marking a significant shift after decades of evasion.

## Broader Context

- The Sinaloa Cartel has been associated with international trafficking routes and, in recent years, with the production and movement of synthetic opioids.
- Leadership changes and factional dynamics within the cartel have been a recurring theme in coverage of Mexican organized crime.

## Public Profile

- The nickname "El Mayo" has been widely used in media and law enforcement descriptions.
- His long period as a fugitive became part of the public narrative about cartel leadership persistence.
