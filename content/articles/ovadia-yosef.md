## Summary

Ovadia Yosef was an Iraqi-Israeli rabbi who served as Sephardi Chief Rabbi of Israel and was the founder and spiritual leader of the Shas political party. He was widely regarded as one of the greatest Talmudic scholars of his generation, producing landmark halakhic rulings. His rulings on the status of Ethiopian Jews and agunot (chained wives) were groundbreaking. His funeral in Jerusalem was attended by an estimated 800,000 people.
