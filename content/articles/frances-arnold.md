## Summary

Frances Arnold is an American chemical engineer and biochemist who shared the 2018 Nobel Prize in Chemistry for the directed evolution of enzymes. She pioneered methods for harnessing evolution in the laboratory to create enzymes with novel functions, useful in green chemistry, pharmaceuticals, and biofuels. She was the fifth woman to win the Nobel Prize in Chemistry.
