## Summary

Already added.

René Gerónimo Favaloro was an Argentine surgeon best known for pioneering the use of the **saphenous vein** as a bypass graft in **coronary artery bypass surgery (CABG)**, a technique that became foundational to modern cardiac surgery. Beyond his technical contributions, he is frequently cited in Argentina for public advocacy around medical ethics, equitable access to care, and sustainable health systems.

## Key Contributions

- **Coronary bypass technique:** Helped popularize reliable surgical revascularization by using autologous vein grafts to bypass obstructed coronary arteries.
- **Training and dissemination:** Contributed to the spread of modern cardiac surgical methods through publications, clinical teaching, and institutional leadership.
- **Public health and ethics:** Wrote and spoke widely on physician responsibility, transparency, and the social obligations of medical institutions.

## Context and Legacy

Favaloro’s name remains closely associated with the maturation of CABG from an experimental procedure into a widely adopted, life-saving operation. In Argentina, he is also remembered for efforts to build durable medical education and research infrastructure via the Favaloro Foundation and for his high-profile calls for ethical governance in healthcare.

### Notes

- **CABG** (coronary artery bypass grafting) aims to restore blood flow to the heart muscle by routing blood around narrowed or blocked coronary arteries.
- Favaloro is often credited with helping make bypass surgery reproducible at scale by formalizing graft selection and operative technique in the late 1960s.
