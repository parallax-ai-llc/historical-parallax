## Additional Context

### Background

- Salman Raduyev (1967–2002) was a Chechen militant figure active during the First Chechen War and the period between the first and second wars.
- He became publicly known outside Chechnya largely through the 1996 hostage crisis centered on Kizlyar and Pervomayskoye in Russia.

### 1996 Kizlyar–Pervomayskoye Hostage Crisis (high level)

- In January 1996, a militant detachment associated with Raduyev carried out a raid in Dagestan, taking hostages and triggering a multi-day standoff.
- The crisis moved from the town of Kizlyar to the village of Pervomayskoye, where Russian forces attempted to resolve the situation.
- The episode is commonly cited as one of the major hostage incidents of the 1990s connected to the Chechen conflict.

### Arrest, Trial, and Death

- Raduyev was later arrested by Russian authorities (reported in the early 2000s) and prosecuted in connection with terrorism and hostage-taking.
- He died in Russian custody in 2002.
