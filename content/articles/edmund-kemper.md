## Summary
Edmund Emil Kemper III, the 'Co-ed Killer,' murdered 10 people including his grandparents and mother. Standing 6'9" tall, he killed six female college students near Santa Cruz, California in 1972-1973. Notable for his high IQ and extensive cooperation with FBI profilers that helped develop criminal profiling.

## Early Life
Born December 18, 1948, in Burbank, California. His parents divorced when he was nine. His mother Clarnell was reportedly abusive. He tortured animals as a boy. At 15, he shot and killed both grandparents at their ranch and was committed to Atascadero State Hospital. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Released in 1969, Kemper moved in with his mother. Between 1972-1973, he killed six female hitchhikers. He engaged in necrophilia and dismemberment. On Easter 1973, he murdered his mother, using her severed head as a dartboard, then killed her friend Sally Hallett. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
After killing his mother, Kemper drove to Colorado and called Santa Cruz police from a phone booth to confess. Arrested April 24, 1973, he was convicted of eight counts of first-degree murder and sentenced to life. He has cooperated extensively with FBI profilers. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- Provided invaluable cooperation with FBI Behavioral Science Unit [^4]
- Helped establish foundational knowledge about serial killer psychology
- Turned himself in voluntarily

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered 10 people including family members [^5]
- Targeted young female college students
- Committed necrophilia and dismemberment
- Was released despite killing two people as a teenager
- Demonstrated catastrophic failures in mental health risk assessment
