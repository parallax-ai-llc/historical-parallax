## Summary

British gangster who, with twin brother Reggie, controlled organized crime in London's East End. He murdered George Cornell in a pub in 1966. He was diagnosed as a paranoid schizophrenic. Died in prison.

## Context and Background

Ronnie Kray (born Ronald Kray) was one of the Kray twins, raised in London's East End and associated with a criminal organization often described as "the Firm." The twins became prominent in the 1960s, combining violence, intimidation, and nightclub connections to extend their influence.

## Criminal Activities and Prosecution

Accounts typically emphasize the Krays' involvement in protection rackets and violent crime, as well as their high public profile for gangsters of the era. Ronnie Kray's conviction for murder and the life sentences handed down in 1969 were a major turning point that effectively ended their control of the organization.

## Mental Health and Imprisonment

Ronnie Kray was diagnosed with paranoid schizophrenia and spent periods in secure hospital settings during his incarceration. He ultimately died while still in custody.

## Cultural Impact

The Kray twins have been the subject of books, films, and broader popular discussion about organized crime in postwar Britain, with portrayals often focusing on celebrity, notoriety, and the mythologizing of East End gang culture.
