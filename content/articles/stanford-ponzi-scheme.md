## What a Ponzi Scheme Is (high level)

A Ponzi scheme is a fraudulent investment operation where:

- returns to earlier investors are paid using funds from newer investors,
- the operation depends on continuous inflows of new money, and
- the scheme typically collapses when redemptions rise or new investment slows.

## Why “Certificates of Deposit” Matter

When a scheme is sold through instruments that resemble conservative bank products (such as certificates of deposit), the perceived risk can be lower for victims. That can increase:

- the pool of potential investors,
- the duration of the fraud, and
- the eventual losses when the scheme unwinds.

## Common Aftermath in Large Financial Frauds

Large cases often involve:

- asset freezes and forfeiture,
- receiver-led recovery efforts,
- victim claims processes, and
- long timelines for restitution due to litigation and complex asset tracing.
