## Context and Background

- Omar Hassan Ahmad al-Bashir (born 1944) is a Sudanese military officer and political leader who ruled Sudan from 1989 until he was removed in 2019.
- He came to power through a military coup and governed through a security-centered state apparatus and a dominant ruling party structure.

## Rise to Power (1989 Coup)

- In June 1989, al-Bashir led a coup that overthrew Sudan's elected government amid civil conflict and economic stress.
- His early rule was closely associated with Islamist political currents in Sudan, particularly through alliances with figures such as Hassan al-Turabi (relationship and influence varied over time).

## Presidency Overview (High-Level)

- Al-Bashir's tenure is commonly characterized by:
  - Centralization of authority in the presidency and military-security institutions.
  - Prolonged internal conflicts (including civil wars and regional insurgencies).
  - International isolation and sanctions during parts of his rule.
  - Shifts in alliances domestically and regionally as the political landscape evolved.

## Major Conflicts and Humanitarian Crises

- **Second Sudanese Civil War (1983–2005):** The long-running north–south conflict continued during al-Bashir's early decades in power and ended with the Comprehensive Peace Agreement (CPA) in 2005.
- **Darfur conflict (from 2003):** Violence in Darfur drew major international attention and accusations of mass atrocities; the conflict became a central element of the international legal case against al-Bashir.

## International Criminal Court (ICC)

- Al-Bashir was the subject of ICC arrest warrants (2009 and 2010) related to alleged crimes in Darfur.
- He maintained political power for years after the warrants, while international travel and diplomacy became increasingly constrained by the legal and political implications.

## Key Political and Economic Milestones

- **2005 CPA:** Established a framework to end the north–south war and set a path to a referendum on southern independence.
- **2011 South Sudan independence:** The secession of South Sudan reshaped Sudan's politics and economy, including major impacts linked to oil revenues and fiscal stability.
- **Sanctions and foreign relations:** Sudan's external relations fluctuated across the period, with phases of heightened isolation and later attempts at normalization.

## 2019 Overthrow and Aftermath

- In 2019, mass protests and political crisis culminated in al-Bashir being removed by the military.
- Subsequent transitional arrangements and negotiations over accountability, governance, and future political order continued to shape Sudan's post-2019 trajectory.
