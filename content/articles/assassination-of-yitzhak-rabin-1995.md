## Overview
On November 4, 1995, Prime Minister Yitzhak Rabin was assassinated at the end of a rally in support of the Oslo Accords at the Kings of Israel Square in Tel Aviv.

## The Event
Yigal Amir, a right-wing law student and fierce opponent of Rabins peace initiative with the Palestinians, fired three shots at Rabin as he was entering his car. Rabin died later in surgery.

## Legacy
Rabins assassination derailed the peace process and deeply polarized Israeli society. The square where he was killed was renamed Rabin Square in his honor.
