## Summary

Charles Chamberland developed the Chamberland filter (porcelain filter) that could remove bacteria from liquids, essential for producing sterile solutions. His filter also led to the discovery of viruses when Ivanovsky showed that infectious agents could pass through it. He was one of Louis Pasteur's most important collaborators at the Pasteur Institute.
