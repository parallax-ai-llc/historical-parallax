## Summary

Capitol rioter accused of stealing Nancy Pelosi's laptop and trying to send it to Russia.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 2023 | Convicted of interference with police |

## Key Allegations (Jan 6)

Public reporting and federal court proceedings describe Williams as having entered the U.S. Capitol during the January 6, 2021 attack and later becoming widely known for allegations involving property associated with Speaker Nancy Pelosi.

Commonly cited allegations include:

- Entering restricted areas of the U.S. Capitol during the riot.
- Taking items from an office area associated with Pelosi, including a government-issued laptop used for presentations (per Pelosi staff statements reported in the press).
- Online messages in which she allegedly discussed selling or transferring the laptop.

## Legal Proceedings & Outcome

- **2021-01-18**: Arrested by federal authorities (per reporting).
- **2023-03-21**: Found guilty in U.S. District Court and sentenced to **36 months** in prison (per reporting on the sentencing).
- **2025-01-20**: Reported release following a **presidential pardon**.

> Note: Some reporting also states that the laptop referenced in the allegations was not recovered.

## Additional Context

- The case drew attention because it combined an on-site alleged theft with subsequent online chatter (including on chat platforms) that investigators and journalists cited.
- Coverage has also discussed Williams' presence in, or proximity to, online far-right communities and influencers, based on publicly reported posts and messages.
- Reporting on the case is often framed within the broader set of January 6 prosecutions and sentencing patterns.
