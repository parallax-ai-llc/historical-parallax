## Summary

Isnilon Hapilon became ISIS's top leader in Southeast Asia and led the 2017 siege of Marawi that destroyed the Philippine city. The five-month battle was the Philippine military's longest urban combat since World War II.

## Additional Context

## Abu Sayyaf as an Organization

Abu Sayyaf is a militant Islamist group based primarily in the southern Philippines, particularly the Sulu Archipelago and parts of Mindanao. It has been associated with kidnappings for ransom, attacks on civilians and security forces, and shifting alliances with other militant networks in the region.

## Leadership and Factions

The group has not been a single unified hierarchy at all times; reporting often describes semi-autonomous factions led by different commanders. Leadership changes have frequently followed killings or arrests, contributing to fragmentation and intermittent rebranding.

## Isnilon Hapilon and the ISIS Link

Hapilon’s pledge of allegiance to ISIS was widely cited as a step toward aligning certain Philippine-based militants with ISIS messaging and recruitment. His role during the Marawi siege made the conflict a major reference point for concerns about transnational jihadist influence in Southeast Asia.

## Marawi Siege (2017)

The five-month battle resulted in large-scale destruction, civilian displacement, and a prolonged military operation. The aftermath included reconstruction efforts and ongoing security measures aimed at preventing renewed insurgent control in urban centers.
