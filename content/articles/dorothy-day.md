## Summary

Dorothy Day was an American Catholic social activist and journalist who co-founded the Catholic Worker Movement with Peter Maurin. She combined radical political activism with deep Catholic piety, advocating for the poor and marginalized. The Catholic Worker newspaper, which she edited for nearly 50 years, promoted pacifism and social justice. Her cause for canonization is ongoing.
