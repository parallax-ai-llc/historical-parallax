## Overview

- Shyam Benegal is an Indian film director and screenwriter known for work associated with "parallel cinema" (a realist, socially engaged strand of Indian filmmaking).
- His films frequently depict social conflict, political change, and questions of class, gender, and power.
- He also directed documentary and television work and was involved in institutional and cultural projects related to Indian cinema.

## Selected themes and style (high-level)

- Ensemble casts and character-driven storytelling.
- Stories grounded in specific regions and historical moments.
- Focus on institutional forces (state, landlordism, bureaucracy) and their effects on ordinary life.

## Recognition

- Benegal received major Indian film honors over his career, including awards associated with the National Film Awards.
- Retrospectives and academic writing commonly treat him as a significant figure in post-independence Indian cinema.

## Notable works (examples)

- Feature films often mentioned in overviews include *Ankur*, *Nishant*, *Manthan*, and *Bhumika*.
- He also directed later historical and biographical projects, reflecting an interest in social history and public institutions.
