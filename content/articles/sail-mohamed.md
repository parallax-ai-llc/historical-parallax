## Additional Context

### Crimes (High-Level)

- Sail Mohamed is described in English-language sources as a robber who escalated to serial murder in **Paris** in the **late 1940s**.
- The attacks are commonly summarized as targeting **elderly women**, with robbery as the principal motive.

### Historical Setting

- The reported timeline places the crimes in the aftermath of World War II, a period often associated with displacement, poverty, and strained policing resources in major European cities.
- Accounts typically emphasize that the victims were selected for vulnerability and relative isolation.

### Arrest and Conviction

- He was reportedly arrested in **1951**.
- Summaries generally note that investigators tied multiple killings together as part of a single series, leading to a conviction for several murders.

### Sentence and Execution

- Sources report a sentence of death and execution by **guillotine** in **1953**, reflecting France’s use of capital punishment at the time.
