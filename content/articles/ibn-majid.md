## Summary

Ahmad ibn Majid was an Arab navigator known as the 'Lion of the Sea.' He is credited with helping Vasco da Gama navigate across the Indian Ocean from Africa to India.

## Additional Context

### Nautical science and writings

- Ibn Majid is associated with the tradition of Arab seafaring in the Red Sea, Arabian Sea, and Indian Ocean.
- His works are typically described as practical navigational guides, covering winds, currents, coastal landmarks, and techniques for open-sea travel.
- They are also a source for understanding how maritime knowledge was transmitted in verse and prose in the pre-modern Islamic world.

### The Vasco da Gama attribution

- The claim that he personally guided Vasco da Gama is often presented as contested or uncertain in modern historical discussions.
- Some accounts treat it as later tradition, while others argue for alternative pilots and intermediaries involved in the Portuguese voyage.
- As a result, summaries frequently distinguish between Ibn Majid’s documented writings and the more debated biographical anecdotes.

### Legacy

- Regardless of the da Gama story, he is widely cited as an important figure in the history of navigation and Indian Ocean maritime networks.
- His reputation reflects both technical contributions and the broader cultural memory of seafaring expertise in the region.

### Historical sources

- Modern biographies rely on a mix of attributed texts, manuscript traditions, and later historical commentary, which can complicate definitive claims.
