## Summary

The Boxer Rebellion (1899-1901) was an anti-foreign, anti-Christian uprising in China supported by the Qing dynasty. An eight-nation alliance intervened, captured Beijing, and imposed the humiliating Boxer Protocol. The rebellion hastened the Qing dynasty's fall.
