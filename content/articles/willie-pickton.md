## Background

Pickton was a pig farmer who operated a property in Port Coquitlam, British Columbia, near Vancouver. The farm became the focal point of one of Canada's most high-profile serial murder investigations.

## Investigation

Police attention intensified amid reports of missing women, many of whom were connected to Vancouver's Downtown Eastside. In 2002, Pickton was arrested and investigators searched his property, leading to the forensic work that supported homicide charges.

## Trial and Convictions

In 2007, a jury convicted Pickton of six counts of second-degree murder. He received a life sentence, and the case generated extensive discussion in Canada about policing, protection of marginalized communities, and the handling of missing-persons investigations.

## Public Impact

The case is frequently cited in conversations about systemic risk factors faced by vulnerable populations and the importance of timely investigative response to patterns of disappearances.

## Notes

Biographical and case summaries commonly distinguish between the six murders proven at trial and additional allegations discussed in reporting and later proceedings.
