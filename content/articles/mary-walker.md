## Summary

Mary Edwards Walker was the first female surgeon in the U.S. Army and the only woman to receive the Medal of Honor. She served as a surgeon during the Civil War, was captured as a prisoner of war, and fought tirelessly for women's rights and dress reform. Her military medical career broke multiple gender barriers.
