## Summary

Robert Arthur Kardashian Jr., born March 17, 1987, is an American television personality and entrepreneur best known as a member of the Kardashian-Jenner family, whose lives have been documented on the long-running reality series *Keeping Up with the Kardashians* (2007–2021). The son of attorney Robert Kardashian Sr. and Kris Jenner, Rob became a cultural fixture through his family's media empire while also carving out his own business ventures.

Though often overshadowed by his sisters Kim, Khloé, and Kourtney, Rob has remained a notable figure through his personal struggles, entrepreneurial efforts, and brief but memorable appearances in competitive reality television. His candid public journey with health and personal challenges has resonated with audiences, making him a symbol of vulnerability within a family brand built on image.

## Early Life & Background

Rob Kardashian was born in Los Angeles, California, to Robert Kardashian Sr., a prominent attorney known for his role in the O.J. Simpson murder trial, and Kris Jenner. He grew up alongside sisters Kourtney, Kim, and Khloé, as well as half-siblings from his mother's later marriage to Caitlyn Jenner. His father passed away from esophageal cancer in September 2003, when Rob was just 16, an event that profoundly affected him. Rob attended the University of Southern California, graduating in 2009 with a degree in business administration from the Marshall School of Business.

## Rise to Prominence

Rob first gained wide public attention as a cast member on *Keeping Up with the Kardashians*, which premiered on E! in October 2007. His natural charisma and humor distinguished him within the ensemble. In 2011, he competed on Season 13 of *Dancing with the Stars*, finishing as runner-up alongside professional partner Cheryl Burke — a personal high point that showcased his competitive drive. He and Blac Chyna starred in the spin-off *Rob & Chyna* in 2016, which drew significant ratings during its debut season.

## Key Achievements

Rob's most recognized business venture is Arthur George, a sock company he launched in 2012, named after his and his father's middle names. The brand gained traction through celebrity endorsements and retail partnerships, including a deal with Lord & Taylor. The company demonstrated Rob's ability to build an independent identity outside his family's broader enterprises. His *Dancing with the Stars* runner-up finish in 2011 remains one of his most celebrated individual accomplishments.

## Controversies & Criticism

Rob's personal life attracted significant media scrutiny. His turbulent relationship with model Blac Chyna, with whom he has a daughter, Dream Kardashian (born November 2016), became highly publicized after their 2017 breakup. In July 2017, Rob posted explicit images of Chyna on social media without her consent, drawing widespread condemnation and legal action. Chyna filed lawsuits against him and members of his family; legal proceedings extended into the 2020s. Rob has also been open about struggles with weight gain, depression, and diabetes, topics he addressed publicly over multiple years.

## Legacy & Influence

Rob Kardashian's legacy is nuanced. He represents the pressures faced by men within highly image-conscious public families and has been candid about mental health in ways that resonated beyond celebrity culture. His entrepreneurial work with Arthur George demonstrated that his business school education translated into real-world application. As the only son in a family that redefined celebrity branding, his quieter but enduring presence within that narrative remains part of the broader Kardashian-Jenner cultural footprint.
