## Summary

Willard Boyle co-invented the charge-coupled device (CCD) with George Smith at Bell Labs in 1969. The CCD became the digital image sensor used in cameras, telescopes, and medical imaging, revolutionizing photography and astronomy. He shared the 2009 Nobel Prize in Physics for this invention.
