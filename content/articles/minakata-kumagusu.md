## Summary

Minakata Kumagusu was a Japanese naturalist and polymath who made significant contributions to mycology, discovering over 70 new species of slime molds. Self-taught in multiple languages, he published in Nature and was one of the first Japanese ecologists, campaigning to preserve ancient temple forests. He was also an expert in folklore and anthropology.
