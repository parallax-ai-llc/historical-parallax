## Additional Context

- Carolus Linnaeus is the Latinized form of **Carl Linnaeus**, a Swedish naturalist whose work helped standardize how organisms are named and classified.
- He is most closely associated with **binomial nomenclature**: assigning each species a two-part Latin name (genus + specific epithet), which improved clarity and stability in scientific communication.

## Major Contributions

- **Taxonomy and classification:** Linnaeus proposed hierarchical groupings (e.g., kingdom, class, order, genus, species) as a practical organizing scheme for natural history.
- **Naming conventions:** His approach made it easier for scientists across countries and languages to refer to the same organism unambiguously.
- **Botanical study:** Linnaeus promoted systematic collection, description, and comparison of plants through fieldwork and specimen-based study.

## Key Works (High-Level)

- Linnaeus published influential catalogs and manuals that compiled species descriptions and naming conventions used by later botanists and zoologists.
- These publications became reference points for subsequent taxonomic work and helped popularize standardized scientific naming.

## Influence and Legacy

- Modern biological nomenclature was later formalized through international codes, but Linnaeus’s framework is widely treated as foundational.
- His naming system is still visible in everyday science education and in the Latin names used in medicine, ecology, and conservation.

## Notes

- Linnaeus’s classification ideas were later revised as biology incorporated evolution and genetics, but standardized naming remained essential.
- In historical writing, he is sometimes referenced under "Carl Linnaeus" as well as "Carolus Linnaeus," reflecting Latinization conventions.
