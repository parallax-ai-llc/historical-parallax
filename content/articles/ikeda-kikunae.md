## Summary

Ikeda Kikunae was a Japanese chemist who discovered umami as a fundamental taste and isolated monosodium glutamate (MSG) from kombu seaweed in 1908. His discovery of the fifth basic taste revolutionized food science and led to the founding of the Ajinomoto company. He was a professor at Tokyo Imperial University.
