## Background

- Ljube Boškoski is a Macedonian/North Macedonian politician and former senior security official.
- He served as Minister of Internal Affairs during the 2001 conflict involving the ethnic Albanian insurgency in the Republic of Macedonia (now North Macedonia).

## Roles and Public Profile

- **Ministry of Internal Affairs (Interior Minister):** Oversaw police and internal security policy in a period of heightened ethnic tensions.
- **Public debates:** His name is frequently cited in discussions about state security decisions and accountability during the 2001 conflict.

## ICTY Case (High-Level)

Boškoski was tried at the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) in relation to actions connected to the 2001 conflict.

- **Charge context:** Allegations concerned responsibility for crimes attributed to security forces.
- **Outcome:** Acquitted (2008), as reflected in the ICTY judgment and summarized in general reference sources.
