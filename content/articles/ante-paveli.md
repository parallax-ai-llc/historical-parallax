## Summary

Croatian fascist dictator who led the Independent State of Croatia as a Nazi puppet state during WWII. His Ustasha regime murdered hundreds of thousands of Serbs, Jews, and Roma in concentration camps including Jasenovac.

## Ustasha Regime and Policies

As Poglavnik (leader) of the Independent State of Croatia (NDH), Pavelić headed a regime aligned with Nazi Germany and Fascist Italy. The NDH implemented racial laws and pursued mass violence and persecution against Serbs, Jews, Roma, and political opponents, including through a network of camps and summary killings.

## Relationship with Axis Powers

The NDH's sovereignty was constrained by German and Italian strategic interests, and the state functioned as a wartime client regime. Internal power struggles, partisan resistance, and shifting military fortunes shaped the regime's actions and brutality during the conflict.

## Flight and Postwar Years

After the Axis defeat, Pavelić escaped Europe through clandestine "ratline" routes used by many wanted collaborators. He lived in Argentina and later Spain. A 1957 assassination attempt left him wounded, and he died in 1959.

## Historical Assessment

Pavelić is widely regarded as a central perpetrator in genocide and mass atrocities in the Balkans during World War II. The memory of the Ustasha regime remains a source of political tension and historical debate in the region.
