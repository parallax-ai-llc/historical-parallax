## Summary

Alan Watts was a British-American philosopher and writer who was a major interpreter of Eastern philosophy for Western audiences. His books, including The Way of Zen and The Book: On the Taboo Against Knowing Who You Are, introduced millions to Zen Buddhism, Taoism, and Hindu philosophy. He was a key figure in the San Francisco counterculture of the 1960s. His recorded lectures continue to attract large audiences online.
