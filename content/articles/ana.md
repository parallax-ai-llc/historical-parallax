## Summary

Anathan "ana" Pham is an Australian professional Dota 2 player widely regarded as one of the greatest carry players in the history of the game. Born on June 29, 1999, in Melbourne, Australia, to Vietnamese parents, he rose to international fame as the position 1 player for OG, the only organization to win The International twice — doing so in consecutive years in 2018 and 2019.

Ana's playing style is defined by extraordinary composure under pressure, aggressive yet calculated decision-making, and an ability to deliver game-winning performances on the biggest stage. His back-to-back International victories cemented his legacy as arguably the most clutch player in competitive Dota 2 history, and his story became emblematic of OG's underdog narrative that captivated the esports world.

## Early Life & Background

Anathan Pham was born on June 29, 1999, in Melbourne, Australia, to parents of Vietnamese descent. He grew up playing Dota 2 from a young age and quickly demonstrated exceptional mechanical skill and game sense. By his mid-teens, ana had already begun competing in the Australian Dota 2 scene, catching the attention of regional teams with his prodigious talent on carry heroes.

In 2016, at just 16 years old, ana relocated to China to join Invictus Gaming as a mid-lane player, marking his entry into the professional tier-one scene. The move was a significant leap for the young Australian, requiring him to adapt to a new country, language, and competitive environment.

## Rise to Prominence

After a brief stint with Invictus Gaming, ana joined OG in August 2016, initially playing the mid-lane role. His first major achievement came at the Boston Major in December 2016, where OG claimed the championship. He also helped OG win the Kiev Major in April 2017, establishing the squad as one of the era's dominant teams.

Ana took a break from competitive play in late 2017, stepping away from the professional scene. He returned to OG in mid-2018 at a critical juncture — the team had lost most of its roster and was scrambling to rebuild ahead of The International 2018 (TI8). Alongside teammates N0tail, JerAx, Ceb, and Topson, ana formed a squad that few expected to contend for the championship.

## Key Achievements

At TI8, held in Vancouver in August 2018, OG entered as the lowest-seeded team in the upper bracket. Ana's performances on heroes like Ember Spirit, Phantom Lancer, and Spectre were pivotal as OG completed a historic lower bracket run to claim the Aegis of Champions and the first-place prize of approximately $11.2 million.

One year later at TI9 in Shanghai in August 2019, OG became the first and only team to win The International twice, and they did so without dropping a single series in the main event. Ana's iconic Io carry strategy in the grand finals against Team Liquid became one of the most memorable moments in Dota 2 history. The victory earned the team approximately $15.6 million, bringing ana's combined TI winnings to over $6 million personally.

Ana's other notable achievements include two Valve Major victories (Boston Major 2016, Kiev Major 2017) and consistent top-tier performances across multiple competitive seasons.

## Controversies & Criticism

Ana's career has been marked more by absences than controversies. His repeated breaks from competitive play — stepping away after major victories — drew criticism from some fans and analysts who questioned his commitment to the game. Others, however, respected his approach to maintaining mental health and avoiding burnout in a demanding esports environment.

His relatively quiet public persona and infrequent streaming or social media activity also led to limited fan engagement compared to other players of his stature, though this added to his mystique as a player who let his in-game performances speak for themselves.

## Legacy & Influence

Ana's legacy in Dota 2 is defined by his unparalleled record at The International. As a two-time TI champion, he holds a distinction matched by only a handful of players in the game's history, and his consecutive victories remain a unique achievement. His willingness to step away from the game at the peak of his career challenged conventional expectations in esports about continuous competition and grinding.

His playing style — patient farming, exceptional teamfight positioning, and a fearless approach to high-pressure moments — influenced a generation of carry players. Ana demonstrated that performing at one's best when it matters most can be more valuable than year-round consistency, leaving an enduring mark on how greatness is measured in competitive Dota 2.
