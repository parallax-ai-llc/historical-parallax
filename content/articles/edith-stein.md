## Summary

Edith Stein was a German Jewish philosopher who converted to Catholicism and became a Carmelite nun. She studied under Edmund Husserl and made important contributions to phenomenology. After entering the Carmelite order as Sister Teresa Benedicta of the Cross, she was arrested by the Nazis and murdered at Auschwitz. She was canonized in 1998 as a martyr.
