## Summary

Zhou Peiyuan was a Chinese theoretical physicist who made contributions to general relativity, fluid mechanics, and turbulence theory. He studied under Einstein's collaborator at Caltech and returned to China where he served as president of Peking University. He was one of the founders of modern physics education in China.
