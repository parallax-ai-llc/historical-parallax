## Summary

21 Savage (born Shéyaa Bin Abraham-Joseph on October 22, 1992, in London, England) is a British-American rapper who became one of the defining voices of Atlanta trap music in the mid-2010s. Raised in Atlanta's Eastside from the age of seven, he built a reputation for stark, deadpan lyricism rooted in the realities of street life. His collaborative mixtape *Savage Mode* (2016) with producer Metro Boomin established him as a major force in hip-hop, and his subsequent commercial breakthroughs — including the Billboard Hot 100 number-one single "Rockstar" and the Grammy-winning "A Lot" — cemented his place among rap's elite. In 2019, a high-profile arrest by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement revealed that he was a British citizen who had overstayed his visa, igniting a national debate about immigration policy. Beyond music, 21 Savage is known for his financial literacy advocacy and community outreach in Atlanta.

## Early Life & Background

Shéyaa Bin Abraham-Joseph was born in Plaistow, a district in the London Borough of Newham, to a British mother and a Dominica-born father. When he was seven years old, his family relocated to Atlanta, Georgia, where he grew up in the Eastside neighborhoods of Decatur and Zone 6. His adolescence was marked by violence: he witnessed the shooting death of his close friend Johnny on his 21st birthday, and in October 2013 he himself was shot six times in a drive-by attack that killed another friend, Johnny Hernandez. The trauma of that period profoundly shaped his music's unflinching worldview.

## Rise to Prominence

21 Savage began releasing mixtapes in Atlanta's underground circuit around 2014. His collaborative project *Savage Mode* (2016), produced almost entirely by Metro Boomin, broke through to mainstream attention with its minimalist, ominous sound and uncompromising subject matter. The project was certified platinum and is widely credited with influencing a generation of trap artists.

His profile skyrocketed in 2017 when "Rockstar," a collaboration with Post Malone from the album *beerbongs & bentleys*, spent eight weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100 and was certified diamond. His debut studio album *Issa Album* followed that same year. The sequel *Savage Mode II* (2020), again with Metro Boomin, debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, broke the record for the most weeks spent at number one by a rap album in 2020, and featured the cultural touchstone narration of Morgan Freeman.

## Key Achievements

- *Savage Mode* (2016) certified platinum; named one of the best albums of the decade by multiple publications
- "Rockstar" with Post Malone reached number one on the Billboard Hot 100 (2017)
- Won **Grammy Award for Best Rap Song** for "A Lot" featuring J. Cole (2020 ceremony)
- *Savage Mode II* (2020) debuted at number one on the Billboard 200
- Appeared on *Forbes* lists of highest-earning hip-hop artists multiple times
- Launched the **Bank Account** financial literacy program, distributing resources to students in Atlanta public schools
- Named a 2020 Rolling Stone cover subject; featured in a *New York Times* op-ed about his immigration case

## The 2019 ICE Arrest & Immigration Controversy

On February 3, 2019 — Super Bowl weekend in Atlanta — 21 Savage was arrested by U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement agents. Authorities alleged he was a British national who had entered the United States legally on a visa in July 2005 but had failed to depart when it expired in July 2006. The arrest shocked fans who had assumed he was American, given his entirely Atlanta-rooted public identity.

His legal team, led by attorney Charles Kuck, argued that he had applied for a U visa (available to crime victims) in 2017 following his 2013 shooting, and that he had existing legal pathways to residency. Critics — including civil liberties groups and politicians — pointed to the arrest as a politically motivated act designed to make an example of a high-profile figure during a period of heightened immigration enforcement. He was held for nine days before being released on bond. After years of legal proceedings, he was granted a U visa and lawful permanent resident status in the United States in 2023.

## Community Activism

21 Savage has consistently directed resources back to Atlanta. His **Bank Account** campaign, launched in partnership with the non-profit Digit, provides free financial literacy workshops and savings tools to teenagers. He has donated school supplies to Atlanta-area students, funded Thanksgiving meal drives, and covered funeral costs for families in his community. Following the 2019 arrest, he publicly channeled attention toward broader immigration reform advocacy, speaking about the vulnerabilities faced by undocumented people across all backgrounds.

## Legacy & Influence

21 Savage's impact on trap music extends well beyond his own catalog. His sparse, conversational delivery — deliberately stripped of melodic flourishes — became a widely imitated blueprint during the late 2010s, influencing artists from Roddy Ricch to Polo G. His willingness to rap about trauma, grief, and survival with clinical directness helped shift the emotional register of mainstream hip-hop. The *Savage Mode* series stands as one of the most critically acclaimed rap projects of its era, and his Grammy victory for "A Lot" — a song meditating on Black generational trauma and material success — was recognized as one of the more substantive wins of that year's ceremony.
