## Scientific Significance (High Level)

Chandrasekhar’s theoretical work is central to modern astrophysics, particularly on compact stellar remnants and the physical principles governing stellar evolution.

- **Chandrasekhar limit**: Upper mass limit for stable white dwarfs supported by electron degeneracy pressure.
- **Stellar structure and stability**: Rigorous mathematical analyses of equilibrium, radiative transfer, and hydrodynamic instabilities.
- **Compact objects**: Foundational contributions that informed later understanding of neutron stars, black holes, and supernova outcomes.

## Recognition

He shared the **1983 Nobel Prize in Physics** for theoretical studies of the physical processes important to the structure and evolution of stars. The award is often cited as recognition of the long-term impact of his mathematical approach to astrophysical problems.
