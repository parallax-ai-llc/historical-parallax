## Summary

Saadi Shirazi was a Persian poet and prose writer regarded as one of the greatest figures of classical Persian literature. His Gulistan (Rose Garden) and Bustan (Orchard) combine poetry with moral and practical wisdom. His works are known for their elegant style, humanistic ethics, and Sufi sensibility. His famous verse on the interconnectedness of humanity adorns the entrance to the United Nations.
