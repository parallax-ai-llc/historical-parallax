## Background

- Manuel Antonio Noriega was a Panamanian military officer who became the country’s de facto leader in the 1980s.
- He rose within Panama’s security and intelligence apparatus during a period in which the military played an outsized role in politics.
- English-language sources commonly describe him as an authoritarian ruler, and later as a convicted drug trafficker in U.S. courts.

## Rise to Power (high level)

- Noriega served in the Panamanian National Guard and held influential intelligence-related positions.
- After the death of Panama’s leader Omar Torrijos (1981), internal military politics intensified.
- By the mid-1980s, Noriega had consolidated control over Panama’s military and exercised decisive influence over the civilian government.

## Relationship with the United States (overview)

- Reporting in major historical accounts emphasizes that Noriega had a long, complex relationship with U.S. agencies and policy.
- Over time, U.S.–Panama relations deteriorated amid allegations involving corruption, repression, and illicit trafficking.
- The breakdown culminated in open confrontation at the end of the 1980s.

## Drug Trafficking Allegations and Indictment

- U.S. authorities accused Noriega of facilitating cocaine trafficking and related criminal activity.
- He was indicted in the United States (late 1980s), becoming one of the most prominent foreign leaders charged in U.S. courts.
- These allegations became central to diplomatic tensions and later legal proceedings.

## 1989 U.S. Invasion and Capture

- In December 1989, the United States launched an invasion of Panama (Operation Just Cause).
- Noriega went into hiding and later sought refuge, before surrendering to U.S. forces in early 1990.
- He was transferred to the United States to face trial.

## Trial, Conviction, and Imprisonment

- Noriega was tried in U.S. federal court and convicted on drug-related charges.
- He served a substantial prison sentence in the United States.
- After U.S. imprisonment, he was transferred to other jurisdictions where additional legal cases awaited.

## Later Years and Death

- Noriega spent later years in custody and faced prosecutions outside the United States.
- He died in 2017.

## Notable Themes (for historical context)

- Civil–military relations: Panama’s late-20th-century politics were heavily shaped by military power.
- Sovereignty and intervention: the 1989 invasion remains a major reference point in debates over U.S. intervention in Latin America.
- Crime and state power: his case is frequently cited in discussions of links between security services, corruption, and transnational narcotics networks.
