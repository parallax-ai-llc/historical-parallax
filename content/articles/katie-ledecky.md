## Summary

Katie Ledecky is the most decorated female Olympic swimmer, dominating distance freestyle since winning gold at London 2012 at age 15.

## Specialties and strengths

- Ledecky is best known for distance freestyle events, where pacing discipline and aerobic endurance are decisive.
- She is often described as winning by building leads steadily rather than relying on a late sprint.
- Across multiple cycles, she has also been prominent in middle-distance freestyle and relay contributions.

## Training and approach (high level)

- Elite distance swimming typically involves high training volume, careful technique work, and precise race-pace sets; Ledecky's dominance is often attributed to consistency in these areas.
- Commentators frequently note her ability to maintain efficient stroke mechanics deep into long races.
- Her longevity at the top level is also associated with adapting to changes in competition and continuing incremental improvement.

## Impact

- She is widely credited with raising expectations for women's distance freestyle performance and inspiring a generation of young swimmers.
- Her medal totals and records are commonly used as benchmarks when discussing modern Olympic-era swimming achievements.
