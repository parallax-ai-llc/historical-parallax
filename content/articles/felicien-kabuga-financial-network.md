## Summary

Félicien Kabuga's financial network provided the infrastructure for the 1994 Rwandan genocide, importing 500,000 machetes and funding hate radio RTLM. He evaded justice for 26 years using false identities across Africa and Europe.

## Additional Context

- **Nature of allegations:** International indictments alleged that Kabuga used business resources and procurement channels to support militia logistics and propaganda during the genocide.
- **Financing and logistics:** In genocide studies, the Kabuga case is often cited to illustrate how mass violence can rely on ordinary commercial mechanisms (imports, financing, and media ownership) rather than only direct battlefield command.
- **Evasion:** Long periods as a fugitive are commonly attributed to a combination of false identities, international mobility, and the practical difficulty of coordinating cross-border investigations.

## Judicial Process

- **International tribunal lineage:** The case moved through successor mechanisms to the ICTR, reflecting how international criminal cases can span decades.
- **Health and trial capacity:** Public reporting emphasized questions of health and fitness to stand trial as key procedural issues after arrest.
