## Summary

Ashton Eaton won consecutive Olympic decathlon golds (2012, 2016) and set the WR of 9,045 points.

## Athletic Profile

- Primary discipline: **decathlon**, a two-day combined event covering sprints, jumps, throws, and middle-distance running.
- Noted for rare balance across speed events (100 m / 400 m), jumping (long jump / pole vault), and endurance (1500 m), which is essential for elite decathlon scoring.

## Major Achievements (High-Level)

- Multiple-time global champion, with Olympic titles in 2012 and 2016.
- Set a world-record score that represented sustained excellence across all ten events rather than dominance in a single discipline.

## Why He Matters

Eaton’s performances helped raise mainstream visibility of the decathlon and provided a modern benchmark for “complete” track-and-field athleticism.

## Notes on Scoring

Decathlon points are awarded via event-specific formulas; improvements in technically demanding events (notably pole vault and javelin) can create outsized gains in total score compared with small improvements in already-strong sprint marks.
