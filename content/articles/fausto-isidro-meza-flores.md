## Criminal organization context

After the breakup of larger Mexican trafficking organizations, regional remnants and splinter factions often compete for:

- Control of production corridors and export routes
- Access to ports, border crossings, and logistics networks
- Local protection arrangements and influence over municipal policing

In Sinaloa and neighboring states, these dynamics have historically contributed to shifting alliances and cycles of violence.

## Alleged activities (high level)

Reporting on Meza Flores typically describes him as involved in:

- Trafficking of narcotics through northwestern Mexico
- Management of armed enforcers and territorial control
- Coordination with corruption networks that facilitate movement and evasion

Because many details come from security reporting and court filings, public accounts can vary in specificity.

## Manhunt and enforcement

Most wanted designations in Mexico and the United States generally reflect a combination of factors:

- Perceived leadership role within a criminal group
- Links to cross-border trafficking and violence
- Intelligence indicating active operational control

Such designations can include reward offers and targeted operations, though outcomes differ widely depending on local conditions.

## Broader significance

Cases like Meza Floress are frequently cited in analysis of Mexican organized crime because they illustrate:

- How cartel fragmentation can decentralize but not eliminate trafficking capacity
- The importance of regional commanders to sustaining routes and markets
- The persistence of violence risks even after high-profile cartel leaders are removed
