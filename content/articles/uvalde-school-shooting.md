## Summary

Mass shooting at Robb Elementary School in Uvalde, Texas on May 24, 2022 killing 21 people including 19 children. Police response was widely criticized for delays.

## Incident overview

The attack occurred at Robb Elementary School and became one of the deadliest school shootings in U.S. history.
The perpetrator was killed by law enforcement at the scene.

## Response and investigations

The law-enforcement response drew intense scrutiny, including questions about command structure, communications, and the timing of the eventual breach of the classroom.
Multiple investigations and reports were undertaken by state bodies, legislative committees, and media organizations.

## Impact on victims and community

The shooting had long-term effects on surviving students, families, and the Uvalde community, including trauma support needs, memorialization, and litigation.

## Policy debate

The event became part of wider U.S. debates over:

- Firearms access and regulation
- School security and emergency-response training
- Coordination between local, state, and federal agencies during active-shooter incidents

## Public accountability

In the aftermath, several officials faced resignations, discipline, or electoral consequences, while families and advocates continued to press for transparency about decision-making during the response.
