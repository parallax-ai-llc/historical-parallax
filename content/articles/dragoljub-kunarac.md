## Summary

Bosnian Serb commander convicted of rape and enslavement of Bosniak women in Foca. First ICTY conviction of rape as crime against humanity.

## Context

The crimes associated with Kunarac occurred during the Bosnian War, in and around Foča, a town that saw widespread persecution of Bosniak civilians after Bosnian Serb forces took control in 1992.

## ICTY Case Significance

The Kunarac case is often cited in discussions of international criminal law because it:

- Treated systematic sexual violence as a central part of the charged conduct rather than a peripheral offense.
- Helped clarify how enslavement can be established through control, coercion, and deprivation of liberty.
- Contributed to the ICTY’s jurisprudence on rape and sexual enslavement as crimes against humanity.

## Aftermath and Legacy

- The judgment is referenced in later international cases dealing with conflict-related sexual violence.
- The case is frequently discussed in human-rights documentation as an example of prosecuting sexual crimes in war.
