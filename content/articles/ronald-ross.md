## Summary

Ronald Ross discovered that malaria parasites were transmitted by Anopheles mosquitoes, a breakthrough made on August 20, 1897, later celebrated as Mosquito Day. He received the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1902 for this discovery. His mathematical models of malaria transmission founded the field of epidemiological modeling.
