## Summary

Hildegard of Bingen was a German Benedictine abbess and polymath who wrote extensively on natural history and medicine. Her works Physica and Causae et Curae documented the medicinal properties of plants, animals, and minerals, making her one of the most important medical writers of the medieval period. She also composed music and wrote on theology.
