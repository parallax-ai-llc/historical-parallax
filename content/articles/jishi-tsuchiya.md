## Summary

Aum Shinrikyo member who helped manufacture sarin gas used in the 1995 Tokyo subway attack.

## Background

Tsuchiya trained as a chemist and became part of Aum Shinrikyo’s technical cadre, which pursued a mix of religious messaging and clandestine weapons programs in the early 1990s.[^1]

## Role in Aum Shinrikyo

Within Aum’s internal structure, Tsuchiya was associated with efforts to develop and produce chemical agents. Reporting on the Tokyo subway attack consistently describes him as one of the members involved in the production of sarin, the nerve agent deployed on 20 March 1995 on multiple Tokyo Metro lines.[^1]

## Criminal Case

After the 1995 attacks and the subsequent crackdown on Aum Shinrikyo, Tsuchiya was arrested and prosecuted in Japan. He was convicted for his involvement in the organization’s chemical weapons activities connected to the subway attack.[^1]

Japan’s courts ultimately upheld a death sentence in the Aum-related cases, reflecting the scale of casualties and the premeditated nature of the chemical attack.[^1]

## Death

Tsuchiya was executed in July 2018 during a set of executions that included multiple senior Aum Shinrikyo figures convicted for the 1995 subway sarin attack and other crimes.[^1]
