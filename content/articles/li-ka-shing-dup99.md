## Early Life and Background

- Born in 1928 in Chaozhou (Teochew), Guangdong, China.
- Moved to Hong Kong as a refugee during the Second Sino-Japanese War period (often cited as early 1940s).
- Left school early and worked in manufacturing and sales; frequently described as having started in a plastics-related business before building larger enterprises.

## Business Empire (High-Level)

- Best known as the long-time chairman of **Cheung Kong (Holdings)** and **Hutchison Whampoa** (later reorganized under **CK Hutchison Holdings**).
- Built a diversified conglomerate spanning:
  - **Property development** and real estate
  - **Ports** and logistics
  - **Retail** (e.g., Watsons-related businesses under the group)
  - **Telecommunications** (multiple mobile network assets in Europe and Asia over time)
  - **Infrastructure/energy** investments

## Major Corporate Reorganizations

- In 2015, the group executed a major reorganization creating:
  - **CK Hutchison Holdings** (a global conglomerate)
  - **CK Asset Holdings** (property-related assets)
- The restructuring is commonly described as part of modernization and global diversification of the corporate structure.

## Philanthropy

- Founder of the **Li Ka Shing Foundation**.
- Noted for large-scale donations to education and medical research, with significant contributions to universities in Hong Kong and overseas.
- Philanthropy is frequently framed around long-term institution-building rather than one-off relief.

## Public Image and Influence

- Often described in media as one of Asia’s wealthiest individuals and a symbolic figure of Hong Kong’s postwar business growth.
- Considered influential in regional business due to the scale of holdings and the breadth of international investments.
