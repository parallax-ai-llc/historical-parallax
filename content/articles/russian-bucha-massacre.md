## Summary

Russian forces occupying Bucha, Ukraine killed hundreds of civilians through executions, torture, and sexual violence. Satellite images and forensic evidence documented the atrocities.

## Additional Context

### Discovery and documentation

- Reports and imagery became widely public after Russian forces withdrew and Ukrainian authorities and journalists entered the area.
- Documentation has included **photos/video**, **satellite imagery**, witness testimony, and subsequent forensic and investigative reporting.

### Investigations and accountability

- Ukraine and international bodies framed the events within broader allegations of **war crimes** during the invasion.
- Public discussion has focused on chains of command, unit identification, and standards of proof needed for prosecutions.

### Competing narratives

- Russian state sources denied responsibility and promoted alternative explanations; multiple independent investigations and open-source analyses examined timelines and evidence.

### Why it matters

- Became a defining episode shaping global perceptions of the invasion and influencing diplomatic, military, and sanctions policy debates.
