## Summary

Tim Berners-Lee publicly launched the World Wide Web on August 6, 1991, when the first website went live at CERN. His invention of HTML, URLs, and HTTP transformed the internet from an academic tool into a global information platform.

## Additional Context

## What “Launch” Commonly Means

The “launch” of the World Wide Web is often described in terms of when the first website and server became accessible and when the underlying web technology began to be shared beyond its initial institutional context.

## Early Milestones (High Level)

- **First web server and site:** early public demonstrations of a web page served via HTTP.
- **Browser and editor tools:** software that made it practical to create and navigate linked documents.
- **Standards and openness:** publication and permission to use the technology broadly were key to adoption.

## Why It Matters

The web’s launch created a standardized, hyperlink‑based way to publish and retrieve information, enabling new forms of communication, commerce, and knowledge sharing. Over time, it became foundational to modern digital life.

## Relationship to Later Growth

Widespread public use expanded alongside improved browsers, search, and increased connectivity, but the initial launch period established the basic architecture that persists today.
