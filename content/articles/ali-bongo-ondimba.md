## Summary

Gabonese president who succeeded his father in 2009, continuing the Bongo dynasty. He won disputed elections in 2016 sparking violence. Overthrown in a military coup in 2023 after 14 years in power.

## Additional Context

### Political background

Ali Bongo Ondimba entered national politics in a state dominated for decades by the Bongo family and the Gabonese Democratic Party. He became president in 2009 after the death of his father, Omar Bongo, whose long rule shaped Gabon’s post‑independence political system.

### Elections and unrest

Bongo’s presidency included elections that were disputed by opposition parties and international observers. The 2016 election in particular led to protests and violence, reflecting broader tensions over electoral legitimacy and the concentration of political power.

### Health and governance questions

After a stroke in 2018, his public appearances and governing capacity became subjects of domestic debate. In many accounts, this period is described as increasing uncertainty inside the political elite and security services.

### 2023 coup

In 2023, senior military officers announced a coup following an election, ending Bongo’s rule. The event is commonly framed as part of a wider pattern of coups in parts of West and Central Africa during the early 2020s.
