## Summary

Bosnian Croat military commander convicted by the ICTY for his role in attacks on Bosniak civilians in the Lašva Valley during the Bosnian War. Initially sentenced to 45 years, reduced to 9 on appeal.

## Role in the Bosnian War

Blaškić is most commonly described as a senior commander in the Croatian Defence Council (HVO) operating in central Bosnia. The Lašva Valley became a focal point of Croat–Bosniak fighting in 1993, and multiple incidents in the area were later investigated and prosecuted as violations of the laws and customs of war.

## ICTY Proceedings

The International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) prosecuted a number of cases related to violence in central Bosnia, including charges involving attacks on civilians, unlawful treatment, and destruction of property. In Blaškić’s case, the trial judgment resulted in a long prison sentence that was later substantially reduced on appeal.

## Appeal and Sentencing Outcome

The appellate proceedings are often summarized as turning on questions of command responsibility, evidentiary issues, and the extent of effective control over perpetrators. The final sentence reduction made the case one of the more notable examples of how ICTY appeals could change both factual findings and legal characterizations.

## Legacy

Discussions of the case frequently appear in broader debates about accountability during the Bosnian War, the standards applied by international tribunals, and the challenges of attributing responsibility in fragmented conflict environments with overlapping chains of command.
