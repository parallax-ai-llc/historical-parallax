## Summary

Dutch filmmaker murdered by an Islamist extremist for making a film critical of Islam's treatment of women.

## Background

- Theo van Gogh (a distant relative of painter Vincent van Gogh) worked across film, television, and print commentary in the Netherlands.
- He became known for provocative, polemical public speech and for targeting what he saw as hypocrisy in politics and religion.

## Notable Work

- *Submission* (2004): a short film made with politician Ayaan Hirsi Ali that criticized the treatment of women under certain interpretations of Islam.
- The film and related commentary drew intense public attention and threats, becoming a flashpoint in wider debates about free expression and multiculturalism.

## Murder (2004)

- On 2 November 2004, van Gogh was attacked and killed in Amsterdam.
- The perpetrator, Mohammed Bouyeri, was arrested shortly after the attack.
- The murder was widely treated as an act of political/religious violence tied to van Gogh’s speech and filmmaking.

## Aftermath and Significance

- The killing intensified Dutch and European debates over:
  - freedom of expression vs. incitement and hate speech
  - integration policy and community cohesion
  - threats to artists, journalists, and public intellectuals
- It also prompted scrutiny of security and threat assessment for high-profile cultural and political figures.
