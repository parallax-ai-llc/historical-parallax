## Summary

Raiden Tameemon was one of the greatest sumo wrestlers in history with a 254-10 record, an incredible 96.2% winning percentage across 21 years. He was never promoted to yokozuna.

## Additional Context

### Edo-Period Sumo Setting

This wrestler’s career is typically discussed in the context of late Edo-period professional sumo, when tournaments and ranking systems were becoming more formalized and top wrestlers could develop national reputations.

### Records and Rankings

Historical sumo records are often presented with caveats because:

- match documentation practices varied over time,
- some bouts were not recorded consistently across venues,
- the modern “yokozuna” promotion system did not operate exactly the same way in earlier eras.

### Style and Legacy (High-Level)

Accounts describing his dominance emphasize physical strength and an ability to control bouts quickly. The frequent mention that he was not promoted to yokozuna despite an extraordinary record is commonly used to illustrate how rank and promotion could be influenced by factors beyond win–loss totals alone.

### Note on Naming

Sumo wrestlers may be associated with ring names (shikona) and other names across different stages of life. Sources may differ in how they present names in English-language summaries.
