## Summary

French citizen of Moroccan descent known as the '20th hijacker' who was arrested before the 9/11 attacks while attending flight school. He pleaded guilty to conspiracy charges and is serving six life sentences at ADX Florence.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|

| 2005 | Pleaded guilty to conspiracy |
| 2006 | Sentenced to six consecutive life terms |

## Background

- Moussaoui’s case drew intense attention because he was detained before September 11, 2001, and later prosecuted in connection with the broader plot.
- The “20th hijacker” label is a media shorthand; his exact intended role has been debated in public reporting and legal filings over time.

## Legal Proceedings

- The prosecution focused on conspiracy charges tied to al-Qaeda and the September 11 attacks.
- The case included disputes over access to classified information and witness testimony, reflecting the national-security context of the trial.
- He ultimately received multiple life sentences and remains incarcerated in the U.S. federal system.

## Significance

- The case is commonly cited in post‑9/11 discussions about intelligence sharing, investigative authority, and how early warning signs are handled.
- It is also referenced in debates over terrorism prosecutions in civilian courts versus alternative legal venues.

## Key Takeaways

- The pre‑9/11 arrest made the case central to later reviews of investigative “connect-the-dots” failures.
- It illustrates how terrorism cases can hinge on procedural questions as much as on the underlying allegations.
- The “20th hijacker” framing shows how media labels can shape public memory of complex events.
