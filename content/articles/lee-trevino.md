## Summary

Lee Trevino won six major championships and was known for his quick wit and self-taught swing. He was struck by lightning during a tournament and still continued his career.

## Context and Significance

- Trevino is widely associated with the modern era of major championship golf, winning at the U.S. Open, The Open Championship, and the PGA Championship.
- His public persona—humor, candor, and an underdog narrative—made him one of the sport’s most recognizable figures beyond tournament results.

## Playing Style

- Trevino was known for a self-taught approach and an emphasis on shot-shaping and controlling ball flight.
- He was frequently described as favoring a reliable, repeatable motion over aesthetic technique, and he became famous for quips about the “feel” of the swing.

## Lightning Strike Incident (1975)

- In 1975, Trevino was struck by lightning during the Western Open, an incident often cited as one of the most unusual episodes involving an elite athlete in competition.
- He later returned to professional play, and the event became part of his public biography as much as his wins.

## Legacy

- Trevino’s career is often used as an example of late entry into elite sport and sustained performance at the highest level.
- He remains a reference point in golf media for competitor personality: a champion who combined results with a distinctive voice in interviews and appearances.
