## Summary

Lars von Trier is a Danish filmmaker known for his provocative, emotionally raw films that push audience boundaries. He co-founded the Dogme 95 movement and created controversial masterworks including Breaking the Waves, Dancer in the Dark, and Melancholia. His confrontational approach and psychological intensity make him one of cinema's most polarizing figures.
