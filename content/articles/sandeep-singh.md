## Summary

Sandeep Singh was an Indian field hockey player known for his powerful drag flicks. He returned to international hockey after being accidentally shot and partially paralyzed.

## Playing style

Singh is most associated with penalty-corner conversion, particularly the drag-flick technique.
Drag-flick specialists can generate high ball speed and late direction changes, making them important in modern field hockey set pieces.
Because penalty corners can decide close matches, players with this skill are often central to team tactics.

## Injury and recovery

A major theme in profiles of Singh is his recovery after a shooting incident that caused serious injury.
His return to top-level competition has been described as unusual in Indian hockey and is frequently framed as a resilience story.

## International career context

India’s men’s hockey program has had periods of transition since its earlier era of dominance, with changing coaching and competition structures.
Singh’s career is commonly situated within this rebuilding period, when teams sought consistent goal-scoring from penalty corners.

## Recognition

Beyond match statistics, Singh is referenced in Indian sports media as an example of a specialist whose skill set can alter game plans.
His captaincy roles also indicate a level of trust placed in him by selectors and teammates.
He is often listed among notable Indian defenders or drag-flickers of his generation.
