## Additional Details

### Background

- Peter Lundin is described by Wikipedia as a Danish-American offender who was active in both the United States and Denmark.[^1]
- In U.S. coverage referenced by Wikipedia, he was convicted for killing his mother in Florida in the early 1990s and later returned to Denmark.[^1]

### Denmark Murders (2000)

- Wikipedia reports that in 2000 he murdered a woman (Marianne Galas) and her two sons in Denmark.[^1]
- The case is commonly summarized as a triple homicide involving an adult victim and two children.[^1]

### Investigation and Trial (high-level)

- Accounts summarized by Wikipedia indicate the investigation centered on Lundin as a suspect after the victims disappeared and were later found deceased.[^1]
- He was convicted in Denmark and sentenced to life imprisonment (as reported by Wikipedia).[^1]

### Impact / Aftermath

- The case is frequently cited in Danish true-crime coverage due to its cross-border background (U.S. prior conviction) and the nature of the 2000 killings.[^1]
- Public discussion also focuses on how prior criminal history and international movement intersected with the later Danish case.[^1]

### Key Points (quick facts)

- **Nationality:** Danish (with U.S. history described in sources).[^1]
- **Known for:** The 2000 Denmark triple homicide; earlier U.S. matricide conviction per Wikipedia.[^1]
- **Sentence:** Life imprisonment in Denmark (per Wikipedia).[^1]
