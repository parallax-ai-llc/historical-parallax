## Summary

Kenzo Tange was a Japanese architect who won the Pritzker Prize in 1987 and was a pioneer of modernist architecture in Japan. While primarily an architect, his engineering innovations in structural design, including the dramatic hyperbolic paraboloid roofs of the Yoyogi National Gymnasium for the 1964 Tokyo Olympics, advanced structural engineering technology.
