## Background

Qais al-Khazali is an Iraqi Shia militia leader best known for heading Asa'ib Ahl al-Haq (AAH), a group that emerged during the post-2003 insurgency period. He is often discussed in the context of Iraqi militia politics, sectarian conflict, and regional influence networks.

## Asa'ib Ahl al-Haq (AAH)

AAH has been described in public reporting as both an armed organization and, over time, a political actor with influence in Iraqi institutions. Analyses of the group frequently focus on its role in violence during the Iraq War era and its later participation in broader militia coalitions.

## Detention and release

Al-Khazali was detained by U.S.-led forces and later released as part of a prisoner exchange, an episode that remains notable in narratives about hostage diplomacy and conflict-era bargaining.

## Political and regional role

In later years, al-Khazali and AAH have been associated with debates over the role of armed groups in the Iraqi state, the balance of power between formal security forces and paramilitaries, and the influence of external patrons.

He and/or associated entities have been subject in some contexts to sanctions or designations by foreign governments, generally tied to allegations involving human-rights abuses or destabilizing activities. The specifics depend on the issuing authority and the time period.
