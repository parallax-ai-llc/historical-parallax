## Summary

German photographer who won the Turner Prize in 2000, the first photographer and first non-British artist to do so. His democratic approach to photography mixes casual snapshots, abstract images, and political activism. He has become one of the most influential artists of his generation.

## Additional Context

## Why He Matters

Tillmans is often cited for expanding what counts as fine‑art photography, treating everyday images, prints, and installations as part of the same visual language. His work helped normalize the idea that a photograph can be both documentary and formally experimental within a single practice.

## Typical Themes and Approach

- **Everyday observation:** casual moments, friends, interiors, and travel scenes presented without heavy dramatization.
- **Abstraction and process:** darkroom experiments and technique‑forward images that emphasize surface, light, and color.
- **Installation as editing:** prints of different scales are frequently displayed in groupings to suggest relationships rather than a single linear narrative.
- **Public discourse:** an interest in civic participation and contemporary politics, including pro‑EU messaging in public campaigns.

## Legacy (High Level)

His influence is frequently discussed in relation to photography’s move toward mixed formats (magazines, posters, gallery prints) and the acceptance of “democratic” image hierarchies where a snapshot and a monumental print can carry comparable weight.
