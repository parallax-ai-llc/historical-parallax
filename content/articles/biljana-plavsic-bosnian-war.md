## Summary

Former biology professor and Bosnian Serb leader who called Bosniaks a genetic defect and pleaded guilty to persecution during the Bosnian War. She served time in a Swedish prison.

## Political Role

- Plavšić was a prominent Bosnian Serb political figure during and after the breakup of Yugoslavia.
- She held senior positions within Republika Srpska leadership structures and later served as President of Republika Srpska in the post-war period.

## ICTY Proceedings

- She was indicted by the International Criminal Tribunal for the former Yugoslavia (ICTY) on charges related to crimes against humanity.
- In 2003, she entered a guilty plea to persecution as a crime against humanity; other counts were withdrawn as part of the plea arrangement.
- The ICTY sentenced her to a term of imprisonment (commonly reported as 11 years), and she served her sentence in Sweden.

## Release and Public Debate

- She was released before completing the full sentence under the applicable release rules.
- Her case is often cited in discussions about plea bargaining, sentencing consistency, and accountability for political leaders in mass-atrocity crimes.

## Historical Significance

- Plavšić’s trajectory—from academic to nationalist political leader to convicted war criminal—has been analyzed in scholarship on elite responsibility and wartime propaganda.
