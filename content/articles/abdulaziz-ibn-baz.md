## Summary

Abdulaziz ibn Baz was the Grand Mufti of Saudi Arabia and one of the most influential Sunni scholars of the 20th century. He served as head of the Council of Senior Scholars and was the leading religious authority in Saudi Arabia for decades. He was known for his conservative Salafi views, his rulings on contemporary issues, and his extensive charitable work. He was blind from a young age but memorized the Quran and became a formidable scholar.
