## Summary

Chey Tae-won is the chairman of SK Group, South Korea's third-largest conglomerate with interests in energy, telecommunications, semiconductors, and biopharmaceuticals. He led SK Hynix's growth into the world's second-largest memory chip manufacturer. He was convicted of embezzlement and sentenced to four years in prison but received a presidential pardon. Under his leadership, SK has invested heavily in electric vehicle batteries and green energy.
