## Summary
Yang Xinhai, the 'Monster Killer,' murdered 67 people and raped 23 women across four Chinese provinces between 1999 and 2003. He broke into homes at night and attacked sleeping families with hammers, axes, and shovels. One of the most prolific serial killers in Chinese history, he was executed in 2004.

## Early Life
Born July 29, 1968, in Zhumadian, Henan, to a poor farming family. He dropped out of school at 17 and became a migrant laborer, serving prison terms for theft and attempted rape. After release from a labor camp in 1999, he began killing. [^1]

## Criminal Career
Between 1999 and 2003, Yang broke into isolated rural homes at night across Henan, Anhui, Shandong, and Hebei provinces. He murdered 67 people and raped 23 women using hammers, axes, and shovels. He moved frequently and changed his appearance. [^2]

## Capture / Downfall
Arrested November 3, 2003, at a karaoke bar after DNA matched across crime scenes. He confessed without remorse, stating 'I had a desire to kill more.' Convicted and executed by lethal injection on February 14, 2004. [^3]

## Positive Perspectives
- Led to development of China's national DNA database [^4]
- Improved inter-provincial police communication
- Spurred investment in rural police infrastructure

## Negative Perspectives
- Murdered 67 people including entire families [^5]
- Raped 23 women during home invasions
- Showed complete lack of remorse
- Targeted vulnerable rural families
- Exposed gaps in inter-provincial law enforcement
