## Summary

15th President of the Philippines (2010–2016), associated with an anti-corruption agenda and economic growth during his term. His administration also faced major crises and controversy, including disaster response and security incidents.

## Political Background

Aquino was part of a prominent political family and rose to national prominence in the post-Marcos democratic period.

His presidency is often interpreted through themes such as:

- institutional reform and governance credibility
- continuity with the "People Power" political legacy
- tensions between technocratic policy goals and coalition politics

## Governance and Policy Themes

High-level areas commonly associated with Aquino's term include:

- anti-corruption initiatives and public procurement reforms
- economic management and efforts to improve investor confidence
- social programs and education-related policies

## Criticism and Controversies

Public debate about his administration frequently points to:

- uneven disaster preparedness and response (including major typhoons)
- security and counter-terrorism controversies
- persistent inequality despite macroeconomic growth

## Post-Presidency and Legacy

After leaving office, Aquino's record remained politically salient in debates about Philippine democracy and the direction of reforms.

Assessments of his legacy often vary by emphasis on:

- growth and governance indicators
- human costs of policy failures or crises
- the broader trajectory of democratic institutions in the 2010s
