## Summary

Indonesian ISIS coordinator who directed the January 2016 Jakarta attacks from Syria using encrypted communications. He was reportedly killed in Syria.

## Background

- Full name reported as **Muhammad Bahrun Naim Anggih Tamtomo**.
- Born in **Pekalongan, Indonesia** and associated in reporting with **Surakarta (Solo)**.
- Described as having an **information technology** background and work experience as a computer technician.

## Role in ISIS-linked networks

- Widely described as an Indonesian militant who traveled to **Syria** and became associated with **ISIS/ISIL**.
- Reporting describes him as using **online platforms and encrypted messaging** to communicate with supporters and coordinate activity remotely.
- Indonesian authorities and media reporting have linked him to efforts to facilitate funding and provide guidance to sympathizers in Indonesia.

## 2016 Jakarta attacks (high-level)

- He is frequently described as an alleged **mastermind/coordinator** connected to the January 2016 attacks in Jakarta.
- Open-source accounts emphasize remote direction and encouragement rather than on-the-ground presence in Indonesia at the time.

## Notes on reported death

The frontmatter death date above reflects one commonly repeated report; other open sources report that he was killed later (e.g., June 2018 in Syria).[^1]
