## Summary

Jenson Button won the 2009 F1 World Championship with Brawn GP in a fairytale season. He won 15 Grand Prix in his career.

## Early life and pathway to Formula One

Button was born in England and progressed through karting and junior single-seater categories.
His rapid rise in the late 1990s made him one of the most prominent British prospects of his generation.
That reputation helped him secure a Formula One seat at an unusually young age.

## Formula One career (high level)

Across his career, Button drove for multiple teams, including Williams, Benetton/Renault, BAR/Honda, Brawn GP, and McLaren.
He is often associated with strong racecraft in changing conditions and with extracting performance through smooth, low-tyre-wear driving.

### 2009 championship season

The 2009 season is widely remembered because Brawn GP entered the championship after a late organizational transition.
Button won several early races and built a points lead that proved decisive over the full season.
His title is frequently described as a “fairytale” outcome because the team’s success exceeded pre-season expectations.

## Wins, podiums, and consistency

Button’s 15 Grand Prix victories reflect both peak performance and longevity.
He was also known for consistency and for converting opportunities when weather, strategy, or safety-car timing reshaped races.

## Later career and post-F1 activity

After stepping back from full-time Formula One competition, Button remained active in motorsport and media.
Like many top drivers, he participated in other series and endurance events while also taking on ambassadorial or commentary roles.

## Legacy

Button is commonly remembered for:

- Winning a world championship in a standout, unexpected season
- A driving style that emphasized smoothness and tyre management
- Longevity across several eras of Formula One technical regulation
