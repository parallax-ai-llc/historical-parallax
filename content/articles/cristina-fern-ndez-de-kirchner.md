## Summary

Argentine former president convicted of corruption for directing public works contracts to a friend. She received a 6-year sentence and lifetime ban from public office. She claimed the charges were politically motivated.

## Political Background

Fernández de Kirchner rose to national prominence within Argentina’s Peronist political tradition and served in multiple elected offices before the presidency. She is also the widow of former president Néstor Kirchner, and their political movement is often referred to as “Kirchnerism” in Argentine politics.

## Presidency (2007–2015)

During her two terms as president, her administrations were associated with an interventionist economic approach and an expanded role for the state in several sectors. Her presidency coincided with high political polarization and frequent disputes between the government, parts of the press, and opposition parties.

## Vice Presidency and Assassination Attempt

After leaving the presidency, she returned to national executive office as vice president in 2019. In 2022, she survived an assassination attempt outside her residence in Buenos Aires; the incident prompted a major political and security response, and the suspect was detained.

## Corruption Conviction and Legal Status

In 2022, a court convicted her in a case involving public works contracts and imposed a prison sentence and a lifetime ban from holding public office, according to widely reported summaries of the ruling. In many jurisdictions, such convictions can remain subject to appeals and final review processes; descriptions of her legal status may therefore depend on the stage of proceedings.

## Public Image and Debate

Supporters often frame the investigations and prosecutions as politically motivated, while critics present them as accountability for corruption. The controversy has made her one of the most debated political figures in contemporary Argentina.
