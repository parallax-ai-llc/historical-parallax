## Summary

Charaka was an ancient Indian physician who is considered the father of Indian medicine (Ayurveda). His text "Charaka Samhita" is one of the foundational texts of Ayurveda, the traditional Indian system of medicine. It describes anatomy, physiology, diagnosis, treatment, and philosophy of healing. The text discusses over 600 drugs and their applications.

## Historical context

Charaka is traditionally associated with the classical period of **Ayurveda**, but details of his life and exact dating are uncertain and largely reconstructed from later references.[^1] Modern scholarship often treats “Charaka” as the name attached to a compilation and redaction tradition rather than a fully documented individual biography.

## The *Charaka Saṃhitā*

The *Charaka Saṃhitā* is typically presented as a systematic medical compendium covering both practical and philosophical aspects of healing.[^1] High-level themes commonly attributed to the work include:

- clinical observation and categorization of disease,
- diet, regimen, and preventive practices,
- pharmacology and the use of plant/mineral/animal-derived substances,
- ethics and the responsibilities of physicians.[^1]

## Influence

Alongside other Ayurvedic classics, the *Charaka Saṃhitā* has had long-term influence on South Asian medical education and on later Sanskrit medical literature.[^1] In contemporary settings it is cited in discussions of traditional medicine, the history of medical systems, and the transmission of scientific and philosophical ideas in the ancient world.

## Notes on interpretation

Because the text has a long transmission history, readers often distinguish between:

- core doctrines associated with early Ayurvedic theory, and
- later additions or commentarial layers reflected in surviving manuscripts.[^1]
