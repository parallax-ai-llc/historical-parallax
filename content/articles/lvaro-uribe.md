## Summary

Colombian former president accused of ties to paramilitaries and drug traffickers. He was investigated for witness tampering and fraud. His father was killed by FARC and he pursued a hardline security policy. Charges were dropped in 2022.

## Political career

Uribe served as President of Colombia from 2002 to 2010. His administration emphasized a "Democratic Security" strategy aimed at reducing the territorial control of insurgent groups such as FARC and improving state presence.

## Security policy and conflict

- Supporters credit his government with reductions in kidnappings and some violence indicators during the 2000s.
- Critics argue that the security approach contributed to human-rights controversies, including the "false positives" scandal in which civilians were killed and falsely presented as combatants.
- His presidency also overlapped with paramilitary demobilization processes, which remain debated in Colombia's transitional-justice history.

## Legal controversies

Uribe has faced multiple investigations and allegations over the years, including matters related to witness tampering. Accounts of his legal status can change with court rulings and prosecutorial decisions; summaries often note that some proceedings were dismissed or closed while public debate continued.

## Legacy

Uribe is one of the most influential and polarizing figures in contemporary Colombian politics, shaping the country's security discourse and party alignments well beyond his presidency.
