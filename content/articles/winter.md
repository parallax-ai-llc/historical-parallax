## Summary

Winter (real name Kim Min-jeong, born January 1, 2001) is a South Korean singer, dancer, and model who rose to fame as a member of the SM Entertainment girl group aespa, which debuted in November 2020. Known for her striking visuals, powerful stage presence, and versatile vocal and dance abilities, she quickly became one of the most recognized fourth-generation K-pop idols globally.

Within months of aespa's debut, Winter had established herself as a standout member, frequently topping popularity polls and earning recognition from industry peers and fans alike. Her dual identity in aespa's metaverse-driven concept — representing both herself and her digital avatar "æ-Winter" — made her a symbol of K-pop's evolution into multimedia storytelling.

Beyond group activities, Winter has built a significant solo profile through modeling campaigns, collaborative projects, and solo releases, cementing her status as one of SM Entertainment's most commercially valuable artists of her generation.

## Early Life & Background

Kim Min-jeong was born on January 1, 2001, in Daegu, South Korea. She trained at SM Entertainment after being recruited through the company's audition process, undergoing several years of rigorous training in singing, dancing, and performance before debuting. Details about her pre-debut life remain relatively private, though she has referenced her hometown of Daegu in interviews and expressed a strong personal connection to dance as a formative passion from a young age.

## Rise to Prominence

Winter debuted as a member of aespa on November 17, 2020, with the single "Black Mamba." The group's concept, which blended real-world members with AI avatar counterparts in a shared virtual universe called the "SM Culture Universe," was unprecedented in K-pop and drew immediate global attention. "Black Mamba" broke the record for the fastest MV by a K-pop debut act to reach 100 million views on YouTube at the time.

Winter's technical precision in dance and her distinctive, cool-toned visuals made her a focal point in aespa's performances and promotional material. The group's subsequent releases — including "Next Level" (2021), "Savage" (2021), and "Girls" (2022) — each charted internationally and expanded aespa's fanbase, known as MYs, across Asia, North America, and Europe.

## Key Achievements

- Co-credited on aespa's debut single "Black Mamba," which debuted at No. 1 on the Gaon Digital Chart in 2020.
- Part of aespa's performance at the 2022 Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival, making the group among the first fourth-generation K-pop acts to perform at the event.
- Featured in multiple high-profile fashion campaigns, including work with luxury and global beauty brands that underscored her crossover appeal beyond music.
- Contributed to aespa's debut full-length album *Armageddon* (2024), which achieved strong charting positions on the Billboard 200 and Gaon Album Chart.

## Controversies & Criticism

Winter has largely avoided major personal controversies. Some criticism has been directed at SM Entertainment's handling of aespa's concept and scheduling, which fans have argued placed excessive demands on the members. Like many high-profile K-pop idols, she has been subject to intense public scrutiny regarding appearance and personal life, though she has generally maintained a low-profile outside of official activities.

## Legacy & Influence

As a core member of aespa, Winter has contributed to reshaping K-pop's engagement with technology and virtual identity. The group's metaverse concept influenced how subsequent acts approached world-building and cross-media storytelling. Her combination of technical dance skill, visual branding, and consistent performance quality has made her a reference point for aspiring K-pop trainees and a recognizable figure in global pop culture by the mid-2020s.
