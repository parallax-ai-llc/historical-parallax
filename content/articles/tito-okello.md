## Summary

Ugandan military leader who overthrew Obote and briefly held power. His forces committed widespread atrocities against civilians in northern Uganda.

## Additional context

Okello’s brief presidency is generally analyzed as part of Uganda’s mid-1980s transition, where military factionalism and civil conflict were intertwined with efforts to negotiate a settlement. His government attempted to pursue talks while also managing a deteriorating security situation and limited administrative capacity.

### Key themes
- **Military politics:** competing factions within armed forces can rapidly reshape governance during coups and counter-coups.
- **Negotiation under pressure:** peace talks are difficult to sustain when major parties believe they can win militarily.
- **Civilian impact:** instability and conflict frequently produce displacement, abuses, and long-lasting regional grievances.
