## Summary

Do Kwon's TerraUSD algorithmic stablecoin collapse erased $40 billion in market value and triggered a crypto market crash. Kwon fled and was arrested with fake passports.

## Background

- Terraform Labs was founded to build blockchain-based payment and financial applications, later centered on the Terra ecosystem.
- The project became closely associated with Do Kwon and the TerraUSD (UST) stablecoin alongside the LUNA token.

## Products and Ecosystem

- TerraUSD was marketed as a stablecoin designed to maintain a $1 peg through algorithmic market incentives.
- Applications in the ecosystem included payments and decentralized finance (DeFi) products that attracted users during the broader crypto bull market.

## Collapse and Immediate Fallout

- In May 2022, UST lost its peg and LUNA entered a rapid collapse, destroying large amounts of market value over a short period.
- The failure triggered emergency proposals within the community, including chain governance actions and attempts to restart or fork the network.

## Investigations and Legal Disputes

- After the collapse, regulators and prosecutors in multiple jurisdictions opened investigations and filed actions focused on investor protection and alleged misrepresentations.
- Do Kwon's later arrest and extradition-related proceedings became a high-profile part of the story, but the broader enforcement focus also included corporate conduct and disclosures.

## Industry Impact

- The episode is widely cited in debates about stablecoin regulation, disclosure standards, and the risks of reflexive token-economic designs.
- It also contributed to a shift in sentiment toward higher scrutiny of crypto lending, leverage, and interconnected counterparties.
