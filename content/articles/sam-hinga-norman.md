## Summary

Sierra Leonean CDF national coordinator charged with war crimes for militia atrocities during the civil war. He died before his trial could be completed.

## Background

The Civil Defence Forces (CDF) were a collection of militia groups aligned with the Sierra Leone government during the civil war. They were often described as community-based “self-defense” forces, but multiple investigations and prosecutions later alleged serious abuses by some CDF units.

## Special Court Charges (High Level)

The **Special Court for Sierra Leone (SCSL)** pursued cases against individuals it said bore the greatest responsibility for war crimes and crimes against humanity. In public summaries of the SCSL process, the CDF case is commonly used to illustrate that accountability efforts were not limited to rebel groups.

## Why the Case Is Notable

- It highlighted the difficulty of distinguishing “pro-government” armed actors from lawful security forces in civil conflicts.
- It underscored that civilian protection obligations apply regardless of a group’s stated purpose.
- Norman’s death before a verdict is often cited as an example of how time and health can affect transitional justice proceedings.
