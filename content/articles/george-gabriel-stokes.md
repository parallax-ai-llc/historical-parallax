## Summary

George Gabriel Stokes was an Irish-British mathematician and physicist who made important contributions to fluid dynamics, optics, and mathematical physics. He formulated the Navier-Stokes equations, discovered fluorescence (Stokes shift), and developed Stokes' theorem. He served as Lucasian Professor of Mathematics at Cambridge and president of the Royal Society.
