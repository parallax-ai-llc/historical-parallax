## Summary

Russias full-scale invasion of Ukraine beginning February 24, 2022 resulting in tens of thousands of civilian deaths, war crimes at Bucha and Irpin, and millions of refugees.

## Background

- The 2022 invasion followed years of conflict after Russia’s 2014 annexation of Crimea and the outbreak of war in eastern Ukraine.
- Russia’s actions triggered major diplomatic ruptures and a reorientation of European security policy.

## Humanitarian Impact

- The war caused large-scale civilian casualties, internal displacement, and refugee flows into neighboring countries and across Europe.
- Critical infrastructure attacks contributed to electricity and heating shortages, particularly during winter periods.

## International Response

- Many states and international bodies condemned the invasion, while Western countries imposed sanctions and provided military, financial, and humanitarian support to Ukraine.
- NATO countries increased readiness and expanded support measures, and several European states accelerated defense spending plans.

## Accountability and Investigations

- Multiple investigations examined alleged violations of international humanitarian law.
- The International Criminal Court issued arrest warrants connected to the conflict, including a 2023 warrant for Vladimir Putin.

## Ongoing Significance

- The invasion is widely regarded as one of the most consequential European conflicts since World War II, reshaping energy policy, defense planning, and global geopolitical alignments.
