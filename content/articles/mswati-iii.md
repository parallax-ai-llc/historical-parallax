## Role and Political System

Mswati III is the monarch of Eswatini (formerly Swaziland) and is often described as one of the last absolute monarchs in Africa. The country has a parliament and elections, but the monarchy retains significant influence over executive authority and key appointments.

## Accession and Governance

He was crowned in 1986. Over subsequent decades, Eswatini's political system has been the subject of domestic debate regarding political parties, civil liberties, and the distribution of state resources.

## Social and Economic Context

Eswatini faces major public-health and economic challenges, including a high HIV prevalence and persistent poverty. Development indicators and public spending priorities are frequent topics in reporting and advocacy related to the country.

## Name Change (2018)

In 2018 the country's English name was changed from Swaziland to Eswatini, which reflects the siSwati name for the nation. The change was presented as a step toward asserting national identity and reducing confusion with similarly named countries.

## Protests and International Attention

Periods of protest and unrest, including in 2021, drew attention from regional and international organizations. Discussions have included calls for political reform, accountability for security-force actions, and dialogue between the government and civil society.
