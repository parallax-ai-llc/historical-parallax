## Summary

Xie Xide was a Chinese physicist who became the first female president of Fudan University and a pioneer of semiconductor physics in China. She helped establish surface physics research in China and trained a generation of Chinese physicists. Despite physical disabilities from childhood tuberculosis, she made major contributions to Chinese science.
