## Summary

Polish serial killer suspected of over 70 murders across northern Poland, convicted of one.

## Names and Public Profile

- Often referred to in media as **"The Vampire of Bytów"**.
- Polish murderer and suspected serial killer; estimates of the number of victims vary widely across reporting and investigations.

## Reported Crimes (High-Level)

- Reported span of crimes: **1984–1992** (as commonly summarized in secondary sources).
- He was suspected of multiple killings, but **only one murder was proven in court** according to widely cited accounts.
- During proceedings and interviews, he reportedly made extremely high admissions (e.g., dozens of victims) that he later **retracted**.

## Investigation and Prosecution (Selected Points)

- **1992:** Initially arrested in connection with the rape of a **17-year-old girl (Sylwia)**; he received a **two-year suspended sentence** in that case (as reported).
- **1996:** Convicted of murdering Sylwia and sentenced to **25 years' imprisonment**; this remained the only homicide conviction attributed to him in court records summarized by major references.

## Detention, Release, and Post-Release Measures

- From **2007**, he was reportedly eligible to apply for **conditional early release**, but applications were repeatedly rejected in public reporting.
- **11 December 2017:** Reported release after serving the custodial sentence, followed by transfer for evaluation to Poland's National Center for the Prevention of Dyssocial Behavior in **Gostynin**.
- Post-release, he has been described as subject to periodic psychiatric evaluation, with a court determining whether continued detention is warranted.

## Context and Uncertainty

- Public narratives about Pękalski include very large claimed victim counts (sometimes "70+" or higher), but these figures are **disputed** and not all are supported by court convictions.
- Summaries typically distinguish between:
  - **Allegations / investigative suspicions**, and
  - **Proven convictions** (notably, the single murder conviction).
