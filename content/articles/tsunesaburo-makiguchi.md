## Summary

Tsunesaburo Makiguchi was a Japanese educator and the founder and first president of Soka Gakkai. He developed a theory of value-creating education and combined it with Nichiren Buddhism. He was imprisoned during World War II for refusing to merge Soka Gakkai with State Shinto and died in prison. His educational philosophy emphasizing value creation remains foundational to the Soka Gakkai movement.
