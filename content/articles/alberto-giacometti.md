## Summary

Alberto Giacometti was a Swiss sculptor and painter known for his elongated, emaciated figures that embody existential solitude and anxiety. His iconic Walking Man and standing figures are among the most powerful sculptures of the 20th century. His work explores the fundamental problem of representing the human figure in space.
