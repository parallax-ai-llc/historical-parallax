## Summary

The 14th Dalai Lama, Tenzin Gyatso, is the spiritual leader of Tibetan Buddhism and former political leader of the Tibetan government-in-exile. He fled Tibet in 1959 after the Chinese invasion and has lived in Dharamsala, India, since then. He received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1989 for his non-violent advocacy for Tibetan autonomy. He is one of the most recognized and respected religious leaders in the world.
