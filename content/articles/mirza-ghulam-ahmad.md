## Summary

Mirza Ghulam Ahmad was an Indian religious leader who founded the Ahmadiyya Muslim Community in 1889. He claimed to be the promised Messiah and Mahdi awaited by Muslims, the second coming of Christ, and the avatar of Krishna. His movement, now with tens of millions of followers, is considered heretical by mainstream Islam. He wrote over 80 books on Islam, comparative religion, and apologetics. The Ahmadiyya face persecution in Pakistan and other Muslim countries.
