## Summary

Nigerian senator and businessman who bankrolled the annulment of the 1993 presidential election. Used his Association for Better Nigeria to derail democracy for personal gain.

## Background

Arthur Nzeribe was a Nigerian businessman and politician associated with Imo State. In national politics he is best known for his involvement in high-profile political controversies during Nigeria's transition era in the early 1990s, as well as for later service as a senator.

## Role in the 1993 Election Crisis

Nigeria's June 12, 1993 presidential election is widely remembered as a pivotal moment in the country's modern political history. After the vote was annulled, multiple political actors, pressure groups, and state institutions contested the outcome.

Nzeribe was linked to the Association for Better Nigeria (ABN), a group reported to have supported the annulment and advocated for the continuation of the existing military-led arrangement. Contemporary and later reporting often portrays ABN activities as part of the broader effort that helped block the transfer of power following the election.

## Political Career and Public Image

In addition to his business activities, Nzeribe held elected office and appeared frequently in Nigerian political discourse as a power broker. Public discussion of his career has often emphasized:

- influence exercised through political organizing and advocacy groups,
- allegations of corruption or political manipulation (contested in public debate),
- recurring involvement in factional or transitional-era disputes.

Because much of his reputation is tied to politically charged events, portrayals of Nzeribe can differ sharply depending on source, era, and the narrator's political alignment.

## Later Life

Nzeribe remained a recognized figure in Nigerian politics into the 2000s and 2010s, including a period in the Senate, before his death in 2022.
