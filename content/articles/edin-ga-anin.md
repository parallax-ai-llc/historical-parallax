## Overview

Edin Gačanin is a Bosnian-born figure described by European law-enforcement reporting as a senior organizer in cocaine trafficking networks operating across multiple jurisdictions. Public reporting frequently associates his network with large-scale importation routes into Western Europe, including logistics tied to major ports.

## Alleged Role in Cocaine Supply Chains

High-level descriptions of the network often emphasize:

- Coordination of sourcing and transport of cocaine into Europe.
- Use of intermediaries and compartmentalized teams to manage procurement, shipping, storage, and distribution.
- Reliance on international logistics hubs to move shipments and reduce detection risk.

## Law-Enforcement Attention and Designations

Gačanin has been publicly named in Europol-related communications and has been referenced in sanction and “most wanted” style listings. Such designations typically reflect an assessment that an individual plays an enabling or leadership role rather than a single isolated offense.
