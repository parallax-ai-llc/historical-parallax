## Case Context (Orderud Farm Murders)

- The **Orderud farm murders** refer to a high-profile Norwegian criminal case involving the killing of **three members of the same family** at the Orderud farm.
- Per Kristian Orderud was **convicted** for arranging (orchestrating) the crime rather than being described as the sole direct perpetrator in summaries of the case.
- The case is often cited in Norwegian media as one of the country’s most **notorious** murder investigations of the late 1990s.

## Investigation and Trial (High-Level)

- The investigation received **substantial national attention**, in part due to the family context and disputes linked to the farm.
- Orderud’s conviction is commonly summarized as being for **planning/ordering** the murders.
- After conviction, the case continued to be discussed in Norway through **appeals and public debate** about evidence and responsibility.

## Why the Case Is Remembered

- The murders became a reference point in Norway for:
  - extended, complex criminal investigations
  - intensive media coverage of court proceedings
  - long-running public discussion around major criminal convictions
