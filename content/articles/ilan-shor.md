## Background

Ilan Shor is a Moldovan (and also reported as Israeli-born) businessman who became a prominent political figure in Moldova. He has been associated with business holdings as well as political party activity.

## "Theft of the century" banking scandal

Shor is widely linked in reporting and court proceedings to the 2014 Moldovan banking scandal often referred to as the "theft of the century," involving the disappearance of roughly $1 billion from the country's financial system via a complex set of loans and transfers.

High-level elements often described include:

- multiple banks affected and the use of related-party lending
- state intervention to stabilize the banking system
- long-running domestic political fallout and public anger

## Political career

Shor served as mayor of Orhei and later led or was associated with political movements commonly known as the Shor Party. His political activities, including protests and campaigning, have been polarizing within Moldova.

## Legal status and international attention

Court outcomes and procedural details have changed over time (including appeals and retrials reported in the press). Shor has also been the target of international measures such as sanctions, reflecting concerns about corruption and foreign influence.
