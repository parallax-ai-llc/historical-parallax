## Summary

Howard Florey was an Australian pharmacologist and pathologist who shared the 1945 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine for the development of penicillin as a therapeutic drug. While Fleming discovered penicillin, Florey and Chain transformed it into a practical medicine that saved millions of lives during World War II and beyond. He is considered one of the most important figures in the history of medicine.
