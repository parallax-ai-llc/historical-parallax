## Summary

Zou Shiming won Olympic boxing medals at three consecutive Games (2004 bronze, 2008 gold, 2012 bronze) for China.

## Amateur Career Highlights

Zou Shiming was one of China’s most successful amateur boxers of his era. In addition to his Olympic results, he competed extensively in international amateur tournaments, including World Championships and Asian competitions, and is often cited as a key figure in raising the visibility of boxing within China’s Olympic sports system.

## Professional Boxing

After the 2012 Olympics, Zou transitioned to professional boxing, a move that drew attention because China’s boxing scene historically had fewer globally prominent professional champions. He fought primarily in the lighter weight classes and pursued a world title.

He later won a major professional championship belt (commonly reported as the WBO flyweight title), marking a rare crossover success from elite amateur boxing to professional world-level status for a Chinese boxer.

## Style and Reputation

Zou was known for technical footwork, speed, and ring craft developed in the amateur system. Discussions of his professional career often focus on how amateur-style tactics translate to longer professional bouts and different judging criteria.

## Legacy

- Helped popularize boxing in China by combining Olympic success with a high-profile professional career.
- Served as a reference point for later Chinese boxers aiming for international titles.
- Frequently cited in Chinese sports media as an example of sustained excellence across multiple Olympic cycles.
