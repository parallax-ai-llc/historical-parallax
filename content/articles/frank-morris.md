## Summary

Mastermind of the 1962 Alcatraz escape who spent months crafting tools and a raft from raincoats. His fate remains one of America's greatest mysteries.

## The 1962 Alcatraz Escape Plan

- The escape is commonly described as a months-long effort involving improvised tools, ventilation openings, and decoy heads placed in beds to delay detection.
- The plan relied on a homemade raft and life preservers assembled from raincoats, illustrating both planning and resourcefulness.

## Uncertain Outcome

- Because no definitive bodies were recovered, the outcome remains disputed: some believe the men drowned in cold, rough waters, while others argue they may have survived.
- US authorities later listed Morris and the other escapees as presumed dead, but public fascination has persisted due to the lack of conclusive evidence.

## Cultural Impact

- The case became one of the most famous prison-escape stories in American history and inspired books, documentaries, and films.
- It is frequently used as an example in discussions of prison security, risk assessment, and the limits of certainty in historical investigations.

## Notes

This page summarizes well-known public reporting; definitive claims about Morris's fate are not possible without new evidence.

## Additional Details

- Alcatraz's setting in San Francisco Bay created natural hazards (cold water, strong currents) that factor into survival estimates.
- Later public tips and claimed sightings have been reported over the years, but none have produced universally accepted proof.
- The case remains open in popular culture primarily because physical evidence supports both escape preparation and the possibility of failure at sea.
