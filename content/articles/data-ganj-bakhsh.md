## Summary

Ali Hujwiri, known as Data Ganj Bakhsh (Bestower of Treasures), was a Persian Sufi scholar who is considered the patron saint of Lahore, Pakistan. He wrote Kashf al-Mahjub (Revelation of the Veiled), the oldest Persian treatise on Sufism. He is credited with bringing Islam to the Punjab region of South Asia. His shrine in Lahore is one of the most visited in South Asia, attracting millions annually.
