## Summary

American thief known as the 'Barefoot Bandit' who committed over 100 burglaries and stole cars and airplanes as a teenager, teaching himself to fly. He became a folk hero with a Facebook fan page. Captured in the Bahamas.

## Additional Context

- **Alias:** "Barefoot Bandit" refers to reporting that he often moved without shoes during burglaries and flight from authorities.
- **Geography:** Many incidents were associated with the U.S. Pacific Northwest (notably Washington state), before his eventual capture abroad.
- **Public fascination:** Online fan communities framed him as a renegade figure; critics emphasized the property damage and risk to public safety.

## Pattern of Offenses (high-level)

- Residential and commercial **burglaries**
- **Vehicle theft** (cars and boats)
- **Aircraft theft** and unauthorized flights, with at least one high-profile crash landing

## Arrest and Legal Outcome

- **Capture:** He was apprehended in the **Bahamas** after an incident involving a stolen aircraft.
- **Sentencing:** He received a multi-year federal sentence (commonly summarized as about **7 years**) and restitution orders.

## Broader Themes

- **Media amplification:** The case is often cited as an early example of social-media-era mythmaking around an active criminal case.
- **Risk profile:** Unauthorized aviation activity created significant danger for bystanders and responders, beyond property loss.
