## Summary

Rumi, also known as Mawlana or Mawlawi, was a 13th-century Persian poet, Islamic scholar, and Sufi mystic. His masterwork, the Masnavi-yi Ma'navi, is a six-volume epic poem considered one of the greatest works of mystical literature. His Divan-i Shams-i Tabrizi contains thousands of lyrical poems. He founded the Mevlevi Order, famous for the whirling dervish ceremony (Sema). He remains the best-selling poet in the United States.
