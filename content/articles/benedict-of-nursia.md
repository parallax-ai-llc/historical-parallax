## Summary

Benedict of Nursia is considered the founder of Western monasticism. His Rule of Saint Benedict became the foundational guide for monastic communities throughout medieval Europe. He established the monastery of Monte Cassino, which became one of the most important centers of Western civilization. His motto 'Ora et Labora' (Pray and Work) defined the monastic ideal.
