## Summary

The pseudonymous Italian novelist whose Neapolitan Quartet became a global sensation. Her intense exploration of female friendship in postwar Naples created one of the most acclaimed literary series of the 21st century.

## Additional Context

- **Pseudonym and anonymity**: “Elena Ferrante” is a pen name; the author has maintained anonymity while communicating through written interviews and letters.
- **Major works**: In addition to the Neapolitan Quartet, her novels include *Troubling Love* (1992), *The Days of Abandonment* (2002), and *The Lost Daughter* (2006).
- **Setting and themes**: Much of her fiction is set in and around Naples and is known for frank depictions of class, family, gender, education, and social mobility.

## Influence and Adaptations

- The Neapolitan Quartet was adapted into the television series *My Brilliant Friend* (Italian: *L'amica geniale*).
- Her international readership expanded significantly through translations, particularly in English.

## Key Takeaways

- Ferrante is notable not only for the popularity of her books but also for the rare combination of global literary acclaim and sustained authorial anonymity.
