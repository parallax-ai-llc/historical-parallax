## Summary

Algerian president who ruled for 20 years and attempted to run for a fifth term despite being incapacitated by a stroke. Mass protests (Hirak movement) forced his resignation in 2019. His brothers and associates were convicted of corruption.

## Overview

Abdelaziz Bouteflika (1937–2021) was an Algerian politician who served as the President of Algeria from 1999 until his resignation in 2019. His presidency followed a period of intense internal conflict in Algeria during the 1990s, and his early years in office were closely associated with state-led reconciliation initiatives.

## Political context and major themes

- **National reconciliation:** His administration promoted policies that aimed to reduce violence and reintegrate some former insurgents, shaping the post–civil conflict period.
- **State institutions and longevity in office:** Bouteflika’s long tenure included several constitutional and political shifts that affected presidential term limits and the balance of power.
- **Late presidency and resignation:** In later years his health became a central public issue, and mass protests in 2019 contributed to his resignation.

## Legacy

Bouteflika remains a debated figure in modern Algerian history, associated both with a relative stabilization after widespread violence and with persistent concerns about governance, corruption, and political openness during his time in power.
