## Summary

Filipino-American journalist and Nobel Peace Prize laureate convicted of cyber libel in a case seen as politically motivated.

## Career Timeline

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1986 | Graduated from Princeton University and began work in U.S. broadcast journalism. |
| 1995 | Joined CNN as bureau chief in Manila; later covered Southeast Asia. |
| 2005 | Became head of CNN’s Jakarta bureau (Indonesia). |
| 2012 | Co-founded Rappler, a Philippine news site focused on investigative reporting and social media distribution. |
| 2016–2019 | Rappler faced sustained government pressure, including multiple investigations and legal cases. |
| 2020 | Convicted of cyber libel in a case widely criticized by press freedom advocates. |
| 2021 | Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize (jointly) for efforts to safeguard freedom of expression. |

## Key Contributions and Themes

- **Press freedom and accountability**: Known for reporting on governance, disinformation, and the impact of social platforms on democratic institutions.
- **Digital-era investigative journalism**: Built Rappler with an emphasis on community-driven distribution and data-informed reporting.
- **Rule-of-law framing**: Frequently emphasizes that attacks on journalism are often pursued through legal and procedural mechanisms rather than overt censorship.

## Notable Work

- **Rappler (co-founder/CEO)**: Philippine-based news organization recognized internationally for investigative reporting and coverage of online disinformation.
- **International broadcasting**: Served in senior editorial roles at CNN, covering conflict, politics, and regional security issues in Southeast Asia.

## Awards and Recognition

- **Nobel Peace Prize (2021)**: Awarded jointly with Dmitry Muratov for efforts to safeguard freedom of expression, described by the Nobel Committee as a prerequisite for democracy and lasting peace.[^2]

## Impact (High-Level)

- Ressa’s cases became widely cited examples in debates about **media independence**, **strategic litigation**, and **state pressure on independent outlets**.
- Her advocacy helped mainstream the concept that **disinformation ecosystems** can be a structural threat comparable to traditional forms of political violence.
