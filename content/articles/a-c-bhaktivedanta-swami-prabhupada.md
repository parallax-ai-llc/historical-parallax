## Summary

A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada was an Indian spiritual teacher who founded the International Society for Krishna Consciousness (ISKCON), popularly known as the Hare Krishna movement. At age 69, he traveled to the United States with little money and spread Krishna bhakti (devotion) worldwide. He translated and commented on over 80 volumes of Vedic literature, including the Bhagavad Gita As It Is. ISKCON grew into a worldwide movement with millions of followers.
