## Summary

Widespread use of child labor in artisanal cobalt mines in the DRC, which produces 60% of the worlds cobalt used in lithium-ion batteries for smartphones and electric vehicles.

## Additional Context

- **Why cobalt matters:** Cobalt is widely used in many lithium-ion battery chemistries to improve **energy density** and **stability**.
- **Where the risk concentrates:** The DRC has both industrial mines and **artisanal and small-scale mining (ASM)**. Child labor concerns are most often reported in ASM supply chains.
- **Typical hazards reported:** Unstable hand-dug tunnels, exposure to dust and heavy metals, long hours, and injuries from collapses.

## Supply-Chain and Accountability Themes

- **Traceability challenge:** Cobalt from many small sites can be aggregated through traders and depots, making origin verification difficult.
- **Due-diligence responses:** Companies and industry groups have promoted audits, third-party traceability programs, and supplier requirements, with uneven adoption and results.
- **Litigation and advocacy:** Lawsuits and NGO reports have been used to pressure technology and automotive firms that rely on cobalt inputs.

## Common Policy Approaches Discussed

- Enforcement of child-labor prohibitions paired with **school access** and **income alternatives** for families.
- Formalization of ASM, including safer work practices and regulated trading channels.
- Transparency commitments for battery materials and downstream reporting.
