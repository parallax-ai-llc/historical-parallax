## Summary

Transitional president of Mali who was arrested and removed from power by Colonel Assimi Goïta in a second coup. He had been appointed to lead the transition to democracy.

## Military and public-service background

Bah Ndaw is described in public sources as a Malian military officer who later held senior governmental roles.
According to Wikipedia, he also served as Mali’s Minister of Defense and Veterans Affairs (mid-2010s).
This combination of military credentials and cabinet experience positioned him as an acceptable compromise figure during a political transition.

## Role in the 2020 transition

Following Mali’s 2020 coup and subsequent negotiations over a civilian-led transition, Ndaw was appointed transitional president.
The transitional arrangement also included a vice president role held by Assimi Goïta.
In practice, transitional governments in Mali have faced competing pressures from:

- Domestic protest movements and political parties.
- Security challenges related to insurgency and instability.
- International partners and regional organizations demanding a return to elections.

## Removal in the 2021 “second coup”

In May 2021, Ndaw and Prime Minister Moctar Ouane were detained by soldiers, and Ndaw was later removed from office.
Reporting summarized on Wikipedia frames the episode as a second coup that consolidated Goïta’s control of the transition.
Ndaw’s removal is often cited as a turning point that altered both the transition timeline and external engagement with Mali.

## Significance (high-level)

Ndaw’s brief presidency is frequently discussed in terms of:

- The fragility of power-sharing arrangements after coups.
- Civil–military dynamics in transitional periods.
- The difficulty of maintaining constitutional pathways amid security crises.
