## Summary

American mafioso who served as boss of the Patriarca crime family in New England. He was convicted of racketeering and extortion.

## Additional Context

## Patriarca Crime Family

Ferrara is associated with the Patriarca crime family, a New England-based Mafia organization historically linked to racketeering and control over illicit and semi-legitimate businesses.

As a reputed leader, his profile is typically framed around:

- Internal organized-crime governance (boss, underboss, captains, crews).
- Revenue streams such as extortion, loansharking, and illegal gambling.
- The use of intermediaries to reduce direct exposure to law enforcement.

## Federal Case and Charges

The U.S. government has commonly pursued Mafia leadership through federal racketeering statutes, especially RICO (Racketeer Influenced and Corrupt Organizations Act).

In Ferrara’s case, reporting centers on convictions for offenses such as:

- Racketeering-related conduct.
- Extortion and coercive collection schemes.

## Sentencing and Aftermath

Convictions of senior figures in a crime family often have two large effects:

- Disrupting leadership succession and creating internal instability.
- Increasing cooperation incentives among lower-ranking members.

Ferrara’s imprisonment is therefore typically presented as part of a broader period of federal pressure on organized crime in the late 20th century.

## Historical Significance

This biography is often cited as an example of:

- How federal prosecutions can target leadership rather than only street-level offenses.
- The shift from localized policing to multi-agency investigations against organized crime networks.
