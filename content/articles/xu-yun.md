## Summary

Xu Yun (Empty Cloud) was a Chinese Chan (Zen) Buddhist master who is considered one of the most important and long-lived Buddhist monks in Chinese history, reportedly living to 119 years. He revitalized Chinese Buddhism during a period of war and revolution, restoring over 80 temples and ordaining thousands of monks. He survived the Chinese Civil War and the early Communist era, enduring beatings at age 112 during a political campaign.
