## Summary

Adolphus Busch co-founded Anheuser-Busch and built Budweiser into America's most popular beer brand. A German immigrant, he married into the Anheuser family and transformed their small St. Louis brewery into a national brand. He pioneered the use of pasteurization, refrigerated railroad cars, and national distribution in brewing. Budweiser became 'The King of Beers' and one of the most recognized brands worldwide.
