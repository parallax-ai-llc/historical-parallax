## Summary

Wi Ha-joon is a South Korean actor who rose to international prominence following his role in the global Netflix phenomenon *Squid Game* (2021). Known for his striking screen presence and ability to portray morally complex characters, he has quickly established himself as one of South Korea's most sought-after leading men. His career trajectory — from modeling to supporting roles to headline-grabbing performances — reflects both his personal discipline and the surging global appetite for Korean entertainment.

Beyond *Squid Game*, Wi has demonstrated range across genres, from thriller to romance, proving he is not a one-project star. His appearances in critically received dramas have cemented his reputation as a versatile actor capable of anchoring major productions, and his growing international fanbase has made him a significant cultural export for the Korean entertainment industry.

---

## Early Life & Background

Wi Ha-joon was born on August 5, 1990, in Seoul, South Korea. He developed an early interest in performance and the arts, eventually transitioning from a modeling career into acting. He studied at Konkuk University, where he pursued a degree related to the performing arts. His modeling background gave him a strong visual foundation, but he worked deliberately to build credible acting credentials rather than relying solely on his appearance.

---

## Rise to Prominence

Wi made his acting debut in 2017 with a small role in the film *Romance Full of Life*. He steadily built his resume through supporting parts in Korean dramas, including *18 Again* (2020), where he began attracting wider attention. The true turning point came in September 2021, when Netflix released *Squid Game*. Wi played Hwang Jun-ho, a police officer who infiltrates the deadly competition searching for his missing brother. The show became Netflix's most-watched series at the time, and Wi's compelling performance earned him global recognition almost overnight.

Following *Squid Game*, he starred in the romance thriller *Little Women* (2022) and the romantic drama *Bad and Crazy* (2021–2022), the latter earning him strong reviews for his comedic and dramatic balance. In 2024, he reprised his role in *Squid Game* Season 2, further consolidating his international profile.

## Key Achievements

- **Squid Game (2021):** Part of the cast of the most-watched Netflix original series in history at the time of release, with over 1.65 billion viewing hours in its first 28 days.
- **Baeksang Arts Awards recognition:** Received nominations and industry attention following his work in multiple acclaimed productions.
- **Squid Game Season 2 (2024):** Returned for the sequel, one of Netflix's most anticipated follow-up productions.
- Established a dual career as both a leading man in romance dramas and a credible presence in high-stakes thriller narratives.

## Controversies & Criticism

Wi Ha-joon has maintained a relatively clean public profile. No significant controversies have surrounded his personal or professional life. Some early critics noted that his modeling background led to skepticism about his acting depth, a perception he has largely dispelled through consistent dramatic work.

## Legacy & Influence

Wi Ha-joon represents a generation of Korean actors who have leveraged the global streaming boom to build careers with genuinely international reach. His role in *Squid Game* made him a recognizable face far beyond East Asia, contributing to the broader soft-power influence of Korean pop culture. As Korean drama and film continue to command global audiences, Wi stands as an example of how disciplined craft combined with the right platform can transform a regional actor into a worldwide presence.
