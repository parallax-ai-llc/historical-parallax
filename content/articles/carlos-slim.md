## Summary

Mexican telecommunications magnate who became the world's richest man through a near-monopoly on Mexican phone services acquired through controversial privatization deals.

## Early Life and Business Formation

Slim was born in Mexico City to a family of Lebanese-Mexican background and began investing at a young age. Over time he consolidated holdings across retail, industrial, and financial sectors, building what became Grupo Carso and related entities.

## Telecommunications Expansion

The acquisition of Telmex during Mexico’s privatization era became the cornerstone of Slim’s wealth, with later growth through América Móvil and other telecom assets across Latin America. Critics have argued that market concentration contributed to high prices and limited competition, while supporters have pointed to investment, network expansion, and modernization.

## Regulation and Competition Debates

Mexican regulators and courts have periodically addressed issues of market power in telecommunications, including measures intended to increase competition and reduce consumer costs. These disputes became a recurring feature of Mexico’s economic policy debates.

## Wealth Rankings and Public Profile

Slim was at times ranked as the world’s richest person in major wealth lists, reflecting the scale of his business interests. His prominence made him a symbol of both Mexico’s global business reach and domestic concerns about oligopoly.

## Philanthropy

Through foundations and charitable programs, Slim has funded health, education, culture, and disaster-relief initiatives. Evaluations of his philanthropy vary, but it has been a significant component of his public image.
