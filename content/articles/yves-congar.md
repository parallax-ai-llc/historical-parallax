## Summary

Yves Congar was a French Dominican theologian and cardinal who was one of the most influential Catholic ecclesiologists of the 20th century. His work on ecclesiology, ecumenism, and the role of the laity helped shape the Second Vatican Council. He was initially silenced by the Vatican but later vindicated. He was created cardinal by Pope John Paul II in 1994.
