## Summary

Kinji Imanishi was a Japanese ecologist and primatologist who founded Japanese primatology and developed a unique theory of evolution emphasizing habitat segregation over competition. He pioneered the study of primate social behavior, identifying cultural behavior in Japanese macaques decades before Western primatologists. His ecological approach influenced conservation biology.
