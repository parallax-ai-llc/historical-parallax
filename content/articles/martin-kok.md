## Background

- Martin Kok was a Dutch figure known publicly for writing about organized crime and criminal cases in the Netherlands.
- He had prior convictions and was also known for operating in Amsterdam’s nightlife milieu.

## Writing and Public Profile

- Kok gained attention through crime-focused blogging, where he commented on underworld dynamics, high-profile cases, and rumors circulating in criminal circles.
- His online presence intersected with broader Dutch discussions about the visibility and influence of organized crime.

## Assassination

- Kok was shot dead on 8 December 2016 in Laren, Netherlands.
- Reporting describes the shooting as occurring outside a sex club, consistent with accounts that the killing resembled a targeted assassination.

## Context and Aftermath (High-level)

- The killing is frequently discussed in the context of escalating violence and “settling of scores” linked to organized-crime conflicts.
- Public commentary around the case often emphasizes the risks faced by individuals who publicize or circulate information about criminal networks.
