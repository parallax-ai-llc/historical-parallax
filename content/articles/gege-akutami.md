## Summary

Gege Akutami (born February 26, 1992) is a Japanese manga artist best known as the creator of *Jujutsu Kaisen*, one of the defining shonen manga series of the 2020s. Published in Weekly Shōnen Jump since 2018, the series propelled Akutami to the upper tier of manga creators, drawing comparisons to industry legends for its intricate power systems, morally complex characters, and willingness to subvert genre conventions.

Akutami's work stands out for its dark tone, philosophical underpinnings, and refusal to guarantee protagonist safety — a sharp departure from the optimistic arc typical of shonen manga. This narrative ruthlessness, combined with dynamic artwork and inventive supernatural mechanics, earned *Jujutsu Kaisen* a global readership in the tens of millions and a prominent anime adaptation from studio MAPPA.

Despite widespread success, Akutami remains one of the more private figures in manga. Little biographical detail has been made public, and the creator has expressed ambivalence about fame, allowing the work itself to carry the conversation.

## Early Life & Background

Gege Akutami was born on February 26, 1992, in Iwate Prefecture, Japan. Details about early life and education are largely undisclosed, consistent with Akutami's preference for privacy. What is known is that the creator developed an early interest in manga and submitted work to Shueisha, the publisher behind Weekly Shōnen Jump, while still relatively young.

Akutami's debut came with the one-shot *Sayounara Sugar Life* in 2015, followed by the serialized series *Kamishiro no Setsu* (also known as *Noritaka*), which ran briefly in Jump GIGA in 2017 before cancellation. These early works established a dark sensibility that would fully emerge in subsequent projects.

## Rise to Prominence

*Jujutsu Kaisen* began serialization in Weekly Shōnen Jump in March 2018, following a well-received prequel one-shot, *Tokyo Metropolitan Curse Technical School*, published in 2017. The series found its footing quickly, blending horror-inflected action with a richly constructed world of cursed spirits and sorcerers.

Popularity surged globally with the 2020 anime adaptation by MAPPA, which became one of the most-watched anime of that year. The prequel film *Jujutsu Kaisen 0*, based on Akutami's 2017 manga, released in December 2021 and grossed over ¥13.8 billion (approximately $100 million USD) at the Japanese box office, becoming one of the highest-grossing anime films in Japanese history. By 2023, the manga had surpassed 80 million copies in circulation worldwide.

## Key Achievements

- *Jujutsu Kaisen* serialized in Weekly Shōnen Jump from March 2018
- Over 80 million copies in circulation globally as of 2023
- *Jujutsu Kaisen 0* film grossed over ¥13.8 billion domestically (2021)
- Winner of the 2021 Harvey Award for Best Manga
- Consistent placement in top-selling manga rankings from 2020 onward

## Controversies & Criticism

Akutami has drawn criticism from readers for the high volume of major character deaths throughout *Jujutsu Kaisen*, particularly during arcs such as the Shibuya Incident. Some fans accused the storytelling of being needlessly nihilistic or of undermining emotional investment. The series' pacing in later arcs drew mixed reception, with debate over whether the dense plot mechanics overshadowed character development.

Akutami has also faced scrutiny for comments made in manga volume author notes, including remarks about burnout and discomfort with the workload of weekly serialization — raising questions about working conditions in the manga industry more broadly.

## Legacy & Influence

Akutami redefined expectations for shonen manga in the 2020s, demonstrating that the genre could sustain morally ambiguous narratives, tragedy, and thematic complexity without sacrificing mainstream appeal. *Jujutsu Kaisen* influenced a wave of darker, more consequence-driven stories in the Jump lineup and beyond.

The series also contributed significantly to the global anime boom of the early 2020s, with MAPPA's adaptation serving as a benchmark for high-production-value action animation. Akutami's willingness to challenge genre norms has cemented a reputation as one of the most distinctive voices in contemporary manga.
