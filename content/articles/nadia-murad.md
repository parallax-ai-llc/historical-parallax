## Career Timeline

| 2014 | ISIS attacked the Sinjar region; Murad (a member of Iraq’s Yazidi minority) was abducted and enslaved. |

## Background

- **Community:** Yazidi (Êzîdî), an ethno-religious minority centered in northern Iraq.
- **From:** Kocho village, near Sinjar (Iraq).
- Her experience became one of the most widely cited survivor testimonies of ISIS’s campaign against Yazidis.

## Advocacy and Public Work

- Documented and publicized crimes including **mass killing**, **forced displacement**, **sexual slavery**, and **human trafficking**.
- Advocated for:
  - survivor-centered justice (legal accountability + protection for witnesses),
  - long-term recovery (mental health, education, livelihoods),
  - safe return and reconstruction for displaced communities.

## Nadia’s Initiative (overview)

Nadia’s Initiative is associated with community-based recovery work in areas affected by mass atrocities, with an emphasis on:

- rebuilding critical infrastructure (schools, clinics),
- supporting women-led and survivor-led programs,
- improving local governance capacity and stability in post-conflict settings.

## Nobel Peace Prize (2018)

- The Nobel Committee recognized Murad’s role in bringing global attention to **conflict-related sexual violence**.
- The prize is frequently referenced as a turning point in mainstream recognition of ISIS’s crimes against Yazidis.

## Key Themes

- Genocide recognition and documentation
- Human trafficking and modern slavery
- Survivor dignity, safety, and agency
- Accountability mechanisms for war crimes
