## Political career (overview)

- Edward Ngoyai Lowassa was a Tanzanian politician associated for much of his career with the long-ruling party Chama Cha Mapinduzi (CCM).
- In addition to serving as Prime Minister (2005–2008), he held senior cabinet roles and maintained a strong political network within Tanzania’s ruling and opposition landscapes.

## Richmond scandal and resignation

- Lowassa’s resignation followed parliamentary scrutiny related to an electricity generation contract commonly referred to as the “Richmond” scandal.
- The episode became a major public integrity controversy in Tanzania, and it is often cited in discussions of procurement oversight and governance.

## Later political activity

- In the 2010s, Lowassa remained a prominent national figure and was linked to presidential politics; international reporting described high-stakes party dynamics and shifting alliances.
- His career is frequently analyzed in terms of patronage networks, reform rhetoric, and the practical constraints of Tanzania’s political system.

## Legacy

- Supporters and critics alike treated Lowassa as an influential “power broker” whose career reflected both the reach of Tanzania’s political establishment and recurring concerns about corruption.
- The Richmond affair in particular is used as a reference point for the political costs of energy-sector scandals and for debates about accountability mechanisms.
